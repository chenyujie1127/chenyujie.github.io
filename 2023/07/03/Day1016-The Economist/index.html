The fight over working from home goes global

居家办公之争已蔓延至全球

The WFH showdown

WFH决战

Employees want to toil in the kitchen. Bosses want them back in the office

员工想在厨房里辛苦工作。老板希望他们回到办公室

image: getty images

Jul 10th 2023

Remote work has a target on its back. Banking ceos, like Jamie Dimon of JPMorgan Chase, are intent on making working from home a relic of the pandemic. Staff at America’s biggest lender and other Wall Street stalwarts like Goldman Sachs are finding that five-day weeks are back for good. Big tech companies are also cracking the whip. Google’s return-to-work mandate threatens to track attendance and factor it in performance reviews for rebellious employees. Meta and Lyft want staff back at their desks, demanding at least three days of the week in the office by the end of the summer. With bosses clamping down on the practice, the pandemic-era days of mutual agreement on the desirability of remote work seem to be over.

远程工作已经成为“攻击对象”。像摩根大通的杰米•戴蒙这样的银行CEO们，决心让居家办公成为疫情的遗留物。美国最大的银行和高盛等华尔街巨头的员工发现，每周五天工作制又回来了。大型科技公司也在“快马加鞭”。谷歌的重返工作岗位规定可能会追踪出勤情况，并将其纳入对叛逆员工的绩效评估。Meta和Lyft希望员工回到办公桌前，要求到今年夏末，每周至少有三天在办公室工作。由于老板们加强了对远程工作的限制，疫情时期互相认同远程工作的美好愿景似乎已经结束了。

Fresh data from a global survey shows just how far this consensus has broken down. Across the world, plans for remote working by employers fall short of what workers want, according to wfh Research, a group that includes Stanford University and the Ifo Institute, a German think-tank, which has tracked the sentiment of full-time workers with at least a secondary education in 34 countries. Corporate bosses fear that fully remote work dents productivity, a worry reinforced by a slew of recent research. One study of data-entry workers in India found those toiling from home to be 18% less productive than their office-frequenting peers; another found that employees at a big Asian it firm were 19% less productive at home than they had been in the office. Communication records of nearly 62,000 employees at Microsoft showed that professional networks within the company ossified and became more isolated as remote work took hold.

一项全球调查的最新数据显示，这种共识已经破裂了。包括斯坦福大学和德国智库Ifo研究所在内的wfh Research小组对34个国家至少受过中等教育的全职员工的情绪进行了跟踪调查。该机构表示，在全球范围内，雇主的远程工作计划未能满足工人的需求。公司老板们担心完全远程工作会降低生产率，最近的一系列研究进一步证实了这种担忧。一项针对印度数据录入工作者的研究发现，在家工作的人的工作效率比经常在办公室工作的同事低18%；另一项研究发现，亚洲一家大型IT公司的员工在家工作的效率比在办公室工作的效率低19%。微软近6.2万名员工的通信记录显示，随着远程工作的盛行，公司内部的“职业网”变得僵化和更加孤立。

Yet all the pressure from above has done little to dent employees’ appetite for remote working. Workers want to be able to work more days from the comfort of their living rooms than they currently do, according to wfh Research. On average, workers across the world want two days at home, a full day more than they get. In English-speaking countries, which already have the highest levels of home-working, there is an appetite for more. And the trend is spreading to places where remote work has been less common (see chart 1). Japanese and South Korean employees, some of the most office-bound anywhere, want more than a quarter of the week to themselves. Europeans and Latin American crave a third and half, respectively.

然而，来自上级的所有压力并没有削弱员工对远程工作的兴趣。根据wfh Research的研究，员工希望能够在舒适的客厅里工作的天数比目前更多。平均而言，世界各地的员工希望在家工作两天，比他们目前得到的多一整天。在已经有最高水平的远程办公率的英语国家仍然渴望更多的天数。这一趋势正蔓延到远程工作不太常见的地方(见表1)。日本和韩国的员工，是世界上最受办公室束缚的“一员”，他们希望每周有超过四分之一的时间可以给到他们。欧洲人和拉丁美洲人分别渴望获得三分之一和一半的时间。

Continued desire for more remote work is not surprising. The time saved not having to battle public transport or congested roads allows for a better work-life balance. On average, 72 minutes each day is saved when working remotely, which adds up to two weeks over a year, according to a working paper* by Nicholas Bloom of Stanford, who helps run wfh Research, and colleagues. Employees also report that they feel most engaged when working remotely, according to a poll last year by Gallup. On average globally, workers value all these benefits to the tune of an 8% rise in their salaries, suggesting that some would take a pay cut to keep their privileges.

对更多远程工作的持续需求并不奇怪。不必与公共交通或拥挤的道路作斗争所节省的时间，有助于更好地实现工作与生活的平衡。据斯坦福大学帮助管理wfh Research的尼古拉斯•布鲁姆及其同事的一份工作报告显示，远程工作平均每天节省72分钟，一年下来总共节省两周时间。盖洛普去年的一项民意调查显示，员工报告称在远程工作时最投入。在全球范围内，平均而言，员工认为这些好处相当于他们薪水的8%的提高，这表明有些人愿意减薪以保持他们的特权。

Until recently, as firms tried to lure workers during the post-pandemic hiring bonanza, employees’ demands and employers’ plans seemed to be converging in America, the best-studied market. This convergence is tailing off (see chart 2). At the same time, the pandemic has entrenched work-from-home patterns. At the moment, a third of workers surveyed by wfh Research have a hybrid or fully remote arrangement. Those practices will not be easy to unwind.

最近，随着公司在后疫情时期的招聘繁荣中试图吸引工人，员工的要求和雇主的计划似乎在美国这个最受关注的市场上趋于一致。但是这种趋同正在减弱（见图2）。与此同时，疫情已经巩固了远程办公的模式。目前，在接受wfh Research调查的员工中，有三分之一的人采用混合或完全远程办公的安排。这些做法将不容易取消。

It is no coincidence that the crackdown on remote work is happening as the labour market begins to cool. Deepening job cuts across Wall Street and Silicon Valley have handed power back to businesses. However, even in tech and finance some employees are standing their ground. In May Amazon said that 300 employees staged walkouts over the e-empire’s return-to-work policies (the organisers said it was closer to 2,000). Other companies are quietly adapting with the times, perhaps recognising that a more flexible approach is inevitable. hsbc, a British bank, is planning to relocate from its 45-storey tower in Canary Wharf to smaller digs in the City of London. Deloitte and kpmg, two professional-services giants, plan to reduce their office footprint in favour of more remote work. The gap between the two sides of the work-from-home battle may yet narrow. The question is whether the bosses or the bossed will yield the most. ■

对远程工作的打击并非巧合，这是劳动力市场开始降温的结果。华尔街和硅谷不断深化的裁员已将权力交还给企业。然而，即使在科技和金融行业，一些员工也坚持自己的立场。今年5月，亚马逊称有300名员工罢工，抗议该公司的重返工作岗位政策(组织者称罢工人数接近2000人)。其他公司也在悄悄顺应时代，或许它们意识到，更灵活的做法是不可避免的。英国汇丰银行正计划将其位于金丝雀码头的45层大楼搬迁到伦敦城较小的地方。德勤和毕马威这两家专业服务巨头计划减少办公室的占地面积，以支持更多的远程工作。在这场居家办公之战中，双方的差距可能会缩小。问题是老板还是员工谁会做出最多的让步。■
