Is big business really getting too big?

大企业真的会越变越大吗？

Size wars

规模战

In a few sectors, corporate concentration is a problem. In most, it needn’t be

在一些行业，企业“高集中度”是个问题。但在大多数行业中，情况并非如此

image: vincent kilbride

Governments are at war with big business. In June Joe Biden, America’s president, spoke for many politicians the world over when he blamed it for greed-fuelled price rises, sluggish wage growth, forgone innovation and fragile supply chains. His trustbusters at the Federal Trade Commission (ftc) have been going after large deals merely because they are large—or at least that is how it feels. Courtroom defeats do not dampen the agency’s zeal. The latest came on July 11th, when a judge rejected its request to block Microsoft’s $69bn acquisition of Activision Blizzard, a developer of video games. The ftc is expected to appeal against the ruling. The eu’s competition authorities are making noises about breaking up Google. Last year Britain’s Competition and Markets Authority (cma) derailed the $40bn acquisition by Nvidia, a semiconductor giant, of Arm, a chip-designer.

政府正与大企业“交战”。今年6月，美国总统乔•拜登代表世界各地的许多政客发声，他将贪婪导致的物价上涨、工资增长缓慢、放弃创新以及脆弱的供应链归咎于大企业。他在联邦贸易委员会的反垄断调查官员一直在追查大型交易，仅仅是因为它们规模大——至少他们感觉是这样的。法庭上的失败并没有削弱该机构的热情。最近的一次“失败”是在7月11日，法官驳回了阻止微软以690亿美元收购视频游戏开发商动视暴雪的请求。预计联邦贸易委员会将对这一裁决提起上诉。欧盟竞争主管部门正闹着要分拆谷歌。去年，英国竞争与市场管理局(cma)阻止了半导体巨头英伟达以400亿美元收购芯片设计公司Arm的交易。

Trustbusters invoke three justifications for their renewed vigour: greater market concentration, reduced churn among the world’s biggest firms and rising corporate profits. On the surface all three point to rising corporate power. Look closely, though, and the trends may be the result of benign factors such as technological progress and globalisation. In some local markets, greater concentration may, paradoxically, have led to more competition, not less. And the covid-19 pandemic may have planted the seeds of a further competitive revival. Some big firms, it is true, have been collecting rents, including in big sectors such as health care. But trustbusters’ strategy—to reflexively question any deal involving a big firm—is wrongheaded.

反垄断调查人员对他们“重新振作”给出了三个理由：“市场集中度”更高，世界上最大的公司之间的流动减少，以及公司利润的增长。从表面上看，这三点都指向企业实力的上升。然而，仔细观察，这些趋势可能是技术进步和全球化等良性因素的结果。矛盾的是，在一些本地市场，集中度的提高可能导致竞争加剧，而不是减少。新冠疫情可能已经为进一步的竞争复苏播下了种子。确实，一些大公司一直在收取租金，包括医疗保健等大行业。但是反垄断调查人员的策略（即条件反射性地质疑任何涉及大公司的交易）是错误的。

That concentration has been rising is not in question. Across America’s economy it is higher today than at any point in at least the past century (see chart 1). Out of some 900 sectors in America tracked by The Economist, the number where the four biggest firms have a market share above two-thirds has grown from 65 in 1997 to 97 by 2017. In Europe, where the data are less comprehensive, market power has been increasing for at least 20 years. Using data on western Europe’s largest economies—Britain, Germany, France, Italy and Spain—Gabor Koltay, Szabolcs Lorincz and Tommaso Valletti, three economists, find that the market share of the four largest firms grew in 73% of some 700-odd industries from 1998 to 2019. The average increase was about seven percentage points. The proportion of firms with a share above 50% increased from 16% of industries to 27% by 1998 to 2019. Britain and France saw the biggest jumps.

市场集中度的上升是毫无疑问的。纵观美国经济，市场集中度比过去一个世纪的任何时候都要高（见图表1）。根据《经济学人》追踪的美国约900个行业中，其最大的四家公司的市场份额超过三分之二的行业数量从1997年的65家增长到2017年的97家。在数据不太全面的欧洲，市场实力至少20年来一直在增强。三位经济学家Gabor Koltay、Szabolcs Lorincz和Tommaso Valletti利用西欧最大经济体（英国、德国、法国、意大利和西班牙）的数据发现，1998年至2019年间，约700多个行业中有73%的四大公司的市场份额增长了。平均增长约为7个百分点。市场份额超过50%的公司比例从1998年的16%增加到2019年的27%。英国和法国的增幅最大。

At the same time, incumbent firms look more entrenched. In Britain, the average number of firms that stick in the top ten of their industries by market share three years later was five before the financial crisis. It is now closer to eight. Thomas Philippon of New York University’s Stern School of Business finds a similar reduction in churn among top American firms.

与此同时，现有公司的地位似乎更加稳固。在英国，在金融危机爆发之前，平均有五家公司在三年之后的市场份额上仍然位居行业前十。现在这个数字接近八家。纽约大学斯特恩商学院的Thomas Philippon发现，美国顶尖公司之间的流动性也减少了。

Most telling, firms are raking in higher profits. The Economist has come up with a crude estimate of “excess” profits for the world’s 3,000 largest listed companies by market value (excluding financial firms). Using reported figures from Bloomberg we calculate a firm’s hurdle rate of return on invested capital above 10% (excluding goodwill and treating research and development as an asset with a ten-year lifespan). This is the rate of return one might expect in a competitive market. In the past year excess profits reached $4trn, or nearly 4% of global gdp (see chart 2). They are highly concentrated in the West, especially America. American firms collect 41% of the total, with European ones taking 21%. The energy, technology and, in America, health-care industries stand out as excess-profit pools relative to their size.

最有说服力的是，公司正在获得更高的利润。《经济学人》对全球市值最大的3,000家上市公司（不包括金融公司）的“超额”利润进行了粗略估算。使用彭博社的报告数据，我们计算出一家公司的投资资本回报率应该在10%以上(（不包括商誉，并将研发视为具有十年寿命的资产）。这是在竞争市场中可能期望的回报率。在过去一年中，超额利润达到4万亿美元，约占全球GDP的4%（见图表2）。它们高度集中在西方，特别是美国。美国公司占总数的41%，欧洲公司占21%。能源、技术和在美国的医疗保健行业相对于其规模而言是超额利润池。

All this looks troubling. And in certain sectors, it is. Four decades ago more than eight in ten hospitals were non-profits with a single location. Now more than six in ten are owned by sprawling for-profit hospital chains or academic networks such as Steward Health Care or Indiana University Health. At first this was a perfectly healthy process of big and efficient chains expanding across America. Two decades—and nearly 2,000 hospital mergers—later, things look ropey. An analysis from 2019 by Martin Gaynor of Carnegie Mellon University and colleagues suggests that such mergers have tended to raise prices without improving quality.

这一切看起来都很麻烦。在某些领域，确实如此。40年前，超过80%的医院都是单一地点的非营利性医院。现在，超过六成的医院为大型盈利性连锁医院或学术网络所有，如Steward Health Care或Indiana University Health。起初，这是一个非常有益的过程，大型和高效的连锁店在美国扩张。二十年后，近2000家医院合并，情况看起来不太乐观。卡内基梅隆大学的Martin Gaynor及其同事从2019年开始的一项分析表明，这些并购往往会提高价格，但并未改善医疗服务质量。

Still, high concentration, low churn and rich profits need not necessarily make consumers worse off. That concentration has been rising for 100 years, during which life has improved for virtually everyone, is the first clue that it may be the result of benign forces. Increases in industry concentration in America over the past century are correlated with greater technological intensity, higher fixed costs and higher output growth, according to Yueran Ma of the University of Chicago Booth School of Business and colleagues. None of these seems particularly nefarious.

尽管存在高度集中、低流动性和丰厚的利润，但这并不一定会使消费者受到损害。这种集中已经持续了100年，而在这期间，几乎每个人的生活都得到了改善，这是可能是良性力量的第一个迹象。根据芝加哥大学布斯商学院的Yueran Ma和同事的研究，美国过去一个世纪的行业集中度上升与更高的技术密集度、更高的固定成本和更高的产出增长有关。这些因素似乎都不是特别险恶的。

That concentration has also risen in Europe, where competition authorities have not been as sleepy as in America, likewise suggests that powerful structural forces are at play. John Van Reenen of the London School of Economics fingers technology and globalisation. The internet has reduced the cost of shopping around, even as software and other technology allow the best firms to scale up their operations around the world. Figures collected by McKinsey, a consultancy, show that the return on invested capital for a firm in the 75th percentile by this measure is 20 percentage points higher than for a median firm. “There are just huge economies of scale with software,” says Sterling Auty of MoffettNathanson, a research firm.

在欧洲，集中度也在上升，而那里的竞争当局并没有像美国那样“沉睡”，这同样表明强大的结构性力量正在发挥作用。伦敦经济学院的John Van Reenen指出，技术和全球化是原因。互联网降低了比价成本，同时软件和其他技术使最好的公司能够在全球范围内扩大业务规模。咨询公司麦肯锡收集的数据显示，按照这个指标，在处于75百分位数的公司的投资回报率比中位数公司高20个百分点。“软件存在巨大的规模经济效应，” 研究公司MoffettNathanson的Sterling Auty说道。

Local anti-heroes

Moreover, higher concentration at the country level may increase competition locally. Service industries in particular, which make up about half the 900-odd sectors in America’s census, are better examined at the local level. Fiona Scott Morton, a former deputy assistant attorney-general now at Yale School of Management, uses the example of coffee shops. With just one café in each neighbourhood, the national market would be hyperfragmented. But every consumer would face a local monopoly. “If I’m looking for a coffee, I’m not going to drive three hours,” she says.

此外，国家层面上的高度集中度可能会增加本地的竞争。特别是服务业，美国人口普查中约900个行业的一半左右都是服务业，它更适合在地方层面上进行研究。现任耶鲁大学管理学院教授、前美国司法部副助理总检察长Fiona Scott Morton以咖啡店为例。如果每个社区只有一个咖啡店，那么全国市场将会非常分散。但每个消费者都将面临本地垄断。“如果我想找一家咖啡店，我不会开车三个小时去找，”她说道。

Academics debate what exactly has happened to concentration in local markets. What seems increasingly clear is that the best firms have expanded into more and more of them. Walmarts, with their “everyday low prices”, cater to shoppers across America, thanks to the retail behemoth’s unrivalled logistics operation. Cheesecake Factory uses a laboratory in California to taste-test dishes that it quickly rolls out to its 200 or so locations around America. A recent paper titled “The Industrial Revolution in Services’‘, by Esteban Rossi-Hansberg at the University of Chicago and his co-author, shows that the geographic expansion of big firms increases competition for local incumbents, whose local market share falls.

学者们对于本地市场的集中度到底发生了什么进行了激烈的辩论。但越来越明晰的是最好的公司已经扩展到了越来越多的本地市场。沃尔玛以其“每日低价”服务于美国各地的购物者，这得益于这家零售巨头无与伦比的物流运营能力。芝士蛋糕工厂使用加利福尼亚州的一个实验室来测试菜肴的味道，然后迅速推向其在美国约200个地点的门店。芝加哥大学的Esteban Rossi-Hansberg及其共同作者最近发表的一篇名为“服务业的工业革命”的论文表明，大公司的地理扩张增加了对本地现有企业的竞争，其本地市场份额下降。

As for low churn, it is not so bad if the incumbents keep innovating—which is what many are doing. Despite central banks pushing interest rates up at the fastest pace in decades in an attempt to quash inflation, American private investment in the first quarter of 2023 was 17.2% of gdp, similar to pre-pandemic highs. Many corporate behemoths are ploughing billions into innovation, including in areas that most worry trustbusters, such as technology. American tech’s big five—Alphabet, Amazon, Apple, Meta and Microsoft—collectively invested around $200bn in r&d last year, about a quarter of America’s total in 2021. Microsoft and Alphabet are at the forefront of the ai race.

至于低流动性，如果现有企业不断创新，情况就不会那么糟糕——“不断创新”正是许多企业正在做的事情。尽管中央银行试图通过迅速提高利率来抑制通胀，但美国私人投资在2023年第一季度占GDP的17.2%，与疫情前的高点相似。许多企业巨头正在向创新领域投入数十亿美元，包括最令反垄断监管机构担忧的技术领域。美国科技界的五大巨头——Alphabet、亚马逊、苹果、Meta和微软——去年共投资约2000亿美元进行研发，约占美国2021年总投资的四分之一。微软和Alphabet处于人工智能竞赛的前沿。

Profits have, it is true, been higher in America since the financial crisis of 2007-09 than in previous decades, especially if you consider free cashflows, which accounts for the changing way companies depreciate assets (see chart 3). But they look somewhat less unusual if you adjust for lower tax rates and firms’ larger global footprint. And they may have peaked: analysts estimate that earnings for the s&p 500 index of American blue chips dipped in the three months to June, year on year, for the third quarter in a row.

确实，自2007-09年金融危机以来，美国的利润率比之前几十年都要高，特别是如果考虑自由现金流，这是由于公司折旧资产方式的变化所导致的（见图3）。但如果考虑税率降低和公司更大的全球业务范围，它们看起来就不那么异常了。而且它们可能已经达到了峰值：分析师估计，美国蓝筹股标普500指数在截至6月的三个月中，连续第三个季度同比下降。

Most heartening, far from being subdued, dynamism may be on the rise. John Haltiwanger of the University of Maryland notes that business formation, which had been “quite anaemic” since the mid-2010s, has surged since the pandemic (see chart 4). In the past few years many more new firms have been created than old ones have been shut down. Whether these startups will dislodge incumbents is still unclear. But venture-capital investment suggests investors see scope for healthy returns. Although it is half what it was at its frothy peak of over $130bn in the fourth quarter of 2021, that has only brought it back to the levels of 2019 and 2020.

最令人振奋的是，经济活力可能正在上升，而非被抑制。马里兰大学的John Haltiwanger指出，自2010年代中期以来一直“相当萎靡不振”的“企业创立数量”自疫情爆发以来激增（见图4）。在过去几年中，新成立公司的数量比关闭的老公司多得多。这些初创企业是否会取代现有企业仍不清楚。但风险投资投资表明，投资者看到了健康回报的空间。虽然与2021年第四季度超过1300亿美元的泡沫高峰相比，风险投资只有一半，但这只是将其带回到2019年和2020年的水平。

One hypothesis is that the remote-friendliness of the post-covid economy reduces startup costs. Young firms no longer need to rent a big office. They can hire from a less local talent pool. By our rough count, around 125 of the Census Bureau’s 900-odd industries benefit from rising e-commerce or can provide their services remotely. Consumers’ growing comfort with such options could inspire more new businesses to set up shop. Mr Haltiwanger already observes a small shift in the size distribution of firms towards smaller fry.

一种假设是，后疫情时代经济的远程友好性降低了初创企业的成本。年轻的企业不再需要租用大型办公室，他们可以从更广泛的人才库中招聘员工。据我们粗略统计，美国人口普查局的约900个行业中，约有125个行业受益于电子商务的兴起或可以远程提供其服务。消费者对这些选项的不断接受可能会激发更多的新企业开张。Haltiwanger先生已经观察到，企业规模分布已经出现了向较小企业的微小转移。

Concentration may also be levelling off as a result of subdued dealmaking, especially in tech. The big five tech firms’ share of all acquisitions by listed firms in America has fallen from nearly 1% in the 2010s to less than 0.5% since the start of Mr Biden’s tenure. Some of the slowdown in mergers and acquisitions (m&a) is caused by the rising cost of capital and risk of recession. But renewed antitrust zeal must be playing a role. On June 27th American authorities updated their merger guidelines for the first time in 45 years, requiring firms to report far more details on deals worth over $110m, half the average deal size in 2022.

由于交易活动的减弱，尤其是在科技领域，集中度也可能出现平稳。美国上市公司进行的所有收购交易中，五大科技公司所占比例已从2010年代的近1%下降到拜登政府上任以来的不到0.5%。一些并购交易放缓是由于资本成本上升和经济衰退风险增加所致。但新的反垄断热情肯定也在发挥作用。6月27日，美国当局首次在45年内更新了其并购指导方针，要求企业对超过1.1亿美元（2022年平均交易规模的一半）的交易报告更多细节。

The biggest transactions are almost sure to be subject to a deep probe, which can add months to a filing process that now takes weeks. Regulators everywhere are throwing “sand in the gears of the m&a machine”, sighs a lawyer. “The ftc has stopped being discerning,” says another. Britain’s cma “has probably overreached”, echoes a British one.

最大的交易几乎肯定会受到深入调查，这可能会使申报过程延长数月，而现在这个过程只需要几周时间。到处都有监管机构在“干预并购”，一位律师叹息道。另一位律师表示，“联邦贸易委员会已经停止了鉴别（一刀切）”。一位英国律师也表示，“英国竞争与市场管理局可能已经过度（干预）了”。

Such overzealous trustbusting carries its own risks. It may distract attention from more immediate threats to economic dynamism from bureaucratic restraints on land use or occupational licensing. Acquisitions can be useful for preserving the value of startups when subdued markets make it hard for founders to raise capital. And some big deals may benefit consumers, as when a biotech startup joins forces with established drugmakers to test and distribute a new therapy. Competition authorities were probably asleep for too long. Now they may be getting up too quickly. ■

这种过度热衷于打破垄断的做法也有其自身的风险。它可能会转移人们对经济活力面临的更直接威胁的注意力，使人们不再关注官僚机构对土地使用或职业许可的限制。在市场低迷时期，收购对于保护初创企业的价值可能非常有用，因为创始人很难筹集资本。一些大型交易可能会让消费者受益，比如一家生物技术初创公司与老牌制药公司联手测试和销售一种新疗法。竞争监管机构可能“沉睡”得太久了。现在他们可能“行动”得太快了。■
