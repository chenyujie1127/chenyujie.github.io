A lesson from America’s Inflation Reduction Act is that size isn’t everything

从美国的通胀削减法案中得到的教训是，规模并不是一切

Feb 14th 2023 | BERLIN

Last summer European leaders began hearing a huge sucking sound. The source of the din? The Inflation Reduction Act (ira), a 725-page doorstopper of legislation passed in August to speed up American decarbonisation. Europe’s budding clean-tech industry, they feared, would be hoovered up across the Atlantic by the promise of handouts, which amount to around $400bn over ten years. To stop this from happening, some eu politicians argued, the bloc would have at the very least to match the ira’s sums.

去年夏天，欧洲领导人开始听到巨大的吸溜声。噪音的来源是什么? 8月通过的长达725页的《通胀削减法案》旨在加快美国脱碳进程。他们担心，欧洲新兴的清洁技术产业将被大西洋彼岸的捐款承诺所“吸走”，这些捐款承诺将在10年内达到4000亿美元左右。一些欧盟政客认为，为了阻止这种情况发生，欧盟至少应该达到与《通胀削减法案》的金额相当的规模。

So far the noise has turned out to be mostly in the politicians’ heads. Worries about a green exodus, bordering on panic in some quarters, have subsided. When the continent’s heads of government gathered for a summit last week in Brussels, they did not shower billions of euros more on the eu’s greening efforts—which are already, it turns out, comparable to the ira in their generosity. Nor did they (for the time being) further water down rules against state aid to favoured businesses, which would have given freer rein to member states keen to splurge. Instead, they focused on making the system for doling out this money more efficient.

到目前为止，噪音大多在政客们的头脑中。对于绿色外流的担忧已经消退，在一些地区已经接近恐慌。当欧洲大陆的政府首脑们上周聚集在布鲁塞尔举行峰会时，他们并没有在欧盟的绿色努力上再投入数十亿欧元，事实证明，欧盟的绿色努力在慷慨程度上已经可以与《通胀削减法案》相媲美。他们(暂时)也没有进一步淡化反对政府对受青睐企业提供援助的规定，这会让热衷于挥霍的成员国更自由地发挥作用。相反，他们专注于提高发放这些资金的系统的效率。

This is music to the ears of Europa sa. In the eyes of its European fans, the beauty of the ira is less its size than its simplicity. Rules are the same all over America. Getting tax credits, grants or soft loans will be straightforward provided a firm meets certain criteria, such as investing in one of the targeted sectors. The law sets aside sums for specific technologies, which can create markets, such as solar energy, carbon capture and storage and “green” hydrogen, made from renewable power (see chart). Producers of such hydrogen, for example, can get tax credits of up to $3 per kilogram of the gas.

这对欧洲公司来说简直是天籁之音。在它的欧洲粉丝眼中，《通胀削减法案》的优点不在于它的大小，而在于它的简单。全美范围内规则都是一样的。如果公司符合某些标准，比如投资于目标行业之一，那么获得税收抵免、补助金或软贷款将是很简单的。该法案为特定技术留出资金，这些技术可以创造市场，如太阳能、碳捕获和储存以及由可再生能源制成的“绿色”氢气(见图表)。例如，生产这种氢气的生产商每公斤可获得高达3美元的税收抵免。

![image-20230215074330408](/Users/chenyujie/Library/Application Support/typora-user-images/image-20230215074330408.png)

Replicating this set-up exactly would be unthinkable in Europe. The eu may see itself as an ever-closer union, but taxes are still a national affair, which rules out continent-wide tax incentives. If member states want to institute their own credits, or other forms of subsidies, they typically need the approval of the European Commission in Brussels, whose job it is to ensure a level playing field in the eu’s single market. To the resulting cacophony of national schemes, the eu has in recent years added a few bloc-wide grant programmes, such as Invest eu and Innovation Fund, to support clean tech.

在欧洲完全复制这种设置是难以想象的。欧盟可能认为自己是一个越来越紧密的联盟，但税收仍然是国家事务，这就排除了在整个大陆范围内实行税收优惠的可能性。如果成员国想建立自己的税收抵免或其他形式的补贴，通常需要得到位于布鲁塞尔的欧盟委员会的批准。欧盟委员会的职责是确保欧盟单一市场的公平竞争环境。为了应对由此产生的国家计划的杂音，近年来，欧盟增加了一些欧盟范围内的资助项目，如InvestEU和Innovation Fund，以支持清洁技术。

The upshot is ear-splitting, particularly for smaller green-tech firms in need of funds to scale up their projects, says Craig Douglas of World Fund, a venture-capital firm, who has a long experience in dealing with the eu’s subsidy bureaucracy. To have a chance at tapping one of the many pots, startups often have to hire pricey consultancies to help them write grant proposals. “We would need at least four people full-time to figure this out,” explains Vaitea Cowan, co-founder of Enapter, a maker of electrolysers, machines that produce hydrogen.

风投公司世界基金的克雷格•道格拉斯长期与欧盟补贴机构打交道，他说，结果是令人震惊的，特别是对那些需要资金来扩大项目规模的小型绿色科技公司而言。为了有机会拿到这些钱，初创公司通常不得不聘请昂贵的顾问来帮助他们撰写拨款申请。“我们至少需要四名全职员工来解决这个问题，” 电解槽（生产氢气的机器）制造商Enapter的联合创始人人瓦蒂亚•考恩解释说道。

Once an application is filed, it often takes months, if not years, before a decision is made. In the case of Plastic Energy, which recycles plastic waste, it once took so long that “we had to file again because the delay made us miss a deadline”, reports Carlos Monreal, the firm’s chief executive. And decisions tend to come without explanation. “It’s a black box. There should be a dialogue,” says Henrik Henriksson, boss of h2 Green Steel, which is erecting a steel mill in northern Sweden that is powered by green hydrogen. The eu’s green subsidies are also often poorly targeted. Jules Besnainou of Cleantech for Europe, a lobby group, points out that most of the European money does not go to the continent’s startups, which tend to be more innovative, but to big established firms, which do not always need government support.

一旦申请提交，通常需要几个月，甚至几年，才能做出决定。以回收塑料垃圾的Plastic Energy公司为例，该公司首席执行官卡洛斯·蒙雷亚尔报告说，该公司曾经花了很长时间，以至于“我们不得不再次提交申请，因为这一延误使我们错过了最后期限”。而且决定往往没有任何解释。“这是一个黑盒子，这里应该有些对话，“h2 Green Steel的老板亨里克·亨里克森说道，该公司正在瑞典北部建造一家以绿色氢气为动力的钢铁厂。欧盟的绿色补贴也往往没有明确的目标。游说团体Cleantech for Europe的朱尔斯·贝斯奈诺指出，欧洲的大部分资金并没有流向欧洲大陆的初创企业，这些企业往往更具创新性，而是流向老牌大公司，而这些公司并不总是需要政府的支持。

The commission’s draft “Green Deal Industrial Plan”, unveiled on February 1st, tries to deal with these shortcomings. The plan is meant to simplify the eu programmes and streamline the approval of national green-finance tools in Brussels. It proposes an “administratively light” auction, the winners of which will receive a premium, based on their bids, for each kilogram of renewable hydrogen produced over ten years. The scheme will offer incentive to the tune of €800m ($860m). The ira has clearly shocked the eu into thinking harder about its green subsidies, says Jeromin Zettelmeyer, who heads Bruegel, a think-tank in Brussels.

2月1日，欧盟委员会公布了“欧盟绿色工业计划”草案，试图解决这些问题。该计划旨在简化欧盟项目，并简化布鲁塞尔对各国绿色金融工具的审批。它提出了一种“轻管理”的拍卖，获胜者将根据他们的出价，在十年内生产每公斤可再生氢获得一笔津贴。该计划将提供总计8亿欧元(合8.6亿美元)的激励。布鲁塞尔智库Bruegel的负责人杰罗明•泽特梅耶说，《通胀削减法案》显然已经震惊了欧盟，使其更加认真地考虑绿色补贴问题。

That may be so. Still, those who have read the eight pages dedicated to “speeding up access to finance”, which mention no fewer than a dozen different acronym-rich programmes, may be excused for not holding their breath. Claudio Spadacini, ceo of Energy Dome, an Italian firm which uses liquid carbon-dioxide to store energy, approves of the eu’s moves but still hopes to take advantage of the ira. Ms Cowan of Enapter, whose firm has just built a factory in Germany, is getting lots of calls from American state governments since the ira was passed. “They are rolling out the red carpet,” she says. Whoosh. ■

也许是这样。尽管如此，那些读过关于“加快融资渠道”的8页报告(其中提到了不下12个不同的缩略词丰富的项目)的人，或许有理由不屏住呼吸。意大利一家使用液态二氧化碳储存能源的公司Energy Dome的首席执行官克劳迪奥·斯帕达奇尼赞同欧盟的举措，但仍希望利用《通胀削减法案》。Enapter的考恩女士的公司刚刚在德国建了一家工厂，自从《通胀削减法案》通过后，她接到了许多来自美国州政府的电话。“他们正在热烈欢迎，”她说道。嗖。■
