But charity is not quite the same as business

但是慈善和商业不完全一样

Feb 9th 2023

It is hard to make a billion dollars. It is also hard to give it away sensibly. “I would as soon leave my son a curse as the almighty dollar,” wrote Andrew Carnegie, a plutocrat-turned-philanthropist, in 1889. Nor was he fond of “indiscriminate charity”, such as handouts for beggars. “It were better for mankind that the millions of the rich were thrown in to the sea than so spent as to encourage the slothful, the drunken, the unworthy.”

赚10亿美元很难。明智地放弃也很难。“我宁愿给我儿子留下诅咒，也不愿给我儿子留下万能的美元，”1889年，由富豪转型为慈善家的安德鲁·卡内基写道。他也不喜欢“不分青红皂白的慈善”，比如施舍乞丐。“对人类来说，把数以百万计的富人扔进海里，总比花了这些钱去鼓励那些懒惰的、醉酒的和不值得的人要好得多。”

Over a century later, a new generation of philanthropists are wrestling with the same issue. Tech founders dominate the list of the world’s wealthiest people (Forbes, which maintains one such list, puts 12 tech moguls among the world’s top 25). A strong culture of charitable giving in Silicon Valley, tech’s spiritual home, means that, when it comes to donations, they punch above their already considerable weight. In 2021 those who made their money in tech accounted for nearly three-quarters of the money given away by America’s 50 biggest donors. That tide of money brings with it the worldview and assumptions of the industry that created it.

一个多世纪后，新一代慈善家也在为同样的问题苦苦挣扎。科技公司创始人主导着世界上最富有的人的榜单(福布斯有一个这样的榜单，在全球前25名中有12名科技巨头)。硅谷是科技行业的精神家园，这里有浓厚的慈善捐赠文化，这意味着，在捐赠方面，它们的影响力会超出已经相当可观的规模。2021年，在美国前50名最大捐赠者的资金中，在科技行业赚钱的人占了近四分之三。这股资金潮给创造它的行业带来了世界观和假设。

Startup culture prizes speed, flexibility and learning from failure. Firms and founders aim to get to market quickly, scale up, and worry about problems as they arise. Their philanthropy operates along similar lines. To see the contrast, compare tech’s young guns with one of the field’s elder statesmen. When Bill Gates, the founder of Microsoft, set up his charitable foundation in 2000, it became the standard-bearer for a hard-nosed, technocratic approach to doing good. Mr Gates and his agents ranked recipients by how much “social return” they could generate, and required regular progress reports. If money was not being spent effectively, funding was cut.

创业公司文化重视速度、灵活性和从失败中吸取教训。公司和创始人的目标是快速进入市场，扩大规模，并在出现问题时去担心。他们的慈善事业也遵循类似的原则。要了解这种对比，可以将科技界的年轻精英与该领域的一位资深政治家进行比较。2000年，当微软创始人比尔•盖茨成立他的慈善基金会时，该基金会成为了务实、以技术官僚方式行善的“旗手”。盖茨先生和他的代理人根据他们能产生多少“社会回报”对受助人进行排名，并要求他们定期报告进度。如果没有有效地使用资金，资金就会被削减。

As we explain in our International section, today’s techies think such bureaucracy merely gets in the way of getting money out of the door. MacKenzie Scott, the ex-wife of Jeff Bezos, Amazon’s founder, exemplifies the new methods. She has given away huge amounts of money ($14bn since 2019) with no strings attached as to what recipients do with it. GiveDirectly, which counts many digital zillionaires among its funders, has handed out $500m over the past decade directly to the world’s poorest people.

正如我们在国际（International）部分所解释的那样，今天的技术人员认为这种官僚主义只是阻碍了资金的流出。亚马逊创始人杰夫·贝佐斯的前妻麦肯齐·斯科特就是新方法的典型代表。她捐出了巨额资金(自2019年以来已达140亿美元)，而且没有附加条件规定受助人如何使用这些资金。GiveDirectly的资助者中包括许多数字领域的亿万富翁，该公司在过去10年里直接向世界上最贫穷的人捐赠了5亿美元。

Foundations, which under American law must dispose of 5% of their assets every year, are out. Lightly-regulated “donor advised funds” (DAFs) are in (it helps that these come with significant tax advantages). Some tech donors even use ordinary limited companies, which fits with the Silicon Valley view (shared by this newspaper) that it is not just charities and not-for-profit organisations that can make the world a better place. Many aim strikingly high. Forget making progress on understanding brain cancer. How about aiming to cure all cancer everywhere?

根据美国法律，基金会每年必须处理掉5%的资产，现在已经过时了。监管宽松的“捐赠人建议基金”(DAFs)正在流行(这些基金会伴随着显著的税收优惠，这是有帮助的)。一些科技捐助者甚至使用普通的有限公司，这符合硅谷的观点(本刊也认同)，即不仅仅是慈善机构和非营利组织可以让世界变得更美好。许多人的目标非常高。忘掉在了解脑癌方面取得的进展吧。致力于治愈世界各地的所有癌症怎么样?

Mostly, this shakeup is welcome. There is no shortage of problems in the world. The more clever people that are trying to fix them, the better. The tech industry receives plenty of criticism on everything from privacy to its supposedly addictive products and alleged monopoly power. But its dedication to philanthropy is an example that other sectors could do worse than copy.

总的来说，这种改变是受欢迎的。世界上从来不缺少问题。越聪明的人试图解决这些问题，效果就越好。从隐私到据称会让人上瘾的产品，再到所谓的垄断力量，科技行业受到了大量批评。但它对慈善事业的奉献是一个榜样，其他行业不会做得比科技行业的更好。

The new sharing economy

Yet would-be tech donors should bear a few things in mind. One is the importance of incentives. The peer pressure among the tech crowd to engage in charitable giving is admirable. But the risk is that the giving itself—or, worse, the status that it bestows upon the donor—becomes the point, rather than the effect of the money when it arrives.

然而，潜在的技术捐助者应该记住几件事。一是激励机制的重要性。科技界人士参与慈善捐赠的同行压力令人钦佩。但风险在于，捐赠本身——或者更糟糕的是，它赋予捐赠者的地位——成为重点，而不是钱到达时的效果。

The second is the value of “first-principle thinking”, a Silicon Valley buzzphrase which holds that understanding the fundamental nature of a problem is a vital step towards finding a good solution. And the first principle of philanthropy is that it is not the same as business. The power of the high-speed, agile, startup-focused approach to business relies on the presence of a feedback mechanism in the form of a market. If no one will pay for a product or service, then the company producing it will fold. That stops good money being thrown after bad, and allows resources and attention to be turned to other, more useful ends.

其次是“第一性原理”的价值，这是硅谷的一个流行短语，它认为理解问题的本质是找到好的解决方案的关键一步。慈善事业的首要原则是它不同于商业。高速、敏捷、以创业公司为中心的商业方法的力量依赖于以市场形式存在的反馈机制。如果没有人愿意为一种产品或服务付费，那么生产它的公司就会倒闭。这样就不会再花冤枉钱了，资源和注意力也会被转移到其他更有用的地方。

No such feedback mechanism exists with philanthropy. That means bad ideas, or projects that do not work, can persist for as long as donors want. In the worst case, charities can evolve into self-serving bureaucracies more concerned with their own survival—and lucrative employment for their staff—than their putative purpose. (DAFs already earn considerable fees for their administrators.)

慈善事业不存在这样的反馈机制。这意味着糟糕的想法或行不通的项目，只要捐助者愿意，就可以一直存在下去。在最坏的情况下，慈善机构可能演变成自私自利的官僚机构，他们更关心自己的生存和员工的高薪就业，而不是他们假定的目的。(DAF已经为管理者赚取了可观的费用。)

One of the reason for the bureaucratic approach taken by the Gates Foundation is to try to minimise such risks, by ensuring that money actually translates into benefit. The sheer amount of cash flowing from the new generation of tech titans will ensure that a great deal of good is done. But learning from the previous generation could help maximise it. ■

盖茨基金会采取官僚主义做法的原因之一是，通过确保资金实际转化为效益，试图将此类风险降至最低。新一代科技巨头带来的巨额现金流，将确保有大量善事发生。但是，向上一代人学习可以帮助最大限度地利用它。■
