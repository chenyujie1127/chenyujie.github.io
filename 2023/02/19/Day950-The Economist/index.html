State subsidies fuel America’s EV boom but risk overcapacity

国家补贴推动了美国电动汽车的繁荣，但面临产能过剩的风险

America’s industrial policy

美国的产业政策

Individually, states are acting sensibly. Collectively, risks are growing

各个州的行动都很明智。总的来说，风险正在加剧

Feb 23rd 2023

An hour east of Atlanta, turn off the interstate highway and drive into a postcard scene of rural Georgia—a road lined by tall pines, country homes and a cluster of churches—when suddenly a vast expanse of clear-cut land appears. Big yellow trucks are flattening the earth and large transmission lines run along its edge. What makes it especially attractive for an industrial investor cannot be seen: tax breaks, direct grants and other assistance that, all in, come to $1.5bn. When Georgia announced this incentive package last May for Rivian, a California-based startup that makes electric trucks and SUVs, it was the biggest corporate subsidy given by the state. Not for long, though. In July it promised an even bigger package, worth $1.8bn, to Hyundai, also for an electric-vehicle (EV) facility.

在亚特兰大以东一小时车程的地方，拐下州际公路，驶入风景如画的佐治亚州乡村——道路两旁是高大的松树、乡村住宅和一群教堂——突然间，一片空旷的土地出现了。黄色大卡车正在压平地面，大型输电线沿着土地边缘延伸。它对产业投资者特别有吸引力的地方是看不见的: 税收减免、直接拨款和其他援助，总额达15亿美元。去年5月，佐治亚州宣布对总部位于加州、生产电动卡车和suv的初创企业Rivian实施这一激励计划，这是该州给予企业的最大一笔补贴。不过，这种情况不会持续太久。今年7月，该州承诺向现代汽车提供价值18亿美元的更大的一揽子计划，同样用于建设电动汽车EV设施。

Subsidies in the EV industry are popping up across America. On February 13th Michigan approved incentives of more than $1bn for a Ford battery factory. On February 8th Ohio’s private development agency gave nearly $240m to Honda. Last year brought $1bn-plus deals for firms in Kansas, Michigan and North Carolina, adding to dozens of smaller dollops. Globally, concern is rife about America’s rapid emergence as a competitive threat in the EV industry, thanks to the federal government’s hefty investments and domestic-content rules. Within America the competition can feel even fiercer, as states battle with one another to lure investors. “But for the right incentive, the project would go elsewhere,” says a spokesperson for the Georgia Department of Economic Development.

电动汽车行业的补贴在美国各地如雨后春笋般涌现。2月13日，密歇根州批准为福特电池厂提供超过10亿美元的奖励。2月8日，俄亥俄州的私人发展机构向本田提供了近2.4亿美元。去年，堪萨斯州、密歇根州和北卡罗来纳州的公司达成了超过10亿美元的交易，再加上数十笔较小的交易。在全球范围内，由于联邦政府的巨额投资和“本土成分”规定，人们普遍担心美国迅速崛起成为电动汽车行业的竞争威胁。在美国国内，竞争可能会更加激烈，因为各州都在竞相吸引投资者。佐治亚州经济发展部的一位发言人说:“如果没有适当的激励措施，这个项目就会走偏。”

States are not neophytes at business handouts, but the current boom is remarkable for its scale and speed. In a report last year Good Jobs First, a corporate-subsidies watchdog, tallied 51 state-level EV subsidy packages, which helped make for the biggest mega-subsidy spree in its records dating back to 1980. One state consultant estimates that, historically, incentives work out to $5,000 per created job, but that the EV ones run to about $30,000.

各州在商业救济方面并不是新手，但目前的繁荣在规模和速度上都令人震惊。在去年的一份报告中，企业补贴监督机构“好工作优先”统计了51个州级电动汽车补贴计划，这些补贴方案是自1980年以来最大的巨额补贴热潮。一位州顾问估计，从历史上看，每创造一个工作岗位，激励措施可达5000美元，但电动汽车的激励措施约为3万美元。

In part state governments have been emboldened by strong finances: covid-19 stimulus payments left most with chunky budget surpluses. They also have extra urgency because of the Inflation Reduction Act (IRA), the cornerstone of the Biden administration’s new industrial-policy push, which will give billions of dollars in tax credits to both buyers and makers of EVs. The IRA expires in 2032, so companies have to move fast to take advantage of it. Adam Jonas, head of global auto research at Morgan Stanley, a bank, explains it with a fishing analogy: “The IRA stocks the lake full of trout. And now the states are there trying to attract the trout with chum.”

在一定程度上，强劲的财政状况让州政府胆大了起来: covid-19刺激支出给大多数州政府留下了巨额预算盈余。此外，由于拜登政府推动新产业政策的基石《通膨削减法案》的出台，他们变得格外紧迫。该法案将为电动汽车的购买者和制造商提供数十亿美元的税收抵免。《通膨削减法案》将于2032年到期，因此企业必须迅速采取行动来利用它。摩根士丹利的全球汽车研究主管亚当·乔纳斯用钓鱼来解释这个问题:“《通膨削减法案》在湖里放满了鲑鱼。现在各州都在试图用鱼饵吸引鲑鱼。”

Given the rush of subsidies, a looming question is how many will end up being wasted. Georgia’s package for Rivian has shone a spotlight on the risks. Residents near its future plant site challenged the bonds that are needed to make the deal work, pointing to Rivian’s record as a loss-making startup. “The government is basically investing in this company as a speculative investor,” says John Christy, a lawyer for the opponents. The trial judge concurred and refused to validate the bonds. The government has appealed, with Rivian’s plans hanging in the balance.

鉴于补贴的激增，一个迫在眉睫的问题是，有多少补贴最终会被浪费掉。佐治亚州对Rivian的一揽子计划让人们注意到了其中的风险。在Rivian未来工厂所在地附近的居民对达成交易所需的债券提出了质疑，他们指出Rivian是一家亏损的初创公司。“政府基本上是在以投机投资者的身份投资这家公司，”反对者的律师约翰•克里斯蒂表示。承审法官表示赞同并拒绝确认债券的有效性。政府已经提起上诉，而Rivian的计划仍悬而未决。

Yet a closer look at Georgia’s package shows how states are trying to guard against waste. Tax credits are contingent on performance. In Rivian’s case the firm has to meet at least 80% of its commitments to invest $5bn and create 7,500 jobs by the end of 2028, and then stay at that threshold until 2047; if it slips, the state can claw back benefits. Tax breaks are also just one element. Nearly $200m will go to acquiring the land, preparing it for development and building road and rail links. Another $90m will go towards training for employees, adding to the local skills base.

然而，仔细看看佐治亚州的一揽子计划，就会发现各州是如何努力防止浪费的。税收抵免取决于业绩。在Rivian的案例中，该公司必须在2028年底之前完成投资50亿美元的至少80%并创造7500个就业岗位的承诺，然后在2047年之前保持这一阈值; 如果下滑，政府可以收回福利。税收优惠也只是其中一个元素。近2亿美元将用于收购土地，为开发和建设公路和铁路做好准备。另外9000万美元将用于员工培训，增加当地的技能基础。

Other states are designing incentives with similar structures, typically featuring a mix of site preparation, infrastructure, worker training and tax breaks. Even if the target company fails, the states still stand to reap benefits. “They’ll get a return because they’re investing in their people. They’re investing in their land and infrastructure. And another user could quickly come along and absorb the capacity,” says Eric Stavriotis, a site-selection specialist with CBRE, a property broker.

其他州也在设计类似结构的激励措施，通常包括场地准备、基础设施、工人培训和税收减免。即使目标公司失败了，各州仍能从中获益。“他们会得到回报，因为他们在他们的人上投资。他们正在投资土地和基础设施。另一个用户可能很快就会出现并承受这些产能，”房地产经纪人世邦魏理仕的选址专家埃里克·斯塔夫里奥蒂斯说道。

Still, there is a risk that, in aggregate, national and state subsidies will result in excesses. As of November, announced plans would increase America’s battery-making capacity from 55 gigawatt-hours a year in 2021 to about 900 by 2030. That would support the production of some 10m all-electric vehicles a year—more than half the cars now bought annually in America, a vast amount, especially when factoring in imports. Moreover, investments are only increasing. The implication is that America could be headed towards EV overcapacity. And given how young the technology is, plants being built now may soon be obsolete. “Even before the completion of many of these projects, you might have breakthroughs in companies that don’t need government money,” says Mr Jonas.

不过，总体而言，国家和州政府的补贴仍有可能导致过量。截至11月，已宣布的计划将把美国的电池制造能力从2021年的每年550千兆瓦时提高到2030年的约900千兆瓦时。这将支持每年约1000万辆纯电动汽车的生产，美国现在每年购买量的一半以上，这是一个巨大的数字，尤其是考虑到进口。此外，投资只会增加。这意味着美国可能会走向电动汽车产能过剩。考虑到这项技术还很年轻，现在在建的工厂可能很快就会过时。乔纳斯表示:“ 甚至在许多此类项目完成之前，不需要政府资金的公司就可能取得突破。”

From a national perspective, that may not necessarily be a bad outcome. Before the boom began, America was facing a severe undercapacity in EVs and batteries. The prospect of reliance on [], its chief rival, for vehicles of the future alarmed officials. Far better to have manufacturing heft, even at the cost of overcapacity, in such a critical industry.

从一个国家的角度来看，这未必是一个坏结果。在繁荣开始之前，美国面临着电动汽车和电池的严重产能不足。未来汽车依赖其主要竞争对手[]的前景让官员们感到担忧。即使以产能过剩为代价，在如此关键的行业拥有制造业影响力也要好得多。

For individual states, however, the challenge will be how to avoid hangovers. In the traditional auto market America’s manufacturing hubs had a solid half-century of prosperity before decline slowly set in. The EV investment cycle, like much else these days, may go much faster. Some of the states now throwing money at gleaming new projects will rue their largesse before the decade is over. ■

然而，对于个别州来说，挑战在于如何避免遗留物。在传统的汽车市场上，美国的制造业中心在衰落开始之前有半个世纪的繁荣。电动汽车投资周期，就像如今的许多其他事情一样，可能会快得多。一些州现在把钱砸在发光的项目上，在这个十年结束之前，他们将会为自己的慷慨而后悔。■
