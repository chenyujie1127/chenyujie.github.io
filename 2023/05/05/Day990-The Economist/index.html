Governments are living in a fiscal fantasyland

各国政府都生活在财政幻镜中

Bust budgets

![image-20230508095853147](/Users/chenyujie/Library/Application Support/typora-user-images/image-20230508095853147.png)

The world over, they are failing to confront the dire state of their finances

在世界范围内，他们都无法面对他们糟糕的财政状况

May 4th 2023

Wrangling over budgets is always part of politics, but today’s fiscal brinkmanship is truly frightening. In America Democrats and Republicans are playing a game of chicken over raising the government’s debt ceiling. As the drama intensifies, the stakes are getting dangerously high. Janet Yellen, the treasury secretary, says her department could run out of cash to pay the government’s bills on June 1st if no deal is struck. Investors are beginning to price in the risk of what would be America’s first-ever sovereign default.

围绕预算展开争论一直是政治的一部分，但今天的财政边缘政策确实令人恐惧。在美国，民主党和共和党在提高政府债务上限的问题上正在玩一场懦夫游戏。随着剧情的升级，赌注也越来越高。财政部长珍妮特•耶伦表示，如果没有达成协议，6月1日财政部支付给政府账单的现金将耗尽。投资者开始将美国有史以来第一次主权债务违约的风险考虑在内。

The political point-scoring also misses a bigger and more enduring problem. America’s budget deficit is set to balloon as its population ages, the cost of handouts swells and the government’s interest bill rises. We estimate that deficits could reach around 7% of gdp a year by the end of this decade—shortfalls America has not seen outside of wars and economic slumps. Worryingly, no one has a sensible plan to shrink them.

政治上的评头论足也忽略了一个更大、更持久的问题。美国的预算赤字将随着人口老龄化、救济成本的膨胀和政府利息账单的上升而膨胀。我们估计，到本十年结束时，赤字将达到每年GDP的7%左右——除了战争和经济衰退之外，美国从未出现过赤字。令人担忧的是，没有人有合理的计划来缩减赤字。

Governments elsewhere face similar pressures—and appear just as oblivious. Those in Europe are locked in a silly debate about how to tweak debt rules, at a time when the European Central Bank is indirectly propping up the finances of its weakest members. China’s official debt figures purport to be healthy even as the central government prepares to bail out a province. Governments are stuck in a fiscal fantasyland, and they must find a way out before disaster strikes.

其他国家的政府也面临着类似的压力，而且似乎同样无动于衷。在欧洲央行间接支持其最弱成员国的财政之际，欧洲各国陷入了一场关于如何调整债务规则的愚蠢辩论。尽管中央政府准备为一个省纾困，但中国的官方债务数据据称是健康的。各国政府陷入了财政幻境，他们必须在灾难降临之前找到一条出路。

For the decade after the global financial crisis of 2007-09 falling interest rates allowed governments to sustain vast debt piles. Although Europe and, to a degree, America took an axe to public spending after the crisis, by the late 2010s it looked as if they needn’t have bothered. Long-term interest rates kept falling even as debt rose. Japan’s net debt passed 150% of gdp without consequence. When covid-19 struck, rich-world governments spent another 10% of gdp; Europe’s energy crisis led to yet more handouts. Hardly anyone worried about more debt.

在2007-2009年全球金融危机之后的十年里，不断下降的利率让各国政府得以维持巨额债务。尽管欧洲和美国(在一定程度上)在危机后大幅削减了公共支出，但到2010年代末，他们似乎没有必要为此费心。即使债务上升，长期利率仍在下降。日本的净债务超过了GDP的150%，却没有产生任何后果。当covid-19爆发时，发达国家的政府又花费了GDP的10%; 欧洲的能源危机导致了更多的援助。几乎没有人担心更多的债务问题。

Those days of forgivingly low interest rates have now passed. This week the Federal Reserve raised rates again, to 5-5.25%. America will spend more on debt interest this year, as a share of gdp, than at any time so far this century; by 2030 the bill will be at an all-time high, even if rates fall as markets expect. Japan no longer looks so safe. Even though rates there are super-low the government spends 8% of its budget on interest, a figure that will shoot up should the central bank begin tightening monetary policy.

那些低利率的日子已经过去了。本周，美联储再次将利率上调至5-5.25%。作为GDP的一部分，美国今年在债务利息上的支出将超过本世纪迄今的任何时候；到2030年，即使利率像市场预期的那样下降，该账单也将达到历史最高水平。日本看上去不再那么安全了。尽管利率极低，但政府在利息上的支出占预算的8%，如果央行开始收紧货币政策，这个数字将会飙升。

Rising rates are squeezing budgets just as pressures to spend are mounting. Ageing populations mean that by the end of the decade the annual health-care and pension bill in the rich world will have risen by 3% of gdp. The figure is 2% even in emerging markets, including China, where by 2035 there will be 420m over-65s. In the West policymakers have yet to deliver on promises to spend more on defence in light of Russia’s invasion of Ukraine. And the whole world needs more green public spending if it is to decarbonise rapidly.

不断上升的利率正在挤压预算，而支出压力也在不断增加。人口老龄化意味着，到本十年末，发达国家每年的医疗保健和养老金支出将上升到GDP的3%。即使在包括中国在内的新兴市场，这个数字也只有2%。到2035年，中国65岁以上人口将达到4.2亿。鉴于俄罗斯入侵乌克兰，西方的政策制定者尚未兑现在国防上投入更多资金的承诺。而且如果全世界想要快速脱碳，就需要更多的绿色公共支出。

All told, the picture is forbidding. Take America’s Inflation Reduction Act, which was supposed to reduce deficits. Its green tax credits were forecast to cost $391bn over a decade, but are now expected by Goldman Sachs to cost an eye-watering $1.2trn. Add that and the likely extension of temporary tax cuts enacted during Donald Trump’s presidency to official projections, and America is on a path to budget deficits of 7% of gdp, even as the economy grows.

总而言之，这一前景令人生畏。以美国旨在减少赤字的《通胀削减法案》为例。据预测，其绿色税收抵免在未来10年将耗资3910亿美元，但高盛目前预计将耗资1.2万亿美元，这一数字令人惊讶。再加上唐纳德•特朗普总统任期内实施的临时减税措施可能会延长到官方预测的范围内，即使经济在增长，美国的预算赤字也将达到GDP的7%。

Such looming pressures make politicians’ proposals look wildly unrealistic. The bill to raise America’s debt ceiling passed by Republicans in the House of Representatives on April 27th caps spending in 2024 at its level in 2022, and then raises budgets by 1% a year. That may sound reasonable but it excludes mandatory spending on pensions and health care and ignores inflation. Exclude defence spending as well and it implies a real-terms budget cut of 27% compared with current plans.

这些迫在眉睫的压力使得政客们的提议看起来非常不现实。共和党于4月27日在众议院通过了提高美国债务上限的法案，该法案将2024年的支出限制在2022年的水平上，然后每年将预算提高1%。这可能听起来很合理，但它不包括养老金和医疗保健的强制性支出，也忽略了通货膨胀。也不包括国防开支，这意味着与目前的计划相比，实际预算减少了27%。

Germany’s government seems to think that a target of 60% for debt-to-gdp ratios can credibly apply to places like Italy, which has net debts of more than twice that amount. In Britain the government makes a mockery of its rules, for example by promising tax rises that are perennially postponed. A few months of better-than-expected receipts are enough to set off a clamour for tax cuts among the ruling Conservatives, only months after Britain faced a bond-market crisis.

德国政府似乎认为，60%的债务与GDP比率目标更适用于意大利这样的国家，意大利的净债务是该数字的两倍多。在英国，政府对其规定进行了嘲弄，例如承诺永久推迟增税。几个月好于预期的财政收入足以在执政的保守党中掀起减税的呼声，而几个月前英国还面临着债券市场危机。

China’s increasing indebtedness is kept off the books in opaque “financing vehicles” used by local governments. Include everything and China’s total public debts are over 120% of gdp, and will rise to nearly 150% by 2027, on imf forecasts. Such levels of debt are affordable only because China has an ocean of domestic savings, kept captive by its restrictions on capital flows. Public indebtedness means that the government cannot achieve its plan to rebalance its economy towards consumption and internationalise the yuan.

中国不断增加的债务，通过地方政府使用的不透明的“融资平台”被隐藏在账簿之外。根据国际货币基金组织的预测，包括所有因素在内，中国的公共债务总额超过GDP的120%，到2027年将上升到近150%。这种债务水平之所以能够负担得起，只是因为中国拥有大量的国内储蓄，而这些储蓄受制于其对资本流动的限制。公共债务意味着政府无法实现其经济再平衡、转向消费和人民币国际化的计划。

Politicians need to get real, fast. Public debts are in danger of becoming unmanageable, especially if interest rates stay high. Every step up in borrowing hampers governments’ ability to respond to the next crisis. And there are limits to how far spending can be controlled. Politicians could dial down their promises to pensioners or ensure that their role in the green transition is not larger than it needs to be. But there is little public appetite for austerity, and spending is bound to rise as populations age. More defence spending and green investment are essential.

政客们需要现实一点，快一点。公共债务有变得难以控制的危险，尤其是在利率居高不下的情况下。借贷的每一步都阻碍了政府应对下一次危机的能力。而且，对支出的控制程度也是有限的。政客们可以减少对养老金领取者的承诺，或者确保他们在绿色转型中的作用不会超过需要的程度。但是公众对紧缩政策没有什么兴趣，而且随着人口老龄化，支出必然会增加。更多的国防开支和绿色投资至关重要。

All this makes tax rises inevitable. And more taxation makes it crucial to raise money in ways that are friendly to economic growth. Britain’s under-taxation of posh houses is scandalous; America lacks a value-added tax and China sorely needs its long-promised property tax. Carbon emissions should be taxed sufficiently everywhere, which would also encourage the private sector to invest more in decarbonisation and thereby reduce the need for public spending to that end.

所有这些都使得增税不可避免。增加税收使得以有利于经济增长的方式筹集资金变得至关重要。英国对豪华住宅的税收不足是可耻的；美国没有增值税，而中国迫切需要承诺已久的房产税。所有地方都应该对碳排放征收足够的税，这也将鼓励私营部门加大对脱碳的投资，从而减少为此目的对公共支出的需求。

Leaving fiscal fantasyland will be painful, and there will undoubtedly be calls to put off consolidation for another day. But it is far better to make a careful exit now than to wait for the illusion to come crashing down. ■

离开财政幻想世界将是痛苦的，而且毫无疑问会有人呼吁将财政整顿推迟到明天。但与其坐等幻想破灭，不如现在谨慎退出。■
