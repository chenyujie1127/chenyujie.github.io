What should Britain’s industrial strategy be?

英国的产业战略应该是什么样？

Handouts and arms races

![image-20230201094807569](/Users/chenyujie/Library/Application Support/typora-user-images/image-20230201094807569.png)

Money matters a lot less than decent government

金钱远没有像样的政府重要

Jan 26th 2023

When rival countries have much deeper pockets and are digging into them, what is a government to do? That is the question facing Britain as it mulls what its industrial strategy should be. A good way to think about the answer is to look at the country’s automotive industry.

当竞争对手的资金雄厚得多，而且正在动用（资金）时，政府该怎么办?这是英国在考虑应该采取什么样的产业战略时面临的问题。思考这个问题的一个好方法是看看这个国家的汽车工业。

Carmaking in Britain has long enjoyed ups and endured downs. But the down it faces now is graver than any in decades. The shift from internal-combustion engines to electric vehicles is prompting an overhaul of the industry, which will spend about $1.2trn on electrification globally by 2030. Carmakers need new supply chains and new skills. Above all, they need batteries—lots of them, ideally within easy reach of assembly plants. To be a base for mass production, Britain must be home to huge battery-making facilities known as “gigafactories”.

长期以来，英国的汽车制造业经历着起起落落。但它现在面临的困境是几十年来最严重的。从内燃机到电动汽车的转变正在推动该行业的全面改革，到2030年，全球将在电气化方面投入约1.2万亿美元。汽车制造商需要新的供应链和新技能。最重要的是，他们需要大量的电池，电池最好是在组装厂附近。要成为大规模生产的基地，英国必须拥有被称为“超级工厂”的大型电池制造设施。

Across Europe, more than 40 of these facilities are being built or planned. Barely any of them are in Britain. One is being set up in Sunderland by Nissan and Envision, a Chinese firm: it will start production in 2025. Britishvolt, a startup with lots of champions but no customers, collapsed earlier this month. Some manufacturers are already voting with their feet: bmw is moving production of electric Minis to China.

在整个欧洲，有40多个这样的设施正在建设或规划中。在英国几乎没有这样的设施。日产和远景动力（一家中国公司）正在桑德兰建立这样一家工厂，该厂将于2025年投产。英国伏，一家拥有众多支持者却没有客户的初创公司Britishvolt在本月早些时候倒闭了。一些制造商已经在迁移: 宝马正将电动mini车型的生产转移到中国。

Other governments are happily splurging to encourage electric-vehicle manufacturing. Arrival, a British maker of electric delivery vans, moved production from Oxfordshire to North Carolina because of the handouts in America’s new Inflation Reduction Act. Northvolt, a battery startup in Sweden, counts the European Investment Bank among its investors. No wonder the pressure is on for the British government to shell out. Talks are thought to be under way with Tata, the Indian conglomerate that owns Jaguar Land Rover, on what subsidies might persuade it to site a gigafactory in Britain.

其他国家的政府也乐于挥霍以鼓励电动汽车制造业。英国电动货车制造商Arrival由于美国新出台的《通胀削减法案》提供的补贴，将生产从牛津郡转移到北卡罗来纳州。瑞典电池初创公司Northvolt的投资者包括欧洲投资银行。难怪英国政府面临着要掏腰包的压力。据悉，捷豹路虎的母公司印度Tata集团正在就何种补贴可能促使其在英国建立一家超级工厂展开谈判。

In a subsidy race Britain will never be able to outspend America or the European Union—nor should it try. If the government wants to help British industry, it should focus on its most basic responsibility, to provide a stable, well-run environment in which the private sector can flourish. And here Britain can do so much better, for its automotive industry and for others, without spending anything at all on subsidies.

在一场补贴竞赛中，英国的支出永远不可能超过美国或欧盟，也不应该尝试超过。如果政府想要帮助英国工业，它应该专注于自己最基本的责任，即提供一个稳定、运行良好的环境，让私营部门能够蓬勃发展。在这方面，无论是对其汽车行业还是其他行业，英国可以做得更好，而且不需要在补贴上花费任何资金。

The real problem for British carmakers is that the country has become a less attractive manufacturing base at just the moment when investment is needed. Britain’s carmakers export 80% of their output, more than half of it to the eu. Brexit has already made seamless access to European markets more difficult. And at the end of this year rules on how much of an electric vehicle’s parts have to be made in either Britain or the eu to qualify for tariff-free trade with Europe will start to tighten. An agreement to extend existing grace periods on these rules-of-origin requirements might help; better relations with the eu definitely would.

对英国汽车制造商来说，真正的问题是，在需要投资的时候，英国却变成了一个不那么有吸引力的制造基地。英国汽车制造商80%的产量用于出口，其中一半以上出口到欧盟。英国脱欧已经使无缝进入欧洲市场变得更加困难。到今年年底，有关电动汽车零部件必须在英国或欧盟生产多少才有资格获得与欧洲的零关税贸易的规定将开始收紧。同意延长这些原产地规则要求的现有宽限期的协议可能会有所帮助; 改善与欧盟的关系肯定会有所帮助。

Instead, unstable policy makes things worse. Boneheaded legislation committing Britain to replacing or repealing all retained eu law by the end of 2023 could mean friction with Brussels over labour-market standards. At home, too, uncertainty reigns. The government announced in 2021 that a zero-emissions vehicle mandate, laying out what share of manufacturers’ new sales needs to be emissions-free, would begin to apply in 2024. With 11 months to go, the industry is still waiting for details. Investment incentives are up in the air. A “super-deduction” on capital investment expires at the end of March; a plan to cut r&d tax deductions for smaller firms that was unveiled in November may be reversed. Industrial policy itself is a muddle. A strategy from 2017 was junked by Boris Johnson’s government.

相反，不稳定的政策让事情变得更糟。愚蠢的立法要求英国在2023年底之前取代或废除所有保留的欧盟法律，这可能意味着英国与布鲁塞尔在劳动力市场标准问题上发生摩擦。在其国内，也存在不确定性。政府于2021年宣布，零排放汽车任务将于2024年开始适用，该任务规定了制造商在新销售中零排放的份额。还有11个月的时间，该行业仍在等待细节。投资激励措施尚未确定。资本投资的“超抵扣”将于3月底到期; 去年11月公布的一项削减小型企业研发税收减免的计划可能会被推翻。产业政策本身就是一片混乱。鲍里斯·约翰逊政府抛弃了2017年的一项战略。

Back to basics

There are times when Britain will need to spend money to promote its national-security interests or environmental priorities. But resources are finite and it is especially unwise to bail out whichever industry is first to get into trouble. Britain is the 18th-biggest carmaker in the world and has no obvious competitive advantage in this area (unlike in life sciences and clean energy). It is good news that the country’s industries would be a lot more successful if the government did the basics well. Sweeteners matter less when other things taste better. ■

有些时候，英国需要花钱来促进国家安全利益或环境优先事项。但资源是有限的，无论哪个行业最先陷入困境，为其纾困都是尤其不明智的。英国是世界上第18大汽车制造商，在这个领域没有明显的竞争优势(不像生命科学和清洁能源行业)。好消息是，如果政府做好基础工作，该国的工业将会更加成功。当其他东西味道更好的时候，甜头就没那么重要了。■
