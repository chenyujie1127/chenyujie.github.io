Take one for the team

为团队着想

The euro zone

欧元区

![image-20210604100206344](/Users/chenyujie/Library/Application Support/typora-user-images/image-20210604100206344.png)

The alternative is a two-speed Europe

另一个选择是一个双速欧洲

To avoid a two-speed Europe, Germany must learn to live with a little inflation

为了避免一个双速欧洲，德国必须学会忍受一点通货膨胀

背景：two-speed Europe也就是One gear with greater integration in the eurozone and a more confederal gear in the European Union（一边是更密切融合的欧元区，另一边是比较接近欧盟的联邦制。）

May 27th 2021

INFLATION IN GERMANY exceeded 2% in April. If the Bundesbank’s latest forecasts prove right, it could soon reach 4% for the first time in nearly 30 years. Inflation angst is rising among the country’s monetary hawks. The exigencies of the pandemic muted German grumbling about the European Central Bank’s ultra-loose monetary policy. But as inflation rises, so will reflexive German demands that the bank should taper its bond-buying.

angst: strong worry and unhappiness, especially about personal problems

exigency 迫切；急切需要；迫切要求

mute 降低；削弱

reflexive 反射（性）的；本能（反应）的

德国4月份的通货膨胀率超过2%。如果德国央行的最新预测是正确的，它可能很快就会达到近30年来的首次4%。对于通货膨胀的担忧在这个国家的货币鹰派中与日俱增。这场疫情的紧急性平息了德国对欧洲央行超宽松货币政策的抱怨。但是随着通货膨胀的上升，德国本能地要求央行减少债券购买也将随之上升。

Such clamouring would be misguided. Europe’s recovery is more subdued than America’s, making the odds even better that the inflation spike will prove temporary. And the interests of the weaker members of the currency union, still crawling their way back to health, should come first.

subdue 克制

the odds 可能性

这样的呼声是错误的。欧洲的复苏比美国的复苏要温和得多，这使得通货膨胀的飙升是暂时的可能性更大。货币联盟中实力较弱仍在艰难地恢复健康状态，应该将他们的利益放在首位。

The hyperinflation of the 1920s etched inflation-phobia deep into the German psyche. After the second world war the Bundesbank earned a fearsome reputation by being the first to tame the inflationary beast. In the early 2000s, shortly after the birth of the euro, Germany restrained wage costs and the southern European economies lost competitiveness against it. As the southerners recovered from their debt crises, they narrowed the gap by clamping down on their own labour costs, while Germany did too little to adjust. That set the euro zone on a demand-deficient, deflationary path. The area’s economy has never fully recovered, even as the ECB has used up most of its ammunition.

hyperinflation 恶性通货膨胀

etch 蚀刻

phobia 恐惧症

clamp down on sb/sth 压制；严格限制

deflationary 通货紧缩的

20世纪20年代的恶性通货膨胀让通胀恐惧深深烙印在德国人的心里。二战后，德国央行因第一个控制住通胀“野兽”而赢得了惊人的声誉。本世纪初，欧元诞生后不久，德国控制住了工资成本，南欧经济体因此失去了对其的竞争力。随着南欧国家从债务危机中恢复过来，他们通过严格限制自己的劳动力成本来缩小差距，而德国几乎没有做出调整。这使得欧元区走上了需求不足、通货紧缩的道路。即使欧洲央行已经用尽了大部分“弹药”，但该地区的经济却从未完全恢复。

That lost decade is one reason why spiralling wages and prices in Europe are vanishingly unlikely, even as overheating is a possibility in America. What is more, the hit from the pandemic in Europe has been worse. Output in the euro zone fell further than in America last year. Despite this, its fiscal stimulus looks stingy compared with the Biden administration’s largesse. The EU’s new €750bn ($919bn) recovery fund will take years to be disbursed fully. Investors expect inflation in five years’ time to languish below the ECB’s target of “close to, but below 2%”. Expectations in America, by contrast, are above 2%.

lost decade：指的是一个国家或地区陷入长期的经济不景气的状况持续达10年左右才逐渐转好的情况

largesse 慷慨解囊

disburse 支付

那失去的十年是“欧洲工资和物价螺旋上升”的原因之一的可能性微乎其微，即使在美国经济过热是有可能的。更重要的是，疫情在欧洲的影响更为严重。去年欧元区的产出比美国下降得更厉害。尽管如此，与拜登政府的慷慨相比，它的财政刺激看起来就很小气。欧盟新的7500亿欧元(合9190亿美元)复苏基金将需要数年时间才能全额拨付。投资者预计五年内通胀率将会低于欧洲央行设定的“接近但低于2%”的目标。相比之下，美国的期望值超过了2%。

Further reassurance can be gained from peering into the mechanics of Germany’s inflation spike. As in America, it reflects some transitory global factors, including the oil-price collapse last spring which depressed the base used to calculate the annual rate. Strip out food and energy prices, and inflation in Germany was only about 1% in April. Some temporary country-specific issues are also in play. A carbon charge has pushed up prices, and an emergency cut to value-added taxes last year will arithmetically boost inflation later this year. Look along the supply chain and you might take fright at Germany’s producer-price inflation, of more than 5% in April. But this should ease as factories and suppliers respond to a surge in orders.

transitory 短暂的

strip out 对..忽略不计

value added 增值

arithmetically：in a way that involves adding, subtracting (= removing a number or amount), multiplying, or dividing numbers

take fright 受惊吓

仔细研究德国通胀飙升的机制，可以得到进一步的保证。就像在美国一样，它反映了一些暂时性的全球因素，其中包括去年春天石油价格的暴跌，这就压低了用于计算年增长率的基数。除去食品和能源价格，德国4月份的通胀率仅为1%左右。一些特定国家的临时问题也造成一定影响。征收碳排放税推高了价格，去年紧急削减增值税将在今年晚些时候推高通货膨胀率。继续看看供应链，你可能会被德国4月份超过5%的生产者价格通胀吓到。但随着工厂和供应商对订单激增作出回应，这种情况应该会有所缓解。

Perhaps the biggest reason why Germany should tolerate a period of above-target inflation is that the euro area’s health depends on it. Activity is resuming across the zone, as vaccines are administered and lockdown restrictions lifted. Germany has less lost ground to make up than most. It suffered a deep downturn last year, with the economy shrinking by 5%, but it fared far better than France, Italy and Spain, where output fell by nearly twice as much. Unemployment in Germany remains low, at 4.5%. Its reliance on manufacturing and exports has been a boon. Other countries, by contrast, must pray that spending on tourism, retail and hospitality returns to the levels of the old days. Goldman Sachs, a bank, reckons that Spain and Italy have twice as much economic slack as Germany.

regain(lost) ground: to begin to be strong or successful again after a period of difficulty

fare 进行

boon 好处；非常有用的东西

The route will be a boon to many travellers 这条路线将使很多旅行者受益无穷

也许德国应该容忍一段时间高于目标的通胀的最大原因是欧元区的健康依赖于它。随着疫苗的接种和封锁限制的解除，欧元区的活动正在恢复。与大多数国家相比，德国要弥补的损失较少。去年德国经济遭到严重衰退，其经济萎缩了5%，但比法国、意大利和西班牙要好得多，这些国家的产出下降了近两倍。德国的失业率仍然很低，只有4.5%。它对制造业和出口的依赖使其受益匪浅。相比之下，其他国家必须祈祷在旅游、零售和酒店方面的支出能够恢复到过去的水平。高盛银行认为西班牙和意大利的经济疲软程度是德国的两倍。

The ECB sets monetary policy for the currency union as a whole, not its largest member alone. And so Germany must seek to keep its economy humming along while the others catch up and the stimulus is withdrawn. That is the price of being one of the strongest members of a currency union. After a lost decade, the euro zone has learned from some of the mistakes of the past crisis. The pursuit of balanced budgets and austerity has been abandoned for the time being. The EU is on the verge of issuing common debt to finance its recovery fund. Both shifts were possible because of a change of thinking in Berlin. A recognition that the German economy must run hot is the missing link in restoring confidence throughout the euro zone. ■

hum 活跃

欧洲央行为整个货币联盟制定货币政策，而非为其最大成员国制定货币政策。因此，德国必须在其他国家迎头赶上、刺激措施撤回的同时，寻求经济稳定增长。这就是作为一个货币联盟中最强大的成员之一所要付出的代价。在失去的十年之后，欧元区已经从过去的危机中吸取了教训。对平衡预算和紧缩的追求已被暂时放弃。欧盟即将发行共同债券，为其复苏基金集资。这两种转变之所以成为可能，是因为柏林方面的想法发生了改变。认识到德国经济必须保持强劲增长，是整个欧元区恢复信心所缺少的一环。■

The George Floyd Act is a police-reform smorgasbord. Would it work?

乔治·弗洛伊德法案是警察改革的“大杂烩”。它会起作用吗?

Body of evidence

大量证据/身体证据【一语双关的感觉：身体证据指的是随身摄像头】

![image-20210603094713019](/Users/chenyujie/Library/Application Support/typora-user-images/image-20210603094713019.png)

Assessing the evidence for and against the bill

评估支持和反对法案的证据

May 27th 2021

ACCORDING TO INITIAL reports from the Louisiana State Police, Ronald Greene died in a car crash. This was in May 2019, one year before the murder of George Floyd. Mr Greene’s family was suspicious, so they pressed the police to release footage from the cameras worn by six of the officers on the scene. Not all the devices were switched on, and it took a 17-month wrangle before they could watch the footage (the public did not see it until the Associated Press leaked it, seven months after that). It showed the troopers repeatedly stunning Mr Greene with a taser, punching him, and leaving him moaning face-down on the road for nine minutes; he died on the way to hospital. The assault was recorded on body cameras.

footage 连续镜头

wrangle 争吵

trooper 州警察

根据路易斯安那州警方的初步报告，Ronald Greene死于车祸。那是2019年5月，在乔治·弗洛伊德被谋杀的前一年。Greene的家人很怀疑，所以他们向警方施压，要求警方公布在现场的六名警察佩戴的摄像头的录像。并不是所有的设备都打开了，经过了17个月的争吵，他们才看到了视频(直到7个月后，美联社泄露了这段录像，公众才看到)。视频显示，州警察们一再用泰瑟枪把Greene先生打晕，对他拳打脚踢，然后让他脸朝下趴在路上呻吟了九分钟;他在去医院的路上去世了。随身摄像头记录下了这次袭击。

After the murder of Mr Floyd a year ago, a police-reform bill named after him was introduced in Congress. It was passed by the House of Representatives but is pending in the Senate. The House version encompasses a range of policies, including restrictions on the use of force (any application of physical restraint other than handcuffing), creating a national database of officer misconduct, requiring federal officers to wear cameras and limiting qualified immunity (a judicial doctrine that shields officers from civil liability). Would it actually improve policing?

misconduct 不端行为

在一年前弗洛伊德先生被谋杀后，一项以他命名的警察改革法案被提交到国会。该法案已在众议院获得通过，但还有待参议院表决。众议院的版本包含了一系列政策，包括限制使用武力(除手铐以外的任何身体约束的实施)，建立一个关于军官不当行为的国家数据库，要求联邦官员佩戴摄像头，限制合格豁免权(保护官员不承担民事责任的司法原则)。它真的能改善治安吗?

A large-scale study by the Becker Friedman Institute at the University of Chicago found that body cameras reduce the number of police misconduct complaints by 17%. They also probably reduce the use of force. But in 2020 only about 8,000 departments (about 45%) used such cameras and just seven states required them. The Floyd Act, as currently written, requires federal uniformed officers to wear body cameras, and requires state and local law enforcement to buy them with federal funds. Much of the Floyd Act applies only to federal officials: the Federal Bureau of Investigation, United States Park Police, officers in the various federal departments, and others. They account for only about one-fifth of America’s 700,000 law-enforcement officers. But the bill could nevertheless serve as a standard for other departments, says Mark Levin, a lawyer at the Council on Criminal Justice, a think-tank.

芝加哥大学贝克尔·弗里德曼研究所的一项大规模研究发现，随身摄像头使得警察不当行为的投诉量减少了17%。它们还可能减少使用武力。但到2020年，只有大约8000个部门(约45%)使用了这种摄像头，只有7个州要求使用。目前的《弗洛伊德法》要求身穿联邦制服的警官佩戴随身摄像头，并要求州和地方执法部门用联邦资金购买摄像头。《弗洛伊德法》的大部分内容只适用于联邦官员: 联邦调查局、美国公园警察、各个联邦部门的官员等等。他们只占美国70万执法人员的五分之一。但智囊团刑事司法委员会的律师Mark Levin表示，该法案仍可作为其他部门的标准。

Just putting cameras on officers is not enough, as shown by Mr Greene’s death. North Carolina requires a judge’s approval to release video footage, as does Louisiana, where Mr Greene was killed. Eleven other states also restrict public access to body-camera footage. By contrast, when Ma’Khia Bryant, a black teenager, was shot and killed by an officer in Ohio, the video footage was released publicly in a few hours. Ohio’s body-camera policy, signed by a Republican governor, John Kasich, in 2019, makes the footage part of the public record. As currently written, the Floyd Act requires federal agencies, in most cases, to release any footage showing a death or serious injury in police custody within five days of being asked to do so.

仅仅在警察身上安装摄像头是不够的，Greene先生的死就证明了这一点。北卡罗来纳州需要法官批准才能公布视频片段，同样的，Greene遇害的路易斯安那州也是如此。其他11个州也限制公众接触随身摄像头的录像视频。相比之下，当黑人少年Ma ‘Khia Bryant在俄亥俄州被一名警察开枪打死时，这段视频在几小时内就公开发布了。俄亥俄州的随身摄像机政策是由共和党州长John Kasich在2019年签署的，这使得该视频成为了公开记录的一部分。根据目前的规定，《弗洛伊德法》要求联邦机构在大多数情况下，在被要求公布任何有关警察拘留期间发生死亡或重伤的录像后，其需要在五天内公布该录像。

Some departments allow officers to decide themselves when to switch them on. Others have strict protocols for when a camera must be activated (at the start of any call, for example). Others use cameras activated automatically by sirens or drawing a gun from its holster. Such details matter. One study found that among officers systematically recording incidents on arrival, rather than using their discretion, use-of-force incidents decreased by 37% in comparison to officers not using body cameras. Among those who chose when to turn their cameras on and off, use-of-force incidents increased by 71% against this control group. Limiting discretion protects officers, too, argues Chad Marlow of the American Civil Liberties Union. “In the climate that we live in nowadays, if police officers have discretion and make the wrong call, a malevolent motive is going to be assigned to that officer.”

sirens 警报器

malevolent 有恶意的

一些部门允许官员自己决定什么时候开机。其他一些部门对相机何时必须被激活有严格的协议(例如，在任何通话开始时)。还有的部门则使用“警笛”或者“从枪套中拔出手枪”来自动启动摄像头。这些细节很重要。一项研究发现，在警察一到达后系统地记录事件，而不是使用他们的自由裁量权（来记录），与不使用随身相机的警察相比，使用武力的事件减少了37%。在那些选择何时打开或关闭摄像机的人当中[指那些有自由裁量权的人]，使用武力的事件比对照组增加了71%。美国公民自由联盟的Chad Marlow认为，限制自由裁量权也能保护警官。“在我们现在所生活的环境中，如果警察有决定权，并做出错误的决定，那个警察就会被质疑有恶意动机。”

Some other parts of the bill are strongly supported by the evidence. De-escalation training—teaching officers techniques that require less frequent and less severe uses of force—was found to reduce use-of-force incidents by 28%, decrease citizen injuries by 26% and lower officer injuries by 36%. This sort of training, though effective, is required in only 16 states. But not all aspects of the Floyd Act are supported by rigorous evidence. Implicit-bias training, teaching officers about their unconscious biases, has been implemented in 69% of police departments. Yet the University of Chicago Crime Lab reckons that, as currently implemented, it is ineffective.■

内隐偏见：即人们虽然公开支持平等，但不知不觉中仍会歧视或区别对待自己不参与也没有归属感的外群体

该法案的其他部分也得到了证据的有力支持。降阶梯培训（教导警官减少频繁和不那么严重使用武力的技巧）使得使用武力事件减少了28%，公民受伤减少了26%，下级官员受伤减少了36%。这种培训虽然有效，但只有16个州要求这样做。但并非弗洛伊德法案的所有方面都有严格的证据支持。69%的警察部门实施了内隐偏见培训，让警官了解他们的无意识偏见。然而，芝加哥大学犯罪实验室(University of Chicago Crime Lab)认为，就目前的实施情况而言，这种方法是无效的。■

An investment bonanza is coming

bonanza 发财机遇；获利丰厚的

一场投资盛宴即将到来

Business investment

商业投资

Capex carnival

资本支出狂欢节

![image-20210602095919535](/Users/chenyujie/Library/Application Support/typora-user-images/image-20210602095919535.png)

Firms across the world are spending big. We analyse their capital-expenditure plans

世界各地的公司都在花大价钱。我们分析了他们的资本支出计划

May 25th 2021

AS LOCKDOWNS LIFT across the rich world, people are going out and spending. Australia’s restaurants have been crammed for months. America’s shopping malls are filled with people splurging stimulus cheques. Cinemas in Britain, which were allowed to reopen in mid-May, are packed once again. Yet behind the scenes another, potentially more significant, spending bonanza is just beginning.

cram 把..塞满

随着发达国家解除封锁，人们开始外出消费。澳大利亚的餐馆已经拥挤了好几个月了。美国的购物中心里到处都是挥霍经济刺激支票的人。5月中旬获准重开的英国电影院再次人满为患。然而，在这些场景的背后，另一个可能更为重要的支出盛宴才刚刚开始。

Businesses are starting to invest in huge numbers. In America capital spending (or capex) by companies is rising at an annual rate of 15%, both on the hard stuff, such as machines and factories, and intangibles, like software. Firms in other parts of the world are also ramping up spending. Forecasts for business investment have never looked so rosy. Analysts at Morgan Stanley, a bank, predict a “red-hot capex cycle”. Overall global investment, they reckon, will soar to 121% of pre-recession levels by the end of 2022 (see chart 1). Oxford Economics, a consultancy, argues that “the time looks right for a boom in capex”, while IHS Markit, a research firm, forecasts that global real fixed investment will rise by more than 6% this year.

rosy 美好的；乐观的

red-hot 强烈的；热门的；最新的

企业开始大量投资。在美国，公司的资本支出正以每年15%的速度增长，无论是机器和工厂这样的硬产品，还是软件这样的无形产品。世界其他地区的公司也在增加支出。商业投资预测从未如此乐观过。摩根士丹利的分析师预测，将出现“热门的资本支出周期”。他们认为，到2022年底，全球投资将飙升至衰退前水平的121%(见图表1)。牛津经济学咨询公司认为,“资本支出的繁荣时机看起来是合适的”, 虽然研究公司IHS Markit的数据预测,今年全球实际固定投资将增加6%以上。

![image-20210602095841412](/Users/chenyujie/Library/Application Support/typora-user-images/image-20210602095841412.png)

Today’s optimism marks quite a change from the pre-pandemic norm. In America gross domestic business investment, as a share of GDP, had been sluggish since the early 1980s. After the financial crisis of 2007-09 it took more than two years for global investment, in real terms, to regain its previous peak. By contrast, although investment fell more steeply at the start of the pandemic, it has been quicker to bounce back this time. The prospect of surging capex holds out promise that the global economy will not face a repeat of the 2010s, when growth in productivity and GDP stayed stubbornly below pre-crisis trends. Investment in new products, technologies and business practices is, after all, the foundation for higher incomes and a better quality of life. So what is behind the capex cheer—and could it last?

hold out 提供；带来

今天的乐观情绪标志着与大流行前的常态有了很大的变化。自20世纪80年代初以来，美国国内商业投资占GDP的比重一直低迷。在2007-09年的金融危机之后，全球实际投资花了两年多的时间才重拾之前的峰值。相比之下，尽管在疫情开始时投资下降得更厉害，但这次反弹得更快。资本支出飙升的前景让人们相信，全球经济将不会面临20世纪20年代的重演，当时生产率和GDP增速顽固地低于危机前的趋势水平。毕竟，对新产品、技术和商业实践的投资是提高收入和提高生活质量的基础。那么，资本支出欢呼背后的原因是什么?这种欢呼能否持续?

To understand why analysts are so upbeat, consider the firms included in the S&P 500, America’s main stockmarket index. Together they account for about one dollar in seven of total rich-world corporate capital formation. In a recent report Bank of America analyses these companies’ earnings calls since 2006, and concludes that executives are at their most bullish about capex. The Economist has looked at the biggest 25 non-financial firms in the S&P 500 and found that analysts’ expectations for capex in 2021 have risen by 10% in the past year.

upbeat 快乐的；乐观的

earning call 是公司讨论一段时期内财政情况的电话会议

bullish 看涨的；非常自信的；乐观的

要理解分析师为何如此乐观，不妨看看标普500(美国主要股票指数)中的公司。它们加起来约占发达国家企业资本形成总额的七分之一。美国银行(Bank of America)在最近的一份报告中分析了这些公司自2006年以来的电话会议，其得出的结论是，高管们最看好资本支出。《经济学人》研究了标普500中最大的25家非金融公司，我们发现分析师们对2021年资本支出的预期在过去一年中上升了10%。

For now the investment recovery is concentrated in a few industries. We find that global tech firms are expected to boost capex by 42% this year, relative to 2019. Apple will invest $430bn in America over a five-year period, an upgrade of 20% on previous plans. Taiwan’s TSMC, the world’s largest semiconductor-maker, recently announced that it would invest $100bn over the next three years in manufacturing. Analysts reckon that Samsung’s capex will rise by 13% this year, having gone up by 45% in 2020.

目前，投资恢复集中在少数几个行业。我们发现，相对于2019年，全球科技公司今年的资本支出预计将增加42%。苹果将在未来五年在美国投资4300亿美元，比之前的计划增加20%。全球最大的半导体制造商台湾台积电(TSMC)最近宣布，未来3年将在制造领域投资1000亿美元。分析师预计，三星的资本支出将在2020年增长45%的基础上，今年将增长13%。

Tech companies are spending so freely in part because the pandemic has created new demands. More shopping happens online. Remote work is on the rise. New equipment and software is needed for these to run smoothly. Recent research by Nicholas Bloom of Stanford University and Steven Davis and Yulia Zhestkova of the University of Chicago finds a big rise in the share of patent filings for work-from-home technologies. UBS, another bank, reckons that shipments of computers for commercial use will rise by nearly 10% this year, an acceleration even over the last.

科技公司如此大手大脚地花钱，部分原因是疫情创造了新的需求。网上购物的次数增多。远程工作正在兴起。这些都需要新的设备和软件才能顺利运行。斯坦福大学的Nicholas Bloom和芝加哥大学的Steven Davis和Yulia Zhestkova最近的研究发现，远程办公技术的专利申请数量大幅上升。另一家银行瑞士联合银行(UBS)估计，商用电脑的出货量今年将增长近10%，这样的增速甚至比去年还快。

Tech firms are not the only enthusiastic spenders. Firms in the S&P 500 that focus on discretionary consumer spending boosted capex by 36% year-on-year in the first quarter. Companies such as Target and Walmart, two retailers, are trying to keep up with the online giants that are eating their lunch. Marks & Spencer, an august British retailer, recently announced that it had launched 46 new websites in overseas markets from Iceland to Uzbekistan.

科技公司并不是唯一热衷于挥霍的公司。标普500指数中专注于可自由支配消费支出的公司在第一季度资本支出同比增长36%。像Target和Walmart这两家零售商这样的公司，正试图跟上正在“分食他们午餐”的网络巨头的步伐。英国领先的零售商玛莎百货最近宣布，其已经在从冰岛到乌兹别克斯坦的海外市场开设了46个新网站。

Other retailers are spending frantically to expand capacity, having been caught out by the surge in household spending. Everything from sofas to hot tubs is in short supply. Earlier this year Peloton announced “substantial incremental investments” in expediting the transport of its exercise bikes from Taiwan. Maersk, a shipping firm, recently said it would buy more containers to ease bottlenecks. The global order-book for enormous container ships has risen from 9% of the existing fleet, in October, to over 15% in April.

catch out 使突陷困境

expedite 加快

其他零售商由于受到家庭支出激增的影响，正在疯狂扩大产能。从沙发到热水浴缸，一切都供不应求。今年早些时候，Peloton宣布“大幅增加投资”，加快从台湾运输健身自行车。航运公司Maersk最近表示，它将购买更多集装箱来缓解瓶颈。全球巨型集装箱船的订单从10月份占现有船队的9%上升到4月份的15%。

The big question is whether the emerging capex boom augurs a broad and lasting shift away from the weakness of the 2010s, or is simply an enthusiastic but temporary response to reopening. Not everyone is boosting capex: our analysis suggests that about half of the companies in the S&P 500 are not expected to invest more in 2021 than they did in 2019. Global oil-and-gas firms are cutting back by a tenth relative to pre-pandemic levels, possibly in response to lower expected demand for their planet-warming fare. Airline operators are also dialling down spending, perhaps because they expect it to be a while before people can travel freely again. Many executives, including those from raw-materials and industrial-goods firms, continue to preach capital discipline. It may be quite a leap for them to go from a decade of austerity to boom time.

augur 预兆

austerity 朴素；严厉；艰苦条件

最大的问题是，新兴的资本支出热潮是否预示着一种从2010年代的疲软走向广泛而持久的转变，还是说这仅仅是对重新开放的热情而出现的短暂回应。并非所有公司都在提高资本支出:我们的分析显示，标普500指数中约一半的公司预计在2021年的投资不会超过2019年。全球的石油和天然气公司的投资相对于疫情爆发前的水平，减少十分之一，这可能是对他们应对全球变暖的预期需求降低的回应。航空公司也在削减开支，也许是因为他们预计人们还需要一段时间才能再次自由旅行。许多高管，包括那些来自原材料和工业产品公司的高管，继续鼓吹资本纪律。对他们来说，从十年的紧缩到繁荣可能是一个相当大的飞跃。

Another worry is the trend towards greater consolidation in industries from hotels to mining, which seems unlikely to have been reversed by covid-19. Research by the IMF suggests that companies with market power may be less keen on investing. In the five years before the pandemic, for instance, American business investment in hotels was barely higher than it was in the five years before the financial crisis, even though demand was far higher.

另一个令人担忧的问题是，从酒店到采矿业等行业出现了更大的整合趋势，而这种趋势似乎不太可能因covid-19而逆转。国际货币基金组织的研究表明，拥有市场影响力的公司可能不那么热衷于投资。例如，在大流行前的5年里，尽管需求远高于金融危机前的5年，但美国对酒店的商业投资仅略高于金融危机前的5年。

![image-20210602095817181](/Users/chenyujie/Library/Application Support/typora-user-images/image-20210602095817181.png)

Set against that, though, economic conditions today could convince reluctant companies to loosen the purse-strings. In contrast to the post­ﬁnancial­crisis per­iod, households have a lot of savings to spend. A more decisive fiscal and monetary response this time has also allowed firms to load up on cash (see chart 2). Bond issuance by investment-grade-rated American companies jumped to a record $1.7trn in 2020, up from $1.1trn in 2019, according to S&P Global Market Intelligence, a research outfit.

set sth against sth 把某事物与某事物相比

然而，与此相反，目前的经济状况可能会促使不情愿的企业放松财政紧缩政策。与金融危机后时期相比，美国家庭有大量储蓄可供消费。这一次更果断的财政和货币应对措施也让企业积累了大量现金(见表2)。根据标准普尔全球市场情报公司的数据，美国投资级公司的债券发行额从2019年的1.1万亿美元跃升至2020年创纪录的1.7万亿美元。

Moreover, the economic reallocation provoked by covid-19, and its investment implications, will be felt for some time. Managers in certain industries, especially semiconductors, already accept that they went into the pandemic with too little spare capacity, and are promising multi-year projects to make up for it. Perhaps most important, the pandemic is leading to an era of greater technological optimism. The rapid deployment of entirely new business models when covid-19 struck, not to mention vaccine discovery, may have reminded bosses of the payoff to investing. All that might explain why the expectations for capex by S&P 500 firms in 2022 are even more ambitious than those for this year. The investment boom may only be getting started. ■

此外，新冠肺炎引发的经济再分配及其对投资的影响将持续一段时间。某些行业的管理人员，特别是半导体行业的管理人员，已经承认他们进入大流行时闲置产能太少，并承诺实施多年项目来弥补这一不足。也许最重要的是，这一流行病正导致一个技术上更加乐观的时代。covid-19爆发时，全新商业模式的迅速部署，更不用说疫苗的发现了，这可能提醒了老板们投资的回报。所有这些都可以解释为什么标准普尔500指数公司对2022年资本支出的预期比今年的还要高。投资热潮可能才刚刚开始。■

It is caught between rich American rivals and an unsympathetic government

它被夹在富有的美国对手和冷漠的政府之间

May 29th 2021

RELATIONS BETWEEN the British Broadcasting Corporation and the government have always been delicate. Stanley Baldwin bridled at the BBC’s coverage of the General Strike of 1926. Margaret Thatcher was infuriated by its reporting from the Falklands. Hugh Carleton Greene, the corporation’s boss in the 1960s, confessed that, when dealing with Harold Wilson’s government, “I found my experience as head of psychological warfare in Malaya in 1947 extremely useful.”

An independent inquiry released on May 20th found that one of its most famous scoops, an interview in 1995 in which Princess Diana claimed that “there were three of us in this marriage”, was secured partly by deception. Oliver Dowden, the culture secretary, wrote in the Rupert Murdoch-owned Times newspaper that the BBC needed “a new emphasis on accuracy, impartiality and diversity of opinion”. To avoid “groupthink”, he said, “cultural change must be a focus”.

BBC承诺将对其编辑行为进行审查，并对其与Bashir的交易进行另一份报告，Bashir本月早些时候因健康状况不佳而离开BBC。包括Hall勋爵在内的两名前高管已经辞去了他们的新工作。威廉王子宣称，这些爆料证明了这次采访“建立了一个关于他母亲的错误叙述”。政府正在吸取更广泛的教训。英国文化大臣Oliver Dowden在Rupert murdoch旗下的《泰晤士报》(Times)上写道，BBC需要“重新强调观点的准确性、公正性和多样性”。他说，为了避免“群体思维”，“文化变革必须是一个重点”。

Conservative politicians have long suspected the BBC of closet liberal leanings. Boris Johnson’s government uses the “Brexit-Bashing Corporation” as a punchbag, as part of a strategy of drawing cultural dividing lines rather than economic ones. But the real danger posed by political skirmishes such as that over Mr Bashir is one of distraction. The more the BBC focuses on government assaults, the less it is able to cope with bigger threats emanating mostly from America.

More than six out of ten British homes now subscribe to an American video-streaming service. The result is more competition for eyeballs. In the past decade Britons have gone from watching four hours a day of broadcast television to just under three. Among under-35s, broadcast TV accounts for less than a third of video viewing (see chart).

超过六成的英国家庭现在订阅了美国的视频流媒体服务。结果就是“眼球竞争”加剧。在过去的十年中，英国人每天看电视的时间从4小时降到不到3小时。在35岁以下的人群中，广播电视在视频观看中所占比例不到三分之一(见图表)。

The BBC is good at new media. Its website is Britain’s fourth-most visited after Google, YouTube and Facebook. The iPlayer streaming service, which the BBC launched in 2007 (the same year Netflix began streaming) is used by more people in Britain than any subscription platform. It is not enough. In 2019 under-35s spent less than an hour a day consuming BBC output, across television, radio and the internet, down by 17 minutes in just two years.

BBC擅长新媒体。在英国, 其网站访问量是第四大的，仅次于谷歌、YouTube和Facebook的网站。BBC在2007年推出的iPlayer流媒体服务(同年Netflix也开始使用流媒体服务)在英国比任何订阅平台的用户都多。这还不够。2019年，35岁以下人群在电视、广播和互联网上每天花在BBC节目上的时间不到1小时，在短短两年内减少了17分钟。

The compulsory licence fee through which it is funded is not cheap: at £159 ($225) a year, it would be enough for a basic subscription to both Netflix and Disney+, with money left over for popcorn. But the BBC is limited to about 25m fee-paying households and cannot borrow. Streaming companies have the world to fish in—Netflix has over 200m subscribers, Disney+ 100m—and are willing to lose money for years in the pursuit of market share. Whereas the BBC commissioned £2.8bn of content in 2020, Netflix and Disney+ have a combined budget this year of more than $20bn. Amazon recently earmarked nearly half a billion dollars for a single “Lord of the Rings” series.

Commercial media outfits are rushing to bulk up. WarnerMedia, creator of “Game of Thrones”, “Harry Potter” and other hits, has announced plans to merge with Discovery. The last stronghold

最后一个势力强大的地方【暗指新闻领域】

No big streamer does news, an area where the BBC remains strong. Last year 70% of Britons with internet access said they had absorbed its reports in one medium or another during the past week, according to the Reuters Institute at Oxford University; among 18- to 24-year-olds the figure was 51%. This represents a drop since 2015, when 79% of all adults and 68% of young people tuned in. But the BBC remains “undoubtedly, and by a massive margin” the main source of news in Britain, says Rasmus Nielsen of the Reuters Institute.

没有哪家流媒体做新闻，而BBC在这一领域的实力仍然很强。根据牛津大学路透社研究所的数据，去年70%上网的英国人表示，在过去一周里，他们通过一种或另一种媒体获得了BBC的报道;在18至24岁的人群中，这一比例为51%。这一数字较2015年有所下降，当时有79%的成年人和68%的年轻人收看该节目。但是路透社研究所的Rasmus Nielsen说，BBC“毫无疑问地，并且有巨大的优势地”仍然是英国新闻的主要来源。

The corporation is at least stronger than other national broadcasters, which have seen similar declines in reach. Fully 86% of Britons say they are satisfied with their public-service media, versus between 50% and 61% of French, German, Spanish and Italian viewers. For all his complaining about the BBC, “Boris is intelligent enough to see that shutting it down would backfire,” says one senior Tory. The elderly, who make up the core of the Conservative vote, remain BBC addicts. And Mr Johnson’s healthy poll ratings mean he owes no favours to Mr Murdoch, who has long lobbied for the corporation to be trimmed.

该公司至少比其他全国性的广播公司更强大，后者的覆盖范围也出现了类似的下降。86%的英国人说他们对自己的公共服务媒体感到满意，而法国、德国、西班牙和意大利的观众对此的满意度在50%到61%之间。一位保守党资深人士“对于他对BBC的抱怨”表示道，“鲍里斯足够聪明，他知道关闭BBC会适得其反，”。老年人是保守党的核心选民，他们仍然对BBC着迷。约翰逊良好的民调支持率意味着他不欠Murdoch任何人情，后者一直在游说削减公司规模。

May 29th 2021

“HAVE A BREAK” is a slogan associated with the popular chocolate snack, KitKat. But it may be pretty good advice for any manager or worker, minus the calories. The longer the shift, the less effective the employee may become.

“休息一下”(HAVE A BREAK)是一条与流行巧克力零食奇巧(KitKat)有关的口号。但这对任何经理或工人来说都是很好的建议，减去卡路里。轮班时间越长，员工的工作效率可能就越低。

In a new paper for Royal Society Open Finance, “Quantifying the cost of decision fatigue: suboptimal risk decisions in finance”, Tobias Baer and Simone Schnall examine the credit decisions of loan officers at a leading bank over the course of their working day. The academics write that decision fatigue “typically involves a tendency to revert to the ‘default’ option, namely whatever choice involves relatively little mental effort”. In other words, as you become tired, you get mentally lazy.

在为英国皇家学会(Royal Society Open Finance)撰写的一篇新论文《量化决策疲劳的成本:金融中的次优风险决策》中，Tobias Baer和Simone Schnall研究了一家顶级银行信贷员在工作日中的信贷决策。学者们写道，决策疲劳“通常涉及一种倾向，即回到‘默认’选项，即任何需要相对较少脑力努力的选择”。换句话说，当你感到疲倦时，你就会在精神上变得懒惰。

The study looked at proposals to restructure loans, with each credit officer analysing 46 requests per day. The approval rate was around 40%, so the default decision was rejection. Officers tended to start work between 8am and 10am, took lunch between 1pm and 3pm, and tended to leave at 6pm.

这项研究研究了重组贷款的建议，每位信贷人员每天分析46项贷款请求。批准率大约是40%，所以默认的决定是拒绝。官员常在早上8点到10点之间开始工作，在下午1点到3点之间吃午饭，并在下午6点下班离开。

The researchers found that the approval rate declined significantly between 11am and 2pm, as lunch approached, then picked up again after 3pm before declining in the last two hours of work. The applications were distributed to credit officers by the bank’s automated system, so they were in effect allocated randomly. There is no sign that the loans assessed at lunchtime were of a different quality from those in the rest of the day.

研究人员发现，在上午11点至下午2点之间，随着午餐时间的临近，批准率显著下降。下午3点之后，批准率再次回升，然后在最后两个小时的工作中下降。这些应用程序是由银行的自动系统分发给信贷人员的，因此它们实际上是随机分配的。没有迹象表明，午餐时间评估的贷款质量与当天其他时间评估的贷款不同。

What makes this study ingenious is that the authors were able to see whether or not the loans were subsequently paid back. They found that rejecting a restructuring request made it less likely that the loan would be repaid. So they calculated that decision fatigue, by causing more rejections, actually cost the bank money; around $500,000 over the course of a single month.

这项研究的巧妙之处在于，作者能够看到这些贷款后来是否被偿还。他们发现，拒绝重组请求会降低贷款偿还的可能性。因此，他们计算出，决策疲劳，会导致更多的拒绝，实际上会让银行损失资金;一个月大约（损失）50万美元。

Similar patterns have been seen in other situations. A much-cited study of Israeli judges found they were less likely to grant parole as lunch approached, but became more lenient once their stomachs were full again. A new approach to debugging

May 20th 2021

RIBONUCLEIC ACID (RNA), once little-known outside biological circles, has recently become the molecule de nos jours. The reason is its role in covid-19 vaccines. The RNA molecules in these encode spike, a coronavirus protein. So, when the protein-making machinery of a body cell encounters such RNA, spike is what it makes. That lets a vaccine-recipient’s immune system learn to recognise a crucial part of the enemy before the real thing turns up.

de nos jours （法语）当代的

核糖核酸(RNA)，曾经在生物圈外鲜为人知，最近成为分子的日常。原因在于它在covid-19疫苗中的作用。其中的RNA分子编码刺突，一种冠状病毒蛋白。所以，当人体细胞的蛋白质制造机制遇到这样的RNA时，它就会制造刺突。这让疫苗接受者的免疫系统学会在真正的敌人出现之前识别出敌人的关键部分。

Helping to make proteins is not, however, RNA’s only job. Among many other things it is central to a process called RNA interference, which prevents, rather than facilitates, the manufacture of specific proteins. RNAi, as this activity is called for short, has also been investigated medically. It has been approved for use against four genetic diseases and is under investigation for the treatment of more than a dozen others. That is good. Some biologists, though, think RNAi may have an important non-medical use as well, as a precisely targeted, environmentally friendly pesticide.

然而，帮助蛋白质的制造并不是RNA的唯一工作。在其他许多方面，它是RNA干扰过程的中心，RNA干扰而非促进特定蛋白质的制造。RNAi(作为这种活动的简称)也在医学上进行了研究。它已被批准用于治疗四种遗传疾病，目前正在调查治疗十多种其他疾病。这是好现象。然而，一些生物学家认为，RNAi也可能有重要的非医疗用途，作为一种精确靶向的、环境友好的杀虫剂。

The theory is simple. Identify a protein crucial to the survival of the pest in question. Tailor a specific interfering RNA molecule to sabotage production of that protein. Deliver it into the bodies of the pests. Then wait for them all to die. In practice, of course, things are more complicated. Delivery mechanisms have to be designed and regulatory hoops jumped through. But until recently, the biggest obstacle was cost. Life-saving medicines can be expensive. Pesticides must be cheap. One effect of all the medical RNA work, however, has been to bring down the cost of making the stuff. As Michael Helmstetter, the boss of RNAissance Ag, a firm in Kansas which is developing RNA-based pesticides, observes, “a gram of RNA cost $100,000 when we started. By 2014 it was $100 a gram. Now it’s a dollar a gram.”

德国Giessen的Fraunhofer研究所校园的昆虫学家Andreas Vilcinskas正在与GreenLight合作，他说德国政府现在支持他们的发展。它有充分的理由这样做。2018年，欧盟禁止户外使用三种新烟碱类杀虫剂，这是一种流行的杀虫剂。从那时起，在蚜虫像野火一样蔓延之后，德国、法国和波兰都不得不紧急撤销这一禁令。讽刺的是，禁止新烟碱是为了帮助蜜蜂。因此，将RNA作为杀虫剂来推广，可以一石二鸟。■
