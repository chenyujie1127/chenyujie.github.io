May 6th 2021

“SAVE AMERICA” is the slogan at the top of Donald Trump’s newly launched blog, where anyone nostalgic for 2020 can find tirades resembling those he plastered on social media before he was kicked o****ﬀ Facebook, Instagram, Twitter and YouTube for his statements during the storming of the Capitol on January 6th. Next to his posts are icons for users to click and share Mr Trump’s messages on Facebook and Twitter, reminders of how those platforms confer the widest reach and influence.

reach 影响力

“拯救美国”是唐纳德·特朗普新开设的博客上的口号，怀念2020年的人都可以在那里找到类似他在社交媒体上贴出的长篇大论，此前他因在1月6日国会大厦风暴中的发言而被踢出Facebook、Instagram、Twitter和YouTube。他的帖子旁边是用户点击并在Facebook和Twitter上分享特朗普的信息的图标，提醒人们这些平台是如何赋予了最广泛的影响力。

For now, Mr Trump’s blog is all he has. On May 5th Facebook’s independent content-review body, the Oversight Board (OB), issued its most anticipated ruling since it began hearing cases last year, upholding the company’s decision to suspend Mr Trump’s accounts but saying that doing so indefinitely was inappropriate.

目前，特朗普只有博客。5月5日，Facebook的独立内容审查机构监督委员会(OB)发布了自去年开始审理案件以来最令人期待的裁决，支持该公司暂停特朗普账户的决定，但表示无限期这样做是不合适的。

In essence, the OB has returned the burden to Facebook, telling it to devise clearer rules and more consistent penalties, and giving it six months to make a final decision regarding Mr Trump’s access to the platform. It also asked Facebook to review its role in helping spread Mr Trump’s lies about November’s election being stolen, and in fomenting the riot of January 6th.

in essence 实质上

从本质上说，OB已经把责任推给了Facebook，要求它制定更清晰的规则和更一致的惩罚措施，并给它6个月的时间，就特朗普使用该平台的问题做出最终决定。它还要求Facebook审查其在帮助传播特朗普关于11月大选被窃取的谎言以及在煽动1月6日的骚乱中所扮演的角色。

Those hoping for a final verdict on whether Mr Trump could return to Facebook will be disappointed. But the OB’s decision is significant nonetheless. It points to the online platforms must strike between free speech, online misinformation and real-world harm—a quick calculus made by too few people with too little transparency. It also highlights the influence that the OB is trying to exert by speaking snark to power. “In applying a vague, standardless penalty and then referring this case to the Board to resolve, Facebook seeks to avoid its responsibilities,” the OB wrote in its decision. According to Michael McConnell, a former judge who is co-chair of the OB, Mr Trump’s “is not the only case in which Facebook has engaged in ad hoc-ery”.

snark 故意中伤的批评

ad hoc 临时的；特别的

-ery …群体(或类)的事物

那些希望“特朗普是否会重返Facebook”得到最终裁决的人将会感到失望。然而，OB的决定意义重大。它指出，网络平台必须在言论自由、网络错误信息和现实世界的伤害之间进行权衡——这是由太少的人在缺乏透明度的情况下迅速做出的计算。这也凸显了OB试图通过对权力采用故意中伤的批评来施加影响。OB在其决定中写道：“Facebook采用了一种模糊的、不符合标准的处罚，然后将此案提交董事会解决，这是在逃避其责任”。据OB联席主席、前法官Michael McConnell表示，特朗普的案例并非Facebook唯一参与的特别案例。

The OB was conceived in 2018 by Facebook’s founder, Mark Zuckerberg, as a “supreme court” for content decisions. Cynics view it as an attempt to deflect responsibility for the company’s thorniest decisions, but it is a worthwhile experiment in creating a middle ground between corporate autonomy and government intervention. The 20 members of the board have an independent streak. Of the nine cases on which the OB has ruled, it has overturned Facebook’s initial decision six times.

Cynics 愤世嫉俗者

streak 性格特征

Facebook创始人马克·扎克伯格于2018年设想成立OB，作为内容裁决的“最高法院”。愤世嫉俗者认为，此举是为了推卸公司最棘手决策的责任，但可以在公司自治和政府干预之间创造一个中间地带，是一个值得尝试的实验。OB的20位成员有独立的性格特征。在OB裁决的9起案件中，其有6次推翻了Facebook的最初决定。

If the decision on Mr Trump’s case pushes the social network to make its policies about how and when bans are imposed clearer, the OB will have proved its worth. But it left a “big gap” by not offering more definitive guidance, says David Kaye, a former UN special rapporteur on free speech. He argues that the board should have been clearer about when politicians should receive different treatment than other users, and also that it should have been given Facebook more direction about investing more to reach faster decisions on questionable but significant posts.

如果关于特朗普一案的决定促使这家社交网络更明确地制定有关如何以及何时实施禁令的政策，OB将证明其价值。但是，前联合国言论自由特别报告员David Kaye说，由于没有提供更明确的指导，它留下了一个“巨大的鸿沟”。他认为，该委员会本应更清楚地说明政客们何时应该得到与其他用户不同的待遇，还应该给Facebook更多的指导，让它加大投资力度，能够更快地对有问题但意义重大的帖子做出决定。

Nor will the OB’s decision shield Facebook from more criticism. Senator Bernie Sanders and Alexei Navalny, the Russian opposition leader, are among those who have expressed concern about social-media platforms censoring Mr Trump. The OB’s decision “will be cheered by people on the left in the short run,” predicts Matthew Perault, who runs the centre on science and technology policy at Duke University and was formerly Facebook’s director of public policy. “But in the long run, regulators will be scared of what this shows about the company’s ability to choose winners and losers on speech.” According to Mr Perault, the chances that the government will eventually take on big tech by rewriting antitrust, privacy and content rules have just gone up. ■

Snippets of RNA are the giveaways

giveaway 表明真相的事物

RNA片段是明显的证据

May 6th 2021

MAJOR DEPRESSION is a serious illness, but also an elusive one. It wrecks lives and may drive people to suicide. It sometimes, though not always, alternates with periods of mania in a condition called bipolar disorder. And it is disturbingly common. Reliable figures are hard to come by, but in some parts of the world as many as one person in four experiences major depression at some point during their life.

bipolar 躁郁的

come by 获得；得到

抑郁症是一种严重的疾病，但也是一种难以捉摸的疾病。它破坏生命，可能导致人们自杀。它有时（尽管不总是）会与躁狂症交替出现，这被称为双相情感障碍。而且令人不安的是，它很普遍。可靠的数据很难获得，但在世界上的一些地方，多达四个人中就有一个人在人生的某个阶段经历过严重的抑郁症。

Depression’s diagnosis has, though, a worryingly arbitrary quality to it, depending as it does on a doctor’s assessment of a patient’s mood against a checklist of symptoms which may be present in different combinations and are often, in any case, subjective. This has led to a search for reliable biochemical markers of the illness. Not only might these assist diagnosis, they may also improve assessments of prognosis and point towards the most effective treatment in a particular case. Now, a group of neuroscientists at Indiana University, in Indianapolis, led by Alexander Niculescu, think they have found a set of markers that can do all this.

然而，抑郁症的诊断有一种令人担忧的随意性，这取决于医生对病人情绪的评估，评估是通过一个症状清单，这些症状可能以不同的组合出现，而且在任何情况下往往都是主观的。这使得人们开始寻找这种疾病的可靠生化标志物。它们不仅可能有助于诊断，还可能改善预后评估，并为特定病例指明最有效的治疗方法。现在，位于印第安纳波利斯的印第安纳大学的一组神经科学家，在Alexander Niculescu的领导下，认为他们已经发现了一组可以做到这一切的标志物。

As they write in Molecular Psychiatry, Dr Niculescu and his colleagues have been working with data and blood samples collected over the course of 15 years from hundreds of patients at the Indianapolis Veterans Administration Medical Centre. The targets of their investigation were small pieces of RNA, a molecule similar to DNA which is copied from the DNA of genes as part of the process by which the information encoded in those genes is used by cells to make proteins.

正如他们在《Molecular Psychiatry》上所写的，Niculescu博士和他的同事一直在研究数据和血液样本，这些数据和样本是在印第安纳波利斯退伍军人管理医疗中心收集的，在过去的15年里收集了数百名病人的血液样本。他们研究的目标是RNA的小片段。RNA是一种类似于DNA的分子，可以从基因的DNA中复制，细胞利用这些基因中编码的信息来制造蛋白质。

Tracking levels in the blood of relevant RNA molecules shows the activity of the underlying genes. That let the researchers identify, in an initial sample of 44 patients’ records, which genes were becoming more and less active as people’s mood disorders waxed and waned. To start with, they found thousands of possible candidate genes in this way, but they first narrowed these down to those that seemed to show the best prediction of mood and then, by turning to the corpus of published research on genes associated with depression, narrowed the selection still further to 26 that had previously been suspected of involvement in the illness. They then followed this clutch up in eight groups of patients, ranging in size from 97 to 226, to see which best predicted the course and details of a patient’s illness.

追踪血液中相关RNA分子的水平可以显示潜在基因的活性。这使得研究人员能够在44名患者的初始记录样本中识别出，随着人们情绪障碍的消长，哪些基因变得越来越活跃或越来越不活跃。首先，他们以这种方式找到了数千种可能的候选基因，但他们首先将范围缩小到那些似乎能最好地预测情绪的基因，然后，通过查阅已发表的与抑郁相关的基因研究语料库，将选择范围进一步缩小到26个先前被怀疑与疾病有关的基因[候选标志物]。然后，他们对八组患者进行了跟踪调查，每组患者的数量从97到226不等，看哪些基因最能预测病人的病情和细节。

Thirteen markers survived this final winnowing. The genes they represent are involved in a range of activities, including running circadian rhythms (the endogenous clocks which keep bodily activities synchronised with each other and with the daily cycle of light and darkness); regulating levels in the brain of a messenger molecule called serotonin, the activity of which is well known to get out of kilter in depression; responding to stress; metabolising glucose to release energy; and signalling within cells.

winnow 辨识

endogenous: found or coming from within something, for example a system or a person’s body or mind

out of kilter 不一致

glucose 葡萄糖

在最后的筛选中，有13个标记幸存了下来。它们所代表的基因涉及一系列活动，包括运行昼夜节律(保持身体活动相互同步以及与每日昼夜周期同步的内源性生物钟); 调节大脑中名为血清素的信使分子的水平，众所周知，血清素的活性会在抑郁症中失衡; 应对压力; 代谢葡萄糖以释放能量; 以及细胞内的信号传递。

Together, these 13 RNA markers form the basis of a blood test that can not only diagnose depression, but also predict who will go on to develop bipolar disorder, who is likely to become ill enough to need hospital treatment in the future, and which drugs will most probably be effective in particular cases. Six of the RNAs were good predictors of depression alone. Another six predicted both depression and mania. One predicted mania alone.

这13个RNA标志物是血液测试的基础形式,不仅可以诊断抑郁症, 还可以预测谁会继而发展成为双相情感障碍, 谁有可能在未来病到需要医院治疗，以及哪些药物在特定情况下最有可能有效。其中6种RNA是单独预测抑郁症的良好指标。另外6个可以同时预测抑郁症和躁狂症。有一个是仅可预言是躁狂症。

On top of their potential role in diagnosis, three of the genes identified are known from previous work to be affected by lithium carbonate, an established treatment for bipolar disorder, and two others are affected by a class of antidepressant drugs called selective serotonin reuptake inhibitors, of which Prozac is probably the best-known example. It is for this reason that Dr Niculescu thinks his blood test may help to pick appropriate treatments.

除了它们在诊断方面的潜在作用外，先前的研究发现，其中三个基因会受到碳酸锂(一种公认的双相情感障碍治疗方法)的影响，另外两个基因会受到一种称为选择性血清素再摄取抑制剂的抗抑郁药物的影响，其中百忧解可能是其中最著名的例子。正是出于这个原因，Niculescu博士认为他的验血可以帮助选择合适的治疗方法。

Tests of reason

The results even indicate some non-psychiatric drugs that might be worth trying, since the analysis showed they had characteristics which could affect some of the biomarkers. A beta blocker called Pindolol, for example, is currently used to treat high blood pressure. But this drug is also known to affect serotonin activity, and Dr Niculescu and his colleagues found, from a search of the published literature, that it has been seen to affect levels of all six of the depression-only biomarkers. That, he thinks, might make it a good candidate for the treatment of depression.

结果甚至表明一些非精神类药物可能值得尝试，因为分析显示它们具有可能影响某些生物标志物的特征。例如，一种叫做Pindolol的β受体阻滞剂目前被用于治疗高血压。但是人们也知道这种药物会影响血清素的活性，Niculescu博士和他的同事们通过对已发表文献的搜索发现，它会影响所有六种仅用于检测抑郁症的生物标志物的水平。他认为，这可能使它成为治疗抑郁症的好方法。

To turn all this into practical help for patients, Dr Niculescu and his colleague and co-author Anantha Shekhar have founded a company called MindX Sciences and are seeking regulatory approval for the test’s medical use. If all goes well, future versions might incorporate additional biomarkers, and might use saliva rather than blood as the fluid sampled. Biomarker-diagnosis of depression is unlikely to replace assessment by checklist, which would still be needed to see who should be sent for screening in the first place. But as a way of confirming and refining diagnoses, and also of suggesting treatment in what is both an uncertain and a sensitive area, it seems an important advance. ■

A brave new world for banks

The transition from banking may make financial services cheaper, quicker and fairer. But it will threaten privacy and sovereignty and expand the role of state

银行业的转型可能会使金融服务变得更便宜、更快、更公平。但这将威胁到隐私和主权，并扩大国家的作用

May 6th 2021

NOBODY LIKES banks. Their technology is often primitive. Their users are hit with unpredictable fees. Their functions matter, yet their coverage is incomplete. This can relegate swathes of people in rich countries and entire populations of poor ones to the fringes of society. Many of the biggest make most profits from trading and fees, not providing services to ordinary customers. And worst of all, their failures can cause catastrophic damage for which they bear only a fraction of the costs.

没人喜欢银行。他们的技术通常很原始。他们的用户受到不可预测的费用的打击。它们的功能很重要，但覆盖范围并不完整。这可能使发达国家的大量人口和贫穷国家的全部人口处于社会边缘。许多大银行的大部分利润来自交易和收费，而不是向普通客户提供服务。最糟糕的是，它们的失败可能导致灾难性的损失，而它们只承担一小部分成本。

It would seem rational, therefore, to cheer the fierce new competition that is reducing banks’ traditional role. As capital markets expand, it is becoming easier to match assets with liabilities that naturally fund them, reducing the risk of bank runs or failures. A proliferation of payment technologies is upending how people and firms conduct their lives, giving millions of underserved customers previously unimaginable access to finance. Data can be used instead of assets to secure loans, making it possible for people and businesses to borrow money against their character, not their collateral. The great promise of finance can be offered to many more people. The great costs are diminished. Better-informed firms should also temper the instability inherent in today’s form of credit provision.

proliferation 激增

underserved 服务不完备的

collateral 抵押物

temper 缓和;使..下降

因此，为正在削弱银行传统角色的激烈新竞争欢呼似乎是合理的。随着资本市场的扩张，将资产与自然为其提供资金的负债相匹配变得越来越容易，从而降低了银行挤兑或倒闭的风险。支付技术的普及正在颠覆人们和公司的生活方式，为数百万未得到充分服务的客户提供此前无法想象的融资渠道。数据可以代替资产来获得贷款，这使得个人和企业可以根据自身的特性(而不是抵押品)来借钱。金融的伟大前景可以提供给更多的人。巨大的成本减少了。消息灵通的公司也应该缓和当前信贷规定形式固有的不稳定性。

But this revolution brings with it new problems. Because they benefit from network effects, payment platforms will tend to concentrate into just one or two giant intermediaries. As more activity migrates away from banks, existing monetary-policy mechanisms may become obsolete. The risk of losing control over the monetary system has lit a fire underneath usually staid central bankers. Projects to accelerate fast payment systems have cropped up worldwide. So have efforts to create central-bank digital currencies, a digital form of paper cash to compete with the digital payments of private giants. In China, where payments firms are most advanced, more than 100,000 people are already using a digital yuan in pilot projects.

staid 古板的

croop up 出现; 被提及

pilot 试用

但这场革命也带来了新的问题。由于受益于网络效应，支付平台往往会集中于一两家大型中介机构。随着更多的活动从银行转移出去，现有的货币政策机制可能会过时。失去对货币体系控制的风险，在通常古板的央行官员们下面点燃了一把火。加速快速支付系统的项目已经在世界各地涌现。创造央行数字货币的努力也是如此，央行数字货币是一种与私营巨头的数字支付相竞争的数字形式的纸币。在支付公司最先进的中国，已经有超过10万人在试点项目中使用数字人民币。

The roll-out of what amounts to a new monetary system thus comes with risks. Many of the issues it raises are as tricky as the financial stability and inclusion problems of old. As people move their money into CBDCs they will pull it out of banks, threatening the system developed to foster loans without overt state interference in credit allocation. The digital involvement of the central bank in everyday transactions would not only increase the risk of such interference but also be a gift to those wishing to snoop on citizens. And the concentration of the economy in digital money, whether it is run by private firms or by a central bank, could provide a new route for malicious actors to destabilise systems through cyberwarfare.

snoop 窥探

malicious 怀有恶意的

因此，相当于新货币体系的推出也伴随着风险。它引出的许多问题与过去的金融稳定和包容性问题一样棘手。当人们将资金转移到“央行数字货币”时，他们将从银行取出资金，这将威胁到为促进贷款而发展起来的、没有国家公开干预信贷分配的体系。央行对日常交易的数字化介入不仅会增加这种干预的风险，还会给那些希望窥探公民的人一份礼物。而且，无论是由私营企业还是央行运营，数字货币经济的集中，可能为恶意行为者通过网络战破坏系统稳定提供了一条新途径。

Yet there should be ways to minimise each of these problems. The best defence against the unhealthy spreading of state influence over lending decisions is to keep the current fractional-reserve system alive. This may mean constraining the scope of CBDCs and managing them at arm’s length from the central bank—perhaps through third parties. exodus 大批离开

背景：金丝雀码头与传统的伦敦金融中心——“一平方英里”（The Square Mile，实为“伦敦市” City of London）相匹敌。英国最高的三栋建筑均坐落于此——235.1高的第一加拿大广场（One Canada Square，亦称“金丝雀码头塔” Canary Wharf Tower）、第八加拿大广场（8 Canada Square，亦称“汇丰银行塔” HSBC Tower）和花旗集团中心（Citigroup Centre），它们均高199.5米。

然而，到目前为止，就业流失一直很小。根据专业服务公司EY的数据，自2016年以来，金融服务公司已将7600个职位从英国转移到欧盟。智囊团 New Financial认为这个数字是7400。EY调查了222家公司，New Financial调查了441。相比之下，伦敦金融城有19万个金融服务岗位，金丝雀码头有6.7万个金融服务岗位，整个行业有110万个金融服务岗位。为什么走的人这么少?

Start with what the changes to Europe’s financial plumbing actually involve. The movement of share-dealing venues evokes an image of open-outcry traders vacating City exchange halls en masse and heading for the Netherlands. In reality, London’s exchanges and Amsterdam’s bourses have long been online platforms. The computer mainframes hosting transactions may now be outside Utrecht rather than in the Home Counties, but traders tapping at keyboards do not need to move.

vacate 辞去

en masse 全体

先从欧洲金融管道的变化实际涉及的内容开始说起。股票交易场所的流动让人想起公开叫价的交易员们集体离开伦敦证券交易所大厅，前往荷兰的情景。事实上，伦敦的交易所和阿姆斯特丹的交易所长期以来都是在线平台。托管交易的计算机主机现在可能在乌得勒支以外的地方，而不是在伦敦周围各郡，但敲打键盘的交易员不需要移动。

Meanwhile, banks are mostly moving as few jobs as they can to satisfy regulators. That includes some senior staff, salespeople, traders, risk managers and compliance. But of the ten banks asked by The Economist about their plans, none said they intended to shift people in numbers greater than the low hundreds. JP Morgan, whose parent company’s chief executive warned in 2016 that Brexit could cause the bank to cut 4,000 of its British jobs, will have moved fewer than 400 by the end of 2021. Morgan Stanley, which spent the aftermath of the referendum denying reports that it had started moving 2,000 staff to Dublin and Frankfurt, has moved 150.

与此同时，为了满足监管机构的要求，银行大多在尽可能少地转移工作岗位。这包括一些高级员工、销售人员、交易员、风险经理和合规人员。但在接受《经济学人》采访的10家银行中，没有一家银行表示他们打算转移人数超过几百人的人员。摩根大通(JP Morgan)的母公司首席执行官曾在2016年警告称，英国脱欧可能导致该行在英国裁员4000人。到2021年底，摩根大通的工作岗位将减少到不到400个。有报道称，摩根士丹利已开始将2000名员工迁往都柏林和法兰克福。在英国公投结束后，摩根士丹利一直否认这一报道，其目前已迁移150人。

In the run-up to Brexit, both Paris and Frankfurt made a play for London’s business, but they do not have its attractions as a place to live. In a survey by the Boston Consulting Group and The Network, a group of online recruiters, published in March, respondents from 190 countries named London as the place they were keenest to move to. Convincing lots of well-heeled bankers to abandon the city’s cultural life—and, more important, posh schools—turns out to be a difficult task.

a run-up to sth 某事的准备阶段

well-heeled 有钱的

在英国脱欧前夕，巴黎和法兰克福都在争取伦敦的业务，但它们并没有伦敦可以作为居住场所的吸引力。波士顿咨询集团和在线招聘机构the Network今年3月发布的一项调查显示，来自190个国家的受访者认为伦敦是他们最希望搬去的地方。说服许多富有的银行家放弃城市的文化生活，更重要的是，放弃上流社会的学校，是一项艰巨的任务。

The competition also lacks critical mass. The north of England exports more financial services than France does, while Frankfurt’s whole financial sector employs fewer workers than London’s fintech companies. And while the French and German governments are keen to attract business, the EU’s more modest aim is to reduce its reliance on Britain, and to deepen its own “capital-markets union”. As a result, firms are sceptical that the bloc will be a driver of future growth.

critical mass 产生质变所需的量

[释义] the size that something needs to reach before a particular change, event, or development can happen

竞争也缺乏产生质变所需要的量。英格兰北部出口的金融服务比法国多，而法兰克福整个金融业雇佣的工人比伦敦的金融科技公司少。当法国和德国政府渴望吸引业务时，欧盟更温和的目标是减少对英国的依赖，并深化自己的“资本市场联盟”。因此，企业对欧盟能否成为未来增长的驱动力持怀疑态度。

That is not to say that it will be plain sailing for the City. A spat over clearing is likely when its temporary-equivalence designation expires next year. A question-mark also hovers over British asset managers’ permission to oversee EU-domiciled investment funds. **But waves of job losses are not yet on the horizon.**■

spat 小争吵

question mark 问号

这并不是说伦敦金融城将一帆风顺。当临时对等规定于明年到期时，一场关于清算的争论很可能会爆发。英国资产管理公司是否获准监管在欧盟注册的投资基金，仍是一个问号。但失业潮尚未出现。■

Why are American workers becoming harder to find?

为什么美国工人越来越难找?

Labour shortages are rising even though unemployment remains high

尽管失业率居高不下，劳动力短缺仍在加剧

Apr 29th 2021

THE PANDEMIC has led to all sorts of weird economic outcomes. The latest oddity is the growing chorus of complaints in America about a shortage of labour, even though 8m fewer people are in work today than before covid-19 struck. In early April Bloomberg reported that Delta Air Lines had cancelled 100 flights for lack of staff. People are so hard to find that one café in Florida has turned to robots to greet customers and deliver food. A branch of McDonald’s is paying potential burger-flippers $50 just to turn up for a job interview.

oddity 怪异的事物

burger-flippers 做汉堡包的厨师；有时也引申为社会底层的劳动者

大流行病导致了各种奇怪的经济结果。最新的奇怪之处是，尽管目前美国的就业人数比新冠肺炎爆发前减少了800万人，但对劳动力短缺的抱怨却越来越多。4月初，彭博社报道称，达美航空(Delta Air Lines)因人手不足取消了100个航班。由于实在是太难找人手以至于佛罗里达的一家café公司已经开始用机器人来招呼顾客和送餐了。麦当劳(McDonald ‘s)的一家分店付给那些想去面试做汉堡包的厨师50美元。

![image-20210505194019379](/Users/chenyujie/Library/Application Support/typora-user-images/image-20210505194019379.png)

The data back up the anecdotes. Total vacancies are running at their highest level for at least two decades (see chart), indicating that firms have plenty of unfilled positions. Furthermore, job openings are leading to fewer hires than you would expect based on the historical relationship between the two. And even accounting for changes in the composition of the workforce, wage growth, at about 3%, has been surprisingly robust, suggesting that firms are offering bigger pay packets to tempt workers. If they persist job shortages could eventually fuel inflation, threatening the economic recovery.

unfilled 空缺的

有数据支持了这些轶事。职位空缺总数达到了至少20年来的最高水平(见图表)，其表明公司有大量空缺职位。此外，根据两者之间的历史关系，职位空缺导致的招聘人数比你预期的要少。即使考虑到劳动力构成的变化，约3%的工资增长也出人意料地强劲，这表明企业正在提供更大的薪酬来吸引工人。如果他们持续就业短缺，最终可能加剧通货膨胀，威胁到经济复苏。

There are three potential explanations for the puzzling shortages: over-generous benefits; fearful workers; and a reallocation of labour between industries. Start with America’s huge fiscal handouts. The latest stimulus cheques, posted in the spring, were for up to $1,400 per person. Seemingly every American knows of a neighbour’s cousin’s boyfriend who received a “stimmy” cheque, then quit his job in order to sit on the sofa. A federal supplement to unemployment insurance (UI), currently $300 a week, ensures that four in ten unemployed people earn more from benefits than they did in their previous job. Economic research has long concluded that more generous benefits blunt incentives to look for work.

blunt 减弱

对于这令人费解的短缺，有三种可能的解释:福利过于慷慨;害怕的工人以及劳动力在行业间的重新分配。先从美国的巨额财政援助说起。今年春天发布的最新刺激支票，每人的金额高达1400美元。似乎每个美国人都知道邻居表亲的男朋友收到了“吝啬”的支票，然后辞掉工作坐在沙发上。一项联邦失业保险(UI)的附加费，目前是每周300美元，确保了四成的失业者比他们在上一份工作中获得更多的福利。长期以来，经济研究得出的结论是，更慷慨的福利会削弱找工作的动力。

Yet this relationship appears to have weakened during the pandemic. The fact that increases in UI payments have been time-limited may make workers reluctant to turn down a job with longer-lasting rewards. In the early part of the pandemic the UI supplement was even more generous, at $600, but its expiry in the summer had “little effect on overall employment”, according to a paper published in February by Arindrajit Dube of the University of Massachusetts-Amherst. Likewise, in the areas where the current $300 is a relatively larger boost to income, employment growth has not weakened since January, when that uplift was introduced.

turn down 拒绝

expiry 终止

然而，这种关系在大流行期间似乎有所减弱。失业救济的增加是有时间限制的，这一事实可能会让员工不愿意拒绝一份有长期奖励的工作。根据马萨诸塞州阿默斯特大学的Arindrajit Dube在2月份发表的一篇论文，在大流行的早期，失业保险补贴金甚至更慷慨，为600美元，但在夏季到期后“对整体就业几乎没有影响”。同样，在那些目前300美元对收入的提振作用相对较大的地区，自1月份引入这一提振以来，就业增长没有减弱过。

This suggests that the second factor, fear, may be important in explaining America’s shortage of staff. Nearly 4m people are not looking for work “because of the coronavirus pandemic”, according to official data. And consider which industries are experiencing the most acute worker shortages. Jobs in health care, recreation and hospitality report the highest level of job openings, relative to employment. Many of these involve plenty of person-to-person contact, making their workers especially vulnerable to infection (a study from California earlier this year found that cooks were most at risk from dying of covid-19). By contrast, in industries where maintaining social distancing or being outside is often easier, labour shortages are less of an issue. The number of job openings per employee in the construction industry is lower today than it was before the pandemic.

这表明第二个因素“恐惧”，可能是解释美国劳动力短缺的重要原因。官方数据显示，近400万人“因新冠疫情”而没有找工作。再考虑一下哪些行业正经历着最严重的劳动力短缺。对于就业率而言，医疗保健、娱乐和酒店行业的职位空缺最高。其中许多都涉及大量的人与人之间的接触，使其工作人员特别容易受到感染(今年早些时候加州的一项研究发现，厨师死于covid-19的风险最大)。相比之下，在那些更容易保持社交距离或在外工作的行业，劳动力短缺不是什么大问题。目前建筑业每名雇员的职位空缺数量比疫情前要少。

The final reason for worker shortages relates to the extraordinary reallocation of resources under way in the economy. The headline growth in vacancies represents the rise in opportunities in some industries—say, clerks in DIY stores—as others decline, reflecting changing consumer demands. Analysis by The Economist of over 400 local areas also finds a wide variation in job churn across geographies: the gap between jobs growth in the most buoyant areas and that in struggling ones is twice as wide as it was before the pandemic. Workers may take time to catch up with this creative destruction. A former bartender looking for work in downtown Manhattan, for instance, may not quickly spot and secure a position as a delivery driver in farther-out Westchester.

underway 正在进行的；正在发生的

buoyant 繁荣的

劳动力短缺的最后一个原因与经济中正在发生的资源的异常重新分配有关。空缺职位的总体增长代表了某些行业机会的增加（比如DIY商店的店员），而其他行业的机会却在减少，这反映了消费者需求的变化。《经济学人》对超过400个地区的分析也发现了不同地区的工作流失有很大的差异:最繁荣地区的就业增长和困难地区的就业增长之间的差距是疫情爆发前的两倍。工人们可能需要时间来赶上这种“创造性破坏”。例如，在曼哈顿市中心找工作的前酒保，可能无法很快地在更远的威斯特彻斯特郡找到并获得送餐司机的工作。

As vaccinations continue to reduce hospitalisations and deaths from covid-19, and limit the spread of the disease, Americans’ fears about taking high-contact jobs should fade too. But if shortages are to dissipate fully, and the threat of inflation is to be contained, some of the unemployed will also have to take up work in sectors and areas that are new to them. ■

随着接种疫苗继续减少因covid-19而住院和死亡的人数，并限制该疾病的传播，美国人对于从事高接触性工作的恐惧也应该消退。但是，如果短缺问题要完全消除，通货膨胀的威胁要得到控制，一些失业者也将不得不在他们不熟悉的行业和领域工作。■
