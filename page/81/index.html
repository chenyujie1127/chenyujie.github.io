AT A CINEMA in Seoul eight middle-aged women in matching sky-blue hoodies, hair bands and face masks are sharing dried figs and persimmons as they chat and snap selfies. They have travelled from all over the country for the opening weekend of “Mr Trot”, a film based on a South Korean television show that aired earlier this year. In the show, a mix of washed-up and aspiring male crooners chosen from 15,000 applicants dress up in pastel suits and semi-unbuttoned silk shirts and sing old South Korean ballads to compete for the title of “Mr Trot”. The women, whose hoodies read “Lim Young-woong, you are my hero”, are members of an online fan club for the winner. They are not alone in their obsession: more than 30% of South Korean television-viewers tuned into the show’s final episode in March.

Trot, which emerged in the early 20th century when the Korean peninsula was a Japanese colony, blends traditional Korean music with elements of the Japanese and Western popular songs of the era. It has long been ubiquitous in South Korea, but it has been a long time since it was considered cool—if it ever was. The ballads, with their cheesy melodies, melodramatic lyrics and repetitive beats are perennial favourites with drunken revellers in noraebang, or “singing rooms”, the South Korean equivalent of karaoke parlours. Songs about doomed romance, lost hometowns and the general tragedy of life blare from speakers at motorway service stations and from the radios of buses and taxis driven by older men. The more up-tempo numbers are beloved of political parties at election time, when candidates and activists regale voters with clunky dancing set to trot blasting from campaign lorries.

Trot，出现在20世纪初朝鲜半岛是日本殖民地的时候，融合了朝鲜传统音乐和当时日本和西方流行歌曲的元素。在韩国，它早已无处不在，但它已经很久没有被认为是酷的了。这些民谣旋律粗俗，歌词夸张，节拍重复，一直是朝鲜半岛醉酒狂欢者的最爱，也就是韩国相当于卡拉OK厅的“歌厅”。

高速公路服务站的演讲者、老年人驾驶的公共汽车和出租车的收音机里，响起了关于注定的浪漫、失落的家乡和人生悲剧的歌曲。

竞选时，竞选人和活动人士会从竞选卡车上跳起笨重的舞蹈，以取悦选民，这些节奏较快的音乐深受政党的喜爱。

But partly thanks to the popularity of “Mr Trot” and its predecessor “Miss Trot”, a similarly popular programme with female contestants that aired last year, the genre has gone from a sentimental throwback to a potent cultural force. The shows’ stars have become celebrities. In 2019 two of the three most popular music acts in South Korea were trot singers, eclipsed only by BTS, the world’s biggest boy band. Trot singers top the charts for streaming and record sales. Several new trot-themed television shows are trying to emulate the success of their famous predecessors. Companies selling everything from coffee to gas boilers have recruited trot stars to front advertising campaigns.

Is there an alternative to Huawei?

5G technology 5G技术

![image-20201111213147314](/Users/chenyujie/Library/Application Support/typora-user-images/image-20201111213147314.png)

OpenRAN could be it 可能是OpenRAN

Nov 7th 2020

THE SIGNAL was easy to miss amid the noise of new lockdowns and America’s elections. Earlier this week, Vodafone, a mobile operator, announced that in Britain it would use a technology called OpenRAN to replace some gear made by Huawei, a Chinese firm whose products are considered too much of a security risk to be used in the new 5G mobile networks. It is a sign that the much-discussed Huawei dilemma is not as intractable as it may seem—and a reminder that OpenRAN deserves more private-sector and government support.

Gear 设备

security risk 安全风险

Much-discussed 备受讨论的

Intractable 棘手的

OpenRAN: 开放无线电接入网络，它使用美国软件来连接由多家公司生产的网络硬件。 OpenRAN技术的目的是将这些网络的复杂性转移到操作软件上，同时将基站上的硬件变成可互换的商品。

在新的封锁和美国大选的喧嚣中，信号很容易被错过。本周早些时候，移动运营商沃达丰宣布，在英国，将使用一种名为OpenRAN的技术来取代华为制造的一些装备，华为是一家中国公司，其产品被认为是新5G移动网络中使用的安全风险太大。这表明，备受讨论的华为困境并不像看上去那么棘手，这提醒人们，OpenRAN值得更多的私营部门和政府支持。

In recent years America has conducted a campaign against Huawei, which it worries poses a threat to Western interests and which has built a commanding position in 5G systems globally. Australia, Canada and Japan have already in effect banned Huawei from their 5G networks. In July Britain said it would phase out its gear, and on October 20th Sweden said it would impose a ban, too. More countries may follow.

Phase out 逐步废除；逐步放弃

近年来，美国对华为展开了一场运动，它担心华为对西方利益构成威胁，华为在全球5G系统中占据了制高点。澳大利亚、加拿大和日本实际上已经禁止华为进入其5G网络。今年7月，英国表示将逐步淘汰其装备，10月20日瑞典也表示将实施禁令。更多的国家可能会跟进。

The trouble is that the costs of ditching Huawei are high: you risk becoming reliant on two big Nordic firms, Nokia and Ericsson, the other main suppliers of 5G gear. In the long run a duopoly is bad for competition and innovation. And in the short run neither firm is infallible. Nokia, in particular, is in trouble. On October 29th it announced a drop in sales of 7% year on year, and its shares plunged by almost 20%. Its new boss said that it had been “clearly behind” on 5G.

Infallible 绝对可靠的

Year on year 与去年同期数据相比

问题在于，放弃华为的成本很高：你有可能依赖两家北欧大公司，诺基亚和爱立信，这两家公司是5G设备的其他主要供应商。从长远来看，双寡头垄断不利于竞争和创新。从短期来看，这两家公司都不是绝对正确的。尤其是诺基亚陷入困境。10月29日，该公司宣布销售额同比下降7%，股价暴跌近20%。它的新老板说它在5G上“明显落后”。

OpenRAN is an alternative to relying on either Huawei or the Nordic duo. Along with a related technology called network virtualization, it changes the rules of the game. To understand why, compare an old tethered telephone with a modern smartphone. One is a dedicated piece of hardware made of customized parts, whereas the other is a general-purpose computer controlled by software that can accommodate any type of app, provided it complies with certain technical rules.

general-purpose computer 通用计算机

OpenRAN是一个替代品，可以替代华为或北欧双雄。与一种叫做网络虚拟化的相关技术一起，它改变了游戏规则。为了理解这一点，我们可以将老式的有线电话和现代的智能手机相比较。一种是由定制部件制成的专用硬件，另一种是由软件控制的通用计算机，只要符合某些技术规则，就可以容纳任何类型的应用程序。

Similarly, conventional mobile networks are made out of specialised equipment, whereas the new OpenRAN kind use mostly off-the-shelf hardware, with lots of code defining what it can do. Because all the gear connects up using standard interfaces, carriers can mix and match products from different suppliers—something that they cannot do today. Operators have more insight into what is going on in their networks and can avoid components they do not trust, such as Chinese chips. They could also save a lot of money and become quicker on their feet.

off-the-shelf hardware 现成设备

insight into 洞察 ; 对…深刻理解 ; 洞察力

类似地，传统的移动网络是由专门的设备组成的，而新的OpenRAN网络主要使用现成的硬件，有大量的代码来定义它的功能。因为所有的设备都是通过标准接口连接起来的，运营商可以混合和匹配来自不同供应商的产品，这是他们今天无法做到的。运营商对网络中发生的事情有了更深入的了解，可以避免使用他们不信任的组件。他们还可以省下很多钱，并且可以更快地站起来。

OpenRAN is gaining momentum. Last month Rakuten Mobile launched the world’s first 5G network based on OpenRAN—which helped the Japanese carrier cut investment by 40% (see ). It can also put together new services within minutes instead of months, as is the case with conventional networks. In September Telefónica, which has 260m mobile subscribers in Europe and Latin America, teamed up with Rakuten to deploy OpenRAN more widely in its networks by 2025. In America Dish has started to build a 5G network based on the technology. With the notable exception of Huawei, even equipment-makers are coming on board. Ericsson has just announced its first related product.

Gain or gather/lose momentum 势头增强/减弱

Carrier 运营商

more widely 普遍地 ; 广泛地 ; 范围广地 ; 很大程度上

OpenRAN的势头越来越大。上个月，乐天移动（Rakuten Mobile）推出了全球首个基于OpenRAN的5G网络，帮助这家日本运营商削减了40%的投资（见）。它还可以像传统网络那样，在几分钟内而不是几个月内就把新的服务组合起来。9月，Telefónica在欧洲和拉丁美洲拥有2.6亿移动用户，它与乐天合作，在2025年之前在其网络中更广泛地部署OpenRAN。在美国，Dish已经开始在这项技术的基础上建立一个5G网络。除了华为（Huawei）这一显著的例外，就连设备制造商也加入了进来。爱立信刚刚发布了第一款相关产品。

Yet OpenRAN still has problems. The supply chain is untested and may face bottlenecks if demand suddenly surges as more mobile operators sign up. Notwithstanding its roll-out in urban parts of Japan, experts fear that the technology may not yet function well in densely populated cities; most carriers, including Vodafone, want to test it in rural areas first. Integrating the many different products that make up an OpenRAN network is hard. And although the technology lowers the potential security threat from China, it creates new openings for hackers.

Roll-up 新产品发布会 ; 新产品的推出;首次发布

然而OpenRAN仍然存在问题。供应链尚未经过测试，如果随着更多移动运营商的加入，需求突然激增，供应链可能面临瓶颈。尽管这项技术已在日本城市地区推广，但专家们担心，这项技术在人口稠密的城市可能还不能很好地发挥作用；包括沃达丰在内的大多数运营商都希望先在农村地区进行测试。集成组成OpenRAN网络的许多不同的产品是很困难的。尽管这项技术降低了潜在安全威胁，但它为黑客创造了新的机会。

All this means that governments still have a role to play. They should help tackle bottlenecks by, for example, encouraging investment in the development of specialist chips that power antennae, as well as laboratories that test integration of a network’s components, as lawmakers in America have proposed. They should also follow Japan by promoting a common set of standards among equipment-makers and network-operators that deals with security and mandates at least some compliance with OpenRAN. The choice between relying on a monolithic Chinese firm that is not fully trusted or on a doddery Western duopoly is a rotten one. It would be a lot better to give a new type of technology a chance to thrive.■

Monolithic 单一庞大的

Doddery 步履蹒跚的

Rotten 糟糕的

所有这些都意味着政府仍然可以发挥作用。他们应该帮助解决瓶颈问题，例如，鼓励投资开发专门的芯片，为天线供电，以及投资于实验室测试网络组件的集成度，正如美国立法者所提议的那样。他们还应该效仿日本，在设备制造商和网络运营商之间推广一套共同的标准，这些标准涉及安全性，并要求至少在一定程度上遵守OpenRAN。在依赖一家不被完全信任的单一的公司，还是依赖一家老态龙钟的西方双寡头垄断公司，这两者之间的选择是一个糟糕的选择。给一种新型技术一个蓬勃发展的机会会更好。■

How hotels are trying to attract remote workers

![image-20201111130117421](/Users/chenyujie/Library/Application Support/typora-user-images/image-20201111130117421.png)

Bartleby
Luxury with your laptop
您的笔记本电脑很奢华

Oct 29th 2020

THE BUSY worker looks at the clock on her laptop and discovers that it is nearly 1pm. Time for lunch. So she picks up the phone and asks to speak to room service. A hot meal appears 20 minutes later; no need to bother with the cooking or washing up.

speak to 向…说 ; 说到 ; 招呼 ; 恳求

忙碌的工作人员看了看笔记本电脑上的时钟，发现已经快下午1点了。午餐时间到了。于是她拿起电话，要求和客房服务人员通话。热腾腾的饭菜在20分钟后出现；不必费心做饭或洗碗。

If that vision appeals, you could be a potential customer for one of the many hotel groups that are trying to induce people to rent a room for use as an office. The idea makes a certain amount of sense. Hotel rooms are short of guests during the pandemic; some workers may find it too difficult (or boring) to sit at the kitchen table every day.

如果这种想法很有吸引力，那么你可能是众多酒店集团中的一家的潜在客户，这些集团正试图诱使人们租一个房间用作办公室。这个想法有一定的道理。在流感大流行期间，酒店客房缺少客人；一些员工可能会觉得每天坐在餐桌旁太难（或无聊）。

The big chains are rushing to test out the size of this market. Hilton has launched a new service called Workspaces in America, Britain and Canada which gives workers the chance to use the gym or swimming pool (where available) and complimentary bicycle hire. The Wyndham chain is offering worker packages at hotels in California, Florida and South Carolina.

complimentary 免费的 ; 赠送的 ; 表示钦佩的 ; 赞美的

大型连锁店正争先恐后地测试这个市场的规模。希尔顿在美国、英国和加拿大推出了一项名为“工作区”的新服务，让员工有机会使用健身房或游泳池（如果有），并免费租用自行车。这家温德姆连锁店正在加州、佛罗里达和南卡罗来纳州的酒店提供员工套餐。

Hotels have long made good money out of the business market, catering for business travellers, conferences and team get-togethers. They have also recognised that they need a good Wi-Fi signal to appeal to laptop-toting businesspeople. But renting rooms by the day has traditionally been aimed at a rather different slice of the market from the solitary desk jockey.

长期以来，酒店从商业市场中获利颇丰，为商务旅行者、会议和团队聚会提供服务。他们还认识到，他们需要一个良好的Wi-Fi信号来吸引携带笔记本电脑的商务人士。但传统上，按日租房的目标市场与孤独的书桌骑师截然不同。

Bartleby wrote part of this column in Sofitel St James, a luxury hotel in the heart of London’s West End. This certainly would be an excellent bolthole, for those who can afford it—£299 ($388) a day, with breakfast, lunch and a cocktail available for another £50. Your columnist’s suite offered a lounge with a desk, printer and shredder, plus a four-seater table, two comfy chairs and a sofa. Nice little touches included extra pens, sellotape, scissors and a stapler. All the staff wore masks and kept a safe distance. The place was extremely quiet, which aided concentration.
