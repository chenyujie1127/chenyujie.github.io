Wages are surging across the rich world

发达国家的工资正在飙升

The pandemic bonus

大流行病的额外好处

![image-20211022135448489](/Users/chenyujie/Library/Application Support/typora-user-images/image-20211022135448489.png)

What it means for the economic recovery

这对经济复苏意味着什么

NOT LONG ago pundits obsessively checked the latest statistics on covid-19 cases. Now they are doing the same with the inflation numbers. American consumer prices rose by 5.4% in the year to September, according to figures published on October 13th, exceeding forecasts. A survey from the New York Federal Reserve released the previous day showed a small pickup in consumers’ inflation expectations. In its semi-annual report on the global economy the IMF warned that the prospects for inflation were “highly uncertain”. Soaring energy costs will push up consumer prices in the near term. Pay, too, is surging, as red-hot demand runs up against a shortage of workers. Does it stand to fuel further price rises?

pick up 好转

red-hot 激动人心的；最新的；强烈的

run up against 遭遇

fuel 使…加剧

不久前，专家们还痴迷于查看新冠肺炎病例的最新统计数据。现在，他们痴迷于通胀数据。10月13日公布的数据显示，截至9月份，美国消费者价格上涨了5.4%，超出了预期。纽约联储前一天公布的一项调查显示，消费者通胀预期小幅上升。国际货币基金组织在其全球经济半年度报告中警告称，通胀前景“高度不确定”。飙升的能源成本将在短期内推高消费价格。工资也在飙升，因为强烈的需求碰上了劳动力短缺。这是否会进一步推动价格上涨?

When covid-19 first struck, most forecasters expected bosses to slash bonuses and yearly rises, or even to cut basic pay, as they did after the global financial crisis in 2007-09. Although wage growth did slow modestly early in the pandemic, that restraint has since been abandoned. Oxford Economics, a consultancy, finds that pay in the rich world is growing at a rate well above its pre-pandemic average. The acceleration in compensation per worker across the OECD, a club of mostly rich countries, is equally arresting (see chart 1).

modestly 适度地

当新冠疫情首次爆发时，大多数预测人士预计老板们会削减奖金和年度加薪，甚至会削减基本工资，就像他们在2007-09年全球金融危机后所做的那样。尽管工资增长在疫情早期确实略有放缓，但自那以后，这种“限制”已被放弃。伦敦咨询公司Oxford Economics发现，发达国家的工资增长速度远远高于疫情前的平均水平。经合组织(一个主要由发达国家组成的俱乐部)中，人均工资的增长同样引人注目(见表1)。

The wage numbers have sometimes misled during the pandemic. When lockdowns were imposed poorly paid people in service jobs dropped out of the workforce, for instance, which had the effect of raising average pay as measured by statisticians. Even so, wage growth seems to have been stronger than the scale of the economic downturn alone would have suggested. Goldman Sachs, a bank, has created a “tracker” that corrects for pandemic-related distortions. Underlying wage growth, at about 2.5% across the G10 group of large economies, is as fast as it was in 2018.

misled 误导

downturn 衰退；下降

Trade Tracker：帮助客户监测交易情况并根据市场变化实时调整仓位。可同时供外汇期权交易客户、内部股票期权交易员、外部股票期权交易客户使用。

在疫情期间，工资数字有时会误导。例如，当实行封锁时，从事服务业的低收入人群退出了劳动力市场，根据统计学家的计算，这提高了平均工资。即便如此，工资增长似乎比经济衰退本身所显示的规模更强劲。高盛银行创建了一个“追踪器”，用以调整与流行病相关的扭曲。在G10的大型经济体中，潜在工资增长约为2.5%，与2018年一样快。

![image-20211022135347979](/Users/chenyujie/Library/Application Support/typora-user-images/image-20211022135347979.png)

No wonder then that pay has become a hot topic in the corporate world. On October 6th Bank of America increased its company-wide minimum wage by 5%. Amazon now boasts of roles in transport and packaging paying $22.50 an hour in America, making left-wing activists’ demands for a federal minimum wage of $15 seem quaint. An index compiled by Goldman suggests that the share prices of American companies most exposed to rising labour costs have fallen by 4% since May, even as the broader stockmarket has risen by 7%. Even bosses in Germany, long used to acquiescent unions, now face demands to pay up.

难怪薪酬已成为企业界的一个热门话题。10月6日，美国银行将全公司的最低工资提高了5%。亚马逊现在吹嘘自己在美国的运输和包装行业的工作，每小时支付22.5美元，这使得左翼激进分子要求联邦最低工资为15美元显得有些古怪。高盛编制的一项指数显示，受劳动力成本上升影响最大的美国公司的股价自5月以来下跌了4%，而整体股市却上涨了7%。即使是（长久以来都习惯于顺从工会）的德国的老板们，现在也面临着加薪的要求。

Some workers are benefiting more than others. Analysis by The Economist of British wage data by industry suggests that annual pay growth is twice as dispersed as it was before the pandemic. Wages in the accommodation and food-service sector, which is struggling to attract workers, rose by 8% in the year to July; increases in manufacturing have been more modest. In America the wages of the least-paid quartile of workers are growing 70% faster than those at the top (see chart 2).

有些工人比其他人受益更多。《经济学人》对英国各行业工资数据的分析表明，年度工资增长的分散性是疫情前的两倍。在努力吸引工人的住宿和食品服务业，截至今年7月的工资增长了8%; 制造业的增长则不太多。在美国，收入最低的四分之一工人的工资增长速度比收入最高的工人快70%(见表2)。

![image-20211022135407621](/Users/chenyujie/Library/Application Support/typora-user-images/image-20211022135407621.png)

Underlying pay is rising about three times as quickly in Anglo-Saxon countries as in continental Europe. That could be because places such as America and Canada rely more on the consumer-facing industries experiencing the worst labour shortages. And France and Italy, where annual pay growth is below 1%, probably do not face the same immigration crunch as Britain, which has Brexited, or Australia and New Zealand, which have closed their borders to keep out covid-19.

crunch 关键时刻

when/if it comes to the crunch … 等到了紧要关头…

在盎格鲁-撒克逊国家，基本工资的增长速度是欧洲大陆的三倍。这可能是因为像美国和加拿大这样的地方更依赖于面对消费者的行业，而这些行业正经历着最严重的劳动力短缺。法国和意大利的年工资增长率低于1%，它们可能不会面临像英国（英国已经脱欧）那样的移民危机，澳大利亚和新西兰已经关闭了边境，以抵御新冠病毒。

Only a few years ago economists were bemoaning weak wage growth. So it may seem churlish not to pop the champagne now that the opposite is happening. But pay can rise for a variety of reasons, some more benign than others. For a given level of productivity, higher wages must show up in one of two ways: as higher inflation or as a higher “labour share” of GDP.

bemoan 哀叹

就在几年前，经济学家还在哀叹工资增长乏力。因此，在相反的情况正在发生时，不开香槟庆祝似乎有些无礼。但薪酬的上涨可能有多种原因，其中一些原因比较温和。对于给定的生产率水平，更高的工资必然以两种方式之一表现出来:要么是更高的通胀率，要么是更高的GDP中的“劳动份额”。

Take inflation first. Costlier staff may force bosses to raise the price of whatever they are selling. At worst, higher inflation could cancel out any rise in cash wages, leaving workers no better off than they were before (and perhaps encouraging them to seek further increases). American real wages are growing on a monthly basis but they remain lower than a year ago.

cancel out 抵消

先看通货膨胀。更昂贵的员工可能会迫使老板提高他们所销售产品的价格。在最坏的情况下，更高的通货膨胀可能会抵消现金工资的增长，让工人们的生活并不比以前更好(也许还会鼓励他们寻求进一步的增长)。美国的实际工资每月都在增长，但仍低于一年前的水平。

Some firms seem happy to pass on a bigger wage bill to consumers. On a recent earnings call an executive at Domino’s Pizza discussed how the firm might offset wage rises (pricier Margheritas might be on the way). Most S&P500 companies are protecting margins “by passing on price increases to consumers”, says Goldman.

一些公司似乎乐于将更高的工资账单转嫁给消费者。在最近的一次收益电话会议上，Domino披萨的一位高管讨论了公司如何抵消工资上涨的影响(更贵的玛格丽塔可能即将上市)。高盛表示，大多数标普500公司“通过将价格上涨转嫁给消费者”来保护利润率。

Other firms, however, may absorb higher wages by accepting lower profits. That would change the distribution of the economic pie, raising the “labour share”, or the proportion of GDP paid to workers as wages. Our analysis suggests that the labour share in the G7 has risen by about one percentage point since the pandemic began—equivalent to $400bn or so of extra real income for households each year.

然而，其他公司可能通过接受较低的利润来承受较高的工资。这将改变经济蛋糕的分配，提高“劳动份额”，即支付给工人的工资占GDP的比例。我们的分析表明，自疫情开始以来，七国集团的劳动份额占比上升了约1个百分点，相当于所有家庭每年总共增加4000亿美元左右的实际收入。

What does this mean for the wider economy? A big topic of debate among economists is whether the labour share before covid-19 had been declining or not. It seems most likely that the share fell in America, so a recovery might be welcome. But an ever-rising labour share would be a worry: it would crimp companies’ profits and thus the investments that are crucial to improving long-run economic growth.

crimp 束缚

这对更广泛的经济意味着什么? 经济学家们争论的一个大话题是，covid-19之前劳动份额占比是否一直在下降。在美国，这一比例似乎很有可能是下降的，因此复苏可能是受欢迎的。但不断上升的劳动份额占比将令人担忧: 它将抑制企业利润，从而抑制对改善长期经济增长至关重要的投资。

There is another, happier, possibility. If productivity rises, then wage growth need not cause sustained inflation, nor push up the labour share. Instead the economic pie would grow, with more for everyone.

还有另一种更令人满意的可能性。如果生产率提高，那么工资增长不一定会导致持续的通胀，也不一定会推高劳动收入占比。相反，经济蛋糕将会增长，每个人都能得到更多。

Some evidence suggests that workers are doing more with less. Firms are investing in new technologies to meet new demands, especially from online commerce. “Hybrid” work may be more efficient than everyone being in the office all the time. Productivity statistics are even cloudier than the wage ones; but since the third quarter of 2020 output per employed person has risen in 25 of the 29 rich countries for which figures are available.

cloudy 混浊的

一些证据表明，工人们正在用更少的钱做更多的事。公司正在投资新技术以满足新需求，特别是来自在线交易的需求。“混合”工作模式可能比所有人都呆在办公室里更有效率。生产率数据甚至比工资数据更加模糊; 但自2020年第三季度以来，在29个有数据可查的发达国家中，有25个国家的人均就业产出有所上升。

The rise in wages, then, seems to reflect a number of underlying economic forces, and need not feed through entirely to inflation. But forecasting prices is just as hard as predicting covid-19 case numbers. One thing is clear: that if the pay surge endures, the consequences will be profound. ■

因此，工资的上涨似乎反映了许多潜在的经济力量，并不一定会完全导致通胀。但预测价格就像预测covid-19病例数量一样困难。但有一件事是清楚的: 如果工资上涨持续下去，后果将是深远的。■

Who should police the web?

谁应该监管网络?

Reluctant regulators

不情愿的监管机构

![image-20211021131945468](/Users/chenyujie/Library/Application Support/typora-user-images/image-20211021131945468.png)

Politicians should not offload the responsibility onto others

offload 排解

to offload the blame on to sb 把过错转嫁到某人身上

政客们不应该把责任推给别人

SHOULD VIDEO websites have to review content before they publish it? Where does the boundary lie between hate speech and incitement to violence? Is pornography created by artificial intelligence an invasion of privacy? These are all hard questions, but behind them lies an even more difficult one: who should provide the answers?

pornography 色情作品

视频网站应该在发布之前对内容进行审查吗?仇恨言论和煽动暴力之间的界限在哪里? 人工智能制作的色情作品是对隐私的侵犯吗? 所有这些都是棘手的问题，但在这些问题的背后还有一个更棘手的问题:应该由谁提供答案呢?

On the internet, such dilemmas are increasingly being resolved by private firms. Social networks are deciding what kinds of misinformation to ban. Web-hosting companies are taking down sites they deem harmful. Now financial firms are more actively restricting what people can buy.

take down sth 将..拿下

在互联网上，这样的困境越来越多地由私营企业来解决。社交网络正在决定什么样的错误信息需要禁止。网络托管公司正在关闭他们认为有害的网站。现在，金融公司更加积极地限制人们可以购买的产品。

The digital gatekeepers are doing a mixed job. But it is becoming clear that it ought not to be their job at all. The trade-offs around what can be said, done and bought online urgently need the input of elected representatives. So far governments have been better at complaining than at taking responsibility.

“数字看门人”的工作很混杂。但越来越明显的是，这根本不应该是他们的工作。围绕在网上说什么、做什么、买什么的权衡，迫切需要民选代表的投入。到目前为止，政府更擅长抱怨而不是承担责任。

For an example of how private firms have become the digital police, consider the rules on internet pornography being introduced by Mastercard on October 15th. In a bid to weed out illegal material, the card firm is demanding that porn sites take steps that go beyond what the law requires, including reviewing footage before publication and checking the identity of those who upload or feature in it. Sites that think these sorts of rules too onerous are under no obligation to work with Mastercard. But Visa is also cracking down, and the two firms handle 90% of card payments outside China, meaning that they are becoming the industry’s de facto regulators.

to feature in sth 在某事物中起重要作用

crack down 镇压；管理

to crack down on sb/sth (with force) 严厉打击某人/某事物； (with rules) 竭力管制某人/某事物

de facto 事实上的；事实上

举个“私人公司怎样成为数字警察”的例子，想想万事达卡在10月15日推出的关于网络色情的规定。为了清除非法材料，信用卡公司要求色情网站对超出法律要求的内容采取措施，包括在视频发布前进行审查，并检查上传者或者视频中起重要作用的人的身份。认为这类规则太过繁琐的网站没有义务与万事达合作。但Visa也在进行打击，这两家公司处理了中国以外90%的银行卡支付业务，这意味着它们正在成为该行业事实上的监管者。

Liberals must strike a balance. Private firms should in general be free to deal with whom they like. Just as Facebook may legally ban people like Donald Trump from its network and Amazon Web Services can decline to host alt-right-friendly apps like Parler, Mastercard is free to drop particular porn sites. Yet the market power of these firms and their often like-minded rivals means that without their approval and support, individuals or businesses may face exclusion, even if they have broken no law. In edge-cases, private gatekeepers will err on the side of caution. What incentive is there for a social network to allow borderline hate-speech, or for a bank to deal with a legally tricky industry like cannabis?

like-minded 志趣相投的

err 犯错误

to err on the side of caution 宁可谨慎过头

cannabis 大麻

背景：Alt-right，完整的拼写方式为alternative right，即“另类右翼”，持有极端保守或反对变革观点的意识形态组织，主要特点是反对主流政治，通过网络媒体故意散布有争议的内容。

自由主义者必须找到一个平衡点。一般来说，私营企业应该可以自由地与他们喜欢的人打交道。正如Facebook可以合法地禁止像特朗普这样的人进入其网络，亚马逊网络服务可以拒绝托管Parler这样对另类右翼友好的应用程序一样，万事达可以自由地放弃某些色情网站。然而，这些公司及其志同道合的竞争对手的市场力量意味着，没有他们的批准和支持，个人或企业可能面临被排斥，即使他们没有违反任何法律。在边缘情况下，“私有的看门人”宁可谨慎过头。有什么样的动机会促使社交网络允许边缘性的仇恨言论，或者促使银行与像大麻这样的法律上复杂的行业打交道?

The best remedy would be more competition, so that if Visa and Mastercard bar a porn site or Facebook cancels a controversial figure, consumers have alternatives. That will not happen soon: network effects are powerful in social media and crushing in the payments business.

crushing 非常严重的 =severe

[例句] The news came as a crushing blow.

这消息是个毁灭性的打击。

[例句] Their army had suffered a crushing defeat.

他们的军队遭到了重挫。

最好的补救办法是增加竞争，这样，如果Visa和万事达禁止一个色情网站，或者Facebook撤销一个有争议的人物，消费者就有了其他选择。这种情况不会很快发生: 网络效应在社交媒体中非常强大，在支付业务中也非常强大。

In the meantime, private companies that find themselves acting as gatekeepers should be transparent about their rules, how they make them and what redress they offer. Under public pressure, social networks have taken steps in this direction. Facebook sends difficult decisions to an independent “oversight board”; Twitter this week published a set of principles for regulating online speech. Financial institutions are more opaque. Mastercard explained its new rules in a blog post. When OnlyFans, a site known for sexually explicit content, blamed banks for forcing a change in its content policy earlier this year, the banks in question made no comment.

opaque 不透明的

与此同时，那些发现自己扮演着“看门人”角色的私营企业，应该对自己的规则、制定规则的方式以及提供何种补偿保持透明。在公众的压力下，社交网络已经朝着这个方向迈出了一步。Facebook将艰难的决定提交给一个独立的“监督委员会”; Twitter本周发布了一套规范网络言论的原则。金融机构更加不透明。万事达在一篇博客文章中解释了它的新规定。当以色情内容著称的OnlyFans指责银行今年早些时候迫使其改变内容政策时，相关银行不予置评。

Yet the bigger responsibility lies with governments. They are right to be wary of constraining speech. In much of the world leaders are only too ready to muzzle online debate (see International section). But carefully laying out a method for drawing lines around free expression and building in safeguards would help clarify where despots have overstepped the boundary.

muzzle (gag) 钳制…的言论 ‹person, the press›

to muzzle freedom of speech 压制言论自由

despots 专制君主

然而，更大的责任落在政府身上。他们对限制言论的警惕是正确的。世界上大多数国家的领导人都准备好了钳制网络讨论(见国际部分)。但是，仔细制定一种“为言论自由划定界限，并建立保障措施”的方法将有助于澄清独裁者在哪些方面越界。

What is more, in many areas the gatekeeping power wielded by private firms is really a consequence of government inaction. Keeping revenge-porn off the web or limiting children’s access to viral content are areas where governments could act if they wanted to. Instead, the approach of many has been to do nothing and then feign shock when profit-maximising companies come up with answers that may not be in the interest of society.

feign 假装

更重要的是，在许多领域，私营企业掌握的把关权力实际上是政府不作为的结果。如果政府愿意，它们可以采取行动，将色情报复内容从网上屏蔽，或限制儿童接触“病毒内容”。相反，许多政府的做法是什么都不做，然后当追求利润最大化的公司想出可能不符合社会利益的答案时，假装震惊。

Politicians are right to warn that some private companies have too much power online. But those firms have ended up with so much responsibility partly because politicians themselves have abdicated it. ■

abdicate 放弃

政客们对于“一些私营公司在网络上的权力过大”的警告是正确的。但这些公司最终承担了如此多的责任，部分原因是政客们自己放弃了这一责任。■

How to run better meetings

如何更好地组织会议

Bartleby

巴托比

![image-20211020140307087](/Users/chenyujie/Library/Application Support/typora-user-images/image-20211020140307087.png)

The jury system offers clues to managers everywhere

陪审团制度为世界各地的经理人提供了想法

MEETINGS ABSORB more time and drain morale more consistently than any other corporate activity. Before the pandemic managers were spending an average of 23 hours a week in meetings. Since then the barriers to calling people together have come down. Now that calendars are routinely shared, an empty diary slot attracts invitations like picnics do wasps.

morale 精神面貌；士气

wasp 黄蜂

开会比其他任何公司活动都更耗时间，更耗士气。在疫情之前，管理人员平均每周花23个小时开会。从那时起，把人们召集在一起的障碍已经减少了。既然日历都是定期共享的，那么一个空的日程表时段就会吸引邀请，就像野餐吸引黄蜂一样。

Ideas abound for how to make meetings better. Make people stand up, so they cannot settle in for the long haul. Write a memo on the topic at hand that everyone silently reads together at the outset. Toss a ball to each other to make it clear who has the floor and to stop the loudmouths from dominating. Most desperately of all, set aside time at the start for “fun”.

at the outset 起初

loudmouth 说话大声大气的人

to get/have/be given the floor 获得/拥有/被给予发言权

关于如何让会议变得更好的想法比比皆是。让人们站起来，这样他们就不会长时间放松下来。就手头的话题写一份备忘录，让大家在开始时一起默读。互相扔一个球，让对方清楚地知道谁有发言权，并阻止那些大嘴巴控制局面。最重要的是，在开始的时候留出一些时间来“娱乐”。

Yet there is a form of meeting that reliably results in good decisions and that commands general respect, even reverence. That meeting is the jury. Any system in which people still believe after more than 800 years is worth a closer look. In its broad principles, if not in its details, it has five lessons for meeting-throwers and meeting-goers.

command 赢得；值得

然而，有一种会议形式能够可靠地产生好的决定，并赢得普遍的尊重，甚至尊敬。那个会议就是陪审团。任何人们在800多年后仍然相信的体系都值得仔细研究。在其宽泛的原则(如果不是细节的话)中，它为会议组织者和与会者提供了五条教训。

First, its purpose is clear. “Why are we here?” is a question that humans grapple with not just in the depth of their souls but also during most Zoom calls. No jury doubts the point of its existence, the nature of its task or the need for multiple people to be involved. That level of shared understanding is something to aspire to in other settings.

首先，它的目的很明确。“我们为什么在这里?” 这是一个人类不仅在灵魂深处，而且在大多数Zoom通话中都在努力解决的问题。没有陪审团怀疑其存在的意义、任务的性质或需要多人参与的必要性。这种程度的共同理解是我们在其他环境中所渴望的。

Second, its size is right. The 12-person formula dates back to 12th-century England and the reign of Henry II. Temporary courts known as assizes summoned this number of men to hear land disputes. It has largely stuck ever since. For good reason. More people would add voices, but not value. Fewer people would mean less diversity of views. The advantages of keeping meeting numbers tight are not lost on Jeff Bezos, who operated a two-pizza rule at Amazon to limit how many people were in a meeting. The one-jury rule works just as well.

两个披萨原则（two pizza rule）：这套设计的背后用意在于，会议厅挤进越多人，整场会议反而会变得越没效率。因此，贝佐斯的解决之道，便是开会人数永远不要超过两个披萨能够喂饱的人数。

其次，它的规模合适。12人的团队可以追溯到12世纪的英格兰和亨利二世统治时期。被称为巡回法庭的临时法庭召集了这么多人来审理土地纠纷。从那以后，人数基本上一直没有改变。这有充分的理由。更多的人会增加声音，但不会增加价值。人越少，观点的多样性就越少。杰夫·贝佐斯并没有忽视会议人数的优势，他在亚马逊实行了
“两个披萨”的原则，以限制参加会议的人数。一个陪审团的规则同样有效。

The third lesson concerns the agenda. Jurors have one, very important, question to consider, and a limited number of choices to make. Clarity keeps people focused. No juror is likely to suggest backing up a bit in order to brainstorm what the criminal-justice system should look like. And whereas many pundits advise keeping meetings short, time is not a constraint: jury members do not leave until a decision is made. “Putting a pin in it” is just not an option.

pundit 行家

第三个教训与工作事项有关。陪审员们有一个非常重要的问题要考虑，要做的选择也很有限。明确性能让人们集中注意力。没有一个陪审员可能会为了讨论刑事司法系统应该是什么样子而提出一点支持意见。尽管许多专家建议会议要短，但时间并不是一个限制因素: 陪审团成员在做出决定之前不会离开。“暂停/延后”根本不是一个选择。

The fourth lesson is about membership. Jurors are less prone to groupthink than the attendees of the average meeting. Prospective members are deliberately drawn from a wide pool, and anyone whose mind is already made up is supposed to be weeded out. Companies cannot convene a bunch of strangers to make decisions for them. But they can consciously try to bring in unfamiliar faces and call on different perspectives. And just as a jury foreman is not chosen by rank, a moderator need not always be the most senior person in the room.

weed out 淘汰

foreman 陪审团团长

第四个教训是关于会员资格。与普通会议的与会者相比，陪审员不太容易产生群体思维。有意从一个很大的池子中抽取潜在成员，任何已经下定决心的人都应该被淘汰。公司不能召集一群陌生人来为他们做决定。但他们可以有意识地尝试引入不熟悉的面孔，利用不同的视角。正如陪审团团长不按级别选择一样，会议主席也不需要总是房间里级别最高的人。

The final lesson concerns psychological safety, the willingness of people to speak up. That can be hard when your boss is frowning at you. But structure helps. Trials are expressly designed to weigh lots of evidence and to take in opposing views. Before juries make decisions, they get to weigh competing accounts of what happened. The best firms echo this approach by structuring discussions in order to test arguments properly. Investment decisions at Blackstone, a private-equity titan, are probed at meetings that systematically focus on the risk factors surrounding a potential deal, as well as what makes it attractive.

最后一个教训与心理安全有关，即人们愿意说出自己的想法。当你的老板对你皱眉头的时候，这就很难了。但组织可以帮你。审判的目的是为了权衡大量的证据并采纳相反的观点。在陪审团做出决定之前，他们要权衡对所发生事情的不同解释。最好的公司通过组织讨论来测验理由的充分性来回应这种方法。私募股权巨头黑石集团的投资决策在会议上受到调查，会议系统地关注潜在交易的风险因素，以及它的吸引力所在。

Things can go wrong in juries. Jury selection can rig outcomes rather than improve them. Domineering personalities can sway meeker ones. Also, people really can be idiots. A murder conviction in a British courtroom in 1994 was quashed after it was found that some members had used a Ouija board to ask the obvious question of one of the deceased. (The defendant was reconvicted at a second trial.)

meek 温顺的

quash 撤销；否决；平息

陪审团可能会出问题。陪审团的选择可能会操纵结果，而不是改善结果。专横跋扈的名人可以左右温顺的人。而且，人真的可以是个傻瓜。1994年，英国一家法庭的一项谋杀定罪被撤销，因为人们发现，一些成员曾用占卜板向一名死者提出一个显而易见的问题。(被告在二审中被再次定罪。)

Evidently, firms are not the same as courtrooms. Many corporate pow-wows are designed to transmit information and build culture, not to deliver verdicts. Unanimity is no way to run an enterprise. And deciding the fate of a fellow citizen is bound to be more engaging than the average business call. But serving on a jury is not an interruption to work. If you get summoned, you can both do your duty and see what makes for a really good meeting.

pow-wows 讨论会

显然，公司和法庭不一样。许多公司的会议是为了传递信息和建立文化而设计的，而不是为了做出裁决。达成一致不是经营企业的办法。而决定一个公民的命运，肯定比普通的商业电话更有吸引力。但当陪审员并不妨碍工作。如果你被传唤了，你既可以履行自己的职责，也可以看看怎样才能让会议开得很好。

conductive 有传导性的

热电效应是由法国物理学家Jean Charles Peltier在1834年发现的。它发生在一个包含两种导电性不同的材料的电路中。电子从导电性较强的物体流向导电性较差的物体会导致冷却。另一个方向的流动会导致加热。

![image-20211015110420448](/Users/chenyujie/Library/Application Support/typora-user-images/image-20211015110420448.png)

The reason for this is that electrons are able to vibrate more freely when pushed into a conductive material. They thereby transfer energy to their surroundings, warming them. When shunted into a less conductive one, electrons’ vibrations are constrained, and they absorb energy from their surroundings, cooling those surroundings down. An array of thermoelectric circuits built with all the high-conductivity materials facing in one direction and all the low conductivity ones in the other can thus move heat in either direction, by switching the polarity of the current. For reasons buried in the mathematics of quantum physics, the heat thus flowing does so in discrete packages, called phonons. Hence the name of the firm.

![image-20211014162033181](/Users/chenyujie/Library/Application Support/typora-user-images/image-20211014162033181.png)

The earnings out-performance for poorer Americans started in 2018. JPMorgan Chase Institute, a think-tank within the bank, parsed data on more than 7m households. Early in the 2010s, as the economy recovered from the global financial crisis, the top quartile of income earners reaped the fastest income gains and the bottom quartile brought up the rear. However, a few years ago their positions flipped. And over the course of the pandemic the gap has widened, such that, by May, incomes for the lowest earners were growing by about 7% annually, compared with 4.5% for the highest earners (see chart).

bring up the rear 走在最后面

释义：to be at the back of a group that is going somewhere

较贫困美国人的收入表现超过预期是在2018年开始的。摩根大通银行的智囊团摩根大通研究所分析了超过700万家庭的数据。本世纪初，随着经济从全球金融危机中复苏，收入最高的四分之一的人获得了最快的收入增长，而收入最低的四分之一的人则走在最后面。然而，几年前，他们的状况发生了变化。随着疫情的蔓延，这一差距不断扩大，到今年5月，最低收入者的年收入增长了约7%，而最高收入者的年增长率为4.5%(见图表)。

What explains the shift in fortunes? Some of the credit goes to policymakers’ willingness to run a hot economy before the pandemic. Traditionally, economists have worried that low unemployment pushes up wages and, by extension, inflation. But even with unemployment rates below 4%, inflation remained subdued. At the same time, wage growth was most pronounced at the lowest rungs of the income ladder (given an additional boost in some states by higher minimum wages), a relationship that was first observed by Arthur Okun, an American economist, in a paper in 1973 about how “high-pressure” economies tend to promote upward labour mobility as companies pay more for workers.

pronounced 明显的；明确的

如何解释这种命运的转变? 这在一定程度上要归功于政策制定者在疫情爆发前愿意让经济过热。传统上，经济学家担心低失业率会推高工资，进而推高通胀。但是，即使失业率低于4%，通货膨胀率仍然很低。与此同时，工资增长在收入阶梯的最底层最为显著(在一些州，提高最低工资会带来额外的推动作用)，这种关系最早是由美国经济学家Arthur Okun观察到的，在1973年的一篇关于‘随着公司为工人支付了更高的工资，“高压”经济会如何促进劳动力向上流动’的论文。

The spread of covid-19 created a low-pressure economy, almost overnight. Unemployment, which would normally depress wage growth, soared. And without any help from the federal government, that is what would have happened. But thanks to an expansion of unemployment insurance as well as a series of stimulus cheques, the reality was very different. Poorer Americans have been the main beneficiaries, with far fewer suffering drops in incomes than would have otherwise been the case.

covid-19的传播几乎在一夜之间创造了一个低压力的经济。通常会抑制工资增长的失业率飙升。如果没有联邦政府的任何帮助，就会发生这种情况。但由于失业保险的扩大以及一系列刺激计划的出台，现实情况大不相同。较贫穷的美国人是主要受益者，收入下降的情况要比其他情况少得多。

Volvo’s IPO will keep it ahead in the electric-car race

沃尔沃的IPO将使其在电动汽车竞赛中保持领先地位

Electric blue and yellow

![image-20211013093941042](/Users/chenyujie/Library/Application Support/typora-user-images/image-20211013093941042.png)

And raise cash for its Chinese owner, Geely

为其中国所有者吉利筹集资金

VOLVOS SPORT subtle reminders of their Swedish heritage, from tiny blue-and-yellow flags adorning some models to the “hammer of Thor” headlights that provide illumination for all its vehicles. A brand coupling Scandi-cool design with concern for safety and the environment has in recent years helped Volvo expand its footprint beyond its European heartland to China and America. It hopes to keep going. An initial public offering (IPO), announced on October 4th will make it both “more Swedish and more global”, says Hakan Samuelsson, Volvo’s boss.

Scandi，指的是Scandinavian斯堪的纳维亚半岛。地理上包括挪威和瑞典，历史文化上多一个丹麦。Scandi Style更多时候指的是一种美学，强调自然、以人为本。

heartland 中心区域

沃尔沃SPORT巧妙地提醒人们他们的瑞典传统，从为一些车型装饰小的蓝黄国旗到为其所有车辆提供照明的“雷神之锤”大灯。近年来，一个结合了Scandi-cool设计和对安全与环境的关注的品牌，帮助沃尔沃将其足迹从欧洲中心地带扩展到中国和美国。它希望继续下去。沃尔沃老板Hakan Samuelsson称，10月4日宣布的IPO将使沃尔沃“更瑞典化，更全球化”。

At the Swedish end, listing in Stockholm will reinforce Volvo’s Norse identity. Globally, the IPO is a chance to draw on a more diverse pool of investors as it broadens its worldwide reach, while remaining small and nimble enough to navigate the fast-changing automotive business. And Mr Samuelsson stresses that not much would change in its relationship with Geely, the Chinese firm that has owned Volvo since acquiring it from Ford in 2010 for $1.8bn. Geely intends to remain the largest shareholder and the two firms will continue to share costs and technology.

nimble 灵活的；敏捷的

在瑞典方面，在斯德哥尔摩上市将强化沃尔沃的挪威身份。在全球范围内，此次IPO是一个机会，可以在扩大全球业务的同时，吸引更多多元化的投资者，同时保持足够的规模和灵活性，以应对快速变化的汽车业务。Samuelsson强调，沃尔沃与吉利的关系不会有太大改变。自2010年吉利以18亿美元从福特手中收购沃尔沃以来，吉利一直是沃尔沃的所有者。吉利仍将是最大股东，两家公司将继续分担成本以及共享技术。

When Geely abandoned an IPO of Volvo in 2018, the ostensible reason was a looming trade war between China and the West. In reality, the decision probably had more to do with no one else thinking the firm was worth $30bn. That valuation now looks more reasonable. Volvo has gone from suffering losses under Ford, and turning out 374,000 cars in the last year of American ownership, to making 773,000 in the 12 months to June at a healthy profit. Its aim of making 1.2m cars a year by 2025 looks attainable. It is also leading the way in selling its cars on subscription or directly to consumers at a fixed price, rather than through dealerships.

ostensible 表面的

attainable 可实现的; 可获得的

on or by subscription 通过订购

当吉利在2018年放弃沃尔沃的IPO时，表面上的原因是中国和西方之间即将爆发的贸易战。实际上，这个决定可能与没有其他人认为该公司价值300亿美元有关。现在看来，这一估值更为合理。在福特的领导下，沃尔沃经历了亏损，在美国所有的最后一年里生产了37.4万辆汽车，其到今年6月为止的12个月里生产了77.3万辆汽车，利润可观。到2025年”年产120万辆汽车”的目标似乎可以实现。此外，它还在以订购价或直接以固定价格,而非通过经销商向消费者销售汽车方面走在了前列。

More important, Volvo is ahead of most rivals in sating the growing appetite for electric vehicles (EVs) among motorists and investors alike. It has pledged to go all electric by 2030, long before most rivals; spun off its internal-combustion-engine business into a stand-alone operation in order to focus on EVs; and joined forces with Northvolt, a Swedish battery firm, to build a gigafactory and ensure supply. It also half-owns Polestar, a pure-EV marque that last month announced plans to go public next year in a reverse merger with a special-purpose acquisition company (and hopes to fetch a valuation of $20bn). Leaning on Volvo for manufacturing capacity and retail and service networks puts Polestar in a better position than most rival EV startups, which lack either.

sate 充分满足

marque 品牌

更重要的是，在满足驾驶者和投资者对电动汽车日益增长的需求方面，沃尔沃领先于大多数竞争对手。它承诺到2030年实现全电动，这一承诺时间比大多数竞争对手早得多; 为了更专注于电动汽车，其将内燃机业务剥离为独立业务; 并与瑞典电池公司Northvolt合作，建立了一个超级工厂以确保供应。该公司还拥有纯电动汽车品牌Polestar的一半股权。Polestar上月宣布，计划通过与一家特殊目的收购公司反向合并于明年上市(并希望获得200亿美元的估值)。依靠沃尔沃的制造能力和零售及服务网络，使得Polestar比大多数电动汽车的竞争对手处于更有利的地位，而这些竞争对手都缺乏这两种优势。

Geely will pocket a handy profit after a decade or so of owning Volvo outright. The Chinese firm will retain a dominant stake but the flotation will also let it concentrate on its reorganisation from a holding company owning various carmakers into a transport-technology group which also makes the smartphones and satellites that enable the provision of transport services and, eventually, autonomous vehicles. Volvo, for its part, expects to raise nearly $3bn in the IPO, cash Mr Samuelsson says the carmaker needs if it is to keep up its electric momentum. If it does, rivals will have even more catching up to do. ■
