The return of the crowded office

拥挤的办公室的回归了

Bartleby

巴托比

![image-20220316082950807](/Users/chenyujie/Library/Application Support/typora-user-images/image-20220316082950807.png)

It will take time to readjust to the presence of real-life colleagues

要重新适应现实生活中同事的存在需要时间

Mar 12th 2022

TWO YEARS ago this month the era of remote working abruptly began. As the first wave of covid-19 cases prompted lockdowns in the West, white-collar workers had to get used to new ways of conducting themselves. Unmuting was not yet a reflex movement, Zoom fatigue not yet a common affliction.

两年前的这个月，远程办公的时代突然开始了。随着第一波新冠肺炎病例促使西方国家实施封锁，白领们不得不习惯新的行事方式。不静音还不是一种反射动作，Zoom疲劳也还不是一种常见的折磨。

Now another era is getting under way. Unless a new variant of the virus again intervenes, more and more workers will go to the office for at least a portion of their working week. Guidance to work from home was lifted in Britain in January. American Express expects to see people back in its offices in America from March 15th; employees of Citigroup, Google and Apple have been given return dates of March 21st, April 4th and April 11th, respectively.

现在，另一个时代正在到来。除非新的病毒变体再次阻碍，否则越来越多的工作人员每周至少会有一部分时间去办公室上班。今年1月，英国取消了居家办公的指导。美国运通公司希望能在3月15日看到员工回到美国的办公室; 花旗集团、谷歌和苹果的员工分别被告知3月21日、4月4日和4月11日回归办公室。

Another period of adjustment is unfolding, and not just to the novel demands of hybrid work. People also have to get used to the physical reality of once again being surrounded by three-dimensional colleagues—people who gaze, chatter, slurp, wheeze, clatter, rustle and fidget.

另一段调整阶段正在展开，这不仅仅是为了适应混合办公的新需求。人们还必须适应再次被三维的同事包围的现实，这些人注视着、喋喋不休、发出啧啧声、喘息声、叮铃咣铛声、沙沙声，还烦躁不安。

Some readjustments are clear: wearing trousers is a requirement, not a lifestyle choice. Others are less obvious. Making eye contact with someone else’s actual eyes is a skill that needs to be relearned as the office fills up again. Too little, and you come across as uninterested. Too much, and you seem uncomfortably intense. A study in 2016 found that three seconds of mutual eye contact was about right for the average person (just don’t count out loud).

一些调整是显而易见的: 穿裤子是一种要求，而不是一种生活方式的选择。其他的就不那么明显了。当办公室里人又多起来的时候，与别人的眼睛进行眼神交流是一项需要重新学习的技能。眼神交流太少，会让人觉得你不感兴趣。眼神交流太多了，你看起来紧张不自在。2016年的一项研究发现，3秒钟的相互眼神交流对普通人来说是合适的(只是不要大声数出来)。

Small talk is another lost skill. You do not have to politely nod and smile at people when working from home. Asking after the family is just weird when you are speaking to your spouse and children. By contrast, a crowded office demands endless casual pleasantries, whether bumping into someone in the corridor and clustering at the coffee machine or holding doors open and waiting for the lift. There is a pay-off to platitudes: researchers from Rutgers University and the University of Exeter found in 2020 that small talk enhanced workers’ sense of well-being and connectedness. But chatting about nothing requires practice, even for extroverts.

闲聊是另一项失去的技能。在家工作时，你不需要礼貌地向别人点头和微笑。当你和你的配偶和孩子说话时，问候家人是很奇怪的。相比之下，拥挤的办公室需要没完没了的随意寒暄，无论是在走廊里撞到某人、挤在咖啡机旁，还是开门等电梯。这些陈词滥调是有回报的:罗格斯大学和埃克塞特大学的研究人员在2020年发现，闲聊能增强员工的幸福感和人际关系。但是闲聊需要练习，即使对外向的人来说也是如此。

Meetings are entirely different in the offline world, in good ways and bad. The good includes greater spontaneity and the fact that no one freezes mid-speech, their face contorted into a hideous rictus. The bad is that many habits developed at home must quickly be unlearned upon returning to the office.

线下世界的会议完全不同，有好有坏。好的方面包括更大的自发性，以及没有人会在演讲过程中僵住，他们的脸会扭曲成可怕的狞笑。不好的是，许多在家里养成的习惯，一回到办公室就必须很快改掉。

You cannot openly do other work: tapping away on a laptop while someone drones on is perfectly acceptable on Zoom, but not in the same room. You cannot magically disguise yourself from view by turning off a camera. Any eye-rolling you do will be seen; headbanging the table in exasperation will be noticed.

你不能在公开的情况下做其他工作: 在Zoom上，当有人在喋喋不休地说着什么时，你在笔记本电脑上敲击键盘是完全可以接受的（但在同一间屋子里不行）。你不能通过关闭摄像机神奇地隐藏自己。你随便翻白眼都会被发现的; 恼怒地用头撞桌子会被注意到。

In theory you could ask all the attendees of a real-life meeting to come with you while you root around in a cupboard for a biscuit, but it is so much simpler to go foraging when you are Zooming. You cannot leave pointless meetings as easily in the office, either. In the virtual world, salvation is just a click and an insincere-apology-in-the-chat away; in the physical world you have to move chairs, mutter excuses and negotiate the door handle. Exit, pursued by a stare.

理论上，当你在橱柜里翻找饼干的时候，你可以要求现实生活中所有的与会者跟你一起去，但当你在Zoom的时候，去寻找就要简单得多。你也不能轻易地离开无意义的会议，就像在办公室里一样。在虚拟世界中，只需点击一下鼠标，然后在聊天中虚情假意地道歉，就能得到解脱; 在现实世界中，你必须移动椅子，喃喃地说些借口，并设法握住门把手。离开时被人盯着。

The realities of corporeal colleagues show up in other ways, too. Take seating. Rarely do you amble into your own living room to find Malcolm from marketing there. In newly crowded offices you will be competing with him to book a desk; worse, he may be your neighbour. Heating is another example. Women are more productive at temperatures warmer than those men prefer, but they are less likely to have control of the thermostat in the office than in their homes.

“实体”同事的真实情况也以其他方式表现出来。请坐。你很少会悠闲地走进自己的客厅，去找市场部的Malcolm。在新的拥挤的办公室里，你要与他争着预定桌子；更糟的是，他可能是你的邻居。供暖是另一个例子。在温度更高的环境中，女性的工作效率要高于男性，但与在家中相比，她们对办公室温度自动调节器的控制力更弱。

And this is to say nothing of the underlying concerns that drove people to vacate their offices in the first place—the infectiousness and virulence of covid-19. Company by company, new norms of physical interaction will emerge and change over the coming months. Handshake, fistbump or simple “hello”? Masks on, off or slung under the chin, ready to be deployed at a moment’s notice? Socially distanced or just social?

更不用说最初促使人们离开办公室的潜在担忧，即新冠的传染性和毒性。在接下来的几个月里，一个又一个公司，新的身体接触规范将会出现并发生改变。握手，碰碰拳头还是简单的“你好”? 戴上、摘下口罩，还是把口罩挂在下巴下面，随时准备戴上? 社交疏远还是只是社交?

The start of the hybrid era is good news. It means that the pandemic has moved into a new and less threatening phase. Companies can now try to blend the benefits of in-person interaction with the flexibility to work remotely that many employees crave. But the proximity of people will still take time to get used to again.

混合办公时代的开始是个好消息。这意味着大流行已进入一个新的、威胁较小的阶段。公司现在可以尝试将面对面交流的好处与许多员工渴望的远程办公的灵活性结合起来。但是人们之间的距离仍然需要时间来适应。

Why Poland has become NATO’s linchpin in the war in Ukraine

为什么波兰成为北约在乌克兰战争中的关键

The frontline state

前线国家

![image-20220315083431020](/Users/chenyujie/Library/Application Support/typora-user-images/image-20220315083431020.png)

The West no longer dismisses its warnings about Russia

西方不再无视其对俄罗斯的警告

Mar 12th 2022

POLISH LEADERS have long pushed their partners in NATO and the EU to forge closer ties with Ukraine, warning of the risk of Russian aggression, only to be dismissed as paranoid. Russia’s murderous and unprovoked invasion of Ukraine has proven them entirely right. Polish denunciations of Russia’s imperial ambitions, which Americans and western Europeans once pooh-poohed as post-communist stress disorder, have now become standard NATO talking points. But it is not just Poland’s view of Russia that is being taken more seriously; it is Poland’s role in the world. In a matter of a few weeks, the country has become the linchpin of the Western effort to defend Ukraine and deter Russia, a task as important as it is dangerous.

长期以来，波兰领导人一直在敦促北约和欧盟的伙伴与乌克兰建立更紧密的关系，警告俄罗斯侵略的风险，结果却被斥为多疑。俄罗斯对乌克兰的置人于死地且无端的入侵证明了他们是完全正确的。波兰对俄罗斯帝国主义野心的谴责，曾经被美国和西欧人嘲笑为后共产主义压力失调症，现在已经成为北约的标准话题。但是，不仅仅是波兰对俄罗斯的看法受到了更多的重视; 而是波兰在世界上的角色。在短短几周的时间里，波兰已经成为西方保卫乌克兰、遏制俄罗斯行动的关键，这项任务既重要又危险。

Hundreds of Stinger missiles, Javelin anti-tank weapons and other munitions have already poured into Ukraine through Poland and Romania, part of America’s $350m package to assist the besieged country. Poland itself has dispatched an ammunition convoy to Ukraine, and plans to send mortars, small drones and man-portable missile systems, known as MANPADS, from its own supplies. Weapons deliveries from other countries, including a €450m ($490m) consignment financed by the EU, are on the way, too. “The biggest share of military equipment, both lethal and non-lethal, will go through Poland,” says Konrad Muzyka of Rochan Consulting, a military-analysis firm. “Like it or not,” says Stanislaw Koziej, a former brigadier-general in Poland’s army, “we are going to be the main link in the chain connecting Ukraine and the West.”

数百枚“毒刺”导弹、标枪反坦克武器和其他军需品已经通过波兰和罗马尼亚涌入乌克兰，这是美国3.5亿美元援助这个被围困国家一揽子计划的一部分。波兰自己也向乌克兰派遣了一支弹药车队，并计划从自己的补给中运送迫击炮、小型无人机和便携式导弹系统(即MANPADS)。来自其他国家的武器，包括欧盟资助的4.5亿欧元(4.9亿美元)，也在运送途中。军事分析公司Rochan Consulting的Konrad Muzyka说:“最大份额的军事装备，无论是致命的还是非致命的，都将通过波兰。”波兰前陆军准将Stanislaw Koziej说“不管你喜不喜欢，我们将成为连接乌克兰和西方的链条上的主要环节。”

Poland is nearer the eye of the storm than any other NATO member. It risks being drawn in further. For days the country mooted giving its MiG-29 fighter jets to Ukraine in exchange for F-16s from America. The Americans, who initially pushed the plan, balked when Poland asked to send the jets via an American airbase in Germany. On March 9th they backed out, saying transferring the jets risked escalation. Poland wants to do as much as possible, say officials in Warsaw, without being dragged into the war.

波兰是北约成员国中最接近风暴中心的国家。它有被进一步卷入的风险。几天来，波兰一直在讨论用MiG-29战机换取美国的F-16s战机。最初推动这一计划的美国人，在波兰要求通过美国在德国的一个空军基地派遣战机时犹豫不决。3月9日，他们放弃了，其称转移飞机会使事态升级。华沙的官员表示，波兰希望在不被拖入战争的情况下，尽其所能。

The crisis has also turned Poland, almost overnight, into the country with the second-largest refugee population in Europe. At least 2m people have escaped Ukraine since the start of the invasion, the most extensive and rapid movement of refugees in Europe’s post-war history. Over 1.3m have reached Poland. The country has coped remarkably well with the influx. But its resources are stretched.

这场危机也使波兰几乎一夜之间成为欧洲难民人数第二大的国家。自入侵开始以来，至少有200万人逃离了乌克兰，这是欧洲战后历史上规模最大、速度最快的难民流动。超过130万难民抵达波兰。这个国家对移民的涌入处理得相当好。但它的资源已经捉襟见肘。

Poland was already home to Europe’s largest Ukrainian diaspora, over a million strong, and so is an important recruiting ground for Ukraine’s resistance. In the first ten days of the war, Polish border guards recorded 217,000 crossings into Ukraine. A large share were Ukrainians heading back home to fight. “Our people are waiting,” says Aleksandr, who arrived at the border by bus from Estonia. “They’re going to tell us what to do, give us ammunition and guns, and then we’re off to work.”

波兰已经是欧洲最大的乌克兰侨民的家园，超过100万人，也是乌克兰抵抗运动的重要招募基地。在战争的头10天里，波兰边防警卫记录了21.7万名越境进入乌克兰的人。其中很大一部分是返回家园参战的乌克兰人。Aleksandr从爱沙尼亚乘公共汽车来到边境，其称：“我们的人民在等着，他们会告诉我们该做什么，给我们弹药和枪支，然后我们就离开去战斗。”

Poland’s size, location and infrastructure make it the West’s most important gateway to Ukraine. The two countries share a 530km (330-mile) border, punctuated by over a dozen crossings. The airport in Rzeszow, about an hour’s drive from the border, is the closest big airport to Ukraine on NATO territory. Flight radar data show an uptick in military aircraft, almost certainly involved in weapons deliveries, flying in and out of Rzeszow since the start of the war. Were Kyiv to fall to the Russians, which is still far from guaranteed, Ukrainian troops and volunteers would probably regroup in the west of the country, in or around Lviv, only 80km from the Polish border. This would further raise Poland’s importance for Ukraine.

波兰的面积、地理位置和基础设施使其成为西方进入乌克兰的最重要途径。两国共有530公里(330英里)的边境线，其间有十多个过境点。波兰热舒夫机场，距离边境大约一个小时的车程，是北约领土上离乌克兰最近的大型机场。飞行雷达数据显示，自战争开始以来，进出热舒夫的军用飞机数量有所增加，几乎可以肯定，这些飞机涉及武器运送。如果基辅落入俄罗斯的手中(这一点还远未得到保证)，乌克兰军队和志愿者很可能会在该国西部、距离波兰边境仅80公里的利沃夫或附近重新集结。这将进一步提高波兰对乌克兰的重要性。

The war has already strengthened Poland’s position in NATO. The alliance is increasing its presence in Poland dramatically. America had deployed nearly 5,000 new troops to Poland in February, even before the Russian invasion, bringing the total to some 9,000. It also recently approved the sale to Poland of 250 Abrams tanks.

这场战争已经加强了波兰在北约的地位。北约正在大幅增加其在波兰的存在。早在俄罗斯入侵波兰之前，美国就在2月份向波兰增派了近5000名士兵，使总数达到约9000人。它最近还批准向波兰出售250辆艾布拉姆斯坦克。

Poland also hopes to reconcile with the rest of the EU. The bloc has repeatedly chided Poland’s nationalist government for stacking its top court with cronies, threatening judges who rule against it and harassing the media. The stand-off prompted the EU to freeze €36bn in pandemic recovery funds earmarked for Poland and to fine it for ignoring rulings by the European Court of Justice. Poland has moved to meet some EU demands, and its efforts in the face of Russia’s aggression may earn it a more sympathetic hearing.

波兰也希望与欧盟其他国家达成和解。欧盟多次指责波兰民族主义政府在最高法院安上亲信，威胁做出不利于其裁决的法官，并骚扰媒体。这一僵局促使欧盟冻结了为波兰提供的360亿欧元疫情恢复资金，并对波兰无视欧洲法院裁决的行为处以罚款。波兰已经采取行动满足欧盟的一些要求，它在面对俄罗斯侵略时所做的努力可能会赢得更多的同情。

But there are incalculable risks involved, too. Analysts say Russian retaliation of one kind or another against Poland is a near certainty. The longer the war lasts, the more desperate Russia will be to destroy the supply lines connecting Poland to Ukraine. “I’m afraid that Russian rocket attacks against those supply lines are something we have to take into account in the coming days and weeks,” says Wojciech Kononczuk of the Centre for Eastern Studies, a think-tank in Warsaw. America clearly takes the possibility seriously: it is sending Patriot missile-defence batteries to Poland. Others mention the threat of Russian sabotage and cyberattacks inside Poland. A social-media monitoring group has already uncovered a Russian disinformation campaign intended to spread panic and drive up resentment towards refugees from Ukraine.

但这也涉及到不可估量的风险。分析人士说，俄罗斯对波兰进行这样或那样的报复几乎是肯定的。战争持续的时间越长，俄罗斯就越急于摧毁连接波兰和乌克兰的补给线。华沙智库东方研究中心的Wojciech Kononczuk说:“我担心俄罗斯对这些补给线的火箭袭击是我们在未来几天和几周内必须考虑的事情。”美国显然很重视这种可能性:它正在向波兰派遣爱国者导弹防御系统。还有人提到了俄罗斯在波兰境内的蓄意破坏和网络攻击的威胁。一个社交媒体监控组织已经发现了俄罗斯的虚假信息宣传活动，其目的是传播恐慌，提高对乌克兰难民的怨恨。

Vladimir Putin’s threats against countries that interfere in Ukraine are not to be taken lightly. But for Poland and NATO, the only risk bigger than that of Russian retaliation is the risk of Western inaction. “There’s no alternative, because we’re in the same boat,” says Mr Kononczuk. “The only difference is that Ukraine’s in the front, and we’re in the back.” ■

普京对干预乌克兰事务的国家发出的威胁不能掉以轻心。但对波兰和北约来说，唯一比俄罗斯报复更大的风险是西方的不作为。Kononczuk先生说：“我们没有其他选择，因为我们在同一条船上。唯一不同的是，乌克兰在前线，而我们在身后(援助)。”■

Taking stock as America moves into a new phase of the pandemic

对美国进入新冠大流行新阶段的评估

The pandemic, two years on

疫情，两年过去了

Planning for the future should involve a frank look at the country’s poor performance over the past two years

对未来的规划应该包括坦率地审视该国过去两年的糟糕表现

Mar 10th 2022

TWO YEARS ago on March 11th, the World Health Organisation declared covid-19 a pandemic. Americans are eager to leave the wretchedness behind them. Some are so anxious that they are driving trucks along the Beltway around Washington, DC, hoping to slow traffic in protest against pandemic restrictions, inspired by disruption in Canada last month. The “People’s Convoy” looks strangely out of touch—not because the truckers are alone in their desire to put covid restrictions in the rear-view mirror, but because so many restrictions have already been falling away.

两年前的3月11日，世界卫生组织宣布covid-19为大流行病。美国人渴望把这种悲惨的生活抛在身后。一些人非常焦虑，以至于他们开着卡车沿着华盛顿特区的环城公路行驶，希望减慢交通速度，以抗议因上个月在加拿大发生的混乱引发的对新冠大流行的限制。“人民车队”奇怪地像看起来不太了解情况，这并不是因为卡车司机们想把对新冠的限制抛到脑后，而是因为很多限制已经被取消了。

Polls suggest concern about covid is declining. Mask-wearing has waned (a maskless President Joe Biden hobnobbed insouciantly with members of Congress after his state-of-the-union message last week). On March 26th, Hawaii will become the final state to drop its indoor mask mandate, and the Centres for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC) now recommends masks only for the 7% of Americans living in high-risk counties. The vast majority of schools are open for in-person learning. Batman fans packed into cinemas for the opening weekend of the latest film in the franchise.

民意调查显示，对新冠病毒的担忧正在减少。戴口罩的人已经少了(上周，美国总统乔•拜登在发表国情咨文后漫不经心地与国会议员闲谈，没有戴口罩)。3月26日，夏威夷将成为最后一个取消室内戴口罩规定的州，美国疾病控制和预防中心(CDC)现在建议，只有生活在高风险县的7%的美国人戴口罩。绝大多数学校都为面授课程开放。《蝙蝠侠》系列最新电影首映周末，蝙蝠侠迷们涌入电影院。

Some states have long been crowding people into small spaces with few restrictions. Over the past year Florida, Tennessee and Texas banned local governments and public schools from enforcing mask mandates. Restrictions generally fell along partisan lines, with Democratic states steadfastly adhering to them and Republican states tossing them aside. Now even New York, one of the first to impose a lockdown, is starting to lighten up. New York state ended its mask mandate for schools on March 2nd; New York City lifted its own on March 7th. Nationwide, the seven-day moving average of deaths is at its lowest since January 2nd; that of reported cases is at its lowest since the Delta variant began surging in July 2021. For most Americans, covid restrictions are in the past.

一些州长期以来一直将人们聚集在几乎没有限制的狭小的空间里。去年，佛罗里达州、田纳西州和德克萨斯州禁止地方政府和公立学校强制佩戴口罩。限制措施通常是按照党派路线制定的，民主党州坚定地遵守这些规定，而共和党州则会将其搁置一边。现在，就连最早实施封锁的纽约也开始放松了。纽约州在3月2日结束了学校戴口罩的规定; 纽约市在3月7日也解除其限制。在全国范围内，七天移动平均死亡人数达到了自1月2日以来的最低水平;报告的病例数量是自2021年7月Delta变种开始激增以来的最低水平。对大多数美国人来说，对新冠的限制已经成为过去。

In short, the pandemic has reached a punctuation point. Even if it is a comma rather than a full stop, it is a good time to look back at how the country has fared, and ahead to the next phase.

简而言之，大流行已经到了一个转折点。即使它只是一个逗号，而不是一个句号，这也是回顾这个国家过去的表现，并迈向下一个阶段的好时机。

America has been hit hard by covid. Over 950,000 people have died from the virus, according to the CDC, though The Economist estimates that the actual count is 1.1m-1.3m. America has the highest death rate among rich countries: nearly double the average (see chart). Many expected America to respond well to a pandemic. Instead, it vastly underperformed.

美国受到了新冠的沉重打击。根据美国疾病控制与预防中心的数据，已有超过95万人死于这种病毒，尽管《经济学人》估计实际死亡人数在110万到130万之间。在发达国家中，美国的死亡率最高，几乎是平均水平的两倍(见图表)。许多人期望美国能很好地应对流感大流行。相反，它的表现远远落后。

It has struggled to vaccinate its people: 65% are fully vaccinated, compared with 72% in Britain, 73% in the European Union, 81% in Canada and 95% in the United Arab Emirates. America also fell behind on detection. Last year it ranked 36th in the world in sequencing SARS-CoV-2, hindering early recognition of new variants. The country also lagged behind in testing. Whereas Britons have had access to free rapid tests for over a year, Americans received their first round only last month. Lab tests were hard to come by, too: queues and waits for results were long.

它一直在努力为国民接种疫苗: 65%的人完全接种了疫苗，相比之下，英国为72%，欧盟为73%，加拿大为81%，阿拉伯联合酋长国为95%。美国在识别方面也落后了。去年，它在SARS-CoV-2的测序中排名世界第36位，这阻碍了对新变体的早期识别。该国在检测方面也落后。英国人已经接受免费快速检测一年多了，而美国人上个月才接受了第一轮（免费）检测。实验室测试也很难得到: 排队等待结果的时间很长。

The United States is the only high-income country without universal health care. One in eight adults reports not going to a doctor in the past year because of the cost. The pandemic has aggravated the problem of access. Hospital capacity was strained, and many elective procedures delayed. Some states enacted crisis standards of care, a protocol to delineate who receives treatment when resources are scarce. “Now that the covid admission numbers are falling, we still have enormous numbers of patients requiring admission because of delays of care that have been occurring all through the pandemic,” says Jeffrey Balser, the CEO of Vanderbilt University Medical Centre in Tennessee.

美国是唯一一个没有全民医保的高收入国家。八分之一的成年人报告称，在过去的一年中，他们没有去看医生，因为费用太高。这一流行病加剧了准入问题。医院的容纳能力受限，许多选择性手术被推迟。一些州颁布了危机护理标准，这是一项规定在资源匮乏时谁能接受治疗的协议。田纳西州范德比尔特大学医学中心CEO Jeffrey Balser说:“尽管因新冠入院的人数正在下降，但由于在整个大流行期间出现的护理延误，我们仍有大量患者需要入院。”

Public-health officials are urging the government to prepare for the next wave. “This is a lull. A moment to prepare so we are never caught again,” says Megan Ranney, a physician and dean at Brown University’s School of Public Health.

公共卫生官员敦促政府为下一波疫情做好准备。布朗大学公共卫生学院的医生兼院长Megan Ranney说：“这是一种平静的时刻。这是准备的时刻，这样我们就不会再措手不及。”

On March 2nd the White House released its National Covid-19 Preparedness Plan. It has four parts: protect and treat covid, prepare for new variants, prevent economic and educational lockdowns, and continue to vaccinate the world. The plan includes a one-stop test-to-treat initiative: Americans can get a test for covid and, if they test positive, get a prescription for an antiviral drug free at pharmacy-based clinics and other health facilities. During the last covid surge, antivirals were hard to find, says Dr Balser. He hopes this plan will increase their availability.

3月2日，白宫发布了针对新冠的国家防备计划。该计划包括四个部分:保护和治疗新冠，为新变体做好准备，防止经济和教育封锁，以及继续为全世界接种疫苗。该计划包括一项一站式的检测治疗倡议: 美国人可以进行covid检测，如果检测呈阳性，就可以在药房诊所和其他卫生设施免费获得抗病毒药物处方。Balser博士说，在上一次疫情激增期间，很难找到抗病毒药物。他希望这一计划将增加他们的可用性。

The programme outlines better methods to detect new strains. It aims for more efficient data collection, wastewater surveillance (to detect prevalence of covid) and virus sequencing. It includes a “surge-response playbook” to provide guidance for setting up mass vaccination and testing sites, deploying medical workers, expanding hospitals and providing supplies.

该计划概述了检测新菌株的更好方法。其旨在更有效地数据收集，废水监测(以检测covid的流行情况)和进行病毒测序。它包括一个“快速反应手册”，以为建立大规模疫苗接种和检测场所、部署医务人员、扩大医院和供应物资提供指导。

“It is a lovely plan. Everything I would focus on is here,” says Dr Ranney. But she fears that the administration will not receive adequate funding. The plan requires money from Congress, and Mr Biden’s last coronavirus package for $1.9trn passed along party lines. “It’s not a typical American thing to say that we’re going to commit resources ahead of time,” says Dr Ranney. “I hope we have learned our lesson.”

Ranney博士说：“这是一个好计划。我所关注的一切都在这里”。但她担心政府得不到足够的资金。该计划需要国会提供资金，而拜登上一个1.9万亿美元的新冠肺炎方案是按照党派路线通过的。Ranney博士说:“我们会提前投入资源，这不是典型的美国作风。我希望我们已经吸取了教训。”

Preparedness might be only part of the answer. According to Howard Koh, former assistant secretary for health in the Obama administration, America’s overall health could be to blame. “Covid is a fast pandemic fuelled by a slower pandemic of preventable chronic conditions,” explains Dr Koh. He points to America’s rate of illness. It is the fattest country in the OECD, a club of mostly rich countries. Almost half of Americans have high blood pressure. Heart disease accounts for one in four deaths. About one in ten has type 2 diabetes. These conditions worsen the effects of covid.

做好准备可能只是解决问题的一部分。奥巴马政府负责卫生事务的前助理部长Howard Koh认为，美国的整体健康状况可能是罪魁祸首。Koh博士解释说:“新冠是一场由“可预防的慢性疾病的缓慢大流行”推动的快速大流行。” 他指出了美国的发病率。美国是经合组织(主要由发达国家组成)中最肥胖的国家。几乎一半的美国人有高血压。心脏病占死亡人数的四分之一。大约十分之一的人患有II型糖尿病。这些疾病使新冠病毒的影响变得更糟。

Dr Koh calls for more investment in public-health infrastructure, both to prepare for covid surges and to deal with long-standing health concerns. What seems clear is that a multipronged approach is needed. As Dr Ranney notes: “There is very rarely a single magic bullet.” ■

Koh博士呼吁加大对公共卫生基础设施的投资，以应新冠的激增，并应对长期存在的卫生问题。显然，需要采取多管齐下的方法。正如Ranney博士所指出的:“几乎没有一种非常有效地解决问题的方法。”■

War and sanctions have caused commodities chaos

战争和制裁导致商品混乱

Fuel, food and fury

燃料，食物以及愤怒

![image-20220313091624245](/Users/chenyujie/Library/Application Support/typora-user-images/image-20220313091624245.png)

The world must rise to the challenge

世界必须迎接挑战

Mar 12th 2022

GLOBAL COMMODITY crises tend to cause severe economic damage and political upheaval. The oil shocks of the 1970s left Western economies with runaway inflation and deep recessions. Oil revenues also helped prop up the Soviet Union and fuelled the export of Saudi extremism. Soaring grain prices in 2010 and 2011 were a trigger for the street protests that led to the Arab spring and the toppling of dictators.

全球大宗商品危机往往会造成严重的经济损失和政治动荡。上世纪70年代的石油危机让西方经济体陷入失控的通货膨胀和严重的经济衰退。石油收入也帮助支撑了苏联，并加剧了沙特极端主义的输出。2010年和2011年飙升的粮食价格引发了街头抗议，最终导致了””阿拉伯之春””和独裁者的下台。

Today Russia’s invasion of Ukraine is unleashing the biggest commodity shock since 1973, and one of the worst disruptions to wheat supplies since the first world war. Although commodity exchanges are already in chaos, ordinary folk have yet to feel the full effects of rising petrol bills, empty stomachs and political instability. But make no mistake, those things are coming—and dramatically so if sanctions on Russia tighten further, and if Vladimir Putin retaliates. Western governments need to respond to the commodity threat as determinedly as to Mr Putin’s aggression.

如今，俄罗斯入侵乌克兰引发了自1973年以来最大的大宗商品冲击，也是自第一次世界大战以来小麦供应最严重的中断之一。尽管商品交易所已经陷入混乱，但普通老百姓还没有感受到油价上涨、饥饿感和政治不稳定的全面影响。但毫无疑问，这些事情正在发生，如果对俄罗斯的制裁进一步加强，如果普京进行报复，那么这些事情就会戏剧性地发生。西方各国政府应对大宗商品威胁的决心，要像应对普京的侵略一样坚定。

The turmoil unfolding in energy, metals and food markets is broad and savage. Overall indices of commodity prices are now 26% higher than at the start of 2022. The cost of a barrel of Brent crude oil has swung wildly around levels that indicate the biggest supply shock since Saddam Hussein’s army crossed from Iraq into Kuwait in 1990. European gas prices have almost trebled amid panic that pipelines from the east will be blown up or starved of supply. The price of nickel, used in all electric cars among other things, has spiralled so high that trading in London has been halted and Chinese speculators are nursing multi-billion-dollar losses.

能源、金属和食品市场的动荡是广泛而猛烈的。目前，大宗商品价格总体指数比2022年初高出26%。布伦特原油每桶的价格已大幅波动，这表明自1990年Saddam Hussein的军队从伊拉克越境进入科威特以来，石油供应遭遇了最严重的冲击。欧洲天然气价格几乎涨了两倍，因为人们担心来自东部的管道会被炸毁或供应不足。用于所有电动汽车和其他产品的镍的价格已经急剧上升，以至于伦敦的交易已经停止，中国的投机者正在承受数十亿美元的损失。

Such are the consequences of Mr Putin’s decision to drive his tanks across the breadbasket of Europe, and the subsequent isolation of Russia, one of the world’s biggest commodities exporters. Western sanctions on Russian banks have made lenders, insurers and shipping firms wary of striking deals to carry Russian cargoes, leaving growing piles of unsold industrial metals and an armada of vessels full of unwanted Urals crude. Stigma and danger have caused others to stay away. Shell has abandoned buying Russian crude oil after a backlash. The Black Sea is a no-go zone for commercial shipping because some vessels have been hit by missiles and Russia is menacing Ukrainian ports. Not many seeds will be planted in Ukraine’s blood-soaked fields this spring.

这就是普京决定开着坦克穿过欧洲粮仓的后果，以及随之而来的俄罗斯（这个全球最大的大宗商品出口国）遭到孤立。西方对俄罗斯银行的制裁使得贷方、保险公司和船运公司对与俄罗斯达成运输俄罗斯货物的协议持谨慎态度，这就导致留下了越来越多未售出的工业金属和一大批满载多余乌拉尔原油的船只。耻辱和危险导致其他人远离。壳牌(Shell)在遭到强烈反对后，已放弃了购买俄罗斯原油。黑海是商业航运的禁区，因为一些船只被导弹击中，俄罗斯正在威胁乌克兰港口。今年春天，在乌克兰被鲜血浸透的土地上播种的种子不多。

It could get worse. On March 8th, in the latest measure to increase pressure on Mr Putin, America announced that it would ban purchases of Russian oil. The United States is a small consumer of Russian crude, but if the European Union were to join the embargo, about two-thirds of the 7m-8m barrels a day of exports of Russian crude and refined products would be affected, equivalent to about 5% of global supply. A full global embargo, enforced by America, could send the oil price towards $200 a barrel. If Russia were to retaliate by limiting gas flows, Europe would reel: last year the EU relied on Russia for 40% of its consumption. Meanwhile, bitter experience teaches that countries often respond to food shortages by banning exports, leading to a tit-for-tat breakdown in global trade.

情况可能会变得更糟。3月8日，美国宣布将禁止购买俄罗斯石油，这是向普京施压的最新举措。美国是俄罗斯原油的一个小消费国，但如果欧盟实施禁运，俄罗斯每天出口的700万到800万桶原油和成品油中的三分之二将受到影响，这相当于全球供应的5%左右。由美国实施的全面全球禁运可能会将油价推至200美元一桶。如果俄罗斯采取限制天然气流动的报复措施，欧洲将陷入困境: 去年欧盟40%的天然气消费依赖于俄罗斯。与此同时，痛苦的经验告诉我们，各国往往通过禁止出口来应对粮食短缺，这会导致全球贸易出现针锋相对的崩溃。

The effects of this commodity calamity could be brutal. If you look narrowly at the economy, the world is far less energy-intensive per unit of GDP than in the 1970s. Nonetheless, global inflation, already at 7%, may rise by another two to three percentage points, to a level last seen for a sustained period in the early 1990s, when Mr Putin was doing mafia deals in St Petersburg and globalisation had yet to flourish. Growth may slow as firms’ confidence is knocked and interest rates rise.

这场大宗商品灾难的影响可能是残酷的。如果你狭隘地看经济，世界单位GDP的能源密集程度远低于上世纪70年代。尽管如此，已经达到7%的全球通货膨胀率可能会再上升2-3个百分点，达到上世纪90年代初的持续水平，当时普京在圣彼得堡从事黑手党交易，全球化尚未繁荣。随着企业信心受到打击和利率上升，经济增长可能会放缓。

In the political realm, leaders in the West will have to face furious voters, not least in America’s mid-term elections in November. Remember the gilets jaunes protesters in France in 2018, furious at the cost of petrol. In poorer countries where food and fuel are a larger part of people’s spending, the backlash could be still more violent: food-price spikes in 2007-08 led to riots in 48 countries, and there are already signs of panic and unrest today.

在政治领域，西方领导人将不得不面对愤怒的选民，尤其是在11月的美国中期选举中。还记得2018年法国的“黄背心”抗议者吗? 他们对汽油价格感到愤怒。在较贫困国家，食品和燃料在人们支出中所占比例较大，这种反对可能会更加剧烈：2007-08年的食品价格飙升导致48个国家发生骚乱，如今已经出现了恐慌和动荡的迹象。

Such a panorama of suffering and instability is worrying in its own right. But it also threatens to undermine the credibility of the Western response to Russia’s decision to start what may become the largest war in Europe since 1945. The greater the global pain, Mr Putin may judge, the harder it will be for the West to sustain the sanctions: all he has to do is wait it out.

这种苦难和不稳定的景象本身就令人担忧。但这也可能削弱西方对“俄罗斯决定发动”的回应的可信度，俄罗斯决定的发动可能成为自1945年以来欧洲最大战争。普京可能会判断，全球的痛苦越大，西方国家就越难维持制裁: 他所要做的就是等待制裁结束。

That is one more reason for Western governments to counter the ill effects of the commodity crunch. The priority is to boost supply. American allies in OPEC, including Saudi Arabia, have declined to pump more oil, but more adept American diplomacy could yield results. Rich countries could speed up the release of the 1.5bn barrels of oil they hold in reserve. Having disparaged America’s shale-frackers, the Biden administration needs to prod them to drill more. The EU must promote or prolong its use of nuclear, renewable and coal-powered generation, so as to stock up on gas for the winter. It should also prepare for the worst case: gas rationing. Rich-country governments may have to protect the poor at home with handouts. Stimulus could mean higher interest rates or taxes, but that is a risk worth taking to protect the world against an aggressor.

这是西方国家政府应对商品危机负面影响的又一个原因。当务之急是增加供应。美国在OPEC的盟友，包括沙特阿拉伯，已经拒绝增加石油产量，但美国更老练的外交手腕可能会起效。发达国家可以加快释放其储备的15亿桶石油。拜登政府在贬低了美国的页岩气开采商之后，需要鼓励他们开采更多的页岩气。欧盟必须提倡或延长使用核能、可再生能源和燃煤发电，以便为冬季储备天然气。它还应该为最坏的情况做准备:天然气定量配给。发达国家的政府可能不得不通过施舍来保护国内的穷人。刺激可能意味着更高的利率或税收，但为了保护世界免受侵略者的攻击，这是值得冒的风险。

Digging deep

Whatever the privations of rich countries, poorer ones are in worse trouble. So the West must strengthen the global financial safety-net. Some food and oil importers may face a balance-of-payments squeeze and tumbling currencies. Even in Europe, some countries, such as the Baltic states, are vulnerable to gas cut-offs. The Federal Reserve and the IMF should make it easier for friendly but fragile countries to gain access to hard-currency loans. And Europe should press ahead with an idea to issue joint debt to help spread the costs of the crisis.

不管发达国家的匮乏状况如何，穷国的麻烦更大。因此，西方国家必须加强全球金融安全网。一些食品和石油进口国可能面临国际收支紧缩和货币贬值。即使在欧洲，一些国家，如波罗的海国家，也很容易受到天然气供应中断的影响。美联储和国际货币基金组织应该让友好但脆弱的国家更容易获得硬通货贷款。欧洲应该推进发行联合债券的想法，以帮助分摊危机的成本。

A world facing a physical shortage of raw materials dug up from the ground seems like a throwback to an earlier age. Yet that is exactly the predicament that lies ahead. After decades of drift, the West has shown resolve and cohesion by confronting Mr Putin’s aggression. Now it must match that by showing leadership in the teeth of the economic storm. ■

一个面临着从地下挖掘的原材料短缺的世界，似乎是倒退到了更早的时代。然而，这正是摆在我们面前的困境。在几十年的疏远后，西方通过对抗普京的侵略表现出了决心和凝聚力。现在，它必须通过在经济风暴中展现出领导力，以此来应对这一挑战。■

The Stalinisation of Russia

俄罗斯的斯大林主义

Vladimir Putin’s dictatorship

普京的独裁统治

![image-20220312091344000](/Users/chenyujie/Library/Application Support/typora-user-images/image-20220312091344000.png)

As it sinks in that he cannot win in Ukraine, Vladimir Putin is resorting to repression at home

随着普京意识到他在乌克兰无法取胜，他开始在国内采取镇压手段

WHEN VLADIMIR PUTIN ordered the invasion of Ukraine, he dreamed of restoring the glory of the Russian empire. He has ended up restoring the terror of Josef Stalin. That is not only because he has unleashed the most violent act of unprovoked aggression in Europe since 1939, but also because, as a result, he is turning himself into a dictator at home—a 21st-century Stalin, resorting as never before to lies, violence and paranoia.

当普京下令入侵乌克兰时，他梦想着恢复俄罗斯帝国的荣耀。他最终恢复了斯大林的恐怖。这不仅是因为他对欧洲发动了自1939年以来最暴力的无端侵略，而且还因为他(因此)正在把自己变成一个国内的独裁者，一个21世纪的斯大林，以前所未有的方式诉诸于谎言、暴力和偏执。

To understand the scale of Mr Putin’s lies, consider how the war was planned. Russia’s president thought Ukraine would rapidly collapse, so he did not prepare his people for the invasion or his soldiers for their mission—indeed, he assured the elites that it would not happen. After two terrible weeks on the battlefield, he is still denying that he is waging what may become Europe’s biggest war since 1945. To sustain this all-encompassing lie, he has shut down almost the entire independent media, threatened journalists with up to 15 years in jail if they do not parrot official falsehoods, and had anti-war protesters arrested in their thousands. By insisting that his military “operation” is de-Nazifying Ukraine, state television is re-Stalinising Russia.

要理解普京谎言的规模，不妨想想这场战争是如何策划的。俄罗斯总统认为乌克兰会迅速崩溃，所以他没有让他的人民为入侵做好准备，也没有让他的士兵为他们的任务做好准备，事实上，他还向精英们保证这不会发生。在战场上经历了可怕的两个星期后，他仍然否认他正在发动一场可能成为1945年以来欧洲最大的战争。为了维持这个弥天大谎，他关闭了几乎所有的独立媒体，威胁记者，如果他们不重复官方的谎言，将被判处最高15年的监禁，他还逮捕了数千名反战抗议者。国家电视台通过坚称他的军事“行动”是去纳粹化乌克兰，正在重新斯大林化俄罗斯。

To grasp Mr Putin’s appetite for violence, look at how the war is being fought. Having failed to win a quick victory, Russia is trying to sow panic by starving Ukrainian cities and pounding them blindly. On March 9th it hit a maternity hospital in Mariupol. If Mr Putin is committing war crimes against the fellow Slavs he eulogised in his writings, he is ready to inflict slaughter at home.

要了解普京对暴力的喜好，看看这场战争是如何进行的。由于未能迅速赢得胜利，俄罗斯正试图通过让乌克兰城市挨饿和盲目地轰炸它们以制造恐慌。3月9日，它袭击了马里乌波尔的一家妇产医院。如果普京对他在文章中赞美的斯拉夫人同胞犯下了战争罪，他就准备好在国内进行屠杀。

And to gauge Mr Putin’s paranoia, imagine how the war ends. Russia has more firepower than Ukraine. It is still making progress, especially in the south. It may yet capture the capital, Kyiv. And yet, even if the war drags on for months, it is hard to see Mr Putin as the victor.

为了评估普京的偏执，想象一下战争将如何结束。俄罗斯拥有比乌克兰更多的火力。它仍在取得进展，特别是在南部。它还可能占领首都基辅。然而，即使战争持续数月，普京也很难成为胜利者。

Suppose that Russia manages to impose a new government. Ukrainians are now united against the invader. Mr Putin’s puppet could not rule without an occupation, but Russia does not have the money or the troops to garrison even half of Ukraine. American army doctrine says that to face down an insurgency—in this case, one backed by NATO—occupiers need 20 to 25 soldiers per 1,000 people; Russia has a little over four.

假设俄罗斯成功地组建了一个新政府。乌克兰人现在团结起来对抗侵略者。普京的傀儡不可能在没有占领的情况下统治乌克兰，但俄罗斯甚至没有足够的资金或军队驻守半个乌克兰。美国军队的信条是，要镇压一场叛乱（在这种情况下，是要由北约支持的一场叛乱），占领者在每千人需要20到25名士兵; 俄罗斯就仅派四个多一点的士兵。

If, as the Kremlin may have started to signal, Mr Putin will not impose a puppet government—because he cannot—then he will have to compromise with Ukraine in peace talks. Yet he will struggle to enforce any such agreement. After all, what will he do if post-war Ukraine resumes its Westward drift: invade?

如果像克林姆林宫已经开始暗示的那样，普京不会强行建立一个傀儡政府，因为他不能，那么他将不得不在和平谈判中与乌克兰妥协。不过，他将很难执行任何这样的协议。毕竟，如果战后乌克兰继续西进，入侵，他会怎么做?

The truth is sinking in that, by attacking Ukraine, Mr Putin has committed a catastrophic error. He has wrecked the reputation of Russia’s supposedly formidable armed forces, which have proved tactically inept against a smaller, worse-armed but motivated opponent. Russia has lost mountains of equipment and endured thousands of casualties, almost as many in two weeks as America has suffered in Iraq since it invaded in 2003.

事实是，普京攻击乌克兰犯了一个灾难性的错误。他破坏了俄罗斯本应强大的武装力量的声誉，事实证明，面对一个规模较小、装备较差但积极性高的对手，俄罗斯的武装力量在战术上是无能的。俄罗斯损失了大量的装备，并遭受了数千人的伤亡，几乎相当于美国自2003年入侵伊拉克以来在两周内的伤亡人数。

Mr Putin has brought ruinous sanctions on his country. The central bank does not have access to the hard currency it needs to support the banking system and stabilise the rouble. Brands that stand for openness, including IKEA and Coca-Cola, have closed their doors. Some goods are being rationed. Western exporters are withholding vital components, leading to factory stoppages. Sanctions on energy—for now, limited—threaten to crimp the foreign exchange Russia needs to pay for its imports.

普京对俄罗斯实施了毁灭性的制裁。俄罗斯央行无法获得支撑银行体系和稳定卢布所需的硬通货。包括宜家和可口可乐在内的一些代表开放的品牌已经关门大吉。一些商品正在实行定量配给。西方出口商扣留了关键零部件，导致工厂停工。目前对能源的制裁有限，这可能会限制俄罗斯为进口支付的外汇。

And, as Stalin did, Mr Putin is destroying the bourgeoisie, the great motor of Russia’s modernisation. Instead of being sent to the gulag, they are fleeing to cities like Istanbul, in Turkey, and Yerevan, in Armenia. Those who choose to stay are being muzzled by restrictions on free speech and free association. They will be battered by high inflation and economic dislocation. In just two weeks, they have lost their country.

而且，正如斯大林所做的那样，普京正在摧毁中产阶级(俄罗斯现代化的主要推动力)。他们没有被送到古拉格，而是逃到土耳其的伊斯坦布尔和亚美尼亚的埃里温等城市。那些选择留下的人被言论自由和结社自由的限制所束缚。他们将受到高通胀和经济混乱的打击。仅仅两周，他们就失去了自己的国家。

Stalin presided over a growing economy. However murderously, he drew on a real ideology. Even as he committed outrages, he consolidated the Soviet empire. After being attacked by Nazi Germany, he was saved by the unbelievable sacrifice of his country, which did more than any other to win the war.

在斯大林的领导下，经济不断增长。不管他有多残忍，他都利用了一种真实的意识形态。即使他犯下了暴行，他也巩固了苏联帝国。在被纳粹德国袭击后，他被他的国家难以置信的牺牲所拯救，他的国家为赢得战争做出了比其他任何国家都多的贡献。

Mr Putin has none of those advantages. Not only is he failing to win a war of choice while impoverishing his people: his regime lacks an ideological core. “Putinism”, such as it is, blends nationalism and orthodox religion for a television audience. Russia’s regions, stretched across 11 time zones, are already muttering about this being Moscow’s war.

普京则没有这些优势。他不仅没能赢得一场有选择的战争，还让他的人民陷入贫困:他的政权缺乏意识形态的核心。对电视观众来说，“普京主义”虽然不怎么样，但它是民族主义和正统宗教的融合。横跨11个时区的俄罗斯地区已经开始抱怨这场战争是莫斯科的战争。

As the scale of Mr Putin’s failure becomes clear, Russia will enter the most dangerous moment in this conflict. Factions in the regime will turn on each other in a spiral of blame. Mr Putin, fearful of a coup, will trust nobody and may have to fight for power. He may also try to change the course of the war by terrifying his Ukrainian foes and driving off their Western backers with chemical weapons, or even a nuclear strike.

随着普京失败的规模越来越明显，俄罗斯将进入这场冲突中最危险的时刻。政权内的派系将开始相互指责。害怕政变的普京将不信任任何人，他可能不得不为权力而战。他还可能试图通过恐吓他的乌克兰敌人、用化学武器赶走他们的西方支持者，甚至发动核战来改变战争进程。

As the world looks on, it should set out to limit the danger ahead. It must puncture Mr Putin’s lies by fostering the truth. Western tech firms are wrong to shut their operations in Russia, because they are handing the regime total control over the flow of information. Governments welcoming Ukrainian refugees should welcome Russian émigrés, too.

在全世界的注视下，它应该会限制未来的危险[这里理解不到位，有好的建议可以评论区留言]。它必须通过宣扬真相来戳穿普京的谎言。西方科技公司关闭在俄罗斯的业务是错误的，因为它们把信息流动的完全控制权交给了俄罗斯政府。欢迎乌克兰难民的政府也应该欢迎俄罗斯“流亡者”。

NATO can help temper Mr Putin’s violence—in Ukraine, at least—by continuing to arm the government of Volodymyr Zelensky and supporting him if he decides that the time has come to enter serious negotiations. It can also increase pressure on Mr Putin by pushing ahead faster and deeper with energy sanctions, though at a cost to the world economy.

北约可以帮助缓和普京的暴力行为（至少是在乌克兰的暴力行为），继续武装Zelensky的政府，并在他决定进入严肃谈判的时候支持他。它还可以通过更快、更深入地推进能源制裁，给普京施压，尽管这会让世界经济付出代价。

And the West can try to contain Mr Putin’s paranoia. NATO should state that it will not shoot at Russian forces, so long as they do not attack first. It must not give Mr Putin a reason to draw Russia into a wider war by a declaring no-fly zone that would need enforcing militarily. However much the West would like a new regime in Moscow, it must state that it will not directly engineer one. Liberation is a task for the Russian people.

西方可以尝试遏制普京的偏执。北约应该声明，只要俄罗斯军队不先进攻，它就不会向他们开枪。它绝不能给普京一个理由，通过宣布禁飞区来将俄罗斯拖入一场更广泛的战争，而禁飞区需要军事强制执行。无论西方多么希望在莫斯科建立一个新政权，它必须声明，它不会直接策划一个新政权。解放是俄罗斯人民的任务。

As Russia sinks, the contrast with the president next door is glaring. Mr Putin is isolated and morally dead; Mr Zelensky is a brave Everyman who has rallied his people and the world. He is Mr Putin’s antithesis—and perhaps his nemesis. Think what Russia might become once freed from its 21st-century Stalin. ■

随着俄罗斯的衰落，与邻国总统的对比愈发明显。普京被孤立，道德沦丧; Zelensky先生是一个勇敢的普通人，他团结了他的人民和全世界。他是普京的对立面，或许也是他的宿敌。想想俄罗斯一旦摆脱了21世纪斯大林的统治，会变成什么样子。■

Europe reconsiders its energy future

欧洲重新考虑其能源未来

How to escape the bear market

如何逃离熊市

Will oil at more than $100 a barrel, gas markets in turmoil and war be enough to wean Europe off Russian gas?

油价突破每桶100美元，天然气市场陷入动荡，战争爆发，这足以让欧洲摆脱对俄罗斯天然气的依赖吗?

Mar 5th 2022

AFTER RUSSIA’S annexation of Crimea in 2014 Europe feared that Vladimir Putin would cut supplies of piped gas passing through Ukraine to European customers. That worry led Poland’s then prime minister, Donald Tusk, to issue a stark warning: “Excessive dependence on Russian energy makes Europe weak.” As a full-scale invasion of Ukraine by Mr Putin’s forces unfolds, Europe looks, if anything, weaker. Despite some efforts to diversify supply, install cross-border gas connections and build plants to import liquefied natural gas (LNG), in the decade to 2020 Russian exports of piped gas to the EU and Britain shot up by a fifth by volume, to make up roughly 38% of all that fossil fuel consumed in Europe. That year more than half of German gas came from Russia.

2014年俄罗斯吞并克里米亚后，欧洲担心普京会切断通过乌克兰向欧洲客户输送的管道天然气。这种担忧导致波兰当时的总理Donald Tusk发出了严厉的警告:“过度依赖俄罗斯能源会使欧洲变得软弱。” 随着普京军队对乌克兰的全面入侵的展开，欧洲看起来(如果有的话)更弱了。尽管欧洲做出了一些努力，实现供应的多样化, 安装跨境气体连接以及构建工厂进口液化天然气(LNG), 到2020年为止的十年俄罗斯出口到欧盟和英国的管道气体激增了五分之一, 约占欧洲化石燃料消费总量的38%。那一年，德国一半以上的天然气来自俄罗斯。

Mr Putin’s latest aggression may at last shake the old continent out of its energy complacency. On February 22nd, as Russian tanks were preparing to roll into Ukraine, Germany suspended final approval of Nord Stream 2, a controversial new gas pipeline linking it with Russia. Days later the chancellor, Olaf Scholz, vowed “to change course in order to overcome our import dependency” with more renewables, bigger domestic stores of gas and coal, and revived plans for LNG terminals. At the EU level, a wide-ranging proposal to guarantee the bloc’s “energy independence”, due to be unveiled by the European Commission on March 2nd but postponed as a result of the war, is expected to advocate strategic stocks and mandatory gas storage to deal with the Russia risk in the short term, and a dramatic expansion of renewable energy and clean technologies such as hydrogen in the long run.

普京最近的挑衅可能最终会使这个古老的大陆从能源自满中摆脱出来。2月22日，当俄罗斯坦克准备开进乌克兰时，德国暂停了对“北溪2号”天然气管道的最终批准，这条备受争议的新管道将其与俄罗斯连接起来。几天后，德国总理朔尔茨誓言要“改变路线，以克服我们对进口的依赖”，增加可再生能源，扩大国内天然气和煤炭储备，并恢复液化天然气接收站的计划。在欧盟层面，欧盟委员会将于3月2日公布一项保障欧盟“能源独立”的广泛提案，但由于战争而推迟，该提案预计将倡导战略储备和强制性天然气储存，以在短期内应对俄罗斯的风险，从长远来看，可再生能源和氢等清洁技术将大幅扩张。

That would be a giant shift in EU energy policy, which used to focus merely on ensuring that energy markets remain competitive. In the past few years, as climate became the dominant concern, the policy’s goals broadened. With the threat of Mr Putin’s weaponisation of energy looming ever larger, even the twin objectives are “not enough”, says Teresa Ribera, a Spanish deputy prime minister. The EU must now reconcile three competing objectives: cost, greenery and security.

这将是欧盟能源政策的一个巨大转变，过去欧盟能源政策的重点仅仅是确保能源市场保持竞争力。在过去的几年里，随着气候问题成为主要的关注点，能源政策的目标扩大了。西班牙副总理Teresa Ribera表示，随着普京将能源武器化的威胁越来越大，甚至这两个目标还“不够”。欧盟现在必须调和三个相互矛盾的目标: 成本、环保和安全。

Europe has made real progress on the first horn of this “energy trilemma”. Liberalisation of energy markets has helped keep prices down through competition. The continent has also got serious about decarbonisation. But if Europe is to shake off its reliance on Russian gas, sacrifices on cost and climate may be unavoidable.

欧洲在这个“能源三难困境”的第一个角上取得了真正的进展。能源市场自由化有助于通过竞争压低价格。欧洲大陆也开始认真对待脱碳问题。但是，如果欧洲要摆脱对俄罗斯天然气的依赖，在成本和气候方面的牺牲可能是不可避免的。

Start with the short term. Last month Ursula von der Leyen, the commission’s president, insisted the EU could survive this winter even with “full disruption of gas supply from Russia”. Gas storage units were emptier than usual a few months ago, owing in part to low levels in those operated by Gazprom, Russia’s state-controlled gas giant which controls 5% of the EU storage capacity. They are fuller now. High prices have lured LNG cargoes from Asia. If Mr Putin turned off the taps, prices would rocket again—attracting more LNG. European governments would squirm, then pay up for the remaining weeks of winter, after which gas consumption drops off sharply. They have also secured promises of emergency supplies from Japan, Qatar, South Korea and other allies if needed. And they could tap “cushion gas”, a layer of stores not normally meant for consumption.

先从短期开始讲。上个月，欧盟委员会主席Ursula von der Leyen坚称，即使“俄罗斯的天然气供应完全中断”，欧盟也能挺过这个冬天。几个月前，由于俄罗斯国有天然气巨头”俄气“运营的天然气储存设施中的天然气储量较低，天然气储存设施比往常更空。俄气控制着欧盟5%的储存容量。它们现在更满了。高价格吸引了来自亚洲的液化天然气货物。如果普京关闭天然气阀门，价格将再次飙升，从而吸引更多的液化天然气。欧洲各国政府会感到不安，然后为冬季剩下的几周支付费用，在此之后天然气消耗量会急剧下降。他们还获得了日本、卡塔尔、韩国和其他盟国在必要时提供紧急供应的承诺。他们还可以利用“缓冲气体”，即通常不用于消耗的储层。

Over the medium term, the outlook darkens. Nikos Tsafos of the Centre for Strategic and International Studies, a think-tank, reckons that Europe imports around 400bn cubic metres of gas a year. Replacing the 175bn-200bn it gets from Russia with a mix of alternative supplies and reduced gas consumption will be “very tough” beyond 2022, he says. Stumbling into spring with badly depleted stocks will make preparing for next winter difficult.

从中期来看，前景黯淡。智库战略与国际研究中心的Nikos Tsafos估计，欧洲每年进口大约4000亿立方米天然气。他表示，2022年以后，用多种替代供应和减少天然气消耗的方式取代从俄罗斯获得的1750亿至2000亿立方米的天然气，将“非常困难”。带着严重枯竭的库存跌跌撞撞地进入春季，将使为明年冬天做准备变得困难。

To gird itself for a possible crunch, Europe needs to stockpile Russian gas while it is still flowing (ideally over the summer, when gas prices tend to dip). It has to find alternatives to Gazprom’s molecules, lest these evaporate. It needs somewhere to keep those alternative molecules until next winter. And it must tap non-gas energy sources to use the reserves sparingly.

为了为可能的危机做好准备，欧洲需要在俄罗斯天然气还在流通的时候(最好是在夏季，因为天然气价格往往会下降)储备俄罗斯的天然气。它必须找到Gazprom分子的替代品，以免蒸发。它还需要一个地方来保存这些替代分子，直到明年冬天。此外，它还必须利用非天然气能源来节约使用天然气储备。

Easier said than done. EU law makes it hard to make Gazprom pump more gas to stockpile even in normal times, which these patently are not. European gasfields in Britain and the Netherlands are past their prime. North Africa, which typically supplies less than a third as much as Gazprom, cannot increase exports enough to offset the Russian deficit.

说起来容易做起来难。欧盟法律规定，即使在正常情况下，俄气都很难增加天然气的供应量，而这显然是不可能的。英国和荷兰的欧洲天然气田已经过了鼎盛时期。北非的天然气供应量通常不到俄气的三分之一，其出口增长不足以抵消俄罗斯的赤字。

Europe could regasify a lot more LNG than it is doing (see map)—if, that is, it could get more of the stuff. Contracted flows and limited global liquefaction capacity make that unlikely, explains Richard Howard of Aurora Energy, a research firm. LNG cargoes can be redirected from Asia at a price, but Asian customers preparing for their own winters will be eyeing them, too.

欧洲可以再气化比现在更多的液化天然气(见地图)，如果它能得到更多的液化天然气的话。研究公司Aurora Energy的Richard Howard解释说，天然气流动的收缩和全球液化能力的限制使得这一设想不太可能实现。液化天然气货物可以以一定的价格从亚洲转运过来，但准备过冬的亚洲客户也在盯着它们。

To complicate matters, much of Europe’s regasification capacity sits on its western coasts in Spain, France and Britain. Trans-border gas connections and “reverse-flow” capabilities are better than a decade ago but still lacking. Spain’s under utilised regasification plants are useless in a crisis because its gas links over the Pyrenees are puny and hard to upgrade. Getting all that gas to Germany and other big inland customers is a (literal) pipe-dream, worries a European regulator.

复杂的是，欧洲大部分的再气化产能都位于西班牙、法国和英国的西海岸。跨境天然气连接和“倒流”能力虽比十年前要好，但仍有不足。在危机中，西班牙未得到充分利用的再气化工厂毫无用处，因为其比利牛斯山脉上的天然气线路很小且难以升级。让欧洲监管机构担心的是，把所有的天然气输送到德国和其他内陆大客户简直就是在幻想。

Given these constraints on supply, European demand may need to fall by 10-15% next winter to cope with a Russian cut-off, estimates Bruegel, a think-tank in Brussels. Matthew Drinkwater of Argus Media, an industry publisher, believes that “some rationing” may be necessary.

布鲁塞尔智库Bruegel估计，考虑到这些供应上的限制，明年冬天欧洲的需求可能需要下降10-15%，以应对俄罗斯的切断供应。行业出版商Argus Media的Matthew Drinkwater认为“一些定量配给”可能是必要的。

The problems do not disappear in the longer term. Shell, a British energy giant, forecasts a gap between global supply of gas and demand for it in the mid-2020s. Europe will feel the pinch more than most because of the ways it has discouraged investment in gas. A reliance on spot markets attracts short-term supplies in a crunch but does not send a clear signal about longer time horizons. Adrian Dorsch of S&P Global Platts, a research firm, notes that despite risk for the winter after next, European utilities have done little to secure future supplies. Without government mandates or subsidies, seasonal price differentials are insufficient to justify investments in more storage, says Michael Stoppard of IHS Markit, a research firm.

从长远来看，这些问题不会消失。英国能源巨头壳牌公司预测，到2020年代中期，全球天然气供应和需求将出现缺口。欧洲将会比大多数国家感受到更大的压力，因为它阻止了对天然气的投资。对现货市场的依赖在紧缺时期吸引了短期供应，但并未发出有关较长期供应的明确信号。标普全球普氏能源咨询公司的Adrian Dorsch指出，尽管明年冬天存在风险，欧洲公用事业公司在确保未来供应方面几乎没有采取什么行动。研究公司IHS Markit的Michael Stoppard表示，如果没有政府的授权或补贴，季节性的价格差异不足以证明投资于更多天然气储存的合理性。

Europe’s green policies aren’t helping. The EU has been schizophrenic about gas. Some member states, like Germany and Ireland, accept that new gas plants are needed as back-up and a bridge to a cleaner future. Others, such as Spain, want to deny natural gas the “green” label for climate reasons. Although the EU has recently reclassified gas as a “green transition” fuel, the designation comes with lots of strings attached. The confused boss of a big American LNG exporter grumbles that no European utility will sign a long-term contract with him “because they don’t know what their governments will or won’t allow” a decade from now.

欧洲的绿色政策并没有起到作用。欧盟一直对天然气持矛盾态度。一些成员国，如德国和爱尔兰，认为新的天然气工厂是必要的，其是通向更清洁未来的桥梁。其他国家，如西班牙，则出于气候原因，想要拒绝给予天然气“绿色”标签。尽管欧盟最近已经将天然气重新归类为“绿色过渡”燃料，但这一定义带有许多附加条件。一位困惑的美国液化天然气出口巨头老板抱怨说，没有一家欧洲公用事业公司会与他签订长期合同，“因为他们不知道他们的政府在十年后会或不会允许什么”。

Various think-tankers reckon Europe can wean itself off gas almost entirely. Simon Müller of Agora estimates that wind and solar energy could generate 80% of Germany’s power in less than eight years. Lauri Myllyvirta of the Centre for Research on Energy and Clean Air thinks it is feasible on paper to replace all of Europe’s Russian gas imports, equivalent to 370 gigawatts (GW), with renewables capacity. China plans to install more than that by 2025.

许多智库认为欧洲可以几乎完全摆脱对天然气的依赖。Agora的Simon Müller估计，风能和太阳能将在不到8年的时间里为德国提供80%的电力。能源和清洁空气研究中心的Lauri Myllyvirta认为，从理论上讲，用可再生能源取代欧洲所有的俄罗斯天然气进口量（相当于370千兆瓦）是可行的。中国计划到2025年安装更多可再生能源发电机。

Such projections look too rosy. Wind and solar farms are harder to build in democratic Europe than they are in command-and-control XX. Christian Gollier of the Toulouse School of Economics points to “massive local opposition” in France to wind projects. Regional squabbles among regulators and other bureaucratic delays can stretch the approval process for Italian wind and solar installations to six years. According to S&P Global Platts, western Europe shut down 9GW of coal power and more than 5GW of nuclear power in 2021. Non-intermittent low-carbon replacements, such as battery storage and biomass, have not kept pace.

这些预测看起来过于乐观。风能和太阳能发电厂在民主的欧洲比在指挥与控制的XX更难建设。图卢兹经济学院的Christian Gollier指出，在法国，风力发电项目“遭到了当地民众的强烈反对”。地区监管机构之间的争吵和其他官僚机构的拖延可能会将意大利风能和太阳能设施的审批过程延长到6年。根据标普全球普氏能源咨询公司的数据，西欧在2021年关闭了9GW的煤电和超过5GW的核电。非间歇性的低碳替代能源，如电池存储和生物质能，并没有跟上步伐。

As with gas, EU member states talk at cross-purposes when discussing alternative energy sources. While Germany has been shutting down its nuclear fleet, France and the Netherlands want to expand theirs. By 2030 Spain will phase out coal, whereas Poland will still get more than half its power from the dirtiest fuel (and replace most decommissioned coal plants with ones burning gas). This confused approach makes it harder to reach the common goal of ditching Russian gas.

与天然气一样，欧盟成员国在讨论替代能源时也存在分歧。德国一直在关闭自己的核电站，而法国和荷兰却希望扩大自己的核电站。到2030年，西班牙将逐步淘汰煤炭，而波兰仍将有一半以上的电力来自这种污染最严重的燃料(并将大多数退役的燃煤电厂替换为燃烧天然气的电厂)。这种令人困惑的做法使得放弃俄罗斯天然气的共同目标更难实现。

Even if Europe managed to pull off the shift to renewables, it would still need gas to heat homes and businesses. Though the power sector is often in the cross-hairs, it represents less than a third of western Europe’s gas demand. Residential use accounts for some 40%. Reducing gas use in homes requires heavy investments in electric heating, better insulation and super-efficient heat pumps.

即使欧洲成功实现了向可再生能源的转变，它仍然需要天然气来为家庭和企业供暖。尽管电力行业经常受到关注，但它只占西欧天然气需求的不到三分之一。住宅使用约占40%。减少家庭燃气的使用需要在电供暖、更好的隔热材料以及超高效热泵方面进行大量投资。

Some uses, like high-temperature heat in industrial processes, cannot be easily replaced by green electricity. On one estimate, only 40% of Europe’s industrial use of gas is in low-temperature applications that can be readily electrified. Hydrogen may one day do the job, as well as powering vehicles, generating electricity or providing long-term energy storage. But even the technology’s boosters like Ms Ribera in Spain concede that the hydrogen dream will take a decade or more to realise.

有些用途，比如工业过程中的高温加热，不能轻易被绿色电力取代。据估计，欧洲工业使用的天然气中，只有40%用于易于电气化的低温应用。将来有一天，氢可能也能做到这一点，还可以为汽车提供动力、发电或提供长期的能源储存。但即使是这项技术的支持者，如西班牙的Ribera女士，也承认氢的梦想需要十年甚至更长时间才能实现。

None of this is impossible for Europe to achieve with wise policymaking and pots of money. If war on its door step doesn’t focus European minds, nothing will. ■

Mar 5th 2022

FIVE YEARS ago a little-known former economy minister won the French presidency in his first bid for elected office. Emmanuel Macron, then 39, became the country’s youngest leader since Napoleon Bonaparte. On April 10th and 24th France will hold its next two-round presidential election. Mr Macron hopes to defy precedent again by securing a second term. This would be quite a feat. For the past 20 years no French president has been re-elected. Only two, François Mitterrand and Jacques Chirac, have won a second term since Charles de Gaulle in 1965.

五年前，一位名不见经传的前经济部长在他第一次竞选公职时赢得了法国总统大选。当时39岁的马克龙成为法国自拿破仑以来最年轻的领导人。4月10日和24日，法国将举行下一届的两轮总统选举。马克龙希望再次打破先例，获得连任。这将是一个相当大的壮举。在过去的20年里，没有一位法国总统获得连任。自1965年的戴高乐以来，只有两位赢得了第二任期，即弗朗索瓦·密特朗和雅克·勒内·希拉克。

Mr Macron is nonetheless the strong favourite. Our model gives him an 88% probability of re-election, even though he has yet to hold a rally or take to the campaign trail. Mr Macron has had a lot on, softening the impact (successfully) of a surge in covid-19 cases in January and then (unsuccessfully) trying to talk Vladimir Putin out of going to war. But this is making for an odd campaign, as his rivals criss-cross France and take part in endless TV debates without the one candidate they all denounce. Assuming he declares by the legal deadline on March 4th, Mr Macron has just five weeks to lay out what he wants to do with a second term, and how he plans to do it.

尽管如此，马克龙仍然是最受欢迎的候选人。我们的模型显示，他连任的可能性为88%，尽管他还没有举行集会或参加竞选旅行。马克龙已经做了大量的工作，他缓和了1月份新冠肺炎病例激增的影响(成功)，然后试图说服普京退出战争(未成功)。但这使得他的竞选活动变得很奇怪，因为他的对手们在法国各地奔波，参加无休止的电视辩论，在电视辩论中却没有一个他们都谴责的候选人的身影。假设他在3月4日的法定截止日期前宣布参选(已于3月3日宣布竞选连任)，马克龙只有五周的时间来阐述他想要在第二任期做什么，以及他打算怎么做。

On Mr Macron’s watch the French economy has become more business-friendly, enterprising and job-creating, and less heavily taxed. Having promised to spend “whatever it costs” during the pandemic, he has kept people in jobs and firms in business. The rich have gained from his tax cuts and other policies, but so has everyone else: average net household incomes have increased across every income decile. Gay and single women can now get free IVF treatment; 300,000 school pupils, free breakfasts. Abroad, Mr Macron’s cherished idea of fostering European “strategic sovereignty” may get some impetus from Germany’s recent decision to increase spending dramatically on defence.

在马克龙的领导下，法国经济变得对企业更友好，更有进取心，更能创造就业，税收也更少。在大流行期间，他承诺“不惜一切代价”，让人们保住了工作，让企业保住了生意。富人从他的减税和其他政策中获益，但其他所有人也一样: 每个收入十分之一的家庭平均净收入都有所增加。同性恋和单身女性现在可以获得免费的体外受精治疗; 30万名学生拥有免费早餐。在国外，德国最近大幅增加国防开支的决定，可能会给马克龙推动欧洲“战略主权”的宝贵想法带来一些动力。

Yet the record is far from faultless. Mr Macron’s diplomatic efforts have been energetic, but the results mixed. He failed to coax Mr Putin to behave better, or to stop France’s counter-terrorism operation in Mali from unravelling. At home, the president’s haughty governing style has not united France: nearly half of voters plan to back a candidate from one or other political extreme, just as they did in 2017. Above all, there is unfinished business. France needs to reform pensions to persuade people to keep working for longer. It also needs to get public finances under control without harming the economy or denting incomes. It is one of the more indebted countries in the euro zone.

然而他的成绩远非完美无缺。马克龙的外交努力十分积极，但结果喜忧参半。他没能劝诱普京表现得更好，也没能阻止法国在马里的反恐行动瓦解。在国内，总统傲慢的执政风格并没有让法国团结起来:近一半的选民计划支持来自某个或其他政治极端的候选人，就像他们在2017年所做的那样。最重要的是，还有未完成的事业。法国需要改革养老金制度，以说服人们延长工作时间。它还需要在不损害经济或减少收入的情况下控制公共财政。它是欧元区负债较多的国家之一。

Such measures will require plenty of hard choices and unpopular decisions. A second-term president, forbidden constitutionally from running again immediately, could afford to be unpopular. But Mr Macron, even if he is re-elected, may lose his parliamentary majority at legislative elections in June. Moreover there is precious little time for voters to engage in a serious debate about these choices. So far the campaign has been mediocre, framed by the identity politics and toxic diatribes against immigration of the candidates on the nationalist hard right, Eric Zemmour and Marine Le Pen. If Mr Macron faces either in the run-off, his probable victory would owe as much to those who want to keep them out as those who want him in.

这些措施将需要许多艰难的选择和不受欢迎的决定。根据宪法，第二任期的总统不能立即再次参选，因此不受欢迎是可以承受的。但马克龙即使再次当选，也可能在6月的立法选举中失去议会多数席位。此外，选民很少有时间就这些选择进行严肃的辩论。迄今为止，由于民族主义极右翼候选人Eric Zemmour和Marine Le Pen的“身份政治”以及“对移民的恶毒抨击”的诽谤，这场竞选表现平平。如果马克龙在决胜选举中面临这两种情况中的任何一种，他可能的胜利既要归功于那些希望他当选的人，也要归功于那些希望他当选的人。

The horrifying spectacle of war on Europe’s eastern flank will draw attention away from the vote. Mr Macron, of all people, knows the value of transparency and clarity before an election. Climate change must be adapted to as well as opposed

既要适应气候变化，也要抵制气候变化

Climate change

气候变化

![image-20220309075621033](/Users/chenyujie/Library/Application Support/typora-user-images/image-20220309075621033.png)

Efforts to reduce its impact on lives and ecosystems are falling ever shorter

“减少其对生命和生态系统的影响的“努力越来越少

Mar 5th 2022

IN NORMAL CIRCUMSTANCES the Intergovernmental Panel on Climate Change (IPCC) can take media attention for granted. Its infrequent and authoritative analyses of how much climate change human activity is causing, and will cause, and its weighty warnings about the consequent rising seas, deepening droughts, failing crops and so forth lead front pages and news bulletins alike. This week, though, circumstances are anything but normal, and the panel found that getting the world to pay attention to a 3,600-page document describing in great detail the current and future impacts of climate change was hard.

在正常情况下，政府间气候变化专门委员会(IPCC)可以将媒体的关注视为理所当然。它对人类活动正在造成和将会造成多大程度的气候变化的罕见而权威的分析，以及随之而来的海平面上升、干旱加剧、农作物歉收等重大警告，占据了报纸的头版和新闻简报等。然而，本周的情况并不正常，专家组发现，要让全世界关注一份3600页的“非常详细地描述了气候变化目前和未来的影响的”文件是困难的。

To add to the sidelining, the fact that the Russian invasion of Ukraine is likely to bring about a profound shift in European energy policies makes the war particularly fascinating to the sort of climate wonks who are normally fixated on the utterances of the IPCC. And to pile on yet more distraction, the biggest climate story of the week could end up being the attitude which America’s Supreme Court takes to the federal government’s powers over greenhouse-gas emissions.

此外，俄罗斯入侵乌克兰可能会给欧洲能源政策带来深刻变化，这一事实使得这场战争对那些通常关注IPCC言论的气候专家来说特别有吸引力。更让人分心的是，本周最大的气候事件可能是美国最高法院对联邦政府在温室气体排放问题上的权力所持的态度。

But if the report’s launch has been overshadowed, it is important that its messages should not be—all the more so because it shows that “usual conditions” of all sorts are a thing of the past. It provides a fuller documentation than any of the IPCC’s previous studies of the profound shifts which the climate is undergoing. This is not because its authors are more diligent than their predecessors, but rather because there is more going on. Previous reports on impacts, vulnerabilities and adaptation relied on prediction. In this one the authors need only to look around them to catalogue increased flooding, more heatwaves, stressed ecosystems and millions of lives that have become harder to live.

但是，如果报告的发布“黯然失色”，那么重要的是报告传达的信息不应该“黯然失色”，尤其是因为报告表明各种“常规条件”都已成为过去。它提供了比IPCC以往任何一份“关于气候正在经历的深刻变化的”研究都更全面的文件。这并不是因为它的作者比他们的前辈更勤奋，而是因为有更多的东西正在进行。以前关于影响、脆弱性和适应的报告都是基于预测。在这篇文章中，作者们只需要环顾四周，就可以记录下不断增加的洪水、更多的热浪、受到压力的生态系统以及数百万生活变得更加艰难的人们。

The “emissions gap” that dogs climate policy is well known. Though the reductions in emissions that the world’s countries have pledged themselves to make are steeper than they used to be, they still fall well short of those needed to give the world a good chance of keeping the rise in average temperature relative to pre-industrial levels well below 2°C. The new report highlights a parallel adaptation gap. Though efforts to reduce the impact of climate change on lives and ecosystems are greater than they once were, the extent to which they fall short of what circumstances require is increasing, as impacts pile up apace.

“排放差距”(减碳承诺与科学家建议值的差距)一直困扰着气候政策，这是众所周知的。尽管世界各国承诺的减排幅度比过去更大，它们仍然远远低于使世界有机会将平均气温相对于工业化前水平的上升幅度保持在远低于2°C的水平所需的水平。这份新报告强调了一个平行的适应差距。尽管减少气候变化对生命和生态系统影响的努力比以往更大，但随着影响迅速累积，这些努力与环境要求的差距正在增加。

There are success stories, though. The use of sand dams in Kenya increases groundwater storage and thus permits the weathering of droughts. In many cities better warning of heatwaves has reduced the number of deaths they bring. Well-thought-through urban planning and building codes can be a protection against heat and flood alike.

不过，也有成功的事件。在肯尼亚使用沙坝增加了地下水的储存，从而允许干旱的风化。在许多城市，更好的热浪预警已经减少了热浪带来的死亡人数。经过深思熟虑的城市规划和建筑规范可以防止炎热和洪水。

Being overshadowed is not a new experience for those who identify the people most vulnerable to climate change and work out ways to help them—and ways to help them help themselves. The fact that the underlying driver of climate change is the total amount of carbon dioxide emitted means that reducing net emissions will always have a logical pride of place that translates into the lion’s share of attention. Without mitigation, adaptation will run out of road. As a result, adaptation gets short shrift and too little investment.

对于那些确定最易受气候变化影响的人，并找出帮助他们的方法，以及帮助他们自救的方法的人来说，“黯然失色”并不是什么新鲜事。事实上，气候变化的根本驱动因素是二氧化碳的排放总量，这意味着减少净排放量将始终具有逻辑上的优势，并转化为最大份额的关注。如果不采取缓解措施，适应将无路可走。因此，适应气候变化受到忽视，投资也太少。

The disparity is less obvious now than it was in past decades. But for as long as it persists, the adaptation gap will widen. The good news from the IPCC is that successful adaptation is still possible, for now, and it makes real improvements to people’s lives. The more this is understood, the more spending on adaptation should grow. ■

这种差距现在不像过去几十年那么明显了。但只要它继续存在，适应差距就会扩大。政府间气候变化专门委员会的好消息是，就目前而言，成功的适应气候变化仍是有可能的，而且它确实改善了人们的生活。人们对这一点了解得越多，在适应气候变化方面的支出就应该增长得越多。■

According to the German press, the American electric-car maker will get the final green light from local authorities to start operations within days. In one way, it already has. On February 28th Tesla workers elected their first works council, a group of employees that in German law co-decide with managers things like working hours, leave and training.

对于位于柏林附近格伦海德镇(勃兰登堡)的特斯拉超级工厂来说，这是重要的一周。据德国媒体报道，这家美国电动汽车制造商将获得当地政府的最终批准，在几天内开始运营。在某种程度上，它已经开始了。2月28日，特斯拉员工选出了他们的第一个工作委员会，这是由一群员工组成的，按照德国法律，他们与管理者共同决定工作时间、休假和培训等事宜。

For Elon Musk, Tesla’s anti-union chief executive, this must rankle. He has tried to shield his first German plant from Germany’s strict labour laws by incorporating the business as a Societas Europaea (se), a public company registered under eu corporate law that is exempt from some “co-determination” rules, such as the requirement for firms with more than 2,000 employees to give workers half the seats on supervisory boards. ses are not, however, exempt from having a works council.

对于特斯拉反工会的首席执行官马斯克来说，这肯定让他耿耿于怀。为了保护他在德国的第一家工厂不受德国严格的劳动法的影响，他将公司注册为“欧洲公司”，这是一家根据欧盟公司法注册的上市公司，其不受某些“共同决定”规则的限制，比如要求员工超过2000人的公司在监事会中给予员工一半的席位。然而，这些“欧洲公司”也不能免于成立工作委员会。

IG Metall, Germany’s mightiest union, which represents auto workers, has been on a collision course with Mr Musk ever since he refused to sign up to collective wage agreements for the industry (the only other firm not included is Volkswagen, which has its own generous wage deal). It has set up an office close to the gigafactory to advise Tesla workers about their rights and listen to their complaints. It has employed a Polish speaker to organise employees that Tesla is hiring across the border in Poland. It hopes that persuading enough Tesla workers to join its ranks would add oomph to its campaign to join the collective wage deal; the union says the company pays senior staff well but that production-line workers get a fifth less than those at BMW and Mercedes-Benz. Most important, it sees the works council as the first step to full co-determination.

代表汽车工人的德国最强大的工会——五金工会(IG Metall)，自从马斯克拒绝签署行业集体工资协议以来，就与他发生了冲突(唯一一家不包括在内的公司是大众汽车，该公司有自己的慷慨工资协议)。其在特斯拉超级工厂附近设立了一个办公室，向特斯拉员工提供有关其权利的建议，并听取他们的投诉。特斯拉还雇佣了一位会讲波兰语的人来组织在波兰雇佣的员工。该公司希望，说服足够多的特斯拉员工加入自己的行列，这将为其加入集体工资协议的运动增添动力; 工会表示，该公司给高级员工的薪酬很高，但生产线工人的薪酬比宝马和梅赛德斯-奔驰的员工低五分之一。最重要的是，它将工作委员会视为全面共同决策的第一步。

Mr Musk must see it differently. He may have fast-tracked the election in order to get a more sympathetic council. Tesla has so far hired only around 2,500 mostly senior and skilled workers, out of a workforce that will grow to 12,000 or so. Such employees are likelier to see eye to eye with management. The rest of Deutschland AG will be watching to see if Germany changes Tesla into something less abrasive or if Tesla changes Germany’s labour relations into something less consensual. ■

马斯克必须有不同的看法。他可能加快了选举进程，以获得一个更有同情心的委员会。到目前为止，特斯拉只雇佣了大约2500名员工，其中大部分是高级技术人员，而公司的员工总数将增加到12000人左右。这样的员工更有可能与管理层意见一致。其他德国股份公司将拭目以待，看德国是否会将特斯拉改造成一种不那么粗暴的公司，或者特斯拉是否会将德国的劳资关系转变成一种不那么一致同意的关系。■

Company or cult?

公司还是邪教？

Bartleby

巴托比

![image-20220307153233614](/Users/chenyujie/Library/Application Support/typora-user-images/image-20220307153233614.png)

The dividing line between firm and sect is often thin. How to tell them apart

公司和邪教之间的界限往往很模糊。如何区分它们

HERE ARE some common characteristics of cults. They have hierarchical structures. They prize charismatic leaders and expect loyalty. They see the world as a hostile place. They have their own jargon, rituals and beliefs. They have a sense of mission. They are stuffed with weirdos. If this sounds a bit familiar, that is because companies share so many of these traits.

以下是邪教的一些共同特征。他们有等级结构。他们看重有魅力的领导者并期望忠诚。他们视世界为充满敌意的地方。他们有自己的行话、仪式和信仰。他们有使命感。他们有很多怪人。如果这听起来有点耳熟，那是因为公司有很多这样的特点。

Some cult-companies are easier to spot than others. Their bosses are more like deities than executives. These leaders have control of the company, and almost certainly founded it. They have name recognition among the masses. They really like rockets and have a brother called Kimbal.

一些“邪教”公司比其他公司更容易被发现。他们的老板更像是神而不是高管。这些领导人控制着公司，而且几乎肯定是公司的创始人。他们在群众中有知名度。他们真的很喜欢火箭，还有个兄弟叫Kimbal[这不就是在说马斯克嘛，马斯克他弟叫 Kimbal Musk]。

But in other cases it can be hard to tell where a company ends and a cult begins. That is true even of employees. So here is a handy guide to help you work out whether you are in a normal workplace or have fallen into the clutches of an even stranger group.

但在其他情况下，很难区分一家公司的结束和一个“邪教”的开始。即使是员工也是如此。因此，这里有一份简便的指南，可以帮助你弄清楚你是在一个正常的工作场所，还是落入了一个更陌生的群体的魔掌之中。

Workforce nicknames. It is not enough to be an employee of a company any more. From Googlers and Microsofties to Pinployees and Bainies, workforce nicknames are meant to create a sense of shared identity. If you belong to one of these tribes and use its nickname without dying a little inside, you may be losing your grasp of reality. If you work in the finance team and are known as one of the Apostles of the Thrice-Tabbed Spreadsheet, you already have.

员工昵称。仅仅成为一家公司的雇员是不够的。从Googlers(谷歌员工昵称)和Microsofties(微软员工昵称)，到Pinployees(Pinterest的员工昵称)和Bainies(Bain员工昵称)，员工昵称意在创造一种共享身份的感觉。如果你属于这些群体中的一员，并使用它的绰号，而内心却一点也没有死去，你可能正在失去你对现实的把握。如果你在财务团队工作，并被称为“Thrice-Tabbed表格的门徒”之一，那么你已经失去了。

Corporate symbols. Uniforms are defensible in some circumstances: firefighters, referees, the pope. And so is some corporate merchandise: an umbrella, a mug, a diary. But it can easily go too far. Warning signs include pulling on a company-branded hoodie at the weekend or ever wearing a lapel pin that proclaims your allegiance to a firm. If your employer’s corporate swag includes an amulet or any kind of hat, that is also somewhat concerning.

公司的象征。在某些情况下，制服是可合乎情理的: 消防队员、裁判、教皇。一些企业商品也是如此: 雨伞、杯子、日记本。但它很容易走得太远。警告标志包括周末穿标有公司品牌的连帽衫，或在衣领上佩戴表明你对公司忠诚的别针。如果你的雇主的公司赠品中包含一个护身符或任何一种帽子，这也有点令人担忧。

Surveillance. It is reasonable for executives to want to know what their workers are up to. But it is not reasonable to track their every move. Monitoring software that takes screenshots of employees’ computer screens, reports which apps people are using or squeals on them if a cursor has not moved for a while are tools of mind control, not management.

监视。高管们想知道他们的员工在做什么是合理的。但追踪他们的一举一动是不合理的。监控软件可以对员工的电脑屏幕进行截屏，报告人们正在使用哪些应用程序，或者如果光标一段时间没有移动，就会对他们发出警告。这些都是精神控制工具，而不是管理工具。

Rituals. Rites are a source of comfort and meaning in settings from sport to religion. The workplace is no exception. Plenty of companies hand out badges and awards to favoured employees. Project managers refer to some meetings as “ceremonies”. IBM used to have its own songbook (“Our reputation sparkles like a gem” was one of the rhymes; “Why the hell do we have this bloody anthem?” was not). Walmart still encourages workers in its supermarkets to bellow a company cheer to start the day. Some of this is merely cringeworthy. But if you are regularly chanting, banging a gong or working with wicker, it becomes sinister.

仪式。从体育到宗教，仪式都是舒适和意义的源泉。工作场所也不例外。许多公司会向自己青睐的员工发放徽章和奖励。项目经理将一些会议称为“仪式”。IBM曾经有自己的歌曲集(其中一首是“我们的声誉像宝石一样闪耀”;“我们为什么要有这首该死的颂歌?”则不再其中)。沃尔玛仍然鼓励其超市的员工在开始一天的工作时大声欢呼。其中一些只是令人尴尬。但如果你经常吟诵、敲锣或用柳条工作，就会变得险恶。

Doctrines. More and more firms espouse a higher purpose, and many write down their guiding principles. Mark Zuckerberg recently updated his company’s “cultural operating system”—which, among other things, urges Metamates (see “Workforce nicknames”) to defy physics and “Live In The Future”. Amazon drums its 16 leadership principles (“Customer Obsession”, “Think Big”, “Are Right, A Lot”, and so on) into employees and job candidates alike. Corporate culture matters, but common sense doesn’t become a belief system just because capital letters are being used. If values are treated like scripture, you are in cult territory.

教义。越来越多的公司追求更高的目标，许多公司都把指导原则写下来。马克·扎克伯格最近更新了他公司的“文化操作系统”，其中包括敦促Metamates(见“职场昵称”)蔑视物理学和“活在未来”。亚马逊把它的16条领导原则(“顾客至上”、“胸怀大志”、“决策正确”等)灌输给员工和求职者。企业文化很重要，但常识不会因为使用了大写字母就变成一种信仰体系。如果价值观被当作圣经来对待，那你就是在邪教的范围内了。

Family. Some companies entreat employees to think of their organisation as a family. The f-word may sound appealing. Who doesn’t want to be accepted for who they are, warts and all? But at best it is untrue: firms ought to pay you for your time and kick you out if you are useless. At worst, it is a red flag. Research conducted in 2019 into the motivations of whistle-blowers found that loyalty to an organisation was associated with people failing to report unethical behaviour. And the defining characteristic of families is that you never leave.

家庭。一些公司恳请员工把他们的组织看作一个家庭。“f”这个词可能听起来很有吸引力。有谁不想被不加掩饰地接受他们本来的样子? 但这充其量是不正确的:公司应该为你的时间付钱，如果你没用，就把你踢出去。在最坏的情况下，这是一个危险信号。2019年对举报人动机进行的研究发现，对组织的忠诚与未能举报不道德行为有关。而家庭的典型特征就是你永远不会离开。

If none of the above resonates, rest easy: you are not in a cult. But you are unemployed. If you recognise your own situation in up to three items on this list, you are in an ordinary workplace. If you tick four or five boxes, you should worry but not yet panic; you may just be working in technology or with Americans, and losing your sense of self may be worth it for the stock options. If you recognise yourself in all six items, you need to plan an escape and then write a memoir.

如果以上这些都没有引起共鸣，那就放心吧:你不是在邪教。但你失业了。如果你发现自己的情况符合这份清单上最多三条，那你就是在一个普通的工作场所。如果你勾选了4到5个选项，你应该担心，但不要恐慌;你可能只是在技术行业工作，或者和美国人一起工作，为了股票期权，失去自我意识可能是值得的。如果你符合这六项，你需要计划一次“逃脱”，然后写一本回忆录。
