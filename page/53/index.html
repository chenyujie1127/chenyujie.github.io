A new prosthetic hand

新的假手

Prosthetics

假体

![image-20210823095952764](/Users/chenyujie/Library/Application Support/typora-user-images/image-20210823095952764.png)

It is cheaper and better than anything now available

它比现有的任何产品都更便宜、更好

PROSTHETIC LIMBS have been around for a long time. The oldest known, a piece of wood carved and painted to replace the lost toe of an Egyptian noblewoman, dates back more than 3,000 years. But prosthetics which behave like the real thing as well as looking like it are still very much a work in progress. And a group at Shanghai Jiao Tong University, in China, have just come up with what looks to be a significant advance—an affordable prosthetic hand that not only responds like a real one to signals from the wearer’s brain, but is also able to signal back to the brain what it is touching and doing.

义肢已经存在很长时间了。已知的最古老的义肢可以追溯到3000多年前，当时是用一块经过雕刻和涂漆的木头来替代一位埃及贵妇人失去的脚趾。但是要让义肢像真肢一样运作并且看起来也像真肢，这仍在很大程度上处于研究阶段。中国上海交大的一个团队, 刚提出了一个重要的进展，即一个负担得起的义肢，它不仅能像真肢一样响应佩戴者大脑发出的信号，而且还能向大脑发出信号，表明它触摸了什么以及做了什么。

Gu Guoying and his colleagues describe their invention in Nature Biomedical Engineering. Its fingers are made of rigid tubes connected by soft joints. These are similarly connected to a 3D-printed plastic palm. The whole is covered with a flexible elastomer layer to mimic skin and is attached to the user’s residual limb via a customised plastic socket. In contrast to current models, which are electrically powered, Dr Gu’s hand is powered pneumatically by a pump held in a waist bag, with the connecting air lines running under the user’s clothes alongside communication cables. This reduces its weight below 300 grams—half that of some current models, and less, indeed, than the weight of a real hand—though the waist bag adds a further 444 grams.

pneumatically 通过气压

谷国迎教授和他的同事介绍了他们发表在《Nature Biomedical Engineering》上的发明。其手指由刚性管制成，通过软接头连接。它们被连接到形状像3D打印的塑料手掌。整个假肢上覆盖着一层柔软的弹性体（共聚酯）来模拟皮肤，并通过定制的塑料插座连接到用户的残肢上。与目前的电动机控制的手不同，谷博士制造的手是由一个装在腰包里的泵通过气压驱动的，与之连接的气管和通信电线在用户衣服下面一起运行。这使它的重量减少到300克以下，只有目前一些模型的一半，甚至比一只真正的手还轻，不过腰围增加了444克。

The hand uses similar signal-processing algorithms to other prosthetics on the market. The big advance is that it does not require invasive surgery or electronic implants into the residual limb to communicate with the user’s brain. Sensors on the skin record electrical activity from the remaining arm muscles. In an intact arm, this activity would tell those muscles how to operate the hand. Instead, they are interpreted by pattern-recognition software that sends appropriate commands to the pump to move the artificial hand in the same way. Meanwhile, other signals travel in the opposite direction from sensors in the hand’s fingertips to nerves in the arm, whence they are relayed to the brain and provide a sensation of touch. The upshot is something which responds like a hand and feels like one to the user.

whence 由此

这种假肢使用的信号处理算法与市场上其他假肢类似。这项技术的一大进步在于，它不需要开刀手术，也不需要在残肢上植入电子设备，就可以与使用者的大脑进行交流。皮肤上的传感器记录手臂剩余肌肉的电活动。在一个完好无缺的手臂上，这种电活动会告诉那些肌肉如何操作手。相反，它们的假肢是由模式识别软件解释的，该软件向泵发送适当的命令，以同样的方式移动智能义肢。与此同时，其他信号则以相反的方向从指尖上的传感器传递到手臂上的神经，再从那里传递到大脑并提供触觉。其结果是，对于用户来讲，它可以像一只真手一样反应，并且像一只真手一样感觉。

Dr Gu and his colleagues compared the efficacy of their invention with that of existing models using tests borrowed from research into strokes and spinal-cord injuries. These included writing, grasping and lifting objects, lifting food to the mouth, and stacking draughtsmen. Normally, they found, it worked better—particularly for delicate tasks like handling fragile objects, petting a cat and shaking hands.

spinal-cord 脊髓

谷博士和他的同事利用从中风和脊髓损伤研究中借鉴的试验，将他们的发明与现有模型的功效进行了比较。这包括书写，抓取和举起物品，把食物送到嘴里，和堆叠国际跳棋棋子（这里描述可能不准确，看论文中的吐倒像是那种搭积木）。他们发现，通常情况下，它的效果更好，尤其是在处理易碎物品、抚摸猫和握手等需要小心处理的任务时。

The other advantage of Dr Gu’s invention is that it is cheap. The components it is made from cost about $500. Existing models may sell for $10,000 or more. If it, or something similar, goes into production, that will permit the transformation of many more of the lives of the 5m people who have lost a hand, or were born without one, than is possible at the moment.

谷博士的发明的另一个优势是价格低廉。制造它的部件成本约为500美元。现有模型的售价可能在1万美元以上。如果它或类似的东西投入生产，将会改变500万人的生活，他们失去一只手或生来就没有一只手，这在目前是不可能的。

How American retailers have adapted to the Amazon effect

美国零售商如何适应亚马逊效应

Retail revival

零售业复苏

![image-20210822102147931](/Users/chenyujie/Library/Application Support/typora-user-images/image-20210822102147931.png)

The e-commerce marketplace is more dynamic than regulators realise

电商市场比监管机构意识到的更有活力

Aug 21st 2021

AFTER REELING from the shock of the pandemic, America’s consumers came roaring back early this year, fuelled by vaccines, stimulus cheques and their instinctive bullishness. Now their enthusiasm is starting to ebb. Retail sales in July were 1.1% lower than a month earlier and a consumer-confidence survey by the University of Michigan suggests that shoppers lost more of their swagger in early August. The Delta variant has played on their nerves while price spikes and supply-chain glitches have dulled enthusiasm for buying some products such as cars—sales of which dropped by 3.9% last month, compared with June. There is now a sense that the rate of growth in consumer spending is returning to a more pedestrian pace after 18 giddy months of wild shrinkages and splurges.

dull 减轻; 减少

giddy 令人陶醉的；令人飘飘然的

pedestrian 乏味的

受到疫情的冲击而不知所措后，美国消费者在疫苗接种、刺激支票和他们本能的乐观情绪的推动下，在今年早些时候重振起来。现在他们的热情开始消退。7月份的零售额比前一个月下降了1.1%，密歇根大学的一项消费者信心调查显示，消费者在8月初失去了更多的自信。当价格飙升和供应链故障降低了人们购买某些产品的热情时（比如汽车，汽车上个月销量比6月份下降了3.9%），Delta 的病毒变种让他们变得紧张不安。现在倒感觉在经历了18个月的疯狂萎缩和挥霍之后，消费支出的增长速度正在恢复到更加缓慢的速度。

Yet even as normality beckons it is ever clearer that the pattern of spending has been transformed. One change is well-known: a lift in the level of e-commerce**. The other is less familiar.** An industry that was supposed to have been annihilated by Amazon has bounced back.

annihilate 彻底击败；摧毁

bounce back 重整旗鼓

然而，就在经济恢复正常的同时，消费模式已经发生了转变，这一点也越来越明显。一种变化就是众所周知的:电商水平的提升；另一种变化则没那么常见。一个本应被亚马逊摧毁的行业已经恢复元气。

In 2017-19 all the talk was of a “retail apocalypse” and “retailmaggedon”. The fear was that a steady rise in e-commerce and Amazon’s relentless expansion into new products would drive traditional retailers towards extinction, just as Kodak failed to adapt to the digital-photography revolution and eventually went bust. When Sears, which led the rise of suburban shopping culture after the second world war, declared bankruptcy in 2018 it seemed possible that many more large retailers would struggle to avoid the same fate. The spectre was of a pile of shopping-mall rubble, 16m lost retail jobs and a mountain of useless inventory, loomed over by a dominant Amazon and the grin of Jeff Bezos.

apocalypse 世界大末日；灾变

armageddon n. 大决战；世界末日善恶决战的战场（源于《圣经》）

spectre [似幽灵般] 缠绕内心的恐惧

2017-19年，所有人都在谈论“零售末日”和“零售大决战”。人们担心，电商的稳步增长和亚马逊在新产品领域的无情扩张，会让传统零售商走向灭亡，就像柯达未能适应数码摄影革命，最终走向破产一样。西尔斯（电商平台）在二战后引领了郊区购物文化的兴起。当它在2018年宣布破产时，似乎有可能会有更多的大型零售商努力避免同样的命运。恐惧的是一堆购物中心废墟，1600万人失去了零售工作，以及堆积如山的无用库存，由占主导地位的亚马逊和杰夫·贝佐斯的笑容笼罩着。

Things have turned out rather differently. The pandemic has certainly sped up the shift towards e-commerce sales, which have risen from 14% of the total in 2018 to 20% this year according to JPMorgan Chase, a bank. Although the pace of growth has slowed in the past few months there will be no return to the past.

结果却大不相同。疫情无疑加快了向电商销售的转变，根据银行摩根大通的数据显示，电商销售占总销售额的比例从2018年的14%上升到今年的20%。尽管过去几个月增长速度有所放缓，但也不会再回到过去。

Meanwhile the industry’s structure is starting to look different. Amazon has thrived: its market share of e-commerce stands at about 40% overall and is far higher than that in some categories, such as books. Shopping centres have struggled to attract the same numbers of visitors as before, and some have defaulted on their debt. Nonetheless, the health of the non-Amazon retail industry looks better than it once did. At $2.5trn, for example, the market value of American listed retailers is 88% higher than at the start of 2018, while their total net debt burden has been easing since the end of 2019. The number of people employed in the retail trade is only 4% below its post-war peak in 2017.

与此同时，该行业的结构开始出现变化。亚马逊蓬勃发展:其电商市场份额约为40%，远高于某些类别的市场份额，如图书。购物中心一直在努力吸引和以前一样多的游客，有些甚至拖欠了债务。尽管如此，非亚马逊零售行业的状况看起来比过去要好。例如，美国上市零售商的市值为2.5万亿美元，比2018年初高出88%，而它们的总净债务负担自2019年底以来一直在减轻。零售业的就业人数仅比2017年的战后峰值低4%。

Behind these numbers there are three types of fightback. First, the biggest retailers have embraced the digital world. This week Walmart predicted that its global e-commerce revenues would reach $75bn for the full year (about 13% of the firm’s total sales). It has made a big push in hybrid types of shopping that involve online activity but harness its stores, such as “click-and-collect” and online memberships. Target has promoted a similar service and digital sales now make up almost a fifth of its total.

在这些数字背后有三种不同的反击。首先，最大的零售商已经拥抱了数字世界。本周沃尔玛预测其全年全球电商收入将达到750亿美元(约占公司总销售额的13%)。它在混合类型的购物方面做出了巨大的努力，这种购物方式包括线上活动，但还利用了它的商店，如“网订店取”和在线会员资格。塔吉特也推出了类似的服务，数字销售目前占其总销售额的近五分之一。

The second fightback is from digital-only alternatives to Amazon. Although the veteran marketplace eBay has struggled over the years, Shopify, which helps merchants sell online and fulfil orders, has seen its share of American online sales reach 9% and its market value soar to $188bn. Many other digital firms are operating in lucrative niches, from Instacart in grocery delivery to Etsy in interactive shopping for artisanal goods.

第二波反击来自于对亚马逊的纯数字替代品。尽管经验丰富的eBay多年来一直在苦苦挣扎，但帮助商家在线销售和完成订单的Shopify却在美国在线销售中占据了9%的份额，其市值飙升至1880亿美元。其他许多数字公司都在有利可图的利基市场经营，从食品杂货配送的Instacart到手工商品互动购物的Etsy。

Finally, some brands are taking control of distribution. Nike stopped selling directly on Amazon in 2019 and instead reaches consumers through its own apps and platforms. Its digital sales rose by two-thirds in the year to May, to 20% of the total.

最后，一些品牌开始控制分销。2019年，耐克停止在亚马逊上直接销售产品，而是通过自己的APP和平台与消费者接触。截至今年5月，其数字销售增长了三分之二，占总销售额的20%。

The retail drama has several lessons. For firms in other industries facing digital disruption the key is to experiment and invest. Before Walmart got back its groove it had innumerable false starts and it has raised its capital spending by 40%. Antitrust regulators need to stay more up to date. Even as they stampede to regulate big tech there is mounting evidence that the e-commerce marketplace is more dynamic than they realise.

groove 常规

零售业的这出戏有几个教训。对于面临数字颠覆的其他行业的公司来说，关键是进行试验和投资。在沃尔玛恢复其最佳状态之前，它有无数次失败的开始，它已经将资本支出提高了40%。反垄断监管机构需要更多地了解最新情况。就在它们争先恐后地监管大型科技公司之际，越来越多的证据表明，电商市场比它们意识到的更有活力。

Apocalypse averted

避开“世界末日”

The wave of experimentation will probably continue. New payment apps and social-media firms with hordes of customers are expanding into e-commerce, and retailers are shifting into online advertising and entertainment. For America’s indefatigable consumers, and for its workers, the good news is that competition—combined with an almighty shock—have led to a more innovative industry, rather than the end of the world. ■

实验的浪潮可能还会继续。新的支付APP和拥有大量客户的社交媒体公司正在向电子商务领域扩张，零售商正转向在线广告和娱乐领域。对于美国不知疲倦的消费者和工人来说，好消息是竞争，加上巨大的冲击，催生了一个更具创新性的产业，而不是世界末日。■

Regardless of how covid-19 spread, we need to better prevent lab leaks

不管新冠如何传播，我们都需要更好地防止实验室泄漏

No more leaks

不再泄漏

![image-20210821125209573](/Users/chenyujie/Library/Application Support/typora-user-images/image-20210821125209573.png)

Even a small reduction in the risk of another pandemic is worth the price

即使应对另一场大流行病风险只是小幅降低，这样的代价也值得付出

Aug 21st 2021

TO PREVENT THE next pandemic, it would help to know how this one started. However, the answer to that question might embarrass XX’s rulers. So they have obstructed investigations into how the virus emerged, and promoted far-fetched theories about it having originated somewhere else. The world thus has only a shaky understanding of what happened in XX province in the second half of 2019.

far-fetched 牵强的

shaky 靠不住的

为了预防下一次大流行病，了解这次疫情是如何开始的将会有所帮助。然而，这个问题的答案可能会让XX的领导人感到尴尬。所以，他们阻碍了对于“病毒是如何出现”的调查，并提出了一些牵强附会的理论，他们认为病毒起源于其他地方。因此，世界对2019年下半年XX省发生的事情不怎么了解。

American spooks are about to finish a 90-day review of covid-19’s possible origins, on President Joe Biden’s orders**. It is unlikely to reach a firm conclusion. Yet it is clear that** the answer XX least wants to hear—that the virus could have leaked from a lab in XX, or been picked up by a researcher collecting samples in the field—is plausible. And it would not be the first time this has happened. The flu pandemic of 1977, which killed 700,000 people, is now believed to have begun with a lab leak in north-east Asia—possibly in XX, possibly in the Soviet Union. With new laboratories being built all the time to handle dangerous pathogens, and with the era of synthetic biology just around the corner, the task of tightening lab biosafety has become urgent.

spook 间谍

(just) round or around the corner (around the bend) 即将来临

biosafety 生物安全性

根据拜登总统的命令，美国情报人员即将完成对covid-19可能来源的90天审查。但不太可能得到一个明确的结论。但很明显，XX最不想听到的答案貌似很合理，即病毒可能是从XX的一个实验室泄露出来的，或者病毒是由在该领域收集样本的研究人员发现的。这也不是第一次发生这样的事情了。1977年造成70万人死亡的流感病毒，现在被认为是由东北亚的实验室泄漏开始的，可能在XX，也可能在苏联。随着处理危险病原体的新实验室不断建立，随着合成生物学时代的到来，加强实验室生物安全的任务变得迫在眉睫。

People who work with deadly pathogens do so with scandalously little transparency or supervision. Rules in some places are not legally binding or subject to independent oversight. That has to change. The world needs to know who is working with which pathogens, where, and under what sort of safety protocols. (Intelligence agencies do not see this as part of their remit, but they should.) International standards are needed, and a global body to advise on the most dangerous kinds of work. This will be hard to accomplish, but there is a precedent. A committee of the World Health Organisation (WHO) monitors and inspects the two labs that hold smallpox, a deadly virus that was eradicated in the wild through vaccination, and reviews proposed research.

scandalously 骇人听闻地

remit 职权范围

那些与致命病原体打交道的人几乎都在极度缺乏透明度或监管的情况下工作。有些地方的规定不具有法律约束力，也不受独立监督。这种情况必须改变。全世界都需要知道谁在用哪种病原体，在哪里用以及在什么样的安全协议下工作。(情报机构不认为这是他们的职责范围，但他们应该这么做。)国际标准是必要的，而且还需要有一个全球性的机构对于最危险的工作提出建议。这将很难实现，但已有先例。世界卫生组织的一个委员会监测和检查了两个存放天花病毒（一种致命病毒，通过接种疫苗可以在野生环境里被消灭）的实验室，并审查了拟议的研究。

America, which could be leading the way, is not. Researchers who receive federal grants to study pathogens have to follow federal rules. Private labs are not so constrained. This will not do. Ideally there would be an agreed set of basic rules, enforced by a watchdog that is independent of the bodies that fund research. The backers of a project cannot be neutral arbiters of its safety.

美国本可以在这方面发挥带头作用，但现在却不是。接受联邦资助研究病原体的研究人员必须遵守联邦规定。私人实验室则没有这么多限制。这是不行的。理想情况下，应该要有一套商定的基本规则，由一个独立于资助研究的机构的监督机构来执行。一个项目的支持者不能对其安全性做中立的仲裁者。

Many other countries follow America’s lead on biosafety. So if it improves its rules and procedures, others will, too. That could be a step towards a proper set of global norms. And it would be in every country’s interest, even XX’s, to abide by such norms. Meetings this year to discuss a pandemic treaty offer a chance to harmonise rules on lab biosafety. Organisations such as the WHO, the G7 and the OECD could also do more to promote transparency and security.

许多其他国家也在生物安全方面效仿美国。因此，如果美国改善其规则和程序，其他国家也会这样做。这可能是朝着一套适当的全球规范迈出的一步。遵守这些规范符合每个国家的利益，甚至是XX的利益。今年讨论大流行病条约的会议给协调实验室生物安全规则提供了一个机会。世界卫生组织、七国集团以及经合组织等组织也可以在促进透明度和安全方面做得更多。

Governments need to ask whether certain kinds of research are too risky to be allowed. Gain-of-function research, which genetically alters organisms such as viruses in ways that can make them more dangerous, deserves special scrutiny. So does viral collection in the wild.

政府需要询问某些类型的研究是否因为风险太大而不能被允许。功能获得性研究是一种从基因上改变生物体(如病毒)，使其变得更危险的研究，它需要特别审查。野外的病毒收集也是如此。

New tools are needed as well as new rules. Real-time genomic surveillance at border crossings could catch novel pathogens as they emerge. Sewage and air could be sampled more widely. Ways to create vaccines even faster should be explored.

我们需要新的工具和规则。在边境口岸进行的实时基因组监测可以在新病原体出现时捕捉到它们。可以更广泛地对污水和空气取样。应该探索研制疫苗的更快地方法。

And if some scientists complain that all this will make their work costlier and harder, there is an obvious rejoinder. Perhaps 15m people have died directly of covid-19, and the resulting economic damage has impoverished many millions more. If better biosafety reduces the chance of another pandemic even slightly, it will be cheap at the price. ■

2006年，著名的大气化学家Paul Crutzen提出，也许有一种方法可以在保持凉快的同时，还能拯救人们的肺部。在工业排放的低层大气中，硫酸盐颗粒的寿命很短。在平流层，它们持续的时间要长得多。因此，在平流层中加入一层相对稀疏的硫酸盐，可以提供与今天低层大气烟雾相同的降温效果，而几乎不会造成直接伤害。

Crutzen’s intervention spurred renewed interest in the idea of intentionally interfering with incoming sunlight, a possibility known as “solar geoengineering”. It is an interest of which the IPCC is wary. Solar geoengineering has no role in the scenarios the panel uses to talk about future climates. As a result its new report, like its predecessors, discusses the idea only in broad terms. In doing so it notes, correctly, that the idea has significant drawbacks. But it also allows that solargeoengineering might, in principle, be used to create significant global cooling much more promptly than reducing carbon-dioxide emissions ever could.

promptly 及时地

背景：太阳能地球工程通过反射太阳光降低气温，最常用的方法是通过释放到平流层的气溶胶粒子将太阳辐射反射回太空。科学家指出， 这些气溶胶一旦释放，就会在地球周围形成一层保护层，反射阳光，从而冷却气候。

Crutzen的干预措施重新激发了人们对有意干扰阳光入射的想法的兴趣，这种想法被称为“太阳能地球工程”。IPCC对此十分关注。太阳能地球工程在该委员会用来讨论未来气候的设想中没有作用。因此，它的新报告和之前的报告一样，只是笼统地讨论了这一观点。在这样做的过程中，它正确地指出了这个想法的重大缺陷。但原则上来讲，这也使得太阳能地球工程可以比减少二氧化碳排放更迅速地产生显著的全球降温效应。

Both the drawbacks and the potential provide good reason for researching geoengineering more thoroughly and systematically. But it would be wrong to think it a quick, easy or necessarily desirable response. Perhaps the biggest worry is that its seeming promise would be used as an excuse for inaction on carbon-dioxide emissions. A world where geoengineering was used simply to cover up, increasingly imperfectly, the effects of ever higher greenhouse-gas levels would be a fearsomely unstable one.

这些缺点和潜力都为更全面、更系统地研究地球工程提供了良好的理由。但如果认为这是一种快速、简单或必然令人满意的回应，那就错了。也许最大的担忧是，它表面上的承诺可能会被用作不采取二氧化碳排放行动的借口。如果地球工程仅仅被用来掩盖日益不不好地温室气体排放，那么这个世界将成为一个可怕不稳定的世界。

A more palatable approach to offsetting the diminishing effects of sulphate pollution, enthusiastically endorsed by the IPCC, is to redouble efforts to reduce emissions of another climate-changing by-product of human civilisation. Methane is a more powerful greenhouse gas than carbon dioxide, but one which lasts in the atmosphere for only about a decade. Reduce methane emissions and you soon reduce methane levels; reduce methane levels and you reduce global warming.

为了抵消硫酸盐污染的影响，一种更容易接受的方法是加倍努力减少人类文明造成的另一种气候变化副产品的排放，该方法得到了IPCC的大力支持。甲烷是一种比二氧化碳更强的温室气体，但它在大气中只能存在大约10年。减少甲烷的排放，你很快就会减少甲烷的水平;减少甲烷水平就能减缓全球变暖。

Its rising atmospheric concentration shows that today’s efforts to abate methane emissions are not up to the job. Happily, there is much more that can be done. Emissions from the energy industry could be more tightly regulated almost everywhere. Because methane is valuable, some would pay for themselves. Reducing emissions from landfill sites is not terribly difficult either. Livestock would burp less with the right feed supplements. Eliminating emissions would be hard; but quite steep reductions are entirely possible.

burp 打嗝

甲烷在大气中的浓度不断上升表明，目前为减少甲烷排放所做的努力还不够。令人高兴的是，还有很多事情可以做。几乎在所有地方，能源行业的排放都应该受到更严格的监管。因为甲烷是有价值的，一些人可以自己支付购买。减少垃圾填埋场的排放也不是非常困难。通过适当的饲料补充，牲畜打嗝会减少。消除排放将是困难的;但大幅削减是完全可能的。

Carbon dioxide remains the heart of the climate problem. Exploring the possibilities, practicalities and dangers of solargeoengineering remains a good idea. But it is on methane emissions that progress can be made most quickly. And the world is getting ever hotter. Methane should be given priority on the agenda at the COP26 climate summit this November.

二氧化碳仍然是气候问题的核心。探索太阳能地球工程的可能性、实用性和危险性仍然是一个好的想法。但在甲烷排放方面，进展最快。世界正变得越来越热。在今年11月举行的COP26气候峰会上，甲烷应该被列为优先事项。

Open up

开放

Most covid-19 travel restrictions should be scrapped

scrap 废除; 取消

应该取消大多数covid-19旅行限制

The rules are ineffective, illiberal and often useless

illiberal 狭隘的；不开明的；专制的

这些规则是无效的、狭隘的，而且往往是无用的

Aug 14th 2021

FOR A LUCKY few, the mid-20th century was a golden age of air travel: there was plenty of room and the cabin crew were attentive. Back then foreign trips were glamorous and mass tourism was unknown. Sound familiar?

glamorous 令人向往的

对于少数幸运的人来说，20世纪中期是航空旅行的黄金时代:飞机上有充足的空间，机组人员也很贴心。在那个时候，出国旅行是令人向往的，大规模旅游也尚未流行。听起来是不是很熟悉?

Because of covid-19, foreign travel is once again the preserve of a happy few. International tourist arrivals are down by 85% from pre-pandemic days. Nearly a third of the world’s borders remain closed. Many of the remainder are open only to those who have been vaccinated or can afford tests. For those who dream of a return to the old days, this might sound appealing. For the rest of humanity, it is a scourge.

scourge 祸害

由于新冠肺炎疫情的影响，出国旅行再次成为少数乐天派的专利。国际游客人数较疫情前下降了85%。世界上近三分之一的边境仍处于关闭状态。那些开放边境的国家大多只对接种过疫苗或能负担得起检测的人开放。对于那些梦想回到过去的人来说，这可能听起来很有吸引力。对其他人来说，这是一场灾难。

Before the pandemic, travel accounted for 4.4% of GDP and nearly 7% of employment in rich countries. In tourist hotspots such as Thailand and the Caribbean the share was much higher. Business travellers helped their employers conquer new markets, while simultaneously creating jobs for concierges and cabbies. Foreign students subsidised their native-born classmates, brought a different perspective to campus and took new ideas back to their homelands. Last year some 280m people lived outside the country of their birth. Border closures often made it impossible for them to visit their loved ones. Some bade farewell to dying parents over WhatsApp.

concierges (尤指法国公寓等处的)看门人，司阍; (宾馆中负责提供信息、订票等的)服务台职员

bid sb farewell/good morning/welcome 向某人道别/问早安/表示欢迎

疫情爆发前，旅行占发达国家GDP的4.4%，就业占近7%。在泰国和加勒比海等旅游热点地区，这一比例要高得多。商务旅行者帮助他们的雇主拿下了新的市场，同时也为门卫和出租车司机创造了就业机会。外国学生“资助”了他们的当地同学，给校园带来了不同的“观点”，并把新的想法带回了祖国。去年大约有2.8亿人居住在他们出生的国家以外。边境关闭常常使他们无法看望亲人。一些人通过WhatsApp向垂死的父母告别。

Today’s travel restrictions are supposed to protect natives from imported covid. Yet they do a poor job of it. A few countries, mostly islands and dictatorships, have managed to keep out the virus through truly draconian restrictions. Even this has come at a cost in terms of reducing pressure to be vaccinated quickly. Only 21% of New Zealanders over 12 are fully vaccinated, for example, compared with 68% of Britons. Countries that put their faith in isolation are thus finding it hard to reopen.

draconian 严苛的

put one’s faith in sth; 对某事物抱有信心

今天的旅行限制是为了保护本国人免受境外输入病毒的影响。然而，他们做得很糟糕。一些国家（主要是岛屿和独裁国家）通过真正严厉的限制措施，成功地将病毒拒之门外。虽然如此，他们也要付出代价，以减轻快速接种疫苗的压力。例如，12岁以上的新西兰人中只有21%完全接种了疫苗，而英国则有68%。因此，那些对隔离抱有信心的国家发现很难重新开放。

Most countries have land borders and voters. For them isolation was never feasible. Instead, they have adopted a confusing, illogical mess of rules. America bars travellers from Britain and the European Union, its closest allies and trade partners, and also two of the most vaccinated big places in the world, while admitting those from South-East Asia, where the Delta variant is rampant. Thailand bans entry from some countries and requires all other travellers to submit to a two-week quarantine. Yet of 21,038 cases identified on August 10th, only 19 were imported. Once a variant of the virus has started to spread in the local population, infections double every couple of weeks. Entry bans make very little difference to the total caseload.

大多数国家都有陆地边界和选民。对他们来说，隔离是永远不可能的。相反，他们采用了一套令人困惑、不合逻辑的混乱规则。美国禁止来自英国和欧盟的游客入境（这两个国家是美国最亲密的盟友以及贸易伙伴，也是世界上接种疫苗最多的两个地区），但却允许来自东南亚的游客入境（东南亚 Delta变种非常猖獗）。泰国禁止来自一些国家的旅客入境，并要求所有其他旅客接受两周的隔离。然而，在8月10日确诊的21038例病例中，只有19例是输入性的。一旦这种病毒的变种开始在当地人群中传播，感染人数每隔几周就会翻一番。入境禁令对总确诊病例数量影响不大。

Many countries are starting to ease entry for vaccinated travellers. This is a good idea, but it has been incompetently executed. Some countries are needlessly fussy about the jabs they recognise. Britain, which has injected its citizens with some 5m doses of the AstraZeneca vaccine made in India, refuses to exempt from quarantine Indians inoculated with the same potion. It has donated doses to other countries but will not exempt people jabbed with them. For a while, China allowed entry only to those dosed with Chinese-made shots.

许多国家开始针对接种疫苗的旅行者放宽入境限制。这是个好主意，但执行得很不到位。一些国家对他们所认可的疫苗是很挑剔的，这是没有必要的。英国已经给其公民注射了大约500万剂印度制造的阿斯利康疫苗，但拒绝免除接种了同样疫苗的印度人的隔离。英国已经向其他国家捐赠了疫苗，但不会豁免那些接种疫苗的人。有一段时间，中国只允许那些接种了中国制造的疫苗的人入境。

There is a better way of regulating global travel. The first principle is to default to open borders. This does not mean a free-for-all, but any restrictions should be limited, temporary and aimed at slowing the import of new variants—rather than the impossible mission of stopping it altogether. Once such variants are established in the destination country, as Delta is pretty much everywhere, restrictions are redundant and should be scrapped.

有一种更好的方式来规范全球旅行。第一个原则是默认开放边境。这并不意味着一场完全开放，只是说任何限制措施应该是在一定限度内的、暂时的，并这些措施旨在减缓新变种的输入，而不是完全阻止它的这样一种不可能的任务。一旦这些变种在目的地国家得到证实（因为Delta变种几乎无处不在），这时候限制就是多余的，应该取消。

The second is for all countries to accept vaccines approved by the World Health Organisation. Few people have a choice about which vaccine they receive; banning only those Filipinos who received the Russian Sputnik V jab while accepting those with Pfizer in their arms turns travel into a lottery. Discriminating against people on the basis of something over which they have no choice is unfair. It also undermines the global vaccination effort by making some vaccines seem second-class.

第二是所有国家接受世界卫生组织批准的疫苗。很少有人能选择接种哪种疫苗;只禁止那些接种了俄罗斯Sputnik V疫苗的菲律宾人，而接受那些接种辉瑞疫苗的人，这就把旅行变成了一种碰运气的事。基于人们无法选择的东西而歧视他们是不公平的。着会破坏全球疫苗接种的成果，使一些疫苗看起来是次等的。

The third is to ensure that rules are transparent and universal. Too often, political expediency trumps science. If Western countries are seen to favour each other while keeping out the rest of the world, the rest of the world will notice and remember.

expediency 适宜; 眼前利益

第三是确保规则是透明和普遍的。政治上的权宜之计往往胜过科学。如果西方国家被视为在相互偏袒的同时将世界其他国家拒之门外，那么世界其他国家将会注意到并铭记在心。

The right to move around is one of the most precious of all freedoms. It should be curtailed only when limits will clearly save lives. It should be restored as soon as it is safe. In most cases that means now. ■

活动的权利是所有自由中最宝贵的一项。只有当限制措施能明显挽救生命时，才应该限制。一旦安全了，就应该尽快恢复原状。在大多数情况下，这也就意味着现在。■
