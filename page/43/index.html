A mass drowning exposes how Britain fails to manage migrants

一场大规模溺亡事件暴露了英国对移民管理不力

Desperate measures

绝望的措施

Voters hate seeing small boats wash up on the Kent coast. But politicians have few feasible options

选民们不喜欢看到小船冲上肯特海岸。但政客们几乎没有可行的选择

ON NOVEMBER 24TH an inflatable dinghy capsized off the coast of Calais, France. At least 27 people drowned; a few others were picked up by the French coastguard. They had been trying to get to Britain via an increasingly popular but perilous route. After years of effort by the authorities to stop migrants arriving from the continent stowed away in lorries, rising numbers have turned to small boats. On November 11th the total arriving on the Kent coast hit 1,185, a daily record.

11月24日，一艘充气小艇在法国加莱海岸倾覆。至少27人溺水身亡; 还有一些被法国海岸警卫队抓获。他们试图通过一条日益流行但危险的路线到达英国。英国当局多年来一直在阻止从欧洲大陆来的移民使用卡车偷渡，现在越来越多的人转向使用小船。11月11日，抵达肯特海岸的总人数达到了1185人，创下了日记录。

The deaths turned a political row into a tragedy. In recent weeks the crossings had become the government’s biggest headache. MPs say that, even as Parliament is consumed with scandal about second jobs, contacts by constituents concerned about immigration in small boats outnumber those about sleaze. Nearly 60% of Conservative voters say that immigration or asylum is one of the top three issues facing the country, and nearly 80% that the government is handling it poorly.

这些人的死亡将一场政治争吵变成了一场悲剧。最近几周，“跨海”过境问题已然成为政府最头疼的问题。议员们表示，尽管议会充斥着关于“第二职业”的丑闻，但是接触到的选民比起“关注丑闻”反而更加关注“小船移民入境”。近60%的保守党选民表示，移民和庇护是英国面临的三大问题之一，而近80%的人认为政府处理不善。

The drownings highlighted the human cost of what had up until then been discussed in terms of crude electoral calculus. The prime minister, Boris Johnson, who campaigned to leave the EU on a pledge to “take back control”—including of Britain’s borders—feared being outflanked on his right by a resurgent anti-immigration movement. Nick Thomas-Symonds, Labour’s shadow home secretary, called the drownings the “most poignant of wake-up calls” for the government.

在此之前，人们对这一事件的讨论一直都围绕着“粗略的选举预测结果”，而溺亡事件强调了这一事件人力成本的上升。英国首相鲍里斯·约翰逊曾在脱欧运动中承诺“夺回控制权”，包括英国的边境，但他担心自己的右翼势力会被再度爆发的反移民运动所包抄。工党影子内政大臣Nick Thomas-Symonds称，对政府来说，这次溺水事件是“最痛心的警钟”。

The previous week Mr Johnson ordered a review aimed at stopping small boats from attempting the crossing. But this is easier said than done. In the run-up to the 2019 election Priti Patel, the home secretary, said she would halve the number of migrants arriving by that route; instead it has increased nearly 14-fold. Migrants set out in the face of extreme risks, and for reasons that no destination country can control, such as war and famine. Aid, which donors hope may keep recipients at home, can instead give them the means to leave.

上周，约翰逊下令进行审查，目的是阻止试图过境的小船。但这说起来容易做起来难。在2019年大选的预备阶段，内政大臣Priti Patel表示，她将把通过这条路线抵达的移民人数减半; 相反，现在它增长了近14倍。移民是冒着极大风险、出于任何目的地国家都无法控制的战争和饥荒等原因才动身的。援助者本希望援助可以让受援者留在国内，结果援助反而成为他们离开的手段。

Many in Calais’s camps want to go to Britain to join family members, says Frances Timberlake of the Refugee Women’s Centre, an aid organisation. Others want to move on because of poor conditions. The European Council on Refugees and Exiles, an NGO network, estimates that fewer than half of applicants for asylum in France in 2019 were provided with housing.

难民妇女中心(一个援助组织)的Frances Timberlake说，许多在加莱营地的人想去英国和家人团聚。另一些人因为条件差而想要离开。非政府组织欧洲难民和流亡者问题理事会估计，2019年在法国申请庇护的人中，只有不到一半得到了住房。

Those migrants who make it to Britain’s shores have the right to seek asylum. As an EU member Britain could invoke the Dublin Convention, a deal struck in 2013 that requires claims to be made in the EU country of first arrival. In fact, returning those who moved on proved difficult. But in any case, Britain is no longer covered.

那些到达英国海岸的移民有权寻求庇护。作为欧盟成员国，英国可以援引2013年达成的《都柏林公约》，该公约要求向率先抵达的欧盟国家提出政府救济申请。事实上，让那些继续前行的人回去是很困难的。但无论如何，英国已不再覆盖范围内。

Many of the migrants who crossed the Channel this year are likely to qualify for asylum. According to the Refugee Council, a charity, arrivals by boat tend to originate in countries with high approval rates. Between January 2020 and May 2021, Iranians were the biggest group. Their approval rate before appeal is 67%, compared with 52% for all nationalities. For Syrians, the fourth-biggest group, it is 88%.

许多今年越过英吉利海峡的移民可能有资格获得庇护。据Refugee Council慈善机构的说法，乘船抵达的人往往来自批准率高的国家。在2020年1月至2021年5月期间，伊朗人是最大的群体。他们在上诉前的支持率为67%，而所有国籍的支持率为52%。对于第四大群体叙利亚人来说，这个比例达到88%。

Since the Brexit referendum voters have become less concerned about immigrants arriving for work or to join family. But 56% have an unfavourable view of those who enter illegally. Other routes are, however, largely closed. Britain has no humanitarian-visa scheme for asylum-seekers. A plan announced in August to resettle Afghans who worked for Western countries and agencies has yet to get off the ground.

自英国脱欧公投以来，选民们对移民来英国工作或与家人团聚的担忧减少了。但56%的人对非法入境的人持反对意见。不过，其他路线基本上关闭了。英国没有针对寻求庇护者的人道主义签证计划。8月份宣布的一项重新安置为西方国家和机构工作的阿富汗人的计划尚未启动。

Ms Patel has considered using Britain’s border force to push boats back into French waters. But that may be illegal under maritime law. Another idea is to hold asylum-seekers offshore while their claims are processed. The Home Office says that it is in talks with Albania’s government—a claim its prime minister has denied.

Patel先生曾考虑利用英国边防部队将船只推回法国水域。但根据海商法，这可能是非法的。另一个想法是，在处理庇护申请期间，将寻求庇护者留在海上。内政部称正在与阿尔巴尼亚政府进行谈判，但阿尔巴尼亚总理否认了这一说法。

The centrepiece of Ms Patel’s plans is a draft nationality and borders bill, which Mr Johnson declared was all the more urgent after the latest tragedy. It would create a two-tier system, with arrivals through informal routes granted less time to stay and denied opportunities for family reunification. It has faced sharp criticism. According to a legal opinion commissioned by Freedom from Torture, an NGO, some parts violate international law. That is unlikely to stop the bill passing, but may delay it in the House of Lords. And in any case, there is little evidence that the harsh asylum policies influence migrants’ choices. An assessment of the draft bill by the Home Office concluded that evidence for the effectiveness of deterrence was “limited”. Researchers who interviewed migrants who crossed the Mediterranean during the crisis of 2015 came to a similar conclusion.

Patel计划的核心是一项国籍与边界法案草案，约翰逊宣称，在最近的悲剧发生后，这项法案变得更加紧迫。这将形成一个双重体系，通过非正式途径抵达的人停留的时间更短，家庭团聚的机会也被剥夺。这面临着尖锐的批评。根据非政府组织“Freedom from Torture”委托出具的一份法律意见书，某些部分违反了国际法。这不大可能阻止法案的通过，但可能会推迟法案在上议院的通过。无论如何，几乎没有证据表明严苛的庇护政策会影响移民的选择。内政部对草案的评估得出结论也证明了“威慑有效性”的证据是“有限的”。研究人员通过采访2015年危机期间穿越地中海的移民，得出了类似的结论。

Reducing the toll of lives will take co-operation between Britain and France. On November 24th Mr Johnson and the French president, Emmanuel Macron, agreed to work together more closely and do “everything possible” to catch the smuggler gangs. “France will not let the Channel become a graveyard,” Mr Macron declared.

减少死亡人数需要英法两国的合作。11月24日，约翰逊和法国总统马克龙同意更加密切地合作，并“尽一切可能”抓住走私团伙。马克龙宣称：“法国不会让英吉利海峡成为墓地”。

But co-operation requires trust, which is in short supply. The two countries are embroiled in Brexit-related rows, notably over fishing permits. Mr Macron is fighting for re-election in April and needs to be seen standing up for national interests. After the drownings Mr Johnson expressed frustration at France’s failure to stop the beach launches, and reiterated a request to allow British police to patrol beaches in northern France, which has so far been refused. According to the Elysée, Mr Macron said the British should “stop exploiting a dreadful situation for political ends”.

但合作需要信任，而现在又缺乏信任。这两个国家又卷入了脱欧相关的争端，尤其是在捕鱼许可证问题上。马克龙先生正在争取4月份的连任，他需要让人们看到他在维护国家利益。在溺水事件发生后，约翰逊对法国未能拦截从海滩出发(的小船)表示失望，并重申”允许英国警方在法国北部海滩巡逻“这一请求，但这一请求迄今仍遭到拒绝。据Elysée网站报道，马克龙表示，英国应该“停止利用糟糕的局势达到政治目的”。

And the French authorities argue that they are already doing all they can to stop departures. So far this year 31,500 migrants have set off, they say, with 7,800 rescued and brought back to France. On November 23rd they arrested 15 people connected to a people-smuggling ring that had brought 250 migrants across the Channel for a fee of €6,000 ($6,700).

法国当局表示，他们已经在尽一切努力阻止难民的离开。他们表示，今年到目前为止，已有31500名移民出发，其中7800人获救并被带回法国。11月23日，他们逮捕了15名与人口走私团伙有关的人，该团伙曾以6000欧元(6700美元)的费用带着250名移民穿越英吉利海峡。

Seen from countries such as Greece and Italy, which are more accessible from some of the world’s trouble spots, the numbers of migrants arriving on Britain’s beaches is tiny. And as the weather grows colder and the seas choppier, the cross-Channel flow is likely to subside. But at least ten migrants have died in the past few weeks, not counting the latest tragedy. The government, already keenly aware of the likely electoral cost of a resurgence in the spring, has been reminded that failing to stop the crossings has a human cost, too. ■

从希腊和意大利这样的国家到达英国海滩的移民数量就很少，从世界上不安定的地区还更容易到达这些国家。随着天气变冷，海面波涛汹涌，横渡英吉利海峡的人群可能会减少。但过去几周至少有10名移民死亡，这还不包括最近的悲剧。政府已经敏锐地意识到，到了春天，移民潮的再次爆发可能会带来选举成本，他们也被提醒，不阻止越境也会带来人力成本。■

How to manage the Great Resignation

如何应对“大辞职”现象

Bartleby

巴托比

![image-20211130135040572](/Users/chenyujie/Library/Application Support/typora-user-images/image-20211130135040572.png)

High staff churn is here to stay. Retention strategies require a rethink

员工流失率居高不下，需要重新思考留存策略

IN THE NOT-SO-DISTANT past, bosses did not have to worry as much about their workforces. Newcomers could absorb the corporate culture osmotically. Workers’ families were invisible, not constantly interrupting Zoom calls. Employees had a job, not a voice. Now firms have to “be intentional” (management-speak for thinking) about everything from the point of the office to how staff communicate with each other. Retention is the latest area to require attention.

在不远的过去，老板们不必担心他们的员工。新来者可以潜移默化地吸收企业文化。你不会看见员工们的家人，也不会经常被打断Zoom的电话。员工有工作，但没有发言权。现在，公司必须“有意识地”(管理思维的说法)考虑从办公室到员工之间如何沟通的所有问题。留存是最需要关注的领域。

The spike in staff departures known as the Great Resignation is centred on America: a record 3% of the workforce there quit their jobs in September. But employees in other places are also footloose. Resignations explain why job-to-job moves in Britain reached a record high in the third quarter of this year.

员工离职潮被称为“大辞职”，主要集中在美国: 9月份，3%的员工辞职，这一数字创下了新的记录。但其他地方的员工也有辞职的情况[自由自在]。辞职解释了为什么英国的工作变动在今年第三季度达到了历史新高。

Some of the churn is transitory. It was hard to act on pent-up job dissatisfaction while economies were in free fall, so there is a post-pandemic backlog of job switches to clear. And more quitting now is not the same as sustained job-hopping later. As Melissa Swift of Mercer, a consultancy, notes, white-collar workers in search of higher purpose will choose a new employer carefully and stay longer.

有些流失是暂时的。在经济处于衰退的情况下，很难对积压的工作不满采取行动，因此疫情后积压的交接工作需要处理。现在“辞职更多”的情况和以后“持续跳槽”是不一样的。Mercer咨询公司的Melissa Swift指出，追求更高目标的白领会谨慎地选择新雇主，并呆得更久。

But there is also reason to believe that higher rates of churn are here to stay. The prevalence of remote working means that more roles are plausible options for more jobseekers. And the pandemic has driven home the precariousness of life at the bottom of the income ladder. Resignation rates are highest in industries, like hospitality, that are full of low-wage workers who have lots of potentially risky face-to-face contact with colleagues and customers.

但我们也有理由相信更高的流失率会持续下去。远程办公的盛行意味着对更多的求职者来说，有更多合理的可供选择的职位。这场疫情清楚地表明了，位于底层收入阶层的人们生活的不稳定。像服务业这样的行业离职率最高，因为这些行业的员工工资都很低，还有与同事和顾客面对面接触的潜在风险。

One conventional solution—identifying a few star performers and bunging them extra money—is not a retention strategy if large chunks of the workforce are thinking differently about their jobs. What should managers be doing?

一个传统的解决方案是找出少数表现出色的员工，然后多给他们点儿钱，如果大部分员工对他们的工作有不同的想法，这就不是一个留存策略。那管理者应该做些什么呢?

First, they should systematically gauge the retention risk that their firm faces. Working out what has driven people to quit is too late; rather than exit interviews, forward-thinking firms conduct “stay interviews” to find out what keeps employees. Focusing on teams cut back during the pandemic is another tactic: burnout rates are likely to be higher in departments that took lay-offs. Understanding a firm’s vulnerability to other employers is also key. When behemoths like Amazon or Walmart raise wages or add perks, the effects ripple beyond retailing.

首先，他们应该系统地评估公司面临的留存风险。找出促使人们离职的原因已经太晚了; 有远见的公司不进行离职面谈，而是进行“留任面谈”，以找出留住员工的方法。关注在疫情期间被砍掉的团队是另一种策略: 在被裁的部门中，工作倦怠率可能更高。了解一家公司在其他雇主面前的弱点也是关键。当亚马逊或沃尔玛等巨头提高工资或增加福利时，其影响会波及到零售业以外的领域。

Second, managers need to pull different levers to retain different types of people. Salaries matter to everyone but for lower-wage workers in particular, benefits like health care have also become central. A recent survey of young Americans by Jefferies, an investment bank, found that health concerns were the prime reason why people with only a high-school education had quit their jobs.

其次，管理者需要使用不同的手段来留住不同类型的员工。工资对每个人都很重要，但对低收入员工来说尤其如此，医疗保健等福利也变得至关重要。投资银行杰富瑞最近对美国年轻人进行的一项调查发现，只有高中学历的人辞职的主要原因是因为担忧身体健康。

It’s a similar story for flexible working. For white-collar types the split between office and home is what counts. For blue-collar workers, single parents especially, scheduling matters—when their shifts start and end, and how much leeway they have to manage their time.

弹性工作制也很重要。对于白领阶层来说，办公室和家庭之间划清界限才是最重要的。对于蓝领工人，尤其是单亲父母来说，时间安排很重要，比如说他们轮班时间在什么时候开始以及什么时间结束，以及他们有多少富余时间可以支配。

Firms also need to think harder about the career paths that entry-level employees can take. In a recent survey of large firms conducted by the Institute for Corporate Productivity, a research outfit, a majority admitted they did not have adequate data about the skills of their workers, making it harder to spot talent. A quarter reckoned that LinkedIn knew more about their workforce’s capabilities than their own firms did.

公司也需要更仔细地考虑初级员工的职业道路。研究机构Corporate Productivity最近对大公司进行的一项调查中，大多数公司承认他们没有足够的关于员工专长的数据，这使得他们更难发现人才。四分之一的人认为领英比他们自己的公司更了解员工的能力。

Third, managers should plan for how to find new workers. Remote working makes it easier to lose people but also to bring freelancers on board quickly. Qualification demands can be relaxed. In recent years IBM has removed the requirement for undergraduate degrees from over half of its American job openings. And there is no better time for firms to take aim at dim-witted regulation. In response to a shortage of lorry drivers, Britain’s government has decided to combine separate tests for driving rigid and articulated lorries into one.

第三，管理者应该计划如何找到新员工。远程工作让员工更容易流失，但也让自由职业者能够迅速加入公司。可以资格要求放宽。近年来，IBM在其美国一半以上的招聘职位中已经取消了对本科学位要求。对企业来说，现在是批评愚蠢的监管政策的最佳时机。为了应对卡车司机的短缺，英国政府决定将驾驶刚性货车和铰接式货车的测试合并了。

The Great Resignation should also prompt a question that rarely gets asked—exactly what level of churn is right? It is more expensive to hire new employees than to keep current ones. Yet by that logic, companies would never want anyone to quit. The mix of old and new is what matters. Existing hands provide cultural ballast; joiners bring fresh skills and perspectives. Keeping good employees happy is vital. But people are like water: there is such a thing as too much retention.

“大辞职”还应该引发一个很少被问及的问题，究竟什么程度的流失才是合理的? 雇佣新员工比留住现有员工的成本更高。然而，按照这种逻辑，公司绝不会希望任何人辞职。新老员工的结合才是最重要的。现有的员工提供文化稳定性; 新员工可以带来新的技能和视角。让优秀员工开心是至关重要的。但人就像水: 也会有“留存”过多的情况。

Joe Biden’s tech policy is becoming clearer

拜登的技术政策越来越清晰

In tech we don’t trust

The administration is pushing back harder against big tech than anyone expected

拜登政府对大型科技公司的打压力度超出了所有人的预期

Dallas and Washington, DC

TO UNDERSTAND how much technology behemoths’ standing in Washington, DC, has changed in the past four years, ask Barry Lynn, founder of the Open Markets Institute, an anti-monopoly group. In 2017 he was allegedly kicked out of the New America Foundation, a think-tank that hosted his team, for being too critical of big tech. At the time, Mr Lynn’s work decrying “platform monopolists” and advocating for breaking up tech giants was provocative but niche. He was like a protester with a megaphone, loud but mostly tuned out.

要想了解过去四年来华盛顿特区科技巨头的地位发生了多大变化，可以问问反垄断研究机构Open Markets Institute的创始人Barry Lynn。2017年，他因为对大型科技公司过于挑剔而被新美国基金会开除，该基金会是一个智库，负责管理他的团队。当时，Lynn谴责“平台垄断者”、主张拆分科技巨头的工作颇具挑衅性，但仅限于某些领域。他就像一个拿着扩音器的抗议者，虽然声音很大，但大多数人都只当没听见。

Today, when Joe Biden talks about corporate “concentration”, he sounds a lot like Mr Lynn, and he has named Mr Lynn’s friends to top posts. Lina Khan, who worked at the Open Markets Institute for seven years, now chairs the Federal Trade Commission (FTC), an agency charged with protecting consumer privacy and enforcing antitrust law. Tim Wu, formerly on Mr Lynn’s advisory board, has a post advising the White House on competition and technology policy. Operating out of a WeWork space a short walk from the White House, Mr Lynn is part of the in-crowd. “For an itty-bitty think-tank, we have a lot of our people in the administration,” he says.

如今，当拜登谈论企业的“集中”时，他的口气有点儿像Lynn，而且他还任命 Lynn的朋友担任了最高职位。Lina Khan曾在Open Markets Institute工作了7年，现在担任联邦贸易委员会的主席，该委员会负责保护消费者隐私和执行反垄断法。Lynn的顾问委员会前成员Tim Wu(吴修铭)在竞争和技术政策方面为白宫提供建议。在距离白宫几步之遥的WeWork办公场所，Lynn是人群中的一员。他说：“作为一个小智库，我们在政府中有很多自己人”。

Beyond Mr Lynn’s colleagues, more tech-bashers hold passports to power. Jonathan Kanter, confirmed to lead antitrust efforts at the Department of Justice, is a staunch opponent of Google and is expected to bring enforcement actions against tech firms. Rohit Chopra, head of the Consumer Financial Protection Bureau, has asked tech titans to turn over records about their payments systems.

除了Lynn的同事，很多的技术抨击者拥有权力的通行证。Jonathan Kanter已被认定为司法部反垄断行动的负责人，他是谷歌的坚定反对者，预计他将对科技公司采取强制行动。美国消费者金融保护局局长Rohit Chopra要求科技巨头交出有关其支付系统的记录。

How did Mr Biden decide to go big on big tech? Unlike opponents in the Democratic primary, such as Elizabeth Warren, a Democratic senator who has urged breaking up tech monopolies, Mr Biden did not spend much time bashing tech. However, once Mr Biden was in office, Ms Warren had the most advanced tech plan and pushed for key positions for people such as Ms Khan, Mr Wu and Bharat Ramamurti, who headed economic policy for her campaign and now serves on the White House’s National Economic Council. “Biden’s tech policy is his nominees,” Ms Warren says. “He has put people in positions of power who understand tech at a whole new level and are deeply sceptical about many of the current practices,” she adds.

拜登是如何决定在大型科技公司上大做文章的? 与民主党初选的对手(如Elizabeth Warren)不同, 拜登没有花很多时间抨击技术，而Elizabeth Warren作为一位民主党参议员，她则一直竭力主张打破技术垄断。然而，拜登上任后，Warren女士就制定了最先进的科技计划，并为Khan女士、Wu先生和Bharat Ramamurti先生等人争取关键职位，Bharat Ramamurti先生曾在她竞选期间负责经济政策，现在就职于白宫国家经济委员会。Warren女士说：“拜登的技术政策就是被他的提名”，她又补充道：“他把那些对技术理解在全新水平上的、对当前许多做法深表怀疑的人推向了掌权地位”。

Many believe that Barack Obama’s administration, of which Mr Biden was a part, became too enamoured with large tech firms and failed to scrutinise mergers, like Facebook’s purchase of Instagram. This is Mr Biden’s chance at a do-over. The mood in Washington is “180” degrees different, according to Luther Lowe, who runs public policy for Yelp, an internet firm that has long been complaining about Google’s bullying of smaller rivals. “It’s a really exciting time if you are a complainant who’s hoping for government enforcement,” he says. Congressional staffers from both parties are now loth to take meetings with large tech companies, especially Meta (née Facebook), perceived to be the most toxic.

许多人认为奥巴马政府（拜登是奥巴马政府的一份子）过于迷恋大型科技公司，未能仔细审查并购交易，比如Facebook收购Instagram。这是拜登重新调查的机会。据网络公司Yelp负责公共政策的Luther Lowe说，美国政府的情绪有“180度”的转变，这家公司长期以来一直抱怨谷歌欺负较小的竞争对手。他说:“如果你是一个希望政府能够强制实施的抱怨者，那这正是一个令人兴奋的时刻。” 两党的国会工作人员现在都不愿与大型科技公司开会，尤其是被认为是最有害的Meta公司(原名Facebook)。

In July Mr Biden bared his trustbusting teeth when he issued a strongly worded executive order, written by Mr Wu, bemoaning corporate concentration. No previous president has issued such a detailed order on competition, says Paul Gallant of Cowen Washington Research, which tracks tech policy. Big tech was not an exclusive focus but a prominent one. Alphabet (Google’s parent), Amazon, Apple and Meta are among the country’s most valuable firms.

今年7月，拜登公开了他的反垄断行动，他发布了一份由Wu先生撰写的措辞强硬的行政命令，表示对企业的集中化的不满。跟踪科技政策的Cowen Washington Research的Paul Gallant表示，此前从未有哪位总统在竞争问题上发布过如此详细的命令。大型科技公司不是唯一关注的焦点，而是一个突出的焦点。Alphabet(谷歌的母公司)、亚马逊(Amazon)、苹果(Apple)和Meta公司都是美国市值最高的公司。

With Mr Biden’s troops now assembled, the focus will turn to which battles against large tech firms his administration can win. Break-ups and antitrust enforcement might get headlines, but those are longer-term plays with uncertain outcomes, given the pro-business bent of the courts. New laws targeting tech firms could have a faster impact. If that fails, the administration could rewrite the rules of business through newly empowered government agencies.

随着拜登“部队”的集结，其重点将转向他领导的政府能够赢得与哪些大型科技公司的战斗。分拆和反垄断实施可能会成为头条新闻，但考虑到法院的“亲商”的倾向，这些都是长期策略，且结果不确定。针对科技公司的新法律可能会更快产生影响。如果失败了，拜登政府可能会通过新授权的政府机构改写商业规则。

Take congressional action first. Just about the only issue on which Republicans and Democrats in Washington agree, besides XX, is that tech firms have become too powerful. At least 14 tech-focused bills have been introduced in Congress this session. Many are bipartisan. One co-sponsored by Amy Klobuchar, a Democratic senator, and Tom Cotton, a Republican, targets tech companies with market values of $600bn or more, and proposes establishing a presumption against new acquisitions of smaller firms. “We’re not talking about destroying these companies; we’re talking about putting some reasonable rules in place, when they have said ‘trust us’, and no one does,” says Ms Klobuchar.

首先采取国会行动。除了XX，美国政府共和党人和民主党人唯一达成共识的问题就是科技公司已经变得太强大了。本届国会至少提出了14项技术法案。许多都是由两党共同提出的。一项由民主党参议员Amy Klobuchar和共和党人Tom Cotton联合发起的提案，针对市值在6000亿美元或以上的科技公司，提议建立一种推定，即反对对小型公司进行新的收购。Klobuchar表示：“我们讨论的并不是要摧毁这些公司; 而是当他们选择相信我们时，我们正在商讨制定一些合理的规则，不过没人愿意相信”。

Mr Biden has not made passing tech legislation a priority, but he is expected to after the Senate decides the fate of his Build Back Better social-spending package. (Built into it is $500m in funding for the FTC to establish a new bureau to investigate privacy violations.) “Be on the lookout” for stronger involvement from the White House, predicts Ms Warren.

拜登先生还没有把通过技术立法作为优先事项，但在参议院通过“重建美好未来”的社会支出计划之后，他预计会这样做。(其中包括为联邦贸易委员会提供5亿美元资金，以建立一个调查侵犯隐私行为的新机构) Warren女士预测，“要注意”白宫是否会加大介入力度。

The most obvious place for bipartisan agreement is on a bill that further constrains online-data collection about children. But Mr Biden is likely to be more ambitious. He could press for a ban on the giants’ ability to ensure preferential treatment for their own services. While campaigning, he expressed support for doing away with “Section 230”, which offers legal immunity to platforms for hosting objectionable content. He could also push for a national privacy bill that would establish more consumer control over data.

两党最明显的共识是进一步限制收集儿童线上数据的法案。但拜登可能更具野心。他可能会施压禁止这些巨头确保他们自己的服务得到优待的能力。在竞选期间，他表示支持废除《通信规范法》中的“第230条”条款，该条款为提供不良内容的平台提供了法律豁免权。他还会推动一项国家隐私法案，让消费者对自己的数据拥有更多的控制权。

Congressional action is far from guaranteed: partisan politics may still get in the way. Among Republicans, there is “disagreement” about how strongly to intervene in markets, says Ken Buck, a Republican congressman from Colorado. The president’s critics could also oppose legislation they might otherwise support, just to kneecap him. “I don’t see eye-to-eye on anything with Joe Biden,” says Mr Buck, who has co-sponsored six bipartisan tech-focused bills with which Mr Biden would ostensibly agree. Democrats, too, could stymie legislation. Tech executives are big Democratic funders. Already some Californian Democrats in Congress have opposed several tech bills.

国会的行动还远未得到保证:党派政治仍可能成为阻碍。科罗拉多州共和党国会议员Ken Buck表示，共和党人对干预市场的力度存在“分歧”。总统的批评者可能只是为了打压总统而选择反对他们本来可能支持的立法。 Buck说:“我和拜登在任何事情上意见都不一致。” 他曾与人共同发起了六项两党共同支持的聚焦于技术法案，拜登表面上同意这些法案。民主党人也可能阻挠立法。科技公司高管可是资助民主党的大头。加州国会的一些民主党人已经反对了几项技术法案。

Even if Congress fails to act soon, Mr Biden’s tech agenda will not be halted. Under the 107-year-old FTC Act, Ms Khan has wide latitude when it comes to rule-making to address “conduct that is unfair or deceptive”. Mr Biden’s executive order on competition encouraged the FTC to set rules on “surveillance and the accumulation of data”, rules “barring unfair methods of competition on internet marketplaces” and rules against “anticompetitive restrictions on using independent repair shops” to fix cell phones and other devices.

即使国会不能很快采取行动，拜登的科技议程也不会被中止。根据已有107年历史的《联邦贸易委员会法》，Khan女士在制定规则来处理“不公平或欺骗性行为”方面拥有广泛的自由。拜登关于竞争的行政命令鼓励联邦贸易委员会就“数据监控以及数据积累”、“禁止互联网市场上的不公平竞争方式”以及“禁止使用独立维修店修理手机和其他设备的反竞争限制”等问题制定规则。

For example, the FTC could try to outlaw certain conduct, such as platforms giving preferential treatment to their own services over others’. The commission could also move to minimise data-collection and sharing between apps, which would limit tech firms’ firepower to track users across the web and combine sources of consumer information, as Meta does through Facebook, Instagram and WhatsApp.

例如，联邦贸易委员会可以试图取缔某些行为，比如平台优待自己的服务而非他人的服务。该委员会还可能采取行动，尽量减少应用程序之间的数据收集和共享，这将限制科技公司通过网络跟踪用户并整合消费者信息来源的能力，就像Meta通过Facebook、Instagram和WhatsApp所做的那样。

“Rule-making presents a bigger risk to the tech companies than traditional antitrust lawsuits, which get all the attention,” says Mr Gallant. Tech firms would surely fire back, suing both over whether the FTC has the right to set such sweeping rules and over the legality of specific measures.

Gallant先生表示：“对科技公司来说，制定规则带来的风险比引起所有人注意的传统反垄断诉讼更大”。科技公司肯定会快速反击，起诉联邦贸易委员会是否有权制定这样的全面的规则，以及这些具体措施的合法性。

Gulliver’s travails

Antitrust enforcement is the final front of Mr Biden’s campaign. This will involve scuttling proposed mergers and attacking firms for anticompetitive behaviour. Those running competition policy for Mr Biden believe that the scope of antitrust law has been artificially narrowed over the past 40 years. They argue that a focus on consumer welfare, popularised by Robert Bork, a conservative judge, from the late 1970s, fails to take into account other harms from increased concentration.

反垄断执法是拜登竞选的最后一个阵地。这将包括阻止拟议中的合并，以及攻击公司的反竞争行为。那些为拜登制定竞争政策的人认为，过去40年里，反垄断法的范围被人为地缩小了。他们认为，由保守派法官Robert Bork在20世纪70年代后期开始流行的对消费者福利的关注，没有考虑到日益集中带来的其他危害。

Mr Biden “grew up under antimonopoly enforcement regimes that were extremely aggressive” and is old enough to remember the world before Bork, says Mr Lynn. In 1987, as chair of the Senate Judiciary Committee, he oversaw the rejection of Mr Bork’s elevation to the Supreme Court.

Lynn表示，拜登“在极端激进的反垄断执法体制下长大”，他的年龄足以记得Bork之前的世界。1987年，作为参议院司法委员会主席，他见证了对Bork升任最高法院大法官的否决。

Antitrust lawsuits have two downsides: they are always long and unpredictable. Mr Biden’s administration inherited lawsuits from Donald Trump’s: one filed by the Justice Department against Google, another by the FTC against Facebook. Such cases take longer than a single presidential term. A trial in the Google case is set for 2023, and a decision and appeal will add another year at least. Most expect Ms Khan and Mr Kanter to try to build an additional case against a tech giant, perhaps Amazon or Apple.

反垄断诉讼有两个缺点:它们总是时间很长且不可预测。拜登政府接手了特朗普的诉讼:一件是司法部对谷歌提起的诉讼，另一件是联邦贸易委员会对Facebook提起的诉讼。这类案件需要的时间比一个总统任期还要长。谷歌案的审判将在2023年进行，判决和上诉将至少再延长一年时间。大多数人预计，Khan女士和Kanter先生会试图对一家科技巨头发起额外的诉讼，可能是亚马逊或苹果。

The first big test of Ms Khan’s agenda will come from whether the FTC will approve Amazon’s proposed $8.5bn acquisition of MGM, a Hollywood studio with the rights to James Bond. Some of Ms Khan’s supporters hope she blocks the deal, but doing so could be risky in court, given how diverse the media market is in America. So far the most high-profile deal to be blocked during the Biden administration is an old-media one: the Justice Department sued to stop two book companies, Penguin Random House and Simon & Schuster, from merging, arguing this would hurt authors’ abilities to gain advances.

Khan工作事项的第一个重大考验，将在于美国联邦贸易委员会是否会批准亚马逊以85亿美元收购米高梅的提议。米高梅是一家好莱坞制片公司，它拥有《007》(詹姆斯·邦德)版权。Khan女士的一些支持者希望她阻止这项交易，但考虑到美国媒体市场的多样性，这样做在法庭上可能会有风险。到目前为止，拜登执政期间被阻止的最引人注目的交易是一桩传统媒体交易:司法部起诉企鹅兰登书屋和西蒙与舒斯特两家图书公司，要求它们停止合并，理由是这将损害作家获得预付薪水的能力。

Whether or not Mr Biden breaks up a large tech firm, the threat of trustbusting “is already changing the world”, says Ms Warren. Mr Biden’s impact can be seen “partly in deals that don’t get announced, and it’s also in the tech giants’ reviewing their own internal practices,” she says. Recently Apple announced it was going to make it easier to repair devices, which appears to be a direct response to Mr Biden’s executive order on competition.

Warren表示，无论拜登是否拆分一家大型科技公司，反垄断的威胁“已经在改变世界”。她表示，拜登的影响“部分体现在未宣布的交易上，也体现在科技巨头对自身内部做法的审查上”。最近，苹果宣布将简化设备维修的流程，这似乎是对拜登关于竞争的行政命令的直接回应。

In Silicon Valley, there is more optimism than a few years ago about startups being able to reach a larger scale without being stamped out by giants. Some believe that without the Biden administration’s reinvigoration of antitrust, sectors that are starting to see more competition, such as social media, would already be further consolidated. Knowing they could be scrutinised or sued, firms go on their best behaviour, as Mr Wu describes in his book, “The Curse of Bigness”, in which he argues for a revival of the big-case tradition of antitrust.

在硅谷，人们比几年前更乐观地认为，初创公司能够在不被巨头镇压的情况下实现更大的规模。一些人认为，如果没有拜登政府重振反垄断，像社交媒体等竞争开始加剧的行业将会进一步整合。正如Wu先生在他的书《大企业的诅咒》中所描述的那样，知道自己可能会被审查或起诉，公司会将最好的表现展示给人们看，Wu先生在他的书中主张反垄断传统大案的复兴。

Several states are also taking aim at big tech with new laws and lawsuits. Mr Lynn relishes this swarm attack. “We may be Lilliputians,” he says, “but there are enough of us that we can drag these folks down.” ■

一些州还通过新的法律和诉讼瞄准了大型科技公司。Lynn喜欢这种群体性攻击。他说，“我们可能是小人，但我们的人数足够多，我们可以把这些巨人拖下水。”■

How Europe should deal with covid-19

欧洲应该如何应对covid-19？

Winter wave

冬季疫情潮

A fourth surge is causing panic and muddled thinking

第四次病例的激增正在引起恐慌和混乱的思考

OVER THE next few years covid-19 will probably settle down as a seasonal disease, a lethal threat to the elderly and the poor in health, but to everyone else mostly a nuisance. However, as Europe is discovering, getting there will be perilous. The European Union is recording nearly a quarter of a million cases a day, more than at any time in the pandemic. Eleven months after vaccination first got under way, intensive-care wards in some regions are almost full. The World Health Organisation warned this week that 700,000 more Europeans could die by March.

在未来几年里，covid-19可能会成为一种季节性疾病，对老年人和健康状况差的人会构成致命威胁，但对其他所有人来说通常只是一个麻烦事儿。然而，正如欧洲正在发现的那样，“成为季节性疾病”将是危险的。欧盟每天有近25万例病例，这比疫情期间的任何时候都多。在疫苗刚开始接种的11个月后，一些地区的重症监护病房几乎人满为患。世界卫生组织在本周警告称，到明年3月，可能还会有70万欧洲人死亡。

Amid growing alarm, governments are once again locking down. Austria has become the first rich country to demand that all of its citizens must be vaccinated or face a fine; Germany may follow. Demonstrators are taking to the streets to protest against new restrictions, including in the Netherlands, Belgium and Italy. As the disease spreads, confusion reigns about what measures are justified in the campaign to slow it.

在日益增加的恐慌中，各国政府再次采取了封锁措施。奥地利成为第一个要求所有公民必须接种疫苗否则将面临罚款的发达国家; 德国可能会效仿。荷兰、比利时和意大利等国的示威者走上街头抗议新的限制措施。随着这种疾病的传播，人们感到困惑，不知道该采取什么措施来减缓这种疾病的传播。

Dealing with the fourth wave begins with understanding what is causing it. Covid is surging in Europe because winter weather sends people indoors, where the virus spreads easily. In the EU slightly over 60% of the population over 12 years old is fully vaccinated. That leaves roughly 150m people unprotected, unless they have already acquired immunity from having survived an infection—as many have in Britain. It is a warning to places like XX with large immunologically naive populations.

要想应对这第四波疫情，首先要理解它的成因。新冠肺炎疫情在欧洲激增，是因为冬天的天气让人们“被迫”呆在室内，而病毒很容易在室内传播。在欧盟，12岁以上的人口中只有略多于60%的人完全接种了疫苗。这使得大约1.5亿人得不到保护，除非这些人已经从感染中幸存且获得了免疫，就像许多英国人一样。这是对XX等拥有大量免疫力低下的人口的地方的一个警告。

At the same time, immunity starts to wane from around ten weeks after the second dose. Booster shots are vital, but the EU has been slow to administer them. In Germany infections are doubling every 12 days. One reason is that, even now, only 8% have received a third dose, compared with 23% in Britain.

与此同时，从第二次接种后大约10周后，免疫能力开始减弱。加强针至关重要，但欧盟在注射加强针方面一直进展缓慢。在德国，感染人数每12天就翻一番。其中一个原因是，即使到现在也只有8%的人接种了第三针，而在英国接种人数是23%。

As so often in the pandemic, governments have been caught out by the exponential growth in cases. Because they are acting late, they must resort to more extreme measures. Protesters say their liberties are being trampled. Many of those who reject vaccination argue that the state has no right to coerce them.

正如在疫情中经常发生的那样，各国政府被病例的指数增长整得措手不及。因为行动缓慢，他们必须采取更极端的措施。抗议者说他们的自由被践踏了。许多拒绝接种疫苗的人认为，国家没有权利强迫他们接种疫苗。

Those arguments are flawed. Two years into the pandemic, it is clear that your behaviour really is your neighbours’ business. One reason is that you are more likely to infect other people if you are unvaccinated. Even if they are protected, some of them will fall gravely ill and die—albeit a small proportion. Also, if the state does nothing, the unjabbed will swamp the health service, causing a lot of deaths, including many that have nothing to do with covid. And more than one liberty is at stake. If governments have to resort to lockdowns to slow the spread of the disease,anti-vaxxers are depriving the vaccinated of freedom.

这些论点是错误的。疫情已经两年了，很明显，你的行为确实对你邻居会产生影响。一个原因是，如果你没有接种疫苗，你更有可能传染给其他人。即使他们受到保护，他们中的一些人会患重病并死亡，尽管只是一小部分。此外，如果政府什么都不做，未接种的人将涌入医疗服务，导致大量的死亡，包括许多没感染新冠的人。不止一项自由受到威胁。如果政府不得不采取封锁措施来减缓疾病的传播，反对接种疫苗的人就是在剥夺接种疫苗的人的自由。

What makes government intervention hard is not the question of principle, but the practical business of being effective and proportionate: of knowing what works at what cost and how this varies from one country to the next. The aim is not to eradicate covid—that would be impossible—but to slow its spread so that cases are manageable. Flattening the curve in this way saves lives by protecting health systems and buying time to administer vaccines and procure powerful new anti-covid medicines, such as molnupiravir.

让政府干预变得困难的并不是原则问题，而是有效和相称的实际业务:知道什么是有效的且需要花费多少成本，以及这在不同国家之间有何不同。其目标不是根除不可能根除的新冠病毒，而是减缓其传播，以便让感染病例处于可控范围内。以这种方式使曲线变平可以拯救生命，保护卫生系统，争取时间接种疫苗，并获得如molnupiravir等强大的新型抗冠状病毒药物。

The easy steps are promoting handwashing and demanding mask-wearing in public indoor spaces. Both reduce the spread of disease, but neither impinges greatly on anyone’s freedom. Next comes accelerating boosters, which protect individuals and society at large by rapidly restoring the partial ability to block transmission. This should be simple, because vaccine hesitancy should not be a factor with those who have already been jabbed. Countries can also enforce existing measures, such as requiring vaccination or a negative test for access to public places.

一个简单的步骤就是提倡洗手，并要求在公共室内空间佩戴口罩。两者都能减少疾病的传播，但都对任何人的自由没有太大影响。其次是加快加强针的注射，它通过迅速恢复阻断传播的部分能力来保护个人和整个社会。这应该很简单，因为对于那些已经注射过疫苗的人来说，对疫苗的犹豫不应该是一个影响因素。各国还可以执行已有的措施，例如要求进入公共场所的人需要接种疫苗或者提供阴性核酸报告。

That may not be enough to contain the surge in places like Germany and Austria, where most of these measures have been in force even as the disease has spread. In principle there is nothing wrong with demanding universal vaccination. In practice, however, it has enough drawbacks to give governments pause. A two-dose course will take weeks to become fully effective, even if anti-vaxxers comply. Austria is not requiring vaccination until February, when the wave is likely to be abating. In addition, if you believe that vaccination is a conspiracy by the state, compulsion only confirms your suspicions and attracts money and people to anti-vax campaigns. The policy could undermine faith in all vaccines for years to come. When, as in some European countries, government policies have failed, the only emergency brake is the misery of more lockdowns. ■

这可能不足以遏制德国和奥地利等地的激增，在这些地方，正当疾病传播时，这些措施的大多数都已经在实施了。原则上，要求全民接种疫苗没有什么错。然而，在实践中，它有足够多的缺点让各国政府踌躇不前。即使反疫苗者同意接种，两剂疫苗“疗程”需要数周才能完全有效。奥地利在2月份之前都不需要疫苗接种，那时疫情可能会减弱。此外，如果你认为接种疫苗是国家的阴谋活动，强迫接种只能加强你的怀疑，并吸引资金和人参与反疫苗运动。这一政策可能会在未来几年削弱人们对所有疫苗的信心。就像在一些欧洲国家一样，当政府政策失败时，唯一的紧急控制就是承受更多封锁的痛苦。■

Adventure capitalism

冒险资本主义

Technology investing

技术投资

The venture-capital industry is being supersized. Good

风险投资业正变得越来越庞大。好！

VLADIMIR LENIN believed that a tiny vanguard could, through force of will, harness historical forces to transform how global capitalism works. He was right. However, the revolutionaries have not been bearded Bolsheviks but a few thousand investors, mostly based in Silicon Valley, running less than 2% of the world’s institutional assets. In the past five decades, the venture-capital (VC) industry has funded enterprising ideas that have gone on to transform global business and the world economy. Seven of the world’s ten largest firms were VC-backed. VC money has financed the companies behind search engines, iPhones, electric cars and mRNA vaccines.

列宁认为，一个小小的领头人可以通过其意志力，利用历史力量改变全球资本主义的运作方式。他是对的。不过，革命者并不是大胡子的布尔什维克，而是几千名投资者，这些投资者大多位于硅谷，管理着不到2%的世界机构资产。在过去的50年里，风投行业资助了一些创业想法，这些想法后来改变了全球商业和世界经济。全球最大的10家公司中有7家是由风投支持的。风投资金已经为搜索引擎、iPhone、电动汽车和mRNA疫苗背后的公司提供了资金。

Now capitalism’s dream machine is itself being scaled up and transformed, as an unprecedented $450bn of fresh cash floods into the VC scene. This turbocharging of the venture world brings significant risks, from egomaniacal founders torching cash to pension pots being squandered on overvalued startups. But in the long run it also promises to make the industry more global, to funnel risk capital into a wider range of industries, and to make VC more accessible to ordinary investors. A larger pool of capital chasing a bigger universe of ideas will boost competition, and is likely to boost innovation, leading to a more dynamic form of capitalism.

如今，随着史无前例的4500亿美元新资金涌入风投领域，资本主义的造梦机本身也在扩大规模和转型。从利己的创始人烧钱，到将养老金挥霍在估值过高的初创公司上，风投世界的这种推动带来了重大风险。但从长远来看，它也有望使这个行业更加全球化，将风险资本引入更广泛的行业，也可以使普通投资者更容易接触到风投。更大的资本池追逐更大的思想领域将会促进竞争，并可能促进创新，导致更有活力的资本主义形式。

The VC scene has its roots in the 1960s and has been a misfit in the financial world. In contrast to Wall Street’s suits, sophistication and Hamptons mansions, it prefers fleeces, nerdiness and Californian villas. Its distinctiveness is also a matter of intellectual emphasis. As mainstream finance has grown bigger, more quantitative and more preoccupied with slicing and dicing the cashflows of mature firms and assets, VC has remained a cottage industry that cuts against the grain, seeking to find and finance entrepreneurs who are too callow or strange to sit in a room with staid bankers, and ideas that are too novel for MBAs to capture in financial models.

风投行业起源于上世纪60年代，在金融世界中一直格格不入。与华尔街的西装、世故以及汉普顿豪宅不同，它更偏向于羊毛衫、书呆子气以及加州别墅。它的独特性也是一个智力强调的问题。主流金融发展规模更大、更多的量化和专注于”分配”成熟公司的现金流和资产, 风投则一直是家庭手工业, 即“筛选”格格不入, 他们试图发现以及资助那些无经验的或者不习惯和呆板的银行家们呆在一个房间的企业家，以及那些对MBA来说太新奇而无法在金融模型中捕捉的想法。

The results have been striking. Despite investing relatively modest amounts over the decades, America’s VC funds have seeded firms that are today worth at least $18trn of the total public market. This record reflects the dizzying ascent of the big tech platforms such as Google. More recently VC-backed unicorns (private startups worth over $1bn) have come of age in a bonanza of public listings, ranging from the spectacular (Rivian) to the everyday (Slack). Over the past golden decade, an index of American VC funds has made compound annual returns of 17%. A few have done much better than that.

结果令人震惊。尽管在过去的几十年里，美国风投基金的投资规模相对较小，但它们已经“培养”出了如今在整个公开市场中至少价值18万亿美元的公司。这一记录也反映出谷歌等大型科技平台惊人的增长。最近，风投支持的独角兽公司(估值超过10亿美元的私人初创公司)通过大量公开上市而成熟起来，从引人注目的Rivian公司到普通的Slack公司。在过去的黄金十年里，美国风险投资基金的一个指数实现了17%的复合年收益率。很少有比这个好的。

This success is now spilling over into the broader financial industry. As VC-backed firms list, the resulting windfall is being redeployed into new funds. Meanwhile, with interest rates still low, pension schemes, sovereign-wealth vehicles and companies have got a feverish dose of VC-envy, and are scrambling to allocate more cash to dedicated funds or setting up their own VCarms. So far this year almost $600bn has been deployed in deals—ten times the level of a decade ago.

这种成功现在正蔓延到更广泛的金融业。随着风投支持的公司上市，由此产生的“意外之财”被重新配置到新的基金中。与此同时，在利率仍然很低的情况下，养老金计划、主权财富基金和公司都对风险资本产生了强烈的嫉妒，并争相将更多的资金分配到专门的基金或建立自己的风投部门。今年迄今为止，近6000亿美元被用于交易，这一数字是10年前水平的10倍。

As money pours in, VC is permeating the economy more deeply and broadly. What once was an American affair is now a global one, with 51% of deals by value in 2021 happening outside America. XX’s VC scene has tapered off recently because of a consumer-tech crackdown by XXX, the country’s president. However, the industry is booming in the rest of Asia and after decades of slumber, innovation in Europe is awakening, with 65 cities hosting unicorns.

随着资金的涌入，风投正更深入、更广泛地渗透到经济中。曾经是美国的事情，现在已经是全球的事情，2021年51%的交易发生在美国以外。由于XXX对消费科技行业的打击，XX国的风投交易最近逐渐减少。不过，该行业在亚洲其他地区正蓬勃发展，在沉睡了几十年之后，欧洲的创新正在觉醒，65个城市拥有独角兽企业。

The VC boom has so far focused on a narrow cohort of consumer-tech firms, such as Airbnb and Deliveroo. Now more cash may finance areas where disruption is less advanced. This year investments in clean energy, space and biotech were double those of 2019. And the industry is becoming more open. Whereas once a cosy elite of funds had unusual power, now mainstream financial firms are involved and there are vehicles that allow ordinary investors to gain exposure cheaply. Competition is forcing VCs of all kinds to experiment with new strategies, including tracking the careers of individual creators.

到目前为止，风投热潮主要集中在少数几家消费科技公司，比如Airbnb和Deliveroo。现在，更多的现金可能会用于那些颠覆较低的领域。今年在清洁能源、太空和生物技术方面的投资是2019年的两倍。这个行业正变得越来越开放。尽管曾经一群自满的基金精英拥有不寻常的权力，但现在主流金融公司也参与其中，并且有一些工具允许普通投资者以较低的成本获得敞口。竞争迫使各种类型的风投公司尝试新的策略，包括跟踪个人创造者的职业生涯。

Obviously, there are dangers. One is that money corrupts. Soaring valuations and abundant capital can make firms and their backers self-indulgent. Of the top 100 firms listed in 2021, 54 are in the red, with $71bn of cumulative losses. Governance can be abysmal. SoftBank’s $100bn Vision Fund, which pioneered writing vast cheques to startups and goading them to grow faster, has been beset by conflicts of interest. Founders go off the rails. Adam Neumann of WeWork built a beer-fuelled personality cult.

显然，这其中存在着危险。一是金钱堕落问题。估值的飙升以及充裕的资本会让公司及其支持者放任自己。在2021年上市的前100家公司中，有54家亏损，累计亏损710亿美元。治理可能是糟糕透顶的。软银旗下1000亿美元的愿景基金一直受到利益冲突的困扰。该基金率先向初创企业开出巨额支票，并激励它们更快增长。创始人的行为越轨了。WeWork的Adam Neumann建立了一种以啤酒为动力的个人崇拜。

Another danger is that, as in any asset class, returns are diluted as money pours in. Mainstream funds may find that, as well as having to cope with VC’s notorious booms and busts, long-run returns are lower than they hoped.

另一个危险是，就像任何资产类别一样，随着资金的涌入，回报会减少。主流基金可能会发现，除了不得不应付风投众人皆知的繁荣和萧条，长期回报比他们希望的要低。

Yet what is humdrum for investors can still be good for the economy. It is better that a marginal dollar goes to fledgling companies than to bloated housing or a flooded bond market. A VC crash triggered by rising interest rates would not destabilise the financial system, because startups have low debts. Even if VC-backed firms recklessly burn money, much of it will flow to consumers: think of all those subsidised car rides and home-delivered meals. At a minimum, the boom will enhance competition. VC investment this year will exceed total capital spending and research-and-development spending by the five biggest tech firms, which are also being discouraged from buying up potential competitors by the threat of tighter antitrust rules.

然而，对投资者来说平淡无奇的事情可能仍然对经济有利。把边际的一美元花在新兴公司身上，总比花在膨胀的房地产市场或泛滥的债券市场上要好。由利率上升引发的风险投资崩溃不会破坏金融系统的稳定，因为初创公司的债务很低。即使风投支持的公司不计后果地烧钱，大部分钱还是会流向消费者:想想那些乘车补贴和外卖补贴吧。至少，繁荣将增加竞争。今年的风投投资将超过五大科技公司的资本支出和研发支出总额。由于面临更严格的反垄断规定，这五家公司也不愿收购潜在竞争对手。

The biggest prize would be more innovation. It is true that no amount of cash can create raw brilliance. And governments often fund basic scientific breakthroughs. Yet the global supply of entrepreneurs is hardly fixed and plenty of ideas remain under-exploited. The previous VC boom saw investors extend the horizon of risk-taking to more difficult and adventurous areas. As venture investment spreads around the world, entrepreneurs outside America will have a better chance of joining in. And the barriers to creating new businesses are falling, thanks to cheap cloud computing and remote working. Venture capital aims to take good ideas and make them bigger and better: it is only right to apply that logic to the industry itself. ■

最大的奖励将是更多的创新。确实，再多的现金也无法创造出原生的才华。政府经常资助基础科学突破。然而，全球企业家的“供应”几乎是固定的，大量的想法仍未得到充分利用。在之前的风投热潮中，投资者将冒险的视野扩展到更困难、更冒险的领域。随着风投在世界各地的蔓延，美国以外的企业家将有更好的机会加入进来。由于廉价的云计算和远程工作，创建新企业的障碍正在减少。风投的目的是抓住好点子，并把它们做得更大更好:把这种逻辑应用到行业本身才是正确的。■

Democrats have a plan to lower drug costs without hurting innovation

民主党计划在不损害创新的情况下降低药品成本

Drug deals

药物特定政策

The party’s latest legislative proposal would bring the cost of medication more in line with the rest of the world

该党最新的立法提案将使医药成本与世界其他地区更多的保持一致

MOST DIABETICS need at least one shot of insulin a day to regulate their blood-sugar levels. Some need four, and in America, drug companies charge about $100 a vial—nearly seven times more than in any other OECD country. Older Americans, a third of whom live with diabetes, could soon pay as little as $35 a month thanks to a bill that the Democrats are trying to fast-track through Congress, much to the dismay of the pharmaceutical industry, which argues that the cost caps will damage innovation.

大多数糖尿病患者每天至少需要注射一次胰岛素来调节血糖水平。有的还需要每天打四针，在美国，制药公司每瓶收费100美元，这一价格几乎是其他经合组织国家的7倍。三分之一的美国老年人患有糖尿病，多亏民主党试图加速国会通过一项法案的进程，他们很快就可以每月支付35美元，这让制药行业大感不安，他们认为“成本上限”将损害创新。

Prescription drugs in America cost two and a half times more on average than in other developed countries. At the moment the federal government cannot negotiate with pharma firms over the prices of the drugs they sell, as other countries do. In Britain, for example, the NHS negotiates discounted prices with pharmaceutical companies, and only after another public body has determined if a drug is beneficial enough to justify the price. In America, pharmaceutical companies are, in effect, the price makers. If passed, this bill would allow the government to have some say in drug prices for the first time.

美国处方药的平均价格是其他发达国家的2.5倍。目前，联邦政府不能像其他国家那样，与制药公司就药品价格进行谈判。例如，在英国，只有在另一个公共机构确定某种药物的益处足以证明其价格合理之后，NHS才会与制药公司协商折扣价格。在美国，制药公司实际上是价格制定者。如果该法案通过，它将首次允许政府在药品价格上拥有一些发言权。

On behalf of Medicare patients (those aged 65 or older and the disabled), the bill could empower the health secretary to negotiate lower prices for ten of the costliest drugs by 2025 and 20 by 2028. This includes treatments typically given in hospitals, such as cancer drugs which can cost payers $20,000 a dose, and also prescription drugs taken at home. The plan would also ensure that these patients don’t spend more than $2,000 of their own money on medicine each year, a desirable development since an estimated 18m Americans can’t afford the drugs that they need.

为了医疗保险的病人(65岁或以上的老人和残疾人)的利益，该法案将授权卫生部长在2025年之前就10种最昂贵的药物的低价进行协商，到2028年，协商药品数要达到20种。这些药物通常包括在医院进行的治疗开的药物，比如一剂癌症药物可能要花费2万美元，也包括在家服用的处方药。该计划还将确保这些患者每年在自己的药物上的花费不超过2000美元，这是一个令人满意的发展，因为估计有1800万美国人负担不起他们需要的药物。

This is, unsurprisingly, not popular with the pharmaceutical industry. Many new drugs developed today rely on venture-capital investments in small biotech firms to carry out risky research. Investors are incentivised by the promise of substantial returns, since America’s pharmaceutical behemoths eventually sell these drugs at high prices. The plan before Congress also curbs pharmaceuticals from increasing some of their drug prices more than the rate of inflation each year. This sounds good for consumers, but PhRMA, the industry’s lobbying group, maintains that this bill will up-end America’s “global leadership in biomedical innovation”. Will it?

不出所料，这个法案并不受制药行业的欢迎。如今许多新药的研发都依赖于风投投资的小型生物技术公司来进行有风险的研究。投资者会被丰厚回报的承诺所激励，因为美国的制药巨头最终以高价出售这些药物。国会面前的法案还限制制药公司将一些药品的价格提高到高于每年的通胀水平。这听起来对消费者来说是件好事，但是制药行业的游说团体美国药品研究与管理协会(PhRMA)坚称，该法案将终结美国“在生物医学创新方面的全球领导地位”。它会吗?

Although high prices can subsidise research, the bill has been tweaked to reduce the impact on new drugs. The Democrats’ previous proposal would have introduced price negotiations and inflation caps for 250 drugs, for everyone. Had this been made law, the non-partisan Congressional Budget Office estimated that eight fewer drugs would have been brought to market over a decade (which is either a little or a lot, depending on your medical prognosis). But in the current plan, only ten of the costliest drugs will be affected at first by inflation caps. What is more, these caps will apply only after the drugs have been on the market for a while: nine years for cheaper small-molecule drugs and 12 years for the more complex biologics.

尽管高价可以补贴研究，但该法案已被调整以减少对新药的影响。民主党先前的提案将为250种药品引入价格协商和通胀上限，这对所有人来说都通用。据无党派的国会预算办公室估计，如果这项法案得以通过，十年内上市药物将会减少八种(这一数字可能少一些，也可能多一些，这取决于医疗预后)。但在目前的计划中，只有10种最昂贵的药物将首先受到通胀上限的影响。更重要的是，这些限制只适用于药物上市一段时间后: 便宜的小分子药物9年，更复杂的生物药物12年。

Pharmaceuticals and their investors often make their money back, and then some, much faster than this. Take Gilead Sciences, for example, which bought the biotech Pharmasset—and their Hepatitis C drug, Sovaldi—for $11.2bn in 2011. Gilead recouped almost all of that in 2014 alone. In short, the bill strikes a balance that minimises innovation risks while still granting the government some much-needed negotiating power. Even the representatives most critical of drug-pricing reforms, such as Senator Kyrsten Sinema, seem to have come around.■

Can an upset gut microbiome cause autism?

肠道菌群紊乱会导致自闭症吗?

Autism

自闭症

The latest evidence suggests not

最新的证据表明并非如此

THE GUT microbiome’s effect on health is so big that it is sometimes referred to as a forgotten organ. The trillions of critters in the lower alimentary canal have an influence that seems to reach all over the body—helping regulate weight, for example. In recent years, though, one idea in particular has intrigued researchers. This is that certain mixtures of gut microbes may help cause autism.

肠道菌群对健康的影响很大，以至于有时它被称为一个被遗忘的器官。下消化道的数万亿的生物似乎可以影响到全身，比如帮助调节体重。然而，近年来，有一个特别的想法引发了研究人员的兴趣。这就是肠道微生物的某些混合物可能会导致自闭症。

It is well known that children with autism suffer gastroenteric problems. They often also seem to have simpler gut floras than their neurotypical peers, and some observations report the presence of unusual species. This has led to the suggestion that these abnormal microecosystems might actually be triggering autism. So tempting is this thought that preliminary experiments intended to tweak the microbiomes of autistic children have been carried out, with apparently positive results.

众所周知，患有自闭症的儿童都会有些肠胃问题。它们的肠道菌群似乎也比神经正常的同类孩子更简单，一些观察报告显示其中存在不寻常的物种。这就导致了这样一种观点，即这些异常的微生态系统实际上可能是引发自闭症的原因。这种想法很诱人，以至于一些人已经进行了初步实验，试图改变自闭症儿童的微生物群，并取得了明显乐观的结果。

Unfortunately, the theory that gut microbes can cause autism is built on shaky foundations. The best evidence comes from work on mice, which is problematic because identifying murine behaviour patterns that correspond to human autism is as much an art as a science. Work on people, meanwhile, has been criticised for relying on samples too small to yield statistically definitive conclusions.

不幸的是，“肠道微生物会导致自闭症”的这一理论是建立在不可靠的基础上的。将“在小鼠上的研究”作为“最有力的证据”是有问题的，因为辨别鼠类的行为模式符合人类自闭症标准，既是一门科学，也更是一门艺术。与此同时，基于“人类的研究”也因样本过少不足以得出统计上权威的结论而遭到指责。

But no longer. A study published in Cell by Jacob Gratten of the University of Queensland, Australia, and his colleagues is reckoned to have enough statistical power to answer the question robustly. And that answer is “no”, gut microbes do not cause autism. Dr Gratten’s work indicates that, while there is indeed a correlation to be explained, it is actually autism which (albeit indirectly) affects the gut flora, not the other way around.

但现在不再是这样了[有可靠的理论基础了]。澳大利亚昆士兰大学的Jacob Gratten和他的同事发表在《Cell 》期刊上的一项研究被认为有足够的统计力能够坚定地回答这个问题。答案是否定的，也就是说肠道微生物不会导致自闭症。Gratten博士的工作表明，虽然确实存在某种相关性需要去解释，但实际上是自闭症(虽然是间接的)影响肠道菌群，而不是肠道菌群引发的自闭症。

Dr Gratten and his colleagues embarked on their project in 2016, using stool samples and dietary information collected as part of two large studies, the Australian Autism Biobank and the Queensland Twin Adolescent Brain Project. Of 247 children they selected for inclusion, 99 had been diagnosed with autism, 51 were neurotypical siblings of some of these, and the remainder were unrelated neurotypical children.

Gratten 博士和他的同事们于2016年开始了他们的项目，他们将收集到的粪便样本和日常饮食信息作为两项大型研究的一部分(数据)，这两项研究分别是澳大利亚自闭症生物样本库和昆士兰双胞胎青少年脑计划研究。在他们选择的247个孩子中，其中有99个被诊断患有自闭症，51个是其中一些孩子的正常的兄弟姐妹，其余的是没有关系的神经正常的孩子。

There was, they found, a clear connection between individual children’s dietary diversity and the diversity of their gut microbes—with more adventurous diets being associated with richer microbiomes. And since the sensory experiences associated with new foods can be overwhelming to them, autistic children are more likely than others to have restricted diets. Subtracting this effect left no remaining signal. The team thus found no connection between autism and microbial diversity beyond that brought about by autistic children’s food preferences. Nor was there any detectable connection between autism and particular bacterial species of the sort suggested by previous studies.

他们发现，儿童的饮食多样性与其肠道微生物的多样性之间存在着明显的联系，更冒险的饮食会导致更丰富的微生物群。由于与新的食物相关的感官体验对他们来说可能是无法抗拒的，所以自闭症儿童比其他儿童更有可能限制饮食。去除这一影响就没有其余讯号了。因此，研究小组发现，除了自闭症儿童的食物偏好之外，自闭症与微生物多样性之间没有联系。他们也没有发现自闭症与先前的研究提到的特定细菌种类之间的联系。

On the face of things, this is at odds with the findings of the treatment-by-microbiome-tweaking camp, led by Rosa Krajmalnik-Brown of Arizona State University. Her initial studies, admittedly small in number, suggested faecal transplants—used in extreme cases to treat infection with an intestinal bug called Clostridium difficile—had a positive effect on the behavioural traits of children on the autism spectrum, as well as on their gastrointestinal symptoms. She is now supervising clinical trials to test these findings more robustly.

表面上看，这与亚利桑那州立大学Rosa Krajmalnik-Brown主持的“通过微生物群落调整治疗”阵营的研究结果不一致。她最初的研究表明（虽然只是少量研究），粪便移植（在极端情况下用于治疗一种名为艰难梭菌的肠道细菌感染）对自闭症谱系儿童的行为特征和胃肠道症状都有积极的影响。她现在正在指导临床试验，以更有力地检验这些发现。

Whether there is really a contradiction remains to be seen. Dr Krajmalnik-Brown, while acknowledging the quality of Dr Gratten’s analysis, still thinks it cannot explain away the results she saw in the preliminary trials. But it is also possible that faecal transplantation, by relieving the uncomfortable symptoms brought about directly by unbalanced microbiomes, improves the behaviour of children with autism, yet does so without affecting the neural underpinnings of the condition. ■

是否真的存在矛盾还有待观察。Krajmalnik Brown博士虽然承认Gratten博士研究分析的质量，但她仍然认为这不能解释她在初步试验中看到的结果。但粪便移植也有可能通过缓解失衡的微生物组直接带来的不适症状，从而改善自闭症儿童的行为，但这并不会影响自闭症的神经基础。■

Drahi先生的商业头脑为辉煌的艺术市场“妙语”带来了新的意义，苏富比是“试图成为绅士的拍卖师”，相比而言，佳士得则是一家“试图成为拍卖师的绅士”。为了给这笔交易融资，这位大亨背负了远超10亿美元的债务，如今他可以接触到全球约30万富豪的详细信息。新的苏富比拍卖行不仅要卖艺术品，还要卖手提包和历史著作。

His timing may prove prescient. Contemporary art, which accounts for the single biggest share of the art market, saw a record-breaking $2.7bn change hands during the year to June, according to Artprice, which tracks sales. Both Sotheby’s and Christie’s say they expect their sales in 2021 to match the $4.8bn and $5.8bn they respectively made in 2019.

他(拿下苏富比的)时机可能证明是有先见之明的。追踪拍卖的Artprice的数据表明，截至今年6月，占艺术品市场最大份额的当代艺术品成交额达到创纪录的27亿美元。苏富比和佳士得拍卖行均表示，他们预计2021年的销售额将与2019年的48亿美元和58亿美元持平。

In part that is because both the main auction houses are expanding beyond their conventional offering of art, watches and wine—the market’s first big shift. In 2020 Christie’s sold a dinosaur fossil named Stan for $31.8m. Earlier this year Sotheby’s auctioned Kanye West’s Yeezy trainers for $1.8m. Both firms have jumped into crypto-art, selling non-fungible tokens (NFTs) to techies. All of these have brought in new buyers, especially from Asia, the fastest-growing market. Of the top 20 lots auctioned by Sotheby’s last year, Asian clients bid on ten and bought nine.

这在一定程度上是因为两家主要拍卖行都在拓展艺术品、手表和葡萄酒的传统业务，这是拍卖市场的第一个重大转变。2020年，佳士得以3180万美元的价格拍出了一具名叫Stan的恐龙化石。今年早些时候，苏富比拍卖行以180万美元的价格拍卖了侃爷的椰子运动鞋。这两家公司都投身于加密艺术，向技术迷出售NFTs。所有这些都引来新的买家，尤其是来自增长最快的亚洲市场。在去年苏富比拍卖的前20件拍品中，亚洲客户竞拍了10件，最终买到了9件。

A second new development is that the two houses are wooing new customers by making buying at auction more fun. Last month Sotheby’s organised a weekend jamboree in Las Vegas for 40 clients. The main business was the auction of $100m-worth of artworks by Picasso. But in an effort to turn the affair into more of an experience, Sotheby’s also laid on wine-tasting, a session on how to game an auction and a talk by Jay Leno about vintage cars. At the party after the sale, the DJ was Picasso’s great-grandson.

第二个新进展是，这两家拍卖行通过让拍卖变得更有趣来吸引新客户。上个月，苏富比在拉斯维加斯为40位客户组织了一场周末狂欢。主要业务是拍卖价值1亿美元的毕加索作品。但为了让这场拍卖会更像是一种体验，苏富比还安排了品酒会、一个关于如何在拍卖中博弈的讨论会，以及一场Jay Leno关于古董车的演讲。在拍卖结束后的派对上，DJ还是毕加索的曾孙。

Going, going, gone

The most far-reaching shift, though, may be the auction houses’ new cosy relationship with commercial galleries and private dealers. Historically these were their great rivals. Galleries know where the art is and what their clients might be prepared to sell, but lack the access to buyers who flock to auction houses. Now the two work more closely together, to find the right buyer for a piece and vice versa.

然而，影响最深远的转变可能是拍卖行与商业画廊和私人经销商之间新的亲密关系。历史上，他们是他们最大的对手。画廊知道艺术品在哪里，也知道他们的客户可能准备卖什么，但却无法接触到涌向拍卖行的买家。现在这两个对手合作更加紧密，为一件作品找到合适的买家，以及为合适的买家找某件作品。

When a Düsseldorf gallerist recently wanted to sell a Gerhard Richter from the 1970s, an under-appreciated period, he turned to Sotheby’s. The private sale to one of its clients was at a far better price than he would have got at auction or selling to one of his own collectors, he says. In April 2020, a month after the pandemic hit, Rafael Valls, an dealer in Old Masters in London, was able to sell nearly 100 pictures in an online Sotheby’s auction; in a normal year the gallery would sell around 200.

一位杜塞尔多夫画廊老板最近想出售格哈德·里希特在上世纪70年代的一幅作品，那是一个没有得到充分赏识的时期，于是他向苏富比拍卖行求助。他说，与他在拍卖会上拍卖或者是卖给自己的收藏者相比，私下出售给一位客户的价格要高得多。2020年4月，即在疫情爆发一个月后，伦敦“老大师”的经销商Rafael Valls在苏富比的在线拍卖会上卖出了近100幅画作; 正常情况下，画廊一年才能卖出200张左右。

In a move that highlights this rapprochement between auction houses and dealers, Sotheby’s recently hired Noah Horowitz, a director of the Art Basel art fair who is known to be particularly close to galleries. “Sotheby’s is tearing up the traditional playbookplaybook,” says a rival. The marriage is partly one of financial convenience: galleries lack the pools of capital big auction houses deploy to offer guarantees and thus lure potential sellers. Teaming up with dealers helps auctioneers find works to sell, which is almost as hard for them as identifying the next generation of buyers.

苏富比最近聘请了巴塞尔艺术展的总监Noah Horowitz，他以与画廊关系特别密切而闻名。此举突显出拍卖行与经销商之间的友好关系的恢复。一位竞争对手说，“苏富比正在撕毁传统的剧本”。这次的紧密结合在一定程度上是为了经济上的便利: 画廊缺少大型拍卖行用来提供担保、从而吸引潜在卖家的资金池。拍卖行与经销商合作有助于拍卖师找到可以出售的作品，这对他们来说几乎和确认下一代买家一样困难。【双赢】

Sotheby’s and Christie’s hope their new approach will help both sides of the trade. When Christie’s sold its first piece of crypto-art earlier this year, its boss Guillaume Cerutti points out, almost all of the 33 bidders were new to the firm. A few days later one of those who had been outbid, a 31-year-old Chinese-American tech entrepreneur named Justin Sun, went on to buy a $20m Picasso—and, in the Macklowe sale, a Giacometti sculpture for $78m. ■

苏富比和佳士得希望他们的新方式将有助于交易双方。佳士得的老板Guillaume Cerutti指出，今年早些时候，当佳士得卖出第一件加密艺术品时，这33名竞拍者中几乎所有的人都是该公司的新买家。几天后，在之前竞标中落败的31岁美籍华人科技企业家孙宇晨以2000万美元的价格买下了毕加索的一幅作品，他还在Macklowe的拍卖会上，以7800万美元的价格买下了贾科梅蒂的一件雕塑。■

The business phrasebook

商业常用手册

Bartleby

巴托比

A short guide to what your colleagues really mean

你的同事真正想表达的意思的简要指南

REED HASTINGS HAS built the culture at Netflix around it. Ray Dalio made it a founding principle at Bridgewater, a successful investment fund. “Radical candour” is the idea that bracing honesty is the best way to run a business: no one dances around the truth, and swifter feedback improves performance.

REED HASTINGS围绕这一主题打造了Netflix的文化。Ray Dalio将其作为成功投资基金Bridgewater的创始原则。“彻底坦诚”是一种“认为诚实是经营企业的最佳方式”的理念: 没有人回避事实，而且更快的反馈能提高业绩。

Most firms rely on a messier doctrine. People rarely say what they mean, but hope that their meaning is nonetheless clear. Think Britain, but with paycheques. To navigate this kind of workplace, you need a phrasebook.

大多数公司依赖于一种更混乱的教条。人们很少说出他们的意思，但又希望他们的意思是清楚的。想想拿着薪水支票的英国吧。想要在这种工作环境中“游刃有余”，你需要一本常用语手册。

“I hear you”

Ostensible meaning: You’re making a legitimate point
Actual meaning: Be quiet

“我听到了”

表面上的意思是：你提出了一个合理的观点

实际意思: 安静点吧！

“Let’s discuss this offline”

Ostensible meaning: We shouldn’t waste other people’s valuable time
Actual meaning: Let’s never speak of this again (see also: “Let’s put a pin in it”)

“我们会后讨论吧”

表面意思: 我们不应该浪费别人的宝贵时间
实际意思: 别再提这件事了(也可以用Let ‘s put a pin in it)

“We should all learn to walk in each other’s shoes”

Ostensible meaning: Shared understanding results in better outcomes
Actual meaning: I need you to know that my job is a living hell

“我们都应该学会设身处地为他人着想”

表面意思: 达成共识会带来更好的结果
实际意思: 我想要你知道我的工作简直就是一个人间地狱

“I’m just curious…”

Ostensible meaning: I’d like to know why you think that…
Actual meaning: …because it makes no sense to anyone else

“我只是好奇……”

表面意思: 我想知道你为什么认为……
实际意思: …因为这对其他人来说毫无意义

“It’s great to have started this conversation”

Ostensible meaning: We’ve raised an important issue here
Actual meaning: We’ve made absolutely no progress

“很高兴能开始这次谈话”

表面意思: 我们在这里提出了一个重要的问题
实际意思: 我们毫无进展

“I wanted to keep you in the loop”

Ostensible meaning: I am informing you of something minor
Actual meaning: I should have told you this weeks ago

“我想让你知道目前的最新进度”

表面意思: 我要向你报告一些不太重要的事儿
实际意思: 我本应该在几周前就告诉你的

“Do you have five minutes?”

Ostensible meaning: I have something trivial to say
Actual meaning: You are in deep, deep trouble

“你有五分钟时间吗?”

表面意思: 我有一些不太重要的事儿要说
实际意思: 你麻烦大了

“Let’s handle this asynchronously”

Ostensible meaning: We’ll each work on this task in our own time
Actual meaning: I have to go to my Pilates class now

“让我们异步处理此问题”

表面意思: 我们将在各自的时间完成这项任务
实际意思: 我现在得去上普拉提课了

“It’s on the product roadmap”

Ostensible meaning: It’ll be done soon
Actual meaning: It won’t be done soon

“它在产品路线图上”

表面意思: 它将很快就会完成
实际意思: 它并不会很快完成的

“We’re moving to an agile framework”

Ostensible meaning: We will work iteratively in response to user feedback
Actual meaning: We’re literally planning to go round in circles

“我们正在转向敏捷框架”

表面意思: 我们将以迭代的方式来回应用户的反馈
实际意思: 我们实际打算在原地兜圈子

“It’s a legacy tech stack”

Ostensible meaning: It’s a rat’s nest of old and incompatible systems
Actual meaning: None of this is our fault

“这是一个传统的技术栈”

表面意思: 这是一个老旧的不兼容系统的老鼠窝
实际意思: 这一切都不是我们的错

“We are a platform business”

Ostensible meaning: We provide an ecosystem in which others can interact
Actual meaning: Let’s pretend we are a tech firm and see what happens to our valuation (see also: “as a service”, “network effects” and “flywheels”)

“我们是一个平台企业”

表面意思: 我们提供了一个其他人可以互动的生态系统
实际意思: 假设我们是一家科技公司，看看我们的估值会发生什么变化(参见:“X即服务”，“网络效应”和“飞轮效应”)

“We are planning for the metaverse”

Ostensible meaning: We are ready for a shared, immersive digital world
Actual meaning: Ooh, look! A bandwagon! (see also: “Web3”)

“我们正在计划元宇宙”

表面意思: 我们已经为一个共享的、沉浸式的数字世界做好了准备
实际意思: 哦，看! 又一个跟风的!

“Bring your whole selves to work”

Ostensible meaning: Be authentic and don’t be afraid to show vulnerability
Actual meaning: But not those bits of your whole self, obviously

“在工作中表现出真实的自己”

表面意思: 真实点，不要害怕表现出你的脆弱
实际意思: 但显然不是你整个自我

In a world of radical candour, there would be less need for translating. Most managers and colleagues could indeed be better at giving unvarnished feedback. Some words and phrases are so opaque they absorb all visible meaning.

在一个彻底坦诚的世界里，这不太需要翻译。大多数管理者和同事确实更善于给出真实的反馈。有些词和短语非常晦涩难懂，它们掌握了所有明显的意思。

But there is an awful lot to be said for coded communication. Work is where people learn to manage social interactions, not define them out of existence. Transparency doesn’t necessarily travel well across borders. And perpetual bluntness is draining; humans constantly finesse and massage the messages they send in order to avoid open conflict. Radical candour is associated with firms that pay very well. That may be because this approach leads to greater success. It may be because otherwise most people wouldn’t put up with it.

不过，对于“编码”后的表达，还有很多话需要说清楚。工作是人们学习完成社交互动的地方，而不是定义他们的存在的地方。透明不一定能很好地跨越国界。永远的直率让人筋疲力尽; 为了避免公开冲突，人类在传递信息时不断运用技巧和手法。彻底坦诚与那些高薪公司有关系。一方面，这种方法会带来更大的成功。另一方面，要不是因为钱到位了，不然大多数人也不会忍受它。
