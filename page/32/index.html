For all America’s success in helping Ukraine, hard times lie ahead

尽管美国在帮助乌克兰方面取得了成功，但艰难的日子还在前面

Ukraine’s backers

乌克兰的支持者

![image-20220326094801995](/Users/chenyujie/Library/Application Support/typora-user-images/image-20220326094801995.png)

Joe Biden must hold together a fractious coalition

乔·拜登必须团结一个易怒的联盟

Mar 26th 2022

VLADIMIR PUTIN’S epoch-changing miscalculation over Ukraine was the outcome of a trio of mission-defining misjudgments. One was that Ukraine’s government would collapse quickly. Another was that his modernised army would be dominant. The last was that America, in irreversible decline, was no longer capable of leadership. For Ukraine to be defeated, Mr Putin had only to get one premise right.

普京在乌克兰问题上改变时代意义的误判，是三种定义使命的误判的结果。其一是乌克兰政府将很快垮台。另一个原因是他的现代化军队将占据主导地位。最后是美国，在不可逆转的衰退中，不再有能力领导。要想打败乌克兰，普京只需正确把握一个前提。

The first two misjudgments have caused general surprise. The third has stirred up general interest, among allies and foes alike. In recent years America has seemed less committed to the institutions it created after the second world war, largely as a result of its own disastrous occupation of Iraq following the attacks of September 11th 2001. Barack Obama chose to “reset” relations with Russia after it invaded Georgia in 2008 and failed to enforce a red line against the use of chemical weapons in Syria. Donald Trump accused his allies of duping America and called NATO “obsolete”. After America’s humiliating flight from Kabul last summer, Mr Putin seemed to have concluded that Joe Biden would be unable or unwilling to do much for Ukraine.

前两个错误的判断引起了普遍的惊讶。第三个问题激起了盟国和敌人的普遍兴趣。近年来，美国似乎没那么忠于二战后建立的机构了，这主要是由于美国在2001年9月11日的袭击后对伊拉克的灾难性占领。在俄罗斯于2008年入侵格鲁吉亚，且未能在叙利亚使用化学武器的问题上划定红线之后，奥巴马选择了“重启”与俄罗斯的关系。特朗普指责他的盟友欺骗美国，并称北约“过时”。在去年夏天美国丢脸的逃离喀布尔之后，普京似乎已经得出结论，乔•拜登将无法或不愿为乌克兰做很多事情。

Today, as Mr Biden lands in Europe for NATO, G7 and EU summits, it is clear that America has confounded Mr Putin by being innovative, agile and resolute. It understands that the security of Europe is at stake in Ukraine. The question is whether that success can survive the tests that lie ahead. At home, destructive political partisanship is once more rearing its head. In Europe, the coalition that America so carefully stitched together is beginning to fray.

如今，当拜登抵达欧洲参加北约(NATO)、七国集团(G7)和欧盟(EU)峰会时，很明显，美国的革新、灵活和果断让普京感到困惑。它明白，乌克兰事关欧洲的安全。问题是，这种成功能否经受住未来的考验。在美国国内，破坏性的政治党派之争再次发生。在欧洲，美国精心打造的联盟开始出现摩擦。

America’s innovation began before the war, with the unprecedented release of intelligence. Along with Britain, the Biden administration issued detailed, up-to-the-minute warnings about Russian troops massing on Ukraine’s borders, would-be provocations, the plan of attack and a blueprint for a puppet government. This robbed Mr Putin of the power to disorientate that served him so well in the seizure of Crimea in 2014. The supply has continued in battle. Information from intercepted communications, NATO aircraft and satellites is rapidly given to Ukrainian forces, who use it for targeting.

美国的革新始于战前，伴随着前所未有的情报发布。与英国一道，拜登政府就俄罗斯军队集结在乌克兰边境、可能的挑衅、袭击计划和傀儡政府蓝图发出了最新的详细警告。这剥夺了普京的迷失方向的控制力，而正是这种控制力让他在2014年占领克里米亚时受益匪浅。在战斗中，供应仍在继续。从截获的通信、北约飞机和卫星中获得的信息会迅速提供给乌克兰军队，乌克兰军队则利用这些信息进行目标定位。

The agility was on display when America changed course in the opening phase of the war. Mr Putin was not alone in thinking that Kyiv would fall within days. The Biden administration offered Volodymyr Zelensky safe passage out of the capital. Ukraine’s president vowed to stay even as his forces repelled Russian paratroopers. America and its allies responded with more weapons and fresh sanctions.

当美国在战争的开始阶段改变路线时，这种灵活性就显现出来了。普京并不是唯一一个认为基辅将在几天内垮台的人。拜登政府为泽连斯基提供了离开首都的安全通道。恰好在乌克兰军队击退了俄罗斯伞兵的时候，乌克兰总统誓言要留下来。美国及其盟友以更多武器和新的制裁作为回应。

And NATO has demonstrated resolve. In 2019 Emmanuel Macron, France’s president, warned that it was suffering “brain death”. Today it is reinforcing its eastern flank. Germany, its second-wealthiest member, has overturned decades of timid defence policy by agreeing to give Ukraine arms and pledging to increase spending. In its wholehearted leadership of NATO, American diplomacy has recovered from the lows of the Trump years.

北约已经展示了决心。2019年，法国总统马克龙(Emmanuel Macron)警告称，北约正在遭受“脑死亡”。今天，它正在加强其东翼。德国是其第二富有的成员国，它通过同意向乌克兰提供武器并承诺增加支出，推翻了几十年来胆小的国防政策。在对北约全心全意领导的过程中，美国的外交已经从特朗普时代的低谷中复苏。

The fact that the war has lasted so long is an endorsement of Mr Biden’s support. But, as it drags on, sustaining that support is becoming harder. At home some Republicans have taken to blaming Mr Biden for the war, arguing however implausibly that the real cause of the invasion was Kabul and American acquiescence over a German gas pipeline coming from Russia. They accuse Mr Biden of being weak.

战争持续如此之久的事实是对拜登支持的认可。但随着时间的推移，维持这种支持正变得越来越困难。在美国国内，一些共和党人已经开始将战争归咎于拜登，他们认为，美国入侵阿富汗的真正原因是喀布尔，以及美国默许德国从俄罗斯进口天然气管道，这一点令人难以置信。他们指责拜登软弱。

In the long run, partisanship is a grave threat to American influence abroad. Mr Biden’s best riposte to his critics is to throw his efforts into dealing with the other, far more urgent political problem, which lies in Europe. This is the first sign of weariness in the coalition helping Ukraine withstand the Russian army.

从长远来看，党派之争对美国在海外的影响力是一个严重的威胁。拜登对批评他的人最好的回击是努力解决另一个更为紧迫的政治问题，即欧洲问题。这是帮助乌克兰抵御俄罗斯军队的联盟中出现的第一个厌倦迹象。

Ukraine says it is short of arms. There have been pledges, including this week from Britain, but supplies could arrive too late. Ukraine also complains that NATO’s distinction between defensive weapons, such as anti-tank missiles, and offensive weapons, such as aircraft, is meaningless when the invader is bent on destruction. Diplomatic sources accuse Germany and Hungary, in particular, of standing in the way of extra sanctions.

乌克兰表示缺少武器。承诺已有(包括本周来自英国的承诺)，不过供应可能来得太迟。乌克兰还抱怨说，当入侵者一心要毁灭时，北约对防御性武器(如反坦克导弹)和进攻性武器(如飞机)的区分毫无意义。外交人士特别指责德国和匈牙利阻碍了进一步的制裁。

All this is becoming an urgent problem. Mariupol, a city with a population of 400,000 before the invasion, is being razed to the ground by Russian artillery. Civilians, including children, have been deported to Russia. Mr Biden has warned that Mr Putin may be about to order the use of chemical or biological weapons. As Russian atrocities mount, Ukraine will need more help. When Mr Biden meets European heads of government he will have to stiffen their resolve. If he fails to unite his allies, his good work will have been squandered. ■

这一切正成为一个紧迫的问题。入侵前拥有40万人口的马里乌波尔正在被俄罗斯的炮火夷为平地。包括儿童在内的平民被流放到俄罗斯。拜登警告称，普京可能会下令使用化学或生物武器。随着俄罗斯暴行的增加，乌克兰将需要更多的帮助。当拜登会见欧洲各国政府首脑时，他将不得不坚定他们的决心。如果他不能团结他的盟友，他的成果就会付诸东流。■

Why energy insecurity is here to stay

为什么能源不安全会持续存在

Geopolitics and energy

地缘政治和能源

![image-20220325093153747](/Users/chenyujie/Library/Application Support/typora-user-images/image-20220325093153747.png)

The war will speed the shift from petrostates to new electrostates

战争将加速石油国家向新的电力国家的转变

Mar 26th 2022

ENERGY AND commodities lie at the dark heart of Vladimir Putin’s regime and the threat it poses to the world. Four trillion dollars of oil and gas exports over the two decades of his rule have paid for the tanks, guns and Grad missiles now killing Ukrainians. Natural-resource earnings have entrenched a rent-seeking elite that has created an offshore archipelago of yachts, nightclubs and Caribbean front companies, stifled representative politics and indulged Mr Putin’s megalomaniacal fantasies.

能源和大宗商品是普京政权及对世界构成威胁的隐秘核心。在他统治的20年里，四万亿美元的石油和天然气出口是为“现在正在侵害乌克兰人的坦克、枪支和Grad导弹”买单。自然资源的收益使寻租的精英阶层根深蒂固，他们创造了一个由游艇、夜总会和加勒比海门面公司组成的离岸群岛，扼杀了代议制政治，纵容了普京狂妄自大的幻想。

As Russia supplies 10-25% of the world’s oil, gas and coal exports, many countries, especially in Europe, are vulnerable to coercion by it. For them, the war in Ukraine has been a shock that adds urgency to the creation of an energy system which depends more on sun, wind and nuclear reactors than on derricks and rigs. Yet don’t fool yourself that this new era will allow an easy escape from the curse of energy crises and autocrats.

由于俄罗斯供应世界10%到25%的石油、天然气和煤炭出口，许多国家，尤其是欧洲国家，很容易受到俄罗斯的胁迫。对他们来说，在乌克兰的战争是一场冲击，它增加了建立能源系统的紧迫性，该系统更多地依赖于太阳能、风能和核反应堆，而不是钻井设备。然而，不要自欺欺人地认为，这个新时代会让我们轻易地摆脱能源危机和独裁者的祸患。

Weeks of chaos in energy markets are beginning to hurt consumers. Petrol prices in Los Angeles are over $6 a gallon for the first time. As sanctions on Russia bite, traders predict, Europe will run short of diesel. Germany is preparing to ration natural gas next winter, in case Russia cuts off supplies. In Asia, oil importers are bracing for a balance-of-payments hit. In a tight market, shocks are hard to absorb. Oil spiked at $122 per barrel this week after a pipeline from Central Asia to the Black Sea suffered storm damage and Iranian-backed Houthi rebels attacked Saudi energy facilities.

能源市场持续数周的混乱已经开始伤害到消费者。洛杉矶的汽油价格首次超过每加仑6美元。随着对俄罗斯的制裁产生不良影响，交易员们预测，欧洲将出现柴油短缺。德国正准备明年冬天对天然气实行定量供应，以防俄罗斯切断供应。在亚洲，石油进口国正面临国际收支方面的冲击。在市场紧张的情况下，冲击很难被吸收。本周油价飙升至每桶122美元，原因是中亚通往黑海的输油管道遭遇风暴破坏，以及伊朗支持的胡塞叛乱者袭击了沙特的能源设施。

The immediate reaction of governments everywhere has been to scramble to find more fossil fuels, however polluting to the environment or painful to their pride. With Western encouragement, Saudi Aramco, the world’s biggest oil firm, is raising investment to $40bn-50bn a year. At one point, the Biden administration buttered up Nicolás Maduro, Venezuela’s dictator, perhaps to get more oil from a state which in 2005 supplied 4% of the world’s crude.

世界各国政府的第一反应是争相寻找更多的化石燃料，无论这种燃料对环境造成多大污染，也无论它对他们的自尊心造成多大伤害。在西方的鼓励下，世界上最大的石油公司沙特阿美正将每年的投资提高到400亿至500亿美元。拜登政府一度奉承委内瑞拉独裁者Nicolás Maduro，或许是为了从这个2005年供应全球4%原油的国家获得更多石油。

The longer-term question being asked by many is: how fast can they abandon fossil fuels altogether? The energy strategy announced this month by the EU envisages independence from Russia by 2030—in part by finding new sources of gas, but also by doubling down on renewables. As the folly of relying on Russia becomes clear, nuclear power is back in fashion. France plans to construct six new plants and is aiming for “total energy independence”. On March 21st Britain said it would build a new generation of reactors at “warp speed”. A redesigned energy system that will belch out less carbon also promises an escape from the 20th century’s great game of relying on energy from despots.

许多人提出的长期问题是:他们能多快完全放弃化石燃料? 欧盟本月宣布的能源战略设想到2030年能从依赖俄罗斯中独立出来，部分是通过寻找新的天然气来源，但也要加倍投资可再生能源。随着依赖俄罗斯的愚蠢之处越来越明显，核能又重新流行起来。法国计划建造6座新核电站，并旨在实现“完全的能源独立”。3月21日，英国宣布将“以惊人的速度”建造新一代核反应堆。一个重新设计的能源系统将释放出更少的碳，这也预示着其有望摆脱20世纪依赖专制君主能源的伟大游戏。

Yet although geopolitics will hasten the climate-driven energy transition, they will not make it risk-free. The transition will disrupt some economies and cause new dependence on others. To gauge this we have simulated spending on a basket of ten natural resources, including oil and coal, and the metals used in power generation and the electrification of industry and transport. As the world decarbonises, spending on this basket will fall from 5.8% of GDP to 3.4% by 2040. Yet in our simulation over half of that will still go to autocracies, including new electrostates that provide green metals such as copper and lithium. The top ten countries will have a market share of over 75% in all our minerals, which means production will be dangerously concentrated.

然而，尽管地缘政治将加速由气候驱动的能源转型，但它们也不会让这种转型毫无风险。这种转变将破坏一些经济体，并导致对其他经济体的新的依赖。为了衡量这一点，我们模拟了一篮子“十种自然资源”的支出，其中包括石油和煤炭，以及用于发电和工业及运输电气化的金属。随着世界脱碳，到2040年，这一篮子的支出将从GDP的5.8%下降到3.4%。然而，在我们的模拟中，其中一半以上支出仍将流向独裁国家，包括提供铜和锂等绿色金属的新电力国家。排名前十的国家在所有矿产中的市场份额将超过75%，这意味着生产将非常集中。

Two problems therefore stand out. First, the geopolitics of shrinking the oil industry are fraught. As Western firms withdraw for environmental reasons and in response to high costs, the market share of OPEC plus Russia will rise from 45% to 57% by 2040, giving them more clout. Higher-cost producers such as Angola and Azerbaijan face a shock as they are squeezed out. The world map will be peppered with distressed ex-petrostates.

因此，有两个问题凸显出来。首先，石油行业萎缩的地缘政治令人担忧。随着西方公司出于环境原因和高成本而退出，到2040年，OPEC和俄罗斯的市场份额将从45%上升到57%，这将给它们更大的影响力。随着安哥拉和阿塞拜疆等成本较高的产油国被挤出市场，它们将面临冲击。世界地图上将遍布陷入困境的前石油国家。

Second, the emerging electrostates face their own battle with the resource curse. Spending on green metals will surge amid a two-decade-long build-out of electric infrastructure. The windfall may be worth over $1trn a year by 2040. Some beneficiaries, such as Australia, are well-equipped to deal with this. More fragile states, including Congo, Guinea and Mongolia, are not. Mountains of cash distort economies and feed grievances. Mining was a source of discord in recent elections in Chile and Peru. Global mining firms are nervous that their property rights will be buried. A resulting lack of investment has sent the price of a basket of green metals up by 64% in the past year. All this is compounded by X, which is hunting for the same resources, but is more tolerant of bad governments.

其次，新兴的电力国家也面临着与资源诅咒的斗争。在长达20年的电力基础设施建设中，在绿色金属上的支出将激增。到2040年，这笔意外之财每年可能价值超过1万亿美元。一些受益者，如澳大利亚，已经做好了应对这一问题的准备。刚果、几内亚和蒙古等更脆弱的国家则不然。大量的现金扭曲了经济，助长了不满情绪。在最近的智利和秘鲁选举中，采矿业是不和谐的根源。全球矿业公司都担心他们的产权会被埋没。由于缺乏投资，一篮子绿色金属的价格在去年上涨了64%。X也在寻找同样的资源，但其对不好的政府的容忍度更高，这让这一切更加复杂。

As with all commodities, soaring prices will eventually trigger a market response. Tight supply gives firms a huge incentive to step up recycling and to innovate. New kinds of small-scale nuclear reactors are emerging. Tesla, which uses minerals to make electric cars, is developing new battery designs. It has also struck a supply deal with New Caledonia, a Pacific territory of 277,000 people you will hear more about because it has a tenth of the world’s nickel reserves. This month Barrick, a Canadian firm, took a deep breath and agreed to develop a $10bn copper mine in Pakistan.

与所有大宗商品一样，价格飙升最终将引发市场反应。供应紧张给了企业巨大的动力来加快回收和创新。新型的小型核反应堆正在出现。使用矿物制造电动汽车的特斯拉正在开发新的电池设计。它还与新喀里多尼亚达成了一项供应协议。新喀里多尼亚是一个拥有27.7万人口的太平洋地区，它拥有世界上十分之一的镍储备。本月，加拿大Barrick公司深吸了一口气，同意在巴基斯坦开发一个价值100亿美元的铜矿。

High-voltage rewiring

Yet even as markets respond, governments must also redouble their efforts. Because self-sufficiency is rarely an option, diversification is the goal. That means new partnerships. On March 20th Germany began talks with Qatar for gas. The invigoration of the rich world’s nuclear industry is key, not least because it frees everyone else from relying on Russian technology. Governments must catalyse mining investment. Firms should not be free to blow up sacred caves or endanger workers, but the transition requires more mining projects in high-risk countries at a cost to the local ecology. Governance rules in wealthy countries need to acknowledge the trade-off. Finally, rich-world governments should help electrostates prepare by, for example, helping design model contracts for a fair split of revenues and establishing sovereign-wealth funds to save the bounty.

然而，即使市场有所反应，政府也必须加倍努力。由于自给自足几乎不算是一种选择，其目标应该是多样化。这意味着新的合作关系。3月20日，德国开始与卡塔尔就天然气问题进行谈判。发达国家核工业的振兴是关键，尤其是因为它使其他国家摆脱了对俄罗斯技术的依赖。政府必须促进矿业投资。公司不应该随意炸毁神圣的洞穴或危及工人，但这种转变需要在高风险国家开展更多采矿项目，以牺牲当地的生态环境为代价。发达国家的治理规则需要承认这种取舍。最后，发达国家的政府应该帮助电力国家做好准备，例如，帮助设计公平分配收入的示范合同，以及建立主权财富基金以节省资金。

Building a cleaner and safer energy system is an epic, risky and daunting task. But whenever resolve flags, ask yourself: would you rather rely on Mr Putin’s Russia? ■

建设一个更清洁、更安全的能源系统是一项艰苦卓绝的、充满风险且艰巨的任务。但无论何时决心衰减，你都要问问自己: 你宁愿依赖普京领导下的俄罗斯吗?■

The invasion of Ukraine has helped entrench Emmanuel Macron

俄乌之战帮助马克龙站稳了脚跟

Unassailable

绝对优势

The mood of the campaign for next month’s election has darkened

下个月选举的竞选气氛变得沉重

Mar 19th 2022

THERE WAS no bass beat to pump up the audience, nor banked rows of flag-waving supporters. Emmanuel Macron’s first campaign event since he declared formally on March 3rd that he is running for re-election as president next month was a low-key affair. In a municipal hall (and former vaccination centre) in the town of Poissy, west of Paris, he took mostly friendly questions from a modest audience of some 250 people. The first two raised a matter of great concern during this campaign: Russia’s war in Ukraine.

没有鼓舞观众的低音，也没有一排排挥舞着旗帜的支持者。马克龙自3月3日正式宣布下月将竞选连任总统以来的首次竞选活动是低调的。在巴黎西部普瓦西镇的市政厅(以前也是疫苗接种中心)，他从不多的观众中选出了250名观众的一些问题进行回答，这些问题大多是友好的。前两个问题在这次竞选中引起了极大的关注: 俄罗斯在乌克兰的战争。

Anxiety about the war has turned Mr Macron’s campaign for re-election, at a two-round vote on April 10th and 24th, into what increasingly looks like a foregone conclusion. By March 15th The Economist’s forecasting model put his chances of winning at 97%. Even as a candidate, he is spending more time telephoning world leaders than shaking hands on the campaign trail. Rivals are struggling to find the right tone to criticise his leadership. “He’s hanging over this campaign at such a height that it’s very difficult to get at him,” grumbles a member of a rival team.

对战争的焦虑已使马克龙在4月10日和24日举行的两轮投票中的连任竞选活动越来越像是一个定局。截至3月15日，《经济学人》的预测模型显示他获胜的几率为97%。即使作为一名候选人，他花在给世界领导人打电话上的时间也比在竞选活动中握手的时间多。竞争对手正努力找到批评他领导能力的正确基调。一个竞争对手团队的成员抱怨道:“他在这场竞选中处于如此高度，以至于很难够得着他。”

Mr Macron certainly knows how to make the most of this. “Before coming here I was on the phone with President Biden,” he dropped casually into the conversation in Poissy; “tomorrow I will be [on a call] with President XXX.” On March 14th a French television channel organised an entire evening event with eight of the 12 candidates entitled “France faced with war”. Most of the aspirants tried hard to show that they have what it takes to serve as head of state and the armed forces; Mr Macron recounted his conversations with Vladimir Putin.

马克龙当然知道如何充分利用这一点。他用普瓦西语漫不经心地聊了起来：“来这里之前，我和拜登总统通了电话。明天我将与XXX通话。”3月14日，法国一家电视频道组织了一场由12位候选人中的8位参加的晚间活动，标题是“法国面临战争”。大多数有抱负的人都努力证明自己具备担任国家元首和军队首脑的能力; 马克龙详细讲述了他与普京的对话。

In some ways, it is unsurprising that war has strengthened the sitting president’s hand. Voters are seeking some form of stability at a time of fear, and Mr Macron is often at his best in a crisis. The war has exposed the contradictions of his three main rivals on the hard left and the hard right, who have all scrambled to deny or withdraw past sympathy for Mr Putin. The mainstream contenders, who have real differences with Mr Macron on matters such as taxation or nuclear energy, see little option but to applaud, broadly, his diplomatic efforts.

在某些方面，战争增强了现任总统的影响力，这并不令人意外。选民们在恐惧之时寻求某种形式的稳定，而马克龙在危机中往往表现得最好。这场战争暴露了他的极左和极右的三个主要对手的矛盾，他们都争相否认或收回过去对普京的同情。主流竞选者在税收或核能等问题上与马克龙存在真正的分歧，他们几乎别无选择，只能咧开嘴称赞他的外交努力。

Moreover, Mr Putin’s war has shifted the debate in Europe in Mr Macron’s direction. The muscular talk about “strategic sovereignty”, and Germany’s decision massively to increase its defence spending, reinforce what he has been saying for years. Before he was elected in 2017, Mr Macron warned that “war and conflict are not behind us” in Europe. Shortly after taking office, in a speech at the Sorbonne, he urged Europe to think in terms of “European sovereignty”, a phrase that at the time seemed as abstract as the ambition felt far-fetched. Yet at a summit in Versailles on March 10th and 11th, EU leaders promised precisely to build “European sovereignty”: to take more joint responsibility for defence, and work towards greater autonomy in energy, medicines and food.

此外，普京发动的战争已使欧洲的辩论转向马克龙的方向。关于“战略主权”的强硬言论，以及德国大幅增加国防开支的决定，强化了他多年来一直的宣称。在2017年当选总统之前，马克龙警告称，欧洲“战争和冲突还没有结束”。上任后不久，在索邦大学的一次演讲中，他敦促欧洲从“欧洲主权”的角度来思考问题，这个词在当时看起来很抽象，又像他的雄心壮志一样遥不可及。然而，3月10日和11日在凡尔赛举行的一次峰会上，欧盟领导人明确承诺要建立“欧洲主权”: 承担更多的共同防务责任，并在能源、药品和食品方面努力实现更大的自主权。

Mr Macron’s diplomacy has its critics. Abroad, especially in eastern Europe, he was seen as a loose cannon and even as an appeaser when he first tried to court Mr Putin with talk of a new European security architecture. Last month, after his shuttle diplomacy to Moscow failed to prevent the war, he was regarded by some as naive. French diplomats had been persuaded that, in all likelihood, Mr Putin would not be reckless enough to invade. General Thierry Burkhard, France’s most senior soldier, conceded as much to Le Monde. “The Americans said that the Russians would attack—they were right,” the general said. “Our services thought, rather, that the conquest of Ukraine would have a monstrous cost and the Russians had other options” to bring down the Ukrainian regime.

马克龙的外交政策也遭到了批评。在国外，特别是在东欧，当他第一次试图通过谈论新的欧洲安全架构来讨好普京时，他被视为一个举止无法预料的人，甚至被视为一个绥靖主义者。上个月，在他对莫斯科的穿梭外交未能阻止战争爆发后，一些人认为他太天真了。法国外交官已经被说服，普京很可能不会鲁莽到入侵。法国最高级军官Thierry Burkhard将军也向《法国世界报》作出了不情愿地承认。将军说：“美国人说俄罗斯人会进攻，他们是对的，相反，我们的军队认为，征服乌克兰将付出巨大的代价，而俄罗斯人还有其他选择”以推翻乌克兰政权。

Yet, as Célia Belin of the Brookings Institution points out, Mr Macron has managed to present his dialogue with Mr Putin as clear-eyed statesmanship: that he engaged in talks precisely because he had perceived how serious Russia’s threats were. “It’s not entirely false,” she says, “and it’s certainly working for him.” Since December the French president has held calls with (or met) Mr Putin 16 times, and Ukraine’s president, Volodymyr Zelensky, 24 times. A poll says 59% think Mr Macron has “risen to the challenge” over the war.

然而，正如布鲁金斯学会的Célia Belin所指出的那样，马克龙成功地将他与普京的对话描绘成一种头脑清晰的政治家风范: 他参与对话，正是因为他意识到俄罗斯的威胁有多严重。她说：“ 这并不完全是错误的，而且对他来说肯定是有效的。”自去年12月以来，法国总统与普京通话(或会晤)16次，与乌克兰总统Volodymyr Zelensky通话24次。一项民意调查显示，59%的人认为马克龙已经在战争中“奋起应对挑战”。

In short, Mr Macron’s efforts are applauded, despite the lack of results. Fully 65% of the French back arms deliveries to Ukraine, which France is carrying out quietly, and 80% support taking in refugees. At a visit this week to a centre to welcome those fleeing Ukraine, Mr Macron promised that France would take in at least 100,000. The war has turned the campaign into a sombre affair. But Mr Macron will have few complaints if, as seems likely, that helps him keep his job. ■

简而言之，马克龙的努力是值得称赞的，尽管缺乏成果。65%的法国人支持武器运送到乌克兰，而法国正在悄悄地进行这项活动，80%的法国人支持接收难民。在本周访问的一个“欢迎从乌克兰逃离的难民”中心时，马克龙承诺法国将接收至少10万难民。战争把这场竞选活动变成了一件沉重的事情。但如果这有助于马克龙保住他的职位(这似乎很可能)，他不会有什么抱怨。■

Britain finally comes up with a scheme to help Ukrainian refugees

英国终于想出了一个帮助乌克兰难民的方案

Homes for refugees

难民的家园

![image-20220323091256497](/Users/chenyujie/Library/Application Support/typora-user-images/image-20220323091256497.png)

Linking refugees with hosts helps them find their feet

将难民与寄主联系起来有助于他们找到立足之地

Mar 19th 2022

IN 1939 MILLIONS of British children were evacuated from cities and towns that might be bombed and sent to live with rural families. It was a wrenching experience. Not only were they separated from their parents; their hosts were often reluctant and unfeeling. One evacuee, John McGuirk, remembered being dumped with other children in a churchyard: “People came around and picked you like they were picking dogs out of pet shops.”

1939年，数百万英国儿童从可能遭到轰炸的城镇中撤离，并被送到农村家庭居住生活。这是一次痛苦的经历。他们不仅与他们的父母分离; 而且他们的寄主往往是不情愿的和无情的。John McGuirk（一位被疏散的人）记得和其他孩子一起被扔在教堂的庭院里:“人们来到你周围，挑选你，就像他们在宠物店挑选狗一样。”

So the response to a new government scheme that will allow Britons to accommodate Ukrainian refugees is a delightful surprise. On March 14th the government asked households to register as potential hosts; by the end of the following day more than 100,000 had signed up. It is not as immediately helpful as simply dropping visa requirements, as other European countries have done. But past experience suggests that inviting ordinary people to help settle refugees works well.

因此，对于一项允许英国人收容乌克兰难民的新的政府计划，人们的反应是一个令人惊喜。3月14日，政府要求家庭登记成为潜在的寄主;到第二天结束时，已有超过10万人登记。这并不像其他欧洲国家所做的那样，简单地取消签证要求，并没有那么立竿见影的帮助。但过去的经验表明，邀请普通人来帮助安置难民很有效。

At first, Britain’s scheme will rely on personal connections. People wishing to offer shelter must name specific Ukrainians and enter their details on an application form. After basic security checks on both parties, the refugees will be allowed to enter Britain for three years. They will be able to work and receive welfare, and their hosts will be paid £350 ($455) a month, tax-free. In time the government expects charities to match Ukrainians with hosts who do not know them.

首先，英国的计划将依靠个人关系。希望提供庇护的人必须说出具体的乌克兰人的名字，并在申请表上填写他们的详细信息。在对双方进行基本的安全检查后，难民将被允许进入英国，为期三年。他们可以工作并享受福利，他们的寄主每月将得到350英镑(455美元)的免税待遇。政府希望慈善机构能及时为乌克兰人与不认识他们的东道主配对。

Canada has allowed communities to sponsor refugees since 1978. More than 300,000 people have arrived under the programme, which seems to help them find their feet quickly. A study by three academics at Canadian universities, Lisa Kaida, Feng Hou and Max Stick, estimated that 90% of male and 71% of female privately sponsored refugees found jobs within a year—17 and 24 percentage points higher than the shares among refugees settled by the government. The privately sponsored refugees were better educated, which explains some of the difference, but not all.

自1978年以来，加拿大允许社区资助难民。已有30多万人通过该计划来到加拿大，这一计划似乎有助于他们迅速适应新环境。加拿大大学的三位学者Lisa Kaida、Feng Hou和Max Stick进行的一项研究估计，由私人资助的难民中，90%的男性和71%的女性在一年内找到了工作，这比政府资助的难民中找到工作的比例高出17个百分点和24个百分点。私人资助的难民受过更好的教育，这解释了部分差异，但不是全部。

In 2016 Britain started to send Syrian refugees to communities willing to host them. That scheme is small, with almost 700 people settled so far, and is much more ponderous and finicky than the new Homes for Ukraine scheme. Communities cannot request particular refugees. The thinking is that the Syrians who most need help are not the ones with contacts in Britain, says Monika Kruesmann of Reset, a charity that assists hosts and refugees. Hosts must provide separate accommodation, not just spare rooms, and are rigorously inspected.

2016年，英国开始将叙利亚难民送往愿意接纳他们的社区。这个计划规模不大，到目前为止已经安置了近700人，而且比新的“乌克兰家园”计划要繁琐且挑剔得多。社区不能请求特定的难民。一家帮助收容者和难民的慈善机构Reset的Monika Kruesmann说，他们的想法是，最需要帮助的叙利亚人并不是那些在英国有联系的人。寄主必须提供单独的住所，而不仅仅是多余的房间，并且要经过严格的检查。

Jenny Phillimore of Birmingham University assessed the scheme and found some problems. Some Syrians became isolated after being settled in rural areas far from other Arabic speakers. Despite good intentions, the British volunteers could not help with everything: “They’re all quite middle-class; they didn’t understand the benefits system,” says Ms Phillimore. But they proved expert at hassling head teachers, doctors and officials, and the refugees were enormously grateful. She thinks the scheme works well overall.

伯明翰大学的Jenny Phillimore对该计划进行了评估，发现了一些问题。一些叙利亚人在远离其他阿拉伯语国家的农村地区定居后，变得孤立。尽管这些英国志愿者的初衷是好的，但他们并不能在所有事情上都提供帮助，Phillimore女士称:“ 他们都是中产阶级; 他们不了解福利制度。” 但事实证明，他们擅长与校长、医生和官员争论，难民们对此非常感激。她认为这个计划总体上很好。

Some fear the Homes for Ukraine scheme will endanger vulnerable, traumatised people and undermine the existing asylum system, in part by convincing Britons that helping refugees is their job rather than the government’s. The first worry is valid. The government has so far been vague about how carefully hosts will be checked. Unlike Syrian refugees, most of the Ukrainians are expected to be single women or mothers with children. The men have been conscripted.

一些人担心，“乌克兰家园”计划将危及脆弱、受创伤的人们，并破坏现有的庇护体系，部分原因是该计划让英国人相信，帮助难民是他们的工作，而不是政府的职责。第一个担忧是有道理的。到目前为止，政府在寄主检查方面一直都不确定。与叙利亚难民不同，大多数乌克兰难民预期是单身女性或有孩子的母亲。男人都被征召入伍了。

Stranger things

The second worry is misplaced. Britain certainly has a dysfunctional asylum system: last June 70,000 people were still waiting for their cases to be heard. Asylum-seekers are usually banned from working, causing their skills to atrophy, and are often stuck in poor housing. The government has been trying to legislate to make the system harsher still. But all that was unfortunately true before Russia invaded Ukraine. The Homes for Ukraine scheme is a point of light that illuminates the awfulness of the mainstream asylum system. It seems odd to complain about that. ■

第二个担忧是杞人忧天。英国的庇护体系确实不健全: 去年6月，仍有7万人在等待他们的案件得到审理。寻求庇护者通常被禁止工作，导致他们的技能退化，而且常常被困在简陋的住房里。政府一直试图通过立法使该系统更加苛刻。但不幸的是，在俄罗斯入侵乌克兰之前，这一切都是事实。“乌克兰家园”计划是一个亮点，它揭示了主流庇护体系的糟糕之处。所以抱怨这一点似乎很奇怪[本身就烂，只是之前没公之于众？所以没必要做无谓的担忧，也没必要去抱怨]。■

Russia’s war is creating corporate winners and losers

俄罗斯的战争正在创造企业赢家和输家

Value-chain reaction

价值链反应

![image-20220322081908149](/Users/chenyujie/Library/Application Support/typora-user-images/image-20220322081908149.png)

As well as enormous volatility

以及巨大的波动性

Mar 19th 2022

MOST MULTINATIONAL companies can live without Russian customers. Living without Russian commodities would be much harder. On March 15th the European Commission announced new economic constraints on Russia, including a ban on exports of European luxury items and cars—the definition of an essential good is, after all, in the eye of the oligarch. But the announcement also included a ban on steel products from Russia. More such restrictions on Russian exports may come.

大多数跨国公司没有俄罗斯客户也能生存。没有俄罗斯商品的生活将会困难得多。3月15日，欧盟委员会宣布对俄罗斯实施新的经济限制，包括禁止出口欧洲奢侈品和汽车，毕竟必需品的定义寡头说了算。但该声明还包括禁止来自俄罗斯的钢铁产品。其可能会出台更多类似的对俄罗斯商品出口的限制措施。

Companies are struggling to contain the fallout of Russia’s brutal war in Ukraine. The first response of those with business in Russia was to rush for the exit. About 400 have announced their withdrawal from Russia, according to one tally, cowed by legal and reputational risks. Executives now face a different, bigger challenge. This concerns not their business within Russia but supply chains that extend beyond it, and other knock-on effects. As the war continues, it is creating corporate winners and losers, as well as an awful lot of volatility.

企业正在努力控制俄罗斯在乌克兰残酷战争的影响。那些在俄罗斯做生意的人的第一反应是急于退出。据一项统计，约有400家企业因为受到法律和声誉风险的威胁而宣布从俄罗斯撤离。高管们现在面临着一个不同的、更大的挑战。这与他们在俄罗斯的业务无关，而是与延伸到俄罗斯以外的供应链和其他连锁反应有关。随着战争的继续，企业中既有赢家，也有输家，同时也出现了大量的波动。

There are two factors that make the shock to supply chains particularly difficult for firms to manage. The first is the breadth of commodities produced by Ukraine and Russia. The two countries together supply 26% of the world’s exports of wheat, 16% of corn, 30% of barley and about 80% of sunflower oil and sunflower-seed meal. Ukraine provides about half the world’s neon, used to etch microchips. Russia is the world’s third-largest oil producer, second-largest producer of gas and top exporter of nickel, used in car batteries, and palladium, used in car-exhaust systems, not to mention a large exporter of aluminium and iron. Even without formal sanctions on most of Russia’s commodities, Western traders are increasingly trying to avoid them, wary of legal risks.

有两个因素使得供应链的冲击对企业来说尤其难以管理。首先是乌克兰和俄罗斯生产的商品的“广度”(商品比较丰富)。这两个国家共供应了全球26%的小麦、16%的玉米、30%的大麦以及约80%的葵花籽油和葵花籽粉。乌克兰提供了世界上大约一半的用于蚀刻微芯片的氖。俄罗斯是全球第三大石油生产国、第二大天然气生产国、镍(用于汽车电池)和钯(用于汽车尾气排放系统)的最大出口国，更不用说也是铝和铁的大型出口国了。即使没有对俄罗斯的大部分大宗商品实施正式制裁，西方贸易商也越来越多地试图避免它们，因为他们担心法律风险。

The second complicating factor is the market’s extraordinary swings. The price of Brent crude surged to $128 a barrel on March 8th, then dipped below $100 a week later as X announced new covid-19 restrictions and investors anticipated the interest-rate increase by America’s Federal Reserve on March 16th. The London Metal Exchange halted trading of nickel on March 8th after its price shot past a record $100,000 a tonne. When trading resumed on March 16th, a technical issue prompted the exchange to suspend trading once more (see Finance & economics section).

第二个使情况复杂化的因素是市场的剧烈波动。3月8日，布伦特原油价格飙升至每桶128美元，一周后，由于X宣布了新的新冠限制措施，以及投资者预计美联储将于3月16日加息，布伦特原油价格跌至每桶100美元以下。3月8日，在镍价突破创纪录的每吨10万美元后，伦敦金属交易所暂停了镍的交易。当3月16日交易恢复时，一个技术问题促使交易所再次暂停交易(见Finance & economics部分)。

The overall American stockmarket is back roughly to where it was before the invasion. But a few industries benefit from the turmoil, from armsmakers to cable news and the lawyers who help firms comply with sanctions. The biggest winners are commodities firms, especially outside Russia (see chart).

美国股市整体大致回到了俄罗斯入侵前的水平。但也有一些行业从这场动荡中获益，从军火制造商到有线新闻，再到帮助公司遵守制裁的律师。最大的赢家是大宗商品公司，尤其是俄罗斯以外的公司(见图表)。

A stockmarket index of American frackers, which benefit from high oil prices and European demand for liquefied natural gas, climbed by a fifth between February 23rd and March 10th. It remains 9% above its pre-invasion level, despite the decline in oil prices. Mining firms are, as a group, likewise performing well, buoyed by higher metals prices, as are steelmakers (except Russian ones). The share prices of US Steel and Tata Steel, with headquarters in Pittsburgh and Mumbai, respectively, have climbed by 38% and 11% since the eve of the invasion. Bunge and ADM, two big listed traders that specialise in rerouting flows of grain, have outperformed the market, too.

从2月23日到3月10日，美国水力压裂公司的股票指数上涨了五分之一，其受益于高油价和欧洲对液化天然气需求。尽管油价下跌，但其仍比入侵前的水平高出9%。矿业公司作为一个整体，同样表现良好，其受到较高金属价格的支撑，钢铁制造商也是如此(俄罗斯的除外)。自入侵前夕以来，总部分别位于匹兹堡和孟买的美国钢铁公司和塔塔钢铁公司的股价分别上涨了38%和11%。Bunge和ADM这两家专注于改变粮食流通路线的大型上市交易商的表现也优于市场。

The war does not affect all commodities firms equally. Rio Tinto, a big miner, announced on March 10th that it would abandon a joint venture with Rusal, a giant Russian aluminium producer. Rocketing electricity costs resulting from the soaring price of natural gas, 40% of which Europe gets from Russia, have forced some Spanish steelmakers to cut output.

战争对所有大宗商品公司的影响并不相同。3月10日，矿业巨头力拓宣布将放弃与俄罗斯铝业巨头Rusal的合资企业。天然气价格飙升(欧洲40%的天然气来自俄罗斯)导致电力成本飙升，这迫使一些西班牙钢铁生产商削减产量。

Pricey inputs are a more widespread problem for sectors further up the value chain. Just as they were preparing to lift off as pandemic travel restrictions are relaxed, airlines got slapped with rising fuel costs. Yara International, a Norwegian fertiliser-maker, said on March 9th that the cost of natural gas had prompted it to cut production at two European factories.

对于价值链上游的行业来说，昂贵的投入是一个更为普遍的问题。由于疫情旅行限制放松，航空公司正准备起飞时，其又受到了不断上涨的燃料成本的打击。挪威化肥制造商Yara International在3月9日表示，天然气的成本促使其削减了在欧洲的两家工厂的产量。

Carmakers, which have not yet recovered from the pandemic’s disruptions to supply chains, face fresh problems. Volkswagen and BMW, two German giants, have cut production in Europe as they seek out new manufacturers of the harnesses that bundle miles of electrical wires in their cars to replace out-of-action Ukrainian suppliers. Morgan Stanley, a bank, reckons that the 67% jump in nickel prices before trading stopped represented an increase of about $1,000 to the input costs of the average American electric vehicle.

汽车制造商还没有从疫情对供应链的破坏中恢复过来，他们又面临着新的问题。德国两大汽车巨头大众和宝马已经削减了在欧洲的产量，因为它们正在寻找新的汽车线束制造商，以取代已经“停止运转”的乌克兰供应商。据摩根斯坦利银行估计，镍价在交易停止前上涨了67%，这意味着平均每辆美国电动汽车的投入成本增加了1000美元。

Gabriel Adler of Citigroup, another bank, notes that carmakers have so far been successful in passing their costs on to consumers. Tesla, America’s electric-car superstar, this month raised prices; Elon Musk, its boss, complained in a tweet about “significant recent inflation pressure in raw materials & logistics”. Such pricing power is enviable. But it has its limits. At some point people will not be willing to absorb any further increases.

另一家银行花旗集团的Gabriel Adler指出，到目前为止，汽车制造商已经成功地将成本转嫁到消费者身上。美国电动汽车巨头特斯拉本月提高了价格; 该公司老板埃隆•马斯克在一条推特上抱怨称，“原材料和物流行业近期面临巨大的通胀压力”。这种定价权令人羡慕。但它也有其局限性。在某种程度上，人们将不愿意承受任何进一步的增长。

In certain cases, consumers are beginning to balk. American food firms have been raising prices for months to offset higher costs of energy, transport and ingredients. However, they have been unable to raise them quickly enough to protect margins. The need to negotiate prices with grocers limits their ability to demand higher ones whenever they desire. And grocers, in turn, are under pressure from shoppers. Robert Moskow of Credit Suisse, one more bank, notes that consumers have in the past year been willing to stomach pricier food. But the war’s impact on commodities prices comes at a moment when their patience is wearing thin, especially in America, where inflation has hit a 40-year high.

在某些情况下，消费者开始犹豫了。几个月来，美国食品公司一直在提高食品价格，以抵消能源、运输和原料成本的上升。然而，它们一直未能以足够快的速度提高价格以保护利润率。与食品商谈判价格的需要限制了他们随时要求更高价格的能力。反过来，食品商也面临着来自购物者的压力。瑞士信贷银行的Robert Moskow指出，在过去的一年中，消费者愿意忍受更昂贵的食品。但这场战争对大宗商品价格的影响是在他们的耐心逐渐消失之际出现的，尤其是在美国，那里的通货膨胀率达到了40年来的最高水平。

“Every food company must be getting a little nervous that they are pushing the consumer too far,” says Mr Moskow. As the costs of inputs continue to climb, it looks increasingly likely that companies will be forced to choose between compressing profits and depressing demand. ■

How to tweak drug-design software to create chemical weapons

如何调整药物设计软件来制造化学武器

Chemical warfare

化学战争

![image-20220321101917562](/Users/chenyujie/Library/Application Support/typora-user-images/image-20220321101917562.png)

Just ask the program to enhance, rather than reduce, toxicity

要求程序去增强毒性，而非减少毒性

Mar 19th 2022

SCIENTIFIC PAPERS are normally models of discreet understatement. They are also (or are at least supposed to be) loaded with the information needed for others to replicate their findings.

科学论文通常是谨慎轻描淡写的典范。他们也(或者至少应该)承载着其他人复现他们的发现所需要的信息。

Not this one. “Dual use of artificial-intelligence-powered drug discovery”, just published in Nature Machine Intelligence, has clearly freaked its authors out. That comes over both in the tone of the text and the deliberate withholding of crucial information. For what Fabio Urbina and Sean Ekins of Collaborations Pharmaceuticals, in Raleigh, North Carolina, and their colleagues are reporting is a virtual machine that can be used to design new and nastier chemical weapons.

但是这篇论文不是。刚刚发表在Nature子刊《Nature Machine Intelligence》上的论文《人工智能驱动的药物发现的双重用途》(Dual - use of artificial Intelligence -powered drug discovery)显然把它的作者吓坏了。这体现在文本的基调和故意隐瞒关键信息上。位于美国北卡罗来纳州罗利的Collaborations Pharmaceuticals公司的Fabio Urbina和Sean Ekins以及他们的同事正在报道的是一种虚拟机器，可以用来设计新的、更可怕的化学武器。

Hiding in plain sight

The story began in 2021, when Collaborations Pharmaceuticals, which uses computers to help its customers identify molecules that look like potential drugs, was invited to present a paper on how such drug-discovery technologies might be misused. The venue was a conference organised by the Spiez Laboratory, in Switzerland. This is a government-funded outfit that studies risks posed by nuclear, biological and chemical weapons. To prepare for the presentation some of Collaborations’ researchers carried out what they describe as a “thought exercise” that turned into a computational proof of concept for making biochemical weapons.

这个故事始于2021年，当时Collaborations Pharmaceuticals公司受邀发表了一篇关于“药物发现技术可能被滥用“的论文。该公司利用计算机帮助客户识别看似潜在药物的分子。地点是瑞士斯皮兹实验室组织的一次会议。这是一个政府资助的机构，研究核武器、生物和化学武器带来的风险。为了准备这次演讲，collaboration公司的一些研究人员进行了一项他们称之为“思考实验”的活动，通过计算证明了制造生化武器的概念。

Their method was disturbingly simple. They took a piece of drug-discovery software, called MegaSyn (a piece of artificial intelligence, AI, which the company has developed for the purpose of putting virtual molecules together and then assessing their potential as medicines), and turned one of its functions upside down. Instead of penalising probable toxicity, as makes sense if a molecule is to be used medically, the modified version of MegaSyn prized it.

他们的方法简单得令人不安。他们使用了一款名为MegaSyn的药物发现软件(这是一款人工智能软件，该公司开发它的目的是将虚拟分子组合在一起，然后评估它们作为药物的潜力)，并将其功能进行反转。如果一种分子要用于医学上，那么惩罚可能的毒性是有道理的，但修改后的MegaSyn版本是要奖励毒性。

The result was terrifying. Trained on the chemical structures of a set of drug-like molecules (defined as substances easily synthesised and likely to be absorbed by the body) taken from a publicly available database, together with those molecules’ known toxicities, the modified software required a mere six hours to generate 40,000 virtual molecules that fell within the researchers’ predefined parameters for possible use as chemical weapons.

结果是可怕的。通过对从公开数据库中提取的一组类药分子(定义为易于合成并容易被身体吸收的物质)的化学结构以及这些分子的已知毒性进行训练，修改后的软件仅需6小时就能生成40000个虚拟分子，这些分子符合研究人员预定义的参数，可能用作化学武器。

The list included many known nerve agents, notably VX, one of the most toxic. But the software also came up with not-yet-synthesised substances predicted to be deadlier still. Worryingly, some of them occupied parts of what chemists call “molecular property space” that were entirely separate from those inhabited by known neurotoxins. This suggests that whole, new classes of chemical weapons might be developed, if anyone wished to try.

该生成列表中包括许多已知的神经毒剂，尤其是VX，它是毒性最大的毒剂之一。但该软件也制造出了尚未合成的、预计更致命的物质。令人担忧的是，它们中的一些占据了化学家所称的“分子特性空间”的部分，而这些空间与已知的神经毒素完全不同。这表明，如果有人愿意尝试，可能会开发出全新类别的化学武器。

Wisely, Dr Urbina and his colleagues went no further than that. They did not try to synthesise any of their putative discoveries and have certainly not published a list of them. Nor have they described the details of their method. But, in the wider scheme of things, it is not those details that matter. What matters is that they have shown this approach works in principle.

明智的是，Urbina博士和他的同事没有进一步研究。他们没有试图合成任何他们假定的发现，当然也没有发表一份列表。他们也没有详细描述他们的方法。但是，从更广泛的角度来看，这些细节并不重要。重要的是，他们已经证明这种方法原则上是可行的。

Moreover, as the authors themselves make clear, many people have the knowledge, if not the motive, to act on that fact. “We are but one very small company in a universe of many hundreds of companies using AI software for drug discovery and de novo design. How many of them have even considered repurposing, or misuse, possibilities?” They admit that, before being prompted by their role in the conference, they certainly had not considered them. “The thought had never previously struck us. We were vaguely aware of security concerns around work with pathogens or toxic chemicals, but that did not relate to us; we primarily operate in a virtual setting…Even our projects on Ebola and neurotoxins… had not set our alarm bells ringing.”

此外，正如作者自己所阐明的那样，许多人即使没有动机，也知道要根据这一事实采取行动。“我们只是世界上数百家使用人工智能软件进行药物研发和‘从头设计’的公司中的一家很小的公司。有多少人考虑过重定位或滥用的可能性?” 他们承认，在受到他们在会议中所扮演角色的影响之前，他们肯定没有考虑过这些问题。“我们以前从未有过这样的想法。我们模糊地意识到与病原体或有毒化学品有关的工作安全问题，但这与我们无关; 我们主要在虚拟环境中运作，甚至我们关于埃博拉和神经毒素的项目…也没有敲响我们的警钟。”

Such naivety is surely widespread in the industry, and the paper’s authors, who include Filippa Lentzos, an expert on biosecurity at King’s College, London—whose idea it was to write the article in the first place—and Cédric Invernizzi of the Spiez Laboratory, are open about this. As the paper observes, “Our own commercial tools, as well as open-source software tools and many datasets that populate public databases, are available with no oversight.”

这种天真的想法肯定在业界很普遍，这篇论文的作者，包括伦敦国王学院的生物安全专家Filippa Lentzos(他最初的想法是写这篇文章)和斯皮兹实验室的Cédric Invernizzi，都对此持开放态度。正如这篇论文所观察到的，“我们自己的商业工具，以及开源软件工具和许多公共数据库中的数据集，都可以在没有监督的情况下使用。”

As to dealing with the problem, the authors ask questions about harms both direct (should software downloads be monitored, or sales to certain groups restricted?) and indirect (will one result be restrictions and reduced investment in an area that has great medical potential?). But they offer few answers.

至于如何处理这一问题，作者提出了直接(是否应该监控软件下载，或限制对特定群体的销售?)和间接(其中一个结果是限制和减少对一个具有巨大医疗潜力的领域的投资?)的问题。但他们提供的答案很少。

They do, though, draw an analogy with GPT-3, a natural-language generator with plenty of potential for abuse (for example, the creation of “deepfakes” purporting to be the words of real people). The inventors of this have so far kept its most crucial parts under wraps by employing what is known as an application-programming interface to stop outsiders prying. That might work for future software releases in the field of drug discovery, but will do little to deal with what is out there already.

不过，他们确实拿GPT-3做了一个类比，GPT-3是一种很有可能被滥用的自然语言生成器(例如，标榜是真人说的话的“deepfakes”)。到目前为止，该技术的发明者通过应用程序接口(API)来阻止外界的窥探，从而对其最关键的部分进行了保密。这可能适用于未来药物发现领域的软件发布，但对已经存在的软件几乎没有帮助。

In any case, even if no company has yet thought along the lines Dr Urbina and Dr Ekins have just opened up, governments probably will have done. And so, perhaps, will terrorist groups.

无论如何，即使还没有公司想到Urbina博士和Ekins博士刚刚开辟的“路线”，政府可能已经做了。恐怖组织或许也会如此。

Governments in rich countries have, it is true, found little use for chemical weapons in regular combat since the first world war, and for good reason. They are no more deadly (and often less so) than high explosives, are easier to protect against, and are also harder to contain. Bombs, shells and rockets are simply more reliable. As agents of terror, though, whether delivered by dysfunctional states against rebel populations or by irregulars against civilians under the protection of their target governments, they are perfect. ■

的确，自第一次世界大战以来，发达国家的政府在常规战斗中很少使用化学武器，这是有充分理由的。它们并不比烈性炸药更具杀伤力(甚至不如烈性炸药)，更容易防范，但也更难控制。炸弹、炮弹和火箭弹更可靠。然而，作为恐怖活动的媒介，无论是由功能失调的国家用来对付叛乱分子，还是由非正规部队用来对付目标政府保护下的平民，它们都是完美的。■

Why loafing can be work

为什么游手好闲也能成为工作?

Bartleby

巴托比

![image-20220320091442902](/Users/chenyujie/Library/Application Support/typora-user-images/image-20220320091442902.png)

Daydreaming, promenading and zoning out pay rich dividends

做白日梦、散步和走神都能带来丰厚的回报

THE FAMILIAR exerts a powerful subliminal appeal. The “name-letter effect” refers to the subconscious bias that people have for the letters in their own name, and for their own initials in particular. They are more likely to choose careers, partners and brands that start with their initials (Joe becomes a joiner, marries Judy and loves Jaffa cakes). A related bias, the “well-travelled-road effect”, describes the tendency of people to ascribe shorter travelling times to familiar routes than is actually the case.

“熟悉”具有强大的潜意识吸引力。“姓名字母效应”指的是人们对自己名字中的字母，尤其是首字母产生的潜意识偏见。他们更有可能选择以自己名字首字母开头的职业、合作伙伴和品牌(Joe成为一名木工[joiner]，与 Judy结婚，喜欢Jaffa蛋糕)。与此相关的一种偏差，即“熟悉路线效应”，描述了人们倾向于将较短的出行时间归因于熟悉的路线，而实际情况并非如此。

A bias towards the familiar shows up at work, too. One such prejudice is about what exactly constitutes work. Being at a desk counts as work, as does looking at a screen above a certain size. Responding to email and being in a meeting are indubitably forms of work. So is any activity that might elicit sympathy if performed on the weekend—typing, taking a phone call from the boss, opening any type of spreadsheet.

对熟悉事物的偏爱也出现在工作中。其中一种偏见是关于工作的构成要素。坐在办公桌前也算工作，盯着超过一定尺寸的屏幕也算工作。毫无疑问，回复邮件和开会都是工作的一种形式。在周末进行的任何可能引起同情的活动，比如打字、接老板的电话、打开任何类型的电子表格，也是工作的一种形式。

This prejudice helps to explain worries about “proximity bias”, the risk that white-collar employees who spend lots of time in the office are more likely to advance than remote workers who are less visible. That is because being inside an office building is itself something that counts as work. Pre-pandemic research showed that “passive face-time”—the mere fact of being seen at your desk, without even interacting with anyone—led observers to think of people as dependable and committed.

这种偏见有助于解释人们对“近距离偏见”的担忧。“近距离偏见”指的是，花大量时间在办公室的白领员工比不太看得见的远程员工更有可能获得晋升。这是因为待在办公楼里本身就是一种工作。大流行前的研究表明，“被动的面对面时间”。即仅仅是被人看到坐在办公桌前，甚至没有与任何人互动的事实，就会让观察者认为这些人是可靠和忠诚的。

But these familiar forms of work can deceive**, for two reasons**. The first is that what looks like a Stakhanovite effort may be no such thing. Keyboard-tappers may just be updating their LinkedIn profiles. Attendees at a meeting are often present in body but not in spirit. Even when actual work is being done, it may not be the most productive use of people’s time.

但这些熟悉的工作形式可能具有欺骗性，原因有二。首先，看似斯达汉诺夫式的努力可能并非如此。敲击键盘的人可能只是在更新他们的领英资料。出席会议的人往往是肉体出席，灵魂没到。即使在实际工作已经完成的时候，这也可能不是最有效利用时间的方式。

The second is that things that look like the opposite of work—loafing about, to use the technical term—can be very useful indeed. Take daydreaming. In most workplaces, staring into space for hours on end is frowned upon; security guards and models can get away with it, but few others. Yet letting the mind wander is not simply part of being human; it can also be a source of creativity, a way to unlock solutions to thorny problems.

第二，那些看起来与工作相反的事情，用专业术语来说，即游手好闲，确实非常有用。以白日梦为例。在大多数工作场所，连续几个小时盯着天空是不可取的; 保安和模特可以不受惩罚，但其他人很少可以避免不良后果。然而，让思维漫游不仅仅是人类的一部分; 它也可以是创造力的源泉，是解决棘手问题的一种方法。

Albert Einstein’s breakthrough moments often came via thought experiments in which he let his imagination drift. What would it be like to travel as fast as a light beam? What happens if double lightning strikes are observed from different perspectives? Einstein is admittedly a pretty high bar, but zoning out can help mere mortals, too. Research published in 2021 found that tricky work-related problems sparked more daydreaming among professional employees, and that this daydreaming in turn boosted creativity.

爱因斯坦的突破性时刻往往来自于他的思想实验，在这些实验中，他让自己的想象力漫游。像光束一样快的旅行会是什么样子? 如果从不同的角度观察到两次雷击会发生什么? 诚然，爱因斯坦是一个相当高的标准，但开小差也能帮助凡人。2021年发表的一项研究发现，与工作有关的棘手问题会引发专业人士走神更多，而这种走神反过来又会提高创造力。

In similar vein, going for a walk is not just a break from work, but can be a form of it. An experiment from 2014 asked participants to think of creative uses for a common object (a button, say) while sitting down and while walking. Perambulation was associated with big increases in creativity. Being outside generally seems to improve lateral thinking. In another study, hikers who had been yomping away in the wilderness did much better on a problem-solving task than those who had yet to set off.

同样，散步不只是工作之余的休息，它也是工作的一种形式。2014年的一项实验要求参与者在坐下或走路的时候思考一个普通物体(比如一个按钮)的创造性用途。漫步与创造力的大幅增长有关。一般来说，置身户外似乎能改善横向思维。在另一项研究中，那些一直在野外负重徒步旅行者在解决问题时比那些还没有动身的人表现得更好。

Loafing has clear limits. If you miss a deadline because you were staring soulfully out of the window, you still missed a deadline. Not every problem requires a backpack and a journey into the countryside. If you don’t much like your work in the first place, you are likely to daydream about other things.

游手好闲有明显的局限性。如果你因为深情地凝视着窗外而错过了截止日期，那么你仍然错过了最后期限。并不是所有的问题都需要背包旅行和乡村之旅。如果你一开始就不太喜欢你的工作，你很可能会幻想其他事情。

But time to muse is valuable in virtually every role. To take one example, customer-service representatives can be good sources of ideas on how to improve a company’s products, but they are often rated on how well they adhere to a schedule of fielding calls. Reflection is not part of the routine.

但在几乎每一个角色中，沉思的时间都是宝贵的。举个例子，客户服务代表可能是改进公司产品的好想法的来源，但他们通常是根据他们遵守接听电话的情况来打分的。沉思不是常规的一部分。

The post-pandemic rethink of work is focused on “when” and “where” questions. Firms are experimenting with four-day workweeks as a way to improve retention and avoid burnout. Asynchronous working is a way for individuals to collaborate at times that suit them. Lots of thought is going into how to make a success of hybrid work.

大流行后对工作的反思集中在“何时”和“在哪里”的问题上。公司正在尝试每周四天工作制，以提高员工留任率，避免员工身心疲惫。异步工作是让个人在他们方便的时候进行协作的一种方式。很多人都在思考如何让混合办公取得成功。

The “what is work” question gets much less attention. The bias towards familiar forms of activity is deeply entrenched. But if you see a colleague meandering through the park or examining the ceiling for hours, don’t assume that work isn’t being done. What looks like idleness may be the very moment when serendipity strikes.

对“什么是工作”的问题得到的关注少得多。对熟悉的活动形式的偏见根深蒂固。但是，如果你看到一个同事在公园里闲逛，或者检查天花板好几个小时，不要认为工作没有完成。看似无所事事的事情，可能恰恰是发现意外的时刻。

Windfall taxes on energy companies are a bad idea

对能源公司征收暴利税是个坏主意

Tilting at windfalls

抨击暴利

![image-20220319075933299](/Users/chenyujie/Library/Application Support/typora-user-images/image-20220319075933299.png)

Governments should not seize the energy industry’s profits

政府不应该攫取能源行业的利润

文章音频

Mar 19th 2022

WHENEVER OIL and gas are expensive, politicians’ eyes turn greedily to the profits of energy firms. Since energy prices began to surge last year Bulgaria, Italy, Romania and Spain have introduced new taxes on the industry. On March 8th the European Commission recommended that governments try to “capture a part of the returns” made by electricity generators. And in America 12 Democratic senators including Elizabeth Warren, a one-time presidential candidate, have proposed a tax on every barrel of oil big firms produce or import, equal to half the difference between the current oil price and the 2015-19 average.

只要石油和天然气价格高企，政客们就会贪婪地把目光转向能源公司的利润。自从去年能源价格开始飙升以来，保加利亚、意大利、罗马尼亚和西班牙都对能源行业征收了新税。3月8日，欧盟委员会建议各国政府设法从发电商获得“部分回报”。在美国，包括曾经的总统候选人Elizabeth Warren在内的12名民主党参议员提议，对大型石油公司生产或进口的每一桶石油征税，相当于当前油价与2015-2019年平均油价之差的一半。

The impulse to levy “windfall taxes” is particularly strong today because Russia’s invasion of Ukraine has caused oil and natural-gas prices to rocket and then to gyrate wildly, giving the perception that firms are profiting from bloodshed. Governments, having run up enormous debts during the pandemic, must now find more cash to protect poor consumers from soaring energy bills and to boost defence spending. And the typical argument against windfall taxes—that even when they are retroactive, they risk deterring future investment—has become less powerful now that most of the world is trying to phase out the burning of fossil fuels.

征收“暴利税”的冲动在今天尤为强烈，因为俄罗斯入侵乌克兰导致石油和天然气价格飙升，然后又剧烈波动，给人一种公司从“流血事件”中获利的感觉。各国政府在疫情期间背负了巨额债务，现在必须找到更多资金，以保护贫困消费者免受能源账单飙升的影响，并增加国防开支。反对暴利税的典型论点是，即使它们具有追溯效力，它们也有可能阻碍未来的投资。现在，由于世界上大多数国家都在试图逐步淘汰化石燃料的燃烧，这种观点已经变得不那么有力。

Imposing windfall taxes is nevertheless a mistake. Start with the fact that energy markets go through cycles of boom and bust. The years Ms Warren has chosen as a benchmark were not good ones: in two of them, 2015 and 2016, the net operating margin of the global listed energy industry was negative. There was an other year of operating losses in 2020, during which the oil price briefly fell below zero owing to the pandemic. If companies must endure the bad times but find chunks of their profits are seized when prices rise, their businesses lose viability.

然而，征收暴利税是一个错误。我们先从能源市场经历繁荣与萧条周期这一事实说起。Warren女士选择的基准年份并不好: 2015年和2016年，全球上市能源行业的净营业利润率为负。2020年又出现了一年的经营亏损，在此期间，由于新冠大流行，石油价格一度降至零以下。如果企业必须忍受经济不景气的时期，但发现价格上涨时，利润又被攫取，那么它们的业务就失去了生存能力。

That may sound appealing to those climate activists who want to drive out of business firms like BP, whose boss recently said that high prices had turned the firm into a “cash machine”. But today’s energy crisis shows that the world needs a carefully managed phase-out of carbon emissions, not a sudden halt in fossil-fuel investment, especially if Europe is to wean itself off Russian gas. Renewable energy cannot immediately replace gas for some tasks, such as heating homes with gas boilers. Even if the infrastructure to run entire economies on electricity were in place, battery storage remains unable to plug gaps when the wind does not blow and the sun does not shine. Nuclear power plants provide a constant supply but take years to build.

这听起来对那些“想要驱逐像英国石油这样的商业公司的”气候活动家来说很有吸引力，英国石油的老板最近说高油价已经把公司变成了一台“现金机器”。但今天的能源危机表明，世界需要谨慎地逐步减少碳排放，而不是突然停止对化石燃料的投资，尤其是如果欧洲想要摆脱对俄罗斯天然气的依赖。在某些任务中，可再生能源不能立即取代天然气，比如用燃气锅炉供暖。即使整个经济依靠电力运行的基础设施已经到位，当风不刮、太阳不照射时，蓄电池仍然无法填补缺口。核电站提供稳定的供应，但需要数年时间才能建成。

The European Commission says that renewable producers, which are also benefiting from high prices, should pay up too. This is doubly misguided. If even clean-energy companies have their profits seized during periods of shortages then the incentive to solve renewables’ intermittency problem, for example by making batteries better or by storing energy as hydrogen, will be blunted. And it is not just power shortages that need to be plugged as economies move to net zero. The private sector will need to find ways around shortages of everything from the minerals used in electric cars to the balsa wood used in wind turbines. It is a fantasy to think that the vast investments that are necessary will happen if the most innovative firms worry that their profits could be seized when their bets pay off.

欧盟委员会表示，同样受益于高价格的可再生能源生产商也应该为此“付出代价”。这是双重误导。如果连清洁能源公司在短缺时期的利润都被攫取，那么解决可再生能源间歇性问题的动力(例如制造更好的电池或将能源储存为氢气)将会减弱。随着经济走向净零，需要解决的不仅仅是电力短缺问题。私营部门将需要找到解决各种短缺的方法，从用于电动汽车的矿物到用于风力涡轮机的轻木。如果最具创新精神的公司担心他们的利润会在他们的“押注”成功后被没收，那么认为这种必要的巨额投资将会发生的想法简直是天方夜谭。

The thorniest argument is that companies are benefiting from war. Windfall taxes live up to their name when firms have profited not from wise decisions, but from unforeseeable events that are unrelated to their investment choices. Yet geopolitics is a top concern of big energy firms, which must lay pipelines that cross borders and anticipate global energy needs far in advance. There is nothing unusual about a conflict affecting their profits, and the risks posed to Europe from Russian gas have been obvious for years. Hiving off the rewards that are on offer for supplying energy during today’s shortage will only make the next supply crunch—even a predictable one—all the worse. ■

最棘手的争论是公司正从战争中获益。当企业不是从明智的决策中获利，而是从与投资选择无关的不可预见的事件中获利时，暴利税就名副其实了。然而，地缘政治是大型能源公司最关心的问题，它们必须铺设跨境管道，并提前很久预测全球能源需求。冲突影响到他们的利润，这没什么不寻常的，而且俄罗斯天然气给欧洲带来的风险多年来一直很明显。在当前能源短缺的情况下，切断供应能源的奖励只会导致下一次供应短缺（甚至是可以预见的）变得更糟。■

Confronting Russia shows the tension between free trade and freedom

与俄罗斯对抗表明了自由贸易和自由之间的紧张关系

The world economy

世界经济

![image-20220318091117761](/Users/chenyujie/Library/Application Support/typora-user-images/image-20220318091117761.png)

Liberal governments need to find a new path that combines openness and security

自由派政府需要找到一条结合开放和安全的新道路

Mar 19th 2022

THE INVASION of Ukraine is the third big blow to globalisation in a decade. First came President Donald Trump’s trade wars. Next was a pandemic in which cross-border flows of capital, goods and people almost stopped. Now armed conflict in Europe’s breadbasket, besieged Black Sea ports and sanctions on Russia have triggered a supply shock that is ripping through the world economy. Wheat prices have risen by 40%, Europeans may face gas shortages later this year, and there is a squeeze on nickel, used in batteries, including for electric cars. Around the world many firms and consumers are grappling with supply chains that have proved too fragile to depend on—yet again.

入侵乌克兰是十年来全球化遭受的第三次重大打击。第一次是特朗普总统发起的贸易战。第二次是一场新冠大流行，届时资本、货物和人员的跨境流动几乎停止。现在欧洲产粮区的武装冲突、被围住的黑海港口以及对俄罗斯的制裁引发了一场供应冲击, 其正席卷世界经济。小麦价格已经上涨了40%，今年晚些时候，欧洲可能会面临天然气短缺，镍(用于电池，包括电动汽车)也会出现短缺。在世界范围内，许多公司和消费者都在努力解决供应链问题，因为事实证明，他们的供应链过于脆弱而无法再次依赖。

If you look beyond the chaos, Vladimir Putin’s warmongering also raises a question about globalisation that is uncomfortable for free-traders such as The Economist. Is it prudent for open societies to conduct normal economic relations with autocratic ones, such as Russia and XX, that abuse human rights, endanger security and grow more threatening the richer they get? In principle, the answer is simple: democracies should seek to maximise trade without compromising national security. In practice, that is a hard line to draw. Russia’s war shows that a surgical redesign of supply chains is needed to prevent autocratic countries from bullying liberal ones. What the world does not need is a dangerous lurch towards self-sufficiency.

撇开混乱不谈，普京的好战言论还提出了一个关于全球化的问题，这让《经济学人》等自由贸易主义者感到不安。开放的社会与专制国家(如俄罗斯和XX)进行正常的经济关系，这是一种谨慎的做法吗? 这些专制国家践踏人权，危及安全，并随着富裕程度的提高而变得更具威胁性。原则上，答案很简单: 民主国家应在不损害国家安全的情况下寻求贸易最大化。实际上，这是一条很难划定的界限。俄罗斯战争表明，为了防止专制国家欺负自由国家，需要对供应链进行“外科手术式“的重新设计。世界不需要的是朝着自给自足的方向危险地蹒跚而行。

For most of the past few decades, it has been clear how to trade with the enemy. In the cold war the West and the totalitarian Soviet bloc conducted trade in energy and grain but had a low overall level of interlinking. After the Berlin Wall fell, it was widely assumed that free trade and freedom would conquer the world together, reinforcing each other. And for a while they did. In the 1990s the share of countries with democratic rule rose as tariffs fell and more container ships crossed the oceans. Russians got their first taste of Big Macs and the ballot box within an 18-month spell. Bill Clinton welcomed XX’s entry into the global trading system in 2000, predicting that it would have “a profound impact on human rights and political liberty” there.

在过去几十年的大部分时间里，如何与敌人进行贸易已经很清楚了。冷战时期，西方和苏联集团进行了能源和粮食贸易，但整体联系水平较低。柏林墙倒塌后，人们普遍认为自由贸易和自由将共同征服世界，相互促进。在一段时间内，他们确实做到了。上世纪90年代，随着关税的降低和更多集装箱船的越洋，实行民主统治的国家比例上升。在18个月的时间里，俄罗斯人第一次尝到了巨无霸的滋味，也第一次拥有了投票权。2000年，比尔•克林顿对XX加入全球贸易体系表示欢迎，并预测这将对那里的“人权和政治自由产生深远影响”。

But in the past decade and a half liberty has retreated, with the share of people living in democracies falling below 50%. In many autocratic places, including XX and the Middle East, political reform appears unlikely. The result is a globalised economy in which autocracies account for 31% of GDP, or 14% excluding XX. Unlike the USSR, these autocracies are economically intertwined with liberal societies. A third of democracies’ goods imports are from them, and a third of multinational investment in autocracies is from democracies. Open societies trade over $15bn a day with closed ones, buying XX-made PCs and Saudi oil, and selling Bulgari and Boeings.

但在过去15年里，自由已经“退却”，生活在民主国家的人口比例降至50%以下。在包括XX和中东在内的许多专制国家，政治改革似乎不太可能。其结果是经济全球化，专制统治占GDP的31%，不包括XX则占14%。与苏联不同，这些专制国家在经济上与自由社会交织在一起。民主国家三分之一的商品进口来自专制国家，而对专制国家的跨国投资有三分之一来自民主国家。开放社会与封闭社会每天的贸易额超过150亿美元，购买XX制造的个人电脑和沙特石油，出售宝格丽和波音。

Russia’s invasion has shown the West the perils of trading with adversaries. One concern is moral. All those deals for Urals crude and Black Sea wheat bankrolled Mr Putin’s repression and his rapidly increasing military spending. Another is security, with Europe addicted to Russian gas and many industries reliant on inputs including fertilisers and metals. Such dependency may make autocracies stronger, weaken democracies’ resolve and expose them to retaliation in a war. No country embodies this Faustian pact more than gas-dependent Germany.

俄罗斯的入侵向西方展示了与对手进行贸易的危险。一个担忧是道德问题。所有这些乌拉尔原油和黑海小麦交易，为普京的镇压和迅速增加的军事开支提供了资金。另一个担忧是安全问题，欧洲依赖俄罗斯的天然气，许多行业依赖化肥和金属等输入。这种依赖可能会让专制政权更加强大，削弱民主国家的决心，并让它们在战争中遭到报复。没有哪个国家比依赖天然气的德国更能体现这一浮士德式契约。

This tension between the logic of free trade and support for political liberalism will create deeper fissures. Already the world has faced years of what The Economist has called slowbalisation, with trade and capital flows falling relative to GDP. Some autocracies may now seek to decouple further from the West. XX views the collapse of Russia’s fortress economy in the face of Western sanctions as a botched experiment from which to learn before it considers going to war over XX. Saudi Arabia is cosying up to XX. The world’s autocracies have too little in common to form a cohesive economic bloc, but they are united in their desire to reduce the influence the West has over them, in areas from tech to currency reserves.

自由贸易的逻辑与对政治自由主义的支持之间的这种紧张关系，将造成更深的裂痕。多年来，世界已经面临着《经济学人》所称的“慢球化”，即贸易和资本流动相对于GDP下降。一些专制国家现在可能寻求与西方进一步脱钩。XX将俄罗斯堡垒经济在面对西方的制裁时的崩溃视为一次拙劣的尝试，在考虑对XX发动战争之前，其可以从中吸取教训。沙特阿拉伯正在讨好XX。世界上的专制国家没有太多共同之处，以至于无法形成一个有凝聚力的经济集团，但他们都希望在从科技到外汇储备等领域减少西方对他们的影响。

The temptation in the West, meanwhile, is to pivot towards a more limited kind of trade with military allies, or even to outright self-reliance. Consider President Joe Biden’s recent state-of-the-union address which included a promise that “everything from the deck of an aircraft-carrier to the steel on highway guardrails is made in America from beginning to end. All of it.”

与此同时，西方面临的诱惑是转向与军事盟友进行更有限的贸易，甚至转向完全自力更生。美国总统乔•拜登在最近的国情咨文演讲中承诺:“从航空母舰的甲板到高速公路护栏上的钢铁，从头到尾都是美国制造。所有都是。”

A retreat by the West to cold-war spheres of influence or self-reliance would be a mistake. The costs would be vast. Roughly $3trn of investment would be written off for less efficient production that fuels inflation and hurts living standards. It would be morally dubious: globalisation has helped over a billion people raise themselves from poverty, and trade and information links with the middle classes in autocracies sustain the cause of liberalism. It would not even boost democracies’ security. Supply chains get stronger through diversification, not concentration. And by walling themselves off, rich democracies would alienate countries that do not want to pick sides between the West, Russia and XX—countries that account for a fifth of world GDP and two-thirds of its people.

西方退回到冷战的势力范围或自力更生将是一个错误。这个代价将是巨大的。大约3万亿美元的投资将被冲销，用于生产效率较低的产品，这些产品加剧了通胀，损害了生活水平。这在道德上是不确定的:全球化已经帮助超过10亿人摆脱了贫困，与专制国家的中产阶级的贸易和信息联系支撑着自由主义的事业。它甚至不会促进民主国家的安全。供应链是通过多元化而不是集中而变得更强大。发达的民主国家通过降自己封闭起来，会疏远那些不想在西方、俄罗斯和XX之间选边站的国家，这些国家的GDP占世界的五分之一，人口占世界的三分之二。

How then should globalisation be reconfigured? In war, severing economic relations makes sense. In peace the goal should be to limit exports of only the most sensitive technologies to illiberal regimes. When autocracies have the power to intimidate, as Russia has with gas, the aim should not be national self-sufficiency, but rather to require firms to diversify their suppliers, in turn stimulating investment in new sources of supply from energy to electronics. These choke-points make up about a tenth of global trade, based on the export earnings of authoritarian powers from goods where they have a leading market share of over 10% and where it is hard to find substitutes.

那么，应该如何重新配置全球化? 在战争中，切断经济关系是有道理的。和平时期的目标应该是限制向反自由政权出口最敏感的技术。当专制国家有能力进行恐吓时，就像俄罗斯在天然气问题上所做的那样，目标不应该是国家自给自足，而是要求企业多元化供应商，进而刺激从能源到电子产品等新供应来源的投资。这些瓶颈约占全球贸易的十分之一，这是基于专制国家的出口收入，这些国家的商品拥有超过10%的领先市场份额，而且很难找到替代品。

Interdependence day

Mr Putin has given a harsh lesson that in these areas democracies must change their posture. The war is a tragedy, but it is also a moment of clarity. The vision of the 1990s, that free trade and freedom would go hand in hand, has fractured. Liberal governments need to find a new path that combines openness and security, and prevents the dream of globalisation turning sour. ■

普京给了我们一个严酷的教训: 在这些领域，民主国家必须改变姿态。这场战争是一场悲剧，但它也是一个清晰的时刻。上世纪90年代自由贸易和自由将携手并进的愿景已经破碎。自由派政府需要找到一条结合开放和安全的新道路，并防止全球化的梦想破灭。■

帕金森氏症是一种进行性神经疾病，由大脑黑质部分神经元死亡引起。症状包括丧失运动控制、情绪紊乱、睡眠被破坏以及嗅觉改变。它是无法治愈的。然而，早期的医疗干预可以缓解这些症状，延长生存期。这就需要能够及时诊断。

Unfortunately, the initial signs of Parkinson’s vary from person to person and there is no specific test at this early stage which can reliably distinguish it from other brain illnesses. It therefore often goes undetected until clear and characteristic manifestations, such as tremors and slowed body movement, appear. But that may soon change. A study published in ACS Omega, by Chen Xing and Liu Jun at Zhejiang University, in China, describes an invention which may be able to detect Parkinson’s before the onset of tremulousness. The device in question is an artificially intelligent electronic nose.

不幸的是，帕金森氏症的最初症状因人而异，在这个早期阶段还没有专门的测试可以可靠地将其与其他脑部疾病区分开来。因此，直到出现明显和典型的症状，如颤抖和身体运动迟缓，它才会被发现。但这种情况可能很快就会改变。中国浙江大学的Chen Xing和Liu Jun发表在《ACS Omega》期刊上的一项研究描述了一项发明，该发明可能能够在震颤发作前检测出帕金森氏症。其讨论的设备是一个人工智能电子鼻。

A whiff of trouble

The quest the two researchers embarked on to build this nose began in 2019. That was when they heard reports of Joy Milne, a retired nurse living in Scotland, who could detect people with Parkinson’s from a distinctive odour they emitted—distinctive to her, at least, though for reasons still obscure, undetectable by others.

这两位研究人员从2019年开始着手构造这种鼻子。就在那时，他们听到了居住在苏格兰的退休护士Joy Milne的报告，她可以通过帕金森患者散发出的一种特殊气味来检测他们，至少这个气味对她来说是特殊的，尽管原因尚不清楚，其他人无法察觉。

Mrs Milne first noticed this odour when her husband developed the illness. She made the general connection later, when she smelled it at sufferers’ support groups attended by her spouse. Tests using clothes worn by patients confirmed her ability. She even noted one seemingly healthy individual as having the disease months before other symptoms developed.

Milne女士第一次注意到这种气味是在她丈夫患病时。后来，当她在她配偶参加的患者支持小组中闻到这种气味时，她把这种感觉联系了起来。通过使用病人穿的衣服进行的测试证实了她的能力。她甚至注意到一个看似健康的人在出现其他症状的几个月前就患上了这种疾病。

Carting Mrs Milne around the world to sniff patients who may have Parkinson’s is, however, not a practical option, so researchers working with her looked for the odour’s source, with a view to detecting it in some other way. They found it in sebum, an oily secretion produced by the skin. The sebum of those with Parkinson’s, they discovered, has unusually high concentrations of certain volatile organic compounds, including dodecane, acetone and ethyl acetate. When these are acted on by yeast cells which live naturally on the skin, the result is the mysterious odour.

然而，让Milne女士周游世界去嗅嗅可能患有帕金森氏症的病人并不是一个切实可行的选择，所以与她一起工作的研究人员寻找气味的来源，以期用其他方式来探测气味。他们在皮脂中发现了它，皮脂是皮肤分泌的一种油性分泌物。他们发现，帕金森氏症患者的皮脂中，某些挥发性有机化合物的浓度异常高，包括十二烷、丙酮和乙酸乙酯。当皮肤上自然生长的酵母细胞对它们起作用时，就会产生神秘的气味。

This Parkinson’s-specific list can be detected using a laboratory technique called gas chromatography-mass spectrometry (GCMS), which is promising—for GCMS machines are a bit more deployable than Mrs Milne is. Unfortunately, they are still too complex and expensive to be used routinely in clinics. However, Dr Chen and Dr Liu, who are both biomedical engineers, reckoned they could come up with a cheaper and more portable alternative.

这种帕金森氏症的特异性列表可以通过一种叫做气相色谱-质谱法(GC-MS)的实验室技术来检测，这是一种很有前途的技术，因为GCMS机器比Milne女士更易部署。不幸的是，这些仪器仍然过于复杂和昂贵，无法在诊所常规使用。然而，Chen博士和Liu博士都是生物医学工程师，他们认为他们可以想出一种更便宜、更便携的替代品。

The upshot is a machine not much bigger than a toaster. It turns a sample of sebum into a vapour that is then probed with sound waves. Different molecular mixtures have different effects on the waves, and, with a bit of training, a computer loaded with an appropriate piece of artificial intelligence can learn to recognise the pattern associated with sebum from Parkinson’s patients.

最终就是一台比烤面包机大不了多少的机器。它将皮脂样本转化为气体，然后用声波探测。不同的分子混合物对声波有不同的影响，只要稍加训练，一台装有适当人工智能的计算机就能学会识别帕金森氏症患者的皮脂相关模式。

The researchers tested their system on samples from 43 people with Parkinson’s disease and 44 who were healthy. They found it could correctly identify a Parkinson’s patient as having the disease about 70% of the time and a healthy control as being clear of it about 80%. That is not yet as good as Mrs Milne’s nose, which has a history of correctly identifying the presence of Parkinson’s all the time. But it is a start. If its reliability can be improved, the compactness and potential cheapness of the system Dr Chen and Dr Liu have come up with may eventually prove a boon for the early diagnosis of the illness. ■

研究人员对43名帕金森氏症患者和44名健康人的样本进行了测试。他们发现，在70%的情况下，该系统可以正确地识别出帕金森病患者，而在80%的情况下，健康对照组中没有识别出帕金森病患者。这还不如 Milne女士的鼻子，它一直以来都能正确地识别出帕金森氏症的存在。但这只是一个开始。如果该系统的可靠性能够得到提高，那么Chen博士和Liu博士所提出的系统的便携性和潜在的廉价性可能最终可能会为疾病的早期诊断带来好处。■
