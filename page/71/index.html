Mario Draghi gives Italy another chance

马里奥·德拉吉(Mario Draghi)给了意大利又一次机会

From banker to prime minister

从银行家到首相

![image-20210223095018918](/Users/chenyujie/Library/Application Support/typora-user-images/image-20210223095018918.png)

His appointment as prime minister is good for the country and for Europe

他被任命为首相，对英国和欧洲都有好处

Feb 20th 2021

ITALY IS BIG enough to break Europe. Some countries, such as Greece or Portugal, are highly indebted but their fellow Europeans can bail them out, if necessary. Others, like France, Spain or indeed Germany, have large debts in absolute terms, but thanks to the size of their economies and a decent record of growth they can cope without spooking the markets. Only Italy has the triple whammy: a big debt stock in both relative and absolute terms, plus an economy that was stagnant even before covid-19 struck. The arrival of Mario Draghi, sworn in as Italy’s prime minister on February 13th, offers some hope that Europe’s sick man may get a vital healing shot.

whammy 打击

triple whammy 三重打击

swear in 使..宣誓就职

意大利（债务）大到足以摧毁欧洲。一些国家，如希腊或葡萄牙，债台高筑，但如有必要，他们的欧洲同胞可以帮助他们摆脱困境。其他国家，如法国、西班牙甚至德国，在绝对价值上拥有巨额债务，但由于他们的经济规模和良好的增长记录，他们可以在不惊动市场的情况下应对。只有意大利面临着三重打击:其相对和绝对债务存量都很大，加上该国经济甚至在covid-19爆发之前就已陷入停滞。2月13日，马里奥•德拉吉(Mario Draghi)宣誓就任意大利总理，他的到来给这个欧洲病夫打了一针至关重要的治愈针，带来了一些希望。

Mr Draghi, an ex-boss of the European Central Bank, is the latest in a long line of technocrats to be installed in the prime minister’s office. That is hardly ideal. Unelected heads of government are in principle a snub to democracy. They are often bad at communicating with the public. Their elevation can play into the hands of populists, who will always claim that the elites are conspiring to do down the masses. When the prime minister in question is a former international banker, the demagogic slogans practically write themselves.

德拉吉是欧洲央行前行长，他是一长串被任命为总理的技术官僚中的最新一位。这并不理想。非选举产生的政府首脑在原则上是对民主的一种冷落。他们通常不善于与公众沟通。他们的晋升可能正中民粹主义者的下心，民粹主义者总是声称精英们正在密谋打压大众。当被讨论的首相是一位前国际银行家时，那些蛊惑人心的口号实际上不言自明【煽动性的口号实际上是自己写的】。

Still, Mr Draghi commands the support of all Italy’s main parties, with the sole exception of the Brothers of Italy, an outfit with neo-fascist origins, which will no doubt snipe dangerously from the sidelines. Mr Draghi is more than just a technocrat; he also has considerable political and diplomatic skills, as he showed when shepherding the euro through its crisis a decade ago. He will need them.

尽管如此，德拉吉还是赢得了意大利所有主要政党的支持，除了意大利兄弟党，这是一个具有新法西斯主义渊源的组织，毫无疑问，它会在一旁进行危险的攻击。德拉吉不仅仅是一位技术官僚;他还有相当的政治和外交技巧，就像他在10年前带领欧元渡过危机时所展示的那样。他将需要他们。

Previous governments have often broadly agreed about what has to be done to rescue Italy from its chronic malaise. It is one of the worst places in the European Union in which to do business, owing to a sluggish and erratic judicial system, a weakness for red tape and a tax system that discourages job creation. Government subsidies have failed to correct the deep structural imbalance between the prosperous north and the mezzogiorno, Italy’s south, one of Europe’s least prosperous regions. All these things need fixing, but a series of weak, cash-strapped coalition governments have made little progress. Mr Draghi has a chance to do better. For now, at least, he has a huge majority in parliament.

malaise 莫名的不安；难以描述的问题

前几届政府经常就如何拯救意大利摆脱长期困境达成广泛共识。它是欧盟(EU)最不适合做生意的地方之一，原因是该国的司法系统迟缓且不稳定，缺乏繁文缛节，税收制度阻碍了创造就业机会。政府补贴未能纠正繁荣的北部和意大利南部（欧洲最不繁荣的地区之一）梅佐戈诺（mezzogiorno）之间严重的结构性失衡。所有这些问题都需要解决，但一系列疲弱、资金短缺的联合政府进展甚微。德拉吉有机会做得更好。至少现在，他在议会拥有绝大多数席位。

He will also have plenty of sugar to help the unpleasant medicine go down. Thanks to a €750bn ($900bn) recovery fund that the EU agreed to last summer, Italy is eligible for around €200bn in grants and loans over the next six years. The money comes with the right kind of conditions attached. Much of it must be spent on green projects or digital ones; and agreeing to a detailed programme of reforms is a key part of the mix. Italy’s draft plan is better than some that other member states have submitted to Brussels. Even so, Mr Draghi needs to beef it up. In a speech to parliament on February 17th he hit the right notes, promising to reform taxes, the courts and public administration, but also promising not to bail out unviable firms.

beef up 增加

他还将有大量的糖来帮助吞下这令人不快的药。由于欧盟(EU)去年夏天同意提供7500亿欧元(合9000亿美元)的复苏基金，意大利有资格在未来6年获得约2000亿欧元的赠款和贷款。这笔钱附带着合适的条件。其中大部分必须用于绿色项目或数字项目;而就一项详细的改革方案达成一致是其中的关键部分。意大利的草案比其他成员国提交给布鲁塞尔的方案要好。即便如此，德拉吉也需要加强这一机制。在2月17日对议会的一次演讲中，他做出了正确的选择，承诺将改革税收、法院和公共管理，但同时也承诺不会救助无法生存的公司。

If Mr Draghi is a good bet for Italy, he looks a good one for the EU as well. The European Council could use another heavyweight. Angela Merkel is on her way out, having promised that her current term as Germany’s chancellor is her last; an election is due on September 26th. Emmanuel Macron faces his own re-election battle early next year. Britain, in the past the swing vote in Europe’s trio of dominant powers, has abandoned the stage.

bet 选择

heavyweight 有影响力的人

如果说德拉吉对意大利来说是一个不错的选择，那么他对欧盟来说也是一个不错的选择。欧洲理事会可能需要另一个重量级人物。安格拉默克尔即将卸任，她曾承诺，目前的德国总理任期是她最后一个任期；大选将于9月26日举行。埃曼纽尔·马克龙明年初将面临自己的连任之战。英国，过去在欧洲三个主要大国的摇摆投票中，已经放弃了舞台。

A powerful and highly regarded Italian will also help shift the EU’s ideological balance in the right direction. If it is to survive and thrive, the bloc needs to invest a good deal more money, raising it on the international markets and so allowing its weaker countries to benefit from the credit of the union as a whole. The recovery fund offers a good template that should be used again in the future; Mr Draghi will be well placed to press for that. This can happen, though, only if the existing plan is a success. If Super Mario cannot make it work for Italy, perhaps no one can. ■

一个实力强大、备受尊敬的意大利人也将有助于将欧盟的意识形态平衡转向正确的方向。如果欧盟想要生存和繁荣，它就需要投入更多资金，在国际市场上筹集资金，从而使其弱国能够从整个欧盟的信贷中受益。复苏基金提供了一个很好的模板，将来应该再次使用;德拉吉将很好地推动这一点。不过，只有在现有计划成功的情况下，这种情况才有可能发生。如果超级马里奥不能为意大利效力，或许也没有人能做到。■

How America can rid itself of both carbon and blackouts

美国如何摆脱碳排放和停电

America and climate change

美国和气候变化

![image-20210222110210728](/Users/chenyujie/Library/Application Support/typora-user-images/image-20210222110210728.png)

This is the moment for an ambitious attempt to deal with climate change

现在正是应对气候变化的雄心勃勃的时刻

Feb 20th 2021

TEXAS PRIDES itself on being different. Yet it is in the grip of a winter storm that typifies the Snowmageddon-size problems facing energy in America. Although nobody can be sure if this particular freeze is a sign of climate change, the growing frequency of extreme weather across the country is. Texan infrastructure has buckled. The problem is not, as some argue, that Texas has too many renewables. Gas-fired plants and a nuclear reactor were hit, as well as wind turbines. Worse, Texas had too little capacity and its poorly connected grid was unable to import power from elsewhere. Texas shows that America needs both a cleaner grid and a more reliable one.

buckle 垮掉；撑不住

clean 边缘整齐的；平滑的；流线型的；简洁流畅的

德州以与众不同而自豪。然而，它正处于一场冬季风暴的控制之中，这是美国能源面临的雪灾规模问题的典型代表。虽然没有人能确定这次特别的冰冻期是否是气候变化的迹象，但全国各地日益频繁的极端天气确实是（气候变化的迹象）。德克萨斯的基础设施已经崩溃。问题并不像一些人所说的那样是德州有太多的可再生能源。燃气发电厂和一个核反应堆以及风力涡轮机都受到了冲击。更糟糕的是，德州的电力容量太少，其连接不良的电网无法从其他地方输送电力。德州的情况表明，美国既需要一个更清洁的电网，也需要一个更可靠的电网。

Plans to overhaul American energy will come before Congress in the next few months. President Joe Biden has said that he wants fossil-fuel emissions from power generation to end by 2035 and the economy to be carbon-neutral by 2050. America is not just the world’s second-largest emitter, but also a source of climate-related policy, technology and, potentially, leadership. What is about to unfold in Washington will set the course in America for the next decade—and quite possibly beyond.

emitter 排放者

美国能源改革计划将在未来几个月提交国会。美国总统乔•拜登(Joe Biden)曾表示，他希望到2035年结束发电产生的化石燃料排放，到2050年实现经济碳中和。美国不仅是世界第二大排放国，还是气候相关政策、技术和潜在领导力的来源。华盛顿即将发生的事情将为美国下一个十年甚至很可能更久的未来定下方向。

Time is pressing. Neither Mr Biden nor his successors may get a second chance to recast policy on such a scale. Global emissions from fossil fuels and cement production in 2019 were 16% higher than in 2009. It will be even harder to limit climate change to less than 2°C above the pre-industrial level, the global threshold from which America’s target for 2050 comes. To be carbon neutral, the world must curb emissions by 7.6% a year for a decade, a steeper decline than in 2020, when covid-19 cut demand for oil and coal. For America, delaying action to 2030 would nearly double the cost of reaching net zero or, more likely, mean it overshoots its targets.

时间是紧迫的。拜登和他的继任者都不会有第二次机会在如此规模上改写政策。2019年全球化石燃料和水泥生产排放比2009年高出16%。将气候变化限制在比工业化前水平(美国2050年目标的全球门槛)高出2摄氏度以内将更加困难。要实现碳中和，全球必须在10年内每年减排7.6%，降幅大于2020年，当时covid-19降低了对石油和煤炭的需求。对美国来说，推迟到2030年采取行动将使达到净零的成本增加近一倍，或者更有可能意味着它超出了目标。

Yet there are grounds for hope. Although the Republican Party is against almost all action, voters are increasingly alarmed by climate change. Two-thirds of them think the federal government is doing too little about it, and that share includes plenty of younger Republicans. Although the fossil-fuel lobby remains powerful, many Republican business donors want more action—partly because asset managers are urging firms to align their strategies with the net-zero world Mr Biden envisions.

然而，有理由抱有希望。尽管共和党反对几乎所有的行动，但选民对气候变化的担忧与日俱增。他们中有三分之二的人认为联邦政府在这方面做得太少，这一比例包括很多年轻的共和党人。尽管化石燃料游说团依然强大，但许多共和党商业捐助者希望采取更多行动——部分原因是资产管理公司正敦促企业将其战略与拜登先生所设想的净零世界相一致。

Most encouraging of all, the costs of power from wind and solar have plunged by 70% and 90% over the past decade. Along with cheap gas, this has already helped America decarbonise at an impressive rate, despite Donald Trump’s rolling back of fossil-fuel regulations. Price has not been the only factor; more than half of the states have some sort of clean-energy mandate, a device that Mr Biden wants to introduce on a national scale.

最鼓舞人心的是，风能和太阳能发电的成本在过去十年里分别下降了70%和90%。再加上廉价的天然气，这已经帮助美国以惊人的速度脱碳，尽管唐纳德·特朗普(Donald Trump)撤销了化石燃料法规。价格并不是唯一的因素;超过一半的州都有某种清洁能源的授权，这是拜登先生想在全国范围内推行的一项措施。

This involves a regulatory framework that favours renewable-energy developments and grid connections to hook them up. It will take a lot of extra investment—about $2.5trn in the coming decade, say researchers at Princeton (see ). In a new book, Bill Gates, a billionaire philanthropist, argues that research is needed into a host of areas such as energy storage, advanced nuclear reactors to complement renewables and technologies for clean concrete-making and other activities that are hard to decarbonise. Without these, even if a clean grid is powering electric cars and light trucks, it will displace only around half of emissions.

这涉及到一个有利于可再生能源开发和电网连接的监管框架。普林斯顿大学的研究人员说，在未来十年，这将需要大量额外投资，大约2.5万亿美元。亿万富翁慈善家比尔•盖茨在他的新书中指出，需要对许多领域进行研究，如能源储存、先进的核反应堆，以补充可再生能源、清洁混凝土制造技术以及其他难以脱碳的活动。如果没有这些，即使一个清洁的电网为电动汽车和轻型卡车提供动力，它也只能替代大约一半的排放。

America is good at innovation, but new ideas need to be deployed at scale, not languish in the lab. One tool is a carbon price which, if it were high enough and if investors believed it would last, would signal what improvements were needed where. But for all its attractions, carbon pricing failed in Congress in 2009. Although many economists and opinion-makers on the right favour it, Republican politicians do not. And even if a carbon price were in place, public-private co-operation would still be needed for America to act as fast as Mr Biden proposes.

美国擅长创新，但新想法需要大规模应用，而不是在实验室里停滞不前/被忽视。一个工具就是碳价格，如果这个价格足够高，而且投资者相信它会持续下去，它就会表明哪些地方需要改进。但尽管碳定价有诸多吸引力，2009年却未能在国会通过。尽管许多经济学家和右翼舆论制造者支持它，但共和党的政客们却不这么认为。而且，即使碳排放价格到位，美国仍需要公私合作才能像拜登先生提议的那样迅速采取行动。

For all those reasons, an ambitious climate-oriented infrastructure bill looks like Mr Biden’s best chance of getting new policy on climate through the Senate. Unfortunately such a plan will be lucky to attract any Republican votes. Yet, if mustering the 60 needed to see off a Senate filibuster is improbable, a plan could be stripped of some measures, including a clean-energy standard, and passed with a simple majority through the parliamentary manoeuvre known as reconciliation. The bill must still be of a scale and ambition that matches America’s challenge.

manoeuvre 策略

基于所有这些原因，一项雄心勃勃的以气候为导向的基础设施法案似乎是拜登先生在参议院通过气候新政策的最佳机会。不幸的是，这样的计划将很幸运地吸引共和党人的选票。然而，如果召集所需的60人来阻止参议院的阻挠是不可能的，这项计划可以取消一些措施，包括清洁能源标准，并通过被称为和解的议会策略以简单多数获得通过。该法案的规模和雄心必须与美国的挑战相匹配。

Failure to act would bring big risks. For a start, it would make America less competitive in the new clean-energy economy. China is the dominant producer of solar panels and batteries; it has also invested in foreign mines to secure minerals needed for them. Europe has its own “green deal” to boost its clean-energy industries. It plans to tax imports from countries that do not pledge to lower their emissions.

不采取行动将带来巨大风险。首先，这会降低美国在新的清洁能源经济中的竞争力。中国是太阳能电池板和电池的主要生产国；中国还投资了外国矿山，以获得它们所需的矿产。欧洲也有自己的“绿色协议”来促进其清洁能源产业。它计划对来自那些不承诺减少排放的国家的进口商品征税。

America would also be deprived of global influence over climate. It has direct control over only about 10% of the world’s greenhouse-gas effluvia. If it wants the benefit of a stabler climate—and with it a stabler world economy, stabler geopolitics and much avoided suffering—it needs to influence the other 90%, too. Mr Biden has appointed John Kerry, a former secretary of state, to spearhead that effort. America is to rejoin the Paris agreement on February 19th, making it a full participant in the UN conference to be held in Glasgow, in Scotland, in November, when countries will be able to lodge new and more ambitious pledges to cut emissions. If America tables goals and gives evidence that it will back them with domestic policy, it will gain influence. China’s two big development banks have doled out $51bn for foreign coal plants since 2008. America should be part of a push against such subsidies.

美国也将失去对全球气候的影响。它只直接控制了世界上10%的温室气体排放。如果它想从一个更稳定的气候，伴随而来的更稳定的世界经济、更稳定的地缘政治以及更多的避免痛苦中获益，它也需要影响其他90%的国家。拜登已经任命前国务卿约翰•克里(John Kerry)作为这项工作的先锋。美国将于2月19日重新加入《巴黎协定》，成为将于11月在苏格兰格拉斯哥举行的联合国大会的正式参与者，届时各国将能够提出新的更雄心勃勃的减排承诺。如果美国提出目标，并提供证据表明它将以国内政策支持这些目标，它将获得影响力。自2008年以来，xx两家大型开发银行已向外国燃煤电厂拨款510亿美元。美国应该参与反对此类补贴的行动。

Enough drifting

Unfortunately, America brings little credibility to action on climate. Mr Trump took pleasure in subverting it, but his country’s poor record precedes him. George W. Bush declined to implement the Kyoto protocol. Congress has not considered serious climate legislation since 2009. Today must be different. There will never be a better chance for Mr Biden to show real ambition. If the blackouts in Texas are any guide, it would not just be the world that would thank him, but Americans, too. ■

不幸的是，美国在气候问题上的行动缺乏可信度。特朗普乐于颠覆它，但美国的糟糕记录早在他之前就出现了。小布什拒绝执行《京都议定书》。自2009年以来，国会就没有认真考虑过气候立法。今天必须有所不同。对拜登来说，再也没有比这更好的机会来展示他真正的野心了。如果德克萨斯州的停电事件能起到什么指导作用的话，那么不仅全世界会感谢他，美国人也会感谢他。■

When will office workers return?

Bartleby 巴托比

![image-20210221085932059](/Users/chenyujie/Library/Application Support/typora-user-images/image-20210221085932059.png)

Getting back to normal will involve a host of challenges

恢复正常将涉及一系列的挑战

Feb 18th 2021

WHEN WILL most people be back at the office? As with Tantalus and the fruit tree, the prize seems to be close, only to recede out of reach. A survey conducted by Morgan Stanley, an investment bank, found that employees have adjusted their expectations for when they are likely to repopulate their desks, moving the date back from April to June. Given the slow pace of vaccinations in some countries, even that may be optimistic.

背景：

反乌托邦字面意思是“不好的地方”（not-good place），它是一种不得人心、令人恐惧的假想社群或社会，是与理想社会相反的，一种极端恶劣的社会最终形态。反乌托邦常常表征为反人类、极权政府、生态灾难或其他社会性的灾难性衰败。

二月/三月:一开始，一切都很混乱。在早期阶段，《在越南最后的日子》的感觉弥漫在市中心。火车和办公室逐渐不那么拥挤了;由于人手不足，越来越多的商店关门了。父母们变成了狩猎采集者，拼命地在超市的过道里寻找最后的意面。成功的拾荒者的手推车里装满了一卷卷的卫生纸。人们已经为反乌托邦做好了准备。

Office workers hastily caught up on the disaster-recovery plans they had previously ignored and were grateful if they were able to get a good broadband connection. Bartleby remembered that he had left all his research back at the office and made a sheepish return to a near-empty building. Heading back out with a rucksack of books and papers, he felt like a very nerdish barbarian participating in the sack of Rome.

hastily 匆忙地；草率地

catch up on sth 赶上

sheepish 难为情的

rucksack 背包，旅行包

nerdish <美><俚>笨得令人讨厌的

Head back 转身，折回

办公室职员们匆忙地赶上了他们之前忽略的灾难恢复计划，如果他们能够获得良好的宽带连接，他们会很感激。巴托比想起他把所有的研究成果都留在了办公室里，于是怯怯地回到了一栋几乎空无一人的大楼。带着一背包的书和报纸，他感觉自己就像一个参与了对罗马的掠夺的非常愚蠢的野蛮人。

April: Some individuals were still struggling to master Zoom etiquette. Faced with an editorial meeting on a bank holiday, Bartleby combined it with a soothing river walk. At some point, his phone (while still in his pocket) became unmuted, meaning that his heavy trudge, and heavy breathing, was audible to every other participant on the call. In blissful ignorance, he returned home to a blizzard of emails, tweets and WhatsApp messages telling him to shut up. Sure enough, “you’re on mute” and “please mute yourself” became the breakout phrases of 2020.

bank holiday 公共假期

blissful 极乐的；满足的

四月:有些人还在努力掌握Zoom礼仪。面对在公共假期召开的编辑会议，巴托比把它与舒缓的河边散步结合起来。在某个时候，他的手机(还在口袋里的时候)调成了非静音，这意味着他沉重的跋涉和沉重的呼吸都能被通话中的其他参与者听到。在极乐般的无知中，他回到家，收到了大量的电子邮件、推特和WhatsApp信息，让他闭嘴。果然，“你在静音”和“请你自己静音”成了2020年的流行语。

May: Perhaps the best month of the lockdown. The British weather was good, with the sunniest spring on record, making it possible to work in the garden. The novelty of working from home had yet to wear off, and the absence of the daily commute was still a blessing.

wear off 逐渐消失

五月：也许是封锁期间最好的一个月。英国的天气很好，有史以来阳光最充足的春天，使人们可以在花园里工作。在家工作的新鲜感还没有消退，没有日常通勤仍然是一件幸事。

June: A “Groundhog Day” syndrome had set in. Every day seemed the same; weekends lost their meaning. The main dilemma for the month was whether to cancel the summer holiday, or to hold on in the hope that the decline in covid-19 cases was permanent. The prospect of any break in routine seemed absurdly alluring.

set in 来临

Groundhog Day：a situation in which events that have happened before happen again, in what seems to be exactly the same way

過去發生的事情又再次同樣發生，周而復始

hold on 等待

六月:“土拨鼠日”综合症开始了。每天似乎都一样;周末失去了意义。这个月的主要困境是，是取消暑假，还是抱着covid-19病例的下降是永久性的希望而等待下去。任何打破常规的可能性似乎都是荒谬地诱人。

July: Foreign holiday cancelled. Start to fantasise about ways of shortening Zoom meetings. How about a countdown clock, like the ones on television game shows, as speakers approach the one-minute mark, with a loud buzzer at the end? Hint to all participants: when the person chairing the meeting asks, “Does anyone else have any comments?”, the correct answer is invariably “No”.

countdown 倒计时

invariably 一贯地

七月:出国度假取消。开始幻想缩短Zoom会议的方法。像电视游戏节目里的那种倒计时钟怎么样?当说话者说了快到一分钟的时候，最后会响起响亮的蜂鸣声。提示所有与会者:当主持会议的人问:“还有其他人有什么意见吗?”，正确答案总是“不”。

August: Rain ruins short domestic holiday. Restaurants reopen and Britons recreate the feasts of Bacchanalia. “Eat, drink and be merry for tomorrow we get locked down” seems to be the (prescient) motto. Meetings no shorter on return to work. More extreme measures clearly required: a mild electric shock for those who speak for more than two minutes? Or the “raise hand” button could be converted into a “thumbs down” function. If more than half the participants press it, the speaker is cut off.

prescient 有先见之明的

八月:大雨破坏了短暂的国内假期。餐馆重新开张，英国人重现了酒神节的盛宴。“吃喝玩乐，因为明天我们会被锁起来”似乎是(有先见之明的)座右铭。回到工作岗位后的会议时间不会缩短。显然需要采取更极端的措施:对发言超过两分钟的人进行轻微电击?或者“举起手”按钮可以转换成“拇指向下”功能。如果超过一半的参与者按下按钮，演讲人就会被打断。

September: Just as The Economist organises weekly in-person gatherings so staff can start the process of returning to work, cases begin to rise. Tip for readers: if Bartleby is invited to a summer party in 2021, it is a sign of the impending apocalypse.

apocalypse 世界末日；大灾变

九月:就在《经济学人》每周组织面对面的员工聚会，以便员工可以开始重返工作岗位的过程时，病例开始上升。给读者的提示:如果巴托比受邀参加2021年的夏季派对，那就预示着世界末日即将来临。

October: Head back to now-empty office to pick up more books. Feel like archaeologist analysing ancient civilisation. In this era, humans sat in glass booths so they could be observed at all times. They also gathered in “meeting rooms” to take part in religious ceremonies conducted by a priest known as the “manager”, who recited a long list of meaningless tasks penitents must undertake.

penitent 忏悔者；悔罪者

十月:回到空无一人的办公室去拿更多的书。感觉就像考古学家在分析古代文明。在这个时代，人类坐在玻璃隔间里，因此他们可以随时被观察到。他们还聚集在“会议室”，参加由一位被称为“经理”的牧师主持的宗教仪式，这位牧师背诵了一长串忏悔者必须承担的毫无意义的任务。

November: British government imposes new national lockdown on November 5th. From this year on, the date will no longer be commemorated as “Guy Fawkes night” but as “Boris Johnson day”. All citizens will celebrate by wearing masks, washing their hands obsessively and avoiding their neighbours.

commemorate 纪念；作为对..的纪念

obsessively 过分地；着魔般地

十一月：11月5日，英国政府实施新的全国封锁。从今年起，这一天将不再作为对“盖伊福克斯之夜”的纪念，而是“鲍里斯约翰逊日”。所有市民都会戴上口罩，拼命洗手，并避开他们的邻居。

December: The house has lights, and a tree. But the real meaning of Christmas now becomes clear: no more Zoom meetings for at least a week. Not just silent nights, but silent days as well.

十二月:房子里有灯，还有一棵树。但圣诞节的真正意义现在变得清晰起来:至少一周内不再召开Zoom会议。不只是安静的夜晚，还有安静的白天。

January: The vaccines are on their way to save us. Perhaps at some point in 2021 Bartleby will be back on the London underground, crammed in like a sardine while waiting for the platform to clear at Earl’s Court. Suddenly, social isolation doesn’t seem so bad after all.

on one’s way 在去的路上；在来的路上；在路上

after all 毕竟 ; 居然 ; 终究 ; 竟然;居然

一月:疫苗即将拯救我们。也许2021年的某个时候，巴托比会回到伦敦地铁，像沙丁鱼一样挤在伯爵宫的站台上等待候车。突然之间，社交孤立似乎并不是那么糟糕。
