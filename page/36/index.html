Rio Tinto and the problem of toxic culture

力拓集团和它的“有毒”文化的问题

Bartleby

巴托比

![image-20220214102112223](/Users/chenyujie/Library/Application Support/typora-user-images/image-20220214102112223.png)

A disturbing report has lessons for other industries

一份令人不安的报告给其他行业带来了教训

Feb 12th 2022

CORPORATE CULTURE is often like mist—indubitably there but hard to pin down. Occasionally it solidifies into something ugly. Take the following figures from an external investigation commissioned by Rio Tinto, a global mining giant, into its workplace culture. Almost half of Rio’s employees report having experienced bullying in the past five years. Almost a third of its female workers have endured sexual harassment; 21 women reported an actual or attempted sexual assault. Two in five Australian Aboriginals and Torres Strait islanders employed by the company have experienced racism.

企业文化就像一团迷雾，毫无疑问是存在的，但很难说清楚。有时它会固化成一些丑陋的东西。以受全球矿业巨头力拓集团委托对其工作场所文化进行的外部调查得出的数据为例。力拓集团几乎一半的员工表示，在过去5年里曾遭受欺凌。该公司近三分之一的女性员工曾遭受性骚扰; 21名女性还报告了实际或未遂性侵犯的案件。该公司雇用的五分之二的澳大利亚土著居民和托雷斯海峡岛民曾经历过种族歧视。

The report is an admirably open attempt to face up to a toxic culture. As well as survey data, it contains testimony from interviews and focus groups. It was published on Rio’s website earlier this month, along with an apology from Jakob Stausholm, the firm’s boss. Such unusual transparency seems to be building trust: half of the firm’s employees said they were extremely or very confident that Rio would make meaningful progress in stamping out sexual harassment and racism.

该报告是一种令人钦佩的坦率尝试，试图直面“有毒”文化。除了调查数据，它还包含访谈和焦点小组的证词。本月早些时候，这篇文章发表在力拓集团的网站上，该公司老板Jakob Stausholm也发表了道歉。这种不同寻常的透明度似乎是在建立信任: 半数公司员工表示，他们非常或非常有信心力拓将在消除性骚扰和种族主义方面取得有意义的进展。

The report is a product of specific circumstances. Rio’s reputation was badly tarnished in 2020, when it destroyed Juukan Gorge, a mining site in Western Australia whose ancient rock shelters were sacred to indigenous people. That cost Mr Stausholm’s predecessor his job, kick-starting efforts to change the way the firm was run.

这份报告则是具体形势的产物。力拓集团的声誉在2020年受到严重损害，当时它摧毁了西澳大利亚州的 Juukan Gorge，那里古老的岩石庇护所对土著居民来说是神圣的。这使得Stausholm的“前任”(当时的CEO)失去了工作，并开始努力改变公司的运营方式。

Rio’s culture is traceable, at least in part, to the idiosyncrasies of the mining industry. Its workforce is 80% male, and the worst behaviour occurred on remote sites where employees fly in or drive in for stays of several days, or live full-time in company housing. Machismo and isolation make for poor bedfellows.

力拓集团的文化至少在一定程度上可以追溯到采矿业的“特点”。该公司80%的员工是男性，最糟糕的行为发生在偏远的地方，员工乘飞机或开车来此逗留几天，或者全职住在公司的房子里。大男子主义和孤立只会让你难以入眠。

But it would be a mistake to regard Rio’s soul-searching as a curio from the world of alpha males and excavators. For both its findings and the fact of its existence hold wider lessons.

但如果把力拓的自我反省看作是来自优等男性和挖掘者世界的奇闻异事，那就错了。因为它的发现和它存在的事实都有更广泛的教训。

First, they show how a corporate culture can rot. The worst abuses may have been more prevalent in the firm’s remote reaches but they were present at its heart, too. The highest rate of sexual harassment was found in the firm’s iron-ore division, but next came Rio’s strategy, sustainability and development group. Widespread suspicion of the company’s internal reporting mechanisms and a fear of speaking out are evident. “The minute you raise an issue about a senior leader, you’re done,” said one employee. “I don’t want to rock the boat so hard that I fall out of it,” echoed another. Interviewees accused Rio of rewarding bullies, and of pushing high performers up the corporate ladder irrespective of how they behaved.

首先，它们表明了企业文化是如何腐败的。最严重的滥用可能在公司的偏远地区更为普遍，但它们也存在于公司的核心。性骚扰率最高的部门是该公司的铁矿石部门，其次是力拓的战略、可持续发展和发展部。人们对该公司内部报告机制存在普遍怀疑以及对公开的恐惧是显而易见的。一名员工表示:“一旦你对一位高级领导提出质疑，你就完蛋了。”另一个附和道:“我可不想把事情搞糟还把我给搭进去了。” 受访者指责力拓公司对恃强凌弱者的奖励，以及它将绩效出色的员工推上公司的晋升阶梯，而不管他们的行为表现如何。

Among other things, the firm says it will set up a specialist unit designed to respond to complaints of harmful behaviour, and to provide support to people who blow the whistle. Whatever the right answer, the report raises questions that executives in all organisations confront: what to do with talented jerks, and how to make sure people voice concerns if something is going badly wrong.

除此之外，该公司还表示将成立一个专门部门，专门负责回应有关有害行为的投诉，并为检举者提供支持。无论正确答案是什么，该报告都提出了所有组织的高管都面临的问题: 如何对待那些有才能的“混蛋”，以及如果在出现严重问题时，如何确保人们表达出担忧。

Second, they may be a harbinger of wider demand for data on corporate culture. For all that managers bang on about people being an organisation’s greatest asset, precious little information is available to outsiders on how staff are treated and encouraged to behave. This may be because of the mist problem: it is hard to measure culture. It may be because investors haven’t much cared.

其次，它们可能预示着对企业文化数据的更广泛需求。尽管经理们大谈特谈员工是一个组织最大的资产，但局外人几乎无法获得如何处理以及鼓舞员工行为的宝贵信息。这可能是因为雾的问题:很难去衡量文化。这可能是因为投资者并不太在意。

Perhaps that is changing. Labour shortages have focused attention on how well firms retain workers. Research from Donald Sull at the Massachusetts Institute of Technology and his co-authors finds that a toxic culture is ten times more important than pay in predicting industry-adjusted staff turnover.

也许这种情况正在改变。劳动力短缺使人们关注公司如何留住工人。麻省理工学院的Donald Sull及其共同作者的研究发现，在预测行业调整后的员工流失率方面，“有毒文化”的重要性要比薪酬高出10倍。

Movements like #MeToo and Black Lives Matter have pushed sexism and racism up the corporate agenda. Allegations of sexual misconduct have battered the reputations of Axel Springer, a media giant, and Activision Blizzard, a video-game publisher just acquired by Microsoft. Late last year Microsoft’s investors adopted a shareholder proposal requiring the software titan to report on its own sexual-harassment policies. Regulators are making noises about more disclosure on human capital; Gary Gensler, chairman of America’s Securities and Exchange Commission, wants proposals in this area.

#MeToo和Black Lives Matter等运动将性别歧视和种族主义推上了公司议程。不当性行为的指控使媒体巨头Axel Springer和刚刚被微软收购的视频游戏发行商动视暴雪名誉扫地。去年年底，微软的投资者采纳了一项股东提案，要求这家软件巨头报告自己的性骚扰政策。监管机构正吵吵嚷嚷地要求披露更多的人力资本; 美国证券交易委员会主席Gary Gensler希望在这一领域提出建议。

Rio Tinto’s problems are extreme. But they are not unique. And in opening up about its corporate culture, it is, in one way at least, ahead of its time.

力拓的问题是极端的。但它们并不是唯一的。在“公开”企业文化方面，它至少在某种程度上走在了时代的前面。

Disney, Netflix, Apple: is anyone winning the streaming wars?

迪士尼，网飞，苹果: 谁是流媒体之战的赢家?

To the victors, the scraps

![image-20220213102302720](/Users/chenyujie/Library/Application Support/typora-user-images/image-20220213102302720.png)

Investors are terrified that the prize may not be worth it

投资者担心这样的回报可能不值得

Feb 12th 2022

A TEENAGED GIRL who periodically transforms into a giant panda is the improbable star of “Turning Red”, a coming-of-age movie from Disney due out next month. The world’s biggest media company, which will celebrate its 100th birthday next year, is no adolescent. But Disney is going through some awkward changes of its own as it reorganises its business—worth $260bn—around the barely two-year-old venture of video-streaming.

一名十几岁的女孩会不时地变成一只大熊猫，她在迪士尼的成长电影《青春变形记》中扮演了一位不可思议的明星。这部电影将于下个月上映。这个世界上最大的媒体公司将在明年庆祝其100岁生日，它已不再是青少年。但迪士尼自身也在经历一些尴尬的变化，因为它重组了仅成立两年的视频流媒体业务，该业务价值2600亿美元。

So far the experiment has been a success. Its streaming operation, Disney+, initially aimed for at least 60m subscribers in its first five years, ending in 2024. It got there in less than 12 months, and now hopes for as many as 260m subscribers by that date. Bob Chapek, who took over as boss just before the pandemic, is convinced that Disney’s future lies in streaming directly to consumers, his “north star”. On February 9th the company reported that Disney+ had added a healthy 11.8m subscribers in the latest quarter, shoring up its position as one of the most likely survivors of the ruthless contest that has become known as the streaming wars.

到目前为止，这个尝试是成功的。其流媒体业务Disney+最初的目标是在头五年(截至2024年)至少吸引6000万订阅用户。它在不到12个月的时间里就实现了这一目标，现在它希望到那时用户数量能达到2.6亿。Bob Chapek在新冠大流行之前接任迪士尼老板，他确信，迪士尼的未来在于直接面向消费者的流媒体，这是他的“北极星”。2月9日，该公司报告称，迪士尼+在最近一个季度新增了1180万名健康用户，这巩固了其在这场被称为流媒体战争的残酷竞争中最有可能存活下来的地位。

But doubts are surfacing across the industry about how much of a prize awaits the victors. Every year Disney and its rivals promise to spend more on content. And yet even as costs rise, the growth in subscribers is showing signs of slowing. A realisation is setting in that old media companies are pivoting from a highly profitable cable-television business to a distinctly less rewarding alternative.

但整个行业都在质疑，等待赢家的“回报”到底有多少。每年，迪士尼及其竞争对手都承诺在内容上投入更多资金。然而，即使成本上升，用户的增长也显示出放缓的迹象。人们逐渐意识到，传统媒体公司正从利润丰厚的有线电视业务转向回报明显较低的替代业务。

Markets took fright last month when Netflix, the leading streamer, forecast that in the first quarter of 2022 it would add just 2.5m new members. That would be the weakest first quarter since 2010, when most Netflix subscribers still got DVDs by mail. Its share price fell by more than a quarter on the news. Disney shares rallied this week following its earnings report, which soundly beat expectations. Yet in the previous quarter Disney+ had added only 2.1m members, the least in its short existence. With some exceptions, streamers’ breakneck growth seems to be slowing.

上个月，领先的流媒体公司网飞预测，到2022年第一季度，该公司将仅增加250万名新会员，这令市场感到恐慌。这将是自2010年以来最疲软的第一季度，当时大多数网飞用户仍然通过邮寄方式购买DVD。消息传出后，其股价下跌了四分之一以上。本周，迪士尼的收益报告大大超出预期，其股价随之上涨。然而，上个季度，Disney+仅增加了210万会员，这是该公司成立不久以来的最低增幅。除了一些例外，流媒体平台的飞速增长似乎正在放缓。

The firms blame temporary headwinds: a covid hangover, content delays and, in the case of Apple TV+, the phasing out of free trials. But some analysts are concluding that the ceiling for subscriptions is lower than they had thought. Morgan Stanley reckons Netflix will end 2024 with 260m global members, down from the investment bank’s earlier estimate of 300m. And though streamers see the potential to raise prices in rich-world markets, that will be harder in the faster-growing poor ones. In India, Netflix recently cut the price of its basic plan from $6.60 to $2.60 a month. Morgan Stanley now expects Netflix’s total revenue to grow by about 10% a year in the medium term, not the 15% or more it had previously predicted.

这些公司将其归咎于暂时的不利因素: 新冠肺炎的遗存、内容延迟，以及Apple TV+免费试用的逐步取消。但一些分析师得出的结论是，订阅用户数量的上限低于他们此前的预期。摩根士丹利估计，到2024年年底，网飞的全球会员数将达到2.6亿人，低于此前投行估计的3亿人。尽管流媒体平台看到了在发达国家市场上提高价格的潜力，但在增长更快的贫困国家，这将变得更加困难。在印度，网飞最近将其基本套餐价格从每月6.60美元降至2.60美元。摩根士丹利现在预计，网飞的总收入在中期内将以每年约10%的速度增长，而不是此前预测的15%或更多。

As revenue growth slows, costs swell. Media firms will spend more than $230bn on video content this year, nearly double the figure a decade ago, forecasts Ampere Analysis, a research firm. Netflix’s weak results came despite what it billed as its “strongest content slate ever”, including “Squid Game”, its most popular series, and “Don’t Look Up”, whose shortlisting for Best Picture on February 8th contributed to Netflix’s haul of 27 Oscar nominations, the most of any studio. Disney+ is doing far better than its parent ever dreamed—but it is costing more, too. Three years ago Disney said it would spend about $2bn on streaming content in 2024. Mr Chapek recently said the figure would surpass $9bn.

随着收入增长放缓，成本会上升。研究公司Ampere Analysis预测，今年媒体公司在视频内容上的支出将超过2300亿美元，这一数字几乎是10年前的两倍。尽管网飞标榜其拥有“有史以来最强大的内容列表”，包括其最受欢迎的系列片《鱿鱼游戏》和《千万别抬头》，但其业绩还是惨淡。《千万别抬头》于2月8日入围最佳影片的候选名单，这使网飞获得了27项奥斯卡提名，它是所有电影公司中获得提名最多的。Disney+的表现远远好于其母公司所想象的，不过它的成本也更高。三年前，迪士尼曾表示，到2024年，它将在流媒体内容上投入约20亿美元。Chapek最近表示，这一数字将超过90亿美元。

Spending is going up partly because costs of filming have risen. The final season of WarnerMedia’s “Game of Thrones”, in 2019, cost around $15m an episode, which then seemed steep. Amazon’s serialised “Lord of the Rings”, due in September, reportedly cost four times as much. Audiences have become more demanding. Most people used to cancel their cable- TV subscription only when they moved house, says Doug Shapiro, a former strategy chief at Turner Broadcasting System, a TV company. Now, he says, they are “becoming accustomed to churning on or off over the quality of content”, signing up to devour the latest hit then cancelling their membership. Apple TV+, which has the most serious retention problem, loses a tenth of its customers every month, according to Antenna, a data firm, meaning that every year it churns through the equivalent of more than 100% of its members (see chart).

支出的增加，部分原因是拍摄成本的增加。2019年，华纳传媒的《权力的游戏》最后一季每集的成本约为1500万美元，在当时看来这似乎有点儿贵的离谱。据报道，亚马逊将于9月推出的连载《指环王》的价格是其四倍。观众的要求越来越高。电视公司特纳广播系统的前战略主管Doug Shapiro说，大多数人过去只有在搬家时才取消有线电视的订阅。现在，他说，他们“正变得习惯于基于内容决定留存与否“，先是签约来入迷地看大热剧，然后再取消其会员。据数据公司Antenna的数据，苹果TV+的用户留存问题最为严重，每月流失十分之一的用户，这意味着它每年都有超过100%的会员流失(见图表)。

The combination of rising costs and slowing revenue growth “calls into question the end-state economics of these businesses”, argues MoffettNathanson, a firm of analysts. Netflix, the most successful of the bunch, expects its operating margin to shrink in 2022, for the first time in at least six years, to 19%; the firm has attributed this to higher spending on programming. MoffettNathanson adds that these figures flatter the company’s performance. Like other streamers, Netflix amortises the cost of content over several years, when in reality most of its shows are binged in a matter of weeks. (The firm insists its amortisation schedule is based on viewing patterns.)

分析师MoffettNathanson认为，成本上升和收入增长放缓的结合“对这些企业的最终经济状况提出了质疑”。其中最成功的网飞公司预计，到2022年，其营业利润率将萎缩至19%，这是至少六年来的首次; 该公司将此归因于更高的编程支出。MoffettNathanson补充说，这些数字夸大了该公司的业绩。和其他流媒体公司一样，Netflix将内容的成本分摊到几年之后，而实际上，它的大多数节目在几周内就会被疯狂观看。(该公司坚称其摊销计划是基于收视模式。)

Streaming’s pinched economics are especially galling for old media companies such as Disney, which are used to the far more profitable cable- TV business. Last year Disney reported an operating margin of 30% for its linear TV networks, a typical figure for the industry. The average American cable bill is nearly $100 a month—and viewers are usually subjected to advertising to boot. Media firms are accelerating the decline of this profitable business by shifting their best content from cable to their streaming services. They are also forgoing box-office revenue by sending movies straight to streaming (though covid-related cinema closures have often forced their hand). Animators at Disney’s Pixar studio are said to be miffed that “Turning Red” is not getting an outing at the cinema in most countries.

流媒体的紧缩经济尤其让迪士尼这样的老媒体公司恼火，因为它们已经习惯了利润丰厚得多的有线电视业务。去年，迪士尼报告其线性电视网络的营业利润率为30%，这是该行业的典型数字。美国有线电视的平均费用为每月100美元，而且观众通常还要接受广告的影响。媒体公司正将他们最好的内容从有线电视转移到流媒体服务，从而加速了这一盈利业务的衰退。他们还通过将电影直接发送到流媒体(尽管是与covid有关的电影院关闭往往迫使他们这么做)，从而放弃了票房收入。据说，迪士尼旗下皮克斯动画工作室的动画师们对《青春变形记》在大多数国家的电影院没有上映感到恼火。

There is little choice but to stick with the strategy. Cable is not coming back; streaming is expected to account for half of TV viewing in America by 2024. The focus is turning to how to make the new business more profitable. Streamers increasingly drip-feed new episodes rather than dropping entire series. Bundling is becoming more common: Disney sells Disney+ along with ESPN+, its sports streamer, and Hulu, a general entertainment service that it jointly owns with Comcast, a cable giant. Apple and Amazon both package TV with other services. WarnerMedia and Discovery plan to merge; regulators have waved the deal through, the companies said on February 9th. There may be more to come. “If Netflix is decelerating more rapidly than expected, the great streaming rebundling may need to begin sooner rather than later,” writes Benjamin Swinburne of Morgan Stanley.

除了坚持这一策略外，别无选择。有线电视不再流行;到2024年，流媒体预计将占美国电视观看量的一半。现在的重点是如何使新业务更有利可图。越来越多的流媒体平台一点点注入新剧集，而不是放弃整部剧集。捆绑销售正变得越来越普遍:迪士尼在出售Disney+的同时，还出售其体育转播商ESPN+，以及与有线电视巨头康卡斯特共同拥有的综合娱乐服务Hulu。苹果和亚马逊都将电视与其他服务打包。华纳传媒和Discovery计划合并; 2月9日，两家公司表示，监管机构已经批准了这笔交易。未来可能还会有更多。摩根士丹利的Benjamin Swinburne写道:“如果网飞减速速度比预期更快，那么大的流媒体重新捆绑业务可能需要尽早启动。”

The hope at the big media firms is that the streaming wars will eventually claim some casualties, leaving the survivors free to raise prices and dial down spending on content. Peacock, Comcast’s streamer, is trailing. Viacom CBS, which owns Paramount+, is the subject of endless takeover rumours. But even their exit would leave some determined rivals. Warner-Discovery is betting its future on streaming. Apple and Amazon are getting better at making hits, and have enough money to run at a loss for as long as they like. Disney and Netflix aren’t going anywhere. It looks like being a long war, short on spoils. ■

Some lessons on inventing the future in Britain

一些关于在英国创造未来的经验

Radical research
How to make the country’s new innovation agency work

如何让这个国家新的创新机构运作起来

![image-20220212101631034](/Users/chenyujie/Library/Application Support/typora-user-images/image-20220212101631034.png)

Feb 12th 2022

文章音频

WHEN THE Soviet Union launched Sputnik, the first ever satellite, in 1957, Americans were blindsided. They could scarcely believe that they had been beaten into space. “Now, somehow, in some new way, the sky seemed almost alien,” said Lyndon Johnson, then the majority leader of the Senate, describing what he called “the profound shock of realising that it might be possible for another nation to achieve technological superiority over this great country of ours”.

1957年，当苏联发射了世界上第一颗人造卫星“伴侣号”时，美国人措手不及。他们简直不敢相信自己在太空领域被“打败”。当时的参议院多数党领袖Lyndon Johnson说:“现在不知怎么地，以某种新的方式，天空看起来几乎是外国的。” 他描述了他所说的“意识到另一个国家有可能在技术上超越我们这个伟大的国家，这让他深感震惊。”

America’s leaders did not want to be beaten again. In 1958 President Dwight Eisenhower approved the creation of a new institution—the Advanced Research Projects Agency (ARPA). Its task would be to scan the technological horizon and “invent the future”. Six decades later ARPA’s modern incarnation, DARPA (the D stands for defence) has proved itself so useful—with decisive roles in creating everything from the internet to mRNA covid-19 vaccines—that many medium-sized countries want their own versions.

美国领导人不想再被打败。1958年，总统Dwight Eisenhower批准成立一个新的机构，即美国国防部高级研究计划署(ARPA)。它的任务将是仔细观察技术领域并“创造未来”。60年后，ARPA的当代化身DARPA (d是国防的缩写)证明了自己是如此有用，其在创造从互联网到新冠病毒mRNA疫苗的一切方面都发挥了决定性作用，以至于许多中等规模的国家都想拥有自己版本的“DARPA ”。

One of those countries is Britain, which this month announced that its new body—the Advanced Research and Invention Agency (ARIA)—would be led by Peter Highnam, a computer scientist poached, promisingly, from DARPA itself. ARIA’s stated purpose is to fund high-risk, high-reward research and it will start with a budget of $1.1bn over four years. This new addition to Britain’s research-funding landscape is welcome—two years after the country left the European Union, it is still unclear whether British scientists will continue to receive any support from the EU’s $108bn Horizon Europe programme.

其中一个国家就是英国，这个月英国宣布其新机构高级研究与发明署(ARIA)将由Peter Highnam领导。Highnam是一名有望从DARPA挖来的计算机科学家。ARIA宣称的目标是为高风险、高回报的研究提供资金，它将在未来4年启动11亿美元的预算。这一新加入到英国的研究资助版图是受欢迎的，在英国离开欧盟两年后，目前尚不清楚英国科学家是否会继续从欧盟1080亿美元的“欧洲地平线”项目中获得任何支持。

Yet Mr Highnam will face challenges in bringing the DARPA model to a country that has never seen anything like it. Copying his previous employer wholesale is unlikely to succeed—DARPA’s scope and scale (it currently has a budget of $3.5bn per year) far outweigh anything possible in Britain. Instead of trying to replicate DARPA, he should focus on bringing two elements of its model across the Atlantic.

然而，要把DARPA的模式带到一个从未见过类似模式的国家，Highnam将面临挑战。大规模仿照他以前的雇主模式是不太可能成功的，DARPA的范围和规模(目前每年的预算为35亿美元)远远超过英国的一切可能。他不应该试图复制DARPA，而应该专注于将其模式的两个元素带到大西洋彼岸。

The first is independence. Government interference has hobbled other experimental research bodies, such as Germany’s version of DARPA. British ministers have promised the new agency will be free from political interference and the bureaucracy associated with the country’s usual research agencies. But the civil service, science establishment and press may not react kindly to a hands-off agency that is given big chunks of money and resists scrutiny. Mr Highnam should go on a charm offensive to explain what ARIA is. That includes the tough task of preparing Britain for failures. Lots of them. DARPA has succeeded over the decades in part because many of its gambles have not paid off. That is a sign that it is investigating truly radical ideas, rather than confining itself to the sorts of safe scientific bets best left to industry or more conventional research institutions.

首先是独立性。政府的干预阻碍了其他实验研究机构的发展，比如德国版的DARPA。英国的大臣们已经承诺，这个新机构将不受政治干预，也不受该国通常的研究机构所存在的官僚作风的影响。但是，行政部门、科学机构和媒体可能不会对一个不插手的机构做出友好的反应，因为它得到了大笔资金，并拒绝接受审查。Highnam应该展开魅力攻势，解释ARIA是什么。这包括让英国为失败做好准备的艰巨任务。很多。DARPA在过去几十年里取得了成功，部分原因是它的许多冒险都没有得到回报。这是一个迹象，表明它正在研究真正根本的想法，而不是把自己限制在那些最好留给工业界或更传统的研究机构的安全的科学选择上。

The second element is to have a centre of gravity. DARPA’s early successes came from its relationship with America’s Department of Defence, which was trying to win a decades-long cold war. It gave a strategic direction to research and acted as a deep-pocketed customer. As a middling power, Britain does not have a defence budget that remotely matches that of Uncle Sam. Instead ARIA should focus on another area, where the country has critical mass: life sciences.

第二个要素是要有一个重心。DARPA的早期成功来自于它与美国国防部的关系，后者正试图赢得一场长达数十年的冷战。它为研究提供了一个战略方向，并扮演了一个财力雄厚的客户。作为一个中等大国，英国的国防预算远不及美国。相反，ARIA应该把重点放在另一个国家拥有临界质量的领域:生命科学。

Britain is world-class in this domain—as demonstrated in both its academic citations and its scientific response to covid-19. British life-sciences firms raised $3.4bn of venture capital in 2021; more than anywhere else in Europe but far less than the American biotech hubs, Massachusetts ($11.5bn) and San Francisco ($4.9bn). There is ample room, therefore, for stimulation and investment that would direct existing British academic and industrial strengths into tackling long-term health concerns. And with Britain’s National Health Service, ARIA has a giant customer with a burning need for technological breakthroughs and the resources to buy them. By identifying the challenges that people and health systems are likely to face in the next few decades, ARIA could show that medium-sized countries can be at science’s cutting edge. ■

英国在这一领域是世界一流的，这体现在其学术引用和对covid-19的科学应对上。英国生命科学公司在2021年筹集了34亿美元的风险投资;比欧洲任何地方都要多，但远低于美国生物技术中心，马萨诸塞州（一百五十五亿美元）和旧金山（49亿美元）。因此，有足够的刺激和投资空间，可以引导英国现有的学术和工业力量解决长期的健康问题。有着英国国家医疗服务体系，ARIA就拥有了一个巨大的客户，这个客户迫切需要技术突破和购买技术突破所需的资源。通过确定人类和卫生体系在未来几十年可能面临的挑战，ARIA可以表明中型国家可以处于科学的前沿。■

What would happen if financial markets crashed?

如果金融市场崩溃会发生什么?

The next crisis

下一次危机

![image-20220211110359154](/Users/chenyujie/Library/Application Support/typora-user-images/image-20220211110359154.png)

Look to history for a guide, but know that next time will be different

可以参考历史，但要知道下一次(危机)将会有所不同

Feb 12th 2022

“FOR HISTORIANS each event is unique,” wrote Charles Kindleberger in his study of financial crises. But whereas “history is particular; economics is general”—it involves searching for patterns which indicate if a cycle is turning. Today America’s financial system looks nothing like it did before the crashes of 2001 and 2008, yet lately there have been some familiar signs of froth and fear on Wall Street: wild trading days on no real news, sudden price swings and a queasy feeling among many investors that they have overdosed on techno-optimism. Having soared in 2021, shares on Wall Street had their worst January since 2009, falling by 5.3%. The prices of assets favoured by retail investors, like tech stocks, cryptocurrencies and shares in electric-car makers, have plunged. The once-giddy mood on r/wallstreetbets, a forum for digital day-traders, is now mournful.

Charles Kindleberger在其金融危机研究中写道:“ 对于历史学家来说，每一个事件都是独一无二的。” 但是 “历史是特殊的; 经济学是一般的”，它涉及到寻找“表明周期是否正在转变的”模式。今天美国金融体系看起来一点也不像之前的2001年和2008年的崩溃, 但最近有一些熟悉的华尔街泡沫和恐惧的迹象: 没有真实新闻的疯狂交易日、价格的突然波动以及众多投资者对技术乐观主义过度的不安情绪。在2021年飙升之后，华尔街股市今年1月出现了自2009年以来的最差表现，跌幅为5.3%。散户投资者青睐的科技股、加密货币和电动汽车制造商的股票等资产价格大幅下跌。数字当日交易者的论坛 r/wallstreetbets 上一度令人“陶醉”的情绪，现在变得悲哀起来。

It is tempting to think that the January sell-off was exactly what was needed, purging the stockmarket of its speculative excesses. But America’s new-look financial system is still loaded with risks. Asset prices are high: the last time shares were so pricey relative to long-run profits was before the slumps of 1929 and 2001, and the extra return for owning risky bonds is near its lowest level for a quarter of a century. Many portfolios have loaded up on “long-duration” assets that yield profits only in the distant future. And central banks are raising interest rates to tame inflation. America’s Federal Reserve is expected to make five quarter-point increases this year. German two-year Bund yields leapt 0.33 points last week, their biggest jump since 2008.

人们很容易认为，1月份的抛售正是需要的，它“净化”了股市的过度投机行为。但是美国新面貌的金融体系仍然充满了风险。资产价格很高:上一次股票相对于长期利润如此昂贵是在1929年和2001年的经济衰退之前，而持有高风险债券的额外回报接近25年来的最低水平。许多投资组合都持有“长期”资产，这些资产只能在遥远的未来产生利润。中央银行正在提高利率以抑制通货膨胀。美联储预计今年将加息五次，每次25个基点。德国两年期国债收益率上周跃升0.33个百分点，这是自2008年以来的最大涨幅。

The mix of sky-high valuations and rising interest rates could easily result in large losses, as the rate used to discount future income rises. If big losses do materialise, the important question, for investors, for central bankers and for the world economy, is whether the financial system will safely absorb them or amplify them. The answer is not obvious, for that system has been transformed over the past 15 years by the twin forces of regulation and technological innovation.

极高的估值加上不断上升的利率，很容易导致巨额亏损，因为用于贴现未来收入的利率上升。如果真的出现巨额亏损，对投资者、央行行长和全球经济来说，重要的问题是，金融体系是会安稳地承受亏损，还是会放大亏损。答案并不明显，因为在过去15年里，在监管和技术创新的双重力量下，这一体系已经发生了改变。

New capital rules have pushed a lot of risk-taking out of banks. Digitisation has given computers more decision-making power, created new platforms for owning assets and cut the cost of trading almost to zero. The result is a high-frequency, market-based system with a new cast of players. Share-trading is no longer dominated by pension funds but by automated exchange-traded funds (ETFs) and swarms of retail investors using slick new apps. Borrowers can tap debt funds as well as banks. Credit flows across borders thanks to asset managers such as BlackRock, which buy foreign bonds, not just global lenders such as Citigroup. Markets operate at breakneck speed: the volume of shares traded in America is 3.8 times what it was a decade ago.

新的资本规则已经将银行的许多冒险行为排除在外。数字化赋予了计算机更多的决策权力，创造了拥有资产的新平台，并将交易成本降至几乎为零。结果就是有了一个高频且以市场为基础的系统，该系统拥有一批新玩家。股票交易不再由养老基金主导，而是由自动交易型开放式指数基金和成群结队的使用精明的新应用程序的散户投资者主导。借款人可以利用债务基金，也可以利用银行。由于BlackRock等购买外国债券的资产管理公司，而不仅仅是花旗集团等全球银行，信贷可以跨境流动。市场运转速度极快:美国的股票交易量是十年前的3.8倍。

Many of these changes have been for the better. They have made it cheaper and easier for all types of investors to deal in a broader range of assets. The crash of 2008-09 showed how dangerous it was to have banks that took deposits from the public exposed to catastrophic losses, which forced governments to bail them out. Today banks are less central to the financial system, better capitalised and hold fewer highly risky assets. More risk-taking is done by funds backed by shareholders or long-term savers who, on paper, are better equipped to absorb losses.

这些变化中有许多是向好的方向发展的。它们使得各类投资者都能以更低的成本和更容易的方式交易范围更广的资产。2008-2009年的金融危机表明，从公众那里吸收存款的银行面临灾难性损失是多么危险，这迫使政府为它们纾困。如今，银行在金融体系中的地位已不那么重要，资本状况更好，风险资产也更少。由股东或长期储蓄者支持的基金将承担更多的风险，从理论上讲，这些人能够更好地承受损失。

Yet the reinvention of finance has not eliminated hubris. Two dangers stand out. First, some leverage is hidden in shadow banks and investment funds. For example the total borrowings and deposit-like liabilities of hedge funds, property trusts and money market funds have risen to 43% of GDP, from 32% a decade ago. Firms can rack up huge debts without anyone noticing. Archegos, an obscure family investment office, defaulted last year, imposing $10bn of losses on its lenders. If asset prices fall, other blow-ups could follow, accelerating the correction.

不过，金融再造并没有消除狂妄自大。两个危险显而易见。首先，一些杠杆隐藏在影子银行和投资基金中。例如，对冲基金、房地产信托和货币市场基金的借款和存款类债务总额已从十年前GDP的32%上升到GDP的43%。公司可以在没有人注意到的情况下积累巨额债务。鲜为人知的家族投资办公室Archegos去年违约，给其贷款人造成了100亿美元的损失。如果资产价格下跌，其他”崩盘”可能会接踵而至，从而加速调整。

The second danger is that, although the new system is more decentralised, it still relies on transactions being channelled through a few nodes that could be overwhelmed by volatility. ETFs, with $10trn of assets, rely on a few small market-making firms to ensure that the price of funds accurately tracks the under lying assets they own. Trillions of dollars of derivatives contracts are routed through five American clearing houses. Many transactions are executed by a new breed of middle men, such as Citadel Securities. The Treasury market now depends on automated high-frequency trading firms to function.

第二个危险是，尽管新系统更加去中心化，但它仍然依赖于通过几个节点进行的交易，而这些节点可能会被不稳定压垮。拥有10万亿美元资产的ETF依赖于一些小的做市商来确保基金的价格准确地跟踪其持有的隐藏资产。数万亿美元的衍生品合约通过美国的五家清算所进行交易。许多交易是由一种新型的中间商执行的，比如Citadel Securities。美国国债市场现在依靠自动高频交易公司运作。

All these firms or institutions hold safety buffers and most can demand further collateral or “margin” to protect themselves from their users’ losses. Yet recent experience suggests reasons for concern. In January 2021 frenzied trading in a single stock, GameStop, led to chaos, prompting large margin calls from the settlement system, which a new generation of app-based brokerage firms, including Robinhood, struggled to pay. The Treasury and money markets, meanwhile, seized up in 2014, 2019 and 2020. The market-based financial system is hyper active most of the time; in times of stress whole areas of trading activity can dry up. That can fuel panic.

所有这些公司或机构都持有安全缓冲，其中大多数可以要求更多的抵押品或“保证金”，以保护自己免受用户损失。然而，最近的经验表明有理由感到担忧。2021年1月，GameStop股票的疯狂交易导致了混乱，这就促使结算系统发出大量保证金通知，包括Robinhood在内的新一代基于APP的经纪公司都难以支付。与此同时，财政部和货币市场在2014年、2019年和2020年陷入停滞。以市场为基础的金融体系在大多数时候都非常活跃; 在压力时期，整个领域的交易活动可能会“枯竭”。这可能加剧恐慌。

Ordinary citizens may not think it matters much if a bunch of day-traders and fund managers get burned. But such a fire could damage the rest of the economy. Fully 53% of American households own shares (up from 37% in 1992), and there are over 100m online brokerage accounts. If credit markets gum up, households and firms will struggle to borrow. That is why, at the start of the pandemic, the Fed acted as a “market-maker of last resort”, promising up to $3trn to support a range of debt markets and to backstop dealers and some mutual funds.

如果一群当日交易者和基金经理承受损失，普通民众可能认为这没什么大不了。但这样一场“大火”可能会损害经济的其他部分。整整53%的美国家庭拥有股票(1992年为37%)，并有超过1亿个在线经纪账户。如果信贷市场不能运转，那么家庭和公司将难以借贷。正因为如此，在疫情爆发之初，美联储扮演了“最后做市商”的角色，承诺拿出高达3万亿美元支持一系列债券市场，并为交易商和一些共同基金提供支持。

Fine margins

Was that bail-out a one-off caused by an exceptional event, or a sign of things to come? Ever since 2008-09 central banks and regulators have had two unspoken goals: to normalise interest rates and to stop using public money to underwrite private risk-taking. It seems that those goals are in tension: the Fed must raise rates, yet that could trigger instability. The financial system is in better shape than in 2008 when the reckless gamblers at Bear Stearns and Lehman Brothers brought the world to a standstill. Make no mistake, though: it faces a stern test. ■

那次纾困是一次偶然事件引起的一次性事件，还是预示着未来的事情? 自2008-2009年以来，中央银行和监管机构一直有两个不言而喻的目标:使利率正常化和停止使用公共资金为私人冒险提供担保。看起来这些目标处于紧张状态: 美联储必须提高利率，但这可能会引发不稳定。与2008年Bear Stearns和Lehman Brothers不计后果的赌徒让世界陷入停顿时相比，现在的金融体系状况要好一些。但毫无疑问，它将面临严峻的考验。■

2018年，同为佛罗里达州人的、正好入主白宫的特朗普改变了DeSantis先生的职业生涯，他出人意料地支持DeSantis先生，帮助这位当时不起眼的众议院议员赢得了共和党州长初选。DeSantis先生对戴口罩命令和推动学校重新开放等抗击covid-19的限制措施的反抗，提升了他在全国的声望，他还经常攻击美国总统乔•拜登及其“生物医学安全国家”。他形容佛罗里达州是“美国最自由的州”。他在covid-19上的记录既为他赢得了朋友，也为他赢得了敌人: 因为他讨厌其他地方常见的限制措施，佛罗里达州的6.4万例死亡人数比美国整体的人均死亡率高，不过也没有一些人预测的那么高。

Mr DeSantis is using Florida as a peninsular podium to advertise his policies. In his proposed $100bn budget, he is pushing for a special police force to oversee state elections, which he calls an “election integrity unit”, and wants to make it easier to penalise companies that “facilitate illegal migration” to Florida. He envisages bonuses for police officers who move to Florida, and wants to create a state militia of volunteers that could work with the National Guard in emergencies.

DeSantis先生正利用佛罗里达作为该半岛的宣传平台宣传他的政策。在他提出的1000亿美元预算中，他正在推动组建一支特别警察部队来监督州选举，他将其称为“选举诚信小组”，并希望让惩罚那些“为非法移民提供便利”的公司变得更容易。他设想给搬到佛罗里达的警察发放奖金，并希望建立一个由志愿者组成的州民兵组织，以便在紧急情况下与国民警卫队合作。

Mr DeSantis proposes pay rises for teachers and police, as well as $1bn to offer Floridians a holiday from the state’s petrol tax. Florida will use at least $3.8bn in federal stimulus for programmes like these in the coming fiscal year, reckons the Florida Policy Institute, a think-tank.

联邦政府和拜登先生可能是DeSantis先生最喜欢的敌人，但他们也为他的部分议程提供资金。DeSantis先生提议给教师和警察加薪，并向佛罗里达人提供10亿美元免税假期。智囊团佛罗里达政策研究所估计，下个财政年度，佛罗里达州将至少用38亿美元的联邦刺激资金来实施类似的计划。

What explains Florida’s lurch to the right? Recently the number of voters registered as Republican exceeded the number of registered Democrats for the first time in modern history. 如何解释佛罗里达突然向右倾斜? 最近登记为共和党的选民人数在现代史上首次超过了登记为民主党的选民人数。现在，35.9%的注册选民认为自己是共和党人。民主党和无党派人士则分别为35.6%和26.8%。共和党人认为势头对他们有利。Sprowls先生表示，搬到佛罗里达州的人们“意识到，他们选择来到这里是有原因的”，也就是共和党的政策。

随着共和党在州众议院接近绝对多数，民主党无力阻止他们。众议院少数党共同领袖Evan Jenne表示，民主党人“有一种明显的黑色幽默”。他将他们目前在州政府工作的经历比作“不得不躺在铁轨上，尽管我们知道(火车)会直接从我们身上碾过”。

这次立法会议将考验DeSantis的权力范围。他干预了立法机关的选区重划过程，提交了他自己提议的地图，这将在未来的选举中加强共和党的地位，这是人们所知的第一次有佛罗里达州州长这么做。反对者称该提议可能违反州法律。另一个考验将是立法机构是否建立他提议的选举警察部队，该部队受到了不温不火的欢迎，而且“比他提议的其他东西更具透明度的政治色彩”，中佛罗里达大学教授Aubrey Jewett说。

目前看来，这位州长似乎正走向连任。佛罗里达州正在蓬勃发展: 尽管有新冠疫情，大部分企业仍在营业，人们也在搬到该州。这使得民主党对手很难做出有说服力的宣传。前共和党州长Charlie Crist现在以民主党人的身份参加竞选，该州农业专员Nikki Fried也是如此(尽管她将DeSantis比作希特勒，认为他的行为像个独裁者，这让她的竞选前景一片混乱)。

In November, Sunshine State voters will be able to express at the polls whether their governor’s pitch for national attention irks them. France’s president has a 79% chance of keeping his job

这位法国总统有79%的机会保住他的职位

Feb 5th 2022

文章音频

MOROSITÉ, MALHEUR, dégagisme: these are gloomy times in France. Covid-19 may be on the way out, but it still hampers daily life and the smooth running of the economy. Inflation, driven by disease-induced supply-chain kinks and spiking natural-gas prices, threatens household budgets. Russia and Ukraine are on the brink of war. Small wonder that only 17% of the French think 2022 will be better than 2021, and that some 45% intend in April to cast their first-round vote for a presidential candidate from the political extremes. Yet despite it all, President Emmanuel Macron has a four-in-five chance of winning re-election, according to the new election model that we launch this week.

萧条, 灾难, 下台主义: 这些都是法国灰暗时刻。虽然新冠肺炎疫情已接近尾声，但仍给日常生活和经济平稳运行带来不利影响。由疾病引发的供应链紊乱和天然气价格飙升导致的通货膨胀威胁着家庭预算。俄罗斯和乌克兰正处于战争的边缘。难怪只有17%的法国人认为2022年的情况会好于2021年，约45%的人打算在今年4月将他们的第一轮投票投给一位政治极端主义的总统候选人。然而，尽管如此，根据我们本周推出的新选举模型，法国总统马克龙有五分之四的机会赢得连任。

Our forecast is the latest in a series of statistical models we have built that use data about the reliability of past polling to analyse elections still under way. In America’s presidential contest in 2020, for example, we gave Joe Biden a very high chance of winning even if the polls were off by more than usual. In Germany’s elections last year we calculated a fair chance of success for the Social Democrats, who now lead the government there. And in 2017 we found that Mr Macron was extremely likely to defeat Marine Le Pen even though pundits maintained she had a good chance of prevailing. In short, our forecasting models have proved their worth.

我们的预测是我们建立的一系列统计模型中最新的一个，这些模型利用过去民意调查的可靠性数据来分析仍在进行中的选举。例如，在2020年的美国总统竞选中，我们认为乔·拜登获胜的几率非常高，即使民调结果比往常差得多。在去年的德国选举中，我们计算出了社民党获得成功的公平机会，该党目前领导着德国政府。2017年，我们发现马克龙先生极有可能击败玛丽娜·勒庞，尽管专家认为她很有可能获胜。总之，我们的预测模型已经证明了它们的价值。

On February 2nd our model, which will be updated daily, gave Emmanuel Macron a 79% chance of victory. This figure will change as the campaign unfolds. We are more bullish than international betting markets, which rate his chances at around 70%. Mr Macron’s comfortable lead in first-round polling suggests only an 8% chance of his being knocked out. And he enjoys a solid advantage over all of his second-round opponents in head-to-head polling, even against Valérie Pécresse, his closest challenger in such match-ups. Against his most likely opponent, Marine Le Pen, our model suggests an 88% chance of victory in round two. Because this is the work of statisticians, not magicians, we are happy to reveal our methods.

2月2日，我们每天更新的模型显示，马克龙获胜的几率为79%。随着竞选活动的展开，这个数字将发生改变。我们比国际博彩市场更看好他，后者认为他成功的几率约为70%。马克龙在第一轮投票中遥遥领先，这表明他被淘汰的可能性只有8%。而且，在第二轮的面对面投票中，他比他的所有对手都享有稳固的优势，即使是与在这类投票中与他最接近的对手Valérie Pécresse相比也是如此。对他最有可能的对手玛丽娜·勒庞，我们的模型显示，他在第二轮获胜的几率为88%。因为这是统计学家的工作，而不是魔术师，我们很乐意透露我们的方法。

![image-20220209100050712](/Users/chenyujie/Library/Application Support/typora-user-images/image-20220209100050712.png)

What the statistics do not explain is the politics behind the polling numbers that are the raw input for our model. In large part they reflect Mr Macron’s success at straddling the centre-right and the centre-left in France, drawing into his government former Socialists and former Republicans alike. The extremes are splintered, which also helps him. And the president has, improbably enough, benefited from covid, which his government has handled reasonably well. The pandemic has engendered a huge expansion of state spending at a time when some of his labour-market reforms might have caused more tensions. He has also used the pandemic as an excuse to shelve pension reforms that might have hurt him. Mrs Pécresse has yet to lay out her full platform, but so far she has not put forward policies that are substantially different.

统计数据没有解释作为我们模型原始输入的民意调查数字背后的政治。这在很大程度上反映了马克龙成功地跨越了法国中右和中左两派，将前社会党人和前共和党人拉拢到他的政府中。极端主义是分裂的，这也帮助了他。而且，总统从新冠肺炎中受益，这令人难以置信，他的政府对于新冠肺炎处理得相当好。在他的劳动力市场的一些改革可能会引发更多紧张局势的时候，疫情导致了国家支出的大幅扩张。他还以新冠大流行为借口，搁置可能对他不利的养老金改革。Pécresse夫人还没有提出她的完整的纲领，但到目前为止，她还没有提出有实质性不同的政策。

Even if the election seems almost a formality today, that could easily change. Mrs Pécresse could well beat the president if she can get to the second round. We currently give her only a 32% chance of getting through, but that probability will change as the campaign ramps up. Besides, a 79% chance of success is by no means a certainty—as anyone would attest who has contemplated a game of Russian roulette, where the odds are even better.

即使选举在今天看来几乎只是走过场，但这种情况很容易改变。如果Pécresse夫人能进入第二轮，她很可能击败总统。我们目前认为她只有32%的机会通过，但随着竞选活动的推进，这种可能性会改变。此外，79%的成功几率绝不是必然的，就像考虑过俄罗斯轮盘赌(成功几率会更大)的任何人可以证明的那样。

This election is worth watching closely. Mr Macron is bidding to be the leader of the European Union, which has a vacancy to fill. Olaf Scholz, Germany’s new chancellor, seems reluctant to take up Angela Merkel’s European mantle. Italy has dodged a bullet by avoiding an early vote, but still faces uncertainty. Britain is gone. France matters more than for a long time. It matters greatly who is in charge. ■

这次选举值得密切关注。马克龙正在竞选欧盟的领导人，目前欧盟有一个空缺需要填补。德国新任总理奥拉夫•朔尔茨似乎不愿接过默克尔的欧洲的“衣钵”。意大利通过避免提前投票躲过了一劫，但仍面临不确定性。英国已经一去不复返了。法国在很长一段时间内都很重要。谁掌权非常重要。■

Body language in the post-pandemic workplace

新冠大流行后工作场所中的肢体语言

Bartleby

巴托比

![image-20220208094721271](/Users/chenyujie/Library/Application Support/typora-user-images/image-20220208094721271.png)

Much of the research into non-verbal communication is now redundant

现在很多非言语交际的研究都是多余的

Feb 5th 2022

文章音频

COMMUNICATION IS AN essential part of leadership. And body language is an essential part of communication. On these slim pillars rests a mini-industry of research and advice into how executives can influence, encourage and ascend without needing to say a word. The pandemic has made much of it redundant.

沟通是领导的重要组成部分。肢体语言则是沟通中必不可少的一部分。在这些纤细的肢体上，存在着一个研究和建议的小型行业，研究高管们如何不需要说一句话就能影响、鼓励和提升。新冠大流行使得它在很大程度上是多余的。

Plenty of studies have looked into the non-verbal behaviour that marks out “emergent leaders”, people who do not have a specified role in the hierarchy but naturally assume a position of authority in groups. They are a bag of tics. They nod; they touch others but not themselves; they gesture; they furrow their brows; they hold themselves erect; their facial expressions are more animated.

大量的研究已经研究了非言语行为，这些非言语行为标志着“新兴的领导者”，这些人在等级制度中没有特定的角色，但自然地在群体中占据权威地位。他们是一群“肌肉抽搐”的人。他们点头示意; 他们触摸别人却不触摸自己; 他们用手势示意; 他们会皱起眉头; 他们挺立着; 他们的面部表情更加活跃。

Other research suggests that, to win votes in an election, candidates should deliver speeches with their feet planted apart. The second-most popular TEDtalk claims that two minutes of private, hands-on-hips “power posing” can infuse a job candidate with confidence and improve others’ perceptions of them.

其他研究表明，为了在选举中赢得选票，候选人在演讲时应该双脚分开。第二受欢迎的TED演讲声称，两分钟不受打扰的双手叉腰的“力量姿势”可以给求职者注入自信，并改善他人对他们的看法。

Gazing can foster a sense of psychological safety as well as confer authority: in a recent paper, a trio of researchers from Harvard Business School found that receiving more eye contact from a bigwig led to greater participation in group interactions. Leaders who adopt open body positions, with arms and legs uncrossed, are also more likely to encourage contributions.

凝视可以培养心理安全感，也可以赋予威信。在最近的一篇论文中，来自哈佛商学院的三位研究人员发现，从大佬那里获得更多的目光接触，可以让他们更多地参与群体互动。采用开放式身体姿势的领导者，双臂和双腿不交叉，也更有可能鼓励人们做出贡献。

There are three problems with this body of research on non-verbal communication. One is that so much of it is blindingly obvious. Nodding at someone rather than shaking your head in incredulity when they are speaking to you—this does indeed send a powerful signal. But so does punching someone in the face, and no one thinks that requires a journal publication.

关于非言语沟通的研究有三个问题。其中一个问题是，其中很多都是显而易见的。当别人和你说话的时候，向他们点头，而不是摇头表示怀疑，这确实释放了一个强有力的信号。但打别人的脸也一样，而且没人认为这需要发表期刊。

A second problem is that people look for different things from their bosses. Frowning is seen as a mark of emergent leaders but not of supportive ones; the reverse is true of smiling. (The effect of smiling with lowered eyebrows cries out for study.) A recent paper found that male recipients regarded bosses who used emojis, a form of not-quite-verbal communication, in an email as more effective, but that female recipients perceived them as less effective.

第二个问题是人们会从老板那里寻找不同的东西。皱眉被认为是新兴领导者的标志，但不是支持型领导者的标志; 微笑则相反。(低眉微笑的效果需要学习。) 最近的一篇论文发现，男性收件人认为在邮件中使用表情符号(一种非语言交流的形式)的老板更有效率，但女性收件人则认为他们效率较低。

The third problem is newer. Almost all of the research on body language dates from a time of in-person interactions. Even when the pandemic wanes and offices in the West refill, most buildings will not return to full capacity. Employees will keep working remotely for at least part of the week; Zoom will remain integral to white-collar working lives. And if there is one thing for which online interactions are not suited, it is body language.

第三个问题是比较新。几乎所有关于肢体语言的研究都起源于面对面交流的时代。即使新冠大流行消退，西部的办公室再次坐满，大多数建筑也无法恢复“座无虚席”的状态。员工每周至少有一段时间会继续远程工作;Zoom仍将是白领工作生活不可或缺的一部分。如果有一件事是在线互动不适合的，那就是肢体语言。

That is partly because bodies themselves are largely hidden from view: whatever language they are speaking, it is hard to hear them. You will know the partners, pets and home-decor choices of new colleagues before you will know how tall they are. And although faces fill the video-conferencing screen, meaningful eye contact is impossible.

这在一定程度上是因为身体本身在很大程度上是隐藏在视野之外的:无论他们(的肢体)在说什么语言，都很难“听”到他们的声音。在你知道新同事有多高之前，你就会知道他们的伴侣、宠物和家居装饰的选择。尽管视频会议的屏幕上满是人脸，但有意义的眼神交流是不可能的。

Once past a basic threshold of attentiveness—not looking down at your mobile phone, say—most people have the same glassy-eyed stare. If several faces appear on screen, these participants have no way of knowing that you are gazing specifically at them. (Anyway, admit it: the face you are looking at with most interest is your own.) If your camera is in the wrong place, you may think you are looking meaningfully at your team but you are actually just giving them a view of your nostrils. Animated expressions are hard to spot, particularly when people attending hybrid meetings in the office are Lowry-like figures seated metres away.

一旦超过了注意力的基本阈值，比如，不要低头看手机，大多数人都会有同样的呆滞的眼神。如果屏幕上出现了几张脸，这些参与者无法知道你是在盯着他们看。(不管怎样，承认吧: 你最感兴趣的是你自己的脸。)如果你的相机放在了错误的位置，你可能会认为你是在有意看着你的团队，但实际上你只是让他们看到了你的鼻孔。人们很难看到生动的表情，尤其是在办公室参加混合会议的像Lowry似的人坐在几米开外的地方时。

There are no good ways to compensate for these problems. One tactic is to go all in on expressiveness, nodding furiously and gesturing dementedly—a small tile of caged energy somewhere in the bottom left-hand corner of the screen. Another is to do a “Zoom loom”, placing yourself so close to the camera that you will give everyone nightmares.

没有好的方法来弥补这些问题。一种策略是全身心投入到表达上，疯狂地点头，疯狂地做手势，屏幕左下角某个地方有一小块被困住的能量。另一种方法是做一个“Zoom放大”，把自己放在离镜头如此近的地方，这会让每个人都做噩梦。

The simpler option is not to think too hard about body language. At a few specific moments, like job interviews and set-piece speeches, first impressions matter and a bit of self-conscious posing pays off. But posture is not leadership. If you want to give people a break from staring at a screen, turning off your camera is a good way to do it. If you want to waggle your eyebrows, up or down, let them loose. And if you need to be told that looking at someone makes them feel valued, you have bigger issues.

更简单的选择是不要过多地考虑肢体语言。在一些特定的时刻，比如工作面试和固定的演讲，第一印象很重要，一点刻意的摆姿势会有回报。但姿势并不是领导力。如果你想让人们从盯着屏幕中解脱出来，关掉相机是一个很好的方法。如果你想上下摆动眉毛，那就随意摆动。如果你需要别人告诉你，看着某人会让他们觉得自己有价值，那你就有更大的问题了。

中央银行会将利率升到多高?

![image-20220207105758668](/Users/chenyujie/Library/Application Support/typora-user-images/image-20220207105758668.png)

But the low-rate era is unlikely to come to a permanent end

但是低利率时代不太可能永久结束

Feb 5th 2022

文章链接

JEROME POWELL, the chairman of the Federal Reserve, once compared setting interest rates to celestial navigation. Today, as inflation spikes, there is a growing sense that the Fed has lost its way. It looks as if it is about to make an abrupt change of course by tightening monetary policy hard and fast. That prospect has battered stockmarkets and led many firms and homeowners to wonder if the era of low rates might be over for good.

美联储主席JEROME POWEL曾将设定利率比作天文导航。如今，随着通胀飙升，人们越来越觉得美联储迷失了方向。它似乎即将通过明确收紧货币政策来做出一个突然的转变。这一前景重创了股票市场，并导致许多公司和房主怀疑“低利率的时代是否会永远结束”。

The reality is more complex. In the short term the Fed does indeed need to get a grip. But, as we explain, in the long run the world’s ageing population will keep a cap on interest rates. That points to an unpleasant financial squeeze, rather than a return to the 1970s.

现实情况更为复杂。短期内，美联储确实需要控制局面。但是，正如我们所解释的那样，从长远来看，世界人口的老龄化将会给利率设定上限。这意味着令人不快的财政紧缩，而不是意味着会回到上世纪70年代。

Interest-rate rises are daunting because much of the world has got used to an era of almost-free money. No G7 central bank has set interest rates above 2.5% in over a decade. Back in 1990 all of them were above 5%. Cheap financing has come to seem like an indelible feature of rich economies. It has let governments run extraordinary deficits, propelled asset prices to astronomical highs and forced policymakers to reach for other tools, such as bond-buying and stimulus cheques, to prop up the economy during slowdowns.

利率上升令人生畏，因为世界上很多国家已经习惯了一个几乎宽松的货币时代。十多年来，七国集团中没有一家央行将利率设定在2.5%以上。而在1990年，七国集团所有的央行都将利率定在5%以上。廉价融资似乎已成为发达经济体不可磨灭的特征。它让政府背负巨额赤字，将资产价格推高至巨额数字，并迫使政策制定者在经济放缓期间寻求购买债券和刺激支票等其他工具来支撑经济。

That is why surging prices over the past 18 months have been such a rude surprise to the Fed and other central banks. In America consumer-price inflation has reached 7% and, far from being transitory, is feeding through into wages as the idea that bills will go up is being baked into households’ and firms’ expectations. Private-sector wages and salaries in America are up 5% in a year. In December the median American consumer expected prices to rise by 6% over 12 months. Many of these trends are being felt around the world: global inflation has now reached 6%.

这就是为什么过去18个月的价格的飙升让美联储和其他央行感到意外。在美国，消费者价格通胀已经达到了7%，而且远不是暂时的，随着账单上涨的想法被家庭和公司的预期所影响，它正在影响工资。美国私营部门的工资和薪水在一年内增长了5%。去年12月，美国消费者平均预期价格将在12个月内上涨6%。世界各地都感受到了这些趋势: 全球通货膨胀率现在已经达到了6%。

As a result, central banks are stirring. Twelve emerging-market rate-setters raised interest rates in 2021. The Bank of England did too. It did so again on February 3rd, and more rises are likely. Investors expect even the glacial European Central Bank, which has not raised rates for more than a decade, to do so twice this year. Yet all eyes are on America and Mr Powell. That is partly because they have a dominant role in the world’s financial system, but also because American inflation is high and the Fed is behind the curve. For months it has been stimulating an economy that is already red hot by buying bonds and keeping interest rates at 0-0.25%.

其结果是，各国央行都“忙碌“起来了。2021年，12个新兴市场的利率制定者提高了利率。英国央行也这么做了。2月3日，美联储再次加息，而且很可能还会继续加息。投资者预计，即使是10多年未加息的冷漠的欧洲央行今年也将加息两次。然而，所有的目光都集中在美国和Powell先生身上。这在一定程度上是因为他们在世界金融体系中占据主导地位，但也因为美国的通货膨胀率高企，而美联储已经落后于形势了。几个月来，它一直通过购买债券和将利率维持在0-0.25%的措施来刺激已经火热的经济。

The Fed says it plans to get interest rates back to about 2% by 2024, not far off most estimates of their neutral level, which in theory neither stimulates the economy nor holds it back. But as the Fed has dragged its feet, the risk has grown that it will have to go further. Higher inflation expectations make the impulse to raise prices harder to eradicate. For any nominal rate, they also reduce the real interest rate that borrowers pay, offsetting the effect of Fed tightening. The real cost of borrowing over five years is still lower now than it was in mid-2020.

美联储表示，计划在2024年之前将利率恢复到2%左右，这离他们的大多数中性水平的估计相差不远。理论上，这既不会刺激经济，也不会抑制经济增长。但因为美联储慢吞吞的“行动”，它不得不采取进一步行动的风险也在增加。更高的通胀预期使得提高物价的冲动更难根除。对于任何名义利率，它们也会降低借款人支付的实际利率，抵消美联储收紧政策的影响。目前，五年期贷款的实际成本仍低于2020年中期的水平。

The most likely prospect is therefore of a year or more of interest rates in America rising more sharply than the Fed has so far indicated. Some forecasters predict that it will raise rates by 1.75 percentage points in 2022, more than in any year since 2005.

因此，最有可能的前景是，美国利率在未来一年或更长时间内的上升幅度将超过美联储迄今为止所暗示的水平。一些预测者预言，美联储将在2022年加息1.75个百分点，这一比例超过2005年以来的任何一年。

What about the longer run? After all most homeowners and firms seek to borrow over years or decades. For an answer, you have to look beyond Mr Powell to the forces driving the economy. Monetary policy is anchored around the neutral rate of interest, the price of money needed to balance the global appetite to save with the desire to invest. This is a fundamental variable over which central bankers have little control.

从长远来看呢? 毕竟，大多数房主和公司寻求的是几年或几十年的贷款。要找到答案，你必须超越Powell先生的视野，看看推动经济的力量。货币政策是围绕中性利率而定的，中性利率是平衡全球储蓄欲望和投资欲望所需的货币价格。这是一个央行行长几乎无法控制的基本变量。

Over the past 20 years this underlying neutral rate has steadily fallen as savings and investment have got out of whack. Rising global savings, caused at first by the hoarding of reserves in Asian economies, meant that vast amounts of money chased any return, however meagre or risky. Meanwhile companies battered and bruised after the global financial crisis of 2007-09 were reluctant to invest.

在过去20年里，随着储蓄和投资的失衡，这一基础中性利率一直在稳步下降。全球储蓄的增加(最初是由亚洲经济体囤积外汇储备引起的)意味着大量资金追逐任何回报，无论回报多么微薄或风险有多大。与此同时，在2007年至2009年的全球金融危机之后遭受重创的公司都不愿投资。

The question is whether these forces have shifted. On one side of the ledger, perhaps they have a little. Business investment may be entering a new phase. Since the pandemic, it has recovered faster than it did after the financial crisis. That is partly thanks to the pandemic stimulus, which is now being withdrawn. But it also reflects optimism about technological progress that is fuelling spending on intellectual property. This now accounts for more than two-fifths of American business investment. Meanwhile the clean-energy transition is creating an investment need of perhaps 60% of GDP in the rich world. If techno-optimism lasts and the world is serious about fighting climate change, investment is likely to be stronger in the 2020s than it was in the 2010s.

问题是这些(推动经济的)“力量”是否已经转变。在账簿的一边
(投资)，也许他们有一点转变。商业投资可能进入一个新阶段。自新冠大流行以来，它比金融危机后恢复得更快。这在一定程度上要归功于新冠大流行的刺激计划，现在该计划正在撤销。但这也反映出人们对技术进步的乐观态度，这种进步正在推动知识产权支出。现在，这占美国商业投资的五分之二以上。与此同时，在发达国家清洁能源的转型创造了大约占GDP 60%的投资需求。如果对技术的乐观情绪持续下去，而且全球会认真对待应对气候变化的问题，那么本世纪20年代的投资可能会比本世纪头十年更加强劲。

However the other side of the ledger, the world’s wish to save, is unlikely to weaken. Since Ben Bernanke, one of Mr Powell’s predecessors, first noticed the “global saving glut”, the growth in the reserves of Asian economies has slowed, and some governments have chalked up more debt. But the share of the world’s inhabitants older than 50 is projected to rise from 25% to 40% by 2100, and may climb higher still, given recent falls in fertility in India and China. Experience shows that ageing leads to more saving, because rising life expectancy causes households to put more aside for their retirement and retirees tend to run down their assets slowly.

然而，账簿的另一边，即全世界的储蓄愿望，则不太可能减弱。自Powell先生的“前任”之一Ben Bernanke首次注意到“全球储蓄过剩”以来，亚洲经济体的储备增长已经放缓，一些国家的政府也增加了债务。但到2100年，全球50岁以上人口的比例预计将从25%上升到40%，考虑到印度和中国最近的生育率下降，这一比例可能还会上升。经验表明，老龄化会导致更多的储蓄，因为预期寿命的增加会导致家庭为退休而更多的去储蓄，而退休人员往往会慢慢消耗他们的资产。

Bumps and slumps

These factors lay down a map for interest rates. In the long run any upward shift is likely to be small, and, to the extent that this reflects a pick-up in investment, welcome. However, between now and then there is likely to be a sharp and potentially painful rise in rates. The world’s debts have reached 355% of GDP, making firms and households more sensitive to even small rate rises. There are few examples of central banks taming inflation without the economy suffering a recession. The last time America’s inflation fell from over 5% without a downturn was over 70 years ago. Fighting inflation could put the world in a slump. If so, the prospect that rates will one day fall back again would be only some consolation. ■

这些因素为利率奠定了基础。从长期来看，任何向上的转变都可能很小，从某种程度上说，这反映了投资的回升，这是值得欢迎的。然而，从现在开始，利率很可能会急剧上升，而且可能会令人痛苦。世界的债务已经达到了GDP的355%，这使得企业和家庭对即使是很小的利率上升也更加敏感。很少有中央银行在抑制通胀的同时不让经济陷入衰退的例子。上一次美国通胀率从5%以上下降而不出现衰退还是在70多年前。对抗通货膨胀可能会使世界陷入衰退。如果是这样的话，利率有朝一日会再次回落的这一前景将只能带来一点安慰。■

The UN is testing technology that processes data confidentially

联合国正在测试可以加密处理数据的技术

Data privacy

数据隐私

![image-20220201114343855](/Users/chenyujie/Library/Application Support/typora-user-images/image-20220201114343855.png)

How to analyse data without revealing their secrets

如何在不泄露数据秘密的情况下分析数据

Jan 29th 2022

文章音频

DATA ARE valuable. But not all of them are as valuable as they could be. Reasons of confidentiality mean that many medical, financial, educational and other personal records, from the analysis of which much public good could be derived, are in practice unavailable. A lot of commercial data are similarly sequestered. For example, firms have more granular and timely information on the economy than governments can obtain from surveys. But such intelligence would be useful to rivals. If companies could be certain it would remain secret, they might be more willing to make it available to officialdom.

数据是有价值的。但并不是所有的都有其应有的价值。由于保密的原因，许多医疗、财务、教育和其他个人记录实际上都无法获得，而可以从分析中获得许多公共利益。许多商业数据也同样被藏匿起来。例如，与政府从调查中获得的经济信息相比，企业拥有更细致、更及时的经济信息。不过这些情报对竞争对手来说是有用的。如果企业能够确定这将是秘密的，他们可能更愿意将其提供给官方。

A range of novel data-processing techniques might make such sharing possible. These so-called privacy-enhancing technologies (PETs) are still in the early stages of development. But they are about to get a boost from a project launched by the United Nations’ statistics division. The UN PETs Lab, which opened for business officially on January 25th, enables national statistics offices, academic researchers and companies to collaborate to carry out projects which will test various PETs, permitting technical and administrative hiccups to be identified and overcome.

一系列新的数据处理技术可能会使这种共享成为可能。这些所谓的隐私增强技术(PETs)仍处于发展的早期阶段。但他们即将从联合国统计部门发起的一个项目中得到提振。联合国PETs实验室于1月25日正式“开门”，这使国家统计局、学术研究人员和公司能够合作开展测试各种”隐私增强技术”的项目，从而能够辨别和克服技术和行政上的困难。

The first such effort, which actually began last summer, before the PETs Lab’s formal inauguration, analysed import and export data from national statistical offices in America, Britain, Canada, Italy and the Netherlands, to look for anomalies. Those could be a result of fraud, of faulty record keeping or of innocuous re-exporting.

第一次这样的尝试实际上开始于去年夏天，在PETs实验室正式成立之前，他们分析了来自美国、英国、加拿大、意大利和荷兰国家统计局的进出口数据，以寻找异常情况。这些异常情况可能是欺诈、错误的记录保存或无害的再出口。

For the pilot scheme, the researchers used categories already in the public domain—in this case international trade in things such as wood pulp and clocks. They thus hoped to show that the system would work, before applying it to information where confidentiality matters.

在试验计划中，研究人员使用已经在公共领域的类别，在这个案例中是国际贸易，如木浆和钟表。因此，他们希望在将该系统应用于涉及保密事项的信息之前，表明该系统是可行的。

They put several kinds of PETs through their paces. In one trial, OpenMined, a charity based in Oxford, tested a technique called secure multiparty computation (SMPC). This approach involves the data to be analysed being encrypted by their keeper and staying on the premises. The organisation running the analysis (in this case OpenMined) sends its algorithm to the keeper, who runs it on the encrypted data. That is mathematically complex, but possible. The findings are then sent back to the original inquirer.

他们对几种PETs进行了测试。在一次试验中，位于牛津的慈善机构OpenMined测试了一种名为安全多方计算(SMPC)技术。这种方法涉及到的待分析的数据由其管理员加密并保存在现场。施行“分析”的组织(在本例中为OpenMined)将其算法发送给其管理员，管理员会在加密的数据上运行该算法。这在数学上很复杂，但却是可能的。然后，“结果”会被送回最初的询问者。

That inquirer thus receives its answers, but never has access to the information on which those answers are based. Moreover, for extra security, the results are processed by another PET, called differential privacy. This employs elaborate maths to add a smidgen of statistical noise to a result. That makes the findings less precise, but means they cannot be reverse-engineered to reveal individual records. It also permits the organisation releasing the findings to set a so-called “privacy budget”, which determines the level of granularity disclosed by the data. The result is a belt-and-braces approach. In the argot of the field, SMPC provides input privacy, while differential privacy offers output privacy.

这样，询问者就能得到答案，但却无法获得这些答案所基于的信息。此外，为了增加安全性，结果由另一个称为“差分隐私“的技术进行处理。这种方法运用了复杂的数学，在结果中加入了一点统计噪声。这使得研究结果不那么精确，但这也意味着它们不能通过逆向工程来揭示单个记录。它还允许公布调查结果的组织设定所谓的“隐私预算”[或者隐私代价]，以决定数据披露的粒度水平。其结果是采取了一种“双保险的”方法。在该字段的隐语中，SMPC提供了输入隐私，而差分隐私则提供了输出隐私。

In a second trial using the same data sets, the PETs Lab arranged for Oblivious Software, a company in Dublin, to test “trusted execution environments”, also called “enclaves”, as a form of input privacy. To set these up data are first encrypted by their keeper and then sent to a special, highly secure server that has been built in a trustworthy way, so that every operation can be tracked and its memory fully cleared after the job is done.

在使用相同数据集的第二次试验中，PETs实验室为都柏林的Oblivious Software公司安排了一个测试“可信执行环境”，也被称为“enclaves”，是一种输入隐私的形式。为了建立好这些环境，数据首先是要被他们的管理员加密，然后发送到一个特殊的、安全性高的服务器上，这个服务器是以一种可信的方式建立的，这样每一个操作都可以被追溯，且其在工作完成时内存会完全清除。

Once safely stored in this server’s hardware, the data are decrypted and the desired analysis performed. For extra security, cryptographic hashes and digital signatures are applied, to prove that only authorised operations have taken place. The output is likewise statistically blurred, using differential privacy, before being sent back to the original inquirer.

一旦安全地存储在此服务器的硬件中，数据就会被解密并执行所需的分析。为了提高安全性，应用了加密哈希和数字签名，以证明只进行了授权的操作。在将输出发送回原始查询程序之前，使用差分隐私，让输出在统计上也是模糊的。

In the tests, both approaches did indeed spot anomalies. For example, although American and Canadian records of the value of wood pulp traded between the two countries were basically the same, their data on the value of the clock trade differed by 80%. “Tech-wise, it worked,” gushed Ronald Jansen of the UN statistics division, who administers the new lab.

在测试中，两种方法都确实发现了异常。例如，虽然美国和加拿大两国之间的木浆贸易价值的记录基本相同，但他们的钟表贸易价值的数据相差80%。管理这个新实验室的联合国统计部门的Ronald Jansen滔滔不绝地说:“从技术层面来说，它是有效的。”

Whether it works bureaucratically remains to be seen. But the putative benefits would be great. The use of PETs offers not only a means of bringing together data sets that cannot currently interact because of worries about privacy, but also a way for all sorts of organisations to collaborate securely across borders.

它在官僚层面是否有效还有待观察。但假定的好处将是巨大的。PETs的使用不仅提供了一种聚集“因担心隐私问题而目前不能交互的”数据集的方法，而且还为各种组织提供了一种跨国界安全合作的方式。

The PETs Lab’s next goals are to dive more deeply into trade data and to add more agencies to the roster. This all comes as many governments take a bigger interest in PETs. In December America and Britain announced they plan, this spring, to launch a “grand challenge” prize around PET systems. The sharing of data—and their use—may now be getting easier. ■

PETs实验室的下一个目标是更深入地研究贸易数据，并将更多的机构加入名册。这一切都发生在许多政府对PETs越来越感兴趣的时候。去年12月，美国和英国宣布，他们计划在今年春天推出一个关于PET系统的“重大挑战”奖。数据的共享和使用现在可能变得更容易了。■

Some people want to change the world. But not everyone

有些人想改变世界。但并不是每个人都想

Jan 29th 2022

文章音频

WHAT IS THE meaning of mayonnaise? For Unilever, a consumer-goods giant whose products are all meant to stand for something, the purpose of its Hellmann’s brand is to reduce food waste by making leftovers tasty. For Terry Smith, a fund manager fed up with Unilever’s dipping share price, this is crazy. “The Hellmann’s brand has existed since 1913,” he wrote earlier this month. “So we would guess that by now consumers have figured out its purpose (spoiler alert—salads and sandwiches).”

蛋黄酱是什么意思? 对于消费品巨头联合利华来说，他们的产品都代表着某种东西，其好乐门品牌的目的就是通过让剩菜变得美味从而减少食物浪费。对Terry Smith来说，这简直是疯了。这位基金经理受够了联合利华不断下跌的股价。他在本月早些时候写道：“好乐门品牌自1913年就存在了，所以我们猜测，到目前为止，消费者已经知道它的用途(剧透提醒——沙拉和三明治)。”

Mr Smith’s concern is the financial performance of Unilever (in the face of investor disquiet, the firm is now planning management cuts and an overhaul of its operating model). But his underlying point, that doing the obvious job well can be purpose enough, is one that has much wider application. For it is true of colleagues as well as condiments.

Smith先生担心的是联合利华的财务表现(面对投资者的不安，该公司正计划削减管理人员并彻底改革其运营模式)。但他的潜在观点是，做好一个明确的工作可以有足够的目的性，这一观点具有更广泛的应用。因为这不仅适用于调味品，也适用于同事。

The very idea of a purposeful employee conjures up a specific type of person. They crave a meaningful job that changes society for the better. When asked about their personal passion projects, they don’t say “huh?” or “playing Wordle”. They are concerned about their legacy and almost certainly have a weird diet.

一提到有目标的员工，人们就会联想到一种特定类型的人。他们渴望一份能让社会变得更好的有意义的工作。当被问及他们的个人爱好的项目时，他们不会说 “嗯?” 或 “玩Wordle”。他们担心自己的遗产，几乎可以肯定他们的饮食很奇怪。

Yet this is not the only way to think about purpose-driven employees. New research from Bain, a consultancy, into the attitudes of 20,000 workers across ten countries confirms that people are motivated by different things.

然而，这并不是思考目标驱动型员工的唯一方式。Bain咨询公司对10个国家2万名员工的态度进行的一项新研究证实，人们被不同的事物所激励。

Bain identifies six different archetypes, far too few to reflect the complexity of individuals but a lot better than a single lump of employees. “Pioneers” are the people on a mission to change the world; “artisans” are interested in mastering a specific skill; “operators” derive a sense of meaning from life outside work; “strivers” are more focused on pay and status; “givers” want to do work that directly improves the lives of others; and “explorers” seek out new experiences.

贝恩公司确定了六种不同的原型，太少不足以反映个体的复杂性，但比单一的员工群体要好得多。“先驱者”是那些以改变世界为使命的人; “工匠”感兴趣的是掌握一种特定的技能;“经营者”从工作之外的生活中获得一种意义;“奋斗者”更关注薪水和地位;“给予者”想要做能直接改善他人生活的工作;而“探险者”则寻求新的体验。

These archetypes are unevenly distributed across different industries and roles. Pioneers in particular are more likely to cluster in management roles. The Bain survey finds that 25% of American executives match this archetype, but only 9% of the overall US sample does so. Another survey of American workers carried out by McKinsey, a consulting firm, in 2020 found that executives were far likelier than other respondents to say that their purpose was fulfilled by their job.

这些原型在不同的行业和角色中分布不均。尤其是“先驱者”更有可能集中在管理角色上。贝恩公司的调查发现，25%的美国高管符合这一原型，但整个美国样本中只有9%的人符合这一原型。麦肯锡咨询公司在2020年对美国工人进行的另一项调查发现，高管们比其他受访者更有可能说，他们的工作实现了他们的目标。

This skew matters if managers blindly project their own ideas of purpose onto others. Having a purpose does not necessarily mean a desire to found a startup, head up the career ladder or log into virtual Davos. Some people are fired up by the prospect of learning new skills or of deepening their expertise.

如果经理们盲目地将自己的目标理念投射到他人身上，这种偏差就会产生影响。有一个目标并不一定意味着想要创建一家初创公司，走上职业阶梯或登录线上的达沃斯论坛。有些人会因为学习新技能或深化专业知识而兴奋。

Others derive purpose from specific kinds of responsibility. Research by a couple of academics at NEOMA Business School and Boston University looked at the experience of employees of the Parisian metro system who had been newly promoted into managerial roles. People who had been working as station agents before their elevation were generally satisfied by their new roles. But supervisors who had previously worked as train drivers were noticeably less content: they felt their roles had less meaning when they no longer had direct responsibility for the well-being of passengers.

另一些人则从特定的责任中获得目标。法国诺欧商学院和波士顿大学的几位学者对巴黎地铁系统新晋升到管理岗位的员工的经历进行了研究。那些在晋升之前一直在地铁站工作的人通常对他们的新角色感到满意。但以前当过火车司机的主管明显不那么满足: 他们觉得，当他们对乘客的健康不再负有直接责任时，他们的角色就没那么多意义了。

Firms need to think more creatively about career progression than promoting people into management jobs. IBM, for example, has a fellowship programme designed to give a handful of its most gifted technical employees their own form of recognition each year.

公司需要更有创造性地思考职业发展，而不是把员工提拔到管理岗位。例如，IBM有一个奖学金项目，旨在每年以自己的方式表彰少数最具天赋的技术员工。

Another mistake is to conflate an employee’s commitment with good performance. A recent paper from Yuna Cho of the University of Hong Kong and Winnie Jiang of INSEAD, a business school, describes an experiment in which groups of people with managerial experience listened to two actors playing the part of colleagues. One group heard an “employee” saying that he was looking forward to retirement; another group heard the employee saying that he did not want to retire at all. In all other respects the conversations were the same. The observers assigned a bigger bonus and a higher raise to the employee who appeared to have more passion.

另一个错误是把员工的责任感与良好的表现混为一谈。香港大学的Yuna Cho和欧洲工商管理学院的Winnie Jiang最近发表的一篇论文描述了一个实验，在这个实验中，一组有管理经验的人听两个演员扮演同事的角色。一组听到一名“员工”说他期待退休;另一组则听到这名员工说他根本不想退休。其他方面的谈话都是一样的。观察者给那些看起来更有激情的员工分配了更大的奖金和更高的加薪。

There is some logic here. Employees with a calling could well be more dedicated. But that doesn’t necessarily make them better at the job. And teams are likelier to perform well if they blend types of employees: visionaries to inspire, specialists to deliver and all those people who want to do a job well but not think about it at weekends. Like mayonnaise, the secret is in the mixture.

这里是有一些道理的。有使命感的员工可能会更加敬业。但这并不一定会让他们更胜任这份工作。而且，如果一个团队融合了不同类型的员工，表现更佳的可能性更大: 激发灵感的远见者、不负所望的专家以及所有那些想把工作做好但周末不去想工作的人。像蛋黄酱一样，秘诀就是“混合”。
