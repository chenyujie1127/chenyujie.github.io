Why the word “woman” is tying people in knots

tie sb (up) in knots 使困惑，把某人弄糊涂

为什么“女人”这个词会把人搞糊涂

Sex and language

性别与语言

![image-20211002112850218](/Users/chenyujie/Library/Application Support/typora-user-images/image-20211002112850218.png)

It is almost always women who are ordered to dispense with a useful word

dispense with sth (manage without) 省掉 ; (get rid of) 摒弃

几乎总是女人被命令省去一个有用的词

“BODIES WITH vaginas” is an odd way to refer to half the human race. Yet it was the quote that the Lancet, a medical journal, chose to feature on the cover of its latest issue, telling readers that “historically, the anatomy and physiology” of such bodies had been neglected. After complaints about dehumanising language, the Lancet apologised. But it is not alone. A growing number of officials and organisations are finding themselves tongue-tied when it comes to using the word “woman”.

anatomy 解剖学

dehumanize 使丧失人性

tongue-tied 张口结舌的

“有阴道的身体”是用一种奇怪的方式来指代一半的人类。然而，医学杂志《柳叶刀》选择在其最新一期的封面上刊登这段引语，告诉读者，“从历史上看，这种身体的解剖学和生理学”一直被忽视。在对非人性化语言的抱怨之后，《柳叶刀》进行了道歉。但它并非特例。越来越多的官员和组织发现，一提到“女人”这个词，他们就说不出话来。

A British hospital has instructed staff on its maternity wards to offer to use the phrase “birthing people”. Alexandra Ocasio-Cortez, a member of America’s Congress, talks of “menstruating people”. On September 18th the American Civil Liberties Union (ACLU) republished a quote from Ruth Bader Ginsburg, a Supreme Court judge, on the anniversary of her death. The quote was a defence of a woman’s right to have an abortion. But the ACLU’s version—for which it, too, later apologised—replaced every instance of “women” with “people”. In Britain the opposition Labour Party is tying itself in very public knots over questions such as whether only women possess cervixes.

menstruate 行经；月经来潮

英国一家医院指示其产科病房的员工主动使用“待产人”一词。美国国会议员Alexandra Ocasio-Cortez谈到“月经来潮的人”。9月18日，美国公民自由联盟在最高法院法官Ruth Bader Ginsburg的逝世周年纪念日上再次引用了她的一段话。这句话是在捍卫女性堕胎的权利。但是美国公民自由联盟的版本（它后来也为此道歉）用“人”代替了“女性”。在英国，反对党工党在诸如是否只有女性拥有子宫颈这样的问题上陷入人尽皆知的困惑。

This linguistic shift is being driven by both compassion and fear. Compassion, because organisations are keen not to be seen to be excluding those whose sense of their gender does not match their sex, such as people who identify as trans or non-binary. And fear, because they are worried about attracting the wrath of online mobs should they be deemed to have violated a set of rapidly changing taboos about gender and sex that hardly existed five years ago—and which, outside a few rarefied circles, still don’t. Most dictionaries define a woman as an “adult human female”. Among some activists, this is a gross provocation, for they see it as denying that males can be women, too.

这种语言上的转变是由同情和恐惧驱动的。同情，因为组织机构不希望被视为排斥那些性别意识与自己性别不相符的人，比如认为自己是跨性别者或非二元性别者。还有恐惧，因为他们担心一旦他们被认为违反了一系列快速变化的关于性别的禁忌，他们就会引起网络暴徒的愤怒。这些禁忌在五年前几乎不存在，而这些禁忌在一些稀罕的圈子之外仍然不存在。大多数字典把女人定义为“成年人类女性”。在一些激进分子眼里，这是一种严重的挑衅，因为他们认为这是在否认男性也可以是女性。

Language changes constantly (the word “taboo”, for instance, is an 18th-century import from Polynesia). But doctors, bosses and politicians should think carefully before throwing away widely understood words, or using them in new and radically different ways. In the rush to seem up to date, they risk doing a disservice to their own patients, employees and voters.

throw away 随口说出

disservice 损害

to do sb/sth a disservice, to do a disservice to sb/sth; 对某人/某事物造成损害

语言在不断变化(例如“taboo”一词是18世纪从波利尼西亚来的文化输入物)。但是，医生、老板和政客们在随口说出广为人知的词汇或以全新的、完全不同的方式使用它们之前，应该仔细考虑。为了赶时髦，他们冒着伤害自己病人、员工和选民的风险。

One reason is that many of the new terms come across as dehumanising. As the Lancet discovered, many people—trans men as much as anyone else—dislike being described as collections of ambulatory body-parts and secretions. More than a whiff of misogyny is in the air. It is striking that there is no comparably zealous campaign to abandon the word “men” in favour of “prostate-havers”, “ejaculators” or “bodies with testicles”. It is almost always women who are being ordered to dispense with a useful word they have used all their lives.

ambulatory 能走动的；步行的

whiff 一丝

misogyny 厌恶女性

一个原因是，许多新术语给人的感觉是不人性化的。正如《柳叶刀》杂志所发现的，许多跨性别男性和其他人一样，不喜欢被描述为流动身体部位和分泌物的集合。空气中弥漫着一股厌恶女性的味道。令人吃惊的是，并没有类似的狂热运动来放弃“男人”这个词，转而支持“前列腺拥有者”、“射精者”或“有睾丸的身体”。几乎总是女人被命令放弃她们用了一辈子的有用词汇。

Furthermore, understanding could suffer. Medical advice, for instance, has to be clear and intelligible by all. That is why Britain’s National Health Service often prefers words like “stomach ache” to “dyspepsia”, or “heart attack” to “myocardial infarction”. One survey conducted by a cervical-cancer charity suggested that around 40% of women are unsure about the details of what exactly a cervix is. This implies that asking “people with cervixes” to turn up for screening appointments may not be clear or intelligible, especially to women who have English as their second language.

dyspepsia 消化不良

此外，理解可能会受到影响。例如，医疗建议必须清晰易懂。这就是为什么英国国民医疗服务体系更喜欢“胃痛”而不是“消化不良”，或者“心脏病发作”而不是“心肌梗死”。一项由宫颈癌慈善机构进行的调查显示，大约40%的女性不确定宫颈到底是什么。这意味着，要求“有子宫颈的人”来参加筛查预约可能不清楚或难以理解，尤其是对英语为第二语言的女性来说。

XX-rated

Most broadly of all, the point of language is to communicate. Insisting on unfamiliar or alien-sounding terms will make it harder to discuss issues that affect only or disproportionately girls and women, such as female genital mutilation, domestic violence, child marriage or the persistence of pay gaps.

最广泛地说，语言的意义在于沟通。坚持使用不熟悉的或听起来像外国人的术语，将使讨论只影响或不成比例地影响女孩和女人的问题变得更加困难，例如女性生殖器切除、家庭暴力、童婚或持续存在的收入差距。

Many countries are debating whether safety or fairness should sometimes trump inclusion. Should male prisoners, even violent ones, who identify as women be housed in women’s prisons, as may happen in America, Britain, Canada and elsewhere? Should women’s sports be reserved for biological females, or should any athlete who identifies as a woman be allowed to join a women’s rugby team?

许多国家正在讨论是否安全或公平有时应该压倒包容。是否应该像美国、英国、加拿大和其他地方发生的那样，将被认定为女性的男性囚犯，甚至暴力的男性囚犯关押在女性监狱中? 女子运动应该保留给生理上的女性，还是应该允许任何被认定为女性的运动员加入女子橄榄球队?

Cowed by the insults and viciousness such discussions provoke, many people are fearful of taking part. If harshly policed, baffling and alien-sounding language is added to the price of joining the debate, even fewer will be willing to elevate their cephalic protuberance above the parapet. ■

cow 恐吓

viciousness 恶毒

to put or stick one’s head above the parapet 贸然出头

许多人被此类讨论所引发的侮辱和恶意所吓到，不敢参与其中。如果严格的监管、令人费解的、听起来陌生的语言成为加入辩论的代价，那么愿意出头就更少了。■

South Korea’s plan to fight fake news could hobble real journalism

韩国打击假新闻的计划可能会阻碍真正的新闻业

Off-target

偏离目标

![image-20211001110553764](/Users/chenyujie/Library/Application Support/typora-user-images/image-20211001110553764.png)

The ruling party wants to make it easier for people to sue the media for “emotional distress”

执政党希望让人们起诉那些造成他们“精神损失”的媒体更容易些

WHEN LAW MAKERS from South Korea’s ruling Minjoo party discuss their plan to impose tougher penalties on the media for reporting “fake news”, they stress that they merely want to safeguard vulnerable citizens. “It’s the least we can do to protect the people from fake news,” one legislator told reporters earlier this month. “We have to remember that our people’s basic rights and freedoms are more important than the freedom of the press,” said another. The proposed changes to the Press Arbitration Act of 2011, the party argues, would improve the quality of reporting and increase the public’s trust in it.

当韩国执政党共同民主党的立法者讨论他们对报道“假新闻”的媒体施加更严厉惩罚的计划时，他们强调，他们只是想保护脆弱的公民。本月早些时候，一名立法者告诉记者:“这是保护人们免受假新闻侵害的最起码的举措。另一个立法者说：“我们必须牢记，我们人民的基本权利和自由比新闻自由更重要”。共同民主党认为，对2011年《媒体仲裁法》的拟议修改将提高报道质量，并增加公众对其的信任。

Hogwash, says the conservative opposition, which quashed a first attempt to pass the bill last month by threatening a filibuster. The proposal, it argues, is an attempt by the government to shield itself from criticism. Other observers appear to agree. Last week, a coalition of activist groups including Human Rights Watch called on legislators from all parties to reject the bill when it is put to another vote in the National Assembly on September 27th.

hogwash 废话，胡说八道，瞎话

filibuster 阻挠行动

quash 撤销；否决

上个月，反对党保守党威胁要阻挠议案通过，从而使第一次通过议案的努力付诸东流。其辩称，该提议是政府试图保护自己免受批评的一种尝试。其他观察人士似乎也同意这一观点。上周，包括人权观察在内的一个激进组织联盟呼吁所有党派的立法者在9月27日国民议会再次投票时否决该法案。

The bill introduces new provisions allowing individuals and small businesses to go after reporters and media organisations for, among other things, causing “emotional distress” by spreading false information either intentionally or as a result of “gross negligence”, for instance without fact-checking. Critics worry the vague wording of some of the clauses would give the authorities too much discretion to decide which reports qualify for corrections or damages. A letter from the UN’s special rapporteur on freedom of expression, sent to the government in August, warned that the law could be used to limit ordinary news reporting and criticism of the government, and could see writers held liable for parody and satire or simply for expressing unpopular opinions.

go after 追赶；追求

rapporteur 特派调查员

parody 滑稽模仿作品

该法案引入了新的条款，允许个人和小企业追究记者和媒体机构故意传播虚假信息或因“重大疏忽”(例如未经核实)而造成“精神损失”等行为。批评人士担心，一些条款的模糊措辞将给当局太多的自由裁量权，以决定哪些报道符合惩罚或损害赔偿的条件。联合国言论自由特派调查员在8月寄给政府的一封信中警告说，这项法律可能被用来限制普通的新闻报道和对政府的批评，而且可能会让作家为模仿和讽刺或仅仅是表达不受欢迎的观点而被追究责任。

The ruling party’s concerns are not entirely unfounded. Like people everywhere, South Koreans are exposed to mountains of humbug. A noisy minority of mostly elderly right-wingers watch YouTube channels that spread misinformation about vaccines, American politics and President Moon Jae-in’s supposed chumminess with North Korea, the XX, the devil, or all three. Even beyond the radical fringe, most people get their news from online aggregators whose algorithms reward sensationalist clickbait and which take little responsibility for sourcing. The biggest newspapers have long-standing links to big conglomerates; the heads of public broadcasters change with the government. Unsurprisingly, less than a third of South Koreans say they trust the news.

unfounded 无根据的

humbug 欺骗行为

sensationalist 追求轰动效应者

执政党的担忧并非毫无根据。像世界各地的人一样，韩国人身处于堆积如山的谎言中。一小部分吵吵嚷嚷的右翼人士(主要是上了年纪的右翼人士)观看YouTube频道，这些频道传播有关疫苗、美国政治以及文在寅总统与朝鲜、XX、魔鬼或三者的所谓亲密关系的错误信息。即使在极端边缘之外，大多数人也从在线“信息聚合网站”获取新闻，这些“信息聚合网站”的算法会奖励那些哗众取众的“标题党”，它们对信息来源几乎不负责。最大的报纸与大企业集团有着长期的联系; 公共广播公司的负责人随政府更换。不出所料，不到三分之一的韩国人表示相信新闻。

But if legislators are indeed trying to solve these problems and bolster their democracy, they are going about it in a curious way, says Park Kyung-sin, a law professor and free-speech activist at Korea University in Seoul. Most fake news and conspiracy-mongering originates on social media, he notes. But the law only applies to newspapers and broadcasters, leaving the main source of falsehoods untouched. Mr Park suspects the party’s real motive is a feeling of being under siege by the press: they find it hard to accept that, in government, “they’re being criticised by all journalists, not just the right-wing media”.

但是，首尔高丽大学的法学教授、言论自由活动家Park Kyung-sin说，如果立法者确实试图解决这些问题，并加强他们的民主，他们是以一种奇怪的方式进行的。他指出，大多数假新闻和阴谋论都起源于社交媒体。但该法律只适用于报纸和广播公司，而不触及谎言的主要来源。Park先生怀疑这个政党的真正动机是一种被媒体包围的感觉:他们发现很难接受，在政府中，“他们受到所有记者的批评，而不仅仅是右翼媒体”。

It is all the more surprising given that press freedom in South Korea has improved significantly since Mr Moon took power in 2017. South Korea climbed 30 places in a global press freedom index compiled by Reporters Without Borders, a watchdog, after his election. And it is an unusual bill for a liberal democracy to propose, carrying as it does elements of similar laws passed in recent years by bossier places such as Singapore.

bossy 爱发号施令的

考虑到自文在寅2017年上台以来，韩国的新闻自由大幅改善，这就更令人惊讶了。在他当选后，韩国在监督机构无国界记者编制的全球新闻自由指数中上升了30位。对于一个自由民主国家来说，这是一项不同寻常的法案，因为它包含了新加坡等更强势的国家近年来通过的类似法律的内容。

The many complaints have not fallen on deaf ears. Minjoo legislators have responded by drafting a revised version of the bill. But it still fails to resolve the main worry, that the law would unduly restrict speech. Given the party’s absolute majority, it is likely to pass, unless legislators decide to withdraw it. If it does, South Korea will surely drop a few places in next year’s press-freedom ranking. ■

An influential ruling about puberty blockers has been overturned

一项关于青春期阻滞剂的有影响力的裁决被推翻

Gender medicine

![image-20210930094319451](/Users/chenyujie/Library/Application Support/typora-user-images/image-20210930094319451.png)

Judges rule that doctors, not courts, should decide on treatment

法官规定应该医生决定治疗而不是法庭

DECISIONS ABOUT medical treatments are usually made in doctors’ offices, not the Victorian splendour of the High Court in London. But in 2020, three judges ruled that children under 16 would be unlikely to be able to consent to one particular medical treatment—the administration of puberty-blocking drugs to those suffering from gender dysphoria, a deep discomfort with one’s sex.

splendour 辉煌

关于医疗的决定通常是在医生的办公室里做出的，而不是在维多利亚时代辉煌的伦敦高等法院。但在2020年，三名法官裁定，16岁以下的儿童不太可能被同意使用一种特定的治疗方法，即对患有性别焦虑症(对自己的性别深感不适)的人使用抑制青春期发育的药物。

On September 17th the Court of Appeal overturned that ruling, saying that decisions about whether to prescribe puberty-blockers should be left to doctors. The ruling might seem of interest only to specialist medical lawyers. But the legal saga has been watched around the world, for gender dysphoria is a fast-growing diagnosis and the treatments prescribed for it are controversial. Referrals to the Gender Identity Development Service (GIDS), England’s only dedicated youth clinic, have risen from 77 in 2009-10 to 2,383 in 2020-21. Other rich countries have seen similarly sharp rises.

9月17日，上诉法院推翻了这一裁决，其称是否开青春期阻滞剂的决定应该留给医生。这项裁决似乎只有专业的医疗律师感兴趣。但这个法律故事在世界各地都受到了关注，因为性别焦虑症是一种快速发展的诊断，而且针对它的治疗方法也存在争议。性别认同发展服务(GIDS)是英国唯一的专门的青年诊所，其转诊人数从2009-2010年的77人增加到2020-2021年的2383人。其他发达国家也出现了类似的大幅增长。

Proponents describe the treatments as life-saving. Sceptics point to the lack of clinical evidence showing that they work, and to worries about severe side-effects later in life. After the original ruling, GIDS suspended puberty-blocker treatments for new patients.

支持者称这种治疗可以挽救生命。持怀疑态度的人指出，缺乏临床证据证明它们是有效的，并担心它们在以后的生活中会产生严重的副作用。在最初的裁决之后，GIDS暂停了对新患者的青春期阻滞剂治疗。

The original case had been brought by Mrs A, the unnamed mother of a child with gender dysphoria, and Keira Bell, a 24-year-old who had been prescribed puberty blockers at GIDS at the age of 16. Ms Bell went on to take cross-sex hormones and to have her breasts surgically removed, only later to regret both decisions. The original ruling referred to the lack of evidence regarding whether puberty blockers actually help alleviate gender dysphoria, and noted that almost all patients prescribed them go on to take cross-sex hormones. These lead to irreversible changes, including the development of cross-sex characteristics such as breasts and beards, and raise the risk of infertility. With all that in mind, the judges ruled that under-16s were unlikely to be able to understand and weigh up the lifelong consequences sufficiently well to consent to taking them.

unnamed 未透露名字的

hormone 激素

infertility 无生育能力

weigh up 掂量

案件最初是由A女士和Keira Bell提起的，A女士（未透露名字）的孩子患有性别焦虑症，而24岁的Keira Bell在16岁的时候就在GIDS开了青春期阻滞剂。Bell女士随后服用了跨性别激素，并做了乳房切除手术，但后来她对这两个决定都感到后悔。最初的裁决提到，关于青春期阻滞剂是否真的有助于缓解性别焦虑的证据不足，并指出，几乎所有开了青春期阻滞剂的患者都会继续服用跨性别激素。这将导致不可逆转的变化，包括乳房和胡须等跨性别特征的发展，并增加不孕的风险。考虑到这一切，法官裁定16岁以下的青少年不太可能充分理解并权衡终身后果，从而同意服用这些药物。

The Court of Appeal however, decided that the lower court had, in thus venturing into clinical waters, gone beyond its proper remit. Previous case law, it said, had established that decisions about the wisdom or otherwise of medical treatments—even controversial or disputed ones—were best left to doctors. It noted that the original judgment had not found that the Tavistock and Portman NHS Trust (which runs GIDS) had broken the law, and ruled that the case should have been dismissed as soon as that had been established.

然而，上诉法院认为下级法院这样冒险进入临床领域，已经超出了其适当的职权范围。它说，以前的判例法规定，关于治疗是否明智的决定，即使是有争议的决定，最好留给医生。它指出，最初的判决并没有发现塔维斯托克波特曼信托基金会(该信托基金经营着GIDS)违反了法律，并裁决该案件一旦成立就应该被驳回。

Ms Bell’s supporters hope to appeal to the Supreme Court. But most lawyers seem to think a further appeal would have little chance of success. “I found the Court of Appeal’s judgment a very compelling read,” says one who has followed the case closely. It is unlikely, says another, that the Supreme Court would want to revisit what is widely seen as a settled area of law.

Bell女士的支持者希望上诉到最高法院。但大多数律师似乎认为，进一步上诉成功的可能性很小。一位密切关注此案的人士表示：“我认为上诉法院的判决非常令人信服”。另一位密切关注此案的人士表示，最高法院不太可能想要重审这个被广泛视为已确立的法律领域。

But kicking decisions back to doctors does not mean that the wider argument about puberty blockers is over. Medical opinion in several countries is becoming more cautious. The car industry

Natural-gas shortages threaten governments’ green goals

天然气短缺威胁着政府的绿色目标

Gas puzzlers

天然气问题

![image-20210925100339095](/Users/chenyujie/Library/Application Support/typora-user-images/image-20210925100339095.png)

The energy transition must be better managed or environmentalism will become unpopular

能源转型必须得到更好的管理，否则环保主义将不再受欢迎

YET ANOTHER crucial global market has gone from glut to shortage at breakneck speed. Last September in Europe it cost €119 ($139) to buy enough gas to heat the average home for a year and the continent’s gas-storage facilities were brimming. Today it costs €738 and stocks are scarce. Even America, which has an abundance of shale gas, has seen prices more than double—albeit from a much lower level—and could see further increases if its winter is a cold one.

glut 供过于求

breakneck 极快而危险的

brim 装满

到目前为止，另一个至关重要的全球市场以极快的速度从过剩变为短缺。去年9月，在欧洲，购买足够一个普通家庭取暖一年的天然气需要花费119欧元(139美元)，而欧洲大陆的天然气储存设施也满当当的。如今它的价格是738欧元，股票也很稀缺。即使是页岩气储量丰富的美国，其价格也上涨了一倍多（尽管从较低的水平开始上涨）如果冬天天气寒冷，价格还会进一步上涨。

The shortage has many causes. A cold European spring and a hot Asian summer boosted energy demand. Rebounding industrial production has lifted the global appetite for liquefied natural gas (LNG). Russia has been piping less gas into European stockpiles. Hawks suspect it of trying to spook the market and ensure its new Nord Stream 2 pipeline is approved. But it has also faced disruptions, including a fire at a processing plant in Siberia.

spook 吓唬

这种短缺有很多原因。欧洲寒冷的春天和亚洲炎热的夏天刺激了能源需求。工业生产的反弹提高了全球对液化天然气(LNG)的需求。俄罗斯一直在减少向欧洲输送的天然气储备。鹰派怀疑它试图恐吓市场，以确保新的北溪2号管道获得批准。但它也面临中断，包括西伯利亚的一家加工厂发生火灾。

Gas has been plugging gaps in power production from other sources. The wind did not blow much in Europe this summer, while droughts interfered with hydropower output. The rising price of the permits needed to emit carbon in the EU has made coal expensive. So there is little alternative to burning gas for electricity as well as for heating homes.

天然气一直在填补其他能源生产的缺口。今年夏天，欧洲风力不大，而干旱影响了水力发电。欧盟碳排放许可价格的上涨使得煤炭价格昂贵。因此，除了用燃气发电和供暖之外，几乎没有其他选择。

Whereas the world economy’s other bottlenecks—for container ships and microchips—have unleashed a capital-expenditure boom, investment in fossil fuels is in long-term decline. American shale can help only so much, because gas markets are imperfectly linked via LNG. High prices, when they strike, will serve mainly to ration limited supply. But it takes big price movements to curb demand. If the coming months are chilly, Europe’s energy may have to become extremely expensive to persuade firms and households to use less.

unleash 发动

世界经济的其他瓶颈（集装箱船和微芯片）引发了资本支出的激增时，不过化石燃料的投资却在长期下降。美国页岩所能起到的作用只有这么多，因为天然气市场通过液化天然气不完全联系在一起。当价格上涨时，它将主要用于限量供应。但需要价格大幅波动才能抑制需求。如果未来几个月天气寒冷，欧洲的能源可能不得不变得极其昂贵，以说服企业和家庭减少使用。

Sorting this out requires accurately diagnosing what has gone wrong. Governments have not made enough allowance for the intermittency of renewable energy. The world has too little nuclear power—a low-carbon energy source that is always on. Interventions and subsidies for gas will only make things worse. Expensive energy angers voters and hurts the poor. But subsidising energy in a squeeze, as Italy is doing, or capping prices, as Britain does, will exacerbate shortages and make politicians’ commitment to greenery look empty. Governments should use the welfare system to support household incomes if they must, while helping energy markets work efficiently.

要想解决这个问题，需要准确诊断出哪里出了问题。政府没有充分考虑可再生能源的间歇性。世界上的核能太少了（一种一直在使用的低碳能源）。对天然气的干预和补贴只会让情况变得更糟。昂贵的能源激怒了选民，伤害了穷人。但像意大利那样在能源紧缺的情况下补贴能源，或者像英国那样限制价格，都会加剧短缺，让政客们对绿色能源的承诺显得空洞。如果有必要，政府应该利用福利制度来支持家庭收入，同时帮助能源市场有效运转。

The long-term challenge is to smooth out volatility as the switch to renewables continues. Eventually cheap battery storage might solve the intermittency problem; right now, more gas storage would help too. In the meantime tweaks to the market could improve things.

smooth out 消除；解决

长期的挑战是在向可再生能源转变的过程中消除波动性。最终，廉价的电池存储可能会解决间歇性问题; 现在，更多的天然气储备也会有所帮助。与此同时，对市场的调整可能会改善情况。

In Britain many small energy suppliers which offer, say, one-year fixed-priced contracts to consumers, but buy energy at floating rates will soon fail. Getting firms selling at fixed rates to hedge against wholesale price increases should encourage more physical storage of gas. Another idea is to invest more in connecting grids (a link between those of Britain and France recently failed) and in LNG infrastructure, so that arbitrage trades can even out disparities in the global supply of energy.

arbitrage 套利

even out 消除

在英国，许多小型能源供应商向消费者提供一年期固定价格合同，却以浮动价格购买能源，这些供应商很快就会出问题。让企业以固定利率出售以对冲批发价格上涨的风险，应该会鼓励更多的天然气实物储存。另一个想法是加大对连接电网(最近英国和法国的电网连接失败了)和液化天然气基础设施的投资，这样套利交易就可以抵消全球能源供应的差异。

Dirty sources of energy should be expensive. But without reliable alternatives, price increases boost inflation, lower living standards and make environmentalism unpopular. If governments do not manage the energy transition more carefully, then today’s crisis will be the first of many that threaten the vital move to a stable climate. ■

污染能源应该是昂贵的。但如果没有可靠的替代方案，价格上涨会推高通胀，降低生活水平，让环保主义不受欢迎。如果各国政府不更加谨慎地管理能源转型，那么今天的危机将是众多危机中的第一个，这些危机将威胁到为实现气候稳定所采取的至关重要的行动。■

Why carnivores host diseases that jump to humans

为什么食肉动物会携带传染给人类的疾病

Zoonotic diseases

人畜共患疾病

![image-20210924104126046](/Users/chenyujie/Library/Application Support/typora-user-images/image-20210924104126046.png)

Meat-eating seems to make animals more likely to incubate human diseases

吃肉似乎使动物更容易感染人类疾病

IN NOVEMBER 2020, at the height of the second covid-19 wave in Europe, the Danish government revealed that a new strain of the virus had been found in farmed mink. Officials worried that the new strain could become widespread in humans, where it might prove deadlier or more resistant to vaccines. For Clare Bryant, a veterinary scientist at the University of Cambridge, this was exactly what she feared might happen. “I thought holy moly, there you go,” she says.

mink 水貂

holy moly 我的天呐！ 【语气助词而已，表示出很惊讶、震惊、希望强调后面的内容】

2020年11月，在欧洲第二波covid-19浪潮的高峰期，丹麦政府透露，在养殖水貂中发现了一种新的病毒毒株。官员们担心这种新菌株可能在人类中广泛传播，在那里它可能会更致命或对疫苗更具抵抗力。对于剑桥大学的兽医科学家Clare Bryant来说，这正是她担心可能发生的事情。她说:“我想，天哪，就是这样。”

At the time, she and her team were investigating the biology of Carnivora, an order of meat-eating mammals that includes cats, dogs, bears and mink, among others. Carnivora is a potent source of “zoonotic” diseases—those that have jumped to humans from their original animal hosts. Almost half of its members carry one or more unique zoonotic pathogens. Biologists have long wondered why that should be. Dr Bryant’s paper, published in Cell Reports, suggests an answer.

当时，她和她的团队正在研究食肉目生物学，食肉目是一种食肉哺乳动物，包括猫、狗、熊和水貂等。食肉目是“人畜共患”疾病的一个有力来源，这些疾病是从最初的动物宿主传染给人类的。几乎一半的成员携带一种或多种独特的人畜共患病病原体。生物学家一直想知道为什么会这样。Bryant博士发表在《Cell Reports》上的论文给出了答案。

Diseases jump from animals to humans when a pathogen stumbles upon a mutation that allows it to infect human cells as well as animal ones. Since mutation is a random process that mostly happens when pathogens reproduce, a bug is most likely to hit the jackpot when it has plenty of time to survive in its host unmolested.

stumble across, stumble on, stumble upon:

stumble across sth 意外发现某事物

unmolested 未受干扰的

to hit the jackpot 赢得一大笔钱；获得巨大成功

bug 病菌

当病原体意外发生一种突变，使其能够感染人类细胞和动物细胞时，疾病就会从动物传染给人类。由于突变是一个随机过程，主要发生在病原体繁殖时，当病菌有足够的时间在宿主体内不受干扰地生存时，它最有可能“中大奖”【突变】。

Healthy animals do not usually allow pathogens such leisure time. Infection triggers an immune response aimed at wiping out the invader. One part of that response relies on proteins governed by chemicals called receptors, adaptors and effectors. Receptors sound the alarm when a pathogen is spotted. They cause adaptors to trigger the release of effectors, which are enzymes that produce proteins capable of destroying the invader.

健康的动物通常不会让病原体有这样的闲暇时间。感染引发了一种旨在消灭入侵者的免疫反应。这种反应的一部分依赖于由受体、接头和效应物等化学物质控制的蛋白质。当发现病原体时，受体就会发出警报。它们导致接头触发效应物的释放，效应物是一种酶，能够产生能够摧毁入侵者的蛋白质。

Dr Bryant found that, in Carnivora, some of this machinery is broken. Immunological pathways in the gut, in particular, seem not to work properly. Genes for receptors are missing or disabled, allowing pathogens to go unseen. Two effector genes are fused, compromising any response that is mounted. After spotting these anomalies in dogs, the researchers edited the fused effector genes into mice. Sure enough, the animals’ immune response was hampered.

compromise 损害

anomaly 反常的事物

Bryant博士发现，在食肉目动物中，这一机制的某些部分被破坏了。特别是肠道的免疫途径似乎无法正常工作。受体的基因缺失或失效，使得病原体无法被发现。两个效应物基因融合，这就损害了任何产生的反应。在狗身上发现这些异常后，研究人员将融合的效应物基因编辑到老鼠身上。果然，动物的免疫反应受到了阻碍。

While Dr Bryant’s team looked only at dogs, the mutations resemble those found in other sorts of carnivorous mammal, including cats, ferrets and bears. All share the fusion in the effector genes, though the specific faults with their receptors differ. That suggests similar defects have evolved several times in different lineages.

虽然Bryant博士的研究小组只研究了狗，但这种突变与其他食肉哺乳动物(包括猫、雪貂和熊)的突变相似。尽管它们受体的特定缺陷不同，但所有的效应物基因都有融合。这表明类似的缺陷在不同的谱系中已经进化了好几次。

But that is only half the story. For an animal to act as a useful carrier from a pathogen’s point of view, it not only has to let the pathogen survive but also stay alive itself in order to infect others. One possibility is that carnivores have alternative lines of defence that partially compensate for their defective immune systems. These extra defences might be potent enough to keep animals from getting sick, but not effective enough to wipe out pathogens completely.

carrier 带菌者

但这只是故事的一半。从病原体的角度来看，动物要想成为有用的携带者，它不仅要让病原体存活，还要让自己存活，以便感染他人。一种可能是，食肉动物有替代的防线，以部分弥补其缺陷的免疫系统。这些额外的防御可能足以防止动物生病，但不足以彻底消灭病原体。

Dietary adaptations, says Dr Bryant, may be the culprit. Earlier experiments on caterpillars have shown that animals with protein-rich diets have fewer infections. The meaty diets of Carnivora may grant its members similar protection. The key, says Dr Bryant, is likely to be the microbiome, the ecosystem of bacteria that live in animals’ guts. Her guess is that these bacteria metabolise dietary protein into antimicrobial compounds which suppress pathogens. That imperfect protection may allow other bits of the animals’ immune systems to fall into evolutionary disrepair.

Bryant博士说，饮食适应可能是罪魁祸首。早期对毛毛虫的实验表明，饮食富含蛋白质的动物感染的几率更低。食肉目的肉食可能给予其成员类似的保护。 Bryant博士说，关键可能在于微生物群，即生活在动物肠道内的细菌生态系统。她猜测，这些细菌将饮食中的蛋白质代谢成抑制病原体的抗菌化合物。这种不完善的保护可能会让动物免疫系统的其他部分陷入进化失修。

The exact details are still to be worked out. But the pet carnivores with which so many humans share their homes are unlikely to pose much risk. The real danger comes from when such animals are herded together in close quarters, giving pathogens access to a bigger pool of individuals in which to mutate. The upshot of all this, says Dr Bryant, is that farming animals in Carnivora is a bad idea. Indeed, after discovering the mutant version of SARS-CoV-2, the Danish government culled millions of those mink—effectively ending the world’s only large industry that did so. ■

cull 限量捕杀；挑出

具体的细节还有待解决。但是，与如此多的人类共享家园的宠物食肉动物不太可能构成太大的风险。真正的危险来自于当这些动物聚集在一起的时候，病原体有机会接触到更大的个体，并在其中发生变异。Bryant博士说，所有这些的结果是，饲养食肉目动物不是一个好主意。事实上，在发现SARS-CoV-2的变异版本后，丹麦政府宰杀了数百万貂，从而有力终结了世界上唯一这么做的大型貂产业。■

Japanese companies try to reduce their reliance on Chinese manufacturing

Asian supply chains

亚洲供应链

![image-20210923133059744](/Users/chenyujie/Library/Application Support/typora-user-images/image-20210923133059744.png)

But only a bit

但只有一点点

AT THE END of the month the production line of a Toshiba factory in Dalian will come to a halt, 30 years after the Japanese electronics giant opened it in the north-eastern Chinese city. Once a totemic example of global supply chains expanding into China, the closure exemplifies how these are being reconfigured. The short answer is: delicately and at the margin.

totemic 图腾的

to have a totemic significance 具有象征意义

本月末，日本电子巨头东芝在中国东北城市大连开设的一条生产线将关停。这曾是全球供应链向中国扩张的标志性例子，它的关闭说明这些供应链正在如何重新配置。简言之，答案就是: 小心翼翼以及看利润率。

Toshiba’s plant in Dalian has spanned a sea change in Asian business patterns. When it opened, Japan was the undisputed linchpin of the region’s trade and manufacturing networks. By 2019 Japan’s $390bn in intermediate-goods trade with big Asian economies was vying for runner-up status with South Korea and Taiwan. China, with $935bn-worth, was way ahead.

undisputed 无可争辩的

linchpin 关键因素; 关键人物

runner-up 亚军

东芝在大连的工厂使亚洲商业模式发生了翻天覆地的变化。当它开放时，日本是该地区贸易和制造业网络无可争议的关键。到2019年，日本与亚洲大型经济体之间3900亿美元的中间产品贸易，正与韩国和中国台湾争夺亚军地位。中国以9350亿美元遥遥领先。

That is still a quarter of Japanese rates but twice the pay of Thai workers, who were at parity with Chinese ones as recently as 2008. If that were not enough, geopolitical tensions are souring relations between the increasingly heavy-handed XXX and the world’s rich democracies.

sour 使..恶化

These trends help explain why China’s share of Japan’s new outbound foreign direct investment has steadily declined since 2012. The number of manufacturing affiliates that Japanese companies have in China stopped growing almost a decade ago, while new affiliates elsewhere in Asia—notably India, Indonesia, Thailand and Vietnam—have continued to mushroom. Toshiba will offset some of the forgone capacity with expansion in some of its 50 factories back home and also in Vietnam, one of its 30 overseas facilities. mushroom 迅速增长

Many global companies, from Hasbro (an American toymaker) to Samsung (a South Korean technology giant) are making a similar calculation. Toshiba itself will maintain a second, part-owned factory in Dalian.

许多其他日本公司也发现自己处于类似的情况。本月，日本一家规模较小的电子产品制造商OKIElectric Industry宣布，其在深圳成立20年的工厂将停止生产打印机。这些产能将转移到泰国和日本的现有工厂。不过，大多数公司并不急于完全退出中国。去年，日本贸易振兴机构(一个政府机构)的一项调查发现，8%的日本公司表示他们计划减少或取消在中国的业务，低于日本公司在其他国家的平均水平。从美国玩具制造商Hasbro到韩国科技巨头三星，许多跨国公司都在进行类似的考虑。东芝本身将在大连保留第二家部分所有权的工厂。

This would disrupt profitable relationships with Chinese suppliers and manufacturing know-how. Such things take years to forge. But at the margin, where companies find themselves pressed by the imperatives to cut costs and guarantee stable future supplies, China no longer looks like the place to be. ■
