Will conversational commerce be the next big thing in online shopping?

会话商务会成为网上购物的下一个大事件吗?

Chat-up lines

![image-20210714101207563](/Users/chenyujie/Library/Application Support/typora-user-images/image-20210714101207563.png)

More companies are reaching consumers with messaging apps

越来越多的公司正在通过信息通讯app接触消费者

Jul 10th 2021

MESSAGING IS AN intimate medium for sharing private views and sentiments. It is a cocktail-party whisper in digital form, as one user of WhatsApp, a service owned by Facebook, put it. Now some of the world’s biggest brands are venturing into this personal realm. Aware of the limitations of conventional communication channels like call centres and email, a few years ago firms started using WhatsApp and its sister app, Facebook’s Messenger, as well as Apple’s iMessage and independent apps such as Line.

信息是一种分享私人观点和情感的亲密媒介。正如Facebook旗下WhatsApp的一名用户所言讲，这是一种数字形式的鸡尾酒会窃窃私语。现在，一些世界上最大的品牌正冒险进入这个个人领域。意识到呼叫中心和电子邮件等传统通信渠道的局限性，公司在几年前就开始使用WhatsApp及其姊妹应用Facebook的Messenger、苹果的iMessage和Line这样的独立运作的应用。

The pandemic gave all such apps a fillip. Messaging on Instagram, Facebook’s photo-sharing app, and on Messenger rose by 40%. Four-fifths of mobile-device time is now spent on chat apps. Companies can usually be relied upon to go where customers are, so messaging has become vital for business, not just experimental, says Javier Mata, founder of Yalo, a startup whose technology connects firms to messaging platforms. Firms once used them chiefly for customer service. Now they want to get people to buy stuff via chat, as hundreds of millions of Chinese do on WeChat, owned by Tencent, China’s mightiest tech giant.

fillip 推动

to rely on or upon sb/sth 信任某人/某事物

疫情推动了所有这些应用程序的发展。在Facebook的照片分享应用Instagram和Messenger平台上的消息发送量增长了40%。如今，五分之四的移动设备时间花在聊天应用上。Yalo（一家用技术将公司与信息平台连接起来的初创公司）的创始人Javier Mata说，公司通常可以知道客户在哪里，因此信息已经成为商业的关键，而不仅仅是处于试验性的。公司曾经主要将“信息”用于客户服务。现在他们想让人们通过聊天来购物，就像数亿中国人在中国最强大的科技巨头腾讯旗下的微信上做的那样。

Because many popular messaging platforms are encrypted, data on transactions are hard to come by. But growth is undoubtedly happening. Over 1bn people now interact with businesses via chat, not counting China. Each day 175m people send a message to WhatsApp business accounts (WhatsApp channels designed for companies). Yalo’s customers include consumer-goods giants such as Coca-Cola, Nestlé, PepsiCo and Unilever, as well as Walmart, the world’s biggest retailer. Apple Business Chat, which started in 2017, is used by Home Depot DIY stores, Hilton hotels and Burberry, a fashion brand. Facebook’s roster includes Sephora, a cosmetics retailer, and IKEA, a furniture giant. LVMH, a French luxury-goods conglomerate, is testing out messaging, according to Jeroen van Glabbeek, chief executive of CM.com, a Dutch conversational-commerce platform.

由于许多流行的消息平台都是加密的，交易数据很难获取。但毋庸置疑的是增长正在发生。不包括中国在内，现在有超过10亿人通过聊天与洽谈业务。每天有1.75亿人向WhatsApp的企业账号(WhatsApp的频道是为企业设计的)发送消息。Yalo的客户包括消费品巨头，如可口可乐，雀巢，百事可乐和联合利华，以及世界最大的零售商沃尔玛。苹果企业聊天始于2017年，家得宝DIY店、希尔顿酒店和时尚品牌巴宝莉都在使用它。Facebook的企业聊天软件的使用者有化妆品零售商丝芙兰和家具巨头宜家。据荷兰会话商务平台CM.com的首席执行官Jeroen van Glabbeek称，法国奢侈品集团LVMH正在测试即时消息功能。

“C-commerce” is already entrenched in Asia and Latin America, where spotty access to broadband and high-quality devices puts e-commerce and company-specific apps out of reach for many. Now Western consumers are beginning to embrace the ease, speed, personalisation and convenience of messaging. For firms, the return on investment seems higher for messaging than for call-centre exchanges or email chains, says Emile Litvak, head of business messaging at Facebook.

spotty 不稳定的

“C-commerce”已经在亚洲和拉丁美洲站稳了位置，在这些地区，宽带和高质量设备的接入不稳定，使得电子商务和针对公司的应用程序对许多人来说遥不可及。现在西方消费者开始拥抱便捷、快速、个性化和便利性的信息传递。Facebook的企业通讯主管Emile Litvak说，对公司来说，通讯的投资回报似乎高于用呼叫中心或一串电子邮件交流。

Boosters of business messaging claim that c-commerce will displace e-commerce within a decade or two. But messaging is best understood as a refinement of e-commerce, and a sibling of “social commerce” (shopping on social media). Most messaging conversations between large firms and consumers start from corporate e-commerce websites equipped with a “click to message” button. Plenty begin on social networks.

企业信息通讯的支持者声称 c-commerce 将在10年或20年内取代电子商务。但人们最好将信息通讯理解为电子商务的改良，是“社交商务”(在社交媒体上购物)的兄弟。大多数大公司和消费者之间的信息通讯都是从配备了“点击发送信息”按钮的企业电子商务网站开始的。很多都是从社交网络开始的。

In some ways c-commerce is a throwback to the past. Apart from mail order and its modern guise, online shopping, trade has relied on conversation for millennia. Yet business messaging does have new elements. It is more personalised than SMS marketing, which has itself had success in recent years in America and Europe. Automatic messaging is moving beyond rudimentary chatbots, which have been around since the mid-2010s. Artificial intelligence (AI) is getting better at unstructured exchanges that shoppers used to have with expert retail assistants.

在某种程度上， c-commerce倒像是回到了过去。除了邮购和网上购物（邮购在当代的伪装），几千年来贸易一直依赖于会话。然而，企业消息传递确实有新的元素。它比近年来在美国和欧洲取得成功的短信营销更个性化。自动短信正在超越2010年代中期出现的初级聊天机器人。人工智能在非结构化交易（过去购物者是与专业销售员交易）方面越来越好。

For now, says Marc Lore, who led Walmart’s digital efforts, a lot of business messaging has humans in the loop. In future, he reckons, AI will be able to answer customer requests as fuzzy as “get me a birthday toy for a five-year-old around science education for roughly $40”, suggesting choices and completing the transaction in seconds. And when AI gets better at natural dialogue, as it will after learning from human interactions, consumer-to-business messaging may sound if not exactly like J.A.R.V.I.S, Tony Stark’s digital butler in the Marvel comics, then close enough.

to be in/out of the loop 属圈内/圈外人士

沃尔玛数字化业务的负责人Marc Lore表示，目前，很多企业信息都有专人参与。他估计，未来，人工智能将能够回答客户的模糊请求，比如“大约40美元为我五岁左右的孩子买一个关于科学教育的生日玩具”，能够在几秒钟内给出建议并完成交易。当人工智能在自然对话方面变得更好时(在学习了人类的互动之后就会变得更好)，消费者对企业的信息传递听起来可能不完全像漫威漫画中Tony Stark的数字管家J.A.R.V.I.S，但也足够接近了。

Until that time, firms must tread delicately. Full of family and friends, chat apps are emotional spaces, says Robert Bennett, CEO of Rehab, an agency that helps brands reach consumers digitally. Try to sell someone yoga leggings after an exchange with their mother, he says, and your firm might find itself deleted faster than an ex. But get it right—think gentle reminder of evening meditation from a purveyor of herbal teas—and the rewards look tasty. ■

在此之前，公司必须小心行事。Rehab（一家帮助品牌通过数字方式接触消费者的机构）的首席执行官Robert Bennett说，聊天应用程序是情感空间，那里充斥着家人和朋友。他说，如果你试着在某人和他的母亲交换信息后向他出售瑜伽紧身裤，你的公司可能会发现它会比你前任删除你的速度更快。手段要正确，但如果你做对了，试想草药茶供应商温和提醒晚间冥想，这样的回报看起来会相当不错。■

Covid-19 deaths in Russia are soaring

俄罗斯covid-19死亡人数飙升

How not to do it

![image-20210713101038369](/Users/chenyujie/Library/Application Support/typora-user-images/image-20210713101038369.png)

Hundreds of thousands are dead, partly thanks to the Kremlin’s incompetence

成千上万的人死亡，部分是由于克里姆林宫(指俄罗斯政府)的无能

Jul 10th 2021

FOR THE past year the Kremlin has trumpeted its success in fighting covid-19. It eschewed lockdowns and hailed its home-grown Sputnik V vaccine. In early June, Vladimir Putin, the Russian president, boasted to thousands of guests at the St Petersburg International Economic Forum, billed as the world’s largest post-pandemic international gathering, that “the current situation in Russia allows us to hold such events without any particular risk of spreading the infection.”

eschew 戒绝；避开

过去一年，克里姆林宫一直在大肆宣传抗击疫情取得了成功。它避免了封锁，并大肆宣传其国产的Sputnik V疫苗。6月初，俄罗斯总统普京在圣彼得堡国际经济论坛（被称为全球疫情爆发后规模最大的国际会议）上向数千名来宾吹嘘说，“俄罗斯目前的形势允许我们举办此类活动，且不存在传播感染的特殊风险。”

![image-20210713101015235](/Users/chenyujie/Library/Application Support/typora-user-images/image-20210713101015235.png)

In fact, by the time Mr Putin was addressing his powwow, Russian doctors had for several weeks been registering an alarming rise in infections and deaths. A month later, Russia is in the midst of its third and most severe wave of covid-19, with more people dying daily than at any point during the pandemic (see chart).

powwow 讨论会

事实上，在普京发表演讲时，俄罗斯医生数周以来记录的感染和死亡人数已经出现了惊人的增长。一个月后，俄罗斯正处于第三次也是最严重的covid-19浪潮中，每天死亡的人数比疫情期间的任何时候都多(见图表)。

The number of new daily cases is currently around 25,000, somewhat fewer than in Britain, and rising. But whereas in Britain this surge has translated into an average of 18 daily deaths over the past week, in Russia it has resulted in an average of 670 deaths a day.

目前每日新增病例约为2.5万例，略低于英国，但还在上涨。但在英国，过去一周平均每天死亡人数为18人，而在俄罗斯，平均每天死亡人数为670人。

The contrast is all the more striking because Russia was the first country in the world to approve a working vaccine, one based on the same science as the British-Swedish AstraZeneca one and apparently just as effective. But whereas in Britain 78% of the population has received at least one jab, in Russia the proportion is only 20%. The difference is not the availability or the efficacy of the jab, but people’s trust in the government and its vaccines.

这种对比更加引人注目，因为俄罗斯是世界上第一个批准有效疫苗的国家，这种疫苗与英国-瑞典联合生产的阿斯利康的科学原理相同，而且显然同样有效。但在英国78%的人口至少注射过一次疫苗，而在俄罗斯这一比例只有20%。这一差异不在于疫苗的可获得性或有效性，而在于人们对政府及其疫苗的信任度。

All of this could have been avoided. A year ago the government decided to lift a partial lockdown (Mr Putin called it “a holiday”), hoping to save itself money and to prop up the president’s faltering popularity after a prolonged slump in incomes. Mr Putin’s ratings did go back up—but so did the risk of infection.

prop up 撑起;扶起

popularity 受欢迎

这一切本来是可以避免的。一年前，政府决定取消部分封锁(普京称之为“假日”)，希望能为自己省钱，并在收入长期下滑后支撑总统摇摇欲坠的支持率。普京的支持率确实上升了，但感染的风险也上升了。

As the second wave hit Europe in late autumn last year, the Kremlin chose not to spend money on supporting people and businesses through a new lockdown. It left people to their own devices, playing down the risks, with Mr Putin boasting about the contrast between lifeless European cities and Moscow, where restaurants, theatres and shops stayed open.

to leave sb to her/his own devices 听任某人自便

play down 降低..的重要性；淡化

去年秋末，第二波疫情袭击了欧洲，俄罗斯政府为了不把钱花在支持个人和企业上，从而又采取了新的封锁措施。它让人们自己看着办，从而淡化风险，普京吹嘘了毫无生气的欧洲城市与莫斯科（其餐馆、剧院和商店都开门营业）之间的反差。

But the numbers lied. By February 2021 Russia had one of the world’s highest excess mortality rates, according to numbers compiled by The Economist. At that point it had registered 460,000 more deaths than normal, while its official covid-19 death toll was a more modest 85,000. But by in effect removing all restrictions and fiddling the numbers, the government managed to create a false sense of security.

超额死亡率即死亡率的升高超过一定的正常临界水平

fiddle 虚报

但（俄罗斯政府）在数字上撒谎了。根据《经济学人》汇编的数据，到2021年2月，俄罗斯是世界上超额死亡率国家中最高的国家之一。当时其登记在册的死亡人数比正常情况多出46万人，而官方公布的covid-19死亡人数为8.5万人。但实际上，政府取消了所有限制，并篡改了数字，从而制造了一种虚假的安全感。

This was just when people should have been urged to get vaccinated, says Denis Volkov of the Levada Centre, an independent pollster. Despite the staggering true number of deaths, in February his polling found that 57% of people were not worried about catching the virus. Most worryingly, two-thirds of the country rejected the idea of getting vaccinated.

stagger 使震惊

Levada中心的独立民意测验专家Denis Volkov说，这正是人们应该呗敦促接种疫苗的时候。尽管真实的死亡人数令人震惊，但在2月份他的民意调查中发现57%的人并不担心感染病毒。最令人担忧的是，全国三分之二的人拒绝接种疫苗。

The government, meanwhile, had other priorities, such as jailing Alexei Navalny, the most prominent opposition figure, and crushing street protests. This is where social-distancing rules came in handy. Mr Navalny’s associates were put under house arrest for “urging the public to violate epidemiological restrictions”. At the same time Mr Putin packed 80,000 people into the Luzhniki stadium in Moscow for a ceremony to mark the seventh anniversary of the annexation of Crimea.

to come in handy 派得上用场

annexation 吞并

期间，政府有其他优先事项，比如监禁最著名的反对派人物Alexei Navalny，以及镇压街头抗议活动。这就是社交距离规则派上用场的地方。Navalny的助手因“敦促公众违反流行病学限制规定”而被软禁了。与此同时，普京将8万人聚集在莫斯科的卢日尼基体育场，举行了纪念克里米亚被吞并七周年的仪式。

Many Russians concluded that the rules were merely a government ploy, to be evaded like so many of its prescriptions. They took a similarly dismissive view of the Sputnik V vaccine after its launch, with much fanfare, in early December.

ploy 策略

be dismissive of sb/sth; 对某人/某物不屑一顾

fanfare 号角齐鸣

许多俄罗斯人得出结论，这些规定只不过是政府的一种伎俩，就像政府的许多规定一样，应该加以规避。在Sputnik V疫苗于12月初大张旗鼓地推出后，他们也对其持类似的轻视态度。

Sputnik V was authorised before the results of its phase-3 clinical trials were ready. Once these were published, questions were raised about some inconsistencies and data availability, says Boris Reizis, a professor of immunology at New York University’s Grossman School of Medicine. Still, he says, the data suggest that Sputnik V is at least as safe and effective as other adenovirus-based vaccines, such as those made by AstraZeneca and Johnson & Johnson. But the damage had been done.

Sputnik V在其第三阶段临床试验结果准备好之前就被批准了。纽约大学格罗斯曼医学院免疫学教授Boris Reizis说，一旦这些研究结果发表，人们就会提出（研究结果）有一些前后不一致和数据可用性的问题。尽管如此，他说，数据表明Sputnik V至少和其他腺病毒疫苗一样安全有效，比如阿斯利康和强生公司生产的疫苗。但是伤害已经造成了。

To be sure, confidence in vaccines is fragile everywhere. But in Russia nobody has done more to undermine such confidence than the Kremlin and its loyal, conspiracy-peddling media. Mr Putin has hijacked Sputnik V for the purpose of geopolitical point-scoring. Yet he dithered with his own vaccination until the end of March and refused to be photographed being injected, despite his usual penchant for bare-chested publicity stunts. He has also suggested that vaccines from other countries are unsafe.

hijack 把持，控制，使用（本不属于自己的东西）

可以肯定的是，全世界对疫苗的信心都很脆弱。但是在俄罗斯，克里姆林宫和它忠诚的、兜售阴谋论的媒体最能破坏这种人们对疫苗的信心。普京使用“Sputnik V”是为了在地缘政治上得分。然而，直到三月底，他还在犹豫是否要自己注射疫苗，并且拒绝拍摄注射疫苗的照片，尽管他酷爱在公众面前坦胸露背。他还表示，来自其他国家的疫苗是不安全的。

That has done little to reassure the many Russians who expect their government to lie to them. They have good reason: it turns out that some people who were told they were getting Sputnik V were in fact given EpiVacCorona, another Russian vaccine praised by Mr Putin, but about which there are serious concerns. Hardly any countries have approved it.

这并没有让许多俄罗斯人放心，他们猜想政府会对他们撒谎。他们有很好的理由:事实证明，一些被告知接种了Sputnik V的人实际上注射的是EpiVacCorona疫苗（普京赞颂的另一种俄罗斯疫苗，但人们对此存在严重的担忧）。几乎没有任何国家批准它。

Failing to win trust, Russian authorities are resorting to sticks and carrots. In Moscow, restaurants and cafés are allowed to serve people indoors only if they have a QR code proving they have been vaccinated. Hospitals refuse routine treatment to anyone without a jab. Public-sector and service workers have been ordered to get vaccinated. But although more people are now signing up for jabs, there is a side-effect: a thriving black market for fake vaccination certificates, QR codes and medical exemptions. None of this bodes well for hospitals across the country, as the numbers continue to mount. ■

俄罗斯当局未能赢得信任，只好采取威逼利诱的手段。在莫斯科，餐馆和cafés只有在有二维码证明他们接种过疫苗的情况下才允许在室内为人们服务。医院拒绝对没有注射疫苗的人进行常规治疗。公共部门和服务业的工作人员被要求接种疫苗。但是，尽管越来越多的人预约接种疫苗，但仍有一个副作用:一个繁荣的贩卖假疫苗接种证明、二维码和医疗豁免的黑市诞生了。这些对全国的医院来说都不是好兆头，因为数字还在继续上升。■

South Korean tech workers are having a lousy time at work

lousy 非常糟糕的

韩国科技行业员工的工作时间很糟糕

(; —,_—)

![image-20210712100100440](/Users/chenyujie/Library/Application Support/typora-user-images/image-20210712100100440.png)

New internet firms suffer from many of the same problems as old corporate giants

新型互联网公司面临着许多与老的巨头公司相同的问题

Jul 10th 2021

WHEN PARK HAE-RYUNG started her job at a big internet company near Seoul, South Korea’s capital, she had high expectations. “They promise you flat hierarchies, flexible work and a young, innovative workplace,” says the 26-year-old graduate. “But what actually happens is that they give you tons of work without telling you how to do it. There’s no structure and no communication. And you can’t complain to HR because the person in charge probably went to school with your boss.” Ms Park (not her real name) is planning to quit as soon as she can. She says many of her colleagues feel the same.

当PARK HAE-RYUNG开始在韩国首都首尔附近的一家大型互联网公司工作时，她抱有很高的期望。这位26岁的毕业生表示:“他们向你承诺扁平化的等级制度、灵活的工作和一个年轻、创新的工作场所。但实际是，他们给了你大量的工作却没有告诉你如何去做。这里没有组织，也没有交流。你也不能向HR抱怨，因为那个负责人可能是你老板的同学。” Park(化名)正计划尽快辞职。她说她的很多同事都有同样的感受。

South Korea’s work culture is notoriously punishing. Its people work some of the longest hours in the OECD, a club mostly of rich countries. Office-goers have scant control over their time and little chance to escape from bad management. They have long complained of gapjil, the authoritarian attitude of senior managers who abuse their power to shout at underlings, insist on unpaid all-nighters and weekend work, assign personal errands, and force juniors to go out drinking for hours upon hours.

gapjil: 指上级对下属滥用权力

underling 走卒

errand 差事

韩国的职场文化是出了名的严苛。在经合组织(一个主要由发达国家组成的俱乐部)中，韩国人的工作时间是最长的。办公室职员很难控制自己的时间，也很少有机会摆脱糟糕的管理。长期以来，他们一直在抱怨gapjil，gapjil指的是高管的专制态度，他们滥用权力对下属大喊大叫，坚持无薪通宵和周末加班，分配私人差事，还强迫下级出去喝上几个小时的酒。

perk 补贴；好处

There are beanbags.

forerunner 先驱

meritocratic 精英领导的

idyll 田园诗；良辰美景

相比之下，相对较新的公司，如Naver(搜索引擎)，Kakao(即时通讯)和Coupang(电子商务)，以及许多小型创业公司，都以他们在美国硅谷的先驱为榜样。它们承诺了一种精英领导的田园式生活，在这里，员工们相互称呼时使用的是自己起的英文名，而不是他们的工作头衔;员工作为独立个体被重视，并且可以根据自己的想法而非基于在办公桌上的时间被评判[应该是想表达不是要按照工龄来去被评判，并不是老员工就好，而是基于个人提出的观点好坏]。这里还有豆袋椅。

Yet a string of incidents in recent months suggests that South Korean workplaces—and bosses—are proving hard to change. In February workers at Kakao took to Blind, an app that allows verified employees to whinge anonymously, to lambast their company’s peer-rating system, which they said encouraged personal attacks and poisoned the atmosphere. whinge 絮絮叨叨地抱怨

Coupang, the country’s biggest delivery service, which has long been criticised for the conditions endured by its logistics workers, faced a consumer boycott in June after a blaze at one of its warehouses killed a firefighter. The company promised to overhaul its management structure. Kakao launched a consultation process for unhappy employees. Krafton doled out extra holidays and said it would look into whether employees were working too much.

The software engineers of the future will, themselves, be software

未来的软件工程师将是软件自己

Jul 7th 2021

GPT-3 IS QUITE a beast. The Generative Pre-Trained Transformer 3, to give its full name, is a language model developed by OpenAI, a part-commercial, part not-for-profit artificial-intelligence (AI) laboratory in San Francisco. GPT-3 was trained on an unprecedented mass of text to teach it the probability that a given word will follow preceding words. When fed a short text “prompt”, it cranks out astonishingly coherent prose written in a similar style.

crank out 机械地完成

GPT-3是个猛兽。它的全称是“生成式预训练Transformer3”，是由OpenAI开发的一种语言模型。OpenAI是旧金山的一家半商业、半非盈利的人工智能实验室。GPT-3模型在空前的海量文本上训练，目的是教会模型能够给定一个单词，输出下一个单词的概率。如果给它一个简短的“提示”，它就能以相似的风格写出令人惊讶的连贯的散文。

Access to GPT-3 is restricted. For one thing, says Jack Clark, former head of policy at the organisation, it might otherwise be used to mass produce fake news or flood social media with “trolling and griefing” messages. But OpenAI also knows that GPT-3 is commercially valuable. Last year the laboratory started letting vetted firms buy its output for approved uses. These include producing answers to typed questions about products, and powering the speech of fictional characters in virtual worlds. But perhaps most important, GPT-3 can also be used to write computer code.

flood 使…充斥

troll 挑衅帖子；挑衅邮件

vet 审查

GPT-3的使用受限。该机构前政策主管Jack Clark表示，首先，它可能被用于大规模制造假新闻，或使社交媒体上充斥着“恶意攻击和悲伤”信息。但OpenAI也知道GPT-3具有商业价值。去年，该实验室开始允许经过审查的公司购买其产品以用于批准用途。已批准用途包括回答有关产品的键入问题，以及在虚拟世界中为虚拟人物的言语提供驱动。但也许最重要的是，GPT-3也可以用于编写计算机代码。

Several firms are already using GPT-3 and its predecessor GPT-2 to add AI to the software that their programmers use to write code. Much of what these programmers type out has already been written elsewhere at some point in the past. This means that by feeding oodles of pre-existing code into such packages, they can be trained to predict the lines a programmer needs next. As a programmer types, potential “code completions” of one or a few lines pop up on the screen.

oodles 大量

有几家公司已经在使用GPT-3及其前身GPT-2，将AI添加到他们的程序员用来编写代码的软件中。这些程序员输入的大部分内容在过去的某个时候已经在别处编写过了。这意味着，通过向这样的包中输入大量已存在的代码，可以训练它们去预测程序员接下来需要的代码行。当程序员写代码时，可能的一行或几行“代码补全”会在屏幕上弹出。

Predict and provide

One company that has created such an AI-completion feature is Tabnine, of Tel Aviv. Tabnine used GPT-2 to feed so much code to its programming software, also named Tabnine, that this software gained a sort of “world knowledge”, says Eran Yahav, the firm’s top technologist. Dr Yahav describes this as “a pretty good notion of how the world behaves”, at least when it comes to programming-speak. Tabnine software may detect that a user has begun to type code to handle, say, purchase orders. It will then suggest code to display product names and prices, as well as code to create fields to be filled with quantities, payment and delivery data. It works even though Tabnine has never been specifically instructed to do that.

特拉维夫的Tabnine公司就创造了这种“AI补全”功能。Tabnine公司的顶尖技术专家Eran Yahav说，它使用GPT-2为它的编程软件(也叫Tabnine)注入了大量代码，以至于这个软件获得了某种“世界知识”。Yahav博士将其描述为“关于世界如何运转的一个很好的概念”，至少在编程语言中是这样。Tabnine软件可以检测到用户已经开始输入代码来处理，比如说，购买订单这项功能。然后，它将推荐“显示产品名称和价格的”代码，以及推荐“用于创建填充数量、支付和交付数据的字段的”代码。尽管从未有人专门指导Tabnine这么做，但它还是很有效的。

Some coding sequences are rare. In these cases, Tabnine lengthens its pop-up list of suggested completions to increase the likelihood of offering a useful one. By clicking on one that is appropriate, the programmer teaches Tabnine to perform better. Tabnine’s professional version seems “almost intelligent” in its ability to understand a programmer’s intent, according to Dror Weiss, the firm’s boss.

有些编码序列很少见。在这些情况下，Tabnine会延长其弹出的“建议补全”列表，以增加提供有用补全代码的可能性。通过点击一个合适的补全代码，程序员可以让Tabnine表现得更好。Tabnine公司的老板Dror Weiss说，它的专业版本在理解程序员意图方面似乎“近乎智能”。

Tabnine is not alone. On June 17th Microsoft, an American software giant, released a new version of an AI-completion feature which it embeds in coding software called Visual Studio. The original version, released in 2018 and named IntelliCode, was trained on a few thousand online repositories in which code for programming projects is stored. Microsoft trained its upgraded system on more than half a million such repositories. Amanda Silver, one of the executives in charge of Visual Studio, says these extra heaps of training fodder allow the new version to glean intent better from hints in code that a programmer has already written.

fodder 素材

Tabnine并非个例。6月17日，美国软件巨头微软发布了一个新版本的AI补全功能，该功能嵌入到名为Visual Studio的编码软件中。最初的版本于2018年发布，名为IntelliCode，它在数千个存储编程项目代码的在线仓库中进行了训练。微软在超过50万个这样的仓库上对其升级后的系统进行了训练。负责Visual Studio的高管之一Amanda Silver表示，这些额外的训练素材使新版本能够更好地从程序员已经编写的代码的提示中收集意图。

The purpose of all this, of course, is to save time. Kite, a firm in San Francisco, claims its AI-completion products cut the number of keystrokes required for some tasks by nearly half. Overall efficiency gains, however, are lower. Vitaly Khudobakhshov, head of AI products at the St Petersburg office of JetBrains, a Czech developer of programming software, sees time savings of 10% to 20%. In the view of Sharif Shameem, the boss of Debuild, a firm in San Francisco that uses GPT-3 to help build websites, the technology also reduces “cognitive overhead”. Selecting from multiple choices is less taxing than devising solutions from scratch.

keystroke 按键

这样做的目的当然是为了节省时间。旧金山的Kite公司声称，其AI补全产品将某些任务所需的按键次数减少了近一半。然而，总体效率的提高较低。捷克编程软件开发商JetBrains圣彼得堡办事处人工智能产品负责人Vitaly Khudobakhshov认为这可以节省10%到20%的时间。在旧金山Debuild公司（该公司使用GPT-3来帮助建网站）的老板Sharif Shameem看来，这项技术也减少了“认知开销”。从多个选项中选择比从头设计解决方案要轻松得多。

Bugs and the system

Nor are those who write code the only beneficiaries. Developers spend nearly as much time searching for bugs in what they have written as they do writing it in the first place. A machine-learning model being built by Brendan Dolan-Gavitt of New York University may speed up the debugging process.

编写代码的人也不是唯一的受益者。开发人员花在寻找所编写内容中的bug上的时间与他们最初编写内容时花费的时间几乎一样多。由纽约大学的Brendan Dolan-Gavitt建立的机器学习模型可能会加快调试过程。

To train it, Dr Dolan-Gavitt is collecting code labelled as buggy by GitHub, a Microsoft subsidiary that hosts the biggest collection of non-proprietary “open source” code in the world. By one estimate, GitHub holds at least a billion snippets of code identified as harbouring a bug. Dr Dolan-Gavitt’s model, provisionally called GPT-CSRC, will devour that code this summer.

provisionally 临时地

为了对其进行训练，Dolan-Gavitt博士正在收集GitHub上标注为buggy的代码。GitHub是微软的一个子公司，拥有世界上最大的非专利“开源”代码集合。据估计，GitHub拥有至少10亿段被认为存在bug的代码。Dolan-Gavitt博士的模型(暂时被称为 GPT-CSRC)今年夏天将“吞食”这一代码【应该是指输入这10亿段代码用于训练】。

Another bug-spotting model is in development at the Massachusetts Institute of Technology (MIT). Shashank Srikant, a PhD student working on the project, says the goal is to train the model to recognise not just inadvertent bugs, but also maliciously inserted vulnerabilities. Rogue employees are sometimes behind trickery of this sort, which is intended to do things like secretly gain access to passwords. The practice is most common, however, in open-source programming projects to which anyone can contribute. Human reviewers typically struggle to spot these “vulnerability injections”, as they are sometimes known.

inadvertent 无意的

maliciously 怀有恶意地

麻省理工学院(MIT)正在开发另一种发现bug的模型。参与该项目的博士生Shashank Srikant说，目标是训练该模型不仅能识别无意的漏洞，还能识别恶意插入的漏洞。胡作非为的员工有时是这类诡计的幕后黑手，他们的目的是做一些事情，比如秘密获取密码。然而，这种做法在任何人都可以贡献的开源编程项目中最为常见。正如人们有时所知道的那样，人类审查人员通常很难发现这些“漏洞注入”。

The reason, Mr Srikant says, is that, in a bid to slip their handiwork past reviewers, devious coders often use deceptive but purely cosmetic names for things like the variables handled by a program. The team at MIT is therefore training its model to flag discrepancies between snippets’ labels and their actual functionality. The difficulty is that good examples of such mischief are much rarer than ordinary errors.

handiwork 所作所为

Srikant说，其原因是，为了不让审查人员看到他们的所作所为，狡猾的程序员经常使用欺骗性但纯粹是装饰性的名称，比如程序处理的变量。因此，麻省理工学院的团队正在训练其模型，以标记“代码片段标签与其实际功能之间的”差异。困难点在于这种有恶意的好样本比普通错误要少得多。

There is, however, an additional sign that a vulnerability injection may be lurking. Malicious coders often conceal these by writing superfluous code intended to throw off reviewers, so Mr Srikant is also feeding MIT’s model with examples of this type of potentially telltale code, which he describes as “dangling” and “dead”.

telltale 泄露秘密的

然而，还有一个迹象表明，可能潜伏着漏洞注入。恶意的程序员经常通过编写多余的代码来掩盖这些漏洞，以避开审查人员，因此Srikant先生也为MIT的模型提供了这种可能泄露秘密的代码的样本，他把这种代码描述为“悬而未决”和“死亡”。

The clear destination of all this activity is the creation of software programmers which can, like the human variety, take an idea and turn it into code. An inkling of things to come is provided by a website created by Dr Dolan-Gavitt. Named “This Code Does Not Exist”, it asks programmers to determine if sections of code dozens of lines long were written by a human or a model based on GPT-2 that he has built. Of more than 329,200 assessments made, less than 51% have been correct. That is only a shade better than random.

inkling 粗浅认识

所有这些活动的明确目标是创造软件程序员，它们可以像人类一样，把一个想法转化为代码。Dolan-Gavitt博士创建的一个网站提供了一些即将发生的事情的粗浅认识。该程序名为“此代码不存在”，要求程序员确定一段几十行长的代码是由人类编写的，还是由他建立的基于GPT-2的模型编写的。在329,200多份评估中，只有不到51%是正确的。那只是比随机的好一点。

Machines, it turns out, are now able to write even longish sequences of functioning code. As John Carmack, a noted American computer engineer, has tweeted, pondering this development “does generate a slight shiver”. Unsurprisingly, a number of firms see an opportunity.

事实证明，机器现在甚至可以编写较长的运行代码序列。正如著名的美国计算机工程师John Carmack在推特上所说的那样，他认为这种发展“确实会让人微微颤抖”。不出所料，许多公司看到了机会。

One is a Parisian firm called SourceAI. It is designing software into which users type, in natural language, a request for code—such as something that will work out the value of numbers in a mathematical formula called the Fibonacci sequence. By tapping into GPT-3, SourceAI’s eponymous software churns out the desired lines of code in a range of programming languages.

eponymous 同名的

其中一家是名为SourceAI的巴黎公司。它正在设计一种软件，让用户用自然语言输入对代码的要求，比如在一个叫做斐波那契数列的数学公式中计算数字的值。通过使用GPT-3, SourceAI的同名软件可以用一系列编程语言生成所需的代码行。

Debuild is testing the same idea. It is trying to create software that lets non-programmers describe, in plain English, a program they want to create, and will then write it**. A request** for, say, a barbershop app that lets patrons choose a barber and an appointment slot can already produce more or less just that. Mr Shameem says the goal is to sweep away the minutiae of code-typing, so that people can focus on what they want done, not how to instruct computers to do it.

Debuild正在尝试同样的想法。它试图创建一种软件，让非程序员用简单的英语描述他们想要创建的程序，然后将其编写出来。比如说，有这样一个要求“一个可以让顾客选择理发师和预约时段的理发店应用程序”已经或多或少产生了这样的效果。Shameem先生说，其目标是扫除代码输入的细枝大节，这样人们就可以专注于他们想要做的事情，而不是如何指示计算机去做。

For its part, Microsoft is also using GPT-3 to power what it calls “no code/low code” programming. Charles Lamanna, who leads the work, envisages a bright future of cheaper software created by untrained “citizen developers”. Some folk fear an alternative, darker outcome. Might AIs eventually write whatever code they fancy running? No such runaway feedback loop is around the corner. But that mainstay of science fiction does now appear a little less far-fetched. ■

runaway 失控的

mainstay 主要部分；支柱

就微软而言，它也在使用GPT-3来支持它所谓的“无代码/少量代码”编程。领导这项工作的Charles Lamanna设想了一个光明的未来，即由未经训练的“公民开发人员”开发出更便宜的软件。一些人担心另一种更黑暗的结局。AI最终会不会写出他们想要运行的代码? 没有这种失控的反馈循环指日可待。但现在看来，这种科幻小说的支柱似乎不那么牵强了。■

给联邦贸易委员会说句公道话，解释数字市场是非常棘手的。和Facebook一样，大多数社交媒体公司都不向用户收费，所以只看一个行业的消费者衍生销售的典型方法是没有用的。Facebook确实有付费客户，也就是那些在其平台上购买广告的公司，但这个市场的范围也很模糊。据《经济学人》的估计，如果算上美国所有的在线广告，其份额为25%。仅看社交媒体广告，它在美国的份额确实上升到了60%(尽管Facebook在全球的份额正在下降)。但是，随着新功能和竞争对手的出现和消失，什么才算是社交媒体并无定形。

![image-20210706100043161](/Users/chenyujie/Library/Application Support/typora-user-images/image-20210706100043161.png)

The judge conceded that Facebook has market power (“no one who hears the title of the 2010 film ‘The Social Network’ wonders which company it is about”) and he has given the FTC 30 days to show this more precisely. However, he also threw out one of the agency’s core claims. The FTC accused Facebook of stifling competition by blocking rivals from its platform. According to Supreme Court precedents, the judge pointed out, such conduct is legal: monopolists have no “duty to deal”.

stifle 扼杀；阻止

法官承认Facebook具有市场影响力(“大家也都知道2010年电影《社交网络》讲的是关于哪家公司的”)，他给了联邦贸易委员会30天的时间来更准确地展示这一点。然而，他也否定了该机构的一项核心主张。美国联邦贸易委员会（FTC）指责Facebook在其平台上封杀竞争对手，扼杀竞争。法官指出，根据最高法院的判例，这种行为是合法的:垄断者没有“交易义务”。

That may make sense in the analogue world. Critics like Ms Khan argue that in the digital one, where dominant platforms look a lot like pipe-owning utilities, it amounts to a licence to kill competition. If more cases against big tech stumble—as may happen to those involving Apple and Google—that would lend weight to demands to reform antitrust laws. Even this may not be enough to get any of the six bills, or anything like them, passed by the gridlocked Senate. Despite a bipartisan consensus in Washington that big tech is too powerful, Democrats and Republicans are unlikely to agree on the details of what to do about it. ■

这在模拟世界中可能是有意义的。像Khan女士这样的批评者认为，在数字领域，占主导地位的平台看起来很像拥有管道的公用事业，这相当于一张扼杀竞争的许可证。如果更多针对大型科技公司的案件败诉，就像可能发生在涉及苹果和谷歌的案件中那样，这将加大要求改革反垄断法的力度。即便如此，这也不足以让陷入僵局的参议院通过这六项法案中的任何一项，或者类似的任何一项。尽管两党一致认为大型科技公司过于强大，但民主党和共和党不太可能就如何应对这些问题的细节达成一致。■

The perils of PR

公关的危害

Bartleby

巴托比

![image-20210705095743173](/Users/chenyujie/Library/Application Support/typora-user-images/image-20210705095743173.png)

Companies can spend a fortune on their image

公司可以在他们的形象上花一大笔钱

Jul 3rd 2021

SOME DECADES ago Bartleby was covering the results of a company that was then in the FTSE 100 index. He was ushered into the offices of the firm’s public-relations outfit, whereupon the smooth-talking PR man (still a titan of the industry today) launched into a ten-minute monologue about the company’s strategy. At that point a subordinate popped his head around the door and the PR man was called away. “Thank goodness he’s gone,” said the chief executive. “Now I can tell you what is really happening.”

pop 突然探出

call away 把..叫走

几十年前，Bartleby曾报道一家当时是富时100指数成份股的公司的业绩。他被领进了公司公关部门的办公室，随后，健谈的公关人员(至今仍是该行业的巨头)开始了一段10分钟的关于公司战略的独白。就在这时，一位下属从门口探出头来，那位公关被叫走了。首席执行官说：“谢天谢地，他走了，现在我可以告诉你到底发生了什么。”

Any big business may need a team to handle its public image and to deal with the “reptiles” of the press, as Denis Thatcher dubbed them. (This extends to PR firms: this week reports of inappropriate behaviour forced the boss of a big one, Teneo, to resign.) But once you start employing PR people, it can be difficult to stop. In a variant of Parkinson’s law, “PR expands to fill the budget available.” Companies can hire an in-house team while also choosing to use an external PR firm. And if bad news strikes, bosses often want to call in a firm that specialises in “crisis management”.

reptile 爬行动物；卑鄙小人

任何大企业可能都需要一个团队来处理其公众形象，并与Denis Thatcher称之为“卑鄙小人”的媒体打交道。(这也延伸到了公关公司:本周有关不当行为的报道迫使一家大公司的老板Teneo辞职)但一旦你开始雇佣公关人员，就很难停下来了。帕金森定律的一个变体是:“公关会扩大规模，以填补可用预算。”公司可以聘请内部团队，也可以选择聘请外部公关公司。如果坏消息来袭，老板们往往想找一家专门从事“危机管理”的公司。

A company’s aim is to frame the narrative in a favourable manner. Any firm wants to be seen not as a money-grubbing corporation that pollutes the environment and exploits its workers but as an innovative pioneer with sustainable operations and a social conscience. An expert PR executive can hone such a message and identify the best way of communicating it to investors and the wider public, for example by selecting the journalists and publications which will lend it a sympathetic ear.

to lend or give a sympathetic ear 同情地倾听

公司的目标是用一种有利的方式构建叙事。任何公司都不想被看作是一个贪图钱财、污染环境、剥削工人的公司，而是想被认为是一个具有可持续经营和社会良知的创新先锋。一个专业的公关主管可以修饰出这样的信息，并确定向投资者和更广泛的公众传达信息的最佳方式，例如，通过选择能够同情地倾听的记者和出版物来传达信息。

That is the theory, at least. In practice, most journalists’ dealings with PR professionals resemble “groundhog day”. Day 1: PR person sends email about client. Day 2: PR person sends follow-up email to check journalist received earlier missive. Day 3: PR person calls journalist to make absolutely certain that they are aware of the emails’ existence. Day 4: PR sends a fresh email about the same client, and the process begins anew.

groundhog 土拨鼠

missive 信函

至少理论上是这样。实际上，大多数记者与公关人员打交道就像“土拨鼠日”。第一天：公关人员发送关于客户的电子邮件。第二天：公关人员发送后续邮件，以核实记者收到之前的邮件。第三天：公关人员打电话给记者，十分确定他们知道这些电子邮件的存在。第四天：公关部发了一封关于同一个客户的新邮件，整个过程重新开始。

Perhaps this frenetic activity has a use. There is a chance that some publication has a desperate need to fill space, or was looking for a random executive’s views on an issue of the day. But in most cases it only serves to irritate the correspondent who has to deal with the pestering.

pester 打扰

也许这种疯狂的行为是有用的。有可能是一些出版物迫切需要填补版面，或者是在寻找一位高管对某一问题的看法。但在大多数情况下，这只会激怒记者，他们不得不处理这些打扰他们的事情。

The poor office juniors at the PR companies are given the thankless task of chasing emails. More senior staff operate face-to-face, or at least did in the pre-pandemic days. They tend to come in three types. The first is the interventionist mentioned earlier, who pontificates as if they were actually on the board of the company concerned. Friendly on the surface, these PR people tend to get patronising or hostile if the journalist asks an uncomfortable question. An unfavourable piece will be followed up with an angry phone call (Lord X will be very disappointed by your article) or even an attempt to influence the editor.

thankless 吃力不讨好的

pontificate 自负地谈论

patronise 屈尊俯就地对待

Don’t patronize me! 别在我面前摆出高人一等的样子！

公关公司里可怜的初级职员被分配了一项费力不讨好的任务:追踪电子邮件。更多高级工作人员面对面工作，至少在疫情前的日子是这样。它们有三种类型。第一个是前面提到的干预主义者，他们自以为是，就好像他们是相关公司的董事会成员一样。这些公关人员表面上很友好，但如果记者问了一个让人不舒服的问题，他们往往会变得傲慢或怀有敌意。一篇负面的文章之后，会有一个愤怒的电话(X勋爵会对你的文章感到非常失望)，甚至试图影响编辑。

The second PR type is much more discreet. Some issue the minimal amount of information as part of a deliberate policy to keep their clients out of the headlines. Others are keen on publicity but maintain a Trappist-like silence in meetings, content only to take notes and enjoy expensive lunches while the client talks. Apart from inflating the revenues of the restaurant industry, it can be hard to discern what function this group serves.

第二种公关方式更为谨慎。有些公司为了不让自己的客户成为头条新闻，故意发布了最少的信息。还有一些人热衷于宣传，但在会议中保持特拉普主义者式的沉默，内容只是做笔记，在客户谈话时享用昂贵的午餐。除了增加了餐饮业的收入外，很难看出这群人的作用是什么。

There is a third group. Some PR people supply useful facts about the company when asked, give an accurate steer on whether market rumours are true, and arrange an interview with the chief executive when required. These helpful PR people are scattered unevenly across the corporate sector. It is virtually impossible to predict where they will be found.

还有第三种。当被问及公司情况时，一些公关人员会提供有用的事实，准确判断市场传言是否属实，必要时还会安排与首席执行官面谈。这些有帮助的公关人员在各个企业部门分布不均。几乎不可能预测它们会在哪里被发现。

The existence of these three categories is not the only reason journalists have a love-hate relationship with the PR industry. However irritating PR people can be, they are often one of the only conduits for information about a company. And many hacks in their 30s and 40s opt to join the industry as a way of earning a bigger salary. In a sense, the relationship is an ecosystem, in which both parties regard the other as the parasites.

conduit 渠道

这三种类型的存在并不是记者对公关行业爱恨交加的唯一原因。不管公关人员多么恼人，他们往往是获取公司信息的仅有渠道之一。许多三四十岁的杂物工选择加入这个行业，以此来获得更高的薪水。从某种意义上说，这种关系是一个生态系统，双方都将对方视为寄生虫。

Whether companies need to finance this system is another matter, as the main benefits go to the participants. A lot of PR activity has zero impact on the client’s public profile. It seems like a more extreme version of the famous quote about advertising: three-quarters of the money I spend on public relations is wasted—the problem is knowing which three-quarters.

企业是否需要为这一体系提供资金则是另一回事，因为主要利益属于参与者。许多公关活动对客户的公众形象没有任何影响。这似乎是一则关于广告的名言的更极端版本:我花在公关上的钱有四分之三都浪费了，问题在于知道是哪四分之三。
