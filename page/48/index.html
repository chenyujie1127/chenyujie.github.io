The government wants to boost British startups through immigration

政府希望通过移民促进英国初创企业

Only the best

只有最好的“人才”才行

![image-20211012141759685](/Users/chenyujie/Library/Application Support/typora-user-images/image-20211012141759685.png)

Even as lorry-drivers and chefs are kept out

尽管卡车司机和厨师被拒之门外

PULLING THE lever marked “uncontrolled immigration”, said the prime minister in an interview broadcast as the Conservative Party’s annual conference kicked off on October 3rd, was the wrong way to solve labour shortages. Britain is experiencing many of these at the moment, but employers should not expect to be able to import their way out of them. Instead, he said, haulage firms, poultry farms and abattoirs that lack workers ought to entice Britons into such jobs by training them up and paying them more.

haulage 陆路货运

abattoir 屠宰场

kick off 开始

首相在10月3日保守党年度会议开始时的一次广播采访中说，取消施压手段意味着“移民不受控制”，这是解决劳动力短缺的错误方法。英国目前正经历着许多这样的情况，但雇主们不应指望能够通过引进来摆脱它们。相反，他说，缺乏工人的运输公司、家禽养殖场和屠宰场应该通过培训他们并给他们更高的工资来吸引英国人从事这些工作。

But when it comes to companies that lack computer programmers, the government is far more sympathetic. That is less inconsistent than it may seem. An HGV driver takes between six and ten weeks to train; a competent coder several years. And demand for the latter has soared among British startups. As a result, limited access to foreign talent is a common gripe of tech founders and venture capitalists.

HGV：heavy goods vehicle 重型货车

grip 抱怨

但当涉及到缺乏程序员的公司时，政府就更加同情了。这并不像看上去那样前后矛盾。一名重型货车司机需要6到10周的培训; 一个有能力的程序员需要几年培训时间。英国初创企业对程序员的需求也在飙升。因此，获得外国人才的机会有限，是科技创业者和风投普遍抱怨的问题。

For much of this year, the prospect of looser immigration rules has been dangled in front of them. The government’s budget in March promised a new fast-track visa scheme for skilled migrants who have a job offer from a high-growth British firm. “High-growth”, it later turned out, meant firms that have increased their revenue or staff numbers by 20% a year for three successive years, having started with at least ten employees (so a firm that went from ten to 18 employees in three years would qualify). Such companies would no longer have to sponsor visa applications, meaning they would be spared bureaucracy and fees, and their recently arrived employees would be able to switch jobs more easily.

dangle 用..诱惑

sponsor 做..的保证人

在今年的大部分时间里，放宽移民规定的前景一直在他们面前摇摆不定。政府在3月份的预算中承诺为从一家高速发展的英国公司获得工作机会的技术移民提供一个新的快速通道签证计划。后来证明，“高增长”指的是公司的收入或员工数量连续三年以每年20%的速度增长，且开始时至少有10名员工(因此，一家公司的员工人数在三年内从10人增加到18人将符合条件)。这些公司将不再需要为签证申请提供担保，这意味着它们将免受官僚主义和费用的困扰，而且它们最近来的员工将能够更容易地换工作。

This is not the only scheme intended to attract talented foreigners to Britain. The government’s innovation strategy, published in July, included plans to make all graduates of top global universities eligible for visas, whether or not they hold a job offer. It also intends to make it easier for entrepreneurs elsewhere to move to Britain in order to found their businesses, rather than doing so at home. Political and industry insiders expect the full details of the new schemes to be laid out in the next budget, on October 27th, and for them to be strikingly generous.

strikingly 明显地

这并不是旨在吸引外国人才来英国的唯一计划。政府在7月份公布的创新策略，其中包括计划让全球顶尖大学的所有毕业生都有资格获得签证，无论他们是否获得了工作机会。它还打算让其他地方的企业家更容易搬到英国创业，而不是在国内创业。政治和行业内部人士希望新方案的全部细节能在10月27日的下一次预算中公布，对他们来说，这将是惊人的慷慨。

“Top global university” may end up meaning any institution that appears in the top 50, or even 100, of one of the main rankings for the subject in question. Eligible entrepreneurs are likely to include any with backing from a reasonably credible venture-capital fund. Such measures will, at least initially, increase the competition faced by well-educated Britons for the most coveted jobs. In the longer term, the government hopes that an influx of skilled risk-takers will fuel innovation, investment and, ultimately, job creation.

covet 贪求

I gave up a coveted job. 我放弃了一份别人梦寐以求的工作

influx 大量涌入

“全球顶尖大学”最终可能指的是任意机构只要在其讨论范围内的学科之一，主要排名在前50，甚至前100名。符合条件的企业家可能包括任何有还算可信的风险投资基金支持的人。这些措施将增加受过良好教育的英国人争夺最令人梦寐以求的工作所面临的竞争，至少在初期是这样。从长远来看，政府希望大量有经验的冒险者的涌入将推动创新、投资，并最终创造就业机会。

All of this represents a significant liberalisation, says John Kiely of Fragomen, a law firm specialising in immigration. Although the existing Global Talent Visa allows companies to hire exceptional migrants without sponsoring their visa applications, “it is a programme designed only for the best—not for the good or the very good”. Outside this programme, Fragomen calculates that a single skilled worker entering Britain to work for a large company for three years would incur a total visa cost of £5,681 ($7,706). That compares poorly with Britain’s neighbours: the equivalent fees in France and Germany are €324 ($374) and €175 respectively.

equivalent 相等的

专门从事移民事务的律师事务所Fragomen的John Kiely说，所有这些都代表着一种重大的自由化。尽管现有的全球人才签证允许企业在不为他们的签证申请提供担保的情况下聘用特殊的移民，但“这是一个只为“最好”的人才而设计的项目，既不是为了“好”的人才，也不是为了“非常好”的人才”。除此之外，Fragomen估计，一名技术工人进入英国为一家大公司工作三年，总共需要支付5681英镑(合7706美元)的签证费用。与英国的邻国相比，这一数字很低: 法国和德国的费用分别为324欧元(374美元)和175欧元。

Entrepreneurs seeking to hire will still have some reasons to grumble. The government has remained tight-lipped about how much cheaper the new schemes will be. For cash-strapped startups, that is a substantial caveat. Moreover, many European candidates are hesitant to move to a country that, after Brexit, they perceive as a hostile environment.

tight-lipped 双唇紧闭的；口紧的

cash-strapped 缺钱的

caveat （进一步行动前的）警告，告诫；限制条款

[释义] a warning to consider something before taking any more action, or a statement that limits a more general statement

寻求招聘的企业家仍有一些抱怨的理由。政府对新计划到底会低多少守口如瓶。对于资金紧张的初创公司来说，这是一个很大的限制。此外，许多欧洲候选人不愿移居英国，因为在英国脱欧后，他们认为英国的环境充满敌意。

“When government ministers bash immigrants in the papers to throw red meat to their base, they should remember the people they’re trying to attract can read English,” advises one industry insider. Such rhetoric is also increasingly unnecessary. Since the Brexit vote, immigration has become a lot less politically toxic. “Now we’ve taken back control,” says the insider, “we can let the coders in.” ■

bash 无端指责；猛击

rhetoric 雄辩；虚夸的言辞

一位业内人士建议说:“当政府部长们在报纸上抨击移民，煽动他们的选民的情绪，他们应该记住，他们试图吸引的是会读英语的人。”这种花言巧语也越来越没有必要了。自英国脱欧公投以来，移民在政治上的危害已经小了很多。内部人士说：“现在我们夺回了控制权，我们可以让程序员进来了。”■

Why the latest series of Facebook leaks is more politically potent

为什么最近一系列的Facebook揭发在政治上更有影响力

Whistle while you work ![image-20211011144435054](/Users/chenyujie/Library/Application Support/typora-user-images/image-20211011144435054.png)
Maybe this time is different for big tech

也许对于大型科技公司来说，这次情况有所不同

“YOU ARE a twenty-first century American hero,” Ed Markey, a Democratic senator from Massachusetts, said on October 5th. He was not addressing the founder of one of the country’s largest companies, Facebook, but the woman who found fault with it. Frances Haugen, who previously worked at the social-media giant before becoming a whistleblower, testified in front of a Senate subcommittee for over three hours, highlighting Facebook’s “moral bankruptcy” and the firm’s downplaying of its harmful impact, including fanning teenage depression and ethnic violence.

to find fault with sb/sth 找某人/某事物的错

subcommittee 小组委员会

downplay 对…轻描淡写

fan 激起

马萨诸塞州民主党参议员 Ed Markey 10月5日说“你是21世纪的美国英雄”。他不是在对美国最大的公司之一Facebook的创始人讲话，而是在对找该公司错误的女性讲话。Frances Haugen在成为举报人之前曾在这家社交媒体巨头工作，她在参议院的一个小组委员会前作证了三个多小时，强调Facebook的“道德沦丧”，以及该公司对其有害影响的轻描淡写，包括助长青少年抑郁和种族暴力。

The public has long suspected Facebook of two-faced toxicity but lacked fresh internal communiqués to prove it. That changed when Ms Haugen released a trove of corporate documents to regulators and the Wall Street Journal. Facebook’s own private research, for example, found that its photo-sharing site, Instagram, worsened teens’ suicidal thoughts and eating disorders, yet it still prioritised sending young users engaging content that stoked their anxiety, all while proceeding to develop a version of its site for those under the age of 13. (Facebook has since paused those plans, due to public pressure.)

two-faced 两面派的

stoke 激起；煽起

to proceed to do sth; 接下来做某事

长期以来，公众一直怀疑Facebook是两面派，但缺乏新的内部公报来证明这一点。当Haugen女士向监管机构和《华尔街日报》公布了大量公司文件后，这一情况发生了变化。例如，Facebook自己的私人研究发现，其照片分享网站Instagram使青少年的自杀想法以及饮食失调的情况变得更糟，但它仍然优先向年轻用户发送引发他们焦虑的吸引人的内容，同时继续为13岁以下的青少年开发一个网站版本。(由于公众压力，Facebook后来暂停了这些计划。)

Senators, who cannot agree on such uncontroversial things as paying for the government’s expenses, united against a common enemy and promised Ms Haugen that they would hold Facebook to account. Could that happen at last?

那些在支付政府费用这类没有争议的事情上无法达成一致的参议员们联合起来反对一个共同的敌人（Facebook），并向Haugen女士承诺他们将追究Facebook的责任。这种情况最终会发生吗?

Facebook has survived scandals before. Congress has repeatedly called in tech bosses for angry questioning and public shaming without taking direct action afterward. Congressional hearings “have become like ‘Groundhog Day’ and haven’t really mattered”, is how Paul Gallant of Cowen Washington Research Group, which tracks tech policy, bluntly puts it. In 2018 a different whistleblower outed Facebook for its sketchy collaboration with a research organisation, Cambridge Analytica, which allowed users’ data to be collected without their consent and used for political profiling by Donald Trump’s campaign. Facebook’s founder, Mark Zuckerberg, went to Washington to apologise, and in 2019 America’s consumer-protection agency, the Federal Trade Commission, agreed to a $5bn settlement with Facebook. That is the largest fine ever levied against a tech firm. However, no laws were ever changed to rein in the company’s misbehaviour.

sketchy 粗略的；草草完成的

Facebook以前也经历过丑闻。国会多次召来科技公司的老板进行愤怒的质询和公开羞辱，但事后没有采取直接行动。追踪科技政策的Cowen Washington研究团队的Paul Gallant坦率地说，国会听证会“已经变得像‘土拨鼠日’一样，并没有真正的意义”。2018年，另一名举报者揭露了Facebook与研究机构Cambridge Analytica的粗略合作，Cambridge Analytica允许在未经用户同意的情况下收集用户数据，并用于特朗普的竞选团队的政治分析。Facebook创始人扎克伯格前往华盛顿道歉，2019年，美国消费者保护机构联邦贸易委员会同意与Facebook达成50亿美元的和解。这是有史以来对科技公司开出的最大罚单。然而，没有任何法律被修改来约束该公司的不当行为。

This new whistleblowing incident could be a turning point, says Blair Levin, a former chief-of-staff to the Federal Communications Commission and now of New Street Research, a private company. Social media’s harmful effects on children and teenagers is a concern that transcends partisanship and is easier to understand than sneaky data-gathering, viral misinformation and other social-networking sins.

sneaky 暗中的

美国联邦通讯委员会前参谋长、现任私人公司New Street Research的Blair Levin表示，这次新的举报事件可能是一个转折点。社交媒体对儿童和青少年的有害影响是超越党派偏见的担忧，这比偷偷收集数据、病毒式的错误信息和其他社交网络罪行更容易得知。

If Congress does follow through with legislation, it is likely to focus narrowly on protecting children online, as opposed to broader reforms, for which there is still no political consensus. For example, Congress could update and strengthen the Children’s Online Privacy Protection Act (COPPA), which was passed in 1998 and bars data-collection of children under the age of 13. Some want to extend protections to teenagers over the age of 13, as well. Other legislative proposals take aim at marketing and design features that make social media so addictive for the young.

如果国会真的通过立法，它很可能只专注于保护上网的儿童，而不是更广泛的改革，目前还没有达成政治共识。例如，国会可以更新和加强儿童网络隐私保护法案(COPPA)，该法案于1998年通过，禁止收集13岁以下儿童的数据。一些人还希望将保护措施扩大到13岁以上的青少年。其他立法提案的目标则是营销和设计功能，使社交媒体对年轻人如此上瘾。

However, Ms Haugen’s most significant impact on big tech may be inspiring others to come forward and blow the whistle on their employers’ practices. Whistleblower Aid, a legal support group that has helped Ms Haugen’s case, has seen more inbound calls since her revelations were made public. “A case like this one opens the floodgates and will trigger hundreds more cases,” predicts Steve Kohn, a lawyer who has represented several high-profile whistleblowers. Just look at Swiss banking or pharma to understand the viral spread of whistleblowing, he says. According to Siri Nelson of the National Whistleblower Centre, a legal defence firm, whistleblowers do not just change companies: they “change whole industries.”

to open the floodgates to sb/sth 使某人不受限制地进入/使某事一发不可收拾

然而，Haugen女士对大型科技公司最重要的影响，可能是激励其他人站出来揭发雇主的行为。帮助Haugen案件的法律支持组织“Whistleblower Aid”，自她的爆料被公开以来，接到了更多的来电。Steve Kohn（他是一名律师，曾为几位知名举报者打过官司）预测道“像这样的案件打开了闸门，并将引发更多的案件”。他表示，只要看看瑞士银行业或制药行业，就能明白举报行为的病毒式传播。National Whistleblower Centre(一家法律辩护公司)的Siri Nelson表示，举报人不仅改变了公司，他们还“改变了整个行业”。

There are several factors that make tech ripe for whistleblowing. One is the industry’s culture of flouting rules. Another is a legal framework that makes whistleblowing less intimidating than it used to be. The Dodd-Frank Act, which was enacted in 2010, gives greater protections to whistleblowers by preventing retaliation from employers and by offering monetary rewards to successful cases of up to 30% of the money collected from sanctions against a firm. To date, the Securities and Exchange Commission has paid out $1bn to 207 whistleblowers, including more than $500m in its 2021 fiscal year.

flout 公然藐视

有几个因素使得揭发科技公司的时机成熟。一个是该行业藐视规则的文化。另一个原因是法律框架使得举报不再像以前那样可怕。2010年颁布的多德-弗兰克法案为举报人提供了更大的保护，防止雇主报复，并向成功举报人提供高达制裁公司所得金额30%的奖金。迄今为止，美国证券交易委员会已向207名举报人支付了10亿美元，其中包括在2021财年支付的逾5亿美元。

If the threat of public shaming encourages corporate accountability, that is a good thing. But it could also make tech less inclusive and transparent, predicts Matt Perault, a former Facebook executive who is director of the Centre for Technology Policy at the University of North Carolina at Chapel Hill. People may become less willing to share ideas if they worry about public leaks; companies may become less open with their staff; and executives could start including only a handful of trusted senior staff in meetings that might have otherwise been less restricted.

如果公开羞辱的威胁能鼓励企业问责，那是一件好事。但前Facebook高管、北卡罗来纳大学教堂山分校技术政策中心主任Matt Perault预测，这也可能会降低技术的包容性和透明度。如果人们担心信息泄露，他们可能就不太愿意分享自己的想法;公司对员工可能变得不那么开放; 此外，高管们可能开始只邀请少数值得信赖的高级员工参加原本可能限制较少的会议。

Executives are going to have to get used to more leaks. Nick Clegg, Facebook’s vice-president of policy and global affairs, has defended the company and pushed back against Ms Haugen’s allegations in a memo to the company’s staff, in which he pointed out that last year the company removed 30m posts that violated its policies on terrorism and 19m posts that crossed company lines for inciting hatred. It was barely any time at all before the memo was shared with journalists. Facebook and other big tech firms, which have been criticised for violating people’s privacy online, can no longer count on any privacy either. ■

高管们将不得不习惯更多的泄密。Facebook的政策和全球事务副总裁Nick Clegg为公司辩护且反击Haugen女士对公司的员工的公告中的指控, 在公告中他指出, 去年该公司删除了3000万的违反反恐政策的帖子以及1900万的跨越公司界限煽动仇恨的帖子。几乎没过多久，这份公告就被分享给了记者。Facebook和其他大型科技公司因侵犯人们的网上隐私而受到批评，它们也不能再依赖任何隐私了。■

Facebook is nearing a reputational point of no return

Facebook的声誉正接近无法挽回的临界点

Facepalm

捂脸

![image-20211010095318100](/Users/chenyujie/Library/Application Support/typora-user-images/image-20211010095318100.png)

The problem starts at the top

这个问题从顶部开始

DISASTER STRUCK the world’s biggest social network on October 4th when Facebook and its sister apps were knocked offline for six hours. It was one of the less embarrassing moments of the company’s week. The next day a whistleblower, Frances Haugen, told Congress of all manner of wickedness at the firm, from promoting eating disorders to endangering democracy. Some wondered whether the world would be a better place if the outage were permanent.

whistleblower 告密者

wickedness 恶行

outage 运行中断

10月4日，世界上最大的社交网络Facebook和它的姐妹应用宕机6个小时。这是公司一周中不那么尴尬的时刻之一。第二天，告密者Frances Haugen向国会告发了该公司的种种恶行，从促进进食障碍到危及民主。一些人想知道，如果运行中断是永久性的，世界是否会变得更好。

A share of the opprobrium heaped on Facebook is incoherent. Politicians are angry but so far seem incapable of co-ordinating reform to rein it in. And investors have kept buying the stock, regardless of the bad headlines. Yet the company should take no comfort from this. The blind fury unleashed shows that its reputational problems have got out of hand.

opprobrium 谴责；耻辱

heap 倾注

to heap scorn on sb 对某人大加嘲弄

incoherent 不连贯的；无条理的

fury 狂怒

unleash 宣泄

Facebook上的大量指责是不连贯的。政客们对此感到愤怒，但到目前为止，他们似乎无力协调改革以遏制它。投资者不顾负面消息，继续买入该股。然而，该公司不应因此而感到安慰。这种不能自制的愤怒宣泄表明，它的声誉问题已经失控。

Some of this week’s criticism was tendentious. Reports highlighted internal research showing that Instagram, Facebook’s photo-sharing app, makes one in five American teenagers feel worse about themselves. They paid less attention to the finding that Instagram makes twice as many feel better about themselves. Facebook’s critics are right that it should be more open. But the firm has half a point when it says that the hysterical reaction to unsurprising findings will lead companies to conclude that it is safer not to do such research at all.

tendentious 有偏见的

hysterical 歇斯底里的；极可笑的

unsurprising 不足为奇的

本周的一些批评带有倾向性。报告强调，内部研究显示，Facebook的照片分享应用Instagram让五分之一的美国青少年自我感觉更差。他们不太注意到Instagram让两倍多的人自我感觉更好。Facebook的批评者认为它应该更加开放，这是对的。但该公司有一半的理由认为，对不足为奇的调查结果的极可笑的反应将导致公司得出结论，即公司认为根本不进行此类调查研究更安全。

Other complaints are really criticisms of the broader internet. The question of how to regulate viral content for children goes beyond Facebook, as any parent who has left their child with YouTube knows. Likewise, dilemmas over how the firm amplifies attention and how to draw the line between upholding free speech and minimising harm. Facebook repeated its plea that Congress should weigh in on matters such as minimum ages, rather than leaving it to firms. It has made a better stab than most at settling free-speech questions with its “oversight board”, a pompous-sounding but quietly useful body which dispenses rulings on matters from misogyny to misinformation.

On September 23rd the Reserve Bank of New Zealand tightened macroprudential housing policy for the third time this year, saying that past tightening had not done enough to tackle unsustainable house prices. The first is that research suggests that the effects on house prices do not seem to be large enough to make much difference to affordability. That causes different fluctuations in the current, allowing the sequence of bases to be read.

背景：Oxford Nanopore Technologies开发了基于纳米孔的DNA和RNA测序技术，旨在为任何环境中的任何人提供按需生物信息。蛋白质纳米孔是微型的小孔，其本质是在膜上形成通道。在我们的技术中，蛋白质纳米孔被嵌入合成膜中，并浸入在电生理溶液中，使离子电流通过纳米孔。当像DNA或RNA这样的分子穿过纳米孔时，它们会对电流带来干扰。该信号会被实时分析，用以确定正在通过纳米孔的DNA或RNA链的碱基序列。

纳米孔测序采用了蛋白质的工程版本，在自然界中，这种蛋白质在细胞膜上开孔，允许离子(钾和氯等带电原子)进出。这些蛋白质被用来在细胞膜上打洞，将两个充满液体的腔体隔开，然后施加电流。然后将要测序的DNA送入孔中。DNA使用四种不同的化学碱基来存储信息，缩写为A、C、G和T。每一种碱基的形状都不同，并且以不同的方式部分堵塞孔洞。这会引起电流的不同波动，从而可以读取碱基序列。

![image-20211005111703030](/Users/chenyujie/Library/Application Support/typora-user-images/image-20211005111703030.png)

The idea of nanopore sequencing dates back to 1990s, says Dr Sanghera. But commercialising it has taken many years. One trick is to ensure DNA molecules move through pores at a predictable speed—and never go backwards—something the firm has solved with a specially designed molecular ratchet. Integrating the biological bits of the technology with the silicon ones has been tricky. And a pore does not, by itself, sense individual bases, but combinations of them. Translating those signals is done with the help of machine learning, a technology that has come into its own only in the past few years.

ratchet 棘轮

come into your own

（在某种情况下）所长得到充分的发挥，显出自己的本事

释义：to be very useful or successful in a particular situation

例句：Eileen really comes into her own in a crisis.

艾琳在危急时刻真正显示出了自己的价值。

Sanghera博士说，纳米孔测序的想法可以追溯到20世纪90年代。但将其商业化已经花了很多年。其中一个技巧是确保DNA分子以可预测的速度通过孔，而且不会后退，该公司已经用一种特殊设计的分子棘轮解决了这个问题。将该技术的生物部分与硅部分整合起来一直很棘手。一个孔本身并不能感知单个碱基，而是它们的组合可以。这些信号的翻译是在机器学习的帮助下完成的，这项技术在过去几年才崭露头角。

Nanopore sequencing offers several advantages over other approaches. Its compactness is one. Oxford Nanopore’s smallest product, the MInION, is the size of a chunky mobile phone. Existing sequencers resemble fridge-freezers or (at best) microwave ovens. The MInION’s minuteness permits field analyses, with no need to send samples off to a distant laboratory. It has been used everywhere from Norwegian glaciers to Welsh coal mines to the International Space Station.

chunky 厚实的

纳米孔测序比其他方法有几个优点。它的紧凑性为1。Oxford Nanopore公司最小的产品MInION只有一部笨重的手机那么大。现有的测序仪类似于冰箱或(充其量)微波炉。MInION的微小特性允许进行实地分析，而不需要将样本送往遥远的实验室。从挪威的冰川到威尔士的煤矿，再到国际空间站，它已经被广泛使用。

Another advantage is speed. Previous technologies have chopped DNA into fragments, made copies and tagged them with fluorescent chemicals. That takes time. The record for sequencing an entire human genome is believed to be 13 hours. Oxford Nanopore reckons it can cut this routinely to a handful of hours. And the nanopore approach also provides a continuous read-out. Other methods deliver a result only at the end.

另一个优势是速度。之前的技术已经将DNA切成片段，复制并贴上荧光化学标签。这需要时间。整个人类基因组测序的记录被认为是13个小时。Oxford Nanopore 公司估计，它可以把这个常规时间缩短到几个小时。纳米孔方法也提供了连续的读出。其他方法只在最后提供结果。

Chopping DNA into tiny fragments, meanwhile, makes it hard to work out what is going on in regions made up of repetitive sequences. Nanopore-based devices can read pieces of DNA millions of bases long, offering a clearer picture of what is happening. And they can sense methylation—a chemical modification of bases that is an important means by which gene expression is regulated in a body.

同时，将DNA切成极小的片段，就很难弄清楚由重复序列组成的区域发生了什么。基于纳米孔的设备可以读取数以百万计碱基长的DNA片段，为正在发生的事情提供更清晰的图像。它们还能感知甲基化（一种碱基的化学修饰，它是调节体内基因表达的重要手段）。

Launch sequence

On paper, nanopore sequencing sounds transformative. Whether that will come to pass in reality remains to be seen. Like all good tech startups, Oxford Nanopore has yet to turn a profit, though it hopes to do so within five years. (Its losses last year were £73m, or $98m, on revenues of £114m.) Julian Roberts, an analyst at Jefferies, a bank, says the world market for gene sequencing is worth perhaps $7.5bn a year and is growing “in the low teens [per cent] per year—healthy, but not spectacular”. It is dominated by Illumina, a firm based in San Diego.

come to pass 发生

理论上，纳米孔测序听起来具有革命性。这是否会在现实中实现还有待观察。像所有优秀的科技创业公司一样，Oxford Nanopore还没有盈利，不过它希望在五年内实现盈利。(该公司去年营收1.14亿英镑，亏损7300万英镑，合9800万美元) Jefferies银行分析师Julian Roberts表示，全球基因测序市场每年的价值约为75亿美元，而且“正以每年10%左右的速度增长，运转良好但并不引人注目”。该领域由总部位于圣地亚哥的Illumina公司主导。

But, says Mr Roberts, as with computers, better technology may create new uses, making the market bigger. Cheap, real-time gene sequencing could boost everything from cancer treatment (researchers in Norway are looking to use it to read the genomes of brain tumours during surgery) to environmental modelling to disease surveillance (the usefulness of sequencing in the covid-19 pandemic has put this application, in particular, uppermost in many epidemiologist’s minds).

但是，Roberts先生说，就像电脑一样，更好的技术可以创造新的用途，使市场更大。廉价、实时的基因测序可以促进从癌症治疗(挪威的研究人员正在寻求在手术期间使用它来读取脑肿瘤的基因组)到环境建模到疾病监测(测序在covid-19疫情中的有用性使这一应用尤其突出，在许多流行病学家的心目中是最重要的)。

The firm’s choice to list in London rather than America goes against conventional wisdom, which holds that America offers savvier investors who are more prepared to tolerate losses today for the prospect of a big payout tomorrow. And it hopes, for now, to stay British. Its shareholding structure gives Dr Sanghera (or, if he is unavailable, two other senior managers) the ability to block unwelcome takeovers. Mr Roberts points to the example of Solexa, a British firm bought by Illumina in 2006 for $600m. These days, Solexa’s technology is a foundation of Ilumina’s business, and the American company is worth $64bn. ■

savvy 精明的；有见识的

该公司选择在伦敦而不是美国上市，这与传统观点相悖，传统观点认为美国提供了更精明的投资者，他们更愿意忍受今天的损失，以迎接明天的巨额回报。目前，它希望继续留在英国。它的股权结构使Sanghera博士(或者，如果他不在，另两位高级经理)有能力阻止不受欢迎的收购。Roberts先生以Solexa公司为例，这家英国公司在2006年被Illumina公司以6亿美元收购。如今，Solexa的技术是Ilumina业务的基础，这家美国公司的市值为640亿美元。■

Why companies need middle managers

为什么公司需要中层管理人员

Bartleby

巴托比

![image-20211004111635827](/Users/chenyujie/Library/Application Support/typora-user-images/image-20211004111635827.png)

Organisations embrace flat hierarchies at their peril

at one’s peril 自冒风险

企业接受扁平的等级制度是有风险的

EVERY LARGE business has a boss and minions, who do most of the work. What comes between the corner office and the shop floor is a matter of managerial preference. Some firms’ organisational charts are towering mille-feuilles, with staff piled into rigid hierarchies stuffed with assorted supervisors. More fashionable of late has been the pancake organigram: fewer layers of workers reporting to a smaller cadre of chieftains. As appealing as such “flat” organisations might seem, the thinning of managerial ranks comes at great cost.

minion 下属

shop floor [工厂的]生产区

cadre 骨干队伍

每个大公司都有一个老板和下属，下属们做大部分的工作。在角落办公室和车间之间的事情是一个管理偏好的问题。一些公司的组织结构图是高耸的千层型，员工按照严格的等级制度排列，而这些严格的等级制度塞满各种各样的主管。最近更流行的是扁平化的组织结构:更少层的工人向更少的领导干部汇报工作。尽管这种“扁平化”的组织看起来很有吸引力，但管理层的减少付出了巨大的代价。

Vice-presidents, area supervisors and other department heads were once the corporate machine’s central cogs. Now such middle managers are derided as pound-shop CEOs, there largely to organise and attend pointless meetings. A few modish startups bill themselves as having no administrative tiers at all, leaving independent employees flitting between tasks as they see fit. Such holacracy, as it is dubbed, clearly won’t do for a Unilever or Goldman Sachs. But even big firms now ritually boast about “delayering” their ranks.

cog 不可缺少的小人物

bill 宣布

tier 阶层

flit 频繁变化

holacracy 合弄制：即全体共治，被认为是一种“无领导管理方式”，它将公司组织架构去中心化，将由人定义工作角色转变为围绕工作来定义，并且经常更新。

delayering 减少员工层次

副总裁、区域主管和其他部门主管曾经是公司重要的、不可缺少的小人物。现在，这些中层管理人员被嘲笑为廉价商店的首席执行官，他们主要负责组织和参加毫无意义的会议。一些时髦的初创公司标榜自己根本没有管理层，让独立的员工在他们认为合适的任务之间穿梭。这种被称为“合弄制”的做法显然不适用于联合利华或高盛。但即使是大公司现在也会仪式性地自吹要减少他们的员工等级层次。

Flat organisations are meant to reflect the modern workplace. Businesses in generations past used to be steeply hierarchical to mimic the armed forces, remembered by bosses of yesteryear as a place where missions were accomplished. Starting off at the bottom of a pyramid, perhaps six or seven rungs below the top brass, wasn’t so bad if you intended to progress at the same firm for your entire career. Millennials and later Gen-Z recruits had other ideas. Reporting to an overbearing boss crimped their ability to make an immediate mark.

overbearing 专横的

crimp 束缚

扁平化的组织机构旨在反映现代工作场所。在过去几代的企业等级森严，以效仿军队，在昔日老板们的记忆中，军队是完成使命的地方。如果你想在同一家公司发展自己的整个职业生涯，那么从金字塔的最底层开始，也许比高层低六到七级，这并不是件坏事。千禧一代和后来的Z世代的新员工有不同的想法。向专横的老板汇报妨碍了他们立竿见影的能力。

Several factors contributed to the “flattening” trend. Businesses discovered that having lots of mini-barons could lead to stultifying silos. New ways of working—starting with modern technology—mean that executives can manage more subordinates, including some far away. Add enough direct reports to each supervisor, and the number of rungs between the chief executive and the graduate trainee shrinks accordingly.

stultifying 阻碍发展的；使僵化的

silo: a part of a company, organization, or system that does not communicate with, understand, or work well with other parts

（公司、机构或系统内部与其他单位不联系、不了解、不合作的）孤立单位

rung 等级

几个因素促成了“扁平化”的趋势。企业发现，拥有大量的“小大亨”可能会导致阻碍发展的“孤立组织”。新的工作方式（从现代技术开始）意味着高管可以管理更多的下属，包括一些遥远的下属。给每个主管增加足够多的直接下属，那么首席执行官和毕业实习生之间的等级就会相应地减少。

Proponents of flat organisations say they give each employee added responsibility: bosses with dozens of flunkeys can hardly be expected to micromanage them. Uncluttered organigrams make companies more agile, enable faster decision-making and trim costs to boot. Business titans like Elon Musk of Tesla, a car firm, have painted delayering as a way of improving communication and shedding corporate deadweight.

flunkey 马屁精

Uncluttered 没有繁琐细节的；整洁的

trim 削减

shed 摆脱；削减

deadweight 重负

扁平化组织的支持者表示，扁平化组织赋予每位员工更多的责任:拥有几十个跟班的老板很难对员工进行微观管理。整洁的组织结构使公司更加灵活，能够更快地做出决策，并节约裁员成本。像汽车公司特斯拉的马斯克这样的商业巨头，已经把“减少员工层级”描绘成一种改善沟通、减轻企业负担的方式。

Clearly there are limits to how far one can go. Not every Tesla factory hand is going to seek their annual appraisal from Mr Musk. In fact any amount of delayering hacks away at what it means for an employee to be part of a company. The key corollary—indeed the enabling factor—of flat organisations is for each employee to have less boss. That will sound appealing to some workers.

hack away 砍去

corollary 必然结果

显然，一个人能走多远是有限度的。并非每位特斯拉工厂员工都会向马斯克寻求年度评估。事实上，任何程度的减少员工层级都是在破坏员工作为公司一员的意义。扁平化组织的关键结果（实际上是促成因素）是让每个员工拥有更少的老板。对一些员工来说这听起来很有吸引力。

Such bosslessness, however, is a false Utopia. Companies that went furthest in scrapping management tiers discovered that getting rid of a formal pecking order resulted in informal hierarchies taking hold instead. A leadership vacuum risks being filled by petty tyranny. It is inevitable when large groups of people spend time together, in the office or elsewhere, that someone ends up in charge (and often lots of people end up in charge of different little tasks). That can be layers of managers put in place formally according to their competence and track record. Or, if everyone is on paper holacratically equal, it might be whoever talks loudest at meetings.

然而，这种无老板状态是一个虚假的乌托邦。在废除管理层方面走得最远的公司发现，取消正式的等级制度会导致非正式的等级制度取而代之。“领导缺乏”有可能被小的专制所填补。当一大群人在一起的时候，不管是在办公室还是在其他地方，总有人最终负责(而且通常是很多人最终负责不同的小任务)。这可以是根据他们的能力和业绩正式任命的管理层。或者，如果每个人在纸上都是完全平等的，那可能就是在会议上发言最大声的那个人负责。

Fewer tiers mean fewer people with day-to-day experience of corralling employees. Yet managing others is not an ancillary task which companies do to reach other aims. It is the precondition for any of their aims to be reached. Sometimes skimping on the degree to which each human resource is supervised doesn’t matter much. But there inevitably comes a time—for the employee or the company—when it matters a great deal. Having lots of organisational tiers means that those in charge of managing lots of people have had experience managing fewer people before.

corral 包围

to skimp on … 节省 ； ‹paint, food›在…方面吝啬

更少的层级意味着拥有日常管理员工经验的人更少。然而，管理他人并非企业为了达到其他目标而做的一项辅助任务。这是实现任何目标的先决条件。有时，对每个人力资源的监管程度略加限制并没有多大关系。但无论对员工还是公司来说，都不可避免地会有一个重要时刻到来。拥有许多组织层级意味着那些负责管理大量人员的人以前有管理较少人员的经验。

Covid-19 has already upended many individuals’ working lives. Gone are the days of sitting every day in the same office as your co-workers. Plenty will be adapting to the new managerial normal for a while yet. That makes keeping employees in the loop and engaged more important than ever. Layers of bosses provide structure. For all the joy of belittling them, middle managers are part of the solution.

Covid-19已经颠覆了许多人的工作生活。每天和同事坐在同一个办公室的日子一去不复返了。许多人将在一段时间内适应新的管理常态。这使得让员工参与其中变得比以往任何时候都重要。老板的层级构成了结构。尽管贬低中层管理人员令人高兴，但他们是解决方案的一部分。

The future of food

未来的食物

![image-20211003111558503](/Users/chenyujie/Library/Application Support/typora-user-images/image-20211003111558503.png)

Consumers and governments should embrace new ways to make food

消费者和政府应该采用新的方法来制造食物

WHAT’S FOR dinner? The answer matters, at every level. Food connects the personal to the planetary. Agriculture uses half the world’s habitable land and accounts for more than 30% of global emissions. Food production links the great biogeochemical cycles of carbon and nitrogen, both on a planetary level and also in specific factories that combine natural gas with nitrogen and oxygen from the air to produce agricultural fertiliser on the one hand, and carbon dioxide for use in food processing on the other. When one such factory in Teesside, in northern England, recently threatened to shut down because of high natural-gas prices, the government had to step in to prevent food supply chains from collapsing.

晚餐吃什么?在每个层面上，答案都很重要。食物将个人与地球联系起来。农业占用了世界上一半的可居住土地，占全球排放的30%以上。食品生产将碳和氮的巨大生物地球化学循环联系起来，无论是在行星层面上，还是在特定工厂层面，一方面特定工厂可以将天然气与空气中的氮和氧结合起来生产农业肥料，另一方面它可以再与二氧化碳结合用于食品加工。最近，由于天然气价格高涨，英格兰北部Teesside的一家这样的工厂可能要关闭，政府不得不介入，以防止食品供应链崩溃。

Globally, food prices have risen in 13 of the past 15 months and are close to their peak of 2011, owing to poor weather, pandemic-related disruption and fallout from a swine-flu outbreak in China in 2018. In the longer term, the food system faces pressure from climate change, population growth and a shift towards more Westernised, meat-heavy diets.

swine-flu 猪流感

fallout 后果

由于天气恶劣、疫情相关的扰乱以及中国2018年猪流感爆发带来的影响，在过去15个月里，全球食品价格有13个月上涨，接近2011年的峰值。从长远来看，食品体系面临着来自气候变化、人口增长和向更西化、偏重肉类饮食转变的压力。

Fortunately, technologies are emerging that promise to produce food in new ways, in large volumes with less inhumane factory farming and a lower environmental footprint. These range from bioreactors that grow meat to indoor “vertical” farms and new ways of producing fish. Such techniques could make a huge difference. Three-quarters of agricultural land is used for livestock, for example, so it is easy to see how steaks made from plant-based protein, or grown in vats from cells, could greatly reduce factory farming and land and water use, and produce fewer emissions.

footprint 对环境的影响

幸运的是，正在出现的技术有望以新的方式生产食物，以更人道的工厂化养殖和更低的环境影响大批量生产食物。这些技术包括从种植肉的生物反应器到室内“垂直”农场和生产鱼类的新方法。这样的技术可以带来巨大的不同。例如，四分之三的农业土地用于饲养牲畜，因此很容易看出，用植物蛋白制作的牛排，或在大桶中用细胞培养的牛排，可以大大减少工厂化养殖、土地和水的使用，并产生更少的排放。

Just because it is possible to make food in new ways does not mean people will be willing to eat it, however. Given food’s cultural importance, and the fact that it is ingested into the body, conservatism and scepticism are common reactions to new foodstuffs and production processes. In 17th-century Europe many people were loth to eat a new vegetable called the potato because it was not mentioned in the Bible, or because they feared it caused leprosy. Today, many European countries ban the cultivation and sale of genetically modified crops, even though they are widely grown and eaten elsewhere. And although much of the world considers insects a mouth-watering treat (and locust-eating is endorsed in the Bible), the very idea revolts many Western consumers.

leprosy 麻风病

mouth-watering 令人垂涎的

revolt 感到惊骇；感到厌恶

然而，仅仅因为有可能以新的方式制作食物，并不意味着人们会愿意吃它。考虑到食物在文化上的重要性，以及它被人体吸收的事实，保守主义和怀疑主义是人们对新食品和生产过程的普遍反应。在17世纪的欧洲，许多人不愿意吃一种叫做土豆的新蔬菜，因为《圣经》中没有提到土豆，或者因为他们担心土豆会引起麻风病。如今，许多欧洲国家禁止种植和销售转基因作物，尽管它们在其他地方被广泛种植和食用。尽管世界上很多人都认为昆虫是令人垂涎的美味(《圣经》中也提到了吃蝗虫)，但这种想法却让许多西方消费者感到厌恶。

At the same time as novel foods are shunned, traditional foods and farming lore are venerated. In California the fanciest restaurants aspire to recreate the humble diet of the Tuscan peasant. Many Western consumers are willing to pay extra for food produced by organic farming, in effect a historical re-enactment of pre-20th-century agriculture, because it avoids “chemicals”. (Everything is made of chemicals.)

在人们拒绝新奇食物的同时，传统食物和农业知识却受到尊崇。在加州，最高档的餐馆渴望重现托斯卡纳农民的简朴饮食。许多西方消费者愿意为有机农业生产的食品支付额外的费用，因为有机农业避免了“化学物质”。有机农业实际上是20世纪前农业的历史重演。(一切都是由化学物质构成的。)

Yet supposedly timeless food traditions are often shallower than they seem. In the “Columbian exchange” in the 16th century, food crops from the Americas rapidly spread around the world. Tomatoes and polenta, staples of the Italian diet, are American in origin and were unknown to the Romans or to Dante. Potatoes were eventually widely adopted in Europe (the invention of French fries helped). It is hard to imagine many Asian cuisines without chili peppers, but they too are American. Coffee from Arabia and tea from China were unknown in Europe before the 17th century.

然而，理应永恒的饮食传统往往比它们看上去的要肤浅。在16世纪的“哥伦比亚交换”中，来自美洲的粮食作物迅速传播到世界各地。西红柿和玉米粥是意大利人的主食，原产于美国，罗马人和但丁对此一无所知。土豆最终在欧洲被广泛食用(炸薯条的发明起到了一定作用)。很难想象很多亚洲菜没有辣椒，但它们也是美国菜。17世纪以前，欧洲不知道阿拉伯的咖啡和中国的茶。

The new foods and processes on offer today present opportunities to create delicious and sustainable new traditions. Western consumers should put aside their reservations about eating crickets and give plant-based burgers, 3D-printed steaks and vat-grown artificial tuna a try. Regulators, especially in Europe and America, should streamline their processes for approving cultured meat, be more open to gene-editing crops (as Britain said it would be this week), and speed up approval of edible insects for animal feed and human consumption. A wholesale reimagining of the food system is needed. But that will be possible only if both consumers and regulators are prepared to be more daring about what to eat for dinner. ■
