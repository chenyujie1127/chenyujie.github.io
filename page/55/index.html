London’s flooding is sure to worsen

伦敦的洪水肯定会恶化

Live by the river

住在河边

![image-20210803095636822](/Users/chenyujie/Library/Application Support/typora-user-images/image-20210803095636822.png)

##### Development means flood plains are being covered by concrete

背景：河漫滩（flood plain）是河流洪水期淹没的河床以外的谷底部分。它由河流的横向迁移和洪水漫堤的沉积作用形成。

发展意味着河漫滩正在被混凝土覆盖

Jul 31st 2021

LONDONERS LIKE to remind visitors that, despite their city’s reputation for dreary weather, rain is in fact less common than in some sunnier climes. Rome gets more total precipitation each year; New York City gets almost twice as much, and has far more rainy days. In general, Britain’s capital is grey but fairly dry, with predictable and moderate weather.

precipitation 降水

伦敦人喜欢提醒游客，虽然他们的城市以阴天而闻名，但事实上雨水并不像在一些阳光充足的地方那么常见。罗马每年的总降水量更多;纽约市的降雨量几乎是罗马的两倍，而且纽约的下雨天要多得多。总的来说，英国的首都是灰色的，但相当干燥，天气是可预测而且温和的。

That may be changing. On July 25th parts of the city saw over 5cm of rain in just a few hours, an amount that would normally take an entire month to fall. The result was flash flooding. In east London two hospitals told patients to stay away; one, Whipps Cross, had to evacuate around 100 patients after a power cut. Hundreds of cars were stranded on roads that suddenly became rivers, while a dozen Tube stations were forced to close. Pudding Mill Lane station, on the Docklands Light Railway, looked like a swimming pool, with several feet of water washing around the ticket barriers. Even in areas far from rivers and flood plains, such as Hampstead, a pretty suburb of north London, shopkeepers had to bail out rain water into the streets.

shopkeeper 店主

bail out 往外舀

这种情况可能正在改变。7月25日，该市部分地区在短短几个小时内降雨量超过5厘米，而这一降雨量通常需要整整一个月才能达到。结果就导致山洪暴发。在伦敦东部，两家医院告诉病人“不要靠近”;其中一家医院Whipps Cross在停电后不得不疏散了大约100名病人。数百辆汽车被困在突然变成河流的道路上，十几个地铁站被迫关闭。码头区轻轨上的布丁坊巷车站看起来就像一个游泳池，几英尺深的水冲刷着检票口周围。即使在远离河流和漫滩的地区，比如伦敦北部美丽的郊区汉普斯特德，店主们也不得不将雨水排入街道。

Unlike the destruction caused by the floods that hit parts of Germany earlier in July, this damage will not be lasting. Most of the railway stations that were closed reopened the next day; so did the hospitals. Yet such incidents are likely to become more common in the capital. Even if global warming is limited to 1.5°C, an international goal that looks increasingly likely to be missed, winter rainfall could increase by 59%, according to a government study of the Thames Estuary flooding risk published in February. A particular issue is the Thames barrier, which stops tidal floods from washing back up into the city. It was closed just ten times in the decade after construction finished in 1981. But since 2010 it has closed 80 times. The study predicted that “once-a-century sea level events are expected to become annual”.

感潮河段是流量及水位受潮汐影响较大的河段。

与7月初袭击德国部分地区的洪水所造成的破坏不同，这次破坏不会持久。大部分关闭的火车站第二天就重新开放了; 医院也一样。然而这样的事件在首都可能会变得更加普遍。根据今年2月发表的一份有关泰晤士河口洪水风险的政府研究报告显示，即使全球变暖限制在1.5°C以内(这一国际目标似乎越来越有可能无法实现)，冬季降雨量也可能增加59%。一个特别的问题是泰晤士河的防潮闸，它阻止潮汐洪水冲回城市。在1981年建成后的十年里，它只关闭了十次。但自2010年以来，它已经关闭了80次。该研究预测，“百年一遇的海平面事件预计将变成每年一次的”。

The problem is exacerbated by the way London has developed. For decades, its water table was artificially suppressed by factories tapping it. Deindustrialisation means it has been rising again since the 1980s. And thanks to planning rules that protect greenfield land, much new housing has been built in places vulnerable to flooding, such as along the Thames Estuary or the Lea River valley, the site of the 2012 Olympic Park. Between 2014 and 2017 the population living near the park more than doubled, from around 10,000 to 26,000. By 2031 it is expected to reach almost 100,000. All that extra concrete means water struggles to flow away.

伦敦的发展形势加剧了这一问题。几十年来，它的地下水位一直被工厂人为压低。去工业化意味着自20世纪80年代以来它一直在上升。多亏了保护绿地的规划规定，许多新住宅都建在了易受洪水影响的地方，比如泰晤士河口或2012年奥林匹克公园所在地利亚河谷。从2014年到2017年，居住在公园附近的人口增加了一倍多，从大约1万人增至2.6万人。到2031年，预计将达到近10万。所有额外的混凝土意味着水很难流走。

“Building on a flood plain is all well and good,” says Asif Din of Perkins & Will, an architectural firm. “But is the developer actually going to be mitigating what they’re putting on the land?” Architects can easily design individual buildings or estates to be resilient (and many do). But they are not responsible for wider infrastructure. Instead, flood risk is managed by a patchwork quilt of government agencies, which do not always fit together well. It is not much good having a resilient office building, says Mr Din, if nobody can reach it because the roads nearby are flooded.

patchwork 东拼西凑

帕金斯威尔建筑设计事务所的Asif Din称:“在漫滩上建房当然很好。但开发商真的会减少他们在土地上的投入吗?”建筑师可以很容易地设计出具有弹性的单个建筑或庄园(许多人确实是这样做的)。但他们不负责更广泛的基础设施。相反，洪水风险是由政府机构拼凑而成的，它们并不总是能很好地配合在一起。Din先生说，如果因为附近的道路被洪水淹没而没人能到达（办公楼），那么拥有一座弹性很强的办公楼也就没多大好处。

Some improvements already under way should help. A new “super sewer”, intended to augment the existing system, which dates from Victorian times, will open in 2025. It will not stop flooding, but ought to stop sewage from flowing untreated into the Thames (or washing back up drains). Past the Thames barrier, in the outer eastern reaches of the city, flood defences are being improved. But none of that may be enough to keep up with the changing climate. The last time central London flooded was in 1928; 14 people drowned in Westminster. It may yet happen again. ■

sewer 下水道

一些正在进行的改进应该会有所帮助。一个新的“超级下水道”将于2025年投入使用，目的是扩大可追溯到维多利亚时代的现有系统。它不能阻止洪水泛滥，但应该能阻止未经处理的污水流入泰晤士河(或冲洗排水沟)。穿过泰晤士河防潮闸，在城市的东部外围地区，防洪设施正在得到改善。但这些都不足以跟上气候变化的步伐。伦敦市中心上一次洪水发生在1928年;14人在威斯敏斯特溺亡。这种情况可能还会再次发生。■

Why businesses use so much jargon

为什么企业用这么多行话

Bartleby

巴托比

![image-20210802101233673](/Users/chenyujie/Library/Application Support/typora-user-images/image-20210802101233673.png)

The reasons behind management gobbledygook

gobbledygook: language, especially used in official letters, forms, and statements, that seems difficult or to mean nothing because you do not understand it

（尤指公文用语中）复杂而无实质内容的语言，冗长而费解的文字；官样文章

管理层官样文章背后的原因

Jul 31st 2021

NO CHILD ASPIRES to a life talking the kind of nonsense that many executives speak. But it seems that, as soon as managers start to climb the corporate ladder, they begin to lose the ability to talk or write clearly. They instead become entangled in a forest of gobbledygook.

没有哪个孩子渴望过一种像许多高管那样胡说八道的生活。但似乎，一旦管理者开始攀上公司的阶梯，他们就开始失去清晰地说话或写作的能力。他们反而陷入了一片官样文章的森林。

The first explanation for this phenomenon is that “jargon abhors a vacuum”. All too often, executives know they have nothing significant to say in a speech or a memo. They could confine their remarks to something like “profits are up (or down)”, which would be relevant information. But executives would rather make some grand statement about team spirit or the corporate ethos. They aim to make the business sound more inspirational than “selling more stuff at less cost”. So they use long words, obscure jargon, and buzzwords like “holistic” to fill the space.

nature abhors a vacuum 自然界里不存在真空

ethos [社会、时代或文化的] 道德观

buzzword 时髦词语

对这一现象的第一种解释是“行话世界里不存在真空”。高管们常常知道，他们在演讲或备忘录中没有什么重要的东西可说。他们可以把自己的言论限制在“利润上升(或下降)”之类的东西上，这还算是相关的信息。但高管们更愿意就团队精神或企业精神发表一些重大声明。他们的目标是让这项业务听起来比“以更低的成本销售更多的东西”更鼓舞人心。所以他们用冗长的词、晦涩的行话和像“全面的”这样的流行语来填补空白。

Another reason why managers indulge in waffle relates to the nature of the modern economy. In the past, work was largely about producing, or selling, physical things such as bricks or electrical gadgets. A service-based economy involves tasks that are difficult to define. When it is hard to describe what you do, it is natural to resort to imprecise terms.

waffle 废话连篇

高管们沉迷于废话连篇的另一个原因与现代经济的本质有关。在过去，工作主要是生产或销售实物，如砖块或电子产品。基于服务的经济涉及难以定义的任务。当很难描述你在做什么时，很自然地就会求助于不精确的术语。

Such terms can have a purpose but still be irritating. Take “onboarding”. A single word to describe the process of a company assimilating a new employee could be useful. But “to board” would do the trick (at least in American English, which is more comfortable than British English with “a plane boarding passengers” and not just “passengers boarding a plane”). The only purpose of adding “on” seems to be to allow the creation of an equally ugly word, “offboarding”, the process of leaving a firm.

do the trick 管用

onboarding: the induction and assimilation of a new employee into a company or organization

这些术语可能有其用途，但仍然令人恼火。以“onboarding”为例，用一个词来描述公司吸收新员工的过程可能是有用的。但是“to board”就可以了(至少在美式英语中是这样的，美式英语中的“a plane boarding passengers”比英式英语中的“passengers boarding a plane”要舒服得多)。添加“on”的唯一目的似乎是创造一个同样丑陋的词，“offboarding”，即离开公司的过程。

Overblown language is also used when the actual business is prosaic. Private Eye, a British satirical magazine that often mocks corporate flimflam, used to have a regular column pointing out the absurd tendency of companies to tag the word “solutions” onto a product; carpets became “floor-covering solutions”. (Bartleby has long wanted to start a business devoted to dissolving items in water so it could be called “Solution Solutions”.) Nowadays, the target for mockery is the use of the term DNA, as in “perfect customer service is in our DNA”.

overblown 浮夸的；过分的

prosaic 平庸的; 平淡的

satirical 讽刺的

flimflam 废话

当实际业务平淡无奇时，也会使用夸张的语言。英国讽刺杂志《私家侦探》经常嘲讽企业的废话，它曾有一个定期专栏，上面会指出企业在产品上贴上“解决方案”一词的荒谬倾向; 地毯成为“覆盖地板的解决方案”。(巴托比一直想创办一家致力于将物品溶解在水中的公司，这样它就可以被称为“溶液解决方案”)。如今，人们嘲笑的对象是DNA这个词的使用，比如“完美的客户服务存在于我们的DNA中”。

In her book about life in the tech industry, “Uncanny Valley”, Anna Wiener used the term “garbage language” to describe “a sort of nonlanguage which was neither beautiful nor especially efficient”. Tech executives spouted a very grand vision of how they would reshape society but their rhetoric often clashed with the hard reality of what they were doing, which was to sell advertising or monopolise users’ time. It is a variation on the old Ralph Waldo Emerson dictum: “The louder he mentioned his honour, the faster we counted our spoons.”

spout 滔滔不绝地谈

rhetoric 言辞

Anna Wiener在她关于科技行业生活的书《Uncanny Valley》中，用了“垃圾语言”这个词来描述“一种既不漂亮也不特别高效的非语言”。科技公司的高管们滔滔不绝地谈着他们将如何重塑社会的宏伟愿景，但他们的言论往往与他们正在做的事情（出售广告或垄断用户的时间）的严酷现实发生冲突。这是Ralph Waldo Emerson那句格言的变体:“他越大声提到自己的荣誉，我们就越快数着自己的勺子。”

The third reason why managers use jargon is to establish their credentials. What makes one person fit to manage another? It is hard to identify any obvious attributes; managers are not like doctors, who prove their expertise through passing exams and practical training. If you can speak the language of management, you appear qualified to rule. If others don’t understand terms like “synergy” and “paradigm”, that only demonstrates their ignorance. In a sense, managers are acting rather like medieval priests, who conducted services in Latin rather than in the local language, adding to the mystical nature of the process.

高管们使用行话的第三个原因是证实他们的资历。是什么让一个人适合管理另一个人?很难识别任何明显的标志; 管理者不像医生，它们可以通过考试和实际培训来证明自己的专业知识。如果你能说管理语言，你就有资格支配。如果别人不懂“协同作用”和“范式”这样的术语，那只能说明他们无知。从某种意义上说，高管们的行为更像是中世纪的牧师，他们用拉丁语而不是当地语言进行仪式，这增加了仪式的神秘性。

Once corporate jargon is established, it is hard for managers to avoid using it. The terms are ever-present in PowerPoint slides, speeches and annual reports. Not to use them would suggest a manager is not sufficiently committed to the job. Junior staff, for their part, dare not question the language for fear of damaging their promotion prospects.

企业行话一旦形成，管理人员就很难避免使用它。这些术语经常出现在PPT、演讲和年度报告中。不使用它们就意味着管理者对工作不够投入。就初级员工而言，他们不敢质疑语言，因为担心损害他们的晋升前景。

Of course, new words will inevitably be coined in the world of business, as in other areas of life. Technology has ushered in a range of terms, such as hardware and software, which were once unfamiliar but are now widely understood. But a lot of the more irritating jargon has been brought in from other areas of life, like the self-help movement.

coin 创造

当然，就像在生活的其他领域一样，在商业领域也不可避免地会出现新词。技术带来了一系列术语，如硬件和软件，这些曾经不为人所知的术语现在已被广泛理解。但很多更令人恼火的术语是从生活的其他领域引入的，比如自救运动。

All this matters because the continued use of obscure language is a sign that the speaker is not thinking clearly. And if those in charge aren’t thinking clearly, that’s bad for the business. People who are in real command of the detail are able to explain things in a way that is easily understood. And if a manager’s colleagues understand the message, they are more likely to get the right things done. Jargon gets in the way.

所有这些都很重要，因为持续使用晦涩的语言是说话者思维不清晰的标志。如果负责的高管们思路不清晰，那对公司是不利的。真正掌握细节的人能够以一种容易理解的方式去解释事情。如果高管们的同事理解了这一信息，他们就更有可能把事情做好。行话碍事。

Remarkable progress has been made in understanding the folding of proteins

在理解蛋白质折叠方面取得了显著进展

Proteinotopia

![image-20210801214358537](/Users/chenyujie/Library/Application Support/typora-user-images/image-20210801214358537.png)

It will help open up almost limitless vistas

它将有助于开辟几乎无限的前景

Jul 31st 2021

WHEN ST JOHN THE EVANGELIST wrote of the Word becoming Flesh, he was drawing on ideas of reason and order derived from classical Greek philosophy. But he was also providing a succinct description of the most basic truth in molecular biology. In a wonderful and ancient mechanism called the ribosome, words—in the form of messages stored in DNA—are translated into flesh, in the form of proteins.

the Word becoming Flesh：道成肉身（来自圣经），也指兑现诺言

succinct 简明的

当福音传道者圣约翰写道“道成肉身”时，他借鉴了古典希腊哲学中关于理性和秩序的观点。但他同时也简明扼要地描述了分子生物学中最基本的真理。在一种叫做核糖体的奇妙而古老的机制中，（以信息形式储存在DNA中的）道被翻译成（以蛋白质的形式的）肉。

Proteins are flesh both literally, in that they give meat the texture and bloodiness that carnivores savour, and figuratively, in that their actions lie behind all the strengths and frailties of body and mind. Both their manipulation and their mass production are fundamental to modern pharmacology. The huge market for statins rests on the way they interact with the workings of a protein called HMG-CoA reductase; Keytruda, the world’s biggest-selling cancer drug, is a protein itself, a subtly tweaked antibody which turns off a mechanism that lets cancers evade the immune system. Understanding the form and function of proteins is crucial to medicine, to agriculture and to replacing the petrochemicals currently produced from oil. And that understanding is fast deepening.

carnivores 食肉动物

figuratively 比喻地

petrochemical 石油化学产品

蛋白质从字面上讲就是肉，因为它们赋予肉以食肉动物所品尝的质感和血色; 从喻意来讲，因为它们的行为隐藏在身体和精神的所有力量和弱点背后。对它们的操控和大量生产都是现代药理学的基础。他汀类降脂药物的巨大市场取决于它们与一种名为HMG-CoA还原酶的蛋白质相互作用的方式; Keytruda是世界上最畅销的抗癌药物，它本身就是一种蛋白质，是一种微调过的抗体，可以关闭一种让癌症逃避免疫系统的机制。了解蛋白质的形式和功能对医学、农业和替代目前从石油中生产的石化产品都至关重要。这种理解正在迅速加深。

Proteins are created as chains of smaller molecules called amino acids. The ribosome can fill each link in the chain with one of 20 different varieties of them. The words stored in DNA set out which of those 20 types of amino acid goes where. The process is explained in this week’s “Biology brief”, the first of six.

蛋白质是由称为氨基酸的小分子链组成的。核糖体可以用20种不同的氨基酸中的一种来填充链中的每一环。储存在DNA里的文字决定了这20种氨基酸的去向。这一过程在本周的“生物学摘要”(六篇中的第一篇)中得到了解释。

In order to take on the shape required of it, a protein must fold itself up into a specific form, a process which produces all manner of kinks, twists, swirls, sheets and cavities. The shape depends on the amino-acid sequence, but the interactions between the hundreds of amino acids are just too complex for the sequence alone to reveal much. To understand the structure of a protein in detail, scientists have had to make comparatively large amounts of it, coax those molecules into forming a crystal (or, more recently, flash-freeze them) and bombard the sample with X-rays (or, if it is a frozen one, electrons). These procedures take time, money and effort. Only a tiny fraction of the proteins whose sequences are known have been studied this way.

为了得到所需的形状，蛋白质必须将自己折叠成特定的形状，这一过程会产生各种各样的扭结、弯曲、螺旋、折叠和空腔。它的形状取决于氨基酸的序列，但数百种氨基酸之间的相互作用太复杂了，单凭序列信息无法揭示太多。为了更详细地了解蛋白质的结构，科学家们不得不制造相对大量的蛋白质，诱导这些分子形成晶体(或者，最近，快速冷冻它们)，并用X射线(或者，如果是冷冻的，用电子)轰击样本。这些程序需要耗费时间、金钱和精力。只有一小部分已知序列的蛋白质用这种方法进行了研究。

Now things look likely to get considerably easier. AlphaFold, a very elegant piece of software developed by DeepMind, a British AI company, has learned from the detailed study of sequence and structure to make predictions of protein shapes using just the amino-acid sequences of their very bendy backbones. On July 22nd DeepMind, which, like Google, is owned by Alphabet, made 350,000 of its predictions freely available to all, having released the code the week before. Millions more such predictions are expected soon.

bendy 易弯曲的；多弯道的

现在看来，事情可能变得容易得多。AlphaFold是英国人工智能公司DeepMind开发的一款非常简明的软件，它通过对蛋白质序列和结构的

详细研究，仅利用蛋白质可弯曲骨架的氨基酸序列，就可以预测蛋白质的形状。7月22日，同谷歌一样，隶属于Alphabet的DeepMind在一周前发布了软件的代码，并向所有人免费提供了35万份蛋白质结构的预测。预计不久之后还会有数百万这样的预测结果发布出来。

As those suspicious of hype rightly point out, not all of these will be equally good and better prediction does not do away with the need for other ways of exploring protein structure. AlphaFold will not be the last word in the application of AI to the problem. All that said, the software looks likely to be massively useful, helping researchers spot possibilities and dead ends more quickly and letting them take on projects they would otherwise have steered clear of. If AlphaFold is not soon providing useful pharmacological results, as well as lots of basic biology, it will not be for want of application.

正如那些对炒作持怀疑态度的人所正确指出的那样，并非所有这些方法都同样有效，更好的预测也并不能消除对其他探索蛋白质结构的方法的需要。AlphaFold不会是人工智能应用于这个问题的最后一个词。综上所述，该软件看起来非常有用，可以帮助研究人员更快地发现可能性和死胡同，并让他们承担原本会避开的项目。如果AlphaFold能很快提供有用的药理学结果，以及大量的基础生物学结果，它将不会缺乏应用。

AlphaFold will also help usher in an era of altogether stranger things. Proteins are typically made from 20 different amino acids, each of which can, in principle, sit at any point in the chain. With 20 choices as to what should follow the first amino acid, you have 20x20=400 possible amino-acid doublets, 20x20x20=8,000 triplets and so on. By the time you get to an eight-amino-acid chain, there are more possibilities than there are people on Earth. Human proteins are typically 400 amino acids long; many have lengths in the thousands. There is no type of physical thing in the observable universe remotely as numerous as the possibilities inherent in a 400-amino-acid protein.

AlphaFold还将有助于开启一个完全陌生事物的时代。蛋白质通常由20种不同的氨基酸组成，原则上，每一种氨基酸都可以位于链条上的任何位置。第一个氨基酸后面有20个不同选择，你就有了20x20=400个可能的由两个氨基酸组成的链，20x20x20= 8000个不同的包含三个氨基酸的链，以此类推。当你得到一个八个氨基酸的链时，这样的可能性比地球上总人口还多。人类蛋白质通常长度有400个氨基酸; 许多甚至都有数千个氨基酸的长度。在遥远的可观测的宇宙中，没有任何一种物质能像含有400个氨基酸的蛋白质那样无限可能存在。

The “potential protein space” such calculations reveal is thus a cosmos unto itself, ordered yet near infinite. The bit that evolution has explored so far—which contains wonders as diverse as proteins that flex like springs, spin like wheels, extend like pistons and crank like ratchets, that turn sunlight into chemical energy, that build, demolish and recycle all the components of life and do more besides—is but the tiniest corner of it. Already some scientists are working on “de novo” proteins well outside nature’s comfort zone as ways of making tiny mechanisms and machines. As tools like AlphaFold increase the ease of molecular design, they will be joined by many more.

这样的计算揭示的“潜在的蛋白质空间”是一个自身的宇宙，有序但接近无限。到目前为止，进化所探索的这一领域只是一个微小的角落，目前其中包含了像蛋白质一样各种各样的奇迹，蛋白质可以像弹簧一样伸缩, 像轮子旋转,像活塞一样延伸，像棘轮一样转动, 把太阳能转化为化学能, 构建、拆除和回收的生活的所有组件以及做更多的事情。一些科学家已经在研究“新生”蛋白质，这些蛋白质远远超出了自然的舒适区，可以用来制造微小的机械装置和机器。随着AlphaFold这样的工具增加了分子设计的便捷性，将会有更多的工具出现。

Compared with the designs evolution has honed over billions of years such things as this will be the crudest of toys, at least to begin with. But who can say what new flesh the designers’ words will eventually bring into being? ■

AT THE START of the century, developing economies were a source of unbounded optimism and fierce ambition. Today South Africa is reeling from an insurrection, Colombia has suffered violent protests and Tunisia faces a constitutional crisis. Illiberal government is in fashion. Peru has just sworn in a Marxist as its president and independent institutions are under attack in Brazil, India and Mexico.

在本世纪初，发展中经济体是无限乐观和雄心壮志的源泉。今天，南非正因一场叛乱而不知所措，哥伦比亚遭受暴力抗议，突尼斯面临宪法危机。专制的政府正在流行。秘鲁一位马克思主义者刚刚宣誓就任总统，其在巴西、印度和墨西哥的独立机构也受到了攻击。

This wave of unrest and authoritarianism partly reflects covid-19, which has exposed and exploited vulnerabilities, from rotten bureaucracies to frayed social safety-nets. And as we explain this week, the despair and chaos threaten to exacerbate a profound economic problem: many poor and middle-income countries are losing the knack of catching up with the richest ones.

knack 习惯

这波动荡和专制主义在一定程度上源于covid-19，它暴露和利用了从腐败的官僚机构到破损的社会安全网的脆弱性。正如《经济学人》本周所解释的，绝望和混乱可能会加剧一个深刻的经济问题:许多贫穷和中等收入国家正在失去追赶最富裕国家的能力。

Our excess-mortality model suggests that 8m-16m people have died in the pandemic. The central estimate is 14m. The developing world is vulnerable to the virus, especially lower-middle-income countries where remote working is rare and plenty of people are fat and old. If you strip out China, non-rich countries have 68% of the world’s population but 87% of its deaths. Only 5% of those aged over 12 are fully vaccinated.

strip out 对..忽略不计

《经济学人》的超额死亡率模型显示，有800万至1600万人死于这场疫情。中央估计是1400万。发展中国家很容易受到这种病毒的影响，特别是中低收入国家，那里很少有远程工作，而且很多人都是肥胖和老年人。如果不考虑中国，非发达国家的人口占世界的68%，但死亡人数占世界的87%。12岁以上的人中，只有5%的人接种完整疫苗。

Alongside the human cost is an economic bill, since emerging markets have less room to spend their way out of trouble. Medium-term GDP forecasts for all emerging economies are in aggregate 5% lower than before the virus struck. People are angry and, even though protesting during a pandemic is risky, violent demonstrations around the world are more common than at any time since 2008.

除了死亡率高以外，还有经济账单的问题，因为新兴市场摆脱困境的空间越来越小。对所有新兴经济体的中期GDP预测总体上比疫情爆发前低5%。人们很愤怒，尽管在疫情期间抗议很危险，但世界各地的暴力示威活动比2008年以来的任何时候都更常见。

Rich places, such as America and Britain**, are no strangers to incompetence and turmoil**. But disappointment has hit emerging economies especially hard. In the early 2000s they buzzed with talk of “catch-up”: the idea that poorer countries could prosper by absorbing foreign technology, investing in manufacturing and opening up their economies to trade, as a handful of East Asian tiger economies had done a generation earlier. Wall Street coined the term BRICs to celebrate Brazil, Russia, India and China—the world economy’s new superstars.

像美国和英国这样的发达国家对无能和混乱并不陌生。但这种失望对新兴经济体的打击尤为严重。在21世纪初，他们谈论着“追赶”:贫穷国家可以通过吸收外国技术、投资制造业和开放贸易来实现繁荣，就像一些东亚的老虎经济体在上一代所做的那样。华尔街创造了“金砖四国”这个词来赞美巴西、俄罗斯、印度和中国，它们是世界经济的新超级明星。

For a while, catch-up worked. The proportion of countries where the level of economic output per head was growing faster than in America rose from 34% in the 1980s to 82% in the 2000s. The implications were momentous. Poverty fell. Multinational companies pivoted away from the boring old West. In geopolitics catch-up promised a new multipolar world in which power was more evenly distributed.

在一段时间内，追赶奏效了。人均经济产出增长速度高于美国的国家比例从1980年代的34%上升到2000年代的82%。这一结果意义重大。贫困下降了。跨国公司开始远离乏味的老西方。在地缘政治上，追赶有望形成一个新的多极世界，在这个世界里，权力分配更加均衡。

This golden age now looks as if it has come to a premature end. In the 2010s the share of countries catching up fell to 59%. China has defied many doomsayers and there have been quieter Asian success stories such as Vietnam, the Philippines and Malaysia. But Brazil and Russia have let down the BRICs and, as a whole, Latin America, the Middle East and sub-Saharan Africa are falling further behind the rich world. Even emerging Asia is catching up more slowly than it was.

let-down 失望

现在看来，这个黄金时代似乎已经提前结束了。在21世纪10年代，迎头赶上的国家所占比例降至59%。中国打败了许多悲观论者，越南、菲律宾和马来西亚等亚洲国家也有不张扬的成功故事。但巴西和俄罗斯让“金砖四国”失望了，整体而言，拉丁美洲、中东和撒哈拉以南非洲国家正进一步落后于发达国家。即使是新兴的亚洲也比过去追赶的速度更慢了。

Bad luck has played a part. The commodity boom of the 2000s fizzled out, global trade stagnated after the financial crisis and bouts of exchange-rate turbulence caused turmoil. But so has complacency as countries have come to think that fast growth was preordained. In many places basic services such as education and health care have been neglected. Crippling problems have been left unfixed, including South Africa’s idle power plants, India’s rotten banks and Russia’s corruption. Instead of defending liberal institutions, such as central banks and the courts, politicians have used them for their own gain.

preordain 注定

坏运气也起了一定作用。2000年代大宗商品繁荣逐渐消退，在金融危机后全球贸易停滞不前，汇率波动引发动荡。但随着各国开始认为快速增长是注定的，自满情绪也随之高涨。在许多地方，教育和医疗保健等基本服务被忽视。严重的问题一直没有得到解决，包括南非闲置的发电厂、印度腐败的银行和俄罗斯的腐败。政客们没有为中央银行和法院等自由机构辩护，而是利用它们为自己谋利。

What happens next? One risk is an emerging-market economic crisis as interest rates in America rise. Fortunately most emerging economies are less brittle than they were, because they have floating exchange rates and rely less on foreign-currency debt. Long-running political crises are a bigger worry. Research suggests that protests suppress the economy, which leads to further discontent—and that the effect is more marked in emerging markets.

接下来会发生什么? 其中一个风险是，随着美国利率的上升，新兴市场将面临经济危机。幸运的是，大多数新兴经济体都没有以前那么脆弱了，因为它们实行浮动汇率，对外债的依赖也更少了。长期的政治危机是更大的担忧。研究表明，抗议活动抑制了经济，这导致了进一步的不满，而且这种影响在新兴市场更为明显。

Even if emerging economies avoid chaos, the legacy of covid-19 and rising protectionism could condemn them to a long period of slower growth. Many of their people will remain unvaccinated until well into 2022. Long-term productivity could be lowered as a result of so many children having missed school.

即使新兴经济体避免了混乱，covid-19的遗留问题和不断上升的贸易保护主义可能会使它们陷入长期的增长放缓。他们中的许多人在2022年之前都不会接种疫苗。由于许多孩子不能上学，长期生产力可能会降低。

贸易也可能变得更加困难。XX正在转向国内，远离使其更加富裕的全面开放政策。如果这种情况持续下去，XX将永远不会像美国近几十年来对XX那样，成为贫穷国家消费需求的巨大来源。

The West’s increasing protectionism will also limit export opportunities for foreign producers which, in any case, will be less advantageous as manufacturing becomes less labour-intensive. Unfortunately, rich countries are unlikely to make up for it by liberalising trade in services, which would open up other paths to growth. And they may fail to help exposed economies such as Bangladesh—a success story—adapt to climate change.

西方日益增长的贸易保护主义也将限制外国生产商的出口机会，无论如何，随着制造业劳动密集型程度的降低，外国生产商的出口机会将变得越来越没有优势。不幸的是，发达国家不太可能通过开放服务贸易来弥补这一点，这将开辟其他增长途径。而且他们可能无法帮助像孟加拉国这样的经济体适应气候变化（这是一个成功的故事）。

Faced with this grim landscape, emerging markets may themselves be tempted to abandon open trade and investment. That would be a grave error. An unforgiving global environment makes it even more important for them to stick to policies that work. Turkey’s notion that raising interest rates causes inflation has been disastrous; Venezuela’s pursuit of socialism has been ruinous; and banning foreign firms from adding customers, as India just has with Mastercard, is self-defeating. When catching up is hard, those emerging markets which stay open will have the best chance.

unforgiving 严酷的

面对这种严峻的形势，新兴市场自身可能也会忍不住放弃开放的贸易和投资。这将是一个严重的错误。无情的全球环境使他们坚持行之有效的政策变得更加重要。土耳其认为加息会导致通胀的观点是灾难性的; 委内瑞拉对社会主义的追求是毁灭性的; 禁止外国公司增加客户，就像印度对Mastercard所做的那样会弄巧成拙。当追赶变得困难时，那些保持开放的新兴市场将有最好的机会。

Catch up, don’t give up

追啊，不要放弃

Some rules have changed: universal access to digital technologies is now vital, as is an adequate social safety-net. But the principles of how to get rich remain the same today as they ever were. Stay open to trade, compete in global markets and invest in infrastructure and education. Before the liberal reforms of recent decades, economies were diverging. There is time yet to avoid a return to the needless hardship of old. ■

一些规则已经改变:普及数字技术现在至关重要，一个适当的社会安全网也是至关重要的。但是如何致富的原则在今天和过去一样没有改变。保持贸易开放，在全球市场上竞争，投资基础设施和教育。在近几十年的自由改革之前，各国经济正在分化。现在避免回到过去那种不必要的苦难还来得及。■

The debate about who goes into the record books as “first billionaire in space” was made live on July 11th, when Sir Richard Branson, a Briton, flew to an altitude of 85km in a rocket-propelled plane built and owned by Virgin Galactic, a firm he founded. The lower bound of outer space is, by convention, something called the Kármán line. This is the altitude above which a craft relying on aerodynamic lift to keep itself aloft would have, because of the thinness of the air at high altitude, to travel faster to obtain that lift than the speed required to go into orbit around Earth.

The exact height of the Kármán line does, though, vary with atmospheric conditions. So again, by convention, it is usually pegged at 100km. This convention is recognised by the Fédération Aéronautique Internationale, the keeper of standards for such matters. One up to Mr Bezos.

然而，卡门线的确切高度会随着大气条件而变化。所以，按照惯例，它通常是在100公里。该惯例得到了国际航空联合会的认可，该组织是此类事务的标准管理机构。贝佐斯达到这一标准。

America’s armed forces, however, lack the means to reach such heights, but still want some of their own pilots recognised as astronauts. One up to Sir Richard.

然而，美国军队缺乏达到这一高度的手段，但仍希望自己的一些飞行员被认可为宇航员。因此他们转而宣称太空始于80千米的高度，这是大气层中层的上界。为了避免在外交上尴尬地区分本国宇航员和军事宇航员，美国航空航天局(NASA)承认了这一点。理查德爵士达到这一标准。

New Shepard itself is named after Alan Shepard, a NASA astronaut who was the first American to make a suborbital ballistic flight of the sort Mr Bezos has just experienced, which he did in 1961 on top of a modified Redstone intercontinental ballistic missile. Exergyn的董事长Tony Ennis认为这种冷却方法不仅比HFC更环保，而且购买成本更低，运行成本更低30-40%。它也会更轻、更小，这是一项重要的要求，尤其是因为空调设备通常要上提升支架才能安装。至于可靠性，他说，原型机的性能表明，其核心单元可以运行40多年而没有问题。

When Dr O’Toole, an expert in memory materials, co-founded Exergyn in 2012, his intention was to use nitinol to create a system that would generate electricity from low-grade waste heat. But the challenges presented by HFCs persuaded him that air conditioning would be a quicker way to bring the technology to market. Development is still continuing, but products may be only a few years away.

How to lead from afar

Bartleby

![image-20210727100629179](/Users/chenyujie/Library/Application Support/typora-user-images/image-20210727100629179.png)

A new book on remote leadership

Jul 24th 2021

WHEN OFFICE workers were sent home in the spring of 2020, managers suddenly faced a new challenge: how to supervise teams that were working remotely. While employees are now gradually heading back to their desks, a much greater share will work from home at least occasionally than before the pandemic. A new book, “Leading at a Distance”, by James Citrin and Darleen Derosa of Spencer Stuart, an executive-search firm, attempts to provide some practical tips for managers dealing with staff whom they do not see face to face.

当办公室员工在2020年春天被遣散回家时，管理者们突然面临着一个新的挑战:如何监督远程工作的团队。虽然员工现在逐渐回到办公桌前，但与疫情前相比，有更大比例的人至少偶尔在家工作。Spencer Stuart(一家高管猎头公司)的James Citrin和Darleen Derosa写了一本新书《远距离领导》，这本书试图为管理者们提供一些实用的技巧，帮助他们与没有面对面接触的员工打交道。

The authors are not in the gloomy camp that believes remote working is a disaster. They think it can be just as effective as face-to-face work. They point out that the ability to hire people who can work anywhere means that businesses will find it easier to develop more diverse workforces. A study by McKinsey, a consultancy, found that 70% of companies thought remote hiring would help in this respect.

camp 阵营

作者们并不认为远程工作是一场灾难（消极阵营认为是一场灾难）。他们认为这和面对面的工作一样有效。他们指出，雇佣在任何地方都能工作的人，意味着企业将更容易发展出更多样化的劳动力。麦肯锡咨询公司的一项研究发现，70%的公司认为远程招聘在这方面有帮助。

The book offers some useful advice. For starters, keep virtual teams small. The upper threshold seems to be around a dozen. A study found that 37% of low-performing teams had 13 or more members, compared with just 24% of high-performing teams. In addition, the best-performing teams tended to be drawn from one department, such as marketing, rather than from across the firm.

这本书提供了一些有用的建议。首先，让远程团队保持小规模。上限似乎在十几个左右。一项研究发现，37%的低绩效团队拥有13名或更多成员，而拥有13个或更多成员的高绩效团队只占24%。此外，表现最好的团队往往来自一个部门，如营销部门，而不是整个公司。

The trickiest part of the manager’s job is building rapport. It is easy for remote workers to feel isolated so supervisors should be in regular contact. But it is a tricky line to walk. There is a difference between checking in to see if someone needs support and constantly monitoring their progress. If team members feel they are being nagged, they will conclude their superiors don’t trust them.

rapport 融洽关系

管理者工作中最棘手的部分是建立融洽的关系。远程工作人员很容易感到孤立，所以管理人应该经常保持联系。但这是一条棘手的路线。查明某人是否需要支持和持续监控他们的进展是有区别的。如果团队成员觉得他们被唠叨了，他们会认为上司不信任他们。

Much communication will be by email, which has its advantages. It is easily shared, can be read several times to aid comprehension and can be referred to long after it is sent. 很多交流将通过电子邮件，这有它的优点。它很容易分享，可以多次阅读以帮助理解，可以在发送后很长时间内引用。但电子邮件也带来了细微差别丢失的风险。两位作者引用的研究表明，发件人认为语气中性的邮件，接收者会认为是消极的;而接收者认为中性的是那些邮件被发送者认为是积极的。

Mr Citrin and Ms Derosa also warn of the dangers of virtual meetings. Just because it is possible to schedule one does not mean it is necessary or wise to do so. Poorly run meetings do not just waste time, they jeopardise the ability to meet deadlines, adding to workers’ stress. And any meeting should be 20 or 50 minutes long, rather than 30 minutes or an hour, to allow for a gap between sessions in an hourly schedule.

In America the Securities and Exchange Commission is beginning to scrutinise the use of cryptocurrencies. Many crypto-exchanges set investors’ pulses racing in last year’s bitcoin rush. Now the market capitalisation of Coinbase, one of the biggest, has shrunk by half, or $56bn, since peaking after its listing in April.

另一个风险来自政治。世界各地的政府部门对让科技公司变得过于庞大或进入金融或医疗保健等受监管的市场越来越谨慎。作为对大型科技公司更广泛打击的一部分，XX最近破坏了某车的经营，在该公司在纽约进行680亿美元IPO几天后，禁止其应用在应用商店上架，表面上理由是滥用用户数据。这些举措降低了投资者对XX初创企业的兴趣，过去两个季度，XX初创企业的融资实际上有所下降。在美国，证券交易委员会开始审查加密货币的使用。在去年的比特币热潮中，许多加密交易所让投资者热血沸腾。如今，Coinbase（市值最大的公司之一）的市值自4月份上市后达到峰值以来，已缩水一半，至560亿美元。

Investors, then, had better beware. For everyone else, the unicorn stampede is a boon. Because venture investments involve mostly equity and little debt, even flops such as WeWork or cautionary tales like XX pose little risk to the financial system. So long as venture capital is bankrolling lossmaking startups while they offer subsidised services or develop clever new products, consumers have no reason to look the gift horned horse in the mouth. ■
