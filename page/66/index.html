Janet Yellen calls for a global minimum tax on companies. Could it happen?

Setting a floor

设置底限

![image-20210415100432973](/Users/chenyujie/Library/Application Support/typora-user-images/image-20210415100432973.png)

Janet Yellen呼吁在全球范围内对公司征收最低税。这会发生吗?

Many countries want to link a deal to the trickier issue of taxing rights on profits

许多国家想将一项协议与对利润征税的棘手问题联系起来

Apr 8th 2021

CORPORATE TAXATION is one of the thorniest issues in international economic policy. Janet Yellen, President Joe Biden’s treasury secretary, and a former head of the Federal Reserve, is duly weighing in. On April 5th she grabbed the attention of the occupants of corner offices worldwide with a speech to the Chicago Council on Global Affairs. The headline was a call for countries to agree on a global minimum tax rate for large companies.

thorny 棘手的

weigh in 发表有分量的意见

公司税是国际经济政策中最棘手的问题之一。前美联储主席、现任美国总统乔•拜登的财政部长Janet Yellen适时地加入了讨论。4月5日，她在芝加哥全球事务委员会发表了一篇演讲，引起了世界各地角落办公人员的注意。标题是呼吁各国就大型企业的全球最低税率达成一致。

Such a levy, Ms Yellen said, would help “make sure the global economy thrives based on a more level playing field”, and would help end a “30-year race to the bottom”. Though the idea of a minimum tax raises hackles in tax havens in the Caribbean, parts of Europe and farther afield, many other big economies will welcome America’s renewed commitment to multilateralism on tax after the prickly unilateralism of the Trump years.

make (sb’s) hackles rise 激怒某人

haven 避难所

prickly 棘手的

multilateralism

[释义] a situation in which several different countries or organizations work together to achieve something or deal with a problem

unilateralism

[释义] the process or fact of deciding a policy or action without involving another group or country

Yellen表示，这样的税收将有助于“确保全球经济在一个更加公平的竞争环境基础上蓬勃发展”，并将有助于结束“长达30年的触底赛跑”。尽管最低税率的想法在加勒比海、欧洲部分地区和更远的地方的避税天堂引起了愤怒，但许多其他大型经济体将欢迎美国在经历了特朗普时代棘手的单边主义之后，在税收方面重新承诺奉行多边主义。

Over the past decade, growing corporate-tax avoidance has met with a growing backlash. Breakneck globalisation allowed multinationals to replace fears of double taxation with the joys of double non-taxation, using havens to game the system. By exploiting mismatches between countries’ tax laws, taxable profits could be cut or even made to disappear. The rise of intan­gible assets, which can be shifted between jurisdictions more easily than buildings or machinery, made the game easier. Big tech has been a big beneﬁciary: the five largest Silicon Valley giants paid $220bn in cash taxes over the past decade, just 16% of their cumulative pre-tax profits.

breakneck 极快而危险的

multinational 跨国公司

double taxation 双重征税

[释义]a situation in which two or more governments charge tax on the same income or property

双重不征税：跨国企业利用不同国家、地区之间的税率、税制结构差异,采取转让定价、关联交易、转移利润等手段，将利润由税负较高的国家转向税负较低的国家。

jurisdiction 司法权；管辖权

在过去的十年里，越来越多的企业避税行为遭到了越来越强烈的反对。快速的全球化让跨国公司得以用“双重不征税”的乐趣，取代对双重征税的担忧，利用避税港来玩弄体系。通过利用各国税法之间的不匹配，可以削减甚至使应税利润消失。与建筑或机器相比，无形资产更容易在不同地区之间转移，这让游戏变得更容易。科技巨头一直是一大受益者:硅谷五大巨头在过去十年缴纳了2200亿美元的现金税，仅占其累计税前利润的16%。

Numerous sets of talks aimed at resolving the problem have been held under the auspices of the OECD, a club of mostly rich countries. Progress, however, has been slow. Frustrated, dozens of countries—from Belgium and Britain to India and Indonesia—have introduced or proposed (DSTs) on the local sales of foreign firms with online platforms. The Trump administration said these levies discriminated against American business and threatened tariffs.

under the auspices of … 在…的支持下

discriminate against sb 歧视某人

在经济合作与发展组织(OECD)的支持下，许多旨在解决这一问题的会谈已经举行。经合组织是一个主要由发达国家组成的俱乐部。然而，进展缓慢。令人沮丧的是，从比利时、英国到印度和印度尼西亚的数十个国家已经引入或提议(DSTs)允许外国公司通过网络平台在当地销售。特朗普政府表示，这些关税歧视美国企业，并威胁征收关税。

Yet the Trump administration had agreed to the idea of a minimum tax; indeed it enacted its own version as part of the Tax Cuts and Jobs Act in 2017. Mr Biden is pushing new reforms. He wants to raise the domestic federal corporate rate (partially reversing Donald Trump’s cuts) from 21% to 28%—and, crucially, increase the rate on American firms’ overseas profits from 10.5% to at least 21%, calculated on a country-by-country basis so that it captures all tax havens.

然而，特朗普政府同意了最低税的想法；事实上，它在2017年颁布了自己版本，作为“减税和就业法案”的一部分。拜登正在推动新的改革。他希望将国内联邦企业税率从21%提高到28%(部分扭转了唐纳德·特朗普的削减政策)，最关键的是，将美国企业海外利润的税率从10.5%提高到至少21%，以各国为基础计算，这样就能抓住所有的避税天堂。

The hope is that the receipts help fund a planned $2trn-plus upgrade to the country’s infrastructure. Republicans in Congress and groups representing big business complain that higher tax rates dent American competitiveness. That argument is blunted if other large economies agree to set a floor for the global rate.

receipts 进款

dent 削弱；削减

人们希望这些收入能够为该国计划中的2万亿美元以上的基础设施升级提供资金。国会的共和党人和代表大企业的团体抱怨说，更高的税率削弱了美国的竞争力。如果其他大型经济体同意为全球税率设定一个下限，这种说法就会变得很生硬。

The minimum tax is one of two “pillars” at the centre of the OECD-brokered negotiations. Talks were reasonably constructive, even with Team Trump, say officials. But queasiness over setting a floor persists, particularly among the EU’s lower-tax members, such as Ireland, with its lean corporate-tax rate of 12.5%. Were a global minimum set at 21%, American firms operating in Ireland—of which there are many—would have to pay top-up tax of 8.5% to their government, on top of the 12.5% paid to Dublin, undercutting the Irish advantage.

broker 掮客

queasiness 不安

最低税率是经合组织斡旋谈判的两大“支柱”之一。官员们表示，会谈相当有建设性，即使是与特朗普团队。但是对于设定最低税率的担忧仍然存在，尤其是在欧盟低税率成员国中，比如爱尔兰，其企业税率仅为12.5%。如果全球最低税率为21%，那么在爱尔兰经营的美国公司（有很多这样的公司），除了向爱尔兰政府缴纳12.5%的税之外，还要再向他们国家的政府缴纳8.5%的补税，这就削弱了爱尔兰的优势。

Moreover, most countries want negotiations over the two pillars kept together—and the second pillar is much less tractable. It involves finding a mutually acceptable way to carve up taxing rights over the profits of firms in markets where they have customers but lack a physical presence (as is often the case for firms like Amazon and Facebook outside America).

mutually 互相

carve up 划分

此外，大多数国家都希望就这两大支柱进行谈判，而第二个支柱则不那么容易处理。它涉及找到一种双方都能接受的方式，在那些有客户但没有实体店的市场上，对公司的利润划分征税权(就像在美国以外的地方，像亚马逊和Facebook这样的公司经常遇到的情况一样)。

Earlier this year it was reported that Ms Yellen had dropped the Trump administration’s proposal to let American companies opt in to any new system for allocating taxing rights (why any company would choose to do so is unclear). That removed a large obstacle to a deal, but by no means the only one. Many of the firms targeted by DSTs pay an outsize share of their taxes to America’s government. To strike a deal, Ms Yellen will have to be unusually willing to share with other countries.

opt in 选择参加

今年早些时候，有报道称Yellen放弃了特朗普政府的提议，即允许美国公司选择加入任何分配税权的新体系(目前尚不清楚为什么会有公司选择这么做)。这消除了达成交易的一个巨大障碍，但绝不是唯一的障碍。许多被DST盯上的公司向美国政府缴纳了巨额税款。要达成协议，Yellen 必须异乎寻常地愿意与其他国家分享。

The most optimistic voices talk of agreement on both pillars being sealed by the end of June. Many doubt that is possible. It took years to agree on plucking and chucking lower-hanging fruit, such as tax trickery involving intra-company loans, or the “Double Irish with a Dutch Sandwich”, which channelled profits through EU-based subsidiaries to tax havens like Bermuda and the Cayman Islands.

最乐观的声音是在六月底就两大支柱达成协议。许多人怀疑这是否可能。人们花了数年时间才同意摘取和放弃那些唾手可得的果实，比如涉及公司内部贷款的税务欺诈，或者“双重爱尔兰加荷兰三明治”，这种做法将利润通过设在欧盟的子公司转移到百慕大和开曼群岛等避税天堂。

A key variable is the rate at which the global minimum is set. Some officials think that, after all the horse-trading, it could be little more than the Irish rate of 12.5%—not very different from the average cash-tax rate that American tech firms actually pay. As for the reallocation of taxing rights, even its champions accept it may not skim much more than $10bn in extra revenue globally. The OECD estimates that corporate profit-shifting robs exchequers of $100bn-240bn a year.

horse-trading [尤指政治中的]精明交易

exchequer 国库

一个关键的可变因素是全球最小税率的设定。一些官员认为，经过一番讨价还价后，这一税率可能会比爱尔兰12.5%的税率略高一点，与美国科技公司实际支付的平均现金税率相差不大。至于税收权利的再分配，即使是它的拥护者也承认，它可能不会在全球范围内获得超过100亿美元的额外收入。经合组织(OECD)估计，企业利润转移每年使国库损失1000亿至2400亿美元。

Meanwhile, the Biden administration continues to flex muscle, even as it speaks with a softer voice than its predecessor. It is pressing on with plans to impose tariffs of as much as 25% on certain goods from six countries with DSTs, including Britain and Turkey. This is, perhaps, a tactic to encourage others to reach a deal at the OECD. If so, it is to be hoped that it works. The alternative is a global tit-for-tat as national tech levies become the norm.

flex one’s muscles 显示力量

tit-for-tat 针锋相对的

与此同时，尽管拜登政府讲话的声音比前任更为柔和，但它仍在继续展示自己的实力。它正在加紧计划，对包括英国和土耳其在内的6个实行DST的国家的某些商品征收高达25%的关税。这或许是一种鼓励其他国家在经合组织达成协议的策略。如果是这样，希望它能奏效。另一种选择是，随着国家科技税成为常态，全球展开一场针锋相对的较量。

House prices in the rich world are booming

发达国家的房价正在飙升

The race for space

争夺空间

![image-20210414143954471](/Users/chenyujie/Library/Application Support/typora-user-images/image-20210414143954471.png)

Unusually, suburbs not cities are feeling the heat

不同寻常的是，郊区正在感受这种“热度”, 而非城市

Apr 8th 2021

ON A SUNNY afternoon in Kingsmere, a new suburb of Bicester, a town 50 miles (80km) north-west of London, the streets are abuzz with people strolling and children playing. In ten years 1,600 homes have been built on the site, and another 900 are soon to follow. In the sales office for Bovis Homes, Flip Baglee says she has “never known it to be so busy”. Sentiment in Rhinebeck, a village 80 miles north of New York City, is similarly buoyant. Many of the properties advertised in the window of Gary DiMauro Real Estate—from mansions to cottages—are already taken.

be abuzz with or about or over sth 因某事物而嘈杂

stroll 溜达

buoyant 看涨的；繁荣的；乐观的

在一个阳光明媚的下午，在伦敦西北50英里(80公里)的比斯特镇(Bicester)的新郊区Kingsmere，街道上到处都是散步的人和玩耍的孩子。在10年里，1600栋房屋已经在这块土地上建成，另外900栋也将很快建成。在Bovis Homes的售楼处，Flip Baglee表示，她“从不知道这里会这么忙”。在纽约市以北80英里的Rhinebeck村，人们的情绪同样高涨。在加里·迪莫罗房地产公司(Gary DiMauro Real estate)的橱窗里打出广告的许多楼盘，从豪宅到别墅，都已经被抢走了。

Kingsmere and Rhinebeck are not the only places warming up. American house prices rose by 11% in the year to January, the fastest pace for 15 years. Those in Britain increased by 8% last year, and in Germany by 9%. The pattern is seen in much of the rich world (see chart 1). Across the 25 countries tracked by The Economist, real house prices have risen by an average of 5% in the latest 12-month period.

warm up 预热；使..活跃起来

Kingsmere和Rhinebeck并不是唯一活跃的地方。今年1月，美国房价上涨了11%，是15年来最快的涨幅。去年，英国的房价增长了8%，德国增长了9%。这种模式在大多数富裕国家都能看到(见表1)。在《经济学人》追踪的25个国家中，最近12个月的实际房价平均上涨了5%。

In many countries, rises have been rapid enough to attract the attention of politicians and central bankers. In a break with the pattern of the past decade it is prices in less populated, but still commutable, places, rather than city centres, that are rising most. Covid-19 seems to have set off a quest for space that could outlast the pandemic.

a break with sb/sth 与某人/某事物的决裂

set off 引发；触发

a quest for … 对…的追求 ‹knowledge, truth›

outlast 比..更长久

在许多国家，房价上涨速度之快足以引起政客和央行官员的注意。与过去十年的模式不同的是，在人口较少但仍可通勤的地方，而不是城市中心，房价上涨最多。Covid-19似乎引发了对可能比大流行持续时间更长的空间的探索。

![image-20210414143858883](/Users/chenyujie/Library/Application Support/typora-user-images/image-20210414143858883.png)

At first glance, the robustness of house prices in the face of the economic turmoil inflicted by covid-19 might seem baffling: property prices typically move in tandem with the economy. But furlough schemes and fiscal stimulus have limited distressed sales this time. Interest rates are ultra-low: in America those on 30-year mortgages are 1.5 percentage points below their level in 2010. Lockdowns and the reduced opportunity to spend mean that those who have kept their jobs have stashed away cash. Lucian Cook of Savills, a property consultancy in Britain, remarks that home values are being “driven by the haves rather than the have-nots”. In America, 14% of all mortgage applications made in February were for second homes, twice the share in April last year.

baffle 使困惑；阻隔

robustness 强劲

in tandem 一前一后地

stash away 藏匿

乍一看，面对covid-19造成的经济动荡，房价的坚挺似乎令人困惑:房地产价格通常与经济走势一致。但这次，休假计划和财政刺激措施限制了廉价出售。利率极低:在美国，30年期抵押贷款利率比2010年低1.5个百分点。封锁和消费机会的减少意味着那些保住工作的人已经把钱藏起来了。英国房地产咨询公司Savills的Lucian Cook表示：房价“是由富人而不是穷人推动的”。在美国，二月份所有的抵押贷款申请中有14%是二套房申请，是去年四月份的两倍。

As covid-19 spread and many countries locked down, people’s homes also became their offices, schools, gyms and bakeries. Many therefore spent more on their properties. Revenues for Home Depot, America’s largest DIY store, rose by 20% last year. In Britain permissions granted for home improvements, such as extensions, increased by a third in 2020 compared with the average in 2016-19, reckons Barbour ABI, a market-research firm.

improvement 整修

extension 扩建部分

随着covid-19的传播和许多国家的封锁，人们的家也变成了他们的办公室、学校、健身房和面包店。因此，许多人在房产上的花费更多。美国最大的DIY商店Home Depot去年的收入增长了20%。据市场研究公司Barbour ABI估计，在英国，与2016-19年的平均水平相比，2020年获准进行房屋装修(如扩建)的许可增加了三分之一。

Other people sought new places to live. Homes in America have taken an average of 47 days to sell since May, compared with 59 days in the previous year. In Britain a temporary holiday on stamp duty (a housing-transaction tax) caused the volume of sales to rise in the final quarter of 2020 to a 14-year high.

holiday 宽限期

其他人则寻找新的地方居住。自五月以来，美国的房屋平均需要47天才能售出，而去年需要59天。在英国，印花税(一种房屋交易税)的临时宽限期导致了2020年最后一个季度的销售量上升到14年来的最高点。

For those wanting more space the best solution has been to move out of city centres. Prices per square foot in London, for instance, are 40% higher than in surrounding counties. House prices in less densely populated, but still commutable, areas of Britain have risen faster over the past year than in more populous ones. A similar pattern is also evident in America (see chart 2).

populous 人口众多的

对于那些想要更多空间的人来说，最好的解决办法是搬出市中心。例如，伦敦每平方英尺的价格就比周边郡高出40%。在英国人口密度较低但仍然可以通勤的地区，房价在过去的一年里比人口稠密的地区上涨得更快。类似的情况在美国也很明显(见表2)。

![image-20210414143841656](/Users/chenyujie/Library/Application Support/typora-user-images/image-20210414143841656.png)

That bucks a trend of the past decade, when megacities such as London and New York surged ahead of quieter locations—a reversal that Zillow, an American property-listings firm, calls the “great reshuffling”. House prices outside Germany’s seven biggest cities rose by 11% last year, compared with 6% within them. Prices in Sydney’s northern beaches, within commuting range of the city, are up by 10%.

buck 抵抗

这与过去十年的趋势相反，当时像伦敦和纽约这样的特大城市的发展超过了那些安静的城市，美国房地产上市公司Zillow称这样的逆转为“大洗牌”。去年，德国七大城市之外的房价上涨了11%，而城市内部的房价上涨了6%。悉尼北部海滩（在该城市通勤范围内）价格上涨了10%。

By contrast, house prices in central London and Sydney rose by just 4% and 3% last year, respectively; those in Manhattan fell by 4%. Rental markets are cooling too. Rents for flats in Sydney fell by 5% last year. Those in Melbourne, which endured a 111-day lockdown last year, fell by 8%. Figures from Zillow suggest that rents fell by 9% in New York City and 15% in Manhattan in particular.

相比之下，伦敦市中心和悉尼的房价去年分别只上涨了4%和3%;曼哈顿则下降了4%。租赁市场也在降温。去年，悉尼的公寓租金下降了5%。墨尔本的公寓去年经历了111天的封锁期，其房价下降了8%。Zillow的数据显示，纽约的房租下降了9%，尤其是曼哈顿的房租下降了15%。

The pandemic has disrupted some of the usual flows into cities. In the years before covid-19, London lost residents to the rest of Britain. But the outflows were made up for by people coming in from abroad. The pandemic, and perhaps Brexit, seem likely to have reduced the inflow. One estimate suggests that London’s resident population may have declined by 8% in 2020. Australia’s borders have been shut to non-residents since March 2020. Recent graduates, faced with the prospect of working from a shared home, may still be living with their parents.

疫情阻断了一些正常流入城市的人流。在covid-19之前的几年里，伦敦的居民流失到英国其他地区。但是流出的人口被从国外涌入的人口所弥补。大流行，或许还有英国脱欧，似乎都可能减少了人口的流入。一项估计显示，2020年伦敦的常住人口可能会减少8%。自2020年3月以来，澳大利亚的边境一直不对非居民开放。近期的毕业生，面临着在共享的家庭工作的前景，可能仍然和他们的父母住在一起。

Outflows from cities have risen, too. A study by the Federal Reserve Bank of Cleveland finds that migration out of cities in America doubled to 56,000 people per month from March 2020 compared with the 2017-19 average.

从城市流出的人口也在增加。克利夫兰联邦储备银行的一项研究发现，与2017-19年的平均水平相比，从2020年3月起，美国城市外的移民人数翻了一番，达到每月5.6万人。

Some of the flows to cities will pick up as the pandemic ends. Students and international migrants will return in droves. Some investors are therefore betting big on big cities. A new development in Manhattan selling large flats for $12m offers the “premier answer to post-pandemic living”. AXA Investment Managers, a firm that owns properties in 15 countries, has snapped up 1,233 flats in the heart of London, Britain’s largest residential site.

pick up 恢复

The Ever Given is not just one of the world’s biggest container ships, it is also the emblem of a backlash that accuses globalisation of going too far. share out 平均分配

buck-passing 推卸责任

然而，Monnet的格言也让人自满。20世纪90年代南斯拉夫的内战导致了“现在是欧洲的时刻”的宣言。随之而来的是数年的大屠杀。同样，去年让欧盟委员会(European Commission)全权负责为4.5亿人购买和平均分配covid-19疫苗的决定，也是一场推卸责任的灾难。

It made sense to pool the research effort of 27 countries and their funds for pre-purchasing vaccines, just as Operation Warp Speed in America brought together 50 states. However, the EU’s bureaucracy mismanaged the contract negotiations, perhaps because national governments generally oversee public health. The project was handled chiefly by the commission president, Ursula von der Leyen, who gleefully called the decision to expand her empire a “European success story”.

pool 集中

bring together 使团结；把人召集起来

gleefully 高兴地

背景：美国政府正开展这项代号为“曲速行动”的项目，旨在将私营药企、军方及政府的资源整合起来，进行大规模的疫苗测试，以便将疫苗的开发时间缩短多达八个月。

将27个国家的研究成果和预先购买疫苗的资金集中起来是有意义的，就像美国的“曲速行动”(Operation Warp Speed)将50个州联合起来一样。然而，欧盟的官僚机构在合同谈判中管理不善，可能是因为各国政府通常对公共卫生进行监督。该项目主要由委员会主席Ursula von der Leyen负责，她兴高采烈地称自己这个扩张帝国的决定是“欧洲的成功故事”。

Hardly. Her team focused too much on price and too little on security of supply. They haggled pointlessly over liability should vaccines cause harm. Europe dithered in the August holidays. It was as if the Monnet-like forging of an ever-closer union was the real prize and the task of actually running vaccination a sideshow. Subsequent bickering, point-scoring and the threatened blockade of vaccine exports have done more to undermine faith in vaccination than restore the commission’s reputation. Were she still a member of a national government it is hard to see how Mrs von der Leyen could stay in her post.

haggle 争论

liability 不利条件；责任

dither 犹豫不决

sideshow 杂耍；次要事件

blockade 阻塞

没门。她的团队过于关注价格，而对供应安全关注过少。他们毫无意义地争论疫苗是否会造成损害的责任问题。欧洲在8月的假期中犹豫不决。就好像Monnet式的建立一个日益紧密的联盟才是真正的奖赏，而实际运作疫苗接种的任务只是个杂事。随后的争吵，得分和疫苗出口的威胁封锁对疫苗的信心的损害大于恢复委员会的声誉。如果她仍然是国家政府的一员，很难想象von der Leyen女士如何能继续留在她的职位上。

Europe has also fallen short economically. Again, it has used the pandemic to make institutional progress, by creating a meaty new instrument known as the Next Generation EU fund, or NGEU. Worth €750bn ($880bn), this is targeted mainly at weaker countries that need it most. More than half the money is grants not loans, lessening the effect on national debt. It is also being paid for by raising debt for which the union as a whole is jointly liable. That is welcome, because it creates a mechanism which severs the link between raising money and the creditworthiness of national governments. In future crises that could protect euro-zone countries from capital flight.

meaty 内容充实的

creditworthiness 信誉度

欧洲在经济上也有所欠缺。它再次利用这一流行病，创建了一个富有成效的新工具，称为“下一代欧盟基金”(Next Generation EU fund，简称NGEU)，以在体制上取得进展。这笔资金价值7500亿欧元(合8800亿美元)，主要针对最需要资金的较弱国家。超过一半的资金是赠款而不是贷款，减少了对国家债务的影响。它还通过举债来偿还，而这些债务是欧盟作为一个整体所共同承担的。这是受欢迎的，因为它创造了一种机制，切断了筹资与各国政府信誉之间的联系。在未来的危机中，这可以保护欧元区国家免受资本外逃的影响。

As with vaccines, however, triumph at the NGEU’s creation belies its slow execution. The first money is still months from being paid out, as member states scrap with the commission over their individual programmes. By the end of next year, only a quarter of the fund will have been disbursed.

belie 掩饰

然而，就像疫苗一样，NGEU的成功掩盖了它缓慢的执行。由于各成员国在各自的项目上与欧盟委员会争执不休，第一笔资金还需要几个月才能支付出去。到明年年底，只有四分之一的资金将得到支付。

This lack of urgency is a symptom of a much bigger problem: the neglect of the underlying health of Europe’s economies. Even with its new money, the EU budget will account for just 2% of GDP in the next seven-year fiscal period. At the national level, where governments typically spend about 40% of GDP, Europeans have been culpably overcautious.

culpably 应受责备地

overcautious 过分谨慎的

这种缺乏紧迫感是一个更大问题的症状:忽视了欧洲经济的基本健康状况。即使有了新资金，欧盟预算在接下来的7年财政期间也只会占到GDP的2%。在国家层面上，政府的支出通常占GDP的40%，欧洲人的过分谨慎是罪魁祸首。

The consequences will be profound. By the end of 2022, America’s economy is expected to be 6% larger than it was in 2019. Europe, by contrast, is unlikely to be producing any more than it did before the pandemic. True, Joe Biden’s $1.9trn stimulus after nearly $4trn in the Trump era risks overheating the economy, but Europe lies at the other extreme. Its budget deficits for 2021 average perhaps half of what America is planning. After the combination of the financial crisis and covid-19, the EU’s output will be 20%, or €3trn, smaller than if it had kept up the growth it managed in 2000-07. The EU has suspended its deficit-limiting fiscal rules. Thanks in part to the ECB’s monetary activism, European governments have the fiscal space to do more. They should use it.

后果将是深远的。到2022年底，美国经济预计将比2019年增长6%。相比之下，欧洲不太可能比疫情爆发前生产更多。的确，在特朗普时代推出近4万亿美元刺激计划后，乔•拜登(Joe Biden)推出的1.9万亿美元刺激计划存在经济过热的风险，但欧洲处于另一个极端。它2021年的平均预算赤字可能是美国的一半。在金融危机和covid-19的共同作用下，欧盟的产出将达到20%，即3万亿欧元，低于2000-07年保持增长的水平。欧盟已经暂停了限制赤字的财政规则。部分由于欧洲央行积极的货币政策，欧洲各国政府有足够的财政空间采取更多行动。他们应该使用它。

Ever-smaller union

越来越小的联盟

Europe can take comfort from the fact that the vaccination programme will catch up over the summer. Across the continent, Euroscepticism has been in decline during the pandemic, and politicians who used to flirt with leaving, like Matteo Salvini or Marine Le Pen, have changed their tune. But, inexorably, the EU is falling behind China and America because it fails to grapple competently with each successive crisis. In a dangerous and unstable world, that is a habit it needs to change. ■

inexorably 不可阻挡地

欧洲可以从“疫苗接种计划将在夏季赶上来”的事实中得到安慰。在整个欧洲大陆，欧洲怀疑主义在疫情大流行期间一直在下降，而那些曾经有意离开的政客，如Matteo Salvini或Marine Le Pen，也改变了他们的论调。但是，不可避免的是，欧盟正落后于中国和美国，因为它未能有效应对每一个连续的危机。在一个危险和不稳定的世界里，这是一种需要改变的习惯。■
