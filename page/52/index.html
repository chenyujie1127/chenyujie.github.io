Many of the Supreme Court’s decisions are reached with no hearings or explanation

最高法院的许多裁决没有听证会或缘由

![image-20210902100053371](/Users/chenyujie/Library/Application Support/typora-user-images/image-20210902100053371.png)

In the shadows

在影子里

The nine justices are making more and more use of the “shadow docket”

docket 备审案件表

九位大法官越来越多地使用“影子诉讼”

背景：The term “shadow docket” was first used by William Baude in his law review article from 2015. The term refers to decisions from the United States Supreme Court, which are decided outside of its regular docket without oral argument. Generally, the Supreme Court allows the parties to argue their points both in written briefs and orally before it issues a detailed explanation of its decision. However, cases on the shadow docket are decided on an application for emergency relief from a lower court’s order. The lower court has not made a final decision on the merits of the case by the time the case is decided on the shadow docket. Further, the Court’s decisions are rarely explained.

IN FIVE WEEKS the Supreme Court will return from its summer break to hear a batch of new disputes, including clashes over abortion and guns. After scrutinising briefs from litigants and amici curiae (friends of the court), the justices will hear oral argument in these cases and—weeks or months later—release opinions explaining why one party won and the other lost. But this methodically adjudicated “merits docket” represents a shrinking proportion of the Supreme Court’s notable business. Although the justices handle about five dozen cases this way each year (down from more than 150 in the 1980s), they dispatch thousands of other legal tangles without fanfare—and often with scant explanation.

methodically 有条不紊地

adjudicate 裁决

dispatch 迅速解决；迅速完成

在五周内，最高法院将结束夏季休假，审理一系列新的争端，包括堕胎和枪支分歧。在仔细审查诉讼当事人和法庭之友的案情摘要后，法官将听取这些案件的口头辩论，并在数周或数月后公布结果，结果中解释了为什么一方胜诉而另一方败诉。但是，这种经过有条不紊地裁决的“案情摘要”在最高法院的重要事务中所占的比例越来越小。尽管法官们每年以这种方式处理约50多起案件(从20世纪80年代的150多起下降到现在的50多起)，但他们却在没有大肆宣传的情况下处理了数以千计的其他法律纠纷，而且往往缺乏解释。

The so-called “shadow docket” (a term coined in 2015 by Will Baude of the University of Chicago) includes emergency appeals from parties who believe they would be irreparably harmed without quick intervention from the justices. Whereas cases arrive on the regular docket if at least four justices agree to take them up, anyone can try their hand at filing an emergency appeal. Most shadow-docket orders are unremarkable. Some, like that of the man seeking to lift the federal airline mask requirement so he could fly to Germany without triggering an anxiety disorder, border on the comical. (His application was denied.)

irreparably 不可弥补地; 无可救药地

border on 接近

comical 滑稽的

所谓的“影子诉讼”(芝加哥大学的Will Baude在2015年创造的一个术语)包括一些政党的紧急上诉，这些政党认为，如果没有法官的迅速干预，他们将受到不可挽回的伤害。如果至少有四名法官同意受理案件，那么案件就会被列入常规的诉讼程序，而任何人都可以尝试提出紧急上诉。大多数影子诉讼的案件都是很平凡。其中一些近乎滑稽，比如一名男子试图取消联邦航空公司对戴口罩的要求，这样他就可以飞往德国，而不会引发焦虑症。(他的申请被拒绝了。)

Other orders, including a pair this week, are big news. On August 24th, with the liberal justices in dissent, the court rejected President Joe Biden’s plea not to be compelled to reinstate Donald Trump’s border policy, which required asylum seekers to await word on their applications while living on the Mexican side of the southern border. And as The Economist went to press, the second iteration of Mr Biden’s moratorium on tenant evictions awaited a quick judgment.

reinstate 恢复；使复原

其他案件（包括本周的两个案件）都是大新闻。8月24日，在自由派大法官持异议的情况下，最高法院驳回了拜登总统的请求，即不要被迫恢复特朗普的边境政策，该政策要求寻求庇护者在居住在南部边境墨西哥一侧的情况下等待申请结果。就在本期《经济学人》付印之时，拜登关于暂停驱逐房客的第二次重申正在等待一个快速的判决。

It has been a busy month in the penumbras. On August 12th, acting on her own, Justice Amy Coney Barrett rebuffed eight students who claimed Indiana University’s covid-19 vaccine requirement violated their constitutional rights. No explanation accompanied the denial. Two hours later, the court agreed to block part of New York state’s moratorium on evictions in response to a plea from a group of landlords. This time the majority offered a short opinion—with a single sentence of justification—and the three liberal justices appended 1,000 words in dissent. Some were not impressed with the majority’s reasoning. On a podcast he co-hosts, Mr Baude, who clerked for Chief Justice John Roberts, panned the terse explanation, asking “How could this even be right?”

penumbra 半阴影

rebuff 断然拒绝

pan 抨击

在半阴影地带，这是忙碌的一个月。8月12日，Amy Coney Barrett法官断然回绝了八名学生，他们声称印第安纳大学的新冠疫苗要求侵犯了他们的宪法权利。否认的同时没有任何解释。两小时后，法院同意阻止纽约州暂停驱逐的部分措施，以回应一群房东的请求。这一次，大多数法官给出了一个简短的意见，只用一句话来说明理由，而三位自由派法官在异议中附加了1000个单词。有些人对多数人的理由不以为然。曾担任首席大法官John Roberts的助理的 Baude在与他人共同主持的一个播客中，严厉批评了这一简洁的解释，并问道:“这怎么可能是正确的?”

The shadow docket—officially, the “orders list”—has always been part of the Supreme Court’s work. Requests from condemned criminals to have their executions blocked or delayed routinely reach the justices this way. Of the 8,000 or so annual petitions to review a decision in a lower court, about 99% are denied. The justices also use the shadow docket to respond to litigants requesting extensions to file their briefs or to handle similarly quotidian matters. A few important orders are sprinkled through Supreme Court history: a (brief) execution reprieve for Julius and Ethel Rosenberg came on the orders list in 1953, as did a halt to the bombing in Cambodia in 1973.

影子诉讼，所谓的“命令清单”一直是最高法院工作的一部分。死刑犯要求阻止或推迟执行死刑的请求通常都是通过这种方式送达法官的。在每年约8000份要求下级法院复审判决的请愿书中，约99%被驳回。法官们也会使用影子诉讼程序来回应诉讼当事人提出的延期提交诉讼摘要或处理类似的日常事务的请求。在最高法院的历史上，流传着几项重要的命令:1953年，Julius和Ethel Rosenberg的(短暂)死刑缓期执行出现在命令列表上，1973年，停止对柬埔寨的轰炸也出现在命令列表上。

Historically, though, the Supreme Court has adjudicated few matters of consequence in the shadows. That changed in 2017 when several policies of the Trump administration ran aground in lower courts. A litany of Mr Trump’s moves—from a ban on travel from Muslim countries and diversion of funds to pay for his border wall to a prohibition on trans soldiers and aggressive use of the federal death penalty—were blocked by federal judges, prompting the administration to beg the Supreme Court to intercede. According to Steve Vladeck, a law professor at the University of Texas, the Trump administration filed a staggering 41 emergency applications in its four years—compared with just eight during the 16-year period when George W. Bush and Barack Obama sat in the Oval Office. That is a 20-fold increase in presidential use of the shadow docket. And it paid off: of Mr Trump’s 41, the justices at least partially came through for him 28 times.

litany 枯燥冗长的陈述

然而，从历史上看，最高法院很少在影子诉讼中裁决重大事件。2017年，当特朗普政府的几项政策在下级法院搁浅时，情况发生了转变。特朗普的一系列举措都被联邦法官阻止，从禁止从穆斯林国家旅行和转移资金支付边境墙，到禁止变性士兵和激进使用联邦死刑，这就促使政府请求最高法院进行调解。德克萨斯大学法学教授Steve Vladeck表示，特朗普政府在四年任期内提交了令人震惊的41份紧急申请，而在布什和奥巴马担任总统期间的16年里，这一数字仅为8份。这是总统使用影子诉讼案件的20倍。而且，这是值得的:在特朗普的41份紧急申请中，部分法官有28次通过了他的申请。

These results may not be surprising given the court’s increasingly conservative complexion. But outcomes aside, the spike in shadow-docket cases involving issues of national importance—including battles over election rules and public-health measures to fight covid-19—highlights a deficit of transparency and accountability. The court usually affirms or denies a request without a word of explanation, though sometimes a short opinion (or dissent) is attached. Individual justices decide whether to reveal their votes—they usually remain hidden. With only a week or so between application and decision, time for briefing is limited and the justices do not have the benefit of questioning the parties in an oral argument.

考虑到法院日益保守的形象，这些结果可能并不令人惊讶。但撇开结果不谈，涉及国家重要性问题的影子案件激增，包括围绕选举规则和抗击covid-19的公共卫生措施的斗争，这突显出透明度和问责制的不足。法院通常在没有任何解释的情况下确认或拒绝请求，尽管有时会附上简短的意见(或异议)。法官个人决定是否公开他们的投票，他们通常选择隐藏。在申请和裁决之间只有一个星期左右的时间，诉讼案情摘要的时间是有限的，法官无法在口头辩论中询问当事人。

What is to be done? Mr Vladeck, who testified before a House subcommittee investigating the shadow docket in February, wants to see the court include “at least a brief explanation” whenever it makes a change to the status quo. Leah Litman, a law professor at the University of Michigan, says a justification is particularly important when the Supreme Court reinstates a death sentence that has been blocked by a lower court. Mr Vladeck would also like the justices to own up to their votes by identifying themselves and, for important issues that do not require immediate resolution, hold fast-tracked oral arguments before rendering a judgment. The court’s pandemic-era pivot towards hearings by telephone may supply a handy way to do this.■

fast-tracked 快速的

该怎么办呢? Vladeck克先生在今年二月众议院一个小组委员会调查影子诉讼时证实，他希望看到法庭在改变现状时“至少有一个简短的解释”。密歇根大学的法学教授 Leah Litman说，当最高法院恢复被下级法院否决的死刑判决时，一个正当的理由就显得尤为重要。Vladeck先生还希望法官们通过表明自己的身份来承认自己的投票，对于不需要立即解决的重要问题，在做出判决之前进行快速的口头辩论。在疫情期间，法院转向电话听证，这可能提供了一种方便的方式。■

France’s Greens prepare to pick a standard-bearer

Green 主张环保的从政者

法国绿党准备挑选一位领袖

Green list

![image-20210901092434096](/Users/chenyujie/Library/Application Support/typora-user-images/image-20210901092434096.png)

The party’s militant base wants to fight climate change without nuclear power

党内的激进分子希望在没有核能的情况下应对气候变化

THESE ARE succulent times to be a French green. Fully 81% of respondents to a recent poll said climate change was a “very serious problem”, up from 69% in 2015. Recent wildfires, floods and a late frost are a grim reminder of environmental dangers. In local elections last year Europe Écologie Les Verts, the French Green party, won control of some big cities, including Bordeaux, Lyon and Strasbourg. And the rise of the German Greens has raised hopes of a similar breakthrough in France, ahead of presidential elections next year.

法国绿党“Europe Écologie Les Verts”是法国的一个环境保护主义政党，成立于2010年11月13日,由绿党和欧洲生态两党合并而成。

对于法国绿党来说，现在是多汁的时期。在最近的一次民意调查中，整整81%的受访者表示气候变化是一个“非常严重的问题”，这一比例高于2015年的69%。最近的野火、洪水和晚霜都是对环境危险的严峻提醒。在去年的地方选举中，法国绿党“Europe Écologie Les Verts”赢得了一些大城市的控制权，包括Bordeaux、Lyon和Strasbourg。德国绿党的崛起也让法国在明年总统大选前有望取得类似的突破。

So it is with a spring in their step that the Greens are preparing to pick a presidential candidate, at a two-round vote in September. Five candidates are standing, and the race is peculiarly open, not least because only 17,000 people have so far signed up to vote. This puts the choice in the hands of party devotees who, says one Green, “are militant and clearly to the left of the Socialists”. The Greens could well end up with a candidate who delights the radical base, but repels voters.

spring 弹性

have a spring in one’s step 脚步轻快

因此，绿党“精神抖擞，步伐轻快地”正准备在9月举行的两轮投票中选出一名总统候选人。有五名候选人参选，而且竞选特别公开，尤其是因为到目前为止只有17000人报名参加投票。一位绿党人士说：“这就把选择权交给了党内的支持者，他们是激进的，显然是社会主义者的左派”。绿党最终很可能会得到一位让激进分子欢欣鼓舞但又让选民反感的候选人。

The French Greens emerged from the anti-nuclear movement of the 1960s, and mix radical environmentalism with calls for more social intervention. Bizarrely, they want to scrap nuclear power, although it generates 70% of France’s electricity while emitting no carbon. They vow to reintroduce a wealth tax, which President Emmanuel Macron ended, and want to shrink the working week from 35 to 32 hours. The party is “clearly to the left of the German Greens”, says a member.

Bizarrely 古怪地

法国绿党源于20世纪60年代的反核运动，并将激进的环保主义与呼吁更多社会干预结合起来。奇怪的是，核能产生了法国70%的电力，而且不排放碳，但是他们想要废弃核能。他们誓言要重新引入总统马克龙终止的财富税，并希望将每周工作时间从35小时减少到32小时。一名成员称该党“显然是德国绿党的左倾党派”。

Yannick Jadot, a national leader, is the most familiar candidate, having won the Greens’ nomination for the presidential election in 2017. He is considered the most moderate, and has distanced himself from the far left and the party’s “anti-growth” campaigners (among them Delphine Batho, a rival candidate). Yet les écolos have an impressive history of disregarding favourites, as Mr Jadot knows, having defeated the previous one, Cécile Duflot.

国家领导人Yannick Jadot是最广为人知的候选人，他在2017年赢得了绿党总统候选人提名。他被认为是最温和的，并与极左和该党的“反增长”活动人士(其中包括竞争对手Delphine Batho)保持距离。然而，正如Jadot所知，les écolos在击败前一位Cécile Duflot之后，才有着无视大众的一段令人印象深刻的历史。

Outsider appeal could help Sandrine Rousseau, a radical economist. A year before the #MeToo movement, she called out a Green deputy for sexual harassment. Ms Rousseau calls herself an “eco-feminist”: against “all forms of predation”, whether of the planet or the human body, and against the attitude that “you take, you use, you throw away”. This chimes with a younger generation, particularly women.

对局外人的呼吁可以帮助激进的经济学家Sandrine Rousseau。在 #MeToo运动的一年前，她曾指责一名绿党议员性骚扰。Rousseau女士称自己为“生态女权主义者”:反对“一切形式的掠夺”，无论是对地球还是对人类的掠夺，反对“你拿，你用，你扔掉”的态度。这迎合了年轻一代，尤其是女性。

Another potential pick of the militant base is Eric Piolle, mayor of Grenoble. He advocates a “humanist arc”, reaching from the Socialists to the far left. Boosted by re-election in 2020, he has been criss-crossing France to show he is not just a big-city, bicycle-riding type. Asked why the Greens struggle to speak to those outside big cities, Mr Piolle retorts: “On the contrary, it’s the president who speaks only to those in big cities.” Recent weather-related disasters, he says, have shifted rural views.

criss-cross 纵横交错

另一个潜在的政党基层分子人选是Grenoble市长 Eric Piolle。他主张一种“人文主义弧线”，从社会主义者延伸到极左翼。受2020年连任的鼓舞，他一直在法国各地奔走，以表明他不只是一个在大城市骑自行车的人。当被问及绿党为何竭力与大城市以外的人对话时，Piolle反驳道:“恰恰相反，只有总统才会与大城市的人对话。” 他说，最近与天气有关的灾害已经改变了农村的观点。

The Greens’ big-city image lingers, though. Mr Jadot hinted as much by insisting last week that he is not into “agri-bashing”. Smallholder farmers in la France profonde, who put pesticides on their fields and petrol in their vehicles, resent being told they are irresponsible. And France learned from the gilets jaunes the perils of increasing the carbon tax on motor fuel—although Mr Piolle argues that the protests were in fact more about “social justice”. Ordinary people felt punished, he says, while the rich, who take flights for weekend trips, were getting tax breaks. The movement has since faded.

不过，绿党的大城市形象依然存在。Jadot上周也暗示了这一点，他坚称自己不会“打击农业”。法国内地的小农们在田地里喷洒杀虫剂，在汽车里加油，他们对被告知他们不负责任而感到愤怒。法国也从“黄背心运动”中学到了提高汽车燃料碳税的危险，尽管Piolle认为抗议活动实际上更多的是为了“社会公正”。他说，普通人感到受到了惩罚，而周末乘飞机旅行的富人则获得了税收减免。自那以后，这场运动逐渐消失。

The Greens also suffer from a reputation for dotty ideas. The mayor of Bordeaux cancelled the city’s annual Christmas tree. The mayor of Lyon lambasted the Tour de France, a national treasure. When Mr Piolle denounced the roll-out of 5G, Mr Macron mocked those who want to emulate an “Amish model”. A lack of experience has largely protected the party from the messy compromises that government imposes.

绿党也因其愚蠢的想法而臭名昭著。Bordeaux 的市长取消了该市一年一度的圣诞树活动。Lyon 的市长痛斥环法自行车赛（这可是国宝）。当Piolle谴责5G的推出时，马克龙嘲笑了那些想效仿“阿米什模式”的人。缺乏经验在很大程度上保护了该党不受政府强加的混乱妥协的影响。

At stake is more than the Greens’ own presidential prospects. Their choice will also help determine whether the Greens or the Socialists become the main force on the French left in coming years. A similar battle is also being fought on the right, between moderate nationalists who seek to win votes back from the hard-nationalist Marine Le Pen and centre-right politicians who hope to peel voters from Mr Macron.

面临危险的不仅仅是绿党自己的总统竞选前景。他们的选择也将决定绿党还是社会党在未来几年成为法国左翼的主要力量。右翼也在进行类似的斗争，温和民族主义者试图从强硬民族主义者Marine Le Pen手中夺回选票，而中右翼政客则希望从马克龙手中夺回选民。

Much depends on whether the left can unite behind one candidate. Alone, Mr Jadot scores 7-8% in polls. Unless rivals sink their differences the Greens will face Socialist, Communist and other rivals too. The space to the left of Mr Macron is as crowded as a Paris cycle lane. Which is good news for the president. ■

这在很大程度上取决于左派能否团结起来支持一位候选人。仅 Jadot一人就在民调中获得了7-8%的支持率。除非竞争对手消除分歧，否则绿党也将面临社会主义、共产主义和其他对手的挑战。马克龙左翼空间就像巴黎的自行车道一样拥挤。这对总统来说是个好消息。■

Amazon’s department-store plans are less surprising than they appear

亚马逊的百货商店计划并不像看上去那么令人惊讶

Jeff & Andy’s

杰夫&安迪

![image-20210831095251715](/Users/chenyujie/Library/Application Support/typora-user-images/image-20210831095251715.png)

The e-commerce giant gets physical

这家电商巨头有了实体店

“THE WORLD wants you to be typical …Don’t let it happen,” Jeff Bezos warned in April in his last annual shareholder letter as CEO of Amazon. Hence bewilderment that his e-empire is to adopt a retail format that is very typical indeed: the department store. Having helped drive many chains out of business, it is now eyeing the format to boost its own retail fortunes.

今年4月，杰夫·贝佐斯在其担任亚马逊首席执行官的最后一封年度致股东信中警告称:“世界希望你成为典型……不要让这种事发生。”因此，令人困惑的是，他的电子帝国将采用一种非常典型的零售模式:百货商店。在促使许多连锁店倒闭之后，它现在正着眼于这种模式来提升自己的零售财富。

As a company, Amazon is entering a more mature phase. Now with a new chief executive, Andy Jassy, it is being forced to recognise that pure e-commerce has limits. It is also facing fresh competition from conventional retailers like Walmart and Target that are belatedly showing that they, too, can do the internet well.

belatedly 迟来地

作为一家公司，亚马逊正进入一个更加成熟的阶段。如今，在新任首席执行官安迪•贾西的带领下，亚马逊被迫承认，纯粹的电子商务有其局限性。它还面临着来自沃尔玛和塔吉特等传统零售商的新竞争，这些迟来地零售商表明，他们也能在互联网上做得很好。

Why women need the office

为什么女性需要去办公室

Bartleby

巴托比

![image-20210830152153741](/Users/chenyujie/Library/Application Support/typora-user-images/image-20210830152153741.png)

Female workers should think twice before opting for more remote work

在选择远程工作之前，女性应该三思而后行

IT IS A truth universally acknowledged that women carry a heavier burden than men when it comes to child care and household chores. It became truer still during the pandemic home-working experiment, and is likely to hold in the likely hybrid future of part-remote work. It is tempting for some women never to set foot in the office again, if their firms allow it, so they can devote time otherwise wasted on commuting or office chit-chat to more pressing family matters. According to research by Nicholas Bloom of Stanford University and colleagues, 32% of college-educated American women with children want to work remotely full-time, compared with 23% of comparable men.

众所周知，在照顾孩子和做家务方面，女性的负担比男性更重。在疫情期间的家庭工作尝试中，这一点变得更加真实，而且未来半远程工作的混合办公的情况可能保持不变。对于一些女性来说，如果公司允许，她们就再也不去办公室了，这样她们就可以把原本浪费在通勤或办公室闲聊上的时间花在更紧迫的家庭事务上。根据斯坦福大学的Nicholas Bloom及其同事的研究，32%受过大学教育、有孩子的美国女性希望全职远程工作，而同等情况的男性这一比例只有23%。

Such decisions are completely understandable—not least because besides more responsibilities at home, women’s lot at work can be no picnic, either. Female managers often end up playing the conventional male and female roles, leading the pack while also nurturing those left behind. It can be tiresome to be many things at the same time.

it’s no picnic! 这不是件轻松愉快的事！

这样的决定是完全可以理解的，不仅仅是因为女性需要承担更多的家庭责任，女性在工作中也不是那么轻松的。女性管理者往往最终扮演传统的男性和女性角色，在领导团队的同时也照顾那些落后的人。同时做很多事情是很烦人的。

Understandable, but still regrettable. Some reasons for that are mundane. Your columnist, a guest female Bartleby, finds that the office offers a welcome break from the never-ending duties of housekeeping and parenting. Other reasons are mercenary. One pre-pandemic study on work-life balance suggested that women were likelier than men to experience “flexibility stigma”.

可以理解，但仍然令人遗憾。其中的一些原因很平常。你的专栏作家、特邀女性巴托比发现，在无休止的家务和育儿职责之外，办公室提供了一个受欢迎的休息时间。其他原因是为了金钱。疫情前一项关于工作与生活平衡的研究表明，女性比男性更有可能遭遇“灵活性耻辱”。

In the wake of covid-19 flexible work arrangements are less stigmatised (for now). A recent British government report warned that their uptake may be unequal between the genders. If more women work from home, and take on an even greater share of family responsibilities, the result may be an ever-bigger gender pay gap and an ever-harder glass ceiling.

在新冠肺炎疫情之后，灵活的工作安排(目前)较少受到指责。英国政府最近的一份报告警告说，男女对这一问题的理解可能不平等。如果更多的女性在家工作，承担更多的家庭责任，结果可能会是更大的性别收入差距和更硬的玻璃天花板。

There is another, more elusive reason why women who do not return to the office are missing out. Not every workplace is as informal as The Economist’s (with its deadpan humour and discussions of muscle tone and QE, alcohol consumption and the equity risk premium). Yet even in duller corporate settings, walking down a corridor, washing hands in the bathroom or making yet another cup of coffee in the kitchen, you are only seconds away from a chat or a joke. That can—admittedly unreliably and in ways that are difficult to measure—spur spontaneity and lead to new ideas.

spontaneity 自发性

还有另一个更难以捉摸的原因，解释为什么不重返职场的女性正在错失良机。并不是所有的工作场所都像《经济学人》那样轻松友好(《经济学人》的工作场所有着假面幽默、关于肌肉张力和QE的讨论、饮酒和股权风险溢价)。然而，即使是在更沉闷的公司环境中，走在走廊上，在浴室里洗手，或者在厨房里再冲一杯咖啡，几秒钟后你就会听到一段聊天或一个笑话。诚然，这是不可靠的，而且难以衡量的，但却能激发自发性，产生新想法。

Compared with that, virtual collaboration is like evaporated milk with 60% of its water removed: safer, mostly up to the job but a sterile version of face-to-face interaction that leaves an unsatisfying aftertaste. Physical proximity brings higher risks (once of death or injury by an enemy, today of a face-to-face snub, more painful than a mean tweet, or of a covid-19 infection). It also brings higher rewards, including emotional ones that are no less important than the pragmatic sort.

与之相比，远程协作就像去掉了60%水分的牛奶:更安全，基本上可以胜任工作，但却是一种“无结果”的面对面互动，余味令人不满意。身体上的接近会带来更高的风险(曾经是被敌人杀死或伤害，今天是面对面的冷落，比一条刻薄的推特更痛苦，或covid-19感染)。它也会带来更高的回报，包括情感上的回报，而情感上的回报并不亚于务实的回报。

Though times have changed, many female workers, including Bartleby, find themselves sympathising with Irina, one of the titular “Three Sisters” in Anton Chekhov’s play from 1900. Holed up with her two siblings in the countryside she longs for Moscow—not only its vibrancy and worldliness but the opportunity it affords for work. Her frantic desire to work reflects an attempt to escape the tedium of domesticity, and invest life with meaning by imposing a framework and a sense of accountability. Many modern executives, male and female, would recognise Chekhov’s belief that being guarded from work is a curse, not a blessing. The same goes for being shielded from the office, notwithstanding its myriad complications.

hole up 躲藏

domesticity 家庭生活

notwithstanding 尽管

myriad 数不清的

尽管时代变了，但包括巴托比在内的许多女性工人都对 Irina表示同情。 Irina是契诃夫1900年戏剧《三姐妹》之一。她和两个兄弟姐妹躲在乡下，她渴望去莫斯科，不仅是因为它的活力和世俗，还有它提供的工作机会。她对工作的疯狂渴望反映出她试图逃离单调乏味的家庭生活，并通过强加框架和责任感赋予生活以意义。许多现代高管，无论男女，都会认同契诃夫的观点，即不去工作是一种诅咒，而不是祝福。同样的道理也适用于远离办公室，尽管它有无数的复杂情况。

There are downsides to being a clinically efficient flexiworker. They include relinquishing the daily banter and sense of complicity among colleagues, many of whom double as friends. Women determined not to waste a single minute when they could be multitasking will give up more than just professional advancement, important though that is. They are also giving up a sense of connection to others. Hyper-efficiency and distance mean less opportunity for interpersonal tension but also less gratuitous joy, which is hard to replicate on Zoom.

做一个客观地高效的弹性工作者也有缺点。其中包括放弃同事之间的日常打趣和共谋感，其中许多同事也是朋友。如果女性下定决心不浪费一分钟的时间来处理多个任务，那么她们将放弃的不仅仅是职业晋升，尽管这很重要。他们也放弃了与他人联系。超高的效率和距离意味着人际关系紧张的机会减少了，但无端的快乐也减少了，这种快乐在Zoom上是很难复制的。

Those brief moments of joy are an important part of working life. It is nowhere and everywhere, like seeing the Virgin Mary in burnt toast. It is to be treasured precisely because it does not last. Bartleby recommends squandering precious minutes, here and there, on camaraderie and pointless glee. The cost, in the tedious aspects of office life, is tolerable. The returns, emotional as well as practical, can be immense.

这些短暂的快乐时光是工作生活的重要组成部分。它无处不在，就像在烤面包片上看到圣母玛利亚。它之所以值得珍惜，正是因为它不会长久。巴托比建议把宝贵的时间浪费在友情和毫无意义的欢乐上。在单调乏味的办公室生活方面，成本是可以承受的。无论是情感上还是实际上，回报都是巨大的。

Despite his shortcomings, Jerome Powell should be reappointed Fed chairman

虽然杰罗姆·鲍威尔有缺点，但他应该被再次任命为美联储主席

The Powell punt

![image-20210829101218371](/Users/chenyujie/Library/Application Support/typora-user-images/image-20210829101218371.png)

It would be a bad time to cast any doubt on the Fed’s independence

现在对美联储的独立性提出任何质疑都不是时候

WHEN NOMINATING the chairman of the Federal Reserve, America’s president picks the person who wields the greatest immediate power over the global economy. Fed leadership brings with it control over American interest rates, responsibility for an $8.3trn balance-sheet and the obligation to regulate the world’s most important banks. A mistake in the Eccles building can throw millions out of work, roil global markets or unleash inflation. The Fed even props up offshore financial markets by offering foreign central banks a ready supply of dollars.

在提名美联储主席时，美国总统挑选的是对全球经济拥有最大直接权力的人。美联储领导意味着对美国利率的控制，对8.3万亿美元资产负债表的责任，而且还有义务监管世界上最重要的银行。Eccles大楼内部的一个错误可能导致数百万人失业、扰乱全球市场或引发通胀。美联储甚至通过向外国央行提供现成的美元供应来支撑离岸金融市场。

The fate of Jerome Powell, the incumbent chairman whose term expires in February, lies in President Joe Biden’s hands. It is unclear how history will view Mr Powell’s first term. Before the pandemic, his loose monetary policy helped unemployment reach historic lows without provoking inflation. When the economy locked down in the spring of 2020, the Fed prevented a collapse of financial markets with enormous and swift interventions, some of which, such as the implicit underwriting of the corporate-bond market, were unprecedented. In August 2020 the central bank fine-tuned its 2% inflation target, which it had hitherto mostly undershot, by making it apply on average over the economic cycle, meaning that the Fed would have to compensate for its past misses.

undershot 未达到

明年2月任期结束的现任主席杰罗姆·鲍威尔的命运掌握在拜登总统的手中。目前尚不清楚历史将如何看待鲍威尔的第一个任期。在疫情之前，他的宽松货币政策在没有引发通胀的情况下，帮助失业率降至历史低点。当经济在2020年春封锁起来，美联储通过大规模和迅速的干预阻止了金融市场的崩溃，其中一些干预措施是前所未有的，如对公司债券市场的隐性承销。2020年8月，美联储微调了2%的通胀目标（迄今为止，这一目标大多还没达到），使其适用于整个经济周期的平均水平，这意味着美联储将不得不弥补过去的失误。

Recently, though, Mr Powell’s hand has looked less steady. By the measure the Fed targets, inflation has surged to 4% on a year earlier, or 6.4% if you annualise quarter-to-quarter price increases. The central bank’s failure to foresee rising prices has made its promises that inflation will subside less reassuring. The Fed could yet be forced into an abrupt reversal of stimulus.

不过，最近，鲍威尔的立场似乎不那么稳定了。按照美联储的目标衡量，通货膨胀率比去年同期飙升至4%，如果按季度环比价格上涨计算，则为6.4%。央行未能预见到物价的上涨，这使得它关于通胀将消退的承诺变得不那么令人放心。美联储仍有可能被迫突然逆转刺激政策。

In addition, the new inflation target, though desirable in theory, remains vague and poorly understood in practice. And the central bank is persisting with $120bn per month of quantitative easing (QE), the buying of bonds with newly created money. Today’s QE is unlikely to benefit the economy directly, but it creates hidden financial risks for taxpayers, because it involves the Fed, which they underwrite, issuing short-term reserves to buy long-term bonds. If short-term rates rise, the interest bill the Fed must pay on those reserves will soar.

此外，尽管新的通胀目标在理论上是可取的，但在实践中仍是模糊的，理解不足。此外，美联储还坚持每月1200亿美元的量化宽松政策，即用新发行的货币购买债券。今天的量化宽松不太可能直接惠及经济，但它为纳税人带来了隐藏的金融风险，因为这涉及到他们所担保的美联储发行短期储备以购买长期债券。如果短期利率上升，美联储必须为这些储备支付的利息将大幅增加。

Despite this mixed record, Mr Biden should reappoint Mr Powell. That is partly because any replacement the White House is likely to consider would probably be even keener on prolonging QE, and may also be sceptical of the welcome tidy-up of bank regulations that Mr Powell has also presided over. (The leading such candidate is Lael Brainard, a Fed governor.) But the most important reason to keep Mr Powell in his job is that this would be a terrible moment to give the impression that the White House wants to influence the Fed.

尽管鲍威尔的履历好坏参半，但拜登应该重新任命他。部分原因是，白宫可能考虑的任何继任者都可能更热衷于延长量化宽松，而且可能对鲍威尔主持的受欢迎的银行监管改革持怀疑态度。(目前领先的候选人是美联储理事莱尔•布雷纳德。) 但让鲍威尔留任的最重要原因是，现在给人们留下白宫想要影响美联储的印象，将是一个糟糕的时机。

In the short term, the danger is that high inflation proves stubbornly persistent. With Mr Powell in charge, that would look like an honest mistake, one that his record suggests he would try to correct quickly. In 2018 the Fed raised rates as the economy heated up; in June it signalled that it would respond to higher inflation with tighter monetary policy, a plan markets took to be credible. However, with a Biden appointee at the helm, chosen primarily for his or her fondness for stimulus, the Fed might look insufficiently independent and thus have to raise rates more—and thus slow the economy more—to convince investors that it was determined to contain inflation.

短期内，危险在于高通胀会顽固地持续下去。在鲍威尔掌权的情况下，这看上去将是一个无心之过，而他的履历表明，他将努力迅速纠正这个错误。2018年，随着经济升温，美联储提高了利率;今年6月，美联储发出信号，表示将以紧缩的货币政策应对更高的通胀，市场认为这一计划是可信的。然而，拜登任命的领导主要是因为他或她喜欢刺激计划，美联储可能显得不够独立，因此不得不进一步加息，从而进一步放缓经济，以让投资者相信美联储决心遏制通胀。

In the long term, the economic environment threatens central banks’ independence. As interest rates have fallen close to zero, they have been left short of ammunition. 切断与日常工作的所有联系对雇主来说也是有益的。精力充沛的员工不太可能精力枯竭。众所周知，公司内部的“行骗者”不愿休假，以免为他们打掩护的同事暴露了他们的“恶作剧”。2008年，“逃避”假期的法国交易员Jérôme Kerviel在大型银行Société Générale的资产负债表上造成了50亿欧元(合74亿美元)的亏空。之后，监管机构坚持要求一些银行家要被迫连续休假两周，以加大未来欺诈的难度。即使没有人在做假账，更彻底的界限分明也会让任务被传递给组织中的其他人。这就建立了弹性。

What is the right balance? Nobody suggests toiling on leave at the same pace as in the (home) office. But most people will conclude that if a few minutes spent answering emails can save a stranded colleague hours of work, it would seem churlish not to step in. If nothing else, keeping even a distant eye on things can make a return to the job—whether after a week off or even a month—less daunting. Who wants to deal with thousands of unread emails on their return?

toil on 费力

churlish 气量小的; 无礼的

什么样的平衡是正确的呢? 没有人会建议休假时保持和(在家里的)办公室一样的节奏辛苦工作。但大多数人会得出这样的结论:如果花几分钟回复电子邮件，可以为陷入困境的同事节省数小时的工作时间，那么不回复似乎有些无礼。如果没有其他的事情，不管你是休假一周还是一个月后，远距离的密切关注着这些也会让你回到工作的时候没那么畏惧。谁会想在回来的时候处理成千上万的未读邮件呢?

And plenty of professionals quite like what they do for a living, even when they aren’t meant to be doing it. Staying loosely connected might encourage people to take their full entitlement of holidays: in America, more than half of workers reported not taking their share even before the pandemic roiled their working lives.

entitlement to sth/to do sth 对某事物享有的权利/做某事的权利

很多专业人士非常喜欢他们赖以谋生的工作，即使他们并不想这样做。保持松散的联系可能会鼓励人们充分享受假期: 在美国，超过一半的员工报告称，即使在疫情扰乱他们的工作生活之前，他们也没有享受自己的那份假期。

An intriguing possibility is that the rise of remote work will spawn a new type of holiday. Among his European contacts Bartleby has glimpsed some exotic Zoom backgrounds of late. Some people are “working from home” in what look to be pretty swish holiday destinations. One executive admitted that, having decamped to a Greek island for a couple of weeks in the past, he is now spending most of the summer there. He is toiling a touch less than full whack during the weeks he is meant to be working—but putting in a few hours a day during what is meant to be time off.

swish 豪华的；华丽的

decamp 潜逃

一种有趣的可能性是，远程工作的兴起将催生一种新型的假期。在巴托比的欧洲联系人中，巴托比最近瞥见了一些具有异国情调的Zoom背景。有些人在看起来很时髦的度假胜地“远程工作”。一位高管承认，他在过去几周一直在希腊的一个小岛上，现在夏天的大部分时间都在那里度过。在本该工作的几周时间里，他工作得有点辛苦，但在本该休息的时间里，他却每天投入工作几个小时。

Thus the binary nature of holidays—either you are working or you aren’t—may yet become another casualty of covid-19. Plenty of thought has been given to how the pandemic has changed how people work. It may end up altering how they don’t work, too.

因此，假期的两面性（要么你在工作，要么你不在工作）可能会成为covid-19的另一个牺牲品。很多人都在思考疫情如何改变了人们的工作方式。这可能最终也会改变它们不工作的方式。

California’s coming bacon crunch

加州即将推出培根脆饼

Hog tied

![image-20210825094645756](/Users/chenyujie/Library/Application Support/typora-user-images/image-20210825094645756.png)

Animal welfare regulations will push up the price of pork

动物福利条例将推高猪肉价格

BRINGING HOME the bacon is about to get harder in California. Proposition 12, a ballot initiative passed in 2018, prevents the pork, veal and egg industries from raising animals within confined spaces for all products sold within the state. And while the egg and veal industries met new rules in 2020 (43 square feet, or 13 square metres, per calf raised for veal, one square foot per egg-laying hen), the pork industry may fail to meet the requirements for breeding pigs (24 square feet) by January 1st.

to bring home the bacon 养家糊口

veal 小牛肉

在加州，养家糊口将变得越来越困难。2018年通过的第12号提案（《农场动物圈养倡议》）是一项投票倡议，该提案规定只要你在加州销售猪肉、小牛肉和鸡蛋行业产品，那产出这些产品的饲养动物就不得被限制在有限的空间里（比如说鸡笼、犊牛笼子等）。尽管鸡蛋和小牛肉行业在2020年达到了新规定(产出小牛肉的每头小牛需要43平方英尺，或13平方米，每只下蛋母鸡则要1平方英尺)，但猪肉行业到1月1日可能无法满足种猪(24平方英尺)的要求。

California’s new restrictions are part of a larger movement to release animals from cages. McDonald’s, Kellogg’s, Kroger’s and other businesses announced plans to use only cage-free eggs by 2025. Eight states, including Massachusetts and Utah, passed laws that in effect ban eggs produced by caged hens .

加州的新限制是将动物从笼子里放出来的这样一种更大的运动的一部分。麦当劳、家乐氏、克罗格和其他企业宣布，计划到2025年只使用散养鸡蛋。包括马萨诸塞州和犹他州在内的八个州通过了法律，实际上禁止了笼养母鸡生产的鸡蛋。

California’s law is the most impactful on the pork industry. As the largest consumer of pig products, 116m kilos per year (about 15% of all produced in America), California’s standards have weight elsewhere.

加州的法律对猪肉行业影响最大。作为最大的猪肉产品消费国，加州每年生产1.16亿公斤猪肉产品(约占美国猪肉产品总量的15%)，其标准在其他地方也有分量。

The industry is, naturally, claiming that the law is unconstitutional. So far, challenges have been unsuccessful. The US Court of Appeals for the Ninth Circuit declined the National Pork Producers Council (NPPC) and American Farm Bureau Federation’s petition to strike down the law in July. And the Supreme Court denied a petition filed by the North American Meat Institute a month earlier.

strike down 废除

该行业自然会声称该法律违宪。到目前为止，这些质疑都没有成功。今年7月，美国第九巡回上诉法院驳回了国家猪肉生产者委员会(NPPC)和美国农场局联合会提出的废除该法律的请求。最高法院驳回了北美肉类协会一个月前提交的一份请愿书。

Michael Formica, general counsel at the NPPC, says that the new law places an unreasonable burden on pork producers. Currently breeding pigs, which typically weigh about 225 kilograms, are placed in 14-square-foot gestation crates. Less than 4% of all sow housing meets the new standards, according to Rabobank, which finances agriculture.

NPPC的总法律顾问Michael Formica称，新法律给猪肉生产商带来了不合理的负担。目前，一般重约225公斤的种猪被放在14平方英尺的妊娠箱中。据资助农业的荷兰合作银行Rabobank称，只有不到4%的母猪养殖场符合新标准。

Activists blame the pork industry. “[They] had three years to change their housing systems. They instead chose to not comply,” says Chris Green of Harvard Law’s Animal Law and Policy Programme. Californians could pay the price. The Hatamiya Group, a consulting firm hired by opponents of the law, estimate that consumers could pay up to 60% more: a $6 pack of bacon could now cost $9.60. Bacon smugglers in Nevada are, presumably, licking their lips.

激进分子指责猪肉行业。哈佛法学院动物法律和政策项目的Chris Green说：“(他们)有三年时间来改变他们的住房体系，但他们反而选择不遵守”。加州人可能会为此付出代价。该法律的反对者雇佣的咨询公司Hatamiya Group估计，消费者可能要多支付60%的费用:一盒6美元的培根现在要9.6美元。据推测，内华达州的培根走私者都迫不及待了。

Out of lockdown, Britons are discovering other viruses still exist

解除封锁后，英国人发现其他病毒仍然存在

![image-20210824094729269](/Users/chenyujie/Library/Application Support/typora-user-images/image-20210824094729269.png)

Nauseous nurseries

[可以理解为托儿所是诺如病毒的高发地，诺如病毒又会引起人们呕吐、腹泻，比较恶心]

The unwelcome return of norovirus

诺如病毒的回归不受欢迎

Raife’s was among the first cases of a wave of sickness that is now spreading across the country. According to data gathered by Public Health England, laboratory-confirmed cases of norovirus, which causes a sharp spell of illness including vomiting and diarrhoea, have been spiking since late May. Having been suppressed almost the entire way through the covid-19 pandemic, in the last week of July the number overtook the average for the previous five years (see chart).

overtake 超过

Raife是目前正在全国蔓延的一波疾病的首批病例之一。根据英国公共卫生部收集的数据，实验室确诊的诺如病毒病例自5月底以来一直在激增。诺如病毒会导致呕吐和腹泻等一系列严重疾病。在疫情期间，这一数字几乎一直被镇压，在7月的最后一周，这一数字超过了此前五年的平均水平(见图表)。

![image-20210824094744985](/Users/chenyujie/Library/Application Support/typora-user-images/image-20210824094744985.png)

The surge is causing particular problems in schools and nurseries, where most cases have so far originated. Unlike covid-19, which spreads through the air, norovirus is thought to spread via infected fluids on surfaces. Small children, who like to touch anything and everything, are therefore especially likely to pick it up and to spread it to family members. Though deeply unpleasant, as Ms Bone can attest, it is not deadly for most. But it can be to elderly or vulnerable people, and it can also force hospitals to shut wards in the case of an outbreak.

ward 病房

这种激增给学校和托儿所带来了特别的问题，到目前为止，大多数病例都是在这些地方发生的。与通过空气传播的covid-19不同，诺如病毒被认为是通过表面上被感染的液体传播的。小孩子喜欢触摸任何东西，因此特别容易感染并传染给家庭成员。正如Bone女士所证明的那样，尽管这让人非常不愉快，但对大多数人来说并不是致命的。但它也可能让老年人或易受伤害的群体感染，在疫情爆发的情况下，它也可能迫使医院关闭病房。

According to a recent study by researchers at the London School of Hygiene and Tropical Medicine and colleagues, the rapid spread is probably a result of lowered immunity, after a year in which most viruses were unable to leap easily from person to person. They estimate that if the amount of social contact increases to its pre-pandemic norm, then Britain could be in for a wave of norovirus this winter of twice the normal size. Other viruses that have been suppressed, such as flu, may also surge.

根据伦敦卫生与热带医学院的研究人员及其同事最近的一项研究，这种快速传播可能是免疫力降低的结果，此前一年，大多数病毒无法在人与人之间轻易传播。他们估计，如果社会接触的数量增加到疫情前的水平，那么英国可能会在今年冬天遭遇一波比正常规模大一倍的诺如病毒。其他被抑制的病毒，如流感，也可能激增。

It is a worrying prospect. Despite the success of the country’s vaccination programme, Britain’s hospitals are already stretched, with more than 6,000 covid-19 patients and an unseasonal spike in other illnesses leaving precious little space for people with upset stomachs. Belatedly, government advice to stop the spread of covid-19 has switched from encouraging people to wash their hands to encouraging them to meet outdoors and to improve ventilation by opening windows. That is all well and good but if the norovirus trend continues, keeping some soap around may also prove smart.

Belatedly 迟来地

ventilation 通风

前景堪忧。尽管英国的疫苗接种项目取得了成功，但英国的医院已经不堪重负，有6000多名covid-19患者，以及其他疾病的非季节性的激增，给胃不舒服的人空出宝贵的空间。政府关于阻止covid-19传播的建议已经从鼓励人们洗手转变为鼓励人们在户外集会，并通过打开窗户改善通风。这一切都很好，但如果诺如病毒的趋势持续下去，随身携带一些肥皂可能也是明智的。
