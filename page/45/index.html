How might climate change affect European gastronomy?

gastronomy 美食学

气候变化会如何影响欧洲美食?

Hot cuisine

热菜

![image-20211111170933432](/Users/chenyujie/Library/Application Support/typora-user-images/image-20211111170933432.png)

Say hello to pan-fried rabbitfish

和煎大西洋银鲛打个招呼吧

FARMERS IN SOUTHERN Italy are cultivating avocados and mangos. Tropical creatures such as the rabbitfish are turning up in Mediterranean nets. And Bordeaux winemakers fret that their Merlot grapes may become extinct. Fifty years ago all this would have been unthinkable. But since the early 1980s rising temperatures have forced some farmers to swap grapes for passion fruit.

avocado 鳄梨

意大利南部的农民正在种植牛油果和芒果。地中海的渔网中出现了像大西洋银鲛这样的热带生物。波尔多的酿酒师们担心他们的梅洛葡萄可能会灭绝。在50年前，这一切都是难以想象的。但自20世纪80年代初以来，不断上升的气温迫使一些农民从种植葡萄变成了种植百香果。

Italy and France have long been proud of their cuisines. Both countries jealously guard the rules that say only ham made in Parma can be called “Prosciutto di Parma”, and only fizzy wine made in Champagne can be called champagne. Roquefort, that most celebrated of blue cheeses, was given special protection by the parliament of Toulouse in 1550.

jealously 小心地

意大利和法国长期以来一直以其美食为荣。这两个国家都小心翼翼地维护着一个规定，即只有在意大利帕尔玛产的火腿才能被称为“帕尔玛火腿”，只有在法国香槟地区产的起泡葡萄酒才能被称为香槟。罗克福奶酪作为最著名的蓝色奶酪，在1550年受到图卢兹议会的特别保护。

The fact of having been grown somewhere famous has traditionally been seen as a guarantee of quality. But climate change could upset that. Take polenta, a popular Italian dish consisting almost entirely of ground maize. High temperatures and drier weather have already reduced maize yields in southern Italy. If this pattern continues and spreads northwards, will Italian polenta-makers have to order their maize from elsewhere? And what about durum wheat, which grows abundantly in Mediterranean lands and is used to make pasta, flatbreads and couscous? Modelling suggests that durum yields will sharply fall there if the temperature keeps on rising.

在著名的地方种植一直被认为是质量的保证。但气候变化可能会破坏这一点。以波伦塔为例，它是一道很受欢迎的意大利菜，几乎全部由玉米粉制成。高温和干燥的天气已经降低了意大利南部的玉米产量。如果这种模式继续并向北蔓延，意大利的波伦塔制造商是否将不得不从其他地方订购玉米? 那么硬质小麦呢? 这种小麦在地中海地区大量生长，被用来制作意大利面、扁面包和古斯古斯面[翻译如果不准确，建议搜图]。模型显示，如果气温持续上升，那里的硬质小麦产量将急剧下降。

So should gourmands worry about the future of spaghetti? Gabriele Cola, a researcher at Milan University, is optimistic about the short term. “I don’t see crops at serious risk, because farming is more informed and technologically capable, so it can always respond to changes,” he says. Increased irrigation can counter the effects of drought. Scientists may also breed more resistant varieties of crops.

那么，美食家们应该担心意大利面的未来吗? 米兰大学的研究员Gabriele Cola对短期前景持乐观态度。他说:“我认为农作物不会面临严重的风险，因为农业种植更“灵通”，技术能力更强，所以它总能对变化做出反应。”增加灌溉可以抵消干旱的影响。科学家们还可能培育出抗性更强的作物品种。

But in the long run deeper change seems likely. If temperatures rise unabated, farmers in northern Europe may find they can grow southern staples; polenta may invade Germany. And southern locavores may have to adapt. If tropical fruit continue to thrive in Sicily, could ham and pineapple pizza one day be considered authentically Italian?

to remain unabated «interest, enthusiasm» 保持不减

但从长远来看，更深层的变化似乎是可能的。如果气温持续上升，北欧的农民可能会发现他们可以种植南方的主食; 波伦塔可能会大批涌入德国。而南方的当地人可能不得不去适应。如果热带水果继续在西西里岛茁壮成长，有一天火腿菠萝披萨会被认为是真正的意大利食物吗?

Soaring newsprint costs make life even harder for newspapers

新闻纸的成本飙升让报社更难了

![image-20211110104116244](/Users/chenyujie/Library/Application Support/typora-user-images/image-20211110104116244.png)

It may accelerate the move online

它可能会加速向互联网的发展

“IT’S LIKE TASERING an elderly person who’s already on a pacemaker,” says a British newspaper boss of the newsprint market, where prices have risen by over 50% in a matter of months. The cost of paper that feeds into presses around the world is rising to record highs, pushing up expenses for newspapers from Mumbai to Sydney. When times were good, before ads shifted online, newspapers had a supportive partnership with paper mills. As ads departed and circulations fell, relations became more transactional. They are now at the shouting stage.

pacemaker 心脏起搏器

paper mill 造纸厂

transactional 交易的

新闻纸的市场价格在短短几个月内上涨了50%以上，新闻纸消费市场的一家英国报社的老板称，“这就像用泰瑟枪对一个已经上了心脏起搏器的老人射击一样”。世界各地印刷机的纸张成本正升至创纪录的高位，这就推高了从孟买到悉尼的报纸的成本。在光景好的时候，广告还没有转移到网上，报社就和造纸厂结成了给予帮助的伙伴关系。随着广告的退出和发行量的下降，双方的关系变得更交易性。他们现在已经到了“呐喊”的阶段。

Paper mills had the worst of it for years as newspapers reduced pagination, went wholly digital or shut for good. The papers were able to hammer down the cost of newsprint from firms fighting for business as demand declined. Price-taking paper mills suffered in silence. Many hesitated to shut massive machines costing hundreds of millions of dollars.

pagination 标页码

随着报纸减少页数，走向完全数字化，或者永远关闭，造纸厂经历了多年来最糟糕的情况。由于需求的下降，报社能够从争夺业务的公司那里压低新闻纸的成本。“价格由市场决定”的造纸厂只能默默承受着损失。许多造纸厂都在犹豫是否要关闭耗资数亿美元的大型机器。

That hesitance has disappeared; mills are taking out newsprint capacity and diversifying. Norske Skog, a Norwegian pulp and paper firm, said in June it would close its 66-year-old Tasman Mill in New Zealand, for example. Many mills are converting machines to make packaging for e-commerce. UPM, a Finnish firm, announced this year the sale of its Shotton newsprint mill in Wales to a Turkish maker of containerboard and packaging. For JCS Volga, a Russian mill, newsprint used to account for 70% of production; now half of what it makes is packaging. The mills “moved from being price takers to being capable co-participants in a declining market,” says Tim Woods of IndustryEdge, a research firm for Australia and New Zealand’s forestry and paper industries.

那种犹豫已经消失了;造纸厂正在削减新闻纸的产能，并实现业务多元化。例如，挪威纸浆和造纸公司Norske Skog在6月份表示，它将关闭其在新西兰拥有66年历史的塔斯曼工厂。许多工厂正在改造机器，为电商制作包装材料。芬兰公司UPM今年宣布将其位于威尔士的Shotton新闻纸厂出售给一家土耳其硬纸板和包装材料制造商。对俄罗斯的JCS Volga来说，新闻纸过去占总产量的70%; 现在它一半的产品是包装材料。澳大利亚和新西兰林业和造纸业研究公司IndustryEdge的Tim Woods表示，造纸厂“从价格接受者转变为衰落市场中有能力的共同参与者”。

The pandemic, with people working from home, meant even fewer newspaper purchases, which depressed demand for newsprint again and increased the pain for paper suppliers. In the past 24 months European mills have responded by shutting almost a fifth of their newsprint capacity, says a buyer for a large British newspaper group.

疫情伴随着人们远程办公，这就意味着报纸的购买量更少，这再次抑制了对新闻纸的需求，增加了纸张供应商的痛苦。英国一家大型报社集团的采购员表示，在过去24个月里，欧洲造纸厂的应对措施是关闭近五分之一的新闻纸产能。

Then economies reopened. Newsprint demand shot up. That, combined with much reduced capacity and coupled with soaring energy prices, has resulted in a price shock. Particularly controversial are energy surcharges that some paper suppliers are seeking to pass on. Newspaper firms reckon this amounts to breaking contracts. European newspapers will have to pay newsprint prices that are 50-70% higher in the first quarter of 2022 compared with the year before. As for their counterparts in Asia and Oceania, they are facing prices around 25% to 45% above their usual level. Kenya’s Nation Media Group is paying around $840 per tonne, compared with $600 at most in the past, says Dorine Ogolo, a procurement manager at the firm. North American prices went up earlier, and more gradually; contracts are fixed monthly rather than half-yearly. But there, too, newsprint prices are 20-30% higher in 2021 than in 2020.

然后经济体重新开放。新闻纸需求激增。再加上产能大幅下降，以及能源价格飙升，这就导致了价格冲击。尤其有争议的是一些纸张供应商试图转嫁的能源附加费。报业公司认为这相当于毁约。2022年第一季度，欧洲报社将不得不支付比去年高出50-70%的新闻纸价格。至于亚洲和大洋洲的报社，他们面临的价格比通常水平高出25%至45%左右。肯尼亚国家媒体集团的采购经理Dorine Ogolo说，与过去每吨最多600美元的价格相比，现在的价格是每吨840美元。北美的价格上涨得更早，也更为缓慢; 合同是按月签的，而不是半年一签。但在2021年，新闻纸的价格也比2020年高出20-30%。

Germany’s print and media industry association has warned that mills are going to force newspapers to dump paper editions, hurting each other in the process. “It’s about the famous branch that both of them are sitting on,” it said recently. But mills can sell packaging instead. “We’re not going to save the publishing industry by being unprofitable ourselves,” says a mill executive in North America.

德国印刷和媒体行业协会警告称，造纸厂将迫使报社出售纸质版本，在这一过程中他们正互相伤害。该协会最近表示:“这是因为他们两个行业都很出名。”【这里可能理解不到位，有好的见解的同学可以在评论区留言】 不过造纸厂仍可以出售包装材料。北美的一位造纸厂的高管说，“我们自己不赚钱是拯救不了出版业的。”

For some publishers, price rises will wipe out profits. They will need to do further restructuring involving axing titles and layoffs. Iwan Le Moine of EMGE, a British paper-industry consultancy, expects a big increase in 2022 of the number of papers that shut compared with a typical year. That will lower demand and nudge the market back towards equilibrium. But newspapers will have more hard conversations about paper, full stop, says Douglas McCabe of Enders Analysis, a research firm. More digital adrenaline is one possible riposte to the paper mills’ tasers. ■

adrenaline 肾上腺素

riposte 即可反驳；还击

对一些出版商来说，新闻纸价格的上涨将抹去利润。他们将需要进行进一步的重组，包括削减职位和裁员。英国纸业咨询公司EMGE的Iwan Le Moine预计，与往年相比，2022年关闭的报社的数量将大幅增加。这将降低需求，推动市场回归平衡。但研究公司Enders Analysis的Douglas McCabe表示，报社将会有更多关于造纸厂的艰难对话，不必多说了。更多的数字化刺激可能是对造纸厂的泰瑟枪的一种回击。■

Why executives like the office

为什么高管们喜欢办公室？

Bartleby

巴托比

![image-20211109102558365](/Users/chenyujie/Library/Application Support/typora-user-images/image-20211109102558365.png)

Blame a mixture of conditioning, carpets and concrete concerns

这要归咎于空调、地毯和具体担忧的混合

AN OFFICE IS meant to bring people together. Instead, it has become a source of division. For some, the post-pandemic return to the workplace is an opportunity to re-establish boundaries between home and job, and to see colleagues in the flesh. For others it represents nothing but pointless travelling and heightened health risks. Many ingredients determine these preferences. But one stands out: seniority.

办公室存在的目的就是把人们聚集在一起。相反，它已成为分歧的根源。对一些人来说，疫情后重新回到工作场所有机会重新树立家庭和工作之间界限，还能够亲眼见到同事。对其他人来说，这只是毫无意义的“行程”，还有更高的健康风险。许多因素决定了这些偏好。但有一个因素很突出，即资历。

Slack, a messaging firm, conducts regular surveys of global knowledge workers on the future of work. Its latest poll, released in October, found that executives are far keener to get back to the office than other employees. Of those higher-ups who were working remotely, 75% wanted to be in the office three days a week or more; only 34% of non-executives felt the same way.

higher-up 上级

信息公司Slack定期对全球知识型员工的未来工作进行调查。该公司10月份发布的最新调查发现，高管们比其他员工更渴望回到办公室。在远程办公的高层中，75%的人希望一周在办公室呆三天或更多时间; 不是高管的人群中只有34%的人有同样的想法。

The divide has played out publicly at some companies. Earlier this year, employees at Apple wrote an open letter to Tim Cook, the firm’s chief executive, objecting to the assumption that they were thirsting to get back to their desks: “It feels like there is a disconnect between how the executive team thinks about remote/location-flexible work and the lived experiences of many of Apple’s employees.” Why are bigwigs so much keener on the office?

这种分歧已经在一些公司公开表现出来。今年早些时候,苹果员工写了一封公开信给该公司的首席执行官库克, 反对“他们渴望回到办公桌”的假设：“管理团队对远程/位置弹性工作的看法与许多苹果员工的生活经历之间似乎存在脱节。”为什么大人物对办公室如此热衷？

Three explanations come to mind: the cynical, the kind and the subconscious. The cynical one is that executives like the status that the office confers. They sit in nicer rooms on higher floors with plusher carpets. Access to them is guarded, politely but ferociously. When they walk the floors, it is an event. When they sit in meeting rooms, they get the best chairs. On Zoom the signals of status are weaker. No one gets a bigger tile. Their biggest privilege is not muting themselves, which isn’t quite the same power rush as using the executive dining room.

to come to mind or come into one’s mind 浮上心头

我想到了三种解释: 愤世嫉俗的、善意的和潜意识的。愤世嫉俗的解释是指高管们喜欢办公室所赋予的地位。他们坐在铺着更豪华的地毯的更高的楼层里的更好的办公室里办公。接近他们的途径礼貌而又残忍地被严加保护。当他们在地板上走动时，这就是一件大事了。当他们坐在会议室时，他们会得到最好的椅子。在Zoom会议上，他们的“地位”的标志较弱。没人能得到更大的瓷砖。他们最大的特权不用让自己静音，不过这种特权和在高管餐厅就餐享受的特权可差得太远了。【感谢 @干净的咸鱼桑 提供的译文解释 】。

The kind explanation is that executives believe that in-person interactions are better for the institutions they lead. Working from home “doesn’t work for people who want to hustle, doesn’t work for culture, doesn’t work for idea generation,” was the verdict of Jamie Dimon, the chief executive of JPMorgan Chase, earlier this year. Ken Griffin, the boss of Citadel, a hedge fund, has warned young people not to work from home: “It’s incredibly difficult to have the managerial experiences and interpersonal experiences that you need to have to take your career forward in a work-remotely environment.”

善意的解释是，高管们认为，面对面的交流对他们所领导的机构更有利。摩根大通首席执行官Jamie Dimon今年早些时候的结论是，远程办公“对那些想拼命工作的人来说行不通，对文化和创意都行不通”。对冲基金Citadel的老板Ken Griffin警告年轻人不要远程办公:“在远程工作的环境中，你很难拥有推动职业发展所需的管理经验和人际交往经验。”

These concerns have substance. Virtual work risks entrenching silos: people are more likely to spend time with colleagues they already know. Corporate culture can be easier to absorb in three dimensions. Deep relationships are harder to form with a laggy internet connection. A study from 2010 found that physical proximity between co-authors was a good predictor of the impact of scientific papers: the greater the distance between them, the less likely they were to be cited. Even evangelists for remote work make time for physical gatherings. “Digital first does not mean never in person,” says Brian Elliott, who runs Slack’s research into the future of work.

evangelist 狂热鼓吹者

这些担忧是有事实依据的。远程工作有可能会形成孤立“团体”:人们更有可能花时间和自己认识的同事在一起。企业文化很容易在三个方面被消减。由于网络连接较慢，很难建立深厚的关系。2010年的一项研究发现，共同作者之间的物理距离可以很好地预测科学论文的影响:他们之间的距离越大，他们被引用的可能性就越小。即使是远程工作的倡导者也会腾出时间亲自去参加聚会。负责Slack未来工作调研的Brian Elliott表示:“数字化优先并不意味着永远不面对面。”

But the advantages of the office can also be exaggerated. The Allen curve, which shows how frequency of communication goes down the farther away colleagues sit from each other, was formulated in the 1970s but still rings true today. Every workplace has corners that people never visit; no gulf is greater than that between floors. And the disadvantages of remote working can be overcome with a bit of thought. Research by a trio of professors at Harvard Business School found that lockdown-era interns who got to spend time with senior managers at a “virtual watercooler” were much likelier to receive full-time job offers than those who did not.

water-cooler 职员偷闲时的；职员闲聊的

但办公室的优势也可能被夸大。Allen曲线显示的是，同事之间的距离越远，交流频率就越低。Allen曲线是在上世纪70年代制定的，但在今天仍然适用。每个工作场所都有人们从未去过的角落; 没有比楼层之间的鸿沟更大的了。远程工作的缺点可以通过一点思考来克服。哈佛商学院三位教授的研究发现，在封锁期用“virtual watercooler”与高级管理人员呆在一起的实习生与没有这样做的实习生相比，更有可能获得全职工作机会。

If physical workspaces have drawbacks, and remote working can be improved upon, why are executives clear in their preferences? The subconscious supplies a third explanation. As Gianpiero Petriglieri of INSEAD, a French business school, observes: “people advising youngsters to go into the office are those who made their way in that environment.” Executives who have achieved success by working in an office are the least likely to question its efficacy.

如果物理工作空间有缺点，而远程工作可以改进，为什么高管们的偏好很明确? 潜意识提供了第三种解释。正如欧洲工商管理学院的Gianpiero Petriglieri所观察到的:“建议年轻人进入办公室的人都是那些在那种环境中取得成功的人。”通过在办公室工作而取得成功的高管们最不可能质疑它的效力。

That is a problem, especially since a majority of executives say that they have designed return-to-work policies with scant input from employees. A hybrid future beckons, in which workers divide their time between home and office. Managers need to improve both environments, not assume that one is obviously superior to the other.

scant 一丁点的

这是个问题，尤其是考虑到大多数高管表示，他们在制定重返工作岗位政策时，很少征求员工的意见。一个混合办公的未来正在向我们招手，在这个未来里，员工们将把时间分配在家庭和办公室。管理者需要改善这两种环境，而不是假设其中一种环境明显优于另一种。

America’s maths wars

美国的数学之战

Problem-solving

解决问题

![image-20211108105837558](/Users/chenyujie/Library/Application Support/typora-user-images/image-20211108105837558.png)

How teaching multiplication tables became another victim of the political divide

教授乘法表如何变成美国政治分歧的又一个受害者

AMERICA HAS a maths problem. Its pupils have ranked poorly in international maths exams for decades. In 2018, American 15-year-olds ranked 25th in the OECD, a club of mostly rich countries. American adults ranked fourth-from-last in numeracy when compared with other rich countries. As many as 30% of American adults are comfortable only with simple maths: basic arithmetic, counting, sorting and similar tasks. American employers are desperate for science, technology, engineering and mathematics skills: nuclear engineers, software developers and machinists are in short supply. And while pupils’ maths scores are bad enough now, they could be getting worse. On the National Assessment of Educational Progress (NAEP), a national exam, 13-year-old pupils’ scores dropped five points in 2020 compared with their peers’ in 2012. The status quo does not add up. But teachers and academics cannot agree on where to go next.

numeracy 计算能力

to be desperate for 渴望得到 ‹affection, help›

The American maths problem is over a century old, says Alan Schoenfeld of the University of California, Berkeley. In 1890 high school was an elite endeavour: less than 7% of 14-year-olds were enrolled and they were educated in rigorous maths. By the beginning of the second world war, by which time army recruits had to be trained in the maths needed for basic bookkeeping and gunnery, nearly three-quarters of children aged 14-17 attended high school. The cold war sparked a second strategic maths panic in the 1950s. A new maths curriculum, focused on conceptual understanding rather than rote memorising, was developed after the launch of the Sputnik satellite by the Soviet Union. 加州大学伯克利分校的Alan Schoenfeld称，美国的数学问题已经有一个多世纪的历史了。1890年，高中是精英的活动: 14岁学生中只有不到7%的入学并接受严格的数学教育。到第二次世界大战开始时，新兵必须接受基本簿记和射击所需的数学训练，近四分之三的14到17岁的孩子都上过高中。20世纪50年代，冷战引发了第二次战略数学的恐慌。苏联发射Sputnik卫星后，一套新的数学课程应运而生，课程重点在于概念的理解，而不是死记硬背。然而，在20世纪70年代的一场回归基础教育的运动中，新的课程遭到了排斥。

Maths teaching became a worry again when America started to fear being overtaken by Japan. In 1981 the secretary of education appointed a commission to evaluate the curriculum. It produced a report called “A Nation at Risk”. “If an unfriendly foreign power had attempted to impose on America the mediocre educational performance that exists today,” the report reported, “we might well have viewed it as an act of war.”

当美国开始担心被日本赶超时，数学教学再次成为人们的担忧。1981年，教育部长任命了一个委员会来评估课程。它制作了一份名为“处于危险中的国家”的报告。报告称:“如果一个不友好的外国势力试图将今天存在的平庸教育表现强加给美国，我们很可能会将其视为一场战争。”

Since the 1990s, though, maths has become more political. Conservatives typically campaign for classical maths: a focus on algorithms (a set of rules to be followed), memorising (of times tables and algorithmic processes) and teacher-led instruction. Pupils in these classrooms focus on the basics, exploring concepts after obtaining traditional skills, explains Bill Evers of the Independent Institute, a think-tank in Oakland. These methods are familiar to many. For two-digit addition, pupils would be taught a paper-and-pencil method. Add 27 + 45 by stacking 27 over 45. Add up the right column (7 + 5 = 12). Write down the 2, and carry the 1 to the left column. Add up the left column (1 + 2 + 4 = 7). Write down the 7. The answer is 72.

然而，自20世纪90年代以来，数学变得越来越政治化。保守主义者通常发起支持传统数学的运动: 重视算法(一套需要遵循的规则)、记忆(乘法表和算法过程)和教师主导的教学。奥克兰智库独立研究所的Bill Evers解释称，这些教室里的学生专注于基础知识，在获得传统技能后才开始探索概念。这些方法为许多人所熟知。对于两位数的加法，学生将被教授一种纸笔法。计算“27 + 45”，就要将27写在45的上方。把右边的一列加起来(7 + 5 = 12)。把2写下来，然后把1移到左边一列。再把左边一列(1 + 2 + 4 = 7)相加，写下7。答案是72。

Progressives typically favour a conceptual approach to maths based on problem-solving and gaining number-sense, with less emphasis on algorithms and memorising. In contrast to the conservative strategy, pupils would learn several ways to solve a problem, by using objects and by other means, before learning algorithms. To solve 27 + 45, pupils could add up the digits in the ones place (7+5=12), and then the tens place (20+40=60), and then add them together to get 72. Or they could realise that 27 is 3 digits away from 30. They would add 3 to get to 30. Then add 45 and subtract 3 to get 72. Conceptual maths strategies encourage pupils to find many potential solutions for the same problem to gain number-sense, rather than relying on an algorithm.

进步主义者通常更喜欢数学概念的方法，这些概念是以解决问题和获得数字感为基础，没有那么强调算法和记忆。与保守主义者的策略相反，学生在学习算法之前，会学习几种解决问题的方法，通过使用事例或其他方法。要解“27 + 45”，学生可以先把个位的数字(7+5=12)加起来，再把十位的数字(20+40=60)加起来，得到72。或者他们可以意识到27和30的距离是3个数字。加3等于30。然后加上45再减去3得72。概念数学策略鼓励学生对于同一个问题寻找许多潜在的解决方案，以获得数字感，而不是去依赖一个算法。

Although most teachers agree that maths education in America is sub-par, they have not been able to agree on how to improve it. Copying methods used in highly ranked mathematical nations such as Singapore would be one way. But that would require agreement on what is actually being taught in other countries. According to Mr Evers, successful Asian curriculums reflect the classical position. “What country do you think has totally adopted progressive education and has been a big success? China is very teacher-led. Singapore math is the best in the world. It’s not progressive,” explains Mr Evers. But Mr Schoenfeld reckons countries such as Japan and Singapore implement conceptual curriculums.

尽管大多数教师都认为美国的数学教育水平低于平均水平，但他们尚未就“如何提高数学教育水平”达成一致意见。在像新加坡这样数学排名靠前的国家，照搬他们的方法是一种方法。但这需要就“其他国家到底是教授的什么内容”达成一致。Evers表示，成功的亚洲课程反映了传统数学的地位。Evers解释称“你觉得哪个国家完全采用了进步主义教育并且已经取得了巨大成功? 中国是一个以教师为主导的国家。新加坡的数学是世界上最好的。这都不是进步主义教育”。但是Schoenfeld先生认为像日本和新加坡这样的国家都施行的是概念性课程[即进步主义教育]。

Part of the confusion stems from the messy implementation of the most recent maths curriculum, the Common Core. Implemented in 2010 under President Barack Obama’s administration, 41 states and the District of Columbia adopted the principles. Texas, Florida and several others opted out. Despite initially being a bipartisan effort (Jeb Bush and other conservatives supported the Obama administration’s effort), it was eventually criticised by both sides. Some view it as an example of progressive education, while others think it was not progressive enough. Now that test results are rolling in, many conservatives claim that the falling scores show the failure of the Common Core and progressive teachers. But supporters of the current curriculum are not prepared to give up. Whereas NAEP scores declined among 13-year-olds, they stayed steady among nine-year-olds, who would have studied Common Core for their whole school lives.

这种困惑部分是因为最新的数学基础课程的混乱施行。奥巴马政府于2010年施行了这个基础课程，41个州和哥伦比亚区采用了这套课程。德克萨斯州、佛罗里达州和其他几个州选择了不采纳。尽管最初是两党共同的努力推行(Jeb Bush和其他保守派支持奥巴马政府的尝试)，但最终还是遭到了两党的批评。一些人认为这是进步主义教育的一个例子，而另一些人认为它不够进步。随着考试成绩的不断公布，许多保守派人士声称，分数的下降表明了“基础课程”和进步主义教师的失败。但是现行课程的支持者并不准备放弃。虽然NAEP分数在13岁的孩子中有所下降，但在9岁的孩子中分数却保持稳定，这些9岁的孩子在学校一直学的都是基础课程。

Further muddling the mess, far-left activists have paired conceptual maths methods with more radical concepts. Many opponents of conceptual maths believe that Common Core, and conceptual maths in general, bans teaching of rote-learning, as in multiplication tables. A prominent maths-education scholar at Stanford University, Jo Boaler, claims that memorising times tables is unnecessary. Conservatives have seized on this idea as an example of liberal activism gone wild. But Mr Schoenfeld claims this does not align with progressive maths values.

极左激进分子将概念数学方法与更激进的概念结合起来，让局面变得更加混乱。许多概念数学的反对者认为，“基础课程”和概念数学多数情况下禁止死记硬背的教学，比如乘法表。斯坦福大学著名的数学教育学者Jo Boaler声称，没有必要去死记硬背乘法表。保守主义者抓住这个想法作为自由激进主义已经疯狂的一个例子。但Schoenfeld先生声称这不符合进步主义数学价值观。

Some activists have also paired conceptual maths with “social-justice maths”, the concept that maths should be used to help pupils solve real-world issues and appraise the world around them. Dubbed “woke math[s]” and tagged with headlines such as “In California 2+2=4 May Be Thought Racist” (as a letter to the Wall Street Journal harrumphed), it has done conceptual maths few favours by association.

一些积极分子还将概念数学与“社会正义数学”结合起来，“社会正义数学”的概念是，数学应该用来帮助学生解决现实世界的问题，并评价他们周围的世界。它被称为“觉醒的数学”，并贴上诸如“在加州2+2=4可能被认为是种族主义”(在一封写给《华尔街日报》的信中对此嗤之以鼻)这样的标题，这导致概念数学几乎没有得到任何协会的支持。

The maths debate in America is polarised and confused. While other countries implement maths curriculums with a balance of rote and conceptual learning, America continues to swing from one pole to another, decade after decade. Just like the country’s politics, in other words.■

The most striking announcement has come from the Glasgow Financial Alliance for Net Zero (GFANZ), a coalition co-chaired by Mark Carney, a former governor of the Bank of England. Its members, which include asset owners, asset managers, banks and insurers, hold about $130trn of assets. They will try to cut the emissions from their lending and investing to net zero by 2050. Can the financial industry really save the world?

但有一个领域的热情正在增长，那就是气候金融。代表近9万亿美元资产的金融机构承诺，将从其投资组合中去除“森林砍伐”。最引人注目的声明来自格拉斯哥净零金融联盟(GFANZ)，该联盟的联合主席是前英格兰银行行长Mark Carney。其成员包括资产所有者、资产管理公司、银行和保险公司，他们持有约130万亿美元的资产。他们将努力通过贷款和投资减少碳排放，到2050年达到零排放。金融业真的能拯救世界吗?

In principle, it has a huge role to play. Shifting the economy from fossil fuels to clean sources of energy requires a vast reallocation of capital. By 2030, around $4trn of investment in clean energy will be needed each year, a tripling of current levels. Spending on fossil fuels must decline. In an ideal world the profit incentive of institutional investors would be aligned with reducing emissions, and these owners and financiers would control the global assets that create emissions. Asset owners would have both the motive and the means to reinvent the economy.

原则上，它可以发挥巨大的作用。将经济从化石燃料转向清洁能源需要大规模的资本重新配置。到2030年，在清洁能源方面每年将需要大约4万亿美元的投资，这一数字是当前水平的三倍。化石燃料的支出必须减少。在一个理想的世界里，机构投资者的利润激励应该与减少排放相一致，这些资产所有者和金融家将控制那些产生排放的全球资产。资产所有者将拥有重塑经济的动机和手段。

The reality of green investing falls short of this ideal. The first problem is coverage. The Economist estimates that listed firms which are not state-controlled account for only 14-32% of the world’s emissions. State-controlled companies, such as Coal India or Saudi Aramco, the world’s biggest oil producer, are a big part of the problem and they do not operate under the sway of institutional fund managers and private-sector bankers.

绿色投资的现实与这一理想相距甚远。第一个问题是覆盖面。《经济学人》预计，非国有上市公司的排放量仅占世界总排放量的14-32%。印度煤炭公司或全球最大石油生产商沙特阿美等国有企业是这一问题的主要原因，它们的运营不受机构基金管理公司和私营部门银行家的支配。

A second issue is measurement. There is as yet no way to accurately assess the carbon footprint of a portfolio without double counting. Emissions from a barrel of oil could appear in the carbon accounts of the firms that are drilling, refining and burning the stuff. Methodologies behind attributing emissions to financial flows are even sketchier. How should shareholders, lenders and insurers divvy up the emissions from a coal-fired power plant, for instance?

第二个问题是测量。目前还没有一种方法可以在不重复计算的情况下准确评估投资组合的碳足迹。一桶石油的排放可能会出现在钻探[钻井探油]、提炼和燃烧这些石油的公司的碳账目中。将排放归因于资金流动的方法甚至更为粗略。例如，股东、贷款机构和保险公司应该如何分配燃煤电厂的排放?

The third problem is incentives. Private financial firms aim to maximise risk-adjusted profits for their clients and owners. This is not well-aligned with cutting carbon. The easiest way to cut the carbon footprint of a diversified portfolio is to sell the part of it invested in dirty assets and put the proceeds in firms that never emitted much, such as, say, Facebook. Together, the five biggest American tech firms have a carbon intensity (emissions per unit of sales) of about 3% of the S&P500 average.

第三个问题是激励。私人金融公司的目标是为其客户和所有者实现风险调整后利润最大化。这与减少碳排放并不一致。减少多元化投资组合的碳足迹最简单的方法是将投资于“肮脏”资产的部分出售，然后将收益投资于那些碳排放从不会太多的公司，比如Facebook。美国最大的五家科技公司的碳排放强度(每单位销售额的排放量)约为标普500指数平均值的3%。

Heavily polluting firms or assets will often find new owners. If you can brush off the stigma, it can be profitable to hold assets that can legally generate untaxed externalities—in this case pollution. As shareholders urge oil majors to clean up, the oilfields they sell are being bought by private-equity firms and hedge funds, away from the public eye. Pledges alone do not alter the fact that firms have little reason to invest trillions in green technologies that still have mediocre risk-adjusted returns.

污染严重的企业或资产通常会找到新的所有者。如果你能摆脱污名，持有那些可以合法产生不纳税的外部效应(在这种情况下是污染)的资产是有利可图的。在股东们敦促石油巨头清理之际，他们出售的油田正被私募股权公司和对冲基金收购，远离公众的视线。仅凭承诺并不能改变这样一个事实:企业没有理由在那些风险调整后回报平平的绿色技术上投资数万亿美元。

What should be done? Fine-tuning can help. Measurement should be improved. The EU is rolling out mandatory carbon reporting for businesses; America is considering it. Some accounting bodies want to standardise how climate measures are disclosed. Asset owners, such as pension funds, should hold on to their investments in polluting firms and use them to help bring about change. Institutional investors also need to build up their venture-capital arms to finance new technologies, such as green cement.

应该做些什么?微调可以帮助。应该改进测量方法。欧盟正在推行对企业的强制性碳排放报告;美国也正在考虑。一些会计机构希望对披露气候测量的方式进行标准化。像养老基金这样的资产所有者应该保留他们在污染企业的投资，并用它们来帮助带来改变。机构投资者也需要建立他们的风险投资部门来资助新技术，比如绿色生态水泥。

Pledges like GFANZ are good as far as they go, but the world needs a widespread price on carbon if finance is to work wonders. That would target all firms, not just those controlled by some institutional investors. The urge to avoid the tax would supercharge efforts to count emissions. Firms and governments would have an incentive to grapple with questions of who is polluting and who should pay. Crucially, a carbon price would align the profit incentive with the goal of reducing greenhouse gases. The job of the financial system would then be to amplify the signal sent by the price of carbon. That combination would be a powerful engine for changing how economies work. ■

Pay up

This will lift the pre-tax pay of a full-time worker by £1,000 a year from next April, in keeping with the government’s promise to deliver a high-wage economy. Yet if the politics of the move were obvious, the economics were less so.

在预算案公布之前的一项“泄密”专门是为了确保在大型事件之前获得正面报道, 此次泄密内容是说“最低工资将从8.91英镑(12.23美元)提高到9.5英镑”。从明年4月开始，这将使全职员工每年的税前收入提高1000英镑，这与政府要实现“高工资经济”的承诺相一致。然而，如果此举的政治意义显而易见，其经济意义就不那么明显了。

The Low Pay Commission (LPC), a group of economists, employer representatives and trade unionists, has advised the government on the minimum wage since 1997. In recent years the government has given it increasingly ambitious goals. The latest, which it received in 2020, was to work out how to put an end to low pay altogether, defined as earning below two-thirds of the median hourly wage.

由经济学家、雇主代表和工会成员组成的低薪委员会(LPC)自1997年以来就一直就最低工资方面提出建议。近年来政府给了它越来越雄心勃勃的目标。最近的一项建议是在2020年收到的，其旨在解决如何彻底终结低薪问题。低薪的定义是收入低于时薪中值的三分之二。

Previous changes have yielded results. The share of people on low pay fell sharply from around 23% in the 2000s and early 2010s, to just 15% in 2019. 之前的改变已经产生了效果。低收入人群的比例从2000年到2010年早期的约23%大幅下降到2019年的仅15%。但目前还不确定进一步攀升是否会如此容易。在主要由发达国家组成的经合组织(OECD)中，只有智利、哥伦比亚、哥斯达黎加和土耳其的最低工资水平更高[可以理解为时薪比较高]。

Worries about the new target paled into insignificance when the covid-19 pandemic struck. The quality of the data available to the LPC plummeted, and the furlough scheme muddled average earnings figures by replacing 80% of people’s pay. A year ago the commission’s best guess suggested that staying on track to meet the target would have implied raising the minimum wage to £9.06. 当疫情爆发时，对新目标的担忧变得微不足道。低薪委员会可获得的数据质量直线下降，而休假计划通过支付每月80%的工资搅乱了平均收入的数据。一年前，低薪委员会的最佳猜测是，要想实现这一目标，就必须将最低工资提高到9.06英镑。考虑到不确定性的程度，他们建议政府采取“紧急刹车”，将其上调至8.91英镑。

The impact the minimum wage had on the labour market during the pandemic is still unclear (when asked, businesses told the LPC they had no idea). A glance at the data suggests the commission was maybe too pessimistic about employers’ capacity to absorb higher wages, as its latest estimates show the number and share of workers paid the minimum wage have fallen slightly. But that same observation may also suggest they were correct to be cautious, since it could be that the lowest-paid workers were more likely to lose their jobs.

疫情期间最低工资对劳动力市场的影响尚不清楚(当被问及时，企业告诉低薪委员会，他们也不知道)。从数据中可以看出，低薪委员会可能对于雇主承受更高工资的能力过于悲观，因为其最新估计显示，支付最低工资的工人数量和比例略有下降。但同样的观察也可能表明，他们的谨慎是正确的，因为收入最低的工人更有可能失业。

The LPC’s latest estimate is that achieving the government’s goal means the minimum wage will need to reach £10.70 in 2024. A number of factors support the case for a meaty rise now. Inflation is higher than usual; earnings growth is strong, particularly in sectors where pay is lowest; unemployment is relatively low in places with lots of minimum-wage workers; and businesses do not seem to be planning waves of redundancies.

redundancy 解雇

低薪委员会的最新估计是，要达到政府的目标意味着在2024年最低工资需要达到10.70英镑。有许多证实“现在要大幅上涨（最低工资）”的因素。通货膨胀率高于正常水平; 收入增长强劲，尤其是在薪酬最低的行业; 在有大量领取最低工资的工人的地方，失业率相对较低; 企业似乎并没有计划大规模裁员。

Next year’s rise is almost—but not quite—on course to hit the target. The slightly cautious increase the LPC and chancellor ultimately plumped for reflects concerns about other business pressures looming in April, including a rise in National Insurance contributions and the end of tenants’ protection from eviction. Despite long-standing fears, higher minimum wages have yet to noticeably increase unemployment. But no one wants to reach the point at which they do. ■

plump for 选中

明年的“提高薪资”几乎(但不是完全)将达到这一目标。低薪委员会和财政大臣最终选择的略微谨慎的提高薪资，反映出他们对4月份即将到来的其他商业压力的担忧，包括提高国民保险缴纳额，以及终止对租户的驱逐保护。尽管长期存在担忧，但提高最低工资尚未明显增加失业率。但没有人希望达到这样的地步。■

How the pandemic has changed the weather in the technology industry

疫情如何改变了科技行业的天

at a fair/good clip 以较快/很快的速度

芯片制造商发出了一个更强烈的信号，其表明硬件正重返行业核心。尽管英特尔在10月21日公布的季度业绩令投资者失望，导致股价下跌，但其销售额增长了5%，达到192亿美元，利润增长了60%，达到68亿美元。世界最大的存储芯片制造商三星电子也将于10月27日公布财报，其利润跃升至三年来的最高水平。而最大的半导体合同制造商台积电在10月14日表示，其销售额持续快速增长，达到149亿美元，净利润为56亿美元，分别增长16.3%和13.8%。

The big question is whether the three companies can profitably follow through on their record-breaking investment plans. These are meant to satisfy growing demand for chips not just from cloud providers, but from firms making gear for what is called the “edge”: devices connecting to the cloud or extending it, from smartphones to intelligent sensors. Intel, for instance, has said that it will invest up to $28bn in 2022. TSMC plans to spend $100bn over the next three years to expand its chip-fabrication capacity.

最大的问题是，这三家公司能否实现创纪录的投资计划，并从中获利。这不仅是为了满足云计算提供商对芯片日益增长的需求，也是为了满足为所谓“边缘”设备(从智能手机到智能传感器)提供连接或扩展云计算的设备的需求。例如，英特尔已表示2022年投资将高达280亿美元。台积电计划在未来3年斥资1000亿美元，扩大芯片制造能力。

The third big change to the tech industry during the pandemic may be the most consequential: increased competition. Although members of GAFAM have yet to attack each other’s main franchises, such as online search in the case of Google and ecommerce for Amazon, rivalries have heated up. So far, vigorously competing clouds and changes in Apple’s privacy policies on the iPhone—which hurt Facebook’s ad revenues according to results released on October 25th—are the main examples. But on October 21st Google announced that it would lower the fee it charges providers of subscriptions in its app store to 15%, putting pressure on Apple to do the same. And with so many people now working remotely and probably continuing to do so, a platform battle has broken out between Google, Microsoft, Salesforce and Zoom, a popular videoconferencing service, over which will dominate the virtual office.

heat up 激化

things are heating up in the election 选举渐渐趋于白热化

疫情期间科技行业的第三个重大变化可能是最重要的:竞争加剧。尽管GAFAM的成员还没有互相攻击对方的主要业务，比如谷歌的在线搜索业务和亚马逊的电子商务业务，但竞争已经趋于白热化。到目前为止，主要的例子是激烈竞争的云计算和苹果对iPhone隐私政策的改变（根据10月25日发布的结果，隐私政策的改变损害了Facebook的广告收入）。但在10月21日谷歌宣布将其向应用商店的订阅提供商收取的费用降低至15%，这就迫使苹果也得这么做。由于现在有很多人远程办公，而且这一情况很可能还会持续，针对谷歌、微软、Salesforce和热门视频会议服务Zoom之间“谁将主导虚拟办公”

的平台大战已经爆发。

Other firms are also picking more fights with GAFAM. Facebook’s social-media fortress looks a lot less safe now that it has at least two serious rivals: America’s Snapchat, a social network owned by Snap, and TikTok, the short-video app operated by ByteDance, a Chinese internet giant. According to data divulged in a recent wave of leaks, Facebook’s teenage users in America now spend two to three times longer on TikTok than on Instagram, which belongs to the American social-media conglomerate. Amazon also faces more competition, both in the form of incumbents that have at last embraced the digital world, including Walmart, and newcomers, such as Shopify, which helps merchants sell online and fulfil orders. PayPal’s attempt to buy Pinterest, another social network, now seems to have been abandoned, but it would have helped PayPal to move deeper into ecommerce.

其他公司也与GAFAM展开了更多的较量。Facebook的社交媒体堡垒看起来不那么安全了，因为它至少有两家强大的竞争对手:美国的Snapchat (Snap旗下的社交网络)和TikTok(由中国互联网巨头字节跳动运营的短视频APP)。根据最近一波披露的数据，Facebook在美国的青少年用户现在花在TikTok上的时间是Instagram的两到三倍，Instagram隶属于这家美国社交媒体集团Facebook。亚马逊也面临着更多的竞争，既有最终融入数字世界的现有企业(包括沃尔玛)，也有帮助商家在线销售和完成订单的Shopify等新企业。PayPal收购另一家社交网络公司Pinterest的尝试如今似乎已被放弃，它本可以帮助PayPal进一步进军电子商务领域。

After nearly two years of covid-19 the tech industry is cloudier, more tied to hardware and more turbulent. Of these trends, the first two are unlikely to last for ever, at least in their current form. Digital meteorologists argue that the cloud has already reached “peak centralisation”, meaning that it will henceforth grow not so much through football-pitch-sized data centres, but at the “edge”, where its digital services touch the physical world. And given the economics of the semiconductor industry—fabrication plants often cost over $10bn and take years to build—the chip shortage could eventually turn into a glut.

henceforth 从此以后

glut 供过于求

在经历了近两年的新冠疫情之后，科技行业变得更加“云化”，与硬件的联系更加紧密，也更加动荡。在这些趋势中，前两种趋势不太可能永远持续下去，至少以目前的形式是这样。数字“气象学家”认为，云已经达到了“集中化的峰值”，这意味着它今后将不再靠着足球场大小的数据中心来增长，而是靠“边缘”设备，即其数字服务与现实世界接触的地方。考虑到半导体行业的经济状况，制造工厂的成本往往超过100亿美元，而且需要数年时间才能建成，芯片短缺可能最终会变成供过于求。

A more open question is how long the new phase of competition will last. Optimists argue that, after a long period of ossification, the pandemic has helped push the industry into a more dynamic period, in which the giants compete with each other as well as with smaller firms. Pessimists say that this phase will not last long—and that the industry’s leaders will sooner or later shore up their fortresses and buy out competitors. And that is why, more than ever before, trustbusters should not let down their guard.■

ossification 僵化

shore up 支撑；支持

一个悬而未决的问题是，新的竞争阶段将持续多久。乐观主义者认为，在经历了一段很长时间的僵化之后，疫情已经帮助该行业进入了一个更有活力的时期，在这个时期，巨头们相互竞争，也与较小的公司竞争。悲观主义者认为这个阶段不会持续太久，行业的领先者迟早会巩固他们的堡垒，并收购竞争对手。这就是为什么反垄断者比以往任何时候都不应该放松警惕的原因。
