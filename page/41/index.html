The Beatles and the art of teamwork

披头士和团队合作的艺术

Bartleby

巴托比

A new documentary on the Fab Four is a must-watch for managers, too

一部关于“披头士四人组”的新纪录片也是管理者们的必看节目

Dec 18th 2021

文章音频

PAUL IS STRUMMING his guitar in a studio in London. George yawns and Ringo looks on listlessly. John is late, as usual. Suddenly, magic. A melody starts to take shape; George joins in on his guitar; Ringo claps out a beat. By the time John arrives, The Beatles’ next single, “Get Back”, is thrillingly recognisable.

PAUL正在伦敦的一间录音室里弹奏吉他。George打了个哈欠，Ringo无精打采地旁观着。 John和往常一样迟到了。突然, 像变魔法似的。旋律开始成形; George也跟着弹吉他; Ringo拍出一个节拍。等John来的时候，披头士的下一首单曲《Get Back》已经非常容易辨认了。

“Get Back” provides both the standout moment and the title of a glorious new documentary by Peter Jackson, charting the days that the band spent together in January 1969, writing and recording songs for a new album. For anyone interested in music, pop culture or creativity, the film is a stocking filled with treats. When George is struggling for a line to follow “Something in the way she moves”, John has advice. “Just say whatever comes into your head each time—‘attracts me like a cauliflower’—until you get the right words.”

《Get Back》不仅展现了这个非凡的时刻，也成为Peter Jackson的一部辉煌的新纪录片名字。这部纪录片记录了乐队在1969年1月一起为新专辑创作和录制歌曲所度过的日子。对于任何对音乐、流行文化或创意感兴趣的人来说，这部电影就像装满”惊喜”的袜子。当George绞尽脑汁想“Something in the way she moves”后面的词句时， John给出了建议。“只要每次想到什么就说什么，‘像花椰菜般如此吸引我’，直到你找到合适的词。”

Executives should watch it, too. The question of what makes a team sing is a staple of management research, and the Beatles documentary is a rare chance to watch a truly world-class team at work. It reinforces known principles, and adds some of its own.

高管们也应该看一看。如何让一个团队合唱是管理研究中的主要问题，而披头士的纪录片则是一个难得的机会，可以看到一个真正的世界一流团队工作。它强调了已有的原则，并添加了一些自己的原则。

Take the role of Ringo, for example. When he is not actually playing, the band’s drummer spends most of his time either asleep or looking bewildered. When the other three musicians bicker, Ringo smiles beatifically. To a casual observer, he might appear dispensable. But musically, nothing works without him, and as a team member he softens conflict and bridges divides.

以Ringo的角色为例。当他不演奏的时候，这个乐队的鼓手大部分时间要么在睡觉，要么一脸迷惑。当其他三位音乐家争吵时，Ringo露出了幸福的微笑。在旁观者看来，他似乎可有可无。但在音乐上，没有他，一切都无法运转。作为一名团队成员，他缓和了冲突，弥合了分歧。

Psychological make-up matters to how teams come together. Academics at Carnegie Mellon University and the Massachusetts Institute of Technology have found that the performance of groups is not correlated with their members’ average intelligence, but with characteristics such as sensitivity and how good teams are at giving everyone time to speak. Ringo provides backing; the band would be less cohesive without him.

心理构成对团队合作很重要。卡耐基梅隆大学和麻省理工学院的学者发现，团队的表现与其成员的平均智力无关，而是与其善解人意和团队给每个人发言时间的能力等特征相关。Ringo提供支持; 没有他，乐队就没有凝聚力了。

Another principle reinforced by the film: look here, there and everywhere for inspiration. In a study from McKinsey, more than 5,000 executives were asked to describe the environment in which they had their own best experiences of being in a team. Among other things, the consultancy identified the importance of “renewal”, the habit of keeping staleness at bay by taking risks, by learning from others and by innovating.

影片还强调了另一个原则: 要到处寻找灵感。在麦肯锡的一项研究中，5000多名高管被要求描述他们在团队中最愉快的经历所在的环境。除此之外，这家咨询公司还指出了“更新”的重要性，这是一种通过冒险、向他人学习和创新来避免过时的习惯。

“Get Back” shows a team of superstars embracing exactly that ethos: playing the songs of other bands, grabbing ideas like magpies and happily taking the advice and help of outsiders. It is the introduction of a pianist called Billy Preston, known to the group from their early days playing in Hamburg, which really makes the recording sessions start to click. (Let’s make him the fifth Beatle, suggests John. “It’s bad enough with four,” sighs Paul.)

《Get Back》展示了一个完全信奉这种“原则”的超级明星团队: 演奏其他乐队的歌曲，攫取来自多种不同风格的人的创意，乐于接受外界的建议和帮助。他们引进了键盘手“Billy Preston“，他在乐队早期在汉堡演奏时认识了该团队，这让录音开始顺畅起来。(John建议说，让他成为第五名披头士成员吧。Paul叹了口气说:“四个已经够糟的了”。)

A third message of the film concerns when and how to let it be. In an effort in 2016 called Project Aristotle, Google tried to define the characteristics of its most effective teams. One of its findings was that goals ought to be “specific, challenging and attainable”.

电影传达的第三个信息是关于何时以及如何让它实现。在2016年一项名为“Project Aristotle”的计划中，谷歌试图定义其最给人印象深刻的团队特征。它的一个发现是，目标应该是“具体的，具有挑战性的和可实现的”。

When they first meet up, on the second day of 1969, the band has a task that fits these criteria snugly: to write an album’s worth of new songs in just a matter of days and perform them on a TV special. But how they get there is left largely to them. That doesn’t always work out. At one point Paul yearns for a “central daddy figure” to set them straight on their scheduling. But the combination of a deadline and autonomy yields remarkable results.

1969年的第二天，当他们第一次见面时，乐队的任务正好符合这些标准: 在几天的时间内写出一张专辑的新歌，并在一个电视特别节目中演唱。但如何实现这一目标，很大程度上取决于他们自己。这并不总是行得通。有一段时间， Paul渴望有一个“核心爸爸人物”来让他们的日程安排更加明确。但是，把最后期限和自主权结合起来会产生显著的结果。

There are limits to what can be learned from “Get Back”. The Beatles are not always supportive of each other—George, feeling disregarded by John and Paul, briefly quits the band. Drugs played a part in their output: LSD may be a red line for some managers. Although technical ability is not the only determinant of success, sheer talent helped. Any band with a Lennon, a McCartney and a Harrison in it would have an advantage.

从《Get Back》中学到的东西是有限的。披头士并不总是互相支持，George感觉被 John和Paul忽视，短暂地退出了乐队。毒品在他们的产出中起到了一定的作用:LSD对一些管理者来说可能是一条红线。虽然技术能力并不是成功的唯一决定因素，但十足的天赋也是有帮助的。有Lennon、McCartney和Harrison的作为乐队的成员就会有优势。

But one wider lesson comes through loud and clear. The Beatles love what they do for a living. When they are not playing music, they are talking about it or thinking about it. They do take after take of their own songs, and jam constantly. Managers who think that building esprit de corps requires a separate activity from work—here-comes-the-fun time, set aside for axe-throwing or GIF battles or something equally ghastly—are missing a fundamental point. The highest-performing teams derive the greatest satisfaction not from each other, but from the work they do together.

但一个更广泛的教训是显而易见的。披头士热爱他们的工作。当他们不演奏音乐的时候，他们会谈论音乐或思考音乐。他们确实会一首接一首地唱自己的歌曲，而且经常即兴演奏。那些认为培养团队精神需要将其活动与工作分开的管理者们（如娱乐时刻，给axe-throwing游戏、GIF battle 或其他同样“可怕的”活动留出时间）忽略了一个基本要点。表现最好的团队不是从彼此身上获得最大的满足，而是从他们一起做的工作中获得最大的满足。

Fashion as an asset class

时尚作为一种资产类别

one woman’s trash

Technology has made it easy and worthwhile to sell old clothes

技术使得卖旧衣服变得容易而且值得

Dec 18th 2021

NEW YORK

文章音频

A FLAT WHITE might last three minutes; a bottle of fine whiskey a year if it is savoured slowly. iPhones are typically replaced after two or three years, and cars after a decade or so. Some possessions are likely to outlive you, most notably your house. Some outlast civilisations: look at Ancient Roman jewellery. Everything you own lies on a spectrum, with consumption goods, such as a coffee or newspaper, at one end and investment goods, such as a house or a diamond, at the other. In the middle are durable or “durable-ish” goods, such as cars, coffee tables and washing machines.

一杯馥芮白能喝个三分钟; 一瓶上等威士忌，如果慢慢品味，能喝个一年。苹果手机通常两三年后就会被更换，汽车大约十年后就会被更换。有些财产可能比你都长寿，特别是你的房子。有些比文明更持久: 看看古罗马珠宝就知道了。你所拥有的一切(东西)都处于一个范围内，这个范围的一端是消费品，如咖啡或报纸，另一端是投资品，如房子或钻石。位于中间的则是耐用或“较耐用”的商品，如汽车、咖啡桌和洗衣机。

Investment goods decay so slowly that, if scarce, their value may increase. Durable ones have utility and thus value for many years, but tend to depreciate while you own them. (Rare exceptions include vintage cars and Moon-landing editions of the New York Times.)

投资品破损速度很慢，以至于如果该投资品稀缺，它们的价值可能会上升。耐用的商品具有实用价值，因此可以使用多年，但当你拥有它们时，它们往往会贬值。(像老爷车和《纽约时报》登月版这种属于罕见的例外。)

Where goods lie on this spectrum determines not only how long they last but what sort of market develops to trade in them. Many more people buy used homes or rent properties than buy new-builds, and only around a quarter of car purchases are of new vehicles. No one, by contrast, tries to resell bagged lettuce. Clothing lies in between. Well-made leather or denim items may last for a decade; a flimsy silk camisole for a season. But durability is not the only factor. Fashion matters, too: desirability can be fleeting.

商品在范围上的位置不仅决定了它们的“持续”时间，还决定了要发展什么样的市场来交易它们。购买二手房或租赁房产的人要比购买新房的人多得多，而购买汽车的人中只有大约四分之一是新车。相比之下，没有人试图转卖袋装生菜。服装则介于两者之间。制作精良的皮革或牛仔商品可以穿十年; 一件轻薄的真丝背心只能穿一个季节。但耐用并不是唯一的因素。时尚也很重要: 受欢迎的程度可能转瞬即逝。

All the more so in an era of just-in-time supply chains and social-media influencers. People now spend a lower share of their income on clothing than ever before, but the number of items purchased each year has ballooned. Many items are worn a few times before being discarded; 95% of the clothes Americans send to landfills are in good enough nick to be reused or resold. This is wasteful and environmentally troubling. Reliable estimates are scarce, but industry studies reckon that clothing manufacture and distribution account for between 2% and 8% of global carbon emissions. The fashion industry probably emits more carbon than aviation (3% of emissions) or shipping (2%).

在按需生产式的供应链以及社交媒体网红盛行的时代，更是如此。人们现在花在服装上的钱比以往任何时候都要少，但每年购买的衣服的数量却急剧增加。许多衣服在被扔之前只穿了几次; 美国人送到垃圾填埋场的衣服中，有95%的衣服状况良好，都可以达到重新利用或转售的程度。这既浪费又不利于环境。可靠的估计很少，不过该行业研究认为，服装制造和分销占全球碳排放的2%到8%。时尚产业的碳排放量可能比航空业(3%)或航运业(2%)还要多。

Yet technology is reducing the friction in trade of all kinds. This started in financial markets, where whizzy algorithms and vast amounts of data have pushed trading costs practically to zero. More recently online platforms such as OpenDoor and Redfin, which use data about property features and locations to estimate values of homes algorithmically, have started to drive down estate agents’ commissions. The trend then extended beyond investment goods. That was entirely more radical since it made markets where none had existed. Look at Airbnb and Uber, which turned empty homes and idle cars into sources of income.

不过，技术正在减少各种贸易中的摩擦。这始于金融市场，在那里，高超的算法和大量的数据已经把交易成本几乎推到了零。最近，OpenDoor和Redfin等在线平台开始压低房地产中介的佣金。这些平台利用有关房产特征和位置的数据，通过算法来估算房屋的价值。这一趋势随后延伸到了投资品之外。这更为彻底，因为它创造了前所未有的市场。看看Airbnb和Uber，它们把空置的房屋和闲置的汽车变成了收入来源。

Now it has moved to goods in the middle of the investment-consumption spectrum. A decade ago you would have struggled to offload second-hand clothing, let alone get paid for it. Emptying your closet meant a trip to a charity shop. A few high-value items could be resold, says Julie Wainwright, the founder of The RealReal, an online second-hand-clothing site, but largely in “pawn shops or local consignment stores, where the experience and the payouts were not good”.

现在，它已转向位于“投资-消费范围”中间的商品。十年前，你卖掉二手衣服都很困难，更别说从中获得报酬了。清空衣橱意味着要跑一趟慈善商店。在线二手服装网站the RealReal的创始人Julie Wainwright表示，少数高价值物品可以转售，但主要是在“当铺或当地寄卖店，这些地方的体验和回报都不太好”。

This all meant that the market, in economist-speak, was thin and illiquid. Matching buyers and sellers was tricky; transactions were rare; commissions were high. “One kind of illiquid market used to be the market for knick-knacks in the attic,” says Alvin Roth, an economist at Stanford University who won a Nobel prize for his work on market structure. “But the internet made it possible to have your lawn sale on eBay.”

用经济学家的话说，这一切都意味着市场不景气且缺乏流动性。撮合买家和卖家是件棘手的事; 交易很少; 佣金又很高。斯坦福大学经济学家Alvin Roth说:“过去流动性不足的市场是那种在阁楼里的小摆设市场。但互联网可以让你在eBay上出售草坪。” Roth曾因在市场结构方面的研究获得诺贝尔奖。

The fashion industry probably emits more carbon than aviation or shipping

时尚产业排放的碳可能比航空或航运还要多

Once Airbnb and Uber had propelled the idea of a sharing economy into the mainstream, firms turning used clothing into an asset class were not far behind. As with accommodation and transport, not just resale but rental was revolutionised. By Rotation and Rotaro act like sharing-economy apps for wardrobes. Now, whether people are reselling knick-knacks, lending out old clothes, renting a spare room or picking up passengers in their spare time, they are making better use of their assets than before.

一旦Airbnb和Uber将共享经济的理念推向主流，将旧衣服转变为资产类别的公司也紧随其后。就像住宿和交通一样，不仅可以在“转售”上革新，“租赁”也产生了革命性的变化。By Rotation和Rotaro有点像是为衣橱设置的共享经济APP。现在，无论是转卖小摆设、出借旧衣服、出租空房间还是在空闲时间接乘客，人们都比以前能够更好地利用自己的资产。

In 2021 resold clothing fetched around $15bn, up from less than $1bn in 2013. A further $21bn was spent on garments from charity and thrift shops. The total spent on second-hand garb, some $36bn, is slightly bigger than the $30bn spent on “fast fashion” in shops such as Zara or H& M. By 2025, according to GlobalData, a research firm, the value of resold and thrifted clothing will climb to $77bn as resale revenues triple to $47bn annually and charity-shop revenues climb to $30bn. Combined revenues will dwarf those from fast fashion which are expected to grow to just $40bn.

2021年，服装转售收入约为150亿美元，这一数字比2013年的不到10亿美元要高。另有210亿美元用于购买来自慈善机构和旧货店的服装。在二手服装上的总花费达到大约360亿美元，略高于花在Zara或H&M这样的快时尚品牌店铺的300亿美元。根据研究公司GlobalData的数据显示，到2025年,转售价值以及旧衣服的价值将攀升至770亿美元，其中转售年收入将增三倍达到470亿美元，慈善机构商店的收入将攀升至300亿美元。合并之后的营收将超过那些预计仅为400亿美元收入的快时尚品牌。

Online clothing resellers’ business models vary. The RealReal and Vestiaire Collective target higher-end fashion—think Chanel bags and Gucci loafers. They make selling easy by, for example, sending couriers to collect items. But they are fussy about what they take. The RealReal charges at least 20% of the sale price (and as much as 60%). In return they stand between buyers and sellers, setting or suggesting prices, organising shipping and authenticating garments so that buyers can trust that their purchases are genuine.

在线服装转售生意的经营模式各不相同。RealReal和Vestiaire Collective的目标是高端时尚产品，比如香奈儿包和古驰乐福鞋。他们让销售变得容易，例如，他们派“快递员”去收商品。但他们对所接受的东西很挑剔。RealReal至少收取售价的20%(最高可达60%)。作为回报，他们站在买家和卖家之间，设定或建议价格，安排运输和鉴定服装，以便让买家相信他们购买的是真货。

ThredUP also takes possession of items, but will accept anything a seller wants to get rid of, from high-street fast-fashion brands through to designer labels, before sorting, pricing and listing items that pass a quality inspection (rejects are returned or recycled). Users get paid a fraction of the sale price (as little as 5% for a $5 item; up to 80% for those that sell for more than $200). Others, such as Depop and Poshmark, are peer-to-peer platforms. These allow users to list their own items at a price of their choosing, but also leave them to do the legwork and shipping. They take simple flat-rate commissions: Depop’s is 10%; Poshmark 20%.

ThredUP也收商品，但它也将接受卖家想要处理的任何东西，从商业大街的快时尚品牌到设计师时装品牌，然后对通过质量检查的商品进行分类、定价和挂牌(不合格的商品将被退回或回收)。用户将获得销售价格的一小部分收益(5美元商品的收益仅为5%; 售价超过200美元的可达80%)。其他的，如Depop和Poshmark，则是点对点平台。它们允许用户以自己选择的价格上架自己的商品，但跑腿和运输的事情他们自己负责。他们收取简单的固定费率佣金:Depop的是10%; Poshmark 20%。

From bags to riches

All are now firmly established. The RealReal became the first to go public in 2019. ThredUp and Posh mark listed in 2021. Depop, which was founded in Britain, was acquired by Etsy, a New York-based online marketplace, in June. Vestiaire remains privately held. Between them these fashion resellers are valued at around $8.4bn, a tiny fraction of the market capitalisation of the fast-fashion giants, Inditex (which owns Zara) and Hennes and Mauritz (which owns H& M along with & Other Stories, COS and Weekday), of $100bn and $30bn respectively.

所有这些公司地位都已稳固确立了。RealReal是这些公司中第一家上市的公司(2019年上市)。ThredUp和Posh mark于2021年上市。Depop成立于英国，今年6月被总部在纽约的在线市场Etsy收购。Vestiaire仍为私人所有。这些时装再销售的企业市值总计约为84亿美元，仅相当于快时尚巨头Inditex(旗下拥有Zara)和Hennes and Mauritz(旗下拥有 H& M、& Other Stories、COS和Weekday)市值分别为1000亿美元和300亿美元的一小部分。

But retailers, too, have cottoned on to the idea that old clothes can sell. ThredUP works with high-street brands such as Madewell, which now offer used (or “preloved”) items alongside new stuff in stores and online. Brands can customise what they take. “Madewell wanted to tell a particular story about denim,” says James Reinhart, the co-founder of thredUP. Merchandise and tech from thredUP lie behind the resale arms of many major retailers, such as Walmart. The proceeds are split between the retailer, thredUP and the sellers who ship stuff in to be resold.

但零售商们也意识到旧衣服可以卖出去。ThredUP与Madewell等商业街品牌合作，这些品牌现在在商店和网上出售新商品的同时也提供二手(或“旧的”)商品。品牌可以定制他们所接受的产品。thredUP的联合创始人James Reinhart说:“Madewell想讲述一个关于牛仔服装的特殊故事。” thredUP的商品和技术是沃尔玛等许多大型零售商转售业务的”幕后推手”。销售所得收入将在零售商、thredUP以及将货物运至转售的卖家之间进行分摊。

The boredom of covid-19 lockdowns may have boosted resale by giving people time to clear out their wardrobes and browse second-hand fashion online. According to estimates from GlobalData last year saw over 33m new buyers and 36m new sellers of old garb.

那些厌倦了新冠疫情的封锁的人，可能会有时间清理自己的衣橱，并在网上浏览二手时装，从而推动转售。据GlobalData的估计，去年旧服装的新买家超过3300万，新卖家超过3600万。

The idea that clothing is for a season, not forever, is even clearer in the peer-to-peer rental market. Eshita Kabra-Davies set up By Rotation, a wardrobe-swapping app, in 2019. It allows users to list items available to borrow. The fee is usually around 5% of a garment’s retail price per day. Dresses tend to be rented for three or four days, to wear over a weekend, or to take on holiday. Ms Kabra-Davies was inspired by trying out an American service that rented garments it owned, only to be disappointed when the selection felt outdated. “I actually want to borrow the outfits that women are wearing on Instagram right now,” she says. “That was when I thought we should just let people share.”

在点对点的租赁市场上，“服装只穿一季，而不是永久穿”的理念更为明显。Eshita Kabra-Davies于2019年创建了一款名为By Rotation的衣橱交换APP。它允许用户上架可供借出的商品。租借一天的费用通常是服装零售价的5%左右。裙子一般租三到四天，周末穿，或者度假时带上。Kabra-Davies女士是在尝试一项美国服务时受到启发的，该服务租借它拥有的服装，结果当选择的服装感觉过时时，她感到失望。她说:“实际上，我想借女性现在在Instagram上穿的服装。从那时起，我就认为我们应该让人们共享。”

With renting out clothes, as with allowing strangers into your car or home, comes the worry that they will ruin your prize possessions. By Rotation allows lenders to bill borrowers extra if a hem is ripped or a dress stained (it steps in if owner and borrower cannot agree). But mishaps, especially ones requiring intervention, are rare, says Ms Kabra-Davies. And rental yields can quickly add up. Some frequent lenders with big wardrobes make up to £2,500 ($3,300) per month.

出租衣服，就像允许陌生人进入你的车或房子一样，会让人担心他们会毁了你珍贵的财产。By Rotation允许借出去的人像借的人收取额外费用，如果裙边被撕破或裙子被弄脏(如果两方无法达成一致，它会介入)。但是，Kabra-Davies女士说，事故，尤其是需要干预的事故，是很少发生的。而且租金收益可以迅速增加。一些有着大衣橱的经常“借”衣服的人每月的收入高达2500英镑(3300美元)。

Clothing is not the only high-value durable good being shared for a fee. FatLlama, a British platform, allows people to rent out anything (fancy camera equipment does well). Indeed, the idea of renting out durable, or durable-ish, goods makes such sense that it is surprising that it did not take off before. In the case of clothing that may be because perceptions needed to shift. Only close friends could have been asked for the loan of a jacket or dress—and even then they might not share your taste, or indeed vital statistics.

衣服并不是唯一一种可以有偿分享的高价值耐用物品。一个英国平台FatLlama允许人们出租任何东西(高档的摄影设备很受欢迎)。事实上，出租耐用品或较为耐用的商品的想法是很有意义的，以致于它以前没有流行起来是令人惊讶的。就服装而言，这可能是因为人们的观念需要转变。只有亲密的朋友才会向你借一件夹克或一件连衣裙，即使是这样，他们也可能和你品味不同，甚至在重要数据上也不一样。

The very thought that an item might be rented out or resold in the future changes how consumers approach buying it in the first place. Ms Wainwright of The RealReal says that most of its users regularly shop at posh department stores. Its proprietary surveys find that they “are starting to check The RealReal first to see how a luxury item retains value on the secondary market before making primary market purchases”. That is, they are more likely to buy high-quality garments, knowing that at least part of the cost may be recouped.

一件商品可能在未来被出租或转售的想法，首先会改变消费者购买它的方式。RealReal的Wainwright女士表示，它们APP的大多数用户经常在高档百货商店购物。该公司的自营调查发现，他们在一级市场购买之前“会先上RealReal，看看一件奢侈品在二级市场的保值”。也就是说，他们更有可能购买高质量的服装，因为他们知道至少可以收回一部分成本。

The biggest shift in perception, however, is not among people who sell or rent their clothing, but at the other end of the deal. A poll in 2016 by GlobalData found that 45% of adults had bought second-hand clothing, or said they would consider doing so. That share is now 86%. Influencers document trips to charity shops and show off their purchases. A decade ago wearing second-hand clothes was uncool, and teens hung out in Abercrombie & Fitch or Jack Wills. Stroll a hipster neighbourhood today—Williamsburg in Brooklyn, say—and passers-by will have bought their outfits in thrift stores like Goodwill and Housing Works, or curated shops like Awoke Vintage.

然而，观念上的最大转变并不发生在出售或出租服装的人身上，而是发生在交易的另一端。2016年GlobalData的一项调查发现，45%的成年人购买过二手衣服，或表示他们会考虑这么做。现在这个比例是86%。网红会记录他们去慈善商店的行程，并炫耀他们买的东西。十年前，穿着二手衣服并不酷，青少年们在Abercrombie & Fitch或Jack Wills闲逛。如今漫步在一个潮流的街区，比如布鲁克林的威廉斯堡，路人会在Goodwill和Housing Works这样的旧货店，或者像Awoke Vintage这样的策划店买他们的服装。

In this way the shift towards second-hand fashion is self-reinforcing, with fashion tastes changing because…well, because fashion tastes have changed. Once a trendsetter wears something, others seek to emulate the look. The more people sell their old stuff, the cooler wearing it becomes, too. ■

在这种情况下，随着时尚品味的改变，人们向二手时装的转变是自我强化的，因为……嗯，因为时尚品味已经改变了。一旦引领潮流的人穿了某件衣服，其他人就会试图模仿。卖旧衣服的人越多，穿旧衣服就越酷。■

And how the pandemic may change them

疫情时是如何改变它们的

Dec 18th 2021

new york, paris and yountville

纽约，巴黎，扬特维尔

文章音频

APRIL 9th 2020 was the restaurant industry’s darkest day. The imposition of lockdowns to slow the spread of covid-19, combined with people voluntarily avoiding others, meant that on that Thursday bookings in America, Australia, Britain, Canada, Germany, Ireland and Mexico via OpenTable, a restaurant-reservation website, normally in their millions, fell to zero. Now, as economies unlock, many restaurants, even the fanciest, are facing labour shortages. Le Gavroche, one of London’s swankiest French offerings, has had to stop its lunch service and has lost its general manager.

2020年4月9日是餐饮业最惨淡的一天。为减缓covid-19的传播而实施的封锁的这一“过分要求”，再加上人们自愿避开他人，这就意味着在那个周四，美国、澳大利亚、英国、加拿大、德国、爱尔兰和墨西哥通过OpenTable(餐厅预订网站)进行预订的人降为零, 通常是会有数百万人进行预订。现在，随着经济的再次开放，许多餐馆，甚至是最高档的餐馆，都面临着劳动力短缺的问题。伦敦最豪华的法国餐厅之一Le Gavroche不得不停止其午餐服务，并且失去了它的总经理。

Covid brought to a halt an astonishing expansion. In 2010-19 the number of licensed restaurants in Britain grew by 26%. Americans were, for the first time, spending more than half their total food budget on eating out. Well-paid folk from Hong Kong to Los Angeles were happily renting kitchenless apartments: why bother cooking when good food was so lavishly available beyond your front door?

新冠肺炎阻止了餐馆的惊人的扩张。2010-2019年，英国持牌餐馆的数量增长了26%。美国人在外出就餐上的花费首次超过了食物总预算的一半。那些收入颇丰的人(从香港到洛杉矶)都很高兴地租下了没有厨房的公寓: 当你家门口的美食如此丰富时，为什么还要麻烦地下厨呢?

Being deprived of restaurants has made people realise how much they value them. Eating out fulfils needs which seem fundamental to human nature. People need to date, to seal deals and to peer at their fellow humans. At a good restaurant you can travel without travelling, or simply feel coddled.

“失去”餐馆让人们意识到餐馆对他们有多重要。外出就餐满足了人类基本的需求。人们需要约会，敲定交易，仔细观察他们的同伴。在一家好餐馆里，你可以不用出门旅行就能拥有“旅行”的体验，或仅是感觉到被悉心照顾。

Yet restaurants in their current form are a few hundred years old at most. They do not satisfy some primeval urge, but rather those of particular sorts of societies. Economic and social forces, from political reform to urbanisation to changing labour markets, have created both the supply of and demand for restaurants. Their history also hints at what their future could look like in a post-pandemic world.

然而，目前形式的餐馆最多也只有几百年的历史。它们不是满足某些原始的欲望，而是满足某些特定的上层社会的人的欲望。从政治改革到城市化再到不断变化的劳动力市场，经济和社会力量共同创造了餐馆的供给和需求。它们的历史也暗示了在大流行病之后的世界，它们的未来可能是什么样子。

People have long feasted outside the home. Archaeologists have counted 158 snack bars in Pompeii, a city destroyed by a volcano in 79AD—one for every 60-100 people, a higher ratio than many global cities today. Ready-cooked meat, game and fish were available for Londoners to eat from at least the 1170s. Samuel Cole, an early settler, opened what is considered to be the first American tavern in 1634, in Boston.

长期以来，人们一直在外面大吃大喝。据考古学家统计，庞贝古城(公元79年被维苏威火山爆发摧毁)有158家快餐店，每家为60-100人服务，这一比例高于当今许多全球城市。至少从12世纪70年代起，伦敦人就可以吃到即食的肉、野味和鱼。1634年，早期移民Samuel Cole在波士顿开了一家酒馆，这家小酒馆被认为是美国第一家酒馆。

These were more like takeaways, though, or stands where food might be thrown in with a drink, than restaurants. The table d’hôte, which appeared in France around Cole’s time, most closely resembled a modern restaurant. Clients sat at a single table and ate what they were given (trends now making a comeback). Many of these proto-restaurants resembled community kitchens, or quasi-charities, which existed for the benefit of locals. Strangers were not always welcome.

不过，比起餐馆，这些地方更像外卖餐馆或小吃摊，在那里食物会附赠饮料。大约在Cole那个时代在法国出现的套餐，与现代餐厅最相似。客户们坐在单人的桌子旁，吃着给他们的食物(现在这种趋势再次流行起来)。这些早期餐馆中有许多都类似于社区厨房，或类似于慈善机构，它们的存在是为了造福当地人。“陌生人“并不总是受欢迎的。

Nor were they destinations predominantly for the well-heeled. Before the use of coal became widespread in England in the 17th century, preparing food at home involved spending a lot on wood or peat. Professional kitchens, by contrast, benefited from economies of scale in energy consumption and so could provide meals at a lower cost than people could themselves. Today dining out is seen as an indulgence, but it was the cheapest way to eat for most of human history.

它们也不是富人的主要目的地。在17世纪英国广泛使用煤炭之前，在家做饭需要花费大量的木材或泥炭块。相比之下，专业厨房受益于能源消耗的规模经济，因此在一顿饭比自己在家做一顿的成本要低很多。现在，“外出就餐”被视为一种放纵，但在人类历史的大部分时间里，外出就餐是最便宜的方式。

It was, thus, a low-status activity. Cicero and Horace reckoned that a visitor to a bar might as well have visited a brothel. According to “Piers Plowman”, a late-14th-century poem, cooks would “poison the people privily and oft”. Some rich types rented private dining rooms; Samuel Pepys, a 17th-century diarist, enjoyed eating “in the French style” (that is, with communal dishes) at one in London. But most wealthy people preferred to eat at home, enjoying the luxury of having staff to cook and clean up.

因此，这是一项“地位低下”的人的活动。Cicero和Horace认为，一个去酒吧的人就好像去了妓院一样。根据一首14世纪晚期的诗《Piers Plowman》，厨师会“私下里经常毒害人们”。有些富人租用私人餐厅; 17世纪的日记作家Samuel Pepys喜欢在伦敦的一家餐馆吃“法国风格”的食物(也就是说，搭配公共菜肴)。但大多数富人更喜欢在家里吃饭，享受有员工做饭和收拾的奢华生活。

Over time, however, the notion that a respectable person might eat a meal in public gradually took hold. Wilton’s, a fish restaurant in London, got going in 1742. Dublin’s oldest, established in 1775, traded under the name of the “Three Blackbirds” and was “noted for a good bottle of Madeira, as well as for a Chop from the Charcoal Grill”. Fraunces Tavern, New York City’s oldest restaurant, probably opened in 1762 (it is still open today and serves determinedly American fare from clam chowder to New York prime strip steaks).

然而，随着时间的推移，“一个体面的人可能会在公共场合吃饭”的观念逐渐形成。伦敦的一家鱼味馆Wilton ‘s餐厅于1742年开业。都柏林最古老的一家餐厅建于1775年，以Three Blackbirds为名字做生意，并以“优质的马德拉白葡萄酒和炭烤排骨闻名”。Fraunces Tavern酒馆是纽约市最古老的餐馆，可能开业于1762年(它至今仍在营业，坚持提供从蛤蜊浓汤到纽约上等牛排等各种美式食物)。

Some historians look to the supply side to explain this shift, arguing that the restaurant emerged as a result of improvements in competition policy. Powerful guilds often made it hard for a business to sell two different products simultaneously. Butchers monopolised the sale of meat; vintners that of wine. The growth of the restaurant, which serves many different things, required breaking down these barriers to trade.

一些历史学家从供应方面来解释这种转变，他们认为餐馆的出现是竞争政策改善的结果。强大的同业公会通常会让做生意的人很难同时销售两种不同的产品。肉贩垄断了肉类的销售; 酿葡萄酒的人垄断了葡萄酒的销售。提供多种东西的餐厅要想发展，就需要打破这些贸易壁垒。

A Monsieur Boulanger, a soup-maker in Paris, may have been the first to do so. He dared sell a dish of “sheep’s feet in white-wine sauce”. The city’s traiteurs(caterers) claimed the dish contained a ragout, a meat dish only they were allowed to prepare, and was therefore illegal. They took their case to court, but Boulanger triumphed. The tale, supposedly marking the beginning of a movement in mid-18th-century France towards more open markets, is probably apocryphal. But other regulatory changes did help. In Britain reformers worried about public drunkenness passed a law in 1860 allowing places serving food to serve wine as well (thus encouraging people to eat something to sop up the booze). Around the same time American states started passing food-safety laws, giving customers more confidence in the quality of the food.

巴黎的一个做汤师傅Monsieur Boulanger也许是第一个“打破壁垒”的人。他敢卖“白葡萄酒炖羊蹄”这道菜。该市餐饮供应商称，这道菜中含有“蔬菜炖肉”，这是一种只有他们才能做的肉类菜肴，因此是违法的。他们上了法庭，不过Boulanger赢了。据说，这一故事标志着18世纪中期法国走向更开放市场的运动的开始，但它很可能是杜撰的故事。但其他监管改革确实有所帮助。在英国，改革派担心公共场合酗酒，在1860年通过了一项法律，允许提供食物的地方也提供葡萄酒(因此鼓励人们吃点东西来消化酒精)。与此同时，美国各州开始通过食品安全法，让消费者对食品质量更有信心。

Yet for restaurants to flourish, richer people had to demand what Pepys did not: eating in full view of others. Until the 18th century elites largely viewed public spaces as dirty and dangerous, or as an arena of spectacle. But as capitalism took off, public spaces became sites of rational dialogue which were (putatively) open to all. And, as Charles Baudelaire, a French poet, observed, 19th-century cities also became places where people indulged in conspicuous consumption.

然而，为了让餐馆兴旺发达，富人必须要求Pepys所没有的: 在众人面前用餐。直到18世纪，“精英们”大多都认为公共场所既肮脏又危险，或者是当作一个引入注目的场所。但随着资本主义的兴起，公共场所成为理性对话的场所，并(公认地)向所有人开放。而且，正如法国诗人Charles Baudelaire所观察到的，19世纪的城市也变成了人们沉溺于“炫耀性消费”的地方。

The restaurant was the natural habitat of the flâneur, Baudelaire’s wandering observer of city life. Where better than a restaurant to see and be seen? Out went the set menu of the table d’hôte; in came the à la carte kind. Shared tables gave way to private ones. Eating out became less of a communal activity focused on calorie intake and more of a cultural experience—and a place, as Baudelaire wrote, where people could show off their wealth by ordering more food than they could eat and drinking more than they needed.

餐馆是“游手好闲的人”的正常的栖息地，他们则是Baudelaire所指的城市生活的“漫步”观察家。还有什么地方比餐馆更能看到(别人)和被人看到呢? (价格固定的)套餐消失了; 引入了“按菜单点菜的”模式。公用桌子让位给私人桌子。外出就餐不再是一种关注热量摄入的集体活动，而更多的是一种文化体验，正如Baudelaire所写的那样，在这里，人们可以点“远超于他们所能吃得下的“更多的食物、喝(远超于他们所需的)更多的酒来炫耀自己的财富。

Restaurants’ growth accelerated in the 20th century. American employment in food service quadrupled as a share of the workforce over this period. The Michelin Guide was first published in 1900; the stars came 26 years later. And yet the continued rise of the restaurant up until the pandemic nonetheless presents an economic puzzle. Cooking at home was becoming ever easier. Average house sizes grew. Appliances such as the food processor and the dishwasher reduced preparation and clean-up times. Dining out became relatively more expensive: in America in 1930 a restaurant meal was 25% costlier than an equivalent meal at home, but by 2014 the gap had risen to 280%. From 2007-20 “French Laundry inflation”, describing the cost of a meal at a three-Michelin-star restaurant in California, was twice the core inflation rate.

餐馆在20世纪加速增多。在此期间，美国在食品服务业的就业人数占劳动力总数的比例翻了两番。《米其林指南》于1900年首次出版; 26年后，星级出现了。然而，直到疫情爆发之前，餐馆的持续增多在经济上仍是个“谜”。在家做饭变得越来越容易了。房屋平均面积增加了。像食品加工机和洗碗机这样的设备减少了准备和清理的时间。外出就餐变得相对昂贵:在1930年的美国，在餐馆吃饭比在家吃饭要贵25%，但到2014年，这一差距上升到了280%。从2007年到2020年，“法国洗衣房通胀”(描述的是加州一家米其林三星餐厅的一顿饭的成本)是核心通胀率的两倍。

And yet three economic changes ensured that demand for restaurants grew despite rising prices. The first is immigration. In the 50 years after the second world war the net flow of migrants into rich countries, relative to population, more than quadrupled. Starting a restaurant is a good career move for new arrivals; it neither requires formal qualifications nor, at least for chefs, fluency in the local language. Migrants tend to improve the quality of an area’s restaurants. London’s became far better in the era of free movement with the European Union. The melting pot that is Singapore has some of the best food in the world. Restaurants became more tempting, even as prices went up.

不过，有三个经济变化确保了“尽管价格上涨，对餐馆的需求仍在增长”。首先是移民问题。在第二次世界大战后的50年里，相对于人口而言，发达国家的移民净流入翻了两番多。对新来者来说，开餐厅是一个很好的职业发展方向; 它既不需要正式的资格，也不需要精通当地语言(至少对厨师来说是这样)。移民往往会提高一个地区餐馆的质量。在欧盟自由流动时代，伦敦的餐厅情况要好得多。新加坡这个“大熔炉”有一些世界上最好的食物。即使价格上涨，餐馆也变得更有吸引力。

Gastronomics

The second factor was the changing microeconomics of the family. As a new paper by Rachel Griffith of the Institute for Fiscal Studies, a think-tank, and colleagues, shows, households’ choices about whether to make their own food or to buy it premade are shaped not only by the upfront cost of those things. They also depend on what economists call “shadow costs”.

第二个因素是家庭微观经济的变化。智库Institute for Fiscal Studies的Rachel Griffith 和他的同事最新的一篇论文中指出，家庭对于自己做饭还是买现成食物的选择不仅仅取决于这些东西的前期成本。它们还取决于经济学家所说的“影子成本”。

The true cost of an at-home meal involves not just the outlay for the ingredients, but the time spent on shopping and preparation. In an era of low female labour-force participation, shadow costs were low. A stay-at-home mother who cooked instead of eating out would have less leisure time. But as more women entered the workforce during the 20th century this equation changed, raising the shadow cost of cooking. Now a working woman who cooked dinner would be sacrificing time which might otherwise be used to earn money. And so eating out made increasing economic sense, even as it became more expensive.

在家吃一顿饭的真正成本不仅包括食材的费用，还包括花在购物和准备上的时间。在女性劳动力参与率低的时代，影子成本很低。一个在家做饭而不是在外面吃饭的母亲会有更少的空闲时间。但随着20世纪越来越多的女性进入劳动力市场，这一等式发生了改变，烹饪的影子成本也随之增加。现在一个做饭的工作的女性将会牺牲原本可以用来挣钱的时间。因此，外出就餐在经济上越来越有意义，尽管它变得越来越贵。

The third factor was changing working patterns. Historically poor people have tended to work longer hours than rich ones. But in the latter half of the 20th century the opposite became true. The rise of knowledge-intensive jobs, and globalisation, made rich people’s work more financially rewarding—and enjoyable. Toiling into the night became a sign of status. The upshot was that the people with the most money to spend on dining out increasingly needed it most, since they had the least free time. In Britain the richest tenth of households devote a much bigger chunk of their overall spending to dining and drinking out than the poorest tenth, and the gap has grown in recent years.

第三个因素是工作模式的改变。从历史上看，穷人往往比富人工作更长时间。但在20世纪下半叶，情况正好相反。知识密集型工作的兴起，以及全球化，使得富人的工作在经济上更有回报，也更有乐趣。工作到深夜成了一种地位的象征。结果是，那些花在外出就餐上的钱最多的人越来越需要外出就餐，因为他们的空闲时间最少。在英国，最富有的十分之一的家庭比最贫穷的十分之一的家庭花在外出就餐和喝酒上的开支要大得多，而且这一差距近年来还在扩大。

What does the history of the restaurant say about its future? People have relished their reopening. In recent weeks global restaurant reservations have been near their pre-pandemic levels. The best ones are booked up for months: Silicon Valley nerds have created automated bots which instantly reserve tables.

从餐馆的历史可以看出它的未来吗? 人们很享受餐馆的重新开放。最近几周，全球餐厅预订量已接近疫情爆发前的水平。最好的餐厅几个月后都被约满了: 硅谷的技术迷发明了可以立即预定餐位的自动化机器人。

The long-term future of the restaurant is less clear. The pandemic has led to many people buying more takeout than before (Uber’s revenue from delivery now exceeds what it earns from helping people get around), while others have a newfound love of cooking. Restaurants have little choice but to continue to adapt. That means moving still further from the utilitarian model of the 18th century and before, and instead doubling down on what they do best: offering those who need to eat a taste of romance, glamour and love. ■

餐馆的长期前景并不明朗。疫情导致许多人比以前更多购买外卖(现在Uber的外卖收入超过了它帮助人们出行的收入)，与此同时，另一些人则对烹饪产生了新兴趣。餐馆别无选择，只能继续适应。这意味着他们要进一步脱离18世纪及之前的功利主义模式，转而在他们最擅长的方面加倍努力: 为那些吃的是浪漫、魅力和爱的味道的人提供服务。■

IN THE “Society of the Spectacle”, published in the 1960s, Guy Debord, a leading theorist in a group of provocateurs known as the Situationists, depicts a culture in thrall to mass media where appearance is more important than fact and representation is preferred to reality. It is an unsettling book and not one that finance types would normally reach for. But after a strange year, the idea of the markets as spectacle has an appealing logic. A Debordian might say that their primary role is no longer capital allocation, or even enrichment, but entertainment. How else to make sense of stock prices driven haywire by social-media mobs or celebrities selling shares in shell companies?

在20世纪60年代出版的《景观社会》一书中，Guy Debord (一群被称为情境主义者的煽动者的主要理论家) 描绘了一种深受大众媒体影响的文化，在这种文化中，表象比事实更重要，描述比现实更受欢迎。这是一本令人不安的书，金融界人士通常也不会去读的书。但在经历了奇怪的一年之后，将市场视为“景观”的想法有了一个吸引人的逻辑。一个受到Debord影响的人可能会说他们的主要角色不再是资本分配，或者甚至不再是致富，而是娱乐。不然如何理解社交媒体上的乌合之众或名人抛售空壳公司股票会导致股价失控?

A reckoning feels due. And as 2021 draws to a close, markets seem nervy. Speculative assets, such as bitcoin, are down from their peaks. Even habitual bulls agree that 2022 will be difficult. On December 15th the Federal Reserve talked tough on inflation and signalled that it would end its asset purchases by mid-March and then start raising rates.

是时候算算账了。随着2021年接近尾声，市场似乎紧张不安。比特币等投机性资产正从峰值水平回落。就连一贯看涨的人士也认为，2022年将是艰难的一年。12月15日，美联储在通货膨胀问题上言辞强硬，并暗示其将在明年3月中旬结束资产购买，然后开始加息。

Yet there is an even more troubling possibility: that investors conquer their nerves and bid prices of stocks still higher. America’s stockmarket has proved surprisingly resilient. The economy has had a banner year, fuelled by a $1.9trn fiscal-stimulus package and an indulgent Fed. The S&P index of leading companies rose by 25% in the year to December 15th. It is around 40% higher than it was before the pandemic took hold in February 2020—and few thought then that stock prices were unduly low.

然而，还有一种更令人不安的可能的事: 投资者克服紧张情绪，将股价推得更高。事实证明，美国股市的弹性惊人。在1.9万亿美元的财政刺激法案和放纵的美联储的推动下，美国经济度过了辉煌的一年。截至12月15日，主要公司的标普指数在一年中上涨了25%。这比2020年2月疫情蔓延之前高出约40%，当时很少有人认为股价过低。

The bull market has kept going in spite of some big challenges. XX’s tech backlash and property-market troubles seem only to have reinforced the desire to own American shares. And although inflation in America is at its highest for decades, that has not hurt the profits of big listed firms. Indeed, bumper earnings have helped lift share prices in 2021.

尽管面临一些重大挑战，但牛市仍在继续。XX对科技行业的强烈反对以及房地产市场的麻烦似乎只是增强了人们持有美股的愿望。尽管美国的通货膨胀率达到了几十年来的最高点，但这并没有影响大型上市公司的利润。事实上，丰厚的盈利帮助推高了2021年的股价。

Take a longer view, though, and shares look terrifyingly expensive. A valuation measure popularised by Robert Shiller of Yale University puts the United States stockmarket on almost 40 times its earnings adjusted for the business cycle. Crypto-currencies have moved from the investment fringes to the mainstream. America has seen a rush of initial public offerings, often a signal that a bull market is coming to an end. So is the growing presence of retail investors. New smartphone apps and low-cost exchange-traded funds and brokerages that cater to small investors have opened up access to financial markets—including to derivatives, which small investors use in huge numbers to gamble on rising stocks.

不过，从长远来看，股价看起来高得吓人。由耶鲁大学的Robert Shiller推广的一种估值指标(CAPE席勒市盈率)，计算的美国股市的周期调整市盈率已上涨近40倍。加密货币已经从投资边缘走向主流。美国出现了大量IPO，这通常是牛市即将结束的信号。越来越多的散户投资者的出现也是牛市即将结束的信号。新的智能手机APP以及低成本的ETF基金以及迎合小投资者需求的券商为投资者打开了进入金融市场的大门，其中包括大量小投资者用来押注股价上涨的衍生品。

Perhaps investors are sobering up. Already the blank-cheque-company craze has ebbed, with less-frothy prices and a slower pace of launches. Speculative shares have taken a beating, too: witness the 40% fall in the value of ARK’s flagship exchange-traded fund, which invests in fledgling technology companies.

或许投资者正在清醒过来。空白支票公司的热潮已经消退，价格泡沫减少，发行速度减慢。投机性股票也受到了打击: ARK旗舰产品ETF基金的价值下跌了40%，该基金投资于初创科技公司。

And yet, despite all this, it is easy to imagine the bull market powering through 2022 with renewed vigour. One reason is that risk-free interest rates remain near all-time lows. The yield on a ten-year inflation-protected Treasury bond is around -1%, roughly where it started 2021. The negative real returns on bonds and cash help push investors into riskier assets, such as shares and private-market vehicles, including buy-out funds, direct lending and venture capital. Perhaps the Fed will raise interest rates dramatically in the coming years. But nothing like that is priced into bond markets.

不过，尽管如此，很容易想象牛市将以新的活力持续到2022年。原因之一是无风险利率仍接近历史低点。10年期通货膨胀保值国债的收益率约为-1%，与2021年初大致持平。债券和现金的负实际回报推动投资者进入风险较高的资产，如股票和私募市场工具，包括并购基金、直接贷款和风险投资。也许美联储会在未来几年大幅提高利率。但债券市场没有这种定价。

A second prop for bulls is the buy-the-dip reflex that has become wired into markets. Experience has taught investors to see a fall in prices as a chance to buy more assets at a better price. In February-March 2020 the S&P 500 fell by a third in a matter of weeks; it then suddenly revived and kept going up. Swift action by the Fed helped keep finance flowing to companies at the pandemic’s onset. But in acting this way**, it gave extra credence to the idea that** it would always put a floor under asset prices.

牛市的第二个支撑因素是市场中形成的“逢低买进”的本能反应。经验告诉投资者，看到价格下跌，就是以更好的价格买入更多资产的好机会。2020年2月至3月，标准普尔500指数在几周内下跌了三分之一;然后它突然复苏并持续上升。在疫情开始时，美联储的迅速行动帮助保持了资金流向企业。但通过这种方式，它让人们更加相信，它总是会为资产价格兜底。

Spactacular

Big tests lie ahead. It is easy to forget the sell-offs in 1998-99 as the dotcom bubble inflated. Likewise the coming months may witness market declines that seem to signal disaster, only for a revival to boost prices to even more worrying levels. The market’s performance has its own logic. “Real life is materially invaded by the contemplation of the spectacle and ends up absorbing it and aligning itself with it,” wrote Debord. Well, quite. However strange things look now, they could get stranger. ■

巨大的考验还在前方。人们很容易忘记1998-1999年互联网泡沫膨胀时的抛售。同样，未来几个月可能会出现市场下跌，这似乎是灾难的信号，但复苏只会将价格推高到更令人担忧的水平。市场的表现有其自身的逻辑。Debord写道：“现实生活在物质上已经被景观凝视所侵袭，最终吸收它并与它保持一致” [这里没有理解到位，有理解的可以在评论区留言]。没错。无论现在的事情看起来多么奇怪，它们可能会变得更奇怪。■

A lack of chargers could stall the electric-vehicle revolution

Dec 6th 2021

文章音频

CAR-BUYERS are getting behind the wheel of an electric vehicle (EV) in ever greater numbers. As battery costs tumble, prices are falling. Compared with internal combustion engine (ICE) cars, which can be a pain to drive and service, electric cars are a thrillseeking motorist’s dream. But the shift to EVs is about more than driving pleasure. Transport accounts for around a quarter of the world’s carbon emissions, and road vehicles for around three-quarters of that share. If the world is to have any chance of reaching net-zero by 2050, EVs will need to take over, and soon.

越来越多的汽车购买者开始支持电动汽车(EV)。随着电池成本的骤降，电动汽车的价格也在下降。与驾驶和服务都很痛苦的内燃机汽车相比，电动汽车是寻求刺激的驾驶者的梦想。但向电动汽车的转变不仅仅关乎驾驶乐趣。交通运输的排放约占世界碳排放的四分之一，道路交通约占这一比例的四分之三。如果世界要想在2050年达到净零排放，电动汽车将需要起支配作用，而且还要迅速(take over)。

The 6m pioneers who opt for EVs this year will still represent only 8% of all car purchasers. That figure must rise to two-thirds by 2030 and to 100% by 2050. Many investors are operating on the assumption that this will all happen as smoothly as a Tesla accelerates. The soaring market values of Elon Musk’s $1trn company, newcomers such as Rivian, which makes electric pickup trucks, and Chinese luxury EV firms, attest to sky-high confidence. Electric-battery makers, too, are booming.

今年有600万先驱者选择电动汽车，这仍然只占所有购车者的8%。到2030年，这一数字必须上升到三分之二，到2050年必须达到100%。许多投资者都认为，随着特斯拉的“加速”，这一切都会顺利实现。马斯克市值1万亿美元的公司、生产电动皮卡的Rivian和中国豪华电动汽车公司等“新来者”的市值的不断飙升，都证明了投资者的超高信心。电池制造商也在蓬勃发展。

Look beyond the glamorous, high-tech-filled automobiles that most obviously embody the EV revolution, however, and a merciless bottleneck appears. Not even those eyeing a new EV are sufficiently aware of it. Governments are only waking up to the problem. Put simply: how will all the electric cars get charged?

然而，除了那些最明显地体现了电动汽车革命的迷人的、充满高科技的汽车，一个无情的瓶颈出现了。甚至那些关注新电动汽车的人也没有充分意识到这一点。政府只是刚刚意识到这个问题。简而言之: 所有的电动汽车将如何充电?

The current number of public chargers—1.3m—cannot begin to satisfy the demands of the world’s rapidly expanding electric fleet. According to an estimate by the International Energy Agency (IEA), a forecaster, by the end of this decade 40m public charging points will be needed, requiring an annual investment of $90bn a year as 2030 approaches. If net-zero goals are to be met, by 2050 the world will need five times as many.

目前公共充电站的数量(130万个)已经无法满足世界上电动汽车快速增长的需求。根据预测机构IEA的估计，到本世纪末，将需要4000万个公共充电站，随着2030年的临近，每年需要900亿美元的投资。如果要实现净零排放的目标，到2050年，全球需要的公共充电站的数量将是现在的5倍。

Governments’ current pledges to phase out ICE cars and shift to EVs are, it is true, not quite consistent with net-zero. Even if roads turn electric less speedily than they should, though, the sums the world needs to spend on charging infrastructure are still stupendous. In a slower scenario envisaged by BloombergNEF (BNEF), a research firm, in which EV sales keep rising as battery prices fall but reach just under a third of all vehicles sold by 2030, roughly $600bn of investment would still be needed by 2040. That would pay for fewer chargers than the IEA foresees—24m public points alone by 2040 (and 309m in total, see chart 1). If net-zero is to be achieved by 2050, BNEF puts the cumulative charging investment required at a whopping $1.6trn.

各国政府目前承诺逐步淘汰内燃机汽车，转而使用电动汽车，这确实与净零并不完全一致。然而，即使道路转向电动的速度比它们应该的(速度)要慢，世界需要在充电基础设施上花费的金额仍然是超乎想象的。根据研究公司BNEF较慢的设想，即到2030年，电动汽车销量将在电池价格下降的情况下持续增长，但在所有汽车销量中所占比例还不到三分之一，到2040年仍需要约6000亿美元的投资。这一投资在充电站上的钱比IEA预测的要少，据BNEF预测，到2040年仅公共充电站就有2400万个(总共3.09亿个充电桩，见表1)。如果到2050年要实现净零，BNEF估计所需的累计充电投资将高达1.6万亿美元。

Besides installing too few public chargers, the charging industry’s operational record is poor. The official number currently exceeds what some authorities reckon is needed. The European Commission, for example, thinks every ten EVs require one public charger. According to the Boston Consulting Group (BCG), there are now five EVs per charging point in the EU and China, and nine in America.

除了安装的公共充电站太少，充电行业的运营记录也很差。官方(所需的充电站的)数字目前超过了一些当局认为所需要的数字。例如，欧盟委员会认为每10辆电动汽车需要一个公共充电站。根据波士顿咨询集团(BCG)的数据，目前在欧盟和中国每个充电站可以给5辆电动汽车充电，在美国是9辆。

That is in theory. In practice, a survey of chargers in XX by Volkswagen (VW) found many inoperable or “ ICEd” chargers (those blocked inadvertently or deliberately by fossil-fuelled cars). Only 30-40% of XX’s 1m public points were available at any time. It is safe to assume some inoperability in the EU and America. This summer Herbert Diess, VW’s boss, complained on LinkedIn, a social network, that his holiday had gone less than smoothly because Ionity, a European charging network, provided too few points on the Brenner Pass between Austria and Italy. “Anything but a premium charging experience,” Mr Diess wrote. That VW part-owns Ionity made the criticism sting more.

这是理论上的。实际上，根据大众汽车对XX充电站进行的一项调查发现，许多充电站无法使用或被“ICEd”占用(即被化石燃料汽车无意或有意挡住的充电站)。在XX的100万公共充电站中，只有30-40%的充电站是随时可用的。可以有把握地认为，欧盟和美国之间存在某种无法使用的情况。今年夏天，大众汽车的老板Herbert Diess在社交网络领英上抱怨说，由于欧洲充电网络Ionity在奥地利和意大利之间的勃伦纳山口提供的充电站太少，他的假期过得不太顺利。Diess写道:“绝不是一次优质的充电体验。” 大众拥有Ionity的部分股权，这让批评更加尖锐。

Drivers can smell trouble ahead. Range anxiety and the availability of public charging is a huge issue (see chart 2). In a recent survey by AlixPartners, a consultancy, in the seven countries that make up 85% of global EV sales the cars’ high prices came third on the list of top five reasons not to switch to battery power; the four others were all worries related to charging.

司机们能察觉到前方有麻烦。里程焦虑和公共充电站的可用性是一个巨大的问题(见图表2)。在咨询公司AlixPartners最近的一次调查显示, 电动汽车销量占全球85%的七个国家中，“汽车的价格”在不转向使用电池的五大原因中排名第三；另外四个原因都与充电有关。

To assess the scale of the challenge start with the basics. A big advantage of EVs is that they can be charged at home—or at workplaces, if employers install chargers. In America 70% of homes have off-street parking where a charger can be installed (the equivalent figure is lower in Europe and China). BCG estimates that in 2020 home and workplace charging accounted for nearly three-quarters of the total charging energy use in America, seven-tenths in Europe and three-fifths in China.

要评估挑战的规模，首先要从基础开始。电动汽车的一大优势是它们可以在家里充电，如果雇主安装充电站的话，也可以在工作场所充电。在美国，70%的家庭都有可以安装充电站的街边停车场(欧洲和中国的比例要低一些)。BCG估计，到2020年，在美国，在家庭和工作场所的充电占总充电能源使用量的近四分之三，欧洲占十分之七，中国占五分之三。

Current EV models typically have batteries with ranges of around 400km. Some go over 650km. The average American drives 50km a day, according to Bank of America. Europeans and Chinese drive less. Two types of charger are good enough to top up vehicles, or give them a boost overnight at home or during the working day. The slowest, providing up to 8km of range an hour, can do the job. So do “level 2” chargers that provide 16-32km. Both are easy on the wallet. Drivers can use dedicated sockets that cost a few hundred dollars (and are often subsidised by governments) to tap the cheapest electricity tariffs.

目前的电动汽车的电池续航里程一般在400公里左右。有些超过650公里。根据美国银行的数据，美国人平均每天开50公里车。欧洲人和中国人开的里程数比较少。有两种类型的充电站可以很好地给汽车充电，或者在家里或工作日给汽车充电。最慢的充电一小时能跑8公里。“level 2”充一小时可以跑16-32公里。这两种方法都很省钱。司机可以使用几百美元的专用插座(通常由政府补贴)，以选择最便宜的电费。

Nonetheless, home and workplace charging only gets you so far. As EVownership spreads from wealthier households to people living in flats or dwellings without the ability to plug in, a public network becomes vital. In America, Europe and China demand for public charging is expected to increase (see chart 3). Public chargers come in three varieties. A common kind is kerbside charging, via converted lampposts or other dedicated points, where cars might park overnight. Then there is “destination” charging, of the sort that is becoming more widely available in car parks at shopping centres, restaurants, cinemas and the like. Both kinds are level-2, with installation costs usually between $2,000 and $10,000 per point.

尽管如此，在家里和工作场所充电的作用就仅此而已了。随着电动汽车的拥有者从较富裕的家庭蔓延到没法儿接入“电源”的住在公寓或住宅的人，公共(充电站)网络变得至关重要。在美国、欧洲和中国，对公共充电站的需求预计将会增加(见表3)。公共充电站有三种类型。一种常见的方式是路边充电，通过改装灯柱或其他专用点，汽车可能在那里停一夜。然后是“目的地”充电站，这种充电站在购物中心、餐馆、电影院等场所的停车场越来越普遍。这两种类型都是“level 2”，每个充电桩的安装成本通常在2,000美元到10,000美元之间。

Fast charging, which typically adds 100-130km of range every 20 minutes, is vital on main roads for drivers making long inter-city trips and in cities for a quick emergency jolt. Commercial vehicles driving longer distances, such as taxis, need fast charging, too. But since charging firms need to recoup hefty costs of $100,000 or more per fast charger, using such facilities is pricey. To make life easier for customers, Tesla’s mapping software directs its cars on long journeys and works out the best route weaving through its dedicated “Supercharger” network. Other new EV models come with similar features.

“快速充电”通常每20分钟就能增加100-130公里的续航里程，这对在主干道上长途城际行驶的司机以及在城市中用于快速紧急颠簸的司机至关重要。【这里翻译的不到位的地方，可以评论区指出来，特别是“快速紧急颠簸”的含义】长距离行驶的商用车，比如出租车，也需要快速充电。但由于充电公司需要收回每台快速充电器10万美元或更多的巨额成本，使用这样的设备是昂贵的。为了让客户的生活更轻松，特斯拉的地图软件指导其汽车进行长途旅行，并能够计算出穿梭在其专用的“超级充电桩”网络的最佳路线。其他新型电动汽车也有类似的功能。

Charging-industry insiders point out, reasonably, that both EV ownership and charging are in their infancy. Pessimism is unwarranted, they argue, based on a few short years of experience. Only one in 100 cars on the world’s roads is an EV, after all. Pat Romano of ChargePoint, one of the world’s biggest charging firms, talks of the start of “a 20-year arc”.

充电行业内部人士合理地指出，拥有电动汽车以及充电都还处于起步阶段。他们认为，仅基于短短几年的经验，没必要悲观。毕竟，世界上每100辆汽车中只有一辆是电动汽车。世界上最大的充电公司之一ChargePoint的Pat Romano称之为“20年进程”的开始(弧形的开始)。

Fair enough. Still, future demand for charging at scale is impossible to know as yet. Expansion is coming fast, say some. Along with the momentum from electrophile governments, the opportunity to make money charging the world’s expanding fleet means that “hyperbolic growth” is on the way, says James West of Evercore ISI, a bank. But exactly how many public chargers are needed for each EV on the road is “an open question”, notes Bank of America. Scott Bishop of Yunex Traffic, a division of Siemens, a German firm that makes charging hardware, hears many different answers when he asks insiders what share of chargers should be slow versus fast.

有道理。不过，目前还不可能知道未来大规模充电的需求。一些人说，扩张速度很快。Evercore ISI银行的James West表示，随着政府“亲电”势头的增强，向全球不断扩张的电动汽车充电来赚钱的机会意味着“双曲线式增长”即将到来。但美国银行指出，每辆行驶中的电动汽车究竟需要多少公共充电桩，这是一个“悬而未决的问题”。德国充电硬件制造商西门子公司旗下的Yunex Traffic部门的Scott Bishop在向内部人士询问慢速充电桩和快速充电桩的比例时，听到了许多不同的答案。

Another problem is the industry’s structure. Aakash Arora of BCG’s automotive practice calls its many complex layers the “gnarliest problem of all”. The need to co-ordinate with and get permission from many parties helps explain the slow roll-out. First, there are firms that make the chargers themselves. Then there are the operators. These might own the points, earning money from charging. Or they might collect fees for maintaining chargers operated by site-owners. Site-owners, usually businesses, other private landlords or local authorities, provide the locations for chargers and charge rent to point-owning operators. Service providers are middlemen who allow the charging to happen, with apps or cards that give access to charge points and facilitate payment.

另一个问题是该行业的结构。BCG汽车业务部门的Aakash Arora称其许多复杂的层是“最棘手的问题”。需要与各方协调并获得各方的许可，这有助于解释推广缓慢的原因。首先，有些公司自己生产充电桩。然后是运营者。这些运营者可能拥有公共充电站，靠充电赚钱。或者他们可能会收取由站点所有者运营的充电桩的维护费。站点所有者，通常是企业、其他私人地主或地方当局，提供充电地点，并向站点运营商收取租金。服务提供商是允许充电的中间人，他们通过APP或卡进入充电站，方便支付。

Watt a business

Three kinds of firm are coming to rule the EV-charging roost. One is the vertically integrated car giant. Tesla has not revealed what it has spent on its “Supercharger” network, which now numbers 30,000 points worldwide. But it is probably several billion dollars. Other car firms are following, to a point. BMW, Ford, Hyundai and Daimler are partners with VW in Ionity. Its fast-charging network hopes to expand from 1,500 points to 7,000 by 2025. Electrify America, set up by VW in 2016 as part of its settlement with American regulators over its emissions-cheating scandal, now has 2,200 fast chargers in the United States. General Motors says it will spend $750m on charging. Its first move will be to install 40,000 points at dealerships.

三种类型的公司将统治电动汽车充电领域。一个是垂直整合的汽车巨头。特斯拉没有透露它在“超级快充”网络上花了多少钱，该网络目前在全球有3万个站点。但可能已经花了几十亿美元。在某种程度上，其他汽车公司也在效仿。宝马、福特、现代和戴姆勒与大众在Ionity上是合作伙伴。它的快速充电网络希望到2025年从1500站点扩大到7000站点。2016年，大众汽车与美国监管机构就其“尾气排放作弊丑闻“达成和解，成立了Electrify America公司。目前，该公司在美国拥有2200个快速充电桩。通用汽车表示将在充电上花费7.5亿美元。它的第一个举措将是在专卖店安装4万个站点。

Specialist charging businesses are also expanding. Several have gone public in the past year. None is profitable, and their revenues are tiny for now, but their market values are rising. The most richly valued (at around $7bn) is ChargePoint, which controls 44% of America’s public-charging market and is expanding in Europe. EVBox, a Dutch firm, has 300,000 points worldwide, including a quarter of Europe’s public level-2 chargers and a third of fast-charging points. EVgo has half the fast-charging market in America (excluding Tesla). But as Ryan Fisher of BNEF notes, in the next decade charging firms will have to find business models that reliably produce profits even if governments cut subsidies.

专门充电的企业也在扩大。去年有几家公司上市。但没有一家公司是盈利的，目前它们的收入很少，但它们的市值正在上升。市值最高的是ChargePoint公司(约70亿美元)，该公司控制着美国44%的公共充电市场，并且正在欧洲扩张。荷兰EVBox公司在全球拥有30万个充电站，其中包括四分之一的欧洲公共“level 2”充电站以及三分之一的快速充电站。EVgo拥有美国一半的快充市场(不包括特斯拉)。但正如BNEF的Ryan Fisher所指出的那样，在未来10年里，如果政府削减补贴，充电公司也必须找到能够产生可靠利润的商业模式。

A third category is energy firms. Fearful of losing business at petrol stations, they are developing ambitious schemes. After buying Ubitricity, a big European on-street charging firm, in February, Royal Dutch Shell, an Anglo-Dutch oil major, said in August that it planned to roll out 500,000 charging points around the world by 2025, both kerbside and fast charging. BP and Total have also been buying charging firms. Utilities are making a push, too. Wallbox, part-owned by Spain’s Iberdrola, sells chargers for homes and workplaces. The Electric Highway Coalition, made up of 17 American power companies, plans to install fast charging along inter-city routes.

第三类是能源公司。由于担心失去加油站的业务，他们正在制定雄心勃勃的计划。今年2月，英荷石油巨头荷兰皇家壳牌公司收购了欧洲一家大型街头充电公司Ubitricity之后，于8月表示，计划到2025年在全球范围内推出50万个路边和快速充电充电站。BP和Total也一直在收购充电公司。公用事业公司也在努力。Wallbox(西班牙Iberdrola公司拥有其部分股权)主要销售家用和办公用充电桩。由17家美国电力公司组成的Electric Highway Coalition计划在城际公路上安装快速充电设备。

Governments will act. America’s new infrastructure law sets aside $7.5bn for the installation of 500,000 public points by 2030. Mandates such as that recently announced in Britain requiring new homes, workplaces and retail sites to have charging points, adding 145,000 every year, are likely to become more common. A reason for optimism is that improvements in batteries should continue to offer ever longer ranges, and so less need for frequent charging. Newer batteries will be replenished more rapidly than today’s are, and chargers will provide current more swiftly.

各国政府将会采取行动。美国的新基础设施法拨出75亿美元，用于在2030年前安装50万个公共充电站。英国最近宣布，要求新建住宅、工作场所和零售场所要有充电站，每年新增14.5万个充电站，这样的规定可能会变得越来越普遍。保持乐观的一个原因是，电池的改进将继续提供更长的续航里程，从而减少频繁充电的需求。更新电池的充电速度会比现在更快，充电桩也会更快地提供电流。

Doubts about the ramp-up nevertheless persist. The numbers are still small relative to the vast scale of charging networks the world needs. More money will be required to update electricity grids to distribute power to the new source of demand. BCG forecasts that America, Europe and China, home to most of the world’s EVs, will have only 6.5m public chargers between them by 2030—not enough to meet the IEA’s global target of 40m. More cars will vie for each charger. Drivers may need to seek patience as well as thrills. ■

然而，对增加公共充电站的疑虑依然存在。与全球所需的庞大充电网络相比，这一数字仍然很小。更多的资金将被用来更新电网，以将电力分配到新的需求来源。BCG公司预测，到2030年，美国、欧洲和中国(世界上大部分电动汽车的所在地)将只有650万个公共充电站，不足以满足IEA的4000万个充电站的全球目标。更多的汽车将争夺每一个充电桩。司机可能需要寻求耐心和刺激。■

Dramatic growth in mental-health apps has created a risky industry

心理健康APP的急剧增多催生了一个高风险行业

Psyber Boom

Customers’ “emotional data” can be hacked, and no one is checking if the apps work

客户的“情感数据”可能会被窃取，也没有人检查这些APP是否有效

Dec 11th 2021

文章音频

WHEN CAROLINA ESCUDERO was severely depressed, going to a therapist’s office became hard to face. So she joined BetterHelp, a popular therapy app. She paid $65 each week but spent most of her time waiting for her assigned counsellor to respond. She got two responses in a month. “It was like texting an acquaintance who has no idea how to deal with mental illness,” she says. BetterHelp says its service does not claim to operate around the clock, all its therapists have advanced degrees and “thousands of hours of hands-on clinical work”, and users are able easily to switch them if scheduling is hard.

当CAROLINA ESCUDERO患有严重抑郁症时，去看心理医生都变得很难。于是，她选择使用流行的治疗APP “BetterHelp”。她每周支付65美元，但大部分时间她都在等待指定的心理咨询师做出回应。她一个月内收到了两次回复。她说：“这就像给一个不知道如何应对精神疾病的熟人发短信”。BetterHelp表示，它的服务并没有声称是全天候的，它的所有心理治疗师都有高等学位以及“数千小时的实际的临床经验”，如果日程安排困难，用户可以很容易地更换治疗师。

Helping people to deal with mental problems has rarely been more urgent. The incidence of depression and anxiety has soared in the pandemic—by more than 25% globally in 2020, according to the Lancet, a medical journal. That, combined with more people using online services, has led to a boom in mental-health apps. The American Psychological Association reckons 10,000-20,000 are available for download. But evidence is mounting that privacy risks to users are being ignored. No one is checking if the apps work, either.

帮助人们处理“心理问题”从来没有像现在这样迫切过。根据医学期刊《柳叶刀》的数据，在疫情期间，抑郁和焦虑的发病率飙升，2020年全球范围内发病率上升了25%以上。再加上越来越多的人使用在线服务，导致了心理健康APP的繁荣。美国心理协会估计有1-2万种APP可供下载。但越来越多的证据表明，用户的隐私风险被忽视了。也没有人检查这些APP是否有效。

Mental-health-tech firms raised nearly $2bn in equity funding in 2020, according to CB Insights, a data firm. Their products tackle problems from general stress to serious bipolar disorder. Telehealth apps like BetterHelp or Talkspace connect users to licensed therapists. Also common are subscription-based meditation apps like Headspace. In October Headspace bought Ginger, a therapy app, for $3bn. Now that big companies are prioritising employees’ mental health, some apps are working with them to help entire workforces. One such app, Lyra, supports 2.2m employee users globally and is valued at $4.6bn.

据数据公司CB Insights的数据显示，心理健康技术公司在2020年筹集了近20亿美元的股权融资。他们的产品可以解决从一般压力到严重的躁郁症的问题。BetterHelp或Talkspace等远程医疗APP将用户与注册治疗师联系起来。Headspace等基于订阅的冥想APP也很常见。去年10月，Headspace斥资30亿美元收购了治疗APP “Ginger”。由于大公司都在优先考虑员工的心理健康，一些APP正与这些大公司合作来帮助整个员工队伍。其中一款APP“Lyra”为全球220万员工用户提供支持，其估值为46亿美元。

Underneath, though, a trauma lurks in some corners of the industry. In October 2020 hackers who had breached Vastaamo, a popular Finnish startup, began blackmailing some of its users. Vastaamo required therapists to back up patient notes online but reportedly did not anonymise or encrypt them. Threatening to share details of extramarital affairs and, in some cases, thoughts about paedophilia, on the dark web, the hackers reportedly demanded bitcoin ransoms from some 30,000 patients. Vastaamo has filed for bankruptcy but left many Finns wary of telling doctors personal details, says Joni Siikavirta, a lawyer representing the company’s patients.

然而，在这个行业的某些角落里，潜藏着一种“精神创伤”。2020年10月，黑客入侵了芬兰人气创业公司Vastaamo，开始勒索其部分用户。Vastaamo要求治疗师在网上备份患者记录，但据报道并未将其匿名化或加密。据报道，这些黑客威胁要在暗网上分享婚外情的细节，在某些情况下，还会分享关于恋童癖的想法，并要求约3万名患者支付比特币赎金。代表公司的患者的律师Joni Siikavirta说，Vastaamo已经申请破产，但这让许多芬兰人不敢告诉医生个人的细节。

Other cases may arise. No universal standards for storing “emotional data” exist. John Torous of Harvard Medical School, who has reviewed 650 mental-health apps, describes their privacy policies as abysmal. Some share information with advertisers. “When I first joined BetterHelp, I started to see targeted ads with words that I had used on the app to describe my personal experiences,” reports one user. BetterHelp says it shares with marketing partners only device identifiers associated with “generic event names”, only for measurement and optimisation, and only if users agree. No private information, such as dialogue with therapists, is shared, it says.

其他情况也可能会发生。存储“情感数据”的通用标准并不存在。哈佛医学院的John Torous已经审查了650款心理健康应用，他认为这些APP的隐私政策糟透了。有些公司还与广告商共享信息。一位用户说：“当我第一次‘加入’BetterHelp时，我开始看到有针对性的广告，上面的话是我曾在APP上用来描述自己的个人经历的。“ BetterHelp表示，它只与营销合作伙伴共享与“通用事件名称”相关的设备标识符，且仅用于测量和优化，并且仅在用户同意的情况下共享。它说，它不会共享私人信息，比如与治疗师的对话。

As for effectiveness, the apps’ methods are notoriously difficult to evaluate. Woebot, for instance, is a chatbot which uses artificial intelligence to reproduce the experience of cognitive behavioural therapy. The product is marketed as clinically validated based in part on a scientific study which concluded that humans can form meaningful bonds with bots. But the study was written by people with financial links to Woebot. Of its ten peer-reviewed reports to date, says Woebot, eight feature partnerships with a main investigator with no financial ties to it. Any co-authors with financial ties are disclosed, it says.

至于有效性，这些APP的方法是出了名的难以评估。例如，Woebot是一款聊天机器人，它使用人工智能重现认知行为疗法的经历。该产品推销自己的临床验证部分是基于一项科学研究，该研究认为人类可以与机器人建立有意义的联系。但这项研究是由与Woebot有财务关系的人撰写的。Woebot表示，在迄今为止的10份同行评议的报告中，有8份报告是与一名主要的研究人员有合作关系，并没有财务关系。报告称，任何有财务关系的共同作者都将被披露。

Mental-health apps were designed to be used in addition to clinical care, not in lieu of them. With that in mind, the European Commission is reviewing the field. It is getting ready to promote a new standard that will apply to all health apps. A letter-based scale will rank safety, user friendliness and data security. Liz Ashall-Payne, founder of ORCHA, a British startup that has reviewed thousands of apps, including for the National Health Service, says that 68% did not meet the firm’s quality criteria. Time to head back to the couch? ■

心理健康APP设计初衷是用于临床护理之外，而不是代替临床护理。考虑到这一点，欧盟委员会正在审查这一领域。它正准备推广一项适用于所有健康APP的新标准。以字母为基础的评分标准将对安全性、用户友好性和数据安全性进行排名。英国初创公司ORCHA的创始人Liz Ashall-Payne表示，68%的APP不符合公司的质量标准。ORCHA已经审核了数千款APP，其中包括英国国家医疗服务系统。是时候回到沙发上了?■

Editor’s note (December 8th 2021): This article has been updated.

TEN YEARS ago Christoph Holstein received a summons to the office of Olaf Scholz. Mr Scholz had just led the Social Democrats (SPD) to a stunning election win in the city-state of Hamburg, and Mr Holstein was to be his government spokesman. Mr Scholz offered two pieces of guidance: “We are never offended, and we are never hysterical.” Mr Holstein, now on Hamburg’s state council, found the advice so useful that it remains pinned up in his office under the words: “Scholz’s first law”. Germans and others who learned to appreciate the calm demeanour of Angela Merkel during her 16 years in office will find reassurance in Mr Scholz, who replaced her as German chancellor on December 8th.

十年前，Christoph Holstein接到朔尔茨办公室的召唤。朔尔茨刚刚领导社民党在汉堡这个州级市赢得了令人震惊的选举胜利，而Holstein先生当时是汉堡市政府发言人。朔尔茨先生提供了两条法则: “我们从不生气，我们也从不歇斯底里。” 现为汉堡市政委员的Holstein先生发现，这条建议非常有用，至今仍钉在他的办公室里，下面写着“朔尔茨第一法则”。德国人和其他在默克尔执政的16年里学会欣赏她的平静举止的人将会在12月8日接替她成为德国总理的朔尔茨身上得到安慰。

Talk to those who have got to know the 63-year-old Mr Scholz throughout his career, and several leitmotifs emerge. Reliability is one. “He only ever promises things he can achieve,” says Dorothee Martin, an SPD MP from Hamburg. Campaigning for election this summer, he boiled down his offer to a handful of modest proposals, all of which are in the coalition deal agreed by the SPD with two smaller parties: lifting Germany’s hourly minimum wage to €12 ($13.50), maintaining the state pension and building 400,000 housing units a year. Other colleagues praise Mr Scholz’s Hanseatic work ethic and his pragmatism.

与那些在63岁的朔尔茨先生的职业生涯中认识他的人交谈，会发现几个主题。可靠性是一个。来自汉堡的社民党议员Dorothee Martin说:“他只承诺他能实现的事情。” 在今年夏天的竞选活动中，他将自己的倡议归结为几个谦虚的提议（所有这些在联合执政协议中的提议都由社民党与其他两个较小的党派共同达成一致意见）: 将德国的每小时最低工资提升至€12(13.50美元), 养老金不会削减并会每年兴建400000套新公寓。其他同事则称赞朔尔茨的汉萨同盟的职业道德以及他的实用主义。

Another thread is impatience. Mr Scholz shares Mrs Merkel’s air of serenity and competence. Unlike her, he struggles to mask his disdain for those he considers ill-briefed, a trait that can infuriate antagonists. In Hamburg, his aides would shut down dissenters by exclaiming “ OWD”, short for Olaf will das! (“Olaf wants that”). Colleagues swear that Mr Scholz can be hilarious company. But his circuitous, convoluted style of public speaking will hardly satisfy those craving a more direct mode of communication from their chancellor. When challenged on his robotic persona earlier this year, Mr Scholz said he hoped to become chancellor, not a circus ring master. His press conferences are deathly.

另一个主题是缺乏耐心。朔尔茨先生有着默克尔女士的冷静和能力。与她不同，他努力掩饰自己对那些容易动怒的人的鄙视，这一点可能会激怒对手。在汉堡，他的助手们会大喊“OWD”(奥拉夫想要那样)来阻止持不同政见者。同事们发誓朔尔茨可以成为一个风趣的伙伴。但他那拐弯抹角、错综复杂的公开演讲风格很难满足那些渴望从总理那里获得更直接交流方式的人。今年早些时候，当被问及他的“机器人”形象时，朔尔茨表示，他希望成为总理，而不是马戏团的领班。他的新闻发布会非常糟糕。

Mr Scholz was raised in Hamburg, and trained as an employment lawyer. Like many of today’s ageing social democrats he dabbled in radical leftism before, in his words, “detoxifying” himself into moderate social democracy in his 20s. He bounced between party, city and federal politics for decades, suffering reverses (being booted from a senior SPD role in 2004, the G20 riots in Hamburg in 2017, losing a party leadership bid in 2019) as well as victories (two huge election wins in Hamburg, this year’s triumphant national campaign). In 2018 he pushed his reluctant party to support Mrs Merkel’s Christian Democrats in yet another “grand coalition”. He became her vice-chancellor and finance minister, a role that served as a finishing school for the office he has now achieved.

朔尔茨先生在汉堡长大，并接受了职业律师的培训。就像今天许多上了年纪的社民党人一样，用他的话说，曾涉足激进的左翼主义，并在20多岁的时候将自己“解毒”为温和的社会民主主义。几十年来，他在政党、城市和联邦政治之间辗转，经历过数次挫折(2004年被社民党高级职位革职，2017年G20峰会期间汉堡市骚乱，2019年竞选党魁失败)，也获得过胜利(在汉堡两次大选获胜，今年的全国竞选中大获全胜)。2018年，他推动他的政党不情愿地支持默克尔的基督教民主党组成另一个“大联合政府”。他成为了她的副总统和财政部长，这一角色像是一所“精修学校”，为他赢得了他现在取得的“职位”。

A prolific reader, Mr Scholz turned his finance ministry into a brains trust of sorts. His emphasis on “respect”, by which he means a society with equal regard for binmen and CEOs, is inspired by Michael Sandel, an American theorist sceptical of meritocracy. Too little of the stuff, Mr Scholz argues, breeds populism and illiberalism. “He is often portrayed as a social democrat in conservative clothes, but that’s wrong,” says a colleague.

作为一名多产的读者，朔尔茨先生将他的财政部变成了某种程度上的智囊团。他对“尊重“的重视（他的意思是建立一个对清洁工和首席执行官一视同仁的社会）是受到了一位“怀疑精英管理的社会”的美国理论家Michael Sandel的启发。朔尔茨认为，“尊重”太少会滋生民粹主义和非自由主义。他的一位同事表示:“ 他经常被描绘成一个穿着保守服装的社会民主党人，但这是错误的。”

Mr Scholz also imbibed a lesson offered by previous SPD election-winners: that Germans are a centrist bunch wary of visionaries. Unloved by SPD members who wanted more red meat in their politics, Mr Scholz nonetheless almost single-handedly led his comrades to an unexpected, if narrow, election win in September. If this hardly represented a great renaissance of the European left, it did wonders for a party that had long been in the doldrums.

朔尔茨先生也吸取了前几届社民党大选获胜者的教训:德国人是一群对梦想家心存戒心的中间派。社民党成员希望在政治中有更多的“红肉”，但他们并不喜欢朔尔茨先生，尽管如此，Scholz先生几乎是独自一人带领他的同志们在9月出人意料地赢得了选举(即使是微弱的胜利)。如果说这并不能代表欧洲左翼的伟大复兴，那么对于一个长期处于低迷状态的政党来说，这却是奇迹。

The pandemic had a hand in Mr Scholz’s own recovery. He spent his first two years as finance minister dismaying those who hoped a Social Democrat might end Germany’s excessive fiscal prudence. But when covid-19 struck Mr Scholz cast aside Germany’s fiscal rules to splash hundreds of billions on furlough and corporate-support schemes. He helped design the EU’s €750bn ($845bn) recovery fund, and earlier this year helped push through an international corporate-tax deal.

疫情也参与到Scholz先生的“复苏”。他担任财政部长的头两年，让那些希望社民党人能够结束德国过度审慎的财政政策的人感到失望。但当新冠疫情袭来时，朔尔茨抛弃了德国的财政规则，在休假和企业支持计划上投入了数千亿美元。他帮助设计了7500亿欧元(合8450亿美元)的欧盟复苏基金，并在今年早些时候帮助推动了一项国际企业税协议的通过。

Foreign and European policy will command much of Mr Scholz’s time. His domestic ambitions will focus on climate. He calls the transition to a carbon-free future Germany’s biggest industrial test in a century. It will be a political challenge, too. The coalition deal commits the new government to exacting targets, including an 80% share of renewables in electricity generation by 2030, but is vague on how to finance the required investments. That riddle could trigger rows between Mr Scholz’s governing partners, the tax-cutting Free Democrats and the left-leaning Greens.

外交和欧洲政策将占用朔尔茨的大部分时间。他在国内的雄心将集中在气候问题上。他将“向无碳未来的转变”称为德国一个世纪以来最大的工业考验。这也将是一个政治挑战。联合执政协议承诺新政府制定严格的目标，包括到2030年将可再生能源在发电中所占比例达到80%，但对于如何为所需投资融资却含糊其词。这个谜题可能会引发朔尔茨先生的执政伙伴，即减税的自民党和左倾的绿党之间的争吵。

Managing clashes inside his coalition will test Mr Scholz’s celebrated mediation skills. So could soothing the inevitable frustration of the Greens, some of whom emerged disappointed from the coalition negotiations, as well as potential restiveness in the SPD’s own ranks. To manage disputes Mr Scholz will rely on a handful of trusted aides, chief among them Wolfgang Schmidt, an ebullient figure whose reward for decades of service to Mr Scholz will be the job of running his chancellery.

处理联盟内部的冲突将考验朔尔茨先生著名的调解技巧。这也可以缓解绿党不可避免的沮丧情绪，他们中的一些人对联合政府的谈判感到失望，以及缓解社民党内部潜在的躁动不安的情绪。为了处理争端，朔尔茨将依靠少数可信赖的助手，其中最主要的是Wolfgang Schmidt，一个热情洋溢的人物，他为朔尔茨服务了几十年的报酬将是管理他的总理府的工作【现任财政部国务秘书】。

Mr Scholz’s quiet bearing may belie serious ambitions for Germany. “He thinks that under Merkel Germany failed to live up to its potential, and now needs a progressive renewal,” says Dominic Schwickert of Das Progressive Zentrum, a left-leaning think-tank in Berlin. Yet despite her original reform plans Mrs Merkel will be remembered mainly as Europe’s crisis-manager-in-chief. Unexpected events will prove at least as testing for Mr Scholz. He takes office amid a brutal fourth wave of covid-19 and the prospect of renewed conflagration in Ukraine. For now, a surprisingly heady air of optimism surrounds his new, untested coalition government. Olaf will das. But the new chancellor may not always get what he wants. ■

朔尔茨的冷静举止可能掩盖了他对德国的雄心壮志。柏林左倾智库Das Progressive Zentrum的Dominic Schwickert称：“他认为在默克尔的领导下，德国未能发挥其潜力，现在需要一场进步的复兴”。然而，尽管默克尔最初提出了改革计划，人们仍将主要记住她作为欧洲危机总管的形象。意料之外的事件将至少证明是对朔尔茨的考验。他是在第四波残酷的新冠疫情和重燃的乌克兰大火的前景下上台的。目前，一种令人惊讶的乐观气氛围绕着他的新成立的未经考验的联合政府。“奥拉夫想要那样”。但新总理可能并不总是能得到他想要的。■

How landlords thwart America’s attempts to house poor people

房东如何阻挠美国为穷人提供住房的努力

To rent or not to rent

租还是不租

It is one thing to receive a housing voucher and quite another to successfully use it

获得凭证是一回事，而成功使用是另一回事

Dec 11th 2021

文章音频

“WHY THE fuck does this county even offer Section 8 if it’s a mythical unicorn that nobody ever gets?” asks Alex, the main character in Netflix’s new series “Maid”. The show, based on Stephanie Land’s book “Maid: Hard Work, Low Pay, and a Mother’s Will to Survive”, is a portrait of poverty and domestic work in Washington State. Section 8, now known as the Housing Choice Voucher Programme (HCVP), is a federal housing-assistance scheme that subsidises rent for 2.3m poor American households lucky enough to get their hands on a voucher. Others can spend years on waiting lists, hoping to be chosen.

Netflix新剧《女佣》中的主角Alex问道：“如果这是一个没人能得到的神话中的‘独角兽’，为什么这个县要提出 Section 8 ?”。这部电视剧改编自斯蒂芬妮·兰德的书《女佣:辛勤工作、微薄工资和一个母亲的生存意志》，该剧描绘了华盛顿州的贫困以及家务劳动。Section 8（现在被称为房租补贴凭证计划）是一个美国联邦政府住房援助计划，为230万足够幸运得到凭证的贫困美国家庭提供租金补贴。那些没那么幸运的人则已经在等待名单上呆了数年时间，希望能够被选中。

Housing-policy wonks often refer to the voucher programme as a kind of lottery: win and your life may fundamentally change. When towering public-housing projects were demolished in the 1990s, the Department of Housing and Urban Development (HUD) used vouchers as a way to house America’s poor through the private market. With a voucher, tenants put 30% of their monthly income towards rent and the federal government covers the rest. Many people on voucher waiting lists are homeless or living with their families. Nearly half of all voucher-holders are black, 70% are racial minorities and about a third earn less than $10,000 a year.

住房政策专家经常把“凭证计划”比作一种彩票: 中奖，你的生活可能会从根本上改变。当高耸的公共住房项目在20世纪90年代被”推翻”时，美国住房和城市发展部(HUD)使用“凭证”作为通过私人市场为美国穷人提供住房的一种方式。有了“凭证”，房客将每月收入的30%用于交房租，房租的其余部分则由联邦政府支付。许多等待发放“凭证”的人要么无家可归，要么和家人住在一起。近一半的“凭证”持有者是黑人，70%是少数种族，约三分之一的人年收入低于1万美元。

Vouchers are not just about housing: cheaper rent means more money for expenses such as food, bills or school. Because vouchers theoretically allow tenants to rent a home anywhere, they can help poor families move to wealthier, safer neighbourhoods. Many researchers view them as a way to increase social mobility.

“凭证”不仅仅与住房有关: 更便宜的租金意味着更多的钱将用于食品、账单或上学等支出。因为从理论上讲，“凭证”允许租户在任何地方租房，它们可以帮助贫困家庭搬到更富裕、更安全的社区。许多研究人员将“凭证”视为提高社会流动性的一种方式。

But not everyone wins the lottery. Many cities have had to close their waiting lists. A new study from the Housing Initiative at Penn, a research outfit at the University of Pennsylvania, estimates that 10.4m households would be eligible for a voucher under HUD’s criteria, four times as many families as there are vouchers for. By comparing the gap between existing and needed vouchers with local renter populations, researchers found that Orlando, Charlotte and Phoenix would benefit most from a policy where vouchers were given to all who qualify.

但并不是每个人都能中彩票。许多城市不得不关闭其等候名单(申请)。宾夕法尼亚大学的研究机构Penn住房计划的一项新研究估计，根据住房与城市发展部的标准，将有1040万户家庭有资格获得“凭证”，这一数字是已有“凭证”的家庭数量的四倍。通过比较现有的和有需要“凭证”的人数与当地租房人口之间的差距，研究人员发现，奥兰多、夏洛特和凤凰城将从一项“向所有符合条件的人发放凭证的”政策中获益最大。

When the Democrats unveiled their mammoth $3.5trn Build Back Better bill, it included $75bn for housing vouchers. In the version that the House of Representatives passed in November, that was winnowed to about 300,000 new vouchers costing $24bn. Yet receiving a voucher and successfully leasing a unit with it are two very different things. The Penn researchers found that only about one in five households who were eligible for a voucher successfully obtained and used one in 2019. It is tricky to find a home to rent at all in a tight market. But poor renters face extra barriers. The HCVP only gives voucher-holders two months to sign a lease before they need an extension; security deposits can be pricey; and voucher-holders may not have access to a car or a computer to help with their search.

当民主党公布其3.5万亿美元的《重建更好未来》重大法案时，其中包括750亿美元的住房凭证。在众议院11月通过的版本中，这一方案被筛到大约30万张新凭证，耗资240亿美元。然而，获得凭证和使用凭证成功租到一套房子是完全不同的两件事。Penn的研究人员发现，在2019年，那些有资格申请凭证的家庭中成功获得凭证并使用凭证的只有大约五分之一数量。在供不应求的市场中，要想租到一套房子是很困难的。但是贫穷的租房者面临着额外的障碍。HCVP只给凭证持有者两个月的时间来签署租约，超时他们就需要延期; 押金可能很贵; 持有凭证的人可能没有汽车或电脑来帮助他们寻找。

The biggest barrier to using a voucher may be the outsize role that landlords play in choosing whom to rent to. Eva Rosen, of the McCourt School of Public Policy at Georgetown University, says landlords exist on a spectrum. On one end, there are property-owners in poor neighbourhoods who seek out voucher-holders because they like the security of knowing that the government will pay some of their tenants’ rent each month. On the other side are landlords who refuse to rent to families with vouchers because they don’t want to deal with the paperwork and extra inspections that come with the subsidy—or because of outright discrimination.

使用凭证的最大障碍可能是房东在选择租赁者时所扮演的巨大角色。乔治城大学麦考特公共政策学院的Eva Rosen说，房东们存在不同意见。一方面，贫困社区的业主会寻找持有凭证的人，因为他们知道政府会为他们的租客每月支付一部分租金，这样他们就放心了。另一方面，房东拒绝把房子租给有凭证的家庭，因为他们不想处理伴随补贴而来的文书工作和额外检查，又或者是因为公然的歧视。

A study published in 2018 by researchers at the Urban Institute, a think-tank, documents just how hard it is for voucher-holders to sign a lease. Researchers screened 341,000 rental advertisements and called landlords in five cities over 16 months. The authors found that they had to look at 39 adverts, on average, to find one potential home. When they called landlords to check whether they would accept a housing voucher, more than 75% of property-owners in Fort Worth and Los Angeles immediately declined. Denial rates were lower in Newark (31%) and Washington, DC (15%), in part because both cities have laws protecting families with vouchers from discrimination. Los Angeles passed such a law after the study was published.

2018年，智库城市研究所的研究人员发表了一项研究，证明了凭证持有者签署租约有多么困难。研究人员在16个月的时间里筛选了34.1万个租房广告，并联系了五个城市的房东。研究人员发现，他们平均要看39个广告才能找到一个可能的家。当他们打电话给房东，询问他们是否愿意接受住房凭证时，沃斯堡和洛杉矶超过75%的业主立即拒绝了。纽瓦克(31%)和华盛顿特区(15%)的拒绝率较低，部分原因是这两个城市都有法律保护有凭证的家庭不受歧视。这项研究发表后，洛杉矶通过了这样一项法律。

It is not yet clear how the covid-19 pandemic has affected the HCVP. Stefanie DeLuca of Johns Hopkins University says extra rental assistance might have helped some small landlords struggling with their mortgages. For others, the lengthy eviction moratoriums may have eroded trust between property-owners and the government. While the Democrats’ Build Back Better bill focuses on increasing the number of vouchers, it also includes $230m in incentive programmes to entice more landlords to accept them.

目前尚不清楚covid-19大流行病如何影响HCVP。约翰霍普金斯大学的Stefanie DeLuca表示，额外的租金援助可能会帮助一些挣扎于抵押贷款的小房东。对其他人来说，长时间的暂停驱逐可能已经削弱了业主和政府之间的信任。尽管民主党的《重建更好未来》法案侧重于增加凭证的数量，但它也包括2.3亿美元的激励计划，以吸引更多房东接受凭证。

Other adjustments could make the programme more effective. The maximum amount of money a landlord can get from the government is based on the average rent for an entire metropolitan area. Some landlords in poor neighbourhoods covet voucher-holders because they can charge much more for a unit than it would otherwise fetch. Ms Rosen argues that switching to a system where maximum rent varies by zip code will shut down such predatory tactics. And no federal law exists protecting voucher-holders from discrimination. Only 15 states and Washington, DC, can boast of such a measure. Several cities have followed suit. Still, landlords can skirt around these protections by failing inspections or setting rent just above market rates. “No one has paid any attention to landlords since the 1970s,” says Ms DeLuca. It might be time to start. ■

其他调整可以使方案更有效。房东从政府获得的最高金额是根据整个大都市区域的平均租金。一些贫困社区的房东觊觎持有凭证的人，因为他们可以收取比其他情况下更高的房费。Rosen女士认为，转换到“最高租金随邮政编码而变化的”系统将会关闭这种掠夺性策略。而且没有任何联邦法律保护凭证持有者不受歧视。只有15个州和华盛顿特区可以夸耀这样的措施。几个城市也纷纷效仿。不过，房东可以通过“未通过检查”或“将租金定在略高于市场价格的水平”来绕开这些保护措施。DeLuca女士说：“自20世纪70年代以来，没人再关注过房东”。也许是时候开始关注他们了。■

What the world can learn from Japan

世界可以从日本学到什么？

Ageing creatively

The oldest big country has lessons for those that will soon age and shrink

这个最古老的大国为那些面临人口萎缩和老龄化的国家提供了经验教训

Dec 11th 2021

文章音频

TWO TALES are often told about Japan. The first is of a nation in decline, with a shrinking and ageing population, sapped of its vitality. The second is of an alluring, hyper-functional, somewhat eccentric society—a nice place to eat sushi or explore strange subcultures, but of little wider relevance to the outside world. Both tales lead people to dismiss Japan. That is a mistake.

关于日本，有两个常提及的流言。第一个是日本是一个衰落的国家，经历着人口萎缩和老龄化，其活力逐渐消失。第二个是日本是一个诱人的、超功能的且有点古怪的社会，这是一个吃寿司或探索奇怪亚文化的好地方，但它又与外部世界没有更广泛的联系。这两个流言都让人们对日本不屑一顾。这是错误的。

As our special report this week argues, Japan is not an outlier—it is a harbinger. Many of the challenges it faces already affect other countries, or soon will, including rapid ageing, secular stagnation, the risk of natural disasters, and the peril of being caught between XX and America. The fact that some of these problems hit Japan early makes it a useful laboratory for observing their effects and working out how to respond.

正如我们本周的“特别报道”所指出的，日本并不是一个局外人，它是一个预告者。它面临的许多挑战已经或即将影响其他国家，包括快速老龄化、长期停滞、自然灾害风险以及夹在XX美之间的危险。日本很早就遇到了这些问题，这使它成为一个有用的实验室，可以观察它们的影响并研究如何应对。

One lesson is that societies must learn to live with risk. As the climate changes and natural hazards proliferate, countries must be able to bounce back from shocks. Painful experience has led Japan to invest in resilience. Bridges and buildings are retro fitted to make them earthquake-proof. After a big quake hit Kobe in 1995, leaving many without water, the city built an underground system to store 12 days’ supply for residents.

日本带来的一个教训是，社会必须学会与风险共存。随着气候变化和自然灾害的增加，各国必须能够从冲击中恢复过来。痛苦的经历促使日本投资于“还原能力”。为了防震，桥梁和建筑物再度流行。1995年大地震袭击神户后，许多人缺水，该市建立了一个地下系统，为居民储存12天的水。

Many Japanese people understand that responding to disasters is everyone’s problem, not just the state’s. That has helped during the pandemic: mask-wearing has been virtually universal. Among G7 countries, Japan has the lowest death rate from covid-19 and the highest rate of double-vaccination.

许多日本人明白，应对灾难是每个人的问题，而不仅仅是国家的问题。这在疫情期间起到了帮助作用: 人们几乎普遍都戴口罩。在七国集团(G7)中，日本新冠死亡率最低且两针疫苗接种率最高。

Another lesson is that demography matters. Most societies will ultimately age and shrink like Japan. By 2050, one in six people in the world will be over 65 years old, up from one in 11 in 2019. The populations of 55 countries, including XX, are projected to decline between now and 2050. Recent data suggest India will shrink sooner than expected.

另一个教训是人口统计很重要。大多数社会最终都会像日本一样老龄化和萎缩。到2050年，世界上六分之一的人将超过65岁，而2019年这一比例仅为十一分之一。从现在到2050年，包括XX在内的55个国家的人口预期将会减少。最近的数据显示，印度人口萎缩的速度将快于预期。

Like climate change, the demographic sort is vast, gradual and seems abstract—until it is not. And like climate change, it will demand a transformation both of institutions and of individual behaviour. Remaining active for longer is essential. The Japanese government urges firms to keep staff until they are 70. Many stay on: 33% of 70- to 74 -year-olds now have jobs, up from 23% a decade ago.

就像气候变化一样，人口类型是庞大的，渐进的，看起来抽象，直到它不再是这样[这里理解可能不到位，懂得可以在评论里留言]。就像气候变化一样，它也需要机构和个人行为的转变。保持更长时间的“活跃”是必要的[就是说要推迟退休年龄的意思？]。日本政府敦促企业将员工保留到70岁。许多人继续留任: 70-74岁的人中有33%有工作，这一比例高于10年前的23%。

Demographic change brings big economic challenges. Japan owes its sluggish growth in large measure to its shrinking population. If you look at the well-being of individual Japanese people, however, the picture is far rosier. In the decade from 2010 to 2019, Japan enjoyed the third-highest average rate of GDP growth per head in the G7, behind only Germany and America.

人口变化给经济带来了巨大的挑战。日本经济增长缓慢在很大程度上归因于人口的减少。然而，如果你看一看日本人的个人幸福状况，情况要乐观得多。从2010年到2019年的十年间，日本的人均GDP增长率在七国集团中排名第三，仅次于德国和美国。

Japan is a major creditor and the third-largest economy at current exchange rates. Its people live longer than the citizens of any other country. It is home to the biggest technology investor on the planet, a pioneering 5 G firm, and a host of global brands, from Uniqlo to Nintendo. Expertise in robots and sensors will help its firms make money from a wide range of new industrial technologies. Geopolitically, Japan plays a pivotal role between XX, its largest trading partner, and America, its key security partner. It should not, in short, be a global afterthought.

日本是主要债权国，按当前汇率计算，日本是第三大经济体。它的人民比任何其他国家的公民寿命都长。它是地球上最大的技术投资者，领先的5G公司，以及从优衣库到任天堂等一系列全球品牌的所在地。在机器人和传感器方面的专业知识将帮助其公司从广泛的新工业技术中获利。在地缘政治上，日本在其最大贸易伙伴XX和关键的安全伙伴美国之间扮演着关键角色。简而言之，它不应该是一个全球在事后才想到的国家。

Japan’s mistakes offer another set of lessons. Living with lots of risk makes setting priorities harder. In the face of so many potential hazards, Japan took its eye off climate change, the greatest ongoing disaster of all. In 2020 it at last pledged to reach net-zero carbon emissions by 2050, but the details are sketchy. Politicians pin their hopes on restarting nuclear plants mothballed after the Fukushima meltdown in 2011; this is unlikely as long as the public overestimates the dangers of nuclear power. Many bureaucrats, meanwhile, remain stubbornly sceptical of renewable energy. So Japan keeps burning coal, the filthiest fuel.

日本的错误提供了另一套教训。生活在风险重重的环境中会让你更难分清轻重缓急。面对如此多的潜在危险，日本忽视了气候变化，而这是所有灾难中最严重的。到2020年，它最终承诺到2050年实现碳净零排放，但具体细节尚不明确。政客们把希望寄托在它能够重启2011年福岛核泄漏后被封存的核电站上; 只要公众高估了核能的危险性，这就不太可能。与此同时，许多官员仍对可再生能源持顽固的怀疑态度。所以日本继续燃烧最脏的燃料煤炭。

One way to cope with a shrinking population is to get the most out of people. Japan will never live up to its potential while so many of its highly educated citizens are denied the chance to live up to theirs. Seniority-based promotion at traditional companies, combined with excessive deference to grey hairs, silences young voices and stifles innovation. That is why many of the brightest new graduates prefer to work for startups. Japan has done a good job of getting more women into the workforce in recent years, but they still have too few chances to rise. A dual-track labour system traps young people and women in precarious part-time jobs (which, among other things, makes them less keen to have children).

应对人口萎缩的一个办法是充分利用人口。当如此多受过高等教育的日本公民被剥夺了实现自己的潜力的机会时，日本永远无法发挥其潜力。传统公司基于资历的晋升机制，再加上对老年人的尊重，这压制了年轻人的“声音”，且扼杀了创新。这就是为什么许多最聪明的应届毕业生更喜欢为初创企业工作。近年来，日本在让更多女性进入职场方面做得很好，但她们晋升的机会仍然太少。双轨制的劳动体系将年轻人和妇女困在不稳定的兼职工作中(除了其他外，这使得他们不太愿意要孩子)。

Politicians tolerate all this in part because they feel little pressure to do otherwise. The Liberal Democratic Party has remained in power almost uninterrupted since 1955, thanks to a pathetically weak opposition. Senior figures, typically old men from political dynasties, are more conservative than the public they supposedly represent. For the public, in turn, today’s comfort dulls the impulse to press for a brighter tomorrow. Japan’s final lesson is about the danger of complacency. ■

政客们之所以容忍这一切，部分原因是他们没有感到有什么压力去做其他事情。自1955年以来，自民党一直保持着几乎不间断的执政，这要归功于一个可怜的弱小反对党。高层人物(通常是来自政治王朝的老人)比他们所代表的公众更加保守。反过来，对公众来说，今天的舒适削弱了迫切要求一个更美好的明天的冲动。日本的最后一个教训是关于自满的危险。■

America’s economy needs tighter monetary policy

美国经济需要收紧货币政策

Power off the money printer

关掉印钞机

Why the Fed should raise interest rates soon

为什么美联储应该尽快提高利率

Dec 8th 2021

The federal reserve has spent most of 2021 saying that high inflation would be temporary. And yet price rises have persistently overshot forecasts, reaching 5% in October, on the Fed’s preferred measure, even as employment remains about 4m short of its pre-pandemic level. On December 15th the Fed will decide whether to tighten monetary policy, probably by accelerating the pace at which it “tapers” its monthly purchases of assets, mostly government bonds, which are currently running at $90bn per month. It should go ahead and take action. Though uncertainty is high, the Fed must rapidly respond to the data it has today and then adjust as necessary as conditions evolve. Those data indicate that it has already fallen behind.

美联储在2021年的大部分时间里都表示，高通胀只是暂时的。然而，根据美联储首选指标，物价的上涨持续超出预期，在10月份达到了5%，尽管就业人数仍比疫情前少400万。12月15日，美联储将决定是否收紧货币政策，它可能会通过加快“缩减”每月购买资产(主要是购买政府债券)的步伐，目前每月购买规模为900亿美元。它应该继续进行并采取行动。尽管不确定性很高，但美联储必须迅速对现有数据做出反应，然后根据情况的变化进行必要的调整。这些数据表明，美联储已经落后了。

The rise in prices cannot be explained by a few shortages, such as of second-hand cars. In October the median item in the consumer-price index was 3.1% more expensive than a year earlier. Clothes prices were up 4.3%, shelter (such as rent) was 3.5% dearer, and transport cost 4.5% more. In the third quarter private-sector wages and salaries grew at an annualised rate of 6.5%—too fast to be compatible with the Fed’s 2% inflation target without incredible productivity growth.

物价的上涨不能用“一些东西的短缺”来解释，比如二手车。10月份，消费者价格指数中位数比去年同期上涨了3.1%。服装价格上涨了4.3%，住房(如房租)价格上涨了3.5%，交通成本上涨了4.5%。在第三季度，私营部门的工资薪金以6.5%的年率增长，这一速度太快了，如果没有令人难以置信的生产率增长，就无法满足美联储2%的通胀目标。

It is true that temporary factors have driven up inflation. The $1.9trn fiscal stimulus President Joe Biden signed in March will not be repeated (the outlay proposed in the Democrats’ social-spending bill is more spread out and partly offset by tax rises). During the pandemic, consumers have binged on goods. Supply chains have been bunged up, especially as the world’s factories have faced lockdowns and staff absences. Despite an abnormal number of Americans out of work, firms have struggled to fill vacancies.

确实，短期因素推高了通货膨胀。美国总统拜登在3月份签署的1.9万亿美元的财政刺激法案不会再有(民主党社会支出法案中提出的支出更分散，部分开支由增税抵消)。在疫情期间，消费者大肆购买商品。供应链已被堵塞，尤其是在全球工厂面临封锁、员工缺乏的情况。尽管失业的美国人数量异常庞大，但各公司仍在努力填补职位空缺。

However, predicting when these pandemic-related forces will ease is a fool’s errand, especially now that the Omicron variant is spreading. For as long as inflation remains high, there is a growing danger that it will become entrenched. The New York Fed estimates that the median consumer expects prices to rise at an annual pace of 4.2% over the next three years, up from 3% in January 2021, suggesting they may demand higher wages. Rising inflation expectations also reduce the effective cost of credit, because inflation makes debts easier to repay. The real interest rate over five years on government bonds is about -1.6%, lower than in almost all of 2020, when the economy was far weaker.

然而，预测与疫情相关的“力量”何时会减弱是徒劳的，尤其是现在奥密克戎变种还正在传播。只要通胀保持在高位，通胀“根深蒂固”的危险就会越来越大。纽约联邦储备银行估计，消费者预期未来三年物价的中位数将以每年4.2%的速度上涨，高于2021年1月的3%，这意味着他们可能要求加薪。通胀预期的上升也降低了信贷的实际成本，因为通胀使债务更容易偿还。5年期政府债券的实际利率约为-1.6%，几乎低于2020年全年的水平，2020年的时候经济远比现在疲软。

The latest argument from some doves is that nominal GDP, or total cash spending in the economy, is merely on its pre-crisis trend. This proves that pandemic-related distortions, not excessive demand, have driven up prices, they say. Yet though this argument held in the third quarter, it may already be out of date. Nominal GDP is expected to grow at annual rates of over 10% in the fourth quarter, compared with the trend rate of just 4%. America is seeing an unusual surge in demand, not just constrained supply.

一些鸽派人士的最新观点是，名义GDP(或经济中的总现金支出)仅处于危机前的趋势水平。他们说，这证明，导致价格上涨的是与疫情相关的“扭曲”，而不是“过度的需求”。然而，尽管这种观点在第三季度还站得住脚，但它可能已经过时了。预计第四季度名义GDP的年增长率将超过10%，而趋势增长率仅为4%。美国正经历着不寻常的需求激增，而不仅仅是供应受限。

Tighter monetary policy is therefore justified. But if you believe the Fed’s theory of how its asset purchases work, every bond it buys adds fresh stimulus to the economy. It follows that merely tapering the pace of purchases is not tightening. So why not raise interest rates instead? The answer is that the Fed is bound by its past guidance that it would stop buying bonds before raising rates, and that it would avoid ending purchases abruptly. Abandoning that framework would lead investors to question the central bank’s trustworthiness and to expect an excessive number of additional interest-rate increases in 2022.

因此，收紧货币政策是合理的。但如果你相信美联储的资产购买理论起作用，它购买的每一种债券都会给经济带来新的刺激。由此可见，仅仅缩减购买的步伐并不是紧缩。那么，为什么不提高利率呢? 答案是，美联储受其过去的指导方针所约束，即在加息之前停止购买债券，并避免突然停止购买。放弃这一框架将导致投资者质疑央行的可信度，并预期2022年还会出现过多的加息。

The good news is that the Fed can taper fast enough to let it raise interest rates in March. If between now and then the pandemic greatly worsens, consumers slash their spending on goods or many missing workers return to the labour force, monetary policymakers can change course again. But they must give themselves scope to raise rates soon. In an ideal world it is an option that would already be on the table. ■

好消息是，美联储可以以足够快的速度缩减购债规模，从而在3月份提高利率。如果从现在到那时，疫情严重恶化，消费者削减商品支出，或者许多失业工人重返劳动力市场，货币政策制定者可能再次改变方向。但他们必须给自己尽快加息的空间。在理想的情况下，这是一个已经摆在桌面上的选择。■
