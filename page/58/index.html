The real risk to America’s democracy

美国民主面临的真正风险

Elections

选举

![image-20210704113203969](/Users/chenyujie/Library/Application Support/typora-user-images/image-20210704113203969.png)

Partisan election administration is a greater worry than voter suppression

党派选举管理比选民压制更令人担忧

Jul 3rd 2021

HAVING CAMPAIGNED for the presidency on a promise to rejuvenate democracy around the world, Joe Biden finds himself in a battle to defend it at home. In June, 200 prominent American scholars of democracy signed a letter warning that changes to state laws are “transforming several states into political systems that no longer meet the minimum conditions for free and fair elections”. Another longtime student of American democracy, the Republican leader in the Senate, Mitch McConnell, said in January that if an election could be overturned by fact-free allegations from the losing side, “Our democracy would enter a death spiral.” Yet that is just what his party is facilitating.

student 研究者

death spiral：a situation that keeps getting worse and that is likely to end badly, with great harm or damage being caused

死亡漩涡，死亡螺旋（情况越来越糟，可能造成巨大的伤害或破坏）

facilitate 加快；促进

拜登在竞选总统时曾承诺要在全世界重振民主，但现在他发现自己在国内正处于捍卫民主的战斗中。今年6月，200名著名的美国民主学者签署了一封公开信，警告称，州法律的变化正在“将几个州转变为不再满足自由公正选举的最低条件的政治制度”。另一位长期研究美国民主的人、参议院共和党领袖Mitch McConnell今年1月表示，如果败选一方的不实指控能够推翻选举，“我们的民主就会进入死亡螺旋。”然而，这正是他的政党所推动的。

For Democrats the threat to elections is about who can cast votes. They decry changes to laws on identification, postal ballots and so on, which they call “the new Jim Crow”. Although there is no excuse for restricting such things as Sunday voting, which is popular with African-American churches, their fears are overblown. Under the old Jim Crow, only 2% of African-Americans were registered to vote in some southern states. By contrast, political scientists are unsure whether today’s schemes will affect turnout at all.

overblown 浮夸的；过分的

turnout 参加人数

背景：吉姆·克劳法 (Jim Crow laws) 泛指1876年至1965年间美国南部各州以及边境各州对有色人种（主要针对非洲裔美国人，但同时也包含其他族群）实行种族隔离制度的法律。这些法律上的种族隔离强制公共设施必须依照种族的不同而隔离使用，且在隔离但平等的原则下，种族隔离被解释为不违反宪法保障的同等保护权，因此得以持续存在。但事实上黑人所能享有的部份与白人相较往往是较差的，而这样的差别待遇也造成了黑人长久以来处于经济、教育及社会上较为弱势的地位。

对民主党来说，选举的威胁在于谁可以投票。他们谴责有关身份识别、邮寄选票等法律的改变，并称其为“Jim Crow”。虽然没有理由限制像周日投票这样在非裔美国人教堂中很流行的事情，但他们的担心有点过分了。在旧的种族隔离制度下，只有2%的非洲裔美国人在南部的一些州登记投票。相比之下，政治学家不确定今天的方案是否会影响投票率。

Instead the real threat comes after votes have been cast. In Arizona, for example, the legislature wants to limit the independence of the chief elections officer; a state representative introduced a law letting the legislature overturn the results of a presidential election, and then started campaigning to oversee elections herself. In Georgia the state legislature can now replace the leadership of county election boards. Texas is considering a bill that makes it easier to prosecute election officials. Across the country, the officials who administer elections in states where Republicans hold sway have been attacked for upholding the election results. Many are at risk of being replaced.

uphold 维持；保持

相反，真正的威胁出现在投票之后。例如，在亚利桑那州，州议会想要限制首席选举官的独立性;一名州代表提出了一项法律，允许州议会推翻总统选举的结果，然后开始竞选，亲自监督选举。在佐治亚州，州议会现在可以取代县选举委员会的领导。得克萨斯州正在考虑一项法案，使起诉选举官员变得更容易。在全国范围内，在共和党人占主导地位的州管理选举的官员因维持选举结果而受到攻击。许多人面临被取代的风险。

These might seem like distant, bureaucratic changes. In fact they raise the chances of a contested election that the courts cannot sort out. They weaken America’s voting system in ways that will outlast the hysteria over the 2020 result.

outlast 比…更长久

hysteria 歇斯底里

这些可能看起来像是遥远的、官僚主义的变化。事实上，他们增加了法院无法解决的有争议选举的可能性。他们削弱美国投票制度的方式将比对2020年选举结果的歇斯底里持续更久。

The inspiration behind this is Donald Trump, who continues to use every chance he has to insist that the election was stolen. Though it is hard to know how seriously to take him, Mr Trump is already holding campaign rallies for 2024 (to win the White House for the third time in a row, naturally).

这妙计的背后是来自特朗普，他继续利用一切机会坚称选举失窃。尽管很难知道他有多认真，但特朗普已经在为2024年举行竞选集会(自然是为了连续第三次赢得白宫)。

Claiming to be winning while actually losing might seem a joke. Yet most Republican voters take it literally. Two in three think that Mr Biden did not win November’s election and just short of half think the result should have been overturned. That leaves Trump-sceptics among the Republican elite in a familiar dilemma. Caught between their primary voters and loyalty to the constitution, most have concluded that, unless the Capitol is under siege, the best course of action is simply to stay silent.

to take sth/sb literally 照字面意义理解某事物/某人说的话

声称自己赢了，实际上却输了，这似乎是个笑话。然而，大多数共和党选民都是这么认为的。三分之二的人认为拜登没有赢得11月的大选，只有不到一半的人认为结果应该被推翻。这让共和党精英中对特朗普持怀疑态度的人陷入了熟悉的两难境地。在初选选民和对宪法的忠诚之间左右为难的大多数人得出的结论是，除非国会大厦遭到围攻，否则最好的做法就是保持沉默。

Yet the threats from Mr Trump and the threat to the constitution operate on different time-scales. Mr Trump may or may not run again. By contrast, the changes to state election machinery being made by Republican legislators will be in place in 2024 and beyond for a candidate of either party to exploit. To understand why this is so troubling, consider three fail-safe mechanisms built into American elections.

然而，来自特朗普的威胁和对宪法的威胁在不同的时间尺度上发挥作用。特朗普可能会再次参选，也可能不会。相比之下，共和党议员对州选举机制的改革将在2024年及以后实施，两党候选人都可以利用。要理解为什么如此麻烦，我们来看看美国选举中内置的三种万无一失的机制。

The first is the principle that the loser concedes. Mr Trump ditched that one in 2020. The second is the integrity of local election officials, no matter what their partisan allegiances. Despite coming under great pressure to do otherwise last year, they stood firm. As a reward, their powers have been stripped away or new felonies created that may be used to browbeat them. Many Republican officials who certified the election results have been censured by their local party committees and have also received death threats. Brad Raffensperger, Georgia’s Republican secretary of state, was notable in 2020 for his willingness to stand up to Mr Trump when he was directly asked to “find” the votes needed to overturn the results. Georgia’s state legislature has responded by taking away some of his authority.

felony 重罪

browbeat 威逼
censure 谴责

第一个机制是败者承认。特朗普在2020年丢弃了这一机制。其次是地方选举官员的诚信，无论其党派立场如何。尽管去年他们面临着巨大的压力，要求他们采取其他措施，但他们立场坚定。作为奖励，他们的权力被剥夺，或者有新的重罪被创造出来以威逼他们。许多认可选举结果的共和党官员受到了当地党委员会的谴责，还收到了死亡威胁。共和党人、佐治亚州国务卿Brad Raffensperger在2020年的时候因愿意对抗特朗普而出名，当时特朗普直接被要求“找到”推翻选举结果所需的票数。佐治亚州议会对此作出回应，剥夺了他的一些权力。

That leaves the third fail-safe—the courts. These too performed well under stress, and they probably would do so the next time round. Yet to put the primary responsibility for making elections legitimate onto the judicial branch in election after election risks overloading it and, ultimately, breaking it. How long would it be before a Supreme Court decision were ignored?

这就剩下了第三种万无一失的机制，即法院。这些人在压力下也表现得很好，下次可能也会如此。然而，在一次又一次的选举中，把使选举合法化的主要责任推给司法部门，就有可能使司法部门负担过重，并最终破坏司法部门。还要多久最高法院的裁决才会被忽视?

Catastrophising about democracy in America has been common on parts of the right: remember “the Flight 93 Election” in 2016, which called on patriots to storm the cockpit to deny Hillary Clinton the presidency? It has since spread to the left and centre, too. Talk of democracy in peril raises the spectre of a country under an autocrat of the type renounced on the Fourth of July 1776. The greater risk is that the chaos following the 2020 election becomes normal. By recent standards 2020 was not that close. Imagine a contest so tight that no national consensus could settle on who was ahead. America would be, to quote Mr McConnell again, on “a poisonous path where only the winners of an election actually accept the result”.

在某些右翼人士看来，美国的民主灾难是很常见的:还记得2016年的“93航班选举”吗?那次选举呼吁爱国者们冲进驾驶舱，阻止希拉里当选总统。从那以后，民主灾难也蔓延到了左翼和中间派。谈论民主危在旦夕会让人联想到一个国家被一个在1776年7月4日抛弃的那种独裁者统治的恐惧。更大的风险是，2020年大选后的混乱将成为常态。按照最近的标准，2020年还没有那么势均力敌。想象一下，一场势均力敌的比赛，没有一个全国性的共识能就谁领先达成一致。再次引用麦康奈尔的话说，美国将“走上一条有毒的道路，只有选举的获胜者才会接受选举结果”。

My party, right or wrong

我所在的党派，是对还是错的？

Republican Party elites are in a bind of their own making. Under pressure from Mr Trump and his allies, state legislatures are making changes that will weaken American democracy. The solution is for leaders to uphold the norm that election administrators are above party. However, they have indulged the lie of a stolen election to such an extent that affirming the fraud has become an essential qualification for administering the next vote.

be in a bind 陷入困境

共和党精英们陷入了他们自己造成的困境。在特朗普及其盟友的压力下，各州议会正在做出将削弱美国民主的改变。解决办法是领导人坚持选举管理者高于政党的规范。然而，他们在某种程度上纵容选举舞弊的谎言，以致确认舞弊已成为管理下次选举的一项基本条件。

The silent non-Trump faction of the Republican Party may hope that all this will blow over and that those sounding the alarm about democracy are exaggerating. They may believe they can play a greater role in safeguarding America so long as they stay on good terms with their base. Yet that logic has proved faulty since Mr Trump’s inauguration in 2016. Meanwhile, the composition of their party is changing around them. It would be safer for the constitution, and more in keeping with the flag-waving spirit of the Fourth of July, for Republicans to speak out now before speaking out becomes even harder. ■

blow over 平息

共和党中沉默的非特朗普派可能希望，这一切会平息，那些敲响民主警钟的人是在夸大其词。他们可能认为，只要与基地保持良好关系，他们就可以在保卫美国方面发挥更大的作用。然而，自2016年特朗普就职以来，这种逻辑被证明是错误的。与此同时，他们所在政党的组成也在发生变化。这对宪法来说更安全，也更符合7月4日美国国庆日飘扬旗帜的精神，对于共和党人来说，在发声变得更加困难之前，现在就发声。■

The long goodbye to covid-19

与covid-19永别

After the disease

疫情之后

![image-20210703110147613](/Users/chenyujie/Library/Application Support/typora-user-images/image-20210703110147613.png)

The pandemic is still far from over, but glimpses of its legacy are emerging

疫情还远未结束，但其后遗症正在显现

Jul 3rd 2021

WHEN WILL it end? For a year and a half, covid-19 has gripped one country after another. Just when you think the virus is beaten, a new variant comes storming back, more infectious than the last. And yet, as the number of vaccinations passes 3bn, glimpses of post-covid life are emerging. Already, two things are clear: that the last phase of the pandemic will be drawn-out and painful; and that covid-19 will leave behind a different world.

（疫情）什么时候会结束?一年半以来，新冠肺炎疫情在一个又一个国家蔓延。就在你认为病毒被打败的时候，一种新的变种如暴风袭来，而且比上一个变种更具传染性。然而，随着疫苗接种数量超过30亿，后冠状病毒时代的生活正在显现。现在，有两件事是明确的:大流行的最后阶段将是漫长而痛苦的; COVID-19将留下一个不同的世界。

This week The Economist publishes a normalcy index, which reflects both these realities. Taking the pre-pandemic average as 100, it tracks such things as flights, traffic and retailing across 50 countries comprising 76% of Earth’s population. Today it stands at 66, almost double the level in April 2020.

normalcy = normality 正常状态

本周，《经济学人》发布了一份反映上述两种现实的“正常状态指数”。以疫情前的平均水平为100，它追踪了50个国家(占地球人口76%)的航班、交通和零售等数据。如今，这一数字仅为66，几乎是2020年4月水平的两倍。

Yet the ravages of covid-19 are still apparent in many countries. Consider our index’s worst performer, Malaysia, which is suffering a wave of infections six times more deadly than the surge in January and scores just 27. The main reason for this is that vaccination remains incomplete.

然而，疫情在许多国家的肆虐依然明显。考虑一下在我们的指数中表现最差的马来西亚，该国正遭受感染浪潮，其致死人数是1月份人数激增时的六倍，它的得分仅为27。造成这种情况的主要原因是疫苗接种仍然不完全。

In sub-Saharan Africa, suffering a lethal outbreak, just 2.4% of the population aged over 12 has had a single dose. Even in America, where vaccines are plentiful, only around 30% of Mississippians and Alabamans are fully protected. Although the world is set to produce around 11bn doses of vaccine this year, it will be months before all those jabs find arms, and longer if rich countries hog doses on the off-chance that they may need them.

I did it on the off chance that … 我做这个想看看…

在撒哈拉以南非洲，爆发了致命的疫情，12岁以上的人口中只有2.4%接种过一剂疫苗。即使在疫苗充足的美国，也只有大约30%的密西西比人和阿拉巴马人得到了充分的保护。尽管全球今年将生产约110亿剂疫苗，但在所有这些疫苗找到接种的人前还需要几个月的时间，如果发达国家为了以防万一会有需要而多占疫苗，则需要更长的时间。

The lack of vaccination is aggravated by new variants. Delta, first spotted in India, is two to three times more infectious than the virus that came out of XX. Cases spread so fast that hospitals can rapidly run out of beds and medical staff (and sometimes oxygen), even in places where 30% of people have had jabs. Today’s variants are spreading even among the vaccinated. No mutation has yet put a dent in the vaccines’ ability to prevent almost all severe disease and death. But the next one might.

疫苗接种的缺乏因新变种而加剧。首先在印度发现的Delta病毒，其传染性是来自XX的病毒的两到三倍。病例传播如此之快，以至于医院的病床和医护人员(有时甚至是氧气)很快就会用完，即使在有30%的人注射过疫苗的地方也是如此。今天的变异病毒甚至在接种疫苗的人群中也在蔓延。目前还没有任何突变能削弱了疫苗预防几乎所有严重疾病和死亡的能力。但下一个突变有可能。

None of this alters the fact that the pandemic will eventually abate, even though the virus itself is likely to survive. For those fortunate enough to have been fully vaccinated and to have access to new treatments, covid-19 is already fast becoming a non-lethal disease. In Britain, where Delta is dominant, the fatality rate if you become infected is now about 0.1%, similar to seasonal flu: a danger, but a manageable one. If a variant required a reformulated vaccine, it would not take long to create.

这些都不能改变疫情最终会减弱的事实，即使病毒本身很可能存活下来。对于那些有幸接种完全了疫苗以及能够获得新的治疗手段的人来说，covid-19已经迅速成为一种非致命性疾病。在Delta病毒占主导地位的英国，现在被感染的致死率约为0.1%，类似于季节性流感:危险但可控。如果一个变种需要一种重新配制的疫苗，它不会花很长时间来制造出来。

However, as vaccines and treatments become more plentiful in rich countries, so will anger at seeing people in poor ones die for want of supplies. That will cause friction between rich countries and the rest. Travel bans will keep the two worlds apart.

然而，随着发达国家的疫苗和治疗手段越来越丰富，看到贫穷国家的人因为缺乏疫苗而死亡，人们的愤怒也会越来越强烈。这将导致发达国家和其他国家之间的摩擦。旅行禁令将把这两个世界分开。

Eventually flights will resume, but other changes in behaviour will last. Some will be profound. Take America, where the booming economy surged past its pre-pandemic level back in March, but which still scores only 73 on our index—partly because big cities are quieter, and more people work from home.

航班最终会恢复，但其他行为的变化将会持续。其中一些影响深远。以美国为例，该国繁荣的经济在3月份超过了流感爆发前的水平，但在我们的指数中仍只有73分，部分原因是大城市更安静，而且更多的人在家工作。

So far it looks as if the legacy of covid-19 will follow the pattern set by past pandemics. Nicholas Christakis of Yale University identifies three shifts: the collective threat prompts a growth in state power; the overturning of everyday life leads to a search for meaning; and the closeness of death which brings caution while the disease rages, spurs audacity when it has passed. Each will mark society in its own way.

到目前为止，covid-19的遗存似乎将遵循以往大流行确立的模式。耶鲁大学的Nicholas Christakis指出了三个转变:集体威胁促使国家权力增长; 日常生活的颠覆导致了对意义的探索;当疾病肆虐时，死亡的迫近带来了谨慎，当疾病过去时，则会激发胆大妄为。每一种转变都将以自己的方式给社会打上烙印。

When people in rich countries retreated into their houses during lockdowns, the state barricaded itself in with them. During the pandemic governments have been the main channel for information, the setters of rules, a source of cash and, ultimately**, providers of vaccines**. Very roughly, rich-country governments paid out 90 cents for every dollar of lost output. Slightly to their own amazement, politicians who restricted civil liberties found that most of their citizens applauded.

当发达国家的人们在封锁期间躲进自己的房子里时，国家就把自己和他们一道关在了里面。在大流行期间，政府一直是信息的主要渠道、规则的制定者、现金来源，并最终成为疫苗的提供者。粗略地说，发达国家政府每损失一美元的产出就要支付90美分。令他们自己略感惊讶的是，限制公民自由的政客们发现，他们的大多数公民都为他们鼓掌。

There is a vigorous academic debate about whether lockdowns were “worth it”. But the big-government legacy of the pandemic is already on display. Just look at the spending plans of the Biden administration. Whatever the problem—inequality, sluggish economic growth, the security of supply chains—a bigger, more activist government seems to be the preferred solution.

big-government *(mainly US)*大政府 [过多干涉公民生活]

关于封锁是否“值得”，学术界展开了一场激烈的辩论。但是大流行病遗留下来的大政府已经开始显现。看看拜登政府的支出计划就知道了。无论问题是什么，比如不平等，缓慢的经济增长，供应链的安全，一个更大，更积极的政府似乎是首选的解决方案。

There is also evidence of a renewed search for meaning. This is reinforcing the shift towards identity politics on both the right and the left, but it goes deeper than that. Roughly one in five people in Italy and the Netherlands told Pew, a pollster, that the pandemic had made their countries more religious. In Spain and Canada about two in five said family ties had become stronger.

背景：当某一特定群体的成员为了影响政治或社会变革而联合起来时，其结果通常被称为“认同政治”。这种现象不仅局限于我们这个时代主要的种族或性别划分，而且还延伸到性取向、种族、种族等方面，公民身份和其他特定群体感到被边缘化或受压迫的情况。

也有证据表明，人们正在重新寻找生命的意义。这加强了左翼和右翼向认同政治的转变，但它比这更深刻。在意大利和荷兰，大约有五分之一的人告诉民意调查机构Pew，疫情让他们的国家变得更加宗教化。在西班牙和加拿大，约五分之二的人表示家庭关系变得更加紧密。

Leisure has been affected, too. People say they have had 15% more time on their hands. In Britain young women spent 50% longer with their nose in a book. Literary agents have been swamped with first novels. Some of this will fade: media firms fear an “attention recession”. But some changes will stick.

How the Victorians fought for cheap food

维多利亚时代的人如何为廉价的食物而战

Jun 26th 2021

背景：

谷物法：是一道于1815年至1846年强制实施的进口关税，藉以“保护”英国农夫及地主免受来自从生产成本较低廉的外国所进口的谷物的竞争。它规定了国产谷物平均价达到或超过某种限度时方可进口。其目的是维护土地贵族的利益。要注意的是，在英式英语里，“玉米”（corn）这个词语泛指所有谷物，与美式英语专指玉米的意思有所不同。

ABOUT HALF of most British people’s income in the 1830s and 1840s was spent on food. Hunger was commonplace, occasionally sparking riots. Contributing to the high cost were tariffs on imported grain, called the Corn Laws, which soared as high as 80%. The system enriched aristocratic landowners when most Britons were not allowed to serve in Parliament or vote.

在19世纪30年代和40年代，大多数英国人大约有一半的收入花在食物上。饥饿是家常便饭，偶尔还会引发骚乱。高成本的原因是因为对进口谷物征收关税，也就是所谓的《谷物法》，关税飙升了80%。当大多数英国人不被允许在议会任职或投票时，这一制度使贵族地主们富裕起来。

Facing public anger, a famine in Ireland and fears of starvation in Britain, the prime minister, Robert Peel, introduced legislation to end the tariffs. On June 25th 1846 the House of Lords repealed the Corn Laws, following a House of Commons vote a month earlier. It marked a major moment in the history of open economies. How it was achieved offers lessons to those defending the global trading system today.

famine 饥荒

背景：皮尔支持降低进口关税，废除“谷物法”，但是此举导致了其内阁倒台。而皮尔在国会继续支持自由贸易原则。

面对公众的愤怒、爱尔兰的饥荒和英国对饥饿的恐惧，英国首相罗伯特·皮尔提出了终止关税的立法。1846年6月25日，上议院废除了《谷物法》，此前一个月，下议院进行了投票。它标志着开放经济体历史上的一个重要时刻。如何实现的这一目标，也为今天捍卫全球贸易体系的人提供了经验教训。

The first lesson is to organise a broad coalition and creatively use the media. It was not simply the poor who had an interest in lower grain prices. A new generation of prosperous manufacturers and moral-minded aristocrats joined forces. They established what might be one of the first lobbying groups, the Anti-Corn Law League, which hosted rallies, financed research and supported political candidates. Books and pamphlets sprang up to make the case. The Economist itself was founded in 1843 to campaign for the abolition of the Corn Laws and for free trade.

pamphlets 小册子；传单

abolition 废除；取消

第一个教训是要组织一个广泛的联盟，创造性地利用媒体。不仅是穷人对较低的粮食价格感兴趣。新一代富有的制造商和有道德的贵族联手起来。他们建立了反谷物法联盟，这可能是最早的游说团体之一。该联盟主办集会，资助研究，并支持政治候选人。为了证明这一点，书籍和传单纷纷涌现。《经济学人》本身成立于1843年，其宗旨是为废除《谷物法》和为自由贸易而战。

The second lesson is the need for small victories to generate momentum, rather than going for big wins immediately—Peel’s policy of “gradualism”. His plan did not fully remove sliding-scale tariffs until 1849, giving time for landowners to adapt. Meanwhile, Britain’s free-trade moves helped usher in a wave of trade agreements across Europe and with America.

gradualism 渐进主义

sliding-scale 浮动计算法

第二个教训是，需要小的胜利来产生动力，而不是要立即取得大的胜利，这是皮尔的“渐进主义”政策。直到1849年，他的计划才完全取消浮动关税，这也给土地所有者时间来适应。与此同时，英国的自由贸易举措在欧洲和美国掀起了一波贸易协定浪潮。

The third lesson is the need for tangible benefits for the public. By 1850, people were paying around a quarter less for bread than if repeal had not occurred, according to Kevin O’Rourke of NYU Abu Dhabi. The real incomes of society’s top 10% fell, while those of the bottom 90% grew slightly, notes Douglas Irwin of Dartmouth College.

第三个教训是公众需要切实的利益。据纽约大学阿布扎比分校的Kevin O ‘Rourke称，到1850年，人们为面包支付的费用比废除该法案之前少了四分之一。达特茅斯学院的Douglas Irwin指出，社会上收入最高的10%的人的实际收入下降了，而收入最低的90%的人的实际收入略有增长。

Much can be learned from Peel’s approach. Today, free trade is promoted by stale policy wonks and rapacious executives, nothing like the broad, energetic coalition of the past. Opponents of globalisation use social media far more effectively than its supporters. Politicians vie for grand gestures rather than quiet incrementalism. And the benefits of free trade are largely hidden from consumers. Those who take to the ramparts to protest against globalisation fail to notice why their smartphones are so cheap.

rapacious 贪婪的

ramparts 壁垒

我们可以从皮尔的方法中学到很多东西。如今，自由贸易是由陈腐的政策专家和贪婪的高管推动的，不像过去那种广泛而充满活力的联盟。全球化的反对者比支持者更有效地利用社交媒体。政客们竞相展示的是大刀阔斧的姿态，而非不事张扬的渐进主义。自由贸易的好处在很大程度上被消费者所掩盖。那些拿起壁垒来抗议全球化的人没有注意到他们的智能手机为什么这么便宜。

Yet the most important lesson is about leadership. Peel had opposed repealing the Corn Laws but, faced with a crisis, he was willing to split his party and lose his job to do the right thing. The divided Conservatives rarely held power during the following 30 years. It was “the whole community” that mattered, Peel wrote in his memoirs, and whether “cheapness and plenty are not [better] ensured for the future” by free trade than by protectionism. What leader would be willing to do that today? ■

然而，最重要的教训是关于领导力的。皮尔曾反对废除《谷物法》，但在面临危机时，为了做正确的事，他愿意分裂自己的政党，甚至丢掉自己的工作。在接下来的30年里，分裂的保守党很少掌权。皮尔在回忆录中写道，重要的是“整个社会”，以及自由贸易是否能比保护主义“更好地保证未来的廉价和富足”。今天有哪位领导人愿意这样做呢?■

FOR THE first time in its august history of more than 100 years, the Adlon, a glitzy hotel within sight of Berlin’s Brandenburg Gate, used Kurzarbeit, a scheme in which the German government pays the bulk of wages of people who temporarily stop working or work reduced hours. “Our business was almost completely gone,” explains Daniela Welter, the hotel’s head of personnel, referring to the hard lockdown imposed last November that banned hotel stays for leisure travellers. Thanks to Kurzarbeit, the Adlon was able to save the jobs of all its 347 staff. Today it is hiring again.

glitzy 耀眼的；浮华的

背景：Kurzarbeit是由于经济困难而暂时减少正常工作时间，员工最多可减少90％的工作时间，并且仍能获得大部分以前的工资，而雇主将从AMS处获得津贴，涵盖了减少时间部分的工资税险。

Kurzarbeit dates back over a century and has been mimicked round the world during the covid-19 crisis. In Germany it has been stretched to unprecedented lengths. Whereas some 1.5m German workers at 56,000 firms were furloughed at the peak of the global financial crisis in May 2009, around 6m employees of 610,000 businesses took advantage of the scheme in April 2020, when lockdowns were at their strictest. Last month 2.3m people, or 7% of the German workforce, were still partially or fully furloughed, according to the IFO Institute, a think-tank. The cost to the federal employment agency is €35bn ($42bn) and counting. In June Germany’s labour minister announced an extension of the scheme until the end of September.

“Kurzarbeit”计划可以追溯到一个多世纪前，在covid-19危机期间被世界各地模仿。在德国，它被延期到了前所未有的长度。2009年5月全球金融危机最严重的时候，56000家德国公司的150万名员工被迫休假，而在2020年4月，61万家企业的600万名员工利用了该计划，当时正是封锁最严格的时候。根据智库IFO研究所的数据，上个月有230万人(占德国劳动力总数的7%)仍有部分或完全休假。联邦就业机构为此付出的成本为350亿欧元(合420亿美元)，而且还在不断增加。6月，德国劳工部长宣布将该计划延长至9月底。

Workers and their representatives applaud the extension. 工人和他们的代表对延期表示赞同。与工会关系密切的基金会Hans-Böckler-Stiftung的研究机构IMK最近的一项研究发现，Kurzarbeit在疫情最严重时保住了220万个工作岗位，而在2007-09年全球金融危机最严重时保住了33万个工作岗位。至于政府，如果没有该计划，它可能会在相对慷慨的失业救济上花费更多。

Employers are more circumspect. Most agree that Kurzarbeit has its uses. Sebastian Dullien, director of the IMK, says that it enables businesses to hold on to workers and restart seamlessly as the economy reopens, even as their American counterparts struggle to find staff. The BDA, one of Germany’s two main employers’ associations, welcomes the extension.

雇主会更加谨慎。大多数人都同意“Kurzarbeit”有其用途。IMK主管Sebastian Dullien表示，这使得企业能够留住工人，并在经济重新开放时无缝重启，即使它们的美国同行很难找到员工。德国两大雇主协会之一的BDA对延期表示欢迎。

More surprising, the BDA opposes the full payment of furloughed workers’ social-security contributions by the government until the end of the year, as demanded by the trade-union federation. This would reduce companies’ labour costs but create “wrong incentives” to enrol in the scheme, the BDA cautions.

尽管如此，游说团体还是警告说，不要用更高的失业保险缴款来为休假提供资金，因为这将增加公司已经很高的劳动力成本。更令人惊讶的是，BDA反对政府按照工会联合会的要求，在今年年底前全额支付休假工人的社会保障金。BDA警告称，这将降低企业的劳动力成本，而且会产生参与该计划的“错误动机”。

That in turn could have potentially undesirable consequences for the firms’ competitiveness. Although companies pay more than 30% of the labour cost of furloughed staff, and so have no reason to keep entirely zombie employees on the books, the scheme can discourage businesses from adapting to the post-pandemic reality.

这反过来又可能对公司的竞争力产生潜在的不良后果。尽管公司支付休假员工超过30%的劳动力成本，因此没有理由留下完全就是“僵尸”的员工，但该计划可能会阻碍企业适应疫情后的现实。

It is not as if the scheme encourages firing. The likelier explanation is that only the worst-affected companies, such as the Adlon or Lufthansa, Germany’s flag-carrier, whose fleet was more or less grounded for months, chose to take part. That in turn suggests that if they can help it, German firms prefer not to. ■

Bartleby

![image-20210630095329957](/Users/chenyujie/Library/Application Support/typora-user-images/image-20210630095329957.png)

Labour may be gaining ground on capital

Jun 24th 2021

THE CHANCE to take a summer holiday after the long lockdown is very appealing. So Bartleby was excited to book rooms in a pub-cum-hotel in the beautiful Yorkshire Dales in July. Not long after the booking, however, the manager called to warn that the restaurant and bar would be closed on the Wednesday night. “As you probably know”, she said, “it is impossible to find staff at the moment.”

在长时间的封闭后享受一个暑假的机会非常吸引人。所以巴托比很兴奋地在7月份在美丽的约克郡山谷的一家酒吧兼酒店预订房间。然而，在预订后不久，经理打电话警告称，餐厅和酒吧将在周三晚上关闭。她说，“你可能知道，目前找不到工作人员。”

The British hospitality sector has relied on overseas employees and is affected by Brexit as well as covid-19. The same is true of construction. Since 2017 the industry has seen a 4% decline in British-born workers and a 42% fall in those from the EU. Many foreigners returned home for the pandemic, which may mean 750,000 fewer total workers are available than in 2019, according to Michael O’Connor and Jonathan Portes of the Economic Statistics Centre of Excellence, a research outfit.

英国酒店业依赖海外员工，而且其受到英国脱欧和新冠肺炎的影响。建筑业也是如此。自2017年以来，英国本土工人在该行业的数量下降了4%，；来自欧盟本土的工人数量下降了42%。根据研究机构卓越经济统计中心的Michael O ‘Connor和Jonathan Portes的说法，许多外国人因疫情回国，这可能意味着可用劳动力总量比2019年减少75万人。

资金短缺促使受影响最严重的行业的雇主提高工资，而且往往是大幅度提高。招聘公司Reed Global的数据显示，与2020年4月至6月相比，英国酒店和餐饮公司的薪资上涨了18%。同期零售业的工资上涨了10%。

It may all turn out to be transitory, as may the recent surge in inflation. But, just maybe, the world has reached a turning point. It has been widely assumed that capital would continue to dominate, especially with the potential for technology and artificial intelligence to replace workers in the service sector, just as automation took its toll on manufacturing employment. Throw in the power of multinational corporations and the future for workers was caricatured as something out of George Orwell’s “1984”—“a boot stamping on a human face, forever”.

The political reaction to the pandemic suggests sentiment may have changed. Governments were quick to try to safeguard workers’ incomes, either by offering furlough schemes to subsidise wages or by increasing and extending unemployment benefits. They are now pursuing higher taxes on companies. Politicians on both the right and the left are happy to bash business in the belief that this will appeal to voters.

政治上对疫情的反应表明，情绪可能已经改变。各国政府迅速采取措施保护工人的收入，要么提供休假计划来补贴工资，要么增加和延长失业救济。他们现在正在追求对公司征收更高的税。左右两派的政客都乐于抨击企业，因为他们相信这样做会吸引选民。

Closing the world’s schools caused children great harm

关闭世界各地的学校给孩子们造成了巨大的伤害

Must try harder

必须更加努力

![image-20210629101320168](/Users/chenyujie/Library/Application Support/typora-user-images/image-20210629101320168.png)

They will need help to catch up on lost learning

Jun 26th 2021

COVID-19 RARELY makes children very ill. In the year to April the chance of an American aged 5-14 catching and dying from the virus was about one in 500,000—roughly a tenth of a child’s chance of dying in a traffic accident in normal times. Yet schools around the world have been wholly or partly closed for about two-thirds of an academic year because of the pandemic.

wholly 全面地；完全

COVID-19很少使儿童病情严重。截至今年4月，美国5-14岁儿童感染并死于该病毒的几率约为50万分之一，约为正常情况下儿童死于交通事故的几率的十分之一。然而，由于疫情，世界各地的学校已经有大约三分之二的学年全部或部分停课。

The immense harm this has done to children’s prospects might be justified if closing classrooms were one of the best ways of preventing lethal infections among adults. But few governments have weighed the costs and risks carefully. Many have kept schools shut even as bars and restaurants open, either to appease teachers’ unions, whose members get paid whether they teach in person or not, or to placate nervous parents.

如果关闭教室是防止成人致命感染的最佳方法之一，那么这种做法对儿童前景造成的巨大伤害可能是合理的。但很少有政府仔细权衡过成本和风险。即使酒吧和餐馆开门营业了，许多学校仍然关闭，这种做法要么是为了安抚教师工会（教师工会的成员无论是否亲自授课都能拿到工资），要么是为了安抚紧张的家长。

As a result, young brains are being starved of stimulation. Primary-school pupils in England are around three months behind where they normally would be; children in Ethiopia learned 60-70% less than usual during 2020. Even before the pandemic things were bad. More than half of ten-year-olds in low- and middle-income countries could not read a simple paragraph. The World Bank warns this could rise to almost two-thirds. In all countries school closures will widen the gap between better-off pupils (who have iPads and quiet bedrooms for remote learning) and worse-off ones (who often don’t).

因此，年轻人的大脑缺乏刺激。英国小学生比正常水平落后3个月左右; 2020年，埃塞俄比亚儿童的学习能力比平时减少了60-70%。甚至在疫情之前，情况就很糟糕。在低收入和中等收入国家，超过一半的10岁儿童连一个简单的段落都看不懂。世界银行警告称，这一比例可能上升至近三分之二。在所有国家，关闭学校将扩大更富裕学生(拥有ipad和安静的卧室进行远程学习的学生)和更贫困学生(通常没有富裕学生拥有的条件)之间的差距。

However, the disruption caused by covid-19 also creates a chance to make schools better than they were before. So many pupils have so much ground to make up that educators are pondering the most effective ways to help them do so. Some rich countries are offering more tutoring for struggling students, individually or in small groups. Some poor countries are simplifying overstuffed curriculums, allowing teachers to deviate from government textbooks and spend more time teaching basic reading and maths. Such reforms seem to work.

overstuffed 塞得满满的

然而，covid-19造成的破坏也创造了一个机会，它使学校比以前更好。如此多的学生有如此多的基础需要弥补，因此教育工作者正在思考帮助他们做到这一点的最有效的方法。一些发达国家正在为学习困难的学生提供更多的个人或小组辅导。一些贫穷国家正在简化满满的课程表，允许教师脱离政府的教科书，花更多的时间教授基础阅读和数学。这些改革似乎奏效了。

crash course 强化速成课

humdrum 乏味的；单调的

Conditions at home matter even more when that is where lessons take place. The past year has shown the need for social workers to help deprived pupils: making sure they get glasses so they can read what’s on the screen, for example, or helping their parents with paperwork so that they are not evicted. Schools can offer mental-health counselling, too, and put pupils in touch with charities or agencies that help solve distracting domestic problems.

这一流行病突显了家庭背景如何影响学业成功。饱餐一顿，鼓励孩子的父母和一间有很多书的房子一直是一种优势。在家里上课的时候，家里的条件更重要。过去的一年表明社会工作者有必要帮助贫困学生:例如，确保他们有眼镜，这样他们就可以阅读屏幕上的内容，或者帮助他们的父母完成文书工作，这样他们就不会被驱逐。学校也可以提供心理健康咨询，让学生与帮助解决家庭问题的慈善机构或专门机构取得联系。

The UN has found that by last autumn only a quarter of children had access to some kind of remedial programme. Children who failed to grasp important lessons during lockdowns may continue to fall behind. By one estimate, a child in a poor country who misses a year of school and does not receive the right help to catch up can eventually trail by almost three years.

remedial 补习的

trail 落后

Next year England plans to spend only a bit more to help pupils catch up than it did in a single month last summer subsidising families to eat out in restaurants. Lawmakers in America, where children have missed more in-person schooling than almost anywhere else in the rich world, have been more generous. But only 20% of the extra money they are giving to schools must be spent on catch-up learning. Much will be devoted to pointless “sanitation theatre”—including plastic dividers between desks, which may make it harder to see the blackboard or hear the teacher.

divider 隔板

明年，英国计划仅比去年夏天的一个月（补贴那些外出就餐的家庭项目）多花一点钱来帮助学生提高学习成绩。美国的立法者们则更为慷慨，因为在美国，孩子们错过了比发达国家其他任何地方都多的亲自上学机会。但是他们给学校的额外资金中只有20%必须用于补习。许多工作将用于毫无意义的“卫生剧场”——包括课桌之间的塑料隔板，这可能会使人们更难看到黑板或听到老师讲课。

Two-thirds of poor countries have cut education spending. Money is not everything, but even in good times the poorest spend only $48 a year for each schoolchild, which is not enough. (Rich countries spend $8,500.) The UN predicts that foreign aid for education will fall by 12% between 2018 and 2022.

三分之二的贫穷国家削减了教育开支。钱不是万能的，但即使是在经济好的时候，最贫困的国家每年也只为每个学生花48美元，这是不够的(发达国家花费8500美元）联合国预测，在2018年至2022年期间，外国对教育的援助将下降12%。

Governments are often tempted to neglect education. Improving schools costs money, and may require confronting powerful interest groups, such as teachers’ unions. The benefits may not come until after today’s politicians have left office.

政府常常倾向于忽视教育。改善学校需要资金，而且可能需要对抗强大的利益集团，比如教师工会。这些好处可能要等到今天的政客们卸任后才会显现。

However, almost nothing matters more for a good life tomorrow than a good education today. At a minimum, governments should step up their efforts to repair the damage caused by school closures during the pandemic. It would be better still if they seized the opportunity to rewrite the rules for schools.

然而，对于明天的美好生活来说，几乎没有什么比今天的良好教育更重要。至少，各国政府应加紧努力，修复疫情期间学校关闭造成的损害。如果他们能抓住这个机会重写学校规则，那就更好了。

现在软银被另一个傲慢的局外人抢了风头。数据提供商Crunchbase的数据显示，在1月至5月间，同样投资于私营科技公司的纽约对冲基金老虎环球管理公司(Tiger Global Management)向118家初创公司投入了资金，企投资数量比它在2020年同期投入的资金多10倍。它的投资组合现在有400多家公司，包括一些落后于“去年一些最引人注目的IPO”的几家公司，比如加密货币交易所Coinbase和视频游戏制造商Roblox。而且，正如它在2月份告诉投资者的那样，它正在“寻找方法，让我们的投资飞轮转得更快”。该公司的新工具计划再融资100亿美元。这可能比不上软银1000亿美元的庞大愿景基金(Vision Fund)，但以风投的标准来看，这仍是一笔不小的数目。与这家资金雄厚的日本竞争对手相比，《纽约客》将会对这个硅谷公司印象更深刻持久。

Similarities between Tiger and SoftBank are easy to see. Both were backers of Alibaba, before the Chinese e-merchant went public and turned into a global giant. VC types commonly describe both firms as “aggressive”, even “crazy”. Once each identifies a target, it pounces; investment contracts are issued in days, skipping lengthy due diligence, often at valuations well above those suggested by conventional VCs. Just as SoftBank would occasionally sign ten-figure cheques when founders asked for eight or nine, Tiger Global sometimes talks entrepreneurs into taking cash when they do not need it. “Even after they have already invested they send text message after text message, asking whether they can put in more money,” says one founder recently backed by the firm.

pounce 猛扑；一下抓住

Tiger和SoftBank的相似之处显而易见。在中国电商阿里巴巴上市并成为全球巨头之前，这两家公司都是阿里巴巴的支持者。风投通常将这两家公司描述为“激进的”，甚至“疯狂的”。一旦认定一个目标，它就会一下抓住它; 投资合同在几天内就签发了，跳过了冗长的尽职调查，估值往往远高于传统风投的建议。就像软银偶尔会在创始人要求8或9位数的支票时候签下10位数的支票一样，老虎环球基金有时也会在创业者不需要现金时劝说他们接受。该公司最近支持的一位创始人表示:“即使他们已经投资了，他们也会不断地发送短信，询问是否可以投资更多的资金。”

Tiger Global abhors such comparisons. And it is indeed distinct from the Japanese group in important ways. SoftBank only got into tech investing in earnest a few years ago, having started out selling software, before moving into online services and telecoms. By contrast, Tiger Global has investing pedigree in spades. It is descended from Tiger Management, a hugely successful hedge fund founded by Julian Robertson, a Wall Street giant. It has been backing tech winners for nearly 20 years, both in China and, later, in America (with investments in, among others, Facebook). Over that period its funds have generated an average internal rate of return of 26% a year, twice that of comparable VC funds. Whereas Son Masayoshi, SoftBank’s messianic boss, calls all the shots at his firm, Tiger Global is no one-man show. And its partners eschew Mr Son’s embrace of individual founders based on a gut feeling in favour of a disciplined strategy centred on collecting a basket of firms in promising markets.

abhor 憎恶

start out 开始起步

pedigree 出身；起源

in spades 大量的

messianic 救世主的；救星似的

a gut feeling 直觉

老虎国际厌恶这种比较。它确实在一些重要方面与软银这个日本集团截然不同。软银几年前才开始认真地进行科技投资，最初是从销售软件起步，后来才进入在线服务和电信领域。相比之下，老虎环球投资集团有着丰富的投资经验。老虎管理公司(Tiger Management)是由华尔街巨头Julian Robertson创立的一家非常成功的对冲基金公司。近20年来，无论是在中国，还是后来在美国，它一直在支持科技行业的赢家(其中包括投资于Facebook)。在此期间，它的基金产生了平均每年26%的内部收益率，是类似风投基金的两倍。尽管软银的救世主般的老板孙正义在他的公司里发号施令，老虎环球却不是一个人的表演。而它的合作伙伴基于直觉，避开了孙正义对个人创始人的拥抱，他们倾向于在有前景的市场上集中收集一篮子公司的有纪律的战略。

There is another difference. Whereas the arrival of Mr Son left denizens of Sand Hill Road in Palo Alto, where Silicon Valley VCs cluster, quaking in their Allbirds, they appear remarkably unfazed by Tiger’s presence. Despite competing with Tiger Global for early-stage investments, many VCs consider it a force for good: a source of capital that helps their portfolio companies grow faster or start projects they may otherwise have forgone. Yet even if the New York firm follows SoftBank’s trajectory and pulls back, which could happen if interest rates rise, capital grows scarcer and the tech rally fizzles, three factors that have contributed to its success are here to stay.

quake 颤抖

to be unfazed by sth 不因某事物慌张

还有一个不同之处。孙正义的到来让硅谷风投云集的帕洛阿尔托Sand Hill Road的居民们浑身颤抖，但他们似乎对老虎的出现毫不畏惧。尽管与Tiger Global争夺早期投资，但许多风投认为这是一种有利的力量:这样一种资本来源，可以帮助他们投资的公司更快地成长，或启动他们可能已经放弃的项目。然而，即使这家纽约公司效仿软银的轨迹并撤出(如果利率上升、资本变得稀缺、科技股反弹失败的话)，推动其成功的三个因素仍将继续存在。

The first is the acceleration of dealmaking. Before the covid-19 pandemic, negotiations happened mostly in person, limiting the number of encounters. Meeting on Zoom and other video-conferencing platforms takes only a few clicks, allowing both founders and investors to talk to many more potential partners. In Silicon Valley, hardly a place known for foot-dragging, the common refrain these days is that “things have never moved faster.” Keeping up with Tiger Global and its fellow New Yorkers such as Coatue Management and Insight Partners is an important reason.

refrain 一再重复的话

首先是交易的加速。在新冠肺炎大流行之前，谈判主要是面对面进行的，限制了会见的次数。在Zoom和其他视频会议平台上开会只需要点击几下鼠标，就可以让创始人和投资者与更多潜在的合作伙伴交谈。在硅谷，这个几乎不以拖沓著称的地方，如今人们常说的是“事情从来没有这么快过。”一个重要的原因是，要跟上老虎环球及其纽约同行的步伐，比如Coatue Management和Insight Partners。

Second, Tiger Global has tried to be more systematic in evaluating startups. Although the firm never asks for board seats, considering it a waste of time, it knows plenty about its investments, thanks to a growing array of ever better metrics with which to judge companies’ performance. It has also created its own early-warning network to identify promising targets. If a new online service takes off in one region, for instance, it may be time to put money in a similar firm in another location. Many VC firms could learn a thing or two from this approach. “We are a bunch of horrible investors,” grimaces another veteran venture capitalist. “More than half of us don’t even return capital.” This recognition is music to the ears of their put-upon limited partners.

grimace 脸部扭曲；做鬼脸

put-upon 被利用的

其次，老虎环球基金在评估初创公司时试图更加系统化。尽管该公司从不要求获得董事会席位，认为这是浪费时间，但由于越来越多更好的衡量公司业绩的指标，该公司对自己的投资了如指掌。它还建立了自己的预警网络来识别有前景的目标。例如，如果一个新的在线服务在一个地区启动，可能是时候把钱投到另一个地方的类似公司。许多风投公司可以从这种方法中学到一些东西。“我们是一群可怕的投资者，”另一位资深风险投资家苦笑着说。“我们中超过一半的人甚至都没有资本收益。”这种认识对于受其欺瞒的有限合伙人来说，就像音乐一般悦耳。

What the hand, dare seize the fire?

什么样的手敢去抓这火焰？

Tiger Global’s final impact may be the most profound. It reflects a shift in the balance of power between investors and entrepreneurs. Traditionally, investors had the upper hand. Startup founders pilgrimaged to Sand Hill Road, seeking not just money but valuable advice that the best VCs would provide. Competition from Tiger Global and other tourists has forced Californian VCs to offer more generous terms, monetary and otherwise. That in turn has made entrepreneurs themselves more confident. “It’s no fun to be an investor these days,” sums up the boss of a startup preparing to go public. The question for moneymen in Silicon Valley (which remains overwhelmingly male) is less what startup to back and more whether a startup lets you invest. Quite the paw print. ■

老虎环球基金的最终影响可能是最深远的。这反映了投资者和企业家之间权力平衡的转变。传统上，投资者占了上风。初创公司的创始人们纷纷来到Sand Hill Road，寻求的不仅仅是资金，还有最好的风投会提供的有价值的建议。来自老虎环球和其他游客的竞争迫使加州的风投公司提供更慷慨的条款，金钱以及其他方面。这反过来又让企业家自己更加自信。一家准备上市的初创公司的老板这样总结道:“如今当投资者一点都不好玩。”硅谷的投资者(绝大多数都是男性)面临的问题不是投资什么初创公司，而更多的是初创公司是否允许你投资。毫无无疑是老虎的爪印。■

The promise of the African genome project

非洲基因组计划的前景

Where human life began

人类生命的起源

![image-20210627121543005](/Users/chenyujie/Library/Application Support/typora-user-images/image-20210627121543005.png)

A Cameroonian professor hopes to illuminate human genetic diversity

一位喀麦隆教授希望阐明人类基因的多样性

Jun 26th 2021

WHEN THE Mutambaras’ first son was a about 18 months old they began to worry about his hearing. The toddler did not respond when asked to “come to Mama”. He was soon diagnosed as deaf, though no doctor could tell the Zimbabwean couple the cause. Several years later their second son was also born deaf.

toddler 学步的儿童

当Mutambaras的大儿子18个月大的时候，他们开始担心他的听力。当被要求“到妈妈那里去”时，这个蹒跚学步的孩子没有回应。他很快被诊断为耳聋，然而没有医生能告诉这对津巴布韦夫妇原因。几年后，他们的第二个儿子也天生就听不见。

This time a doctor referred them to Hearing Impairment Genetics Studies in Africa (HI-GENES), set up in 2018 by Ambroise Wonkam, a Cameroonian professor of genetics now at the University of Cape Town. The project is sequencing the genomes of Africans with hearing loss in seven countries to learn why six babies in every 1,000 are born deaf in Africa, a rate six times that in America. In Cape Town, where Mr and Mrs Mutambara (not their real names) live, a counsellor explained that the boys’ deafness is caused by genetic variants rarely found outside Africa.

这一次，一名医生建议他们去看非洲听力障碍遗传学研究(HI-GENES)，该研究是喀麦隆遗传学教授、现任开普敦大学的Ambroise Wonkam于2018年成立的。该项目正在对7个国家的失聪非洲人的基因组进行测序，以了解为什么在非洲每1000个婴儿中就有6个生来就是失聪的，这一比率是美国的6倍。一名顾问解释说，在Mutambara夫妇(化名)居住的开普敦，这对男孩的失聪是由非洲以外很少发现的基因变异引起的。

What is true of deafness is true of other conditions. The 3bn pairs of nucleotide bases that make up human DNA were first fully mapped in 2003 by the Human Genome Project. Since then scientists have made publicly available the sequencing of around 1m genomes as part of an effort to refine the “reference genome”, a blueprint used by researchers. But less than 2% of all sequenced genomes are African, though Africans are 17% of the world’s population (see chart). “We must fill the gap,” argues Dr Wonkam, who has proposed an initiative to do just that—Three Million African Genomes (3MAG).

耳聋是真的，其他情况也是真的。2003年，人类基因组计划首次对构成人类DNA的30亿对核苷酸碱基对进行了完全绘制。自那以后，科学家们公开了大约100万个基因组的测序，作为完善“参考基因组”（研究人员使用的蓝图）的成果的一部分。尽管非洲人占世界人口的17%(见图表)，但所有已测序的基因组中只有不到2%是非洲人(见图表)。Wonkam博士说，“我们必须填补这一空白”，他提出了一个计划，300万个非洲基因组(3MAG)。

![image-20210627121504714](/Users/chenyujie/Library/Application Support/typora-user-images/image-20210627121504714.png)

The evolutionary line leading to Homo sapiens diverged 5m-6m years ago from that leading to chimpanzees, and for almost all that time the ancestors of modern humans lived in Africa.

Homo sapiens 智人；人类

5百万至6百万年前，智人的进化路线与黑猩猩的进化路线分道扬镳，而且几乎所有的时间里，现代人类的祖先都生活在非洲。

Only about 60,000 years ago did Homo sapiens venture widely beyond the continent, in small bands of adventurers. Most of humanity’s genetic diversity, under-sampled though it is, is therefore found in Africa. Unfortunately, that diversity is also reflected in the greater variety of genetic illnesses found there.

仅仅在大约60000年前，智人就在欧洲大陆以外的地方，以一小群冒险家的形式冒险。因此，大多数人类的基因多样性，尽管取样不足，是在非洲被发现。不幸的是，这种多样性也反映在那里发现的更多种类的遗传疾病上。

The bias in sequencing leads to under-diagnosis of diseases in people of (relatively recent) African descent. Genetic causes of heart failure, such as the one that caused the ultimately fatal collapse of Marc-Vivien Foé, a Cameroonian football player, during a game in 2003, are poorly understood. The variation present in most non-Africans with cystic fibrosis is responsible for only about 30% of cases in people of African origin. This is one reason, along with its relative rarity, that the illness is often missed in black children. Standard genetic tests for hearing loss would not have picked up the Mutambara boys’ variations. And such is the diversity within the continent that tests in some countries would be irrelevant in others. In Ghana HI-GENES found one mutation responsible for 40% of inherited deafness. The same variation has not been found in South Africa.

cystic fibrosis 囊肿性纤维

测序的偏差导致(相对较新的)非洲人后裔的疾病诊断不足。人们对心脏衰竭的遗传原因知之甚少，比如导致喀麦隆足球运动员Marc-Vivien Foé在2003年的一场比赛中最终致命的心脏衰竭的原因。在大多数非非洲人囊性纤维化患者中，这种变异仅占非洲裔患者的30%。这就是这种疾病在黑人儿童中很少被发现的原因之一。对于听力损失的标准基因测试并不能检测出Mutambara男孩的变异。正是非洲大陆的多样性使得一些国家的检测在其他国家是无关紧要的。在加纳，HI-GENES发现了一种导致40%遗传性耳聋的突变。在南非没有发现同样的变异。

Bias also means that little is known about how variations elsewhere in the genome modify conditions. With sickle-cell disease, red blood cells look like bananas rather than, as is normal, round cushions. About 75% of the 300,000 babies born every year with sickle-cell disease are African. The high share reflects a bittersweet twist in the evolutionary tale; sickle-cell genes can confer a degree of protection against malaria. Other mutations are known to lessen sickle-cell’s impact, but most knowledge of genetic modifiers is particular to Europeans.

sickle 镰刀

bittersweet 苦乐参半的；又苦又甜的

这样的偏差还意味着人们对基因组其他部位的变异如何改变健康状况知之甚少。镰状细胞病患者的红细胞看起来像香蕉，而不是像正常情况下的圆形垫子。每年出生的30万名患有镰状细胞病的婴儿中，大约75%来自非洲。较高的比例反映了进化中苦乐参半的曲折;镰状细胞基因可以提供一定程度的抗疟疾保护。其他的基因突变可以减轻镰状细胞的影响，但是大多数关于基因修饰的知识是欧洲人特有的。

Quicker and more accurate diagnosis would mean better treatment. The sooner parents know their children are deaf, the sooner they can begin sign language. Algorithms that incorporate genetic information, such as one for measuring doses of warfarin, a blood-thinner, are often inappropriately calibrated for Africans.

更快更准确的诊断意味着更好的治疗。父母越早知道他们的孩子是聋的，他们就能越早开始学习手语。那些包含遗传信息的算法，比如用于测量血液稀释剂华法林剂量的算法，往往不适合非洲人。

Knowing more about Africans’ genomes will benefit the whole world. The continent’s genetic diversity makes it easier to find rare causes of common diseases. Last year researchers investigating schizophrenia sequenced the genomes of about 900 Xhosas (a South African ethnic group) with the psychiatric disorder. They found some of the same mutations that a team had discovered in Swedes four years earlier. But those researchers had to analyse four times as many of the homogeneous Scandinavians to find it. Research by Olufunmilayo Olopade, a Nigerian-born oncologist, into why breast cancer is relatively common in Nigerian women, has revealed broad insights into tumour growth.

schizophrenia 精神分裂症

更多地了解非洲人的基因组将使整个世界受益。非洲大陆的基因多样性使得发现常见疾病的罕见原因变得更容易。去年，研究人员对大约900名患有精神疾病的科萨人(一个南非种族)的基因组进行了测序。他们发现了一些与四年前瑞典研究小组发现的相同的突变。但这些研究人员分析了四倍多的同种族的斯堪的纳维亚人才能找到它。出生于尼日利亚的肿瘤学家Olufunmilayo Olopade研究了乳腺癌在尼日利亚女性中相对常见的原因，其研究也揭示了对肿瘤生长的广泛见解。

Dr Wonkam’s vision for 3MAG, as outlined in Nature, a scientific journal, is for 300,000 African genomes to be sequenced per year over a decade. That is the minimum needed to capture the continent’s diversity. He notes that the UK biobank is sequencing 500,000 genomes, though Britain’s population is a twentieth the size of Africa’s. The plummeting cost of technology makes 3MAG possible. Sequencing the first genome cost $300m; today the cost of sequencing is around $1,000. If data from people of African descent in similar projects, like the UK biobank, were shared with 3MAG, that would help. So too would collaboration with genetics firms, such as 54Gene, a Nigerian start-up.

科学期刊《Nature》概述了Wonkam博士对3MAG的设想，即在未来十年内每年对30万个非洲基因组进行测序。这是捕捉非洲大陆多样性所需的最低限度。他指出，英国生物样本库正在测序50万个基因组，而英国的人口规模只有非洲的二十分之一。技术成本的急剧下降使3MAG成为可能。测序第一个基因组花费了3亿美元;如今测序的成本约为1000美元。如果类似项目中来自非洲人后裔的数据(比如英国生物样本库)与3MAG共享，将会有所帮助。与基因公司的合作也是如此，比如尼日利亚初创公司54Gene。

The 3MAG project is building on firm foundations. Over the past decade the Human Heredity and Health in Africa consortium, sponsored by America’s National Institutes of Health and the Wellcome Trust, a British charity, has supported research institutes in 30 African countries. It has funded local laboratories for world-class scientists such as Dr Wonkam and Christian Happi, a Nigerian geneticist.

3MAG项目有坚实的基础。在过去的十年里，由美国国立卫生研究院和英国慈善机构维康信托基金赞助的非洲人类遗传与健康协会资助了30个非洲国家的研究机构。它为世界一流科学家如Wonkam博士和尼日利亚遗传学家Christian Happi等所在的当地的实验室提供资金。

There are practical issues to iron out. One is figuring out how to store the vast amounts of data. Another is rules around consent and data use, especially if 3MAG will involve firms understandably keen to commercialise the findings. Dr Wonkam wants to see an ethics committee set up to review this and other matters.

iron out 解决；消除

有一些实际问题需要解决。一个问题是如何存储大量的数据。另一个是关于同意和数据使用的规则，特别是如果3MAG会让热衷于将研究成果商业化的公司参与进来，这是可以理解的。Wonkam博士希望成立一个伦理委员会来审查这一问题和其他问题。

At times he has wondered whether his plan is “too big, too crazy and too expensive”. But similar things were said about the Human Genome Project. Its researchers used the Rosetta Stone as a metaphor for the initiative and its ambition. In a subtle nod, Dr Wonkam has a miniature of the obelisk on a shelf in his office. It is also a reminder of how understanding African languages, whether spoken or genetic, can enlighten all of humanity. ■

obelisk 方尖纪念碑

背景：罗塞塔石碑（Rosetta Stone，也译作罗塞达碑），高1.14米，宽0.73米，制作于公元前196年，刻有古埃及国王托勒密五世登基的诏书。石碑上用希腊文字、古埃及文字和当时的通俗体文字刻了同样的内容，这使得近代的考古学家得以有机会对照各语言版本的内容后，解读出已经失传千余年的埃及象形文之意义与结构，而成为今日研究古埃及历史的重要里程碑。

有时他会怀疑自己的计划是否“太大、太疯狂、太昂贵”。但类似的事情也发生在人类基因组计划上。研究人员用罗塞塔石碑来比喻这项计划及其雄心壮志。Wonkam博士在他办公室的架子上有一个方尖碑的微型模型，他微微点了点头。它也提醒我们，理解非洲语言，无论是口语还是基因语言，可以启发全人类。■

Britain’s uneasy relationship with the EU will get worse before it gets better

英国与欧盟不稳定的关系在好转之前将会变得更糟

Jun 17th 2021

JUNE 23RD marks the fifth anniversary of the Brexit referendum. Even the keenest Brexiteer must feel that the process has been tortuously long. That has been, in large part, because successive British governments have refused to accept the trade-off between untrammelled sovereignty and friction-free access to the EU’s single market, a refusal that shapes today’s increasingly testy relationship.

tortuously 弯弯曲曲地

untrammelled 不受限制的；自由自在的

6月23日是英国脱欧公投五周年纪念日。即使是最渴望的脱欧派也一定会觉得，这个过程漫长得令人痛苦。这在很大程度上是因为，英国历届政府都拒绝接受在“不受限制的主权”和“无障碍进入欧盟单一市场”之间进行取舍，这种拒绝决定了如今日益紧张的关系。

Almost six months after Britain finally left the single market, the impact on the economy is hard to unpick from that of covid-19**. Yet the effects of new barriers to trade are clear.** The Trade Policy Observatory at Sussex University reckons that in the first quarter of 2021 British goods exports to the EU fell by 15%, while EU exports to Britain fell by 32%. The Office for National Statistics finds similarly that total EU-UK trade has shrunk by 20%. And a model constructed by John Springford of the Centre for European Reform, a think-tank with offices in both London and Brussels, concludes that goods trade is 11% lower than it would otherwise have been, on top of an earlier 10% fall since the referendum.

在英国最终脱离单一市场近6个月后，新冠肺炎对英国经济的影响难以消除。然而，新的贸易壁垒的影响是显而易见的。萨塞克斯大学贸易政策观察组织估计，2021年第一季度英国对欧盟的出口下降了15%，而欧盟对英国的出口下降了32%。英国国家统计局也发现，欧盟与英国的贸易总额缩水了20%。欧洲改革中心(一个在伦敦和布鲁塞尔都设有办事处的智囊团)的John Springford建立了一个模型，它得出的结论是，自公投以来，除了早期商品贸易下降了10%，现在的商品贸易比原本应该下降的水平还要低11%。

Such numbers suggest that what was once dismissed as Project Fear is now Project Fact. A survey by consultants at EY found that 75% of firms had experienced business disruption from Brexit. Small firms found adjusting particularly hard because the EU-UK trade deal was struck just a week before exit, meaning there was no transition period. Services have been particularly hard hit. A study by researchers at Aston University concluded that, in the first four years after the referendum, British exports of services were more than £110bn ($155bn) lower than they would have been without the Brexit vote. And a report by New Financial, a London-based think-tank, found 440 financial-service firms that were moving part of their business and some staff, along with a shift of over £900bn of bank assets (10% of the total) to the EU.

Project Fear：Project Fear is also the name given to the Brexit remain propaganda

[释义] a name given to any political campaign that seeks to arouse public alarm about proposed changes to the status quo

这些数字表明，曾经被视为“Project Fear”的项目现在变成了“Project Fact”。安永咨询公司的顾问进行的一项调查发现，75%的公司因英国脱欧而经历了业务中断。小公司发现，调整尤为困难，因为欧盟与英国的贸易协议是在脱欧前一周达成的，这意味着没有过渡期。服务业受到的打击尤其严重。阿斯顿大学研究人员的一项研究得出结论称，在脱欧公投后的头四年，英国的服务出口比没有脱欧公投的情况下减少了逾1100亿英镑(合1550亿美元)。总部位于伦敦的智囊团New Financial的一份报告发现，440家金融服务公司正在转移部分业务和部分员工，同时还将超过9000亿英镑的银行资产(占总资产的10%)转移到欧盟。

The economy’s bounceback and Britain’s successful vaccine roll-out have disguised much of the impact. João Vale de Almeida, the EU’s ambassador to Britain, says the most immediate negative effects of Brexit are loss of mobility and damage to the union. Musicians and others in creative industries are complaining loudly about the first, and employers in hospitality also bemoan shortages of EU workers. As for the second, Brexit has clearly boosted the long-term chances of independence for Scotland, which voted strongly to remain in the EU. But the bigger worry is Northern Ireland.

bemoan 哀叹

经济的反弹和英国疫苗的成功推出掩盖了大部分影响。欧盟驻英国大使João Vale de Almeida表示，英国脱欧最直接的负面影响是流动性的丧失和对欧盟的损害。音乐家和其他创作行业的人都在大声抱怨前者，酒店业的雇主也在抱怨欧盟工人的短缺。至于第二个问题，英国脱欧显然提高了苏格兰长期独立的可能性，苏格兰强烈支持留在欧盟。但更令人担忧的是北爱尔兰。

Accommodating its needs was always going to be devilishly difficult, because its border with Ireland is Britain’s only land border with the EU. All sides agree that a “hard” north-south border—one with physical infrastructure—could threaten the fragile peace. Theresa May’s failed answer was to avoid any border or customs checks with a “backstop” in the protocol that would have kept the entire UK in a customs union and the single market for goods until alternative arrangements were possible. But Mr Johnson came to power having pledged to get rid of the backstop by taking Great Britain out of the single market and the customs union, leaving Northern Ireland alone in both. And although he has often pretended otherwise, that made an east-west customs and regulatory border in the Irish Sea unavoidable.

devilishly 骇人听闻地

backstop 后盾

满足它的需求总是极其困难的，因为它与爱尔兰的边界是英国与欧盟唯一的陆地边界。各方一致认为一个拥有实体基础设施的“硬的”南北边界可能会威胁脆弱的和平。Theresa May的失败回答是，在协议中用一个“后盾”以避免任何边境或海关检查，这个后盾将让整个英国留在一个关税联盟和单一商品市场，直到其他可能的替代协议出现。但约翰逊上台时曾承诺，要让英国退出单一市场和关税同盟，从而让北爱尔兰独自留在单一市场和关税同盟中，从而摆脱“后盾”。尽管他经常假装不同意的样子，但这使得爱尔兰海的东西海关和监管边界不可避免。

Just as inevitable were unionist objections. When supermarket supplies were disrupted straight after Brexit, loyalist youths took to the streets to protest. Unionist hardliners even claimed that, just as the decision to avoid a hard north-south border was motivated by fears of nationalist violence, so the threat of loyalist violence might now get rid of border controls in the Irish Sea. The new Democratic Unionist Party (DUP) leader, Edwin Poots, calls for the protocol to be scrapped. Even if it stays, in 2024 the Northern Irish assembly could vote to end its application. A simple majority would suffice.

loyalist 支持大不列颠和北爱尔兰联合的人

统一派的反对也是不可避免的。当英国脱欧后超市供应直接中断时，支持大不列颠和北爱尔兰联合的年轻人走上街头抗议。统一派主张强硬路线者甚至声称，正如“避免出现一个南北硬边界”的决定是出于对民族主义暴力的恐惧一样，支持大不列颠和北爱尔兰联合的人的暴力威胁现在可能会使爱尔兰海的边境控制解除。新的民主统一党(DUP)领导人Edwin Poots呼吁废除该协议。即使这个协议继续存在，在2024年北爱尔兰议会可以投票结束其实施。一个简单的多数票就足够了。

Arguments over implementation have been stirred by unilateral actions on both sides. The British government first proposed to breach international law in its internal-market bill last autumn, but dropped this plan when it struck a trade deal with the EU. In January the EU threatened to suspend the protocol unilaterally in a bid to stop vaccine exports from the bloc, though it backed down within hours. Mr Johnson has since broken the treaty by extending a grace period for the import of supermarket goods. Now he promises to do the same for the grace period for chilled-meat imports that expires at the end of June. The EU threatens to respond with not just legal action but tariffs, too, as permitted by the terms of the trade deal.

back down 放弃; 取消

双方的单边行动引发了关于实施的争论。去年秋天，英国政府首次提出在其内部市场法案中违反国际法，但在与欧盟达成贸易协议后放弃了这一计划。今年1月，欧盟曾威胁要单方面中止该协议，以阻止欧盟的疫苗出口，尽管它在几小时内就做出了让步。此后，约翰逊违反了该条约，延长了超市商品进口的宽限期。现在他承诺对6月底到期的冷冻肉进口宽限期采取同样的措施。作为回应，欧盟威胁不仅要采取法律行动，还会按照贸易协议条款的规定征收关税。

The prospect of a trade war over sausages may delight the pro-Brexit press, which is itching for a fight with the bully on the other side of the channel. It is unpopular, however, with Northern Irish businesses. And getting rid of the protocol would not be straightforward. The assembly election due within 12 months may see Sinn Fein, the main Irish-nationalist party, take the most seats, making it harder to win a majority in favour of removing the protocol. Katy Hayward of Queen’s University and UK in a Changing Europe, a think-tank, points out that voting to remove its trade clauses would merely trigger a negotiation to find some other option. And as Claire Hanna, a Social Democratic and Labour Party MP, observes, the DUP has not suggested any alternative.

背景：在英国正式“脱欧”后，与欧盟的关系进一步恶化。近日，双方都指责对方没有完全遵守“脱欧”协议中有关向英国北爱尔兰运送货物的规定，其中影响最大的便是针对香肠等冷冻肉制品的运输问题。之后双方的谈判以破裂告终，英国政府威胁不经任何检查将英国肉类运往北爱尔兰，而欧盟威胁要对英国贸易实行全面封锁，至此双方之间的贸易危机加深了，一场“香肠贸易战”一触即发。

就商品而言，北爱尔兰实际上仍是欧洲单一市场的成员，但这意味着需要对从英国其他地区进入该地区的商品进行检查，英国脱欧后，这些地区不在单一市场之内。约翰逊反复承诺，在他同意的这个协议下，不会对爱尔兰海上边界或对英国与北爱尔兰之间的贸易进行检查。但约翰逊表示，欧盟到目前为止在审查方面的要求过于“纯粹”和“过于繁重”，声称这些要求对北爱尔兰造成了不公平的伤害。弗罗斯特威胁要单方面暂停检查，包括对香肠等冷冻肉制品的检查。作为回应，欧盟威胁要对英国贸易征收惩罚性关税，要与英国打“香肠贸易战”（sausage trade war），除非约翰逊恰当地实施他谈判达成的协议。

围绕香肠爆发贸易战的前景可能会让支持英国脱欧的媒体感到高兴，他们渴望与海峡另一边的恶霸开战。然而，它却不受北爱尔兰企业的欢迎。而且取消协议也不是一件容易的事。在12个月内举行的议会选举中，爱尔兰主要的民族主义政党新芬党(Sinn Fein)可能会获得最多席位，这使得赢得赞成废除该协议的多数票变得更加困难。皇后大学和英国智囊团“变化中的欧洲”的Katy Hayward指出，投票取消其贸易条款只会引发一场“寻找其他选择”的谈判。正如社会民主党和工党议员Claire Hanna所观察到的那样，民主统一党并没有提出任何替代方案。

Aodhan Connolly of the Northern Ireland Retail Consortium offers one possible solution. Instead of what he calls diplomacy by rival op-eds, he suggests a temporary commitment by the British government to stick with EU food-safety and veterinary standards. That would cut the number of border and customs checks required under the protocol by 80%, he says. The EU would be happy to agree to such a temporary measure, as would Mr Poots and the DUP.

veterinary 兽医的

北爱尔兰零售协会的Aodhan Connolly提供了一个可能的解决方案。他建议英国政府暂时承诺遵守欧盟的食品安全和兽医标准，而不是他所谓的用竞争对手的专栏文章来外交。他说，这将减少协议规定的边境和海关检查数量的80%。欧盟会很乐意同意这样的临时措施，普茨和民主统一党也会乐意。

Mr Johnson, however, is not keen on any alignment with EU regulations. This may seem odd, since he also insists he has no intention of weakening food-safety standards. Other neighbours of the EU, such as Norway and Switzerland, are happy to abide by its food rules in order to avert trade barriers**. A more substantial objection is that** such a policy would make it harder to negotiate a free-trade deal with America, which always demands better access for its farmers. But at the G7 summit in Cornwall between June 11th and 13th, President Joe Biden, anxious to avert renewed trouble in Northern Ireland or any risk of an intra-European trade war, promised that a trade deal would not be blocked by British alignment to EU food standards. And such a deal would, in any case, take years to negotiate.

然而，约翰逊并不热衷于与欧盟法规保持一致。这似乎有些奇怪，因为他也坚称自己无意削弱食品安全标准。其他欧盟邻国，如挪威和瑞士，为避免贸易壁垒，很乐意遵守欧盟的食品规则。一个更大的反对意见是，这样的政策将使与美国谈判自由贸易协定变得更加困难，因为美国总是要求其农民获得更好的准入。但6月11日至13日在 Cornwall举行的七国集团峰会上，美国总统拜登急于避免北爱尔兰再次出现麻烦或欧洲内部贸易战的任何风险，他承诺贸易协议不会因英国与欧盟食品标准保持一致而受阻。无论如何，这样的协议需要数年的谈判时间。

The biggest obstacle to a pragmatic solution is lack of trust. The EU believes Mr Johnson signed the protocol intending not to stick to it. Mr Johnson thinks that some within the bloc want to drag Britain back into its regulatory orbit. Most worrying is that both sides have something to gain from playing tough: the EU by demonstrating that those who sign deals with it must stick to what they agreed; Mr Johnson by standing up to an overweening neighbour. Diplomats on both sides expect the relationship to worsen. Sadly, Northern Ireland may be the fall guy. ■

overweening 自负的

fall guy 替罪羊

实际解决方案的最大障碍是缺乏信任。欧盟认为，约翰逊签署协议的意图是不遵守协议。约翰逊认为，欧盟内部有些人希望把英国拉回其监管轨道。最令人担忧的是，双方都能从强硬中获益:欧盟通过展示与它签署协议的国家必须坚持他们达成的协议;约翰逊先生敢于面对傲慢的邻居。双方的外交官都预计两国关系将会恶化。可悲的是，北爱尔兰可能是替罪羊。■
