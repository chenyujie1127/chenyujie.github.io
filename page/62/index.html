##### Microchips were a boom-and-bust industry even before covid-19

boom-bust 经济大起大落的

在covid-19之前，微芯片行业就是一个兴衰交替的行业

May 22nd 2021

FOR WANT of a chip, the factory was lost. On May 18th Toyota became the latest carmaker forced to cut production amid a global shortage of microchips, announcing it would suspend work at two of its plants in Japan. Firms including Ford, General Motors and Jaguar Land Rover have also had to send workers home.

for want of sth 由于缺乏某物

由于缺少一块芯片，工厂遭受损失。5月18日，丰田宣布将暂停其在日本的两家工厂的生产工作，成为最新一家因全球微芯片短缺而被迫减产的汽车制造商。包括福特、通用汽车和捷豹路虎在内的公司也不得不让员工回家。

The pain is not confined to the car industry, for the shortage spans all sorts of chips, from the expensive, high-tech devices that power smartphones and data centres to the simple sensors and microcontrollers that have become a vital commodity, scattered across everything from cars to washing machines, and often costing just a few cents each. In the past few weeks companies including Foxconn, Nintendo and Samsung have warned of hits to production, affecting everything from smartphones and games consoles to televisions and home broadband routers.

broadband routers 宽带路由器

痛苦并不局限于汽车行业, 短缺的现象跨越各类芯片,从为智能手机和数据中心供电的昂贵高科技设备，到已成为重要商品的简单传感器和微控制器，它们散落到各种商品中，从汽车到洗衣机，无所不包，而且每种芯片的成本往往只有几美分。过去几周，富士康(Foxconn)、任天堂(Nintendo)和三星(Samsung)等公司都警告称，生产将受到影响，从智能手机、游戏机到电视和家庭宽带路由器，一切都将受到影响。

Governments are worried. America’s called a summit in April; another is due on May 20th. Germany’s finance minister has written to the government of Taiwan, where many chipmakers are based, lobbying for priority for carmakers. A report from Gavekal Research, a consultancy, published on May 4th, said the shortage might soon hit export performance in several East Asian economies. But there is little that ministers can do. The chip drought is the result of the covid-19 pandemic interacting with an industry that is notoriously prone to cycles of boom and bust. It is likely to persist for months, if not years.

各国政府对此忧心忡忡。美国在四月召开峰会;另一个会议日期是5月20日。德国财政部长已致函台湾，游说台湾优先考虑汽车制造商。台湾有许多芯片制造商。咨询公司Gavekal Research 5月4日发布的一份报告称，短缺可能很快会影响几个东亚经济体的出口表现。但是财政部长们无能为力。芯片“旱灾”是covid-19大流行与一个众所周知容易出现繁荣和萧条周期的行业相互作用的结果。这种情况可能会持续数月，甚至数年。

“The most important thing [to recognise]”, says Malcolm Penn, who runs Future Horizons, a chip-industry consultancy, “is that shortages are a natural part of the industry.” Chipmaking, he says, is a good example of what economists call a “pork-cycle” business, named for the regular swings between under- and over-supply first analysed in American pork markets in the 1920s. As with pigs, the supply of chips cannot quickly react to changes in demand. Capacity was tight even before the pandemic, says Mr Penn, pointing out that investment by chipmakers in factory equipment has been below its long-term average for many years (see chart).

经营芯片行业咨询公司Future Horizons的Malcolm Penn表示:“(需要认识到的)最重要的事情是，短缺是该行业的一个自然组成部分。”他说，芯片制造是经济学家所谓的“猪肉周期”的一个很好的例子。“猪肉周期”指的是20世纪20年代首次在美国猪肉市场分析的供给不足和供应过剩之间的规律性波动。和猪的供应一样，芯片的供应不能迅速对需求的变化做出反应。Penn先生指出，即使在流感大流行之前，产能就比较紧张，芯片制造商对工厂设备的投资也低于多年来的长期平均水平(见图表)。

The pandemic thus arrived at the worst possible time. After an early crash, demand in several segments boomed, says Alan Priestley of Gartner, another consultancy. Locked-down consumers bought laptops and other gadgets. Cloud-computing operators, which are big consumers of high-end chips, scrambled to add servers to deal with the wave of home-workers. The car industry was particularly badly hit by a decision to cut orders early in the pandemic. Demand for cars has since recovered. But the complexities of the production process means it takes three to four months to turn a blank silicon wafer into a usable batch of chips. “I can cancel my orders in an afternoon,” says Mr Penn. “If I want to start them up again, that takes months—and anyway, that capacity is now busy serving other customers.”

wafer 晶片

因此，大流行来得正是最糟糕的时候。另一家咨询公司Gartner的Alan Priestley表示，在早期的崩溃之后，几个领域的需求都出现了增长。被锁在家里的消费者购买了笔记本电脑和其他电子产品。云计算运营商是高端芯片的大消费者，他们争相增加服务器以应对在家办公的浪潮。汽车行业受到的打击尤其严重，因为该行业在疫情初期就决定削减订单。此后，对汽车的需求已经恢复。但是复杂的生产过程意味着将一块空白的硅晶片变成一批可用的芯片需要三到四个月的时间。Penn表示:“我可以在一个下午取消订单。”“如果我想重新启动它们，那需要几个月的时间，而且不管怎样，这些产能现在都在忙着为其他客户服务。”

The impact of the pandemic, in turn, has been made worse by industry-specific problems. In March a chip plant owned by Renesas, a Japanese firm, caught fire, piling pain on the car industry. Some chipmakers, meanwhile, face shortages of their own. Many cheap, workaday parts are made in older factories designed to process silicon wafers that are 200mm in diameter, or even smaller. (These days 300mm is the standard.) Efforts to boost capacity are stymied by the fact that few toolmakers still make the old-style machines, says Mr Priestley. The market for second-hand 200mm tools, meanwhile, has been stripped bare.

stymie 阻碍

strip 使空无一物

这一流行病的影响又因特定行业的问题而变得更加严重。今年3月，日本Renesas公司旗下的一家芯片工厂起火，给汽车行业带来了痛苦。与此同时，一些芯片制造商也面临着自身芯片的短缺。许多廉价的普通部件都是在旧工厂里制造的，这些工厂的设计目的是加工直径200毫米甚至更小的硅晶圆。(目前标准是300mm)。Priestley说，提高产能的努力受到了一个事实的阻碍，那就是仍在制造老式机器的工具制造商寥寥无几。与此同时，二手200mm工具市场已被洗劫一空。

But the pork cycle is turning once again. Taiwan Semiconductor Manufacturing Company, the world’s biggest contract chipmaker, plans to spend $30bn on new capacity this year alone. Samsung Electronics and Intel, two other giants, have pencilled in $28bn and $20bn respectively; second-tier chipmakers are ramping up spending too. That will bring relief to the wider economy, says Mr Priestley, but not immediately. On May 14th Jim Whitehurst, the boss of IBM, a computer-maker, said he thought the shortages might last for two years. And, says Mr Penn, when the drought eventually ends, chipmakers may find they face a familiar problem, but on a bigger scale: a capacity splurge in response to serious shortages today could well mean a sizeable glut tomorrow. ■

pencil in 临时安排

glut 供过于求

但猪肉周期正在再次转向。全球最大的代工芯片制造商台湾积体电路制造有限公司(台积电)仅今年就计划投资300亿美元用于新增产能。另外两家巨头三星电子和英特尔分别拟投资280亿美元和200亿美元;二线芯片制造商也在加大支出。Priestley先生说，这将为更广泛的经济带来缓解，但不是立竿见影。5月14日，计算机制造商IBM的老板Jim Whitehurst说，他认为这种短缺可能会持续两年。佩恩表示，当干旱最终结束时，芯片制造商可能会发现他们面临一个熟悉的问题，但规模更大:今天为应对严重短缺而出现的产能挥霍，很可能意味着明天将出现大规模的供过于求的现象。■

Amazon’s future beyond Jeff Bezos

Could an MGM deal be the CEO’s parting shot?

与米高梅的交易会是这位首席执行官离职的机会吗?

May 22nd 2021

IN THE EARLY days of Amazon, its founder, Jeff Bezos, insisted there was some advertising the e-commerce giant wouldn’t touch, such as guns. That extended to James Bond’s Walther PPK. When producers of “Skyfall”, a Bond film released in 2012, sought to run an ad on the site, Amazon at first informed them that it violated the company’s weapons policy. “The studio was like ‘screw you!’” an Amazon executive later recalled. “Who is James Bond in silhouette without a gun? Literally, he’s just a random dude.”

in silhouette 以轮廓形式

screw 让..见鬼去

詹姆斯·邦德（James Bond）是《007》系列小说、电影的主角。

在亚马逊成立初期，其创始人杰夫·贝佐斯(Jeff Bezos)坚持认为，有些广告是这家电子商务巨头不会碰的，比如枪支。詹姆斯·邦德的瓦尔特PPK手枪也是如此。2012年上映的邦德电影《007:大破天幕杀机》(Skyfall)的制片人试图在亚马逊网站上投放广告时，亚马逊最初告知他们，这违反了该公司的武器政策。一名亚马逊高管后来回忆道：“‘XX的!’ 亚马逊影业, 没有枪的詹姆斯·邦德剪影是谁? 实际上，他就只是个普通人。”

This anecdote, one of innumerable gems in Brad Stone’s latest book, “Amazon Unbound”, springs to mind amid reports that Amazon is in talks to acquire Metro-Goldwyn-Mayer (MGM), part-owner of the Bond franchise, for $9bn. The book, by one of the company’s most astute observers, helps answer a question many are pondering as arguably the world’s most accomplished businessman prepares to surrender his role as CEO this summer. Will the Seattle-based e-commerce and cloud-computing juggernaut, whose revenues have grown at a compound annual rate of over 40% for more than two decades, lose momentum without its dome-headed, maths-geek mastermind? Or will it, like the Bond franchise itself, continue to thrive even with newcomers in the starring role?

innumerable 数不清的

gem 精品

astute 精明的

arguably 可论证地

surrender 放弃

juggernaut 不可抗拒的力量

在关于“亚马逊正在谈判以90亿美元收购007系列电影的部分所有者米高梅(Metro-Goldwyn-Mayer)”的报道中，让人想起Brad Stone最新著作《不受约束的亚马逊》中无数精品之一的这则轶事。这本书由该公司最敏锐的观察人士之一撰写，其有助于回答许多人正在思考的一个问题。这位可以说是全球最成功的商人今年夏天准备辞去CEO一职。这家总部位于西雅图的电子商务和云计算巨头，其收入在过去20多年里以超过40%的复合年增长率增长，如果失去了这位头脑圆滚滚、数学迷的统帅，它会失去动力吗? 还是会像邦德系列电影一样，即使有新人出演，它也能继续蓬勃发展?

One of Amazon’s bigger investors reckons it already knows the answer. Baillie Gifford, an Edinburgh-based asset manager, is cutting its stake. To explain why its enthusiasm has waned after 15 years as a shareholder, it borrows Mr Bezos’s mantra that it should always be “Day One” at Amazon—infused with a spirit of long-term focus, customer obsession and bold innovation. “Amazon is now seen as good value, safe and acceptable,” Baillie Gifford recently told its own investors. “It no longer has a founder CEO. We fear that in his inimitable terms it is no longer Day One in Seattle though the road ahead is still long and profitable.”

inimitable 无法效仿的

亚马逊的一个大投资者认为它已经知道答案了。总部位于爱丁堡的资产管理公司Baillie Gifford正在减持该公司的股份。为了解释为何在做了亚马逊15年股东后，亚马逊的热情有所消退，它借用了贝索斯的箴言，即在亚马逊，每一天都是第一天——灌输长期专注、客户至上和大胆创新的精神。Baillie Gifford最近告诉自己的投资者：“亚马逊现在被视为价值高、安全、可接受的公司。它不再有创始人兼首席执行官。我们担心的是，用他独特的话说，在西雅图，它已经不再是“第一天”了，尽管未来的道路依然漫长而有利可图。”

There is plenty in Mr Stone’s book to lend weight to such a characterisation. Central to it is Mr Bezos’s omnipotence within the firm. His business acumen is already legendary. Less well known is his role as a master of in-house invention on everything from Alexa, a voice-operated digital assistant, to Amazon’s own-label “single-cow burger”, his obsession with high standards**, his tolerance of** failure when in service to big ideas, his laser-like focus on financial nitty gritty and his (often exasperating) attention to detail. However much his successor, Andy Jassy, was schooled at his boss’s hip, he may lack the inventive streak that unites the likes of Mr Bezos and Apple’s late founder, Steve Jobs.

omnipotence 全能

acumen 精明

legendary 赫赫有名的；众所周知的

Stone先生的书中有很多东西可以支持这一描述。其核心是贝佐斯先生在公司内的全能。他的商业头脑已经是传奇性的。不太为人所知的是，他是公司内部发明的大师，从语音数字助手Alexa，到亚马逊自己的品牌“单牛汉堡”，他对高标准的执着，他在为大创意服务时对失败的容忍，他对财务细节的专注和(常常令人恼火的)对细节的关注。无论他的继任者Andy Jassy是如何在他的老板身边接受教育的，他可能缺乏将贝佐斯和苹果(Apple)已故创始人史蒂夫•乔布斯(Steve Jobs)等人团结在一起的创新特质。

Yet how much mileage was there left in it anyway? Despite a period last year when Mr Bezos took back day-to-day control during the pandemic, his relationship with Amazon has already become semi-detached. Mr Stone portrays a chief executive who, once he had become the world’s richest man, metamorphosed into a master of the universe. From a focus on Amazon’s self-perpetuating “flywheel” of endless choice, relentless innovation and customer-centricity, his horizons expanded: to space, for which he is building rockets; to newspaper publishing, as owner of the Washington Post; and to Hollywood, where he met Lauren Sanchez, for whom he left his wife. He moved into what Mr Stone calls an “alternate dimension of wealth, glamour and international intrigue”. The geek muscled up. He hung out with A-list celebrities. In short, he appeared to have outgrown his terrestrial creation.

to metamorphose into sth 成为某物

self-perpetuating 自我持续的

glamour 魅力；吸引力

A-list A名单（指重要人物名单）

不过，它还剩下多少潜力呢?尽管在去年流感期间，贝佐斯有一段时间恢复了对亚马逊的日常控制，但他与亚马逊的关系已经变得半独立。Stone先生描绘了一位首席执行官，他曾经是世界上最富有的人，后来却变成了宇宙的主宰。从专注于亚马逊无止境的选择、不懈的创新和以客户为中心的自我延续的“飞轮”，他的视野扩展：到太空，他正在建造火箭; 到报纸出版业，作为《华盛顿邮报》的所有者; 到了好莱坞，在那里他遇到了劳伦·桑切斯，为了她他离开了妻子。他进入了Stone所说的“财富、魅力和国际阴谋的另一个范围”。极客的影响力扩大。他和一流的名人交往。简而言之，他似乎已经长大了，超过了他的陆地作品。

Mr Jassy shares some of Mr Bezos’s virtues, such as what Mr Stone describes as “almost inhuman levels of discipline”. On his watch, Amazon Web Services, the cloud business that is Amazon’s biggest source of profits, has exhibited the parent company’s culture of high standards and unrelenting competitiveness. Since joining the firm in 1997, he has been steeped in Amazon values like frugality. In addition, as Mr Stone writes, Mr Jassy may also be somewhat humbler than Mr Bezos in confronting Amazon’s problems: a marketplace business accused by dissatisfied merchants of fraud and unfair competition (baselessly in Amazon’s eyes), warehouse staff seeking higher pay and better working conditions, and regulatory probes in America and Europe. The company is already splurging on employee health and welfare programmes.

Jassy拥有贝佐斯的一些优点，比如Stone所说的“近乎不人道的纪律水平”。在他的领导下，作为亚马逊最大利润来源的云业务亚马逊网络服务(Amazon Web Services)展现了其母公司高标准的文化和不懈的竞争力。自1997年加入该公司以来，他一直沉浸在节俭等亚马逊价值观中。此外,正如Stone先生写道, 在应对亚马逊的问题, Jassy先生也可能比贝佐斯先生谦逊些，其中的问题包括：其市场业务被不满的商家指控欺诈和不正当竞争（在亚马逊看来毫无根据）,仓库员工寻求更高的工资和更好的工作条件,以及面临在美国和欧洲的监管调查。该公司已经在员工健康和福利项目上大肆挥霍钱。

Mr Jassy’s toughest task may simply be confronting the law of large numbers. With revenues of $386bn last year and a market value of $1.6trn, it is ever harder for Amazon to keep the flywheel going, especially with potential accelerators, such as India, underperforming. Last year the firm had capital expenditures of $40bn. How does it allocate so much money without frittering a lot away?

to fritter sth away on … 把某物浪费在…上

Jassy先生最艰巨的任务可能仅仅是面对大数定律。亚马逊去年的营收为3860亿美元，市值为1.6万亿美元，要想让飞轮继续运转，难度越来越大，尤其是在印度等潜在加速器表现不佳的情况下。去年该公司的资本支出为400亿美元。它如何分配这么多钱而不浪费很多钱呢?

The world is not enough

Such questions make the potential acquisition of MGM to bolster Amazon’s Prime subscription services intriguing. Even at a pricey $9bn, it is easily affordable for Amazon. Last year it spent $11bn on TV series, films and music for Prime. The reports come days after Mr Jassy announced the return of Jeff Blackburn, a veteran Amazon executive who left the firm recently. He will oversee a media and entertainment business that includes sports rights, such as the National Football League’s “Thursday Night Football”, Twitch, Amazon’s gaming platform, and Amazon Studios, which recently won two Oscars. First he will report to Mr Bezos, then to Mr Jassy.

这些问题让收购米高梅以支持亚马逊Prime订阅服务的可能性变得有趣起来。即使售价高达90亿美元，亚马逊也很容易负担得起。去年，它在Prime的电视剧、电影和音乐上投入了110亿美元。这些报道是在Jassy宣布Jeff Blackburn回归几天后发布的。Blackburn是亚马逊的一位资深高管，最近离开了公司。他将负责媒体和娱乐业务，其中包括体育转播权，如美国国家橄榄球联盟(National Football League)的“周四橄榄球之夜”(Thursday Night Football)、亚马逊的游戏平台Twitch和最近获得两项奥斯卡奖的亚马逊影业(Amazon Studios)。他首先向贝佐斯汇报，然后向Jassy汇报。

This could suggest that the post-Bezos era will be marked by the quest for a new source of rapid growth—a flywheel in which blockbuster entertainment, advertising and shopping reinforce each other. Or it could be the departing CEO’s final vanity project. If it is a flywheel, it may form part of a new era of competition among America’s tech giants. As one observer puts it, Amazon is trying to crack mass entertainment before Facebook and Google crack shopping. If it is a sign of hubris, it would be more worrying, suggesting that Mr Bezos will use his new role as executive chairman to drive Amazon from the leather-clad luxury of the back seat. Perhaps stroking a white cat as he does so. ■

stroke 抚摸

这可能意味着，后贝佐斯时代的标志将是寻求一个新的快速增长来源，在这个飞轮上，爆炸性的娱乐、广告和购物相互促进。也可能是这位即将离任的CEO最后的面子工程。如果它是一个飞轮，它可能会成为美国科技巨头竞争新时代的一部分。正如一位观察人士所说，亚马逊正试图赶在Facebook和谷歌攻破购物之前攻破大众娱乐市场。如果这是一种傲慢的迹象，那将更令人担忧，因为这表明贝佐斯将利用自己执行董事长的新角色，把亚马逊从豪华皮后座上赶下来。也许是抚摸着一只白猫。■

George Floyd’s legacy

乔治·弗洛伊德的“遗留问题”

WHEN GEORGE FLOYD was killed by Derek Chauvin a year ago, the sense of injustice was tinged with despair. Why, many Americans asked, is this still happening in our country? Why, many foreigners asked, does the story of race in America never seem to change? Except this time was different. Mr Floyd’s death prompted the biggest civil-rights protests in American history. Mr Chauvin, unusually, was convicted of murder. And institutions in America and beyond looked at themselves in a different light. Something needed to change. But what exactly?

The Biden administration and the Democratic Party have made reducing racial disparities an organising principle of government. That sounds straightforward, but it is not. Despite the gains in legal and political rights made by African-Americans since the civil-rights era, measures of relative poverty and black-white segregation have barely moved for half a century. Tackling enduring injustices requires clear thinking about their causes.

Most racial disparities come about when three things collide: secular economic trends, the aftershocks of slavery and segregation and present-day bigotry and racism. Yet police officers kill Americans of all races more often than they should. Separating the many police shootings that are justified from those that should never have happened ought to be a national priority. May 13th 2021

FOR YEARS governments have grumbled, simmered and raged as multinational companies have shifted profits out of tax collectors’ grasp and into low-tax havens. The OECD, a club of mostly rich countries, estimated in 2015 that avoidance robs public coffers of $100bn-240bn, or 4-10% of global corporation-tax revenues a year. Now the fiscal fallout from covid-19 is adding urgency to governments’ efforts to claw some money back—most notably in America, where President Joe Biden plans to raise taxes on corporate profits, including foreign income.

多年来，当跨国公司将利润从税收人员手中转移到低税收天堂时，各国政府一直在抱怨、酝酿和愤怒。经济合作与发展组织(OECD)，一个主要由发达国家组成的俱乐部，在2015年估计，避税从国库夺走1000亿到2400亿美元，或每年全球公司税收入的4-10%。现在，covid-19带来的财政影响使各国政府追回部分资金的努力更加紧迫，尤其是在美国，美国总统拜登计划对企业利润(包括海外收入)增税。

Mr Biden’s proposals will grind their way through Congress. Finance ministers from the G7 group of countries are likely to discuss global tax reform when they meet in London on June 4th-5th. And later in the summer 139 countries will discuss changing the system for taxing multinational companies. The confluence of a political shift in America and a global push to raise more tax revenue to pay for the pandemic means a degree of optimism is in the air. The proposals under discussion may initially raise only a modest amount of revenue, but they still represent a big break with the past.

拜登的提议将在国会获得通过。6月4日至5日，七国集团的财政部长们可能会在伦敦开会讨论全球税收改革问题。今年夏天晚些时候，139个国家将讨论改变对跨国公司征税的制度。美国的政治转变和全球推动增加税收以支付流感大流行病意味着一定程度的乐观情绪正在弥漫。目前正在讨论的提案最初可能只会带来少量收入，但仍代表着与过去的一个重大突破。

The foundations of the global corporate-tax system were laid a century ago. It recognises that overlapping taxes on the same slice of profits can curb trade and growth. As a result, taxing rights are allocated first to wherever profits are produced (the “source”) and then to wherever the parent company is headquartered (or “resident”). A multinational based in America but with an affiliate in Ireland, for example, typically pays taxes in both places. Where the company makes its sales is irrelevant. Payments between an individual firm’s various legal affiliates are recorded using the “arm’s-length” principle, supposedly on terms equivalent to those found on the open market.

arm’s-length 关系正常的；保持距离的

全球公司税体系的基础是一个世纪前奠定的。它认识到对同一部分利润重复征税会抑制贸易和增长。因此，征税权首先分配到利润产生的地方(“来源”)，然后分配到母公司的总部所在地(或“居民”)。例如，一家总部设在美国但在爱尔兰有分公司的跨国公司通常在两个地方都纳税。公司在哪里销售并不重要。单个公司的各种合法附属公司之间的支付记录采用“保持距离”的原则，假定其条款与公开市场上的条款相同。

![image-20210522100729097](/Users/chenyujie/Library/Application Support/typora-user-images/image-20210522100729097.png)

These principles, now baked into thousands of bilateral tax treaties, have had two unintended consequences. First, they have encouraged governments to compete for investment and revenue by offering tantalisingly low tax rates (see chart 1). In 1985 the global average statutory corporation-tax rate was 49%; in 2018 it was 24%. Ireland boasts a statutory rate of just 12.5%; Bermuda, 0%. Second, tax competition has encouraged companies to shuffle their reported profits to low-tax places. In 2016 around $1trn of global profits were booked in so-called “investment hubs”. These include the Cayman Islands, Ireland and Singapore, which apply an average effective tax rate of 5% on the profits of non-resident companies.

tantalisingly 诱人地

statutory 法定的

这些原则如今已被纳入数千份双边税收协定，产生了两个意想不到的后果。首先，他们通过提供诱人地极低的税率来鼓励各国政府竞争投资和收入(见表1)。1985年，全球平均法定公司税税率为49%;2018年这一比例为24%。爱尔兰的法定利率仅为12.5%;百慕大,0%。其次，税收竞争鼓励企业将申报的利润转移到低税率地区。2016年，全球约有1万亿美元的利润被计入所谓的“投资中心”，其中包括开曼群岛、爱尔兰和新加坡，它们对非居民公司的利润实行5%的平均实际税率。

There is a huge mismatch between where tax is paid and where real activity takes place. Analysis by the OECD suggests that multinationals report 25% of their profits in investment hubs, although only 11% of their tangible assets and less than 5% of their workers are based there. Parents can allocate paper profits to affiliates in tax havens by having them hold intellectual property that is then licensed to other affiliates in high-tax places. The problem seems to have worsened over time, perhaps because more firms make money from intangible services, from software to streaming videos. The share of American multinationals’ foreign profits booked in tax havens has risen from 30% two decades ago to about 60% today. Most investors and bosses view firms’ tax bills as a black box that only a few lawyers and tax experts truly understand.

纳税地点和实际活动发生地点之间存在巨大的不匹配。经合组织(OECD)的分析显示，跨国公司报告其25%的利润来自于投资中心，尽管其有形资产和员工在投资中心的比例分别只有11%和不到5%。通过让子公司持有知识产权，母公司可以将账面利润分配给位于避税天堂的子公司，然后将这些知识产权授权给位于高税收地区的其他子公司。这个问题似乎随着时间的推移越来越严重，也许是因为更多的公司从软件到流媒体视频等无形服务中赚钱。在避税天堂登记的美国跨国公司的海外利润份额已经从20年前的30%上升到今天的60%。大多数投资者和老板将公司的税单视为只有少数律师和税务专家才真正了解的黑盒子。

One way of capturing the scale of manipulation is to examine what would happen if there were a single common tax rate. A recent study by Thomas Torslov of Kraka, a Danish think-tank, and Ludvig Wier and Gabriel Zucman of the University of California, Berkeley, tried to quantify this. A staggering $670bn in paper profits, which are unconnected to things like factories, would have moved in 2016—almost 40% of multinationals’ foreign earnings. Big Western countries are losers from the current system: profits in America and France, for instance, are depressed by around a fifth (see chart 2). By comparison, havens collect more revenue, as a share of GDP, despite their rock-bottom effective rates. XX collects a third of its corporate-tax receipts by attracting profits from high-tax countries; Ireland, over half.

The rise of Silicon Valley has added fuel to the fire. Some governments gripe at giant firms serving customers without any physical presence in their country and while paying no tax. The problems posed by the tech firms are not in fact new: pharmaceutical companies have long held mobile and hard-to-value intellectual property; exporters do not incur tax liabilities where they sell. Still, digital services have become a target. More than 40 governments, from France to India, are either levying or planning to levy digital-services taxes on the revenue of firms such as Amazon, Google and Facebook.

硅谷的崛起更是火上浇油。一些国家的政府抱怨大公司为客户提供服务，却在本国没有任何实体存在，而且不纳税。事实上，科技公司带来的问题并不新: 制药公司长期持有可移动且难以估值的知识产权;出口商在销售商品的地方不必承担税务责任。不过，数字服务已成为目标。从法国到印度，40多个国家的政府正在或计划对亚马逊、谷歌和Facebook等公司的收入征收数字服务税。

The growing sense of anarchy over how to tax Silicon Valley, the global desire to raise more tax revenues and a more conciliatory White House all mean the scene is set for a global deal. The OECD’s forthcoming summit is not the first time it has tried to orchestrate reforms—it helped pass changes to the transfer-pricing regime in 2015. 不过，对于那些能够做到这一点的人来说，这里有很好的生意可做。电动汽车包含很多有价值的东西。发动机中的磁铁充满了稀土金属，电池则是锂和钴。挪威研究公司Rystad Energy预测，随着电动汽车产量的增加，锂的价格将在本世纪末翻三番。与此同时，钴主要来自刚果，这个国家经常饱受战争蹂躏，人权记录糟糕。

Generally speaking, electrical waste is shredded in bulk before it is sorted and reprocessed. But lithium-ion batteries, the type used in EVs, are inflammable, so need careful handling. They are shredded separately in special machines filled with liquids or gases that suppress combustion. The result, called “black mass”, is then processed to extract its valuable components.

shred 撕碎

一般来说，电子废物在分类和再处理之前会先被切碎。但是用于电动汽车的锂离子电池是易燃的，所以需要小心处理。它们被单独在装满液体或气体的特殊机器中粉碎，这些液气可以抑制燃烧。其结果被称为“黑块”，然后被加工提取其有价值的成分。

There are two ways of doing so. The more common at the moment is pyrometallurgy. This treats black mass as an ore, by smelting it in a furnace to liberate a metallic mixture from which pure metals, particularly the cobalt, can be separated. That, though, requires a lot of energy. It also destroys valuable non-metallic components such as the graphite in batteries’ anodes. And it fails to liberate the lithium, which ends up in compounds in the slag that is generated alongside the liquid metal, and must then be extracted separately.

pyrometallurgy 热冶学；火法冶金学

graphite 石墨

anode 阳极

slag 熔渣

有两种方法。目前比较常见的是火法冶金。这种方法将黑块视为矿石，将其在熔炉中熔炼，释出金属混合物，从中可以分离出纯金属，尤其是钴。然而，这需要大量的能量。它还会破坏有价值的非金属成分，比如电池阳极中的石墨。它不能释放出锂，锂最终会在熔渣中形成化合物，其与液态金属一起产生的，然后必须单独提取。

The other approach, hydrometallurgy, works more subtly. It leaches metals, lithium included, out of the shredded material by dissolving them in acids or other solvents. That requires less energy and also permits the recovery of non-metallic materials such as graphite. Hydrometallurgy is more complex than pyrometallurgy, and comes with the added expense of treating the waste water it generates, to prevent pollution. But its overall advantages suggest it is the wave of the future.

hydrometallurgy 湿法冶金术

leach 滤去

另一种方法，即湿法冶金，则更为微妙。它将从粉碎的材料中滤出的金属(包括锂)溶解在酸或其他溶剂中。这需要更少的能量，也允许回收非金属材料，如石墨。湿法冶金比火法冶金更为复杂，而且还伴随着额外的“代价”，为防止污染，它还需要处理产生的废水。但它的总体优势表明，它是未来的潮流。

Li-Cycle, a Canadian company founded in 2016 that is already the biggest recycler of lithium-ion batteries in North America, is one outfit betting on hydrometallurgy. To improve the gathering of its raw material Li-Cycle is testing what it calls spoke-and-hub systems. These collect incoming batteries of all sorts, not just those from EVs, at geographically dispersed receiving stations (the spokes), shred them, and then sort the debris, using automatic separation and sieving systems, into three types of mixture: plastic, and aluminium, and black mass. The plastic and the copper-aluminium mix are sold to other recyclers. The black mass is sent to the system’s hub, a hydrometallurgical processing plant that serves many spokes.

成立于2016年的加拿大公司Li-Cycle已经是北美最大的锂离子电池回收商，它是一家押注于湿法冶金的公司。为了改善原材料的收集，Li-Cycle公司正在测试所谓的轮辐-轮毂系统。它们在地理上分散的接收站(轮辐)收集各种各样的电池，而不仅仅是电动汽车的电池，将它们粉碎，然后利用自动分离和筛分系统将碎片分类为三种混合物:塑料、铝和黑块。塑料和铜铝混合物被卖给其他回收商。黑块被送到系统的“轮毂/中心”，一个为许多“轮幅”服务的湿法冶金加工厂。

Kunal Phalpher, Li-Cycle’s chief commercial officer, says experience with a demonstration hub at the firm’s base in Kingston, Ontario, suggests this approach can recover cobalt, lithium and also nickel (an important ingredient of some non-lithium-ion batteries) in a form pure enough for those metals to be used directly to make new batteries. In all, Mr Phalpher claims, the process recycles 95% of a battery’s materials. Li-Cycle will soon complete its first properly commercial hub, in Rochester, New York, and has plans for three more around the world by 2025.

Li-Cycle首席商务官Kunal Phalpher说该公司安大略省金斯敦基地示范中心的经验表明，此方法可以回收钴、锂和镍(一些非锂离子电池的重要成分)，其纯度足以让这些金属直接用于制造新电池。总的来说，Phalpher 先生声称，该过程可回收电池95%的材料。Li Cycle不久将在纽约罗切斯特建成其第一个完全商业化的“中心”，并计划在2025年前在世界各地再建三个。

Li-Cycle is not alone, though, in its hydrometallurgical ambitions. One rival is Redwood Materials of Carson City, Nevada, which was founded in 2017 by Jeffrey Straubel, formerly chief technology officer of Tesla, a big maker of EVs. Redwood uses a combination of pyro- and hydrometallurgy in its process, with some of the recovered materials providing energy to drive the pyro side of the equation. It already recycles rejects from the American EV-battery factories of two Japanese firms, Panasonic and Nissan, and is now setting up an operation that will take used batteries from general consumer goods.

reject 次品

然而，在湿法冶金方面，Li-Cycle并不是唯一的一家公司。其竞争对手之一是位于内华达州卡森市的Redwood Materials公司，该公司由大型电动汽车制造商特斯拉(Tesla)前首席技术官Jeffrey Straubel于2017年创立。Redwood在其过程中使用了高温冶金和湿法冶金的结合，一些回收的材料提供能量来驱动高温冶金的一边。它已经回收了松下（Panasonic）和日产（Nissan）两家日本公司的美国电动汽车电池工厂的次品，目前正在建立一个从普通消费品中回收旧电池的业务。

Northvolt, another firm started by ex-Tesla-ites (Peter Carlsson, its chief executive, and Paolo Cerruti, its chief operating officer), makes lithium-ion batteries for European carmakers. It is adding a recycling plant to its factory in Sweden, to process the batteries it produces there when they reach the ends of their lives. Their steel and plastic casings, and copper wiring, are removed manually before they are crushed in an inert environment. Nickel, manganese, cobalt and lithium are then removed by hydrometallurgy. Northvolt has also formed a partnership with Hydro, a Norwegian aluminium producer, to recover that metal as well. The firm hopes that, by 2030, half the materials it uses to make new batteries will have been recycled.

casing 外壳

manganese 锰

另一家由前特斯拉人(Peter Carlsson，特斯拉首席执行官，Paolo Cerruti，特斯拉首席运营官)创立的公司Northvolt，为欧洲汽车制造商生产锂离子电池。该公司正在其瑞典工厂的基础上增加一个回收工厂，以便在电池使用寿命结束时对其在瑞典生产的电池进行处理。它们的钢和塑料外壳，以及铜线，在被压碎在惰性环境中之前，都要手动拆除。镍、锰、钴和锂然后通过湿法冶金去除。Northvolt还与挪威铝生产商Hydro建立了合作伙伴关系，以回收这种金属。该公司希望，到2030年，半数用于制造新电池的材料将被回收利用。

Similar “closed-loop” systems are being developed in other parts of the battery supply chain. For example, American Battery Technology, a firm in Nevada that mines and processes lithium, is adding a recycling plant intended to recover lithium and other metals from expired batteries. It will use the lithium in its own production processes and sell the other materials on.

sell sth on 转售

电池供应链的其他部分也在开发类似的“闭环”系统。例如，内华达州一家开采和加工锂的公司American Battery Technology正在增加一个回收工厂，旨在从过期电池中回收锂和其他金属。它将在自己的生产过程中使用锂，并转售其他材料。

The biggest battery-recycling operations of all, though, are not Western, but XX—not surprising, perhaps, given that XX is the world’s largest market for EVs, and the country’s government has been promoting the recycling of lithium-ion batteries for some time. Brunp Reycling, a subsidiary of CATL, the world’s biggest EV-battery-maker, has half-a-dozen hydrometallurgical recycling operations around the country. Brunp says it can recycle 120,000 tonnes of old batteries a year, which it claims represents about half of XX’s current annual battery-recycling capacity. Meanwhile, American Battery Technology’s approach of integrating recycling with primary production of lithium echoes that of Gangfeng Lithium, one of the world’s largest producers of lithium for batteries. Gangfeng, which has already installed a heavily automated recycling plant at its base in Jiangxi province, plans to build another as part of its mining operation in Sonora state, in Mexico.

然而，最大的电池回收企业不是西方，而是XX。这也许并不奇怪，因为XX是世界上最大的电动汽车市场，而且XX政府一段时间以来一直在推动锂离子电池的回收。Brunp recycling是世界上最大的电动汽车电池制造商CATL的子公司，在全国有6家湿法冶金回收公司。Brunp表示，该公司每年可回收12万吨旧电池，约占XX目前电池回收能力的一半。与此同时，American Battery Technology公司将回收与锂的初级生产结合起来的做法，与全球最大的锂电池生产商之一港丰锂业的做法相似。港丰已经在江西省的基地安装了一座高度自动化的回收工厂，并计划在墨西哥索诺拉州再建一座工厂，作为其采矿业务的一部分。

Tesla itself also has trans-Pacific ambitions. It is setting up a battery-recycling facility at its EV factory in Shanghai, to complement one it is developing at its battery factory in Nevada. Nor is Tesla the only vehicle-maker involving itself in the industry. In January, Volkswagen opened a pilot battery-recycling plant in Salzgitter, near Hanover, to recover materials from batteries used in EVs made by its various brands.

特斯拉本身也有跨太平洋的雄心。该公司正在上海的电动汽车工厂建立一个电池回收设施，以补充其内华达州电池工厂正在开发的一个设施。特斯拉也不是唯一一家涉足该行业的汽车制造商。今年1月，大众汽车(Volkswagen)在汉诺威附近的Salzgitter开设了一家电池回收试点工厂，用于回收各品牌电动汽车所用电池中的材料。

Salzgitter is close to the company’s battery factory in Braunschweig, which is being expanded to produce more than 600,000 EV battery packs a year. The idea, says Frank Blome, head of batteries for Volkswagen Group Components, is that the firm’s battery experts will work with its recyclers to make battery packs easier to dismantle. As Mr Blome observes, “anyone who takes something apart first needs to know how it was put together.”

Salzgitter距离该公司位于Braunschweig的电池厂很近，该电池厂正在扩大规模，每年将生产超过60万组电动汽车电池组。大众汽车集团组件电池部门的负责人Frank Blome表示，该公司的电池专家将与其回收人员合作，使电池组更容易拆卸。正如Blome先生所观察到的，“任何要把东西拆开的人首先需要知道它是如何组装起来的。”

Designing recyclability in from the beginning will, in the long run, be crucial to the effective recycling of electric vehicles—and especially their batteries. Shredding lots of different types of e-waste at the same time inevitably results in contamination. Separating components before doing so would yield greater levels of purity. Some components, such as cathodes, might even be reused in their entirety.

cathode 阴极；阳极

从长远来看，从一开始就设计可回收性，对有效回收电动汽车，尤其是电池，至关重要。同时粉碎大量不同类型的电子垃圾不可避免地会造成污染。在此之前分离成分可以产生更高的纯度。有些组件，比如阴极，甚至可以被完全重用。

Deconstructing reality

解构现实

Easing disassembly is also an important goal for Volkswagen’s domestic rival, BMW. According to Frank Weber, a member of the firm’s board, BMW will, from the start, be designing its electric vehicles with mass recycling in mind. This will include the handling of the solid-state lithium-ion batteries which BMW hopes to make in volume by the end of the decade. Solid-state batteries, which are able to store more charge than those using existing gel-based electrolytes, could double the range of EVs. They will also be safer to use for, unlike those containing gel electrolytes, they will not be inflammable.

简化拆卸也是大众国内竞争对手宝马(BMW)的一个重要目标。据公司董事会成员Frank Weber称，宝马在设计电动汽车时，将从一开始就考虑到大规模回收利用。这将包括处理固态锂离子电池，宝马希望在本世纪末实现量产。固态电池比现有的凝胶电解质能够储存更多的电量，可以使电动汽车的续航里程增加一倍。它们也将更安全的使用，不像那些含有凝胶电解质，它们不会易燃。

While gel electrolytes continue to persist, however, it would be best if they too could be recycled. In the case of the most common of them, lithium hexafluorophosphate (known as PF6), that does not yet happen. Instead, this valuable chemical is destroyed during processing and has to be resynthesised from any lithium recovered. But a team led by Anand Bhatt and Thomas Ruether at CSIRO, Australia’s national science organisation, think they have come up with a way to recover PF6 intact. They use a special solvent to extract it from black mass before any further metallurgical process is applied to it. The PF6 obtained in this way is, they say, good enough to be used to make new batteries without further processing.

metallurgic 冶金的

虽然凝胶电解质继续存在，但是，如果它们也能被回收，那就最好了。就其中最常见的六氟磷酸锂（即PF6）而言，这种情况还没有发生。相反，这种有价值的化学物质在加工过程中被破坏，必须从回收的锂中重新合成。但是一个由澳大利亚国家科学组织CSIRO的Anand Bhatt和Thomas Ruether领导的团队认为，他们已经找到了一种完整恢复PF6的方法。他们使用一种特殊的溶剂从黑块中提取，然后再对其进行任何进一步的冶金处理。他们说，通过这种方式获得的PF6足够好，无需进一步处理就可以用来制造新电池。

Also in Australia, a firm called EcoGraf has developed a process that can extract graphite from black mass with a purity that allows it be reused for making anodes. SungEel HiTech, a scrap-merchant that is South Korea’s biggest battery recycler, is now setting up a plant at its factory in Gusan to do just that.

同样在澳大利亚，一家名为EcoGraf的公司开发了一种从黑块中提取石墨的工艺，这种工艺纯度高，可以重复使用，用于制造阳极。SungEel HiTech是韩国最大的电池回收商，现在正在其位于Gusan的工厂里建立一个工厂来进行电池回收。

Scrap merchants have to be flexible. SungEel’s previous main business was recycling plasma-television screens, which have, these days, largely, been superseded by LED versions. Plasma televisions turned out to be a passing fad. EVs, though, are likely to run and run. ■

废料商必须灵活点。SungEel之前的主要业务是回收等离子电视屏幕，而如今，等离子电视屏幕在很大程度上已经被LED屏幕所取代。等离子电视只是一时的流行。不过，电动汽车可能会不断发展。■

PIPELINES, LIKE cables and substations, are the type of dull, critical infrastructure that Americans don’t think about until, suddenly, they must. On May 7th a cyber-attack prompted Colonial Pipeline, a firm headquartered in Georgia, to shut down a tube stretching from Texas to New Jersey that supplies about 45% of the petrol and diesel used on the east coast. Federal officials confirmed that DarkSide, a gang believed to be based in the former Soviet Union, was responsible. “We’re not talking about some small pipeline,” explains Amy Myers Jaffe, author of “Energy’s Digital Future”, a new book. “We’re talking about the jugular.”

diesel 柴油

管道，像电缆和变电站，是美国人在突然之间，必须考虑的那种枯燥、关键的基础设施。5月7日，一场网络攻击促使总部位于佐治亚州的Colonial Pipeline公司关闭了一条从德克萨斯延伸到新泽西的管道，而这条管道为东海岸提供了45%的汽油和柴油。联邦官员证实， DarkSide（一个据信以前苏联为基地的团伙）要对此负责。新书《能源的数字未来》的作者Amy Myers Jaffe解释道：“我们说的不是一些小管道，我们说的是“颈静脉”【要害】。”

On May 12th Colonial Pipeline said it had “initiated the restart of pipeline operations”, a carefully worded statement that conveys both the difficulty of returning to normal and a desire to contain panic. That day average petrol prices topped $3 a gallon for the first time since 2014. Much depends on whether more drivers rush to buy petrol, as they did in the oil shocks of the 1970s. If 30m car-owners with half a tank decide to fill up, reckons S&P Global Platts Analytics, a data group, they would guzzle over 4m barrels, more than the recent daily demand of the entire eastern seaboard. Even if supplies are restored quickly, however, America faces a more stubborn problem. Despite more than a decade of attention to cyber-security, the country’s energy infrastructure—the pipelines, power generators and grids on which the economy depends—remains exposed to hackers. 然而，即使供应迅速恢复，美国也面临着一个更棘手的问题。尽管对网络安全的关注已经超过了10年，但该国的能源基础设施（经济赖以生存的管道、发电机组和电网）仍然容易受到黑客的攻击。实际上，能源系统可能会变得更脆弱，而非更少(脆弱)。

Securing oil and electricity assets used to mean guarding against physical attacks: a bombing of a refinery, for instance. Those risks remain, but hackers can also inflict grave damage. In 2010 the Stuxnet worm, generally believed to have been deployed by Israel and America, targeted a nuclear facility in Iran. In 2015 and 2016 Russian hackers caused blackouts in Ukraine.

Aware of such risks, American executives and politicians have tried to mitigate them. The American Petroleum Institute, the country’s main oil lobby, has held an annual conference on cyber-security since 2006. The Department of Energy dutifully developed “The Energy Sector Specific Plan” for cyberthreats, in 2015, “The Multiyear Plan for Energy Sector Cybersecurity”, in 2018 and “The 2020 Cybersecurity Multi-year Program Plan”, to name but a few.

dutifully 尽职地

美国的高管和政客们在意识到这些风险后，也试图减轻这些风险。自2006年以来，美国主要的石油游说团体“美国石油协会”每年就“网络安全”会召开一次会议。2015年能源部认真制定了针对网络威胁的“能源部门具体计划”，2018年制定了“能源部门网络安全多年计划”，以及“2020年网络安全多年计划”等，仅列举了几例。

Yet weaknesses remain. The shutdown of the Colonial Pipeline exposes gaps in companies’ cyber-armour. It also reveals the idea that shale oil might guarantee energy security to be a fallacy. Oil may be fungible but oil infrastructure is not, notes Michael Tran of RBC Capital Markets. America’s north-east is particularly exposed to attack, Mr Tran says. A dearth of local refineries makes the region dependent on pipelines and tankers for oil products.

fungible 代替的；可取代的

The author focuses on two waves of female leaders. The first group were the baby-boomers, born between 1946 and 1964. These were often the only women in upper management at their firms. They were able to be more open with colleagues about their parental duties. 第二次批浪潮出生于1974年至1985年，有女性同事在高层管理，期望(通常也能得到)她们的配偶的支持，并受益于雇主的津贴，如产假和弹性工作。他们能够更坦诚地与同事谈论自己作为父母的责任。这代人已经掌握了“工作-生活摇摆”，他们每天在个人生活和职业生活之间来回穿梭，在带孩子去体检之前先开会，然后再回到办公室。

blaze a trail 做开路先锋

Our model suggests that covid-19 has already claimed 7.1m-12.7m lives. Our central estimate is that 10m people have died who would otherwise be living. This tally of “excess deaths” is over three times the official count, which nevertheless is the basis for most statistics on the disease, including fatality rates and cross-country comparisons.

利用已知的121个变量数据，从有记录的死亡人数到人口统计数据，我们建立了一种相关性模式，使我们能够填补数据缺失的空白。我们的模型显示，covid-19已经夺走了710万至1270万人的生命。我们最重要的估计是，1000万人已经死亡，他们本应该活着。这一”过多死亡”的统计数字是官方统计数字的三倍多，但官方统计数字是关于该疾病的大多数统计数据的基础，包括死亡率和跨国比较。

The most important insight from our work is that covid-19 has been harder on the poor than anyone knew. Official figures suggest that the pandemic has struck in waves, and that the United States and Europe have been hit hard. Although South America has been ravaged, the rest of the developing world seemed to get off lightly.

从我们的工作中得到的最重要的启示是，covid-19对穷人的影响比任何人知道的都要严重。官方数据显示，大流行病是一波接一波地爆发的，美国和欧洲遭受了严重打击。虽然南美洲遭到了破坏，但其他发展中国家似乎轻松脱身。

Our modelling tells another story. When you count all the bodies, you see that the pandemic has spread remorselessly from the rich, connected world to poorer, more isolated places. As it has done so, the global daily death rate has climbed steeply.

remorselessly 无情地；无休止地

我们的模型讲述了另一个故事。当你清点所有的尸体时，你会看到这一流行病无情地从富裕的、联系紧密的世界蔓延到较贫穷、较孤立的地方。正因此，全球每日死亡率急剧攀升。

Death rates have been very high in some rich countries, but the overwhelming majority of the 6.7m or so deaths that nobody counted were in poor and middle-income ones. In Romania and Iran excess deaths are more than double the number officially put down to covid-19. In Egypt they are 13 times as big. In America the difference is 7.1%.

一些发达国家的死亡率非常高，但是没有人统计的约670万死亡人数中，绝大多数来自贫穷和中等收入国家。在罗马尼亚和伊朗，额外死亡人数是官方认定的covid-19死亡人数的两倍多。在埃及，它们的死亡人数13倍多。在美国，这一差距是7.1%。

![image-20210516110506054](/Users/chenyujie/Library/Application Support/typora-user-images/image-20210516110506054.png)

India, where about 20,000 are dying every day, is not an outlier. Our figures suggest that, in terms of deaths as a share of population, Peru’s pandemic has been 2.5 times worse than India’s. The disease is working its way through Nepal and Pakistan. Infectious variants spread faster and, because of the tyranny of exponential growth, overwhelm health-care systems and fill mortuaries even if the virus is no more lethal.

tyranny 暴虐

the tyranny of domestic routine 沉重的家务负担

mogue 停尸房 =mortuary

在印度，每天大约有2万人死亡，并不是一个例外。我们的数字表明，就死亡人数占人口的比例而言，秘鲁的大流行比印度的严重2.5倍。这种疾病正在尼泊尔和巴基斯坦蔓延。传染性变异传播得更快，而且由于快速的指数增长，即使病毒不再致命，这也使得医疗系统不堪重负，甚至使停尸房拥挤不堪。

Ultimately the way to stop this is vaccination. As an example of collaboration and pioneering science, covid-19 vaccines rank with the Apollo space programme. Within just a year of the virus being discovered, people could be protected from severe disease and death. Hundreds of millions of them have benefited.

最终阻止这种情况的方法是接种疫苗。作为合作和开拓性科学的一个例子，covid-19疫苗与阿波罗空间计划并列。在病毒被发现的一年内，人们就可以免于严重的疾病和死亡。数亿人从中受益。

However, in the short run vaccines will fuel the divide between rich and poor. Soon, the only people to die from covid-19 in rich countries will be exceptionally frail or exceptionally unlucky, as well as those who have spurned the chance to be vaccinated. In poorer countries, by contrast, most people will have no choice. They will remain unprotected for many months or years.

spurn 轻蔑地拒绝

然而，在短期内，疫苗将加剧贫富之间的差距。很快，发达国家中唯一死于covid-19的人将是异常虚弱或异常不幸的人，以及那些拒绝接种疫苗的人。相比之下，在较贫穷的国家，大多数人将没有选择。它们将在好几个月或几年的时间里不受保护。

The world cannot rest while people perish for want of a jab costing as little as $4 for a two-dose course. It is hard to think of a better use of resources than vaccination. Economists’ central estimate for the direct value of a course is $2,900—if you include factors like long covid and the effect of impaired education, the total is much bigger. The benefit from an extra 1bn doses supplied by July would be worth hundreds of billions of dollars. Less circulating virus means less mutation, and so a lower chance of a new variant that reinfects the vaccinated.

当人们因缺乏一个两剂疗程仅需4美元的疫苗而死亡时，全世界都不能安宁。很难想出比接种疫苗更好的资源利用方式。经济学家对一个疗程的直接价值的重要估计是2900美元，如果把长时间的冠状病毒感染和教育受损等因素包括在内，总额要大得多。到7月份再供应10亿剂疫苗的好处将价值数千亿美元。更少的传播病毒意味着更少的变异，因此一种新的变种重新感染疫苗接种者的可能性更低。

Supplies of vaccines are already growing. By the end of April, according to Airfinity, an analytics firm, vaccine-makers produced 1.7bn doses, 700m more than the end of March and ten times more than January. Before the pandemic, annual global vaccine capacity was roughly 3.5bn doses. The latest estimates are that total output in 2021 will be almost 11bn. Some in the industry predict a global surplus in 2022.

疫苗的供应已经在增长。根据分析公司Airfinity的数据，截至4月底，疫苗生产商生产了17亿支疫苗，比3月底多7亿支，比1月份多10倍。在大流行之前，全球每年的疫苗生产能力约为35亿剂。最新的估计是，2021年的总产量将接近110亿剂。一些业内人士预测，到2022年，全球将出现盈余。

And yet the world is right to strive to get more doses in more arms sooner. Hence President Joe Biden has proposed waiving intellectual-property claims on covid-19 vaccines. Many experts argue that, because some manufacturing capacity is going begging, millions more doses might become available if patent-owners shared their secrets, including in countries that today are at the back of the queue. World-trade rules allow for a waiver. When invoke them if not in the throes of a pandemic?

be going begging 没人要

waiver 弃权；免支付协议；弃权声明书

[释义] an agreement that you do not have to pay or obey something

to be in the throes of sth/doing sth 处于某事/做某事的煎熬之中

然而，世界努力更快地为更多胳膊提供更多疫苗是正确的。因此，乔·拜登总统提议放弃对covid-19疫苗的知识产权要求。许多专家认为，由于一些疫苗的生产能力正在恶化，如果专利所有人分享他们的秘密，包括现在排在队伍后面的国家，可能会有更多的百万剂药品可用。世界贸易规则允许豁免。如果不是在大流行的煎熬中援引它们，又要在什么时候援引呢?

We believe that Mr Biden is wrong. A waiver may signal that his administration cares about the world, but it is at best an empty gesture and at worst a cynical one.

我们认为拜登错了。豁免可能表明他的政府关心这个世界，但往好了说，这是装装样子，往坏了说，是一种冷漠自私的表示。

A waiver will do nothing to fill the urgent shortfall of doses in 2021. The head of the World Trade Organisation, the forum where it will be thrashed out, warns there may be no vote until December. Technology transfer would take six months or so to complete even if it started today. With the new mRNA vaccines made by Pfizer and Moderna, it may take longer. Supposing the tech transfer was faster than that, experienced vaccine-makers would be unavailable for hire and makers could not obtain inputs from suppliers whose order books are already bursting. Pfizer’s vaccine requires 280 inputs from suppliers in 19 countries. No firm can recreate that in a hurry.

thrash out 通过讨论解决；通过讨论达成

一项豁免将无助于填补2021年急需的剂量短缺。世界贸易组织（通过讨论解决问题的论坛）的总干事警告称，可能要到12月才会举行投票。即使今天开始，技术转让也需要6个月左右才能完成。辉瑞公司和Moderna公司生产的新mRNA疫苗可能需要更长的时间。假设技术转让的速度比这快，经验丰富的疫苗制造商将无暇雇佣，制造商也无法从订单已经爆满的供应商那里获得（需要的）物品。辉瑞的疫苗需要来自19个国家供应商的280项物品。没有一家公司能在短时间内再创造这些。

In any case, vaccine-makers do not appear to be hoarding their technology—otherwise output would not be increasing so fast. They have struck 214 technology-transfer agreements, an unprecedented number. They are not price-gouging: money is not the constraint on vaccination. Poor countries are not being priced out of the market: their vaccines are coming through COVAX, a global distribution scheme funded by donors.

无论如何，疫苗制造商似乎没有囤积他们的技术，否则产量不会增长得这么快。他们已经签订了214项技术转让协议，这是前所未有的数量。他们并没有哄抬价格:钱并不是限制疫苗接种的因素。贫穷国家并没有因为价格过高而被市场排除在外:它们的疫苗是通过COVAX（一个由捐助者资助的全球分销计划）提供的。

In the longer term, the effect of a waiver is unpredictable. Perhaps it will indeed lead to technology being transferred to poor countries; more likely, though, it will cause harm by disrupting supply chains, wasting resources and, ultimately, deterring in­ novation. Whatever the case, if vaccines are nearing a surplus in 2022, the cavalry will arrive too late.

从长远来看，豁免的影响是不可预测的。也许它确实会导致技术被转移到贫穷国家;但更有可能的是，它会破坏供应链，浪费资源，并最终阻碍创新，从而造成损害。无论如何，如果疫苗在2022年近乎盈余，骑兵将来不及到达。

A needle in time

及时的一针

If Mr Biden really wants to make a difference, he can donate vaccine right now through COVAX. Rich countries over-ordered because they did not know which vaccines would work. Britain has ordered more than nine doses for each adult, Canada more than 13. These will be urgently needed elsewhere. It is wrong to put teenagers, who have a minuscule risk of dying from covid-19, before the elderly and health-care workers in poor countries. The rich world should not stockpile boosters to cover the population many times over on the off-chance that they may be needed. In the next six months, this could yield billions of doses of vaccine.

minuscule 非常小的

on the off chance 碰运气

I did it on the off chance that … 我做这个想看看…

cover over 覆盖；盖住

如果拜登先生真的想有所作为，他现在就可以通过COVAX捐赠疫苗。发达国家过度订购是因为他们不知道哪种疫苗会起作用。英国为每个成年人订购了超过9剂，加拿大为每个成年人订购了超过13剂。其他地方也迫切需要这些。在贫穷国家，青少年死于covid-19的风险很小，而把他们放在老年人和卫生保健工作者之前是错误的。发达国家不应该多次囤积大量的加强剂来以备不时之需。在接下来的六个月里，这可能会产生数十亿剂疫苗。

Countries can also improve supply chains. The Serum Institute, an Indian vaccine-maker, has struggled to get parts such as filters from America because exports were gummed up by the Defence Production Act (DPA), which puts suppliers on a war-footing. Mr Biden authorised a one-off release, but he should be focusing the DPA on supplying the world instead. And better use needs to be made of finished vaccine. In some poor countries, vaccine languishes unused because of hesitancy and chaotic organisation. It makes sense to prioritise getting one shot into every vulnerable arm, before setting about the second.

gum up 使..运转不灵；搞糟

各国还可以改善供应链。印度疫苗制造商血清研究所一直在努力从美国采购过滤器等零部件，因为《国防生产法案》(DPA)破坏了出口，使供应商处于战备状态。拜登批准了一次释放，但他应该把DPA的重点放在向世界供应疫苗上。更好地利用成品疫苗。在一些贫穷国家，由于犹豫不决和组织混乱，疫苗得不到使用。在开始第二针之前，优先在每只脆弱的手臂上注射一针是有道理的。

Our model is not predictive. However it does suggest that some parts of the world are particularly vulnerable—one example is South-East Asia, home to over 650m people, which has so far been spared mass fatalities for no obvious reason. Covid-19 has not yet run its course. But vaccines have created the chance to save millions of lives. The world must not squander it. ■

squander 挥霍；浪费

我们的模型并不非预言性的。然而，它确实表明世界上的一些地区是特别脆弱的，一个例子是拥有超过6.5亿人口的东南亚，到目前为止，没有明显的理由避免了大规模死亡。covid-19尚未结束。但是疫苗创造了拯救数百万生命的机会。世界不能浪费它。■
