私人资产管理（收购基金、私人债务、风险投资等）的承诺是，持久性将得到回报。私人股本投资者必须将资金锁定数年；他们不可能轻易抛售。私人资产的巨额股权交易很少。但也有好处。私人基金经理能够获得比每天交易资产更高的回报。公开市场的投资者喜欢可预测的短期利润和战略确定性。他们太敏感以至于不愿意投资于公司扭亏为盈。如果一家上市公司的老板公布了一个100天的计划，可能会引发股票抛售。

That is the sales pitch—and plenty of investors buy it. Desperate for returns, pension funds have piled into private markets in recent years. A survey by Morgan Stanley finds that 64% of institutional investors plan to increase their allocation to private equity this year and only 5% to reduce it—a net balance of 59%. The balance for venture capital was 39%; for private debt, 33%. For listed assets, the balance was negative. Private markets are at the niche end of asset management. Only around $4trn or so is invested in private equity, about half of total assets under BlackRock’s management alone. But private assets are where the fees are. The question is whether performance and fees can be sustained.

这是一个卖点，很多投资者都买了它。由于急于获得回报，养老基金近年来纷纷涌入私人市场。摩根士丹利（morganstanley）的一项调查发现，64%的机构投资者计划今年增加对私人股本的配置，只有5%的机构投资者计划减少这一比例，净余额为59%。风险投资的余额为39%；私人债务的余额为33%。对于上市资产，余额为负数。私人市场处于资产管理的利基端。只有约4万亿美元投资于私人股本，仅贝莱德管理的总资产就约占一半。但私人资产是收费的地方。问题是业绩和收费能否持续。

Of several influences behind the growing interest in private assets, three stand out. The first is the example of successful pioneers. In the 1980s and 1990s the endowment funds of a handful of big American universities shifted much of their invested funds into private assets. The largest retirement schemes in Canada, led by the Ontario Teachers’ Pension Plan (OTPP), have a similar approach: run the plan like a business, pay for good in-house fund managers and invest in lots of private assets. This model has been copied by sovereign-wealth funds in other parts of the world. The intellectual leader of such investing was David Swensen, at Yale. He argued that, since life-insurance funds, endowments and sovereign-wealth funds have obligations stretching far into the future, they can afford to take a long-term view. It is hard to be rewarded for diligence in listed stocks. Private markets, in contrast, are inefficient. Data are hard to come by, assets are complex and trickier to appraise and waiting for opportunities to pay off requires patience. But the right homework brings rewards.

在私人资产日益增长的兴趣背后的几个影响因素中，有三个是突出的。首先是成功先驱者的榜样。在20世纪80年代和90年代，美国少数几所大大学的捐赠基金将大部分投资资金转为私人资产。由安大略省教师养老金计划（OTPP）领导的加拿大最大的退休计划也有类似的做法：像企业一样经营该计划，为优秀的内部基金经理买单，并投资大量私人资产。世界其他地区的主权财富基金也效仿了这一模式。这种投资的智力领袖是耶鲁大学的大卫·斯文森。他认为，由于人寿保险基金、捐赠基金和主权财富基金的义务延伸到了遥远的未来，他们有能力从长远角度考虑问题。在上市的股票中很难得到回报。相比之下，私人市场效率低下。数据很难获得，资产复杂且更难以评估，等待回报的机会需要耐心。但正确的准备工作会带来回报。

A second factor is disenchantment with public markets. The age-old agency problem means that investing in projects with an uncertain payoff can be a career risk for managers of a listed business. It is easier to explain corporate strategy to a few committed backers than to lots of shareholders. Founders of technology firms who are used to getting their own way often struggle in the glare of public markets, and so prefer to stay private for as long as they can. And the costs and hassle associated with being a public company have grown. The Sarbanes-Oxley act, passed in 2002 in the wake of a slew of corporate scandals in America, introduced tougher disclosure and financial-reporting requirements for public companies. The regulatory requirements on private companies are significantly lighter. And the National Securities Markets Improvement Act of 1996 made it easier to set up pools of private investors.

第二个因素是对公共市场的失望。由来已久的代理问题意味着，投资于回报不确定的项目，对上市公司的管理者来说可能是一种职业风险。向少数忠实的支持者解释公司战略要比向许多股东解释要容易得多。习惯于自行其是的科技公司的创始人常常在公共市场的耀眼光芒中挣扎，因此他们更愿意尽可能长时间地保持私有化。上市公司的成本和麻烦也在增加。萨班斯-奥克斯利法案（Sarbanes-Oxley act）是在美国发生一系列公司丑闻后于2002年通过的，它对上市公司提出了更严格的披露和财务报告要求。对私营公司的监管要求明显较轻。1996年的《国家证券市场改善法案》使得建立私人投资者池变得更加容易。

Asset managers, starved of yield in the government-bond markets, are happy to fill the void. The bigger firms will even take souring loans off the books of banks looking to clean up their balance-sheets. In 2017 PIMCO, the fixed-income giant, led a buy-out of €17.7bn ($20bn) of loans from UniCredit, an Italian bank. There are likely to be more such deals in Europe. 第三个因素是银行业的变化。私人债务的增长在很大程度上是对银行不再向中型企业及其私募股权赞助商放贷的回应。缺乏政府债券市场收益的资产管理公司乐于填补这一空白。更大的公司甚至会将不良贷款从那些希望清理资产负债表的银行的账簿中剥离。2017年，固定收益巨头太平洋投资管理公司（PIMCO）牵头收购了意大利联合信贷银（UniCredit）的177亿欧元（200亿美元）贷款。在欧洲，此类交易可能会更多。

One of the fastest-growing areas of private credit is direct lending to companies which cannot (because they are too small) or will not (for reasons of confidentiality) tap the public markets. A private bond might be sold to only a handful of lenders, or even to just one. Borrowers may feel that they ought to know who their creditors are because they might have to renegotiate with them. That is the case for private-equity firms. Specialist private-credit funds also often prefer to be the sole financiers of a private-equity buy-out if they like the terms and judge the bought-out firm to be a good risk. They might even be the credit division of a buy-out outfit that has lost the bidding war for the borrowing company.

私人信贷增长最快的领域之一是直接向那些不能(因为规模太小)或不愿(由于保密原因)进入公开市场的公司放贷。私人债券可能只卖给少数几个贷款人，甚至只卖给一家。借款人可能觉得他们应该知道债权人是谁，因为他们可能不得不与债权人重新谈判。私募股权公司的情况就是如此。专业的私人信贷基金通常也愿意成为私募股权收购的唯一出资人，如果他们喜欢条款并且认为被收购的公司具有良好的风险。它们甚至可能是一家收购机构的信贷部门，该机构在对借款公司的竞购战中落败。

One suspicion is that allocation to private equity is simply a way for pension funds to get around constraints on borrowing to enhance returns. But the buy-out industry has a decent story to tell on capital allocation. The academic literature finds that private-equity and venture-capital funds mostly add operational nous to businesses. They inspire better management habits than in entrepreneur- or family-owned firms. Buy-outs lead to modest net job losses but big increases in job creation and destruction. They promote efficiency by taking capital off “sunset” firms and putting it into more promising “sunrise” firms.

And returns? Asset managers are adept at presenting statistics in the most favourable light. Dud mutual funds are often quietly merged or folded. Managers can then claim that most of their funds beat the market—these being simply the funds that have survived the cull of underperformers. The private-equity business is notorious for selecting metrics that flatter its performance. Nonetheless, over the long haul, the best private-equity funds do really well. A landmark study led by Steven Kaplan, of the University of Chicago, found that venture-capital and buy-out funds, on average, beat the S&P 500 index over the long term. The range was wide. Funds in the top quartile did much better than average; those in the bottom quartile did a lot worse. Pension-fund managers facing big deficits have an incentive to put money into private assets in the hope that their fund will be one of the winners.

PARTISANSHIP HAS long coloured American perceptions of covid-19. Even so, the contrast between the top echelons of the main parties was striking on November 9th, the day the country passed 10m recorded cases of the disease. Edward Altman of NYU Stern School of Business is worried about what he calls “the enormous build-up of non-financial corporate debt.” By his estimation, firms have issued more than $360bn in high-yield debt (ie, junk bonds) so far this year, surpassing the previous record of $345bn in all of 2012. With debt-earnings ratios reaching critical levels, and a resurgence in corporate defaults, Mr Altman reckons that 6.5% to 7% of junk bonds, by dollar value, will default in 2020.

Pile on 积累

Build-up 积聚

junk bonds 风险债券，垃圾债券

如果说复苏的不一致性是一个担忧，那么另一个担忧则是企业资产负债表的状况。在流感大流行之前，公司债务一直在上升，许多公司为了弥补今年的收入缺口，已经积累了更多的借款。纽约大学斯特恩商学院（NYU Stern School of Business）的爱德华·奥尔特曼（Edward Altman）对他所说的“非金融企业债务的巨大积累”感到担忧。据他估计，今年迄今为止，各公司已发行了3600亿美元的高收益债券（即垃圾债券），超过了2012年全年3450亿美元的历史纪录。随着债务收益率达到临界水平，再加上企业违约事件的重新抬头，奥尔特曼估计，以美元计，6.5%至7%的垃圾债券将在2020年违约。

His fears are echoed by S&P Global, a credit-rating agency. It calculates that the “distress ratio” (distressed credits are junk bonds with spreads of more than ten percentage points relative to US Treasuries) for American companies had come down to 9.5% in September from its peak of 36% in March but that it remains above pre-pandemic levels. Corporate America already leads the world in the tally of corporate defaults this year, with 127 by the end of October. Nicole Serino of S&P Global notes that corporate credit quality is deteriorating, with the number of firms rated a lowly CCC+ or below now 50% higher than at the end of 2019. For such firms, she worries that “excess liquidity and low interest rates are only postponing the inevitable.”

Echo 附和

excess liquidity 过分流动性，超额换现能力，超额流动性资产

他的担忧得到了信用评级机构标准普尔全球（S&P Global）的呼应。它计算出，美国公司的“困境比率”（不良信贷是相对于美国国债利差超过10个百分点的垃圾债券）从3月份36%的峰值下降到9.5%，但仍高于大流行前的水平。今年，美国企业违约率已居全球首位，截至10月底，已达127起。标普全球（S&P Global）的妮可·塞里诺（Nicole Serino）指出，企业信贷质量正在恶化，评级为CCC+或以下的企业数量目前比2019年底增加了50%。对于这些公司，她担心“流动性过剩和低利率只会推迟不可避免的事情。”

With a large share of firms still making losses and given the weakening of balance-sheets it is far from clear that American business is in the clear. What happens next depends on three unknowns. One is the fallout from this week’s presidential vote. A prolonged period of post-election uncertainty would weigh on the mood, notes Mr Levkovich. He points to the 11% fall in the S&P 500 index after the election in 2000 while legal wrangling decided the outcome of the contest for the presidency between George W. Bush and Al Gore.

Weigh on （使）某人焦虑

由于仍有很大一部分公司仍在亏损，而且鉴于资产负债表的疲软，美国的商业状况还远未明朗。接下来会发生什么取决于三个未知数。一是本周总统选举的影响。利夫科维奇指出，选举后长期的不确定性将打压情绪。他指出，2000年大选后，标准普尔500指数下跌11%，而法律纠纷决定了小布什和戈尔总统竞选的结果。

Another unknown is the timing and size of the next package of fiscal stimulus from Congress, which at the moment is frozen by partisan gridlock in Washington, DC, and which could be limited if the Republicans keep firm control of the Senate. This matters to companies because, as Mr Golub puts it, “the government has effectively said, ‘We do not want market forces to drive firms out of business right now and so we are going to backstop a large part of the economy.’” Mr Wilson believes that the number of companies going bankrupt so far this year has been much lower than otherwise feared because of generous stimulus measures.

fiscal stimulus 财政刺激

at the moment 此刻;目前;眼下

market forces 市场调节作用 ; 市场力量

out of business 停业 ; 破产

Effectively 实际上

另一个未知数是国会下一轮财政刺激方案的时间和规模，目前由于华盛顿特区党派僵局而被冻结，如果共和党继续牢牢控制参议院，刺激计划可能会受到限制。这对公司来说很重要，因为正如戈卢布所说，“政府实际上已经说过，“我们现在不希望市场力量使企业破产，因此我们将在很大程度上支撑经济。”威尔逊认为，由于慷慨的刺激措施，今年迄今破产的公司数量远低于人们担心的水平。

The biggest unknown, though, is the pandemic. Moody’s, a credit-rating agency, predicts that corporate-debt defaults will continue to rise until March 2021. The reason it gives is “economic recovery remains fragile amid risks of another pandemic resurgence leading to another round of countrywide lockdowns”. That should serve as a sober reminder to the next president and corporate bosses alike that, despite a rebound, there may yet be difficult days ahead for USA Inc.■

不过，最大的未知是大流行。信用评级机构穆迪（Moody’s）预测，企业债务违约将持续上升，直至2021年3月。它给出的理由是“经济复苏仍然脆弱，因为有可能再次出现大流行，导致另一轮全国性的封锁”。这应该是一个清醒的提醒下一任总统和公司老板们，尽管有反弹，但美国公司未来的日子可能会很艰难。
