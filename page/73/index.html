Buy American is an economic-policy mistake

购买美国货是一个经济政策错误

The folly of buying local

买本地货的愚蠢行为

![image-20210202104415194](/Users/chenyujie/Library/Application Support/typora-user-images/image-20210202104415194.png)

President Biden’s protectionism sullies his economic agenda

protectionism 贸易保护主义

sully 损害

拜登总统的贸易保护主义损害了他的经济议程

Jan 30th 2021

MODERN PRESIDENTS of the United States do not sit in the Oval Office for long before reaching for the pen. Since entering the White House on January 20th Joe Biden has signed nearly 40 executive orders and proclamations. Many are welcome; some are crucial. He is overturning some of the harshest immigration restrictions imposed by Donald Trump and restoring America’s support of the Paris agreement on climate change. One of Mr Biden’s edicts, however, is an early economic-policy mistake: the tightening of rules obliging America’s federal government to prefer domestic suppliers to foreign ones. It could be a sign of worse errors to come.

overturn 撤销

edict 命令；法令

oblige 强迫

最新的美国总统在椭圆形办公室坐不了多久就会伸手去拿笔。自1月20日入主白宫以来，乔·拜登已经签署了近40项行政命令和公告。很多是受欢迎的;有些是至关重要的。他推翻了唐纳德·特朗普施加的一些最严厉的移民限制，恢复了美国对气候变化巴黎协议的支持。然而，拜登的一项法令是一个早期的经济政策错误:强制美国联邦政府偏爱国内供应商而不是外国供应商的规则变得更加严格。这可能预示着更严重的错误即将到来。

The “Buy American” agenda is long-running. A law passed in 1933 requires the federal government to prefer domestically produced goods to foreign ones; it is reinforced by an unwieldy mass of similar state and local rules. The stimulus Mr Biden oversaw as vice-president after the global financial crisis targeted American suppliers with American supply chains. President Trump issued reams of executive orders aiming to favour domestic firms over their foreign competitors. One of his rule changes, which raises the bar for an item to be considered American-made and increases the mark-up over global prices which domestic firms can charge, has still to come into effect.

long-running 长期的

domestically 在国内

domestically produced goods 本国产品

unwieldy 复杂的；运作不灵的

reams 大量的

mark up 给..加价

“购买美国货”议程是长期的。1933年通过的一项法律要求联邦政府偏爱本国产品而不是外国产品;大量类似的州和地方法规又强化了这一点。全球金融危机后，拜登担任副总统期间监管的刺激计划针对的是拥有美国供应链的美国供应商。特朗普总统发布了大量旨在偏袒国内企业而非外国竞争对手的行政命令。他的一项规则修改仍然在生效，这项修改提高了被视为美国制造的商品的门槛，并提高了国内企业可以收取的全球价格的加价幅度。

Mr Biden campaigned on going further than Mr Trump, whom he castigated for not doing enough. It will take more than a stroke of the pen to fulfil that promise. As past administrations have discovered, America’s commitments to the World Trade Organisation (WTO) constrain its ability to discriminate in favour of its own firms. As a result, Mr Biden’s order only fiddles at the margins, confirming Mr Trump’s last changes, giving the White House more oversight of exemptions from the rules and introducing a vague new requirement that Washington’s purchases should promote employment.

castigate 严厉批评

discriminate 区别对待

discriminate in favour of 对…特别优待；另眼看待

discriminate in favour of sb/sth 偏爱某人/某物

fiddle 篡改

拜登在竞选时表示，他比特朗普走得更远，他指责特朗普做得不够。要实现这一承诺，光大笔一挥是不够的。正如历届政府所发现的那样，美国对世界贸易组织(WTO)的承诺限制了其对本国企业特别优待的能力。其结果是，拜登的命令只是在边缘上摆弄，证实了特朗普的最后修改，让白宫对豁免规则有了更多的监督，并引入了一个模糊的新要求，即华盛顿的购买应该促进就业。

Yet the president has the support in Congress to go further if he wants to. He has promised a diplomatic effort to modernise—code for weaken—the WTO rules. And they can in any case be circumvented by sending money to states and attaching conditions on how they spend it, which is how the Democrats might structure their stimulus and infrastructure spending.

in any case 不管怎样 ; 不管怎样，无论如何 ; 不管怎样，再者

circumvented 设法回避 ; 规避 ; 绕过 ; 绕行 ; 绕道旅行

structure 安排

然而，如果总统愿意的话，他在国会有足够的支持可以走得更远。他承诺将通过外交手段使世贸组织规则现代化(即弱化规则)。无论如何，他们都可以通过向各州提供资金并对其支出方式附加条件来规避，这就是民主党可能会采取的结构刺激计划和基础设施支出的方式。

Presidents and voters like Buy American because they think it creates jobs. In a direct sense, it does. But by locking firms out of global supply chains and shielding them from competition it promotes inefficiency, destroying more employment than it creates. By one estimate America would gain a net 300,000 jobs if it got rid of its local-content rules.

总统和选民喜欢“买美国货”，因为他们认为这能创造就业机会。从直接意义上说，确实如此。但是，通过将企业排除在全球供应链之外，保护它们免受竞争的影响，它会导致效率低下，摧毁的就业机会比它创造的就业机会还多。据估计，如果取消本地内容规则，美国将净增加30万个工作岗位。

There is no evidence that buying at home boosts innovation either, whatever its advocates might claim. In fact, it almost certainly hampers productivity increases in the long term. And coddling local firms is a raw deal for taxpayers. From February American firms will be able to charge their government up to 20% more than prevailing global prices.

coddle 悉心照顾

raw 不公正的

a raw deal 不公的待遇

get/have a raw deal 受到不公正待遇

give sb a raw deal 对某人苛刻

无论其倡导者会怎么说，也没有证据表明在国内购买能促进创新。事实上，从长期来看，它几乎肯定会阻碍生产率的提高。溺爱本地公司对纳税人来说是不公平的。从2月份开始，美国公司可以向政府收取比全球现行价格高出20%的费用。

Perhaps the sturdiest economic rationale for buying local is that it stops the short-term stimulatory effect of new spending—of which Mr Biden is planning a lot—from leaking overseas. But that should not be a big concern for America in 2021 because its stimulus is so enormous. Were Mr Biden’s $1.9trn spending proposal to pass, it would take America’s cumulative pandemic spending to more than a quarter of its pre-crisis GDP (before considering the additional effects of any infrastructure bill). America can afford a little leakage. In any case, the lesson from 2009 is that requirements to buy American hamper stimulus by forcing firms to look for new suppliers before they can start projects. It is not encouraging that Mr Biden’s order will make it still harder to obtain exemptions from the existing mountain of red tape.

rationale 根本原因

red tape 繁文缛节

购买本土产品的最坚实的经济理由或许是，它阻止了拜登计划大量增加的新支出的短期刺激效应外泄到海外。但对于2021年的美国来说，这应该不是什么大问题，因为它的经济刺激是如此巨大。如果拜登1.9万亿美元的支出提案获得通过，那么美国在大流行期间的累计支出将超过危机前GDP的四分之一(在考虑任何基础设施法案的额外影响之前)。美国可以承受一点点的泄漏。无论如何，2009年的教训是，购买美国货的要求会迫使企业在开工前寻找新的供应商，从而阻碍经济刺激。拜登的命令将使其更难从目前堆积如山的繁文缛节中获得豁免，这并不令人鼓舞。

It is a relief that, unlike Mr Trump, Mr Biden supports the rules-based system of global trade. He will not sabotage the WTO or go out of his way to antagonise America’s allies, whom he wants to rally against XX. But his soft protectionism—which, thanks to the Trumpification of the Republican Party, is more popular in Congress than it has been in decades—will rankle the outside world; the European Union, which recently reached an investment agreement with XX, is already bristling at his executive order. In his instincts about the economics of trade, America’s new president is not so different from his predecessor. That is bad news for America and for the world. ■

sabotage 蓄意破坏

antagonise 引起..的反感

rankle 使..怨恨

bristle at sth 对..大发光火

令人欣慰的是，与特朗普不同，拜登支持基于规则的全球贸易体系。他不会破坏世贸组织，也不会刻意与他想团结起来对抗XX的美国盟友作对。但是他的软贸易保护主义——多亏了共和党的特朗普化，这在国会比过去几十年都更受欢迎——将激怒外部世界;欧盟最近与XX达成了一项投资协议，已经对他的行政命令感到愤怒。在他对贸易经济的直觉上，美国新总统与他的前任并没有太大的不同。这对美国和世界来说都是坏消息。■

Why you should ignore the siren call of market timing

siren call 危险的诱惑

为什么你要无视市场时机的危险的诱惑

![image-20210201101309180](/Users/chenyujie/Library/Application Support/typora-user-images/image-20210201101309180.png)

Buttonwood

梧桐

Few manage to profitably outwit the herd

profitably 有赢利地

outwit 智胜；骗过

很少有人能成功地智胜大众

Jan 30th 2021

HERE IS A sage piece of investment advice you might not usually find in any tip-sheet, newsletter or “thoughtful” weekly column on capital markets. It comes from Will Rogers, a popular entertainer and wit of interwar America, via the writings of Paul Samuelson, a Nobel prizewinning economist and wit of post-war America. Are you ready? Here it is. You should buy stocks when they are going to go up. When they are going to go down, you should sell them.

sage 睿智的

entertainer 表演者

interwar 两次世界大战之间的

wit 说话风趣的人

writing 作品

go up 上涨，上升

go down 下降，下跌

这里有一条明智的投资建议，你可能不会在任何关于资本市场的报章、时事通讯或“深思熟虑的”每周专栏中找到。这句话是由两次世界大战之间的美国著名艺人、智者威尔·罗杰斯(Will Rogers)通过诺贝尔经济学奖得主、战后美国智者保罗·萨缪尔森(Paul Samuelson)的著作提出的。你准备好了吗?在这儿。你应该在股票要涨的时候买进。当股价下跌时，你应该卖掉它们。

Nice work if you can get it, quipped Samuelson. It is a shame only a few can. Yet the idea of timing the market—all ups, no downs—remains a seductive one. Anyone who invests in equities has at one time or another fancied they can sell at the top and buy again at the bottom, thus enjoying the return from stocks while avoiding the risks. It seems simple. If stocks are dear and investors look heedless, you should get out of the market. When it falls back, as it surely must, fill your boots.

quip 俏皮地说

heedless 不加注意的；漫不经心的

萨缪尔森打趣道:“如果你能明白，那就太好了。”可惜只有少数人能做到。然而，把握市场时机的想法——只涨不跌——仍然是一个诱人的想法。任何投资股票的人都曾幻想自己可以在顶部卖出，在底部再买，从而在享受股票回报的同时避免风险。看起来简单。如果股票很贵，而投资者看起来心不在焉，你就应该退出市场。当它回落时(这是必然的)，把你的靴子填满。

There is a lot of this thinking around just now. The cyclically adjusted price-earnings (CAPE) ratio, a measure of value constructed by Robert Shiller of Yale University, has rarely been higher. The combination of social media and low-cost trading apps aimed at small investors is behind a lot of herding into faddish stocks, a signifier of toppy markets. In such circumstances it is tempting to try to outwit the herd. But market timing is harder than it looks. Few have the skill, temperament or the focus to do it profitably.

herd into sth 成群进入某处

现在有很多这样的想法。由耶鲁大学的罗伯特•希勒(Robert Shiller)构建的价值衡量指标–周期性调整市盈率(CAPE)很少有更高的。社交媒体和面向小投资者的低成本交易应用程序的结合，是大量投资者涌向流行一时的股票的背后原因。流行一时的股票是市场过热的标志。在这种情况下，试图智胜大众是很诱人的。但市场时机选择比看起来要难。很少有人有能力、有性情或有专注力去做一件有利可图的事。

![image-20210201101223944](/Users/chenyujie/Library/Application Support/typora-user-images/image-20210201101223944.png)

Stock prices are noisy. The axiom used to be they are a random walk: their current levels tell us nothing about where they are going. A less purist view has since emerged. This says the earnings yield—the inverse of the CAPE—is a decent guide to the expected returns on equities in the longer run. Put simply, high stock prices now mean lower returns in future. At today’s CAPE, expected returns are well below the long-run average, just as they were in the late 1920s and the late 1990s (see chart).

axiom 公理

股票价格波动很大。过去的公理是它们是一种随机行走:它们当前的水平并不能告诉我们它们要去哪里。此后出现了一种不那么纯粹的观点。这表明收益率——与周期性调整市盈率的倒数——是一个很好的长期股票预期收益的指南。简单地说，现在的高股价意味着未来的低回报。在今天的CAPE下，预期收益远低于长期平均水平，就像20世纪20年代末和90年代末一样(见图表)。

The sharp-eyed will notice that whenever stock prices have gone up a lot faster than earnings, they tend to fall back again. But would-be market timers could not have known precisely when to sell. It is never obvious that the CAPE is close to a peak or trough. Studies of timing guided by valuation metrics such as the CAPE show disappointing results compared with just buying and holding stocks for the duration. A big problem is selling too early. As Samuelson, who wrote a lot about the perils of timing, once put it: “**Anything can be carried on to twice where it has already reached.**” When the market does crater, it is not so easy to act. It takes nerve to buy when everyone is selling. Delay seems wise. Prices could always fall again. The fate of many a market-timer is to buy stocks back at higher prices than those at which they sold.

would-be 想要成为的

peaks and troughs 高峰和低谷

carry on 继续

眼尖的人会注意到，无论什么时候股票价格涨得比收益快得多，它们往往又会回落。但那些想要抢占市场的人不可能准确地知道何时该出手。CAPE接近高峰或低谷从来都不明显。以CAPE等估值指标为指导的时机研究显示，与仅仅买入并持有股票的持续时间相比，结果令人失望。一个大问题是过早出售。萨缪尔森写过很多关于时机的危险的文章，他曾经说过:“任何事情都可以延续到已经达到的两倍。”“当市场真的暴跌时，采取行动就不那么容易了。人人都在卖的时候买需要勇气。拖延似乎是明智的。价格总有可能再次下跌。许多做市者的命运就是以高于卖出价格的价格买回股票。

Despite today’s low expected returns, shares still have some appeal, because of the paucity of returns on offer in other asset markets. In the late 1990s, when the CAPE soared above 40, the yield on inflation-protected Treasuries was close to 4%. Today it is -1% on the ten-year bond. If low real rates are the main prop for share prices, then any attempt to time the stockmarket is in essence a bet on the bond market—and, in turn, on how inflation evolves, and how central banks react to it. Good luck with getting those calls right. The forces behind the decades-long decline in real interest rates and quiescent inflation are not well understood even by people who have spent a lot of time thinking about them.

paucity 不足

prop 支柱者

quiescent 静止的；不活跃的

尽管目前的预期回报率很低，但股票仍具有一定的吸引力，因为其他资产市场的回报率很少。上世纪90年代末，CAPE飙升至40以上时，通胀保值国债的收益率接近4%。现在10年期债券利率是-1%。如果低实际利率是股价的主要支撑因素，那么任何对股市时机的预测都是对债券市场的一种押注——反过来，也是对通胀如何演变以及央行如何应对通胀的一种押注。祝你能准确做决定。几十年来，实际利率持续下降，通货膨胀趋于平静，即便是那些花了很多时间思考这些问题的人，也无法很好地理解它们背后的力量。

It is hard for most investors to make judgments about whether, when and how quickly these secular trends will go into reverse. Yes, there is something screwy about negative real bond yields. In America they are a novelty. But in Europe and Japan they have lasted for far longer than many people thought possible. Agnosticism about their future path is probably the best policy.

secular 世俗的；在俗的

screwy 疯狂的；古怪的

Agnosticism 不可知论

对于大多数投资者来说，很难判断这些长期趋势是否、何时以及以多快的速度逆转。是的，实际债券收益率为负确实有些古怪之处。在美国，它们是新鲜事物。但在欧洲和日本，这种情况持续的时间远远超过了许多人的预期。对他们未来道路的不可知论可能是最好的政策。

It would be lovely to have ups without downs. But investment rewards generally come with risks. The advice from market-timing sceptics like Samuelson is of the mom-and-apple-pie kind. Sell down your stockholdings to the sleeping point, where you can rest easy at night. Spread your bets widely across stocks and geographies—stockmarkets outside America have lower CAPE ratios and higher expected returns. And remember, timing is a snare. If there were reliable trading signals, everyone would follow them. And then there would be no one to sell to at the top and no one to buy from at the bottom.

motherhood and apple pie （对美国人来说）十全十美的东西

snare 陷阱

只有上涨没有下跌是多么美好。但投资回报通常伴随着风险。萨缪尔森等对市场时机持怀疑态度的人给出的建议是一种十全十美的做法。把你的股票减持到你可以安心睡觉的程度。将你的赌注广泛地分散在股票和地域上——美国以外的股票市场的CAPE比率较低，而预期回报较高。记住，时机是一个陷阱。如果有可靠的交易信号，每个人都会跟随它们。这样一来，顶部就没有人卖出，底部也不再有人买入。

You’ll often walk alone

你会经常独自行走

Bartleby 巴托比

![image-20210131103349592](/Users/chenyujie/Library/Application Support/typora-user-images/image-20210131103349592.png)

Loneliness is a widespread problem with complex roots

孤独是一个有着复杂根源的普遍问题

Jan 28th 2021

THERE HAS been a quiet pandemic developing while most people’s attention has been on covid-19. The lockdown has exacerbated a problem that has been spreading in many developed nations for decades: loneliness.

当大多数人的注意力都集中在covid-19时，大流行正在悄然发展。封锁加剧了几十年来在许多发达国家蔓延的一个问题:孤独。

It is a complex issue which covers not only social lives, but the way you work and the way you vote. Noreena Hertz, an academic, tackles the subject in an important new book, “The Lonely Century”.

这是一个复杂的问题，不仅涉及社会生活，还涉及你的工作方式和投票方式。学者Noreena Hertz在其重要的新书《孤独的世纪》中探讨了这个问题。

Loneliness increases the risk of heart disease, strokes and dementia. Those who say they are lonely are likelier to be depressed five years later. In addition, lonely people can become more hostile towards others and more attracted to extremist politics.

孤独会增加患心脏病、中风和痴呆的风险。那些说自己孤独的人更有可能在五年后感到抑郁。此外，孤独的人会变得对他人更有敌意，更容易被极端政治所吸引。

Part of the problem stems from contemporary employment. Globally, two in five office workers feel lonely at work. This rises to three in five in Britain. Gig-economy jobs can leave people with insecure incomes and without the companionship of colleagues. The pandemic has made it more difficult to make, and maintain, friendships, particularly for new employees.

部分问题源于当代就业。在全球范围内，五分之二的上班族在工作时感到孤独。在英国，这一比例上升至五分之三。零工经济工作会让人们收入不稳定，也没有同事的陪伴。疫情使得建立和维持友谊变得更加困难，特别是对新员工而言。

Even before the crisis, the hope that open-plan offices would encourage greater camaraderie proved to be false. Many people find the chatter distracting and retreat with noise-cancelling headphones; they then email colleagues who are sitting only a few desks away.

camaraderie 同志情谊

noise-cancelling 降噪的

甚至在金融危机之前，那种认为开放式办公室能增进同事间友谊的希望就被证明是错误的。许多人发现聊天会分散注意力，于是戴上降噪耳机退避;然后，他们会给坐在几张桌子之外的同事发邮件。

Co-working spaces, where young professionals can take advantage of communal facilities, have not been the answer either. Workers are not there long enough to invest in relationships. As Ms Hertz puts it: “Hot deskers are the workplace equivalent of the renters who’ve never met their neighbours.”

communal 公共的

Hot desk 无固定办公桌的现代办公室

The secrets of successful listening

成功倾听的秘诀

Bartleby

巴托比

![image-20210124100416981](/Users/chenyujie/Library/Application Support/typora-user-images/image-20210124100416981.png)

Lessons from a hostage negotiator. Hear, hear

人质谈判专家的教训。听,听

Jan 21st 2021

“WHEN PEOPLE talk, listen completely.” Those words of Ernest Hemingway might be a pretty good guiding principle for many managers, as might the dictum enunciated by Zeno of Citium, a Greek philosopher: “We have two ears and one mouth, so we should listen more than we say.” For people like being listened to.

guiding principle 方针，指导原则

dictum 名言 ; 格言

enunciate 阐明

“当别人说话的时候，要全心倾听。海明威(Ernest Hemingway)的这句话可能是许多经理人的很好的指导原则，正如希腊哲学家西提姆(Citium)的芝诺(Zeno of Citium)的名言一样:“我们有两只耳朵一张嘴，所以应该多听少说。”因为人们喜欢被倾听。

Some firms use a technique known as a “listening circle” in which participants are encouraged to talk openly and honestly about the issues they face (such as problems with colleagues). In such a circle, only one person can talk at a time and there is no interruption. A study cited in the Harvard Business Review found that employees who had taken part in a listening circle subsequently suffered less social anxiety and had fewer worries about work-related matters than those who did not.

一些公司采用一种被称为“倾听圈”的技术，鼓励参与者开诚布公地谈论他们所面临的问题(比如与同事之间的问题)。在这样的圈子里，一次只能有一个人说话，不会有人打断。《哈佛商业评论》(Harvard Business Review)引用的一项研究发现，参加聆听小组的员工随后比不参加的员工更少患上社交焦虑，对工作相关事宜的担忧也更少。

Listening has been critical to the career of Richard Mullender, who was a British police officer for 30 years. Eventually he became a hostage negotiator, dealing with everything from suicide interventions to international kidnaps. By the end of his stint in uniform, he was the lead trainer for the Metropolitan Police’s hostage-negotiation unit.

倾听对理查德·马伦德(Richard Mullender)的职业生涯至关重要，他在英国当了30年警察。最终，他成为了一名人质谈判专家，处理从自杀干预到国际绑架的一切事务。在制服服役结束时，他成了伦敦警察厅人质谈判小组的首席培训师。

When he left the force in 2007, he realised that his skills might be applicable in the business world. So he set up a firm called the Listening Institute. Mr Mullender defines listening as “the identification, selection and interpretation of the key words that turn information into intelligence”. It is crucial to all effective communication.

2007年离开警队时，他意识到自己的技能可能适用于商界。所以他成立了一家公司，叫做“听力研究所”。马伦德将倾听定义为“识别、选择和解读将信息转化为情报的关键词”。这是所有有效沟通的关键。

Plenty of people think that good listening is about nodding your head or keeping eye contact. But that is not really listening, Mr Mullender argues. A good listener is always looking for facts, emotions and indications of the interlocutor’s values. And when it comes to a negotiation, people are looking for an outcome. The aim of listening is to ascertain what the other side is trying to achieve.

interlocutor 参加谈话者 ; 对话者 ; 中间对话者

很多人认为好的倾听就是点头或保持眼神交流。但马伦德认为，这并不是真正的倾听。一个好的倾听者总是在寻找事实、情感和对方价值观的暗示。当涉及到谈判时，人们都在寻找一个结果。倾听的目的是确定对方想要达到什么目的。

Another important point to bear in mind is that, when you talk, you are not listening. “Every time you share an opinion, you give out information about yourself,” Mr Mullender says. In contrast, a good listener, by keeping quiet, gains an edge over his or her counterpart.

另外要记住的一点是，当你说话的时候，你并不是在倾听。马伦德表示:“每次你分享自己的观点时，你就透露了自己的信息。”相反，一个好的倾听者，如果能保持沉默，就会比对方更有优势。

Hostage negotiators usually work in teams, but the lead negotiator is the only one who talks. “What we teach is that the second person in the team doesn’t really talk at all, because if they are busy thinking about the next question to ask, they aren’t really listening,” Mr Mullender explains.

人质谈判人员通常以团队的形式工作，但首席谈判人员是唯一发言的人。马伦德解释说:“我们教授的是，团队中的第二个人根本就不会真正说话，因为如果他们正忙于思考下一个问题，他们就没有真正在倾听。”

The mistake many people make is to ask too many questions, rather than letting the other person talk. The listener’s focus should be on analysis. If you are trying to persuade someone to do something, you need to know what their beliefs are. If someone is upset, you need to assess their emotional state.

许多人犯的错误是问太多的问题，而不是让别人说话。听众的注意力应该放在分析上。如果你试图说服某人做某事，你需要知道他们的信仰是什么。如果某人很难过，你需要评估他们的情绪状态。

Of course, a listener needs to speak occasionally. One approach is to make an assessment of what the other person is telling you and then check it with them (“It seems to me that what you want is X”). That gives the other party a sense that they are being understood. The fundamental aim is to build up a relationship so the other person likes you and trusts you, Mr Mullender says.

当然，听众需要偶尔说话。一种方法是评估别人告诉你的内容，然后和他们核对(“在我看来，你想要的是某某”)。这会给对方一种他们被理解的感觉。马伦德表示，这样做的根本目的是建立一种关系，让对方喜欢你、信任你。

但马伦德表示，肢体语言构成的成分太多了。如果你能听见却看不见一个人，比你能看见却听不见一个人要容易理解得多。他喜欢用电话谈判。

Another key to good listening is paying attention and avoiding distraction. In the information age, it is all too easy for focus to drift to a news headline, a TikTok video or the latest outrage on Twitter. Mr Mullender thinks that many people have become frustrated in their isolation, which can lead to stress and anger. He thinks there may be a business opportunity in helping managers listen more efficiently, so they can enhance employee well-being. After a year of isolation, many workers would probably love the chance to be heard.
