The shortcuts to Theranos

Theranos的捷径

Bartleby

巴托比

The trial of Elizabeth Holmes contains lessons for decision-makers of all kinds

对Elizabeth Holmes的审判为各种各样的决策者提供了教训

文章音频

EARLY ON IN “The Inventor”, a documentary about Elizabeth Holmes, the founder of Theranos, she talks about her childhood-reading habits and her interest in what makes a great leader. “So much changes in our society technologically but as humans we don’t change a lot,” she says in that slow, deep voice.

在关于Theranos创始人Elizabeth Holmes的纪录片《发明家》的开头，她谈到了自己童年时的阅读习惯，以及她对“如何成为伟大领导者”的兴趣。她用缓慢而低沉的声音说道: “我们的社会在技术上发生了如此多的变化，但作为人类，我们的变化并不大”。

Whatever the verdict in Ms Holmes’s ongoing trial, on charges of defrauding investors and patients by making false claims about the startup’s blood-testing technology, she is right about that. The story of Theranos is not simply about how its leaders behaved, and whether they deliberately misled others. (Ms Holmes denies the charges.) It is also about how people take decisions. The trial has shown how cognitive shortcuts helped propel the firm to a valuation of more than $9bn, before reporting by the Wall Street Journal, revealing that its proprietary technology did not work, sent it spiralling towards oblivion.

Holmes女士被指控通过虚假声明“其初创公司Theranos的血液检测技术”，欺骗投资者和患者，无论目前正在进行的审判会做出什么样的裁决，她的看法是对的。Theranos的故事不仅仅关乎其领导者的行为，以及他们是否故意误导他人。(Holmes女士否认了这些指控。) 它也与人们如何做出决策有关。这次审判表明，认知捷径是如何帮助该公司估值超过90亿美元，后来《华尔街日报》报道称，该公司的专利技术“根本没用”，这就导致其逐渐被遗忘。

One shortcut concerned Ms Holmes herself. On the face of it, Theranos’s product was a blood-testing device. In fact, the firm’s real product was its founder, a fundraising machine in a turtleneck. Her charisma intoxicated investors, lured employees and charmed the elder statesmen who crammed the startup’s board. Her story—a female entrepreneur who had dropped out of Stanford to disrupt the world of health care—was catnip to journalists.

一条捷径与Holmes女士本人有关。从表面上看，Theranos的产品是一种血液检测设备。事实上，该公司真正的产品是它的创始人，一个穿着高领毛衣的筹款“机器”。她非凡的个人魅力迷住了投资者，吸引了员工，还引诱了政界元老挤满该初创公司的董事会。她的故事，即意味从斯坦福大学辍学去颠覆医疗保健行业的女企业家，对记者们很有吸引力。

Charisma is a reasonable trait for investors to like in a startup founder. But people too easily equate confidence with competence. In a study from 2012, researchers from the University of Lausanne taught a bunch of mid-level managers a set of “charismatic leadership tactics”—from three-point lists and moral conviction to hand gestures. Observers’ assessment of the managers’ competence leapt as a result.

个人魅力是投资者喜欢创业公司创始人的一个合理特征。但人们太容易将自信等同于能力。在2012年的一项研究中，洛桑大学的研究人员向一群中层管理者传授了一套“魅力型领导策略”，即三要素清单、道德信念以及手势。结果就是，在观察员的评估发现，管理者能力激增。

Some of Ms Holmes’s charisma may likewise have been learned. Her defence team has entered two handwritten documents into the court records. One is a note in which she lays out a list of instructions to herself for the day ahead. The other is a set of business rules penned for her by Sunny Balwani, Theranos’s one-time chief operating officer and Ms Holmes’s former lover, whose own trial begins next year. (Ms Holmes’s defence rests in part on her allegations that she was abused, moulded and controlled by Mr Balwani, which he denies.)

Holmes女士的一些魅力也可能是后天习得的。她的辩护团队将两份手写文件记入法庭记录。其中一份文件是她为自己列出了一天的指令清单。另一份文件是Sunny Balwani为霍姆斯撰写的一套商业规则。Sunny Balwani曾是Theranos的首席运营官，也是霍姆斯的前男友，他自己的(欺诈罪)审判将于明年开始。（Holmes女士的辩护部分是基于她的指控，即她受到Balwani先生的虐待、塑造以及控制，Balwani先生对此予以否认。）

The documents themselves look risible. “All the laws of nature, all secrets, are imprinted on every cell of our body,” writes Mr Balwani, a somewhat concerning statement from the leader of a medical startup. Ms Holmes’s note includes lines such as “I know the outcome of every encounter”, “I constantly make decisions and change them as needed” and “My hands are always in my pockets or gesturing”. But the formula, if that is what it was, seems to have worked. Seasoned executives at large companies lauded Ms Holmes for “owning the room”, but ignored warning signs that the firm’s product did not work.

这些文件本身看起来很可笑。Balwani先生写道：“所有自然法则，所有秘密，都在我们身体的每一个细胞上留下印记”，这是一家医疗初创公司的领导者发表的一份有点令人担忧的声明。Holmes女士在笔记中写道:“我知道每次会面的结果”、“我不断做出决定，并在需要时改变它们” 和 “我的手总是放在口袋里或在做手势”。但是这个公式，如果是这样的话，似乎是有效的。大公司经验丰富的高管称赞Holmes女士“掌控着整个会议室”，但忽略了公司产品不起作用的警告信号。

That may have been because of a second decision-making shortcut: many investors and executives relied heavily on the judgments of others rather than their own eyes. Large chunks of the trial have focused on the addition of the logos of drugmakers like Pfizer and Schering-Plough, without their knowledge, to reports that seemed to validate Theranos’s technology. A former chief financial officer of Walgreens, a pharmacy chain that teamed up with the startup, testified that he thought the reports were written by the pharmaceutical firms, when in fact they had found the technology wanting. Ms Holmes has said that she added the logos herself, claiming to have done so in good faith.

这可能是因为第二个决策捷径: 许多投资者和高管严重依赖他人的判断，而不是自己的眼睛。大部分的试验都只关注如何在Pfizer和Schering-Plough等制药商不知情的情况下，把他们的商标加到似乎能够证实Theranos技术的报告上。与这家初创公司合作的连锁药店Walgreens的一位前首席财务官作证说，他认为这些报告是制药公司写的，而事实上他们发现这项技术还不够。Holmes女士表示，商标是她自己添加的，她声称自己是出于善意这么做的。

It is simply not practical to fact-check everything, or to run a business on the assumption that all information has been doctored. There was no reasonable way to infer from the documents that Pfizer and Schering-Plough were unimpressed by Theranos’s devices. But at some point due diligence has to extend to seeing a technology in action.

对所有事情进行事实核查，或者假设所有信息都被篡改而去运营一家企业，这都是不现实的。从这些文件中，我们无法合理地推断出，Pfizer和Schering-Plough对Theranos的设备并不感冒。但在某种程度上，尽职调查必须延伸到观察一项技术的应用。

The story of Theranos is not just about Ms Holmes’s guilt or innocence. It raises questions about Silicon Valley’s “fake-it-till-you-make it” culture, investors’ fear of missing out on the next big thing and the scrutiny that private firms receive compared with listed peers. It is also about the thought patterns that helped Ms Holmes soar. When you review a job candidate’s credentials or take comfort from logos on a website, when you are blown away by someone’s charisma, ask yourself what you really know.

Theranos的故事不仅仅关乎Holmes女士有罪或无罪。这引发了人们对硅谷“假装成功直到你真的成功”文化的质疑，对投资者担心错过下一个重大事件的质疑，以及对私营公司与上市公司相比受到的审查的质疑。它也与帮助Holmes女士“腾飞”的思维模式有关。当你看一个求职者的证书或从网站上的标识中获得安慰时，当你被某人的魅力所折服时，问问你自己你真正知道什么。

Who will police Interpol?

谁来监管国际刑警组织?

Red notice alert

红色通缉令

The election of a worrying new president is just the latest thing to go wrong

令人担忧的(国际刑警组织)新主席的当选是最近一件出错的事情

文章音频

MATTHEW HEDGES, a British doctoral student, says he spent nearly seven months mostly in solitary confinement in a prison in the United Arab Emirates (UAE). He tells of being drugged, interrogated, blindfolded and forced to stand all day in manacles. He falsely confessed to being a spy just to end the agony, he says. He was eventually pardoned and freed. To his horror, the man he accuses of complicity in his torture, Ahmed Naser al-Raisi, the inspector-general of the UAEinterior ministry at the time, who was in charge of prisons, was neither sacked nor demoted. The UAE denies the claims and on November 25th Mr al-Raisi was elected Interpol’s new president.

MATTHEW HEDGES是一名英国博士生，他说他在阿联酋的一所监狱里度过了近七个月的时间，其中大部分时间都是单独监禁。他讲述了自己被麻醉，被审讯，被蒙住眼睛，被迫戴着手铐站一整天地经历。他说，为了结束痛苦，他谎称自己是间谍。他最终被赦免并释放。令他震惊的是，被他指控“参与对他实施酷刑”的Ahmed Naser al-Raisi既没有被解雇，也没有被降职。Ahmed Naser al-Raisi当时是阿联酋内政部的监察长，负责监狱管理。阿联酋否认了这些指控，并且在11月25日al-Raisi先生被选为国际刑警组织的新任主席。

Interpol was set up to help countries’ police forces work together to catch crooks. It has an unfortunate habit of employing them instead. Jackie Selebi, its president from 2004 to 2008, was later sentenced to 15 years in jail for corruption in his native South Africa. Meng Hongwei, the boss from 2016 to 2018, was summoned back to XX, disappeared, reappeared in the dock and got 13-and-a-half years for bribery. (His wife says he was framed.) A cynic might ask: whose side is Interpol on?

国际刑警组织的成立是为了帮助各国的警察力量合作抓捕骗子。令人遗憾的是它总是有雇佣“坏蛋”的习惯。2004年至2008年担任国际刑警组织主席的Jackie Selebi后来因在自己的祖国南非腐败而被判处15年监禁。2016年至2018年的国际刑警组织主席Meng Hongwei被召回XX，失踪后又出现在被告席上，且因受贿被判13年半监禁。(他的妻子说他是被陷害的。) 有愤世嫉俗的人可能会问: 国际刑警组织到底站哪一边?

The agency does not arrest people, but is essentially a communications hub. Its main tool is the “red notice”, which is akin to an international arrest warrant. Member states give Interpol the names of criminals on the lam and ask for a red notice. This used to be rare. In 2001 fewer than 1,500 were issued. That figure has grown more than sevenfold. In all, more than 66,000 red notices are active. Most are for real criminals, but autocratic regimes have found they can also be used to persecute exiled dissidents.

该机构不逮捕人，但其本质上是一个通讯枢纽。它的主要工具是“红色通缉令”，类似于国际逮捕令。成员国向国际刑警组织提供在逃罪犯的姓名，并要求发出红色通缉令。这在过去是很少见的。在2001年，红色通缉令发放数量不到1500份。这个数字已经增长了7倍多。总共有逾66000份工色通缉令处于“活跃”状态。大多数都是针对真正的罪犯，但独裁政权发现它们也可以被用来迫害流亡的异见人士。

Russia, responsible for 43% of the 7,500 red notices that have been made public, is a serial abuser. Petr Silaev, a green activist who found asylum in Finland, was accused of “hooliganism”, arrested in Spain and had to fight to avoid extradition to Russia. XX is another. In July a XX living in Turkey was detained in Morocco on the basis of an Interpol red notice. Democracies seldom hand over dissidents to autocratic regimes, but other autocrats have no such qualms. And red notices can do harm even without extradition. They can ruin careers and make it harder to board a plane or open a bank account. Many people wrongly assume that a red notice stems from a thorough investigation by an international body, rather than, say, a despot’s secret police.

在7500张红色通缉令中，俄罗斯占43%，俄罗斯是一个“连环施暴者”。Petr Silaev是一名在芬兰寻求庇护的绿党活动人士，他被指控犯有“流氓罪”，在西班牙被捕，不得不为避免被引渡到俄罗斯而抗争。XX是另一个“连环施暴者”。今年7月，一名生活在土耳其的XX族人因国际刑警组织的红色通缉令而在摩洛哥被拘留。民主国家很少将持不同政见者移交给独裁政权，但其他独裁者却没有这种顾虑。即使没有引渡，红色通缉令也会造成伤害。它们会毁掉你的职业生涯，让你更难登机或开户。许多人错误地认为红色通缉令来自国际机构的彻底调查，而不是独裁者的秘密警察部门。

Interpol staff are trying harder to weed out the most egregious requests. Reportedly, Turkish officials attempted to upload the names of 60,000 Gulenists onto Interpol’s database, but were rebuffed. (Members of this Islamist movement have been especially persecuted in Turkey since some of them backed a coup in 2016.) American senators introduced a bill in May that would require additional checks before American police act on Interpol notices.

国际刑警组织的工作人员正在努力排除最过分的请求。据报道，土耳其官员试图将6万名葛兰分子的姓名上传到国际刑警组织的数据库，但遭到了拒绝。(自2016年支持政变以来，这个伊斯兰教运动的成员在土耳其尤其受到迫害。)美国参议员在五月份提出了一项法案，要求在美国警察对国际刑警组织发出“红色通缉令”行动之前进行额外的检查。

But more is needed. For a start, Interpol’s president should be chosen in an open vote. Today’s secret ballots of all 195 member states make it hard to spot arm-twisting. That would still leave autocratic regimes with a lot of influence. So internal reforms are needed, too.

但还需要更多行动。首先，国际刑警组织的主席应该通过公开投票选出。如今所有195个成员国的无记名投票将很难发现“强迫”的迹象。这仍然会使独裁政权拥有很大的影响力。因此，也需要进行内部改革。

Requests for red notices from abusers should be subject to strict scrutiny. Currently there is not much, and it is opaque. Persistent abusers should be shut out. In 2012 Interpol blocked Syria’s access to its databases, but in October it inexplicably let the blood-drenched regime back in. Making Interpol more transparent and rigorous will cost money. Its annual budget is just €145m ($164m)—less than that of the New Orleans police department. Rich democracies should chip in more.

应严格审查“滥用者”发出红色通缉令的请求。目前没有太多“审查”，而且也不透明。应将长期“滥用者”拒之门外。2012年，国际刑警组织阻止叙利亚进入其数据库，但在10月，它又莫名其妙地让这个血腥的政权可以访问其数据库。要想使国际刑警组织更加透明且更加严格是要花钱的。它的年度预算只有1.45亿欧元(1.64亿美元)，这比新奥尔良警察局的预算还少。富裕的民主国家应该多投点钱。

Interpol belongs to the machinery of international governance, an area of competition between democracies and autocracies. It is supposed to bolster the rule of law, by helping catch real globetrotting criminals. It must do more to resist being captured by dictators and autocrats who would like to turn it into a branch of their own secret police. ■

国际刑警组织属于国际治理机构，这是民主国家和专制国家之间竞争的领域。它被认为是通过帮助抓捕真正的“环球旅行”的罪犯来加强法治。它必须做更多的工作来抵抗那些想要把“国际刑警组织”变成他们自己的秘密警察部门分支的独裁者。■

Spain needs immigrants. But does it still want them?

西班牙需要移民。但它还想要他们吗?

Testing tolerance

考验忍受能力

An unusually welcoming European country has second thoughts

一个异常热情的欧洲国家改变想法

文章音频

IT IS THE season of calm seas off the Sahara, and each week several hundred African migrants turn up on the shores of the Canary Islands, packed tightly into open fishing boats. Some of them, especially those from Mali, are fleeing violence. Many more are economic migrants, lured by the prospect of much higher wages in Europe, if they can somehow get there.

现在是撒哈拉海岸风平浪静的季节，每周都有数百名非洲移民聚集在加那利群岛的海岸上，他们在开放的渔船上挤得严严实实的。他们中的一些人，尤其是来自马里的人，正在逃离暴力。更多的还是经济移民，如果他们能以某种方式到达欧洲的话，他们能够得到更高的工资，这一前景引诱着他们前往(欧洲)。

It is a hazardous trip: at least 900 migrants have died on this route so far this year. But most who make it to the Canaries eventually find their way to the Spanish mainland. There they find a country whose traditional welcome for immigrants is showing signs of strain.

(不过)这是一次危险的旅程: 今年到目前为止，至少有900名移民死在这条路线途中。但大多数到达加那利群岛的人最终都到达西班牙大陆。西班牙，传统上是欢迎(外来)移民的，现在形势十分紧张。

Modern Spain acquired an immigrant population later and more suddenly than other western European countries. In 1998 there were just 1.2m foreign-born residents; by 2010 there were 6.6m (out of a total population of 47m). Many went home during the economic slump of 2008-12. Now their number is rising again, to 7.2m last year. The first wave easily fitted in: the economy was growing, and the largest contingents were from Spanish-speaking Latin America and from Romania, which has cultural and religious similarities to Spain. There were many, too, from Morocco, where Spain was the colonial power in the north of the country.

现代西班牙获得移民人口的时间比其他西欧国家晚，而且来得更突然。1998年，只有120万外国出生的居民; 到2010年，这一数字为660万(总人口为4700万)。许多人在2008-2012年经济衰退期间回家了。现在这一数字再次上升，去年达到720万。容纳第一波移民很容易: 当时经济在增长，最大的移民队伍是来自说西班牙语的拉丁美洲和与西班牙有着相似文化和宗教的罗马尼亚。也有很多来自摩洛哥，西班牙是摩洛哥北部的殖民国家。

In recent years most new arrivals have come not by sea but through the airports, as visa overstayers, many from Latin America. Growing numbers are from Africa. After a previous surge in arrivals in the Canaries in 2006 Spain signed agreements with Morocco, Mauritania and Senegal under which those countries would take their migrants back in return for aid and help with patrolling the seas. But the deportations have been halted during the pandemic. Anyway, the three African governments are reluctant, especially Morocco’s, which has a long-standing dispute with Spain over Western Sahara and which in May allowed some 8,000 migrants to cross into Ceuta, a Spanish enclave.

近年来，由于签证逾期，大多数新移民不是通过海路，而是通过机场，其中很多人来自拉丁美洲。现在越来越多的人来自非洲。在2006年加纳利群岛的入境人数激增之后，西班牙与摩洛哥、毛里塔尼亚以及塞内加尔签署了协议，根据协议，这些国家将接收移民，以换取西班牙的援助以及海上巡逻的帮助。但在疫情期间，驱逐出境的行动已经停止。无论如何，这三个非洲国家的政府是不情愿的，特别是摩洛哥政府。摩洛哥政府在“西撒哈拉问题”上与西班牙长期存在争议，并在5月份允许大约8000名移民越境进入西班牙飞地休达。

For the past two years, arrivals in the Canaries have at times overwhelmed reception facilities. Algerians are landing on Spain’s Mediterranean coast and the Balearics. This year, for the first time, three of the top five nationalities seeking asylum are African. Many Africans move on to the rest of Europe. But they are increasingly visible in Spanish cities. It normally takes three years to get a work permit. Meanwhile, some work as manteros, street vendors who display their wares on blankets on the pavement. They face racism and police harassment, complains Malick Gueye of an association of manteros in Madrid.

过去两年里，来到加那利群岛的移民有时会让接待设施不堪重负。阿尔及利亚人正在西班牙的地中海沿岸和巴利阿里群岛上岸。今年，第一次，前五个寻求庇护的人的国家中有三个是非洲人。许多非洲人移居到欧洲其他地方。但在西班牙的城市中，非洲人越来越多。正常情况下要花三年时间才能拿到工作许可证。期间，一些人会充当街头摊贩，在人行道上的毯子上展示他们的商品。马德里一个“街头摊贩协会”的Malick Gueye抱怨道，他们面临着种族歧视和警察的骚扰。

All this grabs headlines, especially in a changed political climate. Vox, a hard-right party which now has 52 of the 350 seats in parliament, burst onto the scene in 2017 in response to the separatist threat in Catalonia. But as that has waned it has increasingly campaigned against irregular immigration. For a regional election in Madrid in May, it put up posters contrasting the cost of looking after migrants who are unaccompanied minors with pensions for older Spaniards. It is a paradox that Vox does particularly well in areas along the Mediterranean coast where farmers depend on Moroccan and other African labourers for the harvest.

The government estimates that, even if it succeeds in bringing the effective retirement age into line with the legal one, which is gradually being raised to 67, Spain will need an extra 6m-7m workers by 2040 to meet its pension bill. After a campaign by NGOs the government in October made it easier for young migrants to get work permits when they turn 18 and leave reception centres. “These youths shouldn’t have to be wandering the streets for three years until they get papers,” says Emilia Lozano, a retired shop-worker who has organised beds and training for some. A proposed bill on conversion therapy could do more harm than good

一项关于“性倾向”转换疗法的提案可能弊大于利

Missing the mark

By conflating sexual orientation and gender identity, the government risks children’s health

将性取向和性别认同混为一谈，政府危及儿童的健康

文章音频

IN 2013 A Conservative government legalised same-sex marriage. It was the latest step in the social liberalisation of Britain that started in the 1960s, and in the Conservatives’ own modernisation. Despite opposition at the time, within the party the result is now regarded as uncontroversial.

2013年，保守党政府使同性婚姻合法化。这是20世纪60年代开始以来英国社会自由化的最新一步，也是保守党自身现代化的最新一步。尽管当时存在反对意见，但在党内，这一结果现在被认为是没有争议的。

Now another Conservative government is seeking its own modernising moment. A short public consultation on banning “conversion therapy” closes on December 10th, with the aim of passing a law before an international conference on gay and trans issues in London next June. Supporters say it is needed to end a practice that harms sexual minorities; opponents, that it may worsen the harms it aims to end.

现在，另一届保守党政府正在寻求自身的现代化时刻。关于禁止性倾向“转换疗法”的简短公众咨询将于12月10日结束，其目的是在明年6月伦敦举行“关于同性恋和变性人问题“的国际会议之前通过一项法律。支持者表示，有必要结束这种伤害“性少数群体”的做法; 反对者认为，它可能会加剧其旨在结束的伤害。

The plan is to create a criminal offence covering therapy undertaken with the intention of changing a person’s sexual orientation or gender identity. It is supported by campaign groups; the chief executive of one, Stonewall, regards it as so obviously right that she opposed consulting the public at all. But critics point to three problems: its conflation of gay and trans identities; a poor evidence base; and the introduction of “gender identity” into law.

该计划是将“旨在改变一个人的性取向或性别认同而进行的”治疗定为刑事犯罪。它得到了运动团体的支持; 其中一家名为Stonewall的公司的首席执行官认为这显然是正确的，因此她完全反对征求公众的意见。但批评者指出了三个问题: 将同性恋和变性人身份混为一谈;证据不足;以及将“性别认同”引入法律。

Efforts to turn gay people straight by prayer, exhortation or “aversive therapy” such as electric shocks were common until the 1960s, when homosexual acts between men were still illegal. But being gay is now widely accepted as both unproblematic and innate, and such treatments as not only cruel but doomed to failure. To the extent that they still happen, it is largely in religious settings and consists of laying-on of hands and the like, rather than physical abuse (which is in any case already illegal). “There are no legitimate, registered psychotherapists now who would be part of persuading someone they are not gay,” says David Bell, a psychiatrist who wrote a critical report in 2018 about GIDS, England’s specialist gender clinic for children.

在20世纪60年代以前，通过祈祷、劝诫或电击等“厌恶疗法”来使同性恋者变直的做法很常见，当时男性之间的同性恋行为仍然是非法的。但现在，同性恋被普遍认为是没有问题的且是天生的，而这种治疗方法不仅残忍，而且注定要失败。就目前仍在发生的情况来看，这主要发生在宗教场合，包括按手礼之类的行为，而不是身体虐待(无论如何身体虐待都是非法的)。精神病学家David Bell说:“现在没有合法的注册精神治疗师来说服他人‘他们不是同性恋’”。David Bell在2018年写了一份关于英国儿童性别专科诊所GIDS[性别认同发展服务]的批评性报告。

Trans conversion therapy (which the proposed law does not define) is a different matter—not because trans people are inherently problematic, any more than gay people are, but because unlike sexual orientation, gender identity is neither well-defined nor necessarily stable. “Conflating the two is incredibly dangerous,” says Anna Hutchinson, a clinical psychologist who used to work at GIDS. A person may dissociate from their biological sex for a host of reasons, including autistic-spectrum disorders, depression, trauma or a history of sexual abuse. Responsible clinical practice requires investigating such possibilities. But the phrase “trans conversion therapy” is being used to mean any approach other than immediately affirming a person’s stated gender, says Dr Hutchinson. “That goes against what therapy is.”

变性转换治疗(拟议中的法律没有对其进行定义)则是另一回事，不是因为变性人本身就有问题，也不是说他们比同性恋者更有问题，而是因为与性取向不同，性别认同既没有明确的定义，也不一定稳定。曾经在GIDS工作的临床心理学家Anna Hutchinson说:“将两者混为一谈是非常危险的。”一个人可能会因为许多原因与自己的生理性别分离，包括自闭症谱系障碍、抑郁、创伤或性虐待史。负责任的临床实践需要调查这些可能性。但是Hutchinson博士说，“变性转换疗法”一词被用来指‘除了立即确认一个人的宣称性别外的’任何方法。这和治疗是背道而驰的。”

The only safeguard is an acknowledgement that talking therapy may be appropriate if a patient is “questioning their LGBT status”. But “lots of my patients are not questioning; they are very assertive that they are trans,” says Susan Thompson (not her real name), a family doctor who insists on exploring all comorbidities with trans-identifying patients. “The way this is worded at the moment, I could end up in prison,” she says. Even if there are few prosecutions, the law would make medical professionals wary of anything but unquestioning affirmation. It would also reinforce a trend for schools to go along with cross-sex identities asserted by children, sometimes without even telling parents.

唯一的保障是，如果病人“质疑他们的LGBT身份”，那么谈话治疗可能是合适的。但是家庭医生Susan Thompson(化名)称：“我的很多病人都不会质疑; 他们非常肯定自己是变性人”。Susan Thompson坚持研究变性患者的所有共病。她说：“现在这种措辞可能会让我进监狱。” 即使很少有人起诉，法律也会使医疗专业人士对任何事情保持警惕，除非是毫无疑问的肯定。它还会强化一种趋势，即学校会接受孩子们宣称的跨性别身份，有时甚至不告诉父母。

The second complaint about the bill is the poor quality of the evidence upon which it is based. A government-commissioned report by researchers at Coventry University attempts to show that trans conversion therapy is similar to attempts to “pray the gay away”. But that conclusion does not follow from the very limited research presented. The report relies on a handful of articles and interviews, none of which establishes evidence of harm. Much is made of an online perception survey based on a six-minute fictional video clip, and a single question in a unrepresentative survey by an American transgender lobby group. The growing number of “detransitioners”—people who identify as trans, only later to change their mind—is ignored.

对该法案的第二个抱怨是它所依据的证据质量很差。一份政府委托考文垂大学研究人员撰写的报告试图表明，变性转换疗法类似于“祈祷同性恋远离”。但这一结论并不是从所发表的非常有限的研究中得出的。该报告的依据是少数几篇文章和采访，但没有一篇能证明这种伤害的证据。该报告的大部分内容都是基于一段六分钟的虚构视频进行的在线感知调查，以及美国变性游说团体进行的一项不具代表性的调查中的一个问题。越来越多的“去变性人”被忽视了，“去变性人”指的那些自认为是跨性别者，但后来改变了想法的人。

The third big complaint is that the proposals would introduce the nebulous concept of “gender identity” to British law for the first time. In 2020 a grass-roots campaign by feminists succeeded in forcing the government to abandon plans to allow anyone to change their legal sex at will. That would, in effect, have made self-described gender identity the deciding factor in who counted legally as a man or a woman, abolishing single-sex spaces and services at a stroke. The plans are “a smoke screen for bringing gender-identity ideology into law,” says Maya Forstater, one of the founders of Sex Matters, an organisation campaigning for sex-based rights.

第三大抱怨是，该提案将首次在英国法律中引入模糊的“性别认同”概念。2020年，女权主义者发起的一场群众运动成功地迫使政府放弃允许任何人随意改变其合法性别的计划。实际上，这将使自我描述的性别认同成为合法的男性或女性的决定因素，一举废除了单一性别空间和服务。Sex Matters组织的创始人之一Maya Forstater称，这些计划是“将性别认同意识形态纳入法律的障眼法”。Sex Matters组织致力于发起基于性别的权利的运动。

The consultation comes as an independent review of gender-identity services for children is under way, led by Hillary Cass, a former president of the Royal College of Paediatrics and Child Health. Findings are expected next year. “Why not wait until after Cass is published?” asks Dr Bell. But the government faces a dilemma. It wants to appeal to younger voters by showing it is not stodgy, while also signalling to socially conservative older and working-class voters that it is not too “woke”.

英国皇家儿科和儿童健康学院前院长Hillary Cass正在领导一项针对儿童性别认同服务的独立审查。调查结果预计将于明年公布。Bell博士称：“为什么不等Cass公布结果之后再说?”。但政府面临着一个两难境地。它希望通过展示自己的没那么古板落伍来吸引年轻选民，同时也向社会保守的年长选民和工人阶级选民表明，它还没有太“觉醒”。

This complicates a second U-turn on trans issues in quick succession. And if the bill goes before Parliament in its current form, MPs may fear voting against it, even though many Conservatives and growing numbers on the Labour benches are uneasy. Few voters realise how much is concealed by the phrase “conversion therapy”, and MPs may fear being smeared as bigots.

这使得接连不断的变性问题的第二次彻底转变变得更加复杂。如果该法案以目前的形式提交议会，议员们可能会担心投票反对它，尽管许多保守党人和越来越多的工党议员对此感到不安。很少有选民意识到“转换疗法”这个词隐藏了多少东西，而议员们可能害怕被抹黑为偏执者。

Perhaps most worrying is the impact on children. Many, it seems, are interpreting the stirrings of same-sex attraction as indicating a trans identity. In an article last year in BJPsych Bulletin, a medical journal, Lucy Griffin, a consultant psychiatrist in Bristol, and co-authors found that, of the girls referred to GIDS in one calendar year, only 8.5% were primarily attracted to boys.

Once such children reach 16, gender clinics will prescribe synthetic hormones. Such drugs were used to “chemically castrate” Alan Turing, Dr Griffin points out. The gay mathematician, whose code-breaking helped defeat the Nazis, accepted the drugs as an alternative to a prison sentence. “The whole thing”, says Dr Griffin, “risks becoming an exercise in damaging the people they are setting out to protect.” ■

How to generate better, cheaper, more abundant random numbers

如何生成更好、更便宜、更丰富的随机数

Flipping heck!

And why that is important

以及为什么这很重要？

RANDOMNESS IS A valuable commodity. Computer models of complex systems ranging from the weather to the stockmarket are voracious consumers of random numbers. Cryptography, too, relies heavily on random numbers for the generation of unbreakable keys. Better, cheaper ways of generating and handling such numbers are therefore always welcome. And doing just that is the goal of a project with the slightly tongue-in-cheek name of COINFLIPS, which allegedly stands for Co-designed Improved Neural Foundations Leveraging Inherent Physics Stochasticity.

随机性是一种重要的性质。复杂系统(从天气到股市)的计算机模型都是随机数的贪婪消费者。密码学也在很大程度上依赖随机数来生成无法破解的密钥。因此，用更好、更便宜的方法来生成和处理这些随机数总是受欢迎的。COINFLIPS(略微随意的名字)这个项目的目的就是达成这一目标，COINFLIPS的意思是利用固有物理随机性协同设计改进的神经基础。

COINFLIPS operates under the aegis of Brad Aimone, a theoretical neuroscientist at Sandia National Laboratories (originally one of America’s nuclear-weapons laboratories, but which has now branched out into other areas, too). Dr Aimone’s starting-point is the observation that, unlike the circuits of digital computers, which will, if fed a given input, respond with a precise and predictable output, the link between input to and output from a nerve cell is more haphazard—or, in the jargon, “stochastic”. He wants to imitate this stochastic behaviour in something less squishy than a nerve cell. By doing so, he thinks he might be able to tune the distribution of digits that a random-number generator spits out, without affecting their underlying randomness.

COINFLIPS是在美国桑迪亚国家实验室(最初是美国核武器实验室之一，但现在也扩展到其他领域)的理论神经学家Brad Aimone的支持下运作的。Aimone博士的出发点是观察到，与数字计算机的电路不同的是，数字计算机的电路在输入一个给定的输入后，会产生精确而可预测的输出，而神经细胞的输入和输出之间的联系是更随意，或者用行话来说，是“随机的”。他想在不像神经细胞那么软粘的东西上模仿这种随机行为。通过这样做，他认为他可能能够在不影响随机数生成器潜在的随机性的前提下，调整随机数生成器吐出的数字的分布。

Random doodlings

That would be useful. Existing random-number generators produce uniform distributions. (A “3”, say, is exactly as likely to appear as a “7”.) But, as Dr Aimone’s colleague Darby Smith notes, the real world that computer modellers are trying to model does not work like this. For example, the temperature in London in December may vary between -7°C and 17°C, but is most likely to be in the range 3°C to 8°C. Similarly, vessels are more likely to be in trouble close to a busy shipping route than in a remote backwater. Distorting uniform distributions of random numbers to take account of these realities is tedious and unsatisfactory. As Dr Smith observes, it would be more efficient if the random numbers used corresponded to the natural distribution in the first place.

这很有用。现有的随机数生成器产生数字是符合均匀分布的。(比如说，一个“3”和一个“7”出现的可能性是一样的。) 但是，正如Aimone博士的同事Darby Smith指出的那样，计算机建模者试图模拟的真实世界并不是这样工作的。例如，伦敦12月的温度可能在-7°C到17°C之间变化，但最有可能在3°C到8°C之间变化。同样地，船只在繁忙航线附近比在偏远的回水区更容易遇到麻烦。通过扭曲随机数的均匀分布来考虑这些真实情况是乏味的且不令人满意的。正如Smith博士所说的那样，如果随机数一开始就符合自然分布，那么效率会更高。

There is also an abundance problem. Finding random phenomena in nature that can be transformed into computer bits is not easy. Often the source is computing itself—for example, by gathering the last digits in the numbers of milliseconds between keystrokes made by zillions of users. Otherwise, specialist, expensive hardware needs to be used to do things such as measuring heat flux through a silicon chip.

另外还有一个问题。要找到自然界中可以转化为计算机比特的随机现象并不容易。通常，源是计算它本身，例如，通过收集无数用户按键之间的毫秒数的最后一位数。否则，需要使用专业的、昂贵的硬件来做一些事情，例如测量通过硅片的热流。

To eke out these scarce supplies, such truly random numbers are often then employed to seed programs called pseudo-random-number generators. The algorithms behind those generate sequences of numbers that have the statistical properties of randomness. But this is not the same as the real thing. As John von Neumann, one of computing’s pioneers, observed: “Anyone who considers arithmetical methods of producing random digits is, of course, in a state of sin.” Moreover, if the purpose is cryptography, this method is particularly risky. The opposition might be able to work out the algorithm involved.

为了弥补这些稀缺的资源，这些真正的随机数通常被送到叫做“伪随机数生成器”的种子程序里。这些算法产生的数字序列具有随机性的统计特性。但这和真正的随机数是不一样的。正如计算机领域的先驱之一冯•诺伊曼所说:“任何考虑用算术方法产生随机数的人，当然都处于罪恶的状态。” 此外，如果生成随机数的目的是加密，这种方法尤其危险。因为对手也许能“破译”其中涉及的算法。

Another member of the project, Shashank Misra, says COINFLIPS’ researchers have identified two hardware-based approaches for the production of tuneable, abundant random numbers. One relies on the patterns magnetic films make when disturbed, the other on how electrons travel through the barrier of a quantum-tunnelling diode. Both of these things are truly random. And both can be tuned to provide the sort of random-number distributions COINFLIPS requires.

该项目的另一位成员Shashank Misra说，COINFLIPS的研究人员已经确定了两种基于硬件的方法来产生可调的大量随机数。一种依赖于磁性薄膜在受到干扰时所产生的模式，另一种依赖于电子如何通过量子隧穿二极管的位势垒。这两种情况都是真正随机的。两者都可以调整以提供COINFLIPS所需的随机数分布类型。

In a quantum-tunnelling diode, electrons randomly leap (or fail to leap) across a gap, and the distribution of success or failure can be changed (and therefore tuned) by altering the voltage. A magnetic film, meanwhile, is composed of many tiny magnets jostling with each other. Flip the polarity of one of these and others around it flip in response, creating a random pattern. In this case the plan is to build tuneability into the magnetic medium itself, by tinkering with its composition, and also applying strain to it.

在量子隧穿二极管中，电子随机跃过(或未能跃过)一个间隙，而成功或失败的分布可以通过改变电压来改变(并因此可以调整)。同时，磁性薄膜是由许多相互碰撞的微小磁铁组成的。翻转其中一个的极性，它周围的其他的也随之翻转，形成一个随机模式。在这种情况下，其计划是通过调整其构成，并对其施加应变，将可调性植入磁介质本身。[非专业人士，有误可以在评论区指正]

Random-number-generating hardware based on either of these approaches could be built directly into chips. Alternatively, a random-number-generating unit might be added to existing devices to yield an endless supply of high-speed randomness in the way that graphics-processing units are added to machines that have to handle a lot of graphical material. Crucially, unlike the heat-flux method, which requires intermediate hardware involving thousands of transistors to translate the signal into digital bits, the results of the diode and film methods can be read off as bits directly, using only one or two transistors to do so.

基于这两种方法中的任何一种的随机数生成硬件都可以直接内置于芯片中。或者，可以向现有设备添加一个随机数字生成单元，以无限提供高速随机性，就像将图形处理单元(GPU)添加到要处理大量图像数据的机器中一样。至关重要的是，热流法需要包含数千个晶体管的中间硬件将信号转换为比特数字，而二极管和薄膜法的结果可以直接接连读取为比特，只需要一个或两个晶体管。

The efficiency offered by COINFLIPS means some tasks which currently require a supercomputer might be carried out with desktop hardware, depending on how much of the load involves generating and manipulating random numbers. Dr Aimone says the current approach tends to be simply to build bigger computers where needed. But even then some large-scale tasks may be too costly in money and time lost to conduct. You can, for example, run a model of a hurricane’s path only so many times before the real thing makes landfall.

COINFLIPS所提供的效率意味着一些目前需要超级计算机才能执行的任务可能会用台式机的硬件也可以执行，这取决于生成和控制随机数的负载的多少。Aimone博士说，目前的方法倾向于简单地在需要的地方建造更大的计算机。但即便如此，一些大规模的任务可能要花费大量的金钱和时间来执行。例如，在真正的飓风登陆之前，你只能运行飓风路径的模型这么多次。

The heart of the matter

Despite its peregrinations elsewhere, though, Sandia is ultimately in the nuclear business, and one early application of whatever COINFLIPS comes up with is likely to involve interpreting the results of collisions in particle accelerators—something the team have been exploring in collaboration with Temple University, in Philadelphia. The idea is to build a device which incorporates COINFLIPS hardware into the sensor itself. This will allow results from collisions, which will be randomly distributed, but in particular ways, to be compared with artificial random distributions, to see if they match. To be able to do this in real time is useful, because it allows an immediate decision to be made about whether or not to store a particular result. Modern colliders generate so many collisions that such immediacy in decision-making is important.

尽管桑迪亚在其他地方也有涉足，但它最终还是从事核业务，而且COINFLIPS提出的任何一个早期应用都可能涉及到解释粒子加速器碰撞的结果，这是该团队与费城坦普尔大学合作探索的内容。这个想法是建立一个将COINFLIPS硬件集成到传感器本身的设备。这将允许以特定的方式随机分布的碰撞结果与人为的随机分布进行比较，看看它们是否匹配。能够实时执行此操作是非常有用的，因为它允许立即决定是否存储特定的结果。现代对撞机产生了如此多的碰撞，以至于决策的即时性非常重要。

That is one, rather specific, application. But in the longer run, says Dr Aimone, COINFLIPS should enable many types of calculation that are currently impossible because of the volume of random numbers needed—for example, artificial-intelligence systems that capture the uncertainty of the world. This might be done in the form of neural networks which, like the human brain, have randomness available at each synapse. And that, in turn, may lead to COINFLIPS, a project inspired by biology, returning the compliment by providing a better way of understanding of how brains themselves work. ■

这是一个非常特殊的应用。但从长远来看，Aimone博士说，COINFLIPS将使许多目前由于所需随机数的数量而无法实现的计算成为可能，例如，捕捉世界不确定性的人工智能系统。这可能以神经网络的形式实现，神经网络就像人脑一样，每个突触都有随机性。反过来，这可能会导致COINFLIPS，这样一个受生物学启发的项目，通过提供更好的理解大脑本身是如何工作的方式予以回报。■

The office of the future

未来的办公室

Bartleby

巴托比

Cubicles are out. Bars, neighbourhoods and sensors are in

小隔间已经没有了。酒吧、社区和传感器已就位

Enjoy more audio and podcasts on iOS or Android.

THE OFFICE used to be a place people went because they had to. Meetings happened in conference rooms and in person. Desks took up the bulk of the space. The kingdom of Dilbert and of David Brent is now under threat. The pandemic has exposed the office to competition from remote working, and brought up a host of questions about how it should be designed in the future.

办公室人们曾经不得不去的地方。会议是在会议室举行，也有面对面会议。桌子占据了大部分空间。Dilbert和David Brent的王国现在正面临威胁。疫情使“办公室”面临“远程办公”的竞争，并引发了一系列关于“应该如何设计未来的办公室”的问题。

Start with what the office is for. In the past it was a place for employees to get their work done, whatever form that took. Now other conceptions of its role jostle for attention. Some think of the office as the new offsite. Its purpose is to get people together in person so they can do the things that remote working makes harder: forging deeper relationships or collaborating in real time on specific projects. Others talk of the office as a destination, a place that has to make the idea of getting out of bed earlier, in order to mingle with people who may have covid-19, seem attractive.

先从办公室的用途说起。在过去，它是员工完成工作的地方，不管他们是采取什么形式。现在，关于办公室作用的其他构想也在引起大家关注。有些人认为办公室是新的团建场所。它的目的是让人们面对面地聚在一起，这样他们就可以完成远程工作难以做到的事情，即建立更深层次的关系，或者在特定项目上实时协作。另一些人把办公室当成一个目的地，在这个地方，为了避免与可能患有covid-19的人交往，必须让早点起床的想法看起来很有吸引力。

In other words, a layout that is largely devoted to people working at serried desks alongside the same colleagues each day all feels very 2019. With fewer people coming in and more emphasis on collaboration, fewer desks will be assigned to individuals. Instead, there will be more shared areas, or “neighbourhoods”, where people in a team can work together flexibly. (More hot-desking will also necessitate storage space for personal possessions: lockers may soon be back in your life.)

换句话说，这种设计很大程度上是为了让人们每天在排列密集的办公桌前和同样的同事一起工作，这让人感觉非常2019年。随着进来的人越来越少，越来越强调协作，分配给个人的桌子也会越来越少。相反，将会有更多的共享区域，或“社区”，在这里，团队中的人可以灵活地一起工作。(更多的“办公桌轮用制”也使个人物品的存储空间成为必要，储物柜可能很快就会回到你的生活中。）

To bridge gaps between teams, one tactic is to set aside more of the office to showcase the work of each department, so that people who never encounter each other on Zoom can see examples of what their colleagues do. Another option is to ply everyone with drink. Expect more space to be set aside for socialising and events. Bars in offices are apparently going to be a thing. Robin Klehr Avia of Gensler, an architecture firm, says she is seeing lots of requests for places, like large auditoriums, where a company’s clients can have “experiences”.

为了消除团队之间的隔阂，一种策略是留出更多的办公室空间来展示每个部门的工作，这样那些从未在Zoom上见过面的人就可以看到他们同事所做的事情的范例。另一个选择是不断为每个人提供饮料。预期将会有更多的空间留给社交和活动。办公室里的吧台显然将成为一种趋势。建筑公司Gensler的Robin Klehr Avia说，她看到很多人要求有个大礼堂，在这里公司的客户可以有(不一样的)“体验”。

Designs for the post-covid office must also allow for hybrid work. Meetings have to work for virtual participants as well as for in-person contributors: cameras, screens and microphones will proliferate. Gensler’s New York offices feature mini-meeting rooms that have a monitor and a half-table jutting out from the wall below it, with seating for four or five people arranged to face the screen, not each other.

疫情后办公室的设计也必须考虑到混合工作模式。会议既要考虑到线上与会人员，也要考虑到现场发表意见的人，摄像头、屏幕以及麦克风的数量会激增。Gensler在纽约的办公室设有迷你会议室，里面有一台显示器，还有一个半张桌子可以从显示器下方的墙上延伸出来，这里还有四五个面向屏幕的、而非面对面的座位。

Variety will be another theme. People may plan to work in groups in the morning, but need to concentrate on something in the afternoon. Ryan Anderson of Herman Miller, a furniture firm, likens the difference between the pre- and post-pandemic office to that between a hotel and a home. Hotels are largely given over to rooms for individuals. “Home is thought of as a place for a family over years, hosting lots of different activities.”

多样性将是另一个主题。人们可能计划在早上进行小组工作，但在下午需要集中精力做一些事情。家具公司Herman Miller的Ryan Anderson把疫情前和疫情后办公室的区别比作酒店和家的区别。酒店大多给个人提供房间。“多年来，家被认为是一个家庭多年来举办各种活动的地方。”

All of which implies the need for flexibility. Laptop docking stations are simple additions, but other bits of office furniture are harder to overhaul. Desks themselves tend to be tethered to the floor through knotted bundles of cables and plugs. The office of the future may well feature desks with wheels, which ought to go well with all that extra alcohol. Meeting rooms are likely to be more flexible, too, with walls that lift and slide.

所有这些都意味着需要灵活性。笔记本电脑的扩展坞是简单的附加物，但其他办公用品则更难检修。办公桌本身往往是通过一大捆打结的电缆和插头拴在地板上。未来的办公室很可能会有带轮子的桌子，这应该与所有额外的酒精很相配。会议室也可能更加灵活，墙壁可以升降和滑动。

If socialising and flexibility are two of the themes of the post-pandemic office, a third is data. Property and HR managers alike will want more data in order to understand how facilities are being used, and on which days and times people are bunching in the office. Workers will demand more data on health risks: the quality of ventilation within meeting rooms, say, or proper contact-tracing if a colleague tests positive for the latest covid-19 variant.

如果说社交和灵活性是疫情后办公室的两个主题，那么第三个主题就是数据。物业经理和人力资源经理都想要更多的数据，以便了解设备是如何使用的，以及人们在哪些日子和时间聚集在办公室里。员工将要求获得更多关于健康风险的数据:比如会议室内的通风质量，或者如果一名同事最近covid-19变种核酸检测呈阳性，则需要完全的接触者追踪。

And data will flow more copiously in response: from sensors in desks and lighting but also from desk-booking tools and visitor-management apps. The question of who owns data on office occupants and what consent mechanisms are needed to gather this information is about to become more pressing.

作为回应，数据的来源将更为丰富，即有来自办公桌和照明设备的传感器的数据，也有来自办公桌预订工具和访客管理应用程序的数据。“谁拥有办公室使用者的数据”，以及“要收集这些信息需要什么样的同意机制”的问题将变得更加紧迫。

Put this all together and what do you get? If you are an optimist, the office of the future will be a spacious, collaborative environment that makes the commute worth it. If you are a pessimist, it will be a building full of heavily surveilled drunkards. In reality, pragmatic considerations—how much time is left on the lease, the physical constraints of a building’s layout, uncertainty about the path of the pandemic—will determine the pace of change. Whatever happens, the office won’t be what it was.

把这些放在一起，你会得到什么? 如果你是一个乐观主义者，未来的办公室将是一个宽敞、适合协作的环境，你的通勤将变得值得。如果你是悲观主义者，它将是一座满是受到严密监视的酒鬼的大楼。在现实中，务实的考虑，即租约还剩多少时间，建筑布局的物理限制，流行病传播路径的不确定性，将决定改变的速度。无论发生什么，办公室都不会是以前的样子了。

The Democrats’ fiscal policy makes a mockery of their progressive pledges

民主党的财政政策歪曲其进步承诺

SALT in the wounds

伤口上撒“盐”

It is regressive and self-defeating

这是一种倒退以及自我毁灭


THE TAX plans of President Joe Biden were once full of lofty promises. He and Democrats in Congress would reverse Donald Trump’s tax cuts, make the wealthy pay more and fully fund all manner of desperately needed climate and social-policy programmes with the proceeds. The middle class would rise and the top 1% would manage. As the messy drafting of Mr Biden’s main spending bill—the Build Back Better Act—nears its conclusion after months of wheeling and dealing, it is clear that, when it comes to tax, the result is not lofty at all.

拜登总统的税收计划曾充满了崇高的承诺。他和国会中的民主党人将推翻特朗普的减税政策，让富人支付更多的税，并用所得的资金资助各种迫切需要资金的气候和社会政策项目。中产阶级将会增加，而最富有的1%将会被“利用”。经过几个月的周旋和交易，拜登主要支出法案（即《重建更好未来》法案）的混乱起草接近尾声，很明显，在税收方面，结果不及预期。

The president was unable to whip his slim congressional majorities into reversing Mr Trump’s tax law and increasing marginal rates on capital gains, corporate profits or top individual incomes. And so his plan to raise revenue is a hotch-potch of unorthodox measures, including a new minimum tax on corporate-book income, an excise tax on stock buy-backs and a new surtax on those with incomes above $10m. The wisdom of these measures can be debated. What cannot is a last-minute addition to the bill that would spend hundreds of billions of dollars subsidising the richest residents of New York and California.

总统无法利用其在国会微弱的多数优势扭转特朗普的税法，提高资本利得、企业利润或最高个人收入所得的边际税率。因此，拜登的增税计划是一系列另类措施的大杂烩，其中包括对公司账面收入征收新的最低税，对公司股票回购征收消费税，对收入超过1000万美元的人征收新的附加税。这些措施是否明智值得商榷。但不明智的是在最后一刻在法案上增加的一项内容，即支出数千亿美元补贴纽约和加州最富有居民。

The state-and-local-tax (SALT) deduction lets Americans cut their federal-tax liability if they pay lots of income and property tax at the state and local level. Before 2017 this provision was limitless, letting plutocrats in high-tax states deduct all the property tax on their mansions and the state income tax on their millions, at the expense of federal taxpayers everywhere. Mr Trump’s tax law capped the tax exemption at $10,000. Rather than welcome this as a step towards their goal of more redistribution, Democrats in high-tax states moaned that they had been punitively targeted. Just before Build Back Better passed the House of Representatives on November 19th, they raised the cap to $80,000 a year.

如果美国人在州和地方级缴纳了大量的所得税和财产税，州和地方税的扣除将使他们减少联邦税收义务。在2017年之前，这项规定是没有上限的，这可以让高税收州的富豪们以牺牲各地联邦纳税人的利益为代价，扣除他们豪宅的所有财产税和对他们数百万的州所得税。特朗普的税法将免税限额限制在1万美元。在高税收州的民主党人并不欢迎这一“朝着更大的再分配目标迈出的一步”的举措，相反他们抱怨称，他们受到了惩罚性的打击。就在11月19日众议院通过《重建更好未来》法案之前，他们将扣除上限提高到了每年8万美元。

The result is a fiscal fiasco. In the next five years the benefit will cost $275bn. Exactly none of it will go to the bottom 60% of earners. Instead 70% of the gains will go to the top 5%. For a party that came to power condemning Mr Trump’s tax reform for being regressive, the stain of hypocrisy will be hard to wash out.

其结果就是财政惨败。未来5年，这项“扣除上限”的福利措施将损失2750亿美元。确切地说，这其中的钱没有一项会落到最低收入的60%的人手里。相反，70%的收益将流向前5%的富人。对于一个上台时谴责特朗普的税改政策是倒退的政党来说，伪善的污点恐难洗掉。

If the politics are bad, the policy is worse. The central promise of Mr Biden’s agenda was to craft a more muscular state that will be able to grapple with long-term threats such as climate change, pandemics and social dysfunction. Yet what is supposedly built back better will count for nothing if it does not exist. In order to pay for that $275bn giveaway, the Democrats have jettisoned some of Mr Biden’s pledges, including free tuition at community college, and scheduled others to disappear in a few years’ time. 如果说政治不行，政策就更糟了。拜登议程的核心承诺是打造一个更强大的国家，它能够应对气候变化、流行病和社会失调等长期威胁。然而，如果这一承诺根本就不存在，那些“重建更好未来”将毫无意义。为了支付2750亿美元的“赠品”，民主党放弃了拜登的一些承诺，包括社区大学免学费，并计划在几年内取消其他承诺。他们打赌，未来的国会将会感到羞愧，不得不去寻找资金以弥补这些“减免”的钱。这可能是一厢情愿的想法。

It is also callous. The expanded child benefits have significantly reduced poverty among the most needy Americans, but the programme is due to expire after just one more year. If Democrats held back the hundreds of billions from the SALT deduction, they could almost completely fund a permanent child-benefit scheme. They prefer to give each member of the top 1% of earners an average tax cut of $15,000, five times the amount the benefit pays for each child.

这一法案(指最后那一条)也是冷酷无情的。扩大的儿童福利大大减少了最贫穷的美国人的贫困，但该计划将在仅仅一年之后到期。如果民主党人从“州和地方扣除”中收回数千亿美元，他们几乎可以完全资助一个永久的儿童福利计划。(不过) 他们更倾向于给收入最高的1%的富人每人平均减税1.5万美元，这一福利是支付给每个孩子的五倍。

The best hope of improving the bill comes in the Senate, where all Democrats must vote for it to pass. Mr Biden is so desperate to see the bill into law that he will not criticise it. Some, like Bernie Sanders, have proposed a compromise limiting the larger SALT deduction to families making less than $400,000 a year—which tells you just how regressive the current bill is.

改善该法案的最大希望来自参议院，在参议院所有民主党人必须投票支持该法案通过。拜登如此迫切地希望该法案成为法律，以至于他不会对其进行批评。一些人，比如Bernie Sanders，已经提出了一个妥协方案，限制年收入低于40万美元的家庭获得更大的“州和地方扣除”税额，这也说明当前法案是多么的倒退。

What the Omicron variant means for the world economy

奥密克戎变种对世界经济意味着什么

Danger ahead

前方的危险

Look to XX for the likeliest source of a growth slowdown

从XX中找到经济增长放缓最可能的原因


A LITTLE MORE than a year after the first success of a covid-19 vaccine in a clinical trial, a sense of dread has struck much of the world. The Omicron variant of the coronavirus, first publicly identified on November 24th, may be able to circumvent the defences built up by vaccination or infection with covid-19. The World Health Organisation declared that Omicron poses a “very high” global risk. The boss of Moderna, a vaccine-maker, warned that existing jabs may struggle against the heavily mutated new variant. Faced with the ghastly prospect of yet more lockdowns, closed borders and nervous consumers, investors have reacted by selling shares in airlines and hotel chains. The price of oil has slumped by roughly $10 a barrel, the kind of drop often associated with a looming recession.

在新冠疫苗首次在临床试验中获得成功一年多之后，恐惧感在世界上大部分地方蔓延开来。11月24日首次公开确认的冠状病毒变种奥密克戎可能能够绕过通过疫苗接种或感染covid-19建立起来的防御机制。世卫组织宣布，奥密克戎对全球构成“非常高”的风险。疫苗制造商Moderna的老板警告说，现有的疫苗可能会努力与这种严重变异的新变种做斗争。面对更多封锁、边境关闭和消费者紧张不安的可怕前景，投资者的反应是抛售航空公司和连锁酒店的股票。油价每桶下跌了大约10美元，这种下跌通常与即将到来的经济衰退有关。

As we explain this week it is too early to say whether the 35 mutations on Omicron’s spike protein help make it more infectious or lethal than the dominant Delta strain. As scientists analyse the data in the coming weeks, the epidemiological picture will become clearer. But the threat of a wave of illness spreading from one country to the next is once again hanging over the world economy, amplifying three existing dangers.

正如我们本周解释(看Briefing栏目)的那样，现在就断言奥密克戎的刺突蛋白上的35种变异是否会比占主导地位的Delta变种更具传染性或更致命还为时过早。随着科学家们在未来几周对这些数据进行分析，流行病学的情况将变得更加明朗。但是，这一波疫情将从一个国家传播到另一个国家的威胁再次笼罩着世界经济，这放大了三个现存的危险。

The first is that tighter restrictions in the rich world will damage growth. On the news of the variant, countries scrambled to block travellers from southern Africa, where it was first identified. Israel and Japan have closed their borders entirely. Britain has imposed new quarantine requirements. The pandemic abruptly ended a freewheeling era of global travel. Restrictions were being eased this year, but the past week has shown that gates are slammed shut much faster than they are opened.

第一，发达国家更严格的限制措施将损害经济增长。听到这种变异的消息后，各国纷纷阻止来自南非的旅行者，因为该病毒是在那里首次被发现的。以色列和日本已经完全关闭了边境。英国实施了新的检疫要求。突发地疫情结束了全球旅行的自由时代。今年限制本来已经放宽了，但过去一周的情况表明，(边境)大门关上的速度比打开的速度要快得多。

The spread of Omicron is also likely to intensify limits on free movement at home. Europe was curbing many domestic activities even before the variant arrived, in order to fight surging infections of Delta. Italy is keeping most of the unvaccinated out of indoor restaurants, Portugal requires even those who are vaccinated to have a negative test to enter a bar and Austria is in full lockdown. The long-awaited recovery of the rich world’s huge service industries, from hospitality to conferences, has just been postponed.

奥密克戎的传播还可能加剧对国内自由行动的限制。欧洲为了应对急剧增加的Delta病毒感染病例，在变异病毒出现之前就已经限制了许多国内活动。意大利禁止大多数未接种疫苗的人进入室内餐厅，葡萄牙要求即使是接种疫苗的人也要经过核酸检测呈阴性后才能进入酒吧，奥地利则全面封锁。人们期待已久的发达国家庞大的服务业(从接待到举办会议)的复苏已经被推迟了。

A lopsided economy fuels the second danger, that the variant could raise already-high inflation. This risk looms largest in America, where President Joe Biden’s excessive fiscal stimulus has overheated the economy and consumer prices rose by 6.2% in October compared with the previous year, a three-decade high. But inflation is also uncomfortably high elsewhere, at 5.3% globally, according to Bloomberg data.

经济的不平衡加剧了第二种危险，即这种变种可能会加剧本已高企的通胀。这种风险在美国最为突出，美国总统拜登过度的财政刺激政策导致经济过热，10月份消费者价格指数较上年同期上涨6.2%，这创下30年来新高。但根据彭博社的数据，其他地方的通胀率也高得令人不安，全球通胀率为5.3%。

You might think Omicron would lower inflation, by depressing economic activity. In fact it could do the opposite. Prices are rising in part because consumers are bingeing on goods, bunging up the world’s supply chains for everything from Christmas lights to trainers. The cost of shipping a container from the factories of Asia to America remains extraordinarily high. For overall inflation to recede, consumers need to shift spending back towards services like tourism and eating out. Omicron may delay this. The variant could also trigger more lockdowns in key manufacturing nodes such as Vietnam and Malaysia, aggravating supply glitches. And cautious workers may put off their return to the labour force, pushing up wages.

你可能认为奥密克戎会通过抑制经济活动来降低通胀。事实上，它可能反其道而行之。价格上涨的部分原因是消费者大量购买商品，导致从圣诞彩灯到运动鞋等各种商品的全球供应链受到冲击。从亚洲工厂到美国的集装箱运输成本仍然非常高。要想降低整体通胀，消费者需要将支出重新转向旅游业和外出就餐等服务业。奥密克戎可能回延迟这一情况。该变种还可能在越南和马来西亚等关键制造节点造成更多封锁，从而加剧供应故障。而且小心谨慎的工人可能推迟重返劳动力市场，从而推高薪资水平。

That may be one reason why Jerome Powell, the chairman of the Federal Reserve, indicated on November 30th that he favours monetary tightening. That stance is right, but brings its own dangers. The spillover effects could hurt emerging economies, which tend to suffer capital outflows and falling exchange rates when the Fed tightens.

这可能是为什么美联储主席Jerome Powell在11月30日表示他赞成加快货币收紧的原因之一。这种立场是正确的，但也给自身带来了危险。溢出效应可能会伤害新兴经济体，当美联储收紧货币政策时，这些经济体往往会遭遇资本外流以及汇率下跌。

Emerging economies have greater reserves and depend less on foreign-currency debt than they did during the Fed’s botched attempt to unwind stimulus during the taper-tantrum of 2013. Yet they must also cope with Omicron at home. Brazil, Mexico and Russia have already raised interest rates, which helps stave off inflation but may reduce growth just as another wave of infections looms. Turkey has done the opposite, cutting rates, and faces a collapsing currency as a result. More emerging economies could confront an unenviable choice.

新兴经济体拥有更多的外汇储备，对外币债务的依赖也比美联储在2013年“缩减恐慌”期间试图笨拙地解除刺激政策时要少。不过，他们还必须在国内应对奥密克戎。巴西、墨西哥和俄罗斯已经提高了利率，这有助于遏制通货膨胀，但在另一波疫情逼近之际可能会降低经济增长。土耳其的做法正好相反，它下调了利率，其结果就是面临着货币“崩溃”。更多的新兴经济体可能面临一个不令人羡慕的选择。

The final danger is the least well appreciated: a slowdown in XX, the world’s second-biggest economy. Not long ago it was a shining example of economic resilience against the pandemic. But today it is grappling with a debt crisis in its vast property industry, ideological campaigns against private businesses, and an unsustainable “zero-covid” policy that keeps the country isolated and submits it to draconian local lockdowns whenever cases emerge. Even as the government considers stimulating the economy, growth has dropped to about 5%. Barring the brief shock when the pandemic began, that is the lowest for about 30 years.

最后一个危险是最不受重视的:作为世界第二大经济体的XX经济放缓。不久前，它是抵御疫情中，经济具备弹性的一个光辉榜样。但如今，XX正努力应对其庞大的房地产行业的债务危机、针对私营企业的意识形态运动，以及无法维持的对病毒“零容忍”政策，该政策使XX处于隔离状态，一旦出现新病例，当地就会遭受严厉的封锁。就在政府考虑刺激经济的时候，经济增长率已经下降到5%左右。除了疫情开始时的短暂冲击，这是大约30年来的最低水平。

If Omicron turns out to be more transmissible than the earlier Delta variant, it will make XX’s strategy more difficult. Since this strain travels more easily, XX will have to come down even harder on each outbreak in order to eradicate it, hurting growth and disrupting supply chains. Omicron may also make XX’s exit from its zero-covid policy even trickier, because the wave of infections that will inevitably result from letting the virus rip could be larger, straining the economy and the health-care system. That is especially true given XX’s low levels of infection-induced immunity and questions over how well its vaccines work.

如果奥密克戎病毒的传播能力比早期的Delta病毒更强，XX的战略就会更加困难。由于这种病毒更容易传播，XX将不得不采取更严厉的措施，以根除每一次疫情，这将损害增长，扰乱供应链。奥密克戎可能还会让XX退出“零容忍”政策变得更加棘手，因为放任病毒传播将不可避免地导致更大的感染浪潮，使经济和医疗体系不堪重负。考虑到XX的感染诱导的免疫水平较低，以及对其疫苗效果的质疑，这种说法尤其正确。

Vexing variants and worrying weeks

令人恼火的变种以及令人担忧的几周

It is not all gloom. The world will not see a re-run of the spring of 2020, with jaw-dropping drops in GDP. People, firms and governments have adapted to the virus, meaning that the link between GDP and restrictions on movement and behaviour is one-third of what it was, says Goldman Sachs. Some vaccine-makers expect fresh data to show that today’s jabs will still prevent the most severe cases of the disease. And, if they must, firms and governments will be able to roll out new vaccines and drugs some months into 2022. Even so Omicron—or, in the future, Pi, Rho or Sigma—threatens to lower growth and raise inflation. The world has just received a rude reminder that the virus’s path to becoming an endemic disease will not be smooth.

并不全是悲观的。世界2020年春天的情况以及GDP惊人下降将不会重演。高盛公司称，人们、公司以及政府已经适应了这种病毒，这意味着GDP以及对行动和行为限制之间的联系只有过去的三分之一。一些疫苗制造商希望新的数据表明可以表明今天的疫苗仍然可以预防最严重的疾病。而且，如果有必要的话，公司和政府将能够在2022年的几个月内推出新的疫苗和药物。即便如此，奥密克戎，或者未来的Pi, Rho或sigma，仍有降低经济增长和提高通货膨胀的风险。世界刚刚收到了一则突然的提醒，即这种病毒成为一种地方病的道路不会一帆风顺。

Tech investors can’t get enough of Europe’s fizzing startup scene

科技投资者对欧洲蓬勃发展的初创企业如饥似渴

A continent learns to hustle

After a long slumber, Europe’s animal spirits are stirring

经过长时间的沉睡，欧洲的动物精神正在苏醒

THE IDEA of a Europe hostile to entrepreneurs would once have seemed laughable. At its 17th-century peak the Dutch East India Company’s appetite for capital was so voracious that it demanded the invention of the public stockmarket. Investors then did not balk at its violent treatment of native peoples. The turn of the 20th century saw the founding of giants like L’Oréal, today’s highest-earning beauty empire, and Denmark’s AP Moller Maersk, the largest container-shipping line. Most of Germany’s Mittelstand firms, employers of more than half of all the country’s workers, were born at the same time.

“欧洲对企业家怀有敌意”这一想法在过去看来似乎很可笑。在17世纪的鼎盛时期，荷兰东印度公司对资本的需求很大，以至于它要求建立公共股票市场(来筹资)。当时，投资者并没有因为它暴力对待当地居民而退缩。20世纪初，欧莱雅(如今收入最高的美容帝国)和丹麦的马士基集团(最大的集装箱航运公司)等巨头纷纷成立。德国的大多数中小企业都是在同一时期诞生的，这些企业雇佣了全国超过一半的员工。

That a continent shattered by two world wars produced far fewer businesses destined for high growth in the second half of the 20th century is perhaps unsurprising. But Europe never recovered its appetite for high-growth business creation. In the past three decades, America has spawned four behemoths—Google, Amazon, Tesla and Facebook, now known as Meta—whose valuations have topped $1trn. Not one of Europe’s corporate youths, meanwhile, has risen as high as $100bn. One champion of the 2000s, Skype, was in 2011 bought for $8.5bn by Microsoft. The other, Spotify, is today worth only $48bn. SAP, the closest thing the continent has to a tech giant, was founded three years before Microsoft and is worth less than a fifteenth of it.

在20世纪下半叶，一个被两次世界大战破坏的大陆上，产生的高增长的企业要少得多的情况，或许不足为奇。但欧洲从未恢复对“创造高增长企业”的兴趣。在过去的30年里，美国产生了四大巨头，即谷歌、亚马逊、特斯拉和Facebook，现改名为meta，它们的估值已经超过1万亿美元。与此同时，欧洲的年轻企业中，没有一个估值到1000亿美元。2011年，微软以85亿美元收购了本世纪头十年的“冠军企业”Skype。另一家“冠军企业”Spotify目前的市值仅为480亿美元。SAP是欧洲大陆最接近科技巨头的公司，比微软早成立三年，但其市值还不到微软的十五分之一。

Yet change is in the air. Venture capitalists, who want to sniff out the next Google while it is still being run from the founders’ kitchen tables, are homing in on European startups. There are many more to choose from. European entrepreneurs who would once have gone west to start a business are now likely to start up at home rather than in Silicon Valley.

然而，变革正在发生。风投资本家是那些想在下一个“谷歌”公司还在创始人餐桌上酝酿的时候就发现它的人，他们将目光投向了欧洲的初创公司。这里有更多的选择。曾经会去西方创业的欧洲企业家，现在可能会选择在国内创业，而不是在硅谷创业。

A new influx of capital is proof of an altered mood. Ten years ago, European firms grabbed less than a tenth of all venture capital (VC) money invested globally, though Europe’s share of global GDP was a little over a quarter. This year has seen dealmaking volumes soar in many regions, but particularly in Europe, which now attracts around 18% of global VC funding, says Dealroom, a data provider (see chart 1). That funding is at an all-time high, pumping up values for European startups. The continent now boasts 65 “unicorn cities”, or those which have produced a privately held startup worth more than $1bn. That is more than any other region.

新的资本的大量流入证明了(市场)情绪的变化。十年前，尽管欧洲在全球GDP中所占份额略高于四分之一，但欧洲企业获得的风险资本还不到全球投资总额的十分之一。数据提供商Dealroom表示，今年许多地区的交易量激增，尤其是欧洲，它目前吸引了全球18%的风投资金(见表1)。这一资金创下历史新高，这提升了欧洲初创企业的价值。如今，欧洲大陆现拥有65座“独角兽城市”，即那些产生了一家价值超过10亿美元的民营初创企业的城市。这比其他任何地区都要多。

The continent’s previous paucity of funding was not for lack of returns. Measured by total value (cash returned to investors plus current portfolio value) as a multiple of capital risked, the average European VC fund founded in the past two decades has not fared materially worse than the average American one (see chart 2). Nevertheless, American venture capitalists thought of Europe as “a place to take their families on summer holiday not somewhere to start a business,” says Danny Rimer of Index Ventures, a VC firm headquartered in San Francisco and London.

欧洲大陆之前缺乏资金并不是因为缺乏回报。以总价值(回报给投资者的现金加上当前的投资组合价值)作为风险资本的倍数来衡量，过去20年成立的欧洲风投基金的平均表现并不比美国风投基金的平均表现差多少。然而，总部位于旧金山和伦敦的Index Ventures风投公司的Danny Rimer说，美国风险投资家认为欧洲是“带家人去度假的地方，而不是创业的地方”。

Now they are voting with their feet. Sequoia, an American firm that was an early backer of Apple, Google, WhatsApp and YouTube, announced last year that it would open its first European office in London, and started recruiting local partners. Among native investment houses, the muttering is about when, not if, other American VC outfits will follow.

现在他们”改变看法了”。红杉资本(一家美国公司)，是苹果，谷歌，WhatsApp和YouTube的早期支持者，去年它宣布将在伦敦开设第一个欧洲办事处，并开始招募当地的合作伙伴。在本土的投资机构中，人们议论的是其他美国风投公司何时会效仿，而不是是否会效仿。

Venture capitalists are chasing a generation of startups that has benefited from the trail blazed by their predecessors. The likes of Skype and Spotify may not have reached the dizzying valuations of their American peers, but they showed it was possible to start a successful tech company in Europe and scale it at speed, explains Michael Moritz of Sequoia. Now, says Mr Moritz, “it’s no longer frowned upon if you’re young and bright to leave university and join a tech company, or to drop out and found one.” They also provided startups with a pool of potential employees and board members with prior experience of fast-growing, innovative companies.

风投资本家正在追逐一代受益于前人开路的初创公司。红杉资本的Michael Moritz解释称，Skype和Spotify这类公司的估值可能还没有达到美国同行极高的水平，但它们表明，在欧洲创办一家成功的科技公司并迅速扩大规模是有可能的。Moritz先生说，现在，“如果你很年轻、很聪明，离开大学去加入一家科技公司，或者辍学去创办一家科技公司，不再会遭到反对。” 他们还为初创公司提供了一批有在快速增长的创新型公司工作经验的有潜力的员工以及董事会成员。

The combination of seasoned executives and access to experienced talent has fuelled the growth of a cluster of European firms founded after the ructions of the financial crisis that are now reaching maturity. More importantly, notes Hussein Kanji of Hoxton Ventures, another VC firm, they include companies starting to dominate their respective niches. The huge new category of social media was won by Facebook, he notes. “Now Spotify is the winner in music streaming, Klarna is the winner in buy-now-pay-later and UiPath is the winner in robotic process automation—they’re all European,” he says. With returns in the world of tech flowing disproportionately to the firms in first place, that makes Europe too attractive a prospect for global investors to ignore.

经验丰富的高管和有经验的人才的结合，推动了一批在金融危机动荡之后成立的欧洲公司的成长，这些公司现在已经走向成熟。更重要的是，另一家风投公司Hoxton Ventures的Hussein Kanji指出，这些公司开始在各自细分市场占据主导地位。他指出，Facebook赢得了社交媒体这一巨大的新类别。他说：“现在Spotify是音乐流媒体领域的赢家，Klarna(欧洲主流支付供应商)是先买后付的赢家，UiPath是机器人流程自动化领域的赢家，他们都是欧洲公司”。由于科技行业的回报不成比例地流向了第一名的公司，这使得欧洲成为全球投资者难以忽视的一个极具吸引力和前景的“市场”。

The boom extends far beyond a few of the largest companies. For Xavier Niel, a billionaire French tech founder-turned-investor, repeat founders in Europe are key. They are launching new waves of firms, he says, meaning “more entrepreneurs, more talent, more capital, more success, it’s a flywheel in progress”. Rachel Delacour sold her first business, BIME Analytics, a business analytics platform, in 2015 for $45m, six years after co-founding it in Montpellier. She started Sweep, which helps companies track carbon emissions, last year. “Now that I’m starting this second business, I know right from the off that it can be a global story,” says Ms Delacour.

这种繁荣远远超出了几家最大的公司。对于从法国科技公司创始人转型为投资者的亿万富翁Xavier Niel来说，在欧洲，“重复创始人”是关键。他说，他们正在启动新一轮的公司浪潮，这意味着“更多的企业家，更多的人才，更多的资本，更多的成功，这是一个前进中的飞轮”。2015年，Rachel Delacour以4500万美元的价格出售了自己的第一家公司BIME Analytics商业分析平台。去年，她创办了Sweep，帮助企业追踪碳排放。Delacour表示:“既然我正在创办第二家企业，我从一开始就知道，它可以成为一个全球性的故事。”

It also helps that Europeans working for startups are becoming owners. A recent analysis by Index Ventures of 350 European startups found that 15-17% of firms on average is owned by employees. That is up from 10% five years ago, although still below the comparable figure of 20-23% for American startups. Workers’ willingness to be part-remunerated with stock options makes it easier for startups to compete with bigger firms for talent, says Mr Rimer.

为初创公司工作的欧洲人正在成为所有者，这也有所帮助。Index Ventures最近对350家欧洲初创公司的分析发现，平均有15-17%的公司由员工拥有。这一比例高于五年前的10%，但仍低于美国初创企业20-23%的比例。Rimer先生说，员工愿意用股票期权作为部分薪酬，这使得初创公司更容易与大公司竞争人才。

Technological trends have also been driving costs down and enabling would-be founders to get their firms off the ground at home in Europe rather than leaving for California. Starting an internet business used to involve buying banks of servers and the space to store them. The advent of cloud computing means firms can instead rent processing power from hyperscale clouds like Amazon Web Services, and rent smaller offices. The pandemic has forced fund managers to accept doing due diligence and deals over Zoom. That lowers the importance of geographical proximity.

技术趋势也在降低成本，使潜在的创始人能够在欧洲本土创办自己的公司，而不是前往加州去创办企业。过去，创办互联网企业需要购买大量服务器以及存储它们的空间。云计算的出现意味着企业可以从AWS等超大型云服务厂商租用“处理能力”，并租用更小的办公室。疫情迫使资金管理者接受在Zoom上进行尽职调查和交易。这降低了地理邻近的重要性。

Two big questions hover over Europe’s entrepreneurial renaissance. The first is the extent to which the capital being poured into it is spillover from the liquidity that has flooded markets since the onset of the pandemic. Since then, the world’s four largest central banks have collectively pumped more than $9trn-worth of cash into the global financial system, driving down bond yields. That has sent investors into ever-riskier asset classes in pursuit of returns. Early-stage equity investment in a previously calcified continent is a prime candidate. As central banks dial back, the yields on safer assets will start to look less anaemic, putting Europe’s ample VC funding at risk.

欧洲的创业复兴面临着两个大问题。第一个问题是，自疫情爆发以来，涌入市场的流动性在多大程度上产生了溢出效应。自疫情以来，全球四大央行总计向全球金融体系注入了逾9万亿美元的现金，这压低了债券收益率。这促使为了追求收益的投资者投身于风险更高的资产类别。在先前僵化的欧洲大陆进行早期股权投资是一个主要的选择。随着各国央行缩减规模，更安全资产的收益率将开始变得不那么疲弱，这将使欧洲充足的风险投资资金面临风险。

Another question is whether the boom results in Europe building its own, American-style tech behemoths, or a cluster of middling firms that are gobbled up by larger, possibly non-European acquirers. That, in turn, will determine whether the continent’s entrepreneurial moment flares out or ignites something bigger. Governments have devised schemes to catalyse business creation for decades, but the answer turns out to be simple. “There is nothing that beats examples of success to inspire confidence in people,” says Mr Moritz. It is up to today’s European giants-in-waiting to decide how much inspiration to provide.■

另一个问题是，这种繁荣是会导致欧洲建立自己的美国式科技巨头，还是会被更大的、可能是非欧洲的收购方吞并的一群中等规模的公司。这反过来将决定欧洲大陆的创业时刻是突然爆发，还是引发更大的变化。几十年来，各国政府一直在设计促进创业的方案，但答案其实很简单。Moritz表示:“没有什么比成功的例子更能激发人们的信心。” 这将取决于今天欧洲等待成为巨头的公司将决定提供多少鼓舞人心的案例。■

Germany’s new government holds great promise

德国新政府充满希望

Green shift

向绿色转变

It will need luck, too

这也需要运气

NO ONE LIKES waiting for a traffic light. But for Germans the wait is over. On November 24th a new ruling coalition was unveiled. Forming it took only two months after an election. (In the Netherlands parties are still haggling after eight.) Nicknamed “traffic light” after the colours of the parties that will make it up, it is a three-way contraption, Germany’s first since the 1950s, with the Social Democrats in the lead backed up by the Greens and the Free Democrats. But whether the incoming chancellor, Olaf Scholz, will offer dynamic leadership, rather than more of Germany’s recent drift, is hard to say.

没有人喜欢等红绿灯。但对德国人来说，等待已经结束。11月24日，一个新的执政联盟诞生了。在一次选举过后，仅用了两个月就组建了该执政联盟。(荷兰的政党在选举过后8个月后还未达成协议) 这是德国自20世纪50年代以来的第一次三党合作，由绿党(绿)和自民党(黄)支持，由社民党(红)领导，根据三党的代表颜色命名为“红绿灯”。但很难说即将上任的德国总理奥拉夫•朔尔茨是否会提供动态领导力，而不是像德国最近那样更多的是随波逐流。

We choose to be optimistic. The entry into the government of the world’s fourth-largest economy by the mainly pragmatic Greens is good news at a time of environmental peril. The devil is in the detail, and there is a lot of it already; the coalition deal comes in the form of a 177-page document laying out its pre-agreed policies. On climate change, these include a pledge to end the burning of coal for electric power by 2030 (eight years earlier than had previously been planned) and to raise the share of renewables to 80% by 2030, from a previous goal of 65%. There is also welcome attention to the nuts and bolts of how all these commitments will actually be achieved.

我们选择乐观。务实的绿党加入世界第四大经济体政府，在环境危机时期是个好消息。细节决定成败，而细节现在已经很多了; 这份177页的执政联合协议，列出了双方事先商定的政策。在气候变化方面，这些承诺包括到2030年停止燃煤发电(比原计划提前8年)，到2030年将可再生能源的份额从原先的65%提高到80%。值得欢迎的是，人们还关注如何实际实现所有这些承诺的实质性内容。

More than that, it is now reasonable to expect that Germany, whose new foreign minister is set to be Annalena Baerbock, one of the Greens’ co-leaders, will push much harder than before for the EU to do more to fight climate change, and for the EU’s weight as the world’s second-largest consumer market to be used to urge others on. Germany cannot routinely get its own way in the EU, of course; but it is still the most influential member of the club. Likewise, Europe does not always prevail. But its commitment to achieve net-zero greenhouse-gas emissions by 2050, though it binds no one else, has become a global standard that others are under increasing pressure to meet.

更重要的是, 现在有理由期待德国的新外交部长绿党联席主席安娜莱娜·贝尔伯克,将比以往更加努力地推动欧盟采取更多措施应对气候变化,并利用欧盟作为世界第二大消费市场的影响力来敦促其他国家采取行动。当然，德国在欧盟不能向来都一意孤行；但它仍然是这个俱乐部中最有影响力的成员。同样，欧洲也不总是占上风。但它承诺到2050年实现温室气体净零排放，尽管这一承诺对其他国家没有约束力，但它已经成为一个全球标准，为了满足这一标准，其他国家面临着越来越大的压力。

The carbon news is therefore good, and other parts of the package are as well. NATO allies may fret that Germany now plans to attend the first meeting of signatories to the Treaty on the Prohibition of Nuclear Weapons. But it will be as an observer only and, more important, the new government will continue with “nuclear sharing”: the positioning of American nukes on German soil and Germany’s commitment to maintain aircraft to drop them if need be. (A failure to commit itself firmly to NATO’s defence-spending target of 2% GDP is a bigger, but hardly new, worry.) Other pluses include measures to reform Germany’s creaking bureaucracy and a vow to legalise cannabis. A tougher line against Russia and especially XX is also on the cards.

因此，碳排放的消息是好的，一揽子计划的其他部分也是好的。北约盟国可能会担心德国现在计划参加“禁止核武器条约”的第一次签署国会议。但它将只以观察员的身份存在，更重要的是，新政府将继续“核共享”政策: 将美国的核武器部署在德国领土上，并承诺德国将在战机挂载美国核弹，在必要时投放这些核武器。(北约未能坚定地承诺将国防开支控制在GDP的2%是一个更大的担忧，但这已经不是什么新鲜事了。) 其他有利的计划包括改革德国不堪重负的官僚机构的措施，以及承诺将大麻合法化。对俄罗斯、尤其是XX采取更强硬的立场也是可能的。

There are trickier elements. The expected appointment of Christian Lindner, leader of the Free Democrats, as finance minister suggests that ditching Germany’s outmoded debt brake or loosening the EU’s purse strings beyond its €750bn ($840bn) covid-recovery package is unlikely; Mr Lindner has been an opponent of both. Still, none of this looked likely to change much, whoever got the job. Mr Scholz is thrifty, like most German voters, and Germany belongs to a group of frugal northern EU countries. Even so, the deal contains signs of flexibility here, too. And Mr Lindner’s involvement could well be a plus. He is strongly pro-business, and his presence will reassure conservatives if such flexibility is employed.

还有一些更棘手的部分。自民党领袖克里斯蒂安•林德纳预计将出任财政部长，这似乎表明，不太可能放弃德国过时的债务刹车，或放松欧盟超过7500亿欧元（8400亿美元）的新冠肺炎复苏计划的钱袋子是不可能的; 林德纳一直是这两种观点的反对者。不过，无论谁得到这份工作，这一切似乎都不会有太大改变。朔尔茨先生和大多数德国选民一样节俭，而且德国属于节俭的北欧国家。即便如此，该协议在这方面也有灵活性的迹象。林德纳的参与很可能是一大有利因素。他是一个非常亲商的人，如果这种灵活性得到利用的话，他的存在将使保守派放心。

Perhaps the biggest worry about the new coalition is that it may spend too much of its time arguing. On many issues, Mr Scholz can expect his liberal partners to pull in one direction, and his Green partners in another. The laboriously hashed-out plan provides a baseline of agreement, but there will always be things it failed to foresee, or simply ducked. Covid-19 is again raging in Germany (see Leader) and Vladimir Putin is a menace. But the methodical and disciplined way the three parties have worked through their differences gives reason to hope that they will be able to go on doing so. Viel Glück! ■

也许对新联合政府最大的担忧是它可能会花费太多的时间争论。在许多问题上，朔尔茨先生可以期待他的自由派伙伴向一个方向拉，而他的绿党伙伴向另一个方向拉。这样艰难地协商一致的计划提供了一个达成一致的基础，但总会有一些事情是它没有预见到的，或者只是简单地回避了。Covid-19再次在德国肆虐，普京是一个威胁。但是，三党有条不紊地解决了他们之间的分歧，这使人们有理由希望，他们能够继续这样做下去。祝好运! ■
