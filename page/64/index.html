How to tax capital without hurting investment

如何在不损害投资的情况下对资本征税

Biden’s taxing problem

拜登的税收问题

![image-20210504141025790](/Users/chenyujie/Library/Application Support/typora-user-images/image-20210504141025790.png)

President Joe Biden should improve the design of his taxes on Wall Street

拜登总统应该改进他对华尔街的税收设计

Apr 29th 2021

GOVERNMENTS RAISE most of their money by taxing wages, but President Joe Biden has his eyes fixed on the rich, big business and Wall Street. He proposes to fund his $2.7trn infrastructure plan in part by raising the corporate-tax rate from 21% to 28%. And to help pay for more spending on child care and support for parents, he wants to roughly double the top rate of federal tax on capital gains and dividends. For Americans earning more than $1m per year, he would bring levies on capital income into line with the top rate on wage income, which he wants to put up from 37% to 39.6%. That is about double the rate that is currently levied on rich investors, who are only a small fraction of the population but a large proportion of shareholders.

政府的大部分资金都是通过对工资征税来筹集的，但是拜登总统却把目光投向了富人、大企业和华尔街。他提议通过将公司税率从21%提高到28%来资助他的2.7万亿美元的基础设施计划。为了帮助支付更多的儿童保育和父母赡养方面的开支，他希望将联邦资本利得和股息的最高税率提高大约一倍。对于年收入超过100万美元的美国人，他将对资本收入征税，使其与工资收入的最高税率一致，他希望将工资收入的税率从37%提高到39.6%。这大约是目前对富有投资者征收的税率的两倍，这些投资者只占人口的一小部分，但占股东的很大比例。

Many investors, from Wall Street to Silicon Valley, are up in arms, claiming that Mr Biden will crush economic growth. That is an exaggeration; America can bear higher rates of capital tax­ation. Yet levies on capital can have unintended economic consequences. If he is to avoid them, Mr Biden should improve the design of his plans.

up in arms (美国俚语）愤怒，发火

从华尔街到硅谷，许多投资者都怒火上燎，声称拜登将破坏经济增长。这有点儿夸张了; 美国可以承受较高的资本税率。然而，对资本征税可能会产生意想不到的经济后果。如果拜登想要避免这些问题，他应该改进他设定的计划。

Taxing savings and investment income can seem like unfair and inefficient double taxation. Those who earn today to spend today must pay only income and consumption taxes; why should someone who prefers to gain by deferring their gratification face extra levies? Discouraging saving and investment hurts the economy in the long run, which is why a review in 2010 by the OECD, a club of mostly rich countries, ranked corporate taxes as the most harmful of four common taxes to economic growth. Economic models predict that Mr Biden’s business-tax plans would cut the size of America’s economy by around 1% by 2050.

对储蓄和投资收入征税似乎是不公平和低效的双重征税。那些今天挣得今天花的人只需缴纳所得税和消费税;为什么那些喜欢通过延迟满足感来获取利益的人却要面对额外的税收呢? 抑制储蓄和投资从长远来看会损害经济，这就是为什么由发达国家组成的经合组织(OECD)在2010年的一份报告中将企业税列为四种共同税收中对经济增长危害最大的一种。经济模型预测，到2050年，拜登的企业税计划将使美国经济规模减少约1%。

![image-20210504140957991](/Users/chenyujie/Library/Application Support/typora-user-images/image-20210504140957991.png)

Set against this is the scourge of tax avoidance. Tax capital lightly and it pays to disguise wages as capital income—a particularly lucrative pastime for the rich. One problem is the “carried interest” loophole. It lets private-equity and hedge-fund managers class their fees as capital gains rather than income. Another issue is the explosive growth in “pass through” firms, for example partnerships, which accounted for more than half of American business income by 2011, up from about a fifth in 1980. Many capital-light, labour-intensive businesses such as law firms, consultancies and medical offices are organised this way. Nearly half of the earnings that pass-through investors receive are classified as dividends and capital gains. Mr Biden is right that bringing taxes on wages and capital into line would make tax avoidance harder.

scourage 祸害

pass through：an arrangement in which a financial organization buys loans from a bank and sells bonds representing these loans to investors. The payments on the loans are then used to pay interest to the investors and pay back the bonds

与之相对的是避税的祸害。对资本轻征税，把工资伪装成资本收入，这对富人来说是一种特别有利可图的活动。一个问题是“附带权益”漏洞。它允许私募股权和对冲基金经理将其费用归为资本收益而不是收入。另一个问题是“转移”公司(例如合伙企业)的爆炸式增长。到2011年，合伙企业占美国企业收入的一半以上，高于1980年的五分之一。许多资本较少、劳动密集型的企业，如律师事务所、咨询公司和诊所，都是这样组织的。投资者获得的收益中有近一半被归类为股息和资本利得。将工资税和资本税统一起来将使避税更加困难, 拜登是正确的。

The trade-off between inefficiency and tax avoidance is painful, but two principles can help chart a sensible course. The first is to realise that taxes on capital stack up. Before they can return their profits to investors in the form of dividends and capital gains, firms pay corporate taxes. Whack up every capital levy to rates resembling income taxes and you will take a larger bite out of investment income than out of wages. Shareholders in California, for example, face Mr Biden’s proposed 28% corporate-tax rate, his 39.6% federal capital-gains rate, a 13.3% state tax on capital gains, and a 3.8% levy on investment income introduced as part of Obamacare. They could, in theory, keep less than a third of their nominal returns under the Biden plan—and even less of their real returns given that some of those taxes would be paid on the illusory capital gains generated by inflation. In reality it is not as simple as compounding rates, because the corporate tax is leaky and the current system, egregiously, waives capital-gains taxes when assets are inherited. Still, after taking into account the revenues raised from closing loopholes, Mr Biden should ensure that taxes on capital do not rise above taxes on labour.

whack up 评分；瓜分；增加

illusory 虚假的

egregious （错误等）极其严重的，极坏的，令人震惊的

waive 免除

效率低下和避税之间的权衡是痛苦的，但有两条原则可以帮助制定一条合理的路线。首先是要意识到资本税会累积起来。在将利润以股息和资本利得的形式返还给投资者之前，企业要缴纳公司税。把每一项资本税都提高到类似所得税的税率，你将从投资收入中得到比工资更大的收益。例如，加州的股东们面临着拜登提议的28%的企业税，39.6%的联邦资本利得税，13.3%的州资本利得税，以及作为奥巴马医改的一部分引入的3.8%的投资收入税。从理论上讲，在拜登计划下，他们可以保留不到三分之一的名义回报，甚至更少的实际回报，因为其中一些税收将由通货膨胀产生的虚假资本利得支付。事实上，这并不像复利那么简单，因为公司税是漏洞百出的，而且当前的体系在继承资产时免除了资本利得税。不过，在考虑到堵住漏洞所带来的收入后，拜登应该确保资本税不会高于劳工税。

The second principle is to reduce inefficiency with allowances for investment. Exempting from capital taxation modest “normal” returns, which are usually measured by the interest rate on low-risk bonds, cuts distortions, as the normal return is in theory the minimum needed to make private-sector projects worthwhile. The idea is baked into America’s tax code for many types of investment but only until the end of 2022—and Mr Biden plans a new minimum tax on firms’ accounting profits which would interfere with the carve-out for the largest companies. Individual investors should also receive exemptions for the normal rate of return—which already happens in Norway and has been suggested in Britain by the Institute for Fiscal Studies. As Mr Biden proposes, inheritances should not be exempt from capital-gains tax.

第二个原则是通过投资补贴来降低效率低下的问题。免除资本税的适度“正常”回报(通常是用低风险债券的利率来衡量的)可以减少扭曲，因为正常回报在理论上是使私营部门项目物有所值所需的最低限度。这一想法被纳入了美国多种投资的税法，但直到2022年底，拜登还计划对公司的会计利润征收新的最低税，这将干扰最大公司的分拆。个人投资者也应该获得正常回报率的豁免，挪威已经这样做了，英国财政研究所也提出了这样的建议。正如拜登提议的那样，遗产不应免除资本利得税。

Genuine parity between capital and labour taxation, tempered by investment incentives**, might not raise as much money as Mr Biden’s current plans.** But such a reform would still help pay for his spending and reduce tax avoidance—without making the American tax system needlessly inefficient. ■

资本和劳动力税收的真正平等，在投资动机的调节下，可能不会筹集到像拜登目前计划那样多的资金。但这样的改革仍将有助于为他的开支支付并减少避税，同时又不会使美国的税收体系变得毫无必要地低效。■

A new type of ad is heading for your iPhone

一种新型广告正在向你的iPhone进军

The cookie crumbles

![image-20210505185236269](/Users/chenyujie/Library/Application Support/typora-user-images/image-20210505185236269.png)

Stricter privacy rules send digital advertisers back to the drawing board

更严格的隐私规定让数字广告商重回画板上

Apr 29th 2021

ONLINE SHOPPERS often feel they are being watched. Put an item in your basket but fail to buy it, and it may follow you plaintively around the internet for days. Announce your engagement on social media and you will be hit with adverts for the honeymoon. As you turn 40, expect the attention of elasticated-trouser merchants.

plaintively 悲伤地

网上购物者常常觉得自己被监视。把一件东西放在篮子里却没买下来，它可能会在网上哀怨地跟着你好几天。在社交媒体上宣布你的订婚消息，你就会被关于蜜月的广告包围。当你步入40岁的时候，弹力裤商人们会对你产生关注。

On April 26th Apple, which supplies one-fifth of the world’s smartphones and around half of America’s, introduced a software update that will end much of this snooping. Its latest mobile operating system forces apps to ask users if they want to be tracked. Many will decline. It is the latest privacy move forcing marketers to rethink how they target online ads.

4月26日，全球五分之一的智能手机和美国一半的智能手机的供应商苹果公司(Apple)推出了一项软件更新，这将结束这种窥探行为。其最新的移动操作系统迫使应用程序询问用户是否希望被追踪。许多追踪将会减少。正是这一最新隐私举措，才迫使营销人员重新考虑如何定位在线广告。

By micro-profiling audiences and monitoring their behaviour, digital-ad platforms claim to solve advertisers’ age-old quandary of not knowing which half of their budget is being wasted. In the past decade digital ads have gone from less than 20% of the global ad market to more than 60%, according to GroupM, the world’s largest media buyer. Even last year, amid the pandemic, the business grew by 9%. As lockdowns ease it is going gangbusters. On April 27th Alphabet, Google’s parent company and the world’s biggest digital-ad platform, reported first-quarter advertising revenues up by 34%, year on year. The next day Facebook, the second-largest, said its own ad sales had grown by 46%.

quandary 困境

gangbuster 很红火；非常成功

数字广告平台声称，通过对受众进行微分析并监控他们的行为，可以解决广告商长期以来不知道自己预算的哪一半被浪费了的难题。全球最大的媒体买家GroupM的数据显示，过去十年，数字广告在全球广告市场的份额从不足20%上升到60%以上。即使是去年，在疫情期间，该业务也增长了9%。随着封锁的解除，广告市场份额正在暴涨。4月27日，谷歌的母公司、世界上最大的数字广告平台Alphabet公布第一季度广告收入同比增长34%。第二天，排名第二的Facebook宣布其广告销售额增长了46%。

Stronger privacy protections may make their ads less effective. In 2018 the EU imposed its General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) and America’s most-populous state introduced the California Consumer Privacy Act. Both made it harder to harvest users’ data. Since 2020 Apple’s Safari web browser has blocked the “cookies” that advertisers use to see what people get up to online. Google has similar plans for its more popular Chrome browser.

populous 人口众多的

更强的隐私保护可能会降低广告的效果。2018年，欧盟实施了《通用数据保护条例》(GDPR)，美国人口最多的州推出了《加州消费者隐私法》。两者都加大了获取用户数据的难度。自2020年以来，苹果的Safari浏览器已经屏蔽了广告客户用来查看人们上网内容的“cookies”。谷歌对其更受欢迎的Chrome浏览器也有类似的计划。

Apple’s latest change makes explicit an option that was previously hidden deep in its phones’ settings. Users can forbid apps to access their “identifier for advertisers” (IDFA) code, which singles out their device, and from tracking their activity across other firms’ apps and websites. It amounts to a “seismic shift” in in-app advertising, says Jon Mew, head of the Internet Advertising Bureau, an industry body.

single out 单独挑出

seismic 巨大的；重大的

苹果的最新变化明确了一个以前深藏在其手机设置中的选项。用户可以禁止应用程序访问他们的“广告商标识符”(IDFA)代码，该代码用来识别用户的设备，并可以通过其他公司的应用程序和网站来追踪他们的活动。行业组织互联网广告局(Internet advertising Bureau)的负责人Jon Mew表示，这相当于应用内部广告的“重大转变”。

The platforms best-placed to survive the shake-out are those with lots of consumer data of their own. Google’s $147bn ad business gets most of the information it needs from the terms users type into its search bar. Amazon, whose digital-ad business is the third-largest and growing fast, has the advantage of being able to track what people buy after seeing ads on its site—a “closed loop”, as marketers call it. Apple knows where iPhone-users go, what time they wake up and much besides. It has a small but growing ad business, selling prominence in its app store, for instance.

shake-out （在最后结果出现前）变化，演变

那些自己拥有大量消费者数据的平台最有可能在这次变化中幸存下来。谷歌价值1470亿美元的广告业务，从用户在其搜索栏中输入的关键词中获取其所需的大部分信息。亚马逊的数字广告业务是世界第三大且增速快，它的优势在于能够跟踪人们在其网站上看到广告后购买的商品，营销者称之为“闭环”。苹果知道iphone用户去哪里，他们什么时候醒来，还有很多其他的事情。它的广告业务规模虽小，但仍在不断增长，比如在应用商店中销售突出的产品。

For Facebook, which knows more about its users’ interests than about their shopping needs, Apple’s changes are more worrying. In August it warned they might reduce revenues at its Audience Network, through which it sells ads to other apps, by as much as 50%. But the Audience Network represents less than a tenth of its business. Thanks to its intimate knowledge of users, it will still be better at targeting than almost anyone else. “In a world with a lot less data, who has relatively more?” asks Brian Wieser of GroupM. The effect of GDPR was, if anything, to increase Facebook’s and Google’s market shares, he adds.

对于Facebook来说，它更了解用户的兴趣而不是他们的购物需求，苹果的这一变化更令人担忧。今年8月，谷歌警告称，这可能会使其Audience Network的收入减少50%。谷歌通过Audience Network向其他应用程序出售广告。但Audience Network只占其业务的不到十分之一。由于其对用户的深入了解，它仍将比几乎任何其他公司都更善于定位目标用户。GroupM的Brian Wieser问道：“在一个有许多少量数据的世界里，谁拥有的数据相对更多?” 。他补充道，如果说GDPR有什么作用的话，那就是增加了Facebook和谷歌的市场份额。

To improve its tracking of purchases, Facebook is moving to create a closed loop of its own. Last year it introduced Facebook Shops on its flagship social network and Instagram Shops and its sister photo-sharing app. Mark Zuckerberg, Facebook’s boss, speculated in March that “we may even be in a stronger position if Apple’s changes encourage more businesses to conduct more commerce on our platforms, by making it harder for them to use their data…outside of our platforms”.

为了改善购买行为的跟踪，Facebook正在创建其自己的闭环。去年，它在其旗舰社交网络上推出了Facebook商店、并在其姊妹照片分享应用程序上推出Instagram商店。Facebook的老板扎克伯格在3月份曾推测，“如果苹果的改变，让更多企业更难在我们的平台之外使用自己的数据，这一举动能鼓励他们（更多企业）在我们的平台上开展更多的商业活动，我们甚至可能处于更有利的地位。”

Not every ad platform will be able to adapt as easily. Smaller publishers with fewer data and resources will suffer, believes Nicole Perrin of eMarketer, a research firm. Publishers that rely on third-party cookies will be hit hardest. The day Apple launched its new policy, a group of German publishing companies lodged a legal complaint with Germany’s antitrust authorities. Small platforms may also find it harder to persuade phone users to trust them with their data. AppsFlyer, an ad-tech company, found that iPhone users agreed to tracking from shopping and finance apps more than 40% of the time, but 12% of the time with casual gaming apps.

并不是所有的广告平台都能轻松适应（这一改变）。研究公司eMarketer的Nicole Perrin认为，拥有更少数据和资源的小型出版商将受到影响。依赖第三方cookie的出版商将受到最严重的打击。在苹果推出新政策的当天，一批德国出版公司向德国反垄断当局提出了法律诉讼。小型平台可能也会发现更难说服手机用户信任他们（对其）数据（的使用）。广告技术公司AppsFlyer发现，超过40%的iPhone用户同意通过购物和金融应用进行跟踪，但仅有12%的用户同意通过休闲游戏应用进行跟踪。

The inability to share data is forcing advertisers to come up with new ruses. One is to bypass rules banning data transfers between ad-tech companies by consolidating. In February AppLovin, a mobile-software firm, acquired Adjust, which provides mobile-ad attribution, reportedly for $1bn. Another is to ask users to “sign in”, which lets an app monitor their behaviour with no need for IDFAs. And instead of targeting individuals, marketers can target broader interest groups—coffee lovers, Daily Mail readers, and so on—much as they did in the pre-internet age. It’s “back to the future”, says Mr Wieser.

ruse 诡计

无法共享数据正迫使广告商想出新的诡计。一是通过合并绕过禁止广告技术公司之间数据传输的规定。今年2月，移动软件公司AppLovin以10亿美元的价格收购了移动广告归类服务提供商Adjust。另一种方法是要求用户“登录”，市场营销人员可以瞄准更广泛的兴趣群体，如咖啡爱好者、每日邮报读者等，而不是针对个人，就像他们在前互联网时代所做的那样。Wieser说这是“回到未来”。

Stripped of accurate ways of measuring their impact**, “direct-response ads”** that require consumers to take an action (like clicking) lose their appeal. Advertisers will again have to gauge ads’ effectiveness by looking for a rise in sales in a region where an ad ran but not elsewhere. Because campaigns that promote general awareness of a brand never benefited as much from tracking, platforms which mostly attract brand advertising will not feel much difference. Snap, whose social network, popular with teenagers, belongs to that group, posted a year-on-year rise in revenues of 66% in the first quarter.

要求消费者采取行动(比如点击)的“直接响应广告”，失去了衡量其影响力的精确方法，也就失去了其吸引力。广告商将再次评估广告的有效性，他们将在某个地区寻找销售增长的机会，而非其他地方。因为提升品牌知名度的活动从来没有从追踪中获益，那些主要吸引品牌广告的平台不会有太大的区别。在青少年中很受欢迎的社交网站Snap就属于这一群体，该公司第一季度的营收同比增长了66%。

The less advertisers know about their audience, the costlier advertising will be­come. Facebook has argued this will hurt small businesses. It is probably right, thinks William Merchan of Pathmatics, a data company. Digital ads promise to cut waste in media buys, he says. Now that ad­ vertisers are again in the dark about which half of their budget is wasted, they are “go­ing to have to just spend more”.

广告商对他们的受众了解得越少，广告成本就会越高。Facebook认为这将伤害小企业。数据公司Pathmatics的William Merchan认为这可能是正确的。他说，数字广告有望减少媒体购买中的浪费。既然广告商们又不知道他们的预算中哪一半被浪费了，他们“不得不多花点钱”。

Health care and workplaces must adjust for long covid

医疗保健和工作场所必须适应长期冠状病毒

Long covid

长期冠状病毒

![image-20210503094952821](/Users/chenyujie/Library/Application Support/typora-user-images/image-20210503094952821.png)

1.5% of working-age people have lasting symptoms

1.5%的劳动年龄人口有持久性症状

May 1st 2021

背景：越来越多的COVID-19幸存者在感染清除后数周或数月出现新冠病毒症状，这被非正式地称为“Long COVID”。他们在最初确诊后会出现一些持续数月的症状。也有迹象表明，许多新冠肺炎患者会出现他们最初患病时没有的长期症状，这种疾病后来被称为 Long COVID。

AS THE WORLD enters the second year of the pandemic, two crises are unfolding. The more urgent and visible one is in poor countries like India, where a surge of covid-19 cases is threatening to overwhelm the state. 随着世界进入大流行的第二年，两场危机正在展开。更紧迫和明显的危机是在印度等贫穷国家，covid-19病例的激增正威胁着整个国家。印度每天记录在案的病例超过35万例，据信还有更多病例尚未被发现(见亚洲部分)。这种苦难是令人悲痛的。印度医院的氧气供应远远不足，火葬场也人满为患。

The other crisis is more subtle. This is long covid, which is becoming apparent in rich countries like America, Britain and Israel that have , but which will affect poor ones, too. Post-covid syndrome, to give it its formal name, is a set of symptoms affecting any part of the body that persist for at least three months after a bout of covid-19. Three stand out: breathlessness, fatigue and “brain fog”. In Britain three in every five people with long covid say their usual activities are somewhat limited, and one in five says they are limited “a lot”—which often means being unable to do even a part-time, desk-based job.

背景：“大脑三原力”—好的专注力、记忆力和思考理解力，是我们脑神经系统健康运作下，自然会产生的三种扎实基本能力，能够帮助我们进行感知、觉察、推理、判断和决策，是我们想要平顺生活，和追求任何梦想的一切根本和基石。脑雾现象（Brain Fog），“Brain”指的是我们的脑，“Fog”就是浓雾的意思，是大脑三原力产生失调、退化的症状表现。

另一场危机更为微妙。这是一种长期的冠状病毒病，这种病在美国、英国和以色列等发达国家越来越明显，但也会影响到贫穷国家。covid-19正式名称为后冠状病毒综合征，是在covid-19爆发后至少持续三个月的一系列影响身体任何部位的症状。其中三个症状最为突出:呼吸困难、疲劳和“脑雾”。在英国，每五名长冠状病毒感染者中就有三名表示，他们的日常活动在一定程度上受到限制，五分之一的人表示，他们的活动“非常有限”，这通常意味着他们甚至无法从事一份兼职的办公室工作。

The numbers are chilling. Half a million people in Britain have had long covid for more than six months. Their chances of full recovery are probably slim. The vast majority are in their working-age prime. At the last count (which does not fully take in the country’s second wave) 1.1% of Britain’s population had had long covid for at least three months—a group that includes 1.5% of those of working age. About 15% of Britain’s population had been infected by then. Applying this rate to global covid-19 cases, numbering an estimated 1.2bn so far, suggests that more than 80m people may already have long covid.

这些数字令人心寒。在英国，50万人已经有超过6个月的长时间冠状病毒感染。他们完全恢复的机会可能很小。其中绝大多数人正处于工作年龄。根据最近的统计(这还没有完全考虑到英国的第二次爆发浪潮)，1.1%的英国人已经有至少三个月的长时间感染了冠状病毒，这个群体包括1.5%的劳动适龄人口。那时大约15%的英国人已经被感染。将这一比率应用于全球covid-19病例(迄今估计有12亿)，表明超过8000万人可能已经长期患有covid-19。

The costs of the condition have yet to be tallied, but they will be huge. Britain’s National Institute for Health Research found that, in 80% of sufferers, the illness affected the ability to work. Over a third said it had weighed on their finances.

这种状况的代价还没有计算出来，但将是巨大的。英国国家健康研究所发现，在80%的患者中，疾病影响了工作能力。超过三分之一的人表示，这给他们的财务状况带来了压力。

As yet, long covid has no cure. What scientists know so far about the disease points to it being a combination of a persistent viral infection (for which a drug may be found at some point), a chronic autoimmune disorder (which would need expensive, complex care like that for rheumatoid arthritis or multiple sclerosis) and lingering damage to some tissues caused by the original covid-19 infection. Medicines for the first two of these causes may ultimately be found. America alone has put $1.15bn into research. At the moment, though, sufferers need months of rehabilitation to help them cope.

Prompt rehabilitative care can prevent a downward spiral in personal health and finances. Dedicated long-covid clinics will speed things up. Employers, for their part, must rethink how to accommodate workers with a disability that flares up in unpredictable bouts. Governments can help, with incentives that encourage sufferers to stay in work and employers to cater to their condition. If governments miss the boat, millions of young and mid-career workers could permanently drop out of the labour force. One approach could draw on a scheme for disability benefits that is used in the Netherlands. Dutch employers and employees who are too unwell to work as normal are required to come up jointly with a plan on how the sick employee can return to work under new conditions. Remote working and flexible schedules would make it easier for long-covid sufferers to work at least part-time. Many of them will improve, though even that can take months.

The costs of office-less executives outweigh the benefits

无办公室的高管的代价大于收益

May 1st 2021

IT IS A tradition of corporate architecture. A company’s top executives get offices on the top floor, often dubbed the C-suite after the “chiefs” who occupy it. The CEO resides in the “corner office”, with the biggest windows and best views. Junior staff suffer a few moments of trepidation when summoned upstairs.

trepidation 不安

这是企业架构的传统。一家公司的高层管理人员在顶层办公，通常被称为“C-suite”，以“首席执行官”的名字命名。首席执行官坐落在“角落办公室”，其有最大的窗户和最好的风景。级别较低的员工被唤到楼上时，他们会有感到一阵惶恐不安。

Some heterodox bosses shun this tradition. Reed Hastings of Netflix has no office, corner or otherwise, and huddles at random desks, for example. Now more staid firms are following suit. Executives in the London offices of HSBC, a banking giant, will no longer be based on the 42nd floor of the group’s Canary Wharf tower. Instead the floor will be converted into meeting rooms. Senior executives will “hot-desk” with everyone else.

huddle 蜷缩；挤在一起

staid 古板的

一些非正统的老板避开了这一传统。比如，Netflix的Reed Hastings就没有办公室，也没有角落或其他地方，而是蜷缩在任意办公桌。现在更多稳重的公司也在效仿。银行巨头汇丰银行(HSBC)伦敦办事处的高管们将不再驻扎于该集团金丝雀码头大厦42层。相反，这一层将被改造成会议室。高管们将与其他人“轮用办公”。

A plausible argument for such a shift can be made. Staff morale would suffer if the rank-and-file are crammed in open-plan offices while the executives cling to cushy digs with panoramic vistas. When the top brass sit alongside their teams, they will be more in touch with how projects are going, and how staff are feeling. In theory, if the executives are visible, employees are more likely to approach them with problems.

rank-and-file 基层的；普通成员

cushy 舒适的

我们可以提出一个合理的理由来支持这种转变。如果普通员工挤在开放式办公室里，而高管们则坚持呆在能看到全景的舒适办公室，员工的士气就会受到影响。当高级主管坐在团队旁边时，他们将更了解项目的进展情况，以及员工的感受。从理论上讲，如果高管在公众视线内，员工就更有可能向他们提出问题。

But lingering bosses may equally hurt morale. One of the joys of office life is the freedom to enjoy a bit of banter with colleagues. This may include the odd crack about the management. In the presence of their boss staff will be constrained in what they talk about and the tone of their comments. They may feel the need to sound serious at all times, lest the quality of their commitment to their work come into doubt—after all, the manager may be right behind them.

banter with sb 跟某人开玩笑

但是逗留的老板们同样会伤害士气。办公室生活的乐趣之一就是可以自由地和同事开玩笑。这可能包括关于管理的偶尔的漏洞。在老板面前，员工们谈论的内容和评论的语气都很拘束。他们可能觉得有必要在任何时刻都听起来很严肃，以免他们对工作的投入质量受到质疑。毕竟，经理可能就在他们身后。

You also have to wonder whether executives will really spend every morning searching for a place to sit. Some hot desks will be a lot hotter than others. Once the chief financial officer has picked a desk on day one, deferential underlings will steer clear of that particular spot on subsequent days. In contrast, anyone who works closely with a particular executive will be tempted to pick a desk close by. The risk is of a “beach towels on sunbeds” syndrome where employees compete to get the desks nearest to (or farthest from) particular managers.

deferential 恭顺的

underling 走卒

你也不得不怀疑，高管们是否真的会每天早上都在找地方坐。有些办公桌会比其他的要热得多。一旦首席财务官在上任第一天选好了办公桌，恭顺的下属就会在接下来的日子里避开这个特定的位置。相比之下，任何与某位高管密切工作的人都倾向于选择附近的办公桌。这种风险是一种“日光浴床上的沙滩浴巾”综合症，员工们争抢离特定经理最近(或最远)的办公桌。

Of course, the executive may be absent from the open floor for extended periods. Any meeting that involves confidential information, such as a future business plan or a career review, will have to be held in private. So the meeting rooms that HSBC is creating on the executive floor may end up being block-booked by the managers for much of the day.

当然，高管可能会在很长一段时间内不在场。任何涉及机密信息的会议，比如未来的商业计划或职业评估，都必须私下举行。因此，汇丰在高管楼层设立的会议室可能最终会被经理们一天中的大部分时间排满。

Even so, the lingering boss presents other problems. Anyone who has worked in an open-plan office will acknowledge that the babble of others talking can make it hard to concentrate at times. Managers may find themselves constantly being approached by team members with questions or problems for them to solve. Many people resort to headphones to shut out the background noise (and to signal their unavailability). But if managers do that, they run the risk of seeming shut off from their colleagues.

babble 含糊不清的话

即便如此，这位逗留的老板也带来了其他问题。任何在开放式办公室工作过的人都会承认，其他人的闲聊有时会让人难以集中注意力。管理者可能会发现，团队成员经常向他们提出问题，来让他们去解决。很多人会戴着耳机把周围的噪音屏蔽掉(表示自己没空)。但如果管理者这么做，他们就有可能被同事拒之门外。

Studies of open-plan offices have shown that they do not create the hoped-for collaborative effects. One study found that at firms that switched to open-plan design face-to-face interactions fell by 70%. Like an animal caught on open ground without cover, many people do not like being constantly observed. In the absence of a physical barrier, they create a “fourth wall”, indicating their desire for solitude by facial expressions or curt replies to questions.

curt 唐突无礼的

对开放式办公室的研究表明，它们并不能产生预期的协同效果。一项研究发现，在转向开放式设计的公司中，面对面的交流减少了70%。许多人不喜欢经常被人观察，就像在开阔地上捉到的没有遮挡的动物一样。在没有实质障碍的情况下，他们创造了“第四堵墙”，通过面部表情或对问题的简短回答来表明他们独处的愿望。

Where people do need to communicate, it is usually with members of their own team. So using a hot-desking system to mix up different teams, in the hope of creating collaboration, is unlikely to work either. People will avoid contact with their immediate neighbours and will message their other team members electronically.

electronically 用电子方法

人们确实需要交流的地方，通常是与他们自己的团队成员。因此，使用办公桌轮用制来将不同的团队混合，并希望建立协作关系，也不太可能奏效。人们将避免与紧邻的邻居接触，并将电子信息传递给其他团队成员。

In practice, the main benefit for companies of adopting an open-plan design is to save money by cramming more employees in the same space. This explains the willingness of some companies to allow working from home. Recently, HSBC revealed that more than 1,200 staff, mainly in call centres, would be doing so permanently. Indeed, the HSBC executive-office shift is part of a plan that aims to save 40% of head-office costs. As Noel Quinn, the bank’s chief executive, told the Financial Times, the executive offices were empty half the time because the managers were travelling. As reporters are often told, the best way to understand what is really going on is to “follow the money”.

在实践中，采用开放式设计的公司的主要好处是节省资金，因为在同一个空间里可以挤进更多的员工。这就解释了为什么一些公司愿意允许在家工作。最近，汇丰银行透露，将有1200多名员工(主要是呼叫中心)永久在家办公。实际上，汇丰管理层的变动是其计划的一部分，该计划旨在节省40%的总部成本。该行首席执行官Noel Quinn向英国《金融时报》表示，由于经理们在出差，高管办公室有一半时间是空的。正如记者经常被告知的那样，了解真实情况的最佳方式是“跟着钱走”。

The EU wants to become the world’s super-regulator in AI

The Brussels effect

![image-20210430190450002](/Users/chenyujie/Library/Application Support/typora-user-images/image-20210430190450002.png)

And there’s a precedent

这是有先例的

Apr 24th 2021

MOST LAWS are local—except in the digital realm. When the European Union comes up with some new tech regulation, it can quickly spread around the world. Global companies adopt its typically strict rules for all their products and markets in order to avoid having to comply with multiple regimes. Other governments take more than one page from the EU’s rule book to help local firms compete. The textbook example for what has been dubbed the “Brussels effect”, is the EU’s General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR), which went into force in 2018 and swiftly became the global standard.

除了数字领域以外，大多数法律都是地方性的。当欧盟出台一些新的科技法规时，它可以迅速蔓延到世界各地。全球公司对其所有产品和市场采用其典型的严格规则，以避免必须遵守多种制度。其他国家的政府从欧盟的规则手册中获取了不止一页的信息来帮助当地企业竞争。被称为“布鲁塞尔效应”的典型例子是欧盟的《通用数据保护条例》，该条例于2018年生效，并迅速成为全球标准。

Small wonder, then, that all eyes were on Brussels when the European Commission on April 21st published proposed regulations on artificial intelligence (AI)—making it the first influential regulator to craft a big law on AI. Will these rules be as widely adopted as GDPR?

no or small or little wonder 不足为奇

因此，当欧盟委员会4月21日公布了人工智能(AI)法规草案时，所有的目光都集中在布鲁塞尔，这也就不足为奇了。这使得布鲁塞尔成为第一个制定人工智能大法的有影响力的监管机构。这些规则会像《通用数据保护条例》一样被广泛采用吗?

Recent years have seen an explosion of ethics guidelines in AI, in keeping with the hype surrounding the technology. Many hope it will boost economic growth, but others worry that AI could cause great harm, for instance if algorithms end up discriminating against certain groups of people. At least 175 countries, firms and other organisations have drawn up lists of ethical principles. But most of these fall short of describing how such things as “robustness” or “transparency” can be achieved in practice, let alone how they can be backed up by enforceable laws, says Charlotte Stix of the Eindhoven University of Technology.

discriminate against sb; 歧视某人

enforceable 可强制执行的

近年来，人工智能领域的道德准则激增，围绕该技术的炒作与之保持一致，数量也在激增。许多人希望它能促进经济增长，但也有人担心人工智能可能会造成巨大伤害，例如，如果算法最终歧视某些群体。至少有175个国家、公司和其他组织起草了伦理原则清单。但是，埃因霍温理工大学的夏洛特•斯蒂克斯说，这些伦理原则清单大多没有描述如何在实践中实现“健壮性”或“透明度”，更不用说如何得到可执行法律的支持。

With little existing legislation on AI to draw on, the commission opted for a bottom-up approach. It created a 52-member “high-level expert group” to develop its proposals, collected further input via an “AI alliance” of interested parties and published a white paper on which everybody could comment online (1,250 groups and individuals did so). The result is a document of more than 100 pages with 85 articles and no fewer than nine annexes that tries both to mitigate the potential harm of AI and to maximise its opportunities—almost to a fault, as the many exceptions and exceptions to exceptions show.

annex 附件

to a fault 过分地

由于目前几乎没有关于人工智能的立法可以借鉴，欧盟委员会选择了一种自下而上的方法。它创建了一个52人组成的“高级别专家组”来制定提案，通过由感兴趣的各方组成的“人工智能联盟”收集进一步的意见，并发布了一份白皮书，每个人都可以在网上发表评论(1250个团体和个人已经这样做了)。结果就是成了一份超过100页、85篇文章和不少于9个附件的文件，该文件试图减轻人工智能的潜在危害并过分的最大化其带来的机会，正如许多异议所显示的那样。

Rather than regulating all applications of AI, the EU’s rules are meant to focus on the riskiest ones. Some will be banned outright, including services that use “subliminal techniques” to manipulate people. Others, such as facial recognition and credit scoring, are considered “high-risk” and so subject to strict rules on transparency and data quality. As with GDPR, penalties for violations are stiff: up to €30m ($36m) or 6% of global revenues, whichever is higher (in the case of a firm as big as Facebook, for example, that would come to more than $5bn).

subliminal 潜意识的

欧盟的规则旨在关注风险最高的应用，而不是监管人工智能的所有应用。有些应用将被彻底禁止，包括使用“潜意识技术”来操纵用户的服务。其他应用，如面部识别和信用评分，被认为是“高风险”的，因此需要在透明度和数据质量方面遵守严格的规定。与GDPR一样，对违规行为的处罚也很严厉:最高可达3000万欧元(3600万美元)或全球营收的6%，以更高的金额为准(例如，对于Facebook这样的大公司，处罚将超过50亿美元)。

Even more than usual, however, the devil is in the details. Facial recognition for the purpose of law enforcement in public places, which raises the spectre of pervasive surveillance, is prohibited, but only if it is done in real time and barring any other “substantial public interest”, such as finding missing children. All high-risk AI services must be tested for legal conformity, but this can often be done by the provider itself. And EU member states are encouraged to create regulatory “sandboxes”, in which firms can try out novel services without fear of being hit by a penalty.

conformity 遵守

spectre [似幽灵般] 缠绕内心的恐惧

然而，比以往更让人头疼的是细节。为了在公共场所执法而进行面部识别是被禁止，这会让人对无处不在的监控赶到恐惧，但前提是它是实时进行的，并且不涉及任何其他“重大公共利益”，比如寻找失踪儿童。所有高风险的人工智能服务都必须进行符合法律的测试，但这通常可以由提供商自己完成。欧盟成员国也被鼓励建立监管“沙盒”，这样企业就可以尝试新的服务而不用担心受到惩罚。

Unsurprisingly, many interested parties are unhappy. Human-rights advocates criticise mushy language and loopholes. “There’s a real question-mark over whether the regulatory framework is robust enough,” says Sarah Chander of European Digital Rights. In contrast, business groups complain about the regulatory burden. The law will “limit the areas in which AI can realistically be used”, warns Benjamin Mueller of the Centre for Data Innovation, a think-tank supported by tech firms.

question mark 疑问；问号

不出所料，许多利益相关方对此并不满意。人权倡导者批评含糊不清的语言和漏洞。欧洲数字版权协会的Sarah Chander 表示:“监管框架是否足够健全，这确实是一个问号。”相比之下，商业团体抱怨监管负担过重。由科技公司支持的智库数据创新中心的Benjamin Mueller警告称，该法律将“限制人工智能能够实际应用的领域”。

Much will still change in a legislative process that will take years, perhaps even longer than the four years it took GDPR to get from proposal to adoption. But even if things move faster, the EU may have a harder time setting global rules, or at least strict ones, says Anu Bradford of Columbia Law School, who has written a book about the Brussels effect. In the case of some AI applications, such as algorithms that can be perfected without needing to be trained using massive inputs of data, providers may decide that offering special versions in Europe is worth their while. And having been rather surprised by the success of GDPR, lobbyists will redouble their efforts to get their voices heard in Brussels.

立法过程仍将有许多改变，这将会持续数年的时间，可能比GDPR从提案到采纳所花的4年时间还要长。哥伦比亚大学法学院的Anu Bradford写了一本关于布鲁塞尔效应的书，他说，即使事情进展得更快，欧盟可能也很难制定全球规则，或者至少很难制定严格的规则。就某些人工智能应用而言，比如不需要使用大量数据输入进行训练就可以完善的算法，提供商可能会认为在欧洲提供特殊规则是值得的。在对GDPR的成功感到相当惊讶之后，游说者将加倍努力让布鲁塞尔听到他们的声音。

Yet the fate of the “Artificial Intelligence Act” (AIA), as it could end up being called, may well be decided in America. If GDPR took the world by storm, it was partly because Congress not only failed to come up with any data-protection legislation of its own, but also did not bother to co-operate with lawmakers in Brussels. The new administration wants to do better, but so far the transatlantic rapprochement in AI and other things tech is off to a slow start. Only if both sides work together will they beat back China’s ambitions for tech supremacy and keep digital authoritarianism at bay. ■

战后德国的选举更倾向于中年男性之间，最近也出现了和中年女性之间的礼貌竞争，他们都提出了有价值但类似的中间派政策。这一进程上一次达到令人昏昏欲睡的时刻大约是在2017年，两个主要政党开始了竞选活动，此前的过去的四年里两个政党一直以一个“大联盟”的形势联合在一起。那让他们更没有机会向缺乏热情的选民提供相互竞争的愿景。今年，令人高兴的是，事情会有所不同。

For a start, there is a genuine chance that the election, to be held on September 26th, will produce Germany’s (and almost the world’s) first Green head of government in the form of Annalena Baerbock, a 40-year-old parliamentarian with no ministerial experience but plenty of energy, policy nous and a past as an accomplished trampolinist. In a smooth if opaque operation on April 19th, the Green Party anointed Ms Baerbock, rather than her co-leader, the more experienced but more academic Robert Habeck, as its candidate for chancellor. The party saw that she offered it the best chance in September.

in the form of 以某种形式

parliamentarian 议员

ministerial 部长的

nous 智力

trampoling: the sport or activity of performing physical exercises on a trampoline (= a piece of sports equipment that you jump up and down on)

be sb’s anointed heir 成为某人的选定继承人

首先，将于9月26日举行的选举很有可能产生德国（几乎是世界上）第一位绿色政府首脑，Annalena Baerbock，这位40岁的议员没有部长级经验，但精力充沛，有政策头脑，过去曾是一位出色的蹦床运动员。4月19日，绿党在一次顺利但不透明的行动中，任命Baerbock女士为总理候选人，而不是她的共同领导人，经验更丰富但更具学术性的Robert Habeck。该党认为她在九月份给它提供了最好的机会。

The succession process has been messier for its conservative rivals, the Christian Democratic Union (CDU) and its sister party, Bavaria’s Christian Social Union (CSU). On April 20th, after a long struggle that has exposed divisions within and between the two parties, they agreed that the CDU’s boss, Armin Laschet, should be their standard-bearer, even though voters prefer the CSU’s man by a massive margin. Ms Baerbock could hardly have hoped for a better start. Her party has been running a strong second place in the polls, behind the CDU/CSU.

messy 混乱的; 棘手的

standard-bearer 领袖

对其保守派竞争对手基督教民主联盟(CDU)及其姊妹党巴伐利亚基督教社会联盟(CSU)来说，继任过程更为混乱。4月20日，经过长期的斗争，两党内部和之间的分歧已经暴露出来，他们同意CDU的领袖Armin Laschet成为他们的领袖，不过选民们对CSU的人有着巨大优势的偏爱。Baerbock女士几乎没有希望有一个更好的开始。她所在的政党在民意调查中一直位居第二，仅次于基民盟/基社盟。

Voters now face a real choice. The Greens have come a long way from their radical anti-growth Fundi (for “fundamentalist”) roots, and have plenty of experience of government. They form part of the ruling coalition in 11 of Germany’s 16 states. Even so, if they won power at the federal level they would pursue a much more ambitious climate policy. They want, for instance, to cut greenhouse-gas emissions by 70% from the 1990 baseline by 2030, rather than the existing target of 55%. They would more than double the level of carbon taxes, set tougher targets for renewables in power generation and accelerate the closure of Germany’s coal-fired power plants.

选民们现在面临一个真正的选择。绿党已经远离他们激进的反增长的Fundi(“原教旨主义”)根基上，并且拥有丰富的执政经验。他们在德国16个州中的11个州组成了执政联盟。即便如此，如果他们赢得了联邦一级的权力，他们将追求一个更雄心勃勃的气候政策。例如，他们希望到2030年将温室气体排放量从1990年的基准水平减少70%，而不是现有的55%的目标。他们将把碳排放税提高一倍以上，为发电领域的可再生能源设定更严格的目标，并加速关闭德国的燃煤电厂。

In other areas too, the Green programme is a genuine alternative. The Greens will be more receptive to spending money, both at the European level, in the shape of more EU-backed funding for investment in weaker countries, and in Germany itself, where the strictures against deficit financing have resulted in surprisingly creaky infrastructure, especially of the digital variety. The Greens are divided over foreign policy, but may be tougher on Russia and China and less inclined to put trade ahead of human rights.

stricture 限制

creaky 站不住脚的；摇摇欲坠的;陈旧的；老朽的

在其他领域，绿色计划也是一个真正可能的选择。绿党更愿意花钱，无论是在欧洲层面，在更多欧盟支持的资助下，对弱国进行投资，还是在德国其自身层面，其对赤字融资的限制导致了令人惊讶的陈旧的基础设施，尤其是各种数字化的基础设施。绿党在外交政策上存在分歧，但对俄罗斯和XX可能更强硬，不太倾向于将贸易置于人权之上。

The CDU/CSU, in contrast, will offer business as usual. But that appeals to many Germans, especially those who fear the Greens are still anti-business. The parties have won the most seats at every federal election since 2005. The cautious, consensus-building Mr Laschet has demonstrated that he is tenacious, even if he lacks the charisma of his CSU rival, Markus Söder.

charisma 非凡的个人魅力

相比之下，基民盟/基社盟将一如既往地提供业务。但这吸引了许多德国人，尤其是那些担心绿党仍是反商业的人。自2005年以来，两党在每次联邦选举中都赢得了最多的席位。谨慎的、建立共识的Laschet先生已经证明了他的顽强，即使他缺乏他的基社盟对手Markus Söder的魅力。

One way or another, though, Germany’s future looks greener—and Europe’s with it. Even if Ms Baerbock fails to become chancellor, the numbers suggest it will be impossible to construct the next ruling coalition without the Greens. A new grand coalition has been all but ruled out by the Social Democrats, who have seen their popularity plummet as a result of being involved in the past two; the far-right Alternative for Germany remains a political pariah; and the liberal Free Democrats are a marginal force. Ms Baerbock will have a fistful of strong cards to play. ■

pariah 为社会所抛弃者；受蔑视的人；不被信任者

a fistful of sth 一把某物

然而，无论如何，德国的未来看起来更加绿色，欧洲也会如此。即使Baerbock未能成为总理，这些数据表明，没有绿党的支持，下届执政联盟也不可能组建。一个新的大联合政府几乎被社会民主党排除在外，他们的支持率由于参与了过去两年的选举而急剧下降; 极右翼的德国另类选择党(Alternative for Germany)在政治方面仍不被信任; 而自由民主党只是一股边缘力量。Baerbock女士将有一把强有力的牌要打。■
