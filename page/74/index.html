Japanese people may have gained longevity by balancing their diets

日本人可能通过平衡饮食而长寿

The meat spot

一点肉

![image-20210123093604498](/Users/chenyujie/Library/Application Support/typora-user-images/image-20210123093604498.png)

Japan’s rate of strokes fell during a period when it began eating a bit of meat

日本的中风率在开始食用少量肉类的时期有所下降

Jan 16th 2021

TANAKA KANE is one of humanity’s great outliers. On January 2nd she became the third person ever to turn 118, according to the Gerontology Research Group, a team of academics. She is also the first citizen of Japan to reach 118—but is unlikely to be the last. The country has the world’s longest life expectancy, and 80,000 centenarians.

田中凯恩是人类最大的异类之一。1月2日，她成为第三位过118岁生日的老人。她也是日本第一个年满118岁的公民，但不太可能是最后一个。该国是世界上预期寿命最长的国家，有8万名百岁老人。

Mrs Tanaka is an outlier for another reason, too. She claims to love chocolate and fizzy drinks, setting her apart from most of her compatriots. Japan has long had one of the lowest sugar-consumption rates in the OECD, a club of mainly wealthy countries.

田中夫人之所以是局外人还有另一个原因。她声称自己喜欢巧克力和碳酸饮料，这让她在大多数同胞中脱颖而出。在经济合作与发展组织(一个主要由富裕国家组成的俱乐部)中，日本长期以来一直是糖消耗量最低的国家之一。

The unusual longevity enjoyed in Japan is often credited to diet. Yet the idea that the country has extended lifespans by entirely avoiding the West’s sinful culinary delights may be too simple. In fact, recent studies imply that one key to its success may be that its people’s diets have shifted over time towards Western eating patterns.

Japan was not always a longevity champion. In 1970 its age-adjusted mortality rates were average for the OECD. Although its levels of cancer and heart disease were relatively low, it also had the OECD’s highest frequency of cerebrovascular deaths, caused by blood failing to reach the brain.

日本并非一直都是长寿冠军。1970年，其年龄调整后的死亡率为经合组织的平均水平。尽管它的癌症和心脏病水平相对较低，但它的脑血管死亡频率在经合组织中是最高的，脑血管死亡是由血液无法到达大脑引起的。

How did Japan overcome its cerebrovascular woes? Some of its gains simply mirror better treatments and reductions in blood pressure around the world, notes Thomas Truelsen of the University of Copenhagen.

Too much of these can be damaging, since they contain saturated fatty acids, which correlate to heart disease. Studies have also tied eating lots of processed red meat to a greater risk of stroke. But too little may be unwise as well, because they provide cholesterol that may be needed for blood-vessel walls. In a study of 48,000 Britons, vegetarians were unusually resistant to heart disease, but prone to strokes.

In 1960-2013, as the country’s deaths from strokes tumbled, its annual meat intake rose from near zero to 52kg per person (45% of America’s level). Tsugane Shoichiro of the National Cancer Centre in Tokyo says that his compatriots may need meat and dairy to keep their blood vessels robust—though not so much that those vessels get clogged.

Some empirical evidence supports this view. One paper from the 1990s found that the parts of Japan where diets had changed most also had the biggest drops in cerebrovascular mortality. Another study, which tracked 80,000 Japanese people in 1995-2009, showed that strokes were most common among those who ate the least chops and cream. Although Japan’s decline in cerebrovascular deaths could stem entirely from other causes, these data suggest that nutritional shifts may have helped.

The unhappy irony is that Japan’s health gains, paired with a low birth rate, threaten its economy. By 2060, 40% of Japanese could be 60 or older. That would yield more birthday cakes with 118 candles—and fewer great-grandchildren to blow them out. ■

具有讽刺意味的是，日本健康水平的提高，加上低出生率，威胁到了日本经济。到2060年，日本60岁以上的人口将达到40%。这样就会有更多的生日蛋糕和118根蜡烛，而且会有更少的曾孙吹灭蛋糕。■

Since time out of mind they have made a living by robbing visitors of their possessions and then holding those objects hostage until a ransom in the form of food is paid. That is quite clever. But Jean-Baptiste Leca of the University of Lethbridge, in Canada, wondered whether these monkeys are cleverer still. Sometimes, they do not accept the first offer and hold out for more. He therefore asked himself whether they are able to assess how valuable an object is to its owner, and factor that into their negotiations.

Laboratory experiments conducted in the past with various species of monkey and ape suggest such primates can indeed attach a value to something intrinsically worthless to them, like a coloured plastic counter, by learning that tokens of this sort may be exchanged for food, and that different types of token bring different rewards, not all of them equally valued by the animal (a desirable grape versus an undesirable piece of cucumber, for example). That, though, is an artificial protocol in an artificial setting. The macaques of Uluwatu are true wild animals, albeit ones that are familiar with, and comfortable in the presence of, human beings.

Every time they saw a monkey show interest in a particular tourist, they recorded the interaction. Not all attempts at robbery were successful. IT HAD BEEN closed, on and off, for much of the past year. Now signs on the blacked-out windows of the Zara shop on the Champs-Elysées, the Spanish brand’s early outpost in the French capital, announce it will not re-open even after covid-19 passes. Instead, Zara is chasing its young clientele to where they spend the most time: on their phones, shuffling between Instagram and TikTok. The shift to online sales, which has been turbocharged by lockdowns, will require some agile refitting of the way fashion brands do business.

Shops are giant bundles of fixed costs, starting with rent and staff, which turn profitable only once you shift enough product through them—the idea is to stack it high and sell it cheap. The wrinkle for established retailers is that their website often serves customers who would once have rung up the tills in physical stores, leaving them emptier. Unless at least some shops are closed, says Aneesha Sherman of Bernstein, a broker, retailers risk having to cough up new variable costs of online fulfilment while continuing to incur the fixed costs of legacy bricks and mortar.

为什么谷歌和Facebook要把英国的数据转移到美国

Chlorinated data “氯化”数据

![image-20210114094358235](/Users/chenyujie/Library/Application Support/typora-user-images/image-20210114094358235.png)

Projecting British law into California may prove difficult

将英国法律引入加州可能会被证明是困难的

Jan 7th 2021

背景：Facebook Inc将把所有英国用户的用户协议转移至加州公司总部，结束与Facebook爱尔兰子公司当前的协议，摆脱欧洲隐私法的约束。将用户协议移至美国意味着英国用户将不再受到为遵守欧盟严格的隐私制度而制定的数据政策的保护。这项更改与谷歌去年2月份所做的更改类似，是为了避免当英国公民不再是欧盟公民时，仍然在欧盟国家/地区拥有隐私协议的情况。在这种情况下，Facebook必须遵守欧盟的数据保护法。不过，之后的英国用户仍将受到英国数据隐私法的保护，该法律目前与欧盟的相关法律相当。

GOOGLE AND Facebook collect more data about what people are doing on the internet—the web pages they read, the services they use, the links they click—than any other companies. Those data are used to construct profiles of internet users, against which personalised advertisements may be sold. This year, as a consequence of Brexit, the firms are moving legal responsibility for that data from Dublin, where it has sat for the past few years under European law, to California, where both technology firms have their headquarters.

as a consequence of 由于…

GOOGLE和Facebook收集的关于人们在互联网上做什么的数据比其他任何公司都多，比如他们阅读的网页，他们使用的服务，他们点击的链接。这些数据被用于构建互联网用户的个人资料，并据此出售个性化广告。今年，由于英国脱欧，这两家公司将对这些数据的法律责任从都柏林(都柏林过去几年一直是欧洲法律规定的数据存放地)转移到加利福尼亚（这两家科技公司都在那里设立了总部）。

Bits of data themselves will not physically move—datasets are already copied onto servers around the planet in order to ensure reliable service independent from geography. It is legal responsibility for those bits that is moving, some time in 2021. Google and Facebook users in Britain will be informed of the move through one of the regular, tedious sets of terms and conditions which they are asked to accept.

一些数据本身不会在物理上移动——数据集已经被复制到全球各地的服务器上，以确保独立于地理位置的可靠服务。到2021年的某个时候，对于那些正在移动的数据是有法律责任的。英国的谷歌和脸书用户将通过一套常规的、冗长的条款和条件被告知这一行动，他们被要求接受这些条款和条件。

Google’s and Facebook’s move means that British residents will no longer have recourse to European data-protection law. Rather as American food standards are easier on farmers than European ones, which leads to chicken meat being washed in chlorine, so the absence of national privacy or data-protection law in America means that its digital giants can, for instance, deploy face-recognition technology with relative ease. As long as legal authority for a profile remained in Europe, that person, regardless of location, could bring a complaint under European law. Upon acceptance of the new terms, Britons will lose the protections of European law.

recourse 求助；手段

have recourse to sth 诉诸某物

without recourse to.. 无需求助于…

Surgery may be the only recourse. 手术也许是唯一的办法。

谷歌和Facebook的举动意味着英国居民将不再求助于欧洲数据保护法。就像美国的食品标准对农民来说比欧洲的更容易，这导致用氯洗鸡肉，因此美国国家隐私或数据保护法的缺失意味着美国的数字巨头可以相对容易地部署人脸识别技术。只要个人资料的合法权限仍在欧洲，那么此人，无论其身在何处，都可以根据欧洲法律提起申诉。一旦接受新条款，英国人将失去欧洲法律的保护。

In principle that should not matter. British data-protection law is a close copy of Europe’s GDPR, and Britons will still be protected by that regardless of the jurisdiction that administers their data. But practical concerns mean the transfer will make a difference. First, Britain’s Information Commissioner’s Office will face the task of regulating giant companies some 5,000 miles away, which may be a struggle for a body that has not excelled at regulating activity even on British soil. Trying to project British law, should Facebook or Google ever break it, into California will be messy, even for post-Brexit Britain at its most global. Dublin was more convenient.

jurisdiction 管辖区域；管辖权

excel at sth 擅长某事

struggle 难事

It was a real struggle to be ready on time.

要按时做好准备确非易事。

I had a struggle to convince them.

说服他们我费了很大力气。

原则上，这并不重要。英国的数据保护法是欧洲《通用数据保护条例》的翻版，英国人仍将受到该法律的保护，无论他们的数据由哪个司法管辖区管理。但出于实际考虑，这种转移将产生影响。首先，英国信息专员办公室(Information Commissioner’s Office)将面临监管5000英里以外大型公司的任务，对于一个甚至在英国本土都不擅长监管活动的机构来说，这可能是一件难事。如果Facebook或谷歌违反了英国法律，试图将英国法律投射到加利福尼亚将是棘手的，即使是对于脱欧后最全球化的英国来说也是如此。都柏林更方便。

Second, America’s large tech companies are now free to lobby Westminster for favourable changes to Britain’s data-protection law, something which would have been pointless while their legal responsibilities lay in Dublin under European law. It is plausible, says Michael Veale, a lecturer in digital rights at University College London, that tech giants may seek to make Britain a regulatory beacon to Europe through this lobbying, working with the newly flexible, newly sovereign British government to demonstrate the benefits of a new deal on data protection.

be free to do sth 可自由做某事

其次，美国大型科技公司现在可以自由地游说英国议会(Westminster)对英国数据保护法进行有利的修改，而（如果数据）在都柏林，它们的法律责任受欧洲法律的保护，修改数据保护法毫无意义。伦敦大学学院（universitycollege London）数字权利讲师迈克尔•维莱（Michael Veale）表示, 科技巨头可能会通过这一游说活动，与新近变得灵活、拥有主权的英国政府合作，试图让英国成为欧洲的监管灯塔，以展示数据保护新政的好处，这是有道理的。

It is also plausible that Britons’ standards of data protection will be downgraded over time through this lobbying, although that would risk triggering a fight with the EU over adequacy, and threaten the flow of data between Britain and the EU. It is more likely that, when it comes to their relationship with American tech giants, Britons will be stuck in a post-Brexit quagmire, with an under-resourced regulator trying to control powerful companies thousands of miles away. The EU, for all its faults, kept the locus of control closer to home.

be stuck in 困于，停止不前，动弹不得

quagmire 沼泽地；困境

for all 虽然;尽管

locus of control 控制点

通过这种游说，英国的数据保护标准将随着时间的推移而降低，这也是有道理的，尽管这可能会引发与欧盟(EU)就数据保护的适当性发生争执，并威胁到英国与欧盟之间的数据流动。更有可能的是，当涉及到英国与美国科技巨头的关系时，英国人将陷入脱欧后的泥潭，一个资源不足的监管机构试图控制数千英里以外的强大公司。欧盟，尽管有它所有的缺点，仍然把控制点放在离家更近的地方。

Could the pandemic cause economists to rethink welfare?

这种流行病是否会引起经济学家重新思考福利问题？

Free exchange 自由交流

![image-20210113100547957](/Users/chenyujie/Library/Application Support/typora-user-images/image-20210113100547957.png)

At the American Economic Association’s annual shindig, Emmanuel Saez argues for change

在美国经济协会（American Economic Association）的年度盛会上，Emmanuel Saez主张变革

Jan 6th 2021

EVERY JANUARY a curious migration occurs. Thousands of economists from around the world flock to a big American city for the annual meetings of the American Economic Association (AEA). The pandemic upended the rite this year, and instead enabled conference attendees to peer into the living rooms and offices of scholars as they presented their work, much of it focused on the consequences of covid-19, on Zoom. The events of 2020 drew attention to the importance for prosperity of factors that economists often neglect, such as state capacity and social trust. Perhaps in a sign of change to economic thinking to come, some of the fare on offer at this year’s shindig suggested that raising welfare is often more a matter of collective choice than maximising efficiency.

shindig 狂欢聚会

on offer 供出售

每年一月都会发生一次奇怪的”迁徙”。成千上万的经济学家从世界各地涌向美国的一个大城市，参加美国经济协会(AEA)的年度会议。今年的大流行颠覆了这个仪式，相反，在学者们展示他们的工作时，与会者得以在Zoom上窥视他们的客厅和办公室，这些工作主要集中在covid-19的影响上。2020年的事件让人们注意到一些经济学家经常忽视对繁荣的重要性的因素，比如国家能力和社会信任。也许是经济思维即将改变的一个迹象，今年聚会上提供的一些票价表明，提高福利往往是一个集体选择的问题，而不是效率最大化的问题。

Discussions of the role of social norms and values were scattered throughout the conference. Scholars presented work showing that higher levels of trust and social responsibility were associated with less scepticism of media reporting on covid-19 and greater willingness to accept stringent lockdown measures. Other research demonstrated that gender-related aspects of corporate culture are in large part determined by the norms prevailing in local society, but can nonetheless be shaped by national policy. Still other papers examined how economic factors contributed to the unravelling of political norms and the eventual collapse of democracy in Europe before the second world war.

unravelling 解开，拆散，松开 ; 解体 ; 崩溃 ; 瓦解 ;

关于社会规范和价值观的作用的讨论分散在整个会议上。学者们的研究表明，信任度和社会责任感越高，媒体对covid-19报道的怀疑程度就越低，接受严格封锁措施的意愿也就越强。其他研究表明，企业文化中与性别有关的方面在很大程度上是由当地社会普遍存在的规范决定的，但也可能受到国家政策的影响。还有一些论文研究了经济因素是如何在第二次世界大战之前导致政治规范的瓦解和欧洲民主制度的最终崩溃的。

Yet the most pointed discussion of the importance of social forces—and economists’ tendency to overlook them—came in a keynote lecture by Emmanuel Saez of the University of California, Berkeley. He is known for his work assembling historical data on income and wealth inequality, but his lecture was far more philosophical than empirical. In it, he asked his fellow economists to rethink their usual approach to the welfare state.

known for 以…而闻名

historical data 历史数据

welfare state 福利制度 ; 福利国家

背景：福利国家并非一种国家类型，宏观上是国家与社会关系的一种状态，微观上是对政府一系列特定行为的描述，可以说是一种由国家通过立法来承担维护和增进全体国民基本福利职能的政府行为模式。当然，这种政府行为在历史政治过程中会逐渐形成一定的路径依赖，因此具有相对稳定性。

然而，加州大学伯克利分校的Emmanuel Saez在一场主题演讲中对社会力量的重要性以及经济学家们忽视它们的倾向进行了最尖锐的讨论。他以收集收入和财富不平等的历史数据而闻名，但他的演讲更多的是哲学而非实证（但他的演讲远比经验主义更富哲学色彩）。在演讲中，他要求他的经济学同行们重新思考他们对待福利国家的惯常方法。

As Mr Saez noted, the standard framework assumes that society is comprised of rational individuals making decisions based on self-interest. Choices about how much to save or what sort of education to acquire are informed by people’s understanding of their own conditions and preferences. Economists working under these assumptions may support welfare programmes in the event of market failures. Unemployment benefits are needed, say, because credit markets are far from perfect, and a jobless person cannot simply borrow to make up for a temporary loss of income, however confident they are of eventually finding work. But the assumption of rational self-interest constrains the welfare state significantly. Generous benefits, and the high taxes needed to fund them, will put rationally minded people off work, undermining economic growth and the government’s capacity to help people in need.

in the event of … 万一发生..

正如Saez所指出的，标准框架假设社会是由基于自身利益做出决策的理性个体组成的。关于储蓄多少钱或接受何种教育的选择是由人们对自身条件和偏好的理解决定的。在这些假设下工作的经济学家可能会在市场失灵的情况下支持福利计划。失业救济是必需的，因为信贷市场还远远不够完善，而且无论失业者对最终找到工作有多有信心，他们都不能简单地借些钱来弥补暂时的收入损失。但是，理性自利的假设极大地限制了福利国家。慷慨的福利，以及为其提供资金所需的高额税收，会让理性的人们失去工作，削弱经济增长，削弱政府帮助有需要的人的能力。

In practice, though, Mr Saez explained, the world works differently. Societies have largely chosen to tackle problems such as old-age poverty and inadequate schooling with collective solutions rather than individual ones, through universal pensions and compulsory education. These social choices are partly a response to the difficulties people face providing such things for themselves. But they also reflect values, such as ideas about fairness. The fact that society deems education to be a social good and provides the means to achieve it encourages young people to attend school for years, regardless of whether it is a rational economic choice. And as behavioural economists have shown, people do not exclusively behave as hard-bitten rationalists. The fairness of outcomes matters to people, and they are willing to make sacrifices for the greater good in an environment of social trust and reciprocity.

hard-bitten 久经磨练的

exclusively 仅仅

reciprocity 互惠

There is no reciprocity of trade agreements between the two nations.

两国之间没有互惠贸易协议。

然而，Saez先生解释说，在实践中，世界的运作方式是不同的。社会在很大程度上选择通过全民养老金和义务教育等集体解决办法而不是个人解决办法来解决老年贫困和教育不足等问题。这些社会选择在一定程度上是对人们在为自己提供这些东西时所面临的困难的回应。但它们也反映了价值观，比如对公平的看法。社会认为教育是一种社会福利，并提供实现教育的手段，这一事实鼓励年轻人上学多年，而不管这是否是一种理性的经济选择。正如行为经济学家所表明的，人们的行为并不完全是一个顽固的理性主义者。结果的公平性对人们来说很重要，在一个社会信任和互惠的环境中，人们愿意为更大的利益做出牺牲。

These observations should influence analyses of public policies. The decision to work, for instance, may be influenced by norms as well as by financial incentives. By considering only the latter, economists might overestimate the work-discouraging effect of welfare schemes. Employment rates for prime-age men are remarkably similar across rich countries, Mr Saez pointed out, despite big differences in tax and welfare systems. Average tax rates in France are roughly 20 percentage points higher than those in America across the income distribution, yet about 80% of middle-aged men work in each country. (Americans do work more hours, but, as Mr Saez noted, this too reflects social choices, such as the shorter working week specified in French law.) There is strong social pressure for healthy men not to be seen as “freeloaders”, which pushes against the incentives created by higher taxes or bigger welfare cheques. Where social pressures to work are more ambiguous—as for the young or old or, in many places, women—generous benefits tend to have larger effects on employment decisions, Mr Saez noted. But this reinforces rather than undercuts the idea that social factors have important effects on economic decisions.

income distribution 收入分配

这些观察结果应该会影响对公共政策的分析。例如，工作的决定可能受到规范和经济激励的影响。如果只考虑后者，经济学家可能会高估福利计划对工作的抑制作用。Saez先生指出，尽管在税收和福利制度上存在巨大差异，但富裕国家的壮年男性就业率却非常相似。在收入分配上，法国的平均税率大约比美国高20个百分点，然而每个国家都有80%的中年男性在工作。(美国人的确工作时间更长，但正如赛斯指出的那样，这也反映了社会选择，比如法国法律规定的每周工作时间更短。)对于健康男性来说，存在着强大的社会压力，他们不能被视为“白吃白喝的人”，这就阻碍了更高的税收或更高的福利支票所产生的激励。Saez先生指出，在那些工作的社会压力比较模糊的地方——比如对年轻人或老年人，或者在许多地方对妇女——慷慨的福利往往会对就业决策产生更大的影响。但这强化而非削弱了社会因素对经济决策具有重要影响的观点。

Fair’s fair

When social norms guard against “bad” behaviours—those, like voluntary unemployment or tax avoidance, which could be considered to be taking unfair advantage of the system—welfare states present less of a trade-off between efficiency and equity than many economists suspect. Analyses deploying assumptions of hard-nosed self-interest remain useful in such cases, as descriptions of how government schemes could fail if social guardrails are allowed to erode, for example. Yet they are not necessarily the best predictors of what will happen, and the failure to take social preferences seriously could mean that economists’ prescriptions for making societies more egalitarian and dynamic fall flat.

guard against 防止

guard against disease 预防疾病

dangers to be guarded against 需要防范的危险

voluntary unemployment 自愿失业，自动失业

tax avoidance 避税

unfair advantage 不公平的优势

hard-nosed 冷静精明的

egalitarian 平等主义的

fall flat <joke,plan>未达到效果；<party,celebration>没意思

当社会规范防范“不良”行为时，诸如自愿失业或避税等可被视为不公平地利用制度的行为，福利国家在效率和公平之间的权衡就没有许多经济学家所怀疑的那么重要了。在这类情况下，运用精明的利己主义假设的分析仍然有用，例如，如果任由社会壁垒侵蚀，政府计划如何可能失败的描述。然而，它们不一定是未来情况的最佳预测者，而未能认真对待社会偏好可能意味着，经济学家为使社会更平等、更有活力所开出的处方没有效果。

Of course, culture changes slowly, both within economics and without. Mr Saez is much further along than most of his peers in emphasising the role of social factors. Yet just as the global financial crisis of 2007-09 prompted a rethink of the profession’s understanding of financial markets and macroeconomic policy, the pandemic may focus attention on other blind spots. Next year’s conference, when it rolls around, may be a much more social event than this year’s—in more ways than one. ■

macroeconomic 宏观经济的

当然，无论是经济内部还是外部，文化变化都是缓慢的。在强调社会因素的作用方面，Saez先生比他的大多数同行走得更远。然而，正如2007-09年的全球金融危机促使人们重新思考金融业对金融市场和宏观经济政策的理解一样，这场大流行可能会把注意力集中在其他盲点上。明年的会议召开之时，可能比今年的社会事件要多得多，而且方式不只一种。■
