The year ahead in American politics looks daunting for Democrats

对民主党人来说，未来一年的美国政治看起来令人生畏

Biden’s burden

拜登的重负

![image-20220110104207181](/Users/chenyujie/Library/Application Support/typora-user-images/image-20220110104207181.png)

By the end of 2022, momentum will probably have shifted to Donald Trump’s Republicans

到2022年底，势头可能会转向特朗普的共和党人身上

Jan 8th 2022

文章音频

IN 2021 JOE BIDEN’S presidency began with early success, not long after a violent insurrection at the Capitol by diehard supporters of Donald Trump. In 2022, a reversal of fortunes is likely. Whatever verve the Biden administration still has may be killed off after the mid-term elections in November—a date that will also mark the unofficial start of the next presidential contest, which at this point seems a prelude to Mr Trump’s recoronation.

2021年，在特朗普的狂热支持者在国会大厦发动暴力叛乱后不久，拜登的总统任期开始时取得了初步成功。2022年，命运很可能逆转。不管拜登政府还有多少精力，它都可能在11月的中期选举后被“消灭”，这一天也将标志着下一届总统竞选的“非正式开始”，这一次似乎是特朗普重新当选的前奏。

Consider first the flagging fortunes of the son of Scranton. After success at passing a gargantuan stimulus package (in hindsight it was too big), Mr Biden’s presidency became stuck. The blows came one after the other: high inflation, fuelled partly by fiscal stimulus; an unrelenting pandemic; the chaotic withdrawal from Afghanistan; and the White House’s inability to whip Democratic majorities into voting for legislation. His approval rating has declined by 25 percentage points since the start of his term—leaving him nearly as unpopular as Mr Trump at this point of his presidency (see chart). Before Christmas, a pivotal senator dealt a deathblow to the Build Back Better (BBB) act, a package of climate-change mitigation and social benefits which was meant to be the signature legislation of his presidency.

先看看斯克兰顿之子日渐衰落的财富。在成功地通过了一项庞大的经济刺激计划之后(事后看来，该计划规模太大了)，拜登的总统任期陷入僵局。打击接踵而至: 高通胀(部分原因是由于财政刺激推动的);一个持续的疫情; 从阿富汗的混乱撤军; 以及白宫无力促使民主党多数派投票支持立法。自他的任期开始以来，他的支持率已经下降了25个百分点，这使得他在他的任期内几乎和特朗普一样不受欢迎(见图表)。圣诞节前，一位关键的参议员给了“重建更好未来”法案致命一击，该法案包含“缓解气候变化”以及“社会福利”，这本应是他总统任期内的标志性立法。

Heightening the malaise are the elections looming in November, in which Mr Biden will probably lose what ability he still has to legislate. Presidents almost always suffer losses in their first mid-term elections, and the razor-thin majorities Democrats retain in both chambers of Congress are unlikely to hold. Republicans need to take just five seats in the House of Representatives to win a majority and an explicit veto over all Mr Biden’s desired legislation. In 2010 Barack Obama lost 63 seats in his self-described “shellacking”. In 2018 Mr Trump lost 35.

11月即将到来的选举加剧了这种不安情绪，拜登可能会失去他仍然拥有的立法能力。总统在他们的第一次中期选举中几乎总是会遭遇失败，而民主党在参众两院中保持的微弱多数也不太可能继续保持。共和党只需在众议院获得5个席位，就能赢得多数席位，并明确否决拜登所期望的所有立法。2010年，奥巴马在他自称的“惨败”中丢掉了63个席位。2018年，特朗普丢掉了35个席位。

Were Mr Biden’s approval rating higher, he might have reason to hope to escape this eventuality. Unfortunately for him, it is not. Betting markets give Republicans an 82% chance of winning the House, and a 70% chance of retaking the Senate to boot.

如果拜登的支持率更高，他或许有理由希望避免这种可能性。不幸的是，对他来说，事实并非如此。赌博市场里，押共和党有82%的机会赢下众议院，此外有70%的机会夺回参议院。

The retirement announcements of longtime members of Congress, who prefer not to leave office by electoral rejection, are an omen of what probably lies ahead. So far 25 House Democrats have said they will not seek re-election, including some powerful committee chairmen, compared with 12 Republicans. Importantly, 17 of those Democrats are not leaving to seek another office or prominent position (“pure retirements”), as against four of the Republicans. In previous election years retirements have been leading indicators of losses for the party in power. Exogenous events, such as the Supreme Court’s curtailing of constitutional rights to abortion provoking a backlash in the suburbs, may help Democrats at the margin. But the end result is unlikely to remain full Democratic control of Washington.

长期担任国会议员的议员们不愿因选举排斥而离任，他们宣布退休的消息预示着未来可能会发生什么。到目前为止，有25名众议院民主党人表示，他们不会寻求连任，其中包括一些有权势的委员会主席，相比之下，只有12名共和党人表示不会。重要的是，这些民主党人中有17人不是为了寻求其他职位或显赫职位(“纯粹的退休”)而离开，而共和党人中则有4人。在之前的选举年，退休一直是执政党失败的主要标志物。一些外部事件，比如最高法院限制宪法赋予堕胎的权利，这在郊区引发了强烈反对，这可能会帮助在边缘的民主党。但最终结果不太可能让民主党继续完全控制华盛顿。

That leaves just a few months for Democrats to make use of their unified control of Congress and the White House. Much of the ostensibly highest-priority legislation pledged to its partisans—to bolster voting rights, boost unions, reduce police brutality and reform the immigration system—is dead on arrival, because the filibuster in the Senate allows the Republican minority to deny it passage. The filibuster itself, which progressives had once hoped would be dispensed with, is set to stay.

民主党人还剩几个月的时间来利用他们对国会和白宫的统一控制。许多表面上向其坚定支持者承诺的最高优先级的法案（包括扩大投票权、促进工会、减少警察暴行和改革移民制度）都在到达时就夭折了，因为参议院的阻挠行动允许共和党少数派否决它的通过。进步党曾经希望能够废除阻挠行动本身，但它还是被保留下来。

Rather than waste too much time on signalling bills without much chance of passage, Democrats are instead likely to devote months to resuscitating a pale version of BBB (which itself is theoretically passable only because a special budgetary procedure can circumvent the filibuster). Strenuous negotiations with Joe Manchin, the main Democratic holdout, are the only way forward for the president if he wishes to have something substantive to present to voters in November. They are likely to drag for months. The demands of the campaign season and the difficulty of actual passage may leave little time or inclination for anything else.

与其浪费太多时间在没有多少通过机会的议案上，民主党人更愿意花上几个月的时间来恢复“重建更好未来”法案的微弱版本(这个版本本身在理论上是可以通过的，因为一个特殊的预算程序可以绕过阻挠)。如果总统希望在11月向选民展示一些实质性的东西，与民主党主要的“顽敌” Joe Manchin进行艰苦的谈判，是总统前进的唯一途径。他们可能会拖上几个月。竞选季节的要求和实际通过的困难，可能没有多少时间或兴趣去做其他事情。

By year’s end, then, momentum will probably have shifted from Democrats to Republicans. Electoral victory will seem to validate the party’s failure to reckon with its leader’s serious attempts to subvert elections. In fact, it may be even more beholden to him.

到今年年底，势头可能会从民主党转向共和党。选举的胜利似乎证实了该党未能正视其领导人颠覆选举结果的严重企图。事实上，这可能更归功于他。

Few of the Republicans who voted to impeach Mr Trump for his actions after his electoral loss will remain in office. After purges in the party primaries, internal resistance to Mr Trump’s whims will be weaker. The former establishmentarians who made their peace with Trumpism will see rewards. Kevin McCarthy will probably ascend to the speakership of the House that he covets. All this will be achieved with no need for the Republican Party to come up with a policy agenda in response to the defeats in 2020.

对于那些在特朗普竞选失败后投票弹劾他的共和党人，几乎不会有人会继续留任。在党内初选的清洗之后，内部对特朗普奇想的抵制将会减弱。与特朗普主义和平相处的前建制派人士将看到回报。 Kevin McCarthy很可能会成为他梦寐以求的众议院议长。所有这一切都将实现，无需共和党提出政策议程来应对2020年的失败。

The ambitious policy agenda of the Democrats, meanwhile, will probably languish as the usual forces of gridlock and partisanship come to dominate a divided Congress. Like Mr Obama before him, Mr Biden will then have to rely on transient executive actions and orders to get just about anything done.

与此同时，民主党雄心勃勃的政策议程可能会随着政治僵局和党派之争的惯常力量在分裂的国会中占主导地位而黯然失色。就像之前的奥巴马一样，拜登将不得不依靠短暂的行政行动和命令来完成几乎所有的事情。

In both parties, jockeying for the next presidential election is already well under way, though the contest may be a repeat of 2020. Both Mr Biden and Mr Trump are hubristic enough to refuse to relinquish power to younger politicians. Yet both are also old enough for their health to preclude them from running. Kamala Harris, the vice-president, will still be the Democrats’ heir apparent, despite her dismal campaign in 2020 and rocky first year as the second-in-command. If Mr Trump were not to run, a populist crafted in his image, such as Florida’s governor, Ron DeSantis, is his likely successor. Even if the elderly men were to stand aside, America is likely to be treated to a duel of seconds. ■

在两党中，下一届总统选举的角逐已经展开，尽管这场角逐可能是2020年的重演。拜登和特朗普都足够傲慢，拒绝将权力拱手让给更年轻的政客。然而，两人的年龄也足以让他们的健康状况阻止他们“竞选”。副总统Kamala Harris仍将是民主党的法定继承人，尽管她在2020年的竞选中表现不佳，而且在担任总统二把手的第一年处境艰难。如果特朗普不参选，像佛罗里达州州长Ron DeSantis这样的以他形象塑造的民粹主义者可能是他的继任者。即使上了年纪的人退居一旁，美国也很可能面临一场二把手的决斗。■

Democrats seem drawn to hare-brained schemes to control inflation

民主党人似乎对控制通货膨胀的轻率计划很感兴趣

Beware snake oil

It is up to central banks to bring price growth down

降低价格增长取决于央行

Jan 8th 2022

文章音频

RAPIDLY RISING prices are as conspicuous as they are unpopular. Annual inflation of 6.8% has left American consumers even more anxious about the economy than they were during the pandemic-induced crisis in the spring of 2020. They are gloomy (see chart), even as many of them enjoy rising incomes, bargaining power over their employers—the result of a labour shortage—and soaring house prices. Their unease is a problem for President Joe Biden, whose economic management does not poll well. By one count, over two-thirds of voters disapprove of his record on inflation.

快速上涨的物价既不受欢迎，也引人注目。6.8%的年通胀率让美国消费者比2020年春季疫情导致的危机期间更担心经济。尽管他们中的许多人享受着收入的增加、与雇主讨价还价的能力(劳动力短缺的结果)以及飞涨的房价，但他们还是很悲观(见图表)。他们的不安对美国总统乔•拜登来说是个问题，拜登的经济管理在民调中表现不佳。据统计，超过三分之二的选民不赞成他在通货膨胀问题上的政绩。

The White House is therefore now keen to fight the rise in prices. But Democrats are slipping into a bad habit that often afflicts politicians at such a moment: blaming greedy corporations and rigged markets instead of their own flawed policies. It is a recipe for more mistakes.

因此，白宫现在热衷于遏制物价上涨。但是，民主党人正在染上恶习，这一恶习经常在这样的时刻困扰着政客们: 他们指责贪婪的公司和暗箱操纵的市场，而不是指责他们自己有缺陷的政策。这会导致更多的错误。

Over the past year corporate profit margins have surged as the economy has recovered. Elizabeth Warren, a senator, says that this shows how businesses have exploited the pandemic to “gouge” consumers; Mr Biden’s press secretary has castigated firms for “jacking up prices during a pandemic”. The administration thinks that antitrust enforcement can help control price growth. On January 3rd Mr Biden unveiled plans to increase competition among slaughterhouses, which he blames for a 16% rise in meat prices in the year to November. He has also called for investigations into the energy and shipping industries, the sources of recent shortages and bottlenecks.

在过去的一年里，随着经济的复苏，公司的利润率大幅上升。参议员Elizabeth Warren表示，这表明企业是如何利用疫情“欺骗”消费者的; 拜登的新闻秘书严厉指责企业“在疫情期间抬高价格”。政府认为反垄断执法有助于控制价格增长。1月3日，拜登公布了增加屠宰场竞争的计划，他指责屠宰场竞争导致截至11月的一年里肉类价格上涨了16%。他还呼吁对能源和航运业进行调查，这是近期短缺和瓶颈的根源。

Scrutinising concentrated markets may be a good idea, but it will not do much to fight inflation. It is absurd to think that corporations became more greedy over the past year or that markets are suddenly less competitive. Prices have accelerated in part because Mr Biden’s excessive economic stimulus led to a surge in spending on physical goods at a time when the pandemic had already bunged up global supply chains. History shows that companies’ profit margins are a poor guide to inflation. In the mid-2010s, when inflation languished beneath the Federal Reserve’s 2% target, they were almost as high as they are today. In the late 1960s, as inflation took off, they fell steeply.

审查集中市场可能是个好主意，但对“遏制通货膨胀”不会有太大帮助。认为企业在过去一年中变得更加贪婪，或者认为市场竞争突然减弱，这是荒谬的。价格上涨的部分原因是，拜登的过度经济刺激导致实物商品支出激增，而此时疫情已经破坏了全球供应链。历史表明，企业利润率并不能很好地反映通胀。在2010年代中期，当通货膨胀率低于美联储2%的目标时，那时的企业利润率几乎和现在一样高。20世纪60年代末，随着通货膨胀的加剧，企业利润率急剧下降。

The danger of misdiagnosing the cause of inflation is that it eventually leads not just to irrelevant policies but to damaging ones. One risk is price controls. The Roosevelt Institute, a left-wing think-tank, has called for the federal government to “regulate and negotiate” more prices. Isabella Weber, an economist at the University of Massachusetts Amherst, wants “a systematic consideration of strategic price controls”. Both point to high corporate profits to support their arguments.

“误诊”通胀原因的危险在于，它最终不仅会导致不相干的政策，还会导致破坏性的政策。其中一个风险是价格控制。左翼智库罗斯福研究所呼吁联邦政府“规范和协商”更多的价格。马萨诸塞大学阿默斯特分校的经济学家Isabella Weber希望“系统地考虑战略价格控制”。两者都以企业的高额利润来支持自己的观点。

As America learned when President Richard Nixon froze prices and wages in 1971, controls would distort the economy and harm growth while at best delaying inflation. Today’s advocates tend to call for surgical interventions rather than across-the-board freezes, but it is naive to think that the bureaucracy could make a success of micromanaging the price mechanism.

正如美国从“尼克松总统在1971年冻结物价和工资”时所吸取的教训那样，控制会扭曲经济，损害经济增长，同时充其量只能延缓通货膨胀。今天的支持者倾向于呼吁精确干预，而不是全面冻结，但认为官僚机构能够成功地对价格机制进行微观管理的想法是幼稚的。

It may seem far-fetched to think that politicians would resurrect policies that had been so thoroughly discredited. But consider that another plank of America’s hapless inflation-fighting in the 1970s, under President Gerald Ford, was “the vigorous enforcement of antitrust laws”. Then, as now, the government’s eagerness to pin the blame on businesses inspired bad ideas.

认为政客们会重新启用那些已经彻底失去信誉的政策，似乎有些牵强。但想想在1970年代Gerald Ford总统领导下美国抗击通胀的另一个不幸的手段，就是“严格执行反垄断法”。当时和现在一样，政府急于将责任推到企业身上，这就激发了糟糕的想法。

The uncomfortable truth for Mr Biden is that the Federal Reserve, not the White House, controls the tool to bring down inflation: higher interest rates. The government could help cool the economy by cutting spending or raising taxes. But belt-tightening, unlike bashing corporations, is unpopular. Politicians’ unwillingness to do what is necessary to fight inflation is why central banks are independent—and why it is good news that the Fed looks as if it will raise rates as soon as the spring. ■

令拜登不安的事实是，控制降低通胀工具“更高的利率”的是美联储，而不是白宫。政府可以通过削减开支或提高税收来帮助经济降温。但与抨击企业不同，紧缩开支并不受欢迎。政治家们不愿采取必要措施对抗通货膨胀，这就是为什么央行保持独立的原因，也是为什么“美联储似乎会在春季就提高利率”是一个好消息。■

How to talk to Mr Putin

如何与普京对话

Russia and NATO

俄罗斯和北约

![image-20220108082608760](/Users/chenyujie/Library/Application Support/typora-user-images/image-20220108082608760.png)

Russia’s aggression towards Ukraine has created a chance to enhance the security of Europe

俄罗斯对乌克兰的侵略为加强欧洲安全创造了一个机会

Jan 8th 2022

文章音频

IT IS USUALLY a bad sign if talks start with one side brandishing a gun. And so it may prove when Russian diplomats meet their North American and European peers next week, backed by 100,000 troops poised to invade Ukraine. At stake is the future of a country that increasingly sees itself as part of the West, as well as America’s role as the anchor of European security. As the crisis comes to a head, the risk of miscalculation is growing.

如果谈判是以一方挥舞着枪开始的，这通常是一个不好的信号。下周，俄罗斯外交官在10万大军的支持下会见了北美和欧洲的同僚，准备入侵乌克兰。这个“日益认为自己国家是西方一部分、同时也认为美国是欧洲安全的支柱的角色的”国家的未来岌岌可危。随着危机进入紧要关头，误判的风险也在增加。

Russia’s president, Vladimir Putin, has already issued his demands for the talks, which will kick off between Russia and America in Geneva on January 10th, move to Brussels for the NATO-Russia Council two days later and wrap up at the Organisation for Security and Co-operation in Europe on January 13th. Mr Putin wants NATO to forswear all further expansion—everywhere, and not just in Ukraine and Georgia, two former Soviet states. America must no longer protect its allies with tactical nuclear weapons and short- and medium-range missiles. And Russia wants, in effect, a veto over troop deployments and exercises in the eastern parts of NATO territory and over military co-operation with all former Soviet countries.

俄罗斯总统普京已经提出了会谈的要求，俄罗斯-美国会谈将于1月10日在日内瓦开始，两天后将前往布鲁塞尔参加北约-俄罗斯理事会，并于1月13日在欧洲安全与合作组织（欧安组织）结束。普京希望北约放弃所有进一步的扩张，不仅仅是在乌克兰和格鲁吉亚这两个前苏联国家。美国必须不再用战术核武器和中短程导弹来保护盟友。实际上，俄罗斯想要在北约东部地区的军队部署和演习以及与所有前苏联国家的军事合作上拥有否决权。

Many of these demands are so extravagant and so detrimental to Europe’s security that they may really be an ultimatum drafted to be rejected, creating a pretext for another invasion of Ukraine. If Mr Putin is indeed bent on going to war, he will. However, robust diplomacy could yet give him pause and help stop the long decay of relations between Russia and the West. Even if talks fail, NATO could emerge stronger, more united and clearer about the threat it faces.

这些要求中有许多是非常过分的，对欧洲的安全是非常有害的，以至于它们可能真的是起草来被拒绝的最后通牒，为再次入侵乌克兰制造了借口。如果普京真的决心发动战争，他就会(发动)。然而，强劲的外交可能会让他停下来，帮助阻止俄罗斯与西方关系的长期衰退。即使谈判失败，北约也会变得更强大，更团结，更清楚地认识到它所面临的威胁。

Be in no doubt that Mr Putin has brought on this crisis. Perhaps he wants Ukraine to fail because, were it to become a thriving democracy, it would stand as a rebuke to his claim that Western values are unworkable in Orthodox, Slavic Russia. He may also intend to divide and weaken NATO as well as to create an enemy abroad so as to justify repression at home—as with Memorial, a civil-rights group, shut down just before the new year on the trumped-up charge of being a “foreign agent”. And Mr Putin has come to regret the security arrangements that Russia freely signed up to after the cold war. Today, he believes, Russia is stronger and America is declining and distracted by the challenge from XX. For whatever reason, he seems in a hurry to establish his legacy by renewing Russia’s sphere of influence.

毫无疑问，这场危机是普京一手造成的。也许他希望乌克兰失败是因为，如果乌克兰成为一个繁荣的民主国家，这将是对他所声称的西方价值观在正统、斯拉夫的俄罗斯行不通的一种驳斥。他可能还打算分裂和削弱北约，同时在国外建立一个敌人，以证明国内的镇压是正当的，就像民权组织Memorial一样，该组织在新年前被捏造为“外国代理人”的指控而关闭。普京也开始对俄罗斯在冷战后自主签署的安全协议感到后悔。他认为，如今俄罗斯更强大了，而美国正在衰落并被来自XX的挑战分散了注意力。不管出于什么原因，他似乎急于通过重塑俄罗斯的势力范围来确立自己的政治“遗产”。

Some things are in his favour. He has the aggressor’s advantage, controlling the timetable and the scope of an attack, should there be one. Everyone knows that Ukraine matters more to him than to any NATO country, which means that the West will not send troops to defend it.

有些事情对他有利。他有侵略者的优势，如果有的话，他可以控制进攻的时间和范围。每个人都知道乌克兰对他来说比对任何一个北约国家都重要，这意味着西方不会派遣军队保卫乌克兰。

But not everything is going his way. Ukraine is as populous as Iraq. Although Russia’s forces should be able to defeat it in battle, holding swathes of territory there could come at a high price, especially if the Ukrainians mounted an insurgency. And in Kazakhstan, on Russia’s southern border, Mr Putin is being asked to help crush a popular uprising—a distraction and awkward proof of his willingness to oppress. For these reasons, he may have to curb his ambitions and, say, seize enclaves around Donetsk that are already controlled by Russian-backed irregulars, or establish a land bridge to Crimea, which he annexed in 2014. His doubts about how far to go can be exploited.

但并非所有事情都如他所愿。乌克兰的人口与伊拉克一样多。尽管俄罗斯军队应该能够在战斗中击败乌克兰，但要想在那里占领大片领土，代价可能会很高，尤其是如果乌克兰人发起叛乱的话。在俄罗斯南部边境的哈萨克斯坦，普京被要求帮助镇压一场民众起义，这是一件分心之事以及他的镇压意愿的一个尴尬的证明。由于这些原因，他可能不得不抑制自己的野心，比如，夺取顿涅茨克周围已经被俄罗斯支持的非正规部队控制的飞地，或者建立通往克里米亚的大陆桥，克里米亚是他在2014年“吞并”的。他的疑虑是，可以在多大程度上加以利用。

Mr Putin’s other problem is that his aggression has united NATO and given it new purpose. His ultimatum, combined with his willingness to see prices of Russian gas surge in Europe in 2021, has cut the ground from under those arguing for closer ties to the Kremlin. America has helped galvanise Europeans, by sharing detailed intelligence about huge numbers of troops massing near the Ukrainian border.

普京的另一个问题是，他的侵略行为使北约团结起来，并赋予了它新的目标。他的最后通牒，加上他希望看到俄罗斯天然气价格在2021年在欧洲飙升的意愿，让那些主张与俄罗斯政府建立更紧密关系的人失去了理由。美国通过分享大量集结在乌克兰边境的军队的详细情报，激励了欧洲人。

The West should have two objectives in next week’s talks: to deter war in Ukraine if possible, and to enhance European security. Stopping a Russian invasion involves the threat of severe economic sanctions, as well as aid and defensive weapons to help make Ukraine indigestible. At the same time, the West can seek to reassure Mr Putin by clearly stating that, although Russia has no formal veto over who joins NATO, neither Ukraine nor Georgia is about to become a member.

在下周的会谈中，西方应该有两个目标: 一是尽可能阻止乌克兰的战争，二是加强欧洲安全。阻止俄罗斯入侵的威胁包括严厉的经济制裁、援助以及防御性武器，以让乌克兰看不懂。与此同时，西方可以通过明确表示，乌克兰和格鲁吉亚都不会成为北约的一员，以此来安抚普京，尽管俄罗斯对谁加入北约没有正式的否决权。

Done right, the second aim, of enhancing Europe’s security, can also lower tensions over Ukraine. Although some Russian demands would leave Europe vulnerable, others could be the basis for talks that benefit both sides. Consider a regional agreement about the deployment of missile forces or confidence-building measures to make exercises less threatening. There is no shortage of things to discuss, from the Arctic to cyber-security, to new missile technologies. Negotiations would be drawn-out, such is the mistrust between the two sides, but that might be no bad thing, because they could turn into a useful forum.

第二个目标是加强欧洲安全，如果处理得当，也可以缓解乌克兰的紧张局势。尽管俄罗斯的一些要求会让欧洲脆弱不堪，但其他一些要求可能会成为对双方都有利的谈判的基础。考虑一项关于导弹部队的部署或建立信任措施的区域协议，以减少演习的威胁。从北极到网络安全，再到新的导弹技术，讨论的话题并不少。谈判将会旷日持久，这就是双方之间的不信任，但这可能不是坏事，因为它们可能会变成一个有用的讨论会。

The question is not whether such talks are possible—they are clearly in Russia’s interest—but whether Mr Putin really wants them. He has often behaved as if Russia’s security depends on making the West feel less safe. However, negotiations would enhance his status as a world leader. By circumscribing the domains of military competition, they might also help him cope with the fact that Russia cannot begin to match the combined resources of the West.

问题不在于这样的会谈是否可行（这显然符合俄罗斯的利益）而在于普京是否真的想要这样的会谈。他经常表现得好像俄罗斯的安全依赖于让西方感到不安全。然而，谈判将提高他作为世界领袖的地位。通过划定军事竞争的范围，他们或许还能帮助普京应对俄罗斯无法与西方国家的综合资源相匹敌的事实。

The profound doubts about Mr Putin’s real intentions mean that, even if talks begin, NATO needs to demonstrate that it is prepared to defend its members. The most vulnerable are the Baltic states. After Russia seized Crimea, NATO’s western powers started to rotate more troops in the east. Because of Russia’s threats, credible preparations for enhancing those rotations ought to begin immediately. Even if Ukraine is not about to join NATO, Russia is driving Sweden and Finland towards membership. The alliance should be ready to welcome them. Throughout, America should ensure that deals are never struck above European countries’ heads: that is Russia’s style.

对普京真正意图的深切怀疑意味着，即使谈判开始，北约也需要证明它准备保护其成员国。最易受攻击的是波罗的海国家。在俄罗斯占领克里米亚之后，北约的西方大国开始在东部轮换更多的军队。由于俄罗斯的威胁，加强这些轮换的可靠准备工作应该立即开始。即使乌克兰不打算加入北约，俄罗斯也在推动瑞典和芬兰成为其成员国。联盟应该做好迎接他们的准备。在整个过程中，美国应该确保协议不会在欧洲国家头顶之上达成: 这是俄罗斯的风格。

Mr Putin says that his country is threatened. It is not. NATO is a defensive alliance. Even after Crimea, it has held back from putting permanent combat forces into eastern Europe. The real threat is Mr Putin. When he issues his demands down the barrel of a gun, it should stiffen the resolve both of the West and of the resilient Ukrainians to deter and resist him. ■

普京说他的国家受到了威胁。并不是。北约是一个防御联盟。即使在克里米亚事件之后，它也没有向东欧派遣永久性作战部队。真正的威胁来自普京。当他在枪口下提出他的要求时，西方和富有韧性的乌克兰人都应该坚定“阻止和反抗他”的决心。■

The new covid variant

新的冠状病毒变体

![image-20220107084443649](/Users/chenyujie/Library/Application Support/typora-user-images/image-20220107084443649.png)

But it is spreading fast, and options for treating it are more limited

但是它传播得很快，治疗方法也很有限

Jan 1st 2022

文章音频

WITH ITS ability to escape immunity induced by past infections and vaccines, the Omicron variant of SARS-CoV-2, first detected in South Africa on November 9th, has been tearing around the world, causing record numbers of cases of covid-19 as it does so. Australia, Britain, Denmark, France, Italy and South Africa are at the forefront of this rising wave of infections. But Omicron has reached at least 113 other countries, too.

SARS-CoV-2的奥密克戎变体于11月9日首次在南非发现，由于其能够避开过去感染和疫苗引起的免疫，它已经在世界各地肆虐，这就导致了covid-19病例数量创纪录。澳大利亚、英国、丹麦、法国、意大利和南非处于“这波不断上升的感染浪潮”的最前列。但奥密克戎也已经蔓延到至少113个其他国家。

Yet despite its ominous-looking clutch of mutations, particularly in the “spike” protein it uses to attach itself to cells when infecting them, the past week has given grounds for hope that the symptoms Omicron causes are less severe than those induced by its predecessors, and that people who do get infected are thus less likely to end up in hospital, or dead. This good news is tempered by the fact that it is far more contagious than those predecessors. It will thus spread widely in coming weeks. And a higher infection rate, even of a less serious illness, could still overwhelm hospitals and cause many deaths.

然而，尽管奥密克戎有一系列的突变，特别是在它感染细胞时用来附着在细胞上的“刺突”蛋白上，过去的一周让人们有理由相信，奥密克戎引起的症状比它的“前任们”(指Delta病毒等)引起的症状要轻，因此，那些被感染的人最终住院或死亡的可能性更小。但这一好消息却被一个事实冲淡了，那就是它的传染性比之前它的“前任们”更强。因此，它将在未来几周内广泛传播。而更高的感染率，即使是不太严重的疾病，仍可能使医院不堪重负，并导致许多人死亡。

Hope springs eternal

Omicron’s lower severity is probably a result of changes in the virus itself, combined with high levels of immunity (from vaccination or prior infection) that have built up in human populations. A study released on December 21st by the National Institute for Communicable Diseases, in Johannesburg, for example, found, after adjustment for age, illnesses and other factors (including vaccination status and prior infection) which determine the chances of developing severe disease, that Omicron cases are 80% less likely than previous variants to require admission to hospital.

奥密克戎的“严重程度较低”可能是由于病毒本身的变化，再加上在人群中建立的高水平免疫力(这种免疫力是来自疫苗接种或以前的感染)。例如，12月21日由位于约翰内斯堡的国家传染病研究所发布的一项研究发现，在适应了年龄、疾病和其他因素(包括疫苗接种状况和既往感染)之后，奥密克戎的病例比先前的变异病例需要住院治疗的可能性降低了80%。

On December 22nd, work published in Britain pointed in a similar direction. Researchers from Imperial College, in London, showed that people in England who had been infected with Omicron were 40-45% less likely than those with its immediate predecessor, Delta, to be admitted to hospital for a day or more. They also had shorter stays.

12月22日，在英国发表的一篇论文也指向了类似的方向。伦敦帝国理工学院的研究人员表明，在英国，感染了奥密克戎病毒的人比直接感染它的“前任”Delta病毒的人住院一天或更长时间的可能性要低40-45%。他们的“停留”时间也较短。

When the researchers dug more deeply they also found that, broadly speaking, people who had had at least two doses of any of the three vaccines (AstraZeneca, Moderna and Pfizer) used in Britain were “substantially protected” against hospital admission, even if protection against actual infection by Omicron had been lost. The next day, Britain’s Health Security Agency concluded that those in the country catching Omicron are 55-69% less likely than people infected by Delta to need hospital care, and 31-45% less likely to go to accident and emergency units.

当研究者更深入地挖掘后，他们还发现, 一般来说, 对于在英国使用的三种疫苗中(阿斯利康、Moderna、辉瑞)的任意一种，接种了至少两针疫苗的人在住院会得到实质性的保护，即使对于由奥密克戎引起的实际感染的保护已经丧失了。在其后的一天，英国卫生安全局得出结论，该国感染奥密克戎病毒的人比感染Delta病毒的人需要住院治疗的可能性低55-69%，去急诊室的可能性低31-45%。

The findings about vaccination put a new complexion on previous concerns that antibodies raised in people jabbed against the original XX strain, or who have been previously infected, will not effectively neutralise Omicron. Clive Dix, a former chairman of Britain’s Vaccine Taskforce, said, “we have seen a progressive loss of antibody neutralisation as we moved through Alpha, Beta, Gamma and Delta…but to date the vaccines all protect against severe disease and death.” Booster doses of vaccine, given in many countries, will add to protection against both infection and disease. And the Imperial team says that, as more data accumulate, it is possible the risk of the most serious outcomes of Omicron might turn out to be even lower than currently suspected—adding that remaining immune protection against more severe outcomes of infection is expected to be much higher than those against milder disease.

关于疫苗接种的研究结果让人们对之前的担忧有了新的认识，即接种过预防原始的XX病毒株或曾经感染过这种病毒的人体内产生的抗体，不会有效地中和奥密克戎。英国疫苗工作组的前主席Clive Dix说:“随着我们向Alpha, Beta, Gamma和Delta等的转移，我们已经看到了抗体中和作用在逐渐丧失，但到目前为止，疫苗都能预防严重疾病和死亡。” 在许多国家，疫苗的加强针将增加对感染和疾病的保护。帝国理工学院的团队表示, 随着越来越多的数据积累, 奥密克戎最严重后果的风险可能会比目前怀疑的更低。此外，预计针对更严重感染后果的剩余免疫保护将远高于针对较温和疾病的免疫保护。

This good news is tempered by caveats. It is based on early data, and therefore only small numbers of hospital admissions. As of December 20th, for example, 132 British patients with Omicron had been recorded as passing through hospital emergency departments. Of these 14 had died with covid within 28 days of diagnosis, though the precise cause of death is not always clear. The victims ranged in age from 52 to 96. Yet in Britain Omicron infections are currently concentrated in 20- to 29-year-olds. If the virus spreads to older cohorts its impact may thus worsen.

这一好消息又被一些警告冲淡了。这是基于早期数据，因此只有很少的住院人数。例如，截至12月20日，已有132名患有奥密克戎的英国患者被登记为通过医院急诊科就诊。其中14人在确诊后28天内死于covid，但确切的死亡原因并不总是很清楚。这些患者的年龄从52岁到96岁不等。然而在英国，奥密克戎感染目前主要集中在20到29岁的人群中。如果病毒传播到老年人群，其影响可能会进一步恶化。

Moreover, once someone is so sick as to require hospital treatment, doctors may have fewer options available for Omicron than for its antecedents. Previous strains could often be treated with drugs called monoclonal antibodies. Omicron seems oblivious to most of these, and supplies of those that do affect it, newly developed versions made by GlaxoSmithKline and Vir Biotechnology, are limited.

此外，一旦有人病情严重到需要住院治疗，医生对奥密克戎的选择可能比其“前任们”要少。以前的菌株通常可以用单克隆抗体治疗。奥密克戎能“抵抗”其中的大部分抗体，而且葛兰素史克公司和Vir Biotechnology新研发的药物供应也很有限。

The infectiousness of Omicron and the consequent speed of its spread mean that hospitals around the world are wary of an onslaught of admissions happening at a time when many staff are unavailable because they, too, have been infected. All this could have an effect on care. Scientists advising the British government have warned that covid-related admissions to hospital this winter may match or exceed previous peaks. But there is also hope that these fears may not come true.

奥密克戎病毒的传染性和随后的传播速度意味着，世界各地的医院都很警惕会出现大量住院人数，这会导致许多工作人员感染而无法工作。所有这些都可能对护理产生影响。为英国政府提供咨询的科学家警告称，今年冬天与新冠病毒相关的入院人数可能达到或超过之前的峰值。但也有人希望，这些担忧不会成真。

![image-20220107084454927](/Users/chenyujie/Library/Application Support/typora-user-images/image-20220107084454927.png)

In Denmark, for instance, although hospital-admission rates have risen, there are signs this increase is at the low end of the range of projections. And Chris Hopson, boss of NHS Providers, which represents organisations in England’s National Health Service, tweeted on December 27th that though the number of patients with covid was rising, it was not doing so “precipitately”. Across the country it has gone up by around 30% in a week. But many of these are people who were admitted for other reasons and are actually asymptomatic for covid, their infection having been spotted instead by a routine test. For this reason, Mr Hopson urges caution in over-interpreting admissions data.

例如，在丹麦，虽然住院率有所上升，但有迹象表明，这种上升是在预测范围的低边界值。12月27日，代表英国NHS组织的NHS提供商的老板Chris Hopson在推特上表示，尽管新冠肺炎患者的数量在上升，但这并不是“突然”上升的。在全国范围内，它在一周内上涨了大约30%。但其中许多人是因其他原因入院的，实际上是无症状感染者，他们的感染是通过常规检测发现的。出于这个原因，Hopson敦促人们不要过度解读住院数据。

Waving Omicron goodbye?

The Omicron wave could also recede quickly. In South Africa there is a feeling that the country may have passed its peak of cases (see chart). At a pre-Christmas meeting Sir Patrick Vallance, Britain’s chief scientific adviser, said there was “an apparent slowing of growth rates” of covid. Britain, and also Denmark, are both well vaccinated countries and will be watched closely in the days and weeks to come.

奥密克戎浪潮也可能很快退去。在南非，人们有一种感觉，该国可能已经度过了病例的高峰期(见图表)。在圣诞节前的一次会议上，英国首席科学顾问Patrick Vallance爵士表示，新冠肺炎的“增长率明显放缓”。英国和丹麦这两个国家疫苗接种情况都良好，它们将在未来几天和几周内受到密切关注。

Omicron may do more damage in places that are poorly vaccinated—especially those where “zero-covid” strategies intended to stop infections happening at all mean there is little natural immunity around either. But ultimately, there may be a silver lining. By infecting so many people and thus potentially providing widespread immunity to the next variant of concern, Omicron may accelerate covid’s transition from being a dangerous epidemic to something that is an endemic nuisance which people can learn to put up with. ■

奥密克戎可能会在疫苗接种情况不足的地方造成更大的损害，尤其是那些旨在完全阻止感染发生实施“零新冠”策略的地方，这意味着周围几乎没有自然免疫的地方。但最终，还是有一线希望的。通过感染如此多的人，从而可能为下一种担忧的变种提供广泛的免疫力，奥密克戎可能会加速covid的转变，从一种危险的流行病，转变为一种人们可以学会忍受的麻烦的地方病。■

DEAR TOP TABLE, We have discussed many of the risks that threaten us in the coming year: the pandemic, our supply-chain troubles and staff retention. But I want to raise a more personal concern: the possibility that I will have to make a public apology. Everywhere I looked over the past 12 months, executives were grovelling. The thought of promising to work on becoming a better person makes me feel physically sick.

亲爱的高层们，我们已经讨论了未来一年会威胁到我们的许多风险: 疫情、供应链问题和员工留存问题。但我想提出一个更私人的担忧:我可能不得不公开道歉。过去12个月，我所到之处，高管们都“低声下气”。一想到要努力成为一个更好的人，我就感到身体不适。

Let me be clear. I’m not against apologies when they are warranted. Bad behaviour needs to be brought to light and investigated, however damaging the fallout. But there are reasons why fire storms have become more common. Technology records our every action. Employees have become activists. It’s harder to avoid controversy in XX.

我表达的再清楚些。我不反对有正当理由的道歉。无论后果如何，不好的行为都需要被揭露和调查。但”风暴性大火”变得越来越常见是有原因的。科技记录了我们的一举一动。员工们已经变成了积极分子。在XX这很难避免争议。

Start with technology. Almost everything we do now as leaders leaves a digital trace that can come back to haunt us. Vishal Garg’s recent decision to fire 900 members of staff at Better.com over Zoom was a terrible call, and not just for them. Private messages are liable to become public. Chris Kempczinski, the boss of McDonald’s, apologised in November after a freedom-of-information request revealed the contents of thoughtless text messages he had sent to the mayor of Chicago about two shootings in the city. (Let’s not even mention his predecessor’s personal correspondence.)

从技术开始说起。作为领导者，我们现在所做的几乎每一件事都会留下“数字痕迹”，这些“痕迹”可能会反过来困扰我们。Vishal Garg最近决定因为Zoom解雇Better.com的900名员工，这是一个可怕的决定，而且不只是对他们来说。私人信息有可能被公开。去年11月，麦当劳老板Chris Kempczinski在一项信息自由要求中，披露了他发给芝加哥市长的关于该市两起枪击事件的欠考虑的短信内容，随后他为此道歉。(更别提他前任的私人信件了。)

Outrage is everywhere. The boss of Sweetgreen, a salad chain, suffered a backlash earlier in the year when he wrote that hospitalisations caused by covid-19 raised questions about levels of obesity in America. He ended up apologising for his insensitivity—or, as some people like to call it, use of data—and described the episode as an opportunity to “learn forward”. Ugh.

愤怒无处不在。沙拉连锁店Sweetgreen的老板因在covid-19引起的留院治疗问题上对美国的肥胖水平提出质疑而遭到了强烈反对。最后，他为自己的不敏感，或者用一些人喜欢的说法，即“利用数据”而道歉，并将这一事件描述为一个“将来学习”的机会。呸。

Staff are behaving differently. According to a survey of 7,000 employees conducted by Edelman, a public-relations firm, workers now apparently think that they matter more than customers to the long-term success of their organisations. As if that were not bad enough, six in ten employees say they choose where they work based on their beliefs. The line between company and crusade has blurred.

员工的行为有所不同。根据公关公司Edelman对7000名员工的调查，员工们现在明显认为他们机构的长期成功取决于他们而非顾客。更糟糕的是，60%的员工表示，他们根据自己的信仰来选择工作地点。公司和十字军之间的界限已经模糊。

If workers see something they do not like, they are more likely to let the world know about it. Just think about the past year. A group of Netflix employees staged a very public walkout in the autumn over a Dave Chapelle special that they regarded as transphobic. (This was handled pretty well, by the way: Ted Sarandos, the firm’s co- CEO, apologised for failing to “lead with humanity” but did not back down on artistic freedom.)

如果员工看到他们不喜欢的东西，他们更有可能让世界知道它。想想过去的一年。今年秋天，一群Netflix公司的员工举行了一场非常公开的罢工，原因是他们认为Dave Chapelle的一部特别节目(单口喜剧表演)有对跨性别者群体有冒犯。(顺便说一句，这件事处理得很好: 公司的联合首席执行官Ted Sarandos为未能“以人道的方式领导”而道歉，但他没有在艺术自由问题上让步。)

Tim Cook lamented the fact that Apple, once known for secrecy, has become more loose-lipped in a memo that was promptly leaked. Bankers at Goldman Sachs, a group of people designed to test the limits of human empathy, circulated a PowerPoint deck complaining about their workloads. A whistle-blowing product manager did huge reputational damage to Meta, Facebook’s parent company.

Tim Cook对于“一份被迅速泄露的简报”这一事实哀叹道，一度以保密著称的苹果公司变得更加口风不紧。高盛的银行家们是一群旨在测试人类同理心极限的人，他们散布了一份PPT，上面抱怨他们的工作量太大。产品经理的揭发行为给Meta(Facebook的母公司)带来了巨大的名誉损失。

Like many companies, we are looking at how we can tighten the flow of information internally: employees may have to ask for permission to start new Slack channels, for instance. But there is a limit to how far we can go. In April Basecamp, a software company, banned discussion of societal and political issues on its corporate platforms. “We are not a social-impact company,” wrote one of the founders. “Our impact is contained to what we do and how we do it.” A third of the firm’s employees ended up quitting, prompting yet another apology.

与许多公司一样，我们也在研究如何加强内部信息的流动: 例如，员工要开通新的Slack渠道，可能必须申请许可。但我们能走多远是有限度的。今年4月，软件公司Basecamp禁止在其公司平台上讨论社会和政治问题。其中一位创始人写道: “我们不是一家具有社会影响力的公司，我们的影响仅限于我们所做的事情以及我们如何做成。” 该公司三分之一的员工最终辞职，这就又引发了一次道歉。

XX is a problem area, especially for American multinationals trying to navigate choppy geopolitical waters. In late December Intel sparked social-media uproar in XX for sending a letter to suppliers telling them not to use components from XX in its semiconductors. The firm apologised, and made it clear that it was trying to remain in compliance with US laws rather than acting off its own bat.

XX是一个问题地区，尤其是对于试图在动荡的地缘政治水域中航行的美国跨国公司来说。去年12月底，英特尔致信供应商，要求他们在半导体生产中不要使用来自XX的零部件，这在XX的社交媒体上引发了轩然大波。该公司为此道歉，并明确表示，它正努力遵守美国法律，而不是自作主张。

In November Jamie Dimon expressed regret for joking that JPMorgan Chase would last longer than the XXX. One of the bank boss’s two apologies for this unforced error included the line: “It’s never right to joke about or denigrate any group of people, whether it’s a country, its leadership, or any part of a society and culture.” No Netflix comedy special for him.

去年11月，Jamie Dimon曾开玩笑说，摩根大通的寿命将比XXX还长，他对此言辞表示懊悔。这位银行老板两次为这个非故意的错误道歉，其中一次是这样的:“永远不要取笑或诋毁任何群体，无论是一个国家、它的领导层，还是一个社会和文化的任何一部分。” Netflix可没有为他开一个喜剧特别节目。

So to keep the new year as apology-free as possible, remember the following. Nothing we say or do is private. Embrace blandness. Don’t criticise XX but do act as if you live there. And for God’s sake, don’t leak this memo.

因此，为了在新的一年里尽可能不道歉，请记住以下几点。我们说的做的都不再是隐私。拥抱“温和”。不要批评XX，但要表现得好像你生活在XX一样。看在上帝的份上，别泄露这份简报。

Brexit’s many contradictions are coming to a head

英国脱欧的许多矛盾正达到顶点

Time to choose

是时候作出选择了

![image-20220105102024280](/Users/chenyujie/Library/Application Support/typora-user-images/image-20220105102024280.png)

It is time for Boris Johnson to abandon “cakeism”

鲍里斯·约翰逊是时候放弃蛋糕主义了

Jan 1st 2022

文章音频

A YEAR AGO, as Boris Johnson prepared to take Britain out of the European Union’s trading arrangements after an 11-month transition, he was on top of the world. The trade and co-operation agreement he had signed with the EU was, he crowed, “cakeist”—his term for deals that combine the having and eating of cake, in this case decent access to the single market and freedom to diverge from its rules. But as 2021 ended, little cake was to be had. A backbench rebellion, a stunning by-election loss and public anger at revelations that the prime minister and his colleagues partied during lockdowns in 2020 have hit his poll ratings. The year’s final blow came on December 18th, when David Frost, his chief EU negotiator, resigned, citing his frustration at the government’s failure to make progress on an ambitious post-Brexit agenda.

一年前，当鲍里斯•约翰逊准备让英国在11个月的过渡期后退出欧盟贸易安排时，他非常自豪。他得意地说，他与欧盟签署的贸易与合作协议是“cakeist”，这一术语是他用来指“将蛋糕的拥有与享用结合起来”的协议，在这种情况下，是指既能正当地进入单一市场，又有脱离欧盟规则的自由。但随着2021年的结束，蛋糕几乎没有了。后座议员的反抗、令人震惊的补缺选举的惨败以及公众对”首相及其同事在2020年封锁期间聚会“的揭露的愤怒，都打击了他的民意支持率。去年的最后一次重击发生在12月18日，他的首席欧盟谈判代表David Frost辞职，原因是他对政府未能在雄心勃勃的脱欧后议程上取得进展感到失望。

Some of Mr Johnson’s difficulties are tied to his personal flaws: arrogance, sloppiness and a belief that rules are for little people. But Lord Frost, for all that his bellicose approach to the EU made progress harder, was right about his central charge. On Brexit, which the Conservatives promised would kick-start a national renewal, the government is adrift. Growth has been sluggish and ejection from the single market has, as expected, harmed the country’s prospects: government forecasters put the long- run hit to productivity at around 4%. So far the government has failed to identify policies that could come close to making up for that. To regain momentum it must make tough choices and face up to their consequences.

约翰逊的一些麻烦与他的个人缺点有关: 傲慢、马虎和“认为规则是为平民制定的”信念。但是Frost勋爵在核心观点上是正确的，尽管他对欧盟的好战态度使得进展更加艰难。在英国脱欧问题上，保守党曾承诺将推动一场全国性的复兴，政府的态度则摇摆不定。经济增长缓慢，被逐出单一市场，正如预期的那样，损害了该国的前景: 政府预测，长期来看，生产率将受到4%左右的打击。到目前为止，政府还没有找到能够弥补这一点的政策。为了重新获得动力，它必须做出艰难的选择，并正视其后果。

The problem is less the type of Brexit that Britain opted for than a refusal to accept what flows from it. That is clearest in Northern Ireland. Britain chose to maximise sovereignty and national control, at the cost of leaving the EU’s trading system. The EU, mindful of Northern Ireland’s fragile peace, offered a special deal whereby the province remained, in effect, in the single market and customs union so as to avoid a destabilising hard border with the Republic of Ireland. That meant customs checks in the Irish Sea—an outcome spelt out in the treaty that Britain chose to sign, but which Lord Frost subsequently declared unacceptable and tried, fruitlessly, to unpick.

问题不在于英国选择了哪种脱欧，而在于拒绝接受脱欧带来的后果。这一点在北爱尔兰最为明显。英国选择了将主权和国家控制力最大化，以退出欧盟贸易体系为代价。考虑到北爱尔兰脆弱的和平，欧盟提出了一项特殊的协议，即该领域实际上仍然留在单一市场和关税联盟中，以避免与爱尔兰共和国之间不稳定的硬边界。这意味着在爱尔兰海进行海关检查，这是英国选择签署的条约中规定的结果，但Frost勋爵随后宣布这是不可接受的，并试图取消，但无果。

A similar failure to make a choice and stick to it is evident in Britain’s vacillations between the two options of either escaping the EU’s regulatory pull or staying in tight orbit around the single market and its rules. The reason is partly electoral. Brexit’s most ardent salesmen dreamed of lighter taxes and looser regulations—of Britain becoming Singapore-on-Thames. But many voters rather like the EU’s social-democratic model. Indeed, since leaving the EU, Britain has in some ways become more European. It has raised the minimum wage sharply and increased taxes to pay for social care. If Mr Johnson’s promise to “level up” Britain means anything, it is European-style industrial policy and grand public works.

英国在“要么逃避欧盟的监管”，“要么紧紧围绕单一市场及其规则” 这两个选择之间摇摆不定，这就明显体现了英国在做出选择并坚持下去方面也有类似失败。部分原因是选举。脱欧最热心的“推销员”梦想着减税和放松监管，让英国成为泰晤士河上的新加坡。但许多选民更喜欢欧盟的社会民主模式。事实上，自离开欧盟以来，英国在某些方面变得更加欧洲化。政府大幅提高了最低工资标准，并增加了用于社会福利的税收。如果约翰逊先生“拉齐”英国的承诺有什么意义的话，那就是欧洲式的工业政策和宏大的公共工程。

A dose of realism is needed. On Northern Ireland, that means accepting that for the province to be both inside and outside the EU’s trading arrangements is impossible, and moving on. Britain and the EU can then work to minimise the downsides, such as onerous customs checks—and to maximise the upsides by helping businesses in one of the United Kingdom’s poorest regions profit from their privileged access to the EU’s giant market.

这就需要一点现实主义。在北爱尔兰问题上，这意味着要接受“该领域不可能同时处于欧盟贸易安排的内部和外部”的事实并继续前进。然后，英国和欧盟可以努力将不利因素最小化，比如繁重的海关检查，并通过帮助英国最贫穷地区之一的企业从进入欧盟巨大市场的特权中获利，从而将有利因素最大化。

On when to diverge from Europe, Britain needs to assess the costs and benefits, and then move decisively. Often, that will mean concluding that bespoke national regulations, no matter how objectively better than EU ones, are impractical, pointless or impossible to sell to voters. EU regulations on data protection are de facto global standards. In the chemicals industry so much of British trade is with the EU that many British firms have to follow its rules to the letter. When it comes to food safety and animal welfare, Britons like the EU’s high standards and will oppose any relaxation great enough to boost productivity.

在何时与欧洲分道扬镳的问题上，英国需要评估成本和收益，然后果断采取行动。通常情况下，这意味着要得出这样的结论: 定制的国家法规，无论客观上比欧盟法规好多少，都是不切实际、毫无意义或不可能让选民接受的。欧盟关于数据保护的法规实际上是全球标准。在化学工业方面，英国与欧盟的贸易如此之多，以至于许多英国公司不得不严格遵守欧盟的规定。当涉及到食品安全和动物福利时，英国人喜欢欧盟的高标准，并且会反对任何足以提高生产力的放松措施。

In other cases Britain can diverge from the EU in a way that packs a punch. It would not be hard to craft something better than the EU’s wasteful and environmentally damaging common agricultural policy. Competition policy and state-aid rules can also offer opportunities. Nimbler regulation, tax policies and visa rules can help London thrive as a global financial hub.

在其他情况下，英国可能会以一种强有力的方式脱离欧盟。制定出比欧盟浪费和破坏环境的共同农业政策更好的政策并不难。竞争政策和国家援助规则也可以提供机会。更灵活的监管、税收政策和签证规则可以帮助伦敦繁荣，成为全球金融中心。

Taking the cake

Some of the biggest boosts to growth, however, would come from policies that were possible within the EU, such as market-driven reforms to the health service and looser planning rules. These will be hard to sell in the prosperous Tory shires and working-class northern constituencies that voted Conservative in 2019 to “get Brexit done”. Mr Johnson’s cake-based campaign made no mention of tough choices. But unless his government starts making some, Brexit’s future will hold little but crumbs. ■

然而，一些对增长的最大的推动力将来自于欧盟内部才可行的政策，如以市场为导向的医疗服务改革和更宽松的规划规则。在富有的保守党郡和在2019年投票给保守党以“完成脱欧”的北部工薪阶层选区，这些政策将很难得到推广。约翰逊以蛋糕为基础的竞选活动没有提到艰难的选择。但是，除非他的政府开始有所作为，否则英国脱欧的未来只剩蛋糕渣了。■

How to think about the threat to American democracy

如何看待美国民主面临的威胁

Walking away

![image-20220104132412129](/Users/chenyujie/Library/Application Support/typora-user-images/image-20220104132412129.png)

The Republican Party’s continued Trump infatuation is alarming. It should not lead to fatalism

共和党对特朗普的持续迷恋令人担忧。它不应该导致宿命论

Jan 1st 2022

文章音频

AMERICANS ARE anxious about the stability of their democracy. Roughly 40% of the politically active say that members of the other tribe are evil; 60% believe they are a threat to the country. More than 80% think the system needs “major changes” or “complete reform”. Jeremiads from pundits about the decay of political life no longer seem to match the gravity of the threat. Some scholars have gone so far as to warn of the risk of civil war.

美国人对他们的民主制度的稳定性感到焦虑。大约40%的政治活跃人士认为其他党派的成员是邪恶的; 60%的人认为他们对国家来说是个威胁。超过80%的人认为该制度需要“重大变革”或“彻底改革”。权威人士对政治生活衰退的哀叹似乎不再与威胁的严重性相匹配。一些学者甚至警告内战的危险。

All this became dangerously real in the attack by Trump supporters on the Capitol a year ago, which injured 140 police officers, in an attempt to prevent the certification of their champion’s defeat. After a fleeting moment of clarity, the majority of Republican lawmakers reverted to making excuses for Donald Trump because his lies had rapidly taken hold. Today, fully 70% of Republican voters still believe that the election was stolen. In head-to-head polling the former president is one point behind Mr Biden, well within the margin of error and, thanks to the electoral college, possibly ahead in a theoretical match-up.

一年前，特朗普的支持者为了阻止“证明他们的支持者失败”，在国会大厦发动了袭击，导致140名警察受伤，这一切都变成了危险的现实。在短暂的澄清之后，大多数共和党议员又开始为特朗普找借口，因为他的谎言迅速占据了上风。今天，整整70%的共和党选民仍然相信选举结果“被窃取了”。在势均力敌的民调中，这位前总统落后于拜登1个百分点，在误差范围内，而且，由于选举团的存在，在理论上，他可能领先于拜登。

Extreme partisanship and the Republican refusal to accept the results of the election are indeed a dangerous combination. Yet easily lost in the daily diet of outrage is a fundamental truth about two-party politics: Democrats and Republicans need each other for the system to function. Renewal therefore must flow through the Republican Party. That will be hard—but not as hard as the catastrophists say.

极端的党派偏见和共和党拒绝接受选举结果确实是一个危险的组合。然而，关于两党政治的一个基本事实却很容易在日常的愤怒情绪中迷失: 民主党和共和党需要彼此才能使体系运转。因此，共和党内部必须进行“复兴”。这将是困难的，但不会像灾难论者说的那么难。

The threats to the system are real. The greatest is that in several key states the administration of voting has been dragged into the partisan arena. In Arizona some of the candidates running to replace the Republican incumbent, Doug Ducey, this year will argue that he ought to have somehow engineered a victory there for Mr Trump. In Georgia Republicans have weakened the office of secretary of state, after Brad Raffensperger refused to change the results of the elections in 2020 to suit Mr Trump. In Michigan and Pennsylvania Republican candidates who claim that the last presidential election was stolen are running for positions administering and certifying the next one.

美国体系面临的威胁是真实的。最大的问题是，在几个关键的州，选举管理已经被“拖入”了党派纷争。今年，在亚利桑那州，一些试图取代现任共和党人Doug Ducey的候选人将辩称，他本应该以某种方式为特朗普策划一场胜利。在佐治亚州，在Brad Raffensperger拒绝为迎合特朗普而改变2020年大选结果之后，共和党削弱了该州国务卿办公室的权力。在密歇根州和宾夕法尼亚州，声称上次总统选举结果被窃取的共和党候选人正在竞选下届总统选举的“管理和证明”职位。

The mid-term elections in November and the general election of 2024 will take place under this shadow. Republicans are poised to win control of one or both chambers of Congress. Mr Trump could legitimately retake the White House in 2024. The results of any of these races could be disputed by either side, especially in the next presidential election, which is likely to be close. If Democrats win, Republicans could well exploit the election machinery now infected by partisanship to try to block them from taking office. If Republicans win, Democrats could believe that disputed races have been stolen. Many would conclude that voter suppression had tipped the balance, and also note how often victors in the popular vote fail to win office. The loser’s concession, central to the transfer of power, might be withheld for a second time. Contempt for electoral legitimacy would become a bipartisan, and disastrous, conviction.

11月的中期选举和2024年的议会选举都将在这种阴影下进行。共和党人有望赢得国会参众两院中的一个或两个的控制权。特朗普可能会在2024年合法地夺回白宫。任何一场选举的结果都可能被任何一方争议，特别是在下届总统选举中的结果，很可能是势均力敌的。如果民主党获胜，共和党很可能会利用现在被党派偏见影响的选举“机器”，试图阻止他们上台。如果共和党获胜，民主党人可能会认为有争议的竞选结果被窃取了。许多人会得出结论说，选民压制打破了平衡，他们还注意到在普选中获胜的人常常无法赢得公职。输家的让步（核心是要权力的移交）可能会被再次“拒绝移交”。对选举合法性的蔑视将成为两党共同的、灾难性的信念。

The best response would be to protect democracy today by shoring up the voting system before it is too late. However, although Americans say they want voting reform, different sides mean different things by it. Given that Democrats would like to make it easier for people to vote and Republicans are concerned with fraud and how results are verified, a compromise guaranteeing both ought to be possible. An election-security law of this type should be at the top of the agenda for 2022. And yet the passing of legislation assumes the very bipartisanship whose absence is at the root of America’s democratic decay.

最好的应对措施是在为时未晚之前，通过支持选举制度来保护民主。然而，尽管美国人说他们想要选举制度的改革，但不同的方面对此有不同的意思。考虑到民主党人希望让人们更容易投票，而共和党人则担心欺诈和结果验证的方式，保证这两方面的折中应该是可能的。这类选举安全法应该是2022年的首要议程。然而，立法的通过正是建立在两党合作的基础上，而两党合作的缺失正是美国民主衰败的根源。

This is where the future of the Republican Party comes in. Catastrophists reckon that democracy’s defenders are unequal to its assailants partly because one of the country’s two great parties is turning into something that is more like Fidesz, the ruling party in Hungary, than the Republican Party of old. Consider how far-fetched it would have been to suppose that the administrations of the Bushes or Ronald Reagan would act as the vehicle of one man lying about a stolen election.

这就是共和党开始执政的未来。灾变论者认为，民主的捍卫者与攻击者之间是不平等的，部分原因是这个国家的两大政党之一正在变成一个更像匈牙利执政党青民盟的政党，而非过去的共和党。想想看，假设布什政府或罗纳德·里根政府成为“一个在选举上谎称选举结果被盗窃的男人”的工具，那将是多么牵强。

It is true that the party is dominated by America-First Republicans who set out to deter immigration, limit foreign trade and end entanglements abroad. Their ascendancy helps explain why those Republicans who voted to impeach Mr Trump for betraying his oath of office are now being ejected from the party.

确实，共和党是由“美国优先”的共和党人主导的，他们试图阻止移民，限制对外贸易，结束国外的纠纷案。他们的优势地位有助于解释，为什么那些因特朗普背叛就职誓言而投票弹劾他的共和党人，现在正被赶出该党。

However, even the Trump-addled incarnation of the Republican Party is made up of competing factions. Country-club Republicans like Glenn Youngkin, who won the governorship of Virginia in November, want to pay less tax and not be bossed around by Democrats. Just now they see Trumpism as the only available vehicle to further their interests.

然而，即使是被特朗普迷惑的共和党“化身”也由相互竞争的派系组成。像去年11月赢得弗吉尼亚州州长职位的格伦•扬金这样的乡村俱乐部共和党人希望少缴税，而且希望不受民主党人的指使。眼下，他们将特朗普主义视为推进自身利益的唯一途径。

That calculation may well prove to be mistaken. Anyone who thinks they can steer Mr Trump seems to end up discarded and disdained by him. However, people who object to the attempt to undermine the election result, including Mitch McConnell and Mr Raffensperger, are still in office. Although they refrain from condemning Mr Trump in public, only in the crucible of a crisis will you find out how they would choose between having their side in power and the survival of their democracy.

这种估算很可能被证明是错误的。任何自认为能够指使特朗普的人，似乎最终都被他抛弃和鄙视。不过那些反对暗中破坏选举结果的人仍然在位，其中包括Mitch McConnell和Raffensperger。尽管他们没有公开谴责特朗普，但只有在危机的严峻考验下，你才会发现他们将如何在掌权和民主生存之间做出选择。

Such people matter. Republican renewal, if it comes, will not be in the form of some Reaganite renaissance. Voters picking candidates for 2022 will be as many years distant from the Reagan revolution as Reagan was from FDR. And renewal is less likely from a conservative who stood up to Mr Trump than from someone who has enabled him by cowering in his slipstream, but who shares neither his reality-distorting powers nor his belief that the only legitimate result is the one where he wins.

这样的人很重要。共和党的复兴，如果到来的话，也不会以某种里根式的复兴形式出现。在2022年大选中，选择候选人的选民与里根革命有一定的距离，就像里根与罗斯福之间的距离一样遥远。复兴不太可能来自与特朗普对抗的保守派，反而是来自在他“滑铁卢”时畏缩不前“成就”他的人, 他们既没有扭曲现实的权利，也没有“特朗普获胜才是唯一合法结果”的信念。

Crucially, this person will be in charge of a party that still contains a large number of decent, patriotic voters who have been manipulated by a cynical group of leaders and propagandists into believing that, in saying the election was stolen, they are defending democracy. To presume that these people can be permanently treated as dupes would be a mistake.

至关重要的是，这个人所领导的政党仍然拥有大量善良且爱国的选民，这些选民被一群愤世嫉俗的领导人和宣传人士操纵，使他们相信“选举被窃取，他们是在捍卫民主”的说法。如果认为这些人会被永远当作傻瓜对待，那就大错特错了。

Renewal is impossible for as long as Mr Trump remains the Republican Party’s leader. However, that is another way of saying that the direction of the party is tied up with the fate of one man. And that means it can change. ■

只要特朗普仍是共和党领袖，复兴就不可能。然而，换种说法就是，一个政党的方向与一个人的命运紧密相连。这意味着它可以改变。■

How to ensure that the future of work is fair for all

如何确保未来的工作对所有人都公平

The World Ahead 2022
The hybrid workplace will be unequal, unless bosses design it not to be

混合型工作场所将是不平等的，除非老板设计的工作场所并非如此

![image-20220103091004876](/Users/chenyujie/Library/Application Support/typora-user-images/image-20220103091004876.png)

Nov 8th 2021

by Sacha Nauta: Deputy executive editor, The Economist

Greater productivity, happier and healthier workers and lower emissions are just some of the benefits of the great work-from-home experiment. Another hoped-for upside is increased workplace fairness. With workers stuck at home, appearing in similar-sized, randomly arranged rectangles on video calls and unable to suck up to bosses in the office, diversity-and-inclusion types had hoped that the pandemic would be the “great leveller”. It would finally destigmatise remote work and give all employees a fair chance to flourish.

“在家办公”这一重大试验，可以带来一些好处，比如说，更高的生产率，更快乐健康的员工以及更低的排放。人们希望的另一个好处是增加工作场所的公平性。由于员工被困在家里，在视频通话中都是以相似大小、随机排列的矩形出现，而且无法在办公室里讨好老板，多样性和包容性类型的人曾希望疫情会成为“伟大的平等者”。它最终将消除远程工作的污名，并给所有员工一个公平的发展机会。

Whether that really will be covid-19’s legacy depends on what happens next. Fairness—essentially, lack of favouritism—is easier to track when everyone is working in similar circumstances. But the vast majority of knowledge workers, and most employers, now prefer a hybrid approach. Before the pandemic 5% of work in America was done remotely and 27% of employers offered flexible hours; today the numbers are 40% and 88% respectively. The hybrid workplace will be a messy concoction. Left to develop organically, it is more likely to exacerbate existing inequalities than reduce them.

这是否真的会成为covid-19的“遗产”，取决于接下来会发生什么。当每个人都在相似的环境下工作时，公平，本质上是缺乏偏袒，更容易被追踪。但绝大多数知识型员工和大多数雇主现在更喜欢混合工作的模式。在疫情之前，美国有5%的工作是远程完成的，27%的雇主提供灵活的工作时间; 如今，这一数字分别为40%和88%。混合型工作场所将是一个混乱的混合体。如果任由其自然发展，它更有可能加剧而非减少现有的不平等。

That is because workers have different preferences about office work, and those differences are not distributed randomly. Given the choice, women, minorities and parents with young children will spend less time in the office. If the past is anything to go by, they will pay a price for this, losing out on pay rises and promotions because employers—often subconsciously—value physical presence. Women and minorities are also more likely to quit if an employer insists on full-time office-based work. A two-tier workforce could emerge, with a highly rewarded “in” group and a less rewarded “out” group.

这是因为员工对办公室工作有不同的偏好，而这些差异并不是随机分布的。如果可以选择，女性、少数族裔和有小孩的父母在办公室的时间将会减少。如果过去的经验可以借鉴的话，他们将为此付出代价，失去加薪和升职机会，因为雇主通常潜意识里看重“在办公室办公”的存在。如果雇主坚持全职办公室办公，女性和少数族裔也更有可能辞职。可能会出现一个双重的劳动力队伍，一个是报酬高的“进入”群体，另一个是报酬低的“出去”群体。

To avoid that outcome, employers need to engineer the workplace. Fairness will happen only by design. First, bosses will need to define whether they want procedural fairness (same rules for everyone) or fairness in outcomes (no group suffers as a result of a policy). Next, employers will need to be honest with themselves about when physical presence adds genuine value. Conflating physical proximity with productivity, and rewarding workers accordingly, does not make sense.

为了避免这种结果，雇主需要设计工作场所。公平只会通过设计来实现。首先，老板们需要明确他们是想要程序上的公平(对每个人都适用相同的规则)，还是结果上的公平(没有任何群体会因为政策而受到影响)。其次，雇主需要诚实地告诉自己什么时候“在办公室办公”会增加真正的价值。把物理上的接近与生产力混为一谈，并据此奖励员工，是没有意义的。

Even more important is to be clear with workers. Apple was berated for demanding that workers return to the office three days a week, but at least its rules were clear. Many employers promise that workers can work flexibly, but leave them to decipher unwritten rules. Such pledges are understandable in today’s hot labour market, but ring hollow without clarity.

更重要的是要和员工说清楚。苹果公司因要求员工每周在办公室工作三天而受到斥责，但至少它的规定是明确的。许多雇主承诺员工可以灵活地工作，但让他们自己去解读“口头”规则。在当今火热的劳动力市场，这样的承诺是可以理解的，但这样承诺听起来空洞而不明确。

![image-20220103091016186](/Users/chenyujie/Library/Application Support/typora-user-images/image-20220103091016186.png)

Firms that promise not to penalise remote workers must ensure that such workers have access to the same resources as everyone else. This also means investing in fair processes. Covid-19 has not wiped out biases about people who work from home. Workplace design that circumvents such biases will be even more important than before. Decisions about pay and promotion must be made in clear and measurable ways.

承诺不惩罚远程员工的公司必须确保这些员工能和其他人一样接触到相同的资源。这也意味着投资于程序上的公平。Covid-19并没有消除人们对在家工作的人的偏见。设计一个避开这些偏见的工作场所将比以往更加重要。关于薪酬和晋升的决定必须以明确和可衡量的方式做出。

Companies that value presence should consider setting fixed “in” days to ensure nobody gets left behind. Leaders can help reduce the presenteeism bonus by working from home on non-mandatory days, or by specifying that on some days, everyone works from home.

重视“露面”的公司应该考虑设置固定的“上班”天数，以确保没有人掉队。领导者可以通过在非强制性的日子在家工作，或者明确规定在某些日子每个人都在家工作，来帮助减少加班费。

Employers who demand a full-time return to work will lose talent and probably become less diverse. A survey by wfh Research found that 39% of white men were a flight risk in such circumstances, but 47% of non-white workers and 48% of women said they would resign or start looking for a new job. Mothers were two and a half times as likely as fathers to say they would quit.

那些要求全职重返工作岗位的雇主将会失去人才，而且可能会变得更加缺少多样化。wfh Research的一项调查发现，在这种情况下，39%的白人男性有“跳槽”的风险，但47%的非白人员工和48%的女性表示，他们会辞职或开始寻找新工作。母亲表示离职的可能性是父亲的2.5倍。

Phoning it in

One firm’s flight risk is another’s opportunity. Now that many jobs can be done flexibly, or fully remotely, employers can recruit from a wider pool. One study found that offering flexibility in a job advert increased applications by as much as 30%. It should also help with retention. In America black knowledge workers report much greater improvements in workplace satisfaction when working remotely than do white employees.

一家公司的跳槽风险是另一家公司的机会。现在很多工作都可以灵活完成，或者完全远程完成，雇主可以从更大的范围内招聘。一项研究发现，在招聘广告中说明灵活性可以增加30%的申请。这也有助于提高员工留存率。在美国，与白人员工相比，黑人知识型员工在远程工作时对工作场所的满意度有了更大的提高。

Technology has certainly reduced the cost of working remotely, and with that some of the stigma. But there is little evidence of the hoped-for “Zoomocracy”, where all voices would be heard equally. One-third of surveyed women working in tech said they were interrupted or ignored more often in virtual meetings than in person.

技术的确降低了远程工作的成本，同时也减少了一些耻辱。但是，几乎没有证据表明人们希望看到“Zoom民主”，即所有的声音都能被平等地听到。在接受调查的科技行业的女性中，有三分之一的人表示，她们在虚拟会议中被打断或被忽视的次数比面对面时更多。

In all the plans to reopen and bounce back, the need to address inequities that widened during the pandemic is often overlooked. Men are nearly twice as likely as women to say that working from home has positively affected their careers. Women are more likely to say they feel burnt out. Caregivers have been less likely to flourish. And new starters have missed out on crucial on-the-job training and networking. Any employer who wants to get off to a fair start in the hybrid world would be wise to deal with recovery and catch-up first.

在所有重新开放和恢复的计划中，往往忽视了解决疫情期间扩大的不平等现象的必要性。男性认为在家工作对他们的职业生涯有积极影响的可能性几乎是女性的两倍。女性更有可能说她们感到筋疲力尽。照顾者不太可能蓬勃发展。新入职者还错过了重要的在职培训和社交机会。任何想要在这个混合型世界有一个“公平”开端的雇主，都应该先明智地应对复苏和迎头追赶。

Sacha Nauta: Deputy executive editor, The Economist■

Will the world economy return to normal in 2022?

世界经济会在2022年恢复正常吗?

The World Ahead 2022
If it does not, a painful economic adjustment looms

如果没有恢复，一次痛苦的经济调整将迫在眉睫

Nov 8th 2021

by Henry Curr: Economics editor, The Economist

WILL THE stagflationary forces acting on the world economy last? Throughout 2021, central banks and most economists have said that the factors causing inflation to rise and growth to slow would be temporary. Supply-chain bottlenecks would subside, energy prices would return to earth and the rich-world workers staying out of the labour force—for reasons nobody fully understands—would return to work. And yet as 2021 draws to a close financial markets, the public and even central bankers themselves are beginning to lose faith.

滞胀力量对世界经济的影响会持续下去吗? 2021年全年，各国央行和大多数经济学家都表示，导致通胀上升和增长放缓的因素将是暂时的。供应链瓶颈将会消失，能源价格将回归现实，发达国家因没有人完全理解的原因而退出劳动力大军的工人将重返工作岗位。然而，随着2021年金融市场接近尾声，公众甚至央行官员自己都开始失去信心。

The dilemma facing policymakers is acute. The textbook answer to inflation caused by supply disruptions is to ignore it and let it go away on its own. Why damage economies with higher interest rates, which will not unblock ports, conjure up new supplies of natural gas or bring the pandemic to an end? In 2011 inflation in Britain rose to 5.2% as a result of rising commodities prices, but the Bank of England kept interest rates low. In the euro area the European Central Bank raised rates, helping send its economy back into recession, and before long found itself with inflation well below its target. Like then, inflation in 2022 driven by high energy prices is likely to subside. (Inflation is the rate of change of prices, meaning that even if prices do not return to previous levels, merely not rising as quickly is enough.)

政策制定者面临着严峻的困境。对于供应中断导致的通胀，教科书式的答案是忽略它，让它自行消失。高利率既不能疏通港口，也不能带来新的天然气供应，更不能终结大流行，为什么要用高利率来损害经济呢? 2011年，由于商品价格上涨，英国的通胀率上升到5.2%，但英格兰银行将利率维持在低位。在欧元区，欧洲中央银行提高了利率，使其经济重新陷入衰退，不久之后发现自己的通胀率远低于目标水平。与那时一样，由高能源价格推动的2022年通胀可能会消退。(通货膨胀是价格的变化率，这意味着即使价格没有回到以前的水平，只要上涨的速度不要那么快就足够了。)

Yet the comparison with the early 2010s is inexact. The woes of global trade in 2021 have not just been caused by disrupted supply, such as covid-19 outbreaks shutting Vietnamese factories. There has also been excess demand. Massive fiscal and monetary stimulus, combined with social distancing, led consumers to binge on goods, from games consoles to tennis shoes. In the summer of 2021 Americans’ spending on physical stuff was 7% above the pre-pandemic trend. In other countries, too, there is only a shortage of goods relative to unusually high demand for them. For the world economy to return to something like normal, consumers need to spend more of their plentiful cash on services, such as restaurant meals and travel.

不过，与2010年代初相比是不准确的。2021年全球贸易的困境不仅仅是由于供应中断，比如covid-19爆发导致越南工厂关闭。需求也出现了过剩的情况。大规模的财政和货币刺激，再加上保持社交距离，导致消费者疯狂购买从游戏机到网球鞋等各种商品。2021年夏季，美国人在实物上的支出比疫情前的趋势高出7%。在其他国家也一样，相对于对商品的异常高需求而言，商品短缺是唯一的问题。为了让世界经济恢复正常，消费者需要把更多的钱花在服务上，比如去餐馆吃饭和旅游。

The rich world has not seen a wage-price spiral since the 1970s

自20世纪70年代以来，发达国家还没有经历过工资价格的螺旋上升

Unfortunately economies are plagued by shortages of workers needed for service industries to thrive. Wages in leisure and hospitality are soaring. Many economists hoped that workers would return as emergency support for labour markets, such as furlough schemes and emergency unemployment insurance, ended. So far there is surprisingly little sign of that happening. For inflation to be temporary, wage growth as well as price growth probably needs to fall. The alternatives are an unlikely surge in productivity, or lower profit margins, which for businesses such as restaurants are already thin.

不幸的是，服务行业繁荣所需的劳动力短缺困扰着经济。休闲和酒店业的工资正在飙升。许多经济学家希望，随着休假计划和紧急失业保险等对劳动力市场的紧急支援的结束，工人们会重返劳动力市场。令人惊讶的是，到目前为止，几乎没有这种迹象。要让通胀成为暂时现象，工资增长和价格增长可能都需要回落。替代方案是生产率不太可能大幅提高，或者利润率降低，对于餐馆等企业来说，利润率本来就很低。

Some monetary policymakers are beginning to fear the reverse: wage growth that continues to rise as workers come to expect higher inflation. The rich world has not seen a wage-price spiral since the 1970s, and doves argue that in economies without widespread unionisation, workers are unlikely to negotiate higher wages. But if rising inflation expectations do prove self-fulfilling, central banks’ job would suddenly get much harder. They would not be able to keep inflation on target without sacrificing jobs. Emerging markets are used to this painful trade-off between growth and inflation, but it has not bitten hard in the rich world for decades. In big rich countries, the Bank of England is the closest to tightening—purely to preserve the credibility of its inflation target, rather than because it is warranted by underlying economic conditions.

一些货币政策制定者开始担心相反的情况: 随着工人开始预期会发生更高的通胀，工资增长将继续上升。发达国家自20世纪70年代以来就没有经历过工资价格的螺旋上升，而鸽派认为，在没有广泛工会化的经济体中，工人不太可能协商提高工资。但如果不断上升的通胀预期确实能自然实现，那么央行的工作将突然变得困难得多。他们将无法在不牺牲就业的情况下保持通胀目标。新兴市场已经习惯了在增长和通胀之间进行痛苦的权衡，但这对发达国家来说，已经几十年没有造成严重影响了。在大的发达国家中，英格兰银行是最接近紧缩的，纯粹是为了保持其通胀目标的可信度，而不是由其基本的经济条件所决定的。

Central bankers in a tightening spot

It is easy to imagine monetary policymakers raising interest rates and coming to regret it. Though inflation will remain high in the early months of 2022, central bankers typically think it takes a year and a half for higher interest rates to have their full effect on the economy. The forces that previously kept global rates and inflation low—demographic change, inequality and rampant global demand for safe assets—may have reasserted themselves by then. Imminent fiscal retrenchment in many countries will help cool economies: Britain has announced large tax rises and President Joe Biden is struggling to get large spending bills through Congress. And slower growth in China, which is struggling with a property-market slowdown, could spill over globally.

不难想象，货币政策制定者提高利率后会后悔。尽管通胀在2022年的前几个月仍将保持在高位，但央行行长们通常认为，加息需要一年半的时间才能对经济产生全面影响。以前把全球利率和通货膨胀率保持在低位的因素（人口变化、不平等和对安全资产疯长的全球需求）到那时可能会重新显现出来。许多国家即将实施的财政紧缩将有助于经济降温: 英国已宣布大幅增税，美国总统拜登正努力让国会通过大规模支出法案。正在与房地产市场放缓作斗争的中国经济放缓可能会对全球产生溢出效应。

Above all else, the pandemic is not over. The spread of the virus could yet disrupt economies once again if immunity wanes and new variants can evade vaccines. But with supply chains at their limits, the world cannot repeat the trick of maintaining economic growth using stimulus that shifts consumer spending towards goods. Instead central banks would have to choke off spending with higher rates to avoid excessive inflation while the supply-side of the economy adapts to patterns of spending and working that are vastly different from what prevailed in the 2010s. If normality does not return in 2022, the alternative is a painful economic adjustment.

最重要的是，疫情尚未结束。如果免疫力减弱且新的变种可能会避开疫苗，病毒的传播可能会再次扰乱经济。但由于供应链已处于极限，世界不可能重复使用刺激措施来维持经济增长的伎俩，刺激措施是将消费者支出转向商品支出。相反，央行将不得不通过提高利率来抑制支出，以避免过度通胀，同时经济的供给侧将适应与2010年代盛行的消费和工作模式截然不同的模式。如果到2022年还没有恢复常态，那么另一个选择就是痛苦的经济调整。

Henry Curr: Economics editor, The Economist■

The fight against climate change requires action not just words

应对气候变化需要行动，而不仅仅是空谈

The World Ahead 2022

“Implementation” will be the buzzword for the new year

“执行”将成为新年的流行语

Nov 8th 2021

by Catherine Brahic: Environment editor, The Economist

IT IS TRADITIONAL, after a major UN climate summit, for the momentum behind climate policy to enter a lull. It happens most noticeably in years after annual summits that had created a lot of pre-conference fanfare and expectations that were almost impossible to fulfil. This was the case in 2010, after the disastrous COP15 summit in Copenhagen the previous December had promised a new global climate treaty and delivered a devastating postponement.

在重大的联合国气候峰会之后，气候政策背后的动力进入停滞期，这是传统。这种情况最明显的是发生在年度峰会之后的几年里，这些峰会在会前做了大量的宣传以及创造了几乎不可能实现的期望。2010年的情况就是这样，2009年12月在哥本哈根举行的灾难性的COP15峰会承诺了一项新的全球气候条约，结果却造成了极其严重的推迟。

Regardless of the aftertaste left by COP26, which took place in November 2021 in Glasgow, 2022 will not have that luxury. In the first three months of the year, the Intergovernmental Panel on Climate Change will publish its next two major reviews of climate science, detailing the latest findings on the ways in which societies and ecosystems are vulnerable to the effects of climate change and the measures needed to cut greenhouse-gas emissions.

不管2021年11月在格拉斯哥举行的COP26峰会留下的“余味”如何，2022年将不会有那种“奢侈”。在今年前三个月,政府间气候变化专门委员会将公布接下来的两个主要的气候科学评论, 详细介绍最新发现的社会和生态系统很容易受到气候变化的影响的方式以及减少温室气体排放的措施。

Together, the two reports will only serve to underline the urgency of cementing the national climate goals that were presented in Glasgow with national policies. Expect the first report to give details of how much climate change is already affecting lives in both rich and poor countries. This will underline how important it is to limit global warming to no more than 1.5°C above pre-industrial average, in line with the most ambitious goal of the 2015 Paris agreement. The second report is likely to show just how challenging that will be.

这两份报告共同强调了格拉斯哥会议提出的国家气候目标与国家政策相结合的紧迫性。预计第一份报告将详细说明气候变化已经在多大程度上影响了发达国家和穷困国家的生活。这将突显出，将全球变暖幅度控制在比工业化前平均水平高出1.5摄氏度以内是多么重要，这符合2015年《巴黎协定》最雄心勃勃的目标。第二份报告可能会显示，这将是多么具有挑战性。

Cutting emissions quickly and deeply enough to limit global warming to 1.5°C means total future human emissions cannot exceed 400bn-450bn tonnes—roughly a decade of emissions at current annual rates. It is unfortunate but predictable that the promises made in Glasgow will not be sufficient to meet that goal.

快速且足够深入地减排，将全球变暖限制在1.5摄氏度以内，意味着未来人类排放总量不会超过4000亿至4500亿吨，按目前的年排放量计算，大约相当于10年的排放量。格拉斯哥做出的承诺不足以实现这一目标，这是不幸的，但也是可以预见的。

As a result, in 2022 there will be considerable pressure on governments and the private sector to keep their foot on the (decarbonised) accelerator pedal. “Implementation” is the buzzword, referring to the real, measurable efforts that countries and companies are, or are not, making in order to meet their promises to reduce emissions or reach net-zero within a certain timeframe. One concern is that the energy spike which began in the second half of 2021, and the resulting fears of the elderly freezing during the winter, will put a chill on the resolve of weary politicians. “The milestone [for 2022] is this,” says Li Suo, a policy analyst for Greenpeace East Asia. “By June 2022 can we see three new policies or actions that make the new targets achievable?”

因此，到2022年，各国政府和私营部门将面临相当大的压力，要求它们紧踩(脱碳)油门。“实施”是一个流行语，指的是各国和企业为了在一定时间内实现减排或实现净零排放的承诺而做出或不做的真正的、可衡量的努力。一个担忧是，从2021年下半年开始的能源弃用，以及由此产生的老年人在冬季冻僵的恐惧，将使疲惫的政治家们的决心受到打击。Greenpeace East Asia的政策分析师 Li Suo称：“ 这是(2022年)的里程碑，到2022年6月，我们能否看到三项新的政策或行动使新目标得以实现？ ”

As more and more companies adopt net-zero goals, there will be a push for better data and more transparency to ensure that progress towards decarbonisation is genuine. The American Securities and Exchange Commission is likely to introduce rules forcing firms to reveal how climate change, and efforts to fight it, will affect their business. Other regulatory bodies, including those within the European Union, are mulling more stringent rules on disclosure. As voluntary carbon markets expand, guidelines must be established so that, for instance, “an asset is actually an asset and can be discoverable either by an eye in the sky or machine learning,” says Rachel Kyte, dean of the Fletcher School of Law and Diplomacy at Tufts University, who is a veteran of climate negotiations.

随着越来越多的公司采用净零目标，这将推动更好的数据和更大的透明度，以确保在脱碳方面的进展是真实的。美国证券交易委员会可能会出台规定，迫使企业披露气候变化以及应对气候变化的努力将如何影响其业务。其他监管机构，包括欧盟内部的监管机构，正在考虑对信息披露实施更严格的规定。随着自愿碳市场扩张, 必须建立指导方针, 例如, 塔夫茨大学弗莱彻法律和外交学院院长、气候谈判老手Rachel Kyte说的 “资产实际上是一种优势,可以通过天空之眼或机器学习发现” 。

Lurking in the background is the next stick, though a soft one, that the Paris agreement offers, in order to encourage emissions reduction. Parties to the agreement signed up to a global stock-taking exercise, under which they must demonstrate the progress they have made towards meeting their national goals. Data-gathering for the first stock-take begins in 2022. Time to start implementing.

为了鼓励减排，隐藏在幕后的是《巴黎协定》提供的下一根“棍棒”，尽管力度不大。该协议的缔约方签署了一项全球盘点活动，根据该活动，它们必须证明在实现其国家目标方面取得的进展。第一次盘点的数据收集工作将于2022年开始。是时候开始实施了。

Catherine Brahic: Environment editor, The Economist■
