Trump’s legacy—the shame and the opportunity

> Jan 9th 2021

FOUR YEARS ago Donald Trump stood in front of the Capitol building to be sworn into office and promised to end “American carnage”. His term is concluding with a sitting president urging a mob to march on Congress—and then praising it after it had resorted to violence. Be in no doubt that Mr Trump is the author of this lethal attack on the heart of American democracy. His lies fed the grievance, his disregard for the constitution focused it on Congress and his demagoguery lit the fuse. Pictures of the mob storming the Capitol, gleefully broadcast in Moscow and Beijing just as they were lamented in Berlin and Paris, are the defining images of Mr Trump’s unAmerican presidency.

While Mr Trump’s supporters were breaking and entering, Congress was certifying the results of the president’s incontrovertible loss in November. While the mob was smashing windows, Democrats were celebrating a pair of unlikely victories in Georgia that will give them control of the Senate (see ). The last time that happened to Republicans was in 1892, when news of Benjamin Harrison’s humiliation travelled by telegraph.

通常，当一个政党遭遇如此大规模的挫折时，它会吸取一些教训，重新变得更强大。1964年巴里·戈德华特(Barry Goldwater)败选后，共和党就是这么做的;1984年沃尔特·蒙代尔(Walter Mondale)败选后，民主党就是这么做的。

Perhaps 70% of Republicans in the House and a quarter in the Senate connived in his conspiracy by vowing to attempt just that—to their shame, many of them persisted even after the storming of Congress. As an anti-democratic stunt, it had no precedent in the modern era (nor any chance of success). And yet it is also a sign of Mr Trump’s malign grip. After seeing how he ended the careers of loyalists like Jeff Sessions and almost single-handedly elected others, like Florida’s governor, Ron DeSantis, those facing primaries remain terrified of provoking him.

Abandoning someone whom you and most of your friends think is the rightful president, and whose power was taken away in a gigantic fraud by your political enemies, is something else entirely.

因此，特朗普编造的选举神话可能已经打破了共和党变革所需要的反馈循环。抛弃失败的领导者和失败的战略是一回事。抛弃一个你和你的大多数朋友都认为是合法总统的人，而他的权力却被你们的政敌用一场巨大的骗局夺走了，这完全是另一回事。

If something good is to come from this week’s insurrection, it will be that this way of thinking loses some of its purchase. The sight of a Trump supporter lounging in the Speaker’s chair should horrify Republican voters who like to think theirs is the party of order and of the constitution. To hear Mr Trump inciting riots on Capitol Hill may persuade parts of middle America to turn their back on him for good.

For Mr Biden, much depends on whether Trump-sceptic Republicans in the Senate share those conclusions. That is because the victories for Jon Ossoff and Raphael Warnock, the first African-American to be elected as a Democrat to the Senate from the South, have suddenly opened up the possibility that government in Washington, DC, will be less plagued by Republican obstruction and Trumpian stunts.

对拜登来说，这在很大程度上取决于对特朗普持怀疑态度的参议院共和党人是否认同这些结论。这是因为乔恩·奥索夫（Jon Ossoff）和拉斐尔·沃诺克（Raphael Warnock）这两位从南方当选为参议院民主党议员的第一位非裔美国人的胜利，突然开放/开启了一种可能性，即华盛顿特区政府将不再那么受共和党的阻挠和特朗普式的噱头所困扰。

A week ago, when the conventional view was that the Senate would remain in Republican control, it looked as if the ambitions of Mr Biden’s administration would be limited to what he could accomplish through executive orders and appointments to regulatory agencies. A 50-50 split in the Senate, with the vice-president, Kamala Harris, casting the tiebreaking vote, is as narrow a majority as it is possible to get. It will not miraculously let Mr Biden bring about the sweeping reforms many Democrats would like, but it will make a difference.

一周前，当人们普遍认为参议院仍将由共和党控制时，拜登政府的雄心似乎仅限于通过行政命令和任命监管机构来实现。参议院各占一半，副总统卡玛拉·哈里斯(Kamala Harris)投下了决定性的一票，这是可能获得的最微弱多数。这不会奇迹般地让拜登先生带来许多民主党人想要的全面改革，但会有所不同。

For example, Mr Biden will be able to get confirmation of his choices for the judiciary and for his cabinet. Control of the legislative agenda in the Senate will pass from the Republicans to the Democrats. Mitch McConnell, the outgoing Senate majority leader who spoke powerfully this week against Mr Trump’s institutional vandalism, was a master of blocking votes that might divide his caucus. That created the gridlock in Washington that voters usually blame on the president’s party.

Democrats may also be able to get some measures through the Senate via reconciliation, a procedural quirk that allows budget bills to pass with a majority of one or more, rather than the 60 votes needed to avoid a filibuster, which will remain, however much the leftist wing of the party would like to drop it.

Where Republicans come in is in the scope for cross-party votes. 共和党的介入是为了争取跨党派的投票。他们越是觉得美国中产阶级对暴乱感到恐惧，他们中的一些人就越有可能拒绝为了暴乱而封锁一切的虚无主义。他们的党团内部斗争越激烈，他们就越有可能通过完成一些事情以尽自己的一份力量来恢复对共和国的信心。

Why the crazy upward march in stock prices might just continue

为什么股价会继续疯狂上涨

![image-20210110153816241](/Users/chenyujie/Library/Application Support/typora-user-images/image-20210110153816241.png)

The markets in 2021

2021年的市场

Several things could get in the way of a market melt-up. But on a closer look, none seem all that formidable

get in the way 妨碍

all that 那么

formidable 可怕的 ; 令人敬畏的 ; 难对付的

有几件事可能会阻碍市场的崩溃。但仔细看，没有一个看起来那么可怕

Jan 9th 2021

IN A FAST-CHANGING world, the investment bankers of Wall Street and the City of London have clung to certain rituals. One of these is the annual “outlook”, which lays out the path the economy and financial markets might take over the coming year. These hefty documents start to appear in inboxes a few weeks before the year’s end.

outlook 前景

lay out 阐述

inbox （电子邮件）收件箱

在这个瞬息万变的世界，华尔街和伦敦金融城(City of London)的投资银行家们固守着某些惯例。其中之一是年度“展望”，它描绘了经济和金融市场在未来一年可能走向的道路。这些厚重的文件在年底前几周开始出现在收件箱中。

In mid-November a strategist at one bank had just put his outlook to bed with satisfaction. His call on stocks for 2021 was “constructive”: Wall-Street-speak for “bullish, but not mindlessly so”. But a few days later he was feeling a little less pleased with himself. His outlook was not distinctive. Rival strategists too were constructive.

put sth to bed 成功处理某件事情或解决某件事情

We thought we’d put the issue to bed, but it was brought up again at the next meeting.

我们以为问题已经解决了，可是在紧接着的会议上又被提了出来。

bullish 看涨的

11月中旬，一家银行的战略家刚刚满意地完成了展望。他对2021年股市走势的预测是“有建设性的”:华尔街表示“看涨，但不是盲目地看涨”。但几天后，他对自己感到有点不太满意了。他的观点并不与众不同。竞争对手的战略家们也持建设性态度。

It is not hard to see why. An end to the covid-19 pandemic is in sight. Rich-world governments are rediscovering the joys of fiscal pump-priming. Real interest rates are so low as to make sky-high stocks look cheap (see ). In short, the conditions seem ripe for further stockmarket gains. So ripe, indeed, that a persistent thought keeps surfacing in the minds of strategists. What is to stop stock prices worldwide going on a really crazy run?

insight 视野

be in/within (sb’s) sight 在（某人）视野之内；即将到来

The end of the project is at last in sight.

这个项目终于快要完成了。

The war goes on with no end in sight.

战争没完没了地持续着。

Our goal is in sight.

我们的目标就要实现了。

pump-priming 注资刺激经济

sky-high 极高的

不难看出其中的原因。covid-19大流行即将结束。富裕国家的政府正在重新发现财政刺激计划的乐趣。实际利率如此之低，连天价股票看起来都很便宜(见)。简而言之，股市进一步上涨的条件似乎已经成熟。事实上，如此的成熟，以至于战略家们的头脑中不断浮现出一种执着的想法。怎样才能阻止全球股票价格疯狂上涨呢?

Several things could get in the way of a market melt-up. One is the economy. Since April markets have been looking beyond the damage from covid-19 to the post-pandemic recovery. The discovery of workable vaccines seemed to bring that world closer. Economic indicators for America and China towards the end of 2020 were surprisingly strong. But the pandemic is not going quietly. More virulent strains of covid-19 have forced stricter lockdowns in parts of Europe. The harm to the world economy is likely to be more prolonged than hoped.

virulent 致命的

背景：融涨(Melt-Up)指的是，由于不想错过股市上涨赚钱的机会，投资者蜂拥进入(而不是基本面发生变化)，从而导致市场中的多头越来越多，市场情绪超级乐观，市场不断走高，甚至加速 上涨，最终导致市场崩溃。因此，这个概念也表示牛市即将到达终点，泡沫即将破灭。

有几件事可能会阻碍市场的崩盘。一个是经济。自4月份以来，市场一直在关注covid-19对大流行后复苏的损害。有效疫苗的发现似乎让这个世界更近了一步。到2020年底，美国和中国的经济指标惊人地强劲。但大流行并不是悄无声息的。更强的covid-19病毒株迫使欧洲部分地区实行更严格的封锁。这对世界经济的危害可能比预期的更持久。

![image-20210110153726789](/Users/chenyujie/Library/Application Support/typora-user-images/image-20210110153726789.png)

Another obstacle is bullish sentiment. The last time fund mangers were this optimistic about the scope for stockmarket gains was January 2018, according to a monthly survey by Bank of America done in December. A large majority think the world economy is in the “early-cycle” phase (ie, that there is a long runway of growth ahead). Paradoxically, positive sentiment is often seen as a reason to be wary, and that investors have got ahead of themselves. Indeed, 2018 began with much talk of a market melt-up, but ended with heavy stockmarket losses.

paradoxically 自相矛盾地

另一个障碍是看涨情绪。美国银行(Bank of America)去年12月进行的月度调查显示，基金经理上一次对股市上涨空间如此乐观是在2018年1月。绝大多数人认为世界经济正处于“早期周期”阶段(即前方还有很长的增长跑道)。矛盾的是，乐观情绪往往被视为要谨慎的理由，而投资者已经做得超前了。事实上，2018年一开始就有很多关于市场崩盘的言论，但最后却以股市大跌收场。

A lot of the current optimism rests on the idea that policy will continue to support the economy. What if policymakers change tack? Continued fiscal support requires political action and agreement, which cannot always be relied upon. A natural concern is that stimulus might be withdrawn abruptly, as it was after 2010. So far, though, there is little sign of this. In America the $900bn fiscal package passed after Christmas will add two percentage points to GDP growth in 2021, reckon economists at JPMorgan Chase, a bank. The loss of the Republican majority in the Senate may open the door to further fiscal easing. In Europe the boost from the €750bn ($920bn) recovery fund should start to be felt from the middle of the year.

目前的乐观情绪很大程度上取决于政策将继续支持经济的观点。如果政策制定者改变策略呢?持续的财政支持需要政治行动和协议，而这并非总是可靠的。人们自然会担心，刺激措施可能会像2010年之后那样突然退出。然而，到目前为止，几乎没有迹象表明这一点。摩根大通的经济学家估计，在美国，圣诞节后通过的9000亿美元的财政方案将使2021年的GDP增长增加两个百分点。共和党在参议院失去多数席位，可能为进一步放宽财政政策打开大门。在欧洲，7500亿欧元(合9200亿美元)复苏基金的提振作用，应该会从今年年中开始显现。

Yet another risk, and one that keeps some market bulls awake at night, is resurgent inflation. Lockdowns and fiscal transfers have left rich-world consumers with extra savings and a lot of pent-up demand—fuel for a post-pandemic spending spree. Meanwhile recession has also taken out supply capacity: a lot of small firms (and some large ones) have gone under. With enough bottlenecks, a surge in spending could drive up inflation. A dynamic of this kind has been playing out in commodity markets: a revival in industrial demand (notably from China) for copper and iron ore has bumped up against supply constraints and led to a run-up in prices.

bull 多头户；行情看涨的

a bull market 牛市

这是整个行业疯狂收购的最新交易去年，Spotify的老板丹尼尔•埃克(Daniel Ek)宣称“音频——不仅仅是音乐——将是公司的未来”。从那以后，Spotify就以十亿美元的价格大肆收购播客，收购了Gimlet、Anchor和Megaphone等制作和广告技术公司，以及一些节目;5月份，它斥资1亿美元收购了《乔·罗根体验》。最大的播客发行商苹果公司(Apple)收购了播客应用程序Scout FM，并与奥普拉•温弗瑞(Oprah Winfrey)等明星签约。

Podcasting makes most of its money through ads, which last year generated revenue of just $1.3bn, according to Omdia, a data firm—equal to 6% or so of the recorded-music industry’s sales, or the box-office takings of one Hollywood blockbuster. Why the big noise about a small business?

box-office 售票处 ; 票房收入，卖座情况

takings 进账，票房收入

blockbuster 流行大片

根据数据公司Omdia的数据，播客的大部分收入来自广告，去年广告收入仅为13亿美元，相当于唱片行业销售额的6%左右，或者相当于一部好莱坞大片的票房收入。为什么小生意会有这么大的声响?

One reason is growth. Global podcast listeners will exceed 2bn by 2025, Omdia reckons, from 800m in 2019. Ad sales may nearly treble, to $3.5bn. As giants hoover up shows, advertisers put off by fragmentation can buy many spots in one place.

treble 三倍的

hoover up : to use something quickly and eagerly

The country is hoovering up ever more commodities because its economy is growing so quickly.

Tickets were hoovered up when they went on sale last week.

其中一个原因是增长。Omdia估计，到2025年，全球播客听众将从2019年的8亿人增至20亿人。广告销售额可能增长近两倍，至35亿美元。随着巨头们大肆宣传节目, 被碎片化所拖累的广告商可以在一个地方购买许多广告点。

Second, podcasts give audio-streamers a chance to own content, which they cannot do with music. Most of the world’s tunes are owned by three record labels, which skim off about 70% of the streamers’ revenues. No matter how much they grow, firms like Spotify find their costs grow with them. The fixed cost of acquiring a podcast means that growth can boost margins.

skim off 撇去

除此之外，播客给了音频流以拥有内容的机会，而这是他们无法用音乐来做到的。世界上大部分的音乐都被三家唱片公司所拥有，这三家公司收取了主播收入的70%。像Spotify这样的公司发现，不管它们增长了多少，它们的成本也会随之增长。收购播客的固定成本意味着增长可以提高利润率。

Last, the ability to own podcasts gives streaming services a way to differentiate themselves. In contrast to video-streamers, which compete on content, Spotify, Amazon and Apple offer roughly the same library of 40m songs. Artists, who see streaming mainly as a way to promote their more profitable live shows, have little incentive to be exclusive to one service. Original podcasts are a way to lure fans.

最后，拥有播客的能力为流媒体服务提供了一种与众不同的方式。Spotify、亚马逊(Amazon)和苹果(Apple)提供的4000万首歌曲库大致相同，与在内容上相互竞争的视频流媒体不同。艺术家们将流媒体主要视为一种推广他们更有利可图的现场秀的方式，他们几乎没有动力只使用一种服务。原创播客是吸引粉丝的一种方式。

If their valuation model for podcasts is based on music catalogues, the streamers may be overpaying for some of them. Will Page, a former Spotify chief economist, notes that whereas back catalogues like Bob Dylan’s, recently sold to Universal Music Group, are valuable because the songs are replayed for years, podcasts are perishable. “A podcast about Dylan is of its time; a Dylan song is timeless,” he says.

如果他们的播客估值模型是基于音乐目录，那么流媒体可能会为其中一些节目支付过高的价格。Spotify前首席经济学家威尔•佩奇(Will Page)指出，像鲍勃•迪伦(Bob Dylan)最近卖给环球音乐集团(Universal Music Group)的歌曲这样的旧曲目之所以有价值，是因为歌曲被重播了好几年，而播客是容易腐烂的。“一个关于迪伦的播客正合时宜;迪伦的歌是永恒的，”他说。

Spotify’s move into podcasting has helped its share price double this year. But rivals like Apple and Amazon, which offer bundles of audio, video, gaming and more, can attract stars with promises of a spin-off TV show or game. Tech giants can also lean on hardware: iPhones come with Apple’s podcast app and Amazon’s speakers default to Amazon Music. The battle for consumers’ ears is only likely to get noisier. ■

spin-off 衍生节目；衍生电影

a TV spin-off from the film 电影衍生的电视作品

今年，Spotify进军播客业务使其股价翻了一番。但苹果(Apple)和亚马逊(Amazon)等提供音频、视频、游戏等捆绑服务的竞争对手，可以通过承诺推出衍生电视剧或游戏来吸引明星。科技巨头也可以依靠硬件:iphone自带苹果的播客应用，亚马逊的扬声器默认为亚马逊音乐。这场争夺消费者耳朵的争夺战只会越来越激烈。■

How to deal with leftover leave

如何处理剩余假期

Bartleby 巴托比

![image-20210103093528693](/Users/chenyujie/Library/Application Support/typora-user-images/image-20210103093528693.png)

leftover 剩余的

leave 离开 ; 离开居住地点 ; 遗弃 ; 丢弃 ; 假期 ; 休假

Unused holidays are a problem for employers and employees alike

未休完的假期对于雇主和雇员来说都是个问题

> Jan 2nd 2021

THE YEAR 2020 put worker morale to the test. It did not help that many employees were unable to enjoy a normal holiday, or had to change their plans. For Bartleby, two sun-drenched weeks in Spain were converted into a wet week in Cornwall, marked by an attempt to eat a pasty on the beach in the face of a sudden hailstorm. Finding a restaurant was virtually impossible because of the high demand created by the British government’s “eat out to help out” scheme.

put sb/sth to the test 使某人/某物接受考验

sun-drenched 阳光充足的

pasty 馅饼

hailstorm 雹暴

2020年是对员工士气的考验。许多员工无法享受正常的假期，或者不得不改变他们的计划，这也于事无补。对巴托比来说，在西班牙阳光灿烂的两周，在康沃尔却变成了潮湿的一周，他试图在海滩上吃馅饼，却遭遇了突然的冰雹。由于英国政府的“外出就餐帮助解决问题”计划造成了很高的需求，要找到一家餐馆几乎是不可能的。

Like many people, Bartleby is left with unused annual leave. But he is lucky that The Economist is a benign employer, willing to let him carry over a couple of weeks. Not every company can afford to be so kind. The occasional loss of a business columnist is not much of a handicap. Things are rather different when the employee is the client manager for the firm’s largest customer or the production manager at a microprocessor plant.

annual leave 年假

carry over 推迟

this debate has been carried over from the last meeting.

这一争议时上次会议遗留下来的

和很多人一样，巴特比的年假还没休完。但他很幸运，《经济学人》是一家仁慈的雇主，愿意让他继续工作几周。并不是每个公司都能做到这样。偶尔失去一位商业专栏作家并不是多大的障碍。当员工是公司最大客户的客户经理或微处理器工厂的生产经理时，情况就大不相同了。

The more vital the worker, the more likely they will have been to be asked to postpone their annual leave in the pandemic. This may leave employers with headaches in the coming year as workers catch up before their unused holiday is lost. Brian Kropp of Gartner, a consultancy, expects to see the lion’s share of staff shortages to land in the first half of 2021.

员工越重要，他们就越有可能被要求在大流行期间推迟年假。这可能会让雇主在来年头疼，因为员工们赶在未休完的假期结束前赶过来。咨询公司高德纳（Gartner）的布莱恩•克罗普（briankropp）预计，2021年上半年将出现最大比例的员工短缺。

For multinational firms, the different rules and customs that apply across the world further complicate things. America lacks federal laws that guarantee workers vacation time; rules are down to individual states. Even when Americans do get a holiday allowance, 55% of them do not use all of it, according to a survey from 2018. In Japan only 52% of workers took all their paid leave that year.

对于跨国公司来说，世界各地不同的规则和习俗使事情更加复杂。美国缺乏保障员工休假时间的联邦法律;规则取决于各州。2018年的一项调查显示，即使美国人有假期津贴，也有55%的人没有休完。在日本，只有52%的员工在那一年休完他们所有的带薪假。

Unlike their European peers, American and Japanese workers seem to succumb to social pressure: the fear that taking vacation reflects a lack of commitment to their job (or reveals their dispensability). Many American states also allow companies to impose a “use it or lose it” policy, under which they can insist employees cannot carry over unused leave into the following year. That may have prompted a lot of workers to take an extended Christmas break to avoid the loss of their precious allowance.

succumb 屈服

succumb to persuasion/desire 屈从于劝说/欲望

与欧洲同行不同，美国和日本的员工似乎屈服于社会压力:他们担心休假反映出他们对工作缺乏投入(或者暴露出他们的可有可无)。美国许多州还允许公司实施一项“不使用就失去”的政策，根据这项政策，他们可以坚持员工不能将未使用的休假延续到下一年。这可能促使许多工人延长了圣诞假期，以避免失去宝贵的津贴。

Still, wise employers may want to allow a little flexibility after what has been an extraordinarily difficult year. It is no good forcing people to turn up for work if, in the process, you inflict permanent damage on their well-being. Indeed, the evidence seems to suggest that workers put in extra effort in 2020, with those working from home adding an extra hour to their work day. They avoided the agonies of commuting but also lost the clear separation of work from home life, which probably added to stress. Some will have spent a “staycation” at home—which does not offer an invigorating change of scenery or routine for those who have been confined within the same four walls since March.

extraordinarily 异常地

turn up 到来

turn up for/to work /for study 来上班/值班

staycation 本地度假

invigorating 提神的 ; 令人精力充沛的 ; 振奋精神的 ;

不过，在经历了异常艰难的一年之后，明智的雇主可能还是希望能给员工一点灵活性。如果在这个过程中，你对人们的幸福造成了永久性的损害，那么强迫人们去工作是没有好处的。确实，有证据表明，2020年，员工付出了额外的努力，在家工作的人每天要多工作一小时。他们避免了通勤的痛苦，但也失去了工作与家庭生活的明显分离，这可能会增加压力。一些人将在国内度假——对于那些自3月份以来一直被限制在同一面墙内的人来说，这并没有带来令人振奋的风景变化或日常生活。

So there is a balance to be struck by employers between the need for adequate staffing and the need to keep on good terms with hard-working employees. In some countries, governments have intervened on workers’ behalf. Under Britain’s working-time regulations, employees were already entitled to carry eight days of leave over to the following year, if the employer agreed in advance. The rules were amended in 2020 to take account of covid-19, so a further four weeks could be carried over into 2021 and even 2022. The Chartered Institute of Personnel and Development, an association of HR managers, says this means that, in theory, workers can roll over all five weeks of leave from 2020. In Denmark the rules were changed to allow workers to roll over their unused holiday entitlements into 2021.

take account of 考虑到 ; 把…计算在内

因此，雇主需要足够的员工和与努力工作的员工保持良好关系之间找到一个平衡点。在一些国家，政府代表工人进行干预。根据英国的工作时间规定，如果雇主事先同意，雇员已经有权将8天的假期延长到下一年。该规则在2020年进行了修订，以考虑到covid-19的影响，因此可以再延长四周，直到2021年，甚至2022年。人力资源经理协会——英国特许人事与发展协会(Chartered Institute of Personnel and Development)表示，这意味着，从理论上讲，员工可以从2020年起将全部五周的假期提前完成。丹麦修改了相关规定，允许员工将未使用的假期延期至2021年。

In Belgium companies often allow employees to carry over five days of leave but they have to be used by the end of March. Doug Gerke of Willis Towers Watson, a benefits consultant, says that many employers are willing to give workers even more flexibility. Likewise, he says, many European companies may have an official “use it or lose it” policy, but in practice they are reluctant to confront workers over the issue.

在比利时，公司通常允许员工休5天以上的假，但这些假必须在3月底前使用。福利顾问威利斯·托尔斯·沃森（willistowers-Watson）的道格·格克（Doug Gerke）表示，许多雇主都愿意给员工更多的灵活性。他表示，同样，许多欧洲公司可能有一项“不使用就失去”的官方政策，但实际上它们不愿在这个问题上与员工正面交锋。

Mr Kropp says that some firms are asking employees to commit to the amount of leave they are planning to take in the first quarter of 2021, the better to forecast the staffing challenges they face. Others are extending “use it or lose it” deadlines to parcel out any absences over a longer period. Once the vaccines are distributed and travel is possible again airports could see a Gadarene rush. In 2021 managing people when they are not working will be just as important as managing them when they are.

parcel out 分配

Gadarene: relating to or engaged in a headlong rush

克罗普表示，一些公司正要求员工承诺他们计划在2021年第一季度休多少假，以便更好地预测他们面临的人力资源挑战。另一些公司则延长了“要么使用，要么丢掉”的期限，将员工缺勤的时间延长。一旦疫苗分发完毕，旅行再次成为可能，机场可能会迎来一场大热潮。2021年，在人们不工作的时候管理他们和在他们工作的时候管理他们一样重要。
