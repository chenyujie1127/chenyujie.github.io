A new refugee crisis has come to Europe

一场新的难民危机已经来到欧洲

Exodus, again

再次离开

Ukrainians are pouring into Poland. But many are heading the other way, to fight

乌克兰人正涌入波兰。但许多人正朝着另一个方向前进，去战斗

![image-20220306083009857](/Users/chenyujie/Library/Application Support/typora-user-images/image-20220306083009857.png)

A TRAIN PACKED with hundreds of Ukrainians arrives in Przemysl, a city in eastern Poland, after several hours’ delay. Nearly all are women and children, many of them exhausted and crying. At Medyka, the nearby border crossing, the situation is even more desperate. Tens of thousands of Ukrainians, as well as foreigners escaping Ukraine, queue on the Ukrainian side in the cold. Many have left their cars behind in heavy traffic and continued on foot.

一辆满载数百名乌克兰人的火车在延误数小时后抵达波兰东部城市普热梅希尔。几乎所有人都是妇女和儿童，其中许多人精疲力竭，哭泣不止。在附近的边境口岸梅迪卡(波兰与乌克兰边境)，情况更加危急。成千上万的乌克兰人和逃离乌克兰的外国人冒着严寒在乌克兰这边排队。许多人在交通拥挤时把车留下，继续步行。

Holding a bag in one hand, her young daughter’s hand in the other, and carrying her toddler in a sling, Anastasia describes walking 17km to the border. She was greeted by chaos and impenetrable crowds. A young couple saw a village consumed by flames along the way. “It was hell,” says Katya, recalling how her apartment in Kyiv shook as Russian bombs struck the city. “Fuck Russia,” she says, before correcting herself: “Fuck Putin.”

Anastasia一手拿着包，一手抱着女儿，用吊兜背着蹒跚学步的孩子，她描述了自己步行17公里到达边境的情形。迎接她的是混乱不堪的且难以逾越的人群。一对年轻夫妇在路上看到一个村庄被大火烧毁。Katya说: “那简直是地狱”，她回忆起俄罗斯炸弹袭击基辅时她的公寓是如何震动的。“去他的俄罗斯，”她说，然后又纠正了一下自己:“去他的普京。”

There are also heartening scenes. Thousands of Poles, Ukrainians living in Poland, and Europeans from farther away have turned up near the crossings, offering free transport and housing. Buses move the newly-arrived to schools and sports halls, now serving as shelters. Volunteers distribute clothing, sim cards, nappies and food. Muhammed Isa, who came to the border from Norway, embraces his elderly father, who has just crossed from Ukraine. They are Syrians from Aleppo, the father displaced for the second time in under a decade, both times by Russian bombs.

也有令人振奋的场景。数千名波兰人、生活在波兰的乌克兰人，以及来自更远地方的欧洲人出现在过境点附近，他们提供免费的交通和住房。公共汽车将新抵达的人运送到学校和体育馆，这些场所现在已经成为人们的避难所。志愿者分发衣服、手机卡、尿布和食物。从挪威来到边境的Muhammed Isa拥抱着刚从乌克兰过境的年迈父亲。他们是来自阿勒颇(叙利亚北部)的叙利亚人，父亲在不到十年的时间里第二次背井离乡，两次都是因为俄罗斯的炸弹袭击。

This is the kind of refugee crisis that Europe hoped never to see again. More than a million have already left Ukraine since the war began, the UN’s refugee agency said on March 3rd. Around two-thirds are arriving in Poland, with Hungary, Moldova, Romania and Slovakia taking the rest. The European Union’s commissioner for crisis management estimates 4m people could flee Ukraine in the next five months. Millions more will take refuge in the country’s west, which many hope will escape the brunt of Vladimir Putin’s invasion.

这是欧洲希望再也不要看到的难民危机。联合国难民署3月3日表示，自战争开始以来，已经有超过100万人离开乌克兰。大约三分之二的移民抵达波兰，剩下的移民则前往匈牙利、摩尔多瓦、罗马尼亚和斯洛伐克。欧盟危机管理专员估计，未来5个月将有400万人逃离乌克兰。还有数百万人将在该国西部避难，许多人希望那里能逃脱普京入侵的冲击。

The EU’s borders, which have gradually closed to most refugees since a big influx from Syria and Afghanistan in 2015-16, are opening up for Ukrainians. Poland, already home to nearly 1.5m Ukrainians—some displaced by Russia’s earlier aggression in the Donbas—will provide “every refugee from Ukraine” with shelter and assistance, says Mariusz Kamiński, the country’s interior minister. Romania is prepared to accommodate up to 500,000.

自2015到2016年叙利亚和阿富汗难民大量涌入以来，欧盟的边境已逐渐对大多数难民关闭，但现在却向乌克兰人开放。波兰内政部长Mariusz Kamiński表示，波兰将为“每一个来自乌克兰的难民”提供庇护和援助。波兰已经是150万乌克兰人的家园，其中一些人是在俄罗斯早期入侵顿巴斯后流离失所的。罗马尼亚准备容纳多达50万人。

In contrast to 2015, when four-fifths of adult migrants from Syria and Afghanistan were men, the Ukrainians are almost all women and children. Ukrainian men aged 18-60 are subject to conscription and banned from leaving the country. Such rules create heartbreaking scenes at the border. Roman, a 19-year-old economics student from Kyiv, cradles a three-year-old outside a train station in Lviv. His wife Veronika will take their child into Poland; he is heading back, possibly into battle. Another man in Lviv, Siman, is a construction engineer who until recently was working in France. He returned to Ukraine to bury his grandmother, but now finds himself obliged to take up arms. He is happy about it: “I am Ukrainian. I am ready. There is a first time for everything.”

相比之下，2015年，来自叙利亚和阿富汗的成年移民中有五分之四是男性，而乌克兰人几乎都是妇女和儿童。年龄在18-60岁之间的乌克兰男子必须服兵役，并被禁止出境。这样的规定在边境造成了令人心碎的场面。来自基辅的19岁经济学学生Roman在利沃夫火车站外抱着一个3岁的孩子。他的妻子Veronika将带着孩子去波兰; 他正在返回，可能是要投身于战斗中。利沃夫车站的另一名男子Siman是一名建筑工程师，直到最近他还在法国工作。他回到乌克兰埋葬他的祖母，但现在发现自己不得不拿起武器。他对此很高兴:“我是乌克兰人。我准备好了。凡事都有第一次。”

Other aspects of this migrant wave are different, too. Since 2017 Ukrainians have enjoyed visa-free travel around the EU for 90 days, meaning they need not seek asylum in the first EU country they enter. The task of accommodating them will thus be spread between countries. (In 2015 it fell disproportionately on Greece and Italy, which have long Mediterranean coastlines, and on Germany and Sweden, which threw open their doors.)

这波移民潮的其他方面也有所不同。自2017年以来，乌克兰人享受了90天的欧盟免签证旅行，这意味着他们不需要在他们进入的第一个欧盟国家寻求庇护。因此，容纳他们的任务将在国家之间展开。（2015年，它不成比例地落在希腊和意大利身上，这两个国家拥有漫长的地中海海岸线，而德国和瑞典则敞开了大门。）

Travelling to a land border, often in cars, lets people bring more of their life with them, including their pets. “You don’t have to walk across three countries, cross a desert, and sink into the Mediterranean,” says Hanne Beirens of the Migration Policy Institute, a think-tank. And Ukrainians, though poor by European standards, are highly educated, with 79% of those aged 20-26 possessing a degree.

通常乘坐汽车前往陆地边界，可以让人们带上更多生活的东西，包括他们的宠物。智库移民政策研究所的Hanne Beirens说:“你不需要穿越三个国家，跨过沙漠，然后沉入地中海。”乌克兰人虽然以欧洲标准衡量很穷，但受过高等教育，年龄在20-26岁之间的人中有79%拥有学位。

Ukrainians who live in the EU are helping their compatriots when they arrive. Poles have largely welcomed the Ukrainians, too. But foreigners fleeing Ukraine, especially those who are not white, have reported discrimination. Ukraine is home to thousands of students and workers from Africa and South Asia; non-Ukrainians made up 10% of the initial arrivals in Moldova. In Przemysl, patrols of masked youths have harassed dark-skinned migrants and relief workers.

居住在欧盟的乌克兰人正在帮助他们抵达欧盟的同胞。波兰人也在很大程度上欢迎乌克兰人。但逃离乌克兰的外国人，尤其是那些非白人，报告称受到了歧视。乌克兰是成千上万来自非洲和南亚的学生和工人的家园;非乌克兰人占摩尔多瓦最初抵达人数的10%。在普热梅希尔，蒙面青年巡逻骚扰深色皮肤的移民和救援人员。

The European Commission seems willing to let Ukrainians skip the red tape of asylum applications in favour of a new kind of status. On March 3rd member states look set to invoke a law on temporary protection, passed in 2001 but never used, that would let Ukrainians stay in the EU for three years. It will entitle them to benefits such as health care. But the law does not cover non-Ukrainian migrants. It is also unclear what happens after three years. Almost all Ukrainians say they wish to go home when the war ends, but that could change if Ukraine remains dangerous or is partially occupied by Russia.

欧盟委员会似乎愿意让乌克兰人跳过申请庇护的繁文缛节，以获得一种新的身份。3月3日，欧盟成员国似乎将启动一项2001年通过但从未使用过的临时保护法案，该法案将允许乌克兰人在欧盟停留三年。这将使他们有资格享受医疗保健等福利。但该法律不包括非乌克兰移民。也不清楚三年后会发生什么。几乎所有乌克兰人都表示，他们希望战争结束后回家，但如果乌克兰仍然危险或部分被俄罗斯占领，情况可能会改变。

Now that Mr Putin is bombing Ukrainian cities, the exodus is accelerating. But there is also traffic in the other direction. Scores of Ukrainians, most of them without military experience, are heading home to enlist. “I won’t be able to look other Ukrainians in the eye unless I go back and fight,” says Volodymyr Balychok, 23, a construction worker waiting at passport control. Eva Kravchuk, also 23, who runs an advertising agency in Warsaw, organised a convoy of supplies for the refugees. She, too, plans to go home to fight. Her mother, now sheltering in the basement of her home, does not want her to come, but “I can’t leave her there.” ■

现在普京正在轰炸乌克兰的城市，出逃正在加速。但在另一个方向也有交通流量。许多乌克兰人正准备回国应征入伍，其中大多数人都没有参军经验。23岁的建筑工人Volodymyr Balychok在护照检查站等候，他说:“除非我回去战斗，否则我无法直视其他乌克兰人的眼睛。”同样23岁的Eva Kravchuk在华沙经营着一家广告公司，她组织了一个为难民运送物资的车队。她也打算回家战斗。她的母亲现在躲在她家的地下室里，不想让她来，但是“我不能把她留在那里。”■

Joe Biden’s state-of-the-union address fails to impress

乔·拜登的国情咨文没有给人留下深刻印象

State of the union

国情咨文

![image-20220305101001376](/Users/chenyujie/Library/Application Support/typora-user-images/image-20220305101001376.png)

Ukraine aside, a gaffe-laden speech does nothing to turn Democrats’ problems around

撇开乌克兰不谈，一场极度失态的演讲对民主党的问题没有任何帮助

Mar 5th 2022

EVERY WOULD-BE president daydreams of the moment: the very first state-of-the-union address to Congress, the chance to be at the centre of the annual American pageant. After 50 years in Washington, and having attended a fair few of the spectacles himself, President Joe Biden will have fantasised about his turn at speechmaking more than most. The reality, however, was hardly triumphant.

每一个想成为总统的人都梦想着这样一个时刻:向国会发表第一份国情咨文，有机会成为一年一度的美国盛会的中心人物。在华盛顿生活了50年，自己也参加过不少这样的活动，乔·拜登总统比大多数人都更渴望能有机会发表演讲。然而，现实是没那么成功。

After a little more than one year in office, Mr Biden is in a slump. Setback has piled atop setback. After months of agonising negotiations, the signature legislative agenda of the administration—a gargantuan safety-net and climate-change spending package called Build Back Better—is in effect dead. High inflation, exacerbated by the large fiscal stimulus that Mr Biden signed, has wrecked his standing as an economic steward. Having promised to manage the pandemic better than his predecessor, Mr Biden has found himself at the mercy of covid-19 variants. And even his supposed core competence in foreign policy has looked in doubt since the chaotic withdrawal of American forces from Afghanistan (though his handling of the crisis in Ukraine has so far been more adroit).

任职一年多后，拜登陷入了低谷。挫折一个接一个。经过数月痛苦的谈判，政府标志性的立法议程，即一个名为“重建更好未来”的庞大安全网和气候变化支出计划，实际上已经夭折。拜登签署的大规模财政刺激计划加剧了高通胀，这破坏了他作为经济管理者的地位。拜登曾承诺要比前任更好地应对这场新冠大流行，但他发现自己只能任凭新冠病毒变体的摆布。自美国军队从阿富汗混乱撤军以来，就连他在外交政策方面的核心能力也受到了质疑(尽管到目前为止，他在处理乌克兰危机方面更为灵活)。

Voters are unimpressed, and ready to punish his Democratic Party in the mid-term elections to be held in November. Mr Biden’s net approval rating is minus 11 percentage points. That makes him precisely as unpopular as Donald Trump had become at this point in his presidency.

选民们对此无动于衷，并准备在11月举行的中期选举中惩罚他的民主党。拜登的净支持率为-11个百分点。这使得他与特朗普一样，在其总统任期内的这个时候一样不受欢迎。

The importance of the state-of-the-union address is questionable, despite all the attention it gets. Examine the swing in presidential-approval ratings taken by Gallup, a polling outfit, before and after the spectacles over the past four decades, and you will find an average change of zero points. The recitation of the president’s wish-list, which these speeches routinely descend into, has only an indirect relationship with actual policy. A careful study by a trio of political scientists—John Lovett, Shaun Bevan and Frank Baumgartner—found that popular presidents can successfully use the occasion to hector Congress into examining their agenda. “On the other hand, any powers of the president to influence the congressional agenda disappear completely when presidents lose their popular lustre,” they write.

国情咨文尽管得到了所有的关注，但它的重要性值得怀疑。Gallup民意调查机构对过去四十年的大选前后总统支持率的变化进行了调查，你会发现平均变化为零。对总统愿望清单的背诵，也就是这些演讲例行公事的总结，与实际政策只有间接的关系。由三位政治科学家John Lovett、Shaun Bevan和Frank Baumgartner进行的一项仔细研究发现，受欢迎的总统可以成功地利用这个机会威逼国会审查他们的议程。他们写道:“另一方面，当总统失去其受欢迎的光环时，总统影响国会议程的任何权力都将完全消失。”

It is hard to imagine any speech capable of overcoming these realities, not to mention difficulties of partisan animus and gridlock, no matter how well-crafted or excellently delivered. Unfortunately for Mr Biden, his effort on March 1st was neither.

很难想象有什么演讲能够克服这些现实，更不用说党派敌意和僵局的困难了，也不管演讲多么精心策划或出色表达。不幸的是，拜登在3月1日的努力两者皆非。

The answers to America’s domestic ailments were unsatisfying. Mr Biden seems to have lost faith in his ability to legislate—not daring to mention even once the name Build Back Better. The president was reduced to suggesting that components of this sweeping legislation could still pass, in an uninspiringly half-hearted manner.

美国国内问题的解决方案并不令人满意。拜登似乎对他的立法能力失去了信心, 甚至连“重建更好未来”这个名字都不敢提。总统则以一种令人沮丧的敷衍了事的方式，暗示这项全面立法的部分内容仍有可能获得通过。

Once central, climate change was relegated to occasional mentions. The recommendation that Congress reconsider already proposed legislation that stands no chance of passage, such as a pro-unionisation bill and Democrats’ preferred antidote to voter suppression, will prove to be lifeless. Mr Biden unveiled no major new pieces of domestic policy.

气候变化曾经是人们关注的焦点，如今却沦为偶尔提及的话题。建议国会重新考虑已经提出的没有通过机会的立法，例如支持工会的法案和民主党偏爱的选民压制的解药，将被证明是无意义的。拜登没有公布任何新的国内政策。

The president’s proposed solution to the problem of inflation was autarky and populism. “More goods moving faster and cheaper in America. More jobs where you can earn a good living in America. And instead of relying on foreign supply chains, let’s make it in America,” he said, to boisterous chants of “USA!” from Democrats. He also pledged a “crackdown on these companies overcharging American businesses and consumers”.

总统提出的解决通货膨胀问题的办法是自给自足和民粹主义。他向高呼”美国！“的喧闹的民主党人说：“更多的商品在美国流通得更快且更便宜。在美国，有更多的工作可以让你过上好日子。与其依赖国外的供应链，不如让我们在美国生产“。他还承诺“打击这些向美国企业和消费者收取过高费用的公司”。

Much sotu about nothing

Although never regarded as a gifted orator, Mr Biden was in especially poor form, at times stumbling through both his scripted lines and ad libs. He spoke of the “Iranian people” when he meant Ukrainians and confused the word “vaccine” for “virus”. After the perfunctory closing line “May God protect our troops”, the president felt compelled to shout a mystifying postscript into his microphone: “Go get him!” (or perhaps, as some transcribed it, “Go get ’em!”).

尽管人们从不认为拜登是一位有天赋的演说家，但他的演讲表现尤其糟糕，有时会在台词和即兴发挥中磕磕绊绊。他说到“伊朗人民”时，其实指的是乌克兰人，他把“疫苗”和“病毒”混淆了。在敷衍的结束语“愿上帝保佑我们的军队”之后，总统觉得有必要对着麦克风喊出一句神秘的补充:“去抓住他!”(或者，就像有些人抄写的那样，“去抓住他们!”)[不是很理解这里的点，有懂的小伙伴可以评论区留言]。

The president was strongest at the start of his speech, denouncing Vladimir Putin’s war in stark terms and leading the assembly in a standing ovation for the Ukrainian ambassador. “When dictators do not pay a price for their aggression they cause more chaos,” he said. There was chest-thumping over the success of the severe economic sanctions that America and its allies have imposed on Russia over its aggression, warmly received by members of both parties. “He has no idea what’s coming,” Mr Biden said of Mr Putin. The president also stressed that America and its allies would defend “every single inch” of NATO territory. But he reiterated that he would not risk direct confrontation over Ukraine with Mr Putin’s nuclear-armed state.

总统在演讲一开始就表达了最强烈的态度，严厉谴责了普京的战争，并领导议会为乌克兰大使起立鼓掌。他说:“当独裁者不为他们的侵略付出代价时，他们就会造成更多的混乱。” 他夸耀着，由于俄罗斯的侵略行为，美国及其盟友对其实施了严厉的经济制裁，并取得了成功，两党成员都对此表示热心接受了。拜登在谈到普京时表示:“他不知道接下来会发生什么。”总统还强调，美国及其盟国将捍卫北约的“每一寸领土”。但他重申，他不会冒险在乌克兰问题上与普京的核武国家直接对抗。

Unusually for any big political question, Americans of all partisan stripes are in broad agreement with the president’s strategy. Surveys taken by YouGov for The Economist after Mr Putin launched his invasion show that sizeable majorities of Democrats, Republicans and independents support imposing economic sanctions and dispatching weapons to Ukraine (see chart). And there is similar agreement that sending American soldiers into combat—or even conducting air strikes on Russian soil—would be a terrible idea.

不同寻常的是，对于任何重大的政治问题，所有党派的美国人都广泛赞同总统的策略。在普京发动入侵后，YouGov为《经济学人》所做的调查显示，相当多的民主党人、共和党人和独立人士支持对乌克兰实施经济制裁和向其派遣武器(见图表)。还有一个类似的共识，即派遣美国士兵参加战斗，或者甚至对俄罗斯领土进行空袭，这将是一个糟糕的主意。

The bipartisanship goes only so far. Despite broadly agreeing with his policy decisions (and the bipartisan ovations on Ukraine on Tuesday), Republicans are unwilling to laud the president for his handling of the crisis. Just 21% say they approve of his leadership on Ukraine. The hope that a foreign crisis might rally Americans around their president, as happened for George W. Bush after the attacks on September 11th 2001, looks misplaced.

两党合作只能到此为止。尽管共和党人大体上同意他的政策决定(以及周二两党对乌克兰问题的鼓掌)，但他们不愿称赞他对危机的处理方式。只有21%的人表示支持他在乌克兰问题上的领导。像2001年9月11日的恐怖袭击后发生在布什身上的那样，希望一场外国危机能让美国人团结在总统周围，这似乎是错误的。

There were signs of cooling relations between the Democratic Party’s progressive and moderate factions. Having added progressive phraseology like “equity” and “environmental justice” to his administrative and personal lexicon, Mr Biden is now pointedly distancing himself from his party’s leftist flank. “The answer is not to defund the police,” he said, lambasting a slogan that has now become a liability for Democrats running for office. “The answer is to fund the police,” he declared, scoring a rare standing ovation from Republicans. (He also drew their applause when he argued that: “If we are to advance liberty and justice, we need to secure the border.”)

When Vladimir Putin escalates his war, the world must meet him

当普京将战争升级时，世界必须与之“会面”

The invasion of Ukraine

入侵乌克兰

Muttering nuclear threats, Russia’s president vows to prevail in Ukraine whatever it takes

俄罗斯总统嘟囔着核威胁，发誓要不惜一切代价在乌克兰取得胜利

MARVEL AT THE heroism and resilience of Ukraine. In the first days of war, the armoured might of Vladimir Putin shrivelled before the courage of the nation he had attacked. In the face of Mr Putin’s invasion, the Ukrainian people have discovered they are ready to die for the idea that they should choose their own destiny. To a cynical dictator that must be incomprehensible. To the rest of humanity it is an inspiration.

惊叹于乌克兰的英雄主义和韧劲。在战争的最初几天，普京的装甲力量在他所攻击的国家的勇气面前萎缩了。面对普京的入侵，乌克兰人民已经发现，他们准备好为“自己应该选择自身命运”的想法而死。对一个愤世嫉俗的独裁者来说，这一定是不可理解的。对其他人来说，这是一种鼓舞。

If only this week’s bravery were enough to bring the fighting to an end. Alas, Russia’s president will not withdraw so easily. From the start, Mr Putin has made clear that this is a war of escalation—a hygienic word for a dirty and potentially catastrophic reality. At its most brutal, escalation means that, whatever the world does, Mr Putin threatens to be more violent and more destructive even, he growls, if that means resorting to a nuclear weapon. And so he insists that the world back off while he sharpens his knife and sets about his slaughter.

要是本周的勇敢足以结束这场战斗就好了。可惜的是，俄罗斯总统不会这么容易就撤军。从一开始，普京就明确表示，这是一场不断升级的战争，用一个卫生的词来形容肮脏且可能带来灾难性后果的现实。在最残酷的时候，局势升级意味着，无论世界做什么，如果这意味着诉诸于核武器的话，普京咆哮道，他可能会更暴力、更有破坏性。因此，他坚持认为，当他磨刀开始屠杀时，世界应该后退。

Such a retreat must not happen. Not only because to abandon Ukraine to its fate would be wrong, but also because Mr Putin will not stop there. Escalation is a narcotic. If Mr Putin prevails today, his next fix will be in Georgia, Moldova or the Baltic states. He will not stop until he is stopped.

这样的后退绝不能发生。不仅因为任由乌克兰自生自灭是错误的，还因为普京不会就此止步。战事升级是一种麻醉剂。如果普京今天获胜，他的下一个目标将是格鲁吉亚、摩尔多瓦或波罗的海国家。不阻止他，他是不会停下来的。

Escalation is at the heart of this war because it is how Mr Putin tries to turn defeat into victory. The first wave of his invasion proved as rotten as the cabal who planned it—just like his earlier efforts to suborn Ukraine. Mr Putin seems to have believed his own propaganda that the territory he has invaded is not a real country. The initial assault, which led with botched helicopter strikes and raids by lightly armed units, was conceived for an adversary that would implode. Instead, Ukrainian spirits have flourished under fire. The president, Volodymyr Zelensky, has been transformed into a war leader who embodies his people’s courage and defiance.

战事升级是这场战争的核心，因为这是普京试图将失败转化为胜利的方式。他的第一波入侵被证明和阴谋集团一样讨厌，就像他早先收买乌克兰的努力一样。普京似乎相信了自己的宣传，即他入侵的领土不是一个真正的国家。最初的攻击以拙劣的直升机袭击和轻装部队的突袭开始，其目的是让敌人崩溃。相反，乌克兰精神在战火中蓬勃发展。Volodymyr Zelensky已经变成了一名战争领袖，他代表了他的人民的勇气和反抗精神。

The optimism of the warmonger made Mr Putin lazy. He was so sure Ukraine would fall rapidly that he did not prepare his people for it. Some troops have been told they are on exercises, or that they will be welcomed as liberators. Citizens are not ready for a fratricidal conflict with their fellow Slavs. Having been assured that there would be no war, much of the elite feels humiliated. They are horrified at Mr Putin’s recklessness.

战争贩子的乐观让普京变得懒惰。他如此确信乌克兰会迅速垮台，以至于他没有让他的人民做好准备。一些部队被告知他们正在进行演习，或者他们将作为解放者受到欢迎。公民们还没有准备好与斯拉夫人同胞发生自相残杀的冲突。在得到不会发生战争的保证后，许多精英感到羞辱。他们对普京的鲁莽感到震惊。

And Russia’s president believed that the decadent West would always accommodate him. In fact, Ukraine’s example has inspired marches through the capital cities of Europe. Western governments, having listened, have imposed severe sanctions. Germany, which only a week ago drew the line at sending anything more lethal than helmets, is dispatching anti-tank and anti-aircraft weapons, overturning decades of policy based on taming Russia by engaging with it.

俄罗斯总统相信衰落的西方会一直迁就他。事实上，乌克兰的案例激发了欧洲各国首都的游行。西方政府听取了他们的意见，实施了严厉的制裁。就在一周前，德国还在向乌克兰派遣任何比头盔更具杀伤力的武器，而现在它正在派遣反坦克和防空武器，这推翻了数十年来通过与俄罗斯接触来驯服俄罗斯的政策。

Faced with these reverses, Mr Putin is escalating. In Ukraine he is moving to besiege the main cities and calling up his heavy armour to wantonly kill their civilian inhabitants—a war crime. At home he is bringing Russians to heel by redoubling his lies and subjecting his people to the harshest state terror since Stalin. To the West he is issuing threats of nuclear war.

面对这些不利因素，普京正在逐步升级。在乌克兰，他正在包围主要城市，召集他的重型装甲部队，肆意杀害平民居民，这是一种战争罪行。在国内，他通过增加谎言，让人民遭受斯大林以来最严重的国家恐怖袭击，让俄罗斯人屈服。他向西方发出核战争的威胁。

The world must stand up to him, and to be credible it must demonstrate that it is willing to bleed his regime of the resources that enable him to wage war and abuse his own people even if that imposes costs on Western economies. The sanctions devised after Mr Putin annexed Crimea in 2014 were riddled with loopholes and compromises. Instead of being deterred, the Kremlin concluded that it could act with impunity. By contrast, the latest sanctions, imposed on February 28th, have crumpled the rouble and promise to cripple Russia’s financial system. They are effective because they are destructive.

世界必须站起来反抗他，为了让他可信，世界必须证明，它愿意榨干他政权的资源，是这让他能够发动战争，虐待他自己的人民，即使这会让西方经济付出代价。2014年普京吞并克里米亚后制定的制裁措施漏洞百出，妥协不断。克里姆林宫非但没有被吓住，反而断定自己可以不受惩罚地采取行动。相比之下，2月28日实施的最新制裁使卢布贬值，并使俄罗斯的金融体系陷入瘫痪。它们之所以有效，是因为它们具有破坏性。

The danger of escalation is that this can easily become a test of who is most willing and able to go to extremes. Recent wars have been asymmetric. Al-Qaeda and Islamic State would commit any atrocity, but their power was limited. America could destroy the planet, but against foes like the Taliban in Afghanistan, nobody imagined it was willing. The invasion of Ukraine is different, because Mr Putin can charge all the way to Armageddon and he wants the world to believe he is ready to do so.

升级的危险在于，这很容易成为一种考验，看谁更愿意也更有能力走向极端。最近的战争是不对称的。基地组织[伊斯兰极端恐怖组织]和伊斯兰国可以犯下任何暴行，但他们的力量有限。美国可以摧毁地球，但面对像阿富汗塔利班这样的敌人，没有人会想到美国愿意这么做。入侵乌克兰则不同，因为普京可以一路冲向”世界末日”，他希望世界相信他已经做好了这样做的准备。

The idea of Mr Putin using a battlefield nuclear weapon is surely unlikely, but not impossible. He has, after all, just invaded his neighbour. And so the world must deter him.

普京在战场上使用核武器的想法当然不太可能，但也不是不可能。毕竟，他刚刚入侵了他的邻居。所以世界必须阻止他。

Some will say there is no point in saving Ukraine only to trigger a spiral that may destroy civilisation. But that is a false choice. Mr Putin says he wants to drive NATO out of the former Warsaw Pact countries and America out of Europe. If escalation serves him, the next confrontation will be even more dangerous because he will be less ready to believe that, for once, the West will stand its ground.

有些人会说，拯救乌克兰只会引发可能摧毁文明的螺旋式上升,这没有意义的。但这是一个错误的选择。普京表示，他希望将北约赶出前华沙条约签署国家，将美国赶出欧洲。如果局势升级对他有利，下一次的对抗将更加危险，因为他将不再那么容易相信西方会坚守自己的阵地。

Others may conclude that Mr Putin is insane and deterrence is hopeless. True, his goals are abhorrent, as are his means of achieving them. Neither does he have Russia’s true interests at heart. But he nonetheless has an understanding of power and how to keep it. No doubt he is alive to the language of threats.

其他人可能会得出这样的结论:普京疯了，威慑是没有希望的。诚然，他的目标是令人厌恶的，他实现目标的方式也是如此。他也没有把俄罗斯的真正利益放在心上。尽管如此，他还是理解权力以及如何保持权力。毫无疑问，他对威胁的语言很敏感。

By contrast, still others will want to short-circuit escalation, saying that Mr Putin must be stopped before it is too late. As images of suffering emerge from the ruins of Ukraine’s cities, calls are going up for NATO to do something, such as to create a no-fly zone. However, enforcing one requires shooting down Russian aircraft and destroying Russian air-defences. Instead, NATO needs to preserve a clear line between attacking Russia and backing Ukraine, while leaving no doubt that it will defend its members. That is the best brake on escalation.

相比之下，还有一些人希望阻止事态升级，称必须在为时已晚之前阻止普京。随着乌克兰城市废墟中浮现出的苦难景象，呼吁北约采取行动的呼声越来越高，比如设立禁飞区。然而，执行这一命令需要击落俄罗斯的飞机，摧毁俄罗斯的防空系统。相反，北约需要在攻击俄罗斯和支持乌克兰之间保持一条明确的界线，同时让人们确信，它将捍卫其成员国。这是阻止事态升级的最好办法。

What, then, can it do to deter Mr Putin without courting devastation? Only Mr Zelensky and his people can decide how long to fight. But if Mr Putin causes a bloodbath, the West can tighten the screws. An oil-and-gas embargo would further ruin Russia’s economy. Ukraine’s backers can send more and better arms. NATO can deploy more troops in its frontline states.

那么，它能做些什么来威慑普京而又不招致破坏呢? 只有Zelensky和他的人民才能决定战斗多久。但是，如果普京引发了一场大屠杀，西方可以加紧施压。石油和天然气禁运将进一步摧毁俄罗斯的经济。乌克兰的支持者可以提供更多更好的武器。北约可以在前线部署更多的部队。

Diplomacy matters, too. At peace talks in Belarus this week Russia still made outrageous demands, but negotiations should continue because they could help avert a war of attrition. The European Union has done well to open its arms to Ukrainian refugees, who already exceed 1m. A haven can strengthen the hand of the Ukrainian negotiators, as would a path to EU membership. XX and India have so far refused to condemn Mr Putin. As he escalates, they may be sufficiently alarmed to be willing to try to talk him down.

外交事务也可以决定。本周在白俄罗斯举行的和平谈判中，俄罗斯仍然提出了无理的要求，但谈判应该继续进行，因为这有助于避免一场消耗战。欧盟在接纳乌克兰难民方面做得很好，其已接纳超过100万的乌克兰难民。一个避风港可以增强乌克兰谈判代表的力量，就像一条通往欧盟成员国的道路一样。到目前为止，XX和印度都拒绝谴责普京。随着他将战事升级，他们可能会感到足够的恐慌，愿意试图说服他。

And there is work to do in Russia. Military commanders should know they will be prosecuted for war crimes using the evidence generated by innumerable smartphones. So should Mr Putin’s entourage. His enforcers signed up to line their pockets in a kleptocracy, not for a ticket to The Hague. The West can discreetly assure them that, if they remove Russia’s president from power, Russia will get a fresh start. However nauseating, the West should give Mr Putin a route into retirement and obscurity—just as it should give asylum to those fleeing his terror.

在俄罗斯方面也有工作要做。军事指挥官应该知道，他们将因使用无数智能手机生成的证据而被起诉战争罪。普京的随从也应该知道。他的执法者签合同是为了在盗贼统治下中饱私囊，而不是为了一张去海牙的机票。西方可以谨慎地向他们保证，如果他们把俄罗斯总统赶下台，俄罗斯将有一个新的开始。不管多么令人厌恶，西方应该给普京一条退休和默默无闻的道路，就像它应该给那些逃离他的恐怖活动的人提供庇护一样。

A palace coup may come to seem more plausible as the horror of what Mr Putin has done sinks in. The economy faces disaster. Russian military casualties are growing. Russians’ Ukrainian kin are being massacred in a conflict unleashed to satisfy one man. Even now brave Russians are taking to the streets to protest against a crime that stains their country. In a deep sense, Mr Putin’s needless war is one that neither he nor Russia can win. ■

The economic consequences of the war in Ukraine

乌克兰战争的经济后果

Trading with the enemy

与敌人交易

![image-20220303093339203](/Users/chenyujie/Library/Application Support/typora-user-images/image-20220303093339203.png)

Expect higher inflation, lower growth and some disruption to financial markets

预计会出现更高的通胀、更低的增长以及金融市场的一些混乱

Feb 26th 2022

OVER THE past decade intensifying geopolitical risk has been a constant feature of world politics, yet the world economy and financial markets have shrugged it off. From the contest between XX and America to the rise of populist rulers in Latin America and tensions in the Middle East, firms and investors have carried on regardless, judging that the economic consequences will be contained.

过去10年，不断加剧的地缘政治风险一直是世界政治的一个不变特征，但世界经济和金融市场对此不屑一顾。从X美之间的竞争到拉丁美洲民粹主义统治者的崛起，再到中东的紧张局势，企业和投资者都不顾一切，认为经济后果将受到控制。

Russia’s invasion of Ukraine is likely to break this pattern, because it will result in the isolation of the world’s 11th-largest economy and one of its largest commodity producers. The immediate global implications will be higher inflation, lower growth and some disruption to financial markets as deeper sanctions take hold. The longer-term fallout will be a further debilitation of the system of globalised supply chains and integrated financial markets that has dominated the world economy since the Soviet Union collapsed in 1991.

俄罗斯入侵乌克兰可能会打破这种模式，因为这将导致世界第11大经济体和其最大的大宗商品生产国之一被孤立。当前的全球影响将是通胀上升、增长放缓，以及随着制裁力度加大，金融市场将受到一些干扰。长期影响将是，自1991年苏联解体以来主导世界经济的全球化供应链和一体化金融市场体系将进一步削弱。

Start with the commodity shock. As well as being the dominant supplier of gas to Europe, Russia is one of the world’s largest oil producers and a key supplier of industrial metals such as nickel, aluminium and palladium. Both Russia and Ukraine are major wheat exporters, while Russia and Belarus (a Russian proxy) are big in potash, an input into fertilisers. The prices of these commodities have been rising this year and are now likely to rise further. Amid reports of explosions across Ukraine, the price of Brent oil breached $100 per barrel on the morning of February 24th and European gas prices rose by 30%.

先从大宗商品冲击说起。俄罗斯不仅是欧洲天然气的主要供应国，还是世界上最大的石油生产国之一，也是镍、铝和钯等工业金属的主要供应国。俄罗斯和乌克兰都是主要的小麦出口国，而俄罗斯和白俄罗斯(俄罗斯的代表)在钾肥(化肥的一种投入)方面也占有很大份额。这些大宗商品的价格今年一直在上涨，现在可能还会进一步上涨。在乌克兰各地发生爆炸的报道中，布伦特原油价格在2月24日上午突破了每桶100美元，欧洲天然气价格上涨了30%。

The supply of commodities could be damaged in one of two ways. Their delivery might be disrupted if physical infrastructure such as pipelines or Black Sea ports are destroyed. Alternatively, deeper sanctions on Russia’s commodity complex could prevent Western customers from buying from it. Up until now both sides have been wary about weaponising the trade in energy and commodities, which continued throughout the cold war. Sanctions after the invasion of Crimea did not prevent BP, ExxonMobil or Shell from investing in Russia, while American penalties on Rusal, a Russian metals firm, in 2018 were short-lived. Germany’s decision to mothball the Nord Stream 2 gas pipeline on February 22nd was largely symbolic since it does not yet carry gas from Russia to the West.

大宗商品供应可能会以两种方式之一受到破坏。如果管道或黑海港口等有形基础设施遭到破坏，它们的运输可能会中断。另一种选择是，对俄罗斯大宗商品联合体的进一步制裁可能会阻止西方客户从俄罗斯购买商品。直到现在，双方都对将能源和大宗商品贸易武器化持谨慎态度，这种行为在整个冷战期间都在持续。入侵克里米亚后的制裁并没有阻止英国石油、埃克森美孚(世界最大的非政府石油天然气生产商)或壳牌在俄罗斯投资，而美国在2018年对俄罗斯金属公司Rusal的惩罚也只是暂时的。2月22日，德国决定封存北溪2号天然气管道，这在很大程度上是象征性的，因为该管道尚未将天然气从俄罗斯输送到西方。

Nonetheless the prospect now is of more Western restrictions on Russia’s natural-resources industry that curtail global supply. Russia may retaliate by deliberately creating bottlenecks that raise prices. America may lean on Saudi Arabia to increase oil production and prod its domestic shale firms to ramp up output.

尽管如此，现在的前景是西方对俄罗斯自然资源行业的更多限制，从而限制全球供应。俄罗斯可能会故意制造瓶颈以提高价格作为报复。美国可能会依赖沙特阿拉伯增加石油产量，并刺激其国内页岩油企业增产。

The second shock relates to tech and the global financial system. While the trade in natural resources is an area of mutual dependency between the West and Russia, in finance and tech the balance of economic power is more one-sided. America is thus likely to put much tougher Huawei-style sanctions on Russian tech firms, limiting their access to cutting-edge semiconductors and software, and also blacklist Russia’s largest two banks, Sberbank and VTB, or seek to cut Russia off from the SWIFT messaging system that is used for cross-border bank transfers.

第二个冲击与科技和全球金融体系有关。虽然自然资源贸易是西方和俄罗斯之间相互依赖的领域，但在金融和技术方面，经济力量的天平则更向一边倒。因此，美国可能会对俄罗斯科技公司实施更严厉的“华为”式制裁，限制它们获得尖端半导体和软件，并将俄罗斯最大的两家银行俄罗斯联邦储蓄银行以及俄罗斯外贸银行列入黑名单，或试图切断俄罗斯与用于跨境银行转账的SWIFT通讯系统的联系。

The tech measures will act as a drag on Russia’s growth over time and annoy its consumers. The banking restrictions will bite immediately, causing a funding crunch and impeding financial flows in and out of the country. Russia has sought to insulate its economy from precisely this: the share of its invoices denominated in dollars has slumped since its invasion of Crimea in 2014, and it has built up foreign-exchange reserves. Still, it will hurt. Russia will turn to XX for its financial needs. Already trade between the two countries has been insulated from Western sanctions, with only 33% of payments from XX to Russia now taking place in dollars, down from 97% in 2014.

随着时间的推移，这些技术措施将拖累俄罗斯的增长，并惹恼其消费者。对银行的限制将立即产生影响，导致资金短缺，阻碍资金进出该国。俄罗斯一直在寻求使其经济免受这一影响:自2014年入侵克里米亚以来，以美元计价的发票所占比例大幅下降，而且俄罗斯还建立了外汇储备。尽管如此，它还是会带来伤害。俄罗斯将向XX寻求资金援助。两国之间的贸易已经不受西方制裁的影响，XX对俄罗斯的支付中只有33%是用美元支付的，这一比例低于2014年的97%。

Western banks appear to have fairly low exposure to Russia. Nonetheless, since the modern era of globalisation began in the 1990s no major economy has been cut off from the global financial system, and the risk of broader contagion across markets, while apparently low, cannot be ruled out.

西方银行对俄罗斯的敞口似乎相当低。尽管如此，自上世纪90年代开始的现代全球化时代以来，还没有任何一个主要经济体被全球金融体系拒之门外，而在市场中广泛蔓延的风险虽然明显较低，但也不能排除[这里不是很理解，有懂的朋友可以评论区留言]。

What does all this mean for the global economy? Russia faces a serious but not fatal economic shock as its financial system is isolated. For the global economy the prospect is of higher inflation as natural-resource prices rise, intensifying the dilemma that central banks face, and a possible muting of corporate investment as jittery markets dampen confidence.

这一切对全球经济意味着什么?由于金融体系孤立，俄罗斯面临着严重但并非致命的经济冲击。对全球经济而言，未来随着自然资源价格的上涨，通胀可能会更高，这加剧了央行面临的困境，而且随着市场动荡打击信心，企业投资可能会停止。

The longer-term impact will be to accelerate the division of the world into economic blocs. Russia will be forced to tilt east, relying more on trade and financial links with XX. In the West more politicians and firms will ask if a key tenet of globalisation—that you should trade with everyone, not just your geopolitical allies—is still valid, not just for Russia but other autocracies. XX will look at Western sanctions on Russia and conclude that it needs to intensify its campaign of self-sufficiency. The invasion of Ukraine might not cause a global economic crisis today but it will change how the world economy operates for decades to come. ■

长期影响将是加速世界经济集团的分裂。俄罗斯将被迫向东倾斜，更多地依赖与XX的贸易和金融联系。在西方，更多的政治家和公司会问，全球化的一个关键原则（你应该与所有人进行贸易，而不仅仅是你的地缘政治盟友）是否仍然有效，不仅对俄罗斯，对其他独裁国家也是如此。XX将审视西方对俄罗斯的制裁，并得出结论，认为自己需要加强自给自足的努力。入侵乌克兰今天或许不会引发全球经济危机，但它将改变未来几十年世界经济的运行方式。■

Britain’s post-Brexit trade policy is slowly maturing

英国脱欧后的贸易政策正在慢慢成熟

International trade

国际贸易

![image-20220302093348982](/Users/chenyujie/Library/Application Support/typora-user-images/image-20220302093348982.png)

The government is taking a more flexible approach

政府采取了更灵活的方法

THE DEPARTMENT FOR INTERNATIONAL TRADE (DIT) is certainly busy. On January 13th Anne-Marie Trevelyan, the international-trade minister, launched new talks with India. Just over a month later, on February 18th, she announced progress towards her goal of joining the Comprehensive and Progressive Trans-Pacific Partnership (CPTPP), a trade deal with 11 countries around the Pacific Rim. A digital deal with Singapore is imminent. A cruel observer might dismiss all this as a shallow demonstration of the country’s post-Brexit freedoms. A kinder one might note that, although it is not yet perfectly formed, Britain’s trade policy is at last maturing into a more workable form.

国际贸易部(DIT)确实很忙。1月13日，国际贸易部长Anne-Marie Trevelyan与印度展开了新的谈判。就在一个月后的2月18日，她宣布了她加入全面与进步跨太平洋伙伴关系协定(CPTPP)的目标取得的进展。CPTPP是一项与11个环太平洋国家达成的贸易协定。与新加坡的数字交易即将达成。一个刻毒伤人的观察者可能会认为，这一切只是英国脱欧后自由的肤浅表现。一个善意一点的人可能会注意到，尽管英国的贸易政策还没有完全形成，但它终于成熟为一个更可行的形式。

Part of the evolution happened inevitably as Britain moved through the process of Brexit. The first task that DIT faced when Theresa May set it up after the June 2016 referendum was to roll over the free-trade agreements that Britain had signed up to by virtue of being a member of the European Union. Out of 39, it has now managed an impressive 33. But some of these were done only by agreeing to temporary provisions and delaying a full renegotiation. Now, negotiators are working out where they need to tidy up, which in general means reviewing the biggest and oldest FTAs. The agreement with Mexico, for example, is ripe for an update, as its provisions on services do not match up to the most modern standards.

随着英国脱欧进程的推进，这种演变在一定程度上是不可避免的。梅姨在2016年6月公投后设立DIT时，面临的第一个任务是延长英国以欧盟成员国身份签署的自由贸易协定。在39个协定中，它现在已经成功地完成了33个。不过其中一些只是通过同意临时条款和推迟全面重新谈判来实现的。现在，谈判代表们正在制定他们需要整理的地方，这通常意味着审查最大和最古老的自由贸易协定。例如，与墨西哥的协议已经成熟，可以进行更新，因为它关于服务的规定不符合最现代的标准。

Broader progress has happened simply through practice. The government was forced to start rebuilding its trade capacity pretty much from scratch. As of September 2021 there were 521 full-time equivalent DIT staff directly responsible for negotiating and implementing trade agreements. One person who follows discussions at the World Trade Organisation closely says that, although British representatives in Geneva were active right from the beginning, it was mainly to show that they were no longer just part of the EU delegation. Now, it seems, their interventions are more substantive.

更广泛的进步仅仅是通过实践实现的。政府被迫从零开始重建其贸易能力。截至2021年9月，有521名同等全职的DIT人员直接负责谈判和执行贸易协定。一位密切关注世界贸易组织讨论的人士表示，尽管英国驻日内瓦代表从一开始就很活跃，但这主要是为了表明他们不再只是欧盟代表团的一员。现在，他们的干预似乎更加实质性。

Canny negotiators tend to gather and deploy information to their advantage—as do their business stakeholders as they seek to influence the direction of trade talks. But much to the frustration of the second group, a terror of leaks has stifled DIT’s information sharing. In July 2020 the department set up Trade Advisory Groups, as a more formal way of liaising with the outside world. Lobbyists still complain that officials often use meetings only to give updates on what they have already agreed, rather than to find out what they should be asking for. Still, it seems that engagement has improved.

精明的谈判者倾向于收集并利用对他们有利的信息，他们的商业利益相关者也一样，因为他们试图影响贸易谈判的方向。但令商业利益相关者失望的是，对泄密的恐惧扼杀了DIT的信息共享。2020年7月，商务部成立了贸易咨询小组，作为与外部世界联系的一种更正式的方式。游说者们仍然抱怨官员们经常只是利用会议来更新他们已经达成的协议，而不是去找出他们应该要求什么。不过，人们的参与度似乎有所提高。

Politicians have also learned that achieving the vision of post-Brexit Britain as a buccaneering free-trader is not quite as easy to realise as it sounds. The Trade Remedies Authority (TRA) was designed to make independent recommendations on defensive tariffs for the minister to accept or reject. But just weeks into the new system, the government passed emergency legislation allowing it to apply stronger protection for steel than the TRA had recommended. Now the entire framework is under review.

政治家们还认识到，脱欧后的英国要实现成为一个冒险的自由贸易者的愿景，并不像听起来那么容易。贸易救济局(TRA)旨在就防御关税提出独立建议，供部长接受或拒绝。但新体系实施几周后，政府就通过了一项紧急立法，允许其对钢铁实施比TRA建议的更强有力的保护。现在整个框架正在审查中。

Pacific dreaming

At least the approach to trade deals displays elements of consistency. Although it took a while to become visible, political leaders have long coveted membership of the CPTPP. Deals done with the likes of Australia and New Zealand mean that some of the heavy lifting has already been done. Yet it will not make much of a splash. Excluding partners with which Britain already has trade deals, joining the CPTPP would cover trade worth only 0.4% of Britain’s exports between 2017 and 2020. Even so negotiators hope to conclude the deal by the end of the year, and after that to help build on its rules from within the club.

至少，达成贸易协议的方式显示出了一致性的元素。尽管它花了一段时间才被发现，但政治领导人一直渴望成为CPTPP的成员。与澳大利亚和新西兰等国达成的协议意味着一些繁重的工作已经完成。然而，它不会引起多大的轰动。如果不包括已经与英国达成贸易协议的伙伴，加入CPTPP的贸易将只覆盖2017年至2020年英国出口总额的0.4%。即便如此，谈判代表们还是希望能在今年年底前达成协议，然后在俱乐部内部帮助建立规则。

Attitudes to other partners have seen more change. Sam Lowe of Flint Global, a consultancy, remembers a sense of denial from some close to the talks that incompatibility between EU and American food-safety standards would get in the way of a transatlantic deal. That has since faded into recognition that it would, and that Britain is unlikely to relax its regime. The Biden administration’s lack of interest in a trade deal has also diverted energy into a less glamorous effort of working with individual states (potentially useful in services, though not possible in goods). If successful, this could make it easier for professionals to get qualifications mutually recognised or create more opportunities to win public-procurement contracts.

人们对其他伙伴的态度也发生了更大的变化。咨询公司Flint Global的Sam Lowe记得，一些接近谈判的人有一种否认的感觉，他们认为欧盟和美国食品安全标准的不兼容会阻碍跨大西洋协议的达成。自那以后，人们逐渐认识到，英国不太可能放松其政权。拜登政府对贸易协定缺乏兴趣，并也将精力转移到与个别国家合作这一不那么引人注目的努力上(在服务方面可能有用，但在商品方面不可能)。如果成功，这将使专业人员更容易获得相互认可的资格，或创造更多机会赢得公共采购合同。

With hopes of a deal with America in the deep freeze, one with India is the next big shiny prize. India is a notoriously awkward negotiating partner, so officials are not naive about how difficult that will be. But if they were to manage it, it could be a shrewd economic move. Sophie Hale of the Resolution Foundation, a think-tank, points out that India’s demand for imported business services, an area that exploits British comparative advantage, is expected to triple by 2030. Business is more excited about a possible deal with India than about one with America, partly because there is much more regulatory uncertainty to tidy up. The CBI business lobby wants provisions to encourage free flows of data, as well as easier visas for business visitors.

随着与美国达成协议的希望渺茫[这里不知道是否合理]，与印度达成协议是下一个闪亮的大目标。印度是出了名的笨拙的谈判伙伴，所以官员们并不天真地认为这会有多困难。但如果他们能做到这一点，这可能是一个精明的经济举措。智库Resolution Foundation的Sophie Hale指出，到2030年，印度对进口商业服务的需求预计将增长两倍，而这一领域正是利用了英国的相对优势。与和美国的交易相比，商界对与印度的可能交易更为兴奋，部分原因是因为美国监管方面的不确定性要大得多。英国工商业联合会(CBI)的商业游说团体希望制定条款，鼓励数据自由流动，并为商务访问者提供更方便的签证。

As for XX, the policy seems to be not to mention it at all, or else merely to point to the CPTPP as an arena in which Britain could help to set standards that one day XX might move towards. But some change is happening behind the scenes. Having been scared by supply-chain vulnerabilities during the pandemic, DIT has built a team dedicated to examining supply-chain resilience in certain high-priority sectors. There are also plans to launch a forum with the United States Trade Representative to discuss topics including unfair trading practices. (That means XX.)

至于XX，该政策似乎根本不提它，或者只是指出，CPTPP是英国可以帮助制定标准的一个舞台，有朝一日XX可能会朝这个方向迈进。但一些变化正在秘密发生。由于在大流行期间对供应链脆弱性感到恐惧，DIT建立了一个团队，专门研究某些高度优先部门的供应链抵御能力。还计划与美国贸易代表署发起一个论坛，讨论不公平贸易做法等议题。(这指的是XX。)

A report by the National Audit Office, a public watchdog, notes the concerns of business associations, civil society and consumer groups over a lack of clarity in the alignment of trade policy with the government’s other objectives. Michael Gasiorek of the UK Trade Policy Observatory, a think-tank at Sussex University, hopes that in future there will be more emphasis on meaningful services-trade liberalisation. Trade policy has moved beyond a simple rejection of the EU, which remains by far Britain’s biggest trade partner. Yet there is still some way to go. ■

Covid has reset relations between people and robots

新冠调整了人与机器人之间的关系

Robotics

机器人

![image-20220301095132408](/Users/chenyujie/Library/Application Support/typora-user-images/image-20220301095132408.png)

Machines will do the nasty jobs; human beings the nice ones

机器将做令人讨厌的工作; 人类将做好的工作

Feb 25th 2022

ROBOTS HAVE been around for six decades or so. Originally, they were simple devices which did as they were told, working on assembly lines in, well, a robotic manner. They were often kept in cages, like zoo animals, to stop people getting too close. And for similar reasons. They were dangerous. If a mere human being got in the way of a swinging robotic arm, so much the worse for the human.

机器人已经出现了大约60年。最初，它们是简单的设备，按照指令工作，在装配线上以机器人的方式工作。它们经常被关在“笼子”里，就像动物园里的动物一样，以防止人们靠得太近。出于类似的原因。他们是危险的。如果仅仅是一个人挡住了摆动的机械手臂，那么对人类来说就更糟糕了。

Since then, they have got vastly more dexterous, mobile and autonomous. They are also more collaborative. There are now over 3m robots working in factories across the planet, according to the International Federation of Robotics, a worldwide industry association. Millions more move goods around warehouses, clean homes, mow lawns and help surgeons conduct operations. Some have also begun delivering goods, both on land and by air.

从那以后，它们变得更加灵巧、机动以及自主。他们也更愿意合作。根据国际机器人联合会(一个世界性的行业协会)的数据，现在全球有超过300万台机器人在工厂工作。还有数百万机器人在仓库搬运货物，打扫房屋，修剪草坪，帮助外科医生进行手术。一些公司也开始通过陆路和空运运送货物。

The pace of automation is likely to accelerate, for two reasons, a panel of robotics experts told the 2022 meeting of the American Association for the Advancement of Science (AAAS), held for the second year running in cyberspace, rather than in Philadelphia, as originally planned. The first reason is that covid-19 has created social changes which look likely to endure. The “Great Resignation”, in which millions around the world have quit their jobs, may in part be a consequence of lockdowns creating new opportunities for home working. These so-called lifestyle choices about which jobs to do, together with creaking supply chains and a boom in e-commerce, have left warehouses and many other businesses struggling to recruit workers.

一个机器人专家小组在美国科学促进会(AAAS) 2022年的会议上表示，自动化的步伐可能会加快，原因有二。该会议已连续第二年在网络空间举行，而不是按照原定计划在费城举行。第一个原因是，covid-19造成了看起来可能持续的社会变化。全球数百万人辞职的“大辞职”风潮，可能在一定程度上是“为在家办公创造了新机会”地封锁的后果。这些所谓的关于工作的生活方式选择，加上勉强运转的供应链以及电子商务的繁荣，使得仓库和许多其他企业难以招到员工。

Universal robots

The second reason is that the bots are getting better. Instead of just moving goods in warehouses to human “pickers”, who then put items into bags for home delivery, they are learning to do the picking and packing for themselves. In factories, they are stepping out of their cages and, equipped with advanced sensors and machine learning, a form of artificial intelligence (AI), are going to work alongside people. Such robots will increasingly help out in other places too, including hospitals, and in roles, such as caring for an ageing society—which, post-covid, has got used to a more techno future for health care, with “telemedicine” via remote doctors and health-monitoring mobile-phone apps.

第二个原因是机器人变得越来越好。他们不再只是把仓库里的货物交给人类“挑拣者”，然后由“挑拣者”把货物装进袋子送到家，而是学会了自己挑选和打包。在工厂里，它们正走出“笼子”，配备着先进的传感器和机器学习(一种人工智能的形式)，与人一起工作。这种机器人也将在医院等其他地方提供越来越多的帮助，并在一些岗位上发挥作用，比如照顾一个老龄化社会，在新冠肺炎疫情后，人们已经习惯了一个更加科技化的未来，通过远程医生和健康监测手机app实现“远程医疗”。

There is, though, a long way to go. In the field of manufacturing, car plants lead automation. But, as Henrik Christensen, director of the Contextual Robotics Institute at the University of California, San Diego, told the meeting, even the most advanced of them, those in South Korea, average only around one robot per ten workers. So-called “lights-out” car manufacturing, with no human beings on the factory floor, remains a distant dream.

然而，还有很长的路要走。在制造业领域，汽车厂领先于自动化。但是，正如加州大学圣地亚哥分校环境机器人研究所所长Henrik Christensen在会上所说，即使是最先进的机器人(在韩国)，平均每十个工人也只有一个左右的机器人。所谓的“熄灯”汽车制造，工厂里没有人，仍然是一个遥远的梦想。

Even so, the rise of robots makes some people fear for their jobs and ask how they will earn a living. “It’s a good question. I get it every week,” says Dr Christensen. He replies that jobs which robots undertake are usually dull, repetitive and strenuous—and, post-covid, such jobs are getting harder to fill. In many industries it is less a desire to reduce labour costs that is driving automation than the sheer difficulty of recruiting flesh-and-blood workers. Indeed, instead of destroying jobs, robots can create them by making businesses more efficient, allowing firms to expand. As Dr Christensen points out, for the past decade manufacturing employment in America has grown, even as the number of factory robots increased (see chart).

即便如此，机器人的崛起让一些人担心他们的工作，并问他们如何谋生。Christensen博士说：“这是个好问题。我每周都会收到这样的邮件”。他回答说，机器人承担的工作通常是枯燥、重复和艰苦的，而且在新冠肺炎疫情后，这些工作越来越难填补。在许多行业，推动自动化的不是降低劳动力成本，而是招聘有血有肉的工人的绝对困难。事实上，机器人可以通过提高企业效率来创造就业机会，而不是破坏就业机会，从而使公司得以扩张。正如Christensen博士所指出的，在过去的十年里，美国制造业的就业人数一直在增长，即使工厂机器人的数量也在增加(见图表)。

There is a similar fear in health care that robots will destroy jobs. But this is a myth, Michelle Johnson told the meeting. Dr Johnson is the director of the Rehabilitation Robotics Lab at the University of Pennsylvania, and currently works in Botswana on ways to use robots to help people recover from illness and injury. Even in America, let alone Africa, “there are just not enough clinicians to do the job,” she adds.

在医疗保健领域，人们也有类似的担忧，担心机器人会破坏工作岗位。但Michelle Johnson在会上表示，这是一个神话。 Johnson博士是宾夕法尼亚大学康复机器人实验室的主任，目前正在博茨瓦纳研究如何使用机器人帮助人们从疾病和受伤中恢复。她补充道，即使在美国，更不用说非洲了，“也没有足够的临床医生来完成这项工作”。

Dr Johnson has a particular interest in helping people recover from strokes. This sometimes requires intensive therapy for long periods. But public health-care systems are often too stretched to offer anything but limited treatment. Robots can help here, and in some circumstances may be better, even, than human physiotherapists, since they are both tireless and reliable. They can exercise a person’s limbs with consistent movements and take objective measurements of recovery. This, says Dr Johnson, allows a single occupational therapist, assisted by a technician, to look after, at the same time, half-a-dozen or so patients who would otherwise need one-to-one attention.

Johnson博士对帮助中风患者康复特别感兴趣。这有时需要长时间的强化治疗。但公共卫生保健系统往往过于紧张，只能提供有限的治疗。机器人可以在这里提供帮助，在某些情况下，甚至可能比人类理疗师更好，因为它们既不知疲倦又可靠。它们可以持续运动以锻炼一个人的四肢，并对恢复情况进行客观测量。Johnson博士说，这样一来，一名职业治疗师在一名技术人员的协助下，就可以同时照顾五名左右的病人，如果不是这样，这些病人是需要一对一照顾的。

Robots that work with people in such ways do, though, require special training. And there is a long way to go to improve that says Julie Shah, who leads the Interactive Robotics Group at the Massachusetts Institute of Technology. Most robots perform narrowly defined tasks, with mobile ones using their sensors to avoid bumping into people. “Robots need to see us as more than just an obstacle to manoeuvre around,” adds Dr Shah. “They need to work with us and anticipate what we need.”

然而，以这种方式与人合作的机器人需要经过特殊训练。麻省理工学院交互机器人小组的负责人Julie Shah表示，在这方面还有很长的路要走。大多数机器人执行的是狭义的任务，移动机器人通过使用它们的传感器来避免撞到人。Shah博士补充道:“机器人需要把我们不仅仅看作是移动的障碍。他们需要与我们合作，预测我们的需求。”

Studying what happens in factories shows that the most successful applications employ robots programmed by an engineer who is working side-by-side with someone (a so-called “domain expert”) fully versed in the tasks at hand. To make that easier, she and her colleagues are developing AI systems which can school a robot using natural-language commands.

对工厂中发生的事情的研究表明，最成功的应用程序使用的机器人是由一个工程师编程的，这个工程师与一个完全精通手头任务的人(一个所谓的“领域专家”)并肩合作。为了让这变得更容易，她和她的同事正在开发可以使用自然语言命令训练机器人的人工智能系统。

Although all three experts believe robots will enhance human capability, one problem is that regulation lags technology. With covid-19, says Dr Johnson, some clinicians worried that even the spread of telemedicine might affect their indemnity insurance, let alone robots. And although a long road remains ahead for the development of autonomous delivery vans and lorries, Dr Christensen finds it “ludicrous” that a test vehicle driving across a state border in America may thereafter have to comply with a completely different set of regulations from those which pertained in the place whence it came. It seems an awful lot of meetings lie ahead for roboticists and regulators to determine how machines and people will work together. ■

尽管三位专家都认为机器人将提高人类的能力，但有一个问题是监管滞后于技术。Johnson博士说，对于covid-19，一些临床医生担心，甚至远程医疗的传播可能会影响他们的赔偿保险，更不用说机器人了。而且自动送货车和卡车的发展还有很长的路要走，Christensen博士发现，一辆测试车辆在美国跨越州边界之后，可能必须遵守一套完全不同于其出发地的法规，这是“可笑的”。机器人专家和监管机构似乎要召开大量会议，以确定机器和人类将如何合作。■

The unseen costs of dirty work

苦活儿的无形成本

Bartleby

巴托比

![image-20220228092253066](/Users/chenyujie/Library/Application Support/typora-user-images/image-20220228092253066.png)

Work confers dignity. But some jobs are also a source of stigma

工作赋予尊严。但有些工作也是耻辱的来源

Feb 26th 2022

THE TERM “dirty work” was coined by Everett Hughes, an American sociologist, to capture the attitudes of ordinary Germans to the atrocities of the Nazi regime. Hughes used it to convey the idea of something immoral but conveniently distant, activities that were tacitly endorsed by the public but that could also be disavowed by them. The term has since come to embrace a wide array of jobs, in particular those that are essential but stigmatised, both crucial to society and kept at arm’s length from it.

“肮脏的工作”一词是由美国社会学家Everett Hughes创造的，其用来描述普通德国人对纳粹政权暴行的态度。Hughes用它来传达一些不道德但又正巧很遥远的想法，这种活动得到了公众的默许，但也可能遭到他们的否认。自那以后，这个词被广泛用来指代各种工作，尤其是那些必不可少但又被污名化的工作，这些工作对社会既至关重要，又与社会保持一定距离。

In an insightful new book of the same name, Eyal Press, a journalist, reports unflinchingly on occupations in America that carry the taint of stigma. Among others, he interviews prison guards in Florida and slaughterhouse workers in Texas. The pandemic has changed people’s awareness of some essential work: meat-processing plants were designated as critical infrastructure by the Trump administration in 2020, for example. But these jobs remain largely hidden from view; many are in physically isolated locations. People do not know what these workplaces are like and do not care to.

在一本颇具洞察力的同名新书中，记者Eyal Press毫不畏惧地报道了美国那些带有污名的职业。他还采访了佛罗里达州的狱警和德克萨斯州的屠宰场工人。疫情改变了人们对一些重要工作的认识:例如，特朗普政府在2020年将肉类加工厂指定为关键基础设施。但这些工作在很大程度上仍不为人所知;许多工作都在与世隔绝的地方。人们不知道这些工作场所是什么样的，也不关心。

Dirty jobs often pay better than other openings. But they impose unseen costs. They usually involve inflicting harm on others (or on the environment), and they ask emotionally and morally compromising questions of the people who perform them. What is it like to work day in and day out as a “knocker” or a “live hanger” on a slaughterhouse kill floor? Should a prison guard risk her livelihood to speak up about the violence routinely meted out to inmates by her colleagues? Mr Press does not exculpate individuals who behave badly in these jobs. But by forcing readers to confront the context in which they operate, he makes it harder to condemn them as bad apples.

苦活儿通常会比其他职位的薪水高。但它们带来了无形的成本。它们通常会对他人(或环境)造成伤害，并且会向执行这些任务的人提出情感和道德上的妥协问题。在屠宰场里，日复一日地做“敲击工”或“刽子手”是什么感觉?一个狱警应该拿自己的生计冒险，公开谈论她的同事经常对囚犯实施暴力吗? Press先生并不为在这些工作中行为不妥的个人开脱。但是，通过迫使读者面对他们所处的环境，他让人们更难去谴责他们是“害群之马”。

The boundaries of dirty work can be drawn too loosely. Some sociologists include firefighting, on the ground that it exposes people to danger on behalf of others, yet it is difficult to think of jobs that are less morally compromised. Indeed, exposure to danger can be the thing that cleanses work. Mr Press also meets operators of military drones at an air-force base in Nevada. Although drone warfare is a more precise form of combat than many others, operators often struggle with the idea of taking life without taking risk. The personal danger that soldiers on the ground face is what separates an unfair video-game from an exercise in valour.

苦活儿的界限可能划得不太精确。一些社会学家把消防工作也包括在内，理由是它让人们为了他人而置身于危险之中，但很难想象还有什么工作比消防工作在道德上更不受损害。的确，暴露在危险之中可以“净化”工作。Press先生还在内华达州的一个空军基地会见了军用无人机的操作员。尽管无人机作战比其他许多作战形式更为精确，但作战人员经常纠结于不冒险就能夺人性命的想法。作战士兵所面临的个人危险是将不公正的电子游戏和英勇训练区分开。

The definition of dirty work can also be too rigid. Although the dirtiest work often lies at a remove and is concentrated among the low-paid, white-collar organisations have their own types of grubby jobs. Think of the difference between engineers who build social-media platforms in the name of connectedness and the content moderators who monitor the effluvia that result. The very language of decarbonisation points to emerging fractures within energy-firm workforces, between employees developing the clean energies of the future and those pumping the dirty fossil fuels of the past.

苦活儿的定义也可能过于刻板。尽管最苦的工作往往在远处，而且集中在低收入人群中，但白领组织也有属于它们自己的苦活儿类型。想想那些以连通性为名构建社交媒体平台的工程师和那些监控这一结果的内容审查员之间的区别。“脱碳”的说法本身就指向了能源公司员工之间正在出现的裂痕，即开发未来清洁能源的员工与开采过去污染的化石燃料的员工之间的裂痕。

Individual roles can also break into dirtier and cleaner tasks. A piece of research in 2012 found that animal-shelter workers who were involved in putting animals to sleep were less likely to talk to outsiders about their work. “All The News That’s Fit To Click”, a new book by Caitlin Petre, a professor of journalism at Rutgers University, examines the effect that performance metrics are having on newsrooms. As she interviewed people for the book, Ms Petre noticed the frequency with which journalists used metaphors of pollution and contamination to describe the risk that chasing eyeballs might compromise the integrity of their editorial judgments.

个人角色也可以分成更脏和更干净的任务。2012年的一项研究发现，参与帮助动物入睡的动物收容所工作人员不太可能与外界谈论他们的工作。罗格斯大学新闻学教授Caitlin Petre的新书《所有适合点击的新闻》探讨了编辑标准对新闻编辑室的影响。在为写这本书去采访人们时，Petre女士注意到记者们使用“污染”的暗喻来描述追逐眼球可能损害其编辑判断的完整性的风险的频率。

Journalists tend to be good at telling stories, however. Ms Petre describes how many of them have drawn symbolic mental boundaries as a way of mitigating this risk. Analysing audience data to work out how to present their work is a “clean” use of metrics; using data to make decisions on content is impure and to be avoided. Criminal lawyers use a different but deep-rooted narrative to make sense of their own unpleasant tasks. They often defend people who have committed appalling crimes, for example, but because they do so in service of a noble ideal—everyone’s right to a fair trial—they are far less likely to feel morally compromised.

然而，记者往往擅长讲故事。Petre女士描述了他们中有多少人已经划定了象征性的心理界限，以此来减轻这种风险。通过对用户数据进行分析来确定如何呈现他们的作品是对标准的“干净”使用; 根据数据决定内容则是不纯的，应该避免。刑事律师用一种不同但根深蒂固的叙述来解释他们不愉快的工作。例如，他们经常为那些犯下骇人听闻罪行的人辩护，但因为他们这样做是为了一个崇高的理想，即每个人都有获得公平审判的权利，他们不太可能感到道德受到损害。

The idea of dirty work should not obscure the fact that having a job is a source of dignity. But some roles exact a hidden toll. To draw the sting of stigma, employers have to persuade their workers and the public that such jobs are not just essential, but also worthy of respect.

苦活儿的概念不应掩盖“有工作是尊严的来源“这一事实。但有些角色需要付出隐藏的代价。为了消除耻辱感，雇主必须说服员工和公众，这样的工作不仅至关重要，而且值得尊重。

How states are using, and misusing, funds from the American Rescue Plan

各州会如何“使用”和“滥用”美国救援计划的资金

拿钱就跑

![image-20220227101957770](/Users/chenyujie/Library/Application Support/typora-user-images/image-20220227101957770.png)

Governors benefit politically today, but they are creating liabilities for tomorrow

州长们今天在政治上受益，但他们正在为明天创造负债

Feb 25th 2022

WHEN KAY IVEY, Alabama’s governor, announced a plan to build two new 4,000-bed prisons, Democrats and progressive activists were unsurprisingly opposed. One objection was to how the construction would be financed: $400m would come from the state’s $2.1bn share of funds from the American Rescue Plan Act (ARPA). This was intended partly to relieve states from the economic and health toll of the covid-19 pandemic.

当阿拉巴马州州长KAY IVEY宣布计划新建两所拥有4000床位的监狱时，毫不意外，民主党人和进步活动人士表示反对。反对意见之一是如何筹措建设资金: 4亿美元将来自该州政府从《美国救援计划法案》中获得的21亿美元的资金份额。这在一定程度上是为了减轻各州因新冠大流行造成的经济和健康损失。

Critics said the new prisons tackled neither. “This is a gross misuse of funds when Alabama is at the bottom of the country in providing health care,” says JaTaune Bosby, executive director of the American Civil Liberties Union of Alabama. Republicans pushed the plan through in a special legislative session focused on prison construction. Building is set to begin later this year.

批评人士说，新监狱都没有解决这两个问题。阿拉巴马州美国公民自由联盟的执行董事JaTaune Bosby说:“当阿拉巴马州在提供医疗保健方面处于全国的最底层时，这是对资金的严重滥用。” 共和党人在一次以监狱建设为重点的立法特别会议上推动通过了这项计划。该项目将于今年晚些时候开工。

Signed into law in March 2021, the $1.9trn in stimulus from ARPA (equivalent to 9% of GDP) was predicated in part on the belief that state and local governments were in dire financial straits. In fact, tax receipts were recovering quickly even before the law came into effect. As they now start to prepare budgets for the coming fiscal year, governors and state legislators are finding creative ways to use the money—for better and for worse.

ARPA的1.9万亿美元刺激计划(相当于GDP的9%)于2021年3月签署成为法律，其部分是基于州政府和地方政府面临严重的财政困境。事实上，税收收入甚至在法律生效之前就迅速恢复了。当州长和州议员们开始为下个财政年度准备预算时，他们正在寻找创造性的方法来使用这笔钱，不管是好是坏。

Although some cash is being spent on responsible investments that will yield benefits for years, much is being used for massive new infrastructure projects and social programmes with long-term costs. The indulging Democratic and Republican governors are enjoying rising political fortunes. But the money will run out. For the states, the fiscal high will be short-lived.

虽然一些资金被用于负责任的投资，这些投资将带来多年的效益，但大部分资金被用于需要长期成本的大型新基础设施项目和社会计划。放纵的民主党和共和党州长们正享受着日益增长的政治财富。但是钱会用完的。对各州来说，财政高位将是短暂的。

After a sharp contraction when the pandemic first hit America, state general funds (mostly revenues from tax receipts) are overflowing: many states are posting their largest surpluses ever. Federal funds from ARPA, in the form of $350bn in direct transfers and more than $300bn in aid to health infrastructure, schools and transit agencies, have given states unprecedented fiscal resources (see chart 1). They have until 2026 to spend ARPA funds or lose them entirely, by which point many budget analysts expect revenues will have reverted to their pre-pandemic trend. Though the law includes some rules for how the money can be used, states have been adept at deploying it as they see fit.

在新冠首次袭击美国时，各州经历了急剧收缩之后，各州的一般资金(主要来自税收收入)开始过剩:许多州都出现了有史以来最大的盈余。来自ARPA的联邦资金，包括3500亿美元的直接转移支付，以及3000多亿美元对卫生基础设施、学校和交通机构的援助，为各州提供了前所未有的财政资源(见表1)。它们必须在2026年之前花掉ARPA的资金，否则就会完全失去这些资金。许多预算分析师预计，到那时，收入将恢复到新冠大流行前的趋势。尽管该法律对如何使用这些资金有一些规定，但各州一直擅长按照他们认为合适的方式使用这些资金。

Start with the more responsible investments. Most states have stuffed away money for a rainy day. The median state rainy-day fund now stands at the highest level as a share of spending in over three decades. Unemployment-insurance trusts, depleted during covid, have been restocked (see chart 2), though states have yet to use more than $80bn in federal funds remaining for that purpose. These sensible outlays will help states weather the next storm. Many have used ARPA to make one-time investments expected to bring long-term benefits. Nearly every state is putting some money towards clearing their collective $873bn backlog in maintenance, cleaning up pollution and replacing ancient computers. ARPA largesse has helped bolster public-health systems and schools amid the upheaval of the pandemic.

从更负责任的投资开始。大多数州都存了一些钱以备不时之需。目前，该州应急基金的中位数占支出的比例达到了30多年来的最高水平。在新冠肺炎期间被耗尽的失业保险信托基金已经被重新储备(见图2)，尽管各州尚未动用联邦基金用于这一目的的剩余的800多亿美元。这些合理的支出将帮助各州渡过下一场风暴。许多州利用ARPA进行一次性投资，以期带来长期效益。几几乎每个州都在投入一些资金，用于清理其总计8730亿美元的维护积压、用于清理污染以及更换旧电脑。ARPA的慷慨帮助在大流行的动荡中加强了公共卫生系统以及学校。

Many lawmakers are also giving money back to their constituents rather than letting it go to waste. Almost half of states have sent or plan to send bonuses to government employees, such as health-care workers, police officers and teachers. Coupled with pay rises, this is intended not only to retain valuable workers in a tight labour market, but also to curry favour with voters. Inspired by the popularity of the direct payments to households in the CARES Act (a $2.2trn fiscal stimulus passed under Donald Trump), Governor Tim Walz of Minnesota is promoting his proposed “Walz checks”, payments of up to $350 that would be sent to every household in the state. California, Indiana and Pennsylvania have similar schemes afoot.

许多议员也在把钱还给他们的选民，而不是让它们浪费掉。近一半的州已经或计划向医疗工作者、警察和教师等政府雇员发放奖金。伴随的还有加薪，这不仅是为了在紧张的劳动力市场留住有价值的工人，也是为了讨好选民。受《维生法案》(一项在特朗普领导下通过的2.2万亿美元财政刺激计划)中对家庭直接支付的普遍欢迎的启发，明尼苏达州州长Tim Walz正在推广他提出的Walz checks，即向该州的每个家庭发放高达350美元的付款。加州、印第安纳州和宾夕法尼亚州也有类似的计划。

The infrastructure projects that many state governments are embarking on, however, are a mixed bag. On paper, the Treasury Department’s rules administering ARPA allow states to spend funds on only three types of physical infrastructure: broadband, sewerage and water. States have eagerly allocated money to broadband, with an estimated $7.6bn already going to it, though Adie Tomer of the Brookings Institution, a think-tank in Washington, DC, cautions that few states have relevant bureaucratic experience. These efforts will get a further boost this year as the $1.2trn Infrastructure Investment and Jobs Act begins disbursing money, much of it earmarked for broadband infrastructure.

然而，许多州政府正在着手的基础设施项目形形色色。名义上，财政部管理ARPA的规定只允许各州将资金用于三类实体基础设施:宽带、污水处理和供水。尽管华盛顿特区智库Brookings Institution的Adie Tomer警告称，很少有州有相关的官僚经验，但各州都急切地向宽带拨款，预计已经投入了76亿美元。今年，随着1.2万亿美元的《基础设施投资与就业法案》开始拨款，这些努力将得到进一步的推动，其中大部分资金将用于宽带基础设施。

ARPA diem

In practice, however, the ability to use funds for “revenue replacement” has allowed many state policymakers to support cherished priorities, no matter how misguided. This is how Alabama justified its prison construction. From rural broadband to environmental clean-up, it is not hard to think of good long-run investments for the state, but prisons are a priority for Alabama’s Republicans. Iowa’s governor, Kim Reynolds, is handing $11m in ARPA funds to improve the “Field of Dreams”, the baseball diamond made famous in a film starring Kevin Costner.

然而，在实践中，将资金用于“收入替代”的能力，使许多州决策者能够支持他们所珍视的优先事项，无论这些优先事项有多么误导。这就是阿拉巴马州建造监狱的理由。从乡村宽带到环境清理，为该州想出好的长期投资并不难，但监狱是阿拉巴马州共和党的优先考虑事项。爱荷华州州长Kim Reynolds将由ARPA提供的1100万美元资金，用于改善因Kevin Costner主演的一部电影而闻名的棒球场“梦幻之地”。

More worrying are the new social programmes and tax cuts that several states are embarking on. These may prove unsustainable. Colorado will spend $275m on behavioural-health programmes thanks to ARPA, along with $13m just to build the bureaucracy for a future universal pre-kindergarten programme. New York’s governor, Kathy Hochul, has proposed $150m in tuition assistance for part-time students. Jared Walczak of the Tax Foundation, another think-tank, says nearly every state has reduced, or is contemplating trimming, its taxes, after Republicans led the way in 2021. “This is the year of the Democratic tax cut,” he says, pointing to the many Democratic governors who are proposed slashing the more regressive sales tax. Some Republicans are going further—Mississippi, the poorest state in America, may repeal its state income tax entirely.

更令人担忧的是一些州正在实施的新的社会计划和减税。这些可能是不可持续的。科罗拉多州将在行为健康项目上投入2.75亿美元，这要归功于ARPA，另外还有1300万美元，只是为了建立一个未来普及学前教育项目的官僚机构。纽约州州长Kathy Hochul提议为半工半读生提供1.5亿美元的学费援助。另一个智库Tax Foundation的Jared Walczak表示，在共和党2021带头之后，几乎每个州都削减了税收，或正在考虑削减税收。他说:“今年是民主党减税的一年。”他指的是许多民主党州长提议削减递减的销售税。一些共和党人走得更远，美国最穷的密西西比州可能会完全取消州所得税。

If such profligacy is cause for concern, voters hardly seem to notice. For Republicans, so often the apostles of austerity, the funds have been a boon. Vermont’s governor, Phil Scott, who is angling for another term in a state that leans heavily towards the Democrats, is emphasising his large spending commitments. The new governor of Virginia, Glenn Youngkin, is moving to eliminate the state’s grocery tax and raise teachers’ salaries, key planks of his winning campaign. Nearly all Republican incumbent governors facing re-election look safe. Only embattled Brian Kemp of Georgia appears in any danger of losing—he is desperately pushing pay rises for state employees and income-tax refunds.

如果这种挥霍行为值得担忧，选民们似乎不会注意到。对于共和党人来说，这些资金是一种福利，而他们通常是紧缩政策的倡导者。佛蒙特州州长Phil Scott强调了他的巨额支出承诺，他正在一个严重倾向于民主党的州谋求连任。维吉尼亚州新任州长Glenn Youngkin正在着手取消该州的食品杂货税，并提高教师工资，这是他竞选获胜的关键。几乎所有面临连任的共和党现任州长看起来都是安全的。只有处境艰难的佐治亚州的州长Brian Kemp面临着失败的危险，他正在拼命地推动提高州政府雇员的工资和退还所得税。

Those same state leaders will probably have moved on by the time federal funds run dry. For now, states risk squandering the opportunity to make productive investments, and may be exposing themselves to liabilities that will bite when the next recession comes. With patterns of work disrupted by the pandemic, Laura Kalambokidis, Minnesota’s chief economist, warns states to plan their long-term spending with caution: “None of us fully understands yet how the pandemic has permanently changed the economy.” ■

到联邦资金枯竭的时候，这些还是老样子的州的领导人很可能已经离开了。就目前而言，各州可能会浪费进行生产性投资的机会，而且可能会将自己暴露在债务之下，当下一次衰退到来时，这些债务将会带来冲击。由于工作模式被大流行打乱，明尼苏达州首席经济学家Laura Kalambokidis警告各州要谨慎规划长期支出:“我们都还没有完全理解新冠大流行是如何永久性地改变了经济。”■

The world should welcome the rise of the robots

世界应该欢迎机器人的崛起

Automation

自动化

![image-20220226091856863](/Users/chenyujie/Library/Application Support/typora-user-images/image-20220226091856863.png)

Concerns over mass unemployment are unfounded

对大规模失业的担忧是没有根据的

Feb 26th 2022

THE WORD “robot” was coined in 1920 by the Czech playwright Karel Capek. In “R.U.R.” (“Rossum’s Universal Robots”) Capek imagined artificial, fully functional servants. For most of their history, however, robots have been dumb, inelegant mechanical devices sitting out of sight in factories.

“机器人”一词是由捷克作家卡雷尔·恰佩克在1920年创造的。在“R.U.R.(“罗素姆万能机器人”)Capek想象了一个人工的、功能齐全的奴隶。然而，在其历史的大部分时间里，它们一直是工厂里看不见的没法儿说话的、不雅观的机械设备。

Things are starting to change, however. Robots have benefited from rapid innovations in smartphones, which brought cheap cameras and sensors, fast wireless communications and powerful, smaller computer chips. More recent advances in machine learning have added software to make robots better informed about their surroundings and equipped them to make wiser decisions. Robots are leaving carefully managed industrial settings for everyday life and, in the coming years, will increasingly work in supermarkets, clinics, social care and much more.

不过，情况开始发生变化。机器人受益于智能手机的快速创新，智能手机装有廉价的相机和传感器、快速的无线通信和功能强大、体积更小的计算机芯片。机器学习方面的最新进展已经为其增加了软件，使机器人更好地了解周围环境，并使它们能够做出更明智的决定。机器人正在离开精心管理的工业环境，进入日常生活，在未来几年，它们将越来越多地在超市、诊所、社会护理等领域工作。

They could not be coming at a better time. Many industries are facing a shortage of labour—the demand for workers has recovered much faster than expected from the pandemic and some people have left the workforce, particularly in America. Warehousing has grown rapidly thanks to the e-commerce boom. Robots are now indispensable, picking items off shelves and helping people pack an exponentially rising numbers of boxes. They are even beginning to trundle slowly along some pavements, delivering goods or food right to people’s doors. In a pandemic-ravaged world, short of workers but with lots of elderly folk to look after, having more robots to boost productivity would be a good thing.

他们来得正是时候。许多行业正面临劳动力短缺的问题，从疫情中复苏后对工人的需求比预期快得多，一些人已经离开了劳动力市场，尤其是在美国。由于电子商务的繁荣，仓储行业发展迅速。机器人现在是不可或缺的，从货架上取下物品，帮助人们打包数量呈指数增长的箱子。它们甚至开始在一些人行道上缓慢前行，把货物或食物直接送到人们的门口。在一个新冠大流行肆虐的世界，工人短缺，但有很多老人需要照顾，拥有更多的机器人来提高生产力将是一件好事。

And yet many people fear that robots will destroy jobs. A paper in 2013 by economists at Oxford University was widely misinterpreted as meaning that 47% of American jobs were at risk of being automated.

然而，许多人担心机器人会破坏工作。牛津大学经济学家在2013年发表的一篇论文被广泛误解为意味着47%的美国工作有被自动化的风险。

In fact, concerns about mass unemployment are overblown. The evidence suggests robots will be disruptive but ultimately beneficial for labour markets. Japan and South Korea have the highest robot penetration but very strong workforces. A Yale University study that looked at Japanese manufacturing between 1978 and 2017 found that an increase of one robot unit per 1,000 workers boosted a company’s employment by 2.2%. Research from the Bank of Korea found that robotisation moved jobs away from manufacturing into other sectors, but that there was no decrease in overall vacancies. Another study, by researchers at the Massachusetts Institute of Technology and colleagues elsewhere, looked at Finnish firms and concluded that their use of advanced technologies led to increases in hiring.

事实上，对大规模失业的担忧被夸大了。有证据表明，机器人将具有破坏性，但最终有利于劳动力市场。日本和韩国的机器人普及率最高，但劳动力非常强大。耶鲁大学对1978年至2017年期间日本制造业的一项研究发现，每1000名工人增加一个机器人单位，公司的就业率就会增加2.2%。韩国央行的研究发现，机器人化将工作岗位从制造业转移到了其他行业，但总体职位空缺并未减少。另一项研究是由麻省理工学院的研究人员和其他地方的同事进行的，他们观察了芬兰公司，得出的结论是，它们使用先进技术导致了招聘的增加。

For all that, the march of the robots will bring big changes to workplaces. The skills and firms that are rewarded will shift, too. But that need not be the disaster many fear. One supposed example of “bad automation” is self-service checkouts in supermarkets, because they displace human workers. But this is hardly dystopian—robots could perform work, such as butchering, that is unpleasant or stigmatised. Checkout staff who retrain to help customers pick items from aisles may well find that dealing with people in need is more rewarding than spending all day swiping barcodes in front of lasers.

尽管如此，机器人的发展将给工作场所带来巨大的变化。获得奖励的技能和公司也会改变。但这一需求未必是许多人担心的灾难。一个“糟糕的自动化”的例子是超市的自助收银台，因为它们取代了人力。但这很难说是反乌托邦，机器人可以从事令人不快或被人污蔑的工作，比如屠宰。结账人员经过重新培训，去帮助顾客从走道中挑选商品，他们可能会发现，与需要帮助的人打交道比整天在激光前刷条形码更有价值。

Inevitably, some people will be on the losing end of change even as the robots make society as a whole better off. One lesson from the freewheeling globalisation of the 1990s and 2000s is that the growth in trade that was overwhelmingly beneficial triggered a political backlash, because the losers felt left behind. That is one more reason why firms and governments would do well to recognise the value of retraining and lifelong learning. As jobs change, workers should be helped to acquire new skills, including how to work with and manage the robots that will increasingly be their colleagues.

不可避免的是，即使机器人让整个社会变得更好，一些人也会在变革中处于失败的一方。从上世纪90年代和本世纪头十年自由放任的全球化中得到的一个教训是，带来压倒性好处的贸易增长引发了政治上的强烈反对，因为输家感觉被甩在了后面。这也是为什么企业和政府应该充分认识到再培训和终身学习的价值的另一个原因。随着工作岗位的变化，应该帮助工人获得新技能，包括如何与机器人合作和管理，这些机器人将越来越多地成为他们的同事。

The potential gains from the robot revolution are huge. In Capek’s play, the robots revolt against their human masters and cause mass unemployment and worse. The beginnings of the world’s real robots have not matched Capek’s satire. There is no reason to think that their future needs to either. ■

机器人革命的潜在收益是巨大的。在恰佩克的戏剧中，机器人反抗他们的人类主人，导致大规模失业和更糟的情况。世界上真正的机器人的诞生并没有达到恰佩克的讽刺程度。没有理由认为他们的未来定会这样。■

History will judge Vladimir Putin harshly for his war

历史将严厉批判普京发动的战争

Where will he stop?

他将在哪里停下来?

![image-20220225093240064](/Users/chenyujie/Library/Application Support/typora-user-images/image-20220225093240064.png)

Russia’s president has launched an unprovoked assault on his neighbour

俄罗斯总统对其邻国发动了无端攻击

Feb 25th 2022

BY THE TIME it began, early on a gloomy grey morning on February 24th, the onslaught against Ukraine ordered by Russia’s president, Vladimir Putin, had acquired a sickening inevitability. Yet nothing about this war was inevitable. It is a conflict entirely of his own making. In the fighting and the misery that is to come, much Ukrainian and Russian blood will be spilled. Every drop of it will be splattered on Mr Putin’s hands.

2月24日一个阴沉的早晨，当战争开始时，俄罗斯总统普京下令对乌克兰的进攻已经达到了一种令人恐惧的不可避免的地步。然而这场战争并不是必然的。这完全是他自己造成的冲突。在接下来的战斗和苦难中，乌克兰和俄罗斯将付出大量的“鲜血”。每一滴都会溅到普京的手上。

For months, while Mr Putin had remained in seclusion, amassing roughly 190,000 Russian troops on the borders with Ukraine, the question had been: what does this man want? Now that it is clear he craves war, the question is: where will he stop?

几个月来，普京一直“闭关锁国”，在俄乌边境集结了大约19万俄军，但问题是:这个人想要什么? 现在很明显他渴望战争，问题是:他会在哪里止步?

To hear Mr Putin on the eve of the invasion, he would like the world to believe that he will stop at nothing. In his battle speech, recorded on February 21st and released as he unleashed the first volleys of cruise missiles against his fellow Slavs, Russia’s president railed against “the empire of lies” that is the West. Crowing over his nuclear arsenal, he pointedly threatened to “crush” any country that stood in his way.

在入侵前夕普京称，他希望全世界相信，他会不择手段。在他于2月21日录制的战斗演讲中，这位俄罗斯总统怒斥西方是“谎言帝国”，在他第一次向斯拉夫人发射巡航导弹时，该演讲才公布。他对自己的核武库洋洋得意，并刻意地威胁要“摧毁”任何阻碍他的国家。

Early reports, some unconfirmed, only underlined the scale of his ambition. There had been speculation that Russia’s president might be satisfied with control over Donetsk and Luhansk, regions of eastern Ukraine containing small, Russian-backed enclaves that were the object of last-minute diplomacy. But all that has crumpled in the face of a vast assault.

一些未经证实的早期报道只是强调了他的雄心壮志。此前有人猜测，俄罗斯总统可能会对控制顿涅茨克和卢甘斯克感到满意，这两个乌克兰东部地区都有俄罗斯支持的小型飞地，它们是最后一刻外交努力的目标。但在一场大规模的袭击面前，这一切都崩溃了。

The reports said that Russian land forces had crossed from the east, heading for Kharkiv, Ukraine’s second city; from the south, heading from Crimea towards Kherson; and from Belarus to the north, heading for Kyiv, the capital. It was unclear in what strength they were moving. But Mr Putin seemingly covets all of Ukraine, just as American and British intelligence reports had claimed all along. In acting, he has set aside the everyday calculus of political risks and benefits. Instead he is driven by the dangerous, delusional idea that he has an appointment with history.

报道称，俄罗斯陆军已从东部越境，前往乌克兰第二大城市哈尔科夫; 从南面，从克里米亚前往赫尔松; 从白俄罗斯向北，前往乌克兰首都基辅。目前还不清楚他们移动的速度。但普京似乎觊觎整个乌克兰，正如美国和英国情报报告一直宣称的那样。在行动上，他把政治风险和利益的日常风险放在一边。相反，他被一种危险的、妄想的想法所驱使，其认为自己与历史有约。

That is why, should Mr Putin seize a large swathe of Ukraine, the gatherer of the lands will not stop to make peace at its borders. He may not invade the NATO countries that were once in the Soviet empire, at least not at first. But, bloated by victory, he will subject them to the cyber attacks and information warfare that fall short of the threshold of conflict.

这就是为什么，如果普京占领了乌克兰的大片土地，这些土地的收集者将不会停下来在其边境上媾和。他可能不会入侵曾经属于苏联帝国的北约国家，至少一开始不会。但是，由于胜利的膨胀，他将使他们遭受网络攻击和信息战，而这些攻击和信息战还不足以引发冲突。

Mr Putin will threaten NATO in this way, because he has come to believe that NATO threatens Russia and its people. Speaking earlier this week, he raged at the alliance’s eastward expansion. Later, he decried a fictitious “genocide” that he says the West is sponsoring in Ukraine. Mr Putin can’t tell his people that his army is fighting against their Ukrainian brothers and sisters who gained freedom. So he is telling them that Russia is at war with America, NATO and its proxies.

普京将以这种方式威胁北约，因为他已经开始相信北约威胁着俄罗斯及其人民。本周早些时候，他对北约东扩表示愤怒。后来，他谴责了一个虚构的“种族灭绝”，他说西方在乌克兰支持这种种族灭绝。普京不能告诉他的人民，他的军队正在与获得自由的乌克兰兄弟姐妹作战。所以他告诉他们，俄罗斯正与美国、北约及其代理人交战。

The abominable truth is that Mr Putin has launched an unprovoked assault on the sovereign country next door. He is obsessed with the defensive alliance to its west. And he is trampling the principles that underpin peace in the 21st century. That is why the world must inflict a heavy price for his aggression.

令人憎恶的事实是，普京对邻国这个主权国家发动了无端攻击。他痴迷于西方的防御联盟。他践踏了支撑21世纪和平的原则。这就是为什么世界必须为他的侵略付出沉重代价的原因。

This starts with massive punitive sanctions against Russia’s financial system, its high-tech industries and its moneyed elite. Just before the invasion, when Russia recognised the two republics, the West had imposed only modest sanctions. It must not hesitate now. Even though Russia has set out to build a fortress economy, the country is still connected to the world and, as the initial 45% fall in Russia’s stockmarket suggests, it will suffer.

首先要对俄罗斯的金融体系、高科技产业和富有的精英阶层实施大规模的惩罚性制裁。就在入侵前，当俄罗斯承认这两个共和国时，西方只施加了适度的制裁。它现在不能再犹豫了。尽管俄罗斯已经开始建立堡垒经济，但这个国家仍然与世界联系在一起，正如俄罗斯股市最初45%的下跌所表明的那样，它将遭受损失。

True, sanctions will harm the West, too. Oil prices soared above $100 a barrel on the invasion. Russia is Europe’s main supplier of gas. It exports metals like nickel and palladium and along with Ukraine it exports wheat. All of that will present problems at a time when the world economy is struggling with inflation and supply-chain glitches. And yet, by the same measure, the fact that the West is prepared to suffer for sanctions sends Mr Putin the message that it cares about his transgressions.

诚然，制裁也会损害西方。俄罗斯入侵之后，石油价格飙升至每桶100美元以上。俄罗斯是欧洲主要的天然气供应国。它出口镍和钯等金属，并与乌克兰一道出口小麦。在全球经济正与通胀和供应链阻塞问题作斗争之际，所有这些都将带来问题。然而，以同样的标准衡量，西方准备承受制裁的事实向普京传递了这样一个信息:它们在意普京的越权行为。

A second task is to reinforce NATO’s eastern flank. Until now, the alliance has sought to live within the pact signed with Russia in 1997, which limits NATO operations in the former Soviet bloc. NATO should rip it up and use the freedoms that creates to garrison troops in the east. That will take time. Meanwhile NATO should prove its unity and intent by immediately deploying its 40,000-strong rapid-reaction force to the frontline states. These troops will add credibility to its doctrine that an attack on one member is an attack on all. They will also signal to Mr Putin that the further he pushes in Ukraine, the more likely he is to end up strengthening NATO’s presence on its border—the very opposite of what he intends.

第二项任务是加强北约的东翼。到目前为止，北约一直寻求遵守1997年与俄罗斯签署的协议，该协议限制了北约在前苏联地区的行动。北约应该将其撕毁，并利用由此带来的自由在东部驻军。这需要时间。与此同时，北约应该立即向前线国家部署其4万多名快速反应部队，以证明其团结和意图。这些部队将增加其信条的可信性，对一个成员的攻击就是对所有成员的攻击。他们也会向普京发出信号:他在乌克兰的行动越深入，他就越有可能加强北约在其边境的存在，这与他的意图恰恰相反。

And the world should help Ukraine defend itself and its people. They will bear the burden of the suffering. Only hours into the war came the first reports of military and civilian deaths. Volodymyr Zelensky, the president, called on his compatriots to resist. They must choose how and where to repel Mr Putin and his armies and proxies, should he install a puppet government in Kyiv. NATO is not about to deploy troops to Ukraine—rightly so, for fear of a confrontation between nuclear powers. But its members should give Ukraine assistance by providing arms, money and shelter to refugees and, if need be, a government in exile.

世界应该帮助乌克兰保卫自己国家和人民。他们将承受痛苦的负担。战争刚刚开始几个小时，就出现了第一批军人和平民死亡的报道。乌克兰总统Volodymyr Zelensky号召他的同胞们反抗。如果普京在基辅建立一个傀儡政府，他们必须选择如何以及在哪里击退普京及其军队和代理人。由于担心核大国之间的对抗，北约不会向乌克兰部署军队，这是正确的。但其成员国应向乌克兰提供援助，为难民提供武器、资金和庇护所，必要时还应组建流亡政府。

Some will say that it is too risky to challenge Mr Putin in these ways—because he has lost touch with reality, or because he will escalate, miscalculate or hug XX. That would itself be a miscalculation. After 22 years at the top, even a dictator with an overdeveloped sense of his own destiny has a nose for survival and the ebb and flow of power. Many Russians, unclear about a crisis that has come from nowhere, may be unenthusiastic about waging a deadly war against their brothers and sisters in Ukraine. That is something the West can exploit.

有些人会说，用这些方式挑战普京太冒险了，因为他已经脱离了现实，或者因为他会升级、误判或拥抱XX。这本身就是一种误判。在掌权22年之后，即使是一个对自己命运过于敏感的独裁者，也对生存和权力的涨落有着敏锐的嗅觉。许多俄罗斯人不清楚这场不知从何而来的危机，他们可能对自己在乌克兰的兄弟姐妹发动一场致命的战争不感兴趣。这是西方可以利用的。

Accommodating Mr Putin in the hope that he will start to behave nicely would be more dangerous still. Even XX should see that a man who rampages across frontiers is a threat to the stability it seeks. The freer Mr Putin is to advance today, the more determined he will be to impose his vision tomorrow. And the more blood will be spilled in finally getting him to stop. ■

迁就普京，希望他能开始表现得好一点，这将更加危险。就连XX也应该看到，一个越境横行的人对其寻求的稳定是一种威胁。普京今天越是自由地前进，他明天就越有决心将自己的愿景强加于人。更多的鲜血最终会让他停下来。■
