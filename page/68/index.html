Despite high-profile failures, government tech is slowly improving

high-profile 广受关注的

尽管出现了引人注目的失败，但政府的技术正在缓慢改善

Software and the state

软件和政府

Efforts to fix a system that has been on the blink

on the blink 出毛病

努力修复一个一直出毛病的系统

Mar 18th 2021

IT HAS BEEN a bad year for government software. Early in the pandemic, unemployment-insurance systems failed, preventing many jobless from signing up for benefits. Glitchy vaccine-registration software in Pennsylvania, New Jersey and Florida slowed roll-out. Deloitte, a consultancy, was paid more than $40m by the Centres for Disease Control to produce a buggy vaccine-data system. In February whistleblowers reported that prison-administration software was keeping inmates in Arizona locked up past their release dates.

Glitchy （不断）有小故障的，（总是）出小问题的

[释义] often experiencing small technical problems or faults

buggy（计算机程序）因为有太多错误而无法正常工作的

[释义] Computer programs that are buggy contain mistakes and do no work correctly.

buggy code

错误很多的代码

whistle-blower 告密者

inmate 同狱犯人

lock up 关押

对于政府软件来说，今年是糟糕的一年。在大流行早期，失业保险系统失败了，许多失业者无法申请失业救济。宾夕法尼亚州、新泽西州和佛罗里达州不断有小故障的疫苗注册软件延缓了疫苗的推广。疾病控制中心向咨询公司德勤(Deloitte)支付了4000多万美元，开发了一个错误很多的疫苗数据系统。2月份，举报人报告称，亚利桑那州的监狱管理软件使囚犯在释放日期过后仍被关押。

It is not simply that the pandemic has put a strain on harried government boffins: an oft-cited report by Standish Group, an advisory firm, found that only 13% of large government software projects succeed. The roll-out of America’s online health-care marketplace became a national joke. Hackers stole the records of more than 20m federal personnel. A pricey intelligence system was so awful that paratroopers in Afghanistan begged higher-ups to switch. Such foul-ups are the rule.

boffin 科研工作者

paratrooper 伞兵

higher-up 上级

foul-up 混乱

a computer system foul-up 计算机系统故障

这不仅仅是因为大流行给饱受困扰的政府研究人员带来了压力:咨询公司Standish Group经常引用的一份报告发现，只有13%的大型政府软件项目获得了成功。美国医疗保健在线市场的推出成了全国的笑柄。黑客窃取了2000多万名联邦工作人员的记录。一个昂贵的情报系统太可怕了，以至于在阿富汗的伞兵乞求上级调换。这样的失误是常事。

Procurement is the first problem. The way government agencies buy things is so complicated that only a few big companies understand the process. Some firms continue to get state and federal contracts even after delivering error-prone systems at fantastic cost. New York chose Xerox to modernise its Medicaid-claims system even though the company had already been sued by Alaska and Texas for allegedly botching similar efforts.

procurement 采购

Medicaid 医疗救助制度

botch 笨拙地弄糟

采购是第一个问题。政府机构购买东西的方式非常复杂，只有少数大公司了解这个过程。一些公司在以惊人的成本交付了容易出错的系统后，仍继续获得州和联邦政府的合同。尽管施乐公司已经被阿拉斯加州和德克萨斯州指控在类似的活动中搞砸了，但纽约还是选择施乐来使其医疗补助理赔系统现代化。

Poorly written contract solicitations make things worse. Requirements are often so restrictive that many eligible firms are instantly disqualified. Contracts may contain no provision for testing. Deloitte’s vaccine-management system might have benefited from more testing: it doesn’t work on some web browsers, and important buttons are hidden on a smartphone.

solicitation 请求

eligible 有资格的；合意的

disqualify 取消..的资格

糟糕的合同招标会让事情变得更糟。要求往往是非常严格，以至于许多符合条件的公司会立即被取消资格。合同中可能不包含检测条款。德勤的疫苗管理系统可能会从更多的测试中受益:它在一些网络浏览器上不起作用，而且在智能手机上一些重要的按钮隐藏了。

Many agencies lack the expertise to ensure that complex software projects run smoothly. A government report found that an opaque, drawn-out hiring process was a significant barrier to getting tech talent. Agencies can be penny-wise and pound-foolish, allocating plenty of money for tech contracts but not enough for the staff required to ensure projects are a success.

drawn-out 持续很久的；拖长的

[释义] lasting longer than is usual or necessary

be penny-wise and pound-foolish 小钱仔细，大钱挥霍；大处不算小处算

[释义] to be extremely careful about small amounts of money and not careful enough about larger amounts of money

许多机构缺乏确保复杂软件项目顺利运行的专业知识。一份政府报告发现，不透明、冗长的招聘过程是获取科技人才的一个重大障碍。代理机构可能贪小便宜，吃大亏，为技术合同分配了大量资金，但却没有足够的资金提供给能够确保项目成功的员工。

Bureaucracy doesn’t help. Staffers may want to take advantage of modern technology, but fear falling foul of old rules. During the pandemic, unemployment-insurance and vaccine-registration systems were crushed under the weight of traffic that would have been easily handled by cloud-computing infrastructure. Little thought goes into user experience. In 2018 the poor design of a software interface led a state employee to press the wrong button, issuing a warning that a ballistic missile was incoming to Hawaii residents, who scrambled to text goodbyes to loved ones.

to fall or run foul of sth/sb 与某事/某人发生冲突

to fall foul of the law 触犯法律

ballistic 弹道的

missile 导弹

官僚机构没有帮助。员工们可能希望利用现代技术，但又担心与旧规则发生冲突。在大流行期间，失业保险和疫苗登记系统在原本可以通过云计算基础设施轻松处理的流量压力下崩溃。他们很少考虑用户体验。2018年，由于软件界面的糟糕设计，一名州政府工作人员按错了按钮，发出警告说一枚弹道导弹正在向夏威夷居民飞来，导致居民们纷纷用短信向亲人道别。

Change is under way. After the failed roll-out of Healthcare.gov, the American government set up 18F, an internal technology consultancy that has created sleek new digital tools, and the US Digital Service (USDS), which deploys tech whizzes to multi-year “tours” in government. Technology Transformation Services, a unit dedicated to improving government tech, has created digital.gov, which provides resources to improve tech literacy across the government. USDS has pioneered SME-QA, a promising new way to hire software developers and other experts.

underway 正在进行的；正在发生的

whizz 行家

literacy 专业知识

变化正在发生。在Healthcare.gov网站推出失败后，美国政府成立了内部技术咨询公司18F和美国数字服务公司(US digital Service, USDS)，前者创建了时髦的新型数字工具，后者将技术专家派到政府部门进行多年“任期”。Technology Transformation Services是一个致力于提高政府技术水平的部门，它创建了digital.gov，为提高政府的技术素养提供资源。USDS开创了SME-QA，这是一种雇用软件开发人员和其他专家的有前途的新方法。

Mark Lerner, a civic-tech expert, advocates modular contracting—short, bite-sized contracts that are easy to replicate. Spreading tech know-how among contracting officers can stop government from buying lemons. “We need to be building systems”, says Amanda Miklik, a former USDS director of design, “to support contracting officers in being able to sniff out bullshit.” And civic-tech experts agree that government must take user research seriously: the goals of new software must be well defined before contracts are awarded.

civic 市政的

know-how 实际知识

lemon 有毛病的东西；不成功的东西

[释义] something that does not work

sniff out 发现

市政技术专家Mark Lerner提倡模块化合同——短量、小量的合同，很容易复制。在合同管理人员中传播技术知识可以阻止政府买到不成功的东西。前美国国防部设计总监Amanda Miklik说“我们需要建立系统，以支持合同签订官员能够发现哄骗。” 市政技术专家也同意，政府必须认真对待用户研究:在签订合同之前，必须明确新软件的目标。

A new opportunity arose on March 10th, when the House passed a $1.9trn stimulus package. The bill includes $1bn for the Technology Modernisation Fund (TMF), a vehicle intended to improve federal tech. The hefty sum, a 40-fold increase over TMF’s 2020 figure, is a signal that Congress and the Biden administration take IT modernisation seriously. But unless the government improves its ways, the money could all too easily be wasted. ■

vehicle 手段

sum 款项

3月10日，众议院通过了一项1.9万亿美元的经济刺激计划，一个新的机会出现了。该法案包括10亿美元用于技术现代化基金(TMF)，这是一个旨在改善联邦技术的工具。这笔巨额资金是TMF 2020年数字的40倍，表明国会和拜登政府认真对待IT现代化。但是，除非政府改进自己的方式，否则这些钱很容易就会被浪费掉。■

Italy is central to the bull case for euro-zone stockmarkets

意大利是欧元区股市牛市的核心

Buttonwood 梧桐

![image-20210325094639866](/Users/chenyujie/Library/Application Support/typora-user-images/image-20210325094639866.png)

Might there be a Draghi effect?

可能存在德拉吉效应?

Mar 18th 2021

PITY THE broker of European shares. He spends his afternoons making calls to investors in America. Few are returned. His clients are tired of Europe and its vaccine snafus. Even a good-news story, such as Mario Draghi becoming prime minister of Italy, has little traction. Italy is central to the fortunes of the euro zone. Mr Draghi is a reformer to be reckoned with. But a lot of people think the country cannot be fixed. “If God descended from heaven and became Italy’s prime minister,” says a hedge-fund boss, “the market still would not rally.”

snafu 混乱局面

reckon with 重视

She’s a person to be reckoned with 她这个人不可小觑

be a force to be reckoned with 是一股不可小看的力量

rally 止跌回升

可怜的欧洲股票经纪人。他下午都在给美国的投资者打电话。很少有人回电。他的客户厌倦了欧洲和它的疫苗混乱。即使是好消息，比如马里奥•德拉吉(Mario Draghi)成为意大利总理，也没什么吸引力。意大利是欧元区财富的中心。德拉吉是一位不容小觑的改革者。但很多人认为这个国家无法修复。“如果上帝从天堂降临并成为意大利总理，”一位对冲基金老板说，“市场仍然不会反弹。”

The story of 2021 is that America’s economy is reopening with a bang. By comparison, everything in Europe looks sluggish: GDP growth, the roll-out of vaccines, the deployment of fiscal policy, slow-moving companies, and so on. That lack of urgency extends to the stockmarket. Europe has no FOMO (fear of missing out), says Graham Secker of Morgan Stanley, a bank. It is wholly lacking in the froth so evident in America. Yet that is probably a good thing. Because—whisper it—so far this year the euro-zone stockmarket has managed to keep up with America’s.

sluggish 疲软的

miss out 错过机会

wholly 完全；全面地

2021年的故事是，美国经济轰然重开。相比之下，欧洲的一切都显得迟缓:GDP增长，疫苗的推广，财政政策的部署，行动迟缓的公司，等等。这种缺乏紧迫感的情况延伸到了股市。摩根士丹利银行的Graham Secker说，欧洲没有FOMO(害怕错过机会)。它完全缺乏在美国如此明显的泡沫。然而，这可能是一件好事。因为（悄声说）今年到目前为止，欧元区股市已经设法跟上了美国股市的步伐。

Among non-US stocks, the euro area looks attractive on a price-to-earnings basis, says Mr Secker. The perennial appeal of Europe is that it is cheap. That is not always, or even usually, decisive in stock investing. (Cheap for a reason, sneer the sceptics.) But since November, when news first broke of an effective vaccine for covid-19, “value” stocks (ie, those with a low price relative to earnings or book value) have generally done better than “growth” stocks, their antithesis. This rotation towards value ought to favour Europe, where bourses are crammed with cheap-looking banks, widget-makers and commodity firms, the sort that tend to do well in periods of reflation, such as now.

perennial 长期的

antithesis 对立物

reflation 通货再膨胀

Secker表示，在非美国股票中，按市盈率计算，欧元区看起来颇具吸引力。欧洲长久以来的吸引力在于价格便宜。在股票投资中，这并不总是，甚至通常不是决定性的。(怀疑者嘲笑说，便宜是有原因的。)但自去年11月新冠病毒有效疫苗的消息首次传出以来，“价值型”股票(即股价相对于收益或账面价值较低的股票)的表现普遍好于与之相反的“增长型”股票。这种向价值的转变应该有利于欧洲，那里的交易所充斥着看似廉价的银行、小部件制造商和大宗商品公司，这些公司往往在通货再膨胀时期表现良好，比如现在。

Investors looking for deep value could do worse than consider Italy. In any event, if you take a punt on the euro zone, you are in effect taking a punt on Italy. Its stockmarket is more European than most. It is chock-full of energy and financial companies, the kind that have been out of favour with investors until recently, and of which Europe has lots. But this is about more than just the make-up of its stockmarket. Italy is the weakest big economy in continental Europe, itself a locus of weakness. If Italy can do better, then there is upside for the region as a whole.

in any event 无论如何

punt 打赌

chock-full 塞满的

upside 积极方面

寻求深度价值的投资者可能不如考虑意大利。无论如何，如果你押注于欧元区，你实际上就是在押注于意大利。它的股票市场比大多数国家都更欧洲化。这里到处都是能源和金融公司，这类公司直到最近才受到投资者的青睐，而欧洲有很多这样的公司。但这不仅仅是股票市场的组成问题。意大利是欧洲大陆最弱的大型经济体，其自身也是一个弱点所在。如果意大利能做得更好，那么对整个地区都有好处。

Enter Mr Draghi. He is not quite a deity, though he came close to being one in his euro-saving eight-year stint as boss of the European Central Bank. Last month he was asked by Italy’s president to form a government. Parliament quickly fell in behind him. He has since set out some broad reform goals: to fix Italy’s tax code, its sclerotic bureaucracy and its sluggish courts. Each is a bar to enterprise and a stronger economy. Mr Draghi has picked the right spots and is the right man to tackle them. But after an early burst of euphoria in Italy’s markets, doubts have crept in. A big one is that his time as prime minister is likely to be short-lived. There is also the question of whether a few bits of legislation can make a difference. Italy is a low-trust economy. This is reflected in the ubiquity of small family firms. An array of restrictive practices is hard-wired into its society. Changing all this quickly is not easy.

deity 神

stint 规定工作时间

fall in 站队

set out 陈述

euphoria 狂喜

creep in 不知不觉产生

ubiquity 普遍存在的

hardwired 本能的（指不可改变的）

[释义] If someone or something is hardwired to do a particular thing, they automatically do it and cannot change that behaviour.

德拉吉加入。尽管他在担任欧洲央行行长的8年欧元储备工作中差点成为神，他并不是一个真正的神。上个月，意大利总统要求他组建政府。议会很快就支持了他。自那以后，他提出了一些广泛的改革目标:修复意大利的税法、僵化的官僚体系和迟缓的法庭。每一种都是企业发展和经济发展的障碍。德拉吉选择了正确的地点，也是解决这些问题的合适人选。但是，在意大利市场经历了早期的一阵狂喜之后，质疑之声悄然而至。最大的一个问题是，他的首相任期很可能是短暂的。还有一个问题是，几项立法能否带来改变。意大利是一个信任度较低的经济体。这反映在无处不在的小型家族企业上。一系列限制性的做法已经深深融入了它的社会。迅速改变这一切并不容易。

Still, you have to start somewhere. A big problem is the difficulty of making business agreements stick. It takes more than twice as long to enforce a contract in Italy than in France or Germany. A better functioning judicial system is the foundation on which a broader construct of trust might be built. Italy’s tax system is complex and discourages job creation. A little judicious reform might go a long way in improving incentives to work. And in his efforts to cut red tape, Mr Draghi can already point to progress. On March 10th Italy’s three largest union federations signed a pact agreeing to reforms of the government bureaucracy. This is a small start. But it speaks to a shift in the mood in Italy.

discourage 使灰心；阻拦

speak 有意义

不过，你还是得从某个地方开始。一个大问题是很难使商业协议生效。在意大利执行一份合同所需的时间是法国或德国的两倍多。一个运作更好的司法系统是建立更广泛的信任架构的基础。意大利的税收体系很复杂，不利于创造就业机会。一个审慎的改革可能会大大提高工作的积极性。在努力减少繁文缛节的过程中，德拉吉已经取得了进展。3月10日，意大利最大的三个工会联合会签署了一项协议，同意对政府的官僚体制进行改革。这是一个小小的开始。但这表明意大利的情绪发生了转变。

Mr Draghi has the advantage of being able to offer goodies in return for painful changes. Italy will be the largest beneficiary of the €750bn ($900bn) EU recovery fund, the disbursements of which are tied to progress on reforms. This is not quite Joe Biden’s $1.9trn mega-package but it is not nothing either. A lesson from the 1990s, when Italy strove to qualify for the euro, is that reforms can happen if Italians can see a payoff from them. This is a hard story for a broker to sell given the latest vaccine mishaps. Europe is the opposite of a meme stock. But right now, that might actually be a plus.

goody 好东西

disbursement 支出款额

关于memo stock背景: Some of the common characteristics meme stocks share are they’re usually overpriced and experience spikes of rapid growth in short spaces of time. Popular amongst millennials, they are prone to high volatility with valuations based around potential rather than financials — or in GameStop’s case, not potential at all but simply taking advantage of the system. Usually, the sentiment around the stock is positioned around the future problem it solves, with talk of valuations very low down the list and usually only proposed by bears. FOMO is a big motivator to buy, while panic-selling at the slightest headwind is common, adding to the stock’s volatility.

德拉吉的优势在于，他能够用好处来换取痛苦的改变。意大利将成为7500亿欧元(合9000亿美元)欧盟复苏基金(EU recovery fund)的最大受益者，该基金的支出与改革进展挂钩。这与乔•拜登(Joe Biden) 1.9万亿美元的一揽子计划不太一样，但也不是什么都没有。上世纪90年代意大利努力争取加入欧元区的经验教训是，如果意大利人能看到改革带来的回报，改革就会发生。考虑到最近的疫苗事故，这对经纪人来说是一个艰难的故事。欧洲是模因股票的对立面。但现在，这可能是件好事。

The pandemic has changed the shape of global happiness

疫情改变了全球幸福的形态

It might seem crazy

这可能看起来很疯狂

The old have become happier and the young more miserable

老年人越来越快乐，年轻人越来越痛苦

Mar 20th 2021

THE COVID-19 pandemic has done nothing good for the mood of Park Ha-young, an undergraduate at Seoul National University. She spent much of last year worrying about the disease, and her chances of spreading it: “I was terrified of becoming the person to cause a huge outbreak.” Her freedom has been drastically curtailed. The government determines whether she can see friends or attend classes, leaving her frustrated and unable to make plans. She is beginning to worry about finding a job after she graduates.

COVID-19对首尔国立大学(Seoul National University)本科生Park Ha-young的情绪没有任何好处。去年的大部分时间，她都在担心这种疾病，担心她被传播的可能性:“我很害怕成为引发大规模疫情的人。”她的自由受到了极大的限制。政府决定了她是否可以见朋友或去上课，这让她感到沮丧，无法制定计划。她开始为毕业后找工作而发愁。

Politicians and officials frequently talk about how covid-19 affects public health and the economy. But for most people those are abstract considerations. What they experience each day are moods—the sense of being anxious and sad, or, if they are lucky, cheerful and optimistic. To mark World Happiness Day on March 20th, researchers linked to the UN Sustainable Development Solutions Network have tried to pin down these moods and examine how the pandemic has changed them.

pin down 按住

政客和官员经常谈论covid-19如何影响公共卫生和经济。但对大多数人来说，这些都是抽象的考虑。他们每天所经历的都是焦虑和悲伤的情绪，或者，如果他们幸运的话，是快乐和乐观的情绪。为了纪念3月20日的世界幸福日，联合国可持续发展解决方案网络的研究人员试图确定这些情绪，并研究疫情是如何改变它们的。

Gallup, a pollster, asks the same questions in scores of countries. The most revealing one tells people to imagine a ladder, with steps numbered nought to ten. The top rung represents the best life you could have, the bottom rung represents the worst. What rung are you on now?

THE E-COMMERCE company that retailers talk about most these days is neither Amazon, the American juggernaut, nor Alibaba, China’s biggest. It is Pinduoduo (PDD), a Chinese firm that started in 2015 as an online food supplier, but whose success has driven its market value above $200bn. 如今，零售商们谈论最多的电子商务公司既不是美国巨头亚马逊(Amazon)，也不是中国最大的电子商务公司阿里巴巴(Alibaba)。这就是拼多多(PDD)，一家成立于2015年的中国在线食品供应商，但它的成功使其市值超过2000亿美元。去年，它是中国增长最快的互联网股票，上涨了330%。

PDD attracts attention for two reasons. One is its business model. David Liu, vice-president of strategy, explains that it has ridden the rise of smartphone penetration in China to create an e-commerce experience in which people club together to buy products from robot vacuum-cleaners to bananas. During the pandemic this has expanded into a fast-growing business across thousands of towns and villages, in which PDD’s users gather to bid for shipments of local farm produce at bargain prices. Some term this “community group-buy”. Mr Liu calls it “interactive commerce”. It is one of the hottest parts of the Chinese internet.

Even after a period of mandated quarantine is over, the apps may not update to reflect that fact for days. Even so, they look likely to become a permanent fixture.

在中国，强制健康应用程序使用智能手机上的位置数据生成二维码，确定某人是否可以自由进入许多室内空间，是否可以不受限制地旅行。追踪数据似乎与警方共享。风险计算是一个黑匣子，代码似乎有问题。即使在一段强制隔离期结束后，这些应用程序可能几天内都不会更新以反映这一事实。即便如此，它们看起来仍有可能成为一种永久固定制度。

Incompetence and snooping could taint the whole idea of vaccine passports and provide grist to covid-conspiracists’ mill. But privacy worries are not insurmountable. David Chadwick, formerly a computer-science professor at Kent University, in England, is the boss of a spin-off company called Verifiable Credentials. Before the covid-19 pandemic, his firm was working on a privacy-focused scheme for workplace identity cards, parking permits, concert tickets and the like. “I wasn’t thinking about health applications at all,” he says. These days covid-19 is his priority.

snoop 窥探

taint 玷污

grist 有用材料

be grist to the mill 是有利的东西

insurmountable 无法克服的

formerly 以前

spin-off 分公司

无能和窥探可能会玷污疫苗护照的整个概念，而且对于新冠病毒阴谋论者是有利的东西。但对隐私的担忧并非无法克服。英国肯特大学的计算机科学教授大卫·查德威克是一家名为 Verifiable Credentials的分公司的老板。在covid-19大流行之前，他的公司正在研究一项以隐私为重点的计划，包括工作场所身份证、停车许可证、音乐会门票等。他说:“我根本没有考虑过医疗应用。”这些天，新冠肺炎是他的首要任务。

The idea is to ensure there is no connection between the source of a person’s vaccination data and the entity requesting it. Individual users are linked securely with their smartphones using biometrics and some form of government-issue identity document, a process similar to registering for mobile banking. A user seeking entrance to a “covid-secure” venue would have entry rules transmitted to their phone at the door. The app would check those rules against the user’s data and spit out a simple “yes” or “no”—and nothing else. Specifics such as a person’s name, age, address, the date of their vaccination and the like would not be reported, limiting the opportunity for mischief.

这样做是为了确保疫苗接种数据的来源和请求该数据的实体之间没有连接。个人用户可以通过生物识别技术和某种形式的政府颁发的身份证明，安全地与他们的智能手机相连，这一过程类似于手机银行的注册。寻求进入“新型冠状病毒安全”场所的用户，可以在门口将进入规则传输到他们的手机上。该应用程序将根据用户的数据检查这些规则，然后给出一个简单的“是”或“不是”——除此之外就没有别的了。诸如姓名、年龄、住址、接种疫苗日期等细节不会被报道，从而限制了恶作剧的机会。

In April 2020 Verifiable Credentials demonstrated that its prototype would be able to verify vaccine status and covid-test results, as soon as those things existed. Its app is being tested with dummy data at a cinema that actors are using as a rehearsal space, and with real data at a British hospital, where it has replaced existing paper-based methods. The firm is also working on a physical version for use by those without smartphones.

dummy 假的

2020年4月，Verifiable Credentials表明，其原型将能够验证疫苗状态和新冠病毒测试结果，只要这些东西存在。这款应用正在一家电影院（演员们用作排练场地）使用虚拟数据测试，并在一家英国医院（已经取代了现有的纸质方法）使用真实数据进行测试。该公司还在研发一款物理版，供非智能手机用户使用。

Even if privacy worries can be assuaged, public-health bodies fret about the perceived fairness of what vaccine passports would enable. Most countries have put the elderly at the head of the queue for vaccination, since they are most likely to die from covid-19. Passports raise the prospect that vaccinated pensioners will be allowed to roam freely, while the young—who have been confined to quarters largely to protect their elders—remain under lockdown.

assuage 缓解

pensioner 领养老金的人

即使隐私方面的担忧可以得到缓解，公共卫生机构也会对疫苗护照所能提供的公平性感到担忧。大多数国家将老年人排在接种疫苗队列的首位，因为他们最有可能死于covid-19。护照增加了一种可能性，即接种疫苗的退休人员将被允许自由走动，而年轻人（主要是为了保护他们的长辈而被限制在住所里）仍然处于封锁状态。

In some countries those worries may be heightened by racial implications. Black Americans are more dubious about vaccines than white ones, and some who want jabs find it harder to get them. They are also, on average, younger than their white compatriots, which means they are further back in the queue. When vaccine roll-outs are fast and free, and priorities are set justly and transparently, questions of equity will be transient. But in countries where politicians queue-jump or herd immunity is years away, they may cause resentment.

queue-jump 插队

在一些国家，这些担忧可能因种族问题而加剧。美国黑人比白人对疫苗更持怀疑态度，一些想要接种的人发现很难接种。平均而言，他们也比白人同胞年轻，这意味着他们排在队伍后面。当疫苗迅速免费推出时，优先次序公正透明地确定时，公平问题将是短暂的。但是，在那些政客们“插队”或“群体免疫”还需数年才能实现的国家，他们可能会招致不满。

And then there is the question of what to do with those who cannot or will not be vaccinated. Governments will be under pressure to grant exemptions, especially for medical contra-indications. But each unvaccinated person allowed into supposedly covid-safe spaces would make them less so. Another worry is that the unvaccinated could become less employable. A global survey by Manpower, a recruitment agency, published on March 9th found that a fifth of employers planned to start mandating vaccination for at least some roles, and another 14% were undecided. As soon as herd immunity has been reached, it makes little sense for employers to care about such matters—but some may, especially if customers keep asking. That could make vaccines close to compulsory.

接下来的问题是，如何处理那些不能或不愿意接种疫苗的人。各国政府将在压力下给予豁免，特别是医疗禁忌症。但如果每个未接种疫苗的人被允许进入本应对新冠病毒安全的空间，他们就会变得不那么安全。另一个担忧是，未接种疫苗的人可能会变得不那么适合就业。招聘机构万宝盛华(Manpower)在3月9日发布的一项全球调查显示，五分之一的雇主计划至少在某些职位上强制接种疫苗，另有14%的雇主尚未决定。一旦达到群体免疫，雇主关心这些事情就没有什么意义了——但有些雇主可能会关心，尤其是当顾客不断询问的时候。这将使疫苗接近强制性。

The most fundamental criticism is that it remains unclear whether vaccine passports will even do the job they are supposed to. On February 5th a paper from the World Health Organisation (WHO) argued that vaccinated people should not be exempt from lockdown and quarantine rules. It said that using vaccine passports for border crossings would be “premature” (though it is so sure this is imminent that it is nevertheless drawing up suggestions for how best to do it). On February 17th the Ada Lovelace Institute, a think-tank that is tracking proposals for vaccine passports globally, concluded that they are “not currently justified”.

premature 仓促的

最根本的批评是，目前尚不清楚疫苗护照是否能发挥应有的作用。2月5日，世界卫生组织(WHO)的一份报告指出，接种过疫苗的人不应该免于封锁和隔离规定。它说，在过境时使用疫苗护照是“不成熟的” (尽管它很确定这是迫在眉睫的，因此它正在起草建议如何最好地做到这一点)。2月17日，追踪全球疫苗护照提案的智库Ada Lovelace研究所得出结论，这些提案“目前还不合理”。

One reason is that, although existing vaccines seem very effective at preventing illness, it is not clear whether they completely prevent infection with the virus, or remove the ability to transmit it to others. (One paper published in June, before any vaccines were available, estimated that more than a third of those infected with covid-19 display no symptoms but can still infect others.) There are some encouraging signs. A leaked draft of a paper put together by Pfizer and Israel’s health ministry suggests that receiving both doses of the Pfizer/BioNTech vaccine cuts asymptomatic cases of covid-19 by nearly 90%. Another paper, published by researchers at Cambridge University Hospitals NHS Foundation Trust, but not yet peer-reviewed, looked at asymptomatic health-care workers at a British hospital. It found that a single dose of the Pfizer/BioNTech vaccine cut asymptomatic cases by 75% after 12 days. But the evidence is not yet strong enough to convince wary public-health officials.

一个原因是，尽管现有的疫苗似乎在预防疾病方面非常有效，但尚不清楚它们是否能完全预防病毒感染，或消除病毒传播给他人的能力。(今年6月，在疫苗问世之前发表的一篇论文估计，超过三分之一的covid-19感染者没有表现出症状，但仍可感染他人。)有一些令人鼓舞的迹象。辉瑞(Pfizer)和以色列卫生部联合起草的一份泄露的文件草案显示，接受辉瑞/生物科技两剂疫苗可以将covid-19无症状病例减少近90%。剑桥大学医院NHS基金会信托的研究人员发表的另一篇论文(尚未经过同行评议)，研究了英国一家医院的无症状医护人员。研究发现，一剂辉瑞/生物科技公司的疫苗在12天后可以减少75%的无症状病例。但证据还不足以说服警惕的公共卫生官员。

Another reason is that mutations in SARS-CoV-2 mean that whatever conclusions are arrived at today might change in future. Scientists hope that existing vaccines should be able to deal with the variants of the virus that have arisen thus far. But a novel variant against which they are less effective could emerge at any time. New vaccines would almost certainly be developed swiftly. But until they were deployed—a much bigger job—passport systems would be useless.

另一个原因是，SARS-CoV-2的突变意味着，无论今天得出什么结论，未来都可能发生变化。科学家们希望现有的疫苗能够对付迄今为止出现的病毒变种。但是，一种对它们没有那么有效的新变种随时可能出现。几乎可以肯定的是，新疫苗将会迅速研制出来。但是在他们被部署之前(这是一个巨大的工作)，护照系统将是无用的。

A final point is that the usefulness of a vaccine-passport system is inversely related to how quickly a country can vaccinate its citizens. Early in a vaccination programme, few people would benefit. Towards the end, the passports would be of little help. In countries such as Israel, where vaccination is proceeding rapidly, the span of time during which passports are useful could prove quite short. In those countries where vaccine roll-outs are slow, it may be needed as a crutch for longer.

inversely 相反地

crutch 依靠

最后一点是，疫苗护照系统的效用与一个国家为其公民接种疫苗的速度成反比。在疫苗接种计划的早期，很少有人会受益。到最后，护照也不会有什么帮助。在诸如以色列这样的国家，疫苗接种正在迅速进行，护照发挥作用的时间可能会很短。在那些疫苗推广缓慢的国家，疫苗护照可能需要在更长的时间内作为支柱。

But precisely because countries are vaccinating at drastically different rates, covid passports could come into their own for international travel. Even as America, Britain, Israel and a few other countries sprint towards herd immunity, only 7% of EU citizens have had their first jab. In some poor countries vaccination is likely to continue until 2023 or 2024. Without a way to speed the vaccinated through airports, the world will remain locked down even if some individual countries do not.

sprint 冲刺

但正是由于各国接种疫苗的速度差异很大，新冠病毒护照可能会被用于国际旅行。尽管美国、英国、以色列和其他一些国家正在努力实现群体免疫，但只有7%的欧盟公民进行了首次接种。在一些贫穷国家，疫苗接种可能会持续到2023年或2024年。如果没有办法通过机场加速接种疫苗，即使某些国家不这样做，世界仍将处于封锁状态。

Many countries are therefore poised to incorporate vaccine passports into their entry rules, says Nick Careen of the International Air Transport Association (IATA), an airline-industry group. Ordinary people are desperate to see family and friends abroad, and to go on holiday. Within the EU, Greece is the strongest supporter of a bloc-wide vaccine passport. Before the pandemic, tourism accounted for a fifth of its GDP. Its hotel owners and restaurateurs hope that vaccinated tourists could help rescue their summer season.

航空业组织——国际航空运输协会(IATA)的尼克•凯林表示，许多国家正准备将疫苗护照纳入其入境规定。一般人都渴望在国外见到家人和朋友，渴望去度假。在欧盟内部，希腊是全欧盟疫苗护照的最坚定支持者。在大流行之前，旅游业占该国GDP的五分之一。酒店老板和餐馆老板希望接种疫苗的游客可以帮助拯救他们的夏季。

Several nations have already cobbled together systems designed to allow at least a bit of travel to continue. They require a negative covid-19 test before setting off, and quarantine upon arrival. These work in only the narrowest sense. Quarantine deters all but the most desperate (or footloose) travellers. They can be hard to manage. After Britain tightened its rules in January, passengers arriving at Heathrow, its biggest airport, took many socially un-distanced hours to traverse the queues. If vaccine passports lack standardised verification procedures, Mr Careen says, the result could be “total chaos”.

cobble together 草率制订

deter 防止

footloose 自由自在的

一些国家已经草率制订了一些系统，至少可以让旅行继续下去。他们出发前需要进行covid-19阴性检测，抵达后需要进行隔离。这些只能在最狭义的意义上起作用。除了最不顾一切(或最不受约束)的旅行者外，所有人都被隔离了。它们很难管理。英国在1月份收紧了相关规定后，抵达其最大机场希思罗机场的乘客要花很多时间在社交上不受距离限制的队伍中穿梭。Careen先生说，如果疫苗护照缺乏标准化的验证程序，结果可能是“完全混乱”。

Quick release 快速推出

IATA hopes its Travel Pass project will come to the rescue. Under development before the pandemic, it aimed to speed up airport transit by making use of biometric information and secure digital identifiers on passengers’ phones. It relies on Timatic, IATA’s database of visa and entry regulations, which is already used by travel agents, airlines and airports. On a normal pre-covid-19 day, the database needed updating a handful of times. At the height of the pandemic, as governments scrambled to keep out travellers from places with high infection rates and new variants, that spiked to above 200.

国际航空运输协会希望它的“旅行通行证”项目能够帮助解决这个问题。它在大流行之前就在开发中，目的是利用乘客手机上的生物特征信息和安全数字标识符，加快机场过境速度。它依靠的是国际航空运输协会的签证和入境法规数据库Timatic，该数据库已经被旅行社、航空公司和机场使用。在covid-19之前的正常日子里，数据库需要更新几次。在疫情最严重的时候，由于各国政府争相阻止来自高感染率和新变种地区的旅行者，这一数字飙升至200以上。

![image-20210317114927409](/Users/chenyujie/Library/Application Support/typora-user-images/image-20210317114927409.png)

Travel Pass is being tested as a stand-alone app and as a chunk of code that airlines can use in their own apps. Several, including Emirates, Etihad Airways and Gulf Air, have signed up to test it. Other bits of the travel industry, such as cruise lines and resorts, could use it too, says Mr Careen. He hopes that one silver lining of the pandemic might be to speed the arrival of seamless, document-free travel. In ordinary times, he says, that would have required a battery of trials and tests with many different governments. Instead, the pandemic has persuaded countries of the virtues of co-ordinated standards, “practically overnight”.

stand-alone 独立的

chunk 大块

Travel Pass正在作为一个独立的应用程序和一组代码进行测试，航空公司可以在自己的应用程序中使用。包括阿联酋航空(Emirates)、阿提哈德航空(Etihad Airways)和海湾航空(Gulf Air)在内的几家航空公司已经签约测试这一系统。旅游业的其他部分，如邮轮和度假村，也可以使用它，Careen先生说。他希望大流行带来的一线希望可能是加速不间断的(连续的)、无证件旅行的到来。他说，在平时，这需要与许多不同的政府进行一系列的试验和测试。相反，这场大流行“几乎在一夜之间”就使各国相信了协调一致的标准的好处。

Making passports work internationally, though, will be even harder than making them work within countries. IATA says that testing laboratories and health-care providers will have to be certified, as travel agents currently are. Vaccination will take longest in poor countries, here such verification will also be hardest. Incentives to cheat will be high. Europol, the EU’s police agency, says fake covid-test certificates have already started to turn up at borders.

turn up 发现

然而，使护照在国际上有效使用比在国内有效使用更加困难。国际航空运输协会表示，检测实验室和医疗机构必须获得认证，就像目前的旅行社一样。在贫穷国家接种疫苗需要的时间最长，在这里这样的验证也将是最困难的。作弊的动机将会很高。欧盟警察机构欧洲刑警组织(Europol)表示，伪造的新冠病毒测试证书已经开始出现在边境。

And some of the trade-offs visible inside countries are even starker when considered between them. One is between lowering infection rates and raising economic activity. Vaccinated British holidaymakers visiting Greek beaches will need locals to pour their retsina. Unvaccinated hospitality workers brought out of furlough will catch the disease from each other, if not from visitors.

hospitality 款待

当考虑到国家之间的关系时，一些在国家内部可见的权衡甚至更加明显。一个是在降低感染率和提高经济活动之间。接种了疫苗的英国度假者到希腊海滩旅游时，需要当地人给他们倒上松香酒。休假回来的未接种疫苗的接待人员如果不是从游客那里传染上病毒的话，他们也会互相传染。

The world’s poorest countries will have to choose between tourist cash and social mixing, on the one hand, and higher rates of infection, sickness and death, on the other—and not just this year, but for several years to come. “If you rely on tourism, you need to be really honest with your citizens about those additional health risks,” says Elliot Jones of the Ada Lovelace Institute. “There is a case for modelling the trade-offs, and asking people which ones they’re OK with.”

一方面，世界上最贫穷的国家将不得不在旅游收入和社会融合之间作出选择，另一方面，不仅是今年，而且是今后几年，还必须在更高的感染率、疾病率和死亡率之间作出选择。“如果你依赖旅游业，你需要真正诚实地告诉你的公民这些额外的健康风险，”艾达·洛芙莱斯研究所的埃利奥特·琼斯(Elliot Jones)说。“我们有理由做个权衡模型，并询问人们他们对哪一个比较满意。”

Edgar Whitley, a researcher in digital identity and privacy at the London School of Economics, agrees. When big new policy problems arise, he says, governments are attracted by technical fixes that promise a speedy return to the status quo ante. He thinks they would do better to eschew such “techno-naivety” and instead focus on clearer communication regarding risks, and on measures that would enable gradual reopening for everyone as the number of infections falls.

eschew 避开

naivety 天真

伦敦政治经济学院(London School of Economics)数字身份与隐私研究员埃德加•惠特利(Edgar Whitley)对此表示赞同。他说，当新的重大政策问题出现时，政府会被承诺迅速恢复原状的技术解决方案所吸引。他认为，他们最好避免这种“技术幼稚”，而将重点放在关于风险的更清晰的沟通上，以及随着感染人数的下降，能够逐步对所有人重新开放的措施上。

Perhaps the biggest unknown of vaccine passports will be the psychological impact they have. After a year in which few people have crossed a border, and some have barely left home, many may have become more risk-averse. Would a scheme that liberates vaccinated people to mingle with each other provide valuable, though temporary, reassurance on the road to herd immunity? Or would it slow down the return to normality by suggesting to the newly fearful that their fellow citizens are a permanent threat?■

也许疫苗护照最大的未知将是它们所带来的心理影响。一年来，几乎没有人越过边境，有些人几乎没有离开过家，许多人可能变得更加不愿冒险。一个让接种过疫苗的人相互接触的计划会在实现群体免疫的道路上提供有价值的(虽然是暂时的)保证吗?或者，它会通过向刚刚感到恐惧的人暗示，他们的同胞是永久的威胁，从而减缓回归正常的进程吗？
