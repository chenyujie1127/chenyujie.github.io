The pandemic has refashioned corporate dress codes

疫情改变了公司的着装要求

Bartleby

巴托比

![image-20210912213058094](/Users/chenyujie/Library/Application Support/typora-user-images/image-20210912213058094.png)

Suits v sweatpants

西装vs运动裤

IN AN INTERNAL memo to staff in 2016 JPMorgan Chase relaxed its dress code. The American bank’s 240,000 employees could hang up their suits and don business-casual attire—once reserved for casual Fridays—all working week. Some garments remained beyond the pale (T-shirts, flip-flops, tank tops, yoga pants). But many—polo shirts, skirts (of appropriate length), dress sandals—became fair game.

don 穿上

在2016年发给员工的一份内部通知中，摩根大通放宽了着装要求。这家美国银行的24万名员工可以在工作日挂起西装，穿上商务休闲装，以前只有星期五才穿便装。一些服装依然有些出格，如T恤、人字拖、背心、瑜伽裤。但是很多款式都成了合适的对象，如polo衫、(长度合适的)裙子、凉鞋。

JPMorgan was, sartorially speaking, ahead of its time among stuffy corporate giants (turtlenecks and hoodies have long been the fashion choice of Silicon Valley titans). Others followed suit, as it were. Men’s corporate uniform—and the female power suit designed to mirror it—increasingly came to be seen as a vestige of the male-dominated offices of yore and no longer fit for purpose in a world of greater (though still imperfect) workplace equality. As more and more people ran or cycled to work, they found that changing into a full suit was impractical, since jackets folded into rucksacks tend to lose their crispness.

stuffy 古板的

turtleneck 高圆翻领；高领套头衫

hoody/hoodie 兜帽上衣

vestige 残留部分；丝毫

rucksack 帆布背包

从着装上讲，摩根大通在古板的企业巨头中走在了时代的前面(高领毛衣和连帽衫一直是硅谷巨头的时尚选择)。可以说，其他人也纷纷效仿。男性的公司制服，以及为反映女性权利而设计的制服套装越来越被视为过去男性主导的办公室的“残迹”，不再适合在更大（尽管仍不完善）的平等的工作场所世界。随着越来越多的人跑步或骑自行车去上班，他们发现换上全套西装是不现实的，因为折叠在背包里的夹克往往会失去原有的挺括感。

These days ties are no longer de rigueur in client meetings even for pinstriped investment bankers at Goldman Sachs. Purveyors of formal wear have fallen on hard times. Last year Brooks Brothers, which had been sewing button-down shirts since 1818, filed for bankruptcy. Last month Marks & Spencer, a British retailer, announced it would no longer sell men’s suits in more than half of its bigger stores.

de rigueur 社交礼节上需要的

pinstripe 有细条纹的；细条纹；细条纹西装

button-down 领尖有纽扣的

如今，即便是高盛的“穿着细条纹西装的”投资银行家，在与客户会面时也不再戴领带。正装提供商陷入困境。去年，自1818年以来一直缝制纽扣领衬衫的Brooks Brothers申请破产。上个月，英国零售商Marks & Spencer宣布，其半数以上的大型门店将不再销售男士西装。

As the pandemic completely decoupled work and presence in the office, employees at many companies switched into something even less starchy. Unlike JPMorgan, however, most have not put any guidelines in place as to what is and isn’t appropriate. Although the Delta variant is forcing companies to delay a return to the office, that day will come. When workers are back at their desks, at least some of the time, new sartorial rules may be required.

sartorial 男装的; 缝纫的

随着疫情让工作和办公完全脱钩，许多公司的员工换了更不刻板的服装工作。不过与摩根大通不同的是，多数银行并没有就“什么是适当的，什么是不适当的”服装制定任何指导方针。虽然Delta的变种迫使公司推迟重返办公室的时间，但这一天终将到来。当员工们回到办公桌前时，至少在某些时候，可能需要制定新的着装规定。

Much has been written about what people wore on Zoom calls during lockdowns (and what they did not wear: some retailers report that tops significantly outsold trousers in the past year and a half). Fashion designers like Giles Deacon in Britain have launched “work from anywhere” fashion collections, aiming for slightly looser-cut clothing that nevertheless looks smart. Two Japanese companies, Aoki and Whatever Inc, created pyjama suits—a hybrid of a suit and soft, comfy loungewear—perfect for the video conference attended from home. Aoki uses the same fabric as pyjamas but with a suit-like cut. Whatever Inc’s WFH Jammies are “business on the top, loungewear on the bottom”.

comfy 舒服的

Jammies 睡衣

关于人们在封锁期间在Zoom电话上穿什么(以及他们不穿什么:一些零售商报告称，在过去一年半里，上衣的销量远远超过了裤子)，已经有很多文章。英国的Giles Deacon等时装设计师推出了“在任何地方办公”的时装系列，旨在推出剪裁稍宽松但看起来时髦的服装。两家日本公司，Aoki和Whatever公司，发明了睡衣套装（一种西服和柔软舒适的休闲服的混合），非常适合在家参加视频会议。Aoki 用的是和睡衣一样的布料，但是剪裁像西装。Whatever Inc的WFH睡衣“上面是职业装，下面是休闲装”。

That is not to say that business-casual Fridays have given way to athleisure work weeks. Indeed, some workplaces are already experiencing a backlash against informality. In 2017 Britain’s House of Commons decided that male MPs were no longer required to wear ties when attending debates; previously they could go tieless only on hot summer days. But at the beginning of September this year Sir Lindsay Hoyle, the Speaker, announced that he expected all parliamentarians to smarten up. Jeans, chinos and sleeveless tops are out.

parliamentarian 议员

这并不是说商务休闲星期五已经让位于运动休闲工作周。事实上，一些工作场所已经遭遇了对非正式性的强烈反对。2017年，英国下议院决定，不再要求男性议员在参加辩论时打领带; 以前他们只能在炎热的夏天不系领带。但在今年九月初，议长Lindsay Hoyle爵士宣布，他希望所有议员都能打扮打扮。牛仔裤、斜纹布裤和无袖上衣都过时了。

Looked at in the aggregate, individuals’ clothes speak to more than just personal preferences. People’s sartorial choices add up to a zeitgeist. It is no accident that the cheerful glitz of the 1920s came right after the despondency of the first world war and the Spanish flu. Today’s tailoring brands hope that when the pandemic recedes at last male and female professionals will feel a renewed desire to dress up.

zeitgeist 时代精神

despondency 沮丧

glitz 浮华

从总体上看，个人的着装不仅仅关乎个人偏好。人们对服装的选择体现了一种时代精神。在第一次世界大战的萧条和西班牙流感爆发之后，20世纪20年代令人欢欣鼓舞的浮华景象接踵而至，这并非偶然。如今的裁缝品牌希望，当疫情最终消退时，男性和女性专业人士会重新产生打扮的欲望。

So does Bartleby. Like Sir Lindsay, she would recommend that employees maintain a degree of formal presentation. Yes, some people can pull off a dishevelled look—but not everyone. Dressing with taste and elegance does not have to involve designer clothes or expensive watches. It signals commitment and seriousness. A freshly laundered, crisp shirt announces to the world that you have made an effort; a tracksuit does not.

dishevelled 衣冠不整的；凌乱的

巴托比也是如此。与 Lindsay爵士一样，她会建议员工保持一定程度的正式外观。是的，有些人可以弄得衣冠不整的样子，但不是每个人都可以。有品味和优雅的着装并不一定要包括名牌服装或昂贵的手表。它标志着投入和认真。一件刚洗过的挺括的衬衫向世界宣告你已经努力过了;而运动服则不然。

And if going to the office is a ritual, styling an outfit can be a pleasure, not a chore. The way one dresses is part of his or her self-expression. It also separates the public and the private. Peeling off formal office clothes and slipping into something cosy marks a daily transition from work to non-work. That line was blurred during lockdowns and could do with some sharpening. A man in a suit and tie is a man loosening his tie at the end of the day.

peel off 脱衣服

如果去办公室是一种例行公事，那么设计服装就是一种乐趣，而不是一件苦差事。一个人的穿衣方式是他或她自我表达的一部分。它还将公共和私人分隔开来。脱下正式的办公室服装，换上舒适的衣服，标志着日常从工作到非工作的转变。这条界线在封锁期间被模糊了，需要修整一下。一个穿西装打领带的人就是一个在一天结束时松开领带的人。

Boris Johnson deserves credit for reforming Britain’s social care

鲍里斯·约翰逊值得称赞，因为他改革了英国的社会福利

Age and enlightenment

时代与启蒙

![image-20210911091339633](/Users/chenyujie/Library/Application Support/typora-user-images/image-20210911091339633.png)

But national insurance is the wrong way to pay for it

但是国民保险是错误的支付方式

Neither populists in general, nor Boris Johnson in particular, are known for tackling difficult problems if they can be left to future governments. Yet Britain’s prime minister did just that on September 7th when he unveiled a plan to break an election promise and raise taxes by an annual £12bn ($17bn), or 0.5% of GDP. The money will go to the National Health Service (NHS) and social care, including residential care for the elderly. Taxes could be raised more fairly. The government nonetheless deserves praise for taking on an issue that had seemed intractable.

如果棘手的问题可以留给未来的政府来解决的话，无论是从一般意义上的民粹主义者，还是具体上的鲍里斯•约翰逊，都不会以解决这些棘手的问题而闻名。然而，英国首相在9月7日公布了一项计划，打破了选举承诺，将税收每年提高120亿英镑(170亿美元)，占GDP的0.5%。这笔钱将用于国家医疗服务体系和社会关怀，包括老年人的居住护理。税收可以更合理地筹集。尽管如此，政府还是值得称赞的，因为它解决了一个似乎难以解决的问题。

Britain, like many rich countries, is ageing. In 2011 there were four 15- to 64-year-olds for every person aged 65 or over; by 2028 there will be fewer than three. The consequences include a strained NHS and more households discovering the inadequacies of the existing social-care system, which exposes the elderly and their heirs to huge financial risks. Today the government helps to pay for care only for those with assets worth less than about £23,000. But around one person in seven will spend more than £100,000—sometimes much more—on care during their lifetime. The result, which is not unique to Britain, is a lottery in which unlucky households must liquidate most or all of their assets.

liquidate 变卖

和许多发达国家一样，英国也在老龄化。2011年，每4个15- 64岁的人对应着1个65岁或65岁以上的人; 到2028年，这个数字将少于3个。其后果包括NHS不堪重负和越来越多的家庭发现现有社会保障体系的不足，这使老年人和他们的继承人面临巨大的财务风险。如今，政府只帮助那些资产价值低于2.3万英镑的人支付医疗费用。但大约七分之一的人一生中在护理上的花费超过10万英镑，有时甚至更多。其结果是，不幸的家庭必须变卖大部分或全部资产，这并非英国独有。

![image-20210911091320657](/Users/chenyujie/Library/Application Support/typora-user-images/image-20210911091320657.png)

But this necessary expansion of the welfare state is being paid for in the wrong way. A measure that insures the estates of the elderly against catastrophe should have been funded by levying an insurance premium on older people in the form of higher taxes on property or inheritance. The government instead decided to raise all the new money by lifting national insurance (a payroll tax) and taxes on dividends. Outrageously, income from pensions and property will escape higher levies, meaning many well-off elderly voters will be spared from paying anything for a reform of which they are major beneficiaries.

the welfare state 福利制度

Outrageously 骇人听闻地

但这种必要的福利制度的扩张方式是错误的。一项为老年人的财产提供灾难保险的措施本应通过对老年人征收保险费的方式获得资金，即对财产或遗产征收更高的税。相反，政府决定通过提高国民保险(工资税)和股息税来筹集所有新资金。令人震惊的是，养老金和财产收入将避开更高的税收，这意味着许多富裕的老年选民将不用为他们是主要受益者的改革支付任何费用。

Had the government raised income tax instead, every generation would have contributed. Mr Johnson says that national insurance is preferable, because firms pay part of the cost. This is a fiction. In the long run the burden of payroll taxes, even those paid by companies, falls on workers, whose wages fall as their employers’ tax bills rise.

如果政府提高所得税，每一代人都会为此做出贡献。约翰逊先生说国民保险更可取，因为公司支付部分费用。这是假象。从长远来看，工资税的负担，即使是公司支付的，都落在工人身上，他们的工资随着雇主税单的增加而下降。

The reform is not Mr Johnson’s only breach of an election pledge. His government also announced on September 7th that it would temporarily suspend the “triple lock”, which has ensured since 2010 that the state pension rises by the minimum of earnings, inflation or 2.5%. The pandemic has distorted the official measure of earnings growth, partly because employment has fallen in the lowest-paying industries. Maintaining the triple lock, as the Conservatives had promised, could have pushed the increase in the state pension above 8%. Instead, this year it will be set with reference only to inflation and the 2.5% floor.

这项改革并不是约翰逊唯一违背竞选承诺的行为。9月7日，他的政府还宣布将暂停“三重锁”政策，该政策自2010年起保证国家养老金按收入、通货膨胀率或2.5%的最低水平上涨。疫情扭曲了官方对收入增长的衡量标准，部分原因是低收入行业的就业率下降。正如保守党承诺的那样，保持三重锁政策，可以将国家养老金的增幅推到8%以上。相反，今年它将只参考通货膨胀率和2.5%的下限。

The government should go further and abandon the triple lock. The savings should go on working-age people, thus helping to offset the unfairness of higher payroll taxes. Mr Johnson’s reinvention as a politician willing to take an unpopular but necessary decision is welcome. Many more will be needed. ■

政府应该更进一步，放弃三重锁。储蓄应该用于适龄劳动人口，从而帮助抵消更高工资税带来的不公平性。约翰逊重新塑造了一个愿意做出不受欢迎但必要决定的政治家形象，这一点受到欢迎。还需要更多的革新。■

How the pandemic became stagflationary

疫情是如何使得经济滞胀的

Delta and the world economy

Delta病株和世界经济

![image-20210910093728504](/Users/chenyujie/Library/Application Support/typora-user-images/image-20210910093728504.png)

As the virus has changed, so has its relationship to the economy

随着病毒的变化，它与经济的关系也发生了变化

IT HAS BEEN a summer of unpleasant surprises for the world economy. America, Europe and China are growing more slowly than investors had hoped. Consumer prices are rising uncomfortably fast, especially in America. Even in the euro area, used to tepid inflation, prices in August were 3% higher than a year earlier, the most in a decade. Economies are troubled by shortages of parts and labour, slow and expensive shipping and the bewildering variation of lockdown measures.

对于世界经济来说，这个夏天充满了不愉快的意外。美国、欧洲和中国的增长速度比投资者预期的要慢。消费价格正以令人不安的速度上涨，尤其是在美国。即使是在通胀温和的欧元区，8月份的物价也比去年同期高出3%，是10年来的最高水平。各个经济体都受到零部件和劳动力短缺、运输缓慢且昂贵以及各种各样令人困惑的封锁措施的困扰。

The spread of the Delta variant is to blame, but the way the pandemic is affecting the economy is shifting. The world had become accustomed to the virus battering growth, as waves of infection caused a sudden stop in activity, and prices moderated or even fell. Delta, by contrast, looks like a stagflationary force that is sapping growth less dramatically but firing up inflation.

sap 削弱

Delta变种的传播是罪魁祸首，但疫情影响经济的方式正在发生变化。世界已经习惯了病毒对经济增长的打击，因为一波又一波的感染导致经济活动突然停止，价格放缓甚至下跌。相比之下，Delta病株看起来像是一股滞胀的力量，虽然没有那么剧烈地削弱经济增长，但却加剧了通胀。

Delta is weighing on consumer spending in the rich world but not causing a collapse. In countries with lots of vaccine, cases are no longer doing as much to stop consumers from moving around. Europe’s service sector has reopened amid its Delta wave.

Delta病毒正在给发达国家的消费者支出施加压力，但并未导致崩溃。在拥有大量疫苗的国家，（感染）病例不再能阻止消费者四处走动。欧洲的服务业在“Delta浪潮”中重新开放。

Consumers seem less scared of the disease even if there are enough unvaccinated people to fill up hospitals. A year ago the number of diners in American restaurants was nearly half the level in 2019. Now service is about 10% down, even though hospitals are three times fuller. In Japan a state of emergency covering Tokyo does not seem to be keeping consumers away from the shops. Only in countries with draconian policies aimed at eliminating the virus are people stuck at home. Australia and New Zealand face new recessions as a result of their lockdowns and China’s service sector appears to be shrinking.

draconian 严苛的

即使未接种疫苗的人足以挤满医院，消费者似乎没那么害怕这种疾病。一年前，美国餐馆的用餐人数几乎是2019年的一半。现在，尽管医院的“人满为患”的情况是过去的三倍，服务却下降了10%。在日本，覆盖东京的紧急状态似乎并没有让消费者远离商店。只有在实行旨在消灭病毒的严厉政策的国家，人们才会被困在家里。由于封锁，澳大利亚和新西兰面临新的经济衰退，而中国的服务业似乎正在萎缩。

Meanwhile, the spread of Delta continues to interfere with the global supply of goods just as consumers, especially Americans, are intent on buying more cars, devices and sporting gear than ever. Outbreaks in South-East Asian countries with low rates of vaccination are causing production plants and logistics networks to shut down temporarily, prolonging the disruption to supply chains. In America retailers, including Gap and Nike, have lobbied the White House to donate more vaccines to Vietnam, so crucial have its factories become to their businesses. Shortages are driving up prices.

与此同时，随着消费者(尤其是美国人)打算购买比以往更多的汽车、设备和运动装备，Delta病毒的蔓延继续影响全球商品供应。疫苗接种率低的东南亚国家疫情的爆发，导致生产工厂和物流网络暂时关闭，延长了供应链的中断。在美国，包括Gap和耐克在内的零售商已经游说白宫向越南捐赠更多的疫苗，因为越南的工厂对他们的业务至关重要。短缺正在推高价格。

The changing relationship between the virus and the economy has implications for policymakers. They will not be able to repeat the trick from earlier in the pandemic of restricting people’s movement as a way to contain the spread of the virus, while at the same time unleashing stimulus to create a compensating boom in demand for goods.

病毒与经济之间不断变化的关系对政策制定者产生了影响。他们将无法重复疫情早期采取的限制人员流动以遏制病毒传播，同时释放刺激措施，创造商品需求的补偿性繁荣的伎俩。

A service-sector revival is now the only quick route to fast growth because that is where the slack is. In the second quarter of the year spending on services by American households was about 3% below its level in 2019 in real terms. Should the spread of Delta interfere with service industries such as leisure and hospitality, more stimulus will only create more inflation.

现在，服务业的复苏是实现快速增长的唯一捷径，因为这正是经济疲软的地方。今年第二季度，美国家庭在服务上的实际支出比2019年低约3%。如果Delta病毒的蔓延影响到休闲和酒店等服务业，更多的刺激措施只会导致更多的通胀。

![image-20210910093807858](/Users/chenyujie/Library/Application Support/typora-user-images/image-20210910093807858.png)

It is also harder to argue that fear of the virus scares consumers off spending, and that government restrictions to slow the spread of disease therefore have little extra economic cost. A weaker link between cases and people’s movement, and the necessity of service-sector growth, raise the cost of lockdowns. If pressure on hospitals causes even highly vaccinated countries like Britain to restrict services over the winter, the economic damage will be large and the benefits smaller. The Delta wave may subside soon, easing the pressure on the world economy. If it does not or another variant takes its place, the trade-offs involved in fighting the virus will become harder to justify. ■

scare off 把…吓跑

同样，很难说对病毒的恐惧吓得消费者减少了支出，因此政府为减缓疾病传播而采取的限制措施几乎没有额外的经济成本。病例与人员流动之间的联系减弱，以及服务业增长的必要性，提高了封锁的成本。如果医院承受的压力甚至导致像英国这样疫苗接种率高的国家在冬季限制服务，那么经济损失将是巨大的，而好处也会更少。“Delta”浪潮可能很快就会消退，从而缓解世界经济的压力。如果不是这样，或者另一种变体取代了它的位置，那么抗击病毒所涉及的权衡将变得更加困难。■

Why people are always so gloomy about the world of work

为什么人们总是对工作如此悲观

Bartleby

巴托比

![image-20210909110500136](/Users/chenyujie/Library/Application Support/typora-user-images/image-20210909110500136.png)

Critics of capitalism have always said that working life is in decay

资本主义的批评者总是说，工作生活正在衰退

THE NOTION that the modern economy lacks “good jobs” is as uncontroversial as saying that Lionel Messi is good at football. Pundits decry the disappearance of the steady positions of yesteryear, where people did a fair day’s work for a fair day’s pay. “Where have all the good jobs gone?”, wonders one recent book, while another talks about “the rise of polarised and precarious employment systems”. President Joe Biden takes after Donald Trump in promising to bring good jobs “back”. But what if the whole debate rests on shaky foundations?

yesteryear 昔日

现代经济缺乏“好工作”的说法，就像说“梅西擅长足球”一样毫无争议。权威人士谴责昔日那种稳定的工作岗位的消失，在那种岗位上，人们做一天公平的工作，得到一天公平的工资。最近一本书中提到“好工作都去哪儿了?”，而另一本则谈到了“两极分化和不稳定的就业体系的兴起”。美国总统拜登效仿特朗普，承诺“带回来”好的就业机会。但如果整个讨论都建立在不可靠的基础上呢?

It certainly lacks historical awareness. Compare the current discussion with the one during America’s postwar boom. Few people back then believed that they were living in a golden age of labour. Commentators were instead full of angst, worrying about the “blue-collar blues”. They said that unionised factory jobs—the very sort that today’s politicians yearn to restore—consisted of repetitive, dangerous work which involved all brawn and no brain. Others fretted over pay. Workers “are getting increasingly frustrated by a system they think is not giving them a satisfactory return for their labours”, proclaimed a high-ranking official at America’s Department of Labour in 1970.

brawn 体力

(he’s) all brawn, no brains （他）四肢发达，头脑简单

high-ranking 职位高的；显要的

它当然缺乏历史意识。将当前的讨论与美国战后繁荣时期的讨论进行比较。当时很少有人相信他们生活在劳动的黄金时代。相反，评论家们充满了焦虑，担心“蓝领工人沮丧”。他们说，工会化的工厂工作（正是今天的政客们渴望恢复的那种）是重复的、危险的工作，只需要体力，不需要脑力。其他人则对薪酬感到担忧。1970年，美国劳工部的一位高级官员宣称，工人们“对一个他们认为不能给他们的劳动带来满意回报的体系越来越感到沮丧”。

In fact the notion that the world of work is in decay is as old as capitalism itself. Jean Charles Léonard de Sismondi, a Swiss writer who inspired Karl Marx, said that factories would turn people into drones. John Stuart Mill worried in the mid-19th century that the rise of capitalism would provoke social decay. People would focus on nothing other than earning money, he feared, turning them into dullards (just look at Americans, he warned). The rise of big business and white-collar work in America provoked a new set of anxieties. It was soon predicted that the self-made men of yore would be replaced by effete company drones who did what they were told.

self-made 靠自己奋斗成功的；白手起家的

of yore 很久以前的

effete 软弱做作的

雄蜂，就是雄性蜜蜂，体壮腰肥，背宽腹醋，翅大膀厚，视力发达，嗅觉灵敏，具有雄健的体质和高速的飞行能力，是蜜蜂王国的大力士！雄蜂的雄性生殖器官相当发达，但上颚退化，舌蠢吻短，腺体发育不全，以至于不具有保护自己、攻击敌人的武器，更不具备采花酿蜜、泌蜡造脾、保家卫国以及自食其力的能力。在它年轻力盛时，就负责追求蜂王得到交配的机会。平时就只待在蜂巢里面暴饮豪食、无所事事、休闲度日。

事实上，“工作世界正在衰落”的观念与资本主义本身一样古老。启发了Karl Marx的瑞士作家Jean Charles Léonard de Sismondi曾说过，工厂会把人变成雄蜂。John Stuart Mill在19世纪中期曾担心，资本主义的崛起会引发社会衰退。他担心，人们除了赚钱以外什么都不关心，这会让他们变成傻瓜(他警告说，看看美国人就知道了)。美国大企业和白领工作的兴起引发了一系列新的焦虑。很快就有人预言，很久以前靠自己奋斗成功的人将被软弱的公司雄蜂所取代，这些雄蜂按照公司的要求行事。

This is not the only reason to question today’s pessimistic narrative. By any reasonable standard work is better today than it was. Pay is higher, working hours are shorter and industrial accidents rarer.

这并不是质疑当今悲观叙述的唯一理由。以任何合理的标准衡量，今天的工作都比过去好。工资更高，工作时间更短，工业事故更少。

It is harder to tell whether workers enjoy their work more than they did. Gallup, a pollster, has kindly supplied Bartleby with a smattering of job-satisfaction surveys from the 1960s and 1970s. Certainly there is little to suggest that workers back then were any happier than they are today. More comparable data, also from Gallup, from the early 1990s onwards show gradually improving job satisfaction. Last year 56% of American employees said they were “completely” satisfied with their job, an all-time high. A growing “precariat” of insecure workers gets a lot of headlines. But 90% of American employees said in 2020 that they were completely or somewhat satisfied with their job security, up from 79% in 1993.

smattering 少量

很难说工人们是否比以前更喜欢自己的工作。民意调查公司Gallup好心地向巴托比提供了一些从20世纪60年代到70年代的工作满意度调查。当然，没有什么迹象表明当时的工人比现在更幸福。同样来自Gallup的更具可比性的数据显示，自上世纪90年代初以来，工作满意度逐渐提高。去年，56%的美国员工表示他们“完全”满意自己的工作，这一比例创历史新高。越来越多不稳定的“无业游民”成为头条新闻。但在2020年，90%的美国员工表示，他们对自己的工作保障完全或在某种程度上感到满意，高于1993年的79%。

If the jobs-are-bad narrative falls down on the facts, why is it so pervasive—and so intuitive? Partly it is because no one has bothered to look at the evidence. Other observers just don’t like the constant change and churn which has always been part and parcel of capitalism. More still may subscribe, perhaps subconsciously, to what Friedrich Hayek, a philosopher, called an “atavistic” view of markets. The very notion that people must sell their labour, for cash, in order to survive may violate some deeply held notion that humans are a fundamentally co-operative species, rather than a competitive one.

fall down 失败

subconsciously 潜意识地

如果“工作很糟糕”的说法与事实不符，那么为什么它如此普遍、如此直观呢? 部分原因是没有人费心去看证据。其他观察人士只是不喜欢资本主义的不断变化和变动，这也一直是资本主义的一部分。或许还有更多的人会下意识地认同哲学家Friedrich Hayek所称的市场“返祖”观点。为了生存，人们必须出卖自己的劳动来换取现金，这一观念本身可能违反了某些根深蒂固的观念，即人类从根本上是合作的物种，而不是竞争的物种。

Yet perhaps the most important reason is that people dislike acknowledging trade-offs. Mill seemed unable to square his concern about the stultifying effects of capitalism with his argument that the division of labour had massively increased living standards. People often make similar errors today. The decline of trade unions may have hurt some workers’ wages; but it is less commonly acknowledged that this has also made it easier for less “traditional” workers, such as ethnic minorities and women, to enter the labour market. Sedentary office jobs can make people fat; but people are far less likely to die on the job than they once were.

stultify 使呆滞

然而，也许最重要的原因是人们不喜欢承认权衡。Mill认为，劳动分工极大地提高了人们的生活水平，但他对资本主义的愚蠢效应的担忧，似乎无法与这一观点相吻合。如今人们经常犯类似的错误。工会的衰落可能损害了一些工人的工资; 但这也使得少数族裔和女性等“不那么传统”的工人更容易进入劳动力市场，这一点不太为人所知。久坐不动的办公室工作会让人发胖;但人们在工作中死亡的可能性比以前小得多。

A relentless focus on the problems of labour markets still has its uses. It encourages people to think about how to make improvements. The evidence suggests that on average managers have got better in recent years but clearly some firms have a long way to go. Many people are still exploited by their employers. Today’s world of work is far better than its critics would like to admit. But there is every reason to try to make it better still.

对劳动力市场问题的无情关注仍有其作用。它鼓励人们思考如何改进。有证据表明，近年来高管们的平均水平有所提高，但显然有些公司还有很长的路要走。许多人仍受雇主的剥削。如今的职场远比批评者愿意承认的要好。但仍然有充分的理由去努力让它变得更好。

School closures have caused damage that extra lessons cannot fix

学校关闭造成的损害是额外课程无法弥补的

Back to basics

回归基础

![image-20210908111211188](/Users/chenyujie/Library/Application Support/typora-user-images/image-20210908111211188.png)

Helping children catch up will take years

帮助孩子们赶上进度需要几年时间

A DECADE AGO Bowling Park Primary was one of England’s least successful schools. Its neighbourhood in south Bradford is one of England’s poorest. A fifth of pupils are Roma, who tend to do worse at school than any other group. But a turnaround that began in 2008 dragged test scores up to the national average. The progress pupils make from seven to 11 is greater than almost anywhere else.

十年前，保龄公园小学是英国最不成功的学校之一。它在布拉德福德南部的邻居是英国最贫穷的社区之一。五分之一的学生是吉普赛人，他们在学校的表现往往比其他任何群体都差。但是从2008年开始的一场转变将考试成绩拉到了全国平均水平。学生从7岁到11岁的进步几乎比其他任何地方都要大。

Covid-19 has hit hard, says Matthew Langley, the head teacher. Large families and small homes helped the virus spread. Some pupils spent a quarter of the past academic year isolating, on top of two months of remote learning early in the pandemic, when all classrooms in England were shut. “It’s like we’ve hit a brick wall,” says Mr Langley, “and now we have to reverse.”

校长Matthew Langley表示，Covid-19的打击非常严重。大家庭和小房子助长了病毒的传播。一些学生在过去的学年中有四分之一的时间是在隔离中度过的，此外，在疫情早期，英国所有的教室都关闭了，他们还进行了两个月的在线学习。 Langley说:“我们好像陷入一个僵局，现在我们必须扭转局面。”

As children return to school after the summer break, the effects of the past two years of closures are worrying head teachers—and the government. A recent study suggests that in March primary-school pupils were about two months behind expected attainment in reading, and more than three months behind in maths. It also offered some grounds for optimism: during the relatively uninterrupted period last autumn pupils started to make up some of their earlier losses. But the averages disguise wide variations. Pretty much every school, says Sammy Wright, a member of a government advisory panel on social mobility, will have 5-10% of children who have been “devastated” by the disruption.

uninterrupted 不中断的

随着孩子们暑假后重返校园，过去两年学校停课的影响令校长们和政府感到担忧。最近的一项研究表明，小学生在3月份的阅读成绩比预期成绩落后了两个月，数学成绩落后了三个多月。这也提供了一些乐观的理由:在去年秋天相对不间断的一段时间里，学生们开始弥补他们早些时候没学到的东西。但平均数掩盖了巨大的差异。政府社会流动性咨询小组的成员Sammy Wright说，几乎每一所学校都有5-10%的孩子因这次中断而“深受打击”。

Missing out on sport, school trips and other character-building activities has hit disadvantaged pupils’ confidence, says Mr Langley. And the pandemic has amplified the sorts of everyday difficulties that distract them from their studies. Across the country, the number poor enough to qualify for free lunches rose by 20.6% in the year to January. Even during normal times disadvantaged children lose more time than others to common problems such as toothache, says Mark Mon-Williams of the Bradford Institute for Health Research. These will have been worsened as services were cut or suspended during lockdowns.

Langley先生说，错过体育、学校旅行和其他塑造性格的活动打击了弱势学生的信心。疫情扩大了日常生活中的各种困难，这些困难分散了他们的学习精力。截至今年1月，全国有资格享受免费午餐的穷人人数上升了20.6%。布拉德福德健康研究所的Mark Mon-Williams说，即使在正常时期，处于弱势的孩子也会因为牙痛等常见问题而浪费更多的时间。在封锁期间，由于服务被削减或暂停，这些情况将进一步恶化。

The government has promised schools extra funding equal to about £310 ($427) per pupil. That is only a little more than a single month’s spending in 2020 on a scheme that subsidised people to eat out in restaurants. In June Sir Kevan Collins, a former teacher and council boss hired to advise the government on its catch-up strategy, resigned when he heard that only a tenth of the £15bn in extra funding he had asked for would be made available.

政府承诺给学校额外的资金，相当于每个学生310英镑(427美元)。这只比2020年一个月在“餐馆就餐”的支出多一点。今年6月， Kevan Collins爵士（他曾是一名教师，也是委员会主席，被聘请来为政府的追赶战略提供建议）辞职，因为他听说他申请的150亿英镑额外资金中只有十分之一能到位。

Sir Kevan had wanted to increase school spending by about 10% over the next three years, with more than half to be used to add half an hour to the standard school day. The idea was to make time for confidence-building activities, such as sport, music and drama, which risk being squeezed out as schools scramble to catch up on the basics. The extra time would have been mandatory, since voluntary activities are most likely to be skipped by those who need them most. In July the prime minister, Boris Johnson, said that more money might be announced later this year. But the Treasury, faced with a post-pandemic black hole in the budget, is reportedly less keen.

Kevan爵士曾希望在未来三年里将学校支出增加10%，其中一半以上将用于为标准教学日增加半小时。当时的想法是为建立信任的活动腾出时间，比如体育、音乐和戏剧，这些活动可能会因为学校急于赶上基本课程而被占用。额外的时间是强制性的，因为那些最需要的人很可能会跳过志愿活动。7月，英国首相鲍里斯·约翰逊表示，今年晚些时候可能会宣布更多资金。但据报道，面对疫情后的预算黑洞，财政部对此并不热心。

One reason is that many education researchers are dubious that longer school days do much to improve results—in normal times, at least. They tend to recommend using existing time better, not least because that is cheaper. Many teachers and school leaders, meanwhile, fear that a heavier workload might not be fully reflected in extra pay. They argue instead for schemes aimed at pupils with the biggest learning deficits, along with freedom for each school to craft its own plans. In the Netherlands, where catch-up money is a more munificent £2,500 per pupil, the government has given guidance regarding what to spend it on, but not set firm rules.

原因之一是，许多教育研究人员怀疑，延长在校时间是否有助于提高学习成绩，至少在正常时期是这样。他们倾向于建议更好地利用现有的时间，尤其是因为这样更便宜。与此同时，许多教师和学校领导担心，额外的工资可能无法充分反映更重的工作量。相反，他们主张针对最缺乏学习能力的学生制定计划，并让每所学校有制定自己的计划的自由。在荷兰，每名学生的补习金为2500英镑，更为优厚。荷兰政府已经就如何使用补习金给出了建议，但没有制定明确的规定。

In Bradford Mr Langley worries that much talk of catch-up has focused on “short-term solutions” aimed at propping up test scores. Covid-19 has disrupted schooling for much longer than people expected; its after-effects will linger, too. He has no doubt that Bowling Park will outgrow the damage done by the pandemic. “But it won’t be in two or three years.” ■

The rise and fall of sex-selective abortion in Georgia

格鲁吉亚性别选择性堕胎的兴衰

Too many boys

![image-20210907092856008](/Users/chenyujie/Library/Application Support/typora-user-images/image-20210907092856008.png)

How parents in a post-Soviet state shunned girls, then changed their minds

后苏联国家的父母是如何避免生女孩，然后又改变了他们的想法的？

WHEN NILUFER KURBANOVA’S doctor told her she was going to have a boy, he expected a tip for delivering the good news. He did not get one.

当NILUFER KURBANOVA的医生告诉她将会有一个男孩时，他期待着传递这个好消息的小费。他没有得到一点小费。

Ms Kurbanova does not believe, as many older Georgians do, that sons are better than daughters. Some families feel they need a male child to carry on the bloodline; Ms Kurbanova thinks this is hokum. Traditional families expect sons to look after their parents in their old age, whereas a daughter would look after her husband’s parents. Ms Kurbanova does not see why it has to be that way.

hokum 废话

Kurbanova女士不像许多老一辈的佐治亚州人那样认为儿子比女儿好。有些家庭觉得他们需要一个男孩来延续血脉; Kurbanova女士认为这是胡扯。传统家庭希望儿子在父母年老时照顾他们，而女儿则要照顾丈夫的父母。Kurbanova女士不明白为什么会这样。

For centuries Georgians strongly preferred boys. When Georgia was part of the Soviet Union, from 1921 to 1991, its communist rulers suppressed some old sexist customs (such as kidnapping brides) and declared that women were equal, even if they did not always uphold the ideal. In any case, practically no one had access to sex-detecting ultrasound machines in those days. This was partly because they could not afford them, and partly because the West restricted exports since some of the machines’ parts had military uses.

几个世纪以来，格鲁吉亚人非常偏好（生）男孩。从1921年到1991年，当格鲁吉亚还是苏联的一部分时，它的共产主义统治者镇压了一些陈旧的性别歧视习俗(如绑架新娘)，并宣布妇女是平等的，尽管他们并不总是坚持这一理念。无论如何，在那个年代，几乎没有人能接触到性探测超声波仪器。部分原因是他们买不起，部分是由于一些机器的部件有军事用途，西方限制了出口。

That changed when the Soviet Union imploded. Ultrasound became widely available. And patriarchal values made a comeback, perhaps because the collapse of a bossy state prompted Georgians to seek guidance elsewhere. Many turned to the church and to their families. Traditional values, combined with modern technology, led to a surge in the abortion of girl babies. Georgia’s sex ratio swung wildly. In 1991 there were roughly 105 boys born for every 100 girls. (This is the natural rate, since more boys die in childhood and the numbers even out by the time they reach reproductive age.) By 2004 there were 115 boys born for every 100 girls.

苏联解体后，情况发生了变化。超声波被广泛使用。父权价值观卷土重来，或许是因为一个专横的国家的崩溃促使格鲁吉亚人去别处寻求指导。许多人转向求助教会和他们的家人。传统价值观与现代技术相结合，导致了女婴流产的激增。格鲁吉亚的性别比例急剧变化。1991年，大约每出生100个女孩就有105个男孩出生。(这是自然比率，因为更多的男孩在童年时期死亡，到了生育年龄，这一数字趋于平衡。)到2004年，每出生100个女孩就有115个男孩出生。

But since then the sex ratio at birth has returned closer to normal. For the past five years it has hovered around 107 (though in 2020 it reached 109). In neighbouring Azerbaijan, where sex-selective abortion is still rife, it is 114. In China, it is 112.

但从那以后，出生时的性别比例已经回归到接近正常的水平。在过去的五年里，这个数字一直徘徊在107(尽管在2020年达到了109)左右。在邻国阿塞拜疆，性别选择性堕胎仍然盛行，这一数字为114。在中国，这个数字是112。

Several factors may explain why Georgians started valuing girl babies again. After the initial post-communist turmoil, women’s wages rose, giving them more bargaining power at home. This made it easier for them to overrule husbands who wanted to abort a girl (son preference is more common among Georgian men than women). It also planted the idea in parental heads that a daughter may grow up to earn serious money, and therefore be able to support them in their old age.

有几个因素可以解释为什么格鲁吉亚人又开始重视女婴了。在最初的后共产主义骚乱之后，女性的工资上涨了，这让她们在家里有了更大的议价能力。这使得他们更容易否决那些想打掉女孩的丈夫(重男轻女在格鲁吉亚男性中比女性更普遍)。这也使得给父母的脑海中植入了这样一种观念，即女儿长大后可能会挣到大钱，从而能够在他们年老时赡养他们。

Changes in the fertility rate made a difference, too. In 1990 the average Georgian woman had 2.2 children. That plummeted to 1.6 in 2001, as economic turbulence made couples nervous about the future. The patriarch of Georgia’s Orthodox Church was so alarmed that in 2007 he vowed personally to baptise any couple’s third or subsequent child. 界统计模型来工作的，但它缺乏对现实的更深层次的理解。如何让人工智能至少具备一些类似的理解能力，比如一个7个月大的孩子的推理能力，也许现在是一个活跃的研究课题。

Modern AI is based on the idea of machine learning. If an engineer wants a computer to recognise a stop sign, he does not try to write thousands of lines of code that describe every pattern of pixels which could possibly indicate such a sign. This modified output was then fed to the software which drives the vehicle. The concepts involved included the ideas that discrete objects continue to exist over time, that they have spatial relationships with one another—such as “in-front-of” and “behind”—and that they can be fully or partly visible, or completely hidden by another object.

这款软件不像机器学习那样以概率的方式接近世界，而是将基本的物理概念应用到处理无人驾驶汽车传感器信号的程序输出中。然后将修改后的输出输入驱动车辆的软件。所涉及的概念包括:不相关联的物体会随着时间的推移而继续存在，它们彼此之间有空间关系，比如“在前面”和“在后面”，它们可以完全或部分可见，或者完全被另一个物体隐藏。

And it worked. In tests, if one car momentarily blocked the sight of another, the reasoning-enhanced software could keep track of the blocked car, predict where and when it would reappear, and take steps to avoid it if necessary. The improvement was not huge. On standard tests Dr Bhatt’s system scored about 5% better than existing software. But it proved the principle. And it also yielded something else. For, unlike a machine-learning algorithm, a reasoning engine can tell you the reason why it did what it did.

它是有用的。在测试中，如果一辆车暂时挡住了另一辆车的视线，该推理增强软件可以跟踪被挡住的车，预测它将在何时何地再次出现，并在必要时采取措施避开它。这一改进并没有那么大。在标准测试中，Bhatt博士的系统比现有的软件高出约5%。但它证明了原理。它还产生了其他东西。因为，与机器学习算法不同，推理引擎可以告诉你它为什么这么做。

You could, for instance, ask a car fitted with a reasoning engine why it had hit the brakes, and it would be able to tell you that it thought a bicycle hidden by a van was about to enter the intersection ahead. A machine-learning program cannot do that. Besides helping improve program design, such information will, Dr Bhatt reckons, help regulators and insurance companies. It may thus speed up public acceptance of autonomous vehicles.

例如，你可以问一辆装有推理引擎的汽车为什么会踩刹车，它会告诉你，它认为一辆藏在货车后面的自行车即将进入前面的十字路口。机器学习程序无法做到这一点。Bhatt博士认为，这些信息除了有助于改进项目设计之外，还将有助于监管机构和保险公司。因此，它可能会加快公众对无人驾驶汽车的接受程度。

Dr Bhatt’s work is part of a long-standing debate in the field of artificial intelligence. Early AI researchers, working in the 1950s, chalked up some successes using this sort of preprogrammed reasoning. But, beginning in the 1990s, machine learning improved dramatically, thanks to better programming techniques combined with more powerful computers and the availability of more data. Today almost all AI is based on it.

chalk up 赢得; 达到

Bhatt博士的工作是人工智能领域长期争论的一部分。20世纪50年代的早期人工智能研究人员使用这种预编程推理取得了一些成功。但是从20世纪90年代开始，由于更好的编程技术、更强大的计算机以及更多数据的可用性，机器学习得到了极大改进。今天，几乎所有的人工智能都是基于机器学习。

Dr Bhatt is not, though, alone in his scepticism. Gary Marcus, who studies psychology and neural science at New York University and is also the boss of an AI and robotics company called Robust.AI, agrees. To support his point of view, Dr Marcus cites a much-publicised result, albeit from eight years ago. This was when engineers at DeepMind (then an independent company, now part of Google) wrote a program that could learn, without being given any hints about the rules, how to play Breakout, a video game which involves hitting a moving virtual ball with a virtual paddle.

不过，Bhatt博士并不是唯一持怀疑态度的人。Gary Marcus在纽约大学研究心理学和神经科学，他也是一家名为Robust.AI的人工智能和机器人公司的老板, 他表示同意。为了支持他的观点，Marcus博士引用了一个被广泛报道的结果，尽管这是8年前的结果。当时，DeepMind(当时是一家独立公司，现在是谷歌的一部分)的工程师编写了一个程序，可以在不需要任何规则提示的情况下学习如何玩Breakout，这是一款电子游戏，需要用虚拟球拍击打移动中的虚拟球。

DeepMind’s program was a great player. But when another group of researchers tinkered with Breakout’s code—shifting the location of the paddles by just a few pixels—its abilities plummeted. It was not able to generalise what it had learned from a specific situation even to a situation that was only slightly different.

DeepMind的程序是一个牛逼玩家。但是，当另一组研究人员对Breakout的代码进行了修改，将球拍的位置稍稍改变了几个像素后，它的能力就骤降。它无法将它从一个具体情境中学到的东西泛化到一个只有细微差别的情况。

Back to the future

For Dr Marcus, this example highlights the fragility of machine-learning. But others think it is symbolic reasoning which is brittle, and that machine learning still has a lot of mileage left in it. Among them is Jeff Hawke, vice-president of technology at Wayve, a self-driving-car firm in London. Wayve’s approach is to train the software elements running a car’s various components simultaneously, rather than separately. In demonstrations, Wayve’s cars make good decisions while navigating narrow, heavily trafficked London streets—a task that challenges many humans.

对Marcus博士来说，这个例子凸显了机器学习的脆弱性。但也有人认为这是一种脆弱的符号推理，机器学习还有很长的路要走。Jeff Hawke就是其中之一，他是Wayve的技术副总裁，Wayve是一家位于伦敦的无人驾驶汽车公司。Wayve的方法是同时训练驱动汽车各个部件的软件元素，而不是单独训练。在演示中，Wayve的汽车在狭窄、拥挤的伦敦街道上行驶时做出了正确的决定，这项任务对许多人来说都是一项挑战。

As Dr Hawke puts it, “the complexity of most real-world tasks is greater than is possible to solve with handcrafted rules, and it’s well known that expert systems built with rules tend to struggle with complexity. This is true regardless of how well thought out or structured the formal logic is.” Such a system might, for instance, craft a rule that a car should stop at a red light. But lights are designed differently in different countries, and some are intended for pedestrians rather than cars. There are also situations in which you might need to jump a red light, such as to make way for a fire engine. “The beauty of machine learning”, Dr Hawke says, “is that all these factors and concepts can be automatically uncovered and learned from data. And with more data, it continues to learn and become more intelligent.”

think out 仔细考虑

正如Hawke博士所说，“大多数现实世界任务的复杂性都超过了用手工制定的规则所能解决的问题，众所周知，用规则构建的专家系统往往难以解决复杂性问题。不管形式逻辑的构思或结构如何完善，都是如此。” 例如，这样的系统可能会精心制定一条规则，规定汽车在遇到红灯时应该停车。但是不同国家的路灯设计不同，有些路灯是为行人而不是汽车设计的。在某些情况下，你可能需要闯红灯，比如给消防车让路。Hawke博士说：“机器学习的美妙之处在于，所有这些因素和概念都可以从数据中自动揭示和学习。有了更多的数据，它就会继续学习，变得更智能。”

Nicholas Rhinehart, who studies robotics and AI at the University of California, Berkeley, also backs machine learning. He says Dr Bhatt’s approach does indeed show you can combine the two approaches. But he is not sure it is necessary. In his work, and also that of others, machine-learning systems alone can already predict probabilities a few seconds into the future—such as whether another car is likely to give way or not—and make contingency plans based on those predictions.

加州大学伯克利分校研究机器人和人工智能的Nicholas Rhinehart也支持机器学习。他说，Bhatt博士的方法确实表明可以将这两种方法结合起来。但他不确定是否有必要。在他和其他人的研究中，仅机器学习系统就已经可以预测未来几秒钟的概率，比如另一辆车是否可能让路，并根据这些预测制定应急计划。

Dr Bhatt responds that you can train a car with data accumulated over millions of kilometres of driving, and still not be sure you have covered all the necessary situations. In many cases, it might be simpler and more effective to program some of the rules in from the start.

Bhatt博士回答说，你可以用积累了数百万公里行驶里程的数据来训练一辆汽车，但仍然不能确定你已经涵盖了所有必要的情况。在许多情况下，从一开始就编写一些规则可能更简单、更有效。

For champions of both strategies, the question goes beyond self-driving cars to the future of AI itself. “I don’t think we’re taking the right approach right now,” Dr Marcus says. “[Machine learning] has proven useful for some things like speech recognition, but it’s not actually the answer to AI. We haven’t really solved the intelligence problem.” One way or another, then, it seems seven-month-olds still have a lot to teach machines. ■

对于这两种策略的支持者来说，问题不仅仅是无人驾驶汽车，而是人工智能本身的未来。Marcus博士说：“我认为我们现在没有采取正确的方法，(机器学习)已被证明在语音识别等方面有用，但它实际上不是人工智能的答案。我们还没有真正解决智能问题。” 不管怎样，看起来7个月大的婴儿仍然有很多东西要教给机器。■

Big British companies are entering the rental market

英国大公司正在进入租赁市场

Housing

住房

![image-20210903103535728](/Users/chenyujie/Library/Application Support/typora-user-images/image-20210903103535728.png)

The build-to-rent sector is booming

建房出租行业正在蓬勃发展

Chatter about house prices and the property market has long been a dinner-party staple. Now it is spreading to the boardroom. An internal job advert at Lloyds Banking Group has revealed that the lender plans to enter the build-to-rent sector, with an ambitious target of owning 50,000 properties by 2030. John Lewis, which sells everything a dinner-party host might want for the home, announced earlier this summer that it plans to build 10,000 rental properties. Large insurance companies, such as Aviva and Legal & General, have entered the market in recent years, as have several private-equity firms.

长期以来，谈论房价和房地产市场一直是宴会上的主要话题。现在，它正蔓延到董事会会议室。劳埃德银行集团的一则内部招聘广告透露，该银行计划进入“建房出租”行业【英国长租公寓市场】，其雄心勃勃的目标是到2030年拥有5万套房产【这就会成为英国最大的房东之一】。John Lewis出售宴会主人想要的所有房子，该公司今年夏天早些时候宣布，计划建造1万套出租房产。近年来，Aviva和Legal & General等大型保险公司以及几家私募股权公司也进入了这一市场。

Britain’s private rented sector is big and growing. Almost one in five English households rented privately in 2020, up from around one in ten in 2001. It is also strikingly amateur, dominated by individual landlords who own at most a handful of properties. The largest private landlord, Grainger, a listed firm, owns around 9,000 units, giving a market share below 0.2%.

英国的私人租赁业规模庞大，而且还在不断增长。到2020年，几乎有五分之一的英国家庭私下租房，而2001年这一比例约为十分之一。这也是一个相当惊人的业余行业，主要由个人房东主导，每个房东最多拥有几处房产。最大的房东机构Grainger（一家上市公司）拥有大约9000个房产，市场份额低于0.2%。

But during the past decade the rental sector has started to professionalise. And the build-to-rent stock, though still a small share of the total, rose from around 1,000 in 2011 to over 62,000 in mid-2021, with 133,000 more under construction or in the planning process (see chart). Analysts reckon that, by the end of the decade, one in five newly built homes could be institutionally owned and rented out.

但在过去的十年里，租赁行业已经开始专业化。而“先建后租”存量虽然只占总存量的一小部分，但已从2011年的1000个左右增加到2021年年中的超过62000个，还有13.3万个在建或规划中(见图表)。分析人士估计，到本世纪末，五分之一的新建房屋将被机构拥有并出租。

![image-20210903103453039](/Users/chenyujie/Library/Application Support/typora-user-images/image-20210903103453039.png)

One reason for the boom, says Andrew Burrell of Capital Economics, a consultancy, is that retail and office properties are comparatively unappealing. Many investors wish to keep some capital in British real estate, but fear that shopping centres and high streets are in long-term decline, and that office rentals will be hit by the post-pandemic rise in working from home. Residential demand shifts mainly with demography and is less buffeted by the business cycle. People will still need somewhere to sleep, says Lawrence Bowles of Savills, a property firm: “You can’t digitalise a bed.” And in an era of low interest rates, steady rental income becomes more attractive.

comparatively 相比较地

buffet 打击

Capital Economics咨询公司的Andrew Burrell说，房地产繁荣的一个原因是零售和办公房产相对来说没有吸引力。许多投资者希望将部分资金留在英国的房地产市场，但他们担心，购物中心和商业街的规模正在长期下降，而办公室租金也将受到疫情后在家办公人数增加的影响。住宅需求主要随人口变化而变化，受商业周期的冲击较小。人们仍然需要一个睡觉的地方，Savills房地产公司的Lawrence Bowles说:“你无法把一张床数字化。” 在低利率时代，稳定的租金收入变得更有吸引力。

A decade ago most British build-to-rent projects were at the premium end of the market. They came with bells and whistles such as gyms, concierge services and even dog-washing stations. But that has begun to change. The fastest growth is now to be found in single-family homes, rather than flats, typically on the outskirts of big cities such as Birmingham, London and Manchester. Families with children are often unwilling to move, since changing schools would disrupt their children’s education. That makes them ideal tenants for big, professional landlords, who tend to have longer time horizons than individuals whose entire wealth is tied up in just a handful of properties. The coming decade is likely to see further diversification, say those familiar with the sector, for example into schemes that offer shared ownership or are purpose-built for retired people.

diversification 多样化

十年前，英国大多数“先建后租”项目都处于高端市场。它们配备了健身房、礼宾服务、甚至狗狗洗澡站等花哨的设施。但这种情况已经开始改变。如今，增长最快的是独栋住宅，而不是公寓，尤其是在伯明翰、伦敦和曼彻斯特等大城市的郊区。有孩子的家庭通常不愿意搬家，因为换学校会影响孩子的教育。这使得他们成为大型专业房东的理想租客，这些房东的租期往往比个人更长，而个人房东的全部财富都集中在少数几处房产上。熟悉该行业的人士表示，未来10年可能会出现进一步的多元化，例如进入股权共享或专为退休人士设计的计划。

Institutional interest in their wares is a boon for big housebuilders. Lloyds will be working with Britain’s largest, Barratt Developments. And it is well-timed, since another source of demand may soon weaken. Since 2013 the government has subsidised mortgages through “Help to Buy”, which provides equity loans that allow borrowers to buy with smaller deposits. But the scheme’s rules were tightened in April, and it is due to be phased out by 2023. That will make shifting stock much harder, says the boss of one big building firm, so a build-to-rent deal makes a lot of sense. “We can accelerate building because we’ve already got an institutional buyer.” ■

well-timed 适时的

phase out 逐步废除

机构对他们产品的兴趣对大型房屋建筑商来说是有好处的。劳埃德银行将与英国最大的银行Barratt Developments合作。由于另一个需求来源可能很快就会减弱，这是一个好时机。自2013年以来，政府通过“帮助购买”补贴抵押贷款，提供股权贷款，允许借款人用较少的存款购买。但该计划的规则在今年4月收紧，并将于2023年逐步取消。一家大型建筑公司的老板说，这将使库存周转更加困难，因此“先建后租”的交易很有意义。“我们可以加速建设，因为我们已经有了机构买家。”■
