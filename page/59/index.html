Will America’s housing boom lead to another financial crisis?

美国的房地产繁荣会引发另一场金融危机吗?

A prettier picture

一幅绝美的图片

![image-20210624100231244](/Users/chenyujie/Library/Application Support/typora-user-images/image-20210624100231244.png)

It is hard not to feel uneasy, but lending standards are much higher

很难不感到不安，但贷款标准要高得多

Jun 19th 2021

A FAVOURED PASTIME for city dwellers on holiday to quainter towns and villages is to peruse the windows of local property firms and dream of swapping their cramped two-bedroom flat for an entire house and garden. Your correspondent is not immune to the appeal: she gazed wistfully at a pretty house near the Deschutes river in Bend, Oregon, situated among the lakes and peaks of the Cascade mountains (pictured). She dutifully checked the listing price on Zillow, a real-estate platform, only to face grim reality: the three-bedroom house was worth $1.25m, a 44% increase from a year earlier, yielding a price per square foot higher than Queens and most of Washington, DC.

quainter 古色古香的；老派的；老式的

peruse 细读

be immune from/to sth 不受某事影响

wistfully 伤感地

城市居民在去古色古香的城镇和村庄度假时，最喜欢的消遣方式是仔细阅读当地房地产公司的窗户，梦想着把狭小的两居室公寓换成整个房子和花园。我也无法抗拒这种吸引力: 她怅然地凝视着俄勒冈州本德的德舒特河附近的一间漂亮的房子，它坐落于湖泊和喀斯喀特山脉的山峰之间(如图)。她认真地在房地产平台Zillow上查看了挂牌价格，面对她的却是残酷的现实:这套三居室的房子价值125万美元，比一年前上涨了44%，每平方英尺的价格比皇后区和华盛顿特区大部分地区都要高。

It is hard not to feel unease at the spectacle America’s housing market is making of itself. House prices have risen 13% on the year, the biggest jump since before the 2007-09 financial crisis. Inventories of homes for sale have plummeted: there are so few on offer in America that there are currently more agents than there are listings. The typical home sells in 17 days, a record low, for 1.7% more than its asking price, a record high. When Redfin, another property platform, conducted its annual survey of around 2,000 homebuyers, 63% reported having bid for a home they had not seen in person. The last boom in house prices was followed by a deep and painful recession. Is history likely to repeat itself?

对于美国房地产市场正在制造的奇观，人们很难不感到不安。今年房价上涨了13%，这是自2007-09年金融危机以来的最大涨幅。待售房屋的库存直线下降:在美国，可供出售的房屋太少了，以至于目前代理房屋的数量比待售房屋的数量还要多。一般的房屋会在17天内售出，创历史新低，比要价高1.7%，创历史新高。另一家房地产平台Redfin对约2000名购房者进行的年度调查显示，63%的购房者表示，他们在没有亲眼见过的情况下竞购过一套房子。上一次房价的繁荣之后是一场深刻而痛苦的衰退。历史会重演吗?

Consider the mixed news first. The average loan to value of a new mortgage in America is a reasonable-sounding 83%. On the reassuring side, this figure has not crept higher even as prices have soared. The worrying aspect is that borrowers are bifurcated. If a homebuyer can put up 20% of the value of a property, they do not have to buy private mortgage insurance. As such around 40% of borrowers make a 20% or greater down-payment. Most of the rest—more than half—put down less than 10%. Given how rapidly prices in some markets have soared, a house-price slump could leave some of them underwater.

先考虑一下喜忧参半的消息。在美国，新抵押贷款的平均贷款抵押价值比是83%，这听起来还算合理。令人欣慰的是，即使价格飙升，这一数字也没有飙升。令人担忧的方面是，借款者是一分为二的。如果购房者可以支付房产价值的20%，他们就不必购买私人抵押贷款保险。因此，约40%的借款人支付20%或更多的首付。剩下的大多数（超过半数人）首付不到10%。考虑到一些市场的价格飙升速度之快，房价暴跌可能会让一些市场处于水深火热中。

And yet compared with the past, borrowers are in much better financial shape. Just a quarter of mortgages originated between 2004 and 2007 were for people with “very good” credit scores (above 760). An eighth of borrowers were “subprime”, with scores below 620. Standards are higher now. In 2019 60% of mortgages were made to those with scores above 760. This share climbed further during the covid-19 pandemic as banks, fearing losses, tightened lending standards: 73% of mortgages made in the first quarter of 2021 went to borrowers with very good credit scores. Just 1.4% went to subprime borrowers.

然而，与过去相比，借款人的财务状况要好得多。在2004年至2007年间发放的抵押贷款中，仅有四分之一的抵押贷款是为信用评级“非常好”（高于760分）的人。八分之一的借款人属于“次级”借款人，得分低于620分。现在的标准更高了。2019年，60%的抵押贷款发放给了得分在760以上的人。这一比例在新冠肺炎大流行期间进一步上升，因为银行担心损失，收紧了贷款标准:2021年第一季度73%的抵押贷款流向了信用评分非常好的借款人。只有1.4%流向次级借款人。

There is anecdotal evidence of caution from mortgage bankers, too. They are reputedly calling up workplaces to ensure that employees relocating out of the big, high-cost-of-living cities will be allowed to work remotely indefinitely. One bank reports that its busiest lending businesses have been those catering to the well-heeled: mortgages for second homes and “jumbo” mortgages (those bigger than $550,000). If the roaring housing market of the mid-2000s was the product of reckless lending to unreliable borrowers, the boom now is made of different stuff: large loans made to wealthy borrowers with long credit histories in search of greener pastures. ■

reputedly 据说

well-heeled 有钱的

有传闻表明，抵押贷款银行家也持谨慎态度。据说，他们正在给工作场所打电话，以确保从生活成本高的大城市迁来的员工可以无限期地远程工作。一家银行报告称，其最繁忙的贷款业务是为有钱人服务的:为第二套住房抵押贷款和“巨额”抵押贷款(超过55万美元)。如果说2000年代中期房地产市场的繁荣是不顾后果地向不可靠的借款者放贷的产物，那么现在的繁荣则是由不同的东西构成的:向富有的借款者发放巨额贷款，这些借款者拥有长期的信贷记录，目的是寻找更好的发展机会。■

How to pick the best days to work from home

如何选择在家工作的最佳时间

Bartleby

巴托比

![image-20210623100646928](/Users/chenyujie/Library/Application Support/typora-user-images/image-20210623100646928.png)

Timing is everything

选好时机就是一切

Jun 19th 2021

HYBRID WORKING may be the future but that raises the question of how it will actually be organised. Will companies let their employees choose which days they come in to the office, and which days they are at home? And what about working hours? If employees do get a choice, they clearly need a strategy to maximise their visibility and minimise the stress. So this columnist has a few tips about which days you should opt to work from home.

混合式工作可能是未来的趋势，但这引出了一个问题，即它将如何被安排。公司会让员工自己选择哪天来办公室，哪天在家吗?那工作时间呢? 如果员工确实有选择，他们显然需要一种策略来最大化自己的关注度，最小化自己的压力。因此，对于哪些天你应该选择在家工作，本栏作家给出了一些建议。

Monday: Too obvious. You might as well say, “I’ve been drinking all weekend and I’m too hung over to come in.” In the 18th and early 19th centuries, when people were paid on Saturdays, absenteeism on the first official day of the working week was so common it was known as “Saint Monday”, because it felt almost like a second sabbath.

Saint Monday 工作很少的星期一

absenteeism 缺勤；缺课

星期一:太明显了。你可能会说:“我整个周末都在喝酒，宿醉得不能来上班了。”在18世纪和19世纪早期，当人们在周六领取工资时，在每周的第一个工作日旷工就很普遍了，所以被称为“圣周一”，因为它几乎感觉像是第二个安息日。

From the working point of view, Mondays are usually a day when some sort of team meeting is held and the priorities are set for the rest of the week. It would seem best to head in to the office on that day and postpone the time for solitary working until later in the week. Showing your enthusiasm to the boss by turning up on the first day of the week is probably a good idea as well.

turn up 到来

从工作的角度来看，周一通常是召开某种团队会议的日子，以及确定一周其他时间优先事项的日子。似乎最好在那天去办公室，把独处工作的时间推迟到本周晚些时候。在一周的第一天去上班，向老板展示你的热情可能也是一个好主意。

Tuesday: Some people might not like the pattern created by spending Tuesdays at home, as it breaks the working week into two unequal chunks. Not Bartleby, who used to work from home that day before the pandemic struck, completing the column (taking out the typos, axing the non sequiturs and polishing the puns). Still, besides breaking up the week, home working on Tuesday also seems likely to be a day few others might choose. And then there is nothing more satisfying than looking out of the window and watching everyone else heading off to work, while you sit in your slippers, sipping a coffee.

typos 排印错误

non sequiturs 前后不连贯的陈述

puns 双关语

head off 朝着..移动

周二:有些人可能不喜欢周二宅在家里，因为这样会把一周的工作分成两个不平等的部分。巴托比不是这样的人，在疫情爆发前，他曾在家里工作，完成了专栏文章(去掉拼写错误的地方，删掉前后不连贯的地方，润色双关语)。不过，周二除了将一周的工作拆开以外，选择周二在家工作的人似乎也很少。没有什么比看着窗外，看着大家都去上班，而自己却穿着拖鞋，小口抿着咖啡更让人满足的了。

Wednesday: Remote working on this day would appeal to Hercule Poirot, Agatha Christie’s detective who was fond of symmetry and order. Two days of working in the office, a day at home, two more days in the office and then a two-day weekend. It doesn’t fit with The Economist’s schedule (Wednesday is deadline day). It is nevertheless a good one to pick. Ignore the “Wednesday’s child is full of woe” propaganda. Companies will probably be happy to allow employees to choose this day, as Mondays and Fridays will be the most popular options.

周三:对Hercule Poirot(Agatha Christie笔下的侦探，喜欢对称和秩序)来说，这一天是适合远程办公的日子。两天在办公室工作，一天在家，再两天在办公室，然后是两天的周末。它不符合《经济学人》的时间表(周三是截止日)。尽管如此，它还是一个不错的选择。不要理会“周三的孩子充满悲哀”的宣传。公司可能会很乐意让员工选择这一天，因为周一和周五是最受欢迎的选择。

Thursday: Although it breaks up the working week in a similar way, this seems a less satisfactory remote working day than Tuesday. That is because, having avoided the commute on Thursday, you will have to go through the process all over again on a Friday. There is something to be said for this option, however, as it means you can start thinking about the weekend on Wednesday night.

周四:虽然它以类似的方式划分工作周，但似乎没有周二那么令人满意。这是因为，在避免了周四的通勤后，你将不得不在周五再次经历这一过程。不过，这个选择还是有好处的，因为这意味着你可以从周三晚上开始考虑周末的事情。

Friday: Like Monday, this is too suspicious a choice. Colleagues will smile knowingly and use their fingers as inverted commas when you say you are “working from home” on Fridays. Managers will take to calling you at various moments in the day to listen out for tell-tale signs of the beach or golf course.

knowingly 会意地

listen out 留心听

tell-tale 泄露秘密的

星期五:像周一一样，这是一个太令人疑惑的选择。当你说你在周五“在家工作”时，同事们会会心一笑，并手动加上了反引号。经理们会在一天中的不同时刻给你打电话，以留意是否有海滩或高尔夫球场的泄漏秘密的迹象。

There is no need to risk experiencing this managerial suspicion. Slackers have long learned that Fridays tend to be more relaxed at the office; colleagues may disappear for long lunches (or early drinks) and no one will ask too many questions if you are not at your desk after 3pm. So if you genuinely want to bunk off work, go in on Fridays and sneak in your leisure time on a different day. And if you are a diligent employee who wants to maintain a reputation for hard work, don’t choose Friday for remote working.

managerial 管理的；管理人员的

bunk off 离开

没有必要冒险经历这种管理层的怀疑。懒惰的人早就知道，周五往往在办公室更放松;同事们可能会花很长时间吃午餐(或提前喝酒)，如果你下午3点后不在办公桌前，也没有人会问你太多问题。所以，如果你真的想翘班，周五就去上班，换一天偷偷摸摸地利用你的休闲时间。如果你是一个勤奋的员工，想要保持勤奋工作的声誉，不要选择周五远程工作。

Of course, many companies may allow two days of remote working, which leads to another ten possible combinations. To avoid suspicion, don’t pick Monday/Friday or Thursday/Friday as your remote combination. Tuesday and Thursday might be a good selection, as it means you will be at the office (and thus visible) every other day.

当然，许多公司可能允许两天的远程工作，这导致了另外十种可能的组合。为了避免引起怀疑，不要选择周一/周五或周四/周五作为远程工作的组合。周二和周四可能是一个不错的选择，因为这意味着你每隔一天就会出现在办公室(因此能够引起关注)。

And then there is the possibility of flexible hours. Early risers may relish the chance to start the day at 8am, finish by 4pm and have the rest of the day to themselves. But in many companies the boss is up at the crack of dawn, so the early bird may be landed with all the work. Start at noon, and finish at 8pm, and you may find there is nobody around to bother you after 6pm, and you can safely have dinner and watch Netflix. The rules are changing and so is the potential to exploit them. To flourish in the era of remote working, employees will need the cunning of Machiavelli and the tactical brilliance of Napoleon.

an early/a late riser早起/晚起的

tactical 有谋略的；战术的

此外，还有弹性工作时间的可能性。早起的人可能喜欢上午8点开始一天的工作，下午4点结束一天的工作，剩下的时间属于自己。但在许多公司，老板在破晓时分就起床了，所以早起的鸟儿可能会把所有的工作都搞定。从中午开始，到晚上8点结束，你可能会发现周围没有人在6点之后打扰你，你可以安全地吃晚饭，看Netflix。规则在改变，利用规则的可能性也在改变。要想在远程办公的时代健康成长，员工将需要Machiavelli的狡猾和拿破仑的战术智慧。

Loss of biodiversity poses as great a risk to humanity as climate change

生物多样性的丧失和气候变化一样给人类带来巨大风险

The other environmental emergency

另一个环境紧急事件

![image-20210622101314043](/Users/chenyujie/Library/Application Support/typora-user-images/image-20210622101314043.png)

Technology has a growing role to play in monitoring, modelling and protecting ecosystems, writes Catherine Brahic

Catherine Brahic写道，技术在监测、建模和保护生态系统方面发挥着越来越重要的作用

Jun 15th 2021

HUMAN SOCIETIES depend on healthy ecosystems. People consume their products in the shape of fish, meat, crops, timber and fibres such as cotton and silk. Medicines may be directly harvested from the natural world or inspired by molecules and mechanisms found within it. The ecosystems that crops depend upon are regulated by living things. Through photosynthesis, trees and other plants take in carbon and pump out oxygen. In doing so they remove roughly 11bn tonnes of carbon dioxide from the atmosphere each year, equivalent to 27% of what human industry and agriculture emits (the oceans absorb a further 10bn tonnes).

photosynthesis 光合作用

人类社会依赖于健康的生态系统。人们消费的产品是鱼、肉、作物、木材和纤维（如棉花和丝绸）。药物可以直接从自然界中获取，也可以从自然界中发现的分子和机制中获得启发。农作物赖以生存的生态系统受到生物的调节。通过光合作用，树木和其他植物吸收碳，排出氧气。在此过程中，它们每年可以减少大气中大约110亿吨二氧化碳，相当于人类工农业排放的27%(海洋进一步吸收了100亿吨)。

The services that ecosystems provide to humanity depend, in turn, on there being a diversity of living things. More than 75% of global food-crop types, including coffee, cocoa and almonds, are pollinated by animals. The complex web underpinning every food chain and ecosystem means that the narrow range of species that humans eat and exploit cannot be sustained without the existence of a much greater diversity of animals, plants and bacteria.

almond 杏仁

underpin 巩固；加固..的基础

生态系统提供给人类的服务反过来又取决于生物的多样性。全球75%以上的食物作物，包括咖啡、可可和杏仁，都是通过动物授粉的。支撑着每个食物链和生态系统的复杂网络意味着，如果没有更加多样化的动物、植物和细菌的存在，人类食用和利用的有限物种范围将无法维持下去。

More diverse forests store more carbon than monocultures. Skipjack tuna makes up roughly half of the global tuna catch for human consumption. As young animals, they eat zooplankton, which is to say very small floating animals like tunicates, ctenophores and small crustaceans as well as the larvae of larger animals. As adults, they eat smaller fish, squid and crustaceans. To conserve the skipjack, all this diversity in its food chain must also be conserved.

Skipjack tuna 鲣鱼

zooplankton 浮游动物

larvae 幼虫

squid 鱿鱼

crustacean 甲壳纲动物

多样化的森林比单一的森林储存更多的碳。全球供人类食用的金枪鱼约占其捕获量的一半。幼年时，它们吃浮游动物，也就是非常小的漂浮动物，如被囊动物、栉水母和小型甲壳类动物，以及大型动物的幼虫。成年时，它们吃小鱼、鱿鱼和甲壳类动物。为了保护鲣鱼，它的食物链的多样性必须得到保护。

Since the 1990s, alarmed by studies showing rapid declines in animal and plant species around the globe, ecologists have talked of an impending mass extinction. It would be the sixth in the Earth’s history, but one unlike any that has come before. Surveys show that the loss of biodiversity is the result of a combination of factors: climate change, pollution, human exploitation of land, sea, plants and animals, and the displacement of some species into new territories where they play havoc with existing ecosystems. Uniquely in Earth’s history, each of these drivers of ecological change is caused by a single species: Homo sapiens.

displacement 迁移

play havoc with sth 严重扰乱 ‹plans, system›

Homo sapiens 智人；人类

自上世纪90年代以来，生态学家对全球动植物物种迅速减少的研究感到震惊，他们一直在谈论即将到来的大规模灭绝。这将是地球历史上的第六次，但这次与以往任何一次都不同。调查显示，生物多样性的丧失是多种因素共同作用的结果: 气候变化、污染、人类对陆地、海洋、动植物的利用，以及一些物种迁移到新的地区，严重扰乱现有的生态系统。在地球历史上独一无二的是，这些生态变化的驱动力都是由单一物种引起的:人类。

When IPBES (the Intergovernmental Science-Policy Platform on Biodiversity and Ecosystem Services, similar to the Intergovernmental Panel on Climate Change) published its assessment of the state of global biodiversity in 2019, it offered a sobering picture. Roughly 1m animal and plant species were deemed to be at risk of extinction, more than at any other point in human history. These included many that are used in farming. At least 9% of the 6,200 breeds of domesticated mammals that humans eat, or use to produce food, had become extinct by 2016, and at least 1,000 more are threatened. More than one-third of continental land area and nearly three-quarters of freshwater resources are used to produce crops or livestock, but environmental degradation has damaged the land’s ability to support these activities. And one-third of marine fish stocks were being unsustainably exploited in 2015.

Intergovernmental 政府间的

当IPBES(政府间生物多样性和生态系统服务科学政策平台，类似于政府间气候变化专门委员会)于2019年发布其对全球生物多样性状况的评估时，它提供了一幅令人清醒的图景。大约有100万种动植物物种面临灭绝的危险，这个数字比人类历史上任何时候都要多。其中包括许多用于农业的。截至2016年，人类食用或用来生产食物的6200种驯养哺乳动物中至少有9%已经灭绝，另有至少1000种受到威胁。超过三分之一的陆地面积和近四分之三的淡水资源被用于生产作物或牲畜，但环境退化破坏了土地支持这些活动的能力。2015年，三分之一的海洋鱼类资源被不可持续地开发利用。

The biodiversity crisis poses as great a risk to human societies as climate change. Yet it has a fraction of the public profile. In part that is because the loss of biodiversity cannot be neatly quantified, as climate change can, into parts per million of carbon dioxide, or degrees above pre-industrial average temperatures. And the webs that link species within and across ecosystems are even more complex than the processes that drive climate change.

生物多样性危机给人类社会带来的风险与气候变化一样大。然而，它却不能引起公众的注视。这在一定程度上是因为生物多样性的丧失不能像气候变化那样精确地量化为二氧化碳的百万分之几，或者比工业化前的平均温度高几度。同时将生态系统内和跨生态系统的物种连接起来的网络，甚至比驱动气候变化的过程更为复杂。

Understanding a problem, however, is a necessary step towards solving it. And that is where technology can help. This Technology Quarterly will consider its role in monitoring, preserving and restoring ecosystems and species. Only by measuring the state of ecosystems can their health be assessed, losses be quantified, and the effectiveness of interventions be evaluated.

然而，理解问题是解决问题的必要步骤。而这正是科技可以帮助的地方。本栏目（“Technology Quarterly ”）将审议其在监测、保护和恢复生态系统和物种方面的作用。只有通过测量生态系统的状态，才能评估它们的健康程度，量化损失，并评估干预措施的有效性。

As well as monitoring biodiversity, technology can also be deployed to protect it. And in some cases it may even be able to reverse losses, by bringing extinct species back from the dead. Ironically, it is humanity’s use of technology, whether in simple forms such as chainsaws or dragnets, or more complex ones such as modern agriculture and transportation, that is chiefly responsible for biodiversity loss. The challenge now is to deploy it so that it is not just part of the problem, but part of the solution. ■

AN ANTIBODY THERAPY from Regeneron, a firm in upstate New York, improves the survival of patients with covid-19 and offers renewed hope for the treatment of those most seriously ill with the disease. A study in British hospitals found that Regen-Cov saved the lives of many of those unable to make their own antibodies in response to SARS-CoV-2. Such “seronegative” individuals constituted about a third of the 9,785 hospital patients in the study. As compared with a control group given standard treatment (either a steroid called dexamethasone or, for the sickest, an anti-inflammatory known as tocilizumab), 20% more patients survived. Regen-Cov also reduced the median length of hospital stays from 17 to 13 days.

纽约州边远地区的Regeneron公司推出的抗体疗法提高了covid-19患者的存活率，并为治疗最严重的covid-19患者带来了新的希望。英国医院的一项研究发现，Regen-Cov挽救了许多无法产生自身抗体来应对SARS-CoV-2的患者的生命。这类“血清阴性”的个体在9785名研究中的医院病人中占了大约三分之一。与接受标准治疗(类固醇地塞米松或对病情最严重的患者使用消炎药托珠单抗)的对照组相比，存活率增加了20%。Regen-Cov还将平均住院时间从17天缩短到13天。

Regen-Cov is a combination of two monoclonal antibodies, known as casirivimab and imdevimab. Antibodies are immune-system proteins that disable pathogens by locking specifically onto them. Both casirivimab and imdevimab bind to different sites on the coronavirus’s “spike” protein, preventing the virus from infecting cells. Using two antibodies instead of one reduces the risk of the virus evolving resistance to the treatment.

Until now, trials have not made a convincing case for the widespread use of antibody therapies for covid-19. A year ago, the same Recovery trial run by British scientists also discovered that dexamethasone would save patients’ lives.

到目前为止，试验还没有提供令人信服的证据可以证明广泛使用抗体疗法治疗covid-19。其中一个问题是，未能明确确定哪些患者可以从这些昂贵的治疗中获益。这是首次有足够大的研究表明，这种疗法可以降低因严重covid-19住院患者的死亡率。一年前，英国科学家进行的同样的康复试验也发现地塞米松可以挽救病人的生命。

Regen-Cov already has emergency authorisation for use in America, Brazil, Canada, the European Union and India, but remains an investigational drug in Britain and elsewhere. Even if authorisation follows in many other countries, though, access to it is likely to remain extremely restricted.

Regen-Cov已经获得美国、巴西、加拿大、欧盟和印度的紧急批准，但在英国和其他地方仍是一种研究药物。然而，即使在其他许多国家也获得了授权，获取它的途径可能仍然受到极大限制。

So far, trials for covid-19 antibody-therapies have generally been disappointing, but news of Regen-Cov’s success is likely to reinvigorate interest in them. 到目前为止，covid-19抗体疗法的试验总是令人失望，但Regen-Cov成功的消息可能会重新激发人们对这些疗法的兴趣。在去年12月报道的一项临床试验中，Eli Lilly公司的抗体疗法LY-CoV555未能给住院患者带来好处，而在6月15日，阿斯利康公司的一种类似药物在给那些接触过病毒的患者使用后，其结果也令人失望。然而，如果将这些药物用于康复试验中确定的范围较小的患者群体，它们可能会带来益处。

Should demand for Regen-Cov now increase, though, it is not clear how quickly supply could be improved—even in countries that have the money to pay for it. Monoclonal-antibody production lines are currently used to make cancer and arthritis drugs, which are also badly needed. Moreover, supplies of much of the equipment required, including biobags (giant disposable plastic bags used to make drugs), filters and sterilisation gear, are already stretched thin by demands from vaccine manufacturers. The Recovery study also seems to settle a scientific debate about whether it is worth giving antiviral therapies to covid-19 patients when they arrive in hospital. It is. It had been thought that, by the time a patient arrived, only anti-inflammatory drugs, which treat damage already done by the virus, were relevant. Knowledge that patients who are seronegative are able to benefit from antiviral therapies opens up further lines of investigation for antiviral drugs. In the Recovery trial, untreated seronegative patients were twice as likely to die as those who were able to mount an antibody response.

康复研究似乎还解决了一场科学辩论，即是否值得在covid-19患者入院时给予抗病毒治疗。它是值得的。人们一直认为，当病人入院时，只有治疗病毒已经造成的损害的消炎药才是有意义的。了解到血清阴性的患者可以从抗病毒治疗中获益，这为抗病毒药物的进一步研究开辟了道路。在康复试验中，未接受治疗的血清阴性患者的死亡率是那些能够产生抗体反应的患者的两倍。

For doctors, the trial raises an intriguing question. If they have patients languishing at home with covid-19, might it be worth offering a cheap lateral flow test to try to identify those who are not mounting an immune response? If it is possible to spot those most likely to end up in hospital, and stop that happening, it would help to weaken the virus’s hold on patients, and on society at large.

对医生来说，这次试验提出了一个有趣的问题。如果他们有患者在家里受covid-19困扰，是否值得提供一种廉价的横向血流测试，以试图确定哪些人没有产生免疫反应? 如果能够发现最有可能住院的人，并阻止这种情况发生，这将有助于削弱病毒对患者和整个社会的控制。

这在covid-19大流行之前非常重要。现在这一点更加重要了。2010年，在线零售商亚马逊(Amazon)在该市建立了一个50万平方英尺的配送中心，并成为当地最大的雇主。该公司目前正在考虑再增加11万平方英尺的面积。近年来，瑞典家具供应商宜家(IKEA)和英国最大的连锁超市Tesco也将配送仓库搬到了郊区。所有这些都意味着现在是在彼得伯勒寻找仓库工作的好时机。

But it is not just warehouse workers who find themselves in demand. In Peterborough, and across the country, the reopening of bricks-and-mortar retail and hospitality businesses has triggered a surge in hiring. Bosses complain of labour shortages. For the first time in decades, bargaining power is shifting from firms to low-paid workers.

bargaining power：the ability of a person or group to get what they want

但并不只是仓库工人发现自己有需求。在彼得伯勒和全国各地，实体零售和酒店业务的重新开业引发了招聘热潮。老板们抱怨劳动力短缺。几十年来，议价能力第一次从公司转移到了低薪工人身上。

It is an unusual situation for Britain. After the global financial crisis, the country experienced a job-rich, pay-poor recovery—thanks to a flexible labour market created by deregulation in the 1980s and 1990s. Employment rates reached record highs, but wages did not regain their pre-crisis levels until 2020. Economists were repeatedly forced to revise estimates of how far unemployment could fall before generating sustained wage pressure. It was only a year or so ago that “we were starting to see what a tight but flexible jobs market would look like,” says Gavin Kelly of the Resolution Foundation, a think-tank. Then the pandemic hit and recession followed.

这对英国来说是一个不寻常的情况。在全球金融危机之后，由于20世纪80年代和90年代的放松管制创造了一个灵活的劳动力市场，这个国家经历了一个工作机会多，报酬少的复苏阶段。就业率达到了历史新高，但工资直到2020年才恢复到危机前的水平。在产生持续的工资压力之前，经济学家们被迫不断修正对失业率下降幅度的估计。智囊团 Resolution Foundation的Gavin Kelly说，大约一年前，“我们才开始看到一个紧张但灵活的就业市场会是什么样子。”然后，疫情袭来，经济衰退随之而来。

Peterborough’s economy, which began its recovery early, offers a glimpse of the future. An analysis of labour markets in 63 urban areas by the Centre for Cities, another think-tank, found that in April distribution hubs were seeing the fastest growth in vacancies. Online advertisements for positions in Peterborough were already 4% above pre-pandemic levels before retail and hospitality reopened (at a time when they were still down by 15% in the capital). As more firms have reopened, demand for workers has stepped up. Bars and cafés in central Peterborough are littered with “help wanted” posters. A local pub owner says he has stopped serving lunch because he is unable to find a chef.

step up 增加

较早开始复苏的彼得伯勒经济让我们能够一窥未来。另一个智囊团Centre for Cities对63个城市的劳动力市场进行了分析，发现4月份配送中心的职位空缺比例增长最快。零售和酒店重新开业之前，彼得伯勒的在线招聘广告已经比流行病爆发前的水平高出4%(当时在首都的职位仍下降了15%)。随着越来越多的公司重新开业，对工人的需求也在增加。彼得伯勒市中心的酒吧和cafés到处张贴着“招聘”海报。当地一家酒吧的老板说，因为找不到厨师，他已经停止供应午餐。

Rising vacancies have not yet been accompanied by rising wages. But that may change. Pawel Adrjan, an economist at Indeed, an online-recruitment company, notes that advertised pay in food preparation and service has nudged up from £9.25 ($13.11) at the start of the year to £9.40 now. Asked if he might have to raise wages in order to attract a chef, the Peterborough pub owner pauses for a moment, before admitting “it might come to that”. For employers accustomed to being able to hire at will, at a price that suits them, the past few weeks have come as a shock.

at will 自由地

职位空缺的增加并没有伴随着工资的上涨。但这种情况可能会改变。在线招聘公司Indeed的经济学家Pawel Adrjan注意到，食品准备和服务的广告薪酬已经从年初的9.25英镑(13.11美元)上升到现在的9.40英镑。当被问及他是否可能不得不提高工资来吸引厨师时，这位彼得伯勒酒吧老板停顿了一下，然后承认“可能会到那个地步”。对于习惯于以适合他们自己的价格随意招聘员工的雇主来说，过去几周的情况令人震惊。

It is not just the post-pandemic recovery that is causing shortages. Although the exact number is unknown, lots of migrants appear to have left the country—something that is proving especially difficult for firms that rely on seasonal workers and in sectors with low pay and long, anti-social hours. Unless tweaked by the government, the post-Brexit immigration regime will continue to reduce the number of low-paid workers who are able to make it to Britain.

造成短缺的不仅仅是大流行后的复苏。虽然确切的数字还不清楚，但很多移民似乎已经离开了这个国家，这对于那些依赖季节工的公司和那些工资低、工作时间长、反社会的行业来说尤为困难。除非政府做出调整，否则脱欧后的移民制度将继续减少能够来到英国的低收入工人的数量。

Participation rates have also dipped among older workers, suggesting the recession prompted 60-somethings to bring forward their retirement. Some younger folk are reluctant to work, too. Covid-19 vaccine bookings in England only opened for 25-to-29-year-olds on June 8th, meaning most 20-somethings remain at a higher risk of infection. Bosses note that many are reluctant to work in crowded rooms.

bring forward 把..提前

年长工人的参与率也有所下降，这表明经济衰退促使60多岁的人提前退休。一些年轻人也不愿意工作。6月8日，英国2019冠状病毒病疫苗仅向25至29岁的人群开放，这意味着大多数20多岁的人仍然面临更高的感染风险。老板们注意到许多人不愿意在拥挤的房间里工作。

Some of these disruptions will ease over the coming months. Youngsters will receive their jabs. Martin Beck of Oxford Economics, a consultancy, notes that 5m EU workers have applied for settled status in Britain since Brexit. “Even if a lot did go home during the pandemic, the obstacles to coming back won’t be particularly onerous,” he points out. Most forecasters expect unemployment to rise later in the year, as support is pulled back. Tom Clougherty of the Centre for Policy Studies, a think-tank, suggests that the end of the furlough scheme, due to be tapered away by September, “will be a real test of just how strong the labour market is”.

其中一些干扰将在未来几个月得到缓解。年轻人将接受疫苗注射。牛津经济咨询公司的Martin Beck指出，自英国脱欧以来，已有500万欧盟工人申请在英国定居。他指出:“即使很多人在疫情期间回家，但是回来的障碍也不会特别大。”大多数预测人士预计，随着支持措施的撤回，失业率将在今年晚些时候上升。智囊团政策研究中心的Tom Clougherty认为，休假计划将于9月逐步结束，“这将是对劳动力市场有多强劲的真正考验”。

The bite of Britain’s minimum wage, the ratio of that wage to median earnings, has increased markedly in recent years relative to other rich countries (see chart). The government is aiming to increase it further, to 65%, by 2024. The hope is this will force firms to train staff, invest in technology and thus raise productivity.

然而，即使一些议价权回到了雇主手中，这些行业的工人也将从上涨的最低工资中受益。与其他发达国家相比，英国最低工资(即最低工资与中位数收入之比)的影响近年来显著增加(见图表)。政府的目标是到2024年将这一比例进一步提高到65%。希望这将迫使企业培训员工，投资技术，从而提高生产率。

Remote workers work longer, not more efficiently

远程工作者工作的时间更长，而不是效率更高

Bartleby

巴托比

![image-20210616150458568](/Users/chenyujie/Library/Application Support/typora-user-images/image-20210616150458568.png)

A new study on remote working

一项关于远程工作的新研究

Jun 10th 2021

THE RETURN to the office is well under way, just as summer in the northern hemisphere begins. Pretty soon, people will be able to resume the habit of staring wistfully out of the window, hoping it will still be sunny at the weekend. As many workers embrace a hybrid pattern, perhaps commuting 2-3 days a week, the experiment in full-time home-working is ending. At the same time, assessments of its effectiveness are proliferating.

wistfully 伤感地

proliferate 激增

“重返职场”正在进行中，就像北半球的夏天刚刚开始一样。很快，人们就能恢复那种伤感地望着窗外发呆的习惯，希望周末依然是晴天。随着许多员工接受一种混合模式，也许每周通勤2-3天，全职在家工作的试验正在结束。与此同时，对其有效性的评估正在激增。

Early surveys of employees and employers found that remote work did not reduce productivity. But a new study* of more than 10,000 employees at an Asian technology company between April 2019 and August 2020 paints a different picture. The firm uses software installed on employees’ computers that tracked which applications or websites were active, and whether the employee was using the keyboard or a mouse. (Shopping online didn’t count.)

对员工和雇主的早期调查发现，远程工作并没有降低生产率。但2019年4月至2020年8月，对一家亚洲科技公司的1万多名员工进行的一项新研究却呈现出不同的景象。该公司使用安装在员工电脑上的软件来追踪哪些应用程序或网站是活跃的，以及员工是在使用键盘还是鼠标。(网购不算在内。)

The research certainly concluded that the employees were working hard. Total hours worked were 30% higher than before the pandemic, including an 18% increase in working outside normal hours. But this extra effort did not translate into any rise in output. This may explain the earlier survey evidence; both employers and employees felt they were producing as much as before. But the correct way to measure productivity is output per working hour. With all that extra time on the job, this fell by 20%.

研究的结论肯定是员工工作很努力。总工时比大流行前增加了30%，其中非正常工作时间增加了18%。但这一额外努力并没有在产出方面转化为任何的增长。这可以解释早期的调查证据;雇主和雇员都觉得他们的产出和以前一样多。但衡量生产率的正确方法是每小时工作的产出。加上所有这些额外的工作时间，这一数字下降了20%。

The interesting thing is why this happened. The academics were able to analyse how much time the employees spent in “collaboration hours”, defined as various types of meetings, and how much time they had as “focus hours”, uninterrupted by calls or emails, where they could concentrate on their tasks. Despite working longer hours, the employees had less focus time than before the pandemic. Instead, all their extra time was taken up by meetings. Long-time readers may recall Bartleby’s law: 80% of the time of 80% of the people in meetings is wasted. This study certainly offers evidence for the proposition.

有趣的是为什么会这样。这些学者能够分析员工花在“协作时间”(定义为各种类型的会议)上的时间，以及有多少时间是“专注时间”(不受电话或电子邮件的打扰，以便专心完成任务)。尽管工作时间更长，但员工的专注时间比疫情前更少。相反，他们所有的额外时间都被会议占用了。老读者可能还记得巴托比定律:会议中80%的人80%的时间都被浪费了。这项研究无疑为这一命题提供了证据。

One possibility is that managers are less certain of their team’s commitment and are holding more meetings to check on them. Another is that managers call so many meetings to validate their own existence when they are not in the office. However, the academics suggest the greater need for meetings is the result of the greater difficulty of co-ordinating employees when they are working remotely—another hint that the process is inefficient. When working remotely, employees also spend less time being evaluated, trained and coached.

一种可能是经理们对于他们团队的承诺不太确定，因此召开更多的会议来检查他们的承诺。另一个原因是，经理们召开如此多的会议，是为了证明他们不在办公室的时候自己是存在的。然而，学者们认为，对会议的更大需求，是因为当员工远程工作时，协调他们的工作变得更困难了，这是远程工作效率低下的另一个迹象。当远程工作时，员工花在评估、培训和指导上的时间也更少。

This seems a raw deal for the employees. They received no more money for the overtime. Although they saved commuting time, this did not offset the extra hours spent in meetings. Not all workers behaved the same way. Those who had worked at the company the longest tended to be more productive, suggesting that they found it easier to navigate the hazards of home-working. Employees with children worked around 20 minutes a day more than those without, implying an even greater fall in their productivity, presumably because they were distracted by child-care duties.

presumably 大概

distract 使..分心

这对员工来说似乎不公平。他们没有再得到加班费。虽然他们节省了通勤时间，但这并不能抵消花在会议上的额外时间。并非所有工人的行为都是一样的。那些在公司工作时间最长的人往往效率更高，这表明他们会发现自己更容易应对在家工作的危险。有孩子的员工比没有孩子的员工每天多工作20分钟左右，这意味着他们的工作效率下降得更大，可能是因为他们被照顾孩子的责任分散了注意力。

So does this mean companies will abandon remote working altogether, even its hybrid version? The academics point out that the staff at the firm under study are nearly all college-educated and their roles “involve significant cognitive work, developing new software or hardware applications or solutions, collaborating with teams of professionals, working with clients, and engaging in innovation and continuous improvement”. Such work may have posed a particular challenge in remote settings, compared with occupations like responding to customer calls, say, where employees may work to a scripted set of replies.

那么，这是否意味着公司将完全放弃远程工作，甚至是其混合模式?学者们指出，被调查的公司的员工几乎都受过大学教育，他们的角色“涉及重要的认知工作，开发新的软件或硬件应用程序或解决方案，与专业团队合作，与客户合作，并参与创新和持续改进”。与接听客户电话（员工可能会按照一套脚本的回复来工作）等职业相比，这类工作在远程环境中可能会带来特殊的挑战。

It is hardly surprising that there would be some teething and co-ordination problems involved with remote working. The practice was, after all, imposed suddenly. The study stopped last August and one wonders whether employees have learned to use their time more efficiently since then. And the research shows that employees were able to achieve as much output with slightly less “focus time” than they had at the office. The real source of inefficiency—surprise, surprise—was the time spent in meetings. And the answer is simple; don’t call as many, and keep them short.

远程工作可能会出现一些初期和协调问题，这并不令人意外。毕竟，这种做法是突然强加的。这项研究于去年8月停止，人们想知道员工们从那以后是否学会了更有效地利用时间。研究表明，比起在办公室工作，员工在“专注时间”略少的情况下也能取得同样多的产出。令人惊讶的是，效率低下的真正根源是花在会议上的时间。答案很简单;不要打太多电话，尽量简短。

* “Work from home & productivity: evidence from personnel & analytics data on IT professionals”, by Michael Gibbs, Friederike Mengel and Christoph Siemroth

The credibility of American leadership is vital not just for the struggle against the pandemic. It will be essential for the even greater global test on the horizon: grappling with climate change.

rhetoric 虚夸的言辞

拜登喜欢强调“美国回来了”。尽管措辞令人耳目一新，但他的政府的一些行动，尤其是它的“购买美国货”法令，该法令加强了联邦政府对国内供应商的优惠政策，而不是外国供应商，看起来更像是“美国优先”的保护主义，而不是一种新的国际主义。全世界都在等待美国重新介入的更多实际证据，以及它团结盟友的能力。美国领导地位的可信度不仅对抗击疫情至关重要。这对即将到来的更大的全球考验至关重要:应对气候变化。

After four years when America often seemed more interested in kicking sand in the world’s face than helping it to its feet, vaccines provide it with the ideal means to rehabilitate its image. The United States and its allies displayed great technological mettle by developing several highly effective vaccines and scaling up their production in record time. They therefore have something tangible and urgently needed to offer the rest of the world. Mr Biden is unlikely to get a better chance to demonstrate the benefits of American leadership, and the power of democracy and free markets to boot. Making sure that the rest of the world is inoculated as quickly as possible would be a shot in the arm for America and its allies, too. ■

to boot 此外

四年来，美国似乎更感兴趣的是在世界面前踢沙子，而不是帮助它站稳脚跟。如今，疫苗为美国提供了恢复形象的理想手段。美国及其盟国通过开发几种高效疫苗并在创纪录的时间内扩大生产规模，显示出巨大的技术勇气。因此，它们有一些切实的、迫切需要向世界其他国家提供的东西。此外，拜登不太可能有更好的机会来展示美国领导的好处，以及民主和自由市场的力量。确保世界其他国家尽快接种疫苗，对美国及其盟友来说也是一剂强心针。■
