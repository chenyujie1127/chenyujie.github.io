Activist investors are needed more than ever

现在比以往任何时候都更需要激进投资者

Seize the day (and the board)

抓住每一天(和董事会)

![image-20230525090439726](/Users/chenyujie/Library/Application Support/typora-user-images/image-20230525090439726.png)

Low rates, passive investing and ESG have left opportunities for active shareholders

低利率、被动投资和ESG为激进股东留下了机会

May 24th 2023

Little scares the c-suite like shareholder activism. Bosses stay awake worrying about a call, a letter or a 100-page presentation in which a hedge fund outlines the depths of their ineptitude. At the start of the year executives were especially on edge. During this year’s annual “proxy season”—a succession of shareholder meetings—they have mostly avoided votes on dissident nominees to their boards. Nevertheless in recent months some of the world’s largest firms—including Alphabet, Bayer, Disney and Salesforce—have had to tussle with activists, who are increasingly focused on the biggest companies. A battle between Carl Icahn, a prominent activist, and Illumina, a genomics giant, is set to come to a head on May 25th.

没有什么比股东激进主义更让高管们害怕的了。老板们睡不着觉，担心一个电话、一封信或一份长达100页的报告，报告中对冲基金概述了他们的无能到什么程度。今年年初，高管们尤其紧张不安。在今年的年度“代理季”(一连串的股东大会)中，他们大多避免对董事会中持不同意见的候选人进行投票。然而，近几个月来，一些世界上最大的公司（包括Alphabet、拜耳、迪士尼和Salesforce）不得不与越来越关注大公司的激进人士进行斗争。著名激进投资家卡尔•伊坎和基因组学巨头Illumina之间的一场斗争将在5月25日达进入白热化阶段。

Activist hedge funds are often seen as villains who are nasty, brutish and focused on the short term. Sometimes the shoe fits. But more often activists are playing a role that is essential for shareholder capitalism. For several reasons, their campaigns are increasingly important.

激进的对冲基金通常被视为坏人，他们肮脏、野蛮，只关注短期。有时候，这很合适。但更多情况下，激进投资者扮演的角色对股东资本主义至关重要。出于几个原因，他们的活动变得越来越重要。

![image-20230525085954756](/Users/chenyujie/Library/Application Support/typora-user-images/image-20230525085954756.png)

One is the rise of passive investing, which attempts to replicate the returns of an index rather than surpass them. Only one in three dollars invested by institutions in America’s thousand largest public companies is actively allocated, according to Man Group, an investment firm. The biggest passive asset-managers, such as BlackRock, charge low fees and run lean investment-stewardship teams which are not designed to spot empire-building bosses or lazy boards. The result is an increasingly idle corporate electorate. Efforts to enfranchise the ultimate owners of funds are unlikely to solve the problem. They typically want to earn returns but leave the decision-making to somebody else.

其一是被动投资的兴起，这种投资试图复制大盘指数的回报，而不是超越这一回报。投资公司曼氏集团的数据显示，机构投资于美国1000家最大上市公司的资金中，只有三分之一是积极投资。像贝莱德等最大的被动型资产管理公司，收取较低的费用，并运营精简的投资管理团队，这些团队的设计目的不是为了发现建立帝国的老板或懒惰的董事会。其结果是企业选民越来越无所事事。赋予基金最终所有者权利的努力不太可能解决这个问题。他们通常希望获得回报，但把决策权留给别人。

There are other channels by which bosses’ feet might be held to the fire. Since the 1980s leveraged buy-outs by private-equity firms have been a persistent threat to underperforming executives. The easiest way to deter a hostile takeover is to raise your company’s share price. Today, however, the buy-out industry is roiling from the effects of higher interest rates, and is unlikely to recover fully for some time.

还有其他渠道会给老板们施压。自20世纪80年代以来，私募股权公司的杠杆收购一直是对业绩不佳的高管的持续威胁。阻止恶意收购最简单的方法就是提高公司的股价。然而，如今，收购行业正受到高利率的影响，在一段时间内不太可能完全复苏。

While the routes by which managers are held to account have shrunk, the need to boost profits by applying discipline has grown. When interest rates were low, large technology firms hired aggressively and expanded into peripheral lines of business. Now profits are more important than growth. Over the past decade the demand for managers to respond to environmental, social and governance (esg) concerns has also grown. Some shareholders campaign for esg—as is their right—but the danger of firms losing focus and wasting money as they are drawn into politics has increased.

尽管追究管理人员责任的途径减少了，但通过纪律提高利润的需求却增加了。当利率较低时，大型科技公司积极招聘，并向外围业务扩张。现在利润比增长更重要。在过去十年中，对管理人员对环境、社会和治理(ESG)问题作出反应的需求也有所增长。一些股东为ESG发起运动——这是他们的权利——但是公司因卷入政治而失去重点并且浪费资金的风险增加了。

In such an environment the presence of activists is a welcome reminder that it is owners, not managers, in whose interest firms should be run. And because dealmaking is down, activists will be more likely to improve a firm’s operations than force it to sell itself in search of a quick buck. That should assuage the fears of those who see activists as corporate cowboys rather than drivers of efficiency.

在这样的环境下，激进分子的出现是一个受欢迎的提醒，他们提醒着人们，公司的运营应该符合所有者的利益，而不是管理者的利益。而且由于交易减少，激进分子更有可能改善公司的运营，而不是强迫公司出售自己以谋取快钱。这应该可以缓解那些将激进投资者视为企业半吊子而非效率推动者的人的担忧。

Get out of the vote

Fortunately, the job of activists is getting easier. New rules that came into force in America last September should make it easier for them to obtain board seats by letting shareholders vote for candidates individually, rather than as a bloc. The wave of nail-biting shareholder votes some expected to follow immediately has not yet materialised. But more battles between activists and complacent managers would be no bad thing. ■

幸运的是，激进人士的工作正变得越来越容易。去年9月在美国生效的新规则应该会让他们更容易获得董事会席位，让股东单独投票给候选人，而不是作为一个集团投票。一些人预计的随后即将出现的一波紧张的股东投票浪潮尚未出现。但激进主义者和自满的管理者之间爆发更多争斗并非坏事。■

Businesses’ bottleneck bane

企业的瓶颈祸害

Bartleby

![image-20230524085011234](/Users/chenyujie/Library/Application Support/typora-user-images/image-20230524085011234.png)

“The Goal” is a 40-year-old book with a topical message

《目标》是一本有40年历史的书，传达了一个主题信息

May 18, 2023 8:47 PM

“The goal” is a notable business book for two reasons. The first is its unusual genre. First published in 1984, it is a management tome dressed up in the clothes of a thriller. The book, written by Eliyahu Goldratt and Jeff Cox, tells the story of Alex Rogo, a plant manager who has to overhaul his factory within three months or face closure.

《目标》之所以是一本著名的商业书籍，有两个原因。首先是它不同寻常的体裁。该书于1984年首次出版，是一本披着惊险小说外衣的管理学巨著。这本书由伊利亚胡•高德拉特和杰夫•考克斯撰写，讲述了工厂经理亚历克斯•罗戈的故事，罗戈必须在三个月内彻底改革他的工厂，否则工厂将面临关闭。

To the objection that this is not thrilling at all, consider that it could have been a lot worse (“Alan Key must format a slide deck by midnight or he won’t get enough sleep to function properly the next day”). And Rogo’s efforts to reduce excess inventory and win over Bill Peach, his hard-driving boss, are weirdly entertaining. In any case readers lapped it up. “The Goal” sold millions. It has been reprinted several times. It even got turned into a graphic novel.

认为这一点都不惊险，持反对意见的人，考虑一下它可能会更糟(“Alan Key必须在午夜之前整理幻灯片，否则他将没有足够的睡眠来正常工作”)。而罗戈为减少过剩库存和赢得他严厉的老板比尔·皮奇的支持所做的努力，也有着奇怪的娱乐效果。无论如何，读者都欣然接受了。《目标》卖出了数百万本。也已被重印了几次。它甚至被改编成漫画小说。

Its second contribution was to popularise thinking about bottlenecks. The novel was written to get across Goldratt’s “theory of constraints”, a method for identifying those resources whose capacity does not match the demands placed on them. (This definition comes from one of the book’s protagonists—Jonah, a brilliant, globe-trotting business adviser whose resemblance to a real person seems unlikely to be coincidental.)

它的第二个贡献是普及了对瓶颈的思考。这部小说的写作目的是为了阐释高德拉特的“约束理论”，这是一种识别那些能力与对需求不匹配的资源的方法。(这个定义来自书中的一个主人公——乔纳，乔纳是一个聪明的、环游世界的商业顾问，他与真人的相似似乎不太可能是巧合。)

Bottlenecks are often thought of as physical constraints. In Rogo’s factory, for example, the bottlenecks are two particular machines whose through-put must be increased in order to ship orders faster. In recent years the pandemic has increased awareness of such bottlenecks in the wider supply chain, whether because of the impact of semiconductor shortages or the effect of backlogs at congested ports.

瓶颈通常被认为是物理约束。例如，在罗戈的工厂里，瓶颈是两台特殊的机器，为了更快地运送订单，它们的有效产出必须增加。近年来，无论是由于半导体短缺的影响，还是由于拥挤的港口积压货物的影响，大流行提高了人们对更广泛供应链中此类瓶颈的认识。

Policies can be bottlenecks, too. The pandemic also forced vaccine manufacturers to ditch normal patterns of working. In “Vaxxers”, a book about their work to develop the Oxford-AstraZeneca covid-19 jab, Sarah Gilbert and Catherine Green describe how they did more “at risk” work, doing things in parallel that would usually have been done sequentially. That would have meant wasted work if they had hit a problem, but also that scientists got stuff done much faster than usual. Bill Peach would have approved.

政策也可能成为瓶颈。大流行还迫使疫苗制造商放弃正常的工作模式。莎拉·吉尔伯特和凯瑟琳·格林在一本关于他们研发牛津-阿斯利康新冠疫苗的著作《Vaxxers》一书中，描述了他们是如何完成更多“有风险”的工作的，他们并行地完成了通常按序完成的工作。这种模式意味着如果他们遇到了问题，那产品就费了，但同时科学家们完成工作的速度会比平时快得多。比尔·皮奇会同意的。

People are also bottlenecks. Within organisations, managers themselves are frequently the points at which things get bunged up. That might be because executives simply have too much to do. Estimates vary on how many direct reports a manager should ideally have. But if they don’t all fit in a lift, you almost certainly have too many.

人也是瓶颈。在组织内部，管理者本身往往是事情陷入困境的关键。这可能是因为高管们有太多事情要做。对于一个管理者理想的直接下属数量，人们的估计各不相同。但如果他们不能全部装进电梯，你几乎肯定数量太多了。

It might be their own fault—if they are micromanagers, say, offering up helpful opinions on everything from font sizes to office furniture. It might be because no one is comfortable making a decision for fear of being hauled over the coals later by their own boss. It might be because there is jockeying and confusion over who has the right to make a call (an “upward status disagreement”, if you like jargon; a “pissing contest”, if you don’t).

这可能是他们自己的错——如果他们是事无巨细的管理者，比如说，从字体大小到办公室家具，他们都会提供有用的意见。这可能是因为没有人愿意做决定，因为他们害怕自己的老板以后会对他们大发雷霆。这可能是因为在谁有权利打电话的问题上存在着争论和困惑(如果你喜欢行话，这是一种“上层地位的分歧”；如果你不喜欢的话，那就是“撒尿比赛”)。

Bottlenecks can stem from good behaviour as well as bad. Collaboration is normally celebrated, but it can easily result in more delays if people’s time is being soaked up on non-essential tasks. Similarly, the person who responds to every message quickly and clears their inbox every night looks like the very opposite of a bottleneck, but that depends on two things: on what work they are not doing while they manically check their email, and on whether those messages are about trivial things. If people are responding at great speed on matters of zero importance—especially if they are high up the ladder and colleagues are therefore likely to respond to them in turn—they are probably creating trouble of some sort.

瓶颈可能源于好的行为，也可能源于坏的行为。合作通常是值得赞扬的，但如果人们的时间被不必要的任务占用，它很容易导致更多的延误。同样，每天晚上快速回复每条信息并清理收件箱的人看起来与瓶颈恰恰相反，但这取决于两件事: 一是他们在疯狂查看电子邮件时没有做什么工作，二是这些信息是否与琐碎的事情有关。如果人们在无关紧要的事情上快速回应，特别是如果他们职级很高，同事们很可能会反过来回应他们，他们很可能正在制造某种麻烦。

Whatever the causes of congestion, the costs can be material. Daniel Ek, the boss of Spotify, attributed a recent restructuring at the music-streaming company to the need to make faster decisions. Consultants at McKinsey have estimated that about 530,000 days of managers’ time each year may be wasted on broken decision-making processes at a typical Fortune 500 company.

无论“阻塞”的原因是什么，代价都是巨大的。Spotify的老板丹尼尔·埃克将这家音乐流媒体公司最近的重组归因于需要更快地做出决策。麦肯锡的咨询师估计，在一家典型的《财富》世界500强企业，管理者们每年可能会浪费大约53万天的时间在错误的决策过程上。

“The Goal” is not the greatest thriller ever. No one dies. The only real violence done is to the English language (“It’s not until I’m busy with my delicious veal parmesan that my thoughts start to crystallise”). But it is a lot more readable than most business books, and it makes you think about a subject that is relevant not only to supply-chain managers and operations managers but to bosses everywhere. Bottlenecks abound. They just need to be found. ■

《目标》并不是有史以来最伟大的惊险小说。没人会死。唯一真正的暴力是对英语的暴力(“直到我忙着做美味的帕尔马干酪小牛肉时，我的思想才开始成形”)。但它比大多数商业书籍更具可读性，它会让你思考一个不仅与供应链经理和运营经理相关，而且与世界各地的老板相关的主题。瓶颈到处都是。只是需要找到他们。￭

The fight over the future of global payments

争夺全球支付的未来

Payment parity

![image-20230523074844010](/Users/chenyujie/Library/Application Support/typora-user-images/image-20230523074844010.png)

Digital payments have transformed domestic finance. Now competition is going global

数字支付已经改变了国内金融，现在竞争正在全球化

May 18, 2023 5:16 PM

Over the past two decades the ways people pay, receive and transfer money have changed beyond recognition. The revolution began in 2007 when m-pesa made it possible for Kenyans to make payments with a text message. In 2011 Alipay launched payment-by-qr-code in China, a system that has all but replaced cash in cities. Since then India’s state-led Unified Payments Interface (upi) and Brazil’s Pix have vastly widened access to the financial system among the poor. As our special report explains, globally the use of notes and coins has been cut by a third, e-commerce has boomed and life without digital payments has become unimaginable.

在过去的二十年里，人们支付、收款以及转账的方式发生了翻天覆地的变化。这场革命始于2007年，当时M-Pesa让肯尼亚人可以通过短信支付。2011年，支付宝在中国推出了二维码支付，这一系统几乎取代了城市中的现金。从那以后，印度政府主导的统一支付接口UPI和巴西的PIX极大地扩大了穷人接触金融系统的机会。正如我们的特别报道所解释的那样，在全球范围内，纸币和硬币的使用减少了三分之一，电商蓬勃发展，没有数字支付的生活已经变得难以想象。

Having transformed how people use money at home, the race to transform payments is now going global. Cross-border retail spending (including tourism) and remittances will hit $5trn this year; business-to-business payments are worth eight times that. Three big players are duking it out to process these vast flows of funds. The West’s legacy system, including the Visa-Mastercard duopoly and swift, a messaging system for bank payments, is the dominant incumbent. China is the most advanced challenger, with its payment apps, its card network, UnionPay, and cips, its more expansive alternative to swift. In third place is India, whose ambition to deploy upi globally has grown.

在改变了人们在国内使用货币的方式之后，改变支付方式的竞赛如今正走向全球。跨境零售支出(包括旅游)和汇款今年将达到5万亿美元；企业对企业支付的价值是这个数字的8倍。三家大公司正在为处理这些巨额资金流而展开竞争。西方遗留下来的系统，包括visa和万事达卡的双寡头垄断，以及银行支付信息系统swift，占据了主导地位。中国是最超前的挑战者，其拥有支付应用程序、银行卡网络、银联以及比swift更广泛的人民币跨境支付系统CIPS。排在第三位的是印度，其在全球部署UPI的雄心有所增长。

The competition among the three blocs is heating up fast. Alipay is now accepted by 2.5m merchants overseas. UnionPay, which is already the world’s largest card network by transaction volume, is accepted by 65m merchants globally, compared with Visa’s 100m. Most are outside China. India’s upi has been linked with Singapore’s fast payment system, allowing consumers in both countries to pay in the other using their domestic platform. India is in talks with more than 30 other countries to export its payments kit, which would link their systems too. In November four central banks, including China’s, successfully tested a cross-border system for settling transactions using central-bank digital currencies.

三大阵营之间的竞争正在迅速升温。支付宝目前已被250万海外商户接受。按交易量计算，银联已经是全球最大的银行卡网络，在全球有6500万商户接受银联支付，而Visa有1亿商户。大多数都在中国以外。印度的UPI与新加坡的快速支付系统相连，允许两国的消费者使用本国的平台用对方货币支付。印度正在与其他30多个国家谈判出口其支付工具，这也将连接他们的支付系统。去年11月，包括中国央行在内的四家央行成功测试了一个使用央行数字货币结算交易的跨境系统。

The Asian giants have several motives for spreading their wings. The most important is to become less dependent on the West. Russia’s card network, Mir, launched after Vladimir Putin’s illegal annexation of Crimea in 2014, has limited the damage done by the withdrawal of Visa and Mastercard from Russia after its full-scale invasion of Ukraine. Volumes on cips have surged since 2020, helped by Russia being mostly cut out of swift. But building a sanctions-workaround is not the only goal. Countries also crave for themselves the clout that comes with control over the world’s financial infrastructure, as well as seeking more convenience for their people when they transact internationally.

这些亚洲巨头发展跨境支付系统有几个原因。最重要的是减少对西方的依赖。2014年弗拉基米尔•普京非法吞并克里米亚后，俄罗斯启动了银行卡网络Mir，它将Visa和万事达在俄罗斯全面入侵乌克兰后撤出俄罗斯所造成的损害降到了最低。自2020年以来，由于俄罗斯基本上被排除在swift之外，人民币支付跨境系统的交易量大幅飙升。但建立一个制裁的变通方案并不是唯一的目标。各国还渴望获得控制全球金融基础设施所带来的影响力，并为本国人民在国际交易中寻求更多便利。

The West might fear a fragmentation of the global financial infrastructure that allows wrongdoers to escape future sanctions. Yet a more open landscape for global payments will benefit its consumers and businesses. Under competitive pressure swift has already upgraded its once-clunky system and has nearly halved the cost of messaging. The average cost of a remittance has been cut by a third in the past decade partly because of new fintechs. The Western card networks are overdue a shake-up. The typical 1% cross-border fee they charge (on top of a 1-3% levy on merchants) supports company-wide net margins of around 50%, among the highest in the world for listed firms. The spread of Alipay, upi and even other newcomers like GrabPay in South-East Asia or WhatsApp Pay, which just launched in Singapore and Brazil, will give consumers other options.

西方可能会担心全球金融基础设施的分裂，这会让不法分子逃脱未来的制裁。然而，一个更加开放的全球支付环境将有利于其消费者和企业。在竞争压力下，swift已经升级了其一度笨重的系统，并将短信成本降低了近一半。过去10年，汇款的平均成本降低了三分之一，部分原因是新的金融科技。西方信用卡网络早就应该进行改革了。他们通常收取的1%的跨境费用(此外还向商家收取1% 至3% 的费用)，这使得整个公司的净利润率达到50% 左右，在全球上市公司中名列前茅。支付宝、UPI，甚至东南亚的GrabPay和刚刚在新加坡和巴西推出的WhatsApp Pay等其它新兴支付平台的推出，都将给消费者带来其他选择。

Domestic payments markets have tended to be winner-takes-most because people like using a big network with lots of other users. For cross-border payments, consumers and businesses will tend to favour the payments system they use in their respective home countries. A system where people can use their domestic networks to pay abroad promises to be more convenient as well as cheaper.

国内支付市场倾向于赢家通吃，因为人们喜欢使用一个有很多其他用户的大网络。对于跨境支付，消费者和企业将倾向于青睐他们在各自国家使用的支付系统。由于商家越来越容易接受多种不同的支付方式，改变似乎是可行的。人们可以使用国内网络向国外支付的系统将会更方便、更便宜。

Diverse, not divided

The countries that benefit most will be those that stay open to all platforms and let them overlap, rather than forcing people to use national champions. And though the West will lose some power as a result of the proliferation of alternatives to its payments systems, it will maintain the ability to levy the most effective forms of sanctions: on flows of trade and technology. Joe Biden’s global vision is too timid and pessimistic

世界新秩序

In the 1940s and early 1950s America built a new world order out of the chaos of war. For all its shortcomings, it kept the peace between superpowers and underpinned decades of growth that lifted billions out of poverty. Today that order, based on global rules, free markets and an American promise to uphold both, is fraying. Toxic partisanship at home has corroded confidence in America’s government. The financial crisis of 2007-09 dented faith in markets. America’s failures in Iraq and Afghanistan undermined its claim to spread democracy. Today most countries refuse to heed its call to enforce sanctions on Russia. And China’s rise has spurred American politicians to take a more selfish, zero-sum approach to geopolitics.

在20世纪40年代和50年代初，美国在混乱的战争中建立了一个新的世界秩序。尽管存在种种缺陷，但它维持了超级大国之间的和平，并支撑了数十年的增长，使数十亿人摆脱了贫困。如今，以全球规则、自由市场和美国对两者的承诺为基础的秩序正在瓦解。国内有毒的党派之争已经侵蚀了人们对美国政府的信心。2007-2009年的金融危机削弱了人们对市场的信心。美国在伊拉克和阿富汗的失败削弱了其传播民主的主张。如今，大多数国家拒绝听从其对俄罗斯实施制裁的呼吁。中国的崛起促使美国政客在地缘政治上采取更自私、更零和的方式。

China’s rise has also increased the threat of war. In a conversation with The Economist, Henry Kissinger, who will be 100 this month, warns that China and America are “on the path” to confrontation. “Both sides have convinced themselves that the other represents a strategic danger,” he says. The stakes could not be higher: both are nuclear-armed. Both are also dabbling with unpredictable artificial intelligence (ai). The elder statesmen’s eldest statesman worries that, just as before the first world war, the superpowers will stumble into catastrophe.

中国的崛起也增加了战争的威胁。本月将满100岁的亨利•基辛格在接受《经济学人》采访时警告称，中国和美国正“走上”对抗的道路。他表示:“双方确信，对方构成了战略威胁。” 这两个国家都拥有核武器，风险已经很高了。两国也都在涉足无法预测的人工智能。这位政界元老中的元老担心，就像第一次世界大战前一样，超级大国将陷入灾难。

Since arriving in the Oval Office in 2021, Joe Biden has developed a new strategy to preserve American pre-eminence and reduce the risk of conflict. Jake Sullivan, the latest of Mr Kissinger’s successors as national security adviser, recently gave the fullest account yet of this Biden doctrine. His narrative weaves together middle-class prosperity, defence and climate change. He repudiates the free-market “Washington consensus” and calls for the government to play a muscular role in society, with a strong emphasis on national security.

自2021年入主白宫以来，乔·拜登制定了一项新战略，以保持美国的卓越地位，并降低冲突风险。最近接替基辛格担任国家安全顾问的杰克•沙利文对拜登的这一战略进行了迄今为止最全面的阐述。他的叙述将中产阶级繁荣、国防和气候变化联系在一起。他否定了自由市场的“华盛顿共识”，呼吁政府在社会中发挥强有力的作用，并高度重视国家安全。

This means hyperactive industrial policy. Big subsidies will catalyse private investment in semiconductors and clean energy. Export controls will create “a small yard and high fence” to keep selected technology with potential military uses out of unfriendly hands. At the same time, the administration is softening its rhetoric. Instead of “decoupling” from China’s economy, it talks of “de-risking”. It wants to find common ground on climate change, African debt and even Ukraine. On May 10th and 11th Mr Sullivan spent eight hours with his Chinese counterpart, the first high-level contact for months.

这意味着过度活跃的产业政策。巨额补贴将促进半导体和清洁能源领域的私人投资。出口管制将创造一个“小院高墙”，以防止具有潜在军事用途的高尖技术落入不友好之人手中。与此同时，拜登政府的措辞也在变得更委婉。它谈到的不是与中国经济“脱钩”，而是“去风险”。它希望在气候变化、非洲债务甚至乌克兰问题上找到共同点。5月10日和11日，沙利文与中国“同行”进行了8个小时的会谈，这是数月来的首次高层接触。

Behind the doctrine is a belief that a virtuous circle can make America and the world safer. State intervention and protectionism will boost industry, helping the middle class and cooling America’s populist fevers. Less erratic leadership (after Donald Trump’s) will restore America’s authority abroad, even if the Biden team breaks a few global economic rules. The relationship with China will be managed with “strategic maturity”. As a precaution America will keep spending large sums on its military forces to deter China from aggression.

这一战略的背后是一种信念，即良性循环可以使美国和世界更安全。国家干预和保护主义将促进工业发展，帮助中产阶级，冷却美国的民粹主义狂热。在唐纳德•特朗普之后，不那么反复无常的领导层将恢复美国在海外的权威，即使拜登团队打破了一些全球经济规则。与中国的关系将以“战略成熟度”来管理。作为预防措施，美国将继续在其军事力量上投入大量资金，以阻止中国的“侵略”。

He is right that American foreign policy must deal with new challenges, from Chinese coercion to climate change. No other big country is as prosperous or innovative. As we noted last week, the size of China’s economy is unlikely ever to surpass America’s by much. The main source of America’s strength is creative destruction and open markets in a rules-based global economy. So although Mr Biden is right to reinforce the social safety-net, his state-led, insular economic vision may ultimately erode living standards and American clout.

先从经济说起。尽管许多人认为，美国的经济实力并没有下降。其人口占世界4%，产出占全球25%，这一比例自1980年以来未曾改变。没有其他大国像其这样繁荣和创新。正如我们上周所指出的，中国的经济规模不太可能超过美国太多。美国实力的主要来源是基于规则的全球经济中的创造性破坏和开放市场。因此，尽管拜登加强社会保障网络是正确的，但他的国家主导的、狭隘的经济愿景最终可能会侵蚀人们的生活水平以及美国的影响力。

Mr Sullivan wants to combine export controls with co-operative trade, and an arms race with collaboration. But China’s leaders think this strategy is meant to keep China down. America’s case would be stronger if export controls didn’t keep expanding, if Trump-era tariffs were not still in place and if its politicians were not vying to outhawk each other on China. Europe fears a subsidy race and worries escalating tensions with China will cause it severe damage: our calculations show Germany’s economy is twice as exposed to China as America’s is. The decay of global rules is accelerating the embrace of a transactional approach to foreign policy by emerging economies. The post-1945 order rested on American constancy: each administration was guided by predictable interests. Today allies and enemies know chaos may follow the election in 2024. Trumpian dysfunction is not Mr Biden’s fault, but it makes it vital to be predictable and open now.

最后一个缺陷与盟友有关。拜登支持乌克兰，重振北约及其在亚洲的盟友关系。然而，美国不可预测的经济民族主义和不愿提供进入其市场的机会削弱了其影响力。欧洲担心补贴竞争，并担心与中国的紧张关系升级将给自己造成严重损害: 我们的计算显示，德国经济对中国的依赖是对美国的两倍。全球规则的衰落正加速新兴经济体在外交政策上采用交易式的方式。1945年后的秩序建立在美国的坚定不疑之上: 每届政府都以可预见的利益为指导。今天，盟友和敌人都知道2024年大选后可能会出现混乱。特朗普的功能失调不是拜登的错，但这使得现在保持可预测和开放变得至关重要。

Free, open and predictable

Americans need to be persuaded that a more optimistic, positive-sum approach is in their interests. This is the key that will keep their country strong and unlock a better foreign policy, by allowing it to help forge new global rules on trade, climate, ai and more that old allies and new ones can rely on. Such a revived global order would be the best defence against an autocratic one led by China. Unfortunately the Biden doctrine fails to rebut the narrative of American decline and so has not resolved the tension between the country’s toxic politics and its role as the linchpin of a liberal order. Unless America looks out at the world with self-confidence, it will struggle to lead it.■

Such systems can generate text and hold realistic conversations because they have been trained using trillions of words taken from the internet. Training a large LLM takes months and costs tens of millions of dollars. This led to concerns that AI would be dominated by a few deep-pocketed firms.

随着2022年底ChatGPT的推出，人工智能突然进入了公众的视野。ChatGPT是一款由与微软关系密切的初创公司OpenAI开发的“大语言模型”驱动的聊天机器人。它的成功促使谷歌和其他科技公司发布了他们自己的大语言模型驱动的聊天机器人。这样的系统可以生成文本并进行真实的对话，因为它们是用从互联网上获取的数万亿字进行训练的。训练一个大预言模型需要几个月的时间，而且耗资数千万美元。这导致人们担心人工智能将被一些财力雄厚的公司所主导。

But that assumption is wrong, says the Google memo. It notes that researchers in the open-source community, using free, online resources, are now achieving results comparable to the biggest proprietary models. It turns out that LLMs can be “fine-tuned” using a technique called low-rank adaptation, or LoRa. This allows an existing LLM to be optimised for a particular task far more quickly and cheaply than training an LLM from scratch.

但谷歌的备忘录指出，这种假设是错误的。它指出，开源社区的研究人员使用免费的在线资源，现在正在取得与最大的专有模型相当的结果。事实证明，大语言模型可以使用一种称为低秩适应(LoRa)的技术进行“微调”。这使得现有的大语言模型可以针对特定任务进行优化，而且比从头开始训练 要快得多，成本也低得多。

Activity in open-source AI exploded in March, when LLaMa, a model created by Meta, Facebook’s parent, was leaked online. Although it is smaller than the largest LLMs (its smallest version has 7bn parameters, compared with 540bn for Google’s PaLM) it was quickly fine-tuned to produce results comparable to the original version of ChatGPT on some tasks. As open-source researchers built on each other’s work with LLaMa, “a tremendous outpouring of innovation followed,” the memo’s author writes.

今年3月，Facebook母公司Meta创建的一个模型LLaMa被泄露在网上，开源人工智能领域的活动爆发。尽管它比最大的LLM小(其最小的版本有70亿个参数，而谷歌的PaLM有5400亿个参数)，但它能够很快微调，并在某些任务上产生了与原始版本ChatGPT相当的结果。备忘录的作者写道，随着开源研究人员彼此在LLaMa的工作基础上不断发展，“大量的创新随之而来”。

This could have seismic implications for the industry’s future. “The barrier to entry for training and experimentation has dropped from the total output of a major research organisation to one person, an evening, and a beefy laptop,” the Google memo claims. An LLM can now be fine-tuned for $100 in a few hours. With its fast-moving, collaborative and low-cost model, “open-source has some significant advantages that we cannot replicate.” Hence the memo’s title: this may mean Google has no defensive “moat” against open-source competitors. Nor, for that matter, does OpenAI.

这可能会对该行业的未来产生重大影响。谷歌的备忘录称:“训练和实验的门槛已经从一个大型研究机构的总产出下降到一个人、一个晚上和一台功能强大的笔记本电脑。” 一个大语言模型现在可以在几个小时内花费100美元进行微调。由于其快速发展、协作和低成本的模式，“开源具有一些我们无法复制的显著优势。”因此备忘录的标题是: 这可能意味着谷歌对开源竞争对手没有防御性的“护城河”。在这个问题上，OpenAI也没有。

Not everyone agrees with this thesis. It is true that the internet runs on open-source software. But people use paid-for, proprietary software, from Adobe Photoshop to Microsoft Windows, as well. AI may find a similar balance. Moreover, benchmarking AI systems is notoriously hard. Yet even if the memo is partly right, the implication is that access to AI technology will be far more democratised than seemed possible even a year ago. Powerful LLMs can be run on a laptop; anyone who wants to can now fine-tune their own AI.

并非所有人都同意这一观点。互联网的确是在开源软件上运行的。但人们也在使用付费的专有软件，从Adobe Photoshop到微软Windows。人工智能可能会找到类似的平衡。此外，对人工智能系统进行基准测试是出了名的困难。然而，即使这份备忘录在一定程度上是正确的，其含义是，人工智能技术的使用将比一年前看起来更加大众化。功能强大的大语言模型可以在笔记本电脑上运行；现在任何人都可以微调自己的人工智能。

This has both positive and negative implications. On the plus side, it makes monopolistic control of AI by a handful of companies far less likely. It will make access to AI much cheaper, accelerate innovation across the field and make it easier for researchers to analyse the behaviour of AI systems (their access to proprietary models was limited), boosting transparency and safety. But easier access to AI also means bad actors will be able to fine-tune systems for nefarious purposes, such as generating disinformation. It means Western attempts to prevent hostile regimes from gaining access to powerful AI technology will fail. And it makes AI harder to regulate, because the genie is out of the bottle.

这既有积极的影响，也有消极的影响。有利的一面是，它大大降低了少数公司垄断控制人工智能的可能性。它将大大降低人工智能的使用成本，加速整个领域的创新，并使研究人员更容易分析人工智能系统的行为(他们对专有模型的访问受到限制)，从而提高透明度和安全性。但更容易获得人工智能也意味着坏人将能够为邪恶目的对系统进行微调，比如制造虚假信息。这意味着西方试图阻止敌对政权获得强大的人工智能技术的努力将会失败。而且这会使得人工智能更难监管，因为“精灵”已经从瓶子里出来了。

Whether Google and its ilk really have lost their moat in AI will soon become apparent. But as with those previous memos, this feels like another turning point for computing. ■

谷歌及其同类公司是否真的失去了在人工智能领域的护城河，很快就会见分晓。但就像之前的那些备忘录一样，这感觉像是计算机的又一个转折点。■

总统的经济政策的主要影响是提高了物价

May 11th 2023

The way Joe Biden tells it, the only part of his economic record that really matters is jobs. America’s roaring labour market beat expectations again in April. The unemployment rate is lower than in any year since 1969. The share of 15- to 64-year-olds in employment has surpassed its pre-pandemic peak, which was itself the highest seen since 2007. Mr Biden likes to tell people that his presidency, which began in the midst of a rapid recovery from covid lockdowns, has coincided with more monthly job creation, on average, than any other in history. Provided America avoids a debt-ceiling crisis, and the associated halt to federal spending and probable lay-offs, the booming labour market looks like a ticket to re-election in 2024.

按照乔·拜登的说法，他的经济政绩中唯一真正重要的部分是就业。4月，美国劳动力市场的繁荣再次超出预期。失业率自1969年以来最低。15岁至64岁的就业比例已经超过了疫情前的峰值，而这一峰值本身就是2007年以来的最高水平。拜登喜欢告诉人们，他的总统任期开始于美国从疫情封锁中快速复苏的时期，与此同时，平均每月创造的就业机会比历史上任何时候都多。如果美国能避免债务上限危机，相关的联邦支出停止以及可能的裁员，繁荣的劳动力市场看起来就像是2024年连任的入场券。【创造更多的就业机会，劳动市场繁荣】

Unfortunately for Mr Biden, however, another part of his record tells a less flattering story. High inflation continues to imperil the economy and vex voters. And placing his record in a global context reveals that he is more responsible for surging prices than he is for abundant jobs.

然而，对拜登来说不幸的是，他政绩的另一部分却不那么讨人喜欢。高通胀继续危及经济，令选民烦恼。把他的政绩放在全球背景下看，就会发现他要为物价飙升负更大责任，而不是对大量就业负责。【高通胀】

![image-20230516093529586](/Users/chenyujie/Library/Application Support/typora-user-images/image-20230516093529586.png)

Mr Biden is right that America’s post-covid jobs recovery has been exceptional by historical standards. After the global financial crisis it took 13 years, by some measures, for the labour market to regain its health. This time it has taken little more than three years. The White House attributes surging employment to the $1.9trn “rescue plan” Mr Biden unleashed shortly after taking office in 2021. It contributed nearly a third of America’s total pandemic-related fiscal stimulus, which was worth an astonishing 26% of GDP, more than twice the average in the rich world.

拜登说得对，以历史标准衡量，美国后疫情时期的就业复苏是非同寻常的。在全球金融危机之后，以某些标准衡量，劳动力市场用了13年的时间才恢复健康。这一次只花了三年多一点的时间。白宫将就业激增归因于拜登在2021年上任后不久推出的1.9万亿美元“拯救计划”。它贡献了美国与大流行病相关的财政刺激总额的近三分之一，其价值高达GDP的26%，是发达国家平均水平的两倍多。

If the Biden stimulus had been responsible for the jobs boom, though, you would expect America’s labour market to be stronger than those of its peers. But in Canada, France, Germany and Italy working-age employment rates surpassed pre-pandemic highs by the end of 2021; Japan followed in 2022. Among the g7 group of economies America has beaten to a full recovery only Brexit-blighted Britain, where the employment rate is still lower than it was at the end of 2019.

如果拜登的刺激计划是就业繁荣的原因，那么你可以预期美国的劳动力市场会比其他国家更强劲。但在加拿大、法国、德国和意大利，适龄就业率到2021年底超过了大流行前的高点；日本在2022年紧随其后。在七国集团经济体中，美国经济全面复苏的速度仅击败了饱受脱欧困扰的英国，英国的就业率仍低于2019年底的水平。

That employment bounced back in most places suggests that America’s jobs recovery had more to do with the unusual nature of the pandemic recession, brought about by lockdowns and social distancing, than with Mr Biden’s gargantuan stimulus. The extra public spending surely boosted demand for workers, but what followed was a historic surge in job vacancies and worker shortages as the economy overheated. Actual employment would almost certainly have shot up anyway. By the time Mr Biden came to office the jobs recovery was already two-thirds complete, having defied economists’ gloomy predictions.

大多数地方的就业反弹表明，美国的就业复苏更多地与由封锁和保持社交距离带来的大流行衰退的不同寻常性质有关，而不是与拜登的庞大刺激计划有关。额外的公共支出无疑提振了对工人的需求，但随之而来的是，随着经济过热，职位空缺和工人短缺出现了历史性的激增。无论如何，实际就业几乎肯定会激增。到拜登上任时，就业复苏已经完成了三分之二，这打破了经济学家的悲观预测。

Mr Biden’s stimulus did, however, put a rocket under inflation. In April “core” consumer prices, which exclude energy and food, were 13.4% higher than when he came to office. They have risen more than in other g7 countries, and their acceleration coincided with the introduction of Mr Biden’s stimulus. Research suggests that, even by September 2022, the largesse was pushing up core inflation by about four percentage points.

然而，拜登的刺激计划确实使得通胀急剧上升。4月份，不包括能源和食品在内的“核心”消费者价格比他上任时高出13.4%。失业率的上升幅度超过了其他七国集团的成员，而且与拜登的经济刺激计划同时加速。研究表明，即使到2022年9月，这一慷慨举动也将核心通胀率推高了约4个百分点。

The White House is not solely responsible for inflation: the Federal Reserve failed to raise interest rates in time to offset the fiscal stimulus, and the energy crisis that followed Russia’s invasion of Ukraine made a bad problem worse. But it was Mr Biden who lit the inflationary touch-paper—and whose signature policies are probably still boosting prices. It is now clear that the Inflation Reduction Act, which was supposed to cool the economy by shrinking deficits, will in fact widen them, owing to the higher-than-forecast take-up of its clean-energy tax credits.

白宫并不是造成通货膨胀的唯一原因: 美联储未能及时加息以抵消财政刺激的影响，而俄罗斯入侵乌克兰后引发的能源危机使本已严重的问题雪上加霜。但正是拜登点燃了通胀的导火索，他的标志性政策可能仍在推高物价。现在很清楚的是，本应通过缩减赤字来给经济降温的《通胀削减法案》，实际上会扩大赤字，因为清洁能源税收抵免的比例高于预期。

The outperformance reflects better growth in productivity, not a faster employment rebound. America exports more energy than it imports, meaning in aggregate it benefited from surging fuel prices. And its pandemic spending from 2020 onwards focused more on supporting incomes than preserving jobs, resulting in a faster reallocation of workers around the economy than in Europe, which relied on subsidised furlough schemes. Both factors predate Mr Biden’s presidency.

美国最近的记录看起来特别好的地方是经济增长。国际货币基金组织预测，美国今年的人均GDP将比2019年增长4.6%，轻松成为七国集团中增幅最大的经济体。但遗憾的是，这与拜登本人关系不大。这种优异的表现反映了生产率的更好增长，而不是就业的更快反弹。美国的能源出口多于进口，这意味着它总体上受益于飙升的燃料价格。从2020年起，美国的流行病支出更多地集中在支持收入上，而不是保留工作岗位上，这导致劳动力在经济中的重新分配速度比欧洲更快，欧洲则依赖于补贴休假计划。这两个因素在拜登担任总统之前就已经存在了。

Voters seem to sense that the main effect of the president’s economic policies to date has been to worsen inflation. Polls show that far more Americans think Donald Trump, Mr Biden’s predecessor and probable opponent in 2024, did a better job than Mr Biden of handling the economy, than the reverse. The longer inflation persists, the more likely it becomes that the Fed keeps rates high enough to tip America into recession—perhaps around the time of the election. Mr Biden’s largesse could go down as the mistake that let Mr Trump back into office. ■

Is Chinese power about to peak?

中国的实力即将达到顶峰吗?

China and the world

中国和世界

![image-20230515091611006](/Users/chenyujie/Library/Application Support/typora-user-images/image-20230515091611006.png)

The country’s historic ascent is levelling off. That need not make it more dangerous

这个国家历史性的上升正在趋于平稳。这并不会让事情变得更危险

May 11th 2023

The rise of China has been a defining feature of the world for the past four decades. Since the country began to open up and reform its economy in 1978, its gdp has grown by a dizzying 9% a year, on average. That has allowed a staggering 800m Chinese citizens to escape from poverty. Today China accounts for almost a fifth of global output. The sheer size of its market and manufacturing base has reshaped the global economy. Xi Jinping, who has ruled China for the past decade, hopes to use his country’s increasing heft to reshape the geopolitical order, too.

在过去40年里，中国的崛起一直是世界的一个标志性特征。自1978年改革开放以来，中国的GDP以年均9%的速度增长。这使得8亿中国公民摆脱了贫困，这一数字令人惊讶。如今，中国占全球产量的近五分之一。中国庞大的市场规模和制造业基础重塑了全球经济。过去10年一直统治中国的习近平，也希望利用中国日益增长的影响力来重塑地缘政治秩序。

There is just one catch: China’s rapid rise is slowing down. Mr Xi promises a “great rejuvenation” of his country in the coming decades, but the economy is now undergoing something more prosaic: a great maturation. Whereas a decade ago forecasters predicted that China’s gdp would zoom past America’s during the mid-21st century (at market exchange rates) and retain a commanding lead, now a much less dramatic shift is in the offing, resulting in something closer to economic parity.

只有一个问题：中国的快速崛起正在放缓。习承诺在未来几十年实现中国的“伟大复兴”，但中国经济现在正在经历一些更为平淡的事情: 大成熟。十年前，预测者预测中国的GDP将在21世纪中期超过美国(以市场汇率计算)，并保持绝对领先地位，而现在，一个远没有那么戏剧性的转变即将到来，这就导致两国经济接近势均力敌。

This change in economic trajectory is the subject of fierce debate among China-watchers (see our special Briefing). They are thinking again about China’s clout and its rivalry with America. One view is that Chinese power will fall relative to that of its rivals, which could paradoxically make it more dangerous. In a book last year, Hal Brands and Michael Beckley, two scholars, popularised a theory they called “Peak China”. The country faces decay, they argue, and has reached “the point where it is strong enough to aggressively disrupt the existing order but is losing confidence that time is on its side”. Their study opens with an imagined war over Taiwan.

经济轨迹的这种变化是中国观察家们激烈争论的主题(见我们的特别简报)。他们正在重新思考中国的影响力及其与美国的竞争。一种观点认为，中国的实力将相对于其竞争对手下降，这可能会自相矛盾地使其更加危险。在去年的一本书中，两位学者哈尔•布兰兹和迈克尔•贝克利推广了一种他们称之为“中国峰值”的理论。他们认为，这个国家面临着衰退，并且已经达到了“强大到足以积极破坏现有秩序的程度，但正在失去对时间站在自己一边的信心”。他们的研究从一场假想的台湾战争开始。

The Peak China thesis rests on the accurate observation that certain tailwinds are turning to headwinds, hindering Chinese progress. The first big gust comes from demography. China’s working-age population has been declining for about a decade. Last year its population as a whole peaked, and India has now overtaken it. The Communist Party’s attempts to convince Chinese couples to have more children are not working. As a result, the un thinks that by mid-century China’s working-age population could decline by over a quarter. Wave goodbye to the masses of young workers who once filled “the world’s factory”.

“中国峰值”的论点建立在准确观察的基础上，即某些顺风正转向逆风，从而阻碍了中国的进步。第一阵大风来自人口统计学。大约十年来，中国的劳动年龄人口一直在下降。去年，中国人口总量达到顶峰，而印度现在已经超过了中国。共产党试图说服中国夫妇生育更多孩子的努力没有奏效。因此，联合国认为，到本世纪中叶，中国的劳动年龄人口可能会减少四分之一以上。向那些曾经充斥着“世界工厂”的大批年轻工人挥手告别。

Adding workers is one way for an economy to grow. Another is to make better use of the existing population. But China’s second problem is that output per worker is unlikely to rise as fast as forecasters once hoped. More of its resources will go to caring for the elderly. After decades of building houses, roads and railways, spending on infrastructure faces diminishing returns. Mr Xi’s autocratic tendencies have made local entrepreneurs more nervous, which may reduce China’s capacity to innovate in the long run. Geopolitical tensions have made foreign firms eager to diversify supply chains away from China. America wants to hobble China’s capabilities in some “foundational” technologies. Its ban on exporting certain semiconductors and machines to Chinese firms is expected to cut into China’s gdp.

增加劳动力是经济增长的一种方式。另一个是更好地利用现有人口。但中国的第二个问题是，人均产出不太可能像预测者曾经希望的那样快速增长。更多的资源将用于照顾老年人。建了几十年的房屋、公路和铁路之后，基础设施投资面临着回报递减的问题。习的独裁倾向让当地企业家更加紧张，从长远来看，这可能会削弱中国的创新能力。地缘政治紧张局势使得外国公司急于将供应链从中国转移出去。美国想要在一些“基础”技术上阻碍中国的能力。它禁止向中国公司出口某些半导体和机器，预计将削减中国的GDP。

All of this is dampening long-run forecasts of China’s economic potential. Twelve years ago Goldman Sachs thought China’s gdp would overtake America’s in 2026 and become over 50% larger by mid-century. Last year it revised that prediction, saying China would surpass America only in 2035 and peak at less than 15% bigger. Others are more gloomy. Capital Economics, a research firm, argues that the country’s economy will never become top dog, instead peaking at 90% of America’s size in 2035. These forecasts are, of course, uncertain. But the most plausible ones seem to agree that China and America will approach economic parity in the next decade or so—and remain locked in this position for decades to come.

所有这些都削弱了对中国经济潜力的长期预测。12年前，高盛认为中国的GDP将在2026年超过美国，并在本世纪中叶超过美国50%。去年，高盛修改了这一预测，称中国将在2035年超过美国，峰值增幅不到15%。其他人则更为悲观。一家名为资本经济的研究公司认为，这个国家的经济永远不会成为领头羊，而是在2035年达到美国经济总量的90%。当然，这些预测是不确定的。但最可信的观点似乎都认为，中国和美国将在未来十年左右的时间里接近经济平起平坐，并在未来几十年里保持这种状态。

How might China handle this flatter trajectory? In the most optimistic scenario, Mr Xi would make changes to boost productivity growth. With income per person less than half of America’s, China’s population will be keen to improve their living standards. He could try to unleash growth by giving the animal spirits of China’s economy freer rein and his people more freedom of movement. The Chinese government could stop relying on wasteful state-owned banks and enterprises to allocate capital. And it could adopt a less prickly posture abroad, easing geopolitical tensions and reassuring firms that it is safe to do business in China. Such reforms might ultimately make China more powerful—but also, one would hope, less aggressive. The trouble is that Mr Xi, who is 69 and now probably China’s ruler for life, shows no sign of embracing economic or political liberalisation.

中国将如何应对这种更平滑的发展轨迹？在最乐观的情况下，Xi将做出改变，以促进生产率增长。由于人均收入不到美国的一半，中国人将热衷于提高他们的生活水平。他可以通过让中国经济中的“动物精神”更自由地发挥作用，让他的人民更自由地行动来释放增长。政府可以停止依赖挥霍的国有银行和企业来配置资本。此外，中国还可以在海外采取不那么尖锐的姿态，缓解地缘政治紧张局势，并让企业放心，在中国做生意是安全的。这样的改革可能最终会让中国变得更强大——但人们也希望，这样的改革不会那么咄咄逼人。问题在于，现年69岁、现在很可能是中国终身统治者的习，没有表现出任何支持经济或政治自由化的迹象。

Pessimists fear that China will become more combative as its economic trajectory falters. There are plenty of reasons to think this plausible. Mr Xi stokes a dangerous nationalism, to persuade ordinary Chinese that critics of his rule are slighting China itself. China’s military budget is forecast to rise by over 7% this year, in line with nominal gdp. Its military spending is lower than America’s, but still catching up. Its navy could be 50% bigger than America’s by 2030, and its nuclear arsenal will almost quadruple by 2035. “Beijing’s economic power may be peaking, but no other country is so capable of challenging America globally,” write Messrs Brands and Beckley.

悲观主义者担心，随着经济发展轨迹的动摇，中国将变得更加好斗。有很多理由认为这是合理的。习煽动了一种危险的民族主义情绪，目的是让中国老百姓相信，对他的统治持批评态度的人是在贬低中国自己。中国今年的军事预算预计将增长7%以上，与名义GDP一致。中国的军费开支低于美国，但仍在迎头赶上。到2030年，其海军规模可能比美国大50%，到2035年，其核武库将增加近四倍。布兰兹和贝克利写道:“北京的经济实力可能已经达到顶峰，但没有哪个国家有能力在全球范围内挑战美国。”

Peer review

Yet the most likely scenario is in the middle ground. The speed of China’s rise in the past two decades has been destabilising, forcing adjustments in the global economic and geopolitical order. That phase of intense economic disruption may now be over. And for all its troubles China’s economy is unlikely to shrink, triggering the kind of nihilistic and destructive thinking that Messrs Brands and Beckley fear. Mr Xi is unpredictable but his country’s long-run economic prospect is neither triumph nor disaster. Faced with decades of being a near-peer of America, China has good reason to eschew hubris and resist invading Taiwan. A crucial question is whether the superpowers can avoid misreading each other’s intentions, and thus stumbling into a conflict. Next week we will examine America’s global leadership—and how it should respond to China in the coming age of superpower parity. ■

然而，最有可能的情况是处于中间地带。中国过去20年的崛起速度一直在破坏稳定，迫使全球经济和地缘政治秩序做出调整。那个严重的经济混乱阶段现在可能已经结束。尽管中国经济面临诸多麻烦，但它不太可能萎缩，从而引发布兰兹和贝克利担心的那种虚无主义和破坏性思维。习是不可预测的，但他的国家的长期经济前景既不是胜利也不是灾难。面对几十年来与美国势均力敌的局面，中国有充分的理由避免傲慢，抵制入侵台湾。一个关键的问题是，这两个超级大国能否避免误读彼此的意图，从而避免陷入冲突。下周我们将探讨美国的全球领导地位，以及在即将到来的超级大国势均力敌的时代，美国应如何应对中国。■

How to battle superbugs with viruses that “eat” them

如何与“吃掉”超级病菌的病毒作斗争

When viruses are good for you

当病毒对你有益的时候

![image-20230510094707892](/Users/chenyujie/Library/Application Support/typora-user-images/image-20230510094707892.png)

As antibiotic resistance spreads, bacteriophages could help avert a crisis

随着抗生素耐药性的扩散，噬菌体可以帮助避免危机

May 3rd 2023

Antibiotics are vital to modern medicine. Their ability to kill bacteria without harming the patient has saved billions of lives directly and made everything from caesarean sections to chemotherapy much safer. Life expectancy would drop by a third if they did not exist. But after decades of overuse their powers are fading. Some bacteria have evolved resistance, creating a growing army of “superbugs” against which there is no effective treatment. Antimicrobial resistance is expected to kill 10m people a year by 2050, up from around 1m in 2019.

抗生素对现代医学至关重要。它们在不伤害病人的情况下杀死细菌的能力直接挽救了数十亿人的生命，并使从剖腹产到化疗的一切都变得更加安全。如果它们不存在，预期寿命将下降三分之一。但经过几十年的过度使用，它们的力量正在减弱。一些细菌已经进化出耐药性，形成了一支不断壮大的“超级病菌”大军，目前还没有有效的治疗方法。预计到2050年，抗菌素耐药性每年将导致1000万人死亡，高于2019年的100万人左右。

It would be unwise to rely on new antibiotics to solve the problem. The rate at which resistance emerges is accelerating. Some new drugs last only two years before bacteria devise countermeasures. When new antibiotics do arrive, doctors often hoard them, prescribing them only grudgingly and for short periods when faced with the most intransigent infections. That helps limit the spread of resistance to new drugs. But it also limits sales, making new antibiotics an unattractive proposition for most pharmaceutical firms.

依靠新的抗生素来解决这个问题是不明智的。耐药性出现的速度正在加快。一些新药只能维持两年，细菌就会想出对策。当新的抗生素确实出现时，医生往往会囤积它们，只有在面对最顽固的感染时，他们才会不情愿地开一些短时间的处方。这有助于限制新药耐药性的传播。但它也限制了销量，使得新抗生素对大多数制药公司来说是一个没有吸引力的生意。

Governments have been trying to fix the problem by funnelling cash into research and taking stakes in drug firms. That has produced only limited improvements. But there is a promising, if obscure, alternative that is also worth a look. Microbiologists have known for decades that disease-causing bacteria can suffer from illnesses of their own. They are susceptible to attack by bacteriophages (“phages” for short): specialised viruses that infect bacteria, and often kill them.

各国政府一直试图通过向研究投资金以及入股制药公司来解决这个问题。这只带来了有限的改善。但是，还有一个很有希望的替代方案，虽然不太明确，但也值得一看。微生物学家几十年前就知道，致病细菌本身也会患上疾病。它们很容易受到噬菌体的攻击：噬菌体是一种专门感染细菌并经常杀死细菌的病毒。

Using one disease-causing organism to fight another has several advantages. Like antibiotics, phages are picky in their choice of target, leaving human cells alone even as they infect and destroy bacterial ones. Unlike antibiotics, phages can evolve just as readily as bacteria can, meaning that even if bacteria do develop resistance, the phages may be able to evolve around it in turn.

用一种致病生物来对抗另一种致病生物有几个好处。就像抗生素一样，噬菌体在选择目标时也很挑剔，即使它们感染并摧毁细菌细胞，也会对人类细胞置之不理。与抗生素不同，噬菌体可以像细菌一样迅速进化，这意味着即使细菌产生耐药性，噬菌体也可以反过来进化。

That, at least, is the theory. The trouble with phages is that comparatively little is known about them. After the discovery of penicillin, the first antibiotic, in 1928, they were largely ignored in the West. Only the Soviet Union, powered by research and production facilities in Georgia, continued to use them. Given the gravity of the antibiotic-resistance problem, it would be a good idea to find out more.

至少理论上是这样。噬菌体的问题在于，人们对它们的了解相对较少。1928年，第一种抗生素青霉素被发现后，它们在西方基本上被忽视了。只有苏联在格鲁吉亚的研究和生产设施的支持下，继续在使用它们。考虑到抗生素耐药性问题的严重性，找出更多噬菌体是个好主意。

The first step is to run more clinical trials. Interest from Western firms is growing. But it is being held back by the fact that phages are an even less appealing investment than antibiotics. Since they are natural organisms there may be trouble patenting them, making it hard to recoup any investment.

第一步是进行更多的临床试验。西方公司的兴趣正在增长。但由于噬菌体的投资吸引力甚至不如抗生素，这一事实阻碍了它的发展。由于它们是天然生物，申请专利可能会遇到麻烦，因此很难收回任何投资。

Governments can help. They could fund basic research into phage therapy, and clarify the law around exactly what is and is not patentable. In time they could set up phage banks and manufacturing processes, so as to make production cheaper. And they could spread awareness of the risks of overusing antibiotics, and the potential benefits of phages. If you are put off by the thought of ingesting a virus, consider that penicillin was a mould.

政府可以提供帮助。他们可以资助噬菌体疗法的基础研究，并明确法律规定哪些可以申请专利，哪些不能申请专利。假以时日，他们可以建立噬菌体库和生产流程，从而降低生产成本。它们还可以让人们意识到过度使用抗生素的风险，以及噬菌体的潜在好处。如果你对摄入病毒的想法感到反感，那就把青霉素当作一种霉菌吧。

Going viral

The history of antibiotics themselves shows that governments can help nudge the private sector into action. Penicillin was largely ignored at first by doctors, who regarded it as too difficult to produce. It took the tragedy of the second world war, and the intervention of the American and British governments, to kickstart the modern antibiotics industry. Compared with a war, antibiotic resistance is a slow-burning problem. Nonetheless, the time to act is now. ■

抗生素本身的历史表明，政府可以帮助推动私营部门采取行动。起初，医生们基本上忽略了青霉素，认为它太难生产。直到第二次世界大战的悲剧，以及美国和英国政府的干预，才启动了现代抗生素产业。与战争相比，抗生素耐药性是一个缓慢产生的问题。尽管如此，现在是采取行动的时候了。■

Governments are living in a fiscal fantasyland

各国政府都生活在财政幻镜中

Bust budgets

![image-20230508095853147](/Users/chenyujie/Library/Application Support/typora-user-images/image-20230508095853147.png)

The world over, they are failing to confront the dire state of their finances

在世界范围内，他们都无法面对他们糟糕的财政状况

May 4th 2023

Wrangling over budgets is always part of politics, but today’s fiscal brinkmanship is truly frightening. In America Democrats and Republicans are playing a game of chicken over raising the government’s debt ceiling. As the drama intensifies, the stakes are getting dangerously high. Janet Yellen, the treasury secretary, says her department could run out of cash to pay the government’s bills on June 1st if no deal is struck. Investors are beginning to price in the risk of what would be America’s first-ever sovereign default.

围绕预算展开争论一直是政治的一部分，但今天的财政边缘政策确实令人恐惧。在美国，民主党和共和党在提高政府债务上限的问题上正在玩一场懦夫游戏。随着剧情的升级，赌注也越来越高。财政部长珍妮特•耶伦表示，如果没有达成协议，6月1日财政部支付给政府账单的现金将耗尽。投资者开始将美国有史以来第一次主权债务违约的风险考虑在内。

The political point-scoring also misses a bigger and more enduring problem. America’s budget deficit is set to balloon as its population ages, the cost of handouts swells and the government’s interest bill rises. We estimate that deficits could reach around 7% of gdp a year by the end of this decade—shortfalls America has not seen outside of wars and economic slumps. Worryingly, no one has a sensible plan to shrink them.

政治上的评头论足也忽略了一个更大、更持久的问题。美国的预算赤字将随着人口老龄化、救济成本的膨胀和政府利息账单的上升而膨胀。我们估计，到本十年结束时，赤字将达到每年GDP的7%左右——除了战争和经济衰退之外，美国从未出现过赤字。令人担忧的是，没有人有合理的计划来缩减赤字。

Governments elsewhere face similar pressures—and appear just as oblivious. Those in Europe are locked in a silly debate about how to tweak debt rules, at a time when the European Central Bank is indirectly propping up the finances of its weakest members. China’s official debt figures purport to be healthy even as the central government prepares to bail out a province. Governments are stuck in a fiscal fantasyland, and they must find a way out before disaster strikes.

其他国家的政府也面临着类似的压力，而且似乎同样无动于衷。在欧洲央行间接支持其最弱成员国的财政之际，欧洲各国陷入了一场关于如何调整债务规则的愚蠢辩论。尽管中央政府准备为一个省纾困，但中国的官方债务数据据称是健康的。各国政府陷入了财政幻境，他们必须在灾难降临之前找到一条出路。

For the decade after the global financial crisis of 2007-09 falling interest rates allowed governments to sustain vast debt piles. Although Europe and, to a degree, America took an axe to public spending after the crisis, by the late 2010s it looked as if they needn’t have bothered. Long-term interest rates kept falling even as debt rose. Japan’s net debt passed 150% of gdp without consequence. When covid-19 struck, rich-world governments spent another 10% of gdp; Europe’s energy crisis led to yet more handouts. Hardly anyone worried about more debt.

在2007-2009年全球金融危机之后的十年里，不断下降的利率让各国政府得以维持巨额债务。尽管欧洲和美国(在一定程度上)在危机后大幅削减了公共支出，但到2010年代末，他们似乎没有必要为此费心。即使债务上升，长期利率仍在下降。日本的净债务超过了GDP的150%，却没有产生任何后果。当covid-19爆发时，发达国家的政府又花费了GDP的10%; 欧洲的能源危机导致了更多的援助。几乎没有人担心更多的债务问题。

Those days of forgivingly low interest rates have now passed. This week the Federal Reserve raised rates again, to 5-5.25%. America will spend more on debt interest this year, as a share of gdp, than at any time so far this century; by 2030 the bill will be at an all-time high, even if rates fall as markets expect. Japan no longer looks so safe. Even though rates there are super-low the government spends 8% of its budget on interest, a figure that will shoot up should the central bank begin tightening monetary policy.

那些低利率的日子已经过去了。本周，美联储再次将利率上调至5-5.25%。作为GDP的一部分，美国今年在债务利息上的支出将超过本世纪迄今的任何时候；到2030年，即使利率像市场预期的那样下降，该账单也将达到历史最高水平。日本看上去不再那么安全了。尽管利率极低，但政府在利息上的支出占预算的8%，如果央行开始收紧货币政策，这个数字将会飙升。

Rising rates are squeezing budgets just as pressures to spend are mounting. Ageing populations mean that by the end of the decade the annual health-care and pension bill in the rich world will have risen by 3% of gdp. The figure is 2% even in emerging markets, including China, where by 2035 there will be 420m over-65s. In the West policymakers have yet to deliver on promises to spend more on defence in light of Russia’s invasion of Ukraine. And the whole world needs more green public spending if it is to decarbonise rapidly.

不断上升的利率正在挤压预算，而支出压力也在不断增加。人口老龄化意味着，到本十年末，发达国家每年的医疗保健和养老金支出将上升到GDP的3%。即使在包括中国在内的新兴市场，这个数字也只有2%。到2035年，中国65岁以上人口将达到4.2亿。鉴于俄罗斯入侵乌克兰，西方的政策制定者尚未兑现在国防上投入更多资金的承诺。而且如果全世界想要快速脱碳，就需要更多的绿色公共支出。

All told, the picture is forbidding. Take America’s Inflation Reduction Act, which was supposed to reduce deficits. Its green tax credits were forecast to cost $391bn over a decade, but are now expected by Goldman Sachs to cost an eye-watering $1.2trn. Add that and the likely extension of temporary tax cuts enacted during Donald Trump’s presidency to official projections, and America is on a path to budget deficits of 7% of gdp, even as the economy grows.

总而言之，这一前景令人生畏。以美国旨在减少赤字的《通胀削减法案》为例。据预测，其绿色税收抵免在未来10年将耗资3910亿美元，但高盛目前预计将耗资1.2万亿美元，这一数字令人惊讶。再加上唐纳德•特朗普总统任期内实施的临时减税措施可能会延长到官方预测的范围内，即使经济在增长，美国的预算赤字也将达到GDP的7%。

Such looming pressures make politicians’ proposals look wildly unrealistic. The bill to raise America’s debt ceiling passed by Republicans in the House of Representatives on April 27th caps spending in 2024 at its level in 2022, and then raises budgets by 1% a year. That may sound reasonable but it excludes mandatory spending on pensions and health care and ignores inflation. Exclude defence spending as well and it implies a real-terms budget cut of 27% compared with current plans.

这些迫在眉睫的压力使得政客们的提议看起来非常不现实。共和党于4月27日在众议院通过了提高美国债务上限的法案，该法案将2024年的支出限制在2022年的水平上，然后每年将预算提高1%。这可能听起来很合理，但它不包括养老金和医疗保健的强制性支出，也忽略了通货膨胀。也不包括国防开支，这意味着与目前的计划相比，实际预算减少了27%。

Germany’s government seems to think that a target of 60% for debt-to-gdp ratios can credibly apply to places like Italy, which has net debts of more than twice that amount. In Britain the government makes a mockery of its rules, for example by promising tax rises that are perennially postponed. A few months of better-than-expected receipts are enough to set off a clamour for tax cuts among the ruling Conservatives, only months after Britain faced a bond-market crisis.

德国政府似乎认为，60%的债务与GDP比率目标更适用于意大利这样的国家，意大利的净债务是该数字的两倍多。在英国，政府对其规定进行了嘲弄，例如承诺永久推迟增税。几个月好于预期的财政收入足以在执政的保守党中掀起减税的呼声，而几个月前英国还面临着债券市场危机。

China’s increasing indebtedness is kept off the books in opaque “financing vehicles” used by local governments. Include everything and China’s total public debts are over 120% of gdp, and will rise to nearly 150% by 2027, on imf forecasts. Such levels of debt are affordable only because China has an ocean of domestic savings, kept captive by its restrictions on capital flows. Public indebtedness means that the government cannot achieve its plan to rebalance its economy towards consumption and internationalise the yuan.

中国不断增加的债务，通过地方政府使用的不透明的“融资平台”被隐藏在账簿之外。根据国际货币基金组织的预测，包括所有因素在内，中国的公共债务总额超过GDP的120%，到2027年将上升到近150%。这种债务水平之所以能够负担得起，只是因为中国拥有大量的国内储蓄，而这些储蓄受制于其对资本流动的限制。公共债务意味着政府无法实现其经济再平衡、转向消费和人民币国际化的计划。

Politicians need to get real, fast. Public debts are in danger of becoming unmanageable, especially if interest rates stay high. Every step up in borrowing hampers governments’ ability to respond to the next crisis. And there are limits to how far spending can be controlled. Politicians could dial down their promises to pensioners or ensure that their role in the green transition is not larger than it needs to be. But there is little public appetite for austerity, and spending is bound to rise as populations age. More defence spending and green investment are essential.

政客们需要现实一点，快一点。公共债务有变得难以控制的危险，尤其是在利率居高不下的情况下。借贷的每一步都阻碍了政府应对下一次危机的能力。而且，对支出的控制程度也是有限的。政客们可以减少对养老金领取者的承诺，或者确保他们在绿色转型中的作用不会超过需要的程度。但是公众对紧缩政策没有什么兴趣，而且随着人口老龄化，支出必然会增加。更多的国防开支和绿色投资至关重要。

All this makes tax rises inevitable. And more taxation makes it crucial to raise money in ways that are friendly to economic growth. Britain’s under-taxation of posh houses is scandalous; America lacks a value-added tax and China sorely needs its long-promised property tax. Carbon emissions should be taxed sufficiently everywhere, which would also encourage the private sector to invest more in decarbonisation and thereby reduce the need for public spending to that end.

所有这些都使得增税不可避免。增加税收使得以有利于经济增长的方式筹集资金变得至关重要。英国对豪华住宅的税收不足是可耻的；美国没有增值税，而中国迫切需要承诺已久的房产税。所有地方都应该对碳排放征收足够的税，这也将鼓励私营部门加大对脱碳的投资，从而减少为此目的对公共支出的需求。

Leaving fiscal fantasyland will be painful, and there will undoubtedly be calls to put off consolidation for another day. But it is far better to make a careful exit now than to wait for the illusion to come crashing down. ■

离开财政幻想世界将是痛苦的，而且毫无疑问会有人呼吁将财政整顿推迟到明天。但与其坐等幻想破灭，不如现在谨慎退出。■
