Why everyone wants Arm

为什么人人都想要Arm?

Arm twisting and turning

![image-20220629101016710](/Users/chenyujie/Library/Application Support/typora-user-images/image-20220629101016710.png)

The British chip designer’s modest size belies its central role in the digital revolution

这个英国芯片设计公司的有限规模掩盖了他在数字革命中的核心角色

Jun 22nd 2022

Tech giants, governments, trustbusters, investors: all eyes are on the much-anticipated stockmarket listing of Arm. Despite the recent rout in tech stocks, SoftBank, the Japanese group that paid $32bn for the British chip designer in 2016, still plans to refloat its shares by next March. On May 30th Cristiano Amon, boss of Qualcomm, an American chipmaker, told the Financial Times he would like to create a consortium with rivals like Intel or Samsung, either to buy a controlling stake in Arm or to purchase it outright—as Nvidia, another American firm, tried to do in 2020 in an abortive $40bn deal. Some British politicians argue that Arm is so critical that the government should take a controlling “golden share”. On June 14th it was reported that, perhaps in response, SoftBank was considering a secondary listing in London alongside the primary one in New York.

知情人士透露说，软银集团准备将Arm公司部分持股在伦敦股票交易所上市，之前软银只打算在美国上市。软银集团调整了上市计划，它会将Arm持股的大部分在美国上市，还有一部分持股在伦敦上市，目前仍不清楚上市的融资规模及时间，计划仍然存在变数。

科技巨头、政府、反垄断官员、投资者：所有的目光都集中在备受期待的Arm股票上市上。尽管近期科技股大跌，但曾在2016年斥资320亿美元收购这家英国芯片设计公司的日本软银集团仍计划在明年3月前重新上市。5月30日，美国芯片制造商高通的老板克里斯蒂亚诺•安蒙告诉《金融时报》，他希望与英特尔或三星等竞争对手组建一个财团，要么收购Arm的控股权，要么就全盘收购，就像另一家美国公司英伟达在2020年试图做的那样，那笔400亿美元的交易最终以失败告终。一些英国政客辩称，Arm至关重要，政府应该持有控股权的“黄金股”。6月14日，有报道称(也许是为了回应)，软银可能正在考虑在纽约上市的同时，在伦敦进行二次上市。

Look at Arm’s finances and the interest seems puzzling. Its sales rose by 35% last year **to $2.7bn**—not bad, but peanuts next to the giants of chip design. Its valuation, as implied by the Nvidia deal, has risen by a quarter in six years. In the same period Qualcomm’s market capitalisation is up by half and Nvidia’s has risen 13-fold, recent market carnage notwithstanding.

看看Arm的财务状况，投资者的兴趣似乎令人费解。去年，它的销售额增长了35%，达到27亿美元，这还不错，但与芯片设计巨头相比微不足道。正如英伟达交易所暗示的那样，该公司的估值在6年内上涨了四分之一。与此同时，高通的市值增长了一半，英伟达的市值增长了13倍，尽管最近市场遭遇了重创。

There are two explanations of the mismatch between Arm’s size and the covetousness it elicits. The first is the ubiquity of its products. Spun out of the wreckage of Acorn Computers, a British maker of desktops, in 1990, Arm has grown to the point where nearly all big tech firms use its designs. Most modern phones contain at least one chip built atop its technology. That makes it a keystone in the $500bn chip industry. Arm’s second selling point is its potential. After years of trying, its designs are making inroads into lucrative markets such as personal computers and data centres. They could also power everything from cars to light bulbs as more everyday objects become computers.

Arm的规模与其引发的贪婪之间的不匹配有两种解释。首先是它的产品的普遍性。1990年，Arm从英国台式电脑制造商艾康电脑的废墟中剥离出来，现在其已经发展到几乎所有大型科技公司都在使用它的设计。大多数现代手机都包含至少一个基于其技术的芯片。这使得它成为价值5000亿美元的芯片行业的基石。Arm的第二个卖点是它的潜力。经过多年的尝试，它的设计正在进军利润丰厚的市场，如个人电脑和数据中心。随着越来越多的日常物品变成“计算机”，它们还可以为从汽车到灯泡的一切事物供电。

Start with the ubiquity. Unlike firms such as Intel, which sells chips that it both designs and manufactures, Arm trades only in intellectual property (ip). For a fee, anyone can license one of its off-the-shelf designs, tweak it if necessary, and sell the resulting chip. Besides licensing revenue, Arm takes a small royalty from every sale of a chip built with its technology. In 2021 licensing revenues accounted for a bit over $1bn, while royalties brought in $1.5bn.

先从普遍性说起。与像英特尔这样同时销售芯片设计和制造芯片的公司不同，Arm只交易知识产权。只要付费，任何人都可以授权其现成的设计，在必要时对其进行调整，并出售由此产生的芯片。除了授权收入，Arm每销售一款使用其技术制造的芯片，就会从中收取少量版税。2021年，其授权收入占比略高于10亿美元，而版税收入为15亿美元。

![image-20220628093547777](/Users/chenyujie/Library/Application Support/typora-user-images/image-20220628093547777.png)

Removing the need to design a chip—a complicated, highly specialised job—has made Arm’s off-the-shelf designs popular, especially as chips have become more and more complicated. New Street Research, a firm of analysts, reckons Arm has a 99% share of the $25bn market for smartphone chips. Its products are widely used in everything from drones and washing machines to smart watches and cars. Arm says it has sold just under 2,000 licences since its founding (see chart). More than 225bn chips based on its designs have been shipped. It hopes to hit 1trn by 2035.

设计芯片是一项复杂、高度专业化的工作，不再需要设计芯片使得Arm的现成设计受到欢迎，尤其是在芯片变得越来越复杂的情况下。据分析公司NSR估计，在价值250亿美元的智能手机芯片市场中，Arm占有99%的份额。其产品广泛应用于从无人机、洗衣机到智能手表和汽车的各个领域。Arm表示，自其成立以来，它只售出了不到2000个许可证(见图表)。基于其设计的芯片已售出2250多亿块。它希望到2035年达到1万亿块芯片。

The firm’s long customer list explains the backlash against Nvidia’s proposed buy-out. Simon Segars, who stepped down as Arm’s boss this year, used to describe the firm as the neutral “Switzerland of the tech industry”. Other chipmakers feared that giving a rival control of it would undermine this neutrality, explains Geoff Blaber of ccs Insight, a research firm. So did trustbusters in big markets, whose concerns derailed the deal. Few were reassured when Jensen Huang, Nvidia’s ceo, insisted that he had no plans to use Arm to stymie rivals.

Arm公司很长的客户名单就解释了为什么英伟达的收购计划遭到强烈反对。今年卸任Arm老板的西蒙•西格斯曾将Arm形容为中立的“科技行业中的瑞士”。研究公司CCS Insight的杰夫·布雷伯解释说，其他芯片制造商担心让竞争对手控制芯片会破坏这种中立性。大型市场的反垄断检察官也是如此，他们的担忧导致交易失败。英伟达首席执行官黄仁勋坚称，他没有利用Arm来阻挠竞争对手的计划。

Arm’s ability to attract so many customers points to another explanation for the mismatch between its importance and its finances. A big reason why Arm’s technology triumphed over rival chip architectures was low prices. New Street reckons that Arm earns royalties of just $1.50 from the sale of a high-end smartphone, for which a consumer may fork out $1,000 or more. Cheaper gadgets might earn it a few cents.

Arm吸引如此多客户的能力，为其重要性与财务状况之间的不匹配提供了另一种解释。Arm的技术战胜竞争对手芯片架构的一个重要原因是价格低廉。New Street估计，Arm从一款高端智能手机的销售中只获得1.5美元的版税，而消费者可能会为此支付1000美元或更多。更便宜的设备可能只能赚几美分。

The firm has raised its royalty rates over time, notes Pierre Ferragu of New Street, often when a new version of its designs is released. According to one insider, SoftBank wanted to increase them further. But, he says, the plan caused friction with Arm’s bosses, who worried this would irk existing customers. It could also jeopardise Arm’s effort to conquer new markets.

New Street的皮埃尔•费拉古指出，该公司已经多次上调其版税，通常调整是在其新版本设计发布的时候。据一位内部人士透露，软银希望进一步增加这一比例。但是，他说，该计划引起了与Arm的老板的摩擦，他们担心这会惹恼现有的客户。这还可能危及Arm占领新市场的努力。

In 2020 Apple, which has long used Arm chips in iPhones, began replacing Intel silicon in its laptops and desktops with Arm-based designs. Although Apple is not as big in this area as it is in smartphones, it was a vote of confidence for Arm in what had been foreign territory.

一直在iphone上使用Arm芯片的苹果公司，在2020年开始在笔记本电脑和台式电脑上用基于Arm的设计取代英特尔的芯片。尽管苹果在电脑这一领域的影响力不如其在智能手机的影响力，但这仍然给Arm在这一此前陌生的领域投下信任票。

Arm has also increasingly been competing in the high-margin business of servers, the high-spec machines found in data centres. That market has for decades been dominated by Intel, but in recent years Arm has scored notable victories. Amazon Web Services, the e-commerce giant’s cloud division, now uses lots of Arm-derived “Graviton” chips. Ampere, an American firm that sells data-centre chips, also bases its products on Arm’s designs, as do several makers of specialised processors for tasks such as managing networks. TrendForce, another research firm, predicts that Arm processors could account for 22% of installed server chips by 2025.

Arm也在积极竞争服务器这一高利润领域的市场。服务器是数据中心中的高规格机器。几十年来，这个市场一直由英特尔主导，但近年来，Arm取得了显著的胜利。电子商务巨头亚马逊的云部门AWS现在大量使用Arm衍生的“Graviton”芯片。销售数据中心芯片的美国公司安培的产品也以Arm的设计为基础，几家专门用于网络管理等任务的处理器制造商也是如此。另一家研究公司TrendForce预测，到2025年，Arm处理器在服务器芯片装机量中占22%的份额。

Under SoftBank’s ownership Arm has put lots of money into research and development, says Mr Blaber. That will help it maintain its technological edge. It is nevertheless limited in how much it can charge for its products by the emergence of a new challenger: risc-v. This is a novel chip architecture that lacks royalties and licence fees entirely. In 2020 Renesas, an Arm licensee, announced it would use risc-v for a new generation of products. Intel, Qualcomm and Samsung, among others, are also eyeing the technology. Whatever Arm’s fate, then—as a public company, a state-controlled one or the ward of a consortium of chip-industry heavyweights—its future will therefore probably resemble its past: vital but, by Silicon Valley standards, a minnow. ■

布雷伯先生说，在软银的控股下，Arm已经在研发上投入了大量的资金。这将有助于它保持技术优势。然而，由于RISC-V架构这一新的挑战者的出现，它的产品收费受到了限制。RISC-V是一种新颖的芯片架构，完全不需要版税和授权费。2020年，Arm的授权商瑞萨电子宣布将在新一代产品中使用RISC-V架构。英特尔、高通和三星等公司也盯上了这项技术。Arm的未来，无论是作为一家上市公司、一个国家控制的公司，还是被一个芯片行业巨头财团的控制，其可能会和它的过去差不多：至关重要，但按照硅谷的标准，它还是“微不足道”。■

Why managers deserve more understanding

为什么管理者应该得到更多的理解

Bartleby

巴托比

![image-20220628092937595](/Users/chenyujie/Library/Application Support/typora-user-images/image-20220628092937595.png)

Don’t overdo the sympathy, but the job is both necessary and demanding

不要过分同情，但这项工作既是必要的，其要求也很高

Jun 23rd 2022

Management is not a heroic calling. There is no Marvel character called “Captain Slide Deck”. Books and television shows set in offices are more likely to be comedic than admiring. When dramas depict the workplace, managers are almost always covering up some kind of chemical spill. Horrible bosses loom large in reality as well as in the popular imagination: if people leave their jobs, they often do so to escape bad managers. And any praise for decent bosses is tempered by the fact that they are usually paid more than the people they manage: they should be good.

管理不是一个英雄的使命。漫威漫画里可没有“PPT船长”这个角色。以办公室为背景的书籍和电视节目更有可能是喜剧性的，而不是令人赞赏的。当戏剧中描述工作场所时，管理者们几乎总是在掩盖某种化学品泄漏。可怕的老板在现实中和在大众的想象中都令人忧虑：如果人们辞职，通常是为了摆脱糟糕的管理者。而且，任何对好老板的赞扬都会因为他们的薪水通常比他们管理的员工高而有所减弱：他们理应是好的。

A world without managers is a nice idea. But teams need leaders, irrespective of the quality of the people in charge. Someone has to take decisions, even if they are bad ones, to prevent the corporate machine gumming up with endless discussions. That is true even of flatter organisations. In a paper published in 2021, researchers described an experiment in which a number of different teams took part in an escape-room challenge. Some randomly selected groups were asked to choose a leader before the task began; the rest were not. The teams with leaders did much better: 63% of them completed the challenge within an hour, compared with only 44% of those in the control group.

一个没有管理者的世界是个好想法。但是团队需要领导者，不管负责人的素质如何。必须有人做决定，即使是糟糕的决定，这是为了防止企业因无休止的讨论而陷入混乱。即便是那些较为扁平的组织也是如此。在2021年发表的一篇论文中，研究人员描述了一项实验，在该实验中，多个不同的团队参加了“密室逃生”挑战。一些随机选择的小组被要求在任务开始前选择一个领导；其余的小组则无需如此。有领导者的团队做得更好：63%的人在一小时内完成了挑战，而对照组只有44%的人完成了挑战。

The difference between good bosses and bad ones is striking. In one paper published in 2012, a trio of academics looked at the output of workers in a large services company who frequently switched between different supervisors. They found that the gap in output between the best and worst bosses was equivalent to adding an extra person to a nine-member team. Even the average boss enhanced their team’s productivity by enough to justify their higher salary.

好老板和坏老板之间的差别是惊人的。在2012年发表的一篇论文中，三位学者研究了一家经常变换管理者的大型服务公司中的员工的产出。他们发现，最好的老板和最差的老板之间的产出差距，相当于在一个9人团队中增加了一个人。即使是普通的老板，也能通过提高团队的生产力来证明他们的高薪是合理的。

Managers are needed, but they do not have it easy. The job is structurally difficult. Most managers have to meet the expectations, sometimes unreasonable, of people below them and above them. The blurring of work-life boundaries as a result of the covid-19 pandemic seems to have made life tougher for them. Gallup, a pollster, found that in 2021 managers suffered higher levels of self-reported burnout than workers, and that the gap between these groups had widened considerably over the previous year.

我们需要管理人员，但他们过得并不容易。这项工作在结构上是困难的。大多数管理者都必须满足上级和下级的期望，有时这些期望甚至是不合理的。疫情导致工作与生活界限模糊，这似乎让他们的生活更加艰难。民意调查机构盖洛普发现，2021年，管理者们自述倦怠程度高于工人，而且与前一年相比，这两个群体之间的差距扩大了很多。

They are subject to conflicting demands. They are meant to care about members of their teams and be ready to get rid of them. They are supposed to give people agency while making sure that things are done in the way the organisation wants. The concept of the “servant leader” is utter nonsense. (What next? The weepy psychopath? The serf dictator?) It is also a reflection of the different directions in which bosses are pulled.

他们受到相互矛盾的要求的制约。他们应该关心自己的团队成员，又要做好解雇他们的准备。他们应该给人们提供帮助(在自己不出力的情况下)，同时还要确保事情按照组织想要的方式进行。“服务型领袖”的概念完全是无稽之谈。(下一个什么？眼泪汪汪的精神病患者?农奴独裁者?) 这也反映了老板们被拉向的不同方向。

Many of those in positions of power don’t want to be managing at all. True, some of them have found their way into management because of thrusting ambition. But others have wound up there because it is the only route available to more pay and greater influence. Hence another screwed-up office character: the “reluctant leader”.

许多身居要职的人根本不想做管理。诚然，他们中的一些人由于雄心勃勃，进入了管理层。但也有一些人最终选择了那里，是因为这是获得更高薪酬和更大影响力的唯一途径。因此又出现了另一个糟糕的办公室角色:“不情愿的领导”。

Managers are also handling the most baffling material on Earth: people. A study conducted by researchers in Germany found that handing out monetary bonuses for good attendance to apprentices in retail stores led to sharp rises in absenteeism (paying for behaviour that was previously considered normal seems to have made people feel licensed to bunk off). Another piece of research, by academics at iese Business School and the Poole College of Management, found that empowering employees could lead to more unethical behaviour if workers felt under greater pressure to perform. The law of unintended consequences runs through the workplace.

管理者们还在处理人世间最令人困惑的物质：人。德国研究人员进行的一项研究发现，在零售店给考勤好的学徒发放奖金会导致缺勤率急剧上升(为其行为付钱，曾被认为是正常的，现在似乎会让人们觉得自己得到了请假的许可)。IESE商学院和普尔管理学院的学者们进行的另一项研究发现，如果员工感受到更大的工作压力，那么赋予员工权力可能会导致更多不道德的行为。意外后果定律贯穿整个工作场所。

Managers are allegedly human, too, and also susceptible to bias. Bosses who take steps to encourage employees to contribute their ideas are doing the right thing by their organisations and by their teams. But according to research by Hyunsun Park of the University of Maryland and her co-authors, the more they solicit input, the less likely they are to reward people for speaking up. Instead, they credit themselves for creating the right kind of environment. Laudable, no. Natural, yes.

管理者也是人，也容易受到偏见的影响。那些采取措施鼓励员工贡献想法的老板，他们的组织和团队都在做正确的事情。但根据马里兰大学Hyunsun Park及其共同作者的研究，他们征求的意见越多，就越不可能奖励那些大胆发表意见的人。相反，他们认为自己创造了合适的环境。值得称赞，并不。这很正常，确实如此。

It is true that managers do not save lives or nurture young minds. Even the best ones spout jargon and cause unholy amounts of irritation. The worst ones make life a misery. But the job that managers do is almost always necessary, often unpopular, sometimes done reluctantly and pretty difficult to boot. Every so often that is worth remembering.

的确，管理者们不会拯救生命，也不会培养年轻人的思维。即使是最优秀的管理者，也会滔滔不绝地说一些行话，引起大量的愤怒。最坏的管理者则会让生活变得痛苦。但管理者们所做的工作几乎总是必要的，往往不受欢迎，有时是勉强完成的，而且很难被解雇。有时，这都是值得记住的。

Alphabet is spending billions to become a force in health care

Alphabet正在花费数十亿美元成为医疗领域的一支势力

Doctor Google will see you now

谷歌医生现在可以见你了

![image-20220627085129839](/Users/chenyujie/Library/Application Support/typora-user-images/image-20220627085129839.png)

Can it finally shake up the stodgy multi-trillion-dollar industry?

它最终能撼动这个耗资数万亿美元的乏味行业吗?

(Updated Jun 23rd 2022)

Rich countries pour heart-stopping amounts of money into health care. Advanced economies typically spend about 10% of gdp on keeping their citizens in good nick, a share that is rising as populations age. America’s labyrinthine health-industrial complex consumes 17% of gdp, equivalent to $3.6trn a year. The American system’s heft and inertia, perpetuated by the drugmakers, pharmacies, insurers, hospitals and others that benefit from it, have long protected it from disruption. Its size and stodginess also explain why it is being covetously eyed by big tech. Few other industries offer a potential market large enough to move the needle for the trillion-dollar technology titans.

发达国家在医疗保健方面投入了数量惊人的资金。发达经济体通常会花费GDP的10%来保证国民的健康，随着人口老龄化，这一比例还在上升。美国错综复杂的健康行业设施消耗了GDP的17%，相当于每年3.6万亿美元。长期以来，由于制药商、药店、保险公司、医院和其他从中受益的机构的影响，美国医疗体系的影响力和惰性使其免于崩溃。它的规模和古板也解释了为什么大型科技公司对它虎视眈眈。很少有其他行业能提供足够大的潜在市场，从而影响这些市值上万亿美元的科技巨头们。

America’s five tech behemoths have spent billions on various health-care bets. Some of their earlier health-related investments are starting to pay off. Amazon runs an online pharmacy and its telemedicine services reach just about everywhere in America that its packages do, which is to say most of it. Apple’s smartwatch keeps accruing new health features, most recently a drug-tracking one. Meta scrapped its own smartwatch plans earlier this year but offers fitness-related fun through its Oculus virtual-reality goggles. Microsoft keeps expanding its list of health-related cloud-computing offerings (as is Amazon, through aws, its cloud unit).

美国的五大科技巨头已经在各种医疗保健上投入了数十亿美元。他们在健康方面的一些早期投资开始取得成效。亚马逊经营着一家在线药店，它的远程医疗服务几乎覆盖了“它包裹可以送达的”美国的所有地方，也就是说美国的大部分地区。苹果的智能手表不断增加新的健康功能，最近还增加了药物跟踪功能。今年早些时候，Meta放弃了其智能手表计划，但它通过Oculus虚拟现实眼镜提供与健身相关的游戏。微软一直在扩大与健康相关的云计算产品(亚马逊也是如此，通过其云部门AWS)。

![image-20220626111937083](/Users/chenyujie/Library/Application Support/typora-user-images/image-20220626111937083.png)

Yet it is Alphabet, Google’s corporate parent, whose health-care ambitions are the most vaulting. Between 2019 and 2021 Alphabet’s venture-capital arms, Google Ventures and Gradient Ventures, and its private-equity unit, CapitalG, made about 100 deals, a quarter of Alphabet’s combined total, in life sciences and health care. So far this year it has injected $1.7bn into futuristic health ideas, according to cb Insights, a data provider, leaving its fellow tech giants, which have invested around $100m all told, in the dust (see chart on next page). Alphabet is the fifth-highest-ranking business in the Nature Index, which measures the impact of scientific papers, in the area of life sciences, behind four giant drugmakers and 20 spots ahead of Microsoft, the only other tech giant in the running. The company has hired former health regulators to help it navigate America’s health-care bureaucracy.

然而，谷歌的母公司Alphabet在医疗保健方面的野心最为雄心勃勃。在2019年至2021年间，Alphabet旗下的风投部门Google Ventures 和Gradient Ventures以及私募股权部门CapitalG在生命科学和医疗保健领域达成了约100笔交易，占Alphabet总交易数的四分之一。数据提供商CB Insights的数据显示，今年迄今，该公司已向极其新潮的健康理念注入了17亿美元，这令其他已投资约1亿美元的科技巨头相形见绌(见下一页图表)。在衡量科学论文影响的自然指数中，Alphabet在生命科学领域排名第五，落后于四家制药巨头，领先微软20位，微软是唯一一家参与竞争的科技巨头。该公司还聘请了前卫生监管负责人来帮助其应对美国的卫生保健官僚机构。

Alphabet’s approach to innovation—throw lots of money at lots of projects—has served it well in some other businesses beyond its core search engine. It has given rise to clever products, from Gmail and Google Docs to the Android mobile operating system and Google Maps, which support people’s digital lives. Alphabet thinks that some of its health offerings will become as central to their physical existence. Is that an accurate prognosis?

Alphabet的创新方式是在很多项目上投入大量资金，这在其核心搜索引擎之外的一些其他业务上也发挥了良好的作用。它催生了许多智能产品，从Gmail(谷歌邮箱)和Google Docs(谷歌文档)再到Android手机操作系统和Google Maps(谷歌地图)，这些产品支持着人们的数字生活。Alphabet认为，它的一些健康产品将成为其实体存在的核心。这一预测准确吗?

Alphabet has dabbled in health care since 2008, when Google (as it was then known) introduced a service that allowed users to compile their health records in one place. That project was wound up in 2012, resurfaced in 2018 as Google Health, which included Google’s other health ventures, and was again dismantled last year. Today Alphabet’s health adventures can be divided into four broad categories. These are, in rough order of ambition: wearables, health records, health-related artificial intelligence (ai) and the ultimate challenge of extending human longevity.

Alphabet自2008年开始涉足医疗保健领域，当时谷歌(当时的名称)推出了一项服务，允许用户在一个地方编辑他们的健康记录(健康档案)。该项目于2012年结束，2018年以谷歌健康项目重新出现，谷歌健康中包括谷歌的其他健康项目尝试，并于去年再次被废除。如今，Alphabet的健康尝试可以分为四大类。按目标的艰难顺序，它们分别是:可穿戴设备、健康记录、与健康相关的人工智能，以及延长人类寿命的终极挑战。

Google threw itself into the wearables business in 2019 with the $2.1bn acquisition of Fitbit. That firm’s popular fitness tracker has been counting steps and other exertions on around 100m wrists. It has come a long way since the Nintendo Wii motion-detecting game console that inspired Fitbit’s founders. A new feature—a sensor which monitors changes in the heart rate for irregularities that can lead to strokes and heart failure—has just been been approved by America’s Food and Drug Administraton (FDA). Google is also trying to boost the health-care potential of its other devices. To help it along, it has enlisted Bakul Patel, a former official tasked with creating the regulatory classification of “software as a medical device” at the FDA.

谷歌于2019年投身于可穿戴设备业务，其以21亿美元收购了Fitbit。该公司广受欢迎的健身追踪器一直在计算约1亿手腕上的步数和其他运动量。自从任天堂Wii动作检测游戏手柄启发了Fitbit的创始人以来，它已经取得了很大的进步。美国食品和药物管理局刚刚授权了一项新功能，即一种可以监测心律变化的传感器，以防心律失常导致中风和心力衰竭。谷歌还试图提升其其他设备在医疗保健方面的潜力。为了推进这一“事业”，该公司聘请了前FDA官员巴库尔·帕特尔，他曾负责在FDA制定“软件作为医疗设备”的监管分类。

The fda’s stamp of approval for the Fitbit sensor is a big deal. It should make it easier to get a similar thumbs-up for Google’s higher-end Pixel Watch, which uses a lot of the same technology and is due out this autumn, as well as other gadgets. For example, the camera on its Pixel phones can be used to detect respiration and heart rates by tracking the subtle colour difference brought about by the fact that blood with fresh oxygen in it is slightly brighter. Google’s Nest smart-thermostat-turned-home-assistant can listen to snoring to assess your sleep.

手机后置摄像头可检测用户心率，前置摄像头可获取用户的呼吸频率。

想要测量脉搏的用户可将手指放在镜头上，而镜头可以捕捉到与血液流动相对应的细微颜色变化。至于呼吸频率的测量，则将根据用户上半身运动的视频计算得出。

FDA对Fitbit传感器的授权是一件大事。谷歌的高端产品Pixel Watch也采用了很多相同的技术，预计将于今年秋天推出，这应该会让人们同样更容易对它以及其他小玩意表示赞同。例如，Pixel手机上的摄像头可以用来测量呼吸频率以及监测心率，心率可以通过追踪细微的颜色差异得到，这是因为新鲜氧气的血液稍微明亮一些。谷歌的Nest智能恒温器家庭助理可以通过听打鼾来评估你的睡眠状况。

As significant, if not more, is that Google considered the regulatory go-ahead worth getting. It signals that the company intends its products to be more than fun consumer gadgets, actually able to influence the practice of medicine.

同样重要的是(如果不是更重要的话)，谷歌认为获得监管部门的批准是值得的。这表明，该公司希望其产品不仅仅是有趣的消费电子产品，实际上是能够影响医疗实践。

Google is also giving health records another whirl. The new initiative, called Care Studio, is aimed at doctors rather than patients. Google’s earlier efforts in this area were derailed in part by hospitals’ sluggishness in digitising their patient information. That problem has mostly gone away but another has emerged, says Karen DeSalvo, Google’s health chief—the inability of different providers’ records to talk to each other. Dr DeSalvo has been vocal about the need for greater interoperability since her days in the Obama administration, where she was in charge of co-ordinating American health-information technology. Until that happens, Care Studio is meant to act as both translator and repository (which is, naturally, searchable).

谷歌也在“健康记录”方面做了另一个尝试。这项名为Care Studio的新倡议针对的是医生而不是病人。谷歌早期在这一领域的努力之所以失败，部分原因在于医院在数字化病人信息方面的迟缓。谷歌的健康主管Karen DeSalvo说，这个问题已经基本解决了，但是另一个问题又出现了，不同医疗机构的记录无法共享。DeSalvo博士在奥巴马执政期间负责协调美国医疗信息技术，自那时起，她就一直直言需要更强的互操作性。在此之前，Care Studio将同时充当翻译器以及信息库(当然，它是可搜索的)。

Place your Alpha bets

Alphabet’s ai projects are also beginning to produce results. Starting in 2016 DeepMind, a British firm bought by Google in 2014, used data from Britain’s National Health Service (nhs) to create diagnostic tools, in one case training an ai algorithm to detect retinal diseases. (DeepMind’s co-founder sits on the board of The Economist’s parent company.) It made headlines last year with AlphaFold, a groundbreaking piece of software that can predict the structure of proteins, which is responsible for many of the complex molecules’ characteristics. Alphabet has also launched another subsidiary, Isomorphic Labs, which will be run by DeepMind’s boss and use machine learning to build on AlphaFold to accelerate (and cheapen) drug discovery.

Alphabet的人工智能项目也开始出成果。谷歌于2014年收购了英国公司DeepMind，该公司从2016年开始使用英国国家医疗服务体系的数据创建诊断工具，其中一个案例是训练AI算法来检测视网膜疾病。(DeepMind的联合创始人是《经济学人》母公司的董事会成员。)去年，AlphaFold成了头条新闻，这是一款开创性的软件，它可以预测蛋白质的结构，而蛋白质是许多复杂分子特性的决定因素。Alphabet还成立了另一家子公司Isomorphic Labs，其将由DeepMind的老板运营，利用机器学习在AlphaFold的基础上加快药物发现以及降低药物发现的成本。

The most out-there part of Alphabet’s health portfolio is an effort to slow the ageing process—or stop it altogether. The idea is that ageing should be viewed not as an immutable aspect of life but as a condition that can be managed and treated, or a problem that can be solved with the right technology. To that end, one of Alphabet’s life-sciences subsidiaries, Calico, is looking into age-related diseases. Last year its $2.5bn partnership with firms including AbbVie, a big drug firm, was extended until 2030. Another Alphabet subsidiary, Verily, is working with L’Oréal, a French beauty giant, to better understand how ageing affects the biology of the skin—and in the process create better skincare.

Alphabet的健康投资组合中最引人注目的部分是努力延缓衰老过程，或者完全停止衰老过程。这个想法是，衰老不应该被视为生命中不可改变的一个方面，而应该被视为一种可以控制和治疗的状况，或者是一个可以用恰当的技术解决的问题。为此，Alphabet旗下的生命科学子公司Calico正在研究与年龄相关的疾病。去年，它与包括大型制药公司艾伯维在内的多家公司的25亿美元合作期限延长至2030年。Alphabet的另一家子公司Verily正在与法国美容巨头欧莱雅合作，以更好地了解衰老如何影响皮肤的生物学特性，并在此过程中创造更好的护肤产品。

Inspiring stuff, to be sure. But obstacles remain. Some are technical. The nhs data proved hard for DeepMind’s ai to digest. The firm’s ai assistant for doctors, called Streams, is being discontinued. Still, given the strides being made in machine learning, it may be only a matter of time before something like Streams is resuscitated.

这些当然是鼓舞人心的东西。但障碍依然存在。有些是技术障碍。事实证明，DeepMind的人工智能很难理解NHS的数据。该公司的人工智能医生助手Streams正在终止。不过，考虑到机器学习正在取得的进步，像Streams这样的产品的复苏可能只是时间问题。

Other hurdles may be harder to overcome. Trustbusters are increasingly wary of letting through deals that might be seen as stifling nascent rivals. In Europe competition authorities have forbidden Fitbit (but not the Pixel watch) from favouring Google’s own phones and operating system, or from using user data to sell advertising. Governments also fret about privacy breaches, which are more sensitive still when it comes to medical information. Last month plaintiffs filed a class-action lawsuit against DeepMind for misuse of nhs patient data. DeepMind has not made a public statement on the case.

其他障碍可能更难克服。反垄断检察官越来越警惕那些可能被视为扼杀新生竞争对手的交易。在欧洲，竞争监管机构禁止Fitbit (Pixel手表除外)支持谷歌自己的手机和操作系统，也禁止使用用户数据销售广告。政府还担心隐私泄露问题，这在涉及医疗信息时更为敏感。上个月，原告对DeepMind提起集体诉讼，指控其滥用NHS患者数据。DeepMind尚未就此案发表公开声明。

Last, good ideas are not the same things as a good business. The wearables market is highly competitive. So, increasingly, is the one for electronic health records. Google’s reputation for technical brilliance has not exactly made Care Studio into an overnight success; the system is reportedly used by just 200 or so clinicians. Verily, which besides solving ageing also offers various diagnostics, signed $50m-worth of contracts for covid-19 testing during the pandemic, a tidy sum but chump change next to Alphabet’s total annual revenues of nearly $260bn. DeepMind as a whole reportedly turned a profit for the first time in 2020 (seemingly from selling services back to the rest of Alphabet) but it gives away its flagship health product, AlphaFold, for nothing. Calico could be years away from generating real revenues, let alone profits.

最后，好的想法和好的生意不是一回事。可穿戴设备市场竞争激烈。因此，电子健康记录市场的竞争也越来越激烈。谷歌在技术上的卓越声誉并没有让Care Studio一夜成名；据报道，该系统仅被200名左右的临床医生使用。Verily除了解决老龄化问题，还提供各种诊断产品，在疫情期间签署了价值5000万美元的covid-19检测合同，这是一笔可观的费用，但与Alphabet近2600亿美元的年度总收入相比，只是九牛一毛。据报道，DeepMind整体在2020年首次实现盈利(似乎是通过向Alphabet的其他公司出售服务实现的)，但它的旗舰健康产品AlphaFold是开源的。Calico可能还需要数年时间才能产生真正的收入，更不用说利润了。

These are open-ended wagers that a company of Alphabet’s size can absorb with relative ease. In the next decade the challenge will be to show they can graduate from being experiments and vanity projects to something transformative for the firm—and for Americans’ health. ■

这些都是无限期的赌注，打赌像Alphabet这样规模的公司可以相对轻松地承受。在接下来的十年里，其挑战将会是，证明他们能够从实验和面子工程，升级到对公司以及美国人的健康具有变革性的东西。■

More of Britain’s pension assets should be used to drive business growth

更多的英国养老金资产应用于推动企业增长

Britain’s growth crisis

英国的增长危机

![image-20220626103347595](/Users/chenyujie/Library/Application Support/typora-user-images/image-20220626103347595.png)

They are stuck in low-yielding bonds while companies are hungry for capital

当公司急需资本时，它们却困于低收益债券

Jun 23rd 2022

Britain is a good place to be a budding entrepreneur. The country’s share of global venture-capital (vc) funding has doubled since 2018, to twice its share of global gdp. One out of every seven dollars the world invests in the earliest-stage “pre-seed” firms is invested in Britain. Although financial markets have taken a hammering this year, British vc funds have record amounts of unspent capital to throw at tomorrow’s winners.

英国是一个创业的好地方。自2018年以来，该国在全球风投融资中的份额翻了一番，达到其全球GDP份额的两倍。世界上每7美元中就有1美元投资于最早阶段的“种子轮之前”的公司。尽管金融市场今年遭受重创，但英国风投资金仍有创纪录的未动用资本，可以投向未来的赢家。

But Britain is not a good place to turn promising startups into titans. There are many reasons for that, from Brexit to poor productivity. But a big stumbling block is that as British firms grow, the capital they need dries up along the way.

但英国并不是一个把有前景的创业公司变成巨头的好地方。原因有很多，从英国脱欧到生产率低下。但是一个巨大的绊脚石是，在英国企业的增长过程中，他们所需的资本在这个过程中逐渐枯竭。

By the time these companies are raising tens of millions of dollars, rather than a few hundreds of thousands, their share of global vc funding has halved. “Deep tech” ventures, which try to develop industry-disrupting new technologies like quantum computing or artificial intelligence, fare especially poorly. And at the top of the capital ladder, Britain’s once-mighty stockmarket has drifted into insignificance. In 2006 it accounted for 18% of the equity capital raised in global initial public offerings. This year it has raised under 1%.

当这些公司筹集到数千万美元，而非几十万美元时，它们在全球风投融资中所占的份额已经减半。“深科技”企业的表现尤其糟糕，这些企业试图开发量子计算或人工智能等颠覆行业的新技术。英国曾经强大的股市，在资本阶梯的顶部，现已经变得无足轻重。2006年，它在全球首次公开募股中筹集到了18%的股本。今年，它的筹资不足1%。

![image-20220625103806556](/Users/chenyujie/Library/Application Support/typora-user-images/image-20220625103806556.png)

America provides a sobering contrast. It has spawned four trillion-dollar behemoths in the past five decades. Britain has not created a business worth even a tenth as much in more than a century. In 2011 the ftse 100 index of leading shares contained only two globally respected tech firms, Autonomy and Arm. Now it has none. Autonomy has been sold to Hewlett-Packard, an American giant, and its reputation tarnished by fraud claims (which its erstwhile leaders deny). Arm was bought by SoftBank, a Japanese conglomerate, and is now considering relisting on the nasdaq.

美国则与之截然不同。在过去的50年里，它催生了4个万亿美元的“巨头”企业。一个多世纪以来，英国都没有创造出一个价值相当于它十分之一的企业。2011年，英国富时100指数的主要股票只包含两家全球知名的科技公司，Autonomy和Arm。现在它没有了。Autonomy已被卖给美国巨头惠普，其声誉因欺诈指控而受损(其前任领导人对此予以否认)。Arm被日本软银集团收购，目前正考虑在纳斯达克重新上市。

Comparisons between Britain and America are often misplaced. Much of America’s success in nurturing corporate titans was forged in the crucible of Silicon Valley, which has far more than abundant growth capital to recommend it. First-mover advantage in the vc market, long-standing partnerships with America’s Department of Defence and a culture that celebrates enterprise—they all turbocharge innovation. But Britain’s courts, excellent universities and world-class financial centre should make it unusually fertile ground for firms to grow, too.

将英国和美国进行对比往往是不合时宜的。美国在培育企业巨头方面取得的许多成功，都是在硅谷的熔炉中锻造出来的，硅谷的优势在于其拥有极其充足的增长资本。在风投市场的先发优势、与美国国防部的长期合作关系以及推崇创业精神的文化——这些都是推动创新的动力。而英国的法院、优秀的大学和世界顶级的金融中心也应该为企业的发展提供异常“肥沃”的“土壤”。

What should be done? One focus ought to be to simplify corporate-governance rules. Britain has had more iterations of its baroque governance code in the past 25 years than it has had prime ministers. The result is a tangle of worthy disclosure requirements that distracts fledgling firms. Another should be to seek to attract those rare experts capable of making sensible commercial decisions in deep-tech areas like quantum computing. Britain’s excellent research universities are already a lure. One idea would be to create fellowships that combine an investment role with an academic one.

应该做些什么呢？一个重点应该是简化企业治理规则。在过去的25年里，英国对其巴洛克式治理准则的反复修改次数比它的首相还多。结果就导致一堆有价值的披露要求分散了新兴公司的注意力。另一个重点应该是设法吸引那些在量子计算等深科技领域能够做出明智商业决策的罕见专家。英国优秀的研究型大学已经具有吸引力。一种想法是创建兼具投资与学术角色的研究基金。

But the top priority is to direct the £4.6trn ($7.4trn) of assets held in British pension and insurance funds into more productive areas. Less than 1% of these assets is in unlisted equities. Defined-benefit pension schemes’ allocation to the British stockmarket has sunk from 48% to below 3%. The government should dilute the pension-fee cap that crimps investment in early-stage firms. It should reform accounting rules that incentivise funds to load up on low-yielding gilts rather than riskier equities. The plethora of tiny pension funds—like the 90-odd local-government schemes—should be merged so they can invest at scale. What Emmanuel Macron should learn from losing his majority

埃马纽埃尔·马克龙应该从失去多数席位中学到什么

![image-20220625102617249](/Users/chenyujie/Library/Application Support/typora-user-images/image-20220625102617249.png)

Will the French president be able to get anything done in his second term?

法国总统能在他的第二个任期内完成任何事情吗?

Jun 22nd 2022

Less jupiter than Icarus, Emmanuel Macron came crashing down this week. On June 19th French voters did something that was last seen over 30 years ago: they denied a newly elected president a majority in the National Assembly. It is not quite impossible to govern without one, but for the next five years Mr Macron will be scratching around for the votes he needs to get anything done. That is bad news for France—and for Europe too, which sorely lacks a leader of global stature. Angela Merkel left the field last winter, and the man who had hoped to reshape a continent after her departure will have his work cut out just keeping his own house in something that resembles order.

伊卡洛斯（希腊文：Ίκαρος英文名称：Icarus）是希腊神话中代达罗斯的儿子，与代达罗斯使用蜡和羽毛造的翼逃离克里特岛时，他因飞得太高双翼上的蜡遭太阳融化跌落水中丧生，被埋葬在一个海岛上。

埃马纽埃尔·马克龙本周“跌落在地”了，他比伊卡洛斯还小。6月19日，法国选民做了一件事：他们否决了新当选总统在国民议会中的多数席位，上次发生这种事还是在30多年前。没有多数席位也不是完全不可能执政，但在接下来的五年里，马克龙要想完成任何事情，他需要努力争取他所需要的选票。这对法国来说是个坏消息，对欧洲来说也是，因为欧洲极度缺乏一个具有全球声望的领导人。安格拉•默克尔去年冬天离开了政坛，而这位曾希望在她离开后重塑欧洲大陆的人，现在要做的就是努力把自己的国家打理得井井有条。

This outcome should not have been as much of a surprise as it felt. In the first round of the presidential election in April, slightly more than half the electorate cast votes for one or other candidate of the political extremes. Enough of them then rallied round Mr Macron in the run-off to allow him to handily defeat Marine Le Pen, the leader of the populist National Rally. But the impressive result masked the fact that Mr Macron, a buttoned-up technocrat who has never been able to shake off the label of le président des riches, is loved by only a few, and loathed by many. He won just 28% of the vote in the first round, after all. The French gave him his job back, but they also decided to strip him of much of his authority. Clearly**, Mr Macron will need to do things differently.**

这个结果不应该像它感觉的那样令人惊讶。在今年4月举行的第一轮总统选举中，略多于一半的选民将选票投给了一个或另一个政治极端主义候选人。随后，他们中有足够多的选民在决选中合力支持马克龙，让他轻松击败了民粹主义政党“国民联盟“的领袖玛丽娜·勒庞。但这一令人印象深刻的结果掩盖了一个事实：马克龙作为一个始终无法摆脱“富人的总统”标签的沉默寡言的技术官僚，他只受到少数人的喜爱，而许多人却对他深恶痛绝。毕竟，他在第一轮投票中只赢得了28%的选票。法国人让他复职，但他们也决定剥夺他的大部分权力。显然，马克龙将需要采取不同的做法。

But what, exactly, and how? To understand the scale of the task ahead consider how Mr Macron is in some ways the victim of his own success. In his first term his political machine was all-conquering, poaching the best and brightest from the centre-right Republicans and the centre-left Socialists. By straddling the mainstream, he pushed dissatisfied voters to Ms Le Pen’s hard right or the radical left of Jean-Luc Mélenchon. For Mr Macron to pacify the angry, left-behind voters who gave both this pair a huge electoral boost will not be easy, especially without spending even more money that France does not have.

但具体做什么，怎么做？要理解未来任务的规模，请考虑马克龙在某些方面是如何成为自己成功的受害者的。在他的第一个任期内，他的政治集团所向披靡，他从中右翼的共和党人和中左翼的社会党人那里挖来了最优秀最聪明的人才。他融合主流，把不满的选民推到勒庞的右翼或让-吕克·梅朗雄的激进左派。对于马克龙来说，要安抚愤怒的、给这两位候选人带来巨大选举支持的落选选民绝非易事，尤其是在不花法国更多的钱的情况下，法国也没有更多的钱。

Ideally Mr Macron would pursue the difficult long-term reforms that France badly needs—**simplifying its labyrinthine pensions system, laying the foundations for its energy transition and prising open its rigid, highly centralised education system**. Yet the future looks very difficult. Mr Macron’s legislative agenda is up in the air. He has summoned the party leaders to talks. The constitution gives him considerable powers, particularly in the areas of foreign and defence policy, and he also has the right, if need be, to force his budget and one other piece of legislation through parliament by decree in each sitting. But if he is seen to abuse that authority, he may provoke a no-confidence vote that could bring down his government and perhaps force yet another parliamentary election.

理想情况下，马克龙将推行法国迫切需要的艰难的长期改革——简化错综复杂的养老金体系，为能源转型奠定基础，开放僵化、高度集中的教育体系。然而，未来看起来非常艰难。马克龙的立法议程悬而未决。他已召集各党领导人进行会谈。宪法赋予他相当大的权力，尤其是在外交和国防政策方面。如果有必要，他还有权在每次会议上通过法令迫使议会通过他的预算法案以及另一项立法。但如果他被认为滥用权力，他可能会引发不信任投票，从而导致他的政府倒台，或许还会迫使再次举行议会选举。

One option is to try to assemble ad hoc majorities for individual bills. His parliamentary grouping, Ensemble, has 245 seats; 44 short of a majority. He may, for instance, hope to appeal to the Republicans to get his pension reform through; or to the Socialists and the Greens to help him out with the energy transition. Yet both measures will face resistance and if early elections are thought likely, no one will want to risk losing their own voters to the Macron-loathing extremes.

一种选择是为个别法案召集临时多数。他的议会集团“在一起联盟”拥有245个席位；差44票达到多数。例如，他可能希望呼吁共和党人通过他的养老金改革；或者求助于社会党和绿党来帮助他完成能源转型。然而，这两项举措都将面临阻力，而且如果有可能提前进行选举，没有人会愿意冒险失去自己的选民，让他们投向厌恶马克龙的极端。

France, with its almost monarchical presidency, lacks a tradition of coalition government. In Germany, the Netherlands or the Nordic countries such arrangements involve weeks or months of negotiation, to hammer out detailed policy agreements. French politics, as so often in the past, is deeply polarised, perhaps too much so for even the most limited agreement on policies. Mr Macron will therefore need to alter that culture. This will require him to embrace a new political style: no longer Jupiterian, controlled and analytical, but open, transactional and instinctive. It will not come naturally, but if he fails, his second term will be doomed to fail too. ■

How to fix the world’s energy emergency without wrecking the environment

如何在不破坏环境的情况下解决世界能源危机

The energy crisis

能源危机

![image-20220624101520285](/Users/chenyujie/Library/Application Support/typora-user-images/image-20220624101520285.png)

Even as they firefight, governments must resolve the conflict between safe supply and a safe climate

即使双方交火，政府也必须解决安全供应和安全气候之间的冲突

Jun 23rd 2022 (Updated Jun 23rd 2022)

This year’s energy shock is the most serious since the Middle Eastern oil crises of 1973 and 1979. Like those calamities, it promises to inflict short-term pain and in the longer term to transform the energy industry. The pain is all but guaranteed: owing to high fuel and power prices, most countries are facing soggy growth, inflation, squeezed living standards and a savage political backlash. But the long-run consequences are far from preordained. If governments respond ineptly, they could trigger a relapse towards fossil fuels that makes it even harder to stabilise the climate. Instead they must follow a perilous path that combines security of energy supply with climate security.

今年的能源危机是自1973年和1979年中东石油危机以来最严重的一次。就像那些灾难一样，短期内它注定会带来痛苦，从长期来看，它会改变能源行业。痛苦几乎是肯定的：由于高燃料和电力价格，大多数国家正面临着增长缓慢、通货膨胀、生活水平下降以及强烈的政治反对。但长期的后果远非注定。如果政府应对不当，就可能引发对化石燃料的再度依赖，从而使稳定气候变得更加困难。相反，它们必须走一条冒险的道路，将能源供应安全与气候安全结合起来。

In Europe what was long imagined as a nightmare of freezing midwinter nights has instead erupted as a midsummer fever dream. A heatwave forced Spanish gas demand to near-record highs, even as, on June 14th, Russia began to lower the flow of gas along the Nord Stream 1 pipeline to western Europe, sending prices soaring by 50% and raising fears that rationing may be introduced later this year. Elsewhere, Americans are paying $5 for a gallon of petrol (€1.25 a litre), fuelling the inflation that opinion polls say is their biggest worry and President Joe Biden’s worst headache. Australia’s power market has failed. Everywhere you look there are shortages and fragility.

在欧洲，长期以来被认为是寒冷仲冬夜晚的噩梦，现在却变成了仲夏热梦。热浪迫使西班牙的天然气需求接近历史新高，而就在6月14日，俄罗斯开始减少沿北溪1号管道向西欧输送的天然气，这导致天然气价格飙升50%，并引发了人们对今年晚些时候可能实行定量配给的担忧。在其他地方，美国人要为每加仑汽油支付5美元(每升1.25欧元)，这加剧了通胀，民调显示，通胀是他们最担心的问题，这也是美国总统乔•拜登最头疼的问题。澳大利亚的电力市场已经暂停。目光所及之处皆是“短缺”和“脆弱”。

Energy shocks can become political catastrophes. Perhaps a third of the rich world’s inflation of 8% is explained by soaring fuel and power costs. Households struggling to pay bills are angry, leading to policies aimed at insulating them and boosting fossil-fuel production, however dirty.

能源冲击可能成为政治灾难。在发达国家8%的通货膨胀率中，可能有三分之一是由飙升的燃料和电力成本造成的。那些难以支付账单的家庭感到愤怒，于是出台了旨在保护他们并提高化石燃料生产的政策，而不管化石燃料会导致污染有多严重。

Mr Biden, who came to power promising a green revolution, plans to suspend petrol taxes and visit Saudi Arabia to ask it to pump more oil. Europe has emergency windfall levies, subsidies, price caps and more. In Germany, as air-conditioners whine, coal-fired power plants are being taken out of mothballs. Chinese and Indian state-run mining firms that the climate-conscious hoped were on a fast track to extinction are digging up record amounts of coal.

拜登上台时承诺要进行一场绿色革命，他计划暂停汽油税，并访问沙特阿拉伯，要求其增加石油产量。欧洲有紧急暴利税、补贴、价格上限等等。在德国，随着空调发出呜呜声，燃煤电厂正在重新启用。中国和印度的国有矿业公司开采的煤炭数量创下了历史记录，而气候意识强烈的人曾希望这些公司很快就会走向灭绝。

This improvised chaos is understandable but potentially disastrous, because it could stall the clean-energy transition. Public handouts and tax-breaks for fossil fuels will be hard to withdraw. Dirty new power plants and oil- and gasfields with 30- to 40-year lifespans would give their owners more reason to resist fossil-fuel phase-outs. That is why, even as they firefight, governments must focus on tackling the fundamental problems confronting the energy industry.

这种临时的混乱是可以理解的，但有潜在的灾难性，因为它可能会阻碍清洁能源的转型。对化石燃料的公共补贴以及税收优惠将很难收回。污染严重的新发电厂和30-40年使用寿命的油气田会让它们的所有者更有理由抵制淘汰化石燃料。正因为如此，即使各国政府交火，其也必须集中精力解决能源行业面临的根本问题。

One priority is finding a way to ramp up fossil-fuel projects, especially relatively clean natural gas, that have an artificially truncated lifespan of 15-20 years so as to align them with the goal of dramatically cutting emissions by 2050. In particular Europe and Asia, which must wean themselves off Russian gas and coal respectively, have too little liquefied natural gas (lng) capacity. The trick is to get business to back schemes designed to be short-lived. One option is for governments and energy grids to offer guaranteed contracts over this period that provide an adequate return on the understanding that capacity will be shut down early. Another is to pledge eventual state support to make these projects cleaner, for example through carbon capture and storage.

当务之急是找到增加化石燃料的项目，尤其是相对清洁的天然气的项目，这些项目周期被人为地缩短为15-20年，以便与2050年大幅减排的目标保持一致。特别是欧洲和亚洲，它们必须分别摆脱对俄罗斯天然气和煤炭的依赖，它们的液化天然气产能太少。关键是要支持那些设计为短期的计划。一个选择是，政府和能源网在此期间提供担保合同，这些合同将提供足够的回报，但条件是产能将提前关闭。另一个是保证国家最终支持这些项目，例如通过碳捕获和储存，使其更加清洁。

This does not mean easing up on the drive towards renewables—the most successful part, to date, of the world’s generally poor response to the climate crisis. Every extra kilowatt-hour from the sun fed into Europe’s electricity grids is one fewer that comes down a Russian pipeline. Governments must improve the reach, capacity and storage capabilities of their grids and remove the obstacles that continue to make it harder to add renewable capacity than it should be. The design of power grids and markets is squarely a matter for governments and they are too often trapped in 20th-century thinking.

这并不意味着要放松对可再生能源的追求——这是迄今为止全球应对气候危机总体上表现不佳中最成功的部分。从太阳中每多获得一千瓦时(1度电)输送到欧洲电网，通过俄罗斯管道输送的电能就少一千瓦时。各国政府必须提高电网的覆盖范围、容量和存储能力，消除使增加可再生能源容量变得更加困难的障碍。电网和市场的设计完全是政府的事情，他们往往被困在20世纪的思维中。

As our Technology Quarterly reports, 21st-century thinking turns on new ways to provide smart, resilient grids with the zero-carbon “firm” power that makes dependence on renewables safe and effective. Hydrogen stripped from water with renewable electricity, or from natural gas with steam in facilities that store the emissions, may be crucial here. So, in many places, may nuclear power. Climate-conscious atomic enthusiasts often focus on whizzy but unproven small-scale nuclear plants. What matters more is to improve the building of big ones. Where there is strong and co-ordinated anti-nuclear opinion, governments must win support by showing that there are better safeguards against accidents and new ways to store waste, as our report from Finland explains. Politicians need to tell voters that their desire for an energy transition that eschews both fossil fuels and nuclear power is a dangerous illusion.

正如我们的Technology Quarterly所报道的，21世纪的思维转向了提供智能、有弹性的电网的新方法，同时提供零碳的“稳固”电力，使对可再生能源的依赖安全有效。用可再生电力从水中提取氢气(电解水制氢)，或者在储存排放的设施中用蒸汽从天然气中提取氢气(灰氢)，在这里可能至关重要。因此，在许多地方，可能会使用核能。对气候敏感的原子发烧友们经常把注意力放在那些技术先进但未经证实的小型核电站上。更重要的是要增加大型核电站的建设。正如我们在Finland栏目中所解释的那样，在反核意见强烈且协调一致的地方，政府必须通过证明有更好的安全措施来预防事故和储存核废料的新方法来赢得支持。政客们需要告诉选民，他们希望实现能源转型，既不使用化石燃料，也不使用核能，这是一种危险的幻想。

The last necessity is to make the industry predictable. That may sound strange given that 20th-century energy markets coped with wars, coups, revolutions, booming Chinese demand and new technology. But the climate transition has added an extra layer of uncertainty even as it simultaneously requires a massive increase in investment. In order to reach net-zero emissions by 2050, annual investment needs to double to $5trn a year, according to the International Energy Agency. The risk is that this latest crisis, and the chaotic government response to it, make investors warier instead.

最后一个必要条件是使该行业具有可预测性。这听起来可能有些奇怪，因为20世纪的能源市场经历了战争、政变、革命、中国旺盛的需求和新技术。但气候转型在需要大规模增加投资的同时，也增加了一层额外的不确定性。根据国际能源署的数据，为了在2050年前实现净零排放，每年的投资需要翻一番，达到每年5万亿美元。风险在于，最近的这场危机，以及政府对危机的混乱反应，反而会让投资者更加谨慎。

A different kind of revolution

Spurring investment means eschewing gimmicks including greenwashing, protectionist plans to build domestic green supply chains, and silly prohibitions by banks on gas projects. Instead it will require steadily extending measures with more certainty about which energy sources can be used and for how long. That means enhanced disclosure so that firms understand the externalities they create, an expansion of carbon prices so that they have a sense of the cost of pollution, and regulations that mandate the phasing out of dirty technologies. The great energy shock of 2022 is a calamity. But it could also be the moment when better government policy triggers the investment needed to resolve the conflict between having a safer supply of energy and a safer climate. ■

洗绿（Greenwash）是美国环保主义者杰伊·威斯特维尔德创造的概念，指的是企业伪装成“环境之友”，试图掩盖对社会和环境的破坏，以此保全和扩大自己的市场或影响力。

刺激投资意味着避免各种噱头，包括“洗绿”、建立国内绿色供应链的保护主义计划，以及银行对天然气项目的愚蠢禁令。相反，它将需要稳步扩大措施，更加确定哪些能源可以使用以及使用多长时间。这意味着加强信息披露，让企业了解它们创造的外部效应；扩大碳价格，让企业了解污染的成本；以及制定法规，要求逐步淘汰污染技术。2022年的巨大能源冲击是一场灾难。但它也可能是一个更好的政府政策触发所需投资的时刻，以解决拥有更安全的能源供应和更安全的气候之间的冲突。■

That is slower growth than the English average. The Office for National Statistics (ons) will soon update its population estimates to account for the 2021 census. For now, though, it calculates that Oxford’s population of about 150,000 is not growing at all. Even if you include surrounding districts containing lots of commuters—which is how the oecd, a club of mostly rich countries, defines urban areas—its growth is only average.

但在一个重要的方面，牛津几乎很普通。在过去的五年里，这座城市只增加了1440套净住房，住房存量以每年0.5%的速度增长。这一增长速度比英国的平均水平要低。英国国家统计局将很快更新其人口估计，以计入2021年人口普查。不过，据统计，目前牛津约15万人口根本没有增长。即使你把通勤者众多的周边地区也算在内——这是经合组织（一个主要由发达国家组成的俱乐部）对城市地区的定义——它的增长也只是平均水平。

![image-20220623100758708](/Users/chenyujie/Library/Application Support/typora-user-images/image-20220623100758708.png)

When it comes to population growth, British urban areas are all much of a muchness (see chart). Cambridge is just as successful as Oxford, yet the city and its hinterlands are growing slightly slower than its rival. Even London, whose success explains the government’s promise to “level up” the country, only grows by about 1% a year—again counting the commuter belt as part of the city. Not one metropolis is losing inhabitants. This pattern helps to explain why Britain is not more productive.

当谈到人口增长时，英国的城市地区增长水平不相上下(见图表)。剑桥和牛津一样“成功”，但这座城市及其腹地的发展略慢于它的竞争对手。甚至是伦敦，每年也只增长约1%——如果把通勤带算在城市的一部分的话，它的成功解释了政府“对齐”(拉平)国家的承诺。没有一个大都市正在失去居民。这种模式有助于解释为什么英国的生产率没有提高。

Such homogeneity is unusual. Germany has several shrinking metropolises, many in the former East Germany but some, like Saarbrücken, in the industrial west. French cities are also more varied in their growth rates than British ones. The range of population change in American metropolises, which stretches from -0.6% (Mahoning, Ohio) to 2.9% (Austin, Texas), is the widest of all.

这种同质性是不同寻常的。德国有几个正在萎缩的大都市，许多位于前东德，但也有一些，如萨尔布吕肯，位于工业化的西部。与英国城市相比，法国城市的增长速度也更加多样化。美国大城市的人口变化幅度最大，从-0.6%(俄亥俄州的马霍宁)到2.9%(德克萨斯州的奥斯汀)。

Three things determine urban growth rates: domestic migration, international migration and the balance of births and deaths. British cities commonly gain more international migrants than they lose and have a surplus of births. But they tend to lose more people to the rest of Britain than they gain, as middle-aged residents seek quieter, more spacious digs in small towns and villages. That is true of Birmingham, Edinburgh, London, Manchester and Oxford, among many others. By contrast, says William Frey of the Brookings Institution, a think-tank, many cities in fast-growing American states such as Florida and Texas are positive on all three measures.

有三个因素决定了城市增长率：国内移民、国际移民以及出生和死亡的平衡。英国城市获得的国际移民通常比失去的要多，而且有过剩的出生率。但随着中年居民在小城镇和村庄寻找更安静、更宽敞的住所，其倾向于让更多的人“流向”英国其他地方，而不是得到更多的人。伯明翰、爱丁堡、伦敦、曼彻斯特和牛津等城市都是如此。相比之下，智库布鲁金斯学会的威廉•弗雷说，在经济快速发展的美国各州，如佛罗里达州和得克萨斯州，在这三项指标上都很有把握。

The main reason successful British cities do not grow faster is the planning system, which allows local governments to block development. Oxford is surrounded by a 34,500-hectare (85,250-acre) “green belt”, a ring of land in which building is very difficult. Many green belts are much bigger: overall, 12% of England is so designated. “Green belts prevent cities from growing horizontally, height restrictions prevent them from growing vertically, and preservation rules prevent them from renewing themselves,” says Christian Hilber at the London School of Economics. Because most taxes are paid to central government, local authorities have little incentive to override planning restrictions.

成功的英国城市没有更快发展的主要原因是规划体系，这使地方政府可以阻碍发展。牛津被34500公顷(85250英亩)的“绿化带”包围着，在这片土地上建造建筑是非常困难的。许多绿化带要大得多：总的来说，12%的英格兰地区被划定为绿化带。伦敦经济学院的克里斯蒂安·希尔伯说:“绿化带阻碍城市水平发展，高度限制阻碍城市垂直发展，保护规则阻碍城市自我更新。”由于大部分税收都上缴给了中央政府，地方政府几乎没有动力推翻规划限制。

Large cities used to lose inhabitants: Liverpool, London and Manchester all did between the second world war and the 1990s. These days even struggling ones tend not to. They house the people who might move to successful cities if the latter were building more homes. Besides, says Paul Swinney of the Centre for Cities, a think-tank, people living in social housing are reluctant to move because they might have to join the queue in a new city.

大城市曾经也失去过居民：利物浦、伦敦和曼彻斯特都曾在二战至上世纪90年代期间失去过居民。如今，即使是苦苦挣扎的城市也往往不会失去居民。它们为那些可能移居成功城市的人提供住房，如果成功的城市建造更多的住房，他们可能会迁往成功的城市。此外，智库城市中心的保罗·斯文尼说，住在社会保障房的人不愿意搬家，因为他们可能不得不在一个新的城市排队。

There is one exception to the rule, and it is a strange, revealing one. The ons, which generates the numbers used by the oecd, has for several years reported a high population growth rate for Coventry. Far from welcoming this as a sign of success, the people of Coventry have balked at it. Activists, local mps and the mayor of the West Midlands argue that the ons’s estimates are probably wrong. That is because the local authority is obliged to accommodate population growth. If Coventry is really growing quickly, it might have to build on the green belt.

这条规则有一个例外，而且是一个奇怪的、具有启发性的例外。英国国家统计局多年来一直报告考文垂的人口增长率很高，经合组织使用的人口数据来源于英国国家统计局。考文垂的人们非但没有将此视为成功的标志，反而对此进行回避。活动人士、当地议员和西米德兰兹郡市长认为，国家统计局的估计可能是错误的。这是因为地方政府被迫适应人口增长。如果考文垂真的在快速发展，它可能必须在绿化带上扩建。

Another effect of Britain’s stringent planning rules is that new housing is pushed into places with weaker restrictions. South of Oxford’s green belt and west of London’s one is Didcot, a small town that is growing at almost Texan speed. Two- and three-storey houses have sprouted along newly built streets with generic names like Greenwood Way and Orchid Mews. Thousands more are planned.

英国严格的规划规则的另一个影响是，新住房被推到限制较弱的地方。在牛津绿化带的南部和伦敦绿化带的西部是迪德科特，这是一个几乎以德克萨斯州的速度发展的小镇。两层和三层的房子沿着新建的街道拔地而起，这些街道的名字很普通，比如格林伍德路和兰花街。计划会再建数千个住房。

Locals complain that homes are being built faster than roads, leading to traffic jams, and that open space is being lost. “Many people feel like they’ve paved paradise,” says Debby Hallett, a Liberal Democrat member of the Vale of White Horse council. And although the new homes on the outskirts of Didcot are not far from lots of high-paying jobs, they are not close either: central Oxford is 13 miles (22km) away by car. Agglomeration effects, which raise people’s productivity by concentrating minds, are weakened as a result.

当地人抱怨说，房屋的建设速度比道路的建设速度快，这会导致交通堵塞，而且空地正在流失。白马谷议会的自由民主党成员黛比·哈利特说:“许多人感觉他们已经在为天堂铺路。”尽管迪德科特郊区的新房子离很多高薪工作都不远，但也不近：离牛津市中心有13英里(22公里)的车程。通过集中有才智的人来提高生产力的集聚效应也因此被削弱。

In some ways the homogeneity of British cities is a boon. Although politicians complain about neglected, “left-behind” places, Britain has few examples of true urban rot. The sign of a struggling British urban area is a high street with empty shops, not a street of boarded-up homes. Even poor post-industrial towns like Hartlepool and Merthyr Tydfil tick along, gaining a few hundred residents every year. Considering the many problems associated with depopulating urban areas, from a surfeit of infrastructure to vandalism, the lack of them is welcome.

在某些方面，英国城市的同质化是一种恩惠。尽管政客们抱怨那些被忽视的“落后”的地方，但英国并没有几个城市真正衰败的例子。苦苦挣扎的英国城市地区的标志是大街上空荡荡的商店，而不是木板封住的房屋。即使像哈特尔普尔和梅瑟蒂德菲尔这样贫穷的后工业城镇也在缓慢发展，每年增加几百名居民。考虑到与人口减少相关的许多问题，从基础设施过剩到破坏行为，没有这些问题是受欢迎的。

But the consistency of urban population change is worrying, too. It suggests that Britons cannot easily move to places of greater opportunity, where they might become more productive. One question often asked about Britain by politicians keen on “levelling up” the country is why big metropolises like Greater Manchester and the West Midlands conurbation are less dynamic than London. That is not the only problem, however. Another one is that the country’s most successful small cities cannot turn into big metropolises. ■

但城市人口变化的一致性也令人担忧。这表明英国人不容易迁移到机会更多的地方，在那里他们可能会变得更有生产力。热衷于“对齐”的英国政客们经常问的一个问题是，为什么像大曼彻斯特和西米德兰兹郡这样的大都市不如伦敦那么有活力。然而，这并不是唯一的问题。另一个问题是，这个国家最成功的小城市无法变成大城市。■

Work, the wasted years

工作，浪费的岁月

Bartley

巴托比

![image-20220622100809632](/Users/chenyujie/Library/Application Support/typora-user-images/image-20220622100809632.png)

Logging in, deleting emails, mistyping things. It all adds up

登录，删邮件，输错东西。所有这些加起来。

Jun 16th 2022

Few things are more depressing than estimates of how much time people spend on a specific activity over the course of their lives. You know the sort of thing: you will spend one-third of your life asleep, almost a decade looking at your phone and four months deciding what to watch on streaming services.

没有什么比“估算人们一生中花在某项特定活动上的时间”更令人沮丧的了。你知道这样的事情：你一生中有三分之一的时间在睡觉，用了近十年的时间在看手机，花费了四个月的时间在流媒体服务上决定看什么。

A new study, by academics from the Maryland and Delaware Enterprise University Partnership (madeup), applies this approach to the workplace. By conducting a time-use survey of 5,000 office workers in America and Britain, the researchers identify the number of minutes that people waste on pointless activities each working day. (Meetings are excluded: they often turn out to be useless but not always and not for everyone.) The authors then extrapolate these figures to come up with a “weighted total futility” (wtf) lifetime estimate of time that could have been better spent. The results are literally unbelievable.

一项由马里兰和特拉华企业大学合作组织(MADEUP)的学者们进行的新研究对工作场所中的活动时间进行了估算。通过对美国和英国5000名上班族的时间使用调查，研究人员确定了人们在每个工作日浪费在无意义活动上的分钟数。(会议被排除在外:它们往往是无用的，但并非总是如此，也不是对所有人都是这样。) 然后，作者用这些数据外推得出了一个“加权总无意义”的一生时间估计，这些时间本可以更好地使用。结果简直令人难以置信。

Correcting typos takes up an average of 20 minutes in every white-collar worker’s day, the equivalent of 180 days, or half a year, over a 45-year career. Some words are mistyped so frequently that on their own they can waste days of the average employee’s existence. “Thnaks” is the worst offender in the English-speaking world, followed by “teh”, “yuo” and “remeber”. The amount of time the average worker spends writing “Bets wishes” is also counted in days.

每个白领每天平均要花20分钟纠正打字错误，在45年职业生涯中相当于180天，或半年。有些单词经常被打错，就这些单词而言，可能会浪费普通员工几天的时间。在英语国家中，“Thnaks”是最容易犯错误的单词，其次是“teh”、“yuo”和“remeber”。员工花费在写“Bets wishes”的平均时间也以天为单位计算。

The gestation period of a goat is around 145 days. Which is also how long the average worker spends logging into things during his or her working life. Security concerns mean that some time is bound to be absorbed in this way. But months are wasted trying to remember passwords, entering them wrongly or updating them. Just as much time is spent waiting for something to happen, a great economy-wide period of vacant staring at a screen.

山羊的妊娠期在145天左右。这也是员工在他/她的工作生涯中平均花在“登录”上的时间。对安全的担忧意味着，一定会有一些时间会以这样的形式被占用。但是，为了记住密码、输入错误或更新密码，我们浪费了好几个月的时间。人们花在等待某事发生上的时间与茫然的盯着屏幕的一段时间一样多。

If getting into things wastes lots of time, so does closing them down. Eliminating help windows and tool-tip boxes takes up days over a career. Rejecting repeated requests to schedule updates to your operating system is another chunk of existence that you will never get back. Zapping pop-up ads and trying to pause auto-playing video absorbs time that could have been spent learning to knit or visiting Machu Picchu.

如果开始做一件事会浪费很多时间，那么“结束”一件事也会浪费很多时间。在整个职业生涯中，关掉帮助窗口和工具提示框要花费好几天的时间。拒绝重复的更新操作系统的请求是你永远无法挽回的另一个大量生存时间。关掉弹出的广告，暂停自动播放的视频，占用了原本可以用来学习编织或参观马丘比丘的时间。

A bundle of “tidying up” activities absorbs over four months of the average worker’s life. Deleting emails takes up about six weeks of your life. Clicking on Slack channels to read through messages that are not meant for you, or clearing notifications on your phone screen for articles that you will never look at: tasks like these each eat up several days.

一堆“整理”活动占用了一个员工平均4个月以上的时间。删除邮件大约会占用你六个星期的时间。点击Slack频道来阅读那些不是为你准备的信息，或者清空手机屏幕上你永远不会去看的文章的通知：这些任务都要耗费好几天的时间。

Various types of formatting tasks constitute another huge time-suck. Think of those attempts to change the margins on Word or Google documents, or the hours spent trying to work out where exactly you need to put the missing bracket in that broken spreadsheet formula. Shakespeare wrote “King Lear” in the time an average office worker spends changing font sizes during their career.

《李尔王》写于1605—1606年

各种类型的格式化任务是另一个耗费大量时间的任务。想想那些试图改变Word或谷歌文档的页边距的尝试，或者花几个小时试图找出在拆分的电子表格公式中缺失的括号的确切位置。莎士比亚写出《李尔王》的时间，正好是一个上班族在职业生涯中改变字体大小的平均时间。

Redoing work that you have failed to save is in a category all of its own, because of the psychological trauma involved. This problem has been mitigated now that revisions are saved automatically on many programs, but it has not been solved. Batteries still run out at crucial moments, internet connections still fail. Making a series of deeply insightful comments in a Google doc, failing to save them and then closing everything down causes a special kind of despair. So does creating an org chart with hundreds of arrows and text boxes, and realising you missed someone out.

由于所涉及的心理打击，重做你未能保存的工作属于一个单独的类别。这个问题现在已经缓解了，许多程序都会自动保存修订，但这一问题还没有解决。电池仍然在关键时刻耗尽，网络连接仍会中断。在谷歌文档中发表一系列有深刻见解的评论，但未能保存它们，然后关闭所有内容，会导致一种特殊的绝望。创建一个带有数百个箭头和文本框的组织结构图，然后意识到你落了某个人，也是如此。

These are only some of the many ways in which time is routinely wasted. Co-ordinating diaries for meetings that will later be cancelled: another month. Waiting for people to repeat themselves because they were on mute by mistake: a fortnight. Spending hours crafting an email and then leaving it in the drafts folder: two days. Desperately opening and shutting various flaps on a recalcitrant printer: a day.

这些只是日常中浪费的时间的许多方式中的一部分。协调稍后将取消的会议日程：又是一个月的时间。等着人们重复自己说的话，因为他们不小心静音了：消耗两周的时间。花几个小时起草一封电子邮件，然后把它放在草稿里：两天的时间。拼命地打开和关闭一台不听话的打印机上的各种襟翼：花费一天的时间。

The madeup study shows that technology lies at the heart of this squandered time. Technology can also help. Services that sync up diaries and autocorrect options already do; passwords will doubtless end up being replaced by facial recognition and fingerprint logins. Whether the time thereby saved would be put to more productive use, like reading this column, is a reasonable question. But years of workers’ lives are wasted on utterly pointless activities. All improvements warrant heartfelt thnaks.

MADEUP的研究表明，科技是浪费时间的主要原因。科技也有一定帮助。同步日记和自动更正选项的服务已经做到了；毫无疑问，密码最终将被面部识别和指纹登录所取代。因此节省下来的时间是否会被用于更有成效的用途，比如阅读本专栏，这是一个合理的问题。但是，员工们多年的生命被浪费在完全没有意义的活动上。所有的改进都值得衷心感谢(thnaks)。

The Fed and the ECB turn on a dime

美联储和欧洲央行变化无常

The week central banks changed course

本周各国央行改变了方向

![image-20220621103039101](/Users/chenyujie/Library/Application Support/typora-user-images/image-20220621103039101.png)

The contradictions in policymakers’ goals are being exposed

政策制定者目标中的矛盾正在暴露出来

Jun 15th 2022

The multi-trillion dollar world of central banking has just turned on a dime. A week ago the Fed was expected shortly to raise interest rates by half a percentage point. The European Central Bank (ecb) had just announced an end to its bond purchases. The Bank of Japan was unusual in its commitment to maintaining monetary stimulus despite growing disquiet about the plummeting yen. By the time our weekly edition was published, however, the Fed had raised rates by three-quarters of a point; the ecb had held an emergency meeting and said it was working on a new tool to intervene in bond markets; and the Bank of Japan, ahead of its meeting on June 16th-17th, had joined the government in lamenting the yen’s weakness.

在6月17日之前举行的货币政策会议上，日本央行决定维持目前的大规模货币宽松措施，即保持短期利率为负值，并购买政府债券以保持长期利率在0%左右。日本央行将坚持不懈地继续实施货币宽松政策，以支持仍在从新冠的影响中恢复的经济。同时，日本央行加强了被称为“限额操作”的措施，即以特定的收益率购买无限数量的日本国债，并重申了其抑制长期利率上升的立场。

规模达数万亿美元的央行业务发生了重大变化。一周前，市场预计美联储将很快加息50个基点。欧洲央行刚刚宣布结束购债计划。日本央行在对日元暴跌日益感到不安的情况下，仍承诺维持货币刺激，这是不同寻常的。然而，到我们的周刊出版时，美联储已经将利率上调了75个基点；欧洲央行已召开紧急会议，表示正在创造一种干预债券市场的新工具；日本央行在6月16日至17日的会议之前与政府一道哀叹日元的疲软。

Turmoil in policy has coincided with tumult in markets. Tighter monetary policy has been crushing American stocks, which are down by almost 20% from their January peak. Cryptocurrencies are crashing, eviscerating investors who were late to the party. The spread between ten-year yields on safe German debt and risky Italian debt reached over 2.4 percentage points the day before the ecb’s announcement. Bond yields have risen and traders have punished currencies, like the yen and sterling, issued by central banks that are unlikely to keep pace with the Fed.

政策的动荡与市场的混乱同时发生。紧缩的货币政策一直在打压美国股市，美国股市从一月份的最高点下跌了近20%。加密货币正在崩溃，这重创了那些迟来的投资者。在欧洲央行宣布(结束购债计划)这一决定的前一天，安全的德国国债和有风险的意大利国债之间的十年期收益率差超过了2.4个百分点。债券收益率已经上升，交易员们也在打压那些不太可能与美联储步调一致的央行发行的货币，比如日元和英镑。

The turbulence is exposing central banks’ contradictory goals. The Fed insists it will tame inflation, which reached an annual rate of 8.6% in May. Yet it is loth to admit that doing so will probably require a recession. Even its hawkish pivot this week leaves monetary policy looking muddled. Rules of thumb for how central bankers should respond to underlying inflation say rates should be much higher. The Fed’s dawdling over the past year has worsened the conflict between its competing goals of stable prices and low unemployment.

这种动荡正暴露出各国央行相互矛盾的目标。美联储坚持认为它将抑制通货膨胀，五月份通货膨胀率达到8.6%。然而，它不愿承认这样做可能会导致经济衰退。就连美联储本周的强硬立场也让货币政策看起来混乱不堪。关于央行行长应该如何应对潜在通胀的经验法则表明，利率应该变得更高。美联储在过去一年里的拖延，加剧了其稳定物价和低失业率这两个相互矛盾的目标之间的冲突。

![image-20220621082947118](/Users/chenyujie/Library/Application Support/typora-user-images/image-20220621082947118.png)

The ecb faces an even tougher dilemma. A side-effect of tighter monetary policy is a squeeze on indebted countries. Governments’ finances will be put under pressure both by the rise in underlying rates and the increase in spreads permitted by a lack of bond-buying. Without a backstop, Italy’s debts of over 150% of gdp could become unsustainable—a possibility which would leave the country vulnerable to a self-fulfilling market panic. The ecb says it will not tolerate the threat such a crisis would pose to the integrity of the currency union. But it is not supposed to sacrifice its inflation target to debt-sustainability concerns. Nor can it underwrite governments to the extent that they can run up vast tabs at the expense of the central bank.

美联储收紧货币政策可能会对一些经济复苏已经疲软的国家造成冲击，特别是那些美元债务较高的国家，因为美国利率水平上升可能会提高这些国家偿还美元债务的成本。

欧洲央行面临着一个更为艰难的困境。收紧货币政策的一个副作用是会给负债国家带来压力。由于基础利率上升和债券购买不足导致利差扩大，政府财政将面临压力。如果没有担保，意大利超过 GDP 150%的债务将变得不可持续，这种可能性将使这个国家容易受到自我实现的市场恐慌的影响。欧洲央行表示，它不会容忍这种危机对货币联盟完整性构成威胁。但它不应该为了债务可持续性的担忧而牺牲其通胀目标。它也不能以央行为代价，为政府支付巨额费用。

The Bank of Japan is trading off economics against politics. The yen’s weakness could help the country hit its 2% inflation target, having undershot it for almost the entire period since it was announced in 2013. The trouble is that expensive imports are squeezing living standards, alarming the government. Earlier this month Haruhiko Kuroda, the bank’s governor, had to apologise for saying that households were becoming more accepting of price rises.

日本央行正在经济与政治之间进行权衡。日元走软可能有助于日本实现2%的通胀目标，自2013年宣布这一目标以来，日本几乎在整个时期都未能达到这一目标。问题是昂贵的进口商品正在降低人们的生活水平，这给政府敲响了警钟。本月早些时候，日本央行行长黑田东彦不得不为自己所说的“日本家庭越来越能接受物价上涨”而道歉。

In this environment the central-banking playbook of the 2010s is failing. It was built around low global inflation and designed to dispel the notion that rates might rise. Pivots are rare because policymakers have been unwilling to surprise markets by breaking their “forward guidance”—even the Fed’s decision this week appears to have been leaked two days earlier. Restricting themselves to sequential policy changes, signalled in advance, makes central banks slow on their feet. The result is more volatility in markets and interest rates, not less. As competing goals make the course ahead harder to predict, central banks would do well to stay nimble. ■

在这种环境下，央行在2010年代的业务策略正在失效。它是建立在全球低通胀的基础上，旨在消除“利率可能上升”的想法。这种转向是罕见的，因为政策制定者不愿意破坏他们的“前瞻性指引”，让市场感到意外，甚至美联储本周的决定也是在两天前才泄露的。将自己限制在预先发出信号的连续政策变化上，会让央行行动迟缓。其结果是市场和利率波动加剧，而非减弱。由于相互竞争的目标使未来的道路更加难以预测，各国央行最好保持灵活性。■

The tricky restructuring of global supply chains

棘手的全球供应链重组

Reinventing globalisation

重塑全球化

![image-20220620124531240](/Users/chenyujie/Library/Application Support/typora-user-images/image-20220620124531240.png)

Why too much resilience is dangerous

为什么太多的弹性是危险的

Three years ago The Economist used the term “slowbalisation” to describe the fragile state of international trade and commerce. After the go-go 1990s and 2000s the pace of economic integration stalled in the 2010s, as firms grappled with the aftershocks of a financial crisis, a populist revolt against open borders and President Donald Trump’s trade war. The flow of goods and capital stagnated. Many bosses postponed big decisions on investing abroad: just-in-time gave way to wait-and-see. No one knew if globalisation faced a blip or extinction.

三年前，《经济学人》杂志用“slowbalisation”这一术语来形容国际贸易和商业的脆弱状态。在上世纪90年代和本世纪头十年的蓬勃发展之后，由于企业疲于应对金融危机的余波、民粹主义者反抗开放边境以及唐纳德·特朗普总统的贸易战，经济一体化的步伐在2010年代陷入停滞。商品和资本的流动停滞不前。许多老板推迟了在海外投资的重大决定：“及时出手”让位于“观望”。没有人知道全球化是暂时的改变还是会终结。

Now the waiting is over, as the pandemic and war in Ukraine have triggered a once-in-a-generation reimagining of global capitalism in boardrooms and governments. Everywhere you look, supply chains are being transformed, from the $9trn in inventories, stockpiled as insurance against shortages and inflation, to the fight for workers as global firms shift from X into Vietnam. This new kind of globalisation is about security, not efficiency: it prioritises doing business with people you can rely on, in countries your government is friendly with. It could descend into protectionism, big government and worsening inflation. Alternatively, if firms and politicians show restraint, it could change the world economy for the better, keeping the benefits of openness while improving resilience.

现在等待已经结束，因为疫情和乌克兰的战争引发了企业董事会和政府对全球资本主义千年一遇的重新想象。放眼望去，供应链正在发生转变，从为应对短缺和通胀而囤积的9万亿美元库存，到随着全球企业从X转移到越南而争夺工人。这种新型全球化注重的是安全，而不是效率：它优先与可以依赖的人做生意，在政府友好的国家做生意。它可能逐渐陷入贸易保护主义、大政府和不断恶化的通货膨胀。或者，如果企业和政客们表现出克制，它可能会让世界经济变得更好，在保持开放的好处的同时又提高弹性。

After the Berlin Wall fell in 1989, the lodestar of globalisation was efficiency. Companies located production where costs were lowest, while investors deployed capital where returns were highest. Governments aspired to treat firms equally, regardless of their nationality, and to strike trade deals with democracies and autocracies alike. Over two decades this gave rise to dazzlingly sophisticated value chains that account for half of all trade: your car and phone contain components that are better travelled than Phileas Fogg. All this kept prices low for consumers and helped lift 1bn people out of extreme poverty as the emerging world, including China, industrialised.

1989年柏林墙倒塌后，全球化的指导原则是效率。公司把生产地设在成本最低的地方，而投资者把资本配置在回报最高的地方。各国政府渴望平等对待企业，无论其国籍如何，并渴望与民主国家和独裁国家都达成贸易协议。在过去的20年里，这产生了令人眼花缭乱的复杂价值链，这占了所有贸易的一半：你的汽车和电话包含的组件比菲利亚·福克环游的还要多。在包括中国在内的新兴世界实现工业化之际，所有这些都使消费者的消费价格保持在较低水平，帮助10亿人摆脱了极端贫困。

But hyper-efficient globalisation also had problems. Volatile capital flows destabilised financial markets. Many blue-collar workers in rich countries lost out. Recently, two other worries have loomed large. First, some lean supply chains are not as good value as they appear: mostly they keep costs low, but when they break, the bill can be crippling. Today’s bottlenecks have reduced global gdp by at least 1%. Shareholders have been hit as well as consumers: as chip shortages have stalled car production, carmakers’ cashflows have dropped by 80% year on year. Tim Cook, the supply-chain guru who runs Apple, reckons such snafus could reduce sales by up to $8bn, or 10%, this quarter. Covid-19 was a shock, but wars, extreme weather or another virus could easily disrupt supply chains in the next decade.

不过超高效的全球化也有问题。不稳定的资本流动破坏了金融市场的稳定。许多发达国家的蓝领工人都被淘汰了。最近，另外两个担忧也逐渐显现。首先，一些效率高的供应链的价值并不像它们看起来的那样好:大多数情况下，它们保持了低成本，但当它们“断裂”时，账单可能是沉重的。今天的供应链瓶颈已经使全球GDP减少了至少1%。股东和消费者都受到了打击:由于芯片短缺导致汽车生产停滞，汽车制造商的现金流同比下降了80%。经营苹果公司的供应链专家蒂姆•库克认为，这样的混乱可能会使本季度的销售额减少80亿美元，或10%。过去新冠是一个大的冲击，但未来十年，战争、极端天气或其他病毒可能很容易破坏供应链。

The second problem is that the single-minded pursuit of cost advantage has led to a dependency on autocracies that [..从句省略]. Hopes that economic integration would lead to reform—what the Germans call “change through trade”—have been dashed: autocracies account for a third of world gdp. Vladimir Putin’s invasion of Ukraine has painfully exposed Europe’s reliance on Russian energy. This week McDonald’s in Moscow, which opened in 1990, restarted under local control. Big Macs are no longer on the menu. Meanwhile, [主语省略] has a trade footprint seven times as big as Russia’s—and the world relies on it for a variety of goods from active pharmaceutical ingredients to the processed lithium used in batteries.

第二个问题是，一心追求成本优势导致了对专制国家的依赖。经济一体化会带来改革——德国人称之为“通过贸易改变”——的希望破灭了:独裁国家占世界GDP的三分之一。弗拉基米尔•普京入侵乌克兰，极大程度地暴露了欧洲对俄罗斯能源的依赖。本周，1990年开业的莫斯科的麦当劳在当地控制下重新开张。巨无霸已经不在菜单上了。与此同时，[..] 的贸易足迹是俄罗斯的七倍——世界上从有效药物成分到用于电池的加工锂等各种商品都依赖于[..]。

One indication that companies are shifting from efficiency to resilience is the vast build-up in precautionary inventories: for the biggest 3,000 firms globally these have risen from 6% to 9% of world gdp since 2016. Many firms are adopting dual sourcing and longer-term contracts. The pattern of multinational investment has been inverted: 69% is from local subsidiaries reinvesting locally, rather than parent firms sending capital across borders. This echoes the 1930s, when global firms responded to nationalism by making subsidiaries abroad more self-sufficient.

企业正在从效率转向弹性的一个迹象是预防性库存的大量积聚：自2016年以来，全球最大的3000家企业的预防性库存占全球GDP的比重从6%上升到9%。许多公司正在实行双重采购和长期合同。跨国投资的模式已经逆转过来：69%的投资来自当地子公司在当地再投资，而不是母公司跨境输送资金。这让人想起了上世纪30年代，当时跨国公司为了应对民族主义，让海外子公司更加自给自足。

The industries under most pressure are already reinventing their business models, encouraged by governments that from Europe to India are keen on “strategic autonomy”. The car industry is copying Elon Musk’s Tesla by moving towards vertical integration, in which you control everything from nickel mining to chip design. [删除一个示例]. In energy, the West is seeking long-term supply deals from allies rather than relying on spot markets dominated by rivals—one reason it has been cosying up to gas-rich Qatar. Renewables will also make energy markets more regional.

在欧洲和印度等热衷于“战略自主”的政府的鼓励下，压力最大的行业已经在重塑自己的商业模式。汽车行业正在效仿埃隆•马斯克的特斯拉，走向垂直整合，控制从镍矿开采到芯片设计的一切。在能源方面，西方正在寻求与盟友达成长期供应协议，而不是依赖于由竞争对手主导的现货市场——这也是西方一直在讨好天然气资源丰富的卡塔尔的原因之一。可再生能源还将使能源市场更具区域性。

The danger is that a reasonable pursuit of security will morph into rampant protectionism, jobs schemes and hundreds of billions of dollars of industrial subsidies. The short-term effect of this would be more volatility and fragmentation that would push prices yet higher: witness President Joe Biden’s consideration of new tariffs on solar panels, which he paused this month in the face of shortages. The long-run inefficiency from indiscriminately replicating supply chains would be enormous. Were you to duplicate a quarter of all multinational activity, the extra annual operating and financial costs involved could exceed 2% of world gdp.

危险在于，对安全的合理追求将演变为猖獗的贸易保护主义、就业计划和数千亿美元的工业补贴。这种做法的短期影响将是更大的波动性和分散性，从而进一步推高价格：比如美国总统乔•拜登曾考虑对太阳能电池板征收新关税，但由于短缺，他本月暂停了这一计划。从长期效益来看，不加区分地复制供应链导致的低效将是巨大的。如果你复制所有跨国活动的四分之一，所涉及的额外年度运营和财务成本可能超过全球GDP的2%。

The trouble with safe spaces

That is why restraint is crucial. Governments and firms must remember that resilience comes from diversification, not concentration at home. The choke-points autocracies control amount to only about a tenth of global trade, based on their exports of goods in which they have a leading market share of over 10% and for which it is hard to find substitutes. The answer is to require firms to diversify their suppliers in these areas, and let the market adapt. Will today’s governments be up to the task? Myopia and insularity abound. But if you are a consumer of global goods and ideas—that is to say, a citizen of the world—you should hope globalisation’s next phase involves the maximum possible degree of openness. A new balance between efficiency and security is a reasonable goal. Living in a subsidised bunker is not.■

这就是为什么克制至关重要。政府和企业必须记住，弹性来自于多样化，而不是来自于集中于国内。独裁统治控制的瓶颈只占全球贸易的十分之一，这是基于他们的商品出口，他们拥有超过10%的领先市场份额，而且很难找到替代品。解决方案是要求企业在这些领域使其供应商多样化，并让市场适应。今天的政府能胜任这项任务吗？短视和偏狭比比皆是。但如果你是全球商品和理念的消费者，也就是说，你是世界公民，你应该希望全球化的下一阶段涉及到最大程度的开放。在效率和安全之间取得新的平衡是一个合理的目标。而住在有补贴的掩体里则不然。■
