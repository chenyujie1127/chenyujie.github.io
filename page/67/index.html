What the enthusiasm for funding startups means for the VC world

投资初创公司的热情对风投界意味着什么

Buttonwood

梧桐

![image-20210405111934489](/Users/chenyujie/Library/Application Support/typora-user-images/image-20210405111934489.png)

More money chasing scarce ideas means that the prices paid for startups rise

更多的资金追逐稀缺的创意意味着创业公司的价格上涨

Mar 31st 2021

FINANCIAL MARKETS are fuelled by stories, and the most skilful storytellers are found in venture capital. For a start, venture capitalists have to listen to a lot of fairy tales from the would-be entrepreneur. “The world will look different in a decade,” he says. “My startup will be the leading business in a new industry.” VCs tell themselves stories about how they can foresee what others cannot, and how this stands to make them a lot of money. And they retell them to potential investors in their funds.

金融市场是由故事推动的，而最有技巧的故事讲述者出现在风险投资领域。首先，风险投资家必须未来企业家讲很多童话故事。他表示:“十年后，世界看起来会有所不同。”“我的初创公司将成为一个新行业的领军企业。“风投告诉自己，他们如何能够预见别人无法预见的事情，以及这将如何为他们带来大笔收入。他们会把这些信息告诉基金的潜在投资者。

Who to believe, when you cannot easily verify such tales? This difficulty is a version of the agency problem of asset management. When venture capital was clubbier, it was manageable. But in recent years investors who are new to the terrain have been piling in. A VC trying to raise money may favour startups of the kind that has done well recently, even if they are not the best long-term bet. Oddball startups with more potential might then be starved of capital.

terrain 地带

bet 选择

oddball 古怪的

当你无法轻易证实这些故事时，还能相信谁呢? 这种困难相当于资产管理中的代理问题。当风险投资变得更加“俱乐部化”时，它是可控的。但近年来，对这一领域不熟悉的投资者不断涌入。试图筹集资金的风投公司可能会青睐那些最近表现不错的初创公司，即使它们不是最好的长期投资选择。那些更有潜力的古怪创业公司可能会因此而缺乏资金。

There is thus a nagging fear that the more money that is funnelled into Silicon Valley and other centres of venture capital, the less “true” innovation will occur. Well perhaps. But it is not obvious that great business ideas are being ignored. The downside of the flood of venture capital is more prosaic. Run-of-the-mill startups are overindulged. And prospective returns are depressed.

nagging 令人心烦不已的；纠缠不休的

nagging doubts/pain

难以消除的疑虑／令人不得安宁的疼痛

to funnel sth into/through/out sth 使某物流入/流过/流出某物

Run-of-the-mill 平凡的

overindulge 过分纵容

因此人们一直担心，流入硅谷和其他风险投资中心的资金越多，就越不会出现“真正的”创新。也许吧。但伟大的商业创意是否被忽视，这一点并不明显。风险投资泛滥的负面影响则更为平淡无奇。普通的创业公司被过度纵容。预期回报率也很低。

More and more pension schemes are looking to alternative investments, including venture capital, to juice up their returns. VC funds have on average beaten the public market, net of fees, over the long run. The best funds do a lot better than the average. But it is not only a matter of returns. Smart investors in public markets realise that they own a lot of companies that are at risk of disruption from emerging technology firms. A good way to balance that risk is to own a stake in the next generation.

net of tax/packaging 税后净额/扣除包装材料后净重

越来越多的养老金计划正寻求包括风险投资在内的另类投资，以提高其回报。长期来看，扣除费用后，风投基金的平均表现跑赢了公开市场。最好的基金比平均水平要好得多。但这不仅仅是回报的问题。公开市场上聪明的投资者意识到，他们拥有的很多公司都面临着被新兴科技公司颠覆的风险。平衡这种风险的一个好办法是对下一代持有股份。

More money for new businesses is surely a good thing. Nevertheless there is a lingering disquiet. One source of discomfort is that funds are often narrowly segmented by region, industry, or stage of investment—and sometimes all three. This helps with marketing. Money is attracted to themes that have worked well recently. As Hollywood has discovered, it is easier to sell a variant of an old story than a brand-new one.

disquiet 不安

为新企业投入更多资金无疑是件好事。然而，仍有挥之不去的不安。令人不安的一个原因是，基金往往是按地区、行业或投资阶段进行狭义划分的，有时三者兼而有之。这有助于市场营销。资金被最近运行良好的主题所吸引。正如好莱坞所发现的那样，卖一个老故事的变种比卖一个全新的故事更容易。

There are drawbacks, though. A truly game-changing business may sit astride several themes and be ignored, says Ajay Royan of Mithril, a VC firm based in Austin, Texas. In the public markets buying stuff that has worked well recently is called momentum trading. It does fine much of the time, and has an appeal to the investor who is out of his element in venture-land. By “social-proofing” VC investments the anxious can get more comfortable with the risks, says Mr Royan. “But it can devolve into the VC equivalent of ‘you can’t get fired for buying IBM’.” And the trouble with crowded trades is that they are prone to crashes.

astride 跨着

devolve 转让；被移交

不过，它也有缺点。德克萨斯州奥斯丁的风投公司Mithril的Ajay Royan说，一个真正改变游戏规则的商业可能跨越几个主题而被忽视。在公开市场上，购买最近运行良好的股票被称为动量交易。它在大多数情况下都很不错，而且对那些在风险投资领域不适应的投资者也有吸引力。Royan说，通过“社会保障”风险投资，焦虑的人可以更轻松地应对风险。“但这可能会演变成风投领域的‘你不会因为收购IBM而被解雇’。”拥挤交易带来的问题是，它们容易崩溃。

Some VC funds will fall prey to the vices of asset gathering—telling a good story about the latest fad to maximise the amount of fee-paying money under management. But there is a culture that militates against this. The best VC firms pride themselves on being oversubscribed. They turn money away. And an industry that hears a lot of fairy tales has some inbuilt discipline. Venture capitalists have to kiss a lot of frogs to find a prince—even a halfway handsome frog. The average VC firm screens 200 targets a year, but makes only four investments, according to one study.

fall prey to sth/sb literal 被某物/某人捕食；成为某事物/某人的牺牲品

militate 产生影响

to militate against sb/sth 对某人/某事物产生不利影响

oversubscribed 超额认购

一些风投基金会沦为资产聚集的牺牲品，他们会讲述一个最新风潮的好故事来最大限度地增加管理下的付费资金。但有一种文化不利于这一点。最好的风投公司都以超额认购而自豪。他们把钱拒之门外。而一个听了很多童话故事的行业也有一些内在的纪律。风险投资家必须亲吻许多青蛙才能找到王子，哪怕是一只长得有点帅的青蛙。一项研究显示，风投公司平均每年筛选200个投资目标，但只进行4笔投资。

And thematic VC can sometimes have a logic to it. Regional funds make sense for consumer-facing startups, because of local variations in tastes and habits. Increasingly, the seed or early-stage venture funds with better returns tend to have a thematic focus, says Simon Levene of Mosaic Ventures, a London-based VC firm. Nor is it obvious that moonshot ideas are starved of funding. SpaceX, Elon Musk’s space-exploration firm, was valued at a whopping $74bn at its most recent funding round. Even borderline scams are given a respectful hearing.

thematic 主题的

whop 打；击；打败

scam 诈骗；骗局

主题型VC有时也会有逻辑。地区性基金对面向消费者的初创公司来说是有意义的，因为各地的品味和习惯各不相同。总部位于伦敦的风投公司Mosaic Ventures的西蒙•列文(Simon Levene)表示，那些具有较高回报率的种子选手或早期风险投资基金往往会有一个主题重点。登月计划显然也不缺资金。埃隆·马斯克(Elon Musk)的太空探索公司SpaceX在最近一轮融资中估值高达740亿美元。即使是边缘骗局也会受到尊重。

The trouble with abundant capital instead is more straightforward. More money chasing scarce ideas and talent means that the prices paid for startups rise, which all else equal means returns fall. And the absence of cash constraints can spoil a promising startup. If it blows a lot of money on marketing, the resulting growth can distract the founders from underlying faults with the product. Telling a good story is vital in the startup business. But there is a danger in believing your own fairy tales.

相反，资本充裕带来的麻烦更为直接。更多的资本追逐稀缺的创意和人才，意味着创业公司的价格上升，而其他条件不变则意味着回报下降。而没有现金约束可能会毁掉一家有前途的初创公司。如果它在市场营销上花了很多钱，由此带来的增长可以分散创始人对产品潜在缺陷的注意力。在初创企业中，讲一个好故事至关重要。但相信自己的童话故事是危险的。

The benefits of part-time work

兼职的好处

Bartleby

巴托比

![image-20210404135640064](/Users/chenyujie/Library/Application Support/typora-user-images/image-20210404135640064.png)

Happiness on just a day a week

一周一天的快乐

Apr 3rd 2021

PEOPLE’S RELATIONSHIP with work is complex. For all the complaining about the tedium and bureaucracy, the power-crazed bosses and recalcitrant colleagues, individuals need the security of a job. A century of research has shown that unemployment is bad for mental health, leading to depression, anxiety and reduced self-esteem. On average, it has an even greater effect than divorce.

recalcitrant 难驯服的；桀骜不驯的

人们与工作的关系是复杂的。尽管人们对单调乏味、官僚主义、迷恋权力的老板和顽固不化的同事怨声不断，但个人还是需要一份稳定的工作。一个世纪以来的研究表明，失业对心理健康有害，会导致抑郁、焦虑和自尊心下降。平均而言，它比离婚的影响更大。

But how much work do you need to do? A recent paper by the Centre for Business Research at Cambridge University took the opportunity of the pandemic to examine the impact of reduced working hours on well-being. Many British employees were placed in a furlough scheme, under which their hours were reduced and their wages were subsidised by the government. “We found that people working reduced working hours or being furloughed **do not have poorer mental health,**” the authors conclude. This suggests that social welfare would be improved if governments adopted furlough schemes in future recessions, even when they are not triggered by a virus.

但是你需要做多少工作呢?剑桥大学商业研究中心(Centre for Business Research at Cambridge University)最近的一篇论文利用疫情的机会，研究了工作时间减少对幸福感的影响。许多英国员工被安排在休假计划中，在该计划下，他们的工作时间减少，工资由政府补贴。作者总结说:“我们发现，工作时间缩短或休假的人的心理健康状况并不会更差。”这表明，如果政府在未来经济衰退中采取休假计划，即使这些计划不是由病毒引发的，社会福利也会得到改善。

What was particularly surprising is how little work was needed to keep people happy. The threshold for good mental health was just one day a week—after that, it seemed to make little difference to individuals’ well-being if they worked eight hours or 48 hours a week. The boost from working clearly comes from the feeling of purpose, from the social status it creates and from the camaraderie of colleagues engaged in the same tasks.

camaraderie 同志情谊

特别令人惊讶的是，人们只需要很少的工作就能保持快乐。良好心理健康的临界值是每周工作一天，此后，每周工作8小时或48小时对个人幸福感的影响似乎微乎其微。工作的推动力显然来自于目标感，来自它所创造的社会地位，以及从事同样工作的同事之间的友情。

A little bit of work may be satisfying but too much is not. An enterprising junior analyst at Goldman Sachs recently surveyed his peers and fashioned his report in the style of a research presentation from the investment bank itself. The survey found those first-year analysts had worked an average 98 hours a week since the start of 2021, and only managed five hours of sleep a night. It found that 77% of them had been the subject of workplace abuse, that 75% had sought, or considered seeking, counselling, and that, on average, the cohort had suffered sharp declines in mental and physical health. Unsurprisingly, the analysts thought it was unlikely they would still be working at the bank in six months’ time.

做一点点工作可能会令人满意，但做太多就不行了。高盛(Goldman Sachs)一位有事业心的初级分析师最近调查了他的同行，并以投行本身的研究报告风格撰写了自己的报告。该调查发现，自2021年初以来，就职第一年的分析师平均每周工作98个小时，每晚只睡5个小时。调查发现，其中77%的人曾在工作场所受到虐待，75%的人曾寻求或考虑寻求咨询，平均而言，这一群体的精神和身体健康状况急剧下降。不出所料，分析师们认为，他们不太可能在6个月后仍在该行工作。

To be fair to Goldman Sachs, the survey had only 13 respondents. Discontented workers may have been the most likely to respond. And the bank’s reaction was restrained. “A year into covid, people are understandably quite stretched, and that’s why we are listening to their concerns and taking multiple steps to address them,” a spokesperson said.

公平地说，高盛只有13名受访者。不满的工人可能是最有可能做出回应的。银行的反应也受到了抑制。一位发言人表示:“在新冠肺炎疫情爆发的一年里，人们很紧张，这是可以理解的，这就是为什么我们正在倾听他们的担忧，并采取多项措施来解决它们。”

Nevertheless, the general reaction to the story was remarkably unsympathetic. In some people’s eyes, the young analysts should have expected to be treated in such a fashion; after all, that is why they were being paid so well. Veterans of the investment-banking industry reminisced along the lines of “It was the same in my day. Never did me any harm.” But it is hard to see why young Goldman analysts (or indeed, anyone) should expect to suffer workplace abuse. They joined a bank, not the Mafia.

reminisce 缅怀往事

Mafia 黑手党

尽管如此，人们对这个故事的普遍反应还是非常冷淡。在一些人看来，这些年轻的分析师本应得到这样的待遇; 毕竟，这就是他们获得如此优厚薪酬的原因。投资银行界的资深人士回忆道:“我那个年代也是这样。但是我从来没有受到伤害” 但很难想象年轻的高盛分析师(或者说任何人)会在工作场所受到虐待。他们加入的是银行，不是黑手党。

Nor does it make much business sense to have employees working so long that they only get to sleep five hours a night. They can hardly be operating at full efficiency when they are dog-tired. Surely it is better to hire more analysts, and pay each of them a little less. That junior professional staff have always worked long hours is not a good explanation for piling stress onto young people at the start of their careers. It smacks of the legendary navy motto: “The beatings will continue until morale improves.”

dog-tired 极度疲乏的

legendary 传说中的；众所周知的

smack 暗示

让员工长时间工作，每晚只睡5个小时，在商业上也没有多大意义。当他们疲惫不堪时，他们几乎不可能全力工作。当然，雇佣更多的分析师，并给他们每人少一点工资会更好。初级专业人员总是工作很长时间，这并不能很好地解释为什么年轻人在职业生涯刚开始就有压力。这有点像海军的传奇格言:“打击将继续，直到士气提高。”

Clearly, economies cannot prosper if everyone works one day a week. But the need to limit excessive working hours was realised back in Victorian times. For much of the 20th century the length of the average working week fell while output continued to climb.

显然，如果每个人每周都工作一天，经济就不可能繁荣。但限制过度工作时间的必要性早在维多利亚时代就已经实现了。在20世纪的大部分时间里，平均每周的工作时长都在下降，而产量却在继续攀升。

There will be occasions when people have to work late or rise early to finish a project (Bartleby writes this on a day when the needs of The Economist required him to get up at 5am). Day after day, however, a long-hours lifestyle is bad for workers’ health. Some organisations may see the appetite for slogging it out as a sign of an employee’s willingness to put their job ahead of their families and friends. If so, they don’t need to have better employees. They need better managers.

slog 埋头苦干

有时人们会为了完成一个项目不得不工作到很晚或者很早就起床(巴托比写这篇文章的时候，《经济学人》需要他早上5点起床)。然而日复一日，长时间的生活方式对工人的健康有害。一些机构可能会将苦干的兴趣视为员工愿意把工作放在家人和朋友前面的一种表现。如果是这样，他们不需要更好的员工。他们需要更好的管理者。

Putting the viruses of the world into a panopticon is no longer impossible

panopticon （看守室在中央的）全景监狱，环形监狱

把世界上的病毒放进一个全景图是有可能的

All in the blood

都在血液里

![image-20210402104030128](/Users/chenyujie/Library/Application Support/typora-user-images/image-20210402104030128.png)

It is still, however, wildly ambitious

然而，它仍然雄心勃勃

Mar 23rd 2021

TO KEEP AN eye on how a single pathogen navigates, mutation by mutation, through a worldwide landscape of adaptation would have been unthinkable more than ten years ago. In the face of a pandemic, it has proved possible. Now, to forestall future pandemics, some researchers want to go much further. There are 264 virus species known to infect humans. Why not track them all?

pathogen 病原体

在十多年前，观察一个单一的病原体如何通过一个又一个的突变，在世界范围内的适应环境中导航是不可想象的。面对大流行病，事实证明这是可能的。现在，为了预防未来的大流行病，一些研究人员想要走得更远。已知有264种病毒可以感染人类。为什么不把它们都追踪一遍呢?

And why stop there? Scientists estimate that among all the viruses that infect all the animals of the world there may be around 800,000 which could, in the right circumstances, jump from their habitual hosts into humans and start spreading. Could all of them be identified, too, and sentry posts set up that would provide news of their incursions?

sentry 哨兵

为什么止步于此呢?科学家估计，在感染世界上所有动物的所有病毒中，可能有大约80万种病毒，在适当的情况下，可以从它们的习惯宿主跳到人类体内，并开始传播。是否也能确认他们的身份，并设立哨所来提供他们入侵的消息?

Before covid-19 such ideas seemed both expensive and outlandish. As the pandemic has progressed, however, expense has come to seem less of a barrier: a covid-pandemic bill in the trillions of dollars puts up-front costs of $1bn or so into a new context. As to outlandishness: RNA vaccination seemed like a wild idea less than a decade ago, and impractical to implement much more recently. Now it has shifted the boundaries of the possible. It cannot be the only elegant biotechnological notion lurking in relative obscurity that has the potential to be realised.

up-front 预先；在前面

在covid-19之前，这样的想法似乎既昂贵又古怪。然而，随着大流行的发展，费用似乎不再是一个障碍:一笔数万亿美元的新冠病毒大流行账单，将10亿美元左右的前期成本置于一个新的背景下。说到稀奇古怪: RNA疫苗在不到十年前似乎是一个疯狂的想法，在最近实施起来也不太现实。现在，它已经改变了可能性的界限。它不可能是唯一一个隐藏在相对模糊之中、有可能实现的优雅的生物技术概念。

The Global Immunological Observatory (GIO), an idea conceived by Jessica Metcalf, a disease ecologist at Princeton University, and Michael Mina, an epidemiologist at Harvard University, aim to use modern lab techniques to spot incipient health crises before they take off by providing near-real-time insight into what infections are where. It would do so by looking at hundreds of thousands of blood samples every day.

incipent 早期的

普林斯顿大学疾病生态学家杰西卡·梅特卡夫（Jessica Metcalf）和哈佛大学流行病学家迈克尔·米纳（Michael Mina）提出了全球免疫观察站（GIO）的想法，目的是利用现代实验室技术，通过近实时地了解哪些感染发生在哪里，从而在健康危机爆发之前发现它们。它将通过每天查看数十万份血液样本来做到这一点。

The blood stores a history of the immune system written in antibodies. Seeing what antibodies someone’s blood contains can show, for instance, whether they had measles as a child, which flu virus laid them low last winter or which cold virus they sniffed their way through with mild irritation three weeks ago. Even if a pathogen never made them sick, the memory of its attempt to do so may still circulate.

circulate 循环；流通

血液中储存着用抗体记录的免疫系统的历史。例如，通过观察一个人血液中含有的抗体可以看出，他们小时候是否患过麻疹，去年冬天是哪种流感病毒让他们情绪低落，或者三周前哪种感冒病毒让他们轻微发炎。即使一种病原体从来没有使他们生病，这个病原体试图这样做的记忆可能仍然在（血液中）传播。

The GIO would ideally test every tiny sample of blood for hundreds of thousands of distinct antibodies. Considering how hard large-scale testing for just SARS-COV-2 has proved, this might seem impossibly ambitious. But there are tools that might be up to the job. One is VirScan, a platform developed by scientists at Harvard Medical School which looks for antibodies to more than 1,000 strains of 206 different viruses in a single tiny sample of blood using a canny combination of DNA synthesis and DNA sequencing.

Though covid-19 has not brought the GVP any windfalls so far, Dr Carroll says its ideas are catalysing conversations on pandemic preparedness around the world. “It’s inverting what we would typically think,” says Dr Carroll. If every country can see that the set-up is “open, transparent, and [protected] against misuse and abuse, that…will enable everything else to happen.” With the right information infrastructure, the next pandemic could be spotted almost before it has begun. ■

The digital-payments industry is rather like a transport system. “Acquirers” connect the shop’s app or website to the infrastructure and check details, including a buyer’s identity or available funds, to authorise travel. That should give them even more room to roam, as well as access to far bigger revenue pools. It may have taken a while for digital payments to hit the big time in America and the West. Delivery driving will, as many people foretold, be a big source of jobs, at least until the arrival of self-driving lorries. Bricks-and-mortar shops will not. A hybrid system, in which employees are in the office for part of the week, is here to stay. This approach would reduce social isolation while saving employees the grind of the daily commute. grind 苦差

America’s own effort at instant payments, backed by the Federal Reserve and known as FedNow, is to launch in 2023. The big banks and credit-card firms are keen to delay a system that could disrupt the status quo. The government and the Fed should not just ignore their grumbles but bring forward the timetable. The pandemic has shown that online transactions have come of age. It has also shown that the public sector can act quickly and effectively when it has to. Cheap and swift digital payments are a prize that should be viewed as a priority. ■

The spate of data, experiments and insights has had profound effects on the pandemic—and, indeed, on the future of medicine. It is also an inspiration. The basis of all this is the application of genetics to medicine in a systematic and transformative way—not just in understanding the pathology of diseases but in tracking their spread and curing and preventing them. They are now in an orbit that takes them over both of Earth’s poles. This means that, as the planet revolves beneath them, every point on its surface can be monitored at regular intervals.

take over 接管；占领

所谓的“鹰眼”卫星，大约有一个大微波炉那么大。因此，它们的建造和发射成本都很低。“鹰眼”在2018年部署了其中三个集群中的第一个集群。它们现在处于环绕地球两极的轨道上。这意味着，当行星在它们下面旋转时，它表面的每一点都可以被定期监测。

Initially, the data the satellites collected were downloaded to a tracking station on Svalbard, a Norwegian island in the Arctic Ocean. But business has since boomed. HawkEye now counts a dozen governments among its customers, as well as private clients. The firm has therefore recruited the services of a second ground station, in Antarctica, and it put a second cluster into orbit on January 24th. It plans three more such launches this year, and also intends to widen its network of ground stations yet further.

最初，卫星收集的数据被下载到位于北冰洋挪威斯瓦尔巴特群岛的一个跟踪站。但从那以后，生意兴隆起来。鹰眼目前的客户中有12个是政府，也有私人客户。因此，该公司在南极洲招募了第二个地面站，并于1月24日将第二个集群送入轨道。它计划今年再发射三次这样的卫星，并打算进一步扩大地面站网络。

Given this success, it is hardly surprising that at least six other companies are operating or developing similar systems. Quilty Analytics, a research firm in Florida, expects the number of radio-frequency (RF) intelligence satellites of this sort in orbit to multiply from a dozen at the beginning of January to more than 60 by the end of next year.

鉴于这种成功，至少还有6家公司正在运行或开发类似的系统也就不足为奇了。佛罗里达州的一家研究公司Quilty Analytics预计，在轨运行的这类射频智能卫星的数量将从1月初的12颗增加到明年年底的60多颗。

Unmixed signals 纯粹的信号

RF-intelligence satellites detect where a transmission is coming from in two ways. One, trilateration, relies on measuring minute differences in a signal’s arrival time at each member of a cluster. The other uses the Doppler effect—the shift in a signal’s frequency if the transmitter is moving relative to the receiver. Together, according to HawkEye, these can pinpoint a signal’s source to within 500 metres of its true origin. Kleos Space, a Luxembourgeois company that launched its first cluster in November and hopes to put two more up later this year, says its accuracy ranges between 3,000 and 200 metres.

射频情报卫星通过两种方式探测传输信号的来源。一种是三边测量，它依赖于测量信号到达集群中每个成员的时间的微小差异。另一种是利用多普勒效应，即如果发射器相对于接收器移动，信号的频率就会发生偏移。根据“鹰眼”的说法，这些技术结合起来可以将信号源精确定位到距离其真正来源500米以内。卢森堡的一家公司Kleos Space在去年11月推出了第一个集群，并希望在今年晚些时候再安装两个集群。该公司表示，其精度在3000米到200米之间。

A cluster sweeps a band of territory 2,000km wide so, circling the planet every 90 minutes or so, it can revisit many areas several times a day. Moreover, unlike spy satellites fitted with optical cameras, RF satellites can see through clouds. Their receivers are not sensitive enough to detect standard mobile phones. But they can pick up satellite phones, walkie-talkies and all manner of radar. And, while vessels can and do illicitly disable their AIS, switching off their communications gear and the radar they use for navigation and collision-avoidance is another matter entirely. “Even pirates don’t turn those things off,” says John Beckner, boss of Horizon Technologies, a British firm that plans its first launch in August.

walkie-talkies 步话机

pirate 海盗

一个集群扫过一个2000公里宽的区域，因此，它每90分钟左右环绕地球一圈，一天可以重访许多地区几次。此外，与装有光学相机的间谍卫星不同，射频卫星可以穿透云层。他们的接收器不够灵敏，无法探测标准手机。但他们可以接收卫星电话，对讲机和各种雷达信号。而且，虽然船只可以而且确实非法地禁用其自动识别系统，但关闭其用于导航和避碰的通信设备和雷达则完全是另一回事。英国公司Horizon Technologies的老板约翰•贝克纳（johnbeckner）表示：“即使是海盗也不会关掉这些东西。” Horizon Technologies计划于今年8月首次上市。

RF data are also cheap to collect. Satellites fitted with robotic high-resolution cameras are costly. Flying microwave ovens that capture and timestamp radio signals are not. Horizon says that building, insuring and launching its August mission should cost no more than about $1.4m.

射频数据的收集成本也很低。装有机器人高分辨率摄像机的卫星价格昂贵。可捕捉和时间戳无线电信号的射频情报卫星则不用那么贵。Horizon公司表示，建造、投保和发行8月的任务的成本不超过140万美元。

America’s National Geospatial-Intelligence Agency (NGA), one of that country’s numerous spying operations, is a big user of RF intelligence. It employs HawkEye’s data to find guerrilla camps and mobile missile-launchers, and to track both conventional warships and unconventional ones, like the weaponised speedboats sometimes deployed by Iran. Robert Cardillo, a former director of the agency who now advises HawkEye, says dozens of navies, Russia’s included, spoof AIS signals to make warships appear to be in places which they are not. RF intelligence is not fooled by this. Mr Cardillo says, too, that the tininess of RF satellites makes them hard for an enemy to destroy.

guerrilla 游击队的

speedboat 快艇

warship 军舰

美国国家地理空间情报局(NGA)是该国众多间谍活动之一，也是射频情报的大用户。它利用“鹰眼”的数据来寻找游击队营地和移动导弹发射器，并跟踪常规军舰和非常规军舰，比如伊朗有时部署的武器化快艇。该机构前主任罗伯特·卡迪罗(Robert Cardillo)现在是鹰眼的顾问，他说，包括俄罗斯在内的几十个国家的海军欺骗AIS信号，以使军舰看起来在它们不在的地方。射频情报不会被这愚弄。卡迪罗先生还说，射频卫星的体积太小，敌人很难摧毁它们。

Beside matters military, the NGA also uses RF data to unearth illicit economic activity—of which unauthorised fishing is merely one instance. Outright piracy is another. And the technique also works on land. In 2019, for example, it led to the discovery of an illegal gold mine being run by a Chinese company in a jungle in Gabon. And in 2020 the managers of Garamba National Park in the Democratic Republic of Congo began using HawkEye data to spot elephant poachers and dispatch rangers to deal with them.

unearth 发现

unauthorised 未经授权的

ranger 护林员

除了军事方面的问题，NGA还利用射频数据来揭露非法经济活动——非法捕鱼只是其中一个例子。公然的劫持是另一方面。这项技术也适用于陆地。例如，2019年，在加蓬的丛林中发现了一家X公司经营的非法金矿。在2020年，刚果民主共和国的加兰巴国家公园的管理者开始使用鹰眼的数据来发现大象偷猎者，并派遣护林员来对付他们。

There are commercial uses, too. Andy Bowyer, Kleos’s boss, reports interest among telecoms firms keen to locate rogue transmitters, such as unlicensed ham radios, that are operating within their domains. Regulators, meanwhile, would like the firm to create “heat maps” of shifting patterns of legitimate transmissions. These would help them select sites for mobile-phone towers and also give them a better idea of the value in particular places of licences to use parts of the radio spectrum that are going up for auction. Some charities, too, have an interest in Kleos’s data. RF information can, for example, flag up routes taken by migrants likely to need food and other aid.

rogue 异常的

flag up 使人们对..关注

还有商业用途。Kleos的老板Andy Bowyer报告说，电信公司对定位异常发射机很感兴趣，比如在他们的业务范围内经营的未经许可的业余无线电。与此同时，监管机构希望该公司创建合法传输模式变化的“热图”。这将帮助他们为移动电话发射塔选择地点，也让他们更好地了解将要拍卖的部分无线频谱的使用牌照在特定地点的价值。一些慈善机构也对Kleos的数据感兴趣。例如，射频信息可以标示出可能需要食物和其他援助的移民所走的路线。

Declustering

离群

Using satellite clusters to gather RF intelligence is clever. But engineers at Unseenlabs, a firm in Rennes, France, reckon it is already outdated. At the moment, Unseen has three satellites in orbit and sells data to about ten navies, including France’s, as well as to maritime insurers and a handful of big defence contractors. But its satellites operate independently, rather than as a cluster, for Unseenlabs’ engineers have devised a detection system, which they claim is accurate to within 5,000 metres, that requires but a single satellite.

使用卫星群来收集射频情报是聪明的。但是法国雷恩Unseenlabs公司的工程师们认为这种技术已经过时了。目前，Unseen已在轨道上运行了三颗卫星，并向包括法国在内的十多个国家的海军出售数据，同时也向海事保险公司和少数几家大型国防承包商出售数据。但是它的卫星是独立运行而不是集群运行的，因为Unseenlabs的工程师已经设计出了一个探测系统，他们声称这个系统只需要一颗卫星就可以精确到5000米以内。

How this system works remains a secret—and one that, according to Clément Galic, Unseenlabs’ boss, is protected by the French state. After several attempts were made to steal it, he says, the defence ministry’s Directorate General of Armaments offered its assistance in defending the details from cybertheft.

这个系统是如何运作的仍然是一个秘密——根据Unseenlabs的老板Clément Galic的说法，这个系统是受法国政府保护的。他说，在几次被试图窃取之后，国防部的军备总局提供了帮助，保护这些信息免受网络窃取。

Secret or not, though, Unseenlabs may soon have competitors in the single-satellite-RF-intelligence market, for Horizon, too, says that it has worked out how to perform the trick—a claim backed up by the fact that its launch in August will loft but a single device. Shortly after it filed an application for a patent in America on the wizardry involved, the government there classified it. Even so, Mr Beckner drops a hint. The method involves assessing differences in the angles at which a target’s signals arrive during the satellite’s arc across the sky. Horizon says its system will be accurate to within 3,000 metres. By the middle of next year, it, too, plans to operate three satellites in different orbits—enough to scan most of the planet every two hours or so.

wizardry 非凡才能；奇异事物

不管秘密不秘密，Unseenlabs可能很快就会在单卫星RF情报市场上遇到竞争对手，因为Horizon也说他们已经找到了实现这一技术的方法，这一说法得到了一个事实的支持，即它在8月份的发射将只发射一个设备。就在它向美国申请了一项有关非凡才能的专利后不久，美国政府对其进行了保密。即便如此，Beckner还是给出了一些暗示。该方法包括评估卫星划过天空时目标信号到达的角度差异。Horizon表示，其系统将精确到3000米以内。到明年年中，它也计划在不同的轨道上运行三颗卫星——足够每两个小时左右扫描地球的大部分。

Horizon also plans to compile a library of unique radar-pulse “fingerprints” of the world’s vessels, for the tiny differences in componentry that exist even between examples of the same make and model of equipment mean that signals can often be linked to a specific device. It will thus be able to determine not merely that a vessel of some sort is in a certain place, but which vessel it is, and where else it has been.

Horizon还计划编制一个世界船只独特的雷达脉冲“指纹”库，因为即使是同一品牌和型号的设备，其组成部分也存在微小的差异，这意味着信号往往可以连接到特定的设备上。因此，它不仅能够确定某种船只在某个地方，而且能够确定它是哪一艘船只，以及它曾在哪里。

Unseenlabs, for its part, has already catalogued the radar fingerprints of many thousands of vessels, several hundred of which have, subsequent to the events of last summer, spent time in the Galapagos EEZ with their AIS beacons switched off. It remains to be seen what Ecuador’s authorities will do with that information. But no one can say they weren’t told.■

Unseenlabs则已经对数千艘船只的雷达指纹进行了分类，其中数百艘船只在去年夏天的事件之后，在加拉帕戈斯专属经济区停留，AIS信标关闭。厄瓜多尔当局将如何处理这些信息还有待观察。但是没有人能说他们没有被告知。
