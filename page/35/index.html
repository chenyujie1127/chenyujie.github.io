Workers have the most to lose from a wage-price spiral

工人在工资-物价螺旋上升中损失最大

The chicken and the peg

鸡和钉子

![image-20220224093417427](/Users/chenyujie/Library/Application Support/typora-user-images/image-20220224093417427.png)

As prices rise, real wages are falling

随着物价上涨，实际工资正在下降

Feb 19th 2022

THE WORLD economy keeps producing nasty inflation surprises. In January consumer prices grew by more than expected in America, Britain and the euro zone, and America’s rate of annual producer-price inflation stayed close to 10%. Making matters worse, fears that Russia would invade Ukraine sent the oil price to over $96 a barrel on February 14th, its highest since 2014. On both sides of the Atlantic financial markets have rapidly priced in more monetary tightening in 2022, as some central bankers have begun to worry in public that they face a test of their credibility.

世界经济不断制造令人不快的通胀惊喜。1月份，美国、英国和欧元区的消费者价格增长超过预期，美国的年度生产者价格通胀率保持在10%左右。更糟糕的是，2月14日，由于担心俄罗斯会入侵乌克兰，油价超过了96美元一桶，这是2014年以来的最高水平。大西洋两岸的金融市场已迅速消化了2022年将进一步收紧货币政策的预期，一些央行官员已开始公开担心，他们的信誉将面临考验。

Monetary policymakers are scouring labour markets for signs that high inflation is becoming baked into workers’ wage demands, indicating the start of a wage-price spiral. They are in an awkward spot, because the idea that wages are rising too fast is politically toxic. In America annual wage growth of 5.7% is plainly inconsistent with the Federal Reserve’s 2% inflation target. Yet President Joe Biden is celebrating rising pay while left-wing Democrats blame price rises on corporate greed. There is plenty of fuss elsewhere, too. Britain’s government has said it does not support a call by Andrew Bailey, the governor of the Bank of England, for workers to rein in their pay demands. Christine Lagarde, head of the European Central Bank, says she hopes to see wages rise, even as her colleagues warn of the dangers of excessive pay growth.

货币政策制定者正在劳动力市场上寻找高通胀正融入工人工资需求的迹象，这意味着工资-物价螺旋上升已经开始。他们正处于一个尴尬的境地，因为工资上涨过快的想法在政治上是有害的。在美国，5.7%的年工资增长率与美联储2%的通胀目标明显不符。然而，美国总统乔·拜登正在庆祝工资上涨，而左翼民主党人则将物价上涨归咎于企业的贪婪。在其他地方也有大量的争吵。英国政府表示，它不支持英格兰银行行长Andrew Bailey要求工人控制自己的工资要求的呼吁。欧洲央行行长Christine Lagarde表示，她希望看到工资上涨，尽管她的同事警告了工资过度增长的危险。

In the popular imagination workers are often canny first movers in a wage-price spiral, rather than its victims. Rising pay pushes up costs for firms, which then increase prices to protect profits. In part this is based on the experience of the late 1960s and 1970s, when union bosses negotiated above-inflation pay increases for their members. In 1974, in Germany’s infamous Kluncker-Runde, Heinz Kluncker, a combative unionist, won public-sector workers a budget-busting 11% pay rise.

在大众的想象中，工人往往是工资-物价螺旋上升过程中精明的先行者，而不是受害者。工资的上涨推高了企业的成本，企业进而会提高价格以保护利润。这在一定程度上是基于20世纪60年代末和70年代的经验，当时工会的老板们通过谈判为他们的成员增加高于通胀的工资。1974年，在德国臭名昭著的Kluncker- runde中, Heinz Kluncker是一个好斗的工会成员，他为公共部门的工人赢得了超出预算的11%的加薪。

In fact, high inflation often hurts workers. Over the past year inflation has been higher than wage growth in every G7 country, despite widespread labour shortages. Unions are much less powerful today than they were in the 1970s and scholars typically find that prices lead wages, rather than vice versa. Even in the 1970s many workers suffered from the wage-price spiral. In that decade American wages grew only half as fast as workers’ productivity, just as today wages are yet to catch up with such gains.

事实上，高通胀往往会伤害到工人。在过去的一年里，七国集团的通货膨胀率一直高于工资增长，尽管普遍存在劳动力短缺。如今的工会比上世纪70年代的工会势力要小得多，学者们通常会发现，物价决定工资，而非工资决定物价。即使在20世纪70年代，许多工人也遭受着工资-物价螺旋上升的困扰。在那十年里，美国的工资增长速度仅为工人生产率增长速度的一半，正如今天的工资还没有赶上这一增长一样。

In Europe high inflation is mostly down to expensive energy. But in America it is the consequence of a tsunami of spending, the result of stimulus cheques and low interest rates, overwhelming the economy’s capacity to expand production. Firms have raised prices not to pass on costs but to curb demand, sending profit margins soaring. As consumer-price inflation has risen to 7.5%, it is capital, not labour, which has had the upper hand, feeding claims that firms are profiteering. The outcome is perverse, not least because Jerome Powell, the Fed’s chairman, has often said that the main justification for stimulus was to help workers and to reduce inequality.

在欧洲，高通胀主要是由于昂贵的能源。但在美国，这是消费支出过多的结果，这是使用了刺激支票和低利率的结果，这压倒了经济扩大生产的能力。企业提高价格不是为了转嫁成本，而是为了抑制需求，这就导致利润率飙升。随着消费价格通胀上升到7.5%，是资本而非劳动力占了上风，这使得公司暴利的说法更加有根据。这种结果是反常的，尤其是因为美联储主席Jerome Powell经常表示，刺激政策的主要理由是帮助工人和减少不平等。

It is pointless, however, to attempt to bully firms or workers into resisting market forces. Just ask Japan, where the government has for years tried to deal with below-target inflation by badgering firms to raise wages, without success. The correct response to an economy that is too hot or too cold is to adjust macroeconomic policy, not to interfere with wage- and price-setting.

然而，企图强迫公司或工人抵抗市场力量是毫无意义的。试问日本，政府多年来一直试图通过纠缠企业提高工资来解决低于目标的通胀，但没有成功。对于经济过热或过冷的正确反应是调整宏观经济政策，而不是干预工资和定价。

It is important that the Fed raises interest rates quickly—and that European policymakers stay vigilant. The longer inflation is too high, the more painful it could be to bring it back down. The wage-price spirals of the 1970s were contained only after tight monetary policy induced a global downturn in which American unemployment peaked at nearly 11%. If central bankers once again have to induce recessions to restore their credibility on inflation, workers will pay the price for that, too. ■

美联储必须迅速提高利率，欧洲决策者必须保持警惕。通胀过高的时间越长，将其拉低的痛苦就越大。直到紧缩的货币政策导致全球经济衰退，美国失业率达到近11%的峰值后，20世纪70年代的工资价格螺旋上升才得以遏制。如果央行行长再次不得不诱发衰退，以恢复他们对通胀的信誉，工人们也将为此付出代价。■

After expanding in 2021, fast fashion may be squeezed again

在2021年扩张之后，快时尚可能会再次举步维艰

The middle-market corset

![image-20220223093543340](/Users/chenyujie/Library/Application Support/typora-user-images/image-20220223093543340.png)

H&M and Inditex had a good year but face familiar challenges

H&M和Inditex今年业绩不错，但也面临着熟悉的挑战

Feb 19th 2022

AMERICAN CONSUMERS are feeling flush. On February 15th the Commerce Department reported that the country’s shoppers spent 3.8% more in January than they had in December, unfazed by spiking inflation and covid-related uncertainty. That was the fastest monthly rise in nearly a year. Some of this splurge is going on new rags. Elsewhere, too, garment-sellers are booming. In Britain fashion was the only segment to see online sales grow last month, year on year, according to Capgemini, a consultancy. As catwalks and cocktail parties decamp from New York, which has just hosted its Fashion Week, to London, where another one is kicking off, the mood in the clothes business is as bright as the pastel-coloured dresses that are all the rage this season.

美国消费者感觉手头宽裕。2月15日，美国商务部报告称，美国消费者1月份的支出比去年12月高出3.8%，而且并未受到通胀飙升和与新冠肺炎相关的不确定性的影响。这是近一年来最快的月度增长。这些挥霍中有一部分花在了新的“破布”[衣服]上。在其他地方，服装销售商也在蓬勃发展。根据咨询公司Capgemini的数据，上个月，英国时装业是唯一一个在线销售同比增长的行业。随着时装秀和鸡尾酒会从刚刚举办了时装周的纽约转移到伦敦，而伦敦的另一场时装周也即将拉开帷幕，时装业的气氛就像这一季最流行的淡色连衣裙一样明亮。

High-end labels like Christian Dior (owned by LVMH, a luxury colossus) or Gucci (part of Kering, a fellow French group) are relatively immune to economic turmoil. People who can afford their frocks may take a knock in a recession but seldom end up shirtless. The same cannot be said of less luxurious fashion houses. But they, too, have had a good run of late.

像Christian Dior(奢侈品巨头酩悦·轩尼诗-路易·威登集团旗下品牌)或Gucci(法国开云集团旗下品牌)这样的高端品牌相对来说不受经济动荡的影响。那些买得起连衣裙的人可能会受到经济衰退的打击，但他们很少会赤膊上阵。不那么奢侈的时装公司就不能这样说了。但他们最近也有不错的表现。

Ralph Lauren, a relatively upmarket American brand, opened 40 new shops in the third quarter last year alone, including a flagship store in Milan, as well as shops in Atlanta, Chicago, Detroit and Miami, often on those cities’ swankiest shopping streets. Its boss, Patrice Louvet, thinks consumers will keep replenishing their wardrobes and says his firm “is back on the offence”. In the mass market, sales at Hennes & Mauritz (H&M), a fast-fashion giant, are back to pre-pandemic levels and profitability is better than it has been in years. Helena Helmersson, who took over as its chief executive in January 2020, just before covid-19 hit Europe, has proclaimed that she wants to double the Swedish group’s sales by 2030 and reach an operating margin of above 10% within three years, up from less than 2% in 2020 and 7.7% in 2021.

Ralph Lauren，一个相对高端的美国品牌，仅在去年第三季度就开了40家新店，包括在米兰的旗舰店，以及在亚特兰大、芝加哥、底特律和迈阿密的商店，通常都开在这些城市最豪华的购物街上。它的老板Patrice Louvet认为消费者会不断地补充他们的衣柜，并说他的公司“又开始进攻了”。在大众市场上，快时尚巨头H&M的销售额已经恢复到疫情前的水平，盈利能力也比过去几年有所改善。Helena Helmersson在2020年1月接任首席执行官, 就在新冠袭击欧洲之前,她曾宣称她希望到2030年瑞典集团销量翻倍,在三年内达到10%以上的经营利润率, 2020年不到2%, 2021年为7.7%。

Ms Helmersson and Mr Louvet reflect an optimism in the industry as it emerges from the disruptions caused by the pandemic. But they should go easy on the champagne during upcoming Fashion Weeks. Clothes companies, in particular those catering more to the masses, face an assortment of challenges. Some of these, such as digitisation and sustainability, predate covid-19. The pandemic has only heaped on more, from supply-chain bottlenecks and sky-high shipping costs to worker shortages. On top of that, the caprices of the world’s most populous autocracy mean that one false step can cost firms a fortune. H&M sales in XX slumped last year after the company expressed concerns about allegations of forced labour in the XX region.

Helmersson女士和Louvet先生反映出，在该行业摆脱疫情造成的混乱之际，人们表现出一种乐观情绪。但在即将到来的时装周期间，他们应该少喝香槟。服装公司，尤其是那些更迎合大众需求的公司，面临着各种各样的挑战。其中一些挑战，如数字化和可持续性，早在新冠之前就已开始。从供应链瓶颈、高昂的运输成本到劳动力短缺，新冠肺炎疫情只会带来更多后果。最重要的是，这个世界上人口最多的独裁国家的反复无常意味着，一个错误的步骤就会让企业付出巨大的代价。去年，在对XX地区强迫劳动的指控表示担忧后，H&M在XX的销售额大幅下滑。

Fashion retailers’ success last year was driven by unusual circumstances that will not last. Pent-up demand triggered a wave of “revenge buying” when shops reopened at last, in particular for “occasion wear” (jargon for pricey stuff). Shoppers’ pockets were lined with infusions of government cash. And the pandemic was the final nail in the coffin for some weaker firms, reducing competition in the crowded market; Topshop, Laura Ashley and TM Lewin went under in Britain, and Ann Taylor, Brooks Brothers and J. Crew did in America.

时尚零售商去年的成功是由不寻常的情况推动的，但这种情况不会持续很久。当商店终于重新开张时，被压抑的需求引发了一波“报复性购买”，尤其是“场合服装”(术语，指昂贵的物品)。购物者的口袋里塞满了政府投入的现金。对一些实力较弱的公司来说，疫情是致命的一击，这减少了拥挤市场中的竞争; Topshop, Laura Ashley和TM Lewin在英国破产，Ann Taylor, Brooks Brothers和J. Crew在美国破产。

Now that consumers are no longer receiving cheques from the government, and have anyway already spruced up their wardrobes, they may become more parsimonious. Unlike luxury brands’ well-heeled customers, who might hardly notice that a handbag that cost $5,000 in 2019 now goes for $8,000 (as became true in November of Chanel’s Classic Flap), those of mass-market brands may balk at higher price tags. Necessary investments in digitisation and sustainability—Ms Helmersson has launched a vegan collection and invested in Sellpy, a digital platform to trade second-hand clothes—will eat into the fast-fashion houses’ profitability.

既然消费者不再收到政府的支票，而且无论如何都已经把自己的衣柜打扮得光鲜亮丽，他们可能会变得更加节俭。奢侈品牌的有钱的客户可能几乎不会注意到，2019年一款售价5000美元的手袋现在卖到8000美元(去年11月Chanel的Classic Flap就是那样)，与奢侈品牌的有钱的客户不同，大众市场品牌可能会对更高的价格望而却步。在数字化和可持续发展方面的必要投资(Helmersson女士推出了vegan collection，并投资了一个二手服装的数字交易平台Sellpy)将会削弱快时尚品牌的盈利能力。

Younger models

As for competition, some passé brands may be gone but a few fresh faces look much more threatening to the mass-market giants’ market share. Companies like Shein, a Chinese super-discounter, Britain’s Asos or Germany’s Zalando have greater digital nous than mostly offline H&M and Inditex, its Spanish arch-rival and owner of brands including Zara. They are also finding ways to appeal to young fashionistas. All this may be why analysts forecast a more modest increase in H&M sales than Ms Helmersson does, of around 50% by 2030, and less cushy margins. Its share price, like that of Inditex, is below where it was before the pandemic.

至于竞争，一些过时的品牌可能会消失，但一些新面孔看起来对大众市场巨头的市场份额威胁更大。像中国超级折扣商Shein、英国的Asos和德国的Zalando这样的公司，在数字方面比大多数线下品牌H&M和Inditex更懂行。Inditex是H&M的西班牙主要竞争对手，旗下拥有包括Zara在内的多个品牌。他们也在想方设法吸引年轻的时尚达人。所有这一切可能就是为什么分析师们预测H&M的销售额增长会比Helmersson女士预测的更温和，即到2030年大约增长50%，而且利润也不会那么丰厚。与Inditex一样，该公司的股价也低于疫情爆发前的水平。

In its annual report on the state of the clothing business, McKinsey, a consultancy, predicts that discount and luxury fashion will continue to wow investors this year. The middle-market retailers may enjoy another season or two of revenge buying. After that, their prospects are looking more threadbare. ■

麦肯锡咨询公司在其关于服装行业状况的年度报告中预测，今年折扣和奢侈时装将继续吸引投资者。中端市场的零售商可能还会享受到一到两个季度的“报复性购买”。在那之后，他们的前景看起来更加暗淡。■

Meet Valérie Pécresse, the French centre-right hopeful

来看看Valérie Pécresse，法国中右翼的希望之星

The Pécresse file

![image-20220222103935443](/Users/chenyujie/Library/Application Support/typora-user-images/image-20220222103935443.png)

Her campaign is in trouble, but she fights on

她的竞选活动陷入困境，但她仍在继续奋斗

“Mmmm, a nice baguette from the Ardennes!” declares Valérie Pécresse, tearing off a chunk of the warm crusty loaf she has just bought at a boulangerie and popping it into her mouth. The centre-right Republicans’ presidential candidate, and head of the greater Paris region, has taken her campaign to the valleys and forests of north-eastern France on a recent weekday. In the village of Signy-l’Abbaye, no shop or café goes unvisited. As Mrs Pécresse breezes in and out, clutching her loaf, some locals seem bemused. The manager at Le Gibergeon restaurant confesses beforehand to having no idea who the visitor is, but is later charmed. “Oh yes, I recognised her from the telly,” she says. “It would be good to have a female présidente.”

“嗯，阿登的法棍很好吃!” Valérie Pécresse一边说着，一边撕下一大块刚从面包店买的热乎乎的硬面包，塞进嘴里。在最近的一个工作日，这位中右翼共和党总统候选人、大巴黎地区领导人在法国东北部的山谷和森林展开了竞选活动。在Signy-l ‘Abbaye村，没有一家商店或咖啡馆是无人光顾的。当Pécresse夫人轻轻地进来，拿着她的面包轻轻地出去时，一些当地人似乎很困惑。Le Gibergeon餐厅的经理事先承认不知道客人是谁，但后来被迷住了。她说:“哦，是的，我从电视上认出了她。如果能有一位女性总统，那就太好了。”

After winning her party’s primary in December, Mrs Pécresse recorded a poll bump that made her the most serious contender against President Emmanuel Macron at France’s two-round election in April. Polls still suggest she would do about as well against the president in a run-off as would the nationalist-populist Marine Le Pen (though he is tipped to beat either), and much better than the far-right Eric Zemmour. Yet Mrs Pécresse’s first-round numbers have fallen, and her campaign has stalled. On February 13th, at a glitzy rally in Paris, she put in a wooden performance that was criticised even within her camp. The clear danger for Mrs Pécresse is that she will fail to make the run-off at all.

在去年12月赢得党内初选后，Pécresse夫人的民调大幅上升，使她成为今年4月法国两轮选举中对抗总统马克龙的最有力竞争者。民意调查仍然显示，在与总统的决选中，她与民族主义-民粹主义的Marine Le Pen不相上下(尽管他有可能击败其中任何一个)，而且比极右翼的Eric Zemmour要好得多。然而，Pécresse夫人在第一轮选举中的支持率已经下降，她的竞选活动也停滞不前。2月13日，在巴黎一个华丽的集会上，她上演了一场呆板的表演，甚至在她的阵营中也受到了批评。对于Pécresse女士来说，显而易见的危险是她将在决选中失败。

Out in the Ardennes, with its family-run dairy and cattle farms, locals list their troubles: the price of petrol, the distance to the nearest hospital. In and out the former budget minister goes, stopping for coffee in a café and a beer in a bar. A graduate, like Mr Macron, of the Ecole Nationale d’Administration, France’s elite technocratic training college, Mrs Pécresse is well briefed, serious, and tough in debate. But she also knows how to listen. “She was self-confident, very attentive and listened a lot,” says a retired woman in the village. Later, at a town-hall meeting in an industrial warehouse 25 kilometres (16 miles) away, a participant says: “She’s much nicer than she seems on the television.”

在阿登地区，有家庭经营的奶牛场和养牛场，当地人列出了他们的烦恼:汽油的价格，离最近的医院的距离。这位前预算部长进进出出，在一家咖啡店喝咖啡，在一家酒吧喝啤酒。和马克龙一样，她毕业于法国精英技术专家官员的培训学院，即法国国家行政学院。Pécresse女士在辩论中表现得很熟知情况、严肃、强硬。但她也知道如何倾听。村里一位退休妇女表示:“她很自信，非常专注，听的也很多。”后来，在25公里(16英里)外的一个工业仓库举行的市政厅会议上，一位与会者说:“她比电视上看起来好得多。”

Smart, tough and nice, however, may not be enough. Mrs Pécresse is running into two difficulties as she seeks to become France’s first female president. The first is that, on stage, she does not light up a room. After a day campaigning in the Ardennes, she heads for a rally in the town of Charleville-Mézières, where Ms Le Pen topped first-round voting in 2017. A professional crowd-pumper chants “Valérie! Valérie!” as the candidate enters the hall. A mostly grey-haired audience tentatively joins in. At her Paris rally, packed with over 7,000 supporters, Mrs Pécresse gave a stilted performance. The next day she confessed to being “more at ease” in conversation.

然而，聪明、坚强和友善可能还不够。在试图成为法国首位女总统的过程中，Pécresse夫人遇到了两个困难。第一，在舞台上，她不会调动观众气氛。在阿登进行了一天的竞选活动后，她前往Charleville-Mézières镇参加集会。2017年，Le Pen在这里的首轮投票中获胜。当候选人走进大厅时，一名专业的人群中的“气氛组”高呼“Valérie! Valérie!”。一群几乎满头白发的观众试探性地加入了进来。在她的巴黎集会上，有超过7000名支持者，Pécresse夫人表演得很生硬。第二天，她承认自己在谈话中“更自在”。

The other is her political positioning. Mrs Pécresse instinctively belongs to the moderate, pro-European centre-right and was once seen as a potential recruit to Mr Macron’s government. Yet she secured her nomination by defeating Eric Ciotti, a party right-winger, in the primary run-off. He embraces the “great replacement” theory—adopted as a slogan globally by white supremacists—that foreign populations threaten to replace the “indigenous” French. To try to keep this broad church together, Mrs Pécresse nods in Mr Ciotti’s direction. At her Paris rally she deplored Mr Macron’s “failure” to forbid athletes from competing while wearing the Muslim headscarf, and referred, albeit ambiguously, to replacement theory.

另一个是她的政治定位。Pécresse女士本能地属于温和、亲欧的中右翼，她曾被视为马克龙政府的潜在人选。然而，她在初选决选中击败了党内右翼分子Eric Ciotti，从而获得了提名。他信奉“大颠覆”理论，即外国人口将取代“土生土长的”法国人，这一理论被全球白人至上主义者奉为口号。为了把这个广阔的教会组织团结在一起，Pécresse夫人向Ciotti先生的方向点头。在巴黎的集会上，她对马克龙“未能”禁止运动员戴穆斯林头巾参赛表示强烈谴责，并含糊地提到了“大颠覆”理论。

Mrs Pécresse denies that she has hardened her line, arguing that she has always been proud of being on the right, and tracing her lineage to Jacques Chirac, a Gaullist former president and her mentor. She calls herself “two-thirds Merkel and one-third Thatcher”, and a feminist. “I know how to be firm, but also how to hold dialogue,” says Mrs Pécresse during a break on the Ardennes trail, dismissing Mr Macron as “Blairite”, a “left-wing liberal” and a “candidate of the cities”. Her project, she insists, bears “no similarity to Macron’s”.

Pécresse夫人否认她已经坚定了自己的立场，争辩说她一直为自己是右派而自豪，并将她的血统追溯至戴高乐主义的前总统、她的导师 Jacques Chirac。她称自己是“三分之二的默克尔，三分之一的撒切尔”，还是一名女权主义者。Pécresse在阿登竞选活动的休息时间说道：“我知道如何坚定立场，但也知道如何进行对话”，她将马克龙贬为“布莱尔主义者”、“左翼自由主义者”和“城市候选人”。她坚称，她的计划“与马克龙的毫无相似之处”。

Yet many centre-right voters are confused. They are drawn to Mrs Pécresse’s fiscally prudent vow to curb public spending, which has soared under Mr Macron during the pandemic, as well as to trim the 5.6m-strong French civil service by a net 150,000 jobs and raise the state retirement age from 62 to 65 years. But such voters are put off by her nationalist tone on the need to “stop uncontrolled immigration”, and by her attacks on Mr Macron’s broader economic management, which she calls “calamitous”. The economy last year grew at its fastest rate for half a century.

然而，许多中右翼选民感到困惑。吸引他们的是Pécresse夫人在财政上的谨慎承诺，她将削减公共支出(在疫情期间，公共支出在马克龙的领导下飙升)，以及通过净裁15万个工作岗位来“削减”560万极大量的法国公务员，她还会将国家退休年龄从62岁提高到65岁。但这些选民对她的民族主义论调感到反感，她强调有必要“阻止不受控制的移民”，并对马克龙更广泛的经济管理进行了攻击，她称马克龙的管理是“灾难性的”。去年，法国经济在以半个世纪以来最快的速度增长。

Amid these contradictions, Mrs Pécresse’s campaign is fraying. In recent days she has lost Eric Woerth, the Republicans’ former budget minister, who now backs Mr Macron, as does Natacha Bouchart, the Republicans’ mayor of Calais. At the other extreme, Guillaume Peltier, a former party vice-president, has quit for Mr Zemmour. Even Nicolas Sarkozy, the Republicans’ former president, has declined so far to offer public support for la candidate.

在这些矛盾中，Pécresse夫人的竞选活动正在变得紧张起来。最近几天，她失去了支持马克龙的共和党前预算部长Eric Woerth，以及共和党Calais市长Natacha Bouchart。在另一个极端，该党前副主席Guillaume Peltier辞职去支持Zemmour。就连共和党前总统Nicolas Sarkozy也拒绝公开支持某位候选人。

Perhaps voters just find it difficult to relate to Mrs Pécresse, who lives in Versailles and was privately educated in the swanky Paris suburb of Neuilly-sur-Seine. Her most rebellious moment was the time she spent, rather improbably, as a teenager in Soviet youth summer camps, after falling in love with Russian literature. To this day, she can speak the language. “I’ve always been very intrepid,” she says.

也许选民们只是觉得很难与Pécresse夫人建立联系，她住在凡尔赛，在巴黎上流郊区Neuilly-sur-Seine接受过私立教育。她最叛逆的时刻，是在她十几岁的时候，在爱上俄罗斯文学后，在苏联青少年夏令营度过的时光，这很不可思议。直到今天，她还能说这种语言。她说：“我一直都很勇敢”。

The risk for Mrs Pécresse is that she now loses momentum. Mr Zemmour has crept past her in two new polls. When Mr Macron confirms his candidacy, which is expected any day now, the campaign dynamics may shift again. “There’s been a lot of criticism of Macron during the pandemic,” says a retired railway worker in Signy-l’Abbaye. “He’s arrogant, but he hasn’t managed things too badly.”

Pécresse女士面临的风险是她现在失去了动力。Zemmour在两个新的民调中都超过了她。马克龙确认参选后(预计这一天很快就会到来)，竞选格局可能会再次发生变化。Signy-l’Abbaye的一名退休铁路工人说：“新冠大流行期间，马克龙受到了很多批评。他很傲慢，但他事情处理得没有太糟。”

Mrs Pécresse, meanwhile, is off again on her campaign “à la Chirac”, a candidate who delighted in rural French life. Leaving the boulangerie in Signy-l’Abbaye, she asks the baker the secret of a good loaf. “You need patience, you need time for the flavour to develop,” he replies. Time which, for Mrs Pécresse, may be running out. ■

与此同时，Pécresse夫人又开始了她的竞选活动“à la Chirac”，她是一位喜欢法国乡村生活的候选人。离开Signy-l’Abbaye的面包房后，她问面包师制作好面包的秘诀。他回答说:“你需要耐心，需要时间来产生味道。” 对于Pécresse夫人来说，时间可能已经不多了。■

BioNTech plans to make vaccines in shipping containers

BioNTech计划用船运集装箱制造疫苗

Drug manufacturing

制药

![image-20220220120005604](/Users/chenyujie/Library/Application Support/typora-user-images/image-20220220120005604.png)

These standardised factories could be quickly scaled up to expand capacity worldwide

这些标准化工厂可以迅速扩大规模，扩大全球产能

Feb 19th 2022

AT THE HEART of its site in Marburg, Germany, BioNTech is putting the finishing touches on a new kind of factory. The drug company has spent eight months reworking its manufacturing processes to produce its mRNA covid-19 vaccine inside a set of standard shipping containers. By creating a modular approach to drug manufacturing, Ugur Sahin, BioNTech’s boss, says he aims to transform medicine production around the world.

在德国马尔堡的中心，BioNTech正在对一种新型工厂进行最后的润色。这家制药公司花了8个月时间重新改造其生产流程，在一套标准集装箱内生产其mRNA新冠疫苗。BioNTech的老板Ugur Sahin说，通过建立一种模块化的药品生产方法，他的目标是改变全世界的药品生产。

The work is analogous to software developers rewriting their code to run on different kinds of computer—porting a game from Microsoft’s Xbox so that it will run on a Sony PlayStation, for example. Biological porting involves tweaking the 50,000 steps that comprise the manufacturing process of the mRNA vaccine from one environment, BioNTech’s existing production lines around the world, so that they work in another, a series of connected, standard metal shipping containers. The firm plans to send its containerised mRNA factories—which it calls Biontainers—to parts of the world which lack their own vaccine-manufacturing capabilities. The first will arrive in an African country, not yet named, towards the end of 2022.

这项工作类似于软件开发者重写他们的代码以在不同类型的电脑上运行，例如，将微软Xbox上的游戏移植到索尼PlayStation上运行。生物移植包括微调来自一个环境(BioNTech在世界各地的现有生产线)的mRNA疫苗制造过程的50000个步骤，从而能在另一个、一系列连接的标准金属运输集装箱工作。该公司计划将其集装箱化的mRNA工厂，它称之为Biontainers，送到世界上自身缺乏疫苗生产能力的地区。第一个工厂将于2022年底抵达一个尚未命名的非洲国家。

BioNTech has turned to containers as a reliable, repeatable way to achieve “good manufacturing practice” (GMP), a pharmaceutical-industry term for the minimum standards required of a manufacturer as part of their authorisation to sell products. GMP standards exist in order to ensure a consistently high quality of manufacturing output, which in turn protects consumers from badly made drugs. Validating new GMP facilities is a long, slow process. Mr Sahin’s aim is to remove, to some extent, local factors from the equation that governs where vaccines can be produced, with a modular GMP facility that can be installed and run anywhere in the world.

BioNTech已经将容器作为实现《药品生产质量管理规范》(GMP)的可靠、可重复的方式，GMP是制药行业的一个术语，指的是制造商销售产品时所要求的最低标准。GMP标准的存在是为了确保生产产品始终保持高质量，从而保护消费者免受劣质药品的影响。验证新的GMP设施是一个漫长而缓慢的过程。Sahin先生的目标是，在一定程度上，通过一个模块化的GMP设施，从控制疫苗生产地的等式中消除当地因素，该设施可以在世界任何地方安装和运转。

All manufacturing operations consist of a series of steps that are known in their entirety only to the collection of engineers who carry out the work, a sort of industrial recipe. This knowledge about the production process includes everything from the settings of dials on every device to the temperatures, pressures and timings at which chemical reactions should run. These variables are precise to the extent that they can change depending on the weather. The transfer of this recipe to new production facilities presents perhaps the greatest bottleneck to increasing vaccine production. Even when pharmaceutical companies were transferring this knowledge internally, during the height of the pandemic, it took around eight months to increase their own vaccine-production capacity.

所有的生产操作都是由一系列的步骤组成的，这些步骤只有那些从事这项工作的工程师才知道，这是一种行业配方。这些关于生产过程的知识包括从每个设备上的刻度盘的设置到温度、压力和化学反应应该运行的时间。这些变量在一定程度上是精确的，它们可以随天气而变化。将这一配方转让给新的生产设施可能是增加疫苗生产的最大瓶颈。即使制药公司在内部转移这一知识，在大流行最严重的时期，也花了大约8个月的时间来增加它们自己的疫苗生产能力。

Mr Sahin wants his shipping containers to speed things up. His idea is that after the process knowledge has been successfully ported into the standardised environment of the shipping container once, that facility can then be quickly cloned into other containers. Updates to the production method or tweaks to the recipe of the vaccine itself could be transmitted digitally to any containers in the network. “This”, says Mr Sahin, “is the future of manufacturing not only for Africa, but worldwide.”

Sahin先生希望他的船运集装箱能够加快速度。他的想法是，一旦流程知识成功地移植到集装箱的标准化环境中，该设施就可以快速地克隆到其他集装箱中。生产方法的更新或疫苗配方本身的调整可以通过数字方式传输到网络中的任何容器。Sahin先生说:“这不仅是非洲制造的未来，也是全世界制造的未来”

Faster, more productive

After eight months of work to get its first container factory off the ground in Marburg, BioNTech’s approach will be put to the test later this year. By the end of June, Mr Sahin says, the site at its partner country in Africa will be prepared for the arrival of the containers—BioNTech is in discussions with South Africa, Rwanda and Senegal. He expects the containers, the interiors of which will have been set up to the letter in Germany, to have arrived by the end of 2022. Each set of 12 containers will need four or five operators and be capable of producing some 40m-60m doses every year. BioNTech hopes the facility will cost “significantly less” than a traditional vaccine manufacturing factory of equivalent output, which comes with a price tag of at least $170m.

历时八个月，BioNTech在马尔堡建立了第一家集装箱工厂，该公司的方法将在今年晚些时候接受测试。Sahin先生说，到6月底，它在非洲的合作伙伴国家的站点将准备好迎接集装箱的到来，BioNTech正在与南非、卢旺达和塞内加尔进行讨论。他预计，这些集装箱将在2022年底前运抵德国，其内部结构将完全符合德国的要求。每组12个容器需要4到5个操作人员，每年能够生产4000万到6000万剂。BioNTech希望该设施的成本将“大大低于”生产同等产量的传统疫苗工厂，后者的价格至少为1.7亿美元。

The validation and quality-control work will carry on through 2023, as will hiring and training local operators. In parallel, there will be conversations with regulators about the new containerised production process. Mr Sahin says BioNTech is already talking to the African Union, a regional bloc, the Africa Centres for Disease Control and Prevention and national regulators. He hopes that the German and European GMP standards to which the container factories have been built will be acceptable. If they are, then vaccine production will start at the end of 2023.

验证和质量控制工作将持续到2023年，招聘和培训当地操作人员也将持续到2023年。与此同时，他们还将与监管机构讨论新的集装箱生产流程。Sahin先生表示，BioNTech已经在与区域集团非洲联盟、非洲疾病控制和预防中心以及国家监管机构进行谈判。他希望集装箱工厂已经建立的德国和欧洲GMP标准能够被接受。如果可以接受，那么疫苗生产将在2023年底开始。

Although BioNTech’s plan will not see any production facilities in operation for almost two years, it also plans for the Biontainers to have utility beyond the pandemic and the production of covid-19 vaccines. Mr Sahin says the production system could be used to make other vaccines and drugs, for example, against malaria or tuberculosis. In July last year BioNTech announced its aim “to develop a well-tolerated and highly effective malaria vaccine and implement sustainable vaccine supply solutions on the African continent”.

虽然BioNTech的计划将在近两年内不会看到任何生产设施投入运营，但它也计划让Biontainers在大流行和新冠疫苗生产之外发挥作用。Sahin先生说，该生产系统可以用于生产其他疫苗和药物，例如，用于对抗疟疾或肺结核。去年7月，BioNTech宣布其目标是“开发一种耐受性良好的、高效的疟疾疫苗，并在非洲大陆实施可持续的疫苗供应解决方案”。

There may yet be pitfalls in BioNTech’s plan. Containers may prove to offer a less uniform manufacturing environment than Mr Sahin hopes. Regulators may spot issues. Countries around the world may not accept medicines produced in facilities which, although geographically local, are operationally under a foreign company’s control. But the principle of reducing the cost of copying process knowledge is sound. If it works, Mr Sahin is likely not just to boost manufacturing capacity on the African continent, but to change the way drugs are made everywhere. ■

BioNTech的计划可能还存在缺陷。集装箱的制造环境可能不如Sahin 先生所希望的那样统一。监管者可能会发现问题。世界各国可能不接受“虽然地理位置在当地，但操作是由外国公司控制的设施中生产的药品”。但降低复制工艺知识成本的原则是合理的。如果该计划奏效，Sahin先生可能不仅会提高非洲大陆的生产能力，还会改变各地药品的生产方式。■

A guide for wannabe leadership gurus

这是给那些想成为领导大师的人的指南

Bartleby

巴托比

![image-20220220115524369](/Users/chenyujie/Library/Application Support/typora-user-images/image-20220220115524369.png)

How aspiring management writers can stand out in a crowded field

有抱负的管理作家如何在拥挤的领域脱颖而出

Feb 19th 2022

THE SHEER amount of guff written about leadership, management and careers is staggering. Publishers spew out new business titles, some good, most not. Re search papers proliferate, exploring everything from the impact of covid-19 on leadership in dental practices in England to the prevalence of psychopathy among sustainability managers. Blogs, newsletters, podcasts, social-media posts and columns (oh my God, the columns) add to the torrent of advice. It is hard for any would-be business guru to stand out in this ocean of effluent. That leaves a striking gap in the market—for a book on how to write a bestseller about leadership.

关于领导力、管理和职业生涯的瞎扯多到令人震惊。出版商纷纷推出新的商业书籍，有些很好，大多数不好。研究论文激增，其探讨了从新冠对英国牙科诊所领导能力的影响到可持续性的管理人员中精神病态的流行的一切问题。博客、新闻通讯、播客、社交媒体帖子和专栏(哦，天哪，专栏)都是建议的来源。任何想成为商业领袖的人都很难在这大片的污水中脱颖而出。这就为“如何写出一本关于领导力的畅销书”的书籍在市场上留下巨大的“缺口”。

A publication of this sort could start by noting that the most useful writing about business leadership focuses on people who run actual companies and take actual decisions. But usefulness is a terribly old-fashioned path to success. This how-to guide would quickly move on, and point to three other approaches that can help budding authors grab the attention of readers.

这类出版物可以在一开始就指出，最有用的关于商业领导力的文章都集中在真正经营公司、做出实际决策的人身上。但”有用”是一条过时的成功之路。关于如何指导是会很快地“迭代”的，然后再指出另外的“可以帮助刚崭露头角的作者抓住读者的注意力”的三种方法。

The first is striking the right note of unreality. Two things are likely to be true of people searching for leadership advice: they have not made it, and they would like a shortcut to success. These readers do not want to hear that the route to the top is a Darwinian struggle that takes place over many years and that demands highly unusual attributes. They are after something that can be bought on Amazon and delivered the next day. They definitely do not want to be told that, by definition, only a few can succeed. If you are working on a book called “Loser: Why You Are Doomed to Disappointment”, stop. (Actually, don’t.)

首先是正确地指出“不现实”。人们在寻求领导力建议时，可能会遇到两种情况: 一是他们还没有成功; 二是他们希望有一条通往成功的捷径。这些读者不希望听到这样的说法: 通往顶峰的道路是一场进化论式的斗争，要经历多年，还需要具备非常不寻常的特质。他们所追求的是那些可以在亚马逊上购买并在第二天送达的东西。他们肯定不想被告知，从定义上来说，只有少数人能成功。如果你正在写一本名为《失败者:为什么你注定要失望》的书，停下来吧。(事实上,没有。)

The job of the wannabe guru is to make their readers think that unimaginable success is within their reach. If only they believed in themselves a bit more or picked up a few new habits—waking up stupidly early, say, or keeping a journal—wealth will surely follow.

这些想成为大师的人的工作就是让他们的读者相信，他们可以取得难以想象的成功。只要他们再多相信自己一点，或者养成一些新习惯, 比如傻乎乎地早早起床，或者记日记，财富就会随之而来。

This, incidentally, is why management mavens should embrace numbered lists (the fact that this is a numbered list is purely coincidental). Research done in 2011 found that drawing up plans to achieve goals can reduce the cognitive stress caused by unfinished tasks. By the same token the illusion that a finite number of steps will unlock success is itself deeply comforting.

顺便说一句，这就是为什么管理专家应该使用编号列表的原因(这是一个编号列表的事实纯粹是巧合)。2011年的研究发现，为实现目标制定计划可以减少因未完成任务而引起的认知压力。同样，认为有限的步骤就能开启成功的幻想本身也令人深感欣慰。

The second bit of advice for a would-be leadership writer is to find uncontested ground. In the battle for attention, it can help to focus on something wholly unconnected to business and to argue that the subject has something to teach managers. That approach gives the aspiring guru a chance to write about a topic or person that will attract a wider readership. It also builds their reputation as someone who can connect dots even (perhaps especially) when there are no dots to join.

对于想要撰写领导力文章的人的第二条建议是，要找到没有争议的领域。在争夺“注意力”的战斗中，将注意力集中在与商业完全无关的事情上，并辩称这一主题可以教给管理者一些东西，会有所帮助。这种方式让有抱负的大师有机会写出能够吸引更广泛读者的话题或人物。这也为他们建立下了声誉，他们甚至可以在没有连接点的情况下(也许尤其如此)，将点连起来。

Some of these sources of leadership lessons are familiar: sports coaches and military commanders, Shackleton and Shakespeare, Trappist monks and Stoic philosophers. But authors limit themselves unnecessarily by narrowing the horizon to humans. An entire subgenre of internet posts offers leadership lessons from animals, for example. Keen to know how a giraffe would perform as CEO? So useless at managing projects that you are driven to wonder whether an elephant would do better? From inclusivity and lice to change management and dodos, only one thing mentioned in this paragraph is made up.

这些领导力课程的一些来源大家都很熟悉: 体育教练和军事指挥官、探险家Shackleton和莎士比亚、特拉普派僧侣和斯多葛派哲学家。但是作者把视野缩小到人类身上，这不必要地限制了自己。例如，互联网上有一个完整的次文类帖子提供了从动物身上学习领导力的课程。想知道长颈鹿当CEO会有什么表现吗? 在管理项目方面毫无用处，以至于你不得不怀疑大象是否会做得更好? 从包容性和虱子到变更管理和渡渡鸟，这一段提到的只有一件事是编造出来的。

Indeed, why draw the line at sentient beings? “Skin: Leadership Lessons from the World’s Largest Organ” is a book idea crying out for an author. Skin constantly renews itself, as a thriving company should. It has a purpose. It is flexible. Sure, it has zero self-awareness, but look around: that is not an obvious bar to corporate success.

的确，为什么要在众生之间划清界限呢?《皮肤:来自世界最大器官的领导经验》是一个急需作者的关于书籍的想法。皮肤不断自我更新，这是一个蓬勃发展的公司应该做的。它是有目的的。它是灵活的。当然，它没有自我意识，但环顾四周: 这并不是企业成功的明显障碍。

The third piece of advice is to pick the right title. Conveying a sense of urgency is vital: one-syllable words are the norm for a reason. A dollop of physicality—suggestiveness, even—can be helpful, perhaps because potential readers are so likely to be sagging behind a desk. And for all the emphasis on co-operation and purpose, it doesn’t hurt to embrace zero-sum words about winning, victory and coming first. A title like “Love Bomb: Be Kind and Crush Your Rivals” makes for a nice blend of emotional intelligence and pent-up violence.

第三条建议是选择正确的标题。传达一种紧迫感是至关重要的:单音节词成为常态是有原因的。少量的身体暗示，甚至是x暗示，可能是有帮助的，也许是因为潜在的读者很有可能瘫坐在桌子后面。尽管强调合作和目的，但接受“赢”、“胜”、“先来”的零和论调并无坏处。[这里理解可能不到位，有好的想法可以在评论区留言] 像《爱的炸弹:善待并击败你的对手》这样的标题很好地融合了情商和被压抑的暴力。

With the synopsis settled, all that remains is to unveil a name for this how-to-write-a-leadership-book book. “Bollocks: Three Ways to Write and Get Rich” will be in stores this autumn and is available for pre-order now.

剧情梗概确定后，剩下的就是为这本“如何写领导力”的书命名了。《胡扯: 写作致富的三种方法》将于今年秋天上市，现在可以预订了。

![image-20220219093740532](/Users/chenyujie/Library/Application Support/typora-user-images/image-20220219093740532.png)

By seeking to curb free speech, he will aggravate Canada’s divisions

通过寻求限制言论自由，他将加剧加拿大的分裂

Feb 19th 2022

CANADA’S REPUTATION for impeccable politeness is taking a knocking. In recent weeks crowds of lorry-drivers and other Canadians protesting against covid-19 restrictions have blocked public highways and camped outside parliament in Ottawa. Many wave placards reading “Fuck Trudeau”, referring to their youthful prime minister, though the expletive often appears with a maple leaf as an asterisk. Nastier messages have cropped up, too. One or two protesters carried flags emblazoned with swastikas, perhaps to suggest, absurdly, that Canada’s covid restrictions are akin to Nazism.

加拿大无可挑剔的有礼貌的名声正在受到打击。最近几周，大批卡车司机和其他加拿大人抗议新冠肺炎限制措施，他们堵塞了公共高速公路，并在渥太华议会外扎营。许多人挥舞的标语牌上写着“去他的特鲁多”，这指的是他们年轻的总理，尽管这些咒骂语经常以枫叶作为星号标志出现。更恶劣的信息也层出不穷。一两个抗议者举着印有纳粹标志的旗帜，这或许是在荒谬地暗示，加拿大对新冠病毒的限制类似于纳粹主义。

The freedom convoy, as the hundreds of lorry-drivers call themselves, was sparked by the introduction of a covid vaccine mandate in January. This requires all truckers who enter Canada from the United States, as thousands of Canadian drivers do every day, either to be jabbed or to endure a two-week quarantine.

数百名卡车司机自称为“自由车队”，这是由今年1月引入的一项新冠肺炎疫苗强制令引发的。这要求所有从美国进入加拿大的卡车司机（由于每天成千上万的加拿大司机这样做）要么接种疫苗，要么忍受为期两周的隔离。

Although most Canadians think such rules are reasonable, the protesters have struck a chord with some. A vocal minority are fed up with burdensome pandemic restrictions. Many of the young, who have suffered job losses because of lockdowns designed to protect their elders, are especially grumpy. The truckers have received both verbal and monetary support from abroad. Donald Trump, Fox News and a cacophony of populists praise them. Well-wishers have crowdfunded their cause.

尽管大多数加拿大人认为这样的规定是合理的，抗议者还是引起了一些人的共鸣。少数直言不讳的人受够了烦人的流行病限制。许多年轻人的脾气尤其暴躁，他们因为保护老年人的封城措施而失去了工作。卡车司机们从国外得到了口头上和金钱上的支持。特朗普、福克斯新闻和一群不和谐的民粹主义者都在称赞他们。表示祝愿的人们为他们的事业众筹。

Faced with this ruckus, Canada’s government should have drawn a clear distinction between harmful acts and obnoxious or foolish words. Peaceful protests are fine; blocking crucial highways so that others cannot go about their business is not. Some of the truckers shut down a bridge over which 25% of Canada’s goods trade with the United States passes. The police took six days to remove them. Given that the protest blocked an estimated $350m of trade each day, this was needlessly slow.

面对这样的骚动，加拿大政府应该在有害行为和令人讨厌或愚蠢的言论之间做出明确的区分。和平抗议很好; 而封锁重要的高速公路，让其他人无法继续他们的工作则不是。一些卡车司机阻断了一座桥梁，加拿大与美国25%的货物贸易都要经过这座桥梁。警察花了六天时间才把他们赶走。考虑到抗议活动导致每天约有3.5亿美元的贸易受阻，这一进程的缓慢是没有必要的。

The truckers are wrong about the vaccine mandate at the border. Such rules are a reasonable precaution to slow the spread of a deadly and highly infectious disease. Canada’s government is right to enforce them. But the truckers have every right to express their disagreement. A wise government would listen to them and respond politely, taking their complaints seriously and patiently explaining why covid restrictions, though onerous, are necessary for the time being.

卡车司机对于边境疫苗的强制令的理解是错误的。这些规定是一种合理的预防措施，可以减缓一种致命的、传染性很强的疾病的传播。加拿大政府的强制执行是正确的。但卡车司机完全有权利表达他们的意见。明智的政府应该礼貌地倾听和回应，认真对待他们的抱怨，耐心地解释为什么需要在短期内采取繁重的新冠肺炎限制措施。

Justin Trudeau has done the opposite. First, he refused to meet them. Then, seizing on the fact that a few of the protesters appear to be bigots, he attempted to put all of them outside the boundaries of reasonable debate by condemning “the anti-Semitism, Islamophobia, anti-black racism, homophobia and trans phobia that we’ve seen on display in Ottawa over the past number of days”. The police already have ample powers to quell disorder. Yet on February 14th Mr Trudeau invoked emergency powers under a 34-year-old law that had never been used before. It would allow the government to declare protests illegal and freeze the bank accounts of protesters without a court order.

特鲁多的做法正好相反。首先，他拒绝与他们见面。然后, 他抓住少数抗议者似乎是偏执狂的事实，试图通过谴责“过去几天我们在渥太华看到的反犹太主义、仇视伊斯兰教、反黑人种族歧视、仇同性恋、跨性别恐惧现象“把他们都排除在合理辩论的范围之外。警方已经有足够的权力平息骚乱。然而，在2月14日，特鲁多行使了一项有34年历史的法律赋予的紧急权力，这项法律以前从未使用过。它将允许政府宣布抗议活动为非法，并在没有法院命令的情况下冻结抗议者的银行账户。

Meanwhile, his Liberal government is mulling two worrying changes to Canada’s already illiberal hate-speech laws. One would allow Canada’s Human Rights Tribunal to impose large fines on those it deems to have used hateful language. It has in the past taken an expansive view of what counts as hateful, and defendants would enjoy fewer safeguards than they do under criminal law. The other proposed change would let individuals file legal complaints against people pre-emptively, if they fear that they may be about to say something hateful.

与此同时，他的自由党政府正在考虑对加拿大已经狭隘的仇恨言论法进行两项令人担忧的修改。一是允许加拿大人权法庭对那些它认为使用了仇恨语言的人处以巨额罚款。在过去，它对“仇恨”的界定采取了广泛的观点，被告将享受比刑法更少的保障。另一项提议将允许个人先发制人地对他人提起法律投诉，如果他们担心可能会说出一些仇恨的话。

These are both terrible ideas. The Economist has long argued that free speech should be restricted only under exceptional circumstances, such as when the speaker intends to incite physical violence. Canada’s laws are already more restrictive than this, and the country’s illiberal left would like them to be still more so. Academics have been suspended or disciplined for writing that Canada is “not racist” or for holding gender-critical views. The proposed amendments would give illiberal activists legal tools to harass conservative religious folk, traditional feminists and many more besides, simply for holding views that the left finds offensive. Worse, it would allow some to be gagged before they speak.

这两个想法都很糟糕。《经济学人》长期以来一直认为，只有在特殊情况下才应该限制言论自由，比如当演讲者打算煽动身体暴力时。加拿大的法律已经比这更严格了，这个国家狭隘的左派希望他们更加严格。一些学者曾因为在文章中声称加拿大“不是种族主义者”，或持有性别批评观点而被停学或受到纪律处分。拟议的修正案将为狭隘的活动人士提供法律工具，以恐吓保守的宗教人士、传统的女权主义者以及其他许多人，仅仅因为他们持有左翼认为冒犯的观点。更糟糕的是，这将允许一些人在说话之前就受到言论钳制。

Canada is not yet a rancorous or bitterly divided society. If Mr Trudeau wants to keep it that way, he should stop trying to police Canadians’ thoughts. ■

加拿大还不是一个充满仇恨或严重分裂的社会。如果特鲁多想保持现状，他就应该停止控制加拿大人的想法。■

Whether he invades Ukraine or backs down, Putin has harmed Russia

不管他是入侵乌克兰还是选择放弃，普京都伤害了俄罗斯

Russia and Ukraine

俄罗斯和乌克兰

![image-20220218111833440](/Users/chenyujie/Library/Application Support/typora-user-images/image-20220218111833440.png)

He will try to claim victory though

不过，他将试图宣告胜利

Feb 19th 2022

THE NEWS, for a moment, seemed encouraging. In a stage-managed television appearance on February 14th Vladimir Putin grunted a terse “good” to the proposal of his foreign minister that, despite warnings by the West of an imminent invasion of Ukraine, diplomacy should continue. A day later Russia’s defence ministry said that some of the 180,000 or so troops it has deployed at its borders with Ukraine are to be withdrawn to barracks, having completed their military exercises which, it has always maintained, is why they were there in the first place.

这一消息一时间似乎令人鼓舞。2月14日，在一个精心策划的电视节目中，普京对其外交部长的提议咕哝了一句简短的“好”，该提议称，尽管西方国家对于迫在眉睫的入侵乌克兰发出了警告，外交工作仍应继续。一天后，俄罗斯国防部表示，部署在乌克兰边境的大约18万名士兵中的一部分将撤回军营，他们已经完成了军事演习，俄罗斯一直坚称，这就是他们最初在那里的原因。

Officials, and the markets, breathed a small sigh of relief. Alas, open-source intelligence soon showed that, although a few units were moving, many more were preparing to fight. With the candour that has wrong-footed Mr Putin, many Western security officials accused him of lying, redoubling their warnings of a looming Russian invasion. Even if the troops pull back, this crisis is not yet over. And, whatever happens, war or no war, Mr Putin has damaged his country by engineering it.

官员和市场都松了一口气。唉，开源情报很快就显示，虽然有几个小队在移动，但更多的小队在准备战斗。许多西方安全官员指责他撒谎，并加倍警告俄罗斯即将入侵，这样的直言不讳让普京措手不及。即使军队撤退，这场危机也没有结束。而且，不管发生什么，不管有没有战争，普京已经通过策划（战争）破坏了他的国家。

Plenty of Western observers would dispute that judgment. Without firing a shot, they point out, Mr Putin has made himself the centre of global attention, proving that Russia matters once more. He has destabilised Ukraine and impressed on everyone that its future is his business. He may yet win concessions from NATO for avoiding war. And at home he has underlined his statesmanship and distracted from economic hardship and the repression of opposition figures such as Alexei Navalny, who was this week once again hauled before a judge.

许多西方观察人士会质疑这一判断。他们指出，普京不费一枪一弹就使自己成为全球关注的中心，再次证明俄罗斯举足轻重。他破坏了乌克兰的稳定，并让每个人都明白乌克兰的未来是他的事。他可能会在避免战争方面赢得北约的让步。在国内，他强调了自己的政治才能，并把注意力从经济困难和对反对派人物（如Alexei Navalny）的镇压上转移开。本周，Alexei Navalny再次被带到法官面前。

Yet these gains are tactical. Even as Mr Putin has won them, in a longer-lasting and more strategic sense he has lost ground.

然而，这些收获都是战术上的。尽管普京有了这些收获，但从更长远、更具有战略意义的角度来看，他已经失去了优势。

For one thing, although all eyes are on Mr Putin, he has galvanised his opponents. Led by Joe Biden, who once called Mr Putin “a killer” and surely loathes the man who tried to deny him the presidency, the West has agreed on a tougher package of threatened sanctions than in 2014, when Russia annexed Crimea. NATO, dismissed in 2019 by the French president as suffering “brain death”, has found renewed purpose in protecting its Russia-facing flanks. Having always preferred to keep their distance, Sweden and Finland may even join the alliance. Germany, having unwisely backed the new Nord Stream 2 pipeline, has accepted that Russian gas is a liability it must deal with and that an invasion would kill off the project. If Mr Putin imagined that his threats would be met with Western mush, he has been disabused.

一方面，尽管所有的目光都集中在普京身上，但他却激励了他的对手。在乔·拜登的领导下，西方同意采取比2014年俄罗斯吞并克里米亚时更严厉的一揽子制裁措施，拜登曾称普京为“杀手”，当然也厌恶这个试图剥夺他总统职位的人。2019年，被法国总统以“脑死亡”为由抛弃的北约，重新找到了“保护其对俄侧翼的“目标。瑞典和芬兰曾一直以来都倾向于保持距离，它们甚至有可能加入联盟。德国曾不明智地支持了新的北溪2号管道项目，现在它已经承认，俄罗斯的天然气是它必须处理的一个责任，入侵将会扼杀这个项目。如果普京料想他的威胁会遭到西方的打击，他已经醒悟了。

Ukraine has indeed suffered. But the crisis has also affirmed the popular sense among Ukrainians that their destiny lies with the West. True, Mr Putin has extracted assurances that Ukraine is not about to join NATO—but these were cheap, because membership was always remote. What matters more is that, having been neglected in recent years, Ukraine is enjoying the West’s unprecedented diplomatic and military support. Those bonds, forged in crisis, will not suddenly dissolve if Russian forces pull back. Again, it is the opposite of what Mr Putin wanted.

乌克兰确实遭受了损失。但这场危机也证实了乌克兰人的普遍意识，即他们的命运掌握在西方手中。诚然，普京已经得到了乌克兰不会加入北约的保证，但这些保证都是廉价的，因为成员国资格总是遥不可及的。更重要的是，近年来被忽视的乌克兰正享受着西方前所未有的外交和军事支持。这些在危机中建立起来的纽带，不会在俄罗斯撤军后突然消失。这再次与普京想要的背道而驰。

It is also true that Mr Putin has put Europe’s security on the agenda, including discussions over missiles and military exercises. But such talks would be in everyone’s interest, because they reduce the danger of conflict. If win-win negotiations count as victories for Mr Putin, let there be more of them.

普京确实将欧洲安全问题提上了议程，包括讨论导弹和军事演习。但这样的对话将符合所有人的利益，因为它们减少了冲突的危险。如果双赢的谈判对普京来说是胜利，那就让这样的谈判更多些吧。

Mr Putin’s most intriguing loss is at home. Russia has attempted to build a fortress economy. It has boosted its reserves and reduced the share of them held in dollars. It has curtailed firms’ dependency on foreign capital and worked hard to build up its “tech stack” (everything from chips to apps to the network itself). It has also cosied up to China in the hope of finding an alternative buyer for the hydrocarbons that are still its principal source of foreign exchange.

普京最引人注目的损失是在国内。俄罗斯试图建立一个堡垒经济。它增加了外汇储备，并减少了美元储备的比例。它减少了公司对外国资本的依赖，并努力建立自己的“技术栈”(从芯片到应用程序再到网络本身的所有东西)。它还向中国示好，希望为仍然是其主要外汇来源的碳氢化合物找到一个另一个买家。

Although these actions have lessened the potential harm from Western sanctions, they have not eliminated it. The EU still takes 27% of all Russian exports; China about half that. The Power of Siberia gas pipeline that runs towards China will, when completed in 2025, carry only a fifth of what now goes to Europe. In the event of a serious conflict, sanctions through the SWIFT banking-transactions network or on big Russian banks would cut off the financial system. HW-style import restrictions would cause huge difficulties for Russia’s tech firms.

尽管这些行动减轻了西方制裁的潜在危害，但并没有消除这种危害。欧盟仍占俄罗斯出口总额的27%; 中国大约是一半。通往中国的“西伯利亚力量”天然气管道将于2025年完工，届时输送的天然气仅为目前输送欧洲天然气的五分之一。一旦发生严重冲突，通过SWIFT银行交易网络或对俄罗斯大银行的制裁将切断金融系统。HW式的进口限制将给俄罗斯的科技公司带来巨大的困难。

Mr Putin can either live with this interdependency or turn further towards XX. Yet that would condemn Russia to being the junior partner of an unsentimental regime which sees it as a diplomatic sidekick and a backward source of cheap commodities. That is a yoke Mr Putin would chafe under.

普京要么接受这种相互依赖，要么进一步转向XX。然而，这将使俄罗斯成为一个冷酷的政权的次要伙伴，该政权视俄罗斯为外交伙伴以及廉价商品的落后来源。这是一种让普京恼怒的枷锁。

This alliance of autocrats would also have a psychological cost inside Russia. It would demonstrate Mr Putin’s dependence on the siloviki, the security bosses who see in Ukraine’s democracy and deepening ties with the West a threat to their own ability to control and loot Russia. It would be a further sign to the liberal capitalists and technocrats who are the other pillar of the Russian state that they had lost. More of the best and brightest would leave; others would give up. Stagnation and resentment would build into opposition likely to be met with heightened brutality.

这种独裁者联盟也会给俄罗斯国内带来心理上的代价。这将表明普京对“强力集团”的依赖。“强力集团”是俄罗斯的安全老大，他们认为乌克兰的民主和与西方加深的关系对他们控制和掠夺俄罗斯的能力构成了威胁。对于俄罗斯国家的另一个支柱，即自由资本家和技术官僚来说，这将是一个进一步的信号，其表明他们已经失去了。更多最优秀和最聪明的人将离开; 其他人则会放弃。停滞和怨恨会形成反抗，可能会遭遇更大的暴行。

And what if Mr Putin, mindful of all this, were to invade? That may yet be the terrible outcome of this crisis, as each side seeks to outmanoeuvre the other. Just this week the Russian State Duma urged Mr Putin to recognise the self-declared “republics” in the Donbas, which claim big chunks of Ukrainian territory they do not currently control—adding one more trigger that Mr Putin can pull whenever he chooses.

如果意识到这一切的普京还入侵呢? 这可能是这场危机的可怕后果，因为双方都试图以计谋胜过对方。就在本周，俄罗斯国家杜马(下议院)敦促普京承认顿巴斯自行宣布成立的“共和国”，声称拥有乌克兰大片领土，但他们目前并不控制这些领土，这又增加了一个导火线，普京可以在任何时候随心所欲地扣动扳机。

IN 2013 THE EU launched an ambitious project. The aim was to double the share of microchips made in Europe to 20% of the global total by 2020. Nearly a decade later it remains stubbornly stuck at 10%. If that were not bad enough, Europe no longer makes any of the most advanced chips of the sort that go into data centres or smartphones (see chart). So, prompted by shortages of semiconductors and their growing importance for all sorts of industries, the bloc is having another go.

2013年，欧盟启动了一个雄心勃勃的项目。其目标是，到2020年，将欧洲制造的微芯片份额提高一倍，达到全球总量的20%。近十年后，这一比例仍顽固地停留在10%。如果这还不够糟糕的话，欧洲已经不再生产任何用于数据中心或智能手机的最先进的芯片了(见图表)。因此，在半导体短缺及其对各种行业日益重要的推动下，欧盟正在进行另一次尝试。

Judged by numbers alone, the EU’s new Chips Act, unveiled on February 8th, could move the needle. It is meant to generate public and private investment of more than €43bn ($49bn), about as much as a similar package working its way through America’s Congress. More than two-thirds of this money is supposed to take the form of state subsidies for new leading-edge chip-fabrication plants, or “mega fabs”—thanks to a more generous interpretation of EU restrictions on state aid. The rest will go to other chipmaking infrastructure.

仅从数据来看，2月8日公布的欧盟新《芯片法案》可能会起到明显作用。该计划旨在投入超过430亿欧元(合490亿美元)的公共和私人投资，大约相当于美国国会正在审议的一项类似的一揽子计划。这笔钱的三分之二以上应该以国家补贴的形式提供给新的尖端芯片制造厂，或者“巨型晶圆厂”，这要感谢欧盟对国家援助限制的更慷慨的解释。其余部分将用于其它芯片制造基础设施。

Reality is likely to prove trickier. To understand why, it helps to see the semiconductor industry not just as a collection of huge fabs, of which the most sophisticated can cost more than $20bn a pop, but as a global ecosystem of thousands of companies. Even more than in other high-tech industries, research and development (R&D) usually takes years and costs billions. New chips are designed by specialised firms using complex software made by other companies still. And after chips leave a fab, contract manufacturers assemble, test and package them (ATP, in the lingo).

事实可能会更加棘手。要理解其中的原因，不妨将半导体行业视为一个由数千家公司组成的全球生态系统，而不仅仅是一个巨型晶圆厂的集合(其中最复杂的晶圆厂每台成本可能超过200亿美元)。与其他高科技行业相比，研发通常需要数年时间，花费数十亿美元。新的芯片是由专业公司使用其他公司生产的复杂软件来设计的。芯片离开晶圆厂后，合同制造商将其组装、测试和包装(行话来讲是ATP)。

![image-20220217101732053](/Users/chenyujie/Library/Application Support/typora-user-images/image-20220217101732053.png)

Seen through this ecosystemic lens, the EU’s position is both stronger and weaker than its small share of global chip output might suggest. Start with the strengths. The continent maintains a leading position in semiconductor R&D. One of the industry’s main brain trusts, the Interuniversity Microelectronics Centre (better known as IMEC), is based in Belgium.

从生态系统的角度来看，欧盟的地位比它在全球芯片产量中所占的小份额所显示的更强也更弱。先从优势说起。欧洲大陆在半导体研发方面保持着领先地位。该行业的主要智囊团之一，即比利时微电子研究中心(IMEC)位于比利时。

Europe’s firms also produce many of the machines that make fabs tick. ASML, a Dutch firm with a market value of €230bn, is the sole global supplier of the lithographic equipment without which fabs cannot etch the most advanced processors. Only Nvidia, an American chip-designer, and Taiwan Semiconductor Manufacturing Company (TSMC), the world’s biggest contract manufacturer of chips, are worth more. An array of smaller European outfits enjoy dominant positions in the complex chipmaking supply chain. Carl Zeiss SMT makes lenses for ASML’s lithography machines (and is co-owned by it). Siltronic manufactures silicon wafers onto which chips are etched. Aixtron manufactures specialised gear to deposit layers of chemicals onto those wafers to make circuits.

欧洲公司也生产许多制造晶圆厂的机器。市值2300亿欧元的荷兰公司ASML是光刻机全球唯一供应商，没有这种设备，晶圆厂就无法蚀刻最先进的处理器。只有美国芯片设计公司英伟达和全球最大的芯片合同制造商台积电的价值更高。一系列规模较小的欧洲公司在复杂的芯片制造供应链中占据主导地位。Carl Zeiss SMT为ASML的光刻机生产透镜(并由其共同拥有)。Siltronic制造用于蚀刻芯片的硅片。Aixtron生产特殊的设备，在晶圆上沉积化学物质层来制造电路。

Once you widen the aperture to the whole ecosystem, Europe’s biggest chipmakers, Infineon, NXP and STMicroelectronics, also appear less benighted. Yes, half of the continent’s capacity is for chips with structures (“nodes”) measuring 180 nanometres (billionths of a metre) or more, generations behind the technological cutting edge, dominated by TSMC and Samsung of South Korea, whose transistors come in at a few nanometres. But those nano-electronics are most useful for consumer devices, the bulk of which are assembled in Asia. By contrast, the larger European nodes are sufficient for the continent’s many industrial firms that require specialised silicon for things such as cars, machine tools and sensors. “European chipmakers focus on their customer base,” explains Jan-Peter Kleinhans of SNV, a German think-tank.

一旦你把视野扩大到整个生态系统，欧洲最大的芯片制造商Infineon、NXP和STMicroelectronics也显得不那么愚昧了。是的，欧洲大陆一半的产能是用于结构(“节点”)为180纳米(十亿分之一米)或更大的芯片，这些芯片落后于技术前沿一代，技术前沿则由台积电和韩国三星主导，它们的晶体管只有几个纳米。但这些纳米电子产品最适用于消费电子设备，其中大部分是在亚洲组装的。相比之下，更大的欧洲节点足以满足欧洲大陆的许多工业企业的需求，这些企业需要专用的硅来生产汽车、机床和传感器等产品。德国智囊团SNV的Jan-Peter Kleinhans解释称：“欧洲芯片制造商专注于他们的客户群”。

If the Chips Act is a guide, European policymakers worry that these genuine strengths are not enough to offset the EU’s weaknesses. Besides lacking cutting-edge fabs, Europe is short of companies with the know-how to design the smallest chips, such as Nvidia. It is similarly behind in ATP, where most capacity is in China and Taiwan. Once approved by member states and the European Parliament, the EU law is meant to help Europe catch up. Besides the €30bn or so for mega-fabs, it has pencilled in €11bn for things like a virtual chip-design platform open to all comers and other infrastructure, including pilot production lines for leading-edge chips. But half of that is to come from member states and the private sector. The EU’s contribution of less than €6bn will, as with the bloc’s other programmes, come with many bureaucratic strings attached.

如果《芯片法案》是一个指南，欧洲的政策制定者担心这些真正的优势不足以抵消欧盟的弱点。除了缺乏尖端晶圆厂，欧洲还缺乏像英伟达这样拥有设计最小芯片技术的公司。ATP同样也处于落后状态，其产能主要集中在中国大陆和中国台湾。一旦得到成员国和欧洲议会的批准，欧盟法律将帮助欧洲迎头赶上。除了用于大型晶圆厂的300亿欧元左右的投资外，其还计划投资110亿欧元，用于面向所有人的虚拟芯片设计平台以及其他基础设施，其中包括尖端芯片的试点生产线。不过其中一半将来自成员国和私营部门。与欧盟的其他计划一样，欧盟不到60亿欧元的注资将附带许多官僚条件。

A bigger problem is the act’s focus on luring giant chipmakers to build mega-fabs. TSMC and Intel, its American rival, have signalled they would consider Europe only if governments shoulder a big part of the costs (40% in Intel’s case). To enable such deals, the first of which is expected in weeks, the European Commission wants to relax state-aid rules to let member states subsidise such fabs “up to 100% of a proven funding gap” if they are “first-of-a-kind” or would “otherwise not exist in Europe”.

更大的问题是，该法案专注于吸引大型芯片制造商建立巨型晶圆厂。台积电和它的美国竞争对手英特尔表示，只有政府承担大部分成本(英特尔为40%)，他们才会考虑欧洲。为了达成这样的协议(第一个协议预计将在几周内达成)，欧盟委员会希望放松国家援助规定，允许成员国补贴“高达100%的已证实的资金缺口”的此类晶圆厂，前提是它们是“同类第一”或“在欧洲不存在”。

If such criteria were meant to avert a subsidy race, they look copious and fuzzy enough for countries to try to game them. Worse, the resulting fabs may end up underused. By the time they are ready in a few years, the chip shortage may have turned into a glut. And if the EU’s efforts to boost Europe’s chip-design firms fail, European fabs would have to rely on foreign chip-designers for custom. Why, asks Mr Kleinhans, would American firms choose to have their chips manufactured in Europe rather than in Asia or at home?

如果这样的标准是为了避免补贴竞争，那么这些标准看起来既详尽又模糊，足以让各国试图利用它们。更糟糕的是，由此产生的晶圆厂可能最终未得到充分利用。等到几年后它们准备好时，芯片的短缺可能已经变成了过剩的状态。如果欧盟振兴欧洲芯片设计公司的努力失败，欧洲的晶圆厂将不得不依赖外国芯片设计师定制。Kleinhans先生问道，为什么美国公司会选择在欧洲而不是在亚洲或国内制造芯片?

Thierry Breton, the EU commissioner in charge of industrial policy, envisions a Europe of mega-fabs that not only serve the continent’s own demand, but world markets. Europe may be better off propping up its chip ecosystem by investing in things like basic research. Mr Breton doesn’t need to pick Europe’s chipmaking winners. As the EU’s semiconductor stars show, the market can do that just fine. ■

欧盟负责工业政策的委员Thierry Breton设想，欧洲的巨型晶圆厂不仅能满足欧洲大陆自身的需求，还能满足世界市场的需求。欧洲通过投资基础研究等方面来更好地支撑其芯片生态系统。Breton先生不需要去挑选欧洲芯片制造业的赢家。正如欧盟的半导体明星所显示的那样，市场完全可以做到这一点。■

在过去的两年中，伦敦人对“警察对待每个人是公平的”的信念已经减弱，这可能是由于乔治·弗洛伊德在美国的谋杀案以及Sarah Everard的谋杀案(见图表)。但这只是“信心问题”日益严重的一个方面。在过去的五年里，伦敦人越来越不相信警察能在当地做得很好。对他们的处理感到满意的犯罪受害者比例也有所下降。

It also insisted that a full report into the parties by Sue Gray, a civil servant, not be published until its inquiries were over. MPs complained of a “stitch-up” and said the Met was “usurping [Parliament’s] position”. But its inquiry has also shielded the force itself. The government cannot meddle with the coppers who are investigating it.

伦敦警察厅的政治冒险进一步败坏了它的声誉。在新冠肺炎疫情封锁期间，其拒绝调查有关唐宁街内部及其周围的派对报告，但在今年1月，它突然宣布改变方针。它还坚持要求在它的调查结束之前，不要公布一份由英国高级别文官Sue Gray调查的关于派对的完整报告。议员们抱怨这是一种“诬陷”，并表示伦敦警察厅正在“篡夺(议会的)地位”。但它的调查也保护了警队本身。政府不能干涉正在调查此事的警察。

It is not certain who can push the Met around. Dame Cressida answers to both the mayor of London and the home secretary. “It’s not clear who the dominant boss is,” says Tim Newburn, a police-watcher at the London School of Economics. Both politicians—one Labour, the other Conservative—have let it be known that they despair of the force. Neither seems able, or willing, to do much to fix it. ■

谁能对伦敦警察厅发号施令还不确定。Cressida夫人对伦敦市长和内政大臣都要负责。伦敦经济学院的警察观察员Tim Newburn说:“现在还不清楚谁才是老大。” 工党和保守党的两个政治家都公开表示他们对这支警察部队感到绝望。它似乎既没有能力，也不愿意做太多事情来解决这个问题。■

New robots—smarter and faster—are taking over warehouses

更智能、更快的新型机器人正在接管仓库

The bots in the warehouse

仓库里的机器人

![image-20220215115108365](/Users/chenyujie/Library/Application Support/typora-user-images/image-20220215115108365.png)

Most picking jobs will be done by bots

大多数挑选工作将由机器人完成

Feb 12th 2022

文章音频

A DECADE AGO Amazon started to introduce robots into its “fulfilment centres”, as online retailers call their giant distribution warehouses. Instead of having people wandering up and down rows of shelves picking goods to complete orders, the machines would lift and then carry the shelves to the pickers. That saved time and money. Amazon’s sites now have more than 350,000 robots of various sorts deployed worldwide. But even that is not enough to secure its future.

十年前，亚马逊(Amazon)开始在其“物流中心”(在线零售商将其称为巨大的配送仓库)引入机器人。机器人让人们不再需要在成排的货架上来回走动去挑选商品来完成订单，机器会抬起货架，然后把货架送到捡拾者那里。这节省了时间和金钱。亚马逊网站现在在世界各地部署了超过35万个各种各样的机器人。但即便如此，也不足以保障其未来。

Advances in warehouse robotics, coupled with increasing labour costs and difficulty in finding workers, have created a watershed moment in the logistics industry. With covid-19 lockdowns causing supply-chain disruptions and a boom in home deliveries that is likely to endure, fulfilment centres have been working at full tilt.

仓库机器人技术的进步，加上劳动力成本的上升以及寻找工人难度的上升，已经在物流行业创造了一个分水岭时刻。由于新冠病毒的封锁导致供应链中断，而家庭配送的激增可能会持续下去，物流配送中心一直在全力工作。

Despite the robots, many firms have to bring in temporary workers to cope with increased demand during busy periods. Competition for staff is fierce. In the run-up to the holiday shopping season in December, Amazon brought in some 150,000 extra workers in America alone, offering sign-on bonuses of up to $3,000.

尽管有了机器人，许多公司还是不得不在繁忙时期引入临时工来应对不断增长的需求。竞争员工非常激烈。在12月的假日购物季前夕，亚马逊仅在美国就新增了15万名员工，其签约奖金高达3000美元。

The long-term implications of such a high reliance on increasingly hard-to-find labour in distribution is clear, according to a new study by McKinsey, a consultancy: “Automation in warehousing is no longer just nice to have but an imperative for sustainable growth.”

根据咨询公司麦肯锡的一项新研究，配送行业如此高度依赖日益难寻的劳动力，其长期影响是显而易见的:“仓储自动化不再只是一件好事，而是可持续增长的必要条件。”

This means more robots are needed, including newer, more efficient versions to replace those already at work and advanced machines to take over most of the remaining jobs done by humans. As a result, McKinsey forecasts the warehouse-automation market will grow at a compound annual rate of 23% to be worth more than $50bn by 2030.

这意味着需要更多的机器人，包括更新、更高效的版本来取代已经在工作的机器人，以及先进的机器来接管大部分剩余的由人类完成的工作。因此，麦肯锡预测，仓储自动化市场将以每年23%的复合年增长率增长，到2030年，市场价值将超过500亿美元。

The new robots are coming. One of them is the prototype 600 Series bot. This machine “changes everything” according to Tim Steiner, chief executive of Ocado Group, which began in 2002 as an online British grocer and has evolved over the years into one of the leading providers of warehouse robotics.

新的机器人正在出现。其中之一就是600系列机器人原型。Ocado集团首席执行官Tim Steiner表示，这台机器“改变了一切”。Ocado集团成立于2002年，最初是一家英国在线杂货商，多年来已发展成为仓储机器人的领先供应商之一。

The 600 Series is a strange-looking beast, much like a box on wheels made out of skeletal parts. That is because more than half its components are 3D-printed. As 3D-printing builds things up layer by layer it allows the shapes to be optimised, thus using the least amount of material. As a result, the 600 Series is five times lighter than the company’s present generation of bots, which makes it more agile and less demanding on battery power.

600系列像是一个奇怪的“野兽”，很像一个由骨骼部件制成的有轮子的盒子。这是因为它的一半以上的组件都是3D打印的。由于3D打印是一层一层地构建东西，它允许优化形状，从而使用最少的材料。因此，600系列比该公司目前这一代机器人轻五倍，这使得它更灵活，对电池功率的要求也更低。

March of the machines

Ocado’s bots work in what is known as the “Hive”, a giant metallic grid at the centre of its fulfilment centres. Some of these Hives are bigger than a football pitch.

Ocado的机器人工作在被称为“蜂巢”的地方，这是一个巨大的金属网格，位于其物流中心的中心。有些蜂巢比足球场还大。

Each cell on the grid contains products stored in plastic crates, stacked 21 deep. As orders arrive, a bot is dispatched to extract a crate and transport it to a picking station, where a human worker takes all the items they need, scans each one and puts them into a bag, much as happens at a supermarket checkout.

网格上的每个单元都有储存在塑料木箱里的产品，这些塑料木箱深度有21个。当订单到达时，机器人会被派遣去提取一个木箱，并将其运送到一个拣货站，在那里，一名人工工人将他们需要的所有物品取出，扫描每一件物品，然后将它们放入一个袋子，就像在超市结账时发生的那样。

It could take an hour or so walking around a warehouse to collect each item manually for a large order. But as hundreds of bots operate on the grid simultaneously, they are much faster. The bots are choreographed by an artificially intelligent computer system, which communicates with each machine over a wireless network. The system allows Ocado’s current bot, the 500 Series, to gather all the goods required for a 50-item order in less than five minutes.

如果是大订单，人工收集每一件物品可能需要一个小时左右的时间。但由于数以百计的机器人同时在网格上工作，它们的速度要快得多。这些机器人由一个人工智能计算机系统安排，通过无线网络与每台机器进行通信。该系统允许Ocado目前的500系列机器人在不到5分钟的时间内收集一个包含50个商品的订单所需的所有货物。

The new 600 Series will match or better its predecessor’s performance and use less energy. It also “unlocks a cascade of benefits”, says Mr Steiner, allowing Hives to be smaller and lighter. This means they can be installed in weeks rather than months and at a lower cost. That will make “micro” fulfilment centres viable. Most fulfilment centres are housed in large buildings on out-of-town trading estates, but smaller units could be sited in urban areas closer to customers. This would speed up deliveries, in some cases to within hours.

新的600系列将与其前身500系列匹敌，或者性能会更好，而且使用的能源也少。Steiner先生说，这也“释放了一系列的好处”，使蜂巢变得更小更轻。这意味着它们可以在几周内安装，而不是几个月，而且成本更低。这将使“微型”物流中心得以生存。大多数物流配送中心都建在郊区的大型建筑中，但较小的物流配送中心可以建在离客户更近的城市区域。这将加快配送速度，在某些情况下，可以在数小时内完成。

Amazon is also developing more-efficient robots. Its original machines were known as Kivas, after Kiva Systems, the Massachusetts-based firm that manufactured them. The Kiva is a squat device which can slip under a stack of head-height shelves in which goods are stored. The robot then lifts and carries the shelves to a picking station. In 2012 Amazon bought Kiva Systems for $775m and later changed its name to Amazon Robotics.

亚马逊也在开发效率更高的机器人。它最初的机器被称为Kivas，以生产这些机器的马萨诸塞州公司Kiva Systems命名。Kiva是一种蹲着的设备(类似扫地机器人)，它可以滑到一堆与人一样高的放着货物的架子下面。然后，机器人抬起货架，把货架运到一个拣货站。2012年，亚马逊以7.75亿美元收购了Kiva Systems，随后将其更名为亚马逊机器人(Amazon Robotics)。

Welcome to the jungle

Amazon Robotics has since developed a family of bots, including a smaller version of a Kiva called Pegasus. These will allow it to pack more goods into its fulfilment centres and also use bots in smaller inner-city distribution sites. To prepare for a more automated future, Amazon Robotics recently opened a new robot manufacturing plant in Westborough, Massachusetts, to boost its output.

亚马逊机器人公司后来开发了一系列机器人，包括一种名为Pegasus的Kiva小版本机器人。这将使它能够将更多的货物装入其物流中心，并在更小的市中心配送点使用机器人。为了迎接更加自动化的未来，亚马逊机器人公司最近在马萨诸塞州的Westborough开设了一家新的机器人制造厂，以提高其产量。

In 2014, when it became clear that future Kivas would be made exclusively for Amazon, Romain Moulin and Renaud Heitz, a pair of engineers working for a medical firm, decided to set up Exotec, a French rival, to produce a different sort of robotic warehouse. The firm has developed a three-dimensional system, which uses bots called Skypods. Looking a bit like Kivas, they also roam the warehouse floor. But instead of moving shelves, Skypods climb them. Once the robot reaches the necessary level, it extracts a crate, climbs down and delivers it to a picking station.

2014年，当明确Kivas未来将只为亚马逊生产时，Romain Moulin和Renaud Heitz，这对为一家医疗公司工作的工程师，决定成立Exotec(一家法国竞争对手)来生产一个不同种类的机器人仓库。该公司已经开发了一种三维系统，使用名为Skypods的机器人。它们看起来有点像Kivas，也在仓库地板上扫视。但Skypods不是移动货架，而是爬上货架。一旦机器人达到了必要的高度，它就会取出一个木箱，拿下来再把它送到一个拣货站。

Skypods, says Mr Moulin, maximise the space in a warehouse because they can ascend shelving stacked 12 metres high. Being modular, the system can be expanded easily. As well as returning crates to the shelves, Skypods also take them to places to be refilled.

Moulin先生说，Skypods可以将仓库的空间最大化，因为他们可以将架子堆叠到12米高。系统采用模块化设计，易于扩展。除了把木箱放回货架外，Skypods还可以把它们带到需要重新装满的地方。

A number of retailers have started using Skypods, including Carrefour, a giant French supermarket group, GAP, an American clothing firm, and Uniqlo, a Japanese one. Because such robots move quickly and could cause injury—Skypods zoom along at four metres per second (14kph)—they tend to operate in closed areas. If Amazon’s staff need to enter the robot area they don a special safety vest. This contains electronics which signal to any nearby bots that a human is present. The bot will then stop or take an alternative route.

许多零售商已经开始使用Skypods，包括法国大型超市集团家乐福、美国服装公司GAP和日本的优衣库。由于这种机器人移动迅速，可能会造成伤害，Skypods以每秒4米(14公里/小时)的速度移动，它们往往在封闭的区域操作。如果亚马逊的员工需要进入机器人区域，他们会穿上一件特殊的安全背心。它包含的电子设备可以向附近的任何机器人发出有人在的信号。然后机器人将停止或采取另一条路线。

Some robots, however, are designed to work alongside people in warehouses. They often ferry things between people taking goods off shelves and pallets to people putting them into bags and boxes for shipping. Such systems can avoid the cost of installing fixed infrastructure, which lets warehouses be reconfigured quickly—useful for logistics centres that work for multiple retailers and have to deal with constantly changing product lines.

然而，有些机器人被设计用来在仓库里与人一起工作。它们经常在把货物从货架上拿下来的人们之间运送，并将托盘给那些打包的人们，再装箱运送。这样的系统可以避免安装固定基础设施的成本，这使得仓库可以快速重新配置，这对为多家零售商工作的物流中心非常有用，而且必须处理不断变化的产品线。

When robots work among people, however, they have to be fitted with additional safety systems, such as cameras, radar and other sensors, to avoid bumping into staff. Hence they tend to move slowly and are cautious, which can result in bots frequently coming to a standstill and slowing operations. However, machines that are more capable and aware of their surroundings are on the way.

然而，当机器人在人类之间工作时，它们必须安装额外的安全系统，如摄像头、雷达和其他传感器，以避免撞上工作人员。因此，他们往往行动缓慢和谨慎，这可能导致机器人经常陷入停顿，减慢操作。然而，更有能力、更能感知周围环境的机器即将问世。

For instance, NEC, a Japanese electronics group, has started using “risk-sensitive stochastic control technology”, which is software similar to that used in finance to avoid high-risk investments. In this case, though, it allows a robot to weigh up risks when taking any action, such as selecting the safest and fastest route through a warehouse. In trials, NEC says it doubles the average speed of a robot without making compromises on safety.

例如，日本电子集团NEC已开始使用“风险敏感随机控制技术”，这是一种类似于金融领域用来避免高风险投资的软件。不过，在这种情况下，它可以让机器人在采取任何行动时权衡风险，比如选择通过仓库的最安全、最快的路线。NEC表示，在试验中，它在不牺牲安全的情况下，将机器人的平均速度提高了一倍。

New tricks

The toughest job to automate in a warehouse is picking and packing, hence the demand for extra pairs of hands during busy periods. This task is far from easy for robots because fulfilment centres stock tens of thousands of different items, in many shapes, sizes and weights.

仓库中最难自动化的工作是分拣和打包，因此在繁忙时期需要额外的人手。对于机器人来说，这项任务远非易事，因为物流中心储存着成千上万种不同形状、大小和重量的物品。

Nevertheless, Amazon, Ocado, Exotec and others are beginning to automate the task by placing robotic arms at some picking stations. These arms tend to use cameras and read barcodes to identify goods, and suction pads and other mechanisms to pick them up. Machine learning, a form of AI, is employed to teach the robots how to handle specific items, for example not to put potatoes on top of eggs.

尽管如此，亚马逊、Ocado、Exotec和其他公司已经开始在一些拣货站放置机械臂来自动化这项任务。这些手臂倾向于使用相机和读取条形码来识别商品，以及使用吸盘和其他机械装置来捡起它们。机器学习是人工智能的一种形式，被用来教机器人如何处理特定的物品，例如不要把土豆放在鸡蛋上面。

Ocado is also developing an arm which could bypass a picking station and take items directly from crates in the Hive. Fetch Robotics, a Silicon Valley producer of logistics robots that was acquired last year by Zebra Technologies, a computing firm, has developed a mobile picking arm which can travel around a fulfilment centre.

Ocado还在开发一种可以绕过分拣站直接从蜂巢的木箱中取物品的手臂。Fetch Robotics是一家位于硅谷的物流机器人生产商，去年被一家名为Zebra Technologies的计算公司收购。该公司开发了一种可以在物流中心周围移动的可移动拾取臂。

Boston Dynamics, another Massachusetts robot-maker, has come up with a heavyweight mobile version called Stretch, which can unpack lorries and put boxes on pallets. On January 26th DHL, a logistics giant, placed the first order for Stretch robots. It will deploy them in its North American warehouses over the next three years.

马萨诸塞州的另一家机器人制造商Boston Dynamics已经推出了一款名为Stretch的重型移动版机器人，它可以打开卡车，并将箱子放在托盘上。1月26日，物流巨头DHL下了第一份Stretch机器人的订单。未来三年，该公司将在北美的仓库中部署这些设备。

That timetable gives a clue that progress will not be rapid. It will take ten to 15 years before robots begin to be adept at picking and packing goods, reckons Zehao Li, the author of a new report on warehouse robotics for IDTechEx, a firm of British analysts. Some companies think their bots will be able to pick 80% or so of their stock over the coming years, although much depends on the range of goods carried by different operations.

这一时间表表明，进展不会很快。英国分析公司IDTechEx的关于仓库机器人的新报道的作者Zehao Li认为，机器人开始熟练地挑选和包装货物还需要10到15年的时间。一些公司认为，在未来几年，他们的机器人将能够挑选80%左右的货物，尽管这在很大程度上取决于不同业务所承载的商品范围。

Objects with irregular shapes, like bananas or loose vegetables, can be hard for a robot to grasp if it has primarily been built to pick up products in neat packages. The bot might also be restricted in what weight it can lift, so would struggle with a flat-screen television or a heavy cask of beer. Further into the future, systems could emerge to overcome many of these limitations, such as multi-arm robots.

形状不规则的物体，如香蕉或松散的蔬菜，对机器人来说可能很难抓住，如果它的设计主要是为了捡起包装整齐的产品。这款机器人可能还会受到它所能举起的重量的限制，因此它可能会与一台平面电视或一桶沉重的啤酒搏斗。在未来，可能会出现一些来克服这些限制的系统，比如多臂机器人。

So what jobs will remain? On the warehouse floor, at least, that mainly leaves technicians maintaining and fixing robots, says Mr Li. He thinks there are also likely to be a handful of supervisors watching over the bots and lending a hand if there remains anything that their mechanical brethren still can’t handle. It is not just inside the warehouse where jobs will go, but outside, too, once driverless delivery vehicles are allowed. At that point many products will travel through the supply chain and to people’s homes untouched by human hand.

那么还会留下哪些工作岗位呢? Li表示，至少在仓库的地板上，主要是由技术人员维护和修理机器人。他认为，还可能会有一些监管人员监督这些机器人，如果还有什么机械兄弟无法处理的事情，他们就会伸出援手。一旦无人驾驶送货车辆被允许，那就不止是仓库内的工作没了，仓库以外的工作也会被淘汰。到那时，许多产品将通过供应链进入人们的家中，而无需人手。

People will also be employed building robots. Amazon Robotics’s new factory will create more than 200 new manufacturing jobs, although that dwindles into insignificance compared with the more than 1m jobs which the pioneer of e-commerce has created since the first robots arrived in its fulfilment centres. A lot of those jobs are bound to go, although many are monotonous and strenuous, which is why they are hard to fill.

人们也将受雇制造机器人。亚马逊机器人公司的新工厂将创造逾200个新的制造业岗位，尽管与这家电子商务先驱自第一批机器人抵达其物流中心以来创造的逾100万个岗位相比，这将变得微不足道。许多这样的工作注定会被淘汰，尽管其中许多工作单调而费力，这就是为什么它们很难被填补。

However, other jobs will emerge. Technological change inevitably creates new roles for people. In the 1960s there used to be thousands of telephone switchboard operators, a job which has almost disappeared since exchanges became automated. But the number of other jobs in telecoms has soared. As logistics gets more efficient through greater automation, and online businesses grow, the overall level of employment in e-commerce should still increase. Many of these roles will be different sorts of jobs, just as there are many different sorts of robot. ■

不过，其他的工作岗位将会出现。技术变革不可避免地为人们创造了新的角色。在20世纪60年代，曾经有成千上万的电话总机操作员，但自从交换系统自动化后，这一职位几乎消失了。但电信行业的其他工作岗位数量却在飙升。随着物流通过更大的自动化变得更高效，以及在线业务的增长，电子商务的整体就业水平应该还会增加。这些角色中有许多是不同种类的工作，就像有许多不同种类的机器人一样。■
