What is the cheapest way to cut carbon?

减少碳排放最便宜的方法是什么?

Bill Gates is the latest to grapple with a thorny question

Feb 22nd 2021

IN THE TRENDIER parts of Berlin, cargo bikes are the rage. Locals use the bicycles, which have a wheelbarrow-sized box at the front, to do the weekly shop or ferry children around. Because they cut carbon-dioxide emissions, local authorities are subsidising the craze. But the well-intentioned schemes look pricey when you consider how much carbon is abated. One such scheme costs the city €370,000 ($450,000), but is expected to reduce emissions by only seven tonnes a year. That works out at over €50,000 per tonne abated. The equivalent figure for schemes that support the sale of low-carbon heating systems, by contrast, is €200 per tonne.

在柏林的时尚区，货运自行车风靡一时。当地人骑着自行车每周购物或接送孩子。自行车前面有一个手推车大小的箱子。因为它们减少了二氧化碳的排放，地方政府正在资助这股热潮。但是，当你考虑到减少了多少碳排放时，这些善意的计划看起来很昂贵。其中一个这样的计划花费了该市37万欧元(45万美元)，但预计每年只能减少7吨的排放量。这意味着每减少一吨超过5万欧元。相比之下，支持销售低碳供暖系统的计划的相应数字是每吨200欧元。

More than 100 countries and 400 cities (including Berlin) have promised to get to net-zero emissions by 2050 or before. Investors and regulators are encouraging companies to do the same. To meet these goals policymakers and bosses will have to pick from a menu of policies, from building wind farms to subsidising low-carbon jet fuel. That raises an important question: what is the cheapest way to cut carbon?

超过100个国家和400个城市(包括柏林)承诺到2050年或更早达到净零排放。投资者和监管机构也在鼓励企业这样做。为了达到这些目标，决策者和老板们将不得不从一系列政策中做出选择，从建设风力发电场到补贴低碳航空燃料。这就引出了一个重要的问题:减少碳排放最廉价的方式是什么?

One way to discern the answer is to impose a price on carbon, either as a tax or a cap-and-trade scheme. This would encourage firms and consumers to find the cheapest ways to abate. But setting a price is difficult politically. Only a fifth of the world’s emissions are covered by an explicit price. Even in Europe, the world’s biggest liquid carbon market, free credits still allow many industries to maintain emissions.

politically 政治上

要找出答案，一种方法是对碳征收价格，或者通过征税，或者通过限额交易机制。这将鼓励企业和消费者寻找最廉价的方式来减少碳排放。但从政治上讲，设定价格是困难的。世界上只有五分之一的排放被明确的价格覆盖。即使在世界上最大的液态碳排放市场欧洲，免费的碳排放信用仍然允许许多行业保持排放。

So other tools are needed, too. In his new book, “How to Avoid a Climate Disaster”, Bill Gates suggests using a “green premium”, or the gap between the price of clean activities and dirty ones, as a guide. Where the premium is low, zero-carbon alternatives exist, and consumers have no reason not to use them. Where the premium is high, more innovation is needed.

背景：在新书《如何避免气候灾难：已有解决方案及突破方向》中，盖茨使用了“绿色溢价”(green premium)的概念，即缩小传统碳排放技术(如燃油汽车)和其绿色替代品(电动汽车)之间的价格差异。当这些新技术的价格对普通消费者或政府来说太高时，像盖茨和贝索斯这样的人可以利用他们的资金来刺激需求，并将价格降低到所有人都能承受的水平。

盖茨认为，政府的政策引导，以及为产品支付“绿色溢价”的意愿，将促进私人领域进行绿色产品的研发和生产。

盖茨以太阳能电池板为例，称有史以来在气候方面花钱最多的是德国和日本，这两个国家在太阳能电池板价格比较高的时候购买了这些产品，这就是政府支付的“绿色溢价”。

他认为，（政府）应该花一部分钱支持存在高绿色溢价的领域，而对于那些进入了“学习曲线”的企业，例如使用绿色水泥、绿色钢铁等材料的工业企业，应给予其碳信用额。

因此，还需要其他工具。在比尔•盖茨的新书《如何避免气候灾难》中，比尔•盖茨建议使用“绿色溢价”，即清洁活动和污染活动之间的价格差距作为指导。在溢价较低的地方，零碳替代品是存在的，消费者没有理由不使用它们。在溢价高的地方，需要更多的创新。

A similar approach, popular in climate circles for the past decade or so, is to consider the marginal costs of abatement. Like green premiums, these compute the costs of a climate intervention (including operating costs and upfront spending). But it compares them with the emissions that the policy is expected to abate. Plotting the costs and emissions abated on a curve shows the policies that provide the most bang for the buck (see chart 1).

circle 圈子

在过去的十年里，气候学界流行了一种类似的方法，即考虑减排的边际成本。像“绿色溢价”一样，这些计算了气候干预的成本(包括运营成本和前期支出)。但它将这些排放量与该政策预计将减少的排放量进行了比较。将成本和减少的排放量绘制成一条曲线，显示了那些最能带来经济效益的政策(见图1)。

Such curves have been computed by a number of forecasters over the years, including McKinsey and the Boston Consulting Group, two consultancies; Goldman Sachs, a bank; and Britain’s Climate Change Commission, which advises Parliament. As a rule, most show that the biggest bang comes from making buildings more energy-efficient, say by installing insulation or smart cooling and heating systems. Often these have negative costs: analysts think they will eventually save consumers money through cheaper bills.

as a rule 通常

as a general rule 一般来说

say 比方说

insulation 隔热材料

多年来，包括麦肯锡和波士顿咨询集团这两家咨询公司、高盛银行以及为议会提供建议的英国气候变化委员会在内的许多预测机构都计算出了这样的曲线。一般来说，大多数研究表明，最大的影响来自于提高建筑的能效，比如安装隔热材料或智能制冷和供暖系统。这些措施通常都会带来负成本:分析人士认为，它们最终将通过更便宜的账单为消费者省钱。

The next-best bang for the buck tends to be replacing power plants that burn natural gas or coal with renewable-powered ones. There is less agreement about what the next-best option is after that. But the most expensive areas of the economy to decarbonise tend to be transport (planes and ships), heavy industry (steel and cement) and agriculture (cows belching methane). In these cases clean, cheap, scalable alternatives do not yet exist.

buck 钱

belch 喷出

其次最划算的做法是用可再生能源发电厂取代燃烧天然气或煤炭的发电厂。至于接下来的次优选择是什么，人们的看法则不那么一致。但在经济中，去碳成本最高的领域往往是运输业(飞机和船舶)、重工业(钢铁和水泥)和农业(奶牛排放甲烷)。在这些情况下，清洁、廉价、可扩展的替代方案还不存在。

Just as abatement-cost curves provide a rough guide for policymakers, they also show how difficult the maths is. Estimates of costs vary widely, for instance (see chart 2). A paper by Kenneth Gillingham of Yale University and James Stock of Harvard University compares the marginal costs of policies across 50-odd studies. The cost of wind-energy subsidies can range from more than $260 per tonne of carbon dioxide avoided, to next to nothing.

-odd 略多

正如减排成本曲线为政策制定者提供了一个粗略的指导一样，它们也表明了数学计算是多么困难。例如，对成本的估计差异很大(见表2)。耶鲁大学的肯尼斯•吉林厄姆和哈佛大学的詹姆斯•斯托克在一篇论文中比较了50多项研究中政策边际成本。风能补贴的成本从每避免排放一吨二氧化碳超过260美元到几乎为零不等。

This is partly because a technology’s abatement potential can vary from place to place. Some countries, such as Britain, are blessed with high winds and shallow seas that are ideal for offshore wind farms. In other places, wind energy will provide scant abatement.

这在一定程度上是因为一项技术的减排潜力可能因地而异。一些国家，如英国，有强风和浅海，是理想的海上风力发电场。在在其他地方，风能的减排作用微乎其微。

Working out costs is tricky, too. The International Energy Agency (IEA), for instance, has routinely underestimated the pace of deployment of renewables. And because economies of scale drive down prices, that means it has overestimated the costs of switching, too. In 2010 the lowest the IEA expected solar prices to drop to over the next decade was about $195 per megawatt hour. Today the price in America and Europe is $30-60 (see chart 3).

routinely 例行地

drive down 压低

计算成本也很棘手。例如，国际能源机构(IEA)经常低估可再生能源的部署速度。而且由于规模经济压低了价格，这意味着它也高估了转换的成本。2010年，国际能源署预计未来10年太阳能价格将降至每兆瓦时195美元左右的最低水平。如今，美国和欧洲的油价为30-60美元(见图3)。

![image-20210306104304500](/Users/chenyujie/Library/Application Support/typora-user-images/image-20210306104304500.png)

Nor do abatement-cost curves show how technologies interact. Hydrogen is rarely produced without emissions. But if it were, the Hydrogen Council reckons, it could be used in 35 different green applications, from storing energy to heating buildings. Ignoring this could lead to underinvestment in hydrogen power today.

underinvestment 投资不足

减排成本曲线也不能说明技术是如何相互作用的。氢很少是在没有排放的情况下产生的。但氢能源委员会认为，如果是这样的话，它可以用于35种不同的绿色应用，从储存能源到建筑供暖。忽视这一点可能会导致目前对氢能源的投资不足。

Interactions also affect how much interventions reduce emissions. Consider two things needed to decarbonise the economy: converting the grid to low-carbon power, and electrifying transport. The order in which you do these matters. According to a model developed by the Massachusetts Institute of Technology and others, if transport were electrified, there would be less demand for oil to fill tanks with petrol. Yet, as the demand for dirty power for electricity would surge, overall emissions would drop by only 2% by 2050 (compared with a business-as-usual baseline). If the grid were cleaned up first, though, then emissions would fall by about 30%.

相互作用也会影响干预措施在多大程度上减少排放。考虑一下实现经济脱碳需要做的两件事: 将电网转换为低碳电力，以及使交通电气化。你做这些事情的顺序很重要。根据麻省理工学院(Massachusetts Institute of Technology)和其他机构开发的一个模型，如果运输实现电气化，用汽油填充油箱的石油需求将会减少。然而，随着对肮脏电力的需求激增，到2050年，总排放量只会下降2%(与正常情况下的基准相比)。不过，如果先清理电网，那么排放量将下降约30%。

Faced with all these difficulties, forecasters are taking a more sophisticated approach, rather than simply working their way along the marginal-cost curve. Goldman Sachs is incorporating different scenarios and a wider range of costs into its analysis. Others are turning to “energy-systems” modelling, which estimates models over and over again with different assumptions. That lets technologies interact, and means that forecasts rely less on one set of assumptions for, say, prices.

面对所有这些困难，预测者正在采取一种更复杂的方法，而不是简单地沿着边际成本曲线计算。高盛(Goldman Sachs)正在将不同的情景和更广泛的成本纳入其分析。另一些则转向了“能源系统”模型，即用不同的假设反复评估模型。这使得技术可以相互作用，也意味着预测更少地依赖于一组假设，比如价格。

This type of analysis lets you sort climate actions into three categories, says Jesse Jenkins of Princeton University, all of which require funding. The first are what he calls “robust” interventions, such as improving energy efficiency, which are valuable across lots of scenarios. Next come “shaping” interventions, such as investing in hydrogen and batteries, which improve the likelihood of arriving at a low-carbon future. Then come “hedging” strategies: long-shot options to develop, just in case, such as direct-air-capture, which sucks carbon dioxide from the atmosphere. The result is a more complex framework better suited to deal with the complex, ever more urgent task of decarbonisation. ■

long shot 希望不大的尝试

普林斯顿大学的杰西·詹金斯(Jesse Jenkins)说，这种分析可以让你把气候行动分为三类，所有这些都需要资金。第一种是他所说的“稳健”的干预措施，例如提高能源效率，这在许多情况下都很有价值。接下来是“塑造”干预措施，如投资于氢和电池，这将提高实现低碳未来的可能性。然后是“对冲”策略:为了以防万一而开发的不太可能成功的选项，比如直接空气捕捉，它可以从大气中吸收二氧化碳。其结果是一个更加复杂的框架，更适合于处理复杂而紧迫的脱碳任务。■

A HEALTHY CORRECTION, or the first signs of a crypto-crash? On February 21st bitcoin hit a new high of more than $58,000, double its price at the start of the year, after several big firms and investors, led by Tesla, signalled that they were starting to take the cryptocurrency seriously. Within two days, though, the price had tumbled by over a fifth (before recovering slightly), jangling the nerves of HODLers, as bitcoin diehards are known. A trigger for the fall was Elon Musk, Tesla’s boss and bitcoin’s cheerleader-in-chief, musing that its price “seems high”. The news that Tether, an integral component of crypto-markets, had fallen foul of American regulators hardly helped calm the faithful.

Tether is a so-called stablecoin. Its issuer, a company of the same name, has long claimed that Tethers—of which more than 34bn are in circulation—are backed one-to-one by dollars. One purported advantage of such pegging is lower volatility; bitcoin’s price, by contrast, is notoriously erratic. Another is that stablecoins make it easier to move between cryptocurrencies and the ordinary sort.

Doubts have long swirled around Tether’s claim to be a sort of digital dollar. Critics say the one-to-one-backing claim looks flaky. GENDER DYSPHORIA—the miserable feeling of being at odds with one’s sex—is one of the fastest-rising medical complaints among children. America had one paediatric gender clinic in 2007. It now has at least 50. The sole paediatric gender clinic for England and Wales, known by its acronym, GIDS, has seen referrals rise 30-fold in a decade. A similar pattern is evident across the rich world.

referral 转诊病人

性别焦虑症——与自己的性别不一致的痛苦感觉——是儿童中上升最快的医学投诉之一。2007年，美国有一家儿科性别诊所。现在至少有50个。英格兰和威尔士唯一的儿科性别诊所(简称GIDS)的转诊人数在十年内增长了30倍。类似的模式在发达国家也很明显。

Many attending such clinics are given gonadotropin-releasing hormone (GNRH) agonists, or “puberty blockers”. These drugs, licensed to treat cancers of the breast and prostate, endometriosis and “central precocious puberty”—a rare condition in which puberty starts far earlier than normal—are prescribed off-label to stop the signals that stimulate the testicles or ovaries to ramp up sex-hormone production. The idea is to delay puberty, buying time for patients to decide whether to proceed to cross-sex hormones and surgery with the aim of “passing” as adult members of the opposite sex.

prostate 前列腺

off-label : used to describe a medical drug that is used for a different purpose than the one it was originally intended for

许多到此类诊所就诊的人都服用促性腺激素释放激素(GNRH)激动剂，或“青春期阻滞剂”。这些药物是有着不同的目的，获准用于治疗乳腺癌、前列腺癌、子宫内膜异位症和“中枢性早熟”(青春期开始得比正常时间早得多的罕见疾病)，它们被用来阻止刺激睾丸或卵巢的信号来增加性激素的产生。他们的想法是延迟青春期，为患者争取时间来决定是否进行跨性别激素和手术，以“通过”成为异性的成年成员。

All drugs offer a mix of harms and benefits. But despite their popularity, the effects of puberty blockers remain unclear. Because they are not licensed for gender medicine, drug firms have done no trials. Record-keeping in many clinics is poor. A 2018 review by researchers at the University of Melbourne described the evidence for their use as “low-quality”. In December British judges likewise flagged the lack of a “firm evidence base” when ruling that children were unlikely to be able to give meaningful consent to taking them. Britain’s National Health Service recently withdrew a claim, still made elsewhere, that their effects are “fully reversible”.

所有的药物都有利有弊。尽管青春期阻滞剂很受欢迎，但其效果仍不清楚。因为它们没有获得性别药物的许可，制药公司没有进行任何试验。许多诊所的记录保存很差。墨尔本大学研究人员2018年的一项研究将其使用的证据描述为“低质量”。去年12月，英国法官也同样指出，在裁定儿童不太可能对服用这些药物给予有意义的同意时，缺乏“坚实的证据基础”。英国国家医疗服务体系最近撤回了一项仍在别处提出的说法，即其影响是“完全可逆的”。

The studies that do exist are at once weak and worrying. The day after the court ruling, GIDS published a study that found children were happy to receive the drugs. But there was little other evidence of benefit—not even a reduction in gender dysphoria. Two older studies of Dutch patients given puberty blockers in the 1990s found that gender dysphoria eased afterwards. But without a control group, it is impossible to tell how patients would have felt had they not taken the drugs.

现有的研究既薄弱又令人担忧。在法院裁决的第二天，GIDS发表了一项研究，发现孩子们很乐意接受药物。但是几乎没有其他的证据表明这是有益的——甚至没有减少性别焦虑。上世纪90年代，两项更早的研究对服用青春期阻滞剂的荷兰患者进行了研究，结果发现，性别焦虑随后有所缓解。但是，如果没有对照组，就不可能知道患者如果没有服用这些药物会有什么感觉。

A 2020 paper analysed responses to an online survey and concluded that people who had taken puberty blockers were less prone to suicidal thoughts. But online surveys capture convenient samples, not representative ones. People may answer repeatedly, or at random. Much of the data appeared to be misreported: many who said they had taken puberty blockers were too old to have plausibly done so.

2020年的一篇论文分析了对一项在线调查的回应，得出的结论是，服用青春期阻滞剂的人更不容易产生自杀想法。但在线调查只收集了方便的样本，而不是有代表性的样本。人们可能会反复回答，也可能会随机回答。很多数据似乎是错误的: 许多说他们服用过青春期阻滞剂的人年纪太大了，不可能这么做。

In the absence of direct, robust evidence, researchers can try to extrapolate from other findings. Off-label prescribing is common in paediatric medicine, because drug firms do not generally like running trials on children. So doctors look for second-hand evidence from elsewhere to guide their decisions. One source is studies that look at how GNRH agonists are used to treat other conditions.

在缺乏直接、有力的证据的情况下，研究人员可以尝试从其他发现中进行推断。超适应症处方在儿科医学中很常见，因为制药公司通常不喜欢在儿童身上进行试验。因此，医生们会从其他地方寻找二手证据来指导他们的决定。一个来源是关于GNRH激动剂如何用于治疗其他疾病的研究。

Interrupting normal adolescence is not the same as treating cancer, endometriosis or precocious puberty. Nevertheless, data from these conditions have flagged unpleasant side-effects. Men who take GNRH agonists lose energy and sexual desire. (This is why some countries prescribe them to sex offenders.) Women are thrust into an artificial menopause, an experience unpleasant enough that, in endometriosis, drugs are typically prescribed for six months at most. Several legal cases have been brought against drug firms by adults who took puberty blockers for precocious puberty. They allege cognitive deficits, brittle bones and chronic pain in later life, though none has made it to court.

Animal studies suggest such concerns may be worth investigating. One 2017 study looked at sheep, which go through a developmental spurt similar to human adolescence. 一个很大的担忧是，青春期阻滞剂似乎可靠地导致了跨性别荷尔蒙的产生，这被医生们称为“级联干预”。从20世纪70年代开始的研究中得出的最佳估计是，大约80%的性别厌恶儿童被允许随心所欲地表达自己，但他们不会进行社会转型–改变自己的衣服、代词等，以异性的身份出现。随着他们的成长，他们将与自己的生理性别和解。然而，青春期阻滞剂似乎阻止了这种和解。在欧洲诊所报告的数字中，只有2-4%的儿童服用了这种药物。美国的诊所很少公布这一数据，但有趣的是情况也差不多。

The additional risk grew the longer the patients remained on hormones.

Are SPACs a useful innovation, a mania, or both?

SPAC是一种有用的创新，一种狂热，还是两者兼而有之？

Feb 20th 2021

IT IS EASY to mock SPACs. For decades these “special purpose acquisition vehicles”, publicly listed pots of capital raised by investors who seek out private firms to merge with, have ushered a small number of flaky and irrelevant companies onto public markets. The present SPAC boom on Wall Street began last year and, true to form, features celebrities-on-the-make, failed bosses looking for redemption and credulous investors keeping their eyes wide shut. Yet it is undeniable that something more serious is also now taking place. The amount of money raised by SPACs in the past 12 months has soared to over $120bn, according to Bloomberg. In a few weeks this year as much has been raised as in the first half of 2020. The boom is spreading to Europe, and Amsterdam is racing ahead of London as a favoured venue. Serious companies are getting involved, too. Two of Asia’s leading digital firms, Gojek and Tokopedia, are said to be considering using a SPAC to list in New York, which would be an alternative to a conventional initial public offering (IPO).

true to form 一如既往；和平常一样

模仿SPAC很容易。几十年来，这些“特殊目的收购工具”(由寻求与私人公司合并的投资者募集的公开上市资金池)已经将少量不稳定且不相关的公司引入了公开市场。华尔街目前的SPAC热潮始于去年，一如既往的是，名人崭露头角、失败的老板寻求赎回以及轻信的投资者睁大了眼睛。然而，不可否认的是，更严重的事情也正在发生。据彭博社(Bloomberg)报道，过去12个月SPACs筹集的资金已飙升至逾1200亿美元。在今年的几周内，筹集到的资金已达到2020年上半年的水平。这股热潮正蔓延到欧洲，阿姆斯特丹正领先于伦敦，成为最受欢迎的欧洲最大股票交易中心。一些重要的公司也在参与进来。据称，亚洲两家领先的数字公司Gojek和Tokopedia正在考虑利用SPAC在纽约上市，SPAC是传统的首次公开募股(IPO)的替代选择。

In an IPO a firm hires bankers who help it sell shares at an agreed price to mainly institutional investors in an elaborate process set out by regulators. The SPAC approach turns that on its head. A group of investors float a shell company, giving it a pot of cash. It then hunts for an unlisted target firm and offers to merge with it, raising a second round of cash from investors as it does so. If you are a firm that wants to go public, a marriage with a SPAC is relatively quick and certain.

在首次公开募股(IPO)中，一家公司雇佣银行家，帮助其以约定的价格向大部分机构投资者出售股票，这是监管机构制定的一个复杂过程。SPAC的方法完全扭转了这一局面。一群投资者让一家空壳公司上市，给了它一大笔现金。然后，它会寻找一家未上市的目标公司，并提出与之合并，同时从投资者那里筹集第二轮资金。如果你是一家想要上市的公司，与SPAC联姻/紧密结合是相对快速和确定的。

The SPAC boom partly reflects a rebellion among Silicon Valley types, who have long grumbled about having to go through an IPO. Banks charge fees of 5-7% of the capital raised. Bankers manage the IPO price, and stand accused of setting it artificially low in order to give a “pop” to the pals they deal with routinely in the public markets at the expense of founders and early backers. For over a decade many exciting firms have stayed sheltered in private markets, an option afforded them by well-funded venture-capital investors like Son Masayoshi of SoftBank. As a result, the value of cash raised by IPOs as a share of America’s overall stockmarket value had been in decline for years.

routinely 例行地

backer 赞助者；支持者

SPAC的繁荣在一定程度上反映了硅谷人士的反叛，他们长期以来一直抱怨必须通过IPO才能上市。银行收取5-7%的融资费用。银行家们控制着IPO价格，他们被指控人为压低IPO价格，以牺牲创始人和早期投资者的利益，让他们在公开市场上经常与之打交道的朋友们“大吃一惊”。十多年来，许多令人兴奋的公司一直在私人市场中寻求庇护，这是由像软银的孙正义(Son Masayoshi)这样资金充足的风险资本投资者提供的一种选择。其结果是，通过首次公开募股筹集的现金在美国股市总市值中所占的比例多年来一直在下降。

![image-20210223105835798](/Users/chenyujie/Library/Application Support/typora-user-images/image-20210223105835798.png)

However, the pent-up demand to go public is being unleashed at last. As well as SPACs, some firms are trying a third technique to go public called a direct listing. Founders and employees sell shares on an exchange for whatever investors are willing to pay—an option made possible by high-frequency traders, like Citadel Securities, who helped Slack and Spotify, two tech stars, to debut in this way.

pent-up 压抑的；积压的

背景：直接公开发行(DPO)，又称直接上市，是一种使公司股票公开交易但没有银行帮助做首次公开发行(IPO)的方法。直接上市是首次公开发行(IPO)的一种替代选择，公司不用与投资银行合作承销发行的股票。虽然放弃了承销商提供的安全网来以更快、更便宜的方法筹集资金，但开盘价将完全取决于市场的需求并受潜在市场波动的影响。

直接上市（DPO）不像首次公开发行(IPO)对外筹集资本，而是公司雇员和投资者将其拥有权转换为股票，然后在股票交易所挂牌。股票一旦上市后，公众可以购买股份，而现有的投资者可随时兑现，而不用像传统的IPO有锁定期（DPO没有锁定期）。Spotify和Slack科技就是最近选择在交易所挂牌、跳过传统上市程序DPO的例子。

然而，被压抑的上市需求终于得到释放。和SPACs一样，一些公司也在尝试第三种上市方式——直接上市。创始人和员工在交易所出售股票，以换取愿意支付的任何投资者。这种选择是由Citadel Securities【全球顶级做市商】等高频交易员提供的，他们帮助两位科技明星Slack和Spotify以这种方式上市。

There are two big dangers. One is that the SPAC boom becomes a bubble. Financial markets show many other signs of froth, including the recent GameStop retail-investor frenzy and the surge in Bitcoin’s price. If interest rates were to rise suddenly as a result of inflation, and the music were to stop in markets, the SPAC boom might end abruptly. That is unlikely to pose a risk to the financial system, but some firms would be left stranded at the altar. And SPAC investors might be clobbered.

clobber 彻底击败

有两大危险。一是SPAC的繁荣变成了泡沫。金融市场出现了许多其他泡沫迹象，包括最近GameStop散户投资者的狂热和比特币价格的飙升。如果利率因通货膨胀而突然上升，并且市场的“音乐”【好消息】戛然而止，SPAC的繁荣可能会戛然而止。这不大可能给金融系统带来风险，但一些公司会被困在祭坛上。而SPAC投资者可能会一败涂地。

The second danger lies within SPACs’ design, which can range from being efficient to being a rip-off. The typical SPAC creator receives “promote” shares—the median stake is 8% of the post-merger equity—for a trivial cost, meaning they make decent returns even if the merged firm’s shares sink after it goes public. Warrants (the right to buy shares at a given price in the future) are given to early backers as an incentive, and can also dilute the returns of outside shareholders. The presence of a cohort of badly designed SPACs is one reason why, on average, SPACs have underperformed both firms that debut via IPO, and the broader market.

rip-off [价格离谱的]骗人的东西

第二个危险存在于SPACs的设计中，它可能是高效的，也可能是索价过高的东西。典型的SPAC创建者只需花费很少的成本就可以获得“提升”的股份（平均持股比例为合并后股本的8%），这意味着即使合并后公司的股票在上市后下跌，他们也能获得可观的回报。认股权证(在未来以特定价格购买股票的权利)作为一种激励给予早期投资者，同时也会稀释外部股东的回报。一群设计糟糕的SPAC的存在，是SPAC平均表现逊于通过IPO上市的公司和整个市场的原因之一。

Fixing these problems will require investors to be vigilant. They should demand that SPAC creators forgo their fat promote shares in favour of shares or warrants that pay out only once other investors have seen returns. The incentives doled out to early backers should be trimmed, and restricted to those who are prepared to hold on to their shares long after a target has been acquired. These changes would both improve the long-term returns for investors and also discourage more dubious ventures from being set up. Some SPACs, such as the one launched by Bill Ackman, a hedge-fund manager, have already adopted more sensible terms.

解决这些问题需要投资者保持警惕。他们应该要求SPAC创建者放弃他们丰厚的推广股，转而购买只有在其他投资者看到回报时才会支付的股票或权证。发放给早期投资者的激励应该被削减，并且仅限于那些准备在目标公司被收购后很长时间内继续持有股份的投资者。这些改变既能提高投资者的长期回报，也能阻止更多可疑的企业成立。一些SPAC，比如由对冲基金经理比尔•阿克曼发起的SPAC，已经采用了更合理的条款。

The SPAC boom has a whiff of the bubble about it—and some projects will end in tears. But with the right design, SPACs can become a familiar and useful device for investors. They give firms more options for going public and will encourage regulators and bankers to improve the IPO process. Rational risk-taking by investors, capital-raising by exciting firms and the expansion of public markets are all things that should be welcomed. Even if some SPACs crash and burn, the idea behind them is a buy.■

SPAC的繁荣有一点泡沫的味道——一些项目将以泪水告终。但如果设计得当，SPACs将成为投资者熟悉而有用的工具。它们为公司提供了更多的上市选择，并将鼓励监管机构和银行家改善IPO程序。投资者的理性冒险，令人兴奋的公司的融资以及公开市场的扩张都应该受到欢迎。即使有些SPAC彻底失败了，它们背后的理念也是一种购买。
