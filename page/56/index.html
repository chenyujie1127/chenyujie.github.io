A 3°C world has no safe place

3°C的世界将没有安全的地方

Adapting to climate change

适应气候变化

![image-20210724115345287](/Users/chenyujie/Library/Application Support/typora-user-images/image-20210724115345287.png)

The extremes of floods and fires are not going away, but adaptation can lessen their impact

极端的洪水和火灾不会消失，但是适应可以减轻它们的影响

Jul 24th 2021

IN 1745, AS the river Liffey, having broken its banks, clawed at the foundations of the house in which he sat, the young Edmund Burke experienced a strange, perverse thrill. The man who would go on to found modern conservatism drew inspiration from this experience in a later essay on the sublime, writing of the unmatched delight that terrible destruction could stir—provided that it is watched from a certain distance.

perverse 不合常理的

unmatched 无与伦比的

决堤，一般指堤岸被水冲开。决堤是水漫过堤顶后逐步带走了筑堤的泥土，然后堤身变薄变软，挡不住上游的水，堤垮了就叫决堤

1745年，当利菲河决堤，河水冲刷着他房子的地基时，年轻的Edmund Burke感受到一种奇怪的、反常的兴奋。这位后来创立了现代保守主义的人从这次经历中获得了灵感，后来他在一篇关于崇高的文章中写道，只要从一定的距离观看，可怕的破坏可以带来无与伦比的快乐。

The most terrible thing about the spectacular scenes of destruction that have played out around the world over the past weeks is that there is no safe place from which to observe them. The ground under the German town of Erftstadt is torn apart like tissue paper by flood waters; Lytton in British Columbia is burned from the map just a day after setting a freakishly high temperature record; cars float like dead fish through the streets-turned-canals in the Chinese city of Zhengzhou. All the world feels at risk, and most of it is.

tear apart 撕开

freakish 怪异的; 反常的

7月1日，大火已经吞没了加拿大不列颠哥伦比亚省中部的利顿，该镇的镇议员布拉德·维斯宣布，包括镇子中心区在内，90%的地方都已经被烧毁殆尽。

![image-20210724115408741](/Users/chenyujie/Library/Application Support/typora-user-images/image-20210724115408741.png)

在过去的几周里，世界各地上演了惊人的毁灭景象，最可怕的是，没有一个安全的地方可以观察它们。德国Erftstadt镇的地下像纸巾一样被洪水撕裂; 不列颠哥伦比亚省的Lytton在创下异常高温记录的一天后，大火烧毁了整个镇子; 在中国郑州，汽车像死鱼一样漂浮在街道变成的运河中。整个世界都处于危险之中，大部分地区都是如此。

Greenhouse-gas emissions have produced a planet more than 1°C (1.8°F) warmer than it was in Burke’s pre-industrial days. Its atmosphere, stoked up and out of joint, is producing heavy weather in ways both predicted and surprising. And, with emissions continuing, it will get worse.

out of joint 混乱的

stoke up: add fuel to a large fire and move the fuel around with a stick so that it burns well and produces a lot of heat

put sth out of joint：prevent a plan from working correctly

使混乱；使无秩序

温室气体排放导致地球温度比Edmund Burke的前工业化时代高出1°C(1.8°F)。地球的大气层（被加热且处于不稳定状态）正在以预测到的和令人惊讶的方式产生恶劣的天气。而且，随着排放的持续，情况会变得更糟。

Unfortunately, 2021 will probably be one of the 21st century’s coolest years. If temperatures rise by 3°C above pre-industrial levels in the coming decades—as they might even if everyone manages to honour today’s firm pledges—large parts of the tropics risk becoming too hot for outdoor work. Coral reefs and the livelihoods that depend on them will vanish and the Amazon rainforest will become a ghost of itself. Severe harvest failures will be commonplace. Ice sheets in Antarctica and Greenland will shrink past the point of no return, promising sea rises measured not in millimetres, as today’s are, but in metres.

honour 履行;执行

the tropics 热带地区

coral reef 珊瑚礁

不幸的是，2021年可能是21世纪最冷的年份之一。在未来的几十年里，如果气温比工业化前的水平上升3°C （即使每个人都能兑现今天强有力的承诺，气温也有可能上升3°C），热带地区的大部分区域将面临太热而无法进行户外工作的风险。珊瑚礁和依赖它们的生计将消失，亚马逊雨林自身将成为一个幽灵。严重歉收将司空见惯。南极洲和格陵兰岛的冰原将缩小到不可逆转的程度，这意味着海平面上升的单位将不再是今天的毫米，而是米。

Six years ago, in Paris, the countries of the world committed themselves to avoiding the worst of that nightmare by eliminating net greenhouse-gas emissions quickly enough to hold the temperature rise below 2°C. Their progress towards that end remains woefully inadequate. Yet even if their efforts increased dramatically enough to meet the 2°C goal, it would not stop forests from burning today; prairies would still dry out tomorrow, rivers break their banks and mountain glaciers disappear.

woefully 严重地；糟糕地；可悲地

prairie 大草原

六年前，在巴黎，世界各国承诺尽快消除温室气体净排放，将气温升幅控制在2°C以下，以避免最糟糕的噩梦。然而他们在这方面的进展仍然严重不足。即使他们的努力大幅增加，足以达到2°C的目标，也无法阻止今天森林的燃烧; 明天，草原依然会干透，河流将会决堤，高山冰川也将会消失。

Cutting emissions is thus not enough. The world also urgently needs to invest in adapting to the changing climate. The good news is that adaptation makes political sense. People can clearly see the need for it. When a country invests in flood defences it benefits its own citizens above all others—there is no free-rider problem, as there could be for emissions reduction. Nor does all the money come from the public purse; companies and private individuals can see the need for adaptation and act on it. When they do not do so, insurance companies can open their eyes to the risks they are running.

因此，仅仅减排是不够的。世界还迫切需要投资以适应不断变化的气候。好消息是，适应是有政治意义的。人们可以清楚地看到它的必要性。当一个国家投资防洪工程时，它的国民受益高于其他所有人，这里不存在搭便车的问题，就像减少排放一样。也不是所有的钱都来自国库;公司和个人可以看到适应的需要，并采取行动。当他们不这样做的时候，保险公司可以睁开他们的眼睛，让他们看到他们正面临的风险。

Some adaptation is fairly easily set in place. Systems for warning Germans of coming floods will surely now improve. But other problems require much larger public investment, like that which has been put into water-management in the Netherlands. Rich countries can afford such things. Poor countries and poor people need help, which is why the Paris climate agreement calls for annual transfers of $100bn from rich to poor.

一些适应是相当容易的。用来警告德国人洪水即将来临的系统现在肯定会改进。但其他问题则需要更大的公共投资，比如荷兰在水资源管理方面的投资。发达国家负担得起这些东西。穷国和穷人需要帮助，这就是为什么巴黎气候协议呼吁每年从富国向穷国调动1000亿美元。

The rich countries have not yet lived up to their side of this. On July 20th John Kerry, President Joe Biden’s special envoy on climate change, reiterated America’s pledge to triple its support to $1.5bn for adaptation in poorer countries by 2024, part of a broader move to increase investment in adaptation and mitigation in developing countries. More such efforts are vital.

live up to 不辜负；符合

reiterate 重申

发达国家还没有做到这一点。7月20日，拜登总统的气候变化特使John Kerry重申，美国承诺到2024年将对较贫穷国家适应气候变化的支持增加两倍，达到15亿美元，这是加大对发展中国家适应和减缓气候变化投资的更广泛举措的一部分。需要有更多的这样的努力，这是至关重要的。

But they also have limits. Making do with less water may be possible; getting by on none is not. Some levels of temperature and humidity make outdoors activity impossible. There comes one flood too many, after which you abandon the land. When the reef is gone, it is gone.

make do (with sth) 将就用某物

get by on sth 靠某物勉强对付

但它们也有局限性。用更少的水将就生活是可以; 什么都没有是没办法勉强度日的。某些温度和湿度使得户外活动无法进行。一次洪水泛滥，你们就会弃地而去。当珊瑚礁消失时，它就消失了。

If the Paris goal of keeping the rise below 2°C is met, the full extent of those limits will not be tested. But emission-cutting zeal may not accelerate as required. And the climate system could prove more sensitive than it has shown itself to be to date, as some scientists believe possible, producing more warming per tonne of carbon in the atmosphere.

如果巴黎气候大会将气温升幅控制在2°C以下的目标得以实现，将不会对这些限制的全部范围进行检测。但是减排的热情可能不会像要求的那样加速。一些科学家认为，气候系统可能比它迄今表现出的更加敏感，大气中每吨碳的升温幅度更大。

Hence it is also prudent to study the most spectacular, and scary, form of adaptation: solar geoengineering. This seeks to make clouds or particle layers in the atmosphere a bit more mirror-like, reflecting away some sunlight. It cannot provide a straightforward equal and opposite response to greenhouse-gas warming; it will tend, for example, to reduce precipitation somewhat more than temperature, potentially changing rainfall patterns. But research over the past 15 years has suggested that solar geoengineering might significantly reduce some of the harms from greenhouse warming.

precipitation 降水

因此，研究最壮观、最可怕的适应方式，太阳能地球工程，也是谨慎的做法。这试图使大气中的云或粒子层更像镜子，反射一些阳光。它不能对温室气体变暖提供一个直接对等和相反的回应; 例如，它将倾向于减少降水量，而不是减少温度，这可能会改变降雨模式。但过去15年的研究表明，太阳能地球工程可能会显著减少温室效应带来的危害。

What nobody yet knows is how such schemes could be developed so as to reflect not just the interests of their instigators, but also those of all the countries they will affect. Different countries might seek different amounts of cooling; some ways of putting solar geoengineering into effect would help some regions while harming others. Nor is there yet a compelling rejoinder to the risk that the very idea of such things tomorrow reduces the incentive to be ambitious in cutting emissions today.

instigator 煽动者

rejoinder 反驳

目前还没有人知道的是，如何制定这样的计划，以便不仅反映其发起者的利益，而且也反映其所影响的所有国家的利益。不同的国家可能寻求不同程度的冷却;实施太阳能地球工程的一些方法可能会对一些地区有利，但对另一些地区不利。对于这样一种风险，也没有令人信服的反驳，即明日的这样一种想法会降低今日雄心勃勃的减排动机。

When good men do nothing

To think about solar geoengineering requires facing those problems—and the risk that powers with little interest in them may try out such schemes regardless. It also means facing squarely what kind of being humankind has become. Watching the rising waters of the Liffey, Burke “considered how little man is, yet in his mind how great…Master of all things, yet scarce can command anything.” Manipulating the climate that humanity has destabilised—unwittingly, at first—spurs similar thoughts of simultaneous power and impotence. It is not nature that humans cannot command, but themselves, in all their insignificance and world-altering might. ■

geoengineering 地质工程

squarely 完全; 公正地

unwittingly 无意地

insignificance 无足轻重; 微不足道

考虑太阳能地质工程需要面对这些问题，以及对这些问题不感兴趣的电力公司可能不顾一切地尝试这些方案的风险。这也意味着正视人类已经变成了什么样子。看着利菲河不断上涨的河水，Burke“意识到人是多么渺小，但在他的头脑中却有多么伟大……主宰一切，但很少人能指挥任何事情。” 控制人类破坏的稳定的气候（起初是在无意中），会引发类似的权力与无能同时存在的想法。人类无法控制的不是自然，而是他们自己，他们的渺小以及改变世界的力量。■

The management lessons from football

足球的管理经验

Bartleby

巴托比

![image-20210723140530498](/Users/chenyujie/Library/Application Support/typora-user-images/image-20210723140530498.png)

Roberto Mancini and Gareth Southgate embody contrasting styles of leadership

Roberto Mancini和Gareth Southgate的领导风格截然不同

Jul 15th 2021

SQUINT DURING the final of the European football championships and it was possible to imagine that two corporate executives were at work on the touchline. One was the sharply dressed Roberto Mancini, manager of Italy, who was often shown angrily gesticulating at his team. His rival Gareth Southgate, the England manager, was also dressed in a suit but had a much calmer demeanour, often pausing to consult his colleagues.

touchline 边线

gesticulate 做手势

demeanour 外表; 举止

在欧洲足球锦标赛的决赛期间，可以想象有两家公司的高管在边线上工作。其中一个是意大利主教练Roberto Mancini，他的穿着很时髦，经常被拍到愤怒地对他的球队做手势。他的对手、英格兰队主教练Gareth Southgate也穿着西装，但举止要平静得多，经常停下来与同事磋商。

The two represent different styles of football management. Mr Mancini has a domineering approach, akin to that of Sir Alex Ferguson, the former manager of Manchester United, who was famous for getting so close to his players that a tirade became known as the “hairdryer treatment”. Mr Southgate is a more emollient and inclusive character. During the tournament he took the time to praise the efforts of some of the reserve members of the squad who never made it on to the pitch. Like many a modern executive, Mr Southgate was promoted from within, managing the England under-21 squad before becoming coach of the senior team. The result was that he had a strong and lasting connection with many of the players.

domineer 专横跋扈

tirade 长篇激烈演说; 长篇激烈批评

emollient 安慰性的

背景：亚历克斯·弗格森爵士，也就是我们熟知的老佛爷，经常因为球员在场上表现不佳而对球员暴跳如雷、狂暴怒吼。他吼叫时用的力气巨大，球员们说感觉就像被电吹风吹过一样。老佛爷的这种管理方式也就因此成为了 hairdryer treatment “电吹风”管理。

这两者代表了足球管理的不同风格。Mancini有一种霸道的态度，类似于曼联前主教练Alex Ferguson爵士的态度。 Ferguson以亲近球员而闻名，他的长篇激烈批评被称为“电吹风管理”。Southgate的性格则更为柔和和包容。在比赛期间，他会花时间赞扬球队中一些从未上场的预备队成员的努力。和许多现代高管一样，Southgate是从内部提拔上来的，在成为英格兰一线队教练之前，他一直在管理着英格兰U21青年代表队。这就导致他和许多球员都建立了牢固而持久的联系。

In corporate terms, one could see Mr Mancini as akin to Jack Welch, the legendarily hard-boiled former head of General Electric, or the hard-charging boss of a private-equity group. In contrast, the style of Mr Southgate resembles that of the new, socially conscious breed of corporate manager. He supported his players when they “took the knee” to protest against racism and wrote a letter to England fans, stating that it was the duty of players “to continue to interact with the public on matters such as equality, inclusivity and racial injustice, while using the power of their voices to help put debates on the table, raise awareness and educate”. It is easy to imagine those sentiments coming from the mouth of Paul Polman, who as boss of Unilever championed a sustainable business model for the consumer-goods giant. This does not mean that Mr Southgate lacks a ruthless streak. One of his first acts as manager was to drop Wayne Rooney, England’s ageing talisman, and he has suspended some of his stars for misbehaviour.

hard-charging: very aggressive, determined, or ambitious

take a knee是指在足球运动比赛的时候，单膝着地、以表达抗议和不满，尤其是抗议对黑人的不公正对待以及警察暴力

从公司的角度来看，人们认为Mancini先生与Jack Welch（传说中的冷酷强悍的通用电气前总裁，或者说是一家私募股权集团的富有进取心的老板）相似。相比之下，Southgate的风格更类似于新一代的具有社会意识的企业高管。当他的球员“单膝着地”以抗议种族歧视，他表示支持她的球员们，并且写信给英格兰球迷称球员们有责任“就平等、包容性和种族歧视等问题与公众保持互动, 同时他们“发声”的力量可以帮助把辩论摆上桌面, 提高意识和教育”。很容易想象，这些观点来自Paul Polman，作为消费品巨头联合利华的老板，他支持可持续的商业模式。这并不意味着Southgate不无情。他上任后的第一件事就是解雇了英格兰的老护身符Wayne Rooney，他还因一些球星行为不检而将其停职。

Italy’s eventual victory makes it tempting to argue that this proves the greater virtues of a more aggressive style of management pursued by Mr Mancini. After all, the final represented the 34th consecutive game under his leadership in which Italy had avoided defeat. This was a complete turnaround after the squad failed to qualify for the 2018 World Cup under Mr Mancini’s predecessor. Italy started the tournament with a lower world ranking than England, which also enjoyed home advantage for the match.

意大利的最终胜利很容易让人争辩说，这证明了Mancini先生所追求的更激进的管理风格优点更多。毕竟，这场决赛是在他的领导下，意大利连续第34场避免了失败。这是在Mancini的前任的领导下，球队未能获得2018年世界杯的参赛资格后的一个彻底的转变。意大利在世界杯开赛时的世界排名低于英格兰，而英格兰在本场比赛中也占有主场优势。

But the final was exceptionally close. The teams were level after normal time and extra time; had one of England’s penalties hit the inside of the post instead of the outside, the outcome might have been different. Mr Southgate also deserves credit for getting England into a big final for the first time in 55 years, having earlier guided them to a World Cup semi-final in 2018. Under his predecessor, the team was knocked out of the 2016 European championships by Iceland, a footballing minnow. In a time of polarisation, Mr Southgate is widely admired for his politeness and modesty.

knock out 淘汰

但是决赛时两队排名非常接近。两队在正常时间和加时赛后打成平手;如果英格兰队的一个点球击中门柱内侧而不是外侧，结果可能会有所不同。Southgate 也值得称赞，他曾在2018年带领英格兰队进入世界杯半决赛，这是英格兰55年来首次进入决赛。在他的前任的领导下，这支球队在2016年的欧洲锦标赛中被冰岛的鱼腩球队淘汰。这两种不同的极端中，Southgate因其礼貌和谦虚而广受赞赏。

And Mr Mancini’s aggressiveness has led to problems in the past. A year after guiding Manchester City to an English Premier League title in 2012, he was sacked over concerns about his habit of harshly criticising his players in public and being “aloof and icy” with backroom staff. One player texted a journalist “Can we put the champagne on ice yet?” on rumours of the manager’s departure. After leaving City, he had three unsuccessful stints in club management before taking the Italian job in 2018.

stint 规定工作时间

Mancini的挑衅行为在过去也曾导致过一些问题。在2012年带领曼城夺得英超冠军一年后，他被解雇了，原因是他习惯在公开场合严厉批评自己的球员，并且对幕后工作人员“冷漠无情”。针对于Mancini的离开，有传闻称一名球员给记者发短信说:“我们可以把香槟放在冰上了吗?” 在离开曼城后，在2018年接到意大利的工作之前，他有过在俱乐部管理的三次失败经历。

In the corporate world, the fashion has moved more towards the Southgate style of management. In football as in business, aggressive management can work for a while. But it only succeeds if it is accompanied by other qualities.

商业界是越来越趋向于Southgate式的管理方式。在足球场上就和在商业战场上一样，激进的管理可以在一段时间内奏效。但它只有在具备其他品质的情况下才会成功。

What unites Messrs Mancini and Southgate is their meticulous attention to detail. And any manager, in football or business, is only as good as the team at their disposal. If either Mr Mancini or Mr Southgate had been in charge of a team from a smaller nation with fewer resources, they would not have made it to the final. As Warren Buffett wisely remarked, “When a management with a reputation for brilliance tackles a business with a reputation for bad economics, it is the reputation of the business that remains intact.”

Messrs 先生们

meticulous 一丝不苟的

at sb’s disposal 供某人使用

Mancini和Southgate先生的共同点是他们对细节的一丝不苟。无论是在足球还是商业领域，任何一个管理者都只是与他们所掌控的球队一样优秀。如果Mancini或Southgate领导的球队来自一个资源更少的小国，他们就不可能进入决赛。正如巴菲特所言:“当绩效卓著的管理层应对的是一家经济状况糟糕的公司时，这家公司的声誉将保持不变。”

The 2020 Olympics will be memorable, but not in the way Japan hoped

2020奥运会将令人难忘，但不是以日本所希望的方式

Rings on the ropes

濒于失败的奥运会

![image-20210722100704369](/Users/chenyujie/Library/Application Support/typora-user-images/image-20210722100704369.png)

Even if disaster is averted, a sense of national renewal will remain elusive

即使灾难得以避免，国家复兴感依然难以捉摸

Jul 15th 2021

CLOUDS GATHERED over Komazawa stadium in Tokyo as the Olympic torch arrived on July 9th. Because of the pandemic, the traditional public relay was replaced by a small ceremony behind the stadium’s closed doors. Protesters outside held signs that read “Protect lives not the Olympics” and “Extinguish the Olympic torch”. As Kyogoku Noriko, a civil servant, put it, “**Now is not the time for a festival.**” More enthusiastic onlookers lined a nearby footbridge, hoping to catch a glimpse of the flame through the stadium’s rafters. 兴奋剂仍然太普遍了

Competitive sports

![image-20210721095746205](/Users/chenyujie/Library/Application Support/typora-user-images/image-20210721095746205.png)

Lack of will is a bigger problem than lack of means

缺乏意愿是一个比缺乏手段更大的问题

Jul 17th 2021

EIGHT YEARS ago Lance Armstrong, the winner of an unprecedented seven Tours de France, admitted to using performance-enhancing drugs. His confession bookended the “heroic age” of doping in cycling. Between the 1990s and the mid-2000s drug use was widespread and blatant. Doping is harder to get away with now than in Mr Armstrong’s day. But cycling’s reputation has never fully recovered. Fans watching this year’s Tour, due to finish in Paris on July 18th, discuss drugs as much as they discuss tactics or which riders are on form. Fairly or not, suspicion dogs every race.

dog 困扰

八年前，史无前例的七次环法自行车赛冠军Lance Armstrong承认使用了兴奋剂。他的坦白为使用兴奋剂的自行车运动“英雄时代”划上了句号。20世纪90年代至21世纪中期，兴奋剂的使用现象普遍且明目张胆。与Armstrong的时代相比，现在使用兴奋剂更难逃脱处罚。但自行车运动的名声从未完全恢复。今年的巡回赛将于7月18日在巴黎结束，观看比赛的球迷们在讨论战术或哪些车手处于最佳状态的同时，也在讨论药物。不管公平与否，每场竞赛都受到猜疑的困扰。

It is not just cycling. Doping is common in many sports. The Tokyo Olympics start on July 23rd. They will take place without Russia, banned last year for falsifying data in the aftermath of the exposure, in 2015, of an enormous state-sponsored doping programme. The pandemic will force athletes to compete in empty stadiums. Lockdowns, for their part, have disrupted the regimen of regular drug tests to which they are subject. Reduced scrutiny will have benefited cheats from all countries. If estimates from former officials are to be believed, more than a thousand of the 11,000 athletes at the games might be chemically enhanced.

regimen 养生之道

不仅仅是骑自行车。兴奋剂在许多运动项目中都很常见。东京奥运会将于7月23日开幕。它们将在没有俄罗斯的情况下进行，俄罗斯在2015年被曝光一项庞大的国家赞助的兴奋剂项目后在去年又因伪造数据而被禁赛。这场疫情将迫使运动员在空荡荡的体育场比赛。对他们来说，封锁扰乱了他们需要接受的常规药物测试。减少审查将有利于所有国家的作弊行为。如果前官员的估计属实，那么参加本届奥运会的1.1万名运动员中可能有1000多名运动员服用了化学药物。

The earliest Olympic doping tests were in 1968. The apparent intractability of the problem leads some liberals to throw up their hands and suggest removing the restrictions entirely. Humans are a technological species, after all. Physics is already allowed to boost performance—think of lighter bikes, or springy shoes—so why not let chemistry rip, too?

intractability 难对付

最早的奥运会兴奋剂检测是在1968年。这一明显棘手的问题导致一些自由主义者举手投降，建议完全取消这些限制。毕竟，人类是一个科技物种。物理学已经被允许用来提高性能，想想更轻的自行车，或有弹性的鞋子，那么为什么不让化学也发挥作用呢?

However, many doping drugs have grave side-effects. Athletes dosed with steroids by the East German authorities during the cold war suffered problems including severe liver damage and stunted growth. Women—for whom the drugs were most effective—underwent the irreversible development of male characteristics such as deep voices and body hair. A free-for-all would hand victory to those most prepared to take reckless quantities of dope. Adult athletes would not be the only ones to suffer. Sports careers begin in childhood, under the dominant eye of ambitious coaches with much to gain and nothing to lose.

free-for-all 混战; 群体争吵

然而，许多兴奋剂有严重的副作用。冷战期间，东德当局给运动员服用类固醇后，出现了严重的肝损伤和阻碍发育等问题。对女性来说，这些药物最有效，但她们却经历了男性特征不可逆转的发展，比如低沉的嗓音和体毛。一场混战将把胜利拱手让给那些最愿意不计后果地大量服用兴奋剂的人。成年运动员并不是唯一的受害者。运动生涯是始于童年的，在雄心勃勃的教练的主导下，他们（应该是指教练）可以得到很多，不会失去什么。

Better, then, to try to improve policing. Stamping out drugs completely is no more plausible than stamping out burglary. But anti-doping efforts have made a difference. Drug-takers in all sports have to be subtler and more careful than they once were. Three things would improve the system further.

stamp out 镇压；消除

因此，更好的办法是努力提高监管。彻底消灭兴奋剂就像消灭盗窃一样不可信。但反兴奋剂的努力起到了作用。所有运动项目中的服用兴奋剂的人都必须比以前更加小心谨慎。有三件事可以进一步改善这个系统。

The first is a change of emphasis. The science of anti-doping has become sophisticated. Tests can detect drugs at levels of just one part per trillion. But intelligence work can be just as useful. Whistleblowers, suspicious behaviour and tip-offs from pharmaceutical companies have all helped uncover doping scandals in the past. Such efforts should be enhanced.

tip-off 密告

首先是重心的改变。反兴奋剂的科学已经变得复杂起来。测试只能检测到万亿分之一的药物水平。但情报工作也同样有用。过去，举报人、可疑行为和制药公司的举报都有助于揭露兴奋剂丑闻。应加强这种努力。

That will require more money, the second change. The World Anti-Doping Agency (WADA) is funded by governments and sports bodies. Its annual budget is $40m, less than some top athletes earn. The national agencies that implement WADA’s rules have even less to get by with. Sponsors and sports federations are rich enough to bump that up considerably.

Anti-doping relies on international co-operation, and other countries are unlikely to appreciate America throwing its weight around like a Greco-Roman wrestler—especially when its domestic sports (no strangers to doping) are exempt. Aduhelm may not cure Alzheimer’s, but it might help fix drug prices

Blowing the inheritance

![image-20210720094419687](/Users/chenyujie/Library/Application Support/typora-user-images/image-20210720094419687.png)

The controversial drug is so expensive that it has attracted the interest of Congress

这种备受争议的药物非常昂贵，以至于引起了国会的兴趣

Jul 15th 2021

WHEN A LONG-AWAITED drug for Alzheimer’s was approved by the Food and Drug Administration (FDA) last month, controversy quickly followed. In November ten of 11 members of an FDA expert panel rejected the treatment, but it received accelerated approval anyway. Three panel members resigned in protest. It also has a large price tag: about $41,000 for year one and $56,000 for subsequent years. The unusual approval process and high cost has sparked a congressional investigation into the developer’s business practices. The treatment is also becoming the focus of new legislation which, if passed, would allow the government to negotiate drug prices for federal health-insurance plans.

上个月，当一种期待已久的治疗阿尔茨海默症的药物被美国食品和药物管理局批准后，争议极快随之而来。11月，FDA专家小组的11名成员中有10人拒绝了这种疗法，但它还是获得了加速批准。三名小组成员辞职以示抗议。它的价格也很高:第一年约为4.1万美元，随后几年的价格为5.6万美元。不寻常的审批程序以及高昂的成本促使美国国会对这家开发商的商业行为展开调查。这种疗法也成为了新立法的焦点，如果新立法通过，政府就可以就联邦医疗保险计划的药品价格进行谈判。

About 6m Americans (one in nine old people) have Alzheimer’s disease, a form of dementia. Aducanumab, marketed as Aduhelm, successfully clears the brain of beta-amyloid, a protein that clumps between neurons and disrupts their functioning. Some researchers believe that this protein causes Alzheimer’s. Most startups were created to “unbundle” finance: to carve out niches where they could offer a better service than the banks. Now, however, most successful firms are rebundling, adding new products in a bid to become platforms. Acquisitions provide a handy shortcut; their high valuations mean the big firms can often snap up smaller ones on the cheap by swapping equity.

这些投资者开出巨额支票之际，金融科技公司正希望书写新的篇章。大多数初创公司都是为了“拆分”金融业:开拓出比银行更好的利基市场。然而现在，大多数成功的公司都在重新组合，增加新产品，试图成为平台。收购提供了一条便捷的捷径; 这些公司的高估值意味着，大公司通常可以通过股权交换以较低的价格收购小公司。

Stripe, the most valuable private fintech firm in the West, is a good example of the sector’s coming of age. It was set up a decade ago to help firms accept payments online. Now worth $95bn, it also offers services ranging from tax compliance to fraud prevention. That breadth was partly achieved through acquisitions; since October it has bought three other firms.

西方最有价值的私人金融科技公司Stripe是该行业成熟的一个很好的例子。它成立于10年前，旨在帮助企业接入在线支付。该公司目前市值950亿美元，还提供从税务合规到欺诈预防等一系列服务。这种广度在一定程度上是通过收购获得的;自去年10月以来，它已经收购了另外三家公司。

A similar logic animates credit-card giants, which are trying to hedge against innovations in online payments; and the banks, which see fintech as a way to plug gaps in their digital offerings, cut costs, and diversify away from lending. Goldman Sachs and JPMorgan are bringing lots of smaller acquisitions under the umbrella of new, versatile consumer apps. As a consequence, the distinction between fintech and traditional banking could eventually blur, predicts Nik Milanovic of Google Pay, the tech firm’s payments arm.

All this splurging and merging also carries risks. One is that the hefty prices paid for fintechs prove unjustified. 所有这些挥霍和合并也带来了风险。其中一个风险是，为金融科技公司支付的高昂价格是不合理的。Visa收购Tink的价格是这家初创公司年收入的60倍;Wise的估值约为营收的20倍，利润的285倍。特别是银行，只有当有前景的金融科技公司的价格太高时，它们才能发现。

Another risk is that competition and innovation are stifled. Founders of startups that have been acquired often leave at the end of their “vesting” period—the minimum amount of time they must stick around before they can sell their shares, usually one to three years. The culture that allowed a firm to thrive could then wither. Fintechs bought by banks in particular could struggle: after a deal, cultures can clash; customers often leave. Most neobanks acquired by old ones, such as Simple (bought by BBVA, a Spanish bank), have been either shut down or sold.

Neobank是一种新生代的银行,是采用纯线上或纯手机端操作的银行,下称“新兴银行”

Nevertheless, one thing seems clear. Fintechs are inexorably gaining critical mass: their value has risen to $1.1trn, equivalent to 10% of the value of the global banking and payments industry, and up from 4% in 2018. Prices may be stretched today and some firms may flop, but in the long run it seems likely that this share will only rise further. ■

然而，有一件事似乎很清楚。金融科技正不可避免地获得临界规模:它们的价值已升至1.1万亿美元，相当于全球银行和支付行业价值的10%，高于2018年的4%。今天的价格可能是被拉大的，一些公司可能会倒闭，但从长远来看，这一份额似乎只会进一步上升。■

Within five years, the space-tourism firm claimed, it would take a total of over 3,000 passengers on life-changing jaunts in its spaceships. On July 11th, after a last 90-minute delay, Virgin Galactic finally began to make good on that promise. Its VSS Unity rocket plane was released from a support aircraft, fired up an engine that accelerated it to three times the speed of sound and soared 85km above Earth’s surface. Thence, for four minutes, its six temporarily weightless passengers, including the firm’s British co-founder, Sir Richard Branson, beheld the planet’s curvature against the blackness of outer space, before returning to a spaceport in New Mexico.

Inwardly he may have gloated a bit, having just pipped Jeff Bezos, a fellow (much wealthier) billionaire to the heavens. On July 20th Amazon’s recently retired boss plans to go slightly higher, for slightly less time, in the New Shepard, a vertical-launch vehicle built by his own spacefaring company, Blue Origin.

Jul 10th 2021

HOW SHOULD societies punish those who commit the worst crimes? As fewer countries use the death penalty, more are imposing life sentences. The number of people jailed for life worldwide rose by more than 80% between 2000 and 2014, to nearly half a million.

社会应该如何惩罚那些犯下最严重罪行的人? 随着判处死刑的国家越来越少，判处无期徒刑的国家是越来越多。从2000年到2014年，全世界被判终身监禁的人数增加了80%以上，达到近50万人。

And a good thing, too, liberals and conservatives might agree. As a punishment for heinous crimes, life in prison strikes many as a sensible compromise between the inhumanity of the death penalty and the leniency of letting the guilty eventually walk free. The success of that argument is one reason why fewer people are now being put to death.

heinous 十恶不赦的

自由主义者和保守主义者可能也会赞同(判处无期徒刑)，这也是一件好事。作为对十恶不赦罪行的一种惩罚，终身监禁在许多人看来倒像是“不人道的死刑”和“仁慈的让罪犯最终获得自由”之间的一种明智的妥协。这一理由的深受青睐正是现在被处死的人越来越少的原因之一。

In fact, life sentences can also count as acts of punitive excess. They keep people penned up long after most of them have lost the capacity or inclination to be violent. They are also a poor deterrent. Potential criminals are more effectively restrained by a high likelihood of being caught than by a severe punishment.

deterrent 威慑物

事实上，终身监禁也可以算作惩罚过度的行为。在大多数人失去暴力行为的能力或倾向后，他们将这些人长期关在监狱里。他们的威慑力也很弱。被逮捕的可能性比严厉的惩罚更能有效地抑制潜在的罪犯。

Although, in America at least, it is cheaper to lock people up for life than to execute them (because death-row cases involve endless legal wrangling), old codgers make costly prisoners, because of their health problems. By one estimate, they are three times more expensive than younger inmates. Money spent keeping 60- and 70-year-old ex-thugs behind bars for ever would nearly always prevent more crimes if it were spent instead on better policing, or on rehabilitation.

thug 恶棍

behind bars 在狱中

至少在美国，虽然判处终身监禁比执行死刑要省力(因为死刑案件涉及到没完没了的法律纠纷)，但是由于老头儿容易有健康问题，这样的话这些老头儿服刑就要花很多钱。据估计，他们比年轻囚犯的开销多三倍。如果把用来永久关押六七十岁的前暴徒的钱花在改善治安或改造社会上，几乎总能防止更多的犯罪。

Life sentences take many forms, of varying severity. Critics should focus on the substance, not the label. Sentences that offer no possibility of parole are far harsher than those that offer a chance of release, however distant. Some ultra-long sentences, by contrast, are no different from life. Alaska does not impose life sentences, but it does jail people for 99 years.

无期徒刑有多种形式，轻重缓急各不相同。批评者应该关注惩罚实质，而非惩罚的标签（即名字）。不提供假释机会的判决远比那些提供假释机会的判决严厉，尽管这一机会很渺茫。相比之下，有些超长的判决与终身监禁没什么不同。阿拉斯加州没有无期徒刑，但确实有99年的监禁。

Those who want to limit prison terms should start with the cruellest and most egregious. These include sentences imposed on children, or for non-violent crimes, as under “three strikes” laws in some American states. Lifers should not be singled out for harsh treatment as they are in Ukraine, where some are locked in their cells for 23 hours a day. Nor should life sentences be mandatory for particular offences, which bars courts from taking into account any mitigating circumstances.

egregious 极坏的; 极糟糕的;极严重的

lifer 无期徒刑者

背景：在美国，有项法律名为“Three Strikes Law”（“三振出局法”，意在严惩累犯），因暴力或严重罪行“进宫”两次者，以后每被判一次，无论新罪严重与否，至少得服刑25年甚至是终身监禁。

那些想要限制刑期的人应该从最残忍和最严重的开始。这包括对儿童或非暴力犯罪的判决，如美国一些州的“三振出局法”。无期徒刑者不应该像在乌克兰那样受到严酷的待遇，在那里有些人每天被关在牢房里23个小时。也不应该对特定的罪行强制判处终身监禁，这就禁止法庭考虑任何减轻罪行的情节。

What about when crimes are shockingly depraved or cost many lives? The judge who sentenced Brenton Tarrant for murdering 51 people in 2019 at two mosques in Christchurch, New Zealand, a country that abolished the death penalty in 1989, had no qualms about throwing away the key. “Your crimes are so wicked that even if you are detained until you die, it will not exhaust the requirements of punishment,” he told Mr Tarrant.

to have or feel qualms about doing sth 对做某事感到不安

当犯罪行为令人震惊地道德败坏或导致许多人丧生的时候呢? 2019年，Brenton Tarrant在新西兰基督城的两座清真寺杀害了51人。新西兰于1989年废除了死刑，对他判刑的法官并没有对扔掉这把“钥匙”感到不安。他告诉Tarrant，“你的罪行如此恶劣，以至于即使你被拘留至死，也无法满足惩罚的要求”。

That view is understandable. And yet even in this case life without the possibility of parole is wrong. No amount of time can expiate Mr Tarrant’s crimes. But prisons are about harm prevention, not expiation.

expiation 赎罪

这种观点是可以理解的。但即使在这种情况下，没有假释的生活也是错误的。监禁时间再长也无法弥补Tarrant先生的罪行。但监狱是为了预防伤害，而不是赎罪。

A better approach is that of Norway. It sets a maximum sentence of 21 years. However, if a convict is still judged to be a danger to society after that time, he can be detained until he is not. Such a rule protects potential victims while also offering the criminal a measure of hope and a reason to reform.

一个更好的方法是采用挪威的方法。在挪威最高刑期为21年。但是，如果一个罪犯在此之后仍然被认为是社会危险分子，他可以被拘留，直到他不再是社会危险分子为止。这一规则保护了潜在的受害者，同时也给罪犯带来了一丝希望和改过自新的理由。

Keep the keys

In 2013 the European Court of Human Rights ruled that all prisoners are entitled to the prospect of eventual release. For a few who remain dangerous in their dotage, such as orchestrators of genocide, that may be too big a risk. But for most murderers, 20-25 years behind bars will prove long enough. ■

dotage 衰老

2013年，欧洲人权法院裁定，所有囚犯都有权获得最终释放的可能性。对于少数在年老时仍然危险的人，如策划种族灭绝的人来说，这可能是太大的风险。但是对于大多数杀人犯来说，20-25年的监禁已经足够长了。■

Britain’s car industry is finding Brexit far less of a problem than expected

英国汽车业发现，脱欧带来的问题远没有预期的那么严重

Pedal to the metal

把油门踩到底

![image-20210716100345353](/Users/chenyujie/Library/Application Support/typora-user-images/image-20210716100345353.png)

Dollops of government cash are a big part of the reason

大量的政府资金是主要原因

Jul 10th 2021

FIVE YEARS ago, straight after Britain’s vote to leave the European Union (EU), Nissan, a Japanese carmaker, warned that the future of its factory in Sunderland was in doubt. On July 1st it announced a £1bn ($1.3bn) investment in a new battery plant that will secure the factory’s future. And on July 6th Stellantis, another motor manufacturer, said it would be investing £100m to produce electric vans in Ellesmere Port. Further news is expected soon from BMW and Toyota. The trade deal that Britain struck with the EU in December 2020—and eye-watering amounts of government cash—have bosses musing less about upping sticks, and more about the advantages of post-Brexit regulatory freedom.

eye-watering 使人激动万分的

五年前，就在英国公投脱欧后，日本汽车制造商日产警告称，其在桑德兰的工厂未来堪忧。7月1日，它宣布投资10亿英镑(13亿美元)建立一个新的电池厂，以确保工厂的未来。7月6日，另一家汽车制造商Stellantis表示将投资1亿英镑在埃尔斯米尔港生产电动货车。宝马和丰田不久将发布更多消息。英国于2020年12月与欧盟达成的贸易协议以及使人激动万分的大量政府资金，让老板们不再考虑迁居别处，而是更多地考虑脱欧后监管自由的好处。

Before the vote, things had been going swimmingly for Britain’s motor industry. Production hit a two-decade high in 2016, at over 1.8m units. Measured by vehicles per employee, Britain had become one of Europe’s most productive carmakers by 2016, with around 160,000 employees producing almost 11 vehicles each annually, compared with an EU average of less than eight. By making it harder to transport parts across borders, Brexit put this at risk.

to go swimmingly 一切顺利

在脱欧公投之前，英国的汽车行业一直很顺利。2016年，其产量达到了20年来的最高水平，超过180万辆。到2016年，英国已成为欧洲生产率最高的汽车制造商之一（以每位员工的生产车辆数衡量），约16万名员工每年生产近11辆汽车，而欧盟的平均产量不到8辆。英国脱欧让零部件跨境运输变得更加困难，从而使其面临风险。

A modern car is the result of a complex, international production process. It typically has around 3,000 components. For cars assembled in Britain, only around 40% of those components are produced domestically. The crankshaft in a BMW Mini crosses the Channel three times before being installed in Oxford. Around four in five cars made in Britain are exported, over half of them to the EU. Nissan opened its Sunderland plant in 1986, drawn partly by the prospect of a liberalising labour market, but also by easy access to Europe. Others followed, and since the failure of MG Rover in 2005 all the volume manufacturers in Britain have been foreign-owned.

crankshaft 曲轴

现代汽车是复杂的、国际化的生产过程的产物。它通常有大约3000个部件。对于在英国组装的汽车来说，只有大约40%的零部件是在国内生产的。一辆宝马Mini的曲轴在到牛津被安装之前要穿越英吉利海峡三次。英国制造的汽车大约有五分之四用于出口，其中超过一半出口到欧盟。日产汽车1986年在桑德兰开设了工厂，部分原因是看到了劳动力市场自由化的前景，同时也是因为可以轻松进入欧洲。自2005年MG Rover破产以来，英国所有的量产汽车制造商都归外资所有，其他企业紧随其后。

Although the Trade and Co-operation Agreement (TCA) between Britain and the EU did not give the industry all it hoped for, it came close. Trade can mostly continue without tariffs or quotas. Customs checks and fees introduce some friction, but are minor compared with the threat of 10% tariffs under World Trade Organisation rules if no deal had been struck.

尽管英国和欧盟之间的贸易与合作协议没有给该行业带来它所希望的一切，但也差不多了。贸易基本上可以在没有关税或配额的情况下继续。海关检查和收费会带来一些摩擦，但与世界贸易组织规定（如果没有达成协议）的10%关税的威胁相比，微不足道。

“Rules of origin” requirements are more troublesome. These aim to stop companies importing goods from a third country, and re-exporting them as if they were domestically produced. For the purpose of tariffs, the TCA treats Britain and the EU as a single bloc, but excludes countries with which both have a trade deal, such as Japan. By 2027 the percentage of components by value originating from outside Britain or the EU must be cut to 45% for most cars, if tariffs are to be avoided. Manufacturers that sell in the EU and have supply chains that extend into Asia thus have a choice: shift from Britain, or double down on it. They are making different calls. Nissan is investing in Britain; Honda is closing its Swindon factory next year.

“原产地规则”的要求更麻烦。这些措施旨在阻止企业从第三国进口商品，并将进口商品当作国内生产的商品再出口。就关税而言，TCA将英国和欧盟视为一个单一集团，但不包括与英国和欧盟都有贸易协议的国家，比如日本。到2027年，如果要避免征收关税，大多数汽车必须将来自英国或欧盟以外的零部件的价值比例降至45%。因此，在欧盟销售产品并拥有延伸到亚洲的供应链的制造商有一个选择:从英国转移，或者加倍下注于英国。他们正在做出不同的决定。日产正在英国投资;本田将于明年关闭其在Swindon的工厂。

All this coincides with industry-wide reinvention, as carmakers shift to electric vehicles. Batteries, like the engines of petrol-fuelled cars, are expensive. “If you are not sourcing the batteries domestically, I don’t see how you can stay compliant,” says a production manager. Britain currently lacks a so-called “gigafactory”—a word coined by Elon Musk of Tesla—to mass-produce them.

背景：Tesla 的使命是通过提升电动车和能源产品的经济性来加速世界向可持续能源转变。为了实现年产 500,000 辆车的计划，Tesla 自身的需求量就已经达到全球的锂离子电池供应量。Tesla Gigafactory 应时而生，将提供足够的电池来支持 Tesla 所预计的电动汽车的需求。

随着汽车制造商转向电动汽车，所有这一切都与整个行业的转型不谋而合。电池就像汽油燃料汽车的引擎一样，价格昂贵。一位生产经理说:“如果你不从国内采购电池，我不知道你怎么能保持合规。”英国目前缺乏所谓的“gigafactory”（特斯拉的马斯克创造的一个词）来大规模生产电池。

Nissan’s planned new plant, a joint venture with Envision AESC, a Chinese firm, will be able to produce 9 gigawatt-hours (GWh) annually by the mid-2020s, enough to power 100,000 cars. Capacity could reach 25 GWh by 2030. Britishvolt, an independent consortium of battery manufacturers, plans capacity of 30 GWh by the end of the decade. But both together would not reach the 60 GWh that the Society of Motor Manufacturers and Traders, a trade body, reckons will be needed by 2030 if as many cars are to be made then as now.

gigawatt 千兆瓦

日产计划的新工厂（与中国的远景动力的合资企业）到2020年代中期，每年将能够生产9千兆瓦时(GWh)，足以为10万辆汽车提供动力。到2030年，产能可能达到25千兆瓦时。Britishvolt（一个由电池制造商组成的独立财团）计划在2030年之前达到30千兆瓦时的发电能力。但是两者加起来都达不到汽车制造商和贸易协会(一个贸易组织)估计的到2030年所需的60千兆瓦时，如果那时的汽车产量和现在一样多的话。

In the upheaval, Britain’s government spies an opportunity to influence carmakers’ thinking about where to invest. The “super deduction”, an especially generous time-limited tax break announced in the budget in March, will let firms reduce their tax bills by up to 25p for every pound of capital investment this financial year and next. Billed as a pandemic-recovery measure, it is better seen as a “huge subsidy to cover the costs of supply chains adapting to Brexit”, says an accountant. Cash is also being splashed. Nissan and its partners will receive around £100m of direct subsidy for the new gigafactory, on top of £80m from Sunderland city council to build an energy grid connecting it to wind farms and solar parks. Such measures would have been possible while Britain was in the EU—but might not have been needed.

在这场动荡中，英国政府发现了一个影响汽车制造商投资方向的机会。“超级减免”（在3月份的预算安排中宣布的一项特别慷慨的有时间限制的税收减免）将使企业在本财年和下一财年的每一磅的资本投资中减少至多25便士的税单。一名会计师表示，作为一项疫情复苏措施，它最好被视为“为弥补供应链为适应英国脱欧花费的成本而提供的巨额补贴”。现金也正在背挥霍。日产及其合作伙伴将获得约1亿英镑的直接补贴，用于新gigafactory的建设，另外还有桑德兰市议会提供的8000万英镑，用于建设连接风力发电场和太阳能园区的能源电网。在英国还在欧盟的时候，这些措施也是可能的，但可能没有必要。

Power play

Rather more pleasingly for those who hoped Brexit would mean less red tape, rather than more handouts, climate-change targets and new technologies are providing a pretext. Carmakers think the British government will be nimbler and more forward-looking when it comes to regulating electric and autonomous vehicles. European regulators, who must hold the ring between 27 countries, always move slowly. Moreover, they are bound to be heavily lobbied by the big German carmakers, for whom the industry’s reinvention is a serious threat. Industry bosses point to the fact that, whereas Britain has already pledged to ban the sale of new diesel- and petrol-powered cars by 2030, the EU is still only debating banning them from 2035. Big car firms are pressing it to move slower still.

diesel 柴油

对于那些希望英国脱欧意味着更少的繁文缛节而不是更多施舍的人来说，更令人高兴的是，气候变化目标和新技术正提供了一个借口。汽车制造商们认为，在监管电动汽车和自动驾驶汽车方面，英国政府将更加灵活、更具前瞻性。欧洲的监管机构必须掌控27个国家之间的关系，他们总是行动缓慢。此外，他们肯定会受到德国大型汽车制造商的大力游说，对它们来说，汽车行业的重塑将会是一个严重的威胁。行业巨头指出，尽管英国已经承诺到2030年禁止销售新的柴油和汽油提供动力的汽车，但欧盟仍在讨论从2035年开始禁止。大型汽车公司正敦促它放慢脚步。

Overall, Brexit is still likely to be negative for Britain’s economy, reducing potential growth. But subsidies, tax breaks and fewer barriers to innovation mean that for carmakers, life outside the bloc will be cosier than they once feared. ■

总体而言，英国脱欧仍可能对英国经济产生负面影响，降低潜在增长。但补贴、减税和创新障碍的减少意味着，对汽车制造商来说，欧盟以外的生活将比他们曾经担心的要舒适。■
