Who needs expats?

expatriate 移居国外的

谁需要外籍人士？

Bartleby

巴托比

![image-20210922092552778](/Users/chenyujie/Library/Application Support/typora-user-images/image-20210922092552778.png)

The case for shuffling staff around was looking stretched even before the pandemic

甚至在疫情之前前，调动员工的理由就显得有些牵强了

IF CHIEF EXECUTIVES are the monarchs of the corporate world, the cadre of well-paid staff they deploy from head office to oversee operations across the planet are their ambassadors. In the golden era of globalisation, sending an expatriate Western executive to a distant emerging market signalled the place was being taken seriously. That model was starting to feel out of date before covid-19 made foreign travel a misery. As Zoom and remote work have become the norm, is shuffling emissaries across the world even worth it anymore?

cadre 骨干队伍; 干部

emissary 密使

如果说首席执行官是企业世界的君主，那么他们从总部派来监督全球运营的高薪员工干部就是他们的大使。在全球化的黄金时代，派遣一名外籍西方高管到一个遥远的新兴市场，表明这个地方正在受到重视。在covid-19让出国旅行变得痛苦之前，这种模式已经开始过时了。随着Zoom和远程工作已经成为常态，在世界各地调遣使者还值得吗?

Some 280m people live in a country other than their own, often because of their job. Many toil on building sites in the Gulf or mind brats in Manhattan. High-flying expats are the most cosseted of these migrants. Their exalted status, should it need to be pinned down, is secured by snagging a housing allowance, school fees for the brood, annual flights home and a healthy salary bump. Some business folk have turned into perennial business wanderlusters, making a career out of flitting from Mumbai to Abu Dhabi to Lagos.

brat 顽童

cosset 溺爱

High-flying 很有抱负的

exalt 高度赞扬；提拔；提升

flit 频繁变化

大约有2.8亿人生活在国外，这通常是因为他们的工作。许多人在波斯湾地区的建筑工地上辛苦劳作，或者在曼哈顿照看孩童。在这些移民中，雄心勃勃的外国人是最受宠爱的。他们的崇高地位，如果需要明确说明的话，是通过获得住房补贴、子女的学费、每年回家的机票和健康的加薪来确保的。一些商人变成了常年的商业游民，以从孟买到阿布扎比再到拉各斯的频繁飞行为职业。

The business case for expats had started to look stretched in recent years. Moving staff to the ends of the Earth made sense when it was tricky to find globally minded (and English-speaking) employees over there. But globalisation has worked its magic. If an American investment bank in Shanghai wants a bright number-cruncher with a top-tier MBA, it has plenty of local candidates to choose from. They will cost a fraction of what it would take to move a transplant—and already speak the language.

number-cruncher 捣弄数字者

近年来，外派人员的商业理由开始显得牵强。当很难在地球的另一端找到具有全球视野(并会说英语)的员工时，把员工调到地球的另一端是有道理的。但是全球化已经发挥了它的魔力。如果一家在上海的美国投资银行想要一个拥有顶级MBA学位的聪明的数字天才，那么它有很多本地候选人可供选择。他们只需花费员工迁移所需的一小部分，而且他们已经会说这种语言了。

Getting head-office staff to up sticks for Jakarta has been getting harder, too. In decades past a compliant “trailing spouse” took on the responsibility of keeping a household running in far-flung places. Now she (as is still more often the case) is likelier to quibble about the impact on her own career. A survey carried out by the Boston Consulting Group found 57% of workers globally were willing to move to a foreign country for work in 2018, down from 64% four years earlier.

far-flung 遥远的

a quibble about or over sth 对某事物的抱怨

让总部员工为雅加达工作也变得越来越困难。在过去的几十年里，顺从的“随行配偶”承担起了在遥远的地方维持家庭运转的责任。现在，她（更常见的情况是）更有可能对自己的职业生涯受到的影响吹毛求疵。波士顿咨询集团进行的一项调查发现，2018年，全球57%的员工愿意去国外工作，这一比例低于四年前的64%。

The figure fell even further, to 50%, once the pandemic hit. Many expat haunts, such as Hong Kong, Singapore and Dubai, went through looser lockdowns than America or Europe. But that often meant throttling foreign travel or imposing weeks-long quarantines for returnees. The prospect of a trip to see family for Christmas, or of a weekend getaway to Bali, is part of what makes living in Singapore attractive. Once that goes the trade-off between career and personal life starts looking uncomfortably different.

getaway 短假

疫情爆发后，这一数字进一步下降至50%。许多外国人常去的地方，如香港、新加坡和迪拜，都经历了比美国或欧洲更宽松的封锁。但这通常意味着限制出国旅行或对回国人员实施长达数周的隔离。在新加坡生活有吸引力的部分原因是，你可以在圣诞节去看望家人，或者周末去巴厘岛度假。一旦这种情况消失，职业和个人生活之间的取舍就开始出现令人不安的不同。

Many foreigners who had once been given royal treatment felt treated like second-class citizens. Some hesitated to leave their country of assignment for fear of not being able to get back in. Others had to wait longer than locals for vaccines. Clubby communities no longer made space for outsiders. As XX, once the spiritual home of expatdom, has fallen into XX’s ambit, Western imports have started to look like a vestige of the colonial past.

Clubby 排外的

ambit 范围

许多曾经享受过皇家待遇的外国人感到自己被当作二等公民对待。有些人犹豫是否要离开他们的国家，因为他们害怕无法再回来。其他人则不得不比当地人等待更长的时间才能接种疫苗。排外的社区不再为外人腾出空间。XX曾经是外国人的精神家园，如今已落入XX的控制范围，西方的“进口”开始看起来像是殖民历史的残留部分。

This speaks to a broader economic shift that has dented the need for expats. Once upon a time they used to be the ones able to facilitate access to foreign capital and know-how, often from Western sources. Now money is abundant and the most exciting business opportunities are emerging markets doing business with other emerging markets, particularly in Asia. You don’t need a Westerner to show you how to do that. The world they understand is no longer as relevant.

dent 削减

know-how 实际知识

这反映了一种更广泛的经济变化，这种变化削弱了对外派人员的需求。曾几何时，他们曾能够为获得外国资本和技术(通常来自西方)提供便利。现在资金充裕，最令人兴奋的商机是新兴市场与其他新兴市场(尤其是亚洲)开展业务。你不需要一个西方人来告诉你怎么做。他们所理解的世界已不再重要。

Expats are not just expensive perk-baggers (as this stand-in Bartleby, a foreign correspondent in his day job, can attest). Companies have cultures and processes which are forged at headquarters, and which envoys can disseminate. They in turn will imbibe new ways of doing things that can be transferred back to other bits of the business. Having an outsider somewhere in the organisational chart of a distant subsidiary can provide reassurance no funny business is taking place there. However, penny-pinching bosses might now consider whether regular Zoom calls will not achieve much the same thing for a fraction of the cost—not least if employees the world over are going to be working from home at times anyway.

stand-in 替补的；替身的；临时的

imbibe 吸收；接受

penny-pinching 吝啬的

外派人员不仅仅是昂贵的福利领取者(作为巴托比的替代者，他是一名驻外记者，可以证明这一点)。公司的文化和流程是在总部形成的，特使可以传播这些文化和流程。反过来，他们将吸收新的做事方式，这些方式可以转移到业务的其他部分。让一个“局外人”出现在一家遥远的子公司的组织结构图的某个地方，可以让人放心，那里不会发生什么不正当的事情。然而，精打细算的老板们现在可能会考虑，是否只花一小部分的成本的定期拨打Zoom电话不能达到同样的效果，尤其是如果世界各地的员工都时不时地在家工作的话。

The surest way to signal commitment to a market these days is not by importing top talent but by nurturing it locally. Many companies that proudly deployed expats now boast of appointing local bosses to head up each country. That isn’t a reversal of globalisation so much as an affirmation of it.

head up 领导

如今，向一个市场传递承诺的最可靠方式不是引进顶级人才，而是在当地培养。许多曾自豪地派遣外派人员的公司现在都以任命当地老板来领导每个“国家”而自豪。这与其说是对全球化的逆转，不如说是对全球化的肯定。

Britons are snapping up electric vehicles

snap up 抢购

英国人正在抢购电动汽车

Heading for gridlock

面临交通大堵塞【陷入僵局】

![image-20210921122845715](/Users/chenyujie/Library/Application Support/typora-user-images/image-20210921122845715.png)

That is good news for the climate—but a problem for the Treasury

这对气候来说是个好消息，但对财政部来说却是个问题

THE CARS on Britain’s roads are changing. According to the World Economic Forum, a global think-tank, more than a tenth of new cars sold in 2020 were powered by electricity. That is below the levels seen in some other parts of northern Europe, but four times as high as in America. And the shift from fossil fuels to electricity looks set to accelerate. Government plans to decarbonise the economy will require the number of battery-powered electric vehicles (EVs) on Britain’s roads to rise from around 100,000 currently to 3m by 2025, 10m by 2030 and 25m by 2035.

英国道路上的汽车正在发生变化。根据全球智库世界经济论坛的数据，2020年售出的新车中有超过十分之一是由电力驱动的。这低于北欧其他一些地区的水平，但这一数字却是美国的四倍。而且，从化石燃料到电力的转变看来将会加速。政府的经济脱碳计划要求英国道路上的电动汽车数量从目前的10万辆增加到2025年的300万辆，2030年的1000万辆，2035年的2500万辆。

This rapid shift is necessary if Britain is to meet international obligations to reduce its carbon emissions. But an analysis by the Tony Blair Institute for Global Change, a think-tank, highlights three looming risks: a fiscal hole, increased inequality and more traffic jams. All three result from the way driving is taxed in Britain, namely largely through a levy on fuels. This makes driving an EV far cheaper than alternatives. The median British driver currently spends around £1,100 ($1,520) a year in total on fuel and vehicle excise duty (VED), a tax levied on most personal vehicles, of which around £750 is tax. But the typical owner of a battery-powered EV, which is exempt from VED, spends only £320 a year, of which just £20 is tax.

excise duty 消费税

如果英国要履行减少碳排放的国际义务，这种快速转变是必要的。但智库托尼·布莱尔全球变化研究所的一项分析强调了三个迫在眉睫的风险:财政漏洞、日益加剧的不平等和交通堵塞加剧。这三种情况都是英国对驾驶方式征税的导致的，主要是对燃料征税。这使得驾驶电动汽车比其他汽车便宜得多。目前，英国司机每年花在燃油税和车辆消费税(对大多数个人车辆征收的税)上的费用中位数约为1100英镑(合1520美元)，其中约750英镑是税。但是一辆电池驱动的电动汽车的典型车主每年只花320英镑，其中只有20英镑是税。

![image-20210921122807527](/Users/chenyujie/Library/Application Support/typora-user-images/image-20210921122807527.png)

These much lower running costs encourage drivers to make the switch. But as often happens with “sin taxes”, this one is falling victim to its own success. Part of the point of sin taxes is to encourage people to become more virtuous, but the faster this happens, the faster revenues dry up. By 2030 the annual loss in tax revenues from VED and fuel duty is forecast to be around £8bn, or 0.3% of GDP. That is around two-thirds as much as was raised by the politically contentious increase in national insurance, a payroll tax, announced on September 7th. The cumulative cost to public coffers is due to zoom past £50bn around the end of the decade (see chart).

fall victim to 受…的伤害 ‹disease, developers›

dry up 耗尽

contentious 有争议的

coffers 金库

the coffers of the state 国库

zoom 急剧上升

这些低得多的行驶成本鼓励司机们做出改变。但正如“罪恶税”经常发生的那样，这一税收正成为其自身成功的牺牲品。罪恶税的部分意义在于鼓励人们变得更善良，但这种情况发生得越快，收入耗尽得越快。预计到2030年，车辆消费税和燃油税的年度税收损失将达到80亿英镑左右，或占GDP的0.3%。这大约是9月7日宣布的引起政治争议的“提高国民保险(一种工资税)”的三分之二。到本世纪末，国库的累计成本将飙升至500亿英镑以上(见图表)。

The savings will not be distributed evenly. Since wealthier households tend to have newer cars, it is they who are most likely to own EVs. Without a big overhaul, the coming years will see the burden of motoring taxes fall ever more heavily on less-well-off Britons.

储蓄不会被平均分配。由于较富裕的家庭往往拥有较新的汽车，所以他们最有可能拥有电动汽车。如果不进行大的改革，未来几年，汽车税的负担将越来越重地落在不太富裕的英国人身上。

The final problem predicted by the Institute for Global Change is increased congestion. Switching to an EV cuts the effective cost of fuel for driving an additional mile by around 70%, meaning that as EVs become more common, motorists are likely to drive more. The Department for Transport reckons that, unless motoring taxes are reformed, the coming two decades will see a 30% increase in passenger miles driven, and an increase from 7% to 12% in the share spent stuck in congestion.

全球变化研究所预测的最后一个问题是交通拥堵加剧。换用电动汽车每多行驶一英里，燃料的有效成本就会降低约70%，这意味着随着电动汽车变得越来越普遍，驾车者可能会更多地开车。交通部估计，除非汽车税进行改革，否则在未来的20年里，乘客驾车里程将增加30%，交通拥堵所占比例将从7%增加到12%。

The Treasury has long plugged the fiscal gap resulting from lower smoking rates by aggressively hiking tobacco duties. But fuel duty is far more politically sensitive. The Conservative Party enjoys higher support among car owners and has frozen the rate for the past decade.

长期以来，财政部一直通过大幅提高烟草税来填补吸烟率降低造成的财政缺口。但燃油税在政治上要敏感得多。保守党在车主中享有较高的支持率，并在过去十年中冻结了这一比率。

The most obvious solution would be to shift the base upon which driving is taxed, away from fuel and towards road usage. A system of road-pricing would charge the heaviest users the most. It could also allow for variable pricing in congested areas, which would help ease traffic jams. But such proposals are deeply unpopular with car-owning voters. With a general election on the horizon, perhaps as early as 2023, the Conservative government is unlikely to risk upsetting its core voters. Public policy, like Britain’s roads, faces gridlock. ■

on the horizon 即将发生

最明显的解决方案将是改变对驾驶征税的基础，从燃油转向道路使用。一个道路收费系统将向使用最多的人收取最多的费用。它还允许在拥堵地区实行可变定价，这将有助于缓解交通拥堵。但是这样的提议在拥有汽车的选民中非常不受欢迎。随着大选的临近，或许最早在2023年，保守党政府不太可能冒险惹恼关键选民。公共政策，就像英国的道路一样，面临着交通拥堵。■

How France tackled vaccine hesitancy

法国如何应对疫苗犹豫的情况

French covid-19 passports

法国新冠肺炎护照

![image-20210920115116254](/Users/chenyujie/Library/Application Support/typora-user-images/image-20210920115116254.png)

Covid-19 passports have proved efficient, and surprisingly popular

新冠肺炎护照被证明是有效的，而且出人意料地受欢迎

ACROSS EUROPE this summer, vaccine take-up began to slow after the willing got their covid-19 shots. Persuading the hesitant was always going to be harder. Few places looked more challenging than France. In December 2020 61% of the French said they would not get jabbed—twice the share in America. Yet what happened this summer has become a case study in how to nudge a reluctant population.

nudge 轻推；哄劝

今年夏天，在自愿接受covid-19疫苗接种后，整个欧洲的疫苗接种速度开始放缓。说服犹豫不决的人总是比较困难。没有几个地方比法国更具挑战性。到2020年12月，61%的法国人表示他们不会接种疫苗，这一比例是美国的两倍。然而，今年夏天发生的事情已经成为如何推动不情愿人群的一个案例研究。

On July 12th, to general surprise, President Emmanuel Macron announced the introduction of a covid-19 passport. Only those who were fully vaccinated, or had a negative test result, would be allowed into cinemas, sports stadiums, restaurants, bars, shopping centres and nightclubs, or on long-distance trains and flights. Employees in such places would also need the pass sanitaire—a QRcode, in either digital or paper form—or face suspension. Vaccination would be compulsory for health-care workers. Mr Macron was taking a big gamble. He risked further dividing the country, and provoking the sort of national rebellion for which the French are famed.

take a gamble 冒险从事（某事）

provoke 引起；激起

7月12日，出乎大家意料的是，法国总统马克龙宣布推出新冠肺炎护照。只有那些完全接种疫苗或检测结果呈阴性的人才被允许进入电影院、体育场、餐馆、酒吧、购物中心和夜总会，或乘坐长途火车和飞机。在这些地方工作的员工还需要获得通行证【法国出行健康码】，即二维码(数字或纸质形式)，否则会面临停职。卫生保健工作者必须接种疫苗。马克龙是在进行一场豪赌。他冒着进一步分裂国家的危险，挑起法国人闻名遐迩的民族叛乱。

The outcry was indeed instant. Politicians of all stripes denounced the measure as discriminatory. Marine Le Pen, who has remodelled herself as a champion of liberty (though she is not an anti-vaxxer), called it a “serious violation of individual freedom”, and voted against in parliament. A Green politician likened the covid-19 pass to apartheid. In the National Assembly, a heated debate on the measure lasted until dawn. On successive Saturdays throughout the summer, up to 240,000 demonstrators took to the streets, accusing Mr Macron of running a dictatorship, and worse.

outcry 强烈的抗议

stripe 类型

people of all stripes 形形色色的人

to liken sb/sth to …; 把某人/某事物比作…

apartheid 【南非的】种族隔离

强烈抗议确实是来得及时。形形色色的政客都谴责这项措施是歧视性的。Marine Le Pen将自己塑造为自由捍卫者(尽管她不是反疫苗者)，她称这是“对个人自由的严重侵犯”，并在议会投了反对票。一名绿党政客将新冠疫情的通行证比作种族隔离。在国会，围绕该法案的激烈辩论一直持续到黎明。整个夏天，连续几个星期六，多达24万名示威者走上街头，指责马克龙实行独裁统治，甚至更糟。

Yet despite this rumpus the majority of the French masterfully demonstrated that it is usually a mistake to believe what they say in polls. In the hours following Mr Macron’s announcement, over 1m people booked vaccination appointments. In the four weeks that followed, when the French are usually lying on the beach, nearly 10m extra people got a first jab. Even the controversy proved short-lived. By the end of August 77% of the French told a poll they approved of the pass for travel, and 64% did for access to restaurants and bars. Only 34% supported the anti-pass demos.

rumpus 喧闹；吵闹

然而，尽管出现了这种骚动，但大多数法国人巧妙地证明，相信他们在民调中的言论通常是错误的。在马克龙宣布后的几个小时内，超过100万人预约接种疫苗。在随后的四周里，当法国人通常躺在沙滩上时，又有近1000万人接受了第一次疫苗注射。甚至这场争论也被证明是短暂的。在8月底的一项民意调查中，77%的法国人表示他们支持该通行证，64%的人支持进入餐馆和酒吧的措施。只有34%的人支持反对通行证的示威游行。

Mr Macron’s bet was only partly about the intrinsic value of a covid-19 pass. Indeed anecdotal evidence suggests that checks on implementation have been light, rather than punitive. Nor is it a full vaccine mandate: tests remain an alternative to a jab, although from October 15th these will no longer be free. The hope rather was that the pass would provide un nudge to accelerate vaccination take-up, in order to ease pressure on hospitals as the Delta variant spread. In this sense, it has been spectacularly successful. By September 15th 74% of the population (and 88% of the over 12s) had received at least one dose, up from 54% on the day of Mr Macron’s announcement. This figure is now higher than in America, Britain and Germany.

![image-20210919122833482](/Users/chenyujie/Library/Application Support/typora-user-images/image-20210919122833482.png)

As children go back to in-person lessons in America some innovations will stay

在美国，当孩子们回到课堂上时，一些创新将会留下来

Teachers suddenly became more willing to use technology because the alternative was not to teach. Much of this technology will remain as pupils head back to the classroom this term. But the experience has refined what edtech is really for.

教育技术公司Sanoma Learning的前领导John Martin说，COVID-19迫使学校十年的数字转型在一个月内完成。老师们突然变得更愿意使用科技，因为如果不使用科技，另一种选择就是不教书。当学生们在本学期返回教室时，这些技术中的大部分将会保留下来。但这种经历已经完善了教育技术的真正用途。

School closures also forced entrepreneurs to grapple with a stark truth: few are interested in completely disrupting the classroom. For decades innovators imagined a future without traditional learning. MOOCs (massive open online courses), like Udacity and Coursera, were supposed to replace in-person learning. Teachers and school administrators feared that technologists were aiming to replace them. Before the pandemic, most American schools were hesitant to adopt technology, says Jean Hammond, co-founder of LearnLaunch Accelerator, a startup programme. “Lots of cool, amazing little things would come along. come along 出现

学校关闭还迫使企业家们努力应对一个无情的事实:很少有人对彻底扰乱课堂感兴趣。几十年来，创新者们一直在设想一个没有传统学习的未来。像Udacity和Coursera这样的慕课(大规模开放在线课程)本应取代面对面教学。教师和学校管理人员担心技术人员的目标是取代他们。创业项目LearnLaunch Accelerator的联合创始人Jean Hammond表示：“在疫情之前，大多数美国学校对采用科技持犹豫态度。会有很多很酷、很神奇的小事情出现。但由于……学校没有接受过如何采用新技术的培训，改变非常缓慢。”

Tech entrepreneurs “put the tech before the ed” before the pandemic, explains Mr Martin. Innovators have since grasped that their technology must support teachers in the classroom rather than attempt to remove them, he explains. Much of the $2.2bn in venture and private-equity capital raised in 2020 is being invested with this in mind.

Martin解释说，在疫情之前，科技企业家“把科技放在教育之前”。他解释说，自那以后，创新者意识到，他们的技术必须支持课堂上的教师，而不是试图把他们赶走。在2020年筹集的22亿美元的风投和私募股权资本中，有很大一部分都是考虑到这一点进行投资的。

Teacher training is one example of what this means in practice. Traditional teacher training is limited. “When teachers learn, they listen to people talk about teaching, and they talk with each other about teaching. They very rarely teach things,” explains Justin Reich of the Teaching Systems Lab at the Massachusetts Institute of Technology. “If you look at how nurses are trained, how therapists are trained, how clergy members are trained, they practise what they’re doing all the time.”

教师培训就是“这意味着什么”的一个实践中的例子。传统的教师培训是有限的。麻省理工学院教学系统实验室的 Justin Reich解释道，“当教师学习时，他们听人们谈论教学，他们互相谈论教学。他们很少教东西，如果你看看护士是如何接受训练的，治疗师是如何接受训练的，神职人员是如何接受训练的，他们一直在做他们正在做的事情。”

This is starting to change. Researchers at the University of Virginia (UVA) are training their education students using virtual reality simulators created by Mursion, a tech company. The teacher trainees experience several virtual practice scenarios such as a parent-teacher conference, small-group instruction, and large-group instruction. A digital puppet master plays the role of the parent and pupils behind the scenes, but the developers plan for the programme to eventually become automated. The technology is currently being used in over 50 American colleges.

这种情况正在开始改变。弗吉尼亚大学的研究人员正在使用科技公司Mursion开发的虚拟现实模拟器对他们教育学生进行培训。教师学员将体验多个虚拟实践场景，如家长会、小组教学和大团体教学。家长和学生由一位幕后的数字木偶大师扮演，但开发人员计划最终实现自动化。这项技术目前在美国50多所大学使用。

Sarah Kiscaden, a teacher trainee at UVA, is pleased with the experience. “If we didn’t have this simulator, we would be learning everything in classes every day, and then be expected to just keep it all in our brain and apply it all at the same time, the next day in our schools. 弗吉尼亚大学的实习教师Sarah Kiscaden对这种经历感到满意。“如果我们没有这个模拟器，我们就会每天在课堂上学习所有东西，然后被要求把所有东西都记在脑子里，并在第二天在学校里同时应用所有东西。我认为，与你通过学习一种技能，然后你去实践并内化它的方式相比，这种期望要现实得多。” 这些模拟器也首次缓解了在孩子面前教学的压力。

Artificial intelligence could also play a role in teacher training. Researchers at UVA and Worcester Polytechnic Institute are using machine learning, a process by which a computer learns to perform a task by analysing examples, to evaluate videos of teachers in action. Currently teachers get feedback from fellow humans, often administrators, who come to the classroom, observe a lesson and score the teacher. The researchers hope eventually to use machine learning to automatically score teachers, making the current feedback process more frequent, more accurate and less time intensive.

人工智能也可以在教师培训中发挥作用。弗吉尼亚大学和伍斯特理工学院的研究人员正在使用机器学习技术，机器学习是通过分析实例，计算机学会执行任务，并对教师行动的视频进行评估。目前，老师从其他人那里得到反馈，通常是管理人员，他们来到教室，观察一节课并给老师打分。研究人员希望最终能使用机器学习来自动给教师打分，使当前的反馈过程更频繁、更准确，而且不需要太长时间。

Barriers remain for edtech, despite the path cleared by the pandemic. Ms Hammond explains that outdated regulations can limit classroom innovation. Some state standards are rigid, even specifying the number of hours that must be spent in a classroom. Edtech is also unusual in that the end users are not often the buyers.

尽管疫情为教育技术扫清了道路，但障碍仍然存在。Hammond女士解释说，过时的规章制度会限制课堂创新。有些州的教学标准很严格，甚至对学生在课堂上的学习时间做了详细的规定。教育科技的另一个不同寻常之处在于，终端用户往往不是买家。

The buyer might be the school district, the user might be the teacher, and the real user is the learner. “It’s very hard to get the learner voice into the mix of things,” explains Ms Hammond. And the purchasing usually occurs only once per academic year. Tyler Borek, co-founder of Literably, an edtech startup, says that this provides fewer opportunities to iterate. Still, much of the technology used during the pandemic—classroom devices, apps for parents to follow their child’s progress and so on—is here to stay.■

The effects of a warmer world are visible in animals’ bodies

全球变暖的影响可以在动物身上看到

Climate change and evolution

气候变化与进化

![image-20210917101411698](/Users/chenyujie/Library/Application Support/typora-user-images/image-20210917101411698.png)

Hundreds of species show signs of adapting to a warming climate

数百种物种显示出适应气候变暖的迹象

FOR HUMANS, adapting to climate change will mostly be a matter of technology. More air conditioning, better-designed houses and bigger flood defences may help ameliorate the effects of a warmer world. Animals will have to rely on changing their bodies or their behaviour. In a paper published in Trends in Ecology & Evolution, a team led by Sara Ryding, a PhD candidate at Deakin University, in Australia, shows that is already happening. Climate change is already altering the bodies of many animal species, giving them bigger beaks, limbs and ears.

ameliorate 改善

对人类来说，适应气候变化将主要是技术问题。更多的空调、更好的房屋设计和更大的防洪系统可能有助于改善全球变暖带来的影响。动物们将不得不依靠改变他们的身体或行为。在《Trends in Ecology & Evolution》期刊上发表的一篇论文中，由澳大利亚迪肯大学博士研究生Sara Ryding领导的团队表明，这种情况已经发生了。气候变化已经改变了许多动物物种的身体，使它们拥有更大的喙、四肢和耳朵。

In some species of Australian parrot, for instance, beak size has increased by between 4% and 10% since 1871. Another study, this time in North American dark-eyed juncos, another bird, found the same pattern. Similar trends are seen in mammals, with species of mice, shrews and bats evolving bigger ears, tails, legs and wings.

例如，自1871年以来，某些种类的澳大利亚鹦鹉的喙的大小增加了4%到10%。另一项研究，这次是在另一种鸟类（北美黑眼juncos）发现了同样的模式。在哺乳动物中也可以看到类似的趋势，老鼠、鼩鼱和蝙蝠等物种进化出更大的耳朵、尾巴、腿和翅膀。

All that dovetails nicely with evolutionary theory. “Allen’s rule”, named for Joel Asaph Allen, who suggested it in 1877, holds that warm-blooded animals in hot places tend to have larger appendages than those in temperate regions. Such adaptations boost an animal’s surface area relative to its body volume, helping it to shed excess heat. Being richly endowed with blood vessels, and not covered by insulating feathers, beaks make an ideal place for birds to dispose of heat. Fennec foxes, meanwhile, which are native to the Sahara desert, have strikingly large ears, especially compared with their Arctic cousins.

to dovetail with sth 与某物吻合

appendage 附器；附属物

所有这些都与进化论很好地吻合。“Allen’s rule”以1877年提出这一理论的Joel Asaph Allen的名字命名。该法则认为，生活在炎热地区的温血动物比生活在温带地区的温血动物有更大的附属物。这种适应性增长了动物相对于其身体体积的表面积，帮助它释放多余的热量。喙上有丰富的血管，没有隔绝的羽毛覆盖，是鸟类处理热量的理想场所。与此同时，原产于撒哈拉沙漠的耳廓狐有着惊人的大耳朵，尤其是与它们的北极近亲相比。

Ms Ryding examined museum specimens, comparing their bodies to those of their modern counterparts. She is not the first researcher to take that approach. But it is hard, when dealing with individual species, to prove that climate change was the cause of an anatomical alteration. All sorts of other factors, from changes in prey to the evolving reproductive preferences of males or females, might conceivably have been driving the changes.

Ryding女士检查了博物馆的标本，并将它们的身体与现代的同类进行了比较。她并不是第一个采用这种方法的研究人员。但在处理单个物种时，很难证明气候变化是解剖结构变化的原因。各种各样的其他因素，从猎物的变化到雄性或雌性不断进化的生殖偏好，都可能推动了这种变化。

Ryding女士表示，从更大的角度看问题会使模式更加清晰。她的团队结合了来自不同地方不同物种的数据。她说，除了生活在一个变暖的星球上，他们几乎没有共同点，气候变化是最合理的解释。

For now, at least, the increase is small, never much more than 10%. That may change as warming accelerates. Since any evolutionary adaptation comes with trade-offs, it is unclear how far the process might go. Bigger beaks might interfere with feeding, for instance. Larger wings are heavier, and bigger legs cost more energy to grow.

至少就目前而言，增幅很小，从未超过10%。随着气候变暖加速，这种情况可能会改变。由于任何进化适应都需要权衡利弊，尚不清楚这个过程会走多远。例如，更大的喙可能会妨碍进食。更大的翅膀更重，更大的腿需要更多的能量来成长。

And there are other ways to adapt, too. Researchers have already seen changes in the geographical range of many species, from insects to fish. Another evolutionary rule-of-thumb, Bergmann’s rule, holds that animals in hotter places tend to have smaller bodies, another way to boost the ratio between surface area and volume. Other animals may alter their behaviour as well as their bodies, such as by seeking shelter at hot times of the day.

还有其他的适应方式。研究人员已经发现，从昆虫到鱼类，许多物种的地理范围都发生了变化。另一个进化的经验法则，伯格曼法则，认为在更热的地方的动物往往有更小的身体，这是另一种增大表面积和体积比的方法。其他动物可能会改变他们的行为和他们的身体，比如通过在一天的炎热时间寻求庇护。

Studying a broader range of animals will help firm up exactly what is happening. Much of Ms Ryding’s data concern birds, with less information available for other taxa. But it seems clear that the world of the future is not just going to be hotter than humans are used to. The animals living in it will look different, too. ■

对更广泛的动物进行研究将有助于确定到底发生了什么。Ryding女士的大部分数据都是关于鸟类的，其他类群的信息较少。但很明显，未来的世界不仅会比人类习惯的更热。生活在里面的动物看起来也会不一样。■

The other pandemic

![image-20210916111357437](/Users/chenyujie/Library/Application Support/typora-user-images/image-20210916111357437.png)

It uses the same mRNA technology as some covid jabs

ONE SILVER lining to the covid-19 pandemic has been the speed with which effective vaccines have been developed. Victims of other pandemics have not been so lucky. Three decades of attempts to create a vaccine against HIV, the virus that causes AIDS, have proved fruitless. The latest setback came on August 31st, when an experimental vaccine produced by Johnson & Johnson, an American pharmaceutical firm, flunked a clinical trial. One obstacle is HIV’s genetic slipperiness. The virus has a high mutation rate, which helps it adapt to evade both natural immune systems and artificial vaccines.

covid-19大流行病的一线希望是有效疫苗的研发速度。其他流行病的受害者就没有这么幸运了。三十年来，人们一直试图研制出一种对抗HIV(导致艾滋病的病毒)的疫苗，但都没有成效。最近的一次挫折发生在8月31日，当时美国制药公司强生生产的一种实验性疫苗在临床试验中失败。其中一个障碍是HIV基因的不稳定性。该病毒具有高突变率，这有助于它适应自然免疫系统和人工疫苗。

Undaunted, Moderna, a firm based in Massachusetts that has recently found fame by quickly coming up with a viable covid-19 jab, is planning to start human trials of a novel vaccine against HIV. Its researchers hope that the mRNA technology used to produce its covid-19 jab will succeed against HIV too, by creating a vaccine which the virus cannot easily dodge.

Undaunted 不屈不挠的

viable 切实可行的

总部位于马萨诸塞州的Moderna公司百折不挠，正计划开始对一种新型艾滋病毒疫苗进行人体试验。该公司最近因迅速研发出一种可行的covid-19疫苗而出名。该公司的研究人员希望，用于生产covid-19疫苗的mRNA技术也能成功对抗艾滋病毒，创造出一种病毒无法轻易躲避的疫苗。

Moderna’s approach is based in part on work by the International AIDS Vaccine Initiative (IAVI), a charity, and Scripps Research, a not-for-profit institute in San Diego. A joint study published in February showed, for the first time in humans, that it is possible to stimulate activity in immune cells called germline B-cells, which can produce things called broadly neutralising antibodies (bnAbs) against HIV.

Moderna的方法部分基于慈善机构国际艾滋病疫苗倡议协会(IAVI)和圣地亚哥非营利机构Scripps Research的工作。今年2月发表的一项联合研究首次表明，人类有可能促进称为生殖B细胞的免疫细胞的活动，这种细胞可以产生针对艾滋病毒的广泛中和抗体(bnAbs)。

Antibodies are proteins produced by the immune system in response to infection. Through a process of quick-fire trial and error in response to particular pathogen molecules, known as antigens, the body creates specialised molecules designed either to gum up the workings of pathogens, or to flag them for destruction by other parts of the immune system. But most antibodies turned out in response to HIV fail to stem the infection for the same reason vaccines do—the virus’s rapid mutation rate allows it to evolve to avoid them.

quick-fire 一连串的

gum up 使运转不灵；搞糟

抗体是免疫系统对感染作出反应而产生的蛋白质。通过对特定病原体分子(即抗原)的一连串试验和错误反应过程，人体产生了专门的分子，这些分子要么破坏病原体的工作，或者标记它们以供免疫系统的其他部分破坏。但大多数抗体对艾滋病毒的反应都未能阻止感染，原因与疫苗相同——病毒的快速突变率使其进化以避免感染。

As their name suggests, bnAbs are less susceptible to such countermeasures. In the case of HIV, the antigen they recognise is part of a viral protein called gp120. This is the means by which HIV particles “dock” with their target cells prior to infecting them. The part of the protein that does the docking is so well tailored that almost any mutation will make it less effective. Since gp120 is trapped in an evolutionary corner, it is a desirable target for a vaccine.

countermeasure 对策

顾名思义，bnAbs不太容易受到此类对策的影响。就HIV而言，它们识别的抗原是一种叫做gp120的病毒蛋白的一部分。这是HIV粒子在感染目标细胞之前与目标细胞“对接”的方法。蛋白质中进行对接的部分经过了精心剪裁，几乎任何突变都会降低对接的效率。由于gp120被困在进化的角落，它是一个理想的疫苗靶点。

Germline B-cells are diverse but rare. Each carries the blueprint of an antibody, and stands ready to do two things when alerted to the presence of a pathogen. The first is a process called somatic hypermutation, which generates daughter cells capable of producing many slightly different versions of that antibody. The second is clonal expansion, in which cells carrying the most successful of these variants multiply to deal with the infection.

生殖B细胞种类繁多，但数量稀少。每个人都携带着抗体的蓝图，并随时准备做两件事，以应对病原体的出现。第一个是一个被称为体细胞高频突变的过程，它产生的子细胞能够产生许多不同版本的抗体。第二种是克隆扩增，即携带这些变异中最成功的细胞增殖以应对感染。

The study conducted by IAVI and Scripps used a small engineered protein called eOD-GT8 60mer as an antigen. This protein resembles the cell-docking part of gp120. Crucially, empirical work in both mice and humans has shown that it also stimulates somatic hypermutation in germline B-cells carrying a broadly neutralising antibody called VRC01. That makes it a promising basis for a vaccine.

IAVI和Scripps进行的这项研究使用了一种名为eOD-GT8 60mer的小型工程蛋白作为抗原。这种蛋白质类似于gp120的细胞对接部分。至关重要的是，对小鼠和人类的实验研究表明，它还会促进携带一种广泛中和抗体VRC01的生殖系B细胞的体细胞高频突变。这使得它成为研制疫苗的一个有希望的基础。

The problem with this approach is that eOD-GT8 60mer is slow and expensive to make. Which is where Moderna’s technology comes in. mRNA is the messenger molecule that carries, from a cell’s chromosomes, instructions which tell that cell how to make particular proteins. Instead of carrying eOD-GT8 60mer itself, Moderna’s vaccine contains the mRNA instructions on how to make it, and leaves the job of production to the body’s own cells. Once the molecule enters the bloodstream, it should stimulate the VRC01-carrying germline B-cells to do their stuff. After that, booster shots employing mRNAs for other small antigenic proteins will be used to guide the process of hypermutation in the right direction.

这种方法的问题是eOD-GT8 60mer制造速度缓慢且昂贵。而这正是Moderna的技术发挥作用的地方。mRNA是一种信使分子，它携带细胞染色体上的指令，告诉细胞如何制造特定的蛋白质。Moderna的疫苗本身没有携带eOD-GT8 60mer，而是包含了如何制造它的mRNA指令，并将生产工作留给了身体自身的细胞。一旦这种分子进入血液，它应该会刺激携带VRC01的生殖系B细胞发挥作用。在此之后，利用mRNA对其他小抗原蛋白进行增强注射，以引导高突变过程朝着正确的方向发展。

The trial is an early one, designed to test safety and prove the principle. It is testing two approaches. One uses only the mRNA for eOD-GT8 60mer. The other adds mRNA for one of the small, guiding antigens to the shot. It will begin in America later this month, with 56 people. Trials in Rwanda and South Africa should follow shortly after. (UNAIDS, the body charged by the United Nations with combating HIV, estimates that two-thirds of the 38m people infected with the virus by the end of 2020 were in Africa.)

这是一个早期的试验，旨在测试安全性并证明原理。它正在测试两种方法。一个只使用eOD-GT8 60mer的mRNA。另一种是为其中一种小抗原添加mRNA，引导抗原进入注射。它将于本月晚些时候在美国开始，有56人参加。卢旺达和南非的试验也将很快进行。(联合国负责抗击艾滋病的机构联合国艾滋病规划署(UNAIDS)估计，到2020年底，3800万艾滋病毒感染者中有三分之二在非洲。)

The results from the American trial are expected in 2023. Beyond that, the timeline is less clear. Mark Feinberg, IAVI’s boss, citing how difficult HIV vaccine development still is, does not want people thinking that “just because the covid vaccines were developed using RNA technology in less than a year we’re going to have an HIV vaccine in less than a year”. After three decades of waiting, though, a little more patience is not too much to ask. ■

美国的试验结果预计将于2023年公布。除此之外，时间表就不那么明确了。国际艾滋病疫苗倡议协会的主席Mark Feinberg指出，艾滋病毒疫苗的研发仍然非常困难，他不希望人们认为“仅仅因为利用RNA技术在不到一年的时间里研出了新冠病毒疫苗，我们就会在不到一年的时间里研制出艾滋病毒疫苗”。不过，在30年的等待之后，我们需要再多一点耐心。■

But these anecdotes are not representative of the overall picture.

一些乐观主义者曾希望Delta变种的传播可能最终说服反对者接种疫苗，尽管结果令人遗憾。当地的新闻网站和电视台已经对曾持怀疑态度，现在却接种的人进行了描述。住在宾夕法尼亚中南部的道格拉斯一家一直对接种疫苗犹豫不决，直到“Delta变种改变了这一情况”。Columbus Dispatch写道，当地一家工厂的主管说:“真正把我解救出来的是Delta变种。”在俄克拉荷马州，急诊室护士Grace Zeiba告诉当地电视台，由于Delta变种的缘故，她决定“是时候接种疫苗了”。但这些轶事并不能代表整体情况。

One way of measuring whether people are more likely to get vaccinated when their neighbours are very ill with covid-19, is to compare county-level ICU capacities (which tell you whether a hospital is full of covid-19 patients) with the change in vaccination rates in the ensuing weeks. The Economist did this while controlling for potentially confounding variables, like state-level vaccination rates.

衡量人们是否更有可能在邻居患covid-19严重疾病时接种疫苗的一种方法是，比较县级重症监护室的容量(它告诉你一家医院是否挤满了covid-19患者)与随后几周疫苗接种率的变化进行比较。《经济学人》在这样做的同时，还控制了潜在的混杂变量，比如州级的疫苗接种率。

Our calculations show that full hospitals lead to only a slight increase in the number of people getting vaccinated. For every 10% decrease in available ICU beds, there were roughly 14 additional first doses administered per 100,000 people in a county the next week. For a median-sized American county with a population of 26,000, that translates to 3.5 additional first doses, or just half a dose per day.

我们的计算显示，医院的满员只会导致接种疫苗的人数略有增加。可使用的ICU床位每减少10%，接下来一周在一个县每10万人中就有大约14次额外的首次疫苗注射。对于一个人口2.6万的中等规模的美国县来说，这意味着要多注射3.5剂的第一针疫苗，或者每天只注射半剂。

Counties with ICUs that were 80% full or more saw only an additional 104 first doses administered per 100,000 people the next week, compared with counties where ICUs were 20% full or less. That is consistent with what happened this summer, when areas hit by the Delta variant saw only slight upticks in vaccination rates compared with other counties.

与ICU容量整20%或更少的县相比，ICU容量达到整80%或更多的县在接下来的一周中，每10万人中仅增加104次首次注射。这与今年夏天发生的情况是一致的，与其他县相比，Delta变种感染地区的接种率仅略有上升。

Polls paint the same picture. As many Americans have scrambled for futile cures like ivermectin, an anti-parasitic drug, interest in the most effective solution—the vaccine—is stuck. Polling from Morning Consult shows that the share of individuals who say they are unwilling to get the vaccine, or are uncertain if they will, has fallen only slightly—from 31% at the end of May (the month the World Health Organisation declared Delta to be a variant of concern) to 28% on August 30th. By contrast, the average share saying the same across the other 14 countries Morning Consult has surveyed fell from 25% to 14% (see chart).

民意调查也显示了同样的情况。当许多美国人都在努力寻找像伊维菌素(一种抗寄生虫药物)这样无效的治疗方法时，对最有效的解决方案（疫苗）的兴趣却停滞了。Morning Consult 的民调显示，表示不愿意接种疫苗或不确定是否愿意接种疫苗的人所占比例仅略有下降，从5月底（世界卫生组织宣布Delta为关注变种的那个月）的31%降至8月30日的28%。相比之下，在Morning Consult调查的其他14个国家中，持相同观点的平均比例从25%下降到了14%(见图表)。

![image-20210914104131178](/Users/chenyujie/Library/Application Support/typora-user-images/image-20210914104131178.png)

The remaining Americans who have not had their jabs are not just hesitant but rather hardened—committed to shunning the vaccine despite its availability, safety and efficacy. If the Delta variant, and the wards full of patients suffering from it in local hospitals, cannot change their minds, then it is hard to see what will. ■

shun 避开

剩余还没有注射疫苗的美国人，他们不仅犹豫不决，而且态度强硬，决心不顾疫苗的有效性、安全性和有效性，拒绝接种。如果Delta病毒变种，以及当地医院里满是病人的病房都不能改变他们的想法，那么就很难看到有什么能改变他们的想法了。■

Why nations that fail women fail

令女性失望的国家会失败

Sex and geopolitics

性别与地缘政治

![image-20210913114045008](/Users/chenyujie/Library/Application Support/typora-user-images/image-20210913114045008.png)

And why foreign policy should pay more heed to half of humanity

以及为什么外交政策应该更多关注一半的人类

AFTER AMERICA and its allies toppled the Taliban in 2001, primary-school enrolment of Afghan girls rose from 0% to above 80%. Infant mortality fell by half. Forced marriage was made illegal. Many of those schools were ropy, and many families ignored the law. But no one seriously doubts that Afghan women and girls have made great gains in the past 20 years, or that those gains are now in jeopardy.

ropy 差劲的; 体弱的

to be in jeopardy 处于危险中

在2001年美国及其盟友推翻塔利班政权后，阿富汗小学入学率从0%上升到80%以上。婴儿死亡率下降了一半。强迫婚姻被定为非法。许多学校都很差劲，许多家庭无视法律。但没有人真正质疑阿富汗妇女和女孩在过去20年取得的巨大成就，或者这些成就现在处于危险之中。

The United States is “committed to advancing gender equality” through its foreign policy, according to the State Department. Bequeathing billions of dollars-worth of arms and a medium-size country to a group of violent misogynists is an odd way to show it. Of course, foreign policy involves difficult trade-offs. But there is growing evidence that Hillary Clinton was on to something when she said, a decade ago, that “The subjugation of women is…a threat to the common security of our world.” Societies that oppress women are far more likely to be violent and unstable.

bequeath 遗赠；把..传下去

misogynist 厌恶女性的男人

to be on to sth/sb 注意某物/某人

subjugation 征服；克制；从属

美国国务院表示，美国将通过外交政策“致力于促进性别平等”。把价值数十亿美元的武器和一个中等规模的国家遗赠给一群残暴的厌恶女性的人，是一种奇怪的表现方式。当然，外交政策涉及到困难的权衡取舍。但越来越多的证据表明，希拉里·克林顿在十年前说过的话是有道理的，她说:“女性的屈服……对我们世界的共同安全构成了威胁。”压迫女性的社会更有可能充满暴力和不稳定。

There are several possible reasons for this. In many places girls are selectively aborted or fatally neglected. This has led to skewed sex ratios, which mean millions of young men are doomed to remain single. Frustrated young men are more likely to commit violent crimes or join rebel groups. Recruiters for Boko Haram and Islamic State know this, and promise them “wives” as the spoils of war. Polygamy also creates a surplus of single young men. Multiple wives for men at the top means brooding bachelorhood for those at the bottom.

polygamy 一夫多妻

bachelorhood 单身时期

这可能有几个原因。在许多地方，女孩被有选择性的堕胎或被致命地忽视。这导致了性别比例失调，这意味着数百万年轻男性注定要单身。沮丧的年轻人更有可能犯下暴力罪或加入反叛组织。博科圣地和伊斯兰国的招募人员知道这一点，并承诺他们“妻子”是战争的战利品。一夫多妻制还造成了单身年轻男性过剩的情况。社会地位高的男人拥有多个妻子，而社会地位低的男人却只能过着单身生活。

All conflicts have complex causes. But it may be no coincidence that Kashmir has one of the most unbalanced sex ratios in India, or that all of the 20 most turbulent countries on the Fragile States index compiled by the Fund for Peace in Washington practise polygamy. In Guinea, where a coup took place on September 5th, 42% of married women aged 15-49 are in polygamous unions. XX’s police state keeps a lid on its many surplus men, but its neighbours sometimes wonder whether their aggression may some day seek an outlet.

to keep a/the lid on sth 控制住某事物 ；对某事物保密

所有冲突都有复杂的原因。但是，克什米尔是印度性别比例最不平衡的国家之一，或者在华盛顿和平基金会编制的脆弱国家指数中，所有20个最动荡的国家都实行一夫多妻制，这也许不是巧合。在9月5日发生政变的几内亚，15-49岁的已婚女性中有42%是一夫多妻。XX的警察国家对其过剩的男性进行限制【不许一夫多妻制】，但其邻国有时会怀疑，他们的侵略是否有一天会寻求出路。

Outside rich democracies, the male kinship group is still the basic unit of many societies. Such groups emerged largely for self-defence: male cousins would unite to repel outsiders. Today, they mostly cause trouble. Tit-for-tat clan feuds spatter blood across the Middle East and the Sahel. Tribes compete to control the state, often violently, so they can divvy up jobs and loot among their kin. Those states become corrupt and dysfunctional, alienating citizens and boosting support for jihadists who promise to govern more justly.

Tit-for-tat 以牙还牙

divvy up 均分

loot 抢劫

jihadists 圣战组织成员

在发达的民主国家之外，男性亲属群体仍然是许多社会的基本单位。这些组织的出现主要是为了自卫:堂兄弟姐妹会联合起来排斥外人。如今，它们大多会带来麻烦。以牙还牙的家族纷争使整个中东和萨赫勒地区血流成河。各家族为了控制国家而相互竞争，通常是暴力的，这样他们就可以在他们的亲属之间分配工作和掠夺。这些国家变得腐败和失衡，疏远了公民，并增加了对“承诺更加公正治理的”圣战分子的支持。

Societies based on male bonding tend to subjugate women. Fathers choose whom their daughters will marry. Often there is a bride price—the groom’s family pay what are sometimes hefty sums to the bride’s family. This gives fathers an incentive to make their daughters marry early. It is not a small problem. Dowries or bride prices are common in half the world’s countries. A fifth of the world’s young women were married before the age of 18; a twentieth before 15. Child brides are more likely to drop out of school, less able to stand up to abusive husbands and less likely to raise healthy, well-educated children.

建立在男性纽带基础上的社会往往会压制女性。父亲选择女儿将嫁给谁。通常会有彩礼（新郎的家庭要付给新娘的家庭有时是一大笔钱）。这给了父亲们让女儿早婚的动力。这不是一个小问题。嫁妆或彩礼在世界上一半的国家都很普遍。世界上五分之一的年轻女性在18岁之前结婚; 十五岁之前结婚的占二十分之一。童养媳更有可能辍学，更没有能力面对虐待的丈夫，更不可能抚养出健康、受过良好教育的孩子。

Researchers at Texas A&M and Brigham Young universities compiled a global index of pre-modern attitudes to women, including sexist family laws, unequal property rights, early marriage for girls, patrilocal marriage, polygamy, bride prices, son preference, violence against women and legal indulgence of it (for example, can a rapist escape punishment by marrying his victim?). It turned out to be highly correlated with violent instability in a country.

德州农工大学和杨百翰大学的研究人员编制了一个现代前对女性态度的全球指数，包括性别歧视的家庭法律、不平等的财产权、女孩早婚、父权婚姻、一夫多妻、彩礼、重男轻女、对女性的暴力和法律纵容(例如，强奸犯能通过与受害者结婚来逃脱惩罚吗?) 结果证明，这与一个国家的暴力不稳定高度相关。

Various lessons can be drawn from this. In addition to their usual analytical tools, policymakers should study geopolitics through the prism of sex. That index of sexist customs, had it existed 20 years ago, would have warned them how hard nation-building would be in Afghanistan and Iraq. Today, it suggests that stability cannot be taken for granted in Saudi Arabia, Pakistan or even India.

prism 棱镜

我们可以从中吸取各种教训。除了常用的分析工具，政策制定者还应该通过性别棱镜来研究地缘政治。如果这个性别歧视习俗的指数在20年前就存在的话，它会提醒他们，阿富汗和伊拉克的国家建设是多么艰难。如今，它表明，在沙特阿拉伯、巴基斯坦甚至印度，稳定不能被视为理所当然。

Peace talks should include women. Between 1992 and 2019, only 13% of negotiators and 6% of signatories of peace deals were female. Yet peace tends to last longer when women are at the table. This may be because they are more ready to compromise; or perhaps because a room without women implies a stitch-up between the men with guns without input from non-combatants. Liberia got this right and ended a ghastly civil war; Afghanistan’s new rulers have not.

stitch up 诬陷

和平谈判应该包括妇女。1992年至2019年期间，和平协议的谈判者中只有13%是女性，签署者中只有6%是女性。然而，当妇女坐在谈判桌前时，和平往往会持续更久。这可能是因为他们更愿意妥协;或者可能是因为一个没有女人的房间意味着没有非战斗人员参与下，持枪男子之间的算计。利比里亚的清楚认知，结束了一场可怕的内战; 阿富汗的新统治者却没有。

More broadly, governments should mean it when they say they want to liberate half of humanity. Educate girls, many of whom have quit school to work or marry since covid-19 impoverished their families. Enforce bans on child marriage and on female genital mutilation, hard though that is in remote villages. Do not recognise polygamy. Equalise inheritance rights. Teach boys not to hit women. Introduce public pensions, which undermine the tradition whereby couples are expected to live with the man’s parents, because the elderly have no other means of support.

更广泛地说，当政府说他们想解放一半的人类时，他们应该是认真的。让女孩接受教育，自covid-19使家庭陷入贫困以来，许多女孩辍学工作或结婚。强制执行“童婚和切割女性生殖器”的禁令，尽管这在偏远的村庄很难做到。不承认一夫多妻制。平等继承权。教导男孩不要打女人。引入公共养老金，这将破坏传统，即夫妻应该和男方的父母住在一起，因为老年人没有其他的赡养手段。

Most of these are tasks for national governments, but outsiders have some influence. Since Western donors started harping on about girls’ education, more girls have gone to school (primary enrolment has risen from 64% in 1970 to nearly 90% today). Campaigners against early marriage have prompted more than 50 countries to raise the minimum age since 2000. Boys need to learn about non-violence from local mentors, but ideas about how to design such programmes are shared through a global network of charities and think-tanks. Donors such as USAID and the World Bank have done a fair job of promoting property rights for women, even if their Afghan efforts are about to go up in smoke.

to harp on about sth; 喋喋不休地谈论某事

go up in smoke 化为乌有

这些任务大部分是各国政府的任务，但局外人也有一定的影响力。自从西方捐助者开始喋喋不休地谈论女孩的教育问题以来，越来越多的女孩开始上学(小学入学率从1970年的64%上升到今天的近90%)。自2000年以来，反对早婚的活动人士已经促使50多个国家提高了最低结婚年龄。男孩们需要从当地的导师那里学习非暴力，但如何设计这样的项目的想法可以通过慈善机构和智囊团的全球网络分享。美国国际开发署和世界银行等捐助方在促进妇女财产权方面做得相当不错，尽管它们在阿富汗的努力即将化为乌有。

The radical notion

激进的观念

Foreign policy should not be naive. Countries have vital interests, and need to deter foes. Geopolitics should not be viewed solely through a feminist lens, any more than it should be viewed solely in terms of economics or nuclear non-proliferation. But policymakers who fail to consider the interests of half the population cannot hope to understand the world. ■

外交政策不应过于天真。各国利益攸关，需要威慑敌人。地缘政治不应该仅仅从女性主义的角度来看待，也不应该仅仅从经济或核不扩散的角度来看待。但是，如果政策制定者不考虑一半人口的利益，就不可能理解这个世界。■
