The case for updating covid-19 vaccines for the Omicron variant

更新预防奥密克戎变种的新冠疫苗的理由

Omicron and immunity

奥密克戎和免疫

A new study puts the variant in a group apart from its predecessors

一项新的研究将这种变异与它的“前任们”[Delta病毒等]区分开来

Jan 15th 2022

文章音频

VARIANTS VARY, but how much? Since SARS-CoV-2 was first sequenced at the beginning of 2020 dozens of versions have been identified. Five have been designated “variants of concern” by the World Health Organisation (WHO). The latest is Omicron, which was given its name in November last year and looks set to become the dominant form of the virus almost everywhere.

变种各不相同，但有多少呢? 自2020年初首次对SARS-CoV-2进行测序以来，已经发现了数十个版本。其中五种被世界卫生组织指定为“关注变体”。最新的一种变种是奥密克戎，它在去年11月被命名，看起来几乎在所有地方都将成为该病毒的主要形式。

One question occupying both scientists and politicians is whether covid vaccines would work even better if they were updated to deal with novel variants. Up to and including Delta, first identified in India, and designated a variant of concern in May 2021, the answer has been “no”. But new research, which has mapped differences between all of the important versions of SARS-CoV-2, suggests that, although administering existing vaccines is still useful, Omicron is so different from the others that the answer might now be “yes”.

科学家和政界人士都面临的一个问题是，如果更新新冠疫苗以应对新的变异，它们是否会发挥更好的作用。直到包括Delta变种在内，答案一直是“不会”。Delta变种首次是在印度被发现，并于2021年5月被指定为“关注变体”。但一项新研究绘制出了SARS-CoV-2所有重要变种之间的差异，其表明尽管接种现有疫苗仍然有用，但奥密克戎与其他变种的差异很大，以至于现在的答案可能是“是的”。

Researchers in the Netherlands, co-ordinated by Rogier Sanders and Colin Russell of the University of Amsterdam and Dirk Eggink of the Dutch Public Health Institute, acquired blood samples collected from 51 unvaccinated people shortly after they had been infected with various versions of SARS-CoV-2. These included the original, ancestral, strain and the Alpha, Beta, Gamma and Delta variants. (Omicron samples were not available.) They then assessed the antibody response (“neutralising capacity”) of those samples against different antigens—in other words, different versions of the virus, again using the ancestral strain, Alpha, Beta, Gamma and Delta and, this time, Omicron.

荷兰的研究人员在阿姆斯特丹大学的Rogier Sanders和Colin Russell以及荷兰公共卫生研究所的Dirk Eggink的协调下，从51名感染了不同SARS-CoV-2变种后不久未接种疫苗的人身上采集了血液样本。这里包括包括原始的早期菌株和Alpha(英国)、Beta(南非)、Gamma(巴西)和Delta(印度)变种。(没有奥密克戎的样本。) 然后，他们评估了这些样本对不同抗原的抗体反应(“中和能力”)，换句话说，就是不同病毒变种，再次使用早期菌株，Alpha, Beta, Gamma和Delta，这次是Omicron(奥密克戎)。

By measuring the neutralising capacity of the serum of each participant against the various variants and applying what is known as a multidimensional scaling algorithm, Dr Sanders, Dr Russell, Dr Eggink and their colleagues were able to position both the viruses and the serum samples on an “antigenic map” (see chart). This is used to study how mutations make viruses more or less different from the ancestral strain, in a process called antigenic drift. In such maps, one unit of distance is equivalent to a two-fold change in neutralisation titre (a measure of the concentration of antibodies in the blood needed to neutralise a virus). Samples plotted closer together are more similar. Going by a measure conventionally used to group influenza viruses—a distance of three or fewer antigenic units—the researchers found that Omicron would count as the first of a new group of SARS-CoV-2. Whereas all widely circulating variants before it, being less than three units apart from each other, cluster as a single group, Omicron is more than five units away from all others.

通过测量每个参与者血清对各种变体的中和能力，并应用所谓的多维标度法, Sanders博士, Russell博士, Eggink博士以及他们的同事能够在“抗原图谱”上定位病毒和血清样本（见图表）。这被用来研究突变如何使病毒或多或少地不同于早期菌株，这个过程被称为抗原漂移。在这样的图谱中，一个单位的距离相当于两倍的中和滴度变化(血液中要中和病毒所需的抗体浓度的度量)。标绘在一起的样本更相似。根据一种传统的流感病毒分组方法，即三个或更少抗原单位的距离，研究人员发现，奥密克戎可以算作一个新的SARS-CoV-2组中的第一种变种。在此之前，所有广泛传播的变种，彼此之间的距离不足三个单位，聚为一个组，而Omicron与所有其他变种的距离超过五个单位。

A paper describing this research was uploaded to medRxiv, a research portal, on January 3rd, but has not yet been peer-reviewed. The authors also caution that their methods vary slightly from those conventionally used to map influenza. Nevertheless they conclude that “the change in neutralisation between Omicron and other variants of SARS-CoV-2, including the ancestral strain, is striking”. (A separate investigation, published on the same day in Cell, reaches a similar conclusion.) Dr Russell cautions that the study’s findings should not be interpreted to mean that existing vaccinations, designed for the ancestral strain, are ineffective. “Omicron’s substantial reduction in cross-reactivity with previous variants doesn’t mean that there is no reactivity. This is probably why boosting with current vaccines provides some protection,” he says.

1月3日，一篇描述这项研究的论文被上传到研究网站medRxiv，但尚未经过同行评审。这组作者还警告说，他们的方法与常规用于绘制流感图谱的方法略有不同。然而，他们得出的结论是，“Omicron与SARS-CoV-2的其他变体(包括其早期菌株)之间的中和的不同是惊人的”。(同一天发表在《Cell》期刊上的另一项调查得出了类似的结论。) Russell博士警告说，这项研究的发现不应该被解释为现有的针对早期菌株的疫苗无效。他说：“奥密克戎与之前的变种的交叉反应性的大幅降低并不意味着没有反应性。这可能就是为什么用当前的疫苗作为加强针可以提供一些保护。”

As more people catch Omicron or are vaccinated, so more of the population will have some protection than in the past. Other studies have also shown that the response of another part of the immune system, its T-cells, seems less affected by Omicron’s mutations. That is probably one reason why Omicron rarely causes severe disease and death in those who have acquired immunity through infection or vaccination. The other is that Omicron itself seems to be inherently less lethal. But, just as twice a year the WHO organises expert reviews of influenza viruses and issues recommendations on how to tweak vaccine compositions, Dr Russell proposes something similar for covid vaccines. For maximum efficacy, he suggests, vaccines should be updated as soon as possible. ■

随着越来越多的人感染奥密克戎或接种疫苗，更多的人将比过去得到一些保护。其他研究也表明，免疫系统的另一部分，即T细胞的反应似乎较少受到奥密克戎变种的影响。这可能是奥密克戎很少让“那些通过感染或接种疫苗获得免疫的人”引起严重疾病和死亡的原因之一。另一个原因是奥密克戎本身似乎天生就不那么致命。但是，正如世卫组织每年两次组织对流感病毒的专家会议并就如何调整疫苗成分提出建议一样，Russell博士也为新冠疫苗提出了类似的建议。他建议，为了最大限度地发挥功效，疫苗应该尽快更新。■

Remote work and the importance of writing

远程工作和写作的重要性

Bartleby

巴托比

The written word will flourish in the post-pandemic workplace

书面文字将在疫情后的工作场所蓬勃发展

Jan 15th 2022

文章音频

THE PANDEMIC has given a big shove to all forms of digital communication. Video-conferencing platforms have become verbs. Venture capitalists make their bets after watching virtual pitches. Products like Loom and mmhmm help workers send pre-recorded video messages to their colleagues. More than a third of Slack users each week are now “huddling”—using the product’s new audio feature to talk to each other. And all this is before the metaverse turns everyone into an avatar.

新冠大流行病极大地推动了所有形式的数字通信。视频会议平台已经成为动词。风投资本家在观看虚拟宣传后作出选择。像Loom和mmhmm这样的产品可以帮助员工向同事发送预先录制好的视频信息。现在每周有超过三分之一的Slack用户“聚在一起”——使用该产品的新音频功能互相交谈。这一切都发生在元宇宙把每个人都变成虚拟化身之前。

A workplace dominated by time on screens may seem bound to favour newer, faster and more visual ways of transmitting information. But an old form of communication—writing—is also flourishing. And not just dashed-off emails and entries on virtual whiteboards, but slow, time-intensive writing. The strengths of the written word have not been diminished by the pandemic era. In some ways they are ideally suited to it.*

一个被屏幕时间支配的工作场所似乎注定会青睐更新、更快、更直观的信息传递方式。但是一种古老的交流方式，即“写作”也在蓬勃发展。不仅仅是匆忙写下的电子邮件和虚拟白板上日志，而是缓慢、耗时的写作。书面文字的力量并未因新冠大流行时代而减弱。在某些方面，他们最适合它。

The value of writing is a staple in management thinking. “The discipline of writing something down is the first step toward making it happen,” reckoned Lee Iacocca, a quotable titan of the American car industry. Jeff Bezos banned slide decks from meetings of senior Amazon executives back in 2004, in favour of well-structured memos. “PowerPoint-style presentations somehow give permission to gloss over ideas,” he wrote.

写作的价值是管理思维的重要组成部分。美国汽车工业的著名巨头Lee Iacocca说：“把事情写下来是实现它的第一步”。早在2004年，贝佐斯就禁止亚马逊高管在会议上使用幻灯片，而青睐结构良好的备忘录。他写道:“ppt风格的演示在某种程度上允许人们掩饰自己的想法。”

Some executives write for themselves. Andrew Bosworth, a bigwig at Meta (formerly Facebook), has a blog in which he muses interestingly on many topics, including on writing itself: “In my experience, discussion expands the space of possibilities while writing reduces it to its most essential components.” Others do so to reach an audience. Shareholder letters from Larry Fink and Warren Buffett are the corporate equivalent of a blockbuster book launch.

一些高管自己写作。Meta(前身为Facebook)的大人物Andrew Bosworth有一个博客，他在其中对许多话题进行了有趣的思考，包括写作本身:“ 根据我的经验，讨论扩大了可能性的空间，而写作则将其缩减为最重要的组成部分。” 其他人这样做是为了吸引观众。Larry Fink和沃伦•巴菲特写给股东的信，相当于一场轰动的新书发布会。

But the move to remote working has enhanced the value of writing to the entire organisation, not just the corner office. When tasks are being handed off to colleagues in other locations, or people are working on a project “asynchronously”, meaning at a time of their choosing, comprehensive documentation is crucial. When new employees start work on something, they want the back story. When veterans depart an organisation, they should leave knowledge behind. Writing everything down sounds like an almighty pain. But so is turning up to a meeting and not having the foggiest what was decided last time out.

但向远程办公的转变提高了写作对“整个组织”的价值，而不仅仅是对角落办公室的价值。当任务被移交给其他地方的同事，或者是那些正在“异步”地处理一个项目的人们时，这意味着在他们选择的时间，全面的文档是至关重要的。当新员工开始做某件事时，他们想知道背后的故事。当退伍军人离开一个组织时，他们应该把知识留在身后。把所有的事情都写下来听起来像是一种巨大的痛苦。但出席会议，对上次会议的决定一无所知，也是一样很痛苦。

Software developers have already worked out the value of the written word. A research programme from Google into the ingredients of successful technology projects found that teams with high-quality documentation deliver software faster and more reliably. Gitlab, a code-hosting platform whose workforce is wholly remote, frames the secret of successful asynchronous working thus: “How would I deliver this message, present this work, or move this project forward right now if no one else on my team (or in my company) were awake?” Gitlab’s answer is “textual communication”. Its gospel is a handbook that is publicly available, stretches to more than 3,000 pages and lays out all of its internal processes.

软件开发人员已经算出书面文字的价值。谷歌对“成功的技术项目”要素的研究发现，拥有高质量文档的团队交付软件更快、更可靠。Gitlab是一个代码托管平台，它的工作人员完全是远程的，它提出了成功异步工作的秘密:“ 如果我的团队(或公司)中没有其他人醒着，我该如何传递这个消息，展示这个工作，或者现在推动这个项目向前发展?” Gitlab的答案是“文本交流”。它的准则是一本公开可得的3000多页的手册，其中列出了公司的所有内部流程。

The deliberation and discipline required by writing is helpful in other contexts, too. “Brainwriting” is a brainstorming technique, used by Slack among others, in which participants are given time to put down their ideas before discussion begins. Lists of corporate values can make greeting cards seem hard-hitting. But thoughtful codification of a firm’s culture makes more sense in hybrid and remote workplaces, where new joiners have less chance to meet and observe colleagues.

写作所需要的深思熟虑和纪律性在其他情况下也很有帮助。“书面头脑风暴”是一种头脑风暴技术，Slack等公司都使用这种技术。在讨论开始前，参与者有时间把自己的想法写下来。企业价值观的列表可以让贺卡看起来很有冲击力。但在混合型和远程办公的环境中，深思熟虑的企业文化编纂更有意义，因为新入职者很少有机会见到和观察同事。

Purists will sniff that none of this counts as writing. But good prose and useful prose share the same essential qualities: brevity, structure, a clear theme. Cormac McCarthy, a prize-winning novelist, copy-edits scientific papers for fun. Ted Chiang says that his science-fiction short stories and his technical writing both draw on a desire to explain an idea clearly.

纯粹主义者会嗤之以鼻，这些都算不上写作。但是好的散文和有用的散文都有相同的本质特征: 简洁、结构清晰、主题明朗。获奖小说家Cormac McCarthy以编辑科学论文为乐。Ted Chiang说，他的科幻短篇小说和他的技术写作都有一种愿望，那就是清楚地解释一个想法。

Writing is not always the best way to communicate in the workplace. Video is more memorable; a phone call is quicker; even PowerPoint has its place. But for the structured thought it demands, and the ease with which it can be shared and edited, the written word is made for remote work.

写作并不总是职场沟通的最佳方式。视频更令人难忘; 打电话更快;就连ppt都有一定地位。但就其需要的结构化思维，以及分享和编辑的便利性而言，书面文字是为远程工作而设计的。

* Cynical readers may question a paean to the written word in a publication that sells a fine style guide and runs courses on business writing. They are welcome to write in.

愤世嫉俗的读者可能会质疑出版刊物上对书面文字的赞颂，因为这些出版物不仅销售精美的文体指南，还开设商务写作课程。欢迎他们来信。

How health care is turning into a consumer product

医疗保健是如何变成一种消费品的？

Move fast and heal things

快速行动，治愈一切

![image-20220118111904770](/Users/chenyujie/Library/Application Support/typora-user-images/image-20220118111904770.png)

A new tech boom is changing the business of medicine

一股新的科技热潮正在改变医药行业

Jan 15th 2022

文章音频

TECH AND health care have a fraught relationship. On January 3rd Elizabeth Holmes, founder of Theranos, a startup that once epitomised the promise of combining Silicon Valley’s dynamism with a stodgy health-care market, was convicted of lying to investors about the capabilities of her firm’s blood-testing technology. Yet look beyond Theranos, which began to implode back in 2015, and a much healthier story becomes apparent. This week a horde of entrepreneurs and investors gathered virtually at the annual JPMorgan Chase health-care jamboree. Top of mind was artificial intelligence (AI), digital diagnostics and tele-health—and of a new wave of capital flooding into a vast industry.

科技和医疗保健有着令人担忧的关系。1月3日，Theranos公司的创始人Elizabeth Holmes被判“在其公司血液检测技术的能力上向投资者撒谎”。Theranos公司曾是硅谷的活力与沉闷的医疗保健市场相结合的典范。然而，把目光投向Theranos之外，一个状况更好的故事变得显而易见。Theranos从2015年开始出现崩溃。本周，一群企业家和投资者实际上虚拟地聚集在摩根大通的年度医疗保健盛会上。人们最关注的是人工智能、数字诊断和远程医疗，以及新一波资本浪潮涌入这个庞大行业。

Clunky, costly, highly regulated health systems, often dominated by rent-seeking middlemen, are being shaken up by firms that target patients directly, meet them where they are—which is increasingly online—and give them more control over how to access care. Scientific advances in fields such as gene sequencing and AI make new modes of care possible. E-pharmacies fulfil prescriptions, wearable devices monitor wearers’ health in real time, tele-medicine platforms connect patients with physicians, and home tests enable self-diagnosis.

通常由寻租的中间商主导的“笨重的、昂贵的、高度监管的”医疗系统正被那些“直接针对患者、在患者所在的地方与他们会面诊治(越来越多地在网上治疗)以及让患者对于如何获得医疗服务有更多控制权的”公司所撼动。基因测序和人工智能等领域的科学进步使新的护理模式成为可能。电子药店完成处方，可穿戴设备实时监测佩戴者的健康状况，远程医疗平台将患者与医生连接起来，家庭检测使自我诊断成为可能。

The prize is gigantic. Health care consumes 18% of GDP in America, equivalent to $3.6trn a year. In other rich countries the share is lower, around 10%, but rising as populations age. The pandemic has made people more comfortable with online services, including digitally mediated care. Venture capitalists detect a sector that is uniquely ripe for disruption. CB Insights, a data provider, estimates that investments in digital-health startups nearly doubled in 2021, to $57bn (see chart 1). Unlisted health-care startups valued at $1bn or more now number 90, four times the figure five years ago (see chart 2). Such “unicorns” are competing with incumbent health-care companies and technology giants to make people better and prevent them from getting ill in the first place. In the process, they are turning patients into consumers.

回报是巨大的。医疗保健消费占美国GDP的18%，相当于每年3.6万亿美元。在其他发达国家，这一比例较低，约为10%，但这一比例正随着人口老龄化而上升。疫情使人们更容易接受网上服务，包括数字中介护理。风险投资家发现，这个行业是唯一一个颠覆时机已经成熟的行业。数据提供商CB Insights估计，2021年对数字健康初创公司的投资几乎翻了一番，达到570亿美元(见图1)。估值10亿美元或以上的未上市医疗初创公司目前有90家，这些“独角兽”公司正在与现有的医疗保健公司和科技巨头竞争，从而让人们变得更好，并防止他们生病。在这个过程中，他们把病人变成了消费者。

Consumer health care has long been synonymous with over-the-counter painkillers, cough syrup, face creams or Band-Aids peddled by big drugmakers. In a recognition that their uninnovative consumer divisions have become a drag, Johnson & Johnson, America’s (and the world’s) most-valuable pharmaceutical company, and GlaxoSmithKline, a giant British rival, are spinning them off. The hope is that without the cross-subsidy from the more lucrative prescription-drug arms, the rump consumer businesses will spruce up and become more inventive.

长期以来，消费者医疗保健一直是大型制药商兜售的非处方止痛药、止咳糖浆、面霜或创可贴的代名词。美国(以及世界上)最有价值的制药公司强生和其英国的竞争对手葛兰素史克意识到他们缺乏创新的消费部门已经成为了累赘，正将其剥离。他们希望，在没有来自利润更丰厚的处方药部门的交叉补贴，剩下的消费者业务将会变得焕然一新，变得更有创造力。

Some more adventurous incumbents are already experimenting with digitisation and consumerisation. Teva, an Israeli drug company which dates back to 1901, has developed a digitally enabled inhaler equipped with app-connected sensors that tell users if they are employing it properly.

一些更具冒险精神的企业已经在尝试数字化和消费化。可追溯到1901年的以色列制药公司Teva开发了一种数字(哮喘)吸入器，它配备了与App连接的传感器，可以告诉用户是否使用正确。

The second group of companies with new consumer-health ambitions is big tech. After a series of abortive attempts to tiptoe into the health business—as with Google’s short-lived platform for personal health data, scrapped in 2011—the technology giants are finally finding their feet. According to CB Insights, Alphabet, Amazon, Apple, Meta (Facebook’s new parent company) and Microsoft collectively poured some $3.6bn into health-related deals last year. They are particularly active in two areas: devices and data.

第二类对新的消费者健康有野心的公司是大型科技公司。在涉足健康行业的一系列尝试都以失败告终(比如2011年谷歌短命的个人健康数据平台夭折)之后，科技巨头们终于找到了立足之处。根据CB Insights的数据，Alphabet、亚马逊、苹果、Meta (Facebook的新母公司)和微软去年在与医疗相关的交易中总共投入了约36亿美元。他们在两个领域尤为活跃:设备和数据。

Deloitte, a consultancy, reckons that 320m consumer medical wearables will ship globally in 2022 (see chart 3). In 2020 Amazon unveiled its $100 Halo band. Last year Google acquired Fitbit, which makes a fancier fitness tracker, for $2.1bn. The latest Apple watch already offers an electrocardiogram (ECG) function and the iPhone-maker plans to throw in blood-oxygen sensors and a thermometer to help women track ovulation. The latest smartwatch from Samsung, Apple’s South Korean rival, sports ECG and blood-pressure monitors.

德勤咨询公司估计，2022年将有3.2亿消费者医用可穿戴设备销往全球(见图3)。2020年，亚马逊推出了售价100美元的Halo band(健身手环)。去年，谷歌以21亿美元收购了生产更高档健身追踪器的Fitbit。最新款的苹果手表已经提供了心电图功能，这家iphone制造商计划安装血氧传感器和温度计来帮助女性跟踪排卵。苹果的韩国竞争对手三星推出的最新款智能手表有运动心电图和血压监测器的功能。

The technology giants are also injecting health-related services into their cloud-based data-crunching offerings. To that end Microsoft paid $20bn last year for Nuance, an AI firm. Amazon Web Services, the e-emporium’s cloud division, has also launched a health-care offering. Oracle, an increasingly cloud-based business-software firm, is finalising an acquisition of Cerner, a health- IT group, for $28bn.

这些科技巨头还将健康相关服务注入到云计算数据处理产品中。为此，微软去年斥资200亿美元收购了人工智能公司Nuance。电子百货商场亚马逊的云服务部门AWS也推出了医疗保健服务。日益基于云计算的商业软件公司甲骨文，正敲定以280亿美元收购医疗IT集团Cerner。

Then there are the upstarts, which offer products and services of varying degrees of complexity. Some are simple online pharmacies. Truepill, a six-year-old American company valued at $1.6bn, now fulfils 20,000 prescriptions a day and runs last-mile logistics for a range of consumer-facing health brands. One is Hims & Hers Health, a big American e-pharmacy that went public a year ago via a reverse merger with a special-purpose acquisition company. Another is Nurx, which provides pre-exposure prophylactics for people at risk of HIV. PharmEasy, an Indian online pharmacy, raised $500m in capital last year.

还有一些新贵，他们提供不同复杂程度的产品和服务。有些是简单的网上药店。Truepill是一家成立6年、估值16亿美元的美国公司，现在每天要完成2万份处方，并为一系列面向消费者的健康品牌提供最后一英里物流服务。其中一家是Hims & Hers Health，它是一家美国大型电子药店，一年前通过反向并购一家特殊目的收购公司而上市。另一家是Nurx，它为有感染艾滋病毒风险的人提供接触前预防药物(避孕药物和艾滋病阻断药物)。印度网上药店PharmEasy去年筹集了5亿美元资金。

Telemedicine firms, which offer a wider range of services, have thrived as covid-19 has strained clinics’ capacity and put patients off in-person visits. China’s WeDoctor, a privately held operator of what it calls “internet hospitals”, was last valued at nearly $7bn. Teladoc, a listed American firm with a market value of $13bn, reported revenues of $520m in the third quarter of 2021, up by 80% year on year.

提供更广泛服务的“远程医疗”公司蓬勃发展，因为covid-19导致诊所的容纳能力不堪重负，且推迟了患者的当面就诊。中国一家民营被称为“互联网医院”的微医的最新估值接近70亿美元。市值130亿美元的美国上市公司Teladoc报告称，2021年第三季度的营收为5.2亿美元，同比增长80%。

Another, more sophisticated area experiencing rapid growth is at-home diagnostics. The Theranos scandal gave consumer diagnostics a bad name. Now better technology and greater realism about what it can achieve are rehabilitating the field, just as the pandemic has accustomed people to the idea of home testing.

另一个发展迅速的更为复杂的领域是在家诊断。Theranos丑闻给消费者诊断公司带来了坏名声。现在，更好的技术和对其所能实现的更现实的认识正在恢复这一领域的名声，正如疫情已经让人们习惯了家庭检测的想法。

This includes devices to analyse everything from blood sugar to stool samples. Levels Health, a two-year-old American startup, sells app-synced continuous glucose monitors directly to consumers, after seamlessly connecting patients via the internet with prescribing doctors. Its founder, Josh Clemente, was inspired by having to ask a friend to smuggle such a monitor for him from Australia to confirm his hunch that he was, like one-third of Americans, pre-diabetic—in America the devices were available only on prescription to people with uncontrolled diabetes. The startup’s waiting list now stretches to 145,000 people. Digbi Health, another American firm, uses faecal matter to analyse its customers’ gut microbiome to promote gastrointestinal health. Skin+Me, a British one, saves people a trip to the dermatologist by providing prescription-grade skin care on the basis of selfies. Thriva, also from Britain, analyses blood from finger pricks to shed light on conditions such as high cholesterol and anaemia.

这包括分析从血糖到粪便样本的各种设备。Levels Health是一家成立两年的美国初创公司，它通过互联网将患者与开处方的医生无缝连接起来，然后直接向消费者销售App同步的连续血糖监测仪。它的创始人Josh Clemente的灵感来自于他的一个朋友从澳大利亚给他偷带了一个这样的监测仪，以证实他的直觉，即他和三分之一的美国人一样，都是糖尿病前期患者，在美国，这种监测仪只能按处方提供给失控的糖尿病患者。这家初创公司的等待名单已经扩大到14.5万人。另一家美国公司Digbi Health利用粪便分析顾客的肠道微生物群，以促进胃肠道健康。英国的Skin+Me公司以自拍为基础，提供处方级皮肤护理，省去了人们去看皮肤科医生的麻烦。同样来自英国的Thriva通过分析手指刺血来揭示高胆固醇和贫血等情况。

Doctors on demand

A big reason why it has taken so long for consumer technology to disrupt health care is that the highly regulated sector does not lend itself to Silicon Valley’s “move fast and break things” credo. But recent years have shown that disruption is possible even in rule-bound industries. Hamish Grierson founded Thriva after witnessing a digital shake-up in his old job in payments. Levels Health’s Mr Clemente, helped keep astronauts fighting fit at SpaceX, which has prised open the once government-dominated spacefaring business.

消费者科技之所以花了这么长时间才颠覆医疗保健，一个重要原因是，这个高度监管的行业不适合硅谷“快速行动，打破常规”的信条。但近年来的情况表明，即便是在受规则约束的行业，也有可能颠覆。Hamish Grierson在目睹了他以前的支付领域工作在数字化领域的剧变后，创立了Thriva。Levels Health公司的Clemente先生帮助SpaceX公司的宇航员保持身体健康，该公司打开了一度由政府主导的航天业务。

One strategy is to offer “general wellness” products, which evade rigorous scrutiny, and only consult medical professionals for advisory purposes or to convince potential investors that your products are backed by science. Thriva, for example, says its blood tests offer “insights” rather than official diagnoses.

有一种策略是提供“普通的健康”产品，规避严格的审查，仅让医疗专业人士以供咨询，或让潜在投资者相信你的产品有科学依据。例如，Thriva表示，其血液测试提供的是“见解”，而非正式的诊断。

Other companies, especially those with higher-tech offerings, are treading carefully. Manny Montalvo, who oversees “Digihaler” sales at Teva, insists it is not a consumer product. “This is still medicine and the right medicine has to be selected for the patient,” he says categorically. Apple sought clearance from America’s Food and Drug Administration (FDA) for its new watch’s ECG function.

其他公司，尤其是那些提供高科技产品的公司，都在谨慎行事。负责监视Teva公司“数字吸入器”销售的Manny Montalvo坚称，这不是一种消费品。他直截了当地说：“这仍然是药物，必须为病人选择正确的药物”。苹果公司为其新手表的心电图功能寻求FDA的许可。

The regulators, for their part, are trying to move faster themselves. The newly minted FDA chief is a former adviser to Google Health, the tech giant’s health venture. The industry hopes that on his watch the agency will finally adopt long-delayed standards for digital-health software. Australia, Japan, Singapore and the EU have set out digital-health strategies in order to create similar standards for determining the quality, safety and clinical value of new health devices. More countries are adopting data-protection rules that ought to make it clearer to entrepreneurs, investors and consumers what data can be shared, with whom and how.

就监管机构而言，他们也在努力加快行动速度。这位新上任的FDA局长曾是科技巨头谷歌Health的顾问。希望该行业在他的领导下，该机构能最终采用拖延已久的数字健康软件标准。澳大利亚、日本、新加坡和欧盟已经制定了数字健康战略，以创建类似的标准来确定新健康设备的质量、安全性和临床价值。越来越多的国家正在采用数据保护规则，这应该会让企业家、投资者和消费者更清楚哪些数据可以共享，与谁共享，如何共享。

The consumer-health boom has hit snags. Investors who pushed the share prices of online pill-peddlers and digital hospitals up whenever covid-19 spiked have cooled on such firms now that the coronaviral threat has receded somewhat. After exceeding $30bn at the start of 2021, Teladoc’s market value is back where it was before the pandemic hit in early 2020. The prospects of Hims & Hers, whose share price has declined by three-quarters in the past year, may have been additionally dented by Amazon’s launch in late 2020 of its e-pharmacy business. XX’s digital-health companies have been caught up in the XX’s broader tech crackdown. WeDoctor has shelved plans for a blockbuster initial public offering in Hong Kong. The Theranos saga offers a cautionary tale of how tricky biology is compared with much computer science.

消费者健康的繁荣也遇到了障碍。每当疫情飙升时，投资者就会推高网上药店和数字医院股价，现在他们对这些公司的态度已经降温，因为新冠病毒的威胁已经有所减弱。在2021年初超过300亿美元后，Teladoc的市值回到了2020年初疫情爆发前的水平。Hims & Hers的股价在过去一年中下跌了四分之三，亚马逊在2020年底推出的电子药房业务可能进一步削弱了该公司的前景。XX的数字健康公司已经被卷入了XX更广泛的科技打击行动。微医搁置了在香港进行轰动一时的IPO的计划。Theranos的传奇故事提供了一个警示，说明生物学与许多计算机科学相比是多么棘手。

Some products will turn out to be duds, and regulators may yet disrupt the disrupters. Still, as Scott Melville of the Consumer Healthcare Products Association, a trade body, puts it, “There is no going back to the old paternalistic system where you are relying exclusively on a medical professional for your health care.” Enterprising companies want to help people recover more quickly or, better yet, avoid getting ill to begin with. That is a negative prognosis for the hospital-industrial complex, which profits from the very sick. For everyone else, it is mostly a positive one. ■

一些产品最终将被证明是无用的，监管机构可能还会扰乱这些“颠覆者”。不过，正如行业组织“消费者保健品协会”的Scott Melville所说，“不可能回到过去那种完全依赖医疗专业人士提供医疗保健的专制模式。” 有进取心的公司希望帮助人们更快地恢复，或者恢复的更好，避免一开始就生病。这对从病重人群中获利的医院-工业联合体来说是一个负面的展望。对其他人来说，这还算是积极的。■

Boris Johnson’s career of rule-breaking runs into crisis

鲍里斯·约翰逊打破规则的职业生涯陷入了危机

Breaking bad

![image-20220117094247367](/Users/chenyujie/Library/Application Support/typora-user-images/image-20220117094247367.png)

From smashing the political status quo to partying during lockdown

从打破政治现状到封锁期间的聚会

Jan 15th 2022

文章音频

HOLLOW-EYED AND with his shoulders hunched, Boris Johnson offered the sort of apology he has made to many people, many times. In May 2020, with Britain in strict lockdown, Downing Street officials had held a drinks party in the prime minister’s walled garden. He attended for 25 minutes, he admitted to the House of Commons on January 12th. And he understood the “rage” Britons felt at that, after enduring private miseries that spring.

鲍里斯·约翰逊眼睛凹陷、耸起肩膀，做出了他曾向很多人、很多次做出的那种道歉。2020年5月，在英国被严格封锁的情况下，唐宁街官员在首相的围墙花园举行了一场酒会。他参加了25分钟，他于1月12日进入下议院。在经历了那年春天的个人痛苦之后，他理解英国人对此感到的“愤怒”。

He also set out his defence, which will be central to an inquiry by Sue Gray, a senior civil servant: that he was an unwitting participant, since the garden was being used as an office, and that he believed “implicitly” that it was a work event. It looked bad, but “could be said technically to fall within the guidance”. Sir Keir Starmer, Labour’s leader, said that was ridiculous and demanded that he resign.

他还陈述了自己的辩护，这将是高级文官Sue Gray调查的核心内容: 他是一个不知情的参与者，因为花园被用作办公室，而且他“绝对地”相信这是一个工作活动。这看起来很糟糕，但“从技术上可以说是在领导范围内”。工党领袖Keir Starmer爵士表示，这很荒谬，并要求他辞职。

He almost certainly won’t. The mood of Tory MPs is bleak, and donors who funded his election campaign are incensed. “I just feel a bit disgusted,” says a financier, who is considering turning off the taps. A handful of senior figures, among them Douglas Ross, the leader of the Scottish Conservatives, and William Wragg, a select-committee chair, have called for him to go.

他几乎肯定不会这么做。保守党议员的情绪很低落，资助他竞选活动的捐款人被激怒了。一位正在考虑“关掉水龙头”[指不再资助]的金融家表示：“我只是觉得有点恶心”。包括苏格兰保守党领袖Douglas Ross和特别委员会主席William Wragg在内的少数资深人士呼吁他下台。

But his party is unlikely to force him out in short order. That would require 54 MPs to sign a letter of no confidence, and then 180, or half the parliamentary party, to vote to eject him. The Conservatives’ reputation for ruthless regicide has been undeserved since the days of Margaret Thatcher. It has since had a high tolerance for low performance: the hapless Theresa May was got rid of only in 2019, two years after she lost the party its majority. Some backbenchers are happy with a crippled prime minister, since it makes him biddable on corona virus policy and Brexit.

但他的政党不太可能在短期内迫使他下台。这将需要54名议员签署一份不信任信，然后180名议员，也就是议会政党的一半，投票罢免他。自玛格丽特•撒切尔时代以来，保守党“无情弑君”的名声一直是不应有的。自那以来，该党对“低绩效”的容忍度很高: 不幸的特蕾莎·梅直到2019年才被“赶下台”，两年前她失去了该党的多数席位。一些后座议员对一个跛脚首相感到高兴，因为这使他在新冠病毒政策和英国脱欧问题上变得顺从。

For most of his career, Mr Johnson’s political appeal has lain in his rule-breaking. As mayor of London he would break the small rules of politics, which forbid lies and affairs, thumbing his nose at po-faced rivals. Voters who were tired of slickness liked him, just as small boys like slapstick.

在约翰逊职业生涯的大部分时间里，他的政治吸引力在于打破常规。作为伦敦市长，他将打破政治上的小规则，这些规则禁止谎言和婚外情，对一本正经的对手嗤之以鼻。厌倦了花言巧语的选民喜欢他，就像小男孩喜欢打闹剧一样。

As prime minister he promised to break the big rules that framed political reality. A “people’s government” would offer whatever whetted the public’s appetite, in particular things that had been ruled out of bounds because they were uneconomic or impractical. Britain could leave the European Union, radically curtail immigration and lavish funds on pet projects, from bridges to royal yachts, no matter what Treasury bean-counters might say. If Parliament was obstructive, it could be suspended. His would be a good-times administration, and he a merry Charles II to Mrs May’s mirthless Oliver Cromwell.

作为首相，他承诺要打破框定政治现实的大规则。一个“人民政府”会提供任何能激起公众胃口的东西，特别是那些因为不赢利或不切实际而被排除在外的东西。无论财政部的精打细算的人怎么说，英国都可以离开欧盟，从根本上减少移民以及削减从桥梁到皇家游艇等宠儿项目上的奢侈资金，不管财政部那些精于计算的人怎么说。如果议会阻挠，它可能会被暂停。他的政府将是一个繁荣时期的政府，他将是一个快乐的查尔斯二世，而梅姨的政府则像是郁郁寡欢的奥利弗·克伦威尔。

But after weeks of revelations about lockdown parties and irregular donations, the breaking of small rules has lost its charm. Mr Johnson is now badly damaged inside his party, and unpopular in the country. According to Ipsos MORI, a pollster, his net approval rating has slumped to -36, close to Jeremy Corbyn’s shortly before his election defeat of 2019. Sir Keir, a former public prosecutor, will make probity in public office central to his campaign to be prime minister.

但在封锁期间的派对和不定期的捐款被曝光数周后，打破小规则已经失去了吸引力。约翰逊现在在党内(名声)受到严重损害，在国内也不受欢迎。根据民意调查机构Ipsos MORI的数据显示，他的净支持率已跌至-36，接近Jeremy Corbyn在2019年选举失败前不久的支持率。前检察官Keir爵士将把公职廉洁作为他竞选首相的核心。

Meanwhile the big rules of politics are reasserting themselves. The government faces a gruelling spring, preparing for local elections while household incomes are being squeezed and public services are coming under severe strain. The campaign will be led by a diminished prime minister who will be unable to conjure the easy answers and ready fixes that are his style.

与此同时，重大的政治规则正在重新确立。政府面临着一个艰苦的春天，在准备地方选举的同时，家庭收入受到挤压，公共服务面临严重压力。这场活动将由一个被削弱的首相领导，他将无法想出按他的风格来的简单的答案和现成的解决方案。

Before the parties, it was inflation that was keeping Conservative MPs awake at night. Consumer prices rose by 5.1% in November compared with a year earlier, the biggest annual increase since September 2011. Over the next few months, increases should beat anything seen since the early 1990s. Pay is unlikely to keep pace with the prices of cars, clothes and cigarettes. State pensioners, a big chunk of the Tories’ electoral coalition, and people on state benefits will be squeezed particularly hard.

在聚会事件之前，让保守党议员夜不能寐的是通货膨胀。11月消费者价格指数同比上涨5.1%，这是自2011年9月以来的最大年涨幅。在接下来的几个月里，加息幅度应该会超过上世纪90年代初以来的任何一次。工资不太可能与汽车、服装和香烟的价格同步上涨。国家养老金领取者（他们占保守党选举联盟中的一大部分）以及领取国家福利的人将受到特别大的挤压。

A large part of the problem is caused by high global prices for oil and gas, compounded by Britain’s poorly designed regulatory framework. In April the government will raise its cap on retail-energy prices. The average household’s annual bill is expected to rise by 50%. The Labour Party is feasting on Tory discomfort: it has proposed a temporary cut to VAT on domestic energy bills.

这一问题很大程度上是由全球石油和天然气价格高企引起的，而英国糟糕的监管框架又加剧了这一问题。4月，政府将提高零售能源价格上限。平均每个家庭的年度账单预计将增加50%。工党正在利用保守党的不满: 它提议暂时削减国内能源账单的增值税。

This is artful politicking, designed to irritate Tory backbenchers. The measure was promised by Mr Johnson’s Brexit campaign as a potential dividend from leaving the EU, though was not enacted. But such is the scale of the problem, notes Robert Joyce of the Institute for Fiscal Studies, that even if it were, it would offset less than a fifth of the average increase in energy bills. All this is reminiscent of the early 2010s, when Ed Miliband successfully exploited what he termed a “cost-of-living crisis” to torment David Cameron’s government.

这是一种巧妙的政治手段，旨在激怒保守党后座议员。约翰逊的脱欧竞选活动曾承诺，这项措施将是脱欧带来的潜在红利，但并未生效。但财政研究所的Robert Joyce指出，问题的规模很大，即使这样做了，也抵消不到能源账单平均增长的五分之一。这一切让人想起了20世纪10年代初，当时Ed Miliband成功地利用他所说的“生活成本危机”来折磨卡梅伦政府。

Also coming in April is a rise of 2.5 percentage points in national insurance, a payroll tax, half each from employees and employers. Senior Tories are unhappy, including Jacob Rees-Mogg, the leader of the House of Commons. The extra money is supposed to support the National Health Service for three years, before being diverted to fund a new social-care regime.

4月还将提高国民保险(一种工资税)2.5个百分点，其中一半来自雇员和一半来自雇主。包括下议院领袖Jacob Rees-Mogg在内的保守党高层对此表示不满。额外的资金将用于支持NHS三年，然后再用于资助新的社会保障制度。

But the NHS is in the midst of a waiting-times crisis, the result of doctors halting routine work during the pandemic. The national-insurance rise will barely make a dent. Some 6m people are already waiting for a procedure; by 2024, when the next general election is due, that could be 13m, or one Briton in five. Everyone will know someone on a waiting list; Labour will be able to pick and choose between heart-rending stories for its campaign ads. After his apology in the Commons, the first question the prime minister faced came from James Davies, a Tory, who raised his constituent’s eight-week wait at a breast-screening clinic.

America has failed to learn from the safe opening of classrooms abroad

美国未能从国外安全开放的教室中吸取教训

Poor students

可怜的学生

Its children have lost more school days than peers in most rich countries

这些孩子比大多数发达国家的同龄人损失了更多的上学时间

Jan 13th 2022

文章音频

OVER THE past two years America’s children have missed more time in the classroom than those in most of the rich world. School closures that began there in early 2020 dragged on until the summer of 2021. During that time the districts that stayed closed longest forced all or some of their children to learn remotely for twice as long as schools in Ireland, three times longer than schools in Spain and four times longer than in France.

在过去的两年里，美国孩子的缺课时间比大多数发达国家的孩子都要多。学校关闭是从2020年初开始的，一直持续到2021年夏天。在此期间，关闭时间最长的学区强迫所有或部分孩子远程学习的时间是爱尔兰学校的两倍，西班牙学校的三倍，法国学校的四倍。

In recent weeks American schools have started closing once again, as the Omicron variant of covid-19 has brought a fresh wave of infections. About 5,000, equivalent to roughly 5% of schools, were shut for part or all of the first week in January. Sometimes that was because staff had been forced into isolation, but other closures were pre-emptive. In Chicago teachers refused to turn up between January 5th and 11th. Some staff in California urged healthy colleagues to call in sick. America’s shrill debates about schooling continue to set it apart. The new term met with much less fuss in England—even though the country had a higher national infection rate than America and has vaccinated fewer young children.

最近几周，由于新冠病毒的奥密克戎变种带来了新一波的感染浪潮，美国学校再次开始关闭。1月份的第一周，约有5,000所学校(约占学校总数的5%)部分或全部停课。有时这是因为工作人员被迫隔离，但其他学校的关闭则是先发制人的。在芝加哥，老师们拒绝在1月5日到11日之间出现。加州的一些员工敦促健康的同事打电话请病假。美国关于学校教育的激烈辩论继续使其与众不同。尽管英国的全国感染率比美国高，而且儿童接种疫苗的数量也比美国少，但新学期在英国引起的骚动要小得多。

America’s bungling has several explanations. Whereas in Europe national or regional governments have decided when schools close and reopen, in America the choice has largely fallen to its 14,000 or so school districts. That has splintered the conversation about school closures into thousands of noisy arguments. Media coverage has not helped. A study in 2020 found that stories about school reopening run by big American news providers were much more negative in tone compared with similar stories abroad. Teachers’ unions have ignored encouraging findings from other countries, such as research suggesting that teachers in schools that had opened faced no greater risk of severe sickness than other professionals.

美国搞砸有几种解释。在欧洲，学校何时停课、何时复课由国家或地区政府决定，而在美国，这样的选择很大程度上落至大约14000个学区身上。这使得关于“学校关闭”的讨论变成了成千上万次的争吵。媒体的报道也无济于事。2020年的一项研究发现，与国外的类似报道相比，美国大型新闻提供商写的有关学校重新开学的报道语气要负面得多。教师工会忽视了来自其他国家的令人鼓舞的发现，比如有研究表明，已经开学的学校的教师并没有比从事其他职业的人面临更大的严重疾病风险。

Defenders of America’s record claim that its schools are more crowded and poorer than those abroad. But many foreign educators envy America’s advantages. The federal government has earmarked around $200bn for schools since 2020. America was quicker than most countries to make vaccines available to all adults, and to offer jabs to children.

美国历史记录的捍卫者声称，美国的学校比国外的学校更拥挤，更贫穷。但许多外国教育工作者羡慕美国的优势。自2020年以来，联邦政府已为学校拨款约2000亿美元。美国比大多数国家更快地向所有成年人提供疫苗，以及为儿童接种疫苗。

Some of those demanding fresh closures argue that Omicron brings new uncertainties, and that it would cost little for schools to shut their buildings for a while. Remote learning may be more effective than being in a classroom where lots of staff and students are absent, goes the thinking. But once schools have closed temporarily, they tend to stay shut longer than expected. Even short closures seem to depress children’s attendance after they reopen. Lots of children have no safer place to be than a classroom and many parents cannot work from home. Furthermore, without wider lockdowns that nobody is considering, school closures accomplish little, because youngsters continue to catch and pass on the virus, for example in child care, in shops, or while being looked after by relatives.

一些要求重新关闭学校的人认为，奥密克戎带来了新的不确定性，学校暂时关闭教学楼的成本并不高。人们认为，远程学习可能比在有很多师生缺席的教室里更有效。但一旦学校暂时停课，它们往往会比预期的停课时间更长。即使是短暂的关闭，似乎也会降低学校重新开放后学生们的出席率。许多孩子没有比教室更安全的地方，许多家长也不能远程办公。此外，如果不采取没人考虑的更广泛的封锁措施，关闭学校几乎没有什么效果，因为青少年还会在儿童保育园、商店或由亲戚照顾时感染和传播病毒。

Remote teaching has harmed children’s learning, mental health and physical safety. America’s schools should be buoyed by early evidence suggesting that Omicron infections lead to less severe symptoms than other variants of covid (which are themselves mild in most children) and that vaccination still offers strong protection against serious illness.

远程教学危害了儿童的学习、心理健康和人身安全。早期证据表明，奥密克戎病毒感染导致的症状比其他新冠病毒变体(在大多数儿童中这些变种的症状较轻)要轻，而且接种疫苗仍能有效预防严重疾病，这应该让美国的学校感到鼓舞。

Schools must continue to find ways of covering for quarantining teachers, such as pulling administrative staff into classrooms or inviting teachers back from retirement. When staff shortages are severe, it would be better to force only some year groups into remote learning before closing whole schools. Even then, schools should allow vulnerable children and those of key workers to remain in the building. That has been common in Europe, but far from standard in America. Children have little to gain from school closures and much to lose. Teachers’ unions should stop dumping the pandemic’s costs on them. ■

学校必须继续寻找方法来代替隔离教师，比如把行政人员拉进教室或邀请退休教师回来。当人员严重短缺时，最好是在关闭整个学校之前，只强制一些学年组进行远程学习。即使到那时，学校也应该允许脆弱的学生和关键工作人员留在大楼里。这在欧洲很常见，但在美国却远非标准。学校关闭对孩子们没有什么好处，反而会有很大的损失。教师工会应该停止将疫情的成本转嫁给他们。■

Welcome to the era of the bossy state

欢迎来到专横国家的时代

Business and government

企业和政府

Countries around the world want to bend companies to their will

世界各国都想让企业屈从于他们的意愿

Jan 15th 2022

文章音频

THE RELATIONSHIP between governments and businesses is always changing. After 1945, many countries sought to rebuild society using firms that were state-owned and -managed. By the 1980s, faced with sclerosis in the West, the state retreated to become an umpire overseeing the rules for private firms to compete in a global market—a lesson learned, in a fashion, by the XX bloc. Now a new and turbulent phase is under way, as citizens demand action on problems, from social justice to the climate. In response, governments are directing firms to make society safer and fairer, but without controlling their shares or their boards. Instead of being the owner or umpire, the state has become the backseat driver. This bossy business interventionism is well-intentioned. But, ultimately, it is a mistake.

政府和企业之间的关系总是在变化。1945年以后，许多国家试图利用国有和国家管理的企业来重建社会。到了20世纪80年代，面对西方僵化的局面，国家退却成为监管私人公司在全球市场竞争规则的裁判，XX集团在某种程度上得到了这一教训。现在，一个新的动荡的阶段正在进行，因为公民们要求对从社会正义到气候等问题采取行动。作为回应，政府正指示企业使社会更安全、更公平，但不控制其股份或董事会。国家不再是所有者或裁判，而是成了后座司机。这种专横的企业干预主义是善意的。但归根结底，这是一个错误。

Signs of this approach are everywhere, as our special report explains. President Joe Biden is pursuing an agenda of soft protectionism, industrial subsidies and righteous regulation, aimed at making the home of free markets safe for the middle classes. In XX XXX’s “Common Prosperity” crackdown is designed to curb the excesses of its freewheeling boom, and create a business scene that is more self-sufficient, tame and obedient. The European Union is drifting away from free markets to embrace industrial policy and “strategic autonomy”. As the biggest economies pivot, so do medium-sized ones such as Britain, India and Mexico. Crucially, in most democracies, the lure of intervention is bipartisan. Few politicians fancy fighting an election on a platform of open borders and free markets.

正如我们的特别报道栏目所解释的那样，这种做法的迹象随处可见。美国总统乔•拜登正在推行一项温和保护主义、产业补贴和公正监管的事项，旨在为中产阶级提供安全的自由市场家园。在XX，XX对“共同富裕”的镇压旨在遏制其肆意繁荣的过度行为，并创造一个更自给自足、更温顺、更顺从的商业环境。欧盟正在远离自由市场，转而拥抱产业政策和“战略自主”。作为世界上最大的经济体，英国、印度和墨西哥等中等规模经济体也是如此。至关重要的是，在大多数民主国家，干预的诱惑来自两党。很少有政治家愿意以开放边境和自由市场为竞选平台。

That is because many citizens fear that markets and their umpires are not up to the job. The financial crisis and slow recovery amplified anger about inequality. Other concerns are more recent. The world’s ten biggest tech companies are over twice as big as they were five years ago and sometimes seem to behave as if they are above the law. The geopolitical backdrop is a far cry from the 1990s, when the expansion of trade and democracy promised to go hand in hand, and from the cold war when the West and the Soviet Union had few business links. Now the West and totalitarian XX are rivals but economically intertwined. Gummed-up supply chains are causing inflation, reinforcing the perception that globalisation is overextended. And climate change is an ever more pressing threat.

这是因为许多公民担心市场和他们的裁判无法胜任这项工作。金融危机和缓慢的复苏加剧了人们对不平等的愤怒。其他担忧是最近才出现的。全球十大科技公司的规模是五年前的两倍多，有时它们的行为似乎凌驾于法律之上。当前的地缘政治背景与上世纪90年代和冷战时期大不相同，在上世纪90年代贸易和民主的扩张有望携手并进，在冷战时期西方和苏联之间几乎没有商业联系。现在西方和极权主义的XX是竞争对手，但在经济上紧密相连。阻塞的供应链正引发通胀，这强化了“全球化过度扩张”的看法。气候变化是一个日益紧迫的威胁。

Governments are redesigning global capitalism to deal with these fears. But few politicians or voters want to go back to full-scale nationalisation. Not even XX is keen to reconstruct an empire of iron and steel plants run by chain-smoking commissars, while Mr Biden, despite his nostalgia for the 1960s, need only walk through America’s clogged West Coast ports to recall that public ownership can be shambolic. At the same time the pandemic has seen governments experiment with new policies that were unimaginable in December 2019, from perhaps $5trn or more of handouts and guarantees for firms to indicative guidance on optimal spacing of customers in shopping aisles.

各国政府正在重新设计全球资本主义，以应对这些担忧。但很少有政客或选民愿意回到全面国有化的道路上。甚至XX都不希望重建一个由一支烟一支烟抽地人民委员运营的钢铁厂帝国，至于拜登先生，尽管他怀念1960年代,但他只需要穿过美国被堵塞的西海岸港口，就可以回忆起公有制可能是混乱的。与此同时，疫情期间，各国政府试验了在2019年12月无法想象的新政策，从向企业提供可能的5万亿美元或更多的捐赠和担保，到就购物通道内顾客的最佳间隔提供指示性指导。

This opening of the interventionist mind is coalescing around policies that fall short of ownership. One set of measures claims to enhance security, broadly defined. The class of industries in which government direction is legitimate on security grounds has expanded beyond defence to include energy and technology. In these areas governments are acting as de facto central planners, with research and development (R&D) spending to foster indigenous innovation and subsidies to redirect capital spending. In semiconductors America has proposed a $52bn subsidy scheme, one reason why Intel’s investment is forecast to double compared with five years ago. XX is seeking self-sufficiency in semiconductors and Europe in batteries.

这种“干预主义思想的开放”正与“缺乏所有权的政策”相结合。一套广泛定义的措施声称可以加强安全。基于安全理由，政府监管是合法的行业类别已经扩展到国防以外，包括能源和技术。在这些领域，政府实际上扮演着中央计划者的角色，通过研发支出来促进自主创新，并通过补贴来调整资本支出。在半导体领域，美国提出了一项520亿美元的补贴计划，这也是英特尔的投资预计将比5年前翻一番的原因之一。XX正在寻求半导体领域的自给自足，欧洲则在电池领域寻求自给自足。

The definition of what is seen as strategic may well expand further to include vaccines, medical ingredients and minerals, for example. In the name of security, most big countries have tightened rules that screen incoming foreign investment. America’s mesh of punitive sanctions and technology export controls encompasses thousands of foreign individuals and firms.

被视为战略的定义很可能进一步扩大，例如包括疫苗、药物成分和矿物。以安全的名义，大多数大国都收紧了对外来投资的审查规定。美国的惩罚性制裁和技术出口控制交织在一起，涵盖了成千上万的外国个人和公司。

The other set of measures aims to enhance stakeholderism. Shareholders and consumers no longer have uncontested primacy in the hierarchy of groups that firms serve. Managers must weigh the welfare of other constituents more heavily, including staff, suppliers and even competitors. The most visible part of this is voluntary, in the form of “ESG” investing codes that score firms for, say, protecting biodiversity, local people or their own workers. But these wider obligations may become harder for firms to avoid. In XX XXbaba has pledged a $15bn “donation” to the Common Prosperity cause. In the West stakeholderism may be enforced through the bureaucracy. Central banks and public pension funds may shun the securities of firms judged to be anti-social. America’s antitrust agency, which once safeguarded consumers alone, is mulling other aims such as helping small firms.

另一套措施旨在加强利益相关者主义。股东和消费者在公司所服务的群体中不再享有无可争议的地位。管理者必须更加重视其他参与者的利益，包括员工、供应商甚至竞争对手。其中最明显的部分是无偿的，以“ESG”投资准则为公司评分，例如保护生物多样性、当地居民或他们自己的工人。但是这些更广泛的义务可能会让公司更难逃避。在XX，XX巴巴承诺向共同繁荣事业“捐赠”150亿美元。在西方，利益相关者可能是通过官僚机构来执行的。中央银行和公共养老基金可能会避开那些被认为是反社会的公司的证券。美国反垄断机构，曾经只保护消费者，现在正在考虑其他目标，比如帮助小公司。

The ambition to confront economic and social problems is admirable. And so far, outside XX at least, bossier government has not hurt business confidence. America’s main stockmarket index is over 40% higher than it was before the pandemic, while capital spending by the world’s largest 500-odd listed firms is up by 11%. Yet, in the longer term, three dangers loom.

面对经济和社会问题的雄心令人钦佩。到目前为止，至少在XX以外，专横的政府并没有损害企业信心。美国的主要股市指数比疫情前高出40%，而世界上最大的500多家上市公司的资本支出增长了11%。然而，从长远来看，三大危险显现。

High stakes

The first is that the state and business, faced by conflicting aims, will fail to find the best trade-offs. A fossil-fuel firm obliged to preserve good labour relations and jobs may be reluctant to shrink, hurting the climate. An antitrust policy that helps hundreds of thousands of small suppliers will hurt tens of millions of consumers who will end up paying higher prices. Boycotting XX for its human-rights abuses might deprive the West of cheap supplies of solar technologies. Businesses and regulators focused on a single sector are often ill-equipped to cope with these dilemmas, and lack the democratic legitimacy to do so.

首先，政府和企业面对相互冲突的目标，将无法找到最佳的权衡。一个化石燃料公司被迫保持良好的劳资关系和就业机会，可能不愿缩减规模，从而损害气候。一项帮助数十万小供应商的反垄断政策将损害数千万消费者的利益，他们最终将支付更高的价格。由于侵犯人权而抵制XX可能会使西方国家失去太阳能技术的廉价供应。专注于单一领域的企业和监管机构往往缺乏应对这些困境的能力，而且缺乏这样做的民主合法性。

Diminished efficiency and innovation is the second danger. Duplicating global supply chains is extraordinarily expensive: multinational firms have $41trn of cross-border investments. More pernicious in the long run is a weakening of competition. Firms that gorge on subsidies become flabby, whereas those that are protected from foreign competition are more likely to treat customers shabbily. If you want to rein in Facebook, the most credible challenger is TikTok, from China. An economy in which politicians and big business manage the flow of subsidies according to orthodox thinking is not one in which entrepreneurs flourish.

效率和创新的下降是第二个危险。复制全球供应链的成本极高: 跨国公司有41万亿美元的跨境投资。从长远来看，更有害的是竞争的削弱。贪婪地享受补贴的公司变得软弱无力，而那些受到保护不受外国竞争影响的公司更有可能亏待客户。如果你想控制Facebook，最可行的挑战者是来自中国的TikTok。一个由政客和大企业按照正统思维管理补贴流的经济体，是不可能让企业家蓬勃发展的经济体。

The last problem is cronyism, which ends up contaminating business and politics alike. Firms seek advantage by attempting to manipulate government: already in America the boundary is blurred, with more corporate meddling in the electoral process. Meanwhile politicians and officials end up favouring particular firms, having sunk money and their hopes into them. The urge to intervene to soften every shock is habit-forming. In the past six weeks Britain, Germany and India have spent $7bn propping up two energy firms and a telecoms operator whose problems have nothing to do with the pandemic.

最后一个问题是任人唯亲，它最终会污染企业和政治。企业通过试图操纵政府来寻求利益: 在美国，这种界限已经很模糊，越来越多的企业插手选举过程。与此同时，政客和官员们最终倾向于特定的公司，在这些公司身上投入了金钱和希望。通过干预来缓和每次冲击的冲动已经形成了习惯。在过去的六周里，英国、德国和印度花费了70亿美元来扶持两家能源公司和一家电信运营商，而它们的问题与疫情无关。

This newspaper believes that the state should intervene to make markets work better, through, for example, carbon taxes to shift capital towards climate-friendly technologies; R&D to fund science that firms will not; and a benefits system that protects workers and the poor. But the new style of bossy government goes far beyond this. Its adherents hope for prosperity, fairness and security. They are more likely to end up with inefficiency, vested interests and insularity. ■

本刊认为，国家应该进行干预，使市场更好地运转，例如，通过征收碳排放税，将资本转向气候友好型技术; 通过研发部门来为企业不愿资助的科学提供资金；以及保护工人和穷人的福利体系。但这种新的专横政府的作风远不止于此。它的拥护者希望繁荣、公平和安全。他们更有可能以低效率、既得利益和孤立而告终。■

IN THE PAST, studying ecosystems for signs of change has needed lots of boots on the ground. Plants, being sedentary, can be recorded easily by unleashing an infantry of PhD students eager to make a name for themselves. Taking a census of an area’s animals is, however, a different matter. It frequently involves sitting quietly for hours on end, noting which species walk, flutter or slither by, and what they are up to. Sometimes, the troops assigned to do this see a lot. Sometimes not.

在过去，研究生态系统的“变化迹象”需要大量的实地工作。静止不动的植物很容易就能被记录下来，只要发动一群渴望成名的博士生就行了。然而，对一个地区的动物进行普查则是另一回事。它通常需要连续几个小时安静地坐着，观察哪些物种行走、飞来飞去或爬行，以及它们在做什么。有时，被派去做这事的人会看到很多。有时则不会。

Any viewer of crime dramas might think, though, that there is a better way. Just as DNA traces on an unwashed glass or a carelessly discarded cigarette butt can place a suspect as having been in a particular place, so can DNA shed by a creature as it goes about its business. Ecologists have thought of this, and it certainly works for things like animal droppings. But these, too, must first be detected and collected—and they will identify only the animal that dropped them. What would really speed things up would be a means of sampling an entire habitat at one go.

不过，任何犯罪剧的观众可能会认为有一个更好的方法。就像未清洗的玻璃或随手丢弃的烟头上的DNA痕迹可以证明嫌疑人曾去过某个特定的地方一样，生物在其活动过程中也会释放出DNA。生态学家已经想到了这一点，它当然适用于动物粪便之类的东西。但是，这些东西也必须先要被发现和采集，而且它们只能识别出掉粪便的动物。真正能加快速度的方法是一次性对整个栖息地进行采样。

Such an approach is called metagenomics, and it does already exist. But, at the moment, it is applied mainly to bodies of water and to soil, rather than to open-air dry-land habitats. Several groups of researchers would therefore like to extend it more widely—by plucking the DNA concerned from thin air.

这种方法被称为宏基因组学，这种方法也确实已经存在。但是，目前，它主要应用于水体和土壤，而不是露天的旱地栖息地。因此，几组研究人员希望将其扩展到更广泛的范围，即从稀薄的空气中提取相关的DNA。

Sniffing around

Two of these groups, one led by Christina Lynggaard of the University of Copenhagen and the other by Elizabeth Clare of York University, in Toronto, have used zoos to test ways of extracting DNA from the atmosphere. Zoos are ideal for this because they house known animals. Both groups have just published preliminary results in Current Biology. Others, meanwhile, are already looking in the wild.

这其中的两个研究小组（一个由哥本哈根大学的Christina Lynggaard领导，另一个由多伦多约克大学的Elizabeth Clare领导）已经利用动物园来测试从大气中提取DNA的方法。动物园是做这方面的理想场所，因为那里有已知的动物。这两个研究小组刚刚在《Current Biology》上发表了初步结果。与此同时，其他研究小组已经开始将目光投向野外环境。

Dr Clare’s team adapted an existing sample-collection method by pumping air through filters normally employed to extract DNA from water. Dr Lynggaard’s team tried three approaches. The first percolated the air to be analysed through some water, to try to dissolve any DNA it was carrying and so permit that DNA to be analysed by conventional metagenomic methods. The second and third used fans—in one case large, of the sort employed to cool big computers in data centres, and in the other small, used to cool desktop devices. In both instances these fans blew air through filters of the type that air-conditioning systems use to remove particles of pollution.

Clare博士的团队对现有的采样方法进行了改进，将空气抽入过滤器中，过滤器通常用于从水中提取DNA。Lynggaard博士所在的团队尝试了三种方法。第一种方法是通过将要进行分析的空气渗透到水中，以试图将其携带的DNA融入其中，这样就可以用传统的宏基因组学的方法来分析DNA。第二种和第三种则是使用风扇，一种是大风扇，就是那种用来给数据中心的大型计算机降温的风扇，另一种是小风扇，用来冷却桌面设备的那种。在这两种情况下，这些风扇通过（类似空调系统用来清除污染颗粒的）过滤器吹出空气。

Dr Lynggaard’s zoo of choice was Copenhagen’s. She put testing stations inside some of the animal houses and the rainforest house, and also near several outdoor enclosures. She let the water-percolation and large-fan systems run for 30 minutes. The small-fan system was allowed a more generous 30 hours to do its stuff.

Lynggaard博士选择了哥本哈根的动物园。她在一些动物住所和热带雨林场所以及一些户外围栏附近设置了试验站。她让水渗透系统和大风扇系统运行了30分钟。小风扇系统被慷慨地允许在30小时内完成它的工作。

Dr Clare, meanwhile, chose Hamerton Zoo Park, in Britain. She, too, ran her tests for 30 minutes at a time, and did so at a score of sites, both indoors and out.

与此同时，Clare博士选择了英国的哈默顿动物园。她也一次测试30分钟，在室内和室外的多个地点进行测试。

Both groups scored palpable hits. All three of Dr Lynggaard’s methods detected 30 mammal species living nearby. Some, such as white rhinos, golden lion tamarins and Eastern grey kangaroos, were exhibits in the zoo. But many were not. Her equipment also noted, for example, red squirrels, hares, brown rats and domestic cats. Dr Clare’s findings were similarly encouraging. Her group not only logged 17 nearby zoo animals, but also eight other types of mammals and birds. These included hedgehogs, a species that has undergone a precipitous decline in Britain in recent years, and which is thus precisely the sort of creature that is of interest to those conducting ecological censuses.

两组都有明显的成果。Lyngaard博士的三种方法都发现了30种生活在附近的哺乳动物。其中白犀牛、金狮绢毛猴和东部灰袋鼠等是该动物园的“展品”。但许多动物并不是(该动物园的展品)。例如，她的设备还记录了红松鼠、野兔、棕色老鼠和家猫。Clare博士的发现同样令人鼓舞。她的团队不仅记录了17只附近动物园的动物，还记录了8只其他类型的哺乳动物和鸟类。其中包括刺猬，近年来在英国刺猬数量急剧下降，而刺猬正是进行生态普查的人们感兴趣的一种生物。

Aerial metagenomics does, then, seem to work. But vertebrates, big and showy though they are, are by no means the only important fauna in an ecosystem. Arguably, insects are more so. And Fabian Roger, an entomologist now working at ETH Zurich, in Switzerland, has shown that the technique works with them, too, and can even yield novel information about an area’s inhabitants.

因此，空中的宏基因组学似乎起作用了。但是，尽管脊椎动物体型庞大显眼，但它们绝不是生态系统中唯一重要的动物群。可以说，昆虫更是如此。目前在瑞士苏黎世联邦理工学院工作的昆虫学家Fabian Roger表示，这项技术也适用于它们，甚至可以获得有关该地区栖息动物的新信息。

Dr Roger carried out his study in collaboration with colleagues from Lund University in Sweden when he was working there before his move to Zurich. Lund is just across the Oresund bridge from Copenhagen, and it was a system similar to Dr Lynggaard’s water-percolation approach that he used. One of his chosen sites was the roof of Lund’s ecology department—a well-established location for moth surveillance using conventional light traps. The other was in a forest in Smaland, a province of Sweden a little to the north of Lund. Here, the principal targets were bees and butterflies, which in this case had been monitored the old-fashioned way, using the booted ecological infantry.

在搬到苏黎世之前，Roger博士在瑞典隆德大学工作时，与同事合作完成了这项研究。隆德就在哥本哈根的厄勒海峡大桥对面，它的系统类似于Lyngaard博士使用的水渗透方法。他选择的地点之一是隆德生态部的屋顶，这是一个使用传统捕光器监测飞蛾的良好地点。另一个地点是在斯马兰森林里，斯马兰省是瑞典的一个省，在隆德以北一点。在这里，主要的目标是蜜蜂和蝴蝶，在这种情况下，它们是用老式的方式监测的，使用穿靴子的生态“步兵”[参考第一段]。

Dr Roger has not yet published his results in a journal, but he reported them in December at an online conference called Ecology Across Borders. His version of the percolation system detected a combined total of 85 insect species at the two sites, 77 of which had been missed by more conventional sampling. It also recorded nine vertebrates (various frogs, birds and mammals) and lots of invertebrates other than insects. On the other hand, it missed 81 insect species shown by the other methods to have been present. It was especially bad at moths, noting a mere nine species while the traps caught a whopping 48. It did, though, manage to pick up five moth species that the traps had not.

Roger博士还没有在期刊上发表他的研究结果，但他在12月的一个名为“Ecology Across Borders”的在线会议上报告了这些结果。他的渗透系统在这两个地点共检测到85种昆虫，其中77种是传统取样所遗漏的。它还记录了9种脊椎动物(各种青蛙、鸟类和哺乳动物)和许多除昆虫以外的无脊椎动物。另一方面，它漏掉了用其他方法发现的81种昆虫。它对飞蛾的捕捉尤其糟糕，仅发现了9种飞蛾，而捕蛾器却捕获了惊人的48种。尽管如此，它还是成功捕捉到了五种捕蛾器没有捕捉到的蛾子。

Something which surprised both Dr Clare and Dr Lynggaard was that their zooquests detected not only exhibits, local wild animals and pets, but also some species that were none of those things. Dr Lynggaard’s equipment logged three fish: roach, smelt and salmon. Dr Clare’s sniffed DNA from cows, pigs and chickens.

令Clare博士和Lynggaard博士都感到惊讶的是，他们的动物调查人员不仅发现了“展品”、当地的野生动物和宠物，还发现了一些根本不属于这些范畴的物种。Lyngaard博士的设备记录了三种鱼: 拟鲤、胡瓜鱼和鲑鱼。Clare博士嗅出了牛、猪和鸡的DNA。

The probable source of these is feeding time. Copenhagen Zoo keeps storks, seals, sea lions, polar bears and crocodiles. All are piscovores, and the fish Dr Lynggaard detected are regularly on their menus. Hamerton, meanwhile, has a proud collection of terrestrial carnivores, including tigers, cheetahs, lynx and maned wolves. These frequently dine on chicken, pork and beef.

这些可能的来源是进食时间。哥本哈根动物园饲养着鹳、海豹、海狮、北极熊和鳄鱼。它们都是食鱼动物，Lynggaard博士发现的鱼经常出现在他们的菜单上。与此同时，哈默顿动物园则拥有一批令人自豪的陆生食肉动物，包括老虎、猎豹、猞猁和鬃狼。他们通常吃鸡肉、猪肉和牛肉。

It is one thing to pick up DNA from a living animal that is constantly shedding hair, skin cells, sweat and, indeed, urine and faeces. It is quite another to detect it from an inert lump of meat—even allowing for that meat having been mauled while it was being eaten. This discovery might be used to help a branch of conservation different from the habitat monitoring that Dr Clare, Dr Lynggaard and Dr Roger have in mind, for it could be employed to create devices that detect species in which international trade is illegal.

从一个不断脱落毛发、皮肤细胞、汗液、尿液和粪便的活体动物身上提取DNA是一回事。从一团不活动的肉中检测出它就完全是另一回事了，即使考虑到那块肉是在被吃掉时被咬伤的。这个发现可能会被用来帮助保护的一个分支，与Clare博士、Lynggaard博士和Roger博士所想的栖息地监测不同，因为它可以被用来制造检测那些国际贸易是非法的物种的设备。

Such species are often hidden contraband—pangolin meat on its way to market, for example. But the technique could also help distinguish whether something that was being imported openly, like a cargo of fish, was actually what it claimed to be rather than an endangered lookalike.

这些物种通常是被隐藏的违禁品，例如，穿山甲肉在运往市场的途中。但这项技术也可以帮助区分一些公开进口的东西，比如一船鱼，是否真的是它所声称的，而不是一种濒临灭绝的物种。

Early warning

Returning to the question of monitoring wild habitats, sampling airborne DNA might give early notice of the arrival there of new species—whether naturally from nearby, as a consequence of things like climate change, or more artificially, from far away, by the introduction of an alien and potentially disruptive organism. In the second case, early detection might allow action to be taken before the invader became established.

回到监测野生栖息地的问题上，采样空气中的DNA可能可以提前通知新物种的到来，无论是来自附近的自然物种，如气候变化的结果，还是更人为的，远方的外来物种和潜在的破坏性生物的引入。在第二种情况下，早期发现可能使我们能够在入侵者扎根之前采取行动。

Conversely, DNA sniffing might one day give warning when a local species was in trouble, though it is not yet sensitive enough to do that. Also, as Dr Roger’s results demonstrate, it works best at the moment as an addition to, rather than a substitution for, established methods, so eager PhD students need not fret about redundancy just yet.

相反，DNA嗅探有朝一日可能会在当地物种遇到麻烦时发出警告，尽管它还不够敏感，无法做到这一点。而且，正如Roger博士的研究结果所显示的那样，目前它作为现有方法的补充而不是替代方法的效果最好，所以热切的博士生们现在还不必担心“失业”。

But one thing which has proved true so far about DNA-related technology is that it gets better and cheaper as time passes. Whether it will ever get to the point when ramblers through wood and over meadow will have to get used to the sight of DNA sniffers keeping a nose on the local ecology remains to be seen. But the results which Dr Lynggaard, Dr Clare and Dr Roger have come up with suggest that such sniffers will, at the least, be a valuable addition to ecologists’ arsenals. ■

但到目前为止，DNA相关技术已经被证明是正确的，那就是随着时间的推移，它会变得越来越好，越来越便宜。当漫步在树林和草地上的人不得不习惯DNA嗅探器对当地生态环境的监测时，它是否能捕捉到重点，还有待观察。但是Lyngaard博士、Clare博士和Roger博士提出的研究结果表明，这种嗅探器至少会成为生态学家“资源库”中有价值的补充。■

苹果到底想在媒体领域发展到什么程度?

The accidental mogul

The $3trn tech firm is not playing the same game as its rivals

这家市值3万亿美元的科技公司并没有与其竞争对手玩同样的把戏

Jan 8th 2022

文章音频

AS VIOLINS PLAY mournfully, Jon Stewart, an American comic, makes a mock-emotional appeal to viewers. “Every year thousands of hours of high-quality content go unwatched,” he says seriously. “Because good, hard-working people… don’t know how to find Apple TV+.”

当小提琴悲伤地演奏时，美国喜剧演员Jon Stewart向观众发出了一种故作伤感的呼吁。他严肃地说：“每年都有成千上万小时的高质量内容无人观看。因为善良、努力工作的人……不知道怎么找到Apple TV+。”

The world’s most valuable company can afford a few jokes at its own expense. In the past year the tech colossus has raked in $366bn in revenue, a third more than in 2020. On January 3rd its market capitalisation briefly exceeded $3trn (see chart 1). The mere billions that it is investing in media, including a new television show hosted by Mr Stewart, represent pocket change to the Silicon Valley giant.

这家全球市值最高的公司可以自费开几个玩笑。过去一年，这家科技巨头的营收达到3660亿美元，比2020年高出三分之一。1月3日，它的市值一度超过3万亿美元(见图1)。它在媒体上投资的区区数十亿美元（其中包括Stewart先生主持的一个新电视节目），对这家硅谷巨头来说，只是一笔小钱。

Yet some 300 miles (480km) down the coast in Hollywood, where executives used to snigger about the dilettantes from big-tech land up north, Apple’s dabbling in media is no joke. Though it lags well behind Netflix and the like, Apple has enough money to ride out the increasingly expensive streaming wars, which threaten to bankrupt other players. One question keeps its rivals awake at night: What does Apple want out of show business?

然而，在距离好莱坞海岸300英里（480公里）的地方，那里的高管们过去常常嘲笑那些来自北方大型科技公司的“半吊子”，不过苹果涉足媒体可不是闹着玩的。尽管苹果远远落后于Netflix等公司，但它有足够的资金经受住这场日益昂贵的流媒体战争，这场战争有可能使其他参与的公司破产。有一个问题让竞争对手夜不能寐: 苹果想从演艺界得到什么?

Apple became a big noise in music when it launched iTunes 21 years ago this week. It took a cut of songs’ sales, and shifted hundreds of millions of iPods for people to play them. Later iTunes sold movies, too, and the firm hoped to make the same model work in television, where the market is an order of magnitude larger than music. But paying for downloads was superseded by all-you-can-eat subscriptions, pioneered by Spotify in music and Netflix in TV. Unlike downloaded music or films, subscriptions could be easily moved between platforms. So Apple, seeing little opportunity to lock consumers into its devices, sat out the streaming revolution.

21年前的这个星期，苹果公司发布了iTunes，成为了音乐界的一个大人物。它削减了歌曲的价格，并将音乐导入数以亿计的ipods供人们播放。后来，iTunes也销售电影，并且公司希望同样的模式也能在电视上运作，因为电视的市场规模比音乐大一个数量级。但付费下载被“自助餐式”的订阅服务取代，音乐领域的Spotify和电视领域的Netflix率先推出了这种服务。与下载的音乐或电影不同，订阅服务可以很容易地在不同平台之间转移。因此，苹果认为将消费者“锁定”在其设备上的机会微乎其微，因此没有参与到这场流媒体革命。

Today it is back in the media game, and a bigger force than Mr Stewart’s joke implies (see chart 2). Apple Music, launched in 2015, is the second-largest streamer after Spotify. Apple TV+, now two years old, is the fourth-largest video service outside China by the number of subscribers, according to Omdia, a data company. In the past couple of years Apple has made smaller media bets including Arcade, a subscription gaming package, News+, a publishing bundle, and Fitness+, which offers video aerobics classes. There is talk of an audiobooks service later this year.

如今，它又回到了媒体“博弈”中，其影响力比Stewart先生的玩笑所暗示的还要大(见表2)。苹果音乐于2015年推出，是仅次于Spotify的第二大流媒体网站。根据数据公司Omdia的数据，Apple TV+已推出两年，按用户数量计算，是中国以外第四大视频服务。过去几年，苹果在媒体领域的投资规模较小，其中包括订阅游戏包Arcade、捆绑新闻服务News+和提供视频有氧运动课程的Fitness+。今年晚些时候有传言说会推出有声读物服务。

Like Amazon, another tech giant with a sideline in media, Apple has been able to roll out its offerings more quickly in more countries than most of its Hollywood rivals, which have had to build direct-to-consumer businesses from scratch. And it can afford to be generous with free trials: less than a third of Apple TV+ subscribers pay for the service, Omdia believes. It has had some hits, notably “Ted Lasso”, which won a string of Emmy awards in September. But it lacks a back-catalogue, leading to high rates of customer churn. Smaller competitors like Paramount+ (part of Viacom CBS) and Peacock (from NBC Universal) have limited new offerings but decades-old libraries.

与另一家将媒体视为副业的科技巨头亚马逊一样，苹果能够以更快的速度在更多国家推出其产品，而好莱坞的大多数竞争对手不得不从零开始建立直接面向消费者的业务。此外，它还可以慷慨地提供免费试用服务: Omdia认为，只有不到三分之一的Apple TV+用户为这项服务付费。它也有过一些成功的例子，最著名的是《足球教练》，它在九月份获得了一系列的艾美奖。但它缺乏老影片，导致了客户的高流失率。像Paramount+(来自Viacom CBS)和Peacock(来自NBC环球)这样较小的竞争对手，提供的新作品有限，但有几十年前的影片库。

Old-media firms have been puzzled by Apple’s on-off sorties into their territory, which sometimes seem half-hearted. Winning at streaming depends mainly on splurging on content. But deep-pocketed Apple spent just over $2bn on film and TV in 2021, against Amazon’s $9bn and Netflix’s $14bn, estimates Ampere Analysis, a research company. It doesn’t bother to market its efforts much. And although medialand has cooed at the executives that Apple has poached, such as Jamie Erlicht and Zack Van Amburg from Sony and Richard Plepler from HBO, Silicon Valley insiders say that Apple keeps its own top tech people on other projects.

老媒体公司一直对苹果断断续续进攻他们的领域感到困惑，有时又缺乏热情。在流媒体上取胜主要取决于在内容上的挥霍。但据研究公司Ampere Analysis估计，在2021年，财力雄厚的苹果在电影和电视领域的支出略高于20亿美元，而亚马逊支出了90亿美元，Netflix为140亿美元。它不需要费心去推广自己的努力。尽管媒体领域对苹果挖走的高管们表示赞赏，比如索尼的Jamie Erlicht和Zack Van Amburg，以及HBO的Richard Plepler，但硅谷内部人士表示，苹果在其他项目上保留了自己的顶级技术人员。

Indeed, while Hollywood frets about Apple’s next move, many in Silicon Valley wonder why it is in media at all. None of the markets is a big prize for the world’s most valuable firm. The entire global recorded music industry had sales of $22bn in 2020, less than Apple made just from selling iPads. In about a month Apple generates as much revenue as Netflix makes in a year. Apple’s TV business depends on buying shows, rather than extracting rents from others’ creations as it did in the iTunes days (and as it still does in its app store). And the “lock-in” effect on consumers is weak, since Apple’s main media services are available on all platforms.

确实，当好莱坞对苹果的下一步行动感到担忧时，硅谷的许多人想知道苹果为什么要进军媒体领域。对于这家世界上最有价值的公司来说，没有一个(媒体)市场是有大回报的。2020年，整个全球唱片行业的销售额为220亿美元，低于苹果仅销售ipad的销售额。苹果一个月的收入相当于Netflix一年的收入。苹果的电视业务依赖于购买节目，而不是像iTunes时代那样从其他人的创作中榨取“租金”(它在其应用商店中仍然如此)。由于苹果的主要媒体服务在所有平台上都可以使用，因此对消费者的“锁定”效应也很弱。

Apple’s renewed interest in media is best explained by the transformation in the company’s scale, which radically changes the calculation of which side-projects are worthwhile. Fifteen years ago, when Netflix started streaming, the billions involved in running a film studio would have represented close to a double-digit chunk of Apple’s annual revenues. Back then, Silicon Valley executives would fly down to Los Angeles, thinking “We’ve got a big chequebook, we could go and buy a bunch of content,” says Benedict Evans, a tech analyst and former venture capitalist. “And they would go and have their first meeting in LA. And the LA people would tell them the price”—at which point the tech people would go home. In 2021 Apple TV+’s estimated content budget represented 0.6% of company revenues: “play money”, as Mr Evans puts it.

苹果对媒体的兴趣重新燃起，最好的解释是公司规模的转变，这从根本上改变了对“哪些副业值得做”的考虑。15年前，当Netflix开始提供流媒体服务时，运营一家电影制片厂所需的数十亿美元占苹果的年收入比例的近两位数。科技分析师、前风险投资家本Benedict Evans表示，当时，硅谷的高管们会飞到洛杉矶，想着“我们有一大笔钱，我们可以去买一大堆内容。” 然后他们在洛杉矶第一次见面。洛杉矶的人会告诉他们价格”，那时技术人员就会回家了。2021年，苹果TV+估计的内容预算占公司收入的0.6%: 用Evans先生的话说，这是“玩钱”。

The cost of running a studio can therefore be justified by what are only modest benefits to Apple. Streaming subscriptions may not lock people in as strongly as iTunes purchases did, but Apple’s various services still sink “meat hooks” into customers, making them spend more time with their devices and making it a bit more inconvenient to leave Apple’s ecosystem, says Nick Lightle, a former Spotify executive. The iPhone itself, which generated $192bn in sales in the past year, more than half of Apple’s total revenues, is sold as a sort of subscription, points out Mr Evans. Anything that cuts churn among iPhone subscribers by even a small amount is likely to pay for itself.

因此，运营一家制片厂的成本可以用苹果获得的微薄利益来衡量。Spotify前高管Nick Lightle表示，流媒体订阅可能不会像iTunes购买那样牢牢地锁定用户，但苹果的各种服务仍会让用户挂到“肉钩”上，让他们花更多时间在自己的设备上，让离开苹果的生态系统变得更不方便。Evans指出，过去一年，iPhone的销售额达到1920亿美元，占苹果总营收的一半以上，iPhone本身是以某种订阅的方式销售的。任何能够减少iPhone用户流失的举措，哪怕只是一点点，都有可能收回成本。

Media also makes good marketing. Producing films with Steven Spielberg and Tom Hanks reinforces Apple’s premium brand. Partnerships with pop stars keep it cool. And at a time when Silicon Valley is under attack for monopolistic practices, invasion of privacy, subversion of democracy and more, Apple is churning out worthy podcasts by Malala Yousafzai, a Nobel laureate, and teaching fitness routines to children. Not many companies can think of a film studio as a public-relations arm. A $3trn company can.

媒体也能起到很好的营销作用。与Steven Spielberg和Tom Hanks合作制作电影，强化了苹果的优质品牌。与流行明星的合作让它保持冷静。在硅谷因垄断行为、侵犯隐私、颠覆民主等问题受到攻击之际，苹果推出了诺贝尔奖得主Malala Yousafzai制作的有价值的播客，还有教孩子们健身的课程。没有多少公司会把电影制片厂当作公关部门。一家市值3万亿美元的公司可以。

“Apple is not playing the same game as many of its other [media] competitors,” says Julia Alexander of Parrot Analytics, another data firm. For one-trick rivals like Netflix, it is an uncomfortably asymmetric competition. Yet Apple’s broader priorities can also hamstring its media ambitions. Apple TV+’s lack of a library could be solved by buying someone else’s; the firm has been touted as a potential buyer of small studios like Lionsgate as well as giant ones like Disney. But Apple may be wary of provoking America’s Federal Trade Commission (FTC), which has its sights on Silicon Valley. “If you’re Apple and the FTC is looking at big tech, the last thing you want to do is make a huge acquisition,” notes Ms Alexander. Lina Khan, the FTC’s tech-bashing head, is examining Amazon’s recent $8.5bn purchase of MGM Studios; never mind that the target is a relative tiddler in a fragmented market. As firms vie for control of tech’s next commanding heights, from decentralised Web3 to virtual reality, drawing regulators’ attention by buying old TV episodes could be a strategic error.

另一家数据公司Parrot Analytics的Julia Alexander表示:“苹果不像其他许多媒体竞争对手那样玩同样的把戏。” 对于Netflix这样只会耍一招的竞争对手来说，这是一种令人不安的不对称竞争。然而，苹果更广泛的优先事项也可能削弱其媒体雄心。Apple TV+没有影片库的问题可以通过购买别人的来解决; 该公司曾被吹捧为狮门电影公司等小型电影公司以及迪士尼等大型电影公司的潜在买家。但苹果可能会小心翼翼地激怒美国联邦贸易委员会，因为FTC已经把目光投向了硅谷。 Alexander指出:“如果你是苹果公司，而联邦贸易委员会正在调查大型科技公司，你最不想做的事就是进行大规模收购。” 联邦贸易委员会抨击科技公司的负责人Lina Khan正在调查亚马逊最近以85亿美元收购米高梅电影公司的交易; 尽管该公司的目标公司在一个分散的市场中只是一家相对较小的公司。随着各家公司竞相控制科技的下一个制高点，从去中心化的Web3到虚拟现实，通过购买旧电视剧来吸引监管机构的注意可能是一个战略错误。

For as long as they continue to help sell its devices and burnish its brand, Apple will keep dripping investment into its media services. Doing so will get more expensive: global spending on video content will exceed $230bn in 2022, according to Ampere, nearly double what it was a decade ago. As smaller competitors are outspent and give up, Apple’s position could even strengthen. But given its bigger ambitions in other industries, in media Apple is likely to be satisfied to stick to its role as a supporting actor. ■

只要它们继续帮助苹果销售其设备，提升其品牌，苹果就会继续对其媒体服务进行大量投资。这样做将变得更加昂贵: 根据Ampere的数据，到2022年，全球在视频内容上的支出将超过2300亿美元，几乎是10年前的两倍。随着规模较小的竞争对手被超越并放弃，苹果的地位甚至可能会加强。但考虑到苹果在其他行业的雄心，在媒体领域，它很可能满足于继续扮演配角的角色。■

The rise of performative work

表演工作的兴起

Bartleby

巴托比

![image-20220112103234389](/Users/chenyujie/Library/Application Support/typora-user-images/image-20220112103234389.png)

It’s not what you do. It’s how ostentatiously you do it

重要的不是你做什么。重要的是你做的有多么招摇

Jan 8th 2022

文章音频

IN AN EPISODE of “Seinfeld”, a vintage TV sitcom, the character of George Costanza reveals the secret of pretending to work: act irritated. He shakes his head, frowns and sighs to demonstrate the technique. “When you look annoyed all the time, people think that you’re busy.” In comments posted below this clip on YouTube, visitors report with delight that the tactic really does work and offer a few tips of their own: walk around the office carrying manila envelopes, advises one.

在经典电视情景喜剧《宋飞正传》的一集里，George Costanza这个角色揭示了假装工作的秘密: 表现得很生气。他摇头、皱眉、叹气来演示这个技巧。“如果你总是看起来很烦，人们会认为你很忙。” 在YouTube上这段视频下方的评论中，观看视频的人高兴地表示，这一策略确实有效，并提供了一些他们自己的建议: 有人建议，带着马尼拉信封[类似我们经常装文件用的牛皮纸袋]在办公室里走来走去。

Before the pandemic turned everyone into remote employees, managers worried that working from home would be a paradise for slackers like George. People would be out of sight and out of mind: starting late, clocking off early and doing nothing in between. The reality of remote working has turned out to be different. Days have become longer and employees are demonstratively visible. Work has become more performative.

在疫情让所有人都变成远程员工之前，管理者曾担心，在家工作将是George这样的懒鬼的天堂。这样一来，他们就会淡出管理者的视线范围内，而且还可以开小差: 他们可以迟点开始上班，早点下班，其间可以什么都不做。事实上，远程工作已经被证明并不是这样的。白天工作的时间变长了，员工的身影随处可见。工作变得更具表现性。

The simple act of logging on is now public. Green dots by your name on messaging channels are the virtual equivalents of jackets left on chairs and monitors turned on. Calendars are now frequently shared: empty ones look lazy; full ones appear virtuous.

简单的登录行为现在是公开的。在信息通讯频道上，你名字旁边的绿点相当于把夹克放在椅子上、显示器打开的“虚拟”形式。日历现在经常被共享: 空的日历看起来很懒散; 满的日历则看起来品行端正。

Communication is more likely to happen on open messaging channels, where everyone can see who is contributing and who is not. Emails can be performative, too—scheduled for the early morning or the weekend, or the early morning on the weekend, to convey Stakhanovite effort. Repeated noises like Slack’s knock-brush provide a soundtrack of busyness.

交流更可能发生在开放的信息通讯频道上，在那里每个人都可以看到谁在贡献，谁没有贡献。电子邮件可以是表现性的，把发邮件时间安排在清晨或周末，或周末的清晨，以传达工作狂的努力。重复的声音（如懒鬼的敲击刷音）提供了忙碌的音轨。

Meetings, the office’s answer to the theatre, have proliferated. They are harder to avoid now that invitations must be responded to and diaries are public. Even if you don’t say anything, cameras make meetings into a miming performance: an attentive expression and occasional nodding now count as a form of work. The chat function is a new way to project yourself. Satya Nadella, the boss of Microsoft, says that comments in chat help him to meet colleagues he would not otherwise hear from. Maybe so, but that is an irresistible incentive to pose questions that do not need answering and offer observations that are not worth making.

作为办公室对“演习”的回应就是“会议激增”。由于现在的邀请必须得到回应，而且日程安排也是公开的，他们很难避免。即使你什么都不说，摄像头也会把会议变成一场哑剧表演: 专注的表情和偶尔的点头现在也算是一种工作形式了。聊天功能是一种展示自己的新方式。微软的老板Satya Nadella说，聊天中的评论让他认识了以前不会联系到的同事。也许是这样，但这是一个不可抗拒的鼓励措施，他们提出不需要回答的问题，并提出不值得发表的意见。

Shared documents and messaging channels are also playgrounds of performativity. Colleagues can leave public comments in documents, and in the process notify their authors that something approximating work has been done. They can start new channels and invite anyone in; when no one uses them, they can archive them again and appear efficient. By assigning tasks to people or tagging them in a conversation, they can cast long shadows of faux-industriousness. It is telling that one recent research study found that members of high-performing teams are more likely to speak to each other on the phone, the very opposite of public communication.

共享文档和信息通讯频道也是表现性的游乐场。同事可以在文档中留下公开评论，并在这个过程中通知作者已经完成了一些类似的工作。他们可以开启新通道，邀请任何人加入; 当没有人使用它们时，它们可以重新存档，显得高效。通过给人们分配任务或在对话中给他们贴上标签，他们可以给人留下一长串假装勤奋的影子。最近的一项研究表明，高绩效团队的成员更有可能通过电话交流，这与公共交流截然相反。

Performative celebration is another hallmark of the pandemic. Once one person has reacted to a message with a clapping emoji, others are likely to join in until a virtual ovation is under way. At least emojis are fun. The arrival of a round-robin email announcing a promotion is as welcome as a rifle shot in an avalanche zone. Someone responds with congratulations, and then another recipient adds their own well wishes. As more people pile in, pressure builds on the non-responders to reply as well. Within minutes colleagues are telling someone they have never met in person how richly they deserve their new job.

表现性庆祝是疫情的另一个标志。在虚拟热烈鼓掌开始之前，一旦一个人用鼓掌的表情包回应一条信息，其他人很可能会加入进来。至少表情包还有趣。收到一封循环式的电子邮件宣布升职，就像雪崩地带的来复枪射击一样受人欢迎。有些人会回复祝贺，然后另一个人会加上他们自己的祝福。随着越来越多的人“加入进来”，那些没有反应的人也受到了压力，要求他们做出回应。几分钟之内，同事们就开始对一个从未见过面的人说，他们多么配得上这份新工作。

Theatre has always been an important part of the workplace. Open communication is a prerequisite of successful remote working. But the prevalence of performative work is bad news—not just for the George Costanzas of the world, who can no longer truly tune out, but also for employees who have to catch up on actual tasks once the show is over. By extension it is also bad for productivity. Why, then, does it persist?

剧院则一直是工作场所的重要组成部分。开放的交流是远程工作成功的先决条件。但是，表演工作的盛行是一个坏消息，不仅仅是对世界上的George Costanzas这样的人来说(他们再也不能真正地充耳不闻了)，对那些在“演出”结束后还要忙于实际工作的员工来说也是如此。推而广之，这也不利于生产力。那么，它为什么会持续存在呢?

One answer lies in the natural desire of employees to demonstrate how hard they are working, like bowerbirds with a keyboard. Another lies in managers’ need to see what everyone is up to. And a third is hinted at in recent research, from academics at two French business schools, which found that white-collar professionals are drawn to a level of “optimal busyness”, which neither overwhelms them nor leaves them with much time to think. Rushing from meeting to meeting, triaging emails and hitting a succession of small deadlines can deliver a buzz, even if nothing much is actually being achieved. The performance is what counts.

一个原因是员工们想要展示自己工作有多努力的天性，就像有键盘的园丁鸟一样。另一个原因在于管理者需要了解每个人在做什么。法国两所商学院的学者在最近的一项研究中暗示了第三个原因，该研究发现，白领专业人士喜欢上一种“最佳忙碌”的强度，这种忙碌既不会让他们不知所措，也不会让他们有太多时间思考。从一个会议赶到另一个会议，对邮件进行分类处理，按一系列的截止日期完成任务，这些都能让人感到兴奋，即使实际上并没有什么成果。表现才是最重要的。

不过，这些指控仍然对君主制构成了威胁。今年6月，英国将庆祝女王伊丽莎白二世96岁的白金禧年，也就是她在位70周年。这可能是她统治时期最后一次盛大的表演。到那时，查尔斯国王的加冕仪式将重新获得公众对世袭君主制的认可，这种君主制构成了英国宪法的顶点。

Once upon a time, that consent rested on principle. The inheritance of titles, land and property through primogeniture was believed in as a good in itself. That belief was bolstered by widespread religious faith: a British monarch is sworn to defend the Protestant church. As late as 1956, a third of Britons thought the queen had been chosen by God.

曾几何时，这种同意是基于原则的。人们认为，通过“长子继承权”来继承头衔、土地和财产本身就是一种好事。这种信念得到了广泛的宗教信仰的支持:英国君主宣誓保卫新教教会。直到1956年，三分之一的英国人还认为女王是上帝选择的。

But as her reign wore on, principle was no longer enough. Britain’s monarchy became what Vernon Bogdanor, a constitutional historian, calls “utilitarian”, with legitimacy resting on the good it does. It became necessary for royals to be hardworking and do-gooding. It embraced the rhetoric of meritocracy: never mind how the queen got her job, what mattered was that she performed it as well as any elected head of state. Intimacy succeeded reverence: nowadays, commoners love the royal family in part because they think they know it.

但随着她的统治时间的推移，原则已经不够了。英国的君主制变成了宪政历史学家Vernon Bogdanor所说的“功利主义”，其合法性建立在它所做的好事之上。对皇室成员来说，勤劳和行善是必要的。它接受了精英统治的大话空话: 别管女王是如何得到这份工作的，重要的是她的表现和任何民选国家元首一样出色。亲密超越了崇敬: 如今，平民热爱王室，部分原因是他们认为自己了解王室。

Ms Giuffre’s allegations have thrown all this into doubt. Britain’s main anti-monarchist movement, Republic, plans to step up its campaigning during the jubilee. It hopes the succession will open up debate on the constitution. The queen is the royal family’s “heat shield”, says its leader, Graham Smith. “When she is gone they’re going to be very vulnerable.”

Giuffre女士的指控使这一切都受到质疑。英国主要的反君主制运动“共和”计划在周年庆祝期间加强其竞选活动。它希望继任将开启关于宪法的辩论。共和领导人Graham Smith说，女王是王室的“隔热罩”，当她“走”后，他们将非常脆弱。

A utilitarian monarchy is undermined by a prince who has been forced by scandal to abandon his duties, and now passes the time horse-riding in the grounds of Windsor Castle. A meritocratic monarchy is undermined by a prince unsuited to diplomacy, whose alibis have been widely ridiculed and who, even after Epstein’s death, declared he had no regrets about their “very useful” friendship. It is a reminder that heredity is a lottery, sometimes throwing up stars like Queen Elizabeth II, and sometimes duds. As for intimacy, the royal family’s affairs and divorces in the 1990s bound the monarchy closer to ordinary people, with their own messy marriages. The allegations made about Prince Andrew are hardly relatable.

一个功利的君主制被一位王子暗中破坏了，这位王子因为丑闻被迫放弃了他的职责，现在他在温莎城堡的庭院里骑马消磨时间。一个不适合外交的王子削弱了精英领导的君主制，他的不在场证明受到了广泛的嘲笑，甚至在Epstein死后，他还宣称，他对他们“非常不错”的友谊并不感到遗憾。这就提醒了人们，遗传是一种彩票，有时能投出像伊丽莎白女王二世这样的优秀的人，有时也能投出窝囊废。至于亲密关系，上世纪90年代的皇室风流韵事以及离婚，通过王室混乱的婚姻，拉近了王室与普通人的距离。关于安德鲁王子的指控几乎不怎么能引起共鸣。

The bare fact of Ms Giuffre’s case, whether it succeeds or fails, leaves the royal family with no good options for dealing with Prince Andrew. There is talk of sending him into internal exile: asking him not to use his titles and stripping him of his military ranks. That would leave him in the line of succession. But the alternative—him renouncing his claim to the throne—would probably be even more damaging. It is fundamental to a hereditary order that succession is automatic and beyond debate. Start tinkering with the line because one son is better qualified, more popular or more moral, and the logic falls apart.

Giuffre案不加掩饰的事实，无论该案成功还是失败，都让王室在处理安德鲁王子时没有好的选择。有传言说要把他流放到国内: 要求他不要使用他的头衔，并剥夺他的军衔。这将使他踢出继承人的行列。但另一种选择，让他放弃对王位继承权，这可能会更具破坏性。继承是自动的、无可争议的，这是世袭秩序的基础。如果因为一个儿子更有资格、更受欢迎或更有道德就开始修改这个顺序，那么逻辑就会崩溃。

As Edward Bulwer-Lytton, a Whig politician in the 19th century, once put it: “What suicide is to a man, abdication is to a king.” That is why Edward VIII’s abdication in 1936 was so traumatic, and why Prince Harry, despite having “stepped back” from royal duties for a more glamorous life in Hollywood, remains sixth in line. The monarchy cannot tolerate Prince Andrew on the inside, but it cannot eject him either. The best it can do is close its eyes, and pretend he was never there.

正如19世纪辉格党政治家Edward Bulwer-Lytton所言:“退位之于国王，犹如自杀之于人。” 这就是为什么1936年爱德华八世的退位给人留下了如此巨大的创伤，也是为什么哈里王子尽管为了在好莱坞过上更光鲜的生活而“退居”了王室职责，但仍然排在第六位。王室内部不能容忍安德鲁王子，但也不能驱逐他。它能做的最好的事就是闭上眼睛，假装他从未出现过。
