Hostility towards private equity’s push into property is misguided

对私募股权进军房地产的敌意有点误导

Barbarians at the garden gate

花园门口的野蛮人

Big investors are filling a gap in the market

大投资者正在填补市场的空白

“VULTURES OUT” read one of the many placards young protesters waved at a recent rally in Dublin. The cause of their anger was spiralling rents in the Irish capital, pushed higher as the fastest growth in house prices in years has made renting more attractive. The sentiment is spreading. Private-equity firms, insurance companies, pension funds and other institutional investors that have snapped up residential property during the pandemic are becoming the butt of resentment in rich countries. As their share of the residential-property market has grown, so has the backlash. Some blame big landlords for soaring rents. Others accuse them of exploiting crisis for profit.

在都柏林最近举行的一场集会上，年轻的抗议者挥舞着标语牌，其中一块牌子上写着“让趁火打劫的人滚出去”！他们愤怒的原因是因为爱尔兰首都的房租不断上涨，由于近年来房价增长最快，租房变得更有吸引力，房租被推高了。这种情绪正在蔓延。在疫情期间抢购住宅地产的私募股权公司、保险公司、养老基金和其他机构投资者，正成为发达国家怨恨的对象。随着他们在住宅房地产市场的份额增加，强烈反对的人也在增加。一些人将房租飙升归咎于大地主。其他人则指责他们利用危机牟利。

Policymakers have been fast to respond The White House wants to restrict the types of properties that large investors are allowed to buy. New Zealand has scrapped tax breaks for property investors, and Ireland has slapped a 10% tax on the bulk-buying of houses. Canada’s central bank says the role that big investors play in housing requires more scrutiny. In Germany Berlin’s residents voted in September to force their city’s biggest landlords to sell more than 200,000 flats to the state, though the referendum was non-binding and the constitutional court is expected to overturn the result if it becomes law. Spain’s left-wing government is the latest to unleash measures to deal with big landlords. Under new proposals, they will face rent controls, higher taxes on empty property and a ban on buying social housing.

各国政策制定者迅速作出回应。白宫希望限制大型投资者可以购买的房地产类型。新西兰已经取消了对房地产投资者的税收优惠，爱尔兰对大量购买房屋的人征收10%的税。加拿大央行表示，大投资者在房地产市场所扮演的角色需要更严格的审查。在德国，柏林的居民今年9月投票，迫使该市最大的房东向州政府出售20多万套公寓。不过，公投没有约束力，如果公投结果成为法律，宪法法院预计将推翻这一结果。西班牙左翼政府是最新一个对大地主采取措施的政府。在新的提议下，他们将面临房租管制、对空置房产征收更高的税以及禁止购买社会保障性住房。

From this brouhaha, you might think that professional landlords are gobbling up the market. In fact, their share remains modest. In America investors own just 2% of rental homes. Across Europe, publicly listed funds own less than 5%. In Spain the criticism has focused on Blackstone, the country’s biggest residential landlord. After entering the market eight years ago, the private-equity giant now owns 30,000 homes. Yet this amounts to just 1% of the total stock.

从这场骚动中，你可能会认为职业房东正在吞食这个市场。事实上，他们所占的比例不多。在美国，投资者只拥有2%的出租房屋。在整个欧洲，上市基金的持股比例不到5%。在西班牙，批评集中在该国最大的房屋住宅地主黑石集团身上。在8年前进入这个市场后，这家私募股权巨头现在拥有3万套住房。不过，这只占总存量的1%。

There is no doubt that big investors have big ambitions. Single-family homes and flats that are built to rent have become a lucrative line of business. Other Wall Street firms such as KKR and Goldman Sachs are also piling in to the market—and they are building as well as buying. By some estimates, they account for more than 6% of new homes in America each year. Across Britain, institutional investors are expected to supply a tenth of the government’s housing target in the next few years. Since 2018 they have built nearly a quarter of new homes in Liverpool, and more than 15% in Nottingham, Leicester and Sheffield.

毫无疑问，大投资者有着远大的抱负。专为出租而建的独栋住宅和公寓已经成为一项利润丰厚的业务。其他华尔街公司，如KKR和高盛也纷纷涌入市场，他们在买入的同时也在建造。据估计，它们占美国每年新建房屋的6%以上。在英国，机构投资者预计将在未来几年内供应政府住房目标的十分之一。自2018年以来，他们在利物浦建造了近四分之一的新房，在诺丁汉、莱斯特和谢菲尔德建造了超过15%的新房。

This injection of capital should be welcomed, not scorned. The investors want to make money, naturally, but they see a gap in the market that needs filling and they are doing something about it. Demand for rental housing has never been higher. In Britain less than one in ten homes were rented in the mid-1990s. The share today is closer to one in five. A third of households in America are rented. Falling home ownership rates across the rich world mean that decent quality housing in the private rented market is more sought-after than ever. Yet tenancies are insecure and the supply of rental homes has failed to keep up with demand. A number of countries face chronic shortages. Canada’s national rental vacancy rate in 2020 was 3%. In parts of Australia it is below 1%.

这种资金的投入应该受到欢迎，而不是蔑视。投资者自然想赚钱，但他们看到市场上有一个缺口需要填补，他们正在为此做些什么。对出租屋的需求从未如此之高。上世纪90年代中期，英国只有不到十分之一的房屋出租。如今，这一比例接近五分之一。美国三分之一的家庭是租房来住的。发达国家的住房拥有率不断下降，这意味着私人租赁市场上优质的住房比以往任何时候都更受欢迎。然而，租房市场并不稳定，出租房屋的供应也跟不上需求。一些国家面临着长期短缺的问题。加拿大2020年的全国租房空置率为3%。在澳大利亚的部分地区，这一比例低于1%。

The flood of institutional money into the rental market comes at a crucial time. City centres are full of empty buildings, as a result of the pandemic. That has created the chance to expand housing there by converting them. However, this will require not only an overhaul of planning rules but lots of money to pay for the building work. Cities like New York show what is possible. Big investors there have been turning offices into homes for years. As a result, some 60,000 people live in Lower Manhattan today, up from just 14,000 in the mid-1990s. The City of London believes it has room for an extra 1,500 homes by 2030.

大量机构资金涌入租赁市场正值关键时刻。受疫情的影响，市中心到处都是空置的建筑。这就创造了通过改建房屋来扩大住房的机会。然而，这不仅需要彻底修改规划规则，还需要大量资金来支付建筑工作。像纽约这样的城市就展示了“什么是可能的”。这里的大投资者多年来一直在把办公室改造成住宅。因此，如今曼哈顿下城约有6万人居住，而20世纪90年代中期只有1.4万人。伦敦金融城认为，到2030年，它还能再为1500套住房腾出空间。

The crux of the problem is a lack of supply in the places where economic opportunities are greatest. Some say the answer is higher interest rates or macroprudential tools, such as restrictions on the amounts that banks can lend. These policies would temper demand and price growth, but would not bring the economic benefits of letting successful cities grow. Some favour loan schemes for first-time buyers, but these only inflate house prices—failing both homebuyers and taxpayers. Straightforward solutions such as relaxing planning laws can be politically poisonous. Britain seems to have shelved a proposed planning reform that would have encouraged more house-building.

问题的关键是经济机会最大的地方供应短缺。一些人回应称是因为较高利率或宏观审慎工具，比如限制银行的贷款额度。这些政策会抑制需求和价格增长，但不会带来让城市成功发展所带来的经济利益。一些人支持对首次购房者的贷款计划，但这只会导致房价上涨，这对购房者和纳税人都不利。像放宽规划法律这样的直截了当的解决方案可能不利于政治。英国似乎已经搁置了一项旨在鼓励更多房屋建设的规划改革提案。

Rather than rely on gimmicks, countries must build their way out of the crisis. That is why, instead of caricaturing big funds as barbarians at the garden gate, policymakers should lay out the welcome mat. ■

各国必须努力走出危机，而不是依靠噱头。这也就是为什么要政策制定者不应将大投资人讽刺为“花园门口的野蛮人”，而应摆出欢迎的姿态。■

Don’t mock the metaverse

不要嘲笑元宇宙

The future of the internet

互联网的未来

The metaverse is already here. It’s just not evenly distributed

元宇宙已经出现了。只是分布不均匀

“A REVOLUTIONARY APPROACH on how to connect our world without being super-weird…In the Icelandverse, there’s…skies you can see with your eyeballs, volcanic rocks you can caress, and really big geysers you can observe from a safe distance.” So runs a viral advert designed to lure tourists to Iceland. The target of the parody is Mark Zuckerberg in particular, and Silicon Valley in general, for whom the idea of the “metaverse”—a sort of 3D sequel to today’s two-dimensional internet, in which users work, play, buy and sell inside immersive virtual worlds—has become the latest Next Big Thing.

“这是一种革命性的方式，让我们的世界在不变得特别诡异的情况下连接起来。在冰岛宇宙，你可以亲眼看到天空，可以抚摸火山岩，可以在安全距离观察到巨大的间歇泉。”于是就有了一个病毒式广告，旨在吸引游客来冰岛旅游。该广告中拙劣模仿的对象主要是马克·扎克伯格以及整个硅谷，对他们来说，“元宇宙”的想法已经成为最近的“下一个大事件”，元宇宙是指今天二维互联网的3D结局，用户可以在沉浸式的虚拟世界中工作、玩耍、买卖。

Iceland’s tourist board is not the only sceptic. When, on October 28th, Mr Zuckerberg rebranded Facebook as Meta Platforms, to signal his commitment to the new idea, many assumed it was a PR stunt to divert attention from the social-media giant’s scandals. When Satya Nadella and Jensen Huang, the bosses of Microsoft and Nvidia, made their own pitches a couple of weeks later, people muttered about bandwagon-jumping.

冰岛旅游局并不是唯一一个持怀疑观点的。10月28日，扎克伯格先生将脸书更名为Meta平台，以表明他对新理念的支持，许多人认为这只是一个公关噱头，目的是转移人们“对社交媒体巨头的丑闻”的注意力。几周后，当微软老板Satya Nadella和英伟达的老板Jensen Huang也开始给自己公司做宣传，人们都在抱怨他们跟风。

Mr Zuckerberg may well prefer to think about exciting new products than deal with the downsides of his existing ones. And hype is the water in which techies swim. But to stop the analysis there would be too dismissive. There are good reasons to take the metaverse seriously.

扎克伯格很可能更愿意考虑令人兴奋的新产品，而不是处理现有产品的缺点。而天花乱坠的宣传正是技术人员合潮流的地方。但如果要停止分析，就显得过于轻视“元宇宙”。我们有充分的理由认真对待元宇宙。

One is historical: as computers have become more capable, the experiences that they generate have become richer. The internet began its life displaying nothing more exciting than white text on a black background. Flat images were added in the 1990s. Video came to dominate in the 2010s. On that reading, a move into three dimensions is a logical consequence of the steady growth in computing power.

首先从历史角度来看: 随着计算机的功能越来越强大，它们产生的体验也越来越丰富。互联网诞生之初，没有什么比在黑色背景上显示白色文本更令人兴奋的了。平面图像是在20世纪90年代有的。视频在21世纪10年代开始占据主导地位。从这个角度来看，向三维空间迈进是计算能力稳步增长的必然结果。

That progression is more than merely theoretical. Google Maps already offers a virtual space that contains the real world’s stations, shops and streets. The video-game industry—the only type of entertainment fully exposed to the compounding power of Moore’s law—has been selling virtual worlds for years. “EverQuest”, an online game launched in 1999, had half a million subscribers at its peak. (Players quickly co-opted it for socialising, and even weddings, as well as dragon-slaying.) “World of Warcraft”, which arrived five years later, hit 12m. These days 200m people a month hang out on “Roblox”, a video-game-cum-construction-set. Many spend their real money on virtual goods. It is hard to argue that an idea will never catch on when, for millions of people, it already has.

（其次）这种进展不仅仅是理论上的。谷歌地图已经提供了一个包含真实世界的车站、商店和街道的虚拟空间。电子游戏行业（唯一完全揭露摩尔定律力量加剧的娱乐行业）多年来一直在销售虚拟世界。1999年推出的网络游戏《无尽的任务》在鼎盛时期拥有50万用户。(玩家们很快就将其用于社交，甚至举办婚礼，还有屠龙。) 5年后问世的《魔兽世界》的用户达到了1200万。如今，每个月有2亿人在“Roblox”上闲逛，Roblox是一个视频游戏兼创作平台。许多人会花真钱购买虚拟商品。当数百万人已经有一个想法的时候，很难说这个想法不会流行起来。

Finally, mockery is an unreliable guide to the future. When YouTube was launched in 2005, commentators wondered why anyone would want to watch spotty teenagers filming themselves in their bedrooms when the delights of cable TV were a button-push away. In two decades, online dating has gone from being furtive and embarrassing to take its place as a perfectly normal way to meet people. Smartphones are some of the bestselling devices ever built. In the 1990s their brick-sized predecessors were mocked as crass status symbols for insecure investment bankers.

最后，嘲笑是无法可靠地指引未来的。当YouTube在2005年刚推出时，评论员们很奇怪，想看有趣的有线电视只需轻轻一按，为什么还会有人想看青春痘少年在卧室里拍摄他们自己。20年来，网上约会已经从偷偷摸摸、让人尴尬变成了一种非常正常的交友方式。智能手机是有史以来最畅销的设备之一。在20世纪90年代，这些砖块大小的手机被嘲笑为缺乏安全感的投资银行家们的愚蠢的身份象征。

This does not mean every Silicon Valley brainwave will automatically succeed. Nor does it mean a fully fledged metaverse will arrive overnight, any more than the world wide web or the mobile internet did. But it does suggest that something metaverse-shaped lying in the relatively near future is an idea worth taking seriously. ■

但这并不意味着每一个硅谷的想法都会必然成功。这也不意味着一个成熟的元宇宙会在一夜之间到来，就像万维网或移动互联网一样。但它确实表明，在相对较近的将来，有一种元宇宙形状的东西是一个值得认真对待的想法。■

Joe Biden’s infrastructure bill will boost competitiveness for decades

乔·拜登的基础设施法案将在未来几十年提高竞争力

Unlocked

![image-20211119114207961](/Users/chenyujie/Library/Application Support/typora-user-images/image-20211119114207961.png)

Infrastructure year is finally here

基建年终于到来了

LOCK AND DAM 25 on the upper Mississippi River is testament to how good American infrastructure can be. Stretching nearly 1,300 feet (400 metres) across the water, the concrete-and-steel structure is a crucial link in the system that connects Midwestern fields to Southern ports and thence to Asian markets. Roughly 60% of America’s corn and soy exports pass through it every year. But it is almost 100 years old, and engineers wage a constant battle to keep it operational. It has the same capacity as when it was built, while boats have grown bigger. “How many highways built in the 1930s still have the same number of lanes now?” asks Andy Schimpf, a manager at the lock.

thence 从那里；因此

密西西比河上游的25号闸坝证明了美国的基础设施有多好。横跨水面近1300英尺(400米)的混凝土和钢结构是连接中西部油田和南部港口以及从那里到亚洲市场系统的关键环节。每年大约60%的美国玉米和大豆出口都要经过这里。但它已经有将近100年的历史了，工程师们为了保持它的运转不断地进行着奋斗。它的容量和刚建成时一样，不过船只却变大了。该闸的管理者Andy Schimpf问道：“有多少建于20世纪30年代的高速公路现在车道数量还保持不变的?”。

Salvation may be at hand. On November 5th the House of Representatives passed a five-year, $1trn bill to repair and upgrade America’s ageing infrastructure. About $2.5bn has been allocated to inland waterways, and Mr Schimpf is optimistic that some will go towards rebuilding Lock and Dam 25, doubling the size of its chamber. Today, workers sometimes need to break apart barges to get them through, and then reassemble them on the other side, which can take up to three hours. A bigger lock could reduce transit time to 30 minutes.

拯救可能就在眼前。11月5日，众议院通过了一项为期五年、耗资1万亿美元的法案，以修复和升级美国老化的基础设施。大约25亿美元已用于内陆水道建设，Schimpf乐观地认为，其中一些资金将用于重建25号闸坝，将闸室的规模扩大一倍。如今，工人们有时需要把驳船拆开以让它们通过，然后在另一边重新装起来，这可能需要三个小时。一个更大的闸可以将运输时间减少到30分钟。

Hundreds of similarly delayed and neglected public projects around America will get a big boost from the infrastructure bill, which President Joe Biden will soon sign into law (it long ago passed the Senate). It includes $40bn of new funding for fixing bridges, the largest investment since the construction of the interstate highway system. There will be nearly $70bn for passenger rail, aimed both at clearing years-old maintenance backlogs and bringing service to new areas. Another $65bn should ensure that every American has access to high-speed internet. And the list goes on and on, from highways to airports and the electricity grid to water pipes.

美国各地数百个同样被推迟和忽视的公共项目将从基础设施法案中得到巨大的推动，该法案很快将被拜登总统签署为法律(参议院早就通过了)。其中包括400亿美元用于修桥的新资金，这是自州际公路系统建设以来最大的一笔投资。客运铁路将投入近700亿美元，用于清理多年来积压的维修工作，并将服务带到新的地区。另外650亿美元将确保每个美国人都能接入高速互联网。这一清单会越列越多，从高速公路到机场，从电网到水管。

For years American infrastructure has suffered from under-investment. It would take $2.6trn over the next decade to get it up to scratch, according to the American Society of Civil Engineers (ASCE), a professional body (albeit one with an interest in spending more). The bill will not fix everything. But it could be the beginning of a process that will make a real difference.

up to scratch 达标的

多年来，美国的基础设施一直饱受投资不足之苦。根据专业机构美国土木工程师协会(ASCE)的数据，未来十年将需要2.6万亿美元才能使其达标(尽管该协会有意增加支出)。该法案不会解决所有问题。但这可能是一个过程的开始，这一过程将产生真正的改变。

How big a difference? Roughly half the headline amount would have been expected as part of regularly budgeted maintenance, meaning that about $550bn actually constitutes new spending, to be disbursed over five years. That might not sound like much, but it is. From 2022 to 2026 federal infrastructure spending will rise from about 0.8% of annual GDP to 1.3%, well above the trend of the past four decades. Adie Tomer of the Brookings Institution, a think-tank, says that is nearly the same average level as during the New Deal, which helped lift America’s fortunes after the Great Depression.

改变将有多大呢? 预计大约一半的标题金额将作为定期预算维护的一部分，这意味着大约5500亿美元实际上构成了新的支出，这一费用将在5年内支付。这听起来可能不多，但其实挺多的。从2022年到2026年，联邦基础设施支出占GDP的比例将从0.8%上升到1.3%，远高于过去40年的趋势。智库布鲁金斯学会的Adie Tomer说，这几乎与大萧条后帮助提升美国财富的新政时期的平均水平相同。

As a rough rule of thumb, an additional $100bn per year spent on infrastructure could boost growth by about a tenth of a percentage point when the digging actually begins, and potentially more if it catalyses additional private-sector investment, notes Ellen Zentner of Morgan Stanley, a bank. In the longer term, she estimates, a sustained expansion of infrastructure spending could support productivity and raise America’s potential growth by as much as a fifth of a percentage point, a big deal for a large, mature economy. “That’s why whenever you talk to economists, infrastructure is the stuff that we go to bed at night dreaming of,” says Ms Zentner.

摩根士丹利银行的Ellen Zentner指出，粗略来讲，每年在基础设施上额外投入1000亿美元，在实际开挖时，可以推动经济增长约0.1个百分点，如果这能刺激额外的私营部门投资，这一比例可能还会更多。她估计，从长期来看，基础设施支出的持续扩大可能会支持生产率，并将美国的潜在增长率提高至多0.5个百分点，这对一个大型、成熟的经济体来说是一件大事。Zentner表示:“这就是为什么每当你与经济学家交谈时，我们晚上睡觉时做梦都在想基础设施。”

Getting a positive return will, of course, require the money to be allocated well. Considering the shortfalls in investment over the years, it should not be hard to find good projects. On average a water main breaks every two minutes somewhere in America, while nearly half of all roads are in bad shape, according to the ASCE. In some cases the investments will not generate new growth so much as defend America’s existing strengths. Take the locks on the upper Mississippi: they help American soyabeans compete against Brazilian ones. Labour is much cheaper in Brazil, but transportation is better in America. Some projects, such as the development of the first-ever national network of chargers for electric vehicles, naturally lend themselves to co-operation with the private sector, given that is where the technology resides. The government also wants companies to pitch in to make infrastructure more resilient to climate change. Grant programmes, useful in attracting private investment in water systems, are likely to be expanded. “This bill can potentially create a framework for ongoing and necessary public and private investment in infrastructure,” says Aaron Bielenberg of McKinsey, a consultancy.

pitch into 积极投入

从积极的方面来看，该法案为私人投资者加入政府的努力创造了空间，这既可以扩大总支出，也可以加强金融纪律。任何试图为其耗资超过7.5亿美元的交通项目寻求联邦资金的城市或州都被要求评估与私营部门投资者合作是否会带来更好的结果。一些项目，例如第一个全国电动汽车充电网络的开发，自然适合与私营部门合作，因为私营部门是技术所在。政府还希望各公司积极投入建设基础设施，使其更能适应气候变化。为项目的拨款也有可能会扩张，拨款对于吸引私人投资于供水系统是有帮助的。麦肯锡咨询公司的Aaron Bielenberg表示:“这项法案可能会为基础设施领域持续且必要的公共和私人投资建立一个框架。”

But a dose of scepticism is also useful. Ultimately, the amount that America spends on infrastructure is a direct result of the amount that Americans are willing to be taxed. And that, by the standards of other rich countries, is not very much. “Ten years from now, I think we will look back and say that this bill was a historic investment, a great expansion of investments that were neglected,” says Austan Goolsbee, an economist at the University of Chicago and a former adviser to President Barack Obama. “But people are still going to be saying, well, why don’t we have high-speed rail all over the country?” Even so, better to fix roads, expand ports and lay broadband cables than not.■

COP26

“AT THE END of the COP we need to be in a position to say ‘We are still on track to be well below 2 degrees, we still have a shot at the 1.5 degrees.’ All our efforts this week should be directed at that.” Thus spoke Frans Timmermans, an EU commissioner, as the COP26 climate summit in Glasgow rolled into its final week. Unfortunately for Mr Timmermans, political resolve and climate plans do not currently add up to the demanding goals set out by the Paris agreement of limiting global warming to “well below 2°C” above preindustrial averages, let alone to keeping it as low as 1.5°C. Instead, the latest number-crunching suggests that the plans of the 193 parties to the agreement collectively carve a path to approximately 2.4°C of warming by the end of the century.

在格拉斯哥举行的COP26气候峰会进入最后一周之际，欧盟委员Frans Timmermans如是说:“在 COP大会结束时，我们需要能够说，‘我们仍在努力将气温升高降至远低于2摄氏度的水平，我们仍有可能将温度控制在1.5摄氏度。’ 我们本周的所有努力都应该瞄准这个目标。” 不幸的是，对Timmermans来说，政治决心和气候计划目前无法达到《巴黎协定》设定的严格目标，即将全球变暖控制在比工业化前平均水平升高控制在“远低于2摄氏度”的水平，更不用说将升温控制在1.5摄氏度的低水平内。相反，最新的数据表明，193个协议缔约方计划在本世纪末共同开辟一条升温控制约2.4摄氏度的道路。

That is only a modest improvement on where things stood when the Paris agreement was being negotiated in 2015. Under the deal that was struck at the time, governments offered up pledges to reduce their national emissions. Toiling away in the background, climate modellers estimated that the cumulative consequence of these pledges would be to bring about roughly 2.7°C of warming by 2100. Aware of the disconnect between this figure and the agreement’s overall goals, negotiators said they would present new, hopefully improved decarbonisation plans every five years, in the hopes that this “ratchet mechanism” would bring the overall 1.5-2°C Paris goals closer.

与2015年巴黎协定协商时的情况相比，目前情况只是一个小小的改善。根据当时达成的协议，各国政府承诺减少本国的碳排放。气候建模人员在此背景下辛勤工作，他们估计到2100年，这些承诺的累积后果将使全球升温约2.7摄氏度。意识到这一数字与《巴黎协定》总体目标之间的脱节，谈判代表表示，他们将每五年提出新的、有望改进的脱碳计划，希望这一“棘轮机制”将使距离巴黎协定1.5-2摄氏度的总体目标更接近。

Hence the flurry of climate pledges that were made over the past year. They focus on what will be done by the end of the decade, by which time global greenhouse gas emissions must be roughly half what they were in 2010 in order to have a good chance of limiting warming to 1.5°C. So far, no country is on track to do this, says Niklas Höhne of NewClimate Institute, a think-tank. Dr Höhne is part of a consortium of researchers called Climate Action Tracker, which plugs national climate policies and pledges into models in order to give an idea of how they translate into temperatures. The group’s latest results (see chart), published on November 9th, say that if all 2030 decarbonisation plans were to be carried out as advertised but no further efforts were made, there would be a 68% chance that global average temperatures in 2100 would be between 1.9°C and 3.0°C warmer than pre-industrial times, with a median estimate of 2.4°C.

因此，在过去的一年里，各方做出了一系列气候承诺。他们关注的是在这个十年结束前要做些什么，到那时全球温室气体排放量必须大约是2010年的一半，才能有机会将升温控制在1.5摄氏度以内。智库新气候研究所的Niklas Höhne称，到目前为止，还没有一个国家在这样做。Höhne博士是一个名为“气候行动追踪”的研究小组的成员，该小组将各国的气候政策和承诺写入模型，以便了解它们如何“转化”到温度上。气候行动追踪研究小组的最新结果(见图表)发表在11月9日, 其称如果所有2030年脱碳计划都像宣传的那样实施，但没有进一步的努力，2100年全球平均气温有68%的可能性比工业化前高1.9°C至3.0°C，中位数估计为2.4°C。

乍一看，这似乎比为各国政府服务的智库国际能源署(IEA)早些时候所说的要悲观得多:“COP26气候承诺将有助于将全球变暖控制在1.8摄氏度以内。” 该机构负责人Fatih Birol在推特上写道: “大新闻，#COP26[#表示tag,指某个话题]气候承诺意味着格拉斯哥（定的目标）离巴黎（协定目标）越来越近了!” 事实上，这两个数字完全一致。只是国际能源署的建模者们认为，各国不仅会兑现其在2030年的承诺，而且那些声称将在本世纪中叶或之后不久实现净零排放的国家也会兑现承诺。

Yet for now, talk of net-zero is mostly just that: talk. XX, for instance, has said it would ensure its emissions hit a peak before 2030 and reach net-zero by 2060 in spite of the fact that it still generates more than 60% of its electricity from coal. It is becoming easier, cheaper and quicker to diagnose dementia

诊断痴呆症变得更容易、更便宜、更快捷

Knowing the worst

了解最坏的情况

![image-20211117102243580](/Users/chenyujie/Library/Application Support/typora-user-images/image-20211117102243580.png)

The world’s health services will struggle to cope with the consequences

世界卫生服务将努力应对这些后果

OF THE ESTIMATED 55m people living with dementia around the world, only one-quarter have been formally diagnosed with the condition. There are many reasons for this. Two are enduring: many patients and clinicians alike wrongly believe that dementia is an inevitable part of the ageing-human condition and, being incurable, is hardly worth diagnosing; and some people experiencing cognitive impairment fear hearing what sounds like a sentence of brain-death, and so do not seek help.

impairment 损伤

在全球大约5500万痴呆症患者中，只有四分之一的人被正确地诊断出患有这种疾病。原因有很多。有两种原因经久不衰: 许多患者和临床医生都错误地认为，痴呆症是人类衰老过程中不可避免的一部分，而且无法治愈，几乎不值得诊断; 一些有认知障碍的人害怕听到类似于脑死亡的判决，所以他们也不会去寻求帮助。

Some of the reasons for non-diagnosis, however, may be about to change. During the covid-19 pandemic many people have delayed consulting their doctors about non-urgent conditions, and as lockdowns ease, they may begin to ask for professional guidance (moreover, evidence suggests that covid itself heightens the risk of dementia). In addition, diagnostic techniques, hitherto unreliable, time-consuming and costly, are becoming available, and for some forms of dementia hopes are emerging of more effective treatments.

不过，一些未诊断的原因可能即将改变。在疫情期间，许多人推迟了像医生咨询非紧急疾病的时间，随着封锁逐渐放开，他们可能开始寻求专业指导(此外，有证据表明，疫情本身增加了患痴呆症的风险)。此外，迄今为止不可靠、耗时和昂贵的诊断技术正在变得可用，对于某些形式的痴呆症，更有效的治疗有望出现。

Dementia is normally diagnosed by testing cognitive functions such as memory. If mild cognitive impairment (MCI), often a precursor to dementia, is detected, a patient may then be referred for tests to identify which of the dozens of causes of dementia are to blame. By far the most common is Alzheimer’s disease, accounting for 60-80% of cases.

痴呆症通常是通过测试记忆等认知功能来诊断的。如果检测到轻度认知障碍(MCI)，这通常是痴呆症的前兆，患者可能会被转诊进行测试，以确定导致痴呆症的数十个原因中的哪一个是罪魁祸首。到目前为止，最常见的是阿尔茨海默病，占病例的60-80%。

Fear of the needle

害怕针

Identifying Alzheimer’s normally requires a brain scan, and perhaps a lumbar puncture (the insertion of a needle into the lower spine), to extract cerebrospinal fluid, so as to measure its levels of two proteins that build up in the brains of people with Alzheimer’s, known as beta-amyloid and tau. Some patients are reluctant to undergo the intrusive procedure. The scans are usually by magnetic-resonance imaging (MRI), to look at the size of the brain, along with a positron-emission tomography (PET) scan to measure the build-up of beta-amyloid. PET and MRI scanners are expensive pieces of kit, running into the hundreds of thousands of dollars. For most people in the world, they are unobtainable.

unobtainable 得不到的

确诊阿尔茨海默病通常需要进行脑部扫描，或许还需要进行腰椎穿刺(将针插入脊柱下部)，提取脑脊髓液，以测量阿尔茨海默病患者大脑中两种蛋白质的含量，即β -淀粉样蛋白和tau蛋白。有些病人不愿意接受侵入性过程。脑部扫描通常是通过核磁共振成像(MRI)来观察大脑的大小，同时通过正电子发射断层扫描仪(PET)来测量β -淀粉样蛋白的形成。PET和MRI扫描仪都是昂贵的设备，需要花费几十万美元。对世界上大多数人来说，它们是无法获得的[买不起]。

That explains the excitement at the development of simple blood tests to distinguish Alzheimer’s from other neurodegenerative conditions. One, announced last year and likely to be validated for routine use within 12 months, according to Serge Gauthier, professor of neurology and neurosurgery at McGill University in Montreal, measures a form of tau called p-tau217. It has been found to predict Alzheimer’s with 96% accuracy.

这就解释了为什么人们对于开发出一种仅通过“血液检测”就能区分阿尔茨海默病和其他神经退行性疾病感到兴奋。蒙特利尔麦吉尔大学神经病学和神经外科教授Serge Gauthier说，去年宣布的一种检测tau蛋白的方法即测量p-tau217，有可能在12个月内被证实可以常规使用。它可以预测阿尔茨海默病，准确率高达96%。

It is already possible using mass spectrometry, which detects how atoms and molecules are deflected by magnetic fields, to measure the level of beta-amyloid in the blood. But it is not certain how this relates to levels in the brain. The accuracy of the procedure rises to 94% if two other risk factors are considered: age and the presence of a form of the APOE gene, known as APOE4, which heightens the risk of developing Alzheimer’s (and also appears to increase vulnerability to heart disease and covid). This can also be detected by a blood test, so Dr Gauthier envisages symptomatic patients giving blood samples for simultaneous tests for both tau and APOE4.

利用质谱法已经有可能测量血液中β -淀粉样蛋白的水平。质谱法可以检测原子和分子是如何被磁场偏转的。但尚不确定这与大脑中β -淀粉样蛋白的水平有何关系。如果考虑到另外两个风险因素: 年龄和APOE基因APOE4是否存在，这种基因会增加患阿尔茨海默氏症的风险(而且似乎也会增加患心脏病和新冠的几率)，那么该检测的准确率将升至94%。这也可以通过血液测试检测出来，所以Gauthier博士设想有症状的病人提供血液样本，同时检测tau蛋白和APOE4。

Other approaches aim to detect asymptomatic people years or even decades before they begin to show obvious symptoms. George Stothart at the University of Bath in Britain leads a team that has developed very quick passive tests that hold great promise for detecting cognitive impairment early. These tests use electroencephalographic caps which are worn on the head to compare brainwave responses to a series of images. The caps are relatively cheap and the test can be conducted using a tablet computer.

其他方法的目的是在无症状人群开始出现明显症状前几年甚至几十年就检测出来。英国巴斯大学的George Stothart领导了一个团队，他们开发了一种非常快速的被动测试，有望在早期发现认知障碍的情况。这些测试使用戴在头上的脑电图帽来比较脑电波对一系列图像的反应。这种脑电图帽相对来说就便宜多了，测试可以使用平板电脑进行。

Taking the exam

参加测试

Meanwhile, machine-learning and AI are enabling big improvements in cognitive testing, which, like other sorts of pencil-and-paper exams, has been prone to cultural and educational bias, and to a “learning bias” (lessened accuracy as practice improves the participants’ results). Cognetivity Neurosciences, for example, a firm launched by two academics at the University of Cambridge, produces an “integrated cognitive assessment” that has already been deployed by some regions of Britain’s National Health Service. It has also been approved by America’s Food and Drug Administration (FDA) and by regulators in the European Union.

与此同时，机器学习和人工智能正在使认知测试取得重大进步。与其他类型的纸笔考试一样，认知测试也容易出现文化和教育偏见，以及“学习偏见”(训练/练习提高了参与者的成绩，从而降低了准确率)。例如，由剑桥大学的两位学者创办的Cognetivity Neurosciences公司创造的“综合认知评估”已经被英国NHS的一些地区采用。它也得到了FDA和欧盟监管机构的批准。

The Cognetivity test relies on a series of flashing images, among which some animals have been embedded for the person being tested to identify—rather like the CAPTCHA tests used by some websites to weed out robots trying to log on, only these are conducted at rapid speeds. So rapid, indeed, that the tests cannot be conducted remotely, but are done on tablets in memory clinics or at doctors’ surgeries. Already, however, a variety of do-it-yourself cognitive tests of various standards are available online, and some of these could become important diagnostic tools.

认知能力测试依赖于一系列闪烁的图像，其中一些动物被嵌入到被测试者的识别中，这有点像一些网站用来筛选试图登录的机器人的验证码测试，只不过这些测试的速度很快。确实，测试速度如此之快，以至于无法远程进行，而是在记忆诊所或医生手术室的平板电脑上进行。然而，网上已经有各种标准的自己去做的认知测试，其中一些可能成为重要的诊断工具。

Even before the explosion in the use of AI, scientists could detect evidence of dementia from how people use words. A study in 2011, for example, found clear retrospective evidence in the writings in her 40s and 50s of Iris Murdoch, a novelist, of the Alzheimer’s she was to die with in 1999, aged 79. The research arm of IBM, a computing giant, has used data from the Framingham heart study, which has tracked three generations of people in a town in Massachusetts since 1948 to improve knowledge of cardiovascular health. Ajay Royyuru, who heads IBM’s health-care and life-sciences research, says that studying the use of language by participants in the study suggests that changes over time can be used to predict which of them will acquire dementia, seven and a half years before they are diagnosed even with MCI.

甚至在人工智能的使用出现爆炸式增长之前，科学家就可以从人们的用词中发现痴呆症的证据。例如，2011年的一项研究在小说家Iris Murdoch四五十岁时的作品中发现了清晰的回顾性证据，证明她在1999年去世时患了阿尔茨海默病，享年79岁。计算机巨头IBM的研究部门使用了Framingham心脏研究的数据，该研究通过自1948年以来追踪马萨诸塞州一个城镇的三代人的数据以提高对心血管健康的认识。IBM卫生保健和生命科学研究的负责人Ajay Royyuru说，研究参与者的语言使用表明，随着时间的推移，语言的变化可以用来预测哪些人会患上痴呆症，甚至可以在他们被诊断为轻度认知障碍的7年半之前就能预测出来。

Such data—and the massive amounts people compile every day on their smartphones, using various services such as messaging or navigation—could help enable much earlier detection of dementia. This might be possible with apps, although the ethics of any non-consensual diagnosis and the willingness of people to use such services are another matter. One way or another, though, Paola Barbarino, chief executive of Alzheimer’s Disease International, an advocacy group, expects a “tsunami” of demand for treatment.

这些数据，以及人们每天在智能手机上使用各种服务(如短信或导航)收集的大量数据，可能有助于更早地检测出痴呆症。通过应用程序，这或许是可能的，不过任何非自愿诊断的道德准则和人们使用此类服务的意愿就是另一回事了。然而，不管怎样，国际阿尔茨海默病(一个倡导组织)的首席执行官Paola Barbarino预计治疗需求将如“海啸”般增长。

For health-care systems around the world all this may pose more problems than it solves. The main one is the lack of proven treatments. In June the FDAapproved Aduhelm, the first drug to treat Alzheimer’s. A monoclonal antibody shown to reduce accumulations of beta-amyloid, it has so far been little used, because it is expensive and insurers are reluctant to approve reimbursement when there are doubts as to whether it actually slows cognitive degeneration. But it is the first in a queue of drugs, for which Alzheimer’s specialists hold out great hopes.

对世界各地的卫生保健系统来说，所有这一切带来的问题可能比它解决的问题还要多。主要原因是缺乏可靠的治疗方法。今年6月，FDA批准了第一种治疗阿尔茨海默氏症的药物Aduhelm。这是一种单克隆抗体，被证明可以减少β -淀粉样蛋白的积累，但迄今为止它很少被使用，因为它价格昂贵，而且保险公司在怀疑它是否真的能减缓认知退化，不愿批准报销。但这是一系列药物中的第一种，阿尔茨海默病专家对此寄予厚望。

The second difficulty is in assessing when MCI requires medical intervention. As Dr Gauthier points out, some mental decline is indeed part of ageing. People find ways of coping. Telling the difference is hard to do through blood tests or the use of AI. It still requires time and human intervention. This is part of the third and biggest difficulty: that, as the world ages, the number of people with dementia is going to rise rapidly, to more than 80m by 2030 and more than 140m by 2050. Even today health services are buckling. Who knows how they will cope in the decades ahead. ■

第二个困难是评估轻度认知障碍何时需要医疗干预。正如Gauthier博士指出的那样，智力下降确实是衰老的一部分。人们会找到应对的方法。很难通过血液测试或使用人工智能来区分。它仍然需要时间和人为干预。这是第三个也是最大的难题之一:随着世界老龄化，痴呆症患者的数量将迅速上升，到2030年将超过8000万，到2050年将超过1.4亿。即使在今天，医疗服务都不堪重负。谁知道他们在未来几十年将如何应对。■

Expect at least as many possibilities as metaverses to unfold.■

接下来的问题是整个元宇宙经济将如何运作。由于大多数商业活动将被数字化复制，经济学家可能对正在发生的事情有前所未有的洞察力。数字孪生可以相互交换服务，或许可以取代公司成为主要的分析单位。如果数字孪生继续存在于区块链(支撑大多数加密货币的平台)上，它们甚至可能变得独立并拥有自己。期待它至少要有和元宇宙一样多展现的可能性。■

CELEBRITY BOSSES used to have nicknames that made a virtue of short fuses and brutality. “Chainsaw Al” and “Neutron Jack” sounded more like wrestlers than men in suits. [Simpatico 词背景] Simpatico, which derives from the Greek noun sympatheia, meaning “sympathy,” was borrowed into English from both Italian and Spanish. In those languages, the word has been chiefly used to describe people who are well-liked or easy to get along with; early uses of the word in English reflected this, as in Henry James’s 1881 novel The Portrait of a Lady, in which a character says of another’s dying cousin, “Ah, he was so simpatico. In some ways the path to the top of the corporate pyramid is unchanged. It requires people to compete with each other over an extended period. It demands evidence of financial and operational success. It uses the prospect of money—lots of it—as a lever to incentivise ambitious people. And it selects for familiar traits: hard work, impatience, self-confidence and extroversion. If you would rather stay in and watch “The Great British Bake Off” than wine and dine clients, the role is not for you.

A recent study by Steve Kaplan of the University of Chicago and Morten Sorensen of the Tuck School of Business looks at assessments conducted by ghSMART, a consulting firm, of more than 2,600 candidates for different leadership positions. Where aspiring chief financial officers are more analytical and focus on the detail, would-be CEOs score higher on charisma, on getting things done and on strategic thinking. These traits also seem to be predictive. By tracking the subsequent careers of candidates, the academics find that people who were applying for a different position but had “CEO-like” characteristics were more likely eventually to wind up in the top job.

他们在细节上也与其他高管不同。有抱负的首席财务官更善于分析，更注重细节，而一心想当首席执行官的人在个人魅力、办事能力和战略思维方面更强些。这些特征似乎也具有预测性。通过跟踪候选人随后的职业生涯，学者们发现，那些申请不同职位但具有“CEO般的”性格的人最终更有可能身处最高职位。

Yet firms today are after more than a type-A personality. Mr Kaplan and Mr Sorensen note that CEO candidates with better interpersonal skills are more likely to be hired. Another new piece of research, from academics at Imperial College London, Cornell University and Harvard University, analyses the lengthy job descriptions that companies draw up when they work with headhunters to recruit a new leader. Cognitive skills, operational nous and financial knowledge are prerequisites for success. But over the past two decades these descriptions have placed more and more emphasis on social skills—the ability of bosses to co-ordinate and communicate with multiple people.

lengthy 冗长的；长时间的

nous 常识；智力

然而，如今的公司追求的不仅仅是A型人格。Kaplan和Sorensen指出，人际交往能力较强的CEO候选人更有可能被聘用。来自伦敦帝国理工学院、康奈尔大学和哈佛大学的学者们的另一项新研究分析了企业在与猎头公司合作招聘新领导人时起草的冗长的职位描述。认知技能、操作常识和财务知识是成功的先决条件。但在过去的二十年里，这些描述越来越强调社交技能，即老板与多人协调和沟通的能力。

Why are these softer skills prized? The answer, according to Stephen Hansen of Imperial College, lies partly in the rise of knowledge workers. Firms increasingly depend on developers, data scientists and IT managers who are used to operating independently. Chief executives are not going to tell these kinds of workers what to do; their job is to make sure that people understand the firm’s goals and toil together effectively. Sure enough, the paper shows that demand for these skills goes up in larger and more information-intensive firms. Social skills matter more when bosses need to persuade as much as instruct.

为什么这些软技能受到重视?帝国理工学院的Stephen Hansen认为，部分是由于知识型员工的增多。公司越来越依赖习惯于独立运作的开发人员、数据科学家和IT经理。首席执行官不会告诉这类员工该做什么; 他们的工作是确保员工理解公司的目标，并能够有成效地共同努力工作。确实，这篇论文表明，在规模更大、信息密集程度更高的公司，对这些技能的需求会上升。当老板不仅需要指导，还需要说服时，社交技能就更重要了。

The wider environment also rewards softer skills. Polling by Edelman, a public-relations firm, suggests that majorities of customers and employees make choices on what to buy and where to work based on their beliefs. Chief executives must mollify politicians, respond to activists and dampen social-media firestorms. It helps if the boss comes across as a relatable member of society, not a volcano-dwelling villain.

更广泛的环境下也会需要这样的软技能。Edelman公关公司的民意调查显示，大多数顾客和员工都是根据自己的信念来决定买什么和去哪里工作。首席执行官们必须安抚政界人士，对激进分子做出回应，并抑制社交媒体上的风暴。如果老板给人的印象是一个与社会有关联的成员，而不是一个住在火山上的坏蛋，这将有所帮助。

It is not yet time to call time on old-fashioned narcissism. Another study, by a quartet of researchers at Stanford Graduate School of Business, surveyed 182 directors about the personalities of their chief executives. The answers suggest as many as 18% of bosses are considered narcissists by their own board members, a prevalence rate perhaps three times that of the general American population. The researchers also find that firms with narcissistic CEOs tend to have higher scores for their environmental, social and governance policies. What better way for an egomaniac to come across as empathetic than to save the planet?

egomaniac 极端利己主义者

现在还不是终结老式自恋的时候。斯坦福大学商学院的四名研究人员进行的另一项研究对182名董事针对他们的首席执行官的性格进行调查。结果表明，多达18%的老板被他们自己的董事会成员认为是自恋者，这一比例可能是普通美国人的三倍。研究人员还发现，有自恋的CEO的公司往往在环境、社会和治理政策方面得分更高。对于一个自大狂来说，还有什么比拯救地球更好的方式能让他显得有同情心呢?

The demands on chief executives make for an increasingly strange mixture. Be more talented than others in the firm, but don’t tell them what to do. Crush the competition while exuding empathy. Listen charismatically. Be likeably aggressive. CEOs have always been abnormal. The trick now is not to show it.

对首席执行官的要求越来越奇怪。要比公司里的其他人更有才华，但不要告诉员工他们该做什么。击败竞争对手的同时还要表现出富有同理心。充满魅力地去倾听他人。还要有可爱的强硬手段。【什么奇奇怪怪的…】CEO们总是不正常的。现在的诀窍是不要表现出来。

Russia’s new era of repression

俄罗斯新镇压时代

Vladimir Putin

普京

![image-20211114101145593](/Users/chenyujie/Library/Application Support/typora-user-images/image-20211114101145593.png)

It will lead to confrontation with the West

这将导致与西方的对抗

Editor’s note: On November 11th, after The Economist was published, Memorial, the human-rights group mentioned in this editorial, announced that Russian prosecutors have demanded it be shut down.

编者按:11月11日，《经济学人》出版后，这篇社论中提到的人权组织“Memorial”宣布，俄罗斯检察官已要求其关闭。

ANDREI SAKHAROV, a Soviet dissident and physicist, used to argue that repression at home invariably becomes instability abroad. His own life was evidence of it. His internal exile was lifted in 1986 by Mikhail Gorbachev, the Soviet Union’s last leader, who as the architect of glasnost released political prisoners and tolerated free speech. It was no accident that Mr Gorbachev’s rejection of repression coincided with the end of the cold war.

glasnost 公开性[前苏联采取的使政府更公开、新闻传播更自由的政策，1985年由戈尔巴乔夫发起]

苏联持不同政见人士和物理学家安德烈·萨哈罗夫过去常说，国内的镇压必然会导致国外的不稳定。他自己的生活就是证据。1986年，苏联最后一位领导人戈尔巴乔夫解除了对他的国内流放。戈尔巴乔夫作为“公开性”政策的设计者，他释放了政治犯，并允许言论自由。戈尔巴乔夫拒绝镇压的同时，也恰逢冷战的结束，这并非偶然。

Today Sakharov’s thesis is being demonstrated once again—in reverse. According to Memorial, a human-rights group, Russia has more than twice as many political prisoners than at the end of the Soviet era. Memorial, which Sakharov helped set up to document Soviet abuses, has itself been branded a “foreign agent” and attacked by state-sponsored thugs.

今天，萨哈罗夫的论点再次被证明是相反的。据人权组织Memorial称，俄罗斯现在的政治犯数量是苏联时代末期的两倍多。萨哈罗夫帮助建立Memorial也是为了记录苏联的暴行，Memorial本身也被贴上了“外国代理人”的标签，而且还会受到国家支持的暴徒的攻击。

At the same time, Russia’s relations with the West have also entered a dark period. In order to justify repression at home, President Vladimir Putin is telling his people that Western policy is designed to obliterate the Russian way of life. Mr Putin now builds in cold-war confrontation to his dealings with the West. Its leaders need to prepare for what comes next.

obliterate 摧毁；掩盖；清除

与此同时，俄罗斯与西方的关系也进入了黑暗时期。为了证明国内的镇压是正当的，俄罗斯总统普京告诉他的人民，西方政策的目的是消灭俄罗斯的生活方式。普京现在在与西方打交道时建立了冷战对抗。它的领导人需要为接下来发生的事情做好准备。

The latest phase of repression began in 2020 with the poisoning of Alexei Navalny, Russia’s most famous political prisoner, and winner last month of the European Parliament’s Sakharov prize for freedom of thought. Mr Navalny survived the attack, only to be incarcerated and abused in Penal Colony No 2, one of the country’s harshest jails.

incarcerate 关押

a penal colony 罪犯流放地

最新阶段的镇压始于2020年，当时俄罗斯最著名的政治犯、上个月欧洲议会的萨哈罗夫思想自由奖的赢家Alexei Navalny遭到毒杀。Navalny在袭击中幸存下来，却被关押在第二流放地，还遭到虐待。第二流放地是该国最严厉的监狱之一。

Since then, Mr Navalny’s organisation has been outlawed and much of his team chased out of the country. Those who stayed are being pursued. On November 9th Lilia Chanysheva was arrested and now faces ten years in prison for having worked for Mr Navalny while his organisation was still legal. The net is spreading beyond politics. The same day, Sergei Zuev, the 67-year-old head of the top liberal university in Russia, who is recovering from heart treatment, was taken from house arrest to a prison cell, perhaps to force a false confession in a fabricated case.

outlaw 宣布…为非法

自那以后，Navalny的组织被宣布为非法，他的大部分团队成员也被赶出了该国。那些留下来的人则正在被追捕。11月9日，Lilia Chanysheva被逮捕，现在面临10年监禁，因为她在Navalny的组织还合法的时候为他工作。网正在政治之外蔓延。就在同一天，接受心脏治疗后正处于恢复阶段的67岁俄罗斯顶尖自由主义大学校长Sergei Zuev从软禁中被带进了监狱，或许是为了迫使他在一桩捏造的案件中作虚假供述。

A third of the Russian government’s budget is spent on security and defence. Much of this is directed inwards, at the sort of people The Economist features this week in a documentary film (see economist.com/russia-film): people who have had enough of Mr Putin’s rule and the corruption of his regime. As incomes have fallen and discontent has grown, so Russia’s many police and security services have swollen. With 10% more staff than in 2014, they now outnumber Russia’s active-duty military forces.

active-duty 现役

俄罗斯政府预算的三分之一用于安防。这其中大多都是针对国内的人，针对那些《经济学人》在本周的一部纪录片中特写的那些人，他们受够了普京的统治及其政权的腐败。随着收入的下降和不满的增加，俄罗斯的许多警察和安全部门人数开始增长。他们的人数比2014年增加了10%，其人数已超过了俄罗斯现役军队的人数。

For Mr Putin, repression does not have a reverse gear. He will not be able to restore the prosperity that helped buoy his ratings during his first decade in power. True, the fortress economy that the Kremlin has developed since 2014 can withstand sanctions, especially when energy prices are high, as now. But Russia, which is more like Iran than China, does not have the dynamism to generate sustained, robust growth.

对普京来说，镇压也无法让他回到过去。他将无法恢复繁荣，正是繁荣助他在第一个执政的十年中支持率上涨。确实，俄罗斯政府自2014年以来发展起来的堡垒经济能够承受制裁，特别是在能源价格居高不下的情况下，就像现在这样。但俄罗斯，这个更像伊朗而非中国的国家，不具备产生持续、强劲增长的动力。

Hence the logic of confrontation. Soviet rulers waged the cold war from atop the ideology of communism. Russia’s securocrats assert that traditional values of family, culture and history are being corrupted by the liberal and licentious West and that only they can defend them. Fighting back against the West lets the Kremlin portray all those who oppose it—journalists, human-rights lawyers and activists—as foreign agents. In this way, Mr Putin’s regime depends on anti-Western ideology for its politics just as it depends on oil and gas for its prosperity.

atop 在顶上

licentious 淫荡的

因此就有了对抗的理由。苏联统治者在共产主义意识形态的顶端发起了冷战。俄罗斯的安全官员声称，家庭、文化和历史的传统价值观正被自由和放荡的西方所腐蚀，只有他们能捍卫这些价值观。对西方的反击让俄罗斯政府将所有反对它的人描绘成外国代理人，如记者、人权律师和活动家。这样一来，普京政权的政治依赖于反西方的意识形态，就像它的繁荣依赖于石油和天然气一样。

Dictators insist that how they treat their subjects is a question of sovereignty. In fact, repression is everyone’s business. One reason is that human rights are universal. The other is that violence at home spreads beyond a country’s frontiers.

独裁者坚称他们如何对待他们的国民是主权问题。事实上，镇压是每个人的事。原因之一是人权是普遍的。另一个原因是，国内暴力会扩散到国家之外。

Both Russia and Belarus, where the dictator Alexander Lukashenko is propped up by the Kremlin, have murdered dissidents abroad. Russia shot down a passenger plane and Belarus hijacked one to arrest a local dissident. Poland and Lithuania have sheltered the Belarusian opposition in self-exile. Backed by the Kremlin, Mr Lukashenko is taking revenge by flying in refugees from the Middle East and shunting them to its borders to engineer a humanitarian crisis.

俄罗斯和白俄罗斯都曾在海外谋杀那些持不同政见的人士。在白俄罗斯，独裁者Alexander Lukashenko得到了俄罗斯政府的支持。俄罗斯击落了一架客机，白俄罗斯劫持了一架客机，就是为了逮捕当地一名持不同政见者。波兰和立陶宛一直在庇护自我流放的白俄罗斯反对派。在俄罗斯政府的支持下，Lukashenko正采取报复行动，从中东空运难民，将他们转移到俄罗斯边境，以策划一场人道主义危机。

On a greater scale, Mr Putin meddles in Western elections, peddles anti-vaccine propaganda and fights proxy wars with America in Africa and the Middle East. He is using the promise of extra supplies of gas to weaken ties between the European Union and countries like Ukraine and Moldova. He has once again massed troops on the Ukrainian border and is flying nuclear-capable bombers to Belarus.

proxy war 代理战争 [指由大国挑起但不直接参与的战争]

在更大的规模上，普京干预西方选举，散播反疫苗的宣传，在非洲和中东与美国打代理战争。他正在利用额外供应天然气的承诺来削弱欧盟与乌克兰和摩尔多瓦等国之间的关系。他再次在乌克兰边境集结军队，并将携带核弹头的轰炸机派往白俄罗斯。

The good news is that just as most of the Soviet people did not believe in the advantages of communism over capitalism, so most Russians do not believe in the advantages of confrontation. For all Mr Putin’s propaganda, two-thirds have a positive view of the West. Nearly 80% say Russia should see it as a partner and a friend. This is most pronounced among the young, who reject state violence and favour human rights instead.

好消息是，就像大多数苏联人不相信共产主义相对于资本主义的优势一样，大多数俄罗斯人也不相信对抗的优势。对于普京的所有宣传，三分之二的人对西方持正面看法。近80%的人认为俄罗斯应该视其为伙伴和朋友。这一点在年轻人中表现得最为明显，他们拒绝国家暴力，相反他们支持人权。

Western politicians should take note of this divergence between the Kremlin and the Russian people. One response is to harmonise sanctions and focus them on the powerful Russians who loot the state and abuse the people. That entails Western countries standing up to the lobbying of their own service industries, which get rich from helping Mr Putin’s cronies launder their reputations, pursue their legal vendettas and shelter their illicit wealth.

crony 密友

launder 把…转为合法

vendetta 宿怨；家族世仇

西方政客应该注意到俄罗斯政府和俄罗斯人民之间的这种分歧。一种应对措施是协调制裁，将制裁重点放在掠夺国家、虐待人民的有影响力的俄罗斯人身上。这需要西方国家站出来对抗本国服务业的游说，这些服务业的致富是通过帮助普京的亲信洗白自己的名声、追逐他们的法律上的世仇以及庇护他们的非法财富。

Think ahead

提前考虑下

They should also start laying the foundations for a post-Putin Russia. Nobody knows whether that will come in years or decades. But it is hard to see Mr Putin’s system surviving him.

他们还应该开始为后普京时代的俄罗斯打下基础。没人知道这种情况会在几年还是几十年后出现。但很难想象普京的体制可以拯救他。

The West should therefore invest in people who share its values. It should speak out against human-rights abuses inside Russia. The flood of Russian students, journalists and intellectuals seeking a better life will increase. Western governments should accommodate them. Latvia and Lithuania are hosting independent media outlets and dissidents. Russian students should be welcomed to Western universities. By doing so the West would not just be helping the victims of Mr Putin’s repression, it would also be helping itself. ■

因此，西方应该投资于与自己价值观相同的人。它应该公开反对俄罗斯境内侵犯人权的行为。那些寻求更好生活的俄罗斯学生、记者和知识分子的人数将会增加。西方国家政府应该为他们提供便利。拉脱维亚和立陶宛都在接纳独立媒体和持不同政见者。西方大学应该欢迎俄罗斯学生。通过这样做，西方不仅是在帮助普京镇压的受害者，也是在帮助自己。■

The welcome spread of assisted dying

受欢迎的辅助死亡传播

A final choice

最后的选择

![image-20211113101510690](/Users/chenyujie/Library/Application Support/typora-user-images/image-20211113101510690.png)

But too many are still denied this basic freedom

但是仍然有太多的人被剥夺了基本的自由

IN 1995 AUSTRALIA’S Northern Territory enacted the world’s first law explicitly allowing assisted dying. It said that terminally ill, mentally competent adults who wanted to die could ask a doctor for help, using lethal drugs. The law sparked outrage. Within months the federal government had overturned it. Yet today five of Australia’s six states have assisted-dying laws.

terminally ill 病入膏肓的

1995年，澳大利亚北部地区颁布了世界上第一部明确允许辅助死亡的法律。该法称，那些病入膏肓的且精神状态符合要求的想要寻死的成年人，可以向医生寻求帮助，他们将会使用致命药物来帮助他们。这项法律引起了愤怒。几个月后，联邦政府就推翻了该法案。然而，如今澳大利亚的六个州中有五个州都制定了辅助死亡法。

The Economist first made the case for assisted dying in 2015. We argued that freedom should include the right to choose the manner and timing of one’s own death, while also cautioning that the practice should be carefully monitored and regulated to avoid abuses. Since then, it has become more widely available. Assisted dying is now legal in one form or another in a dozen countries, and the trend seems likely to continue. Last week New Zealand enacted a euthanasia law for the terminally ill after 65% of voters backed it in a referendum. The same week Portugal’s parliament passed a broader law. Assisted dying is still illegal in Britain, but the House of Lords is debating a bill to allow it.

euthanasia 安乐死

《经济学人》在2015年首次提出了“辅助死亡”的理由。我们认为，自由应该包括选择自己死亡的方式和死亡的时间的权利，同时我们也警告说，为了避免滥用，这种做法应该受到谨慎地监督和管理。从那以后，“辅助死亡”就变得更加广泛。在许多国家，辅助死亡正以各种形式合法化，而且这一趋势似乎还会继续下去。上周，新西兰在全民公投中通过了一项针对绝症患者的安乐死法案，该法案获得了65%的投票者的支持。同一周，葡萄牙议会通过了一项更广泛的法律。在英国，辅助死亡仍然是非法的，但英国上议院正在讨论一项是否允许辅助死亡的法案。

The number of people who die this way is increasing, though still small. In the Netherlands it rose from roughly 1,800 in 2003 to nearly 7,000 in 2020, or 4% of all deaths. As more countries liberalise, the global total will rise further.

以这种方式死亡的人数正在增加，虽然仍然很少。在荷兰，以这种方式死亡的人数从2003年的约1800人增至2020年的近7000人，这一数字占总死亡人数的4%。随着越来越多的国家实行自由化，全球的“辅助死亡”总量将进一步上升。

Many people object to assisted dying on religious grounds: some faiths deem suicide a sin. Others worry that safeguards will prove insufficient, or that legalisation is a slippery slope. Critics have long predicted that families exhausted by the demands of caring for sick, elderly relatives will place undue pressure on them to end their lives, or that cash-strapped states will encourage the most expensive terminally ill patients to hurry up and die.

slippery slope：a bad situation or habit that, after it has started, is likely to get very much worse

undue 过度的

cash-strapped 缺钱的

许多人出于宗教原因反对“辅助死亡”:有些信仰认为“自杀是一种罪”。其他人则担心“保障措施不够”，或者“合法化越来越糟糕”。批评人士长期以来一直预测，那些因“照顾生病的老年亲属”的需求而疲惫不堪的家庭将给亲属施加过度的压力，迫使他们自愿结束自己的生命，或者资金紧张的州将鼓励花钱最多的病入膏肓的病人赶快死去。

Yet such horrors do not seem to have come to pass. In places with the longest experience of assisted dying, charities that represent the elderly or disabled have not reported any abuse. It is conceivable that some has taken place unobserved, but scrutiny has been intense and in most countries permission to help someone die is revoked if there is even a hint of coercion. Fears that the poor and marginalised might be hastened to their ends have also proved to be unfounded. In America, the Netherlands and Switzerland the overwhelming majority of those who choose an assisted death are educated and middle-class.

coercion 胁迫

不过这样恐怖的事情似乎并没有发生。在那些有着最长时间的“辅助死亡”经验的地方，代表老年人或残疾人的慈善机构没有报告有任何“滥用”行为。可以想象，有些事情是在没有被观察到的情况下发生的，不过审查一直很严格，在大多数国家，如果有一丝胁迫的迹象，辅助某人死亡的许可就会被取消。穷人和被边缘化的人可能被催促去死的担忧也是空穴来风。在美国、荷兰和瑞士，绝大多数选择辅助死亡的人都是受过教育的中产阶级。

Far from being too lax, the rules have often been too restrictive. The Australian state of Victoria, for example, bars doctors from mentioning assisting dying to their patients. The aim is to avoid coercion, but the consequence is that many sufferers do not know that it is an option. In some jurisdictions only those with less than six months to live are allowed help to die. Thus, patients can be terminally ill and in intense pain, but unless a doctor estimates that the end is very near, they cannot end their own suffering. In some cases the diagnosis comes too late. In Victoria in the first six months of 2021 no cases were withdrawn because the patient decided not to proceed, but in 90 cases the patient died before receiving relief. Some countries, such as Spain and Colombia, have liberal laws in theory, but in practice health authorities are reluctant to let anyone make use of them. Last week in Spain a desperate 83-year-old threw herself out of a window after her repeated requests for euthanasia were refused.

这些规定非但不太宽松，反而往往过于严格。比如说，澳大利亚维多利亚州禁止医生向病人提及“辅助死亡”。这样做的目的是避免胁迫，但这就导致很多患者不知道这是一种选择。在一些司法管辖区，只有那些生命不足6个月的人才被允许帮助他们死亡。因此，对于那些身患绝症且处于剧烈的疼痛的病人，除非医生估计他们的生命即将结束，否则他们无法结束自己的痛苦。在某些情况下，诊断又太晚了。在维多利亚州，2021年前6个月没有因为患者决定不继续治疗而撤销病例，但有90个病例，患者在“获得解脱”之前就死了。一些国家，如西班牙和哥伦比亚，理论上有自由法，但实际上卫生当局不愿让任何人使用它们。上周在西班牙，一位83岁的老人在“多次请求安乐死被拒绝后”绝望地跳楼自杀。

Canada offers a better model, because it provides more leeway for individuals to make their own choices. Anyone whose suffering is unbearable can choose an assisted death. They do not have to be terminally ill. And, uniquely, the question of what constitutes “unbearable” suffering is for the patients themselves to decide, so long as they are of sound mind. There is a cooling-off period of ten days, in case they have second thoughts. In many cases, simply having the option of an assisted death gives people a sense of comfort and control. In Oregon a third of those people who receive the prescribed lethal medication ultimately choose not to take it.

leeway 余地

加拿大提供了一个更好的模式，因为它为个人提供了更多的自由选择空间。任何痛苦不堪的人都可以选择安乐死。他们不必是绝症患者。而且，独特的是，只要病人神志清晰，那么“难以忍受的”痛苦包含哪些情况是可以由病人自己决定的。他们有十天的冷静期，以防他们改变主意。在许多情况下，仅仅是有辅助死亡的选择就能给人一种舒适和控制感。在俄勒冈州，三分之一接受致命药物治疗的人最终选择不服用。

Even as more societies accept the principle of assisted dying, hard questions remain. Some people worry that its availability may prompt health services to skimp on palliative care. But that is not ordained. Canada’s assisted-dying bill was explicitly linked to increased funding for palliative and long-term care.

skimp on 节省；在..方面吝啬

尽管越来越多的社会接受了辅助死亡的原则，有一些棘手的问题仍然存在。一些人担心，它的可用性可能会促使医疗服务部门在“保守治疗”方面节省开支。但这不合规定。加拿大的辅助死亡法与“保守治疗和长期护理资金的增加”有着明确联系。

If assisted dying becomes common, will old people who require round-the-clock care feel more social pressure to choose death? Many already worry that they are a burden on their children or carers. Some may feel additional guilt if continuing to live is seen as an individual choice, rather than the blind workings of fate. This is a genuine concern. But the possibility that some may agonise over whether to die should not trump the certainty that others will suffer unendurable pain if their freedom to choose is denied.

round-the-clock 日夜不停的

to agonize over or about sth 为某事物犯愁

如果辅助死亡变得普遍，那些需要24小时护理的老年人是否会因为社会压力而选择死亡呢? 很多老年人已经担心他们会成为孩子或看护的负担。如果继续活下去被看作是个人的选择，而不是命运的盲目起着作用，一些人可能会感到额外的内疚。这是一个真正的担忧。但是，有些人可能会为是否死亡而发愁的这一可能性，不应压倒“另一些如果被剥夺选择的自由的人，将遭受无法忍受的痛苦”的确定性。[一个是可能性，而另一个是确定性]

The trickiest questions arise when an individual’s capacity to make an informed choice is in doubt. Some people with mental disorders have suicidal thoughts that come and go. For them, the bar should be very high. Doctors must be sure they can distinguish between a temporary mental-health crisis and a sustained, considered wish to die. If in doubt, they should offer treatment aimed at helping the patient to live.

当一个人做出知情选择的能力受到怀疑时，最棘手的问题就出现了。一些有精神障碍的人会有自杀的想法。对他们来说，门槛应该很高。医生必须确保他们能够区分暂时的精神健康危机和持续的、经过深思熟虑的死亡愿望。如果有疑问，他们应该提供旨在帮助患者生存的治疗。

Free to choose, to the end

自由选择是否终结自己的生命

Dementia poses the hardest problem of all. Someone diagnosed with the condition may make a living will, asking for an assisted death when it becomes severe. But they may change their mind. Such a document should never be used to kill someone against their wishes, and if those wishes are unknowable, they should be left to live. Assisted dying should be only for those who can make an informed decision at the time they take the drugs.

痴呆症是所有问题中最棘手的。被诊断出患有这种疾病的人可能会立一份遗嘱，要求在病情严重时进行辅助死亡。但他们可能会改变主意。这样的文件不应该被用来杀死违背他们意愿的人，如果那些愿望是不可知的，他们就应该活下去。辅助死亡应该只适用于那些在服药时能够做出知情决定的人。

No rules in this area are perfect. All should be subject to revision in the light of new evidence about how they work in practice, or to take account of medical advances. But the overall principle—that individuals are entitled to choose how they end their lives—is, we believe, a sound one. The evidence from countries that allow assisted dying is that abuses remain largely hypothetical, whereas the benefits are real and substantial. It relieves suffering, and restores a measure of dignity to people at the end of their lives. ■

在这方面没有什么规则是完美的。所有这些都应该根据它们如何在实践中发挥作用的新证据，或者考虑到医学的进步来进行修订。但我们相信，个人有权选择如何结束自己的生命这一总体原则是合理的。允许辅助死亡的国家提供的证据表明，“滥用”在很大程度上仍然只是假设，而好处是真实的和实质性的。它减轻了人们的痛苦，并在他们生命结束时恢复了一定程度的尊严。■

Revolt of the bond traders

债券交易员的反抗

Markets and inflation

市场与通胀

![image-20211112094526041](/Users/chenyujie/Library/Application Support/typora-user-images/image-20211112094526041.png)

The message from unruly fixed-income markets

来自难控制的固定收益市场的信息

Global bond markets are wakening from a long slumber. The Federal Reserve this week said it will wind down its vast bond-buying programme. At the same time, bond investors are reacting to higher inflation: across a group of 35 economies, five-year bond yields have risen by an average of 0.65 percentage points in the past three months. A shakeout is taking place not only in emerging markets but also in rich countries such as Australia and Britain. Sudden moves inevitably spark fears of market turmoil, along the lines of the “taper tantrum” in 2013. However, the bond shift taking place today is actually very different.

wind down 放松；逐步停顿

shakeout 萧条，衰退；倒闭

[释义] a situation in which people lose their jobs, or companies stop doing business, because of economic difficulties

背景：缩减恐慌（Taper Tantrum）。什么是缩减（Taper）呢？简单来说，美国的央行之前告诉大众，自己会保持某个速度，持续去购买债券，向金融市场不断注入更多资金；但是现在打算逐渐降低这个速度，或者说减少每次的购买金额，这个过程就叫做缩减。

全球债券市场正从长期沉睡中醒来。本周美联储表示，将逐步缩减其庞大的债券购买计划。与此同时，债券投资者正在对“通胀加剧”做出回应: 在35个经济体中，5年期债券收益率在过去3个月平均上升了0.65个百分点。经济衰退不仅发生在新兴市场，也发生在像澳大利亚和英国这样的发达国家。突然的举动不可避免地会引发“市场动荡”的担忧，就像2013年的“缩减恐慌”一样。然而，今天发生的债券缩减实际上是非常不同的。

Before the pandemic interest rates across the world were low, reflecting dormant inflation. When the coronavirus struck almost two years ago, most central banks promised to keep their policy rates lower for longer to help the recovery. Many also agreed to buy bonds, reducing their yields.

在疫情之前，世界各地的利率都很低，这就反映出潜伏的通胀。大约两年前冠状病毒爆发时，大多数央行承诺在更长时间内将政策利率保持在较低水平，以帮助复苏。许多央行还同意购买债券，从而降低了债券收益率。

The main reason for the sudden shift today is rising inflation. Among the 38 economies that are members of the OECD, a club of rich countries, inflation rose to an uncomfortable 4.6% year on year in September. Soaring energy and food prices are only part of the story: even if you strip those out, the figure was 3.2%, the highest in almost two decades.

今天这种突然转变的主要原因是通货膨胀不断上升。由发达国家组成的经合组织的38个成员国中，9月份的通货膨胀率与去年同期相比上升了令人不安的4.6%。能源和食品价格的飙升只是部分原因:即使剔除这些因素，这个数字也达到了3.2%，是近20年来的最高水平。

For months central banks have said that high inflation is a blip caused by temporary constraints in supply. But the action in bond markets shows that investors reckon central banks are acting too slowly. Some monetary authorities have already tightened policy. Brazil announced a 1.5-percentage-point rate rise last week. Central banks in Canada and Australia have abandoned forecasts that said rates would stay low. As we write, the Bank of England is due to decide whether to raise rates. Some policymakers are standing firm: Christine Lagarde, the boss of the European Central Bank, has insisted that it is “very unlikely” to raise interest rates next year.

blip 暂时的问题

几个月来，各国央行一直表示，高通胀是由暂时的供应短缺造成的。但债券市场的行动表明，投资者认为央行的行动太慢了。一些货币当局已经收紧了政策。巴西上周宣布加息1.5个百分点。加拿大和澳大利亚央行已经放弃了利率将维持在低位的预测。在我们写这篇文章的时候，英国央行将决定是否加息。一些政策制定者立场坚定:欧洲央行总裁Christine Lagarde坚称明年加息“非常不可能”。

![image-20211112094449149](/Users/chenyujie/Library/Application Support/typora-user-images/image-20211112094449149.png)

The spectre of central banks diverging from markets, and of consequent swings in market interest rates, will unsettle those with memories of 2013, when the Fed clumsily revealed its unexpected intention to begin scaling back its programme of bond purchases. The resulting global mini-panic dented growth and clobbered some emerging economies, particularly those with big dollar debts.

对央行偏离市场，以及随之而来的市场利率波动的恐惧，将让那些还记得2013年的人感到不安。当时，美联储笨拙地透露了其开始缩减债券购买计划的出乎意外的意图。由此引发的全球小恐慌影响了经济增长，打击了一些新兴经济体，尤其是那些背负巨额美元债务的国家。

Yet this is not 2013. One difference is that the shift in bond markets is more nuanced. The increase so far in nominal five-year government-bond yields in America, for example, is less than half what it was eight years ago. Real bond yields, after accounting for expected inflation, are minus 1%, still within spitting distance of record lows. That will support easy conditions in the real economy. And even as shorter-term government-bond yields rise, there has been much less of a move in longer-term bonds.

in/within spitting distance 非常近；一步之遥

但现在已经不是2013年了。一个不同之处在于，债券市场的变化更为微妙。例如，到目前为止，美国名义五年期政府债券收益率的增长还不到八年前的一半。扣除预期通胀因素后，实际债券收益率为- 1%，与历史最低水平仅一步之遥。这将为实体经济的宽松环境提供支持。而且，即使短期政府债券收益率上升，长期债券的变动幅度也要小得多。

The other difference today is the absence of financial panic. A rising cost of debt can cause defaults and capital flight. But many emerging economies have healthy foreign-exchange reserves, making them resilient. Equity markets show no sign of distress—share prices hit a record high this week. Shares in banks are up by 28% this year, because gradually rising interest rates can boost their profits. And bond markets remain open for business. In October emerging markets outside China issued near-record levels of corporate and sovereign debt.

corporate debt 企业债务

[释义] money that is owed by companies rather than by governments or individual people

sovereign debt 主权债务

[释义] the debt of a national government, rather than a person or organization

今天的另一个不同之处在于没有金融恐慌。债务成本上升可能导致违约和资本外逃。但许多新兴经济体拥有健康的外汇储备，这使它们具有弹性。股票市场丝毫没有低迷的迹象，本周股价创下新高。银行的股票今年上涨了28%，逐渐上升的利率可以提高他们的利润。债券市场依然开放。去年10月，中国以外的新兴市场发行了接近历史最高水平的企业和主权债务。

No cause for alarm, then. Markets are betting that central banks need to bring interest-rate rises forward, not that they will lose control of inflation. Still, it is worth bearing in mind the extraordinarily difficult task that central banks face. During the unpredictable tail end of a pandemic, they must try to normalise ultra-loose monetary policy amid sky-high asset prices, heavy debt levels and above-target inflation. Taper tantrum 2.0 is not yet under way. But don’t rule out a bigger bond brawl.

那就没必要担忧了。市场认为央行需要提前加息，而不是认为它们会失去对通胀的控制。不过，有必要牢记央行面临的异常艰巨的任务。在疫情不可预测的末期，在资产价格飙升、债务负担沉重、通胀高于目标的情况下，他们必须努力使超宽松的货币政策正常化。“缩减恐慌2.0”尚未到来。但不排除会有更大的债券纠纷。
