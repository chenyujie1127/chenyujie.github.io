How Britain can benefit from Brexit

英国如何从脱欧中受益

Growing apart

![image-20210316100131152](/Users/chenyujie/Library/Application Support/typora-user-images/image-20210316100131152.png)

Damage is inevitable, but there are ways of mitigating it

损害是不可避免的，但也有减轻的方法

Mar 13th 2021

IN JANUARY BRITAIN faced two simultaneous problems. Its departure from the European Union’s single market and customs union on the first day of the month hit trade. Early data have suggested that exports to France, for instance, were 20% lower in January than six months earlier. (That does not seem to have been due to the pandemic: trade in 2020 was higher than in the previous year.) The second blow came from a surge in covid-19 cases. Britain, which already had the highest death rate in any big economy, saw deaths peak at 1,361 on January 19th.

simultaneous 同时发生的

due to 因为

今年1月，英国同时面临着两个问题。本月的第一天，它退出了欧盟单一市场和关税联盟，影响了贸易。例如，早期数据显示，1月份对法国的出口比6个月前下降了20%。(这似乎与疫情无关:2020年的贸易比前一年要高。)第二个打击来自covid-19病例的激增。英国已经是所有大经济体中死亡率最高的国家，死亡人数在1月19日达到了1361人的峰值。

The way Britain managed the second of these problems holds lessons for how it should deal with the first. The speed with which its medical regulator approved covid-19 vaccines allowed a swifter roll-out than in any other large country, which has helped slash the daily death toll to around 200. If Britain is to derive any benefit from leaving the EU, nimble is one of the ways of doing so.

英国处理第二个问题的方式为它如何处理第一个问题提供了经验。英国医疗监管机构批准新冠肺炎疫苗的速度比其他任何大国都要快，这帮助将每日死亡人数降至200人左右。如果英国想从脱离欧盟中获得任何好处，那么灵活就是其中一个途径。

Britain’s economy has experienced other big shocks in the past century, but the one brought about by Brexit is different from those inflicted after the second world war and in the 1980s. Clement Attlee’s and Margaret Thatcher’s governments had clear ideas about the direction in which they were taking the country. Boris Johnson’s does not. Brexiteers burbled about a British economic model, distinct from the European social-democratic model, without specifying what it should look like. The budget speech on March 3rd by Rishi Sunak, the chancellor, included a single reference to Brexit; the 111-page “plan for growth” that was published alongside the budget offers only a couple of pages of platitudes about Brexit at the end. Rather than producing a plan, the government has been going out of its way to pick fights with the European Commission instead.

bring about 导致

burble 唠叨

burble (on) about sth 喋喋不休地谈论某事物

platitude 陈词滥调

【释义】a remark or statement that may be true but is boring and has no meaning because it has been said so many times before

go out of one’s way (to do sth) 绕道（去做某事）；特地（做某事）

pick a fight (with sb) （与某人）吵架

在过去的一个世纪里，英国经济经历了其他重大冲击，但脱欧带来的冲击不同于二战后和上世纪80年代带来的冲击。克莱门特•艾德礼(Clement Attlee)和玛格丽特•撒切尔(Margaret Thatcher)领导的政府对国家的发展方向有明确的想法。鲍里斯•约翰逊(Boris Johnson)则不然。脱欧派喋喋不休地谈论英国的经济模式(与欧洲社会民主模式不同)，却没有具体说明它应该是什么样子。3月3日，财政大臣里希•苏纳克(Rishi Sunak)在预算演讲中只提到了英国退欧;与预算案一起发布的长达111页的《增长计划》(plan for growth)最后只提供了几页关于英国脱欧的陈词滥调。政府并没有制定出一个计划，而是想尽办法与欧盟委员会(European Commission)挑起争端。

This failure has several causes. Britain’s scope for diverging from the EU is limited. The trade deal agreed on Christmas Eve between the two commits Britain to staying close to European norms. If it does not it may be punished by trade restrictions; anyway, most businesses are fine with the EU’s standards. To sell into the bloc, they need to stick to its rules, and working with one set of regulations is cheaper than working with two. Northern Ireland’s half-in half-out position raises the costs of divergence, for a bigger gap between Britain and the EU hardens the border in the Irish Sea and angers unionists committed to keeping the province inside the United Kingdom. And British voters do not have much appetite for the hyper-liberal economy which some Brexiteers advocated. Britons want their food safe and their employers to be required to treat workers well.

unionist 联合主义者

这次失败有几个原因。英国脱离欧盟的空间有限。两国在平安夜达成的贸易协议要求英国遵守欧洲标准。如果不这样做，可能会受到贸易限制的惩罚;无论如何，大多数企业都能接受欧盟的标准。要向欧元区出售产品，它们需要遵守欧盟的规则，而遵循一套规则要比遵循两套规则不费力得多。北爱尔兰一半支持一半反对的立场增加了分歧的代价，因为英国和欧盟之间更大的分歧使爱尔兰海(Irish Sea)的边界变得更加强硬，也激怒了那些致力于让该省留在英国的联合主义者。英国选民对一些脱欧派人士所倡导的超级自由经济也没有多大兴趣。英国人希望他们的食品安全，他们的雇主被要求善待工人。

In most ways, therefore, Britain should aim to stay close to Europe. Yet there is scope for it to diverge in both damaging and beneficial ways. Britain could, for instance, abandon the EU’s restrictive state-aid regime; indeed, the government is already consulting on how to go about it. Britons should be wary of these moves. No doubt, Britain has room to improve on the EU’s rules, which are pernickety, but voters should regard the prospect of ripping up limits on state aid as a risk, rather than a benefit, of Brexit. Shovelling money in the direction of private companies is not a habit that taxpayers should want their governments to acquire.

scope 机会

scope for sb to do sth 某人做某事的机会

pernickety 吹毛求疵的；爱挑剔的

【释义】giving too much attention to small details that are not important in a way that annoys other people

因此，在大多数方面，英国应该致力于与欧洲保持紧密联系。然而，它在损害和有益的两个方面都有分歧的余地。例如，英国可以放弃欧盟限制性的国家援助制度;事实上，政府已经在就如何实施进行咨询。英国人应该对这些举措保持警惕。毫无疑问，英国在欧盟的规则上还有改进的空间，这些规定是挑剔的，但选民应该把打破国家援助限制的前景视为脱欧的风险，而不是好处。纳税人不希望他们的政府获得向私营企业注资的习惯。

In other ways, divergence could work in Britain’s interests. The process of regulation can be faster for one country than for 27 and, as the medical regulator showed with vaccines, there is much to be said for speeding it up. The nature of rules can be different, too. Where Britain has critical mass—as in finance—and in others in which it has innovative companies, such as fintech, life sciences and artificial intelligence, the country can help set the standard for liberal, nimble regulatory regimes, rather than taking whatever rules Brussels makes. And Britain can sharpen up competition. It will need to, since Brexit will reduce competitive pressure and thus undermine productivity. The Competition and Markets Authority has offered a number of wise proposals for opening up sectors to new challengers—by, for instance, overhauling antiquated EU rules that shield airlines from competition. The turmoil in the travel business makes this an excellent time to do so.

sharpen up 提高；改善

overhaul 全面修订；彻底检修

turmoil 混乱

在其他方面，分歧可能有利于英国的利益。一个国家的监管过程可能比27个国家更快，而且正如医疗监管机构在疫苗方面所表明的那样，加快监管过程有很多值得称道的地方。规则的性质也可能不同。英国在拥有大量资金的领域（比如金融业）以及其他拥有创新公司的领域，比如金融科技、生命科学和人工智能，英国可以帮助制定自由、灵活的监管制度标准，而不是遵循布鲁塞尔制定的任何规则。英国可以加强竞争。英国需要这么做，因为英国脱欧将降低竞争压力，从而削弱生产率。竞争与市场管理局(Competition and Markets Authority)为向新的挑战者开放行业提出了许多明智的建议，例如，改革保护航空公司免受竞争的陈旧欧盟规则。旅游行业的混乱使得这是一个绝佳的时机。

This newspaper still regards the decision to leave the EU as a self-inflicted wound. But Britain will, for the moment at least, have to live with it. It should therefore grab advantages from Brexit where it can find them, and exploit them thoroughly. ■

self-inflicted 加于自身的

《经济学人》仍将英国退出欧盟的决定视为自己造成的伤害。但至少就目前而言，英国将不得不忍受它。因此，英国应该抓住脱欧带来的优势，并充分利用这些优势。■

Joe Biden’s stimulus is a high-stakes gamble for America and the world

对美国和世界来说，乔·拜登的刺激计划是一场高风险的赌博

The world economy

世界经济

![image-20210315101454400](/Users/chenyujie/Library/Application Support/typora-user-images/image-20210315101454400.png)

It is part of a three-pronged economic experiment

prong 分支；方面

这是三管齐下的经济实验的一部分

Mar 13th 2021

WHEN THE pandemic struck it was natural to fear that the world economy would stay in the doldrums for years. America is defying such pessimism. Having outrun gloomy growth forecasts from last summer, it is adding fiscal rocket fuel to an already fiery economic-policy mix. President Joe Biden’s $1.9 trn stimulus bill, which he was poised to sign into law after The Economist went to press, takes to nearly $3trn (14% of pre-crisis GDP) the amount of pandemic-related spending passed since December, and to about $6trn the total paid out since the start of the crisis. On current plans the Federal Reserve and Treasury will also pour some $2.5trn into the banking system this year, and interest rates will stay near zero. For a decade after the global financial crisis of 2007-09 America’s economic policymakers were too timid. Today they are letting rip.

doldrums 停滞

outrun 超出

timid 胆怯的；怯生生的

let rip 为所欲为；不受控制地发展；激动地说

当疫情爆发时，人们自然会担心世界经济将持续多年低迷。美国正在挑战这种悲观主义。在超越了去年夏天悲观的经济增长预期之后，它正在为已经火爆的经济政策组合注入财政火箭燃料。美国总统拜登（joebiden）提出的1.9万亿美元的经济刺激法案在《经济学人》付印后准备签署成为法律，自12月以来，美国与疫情相关的支出总额已达近3万亿美元(相当于危机前GDP的14%)，而自危机开始以来，已支付的总额则达到约6万亿美元。按照目前的计划，美联储和财政部今年也将向银行系统注入约2.5万亿美元，利率将保持在接近于零的水平。在2007-09年全球金融危机之后的10年里，美国的经济政策制定者过于谨慎。今天，他们正在恣意妄为。

The probable result is a bounce-back that was unthinkable in the spring of 2020. In January America’s retail sales were already 7.4% higher than a year earlier, as most Americans received $600 cheques from the government, part of the previous round of stimulus. Stuck at home and unable to spend as much as they normally would in restaurants, bars and cinemas, consumers have accumulated $1.6trn in excess savings during the past year. Mr Biden’s stimulus gives most Americans another $1,400 each. Unusually for a rich country, a big chunk of the cash pile is held by poor households that are likely to spend it once the economy fully reopens. If vaccines continue to reach arms and America avoids a nasty encounter with new variants, the unemployment rate should fall comfortably below 5% by the end of the year.

nasty 严重的

可能的结果是出现2020年春天无法想象的反弹。今年1月，美国零售额已经比一年前增长了7.4%，因为大多数美国人从政府那里收到了600美元的支票，这是上一轮刺激计划的一部分。消费者被困在家里，无法像往常一样在餐馆、酒吧和电影院消费，因此在过去一年累积了1.6万亿美元的超额储蓄。拜登先生的刺激计划给大多数美国人每人额外1400美元。对于一个富裕国家来说，不同寻常的是，这些现金中有很大一部分是由贫困家庭持有的，一旦经济完全恢复开放，他们可能就会把这些钱花掉。如果疫苗继续普及，而且美国避免了一场新的疫苗变种的可怕遭遇，那么到今年年底，失业率应该会轻松地降至5%以下。

The good news is not confined to America. Manufacturing surveys are healthy even in the euro zone, which is behind on vaccinations and battling new variants, and is applying less stimulus. Mr Biden’s spending will further boost global demand for goods. America’s trade deﬁcit is already more than 50% greater than before the pandemic, as the economy sucks in imports. But the rest of the world will not match Uncle Sam’s breakneck pace. On March 9th the OECD, a club of rich countries, forecast that America’s economy will, uniquely among big economies, be larger at the end of 2022 than it had been predicting before the pandemic. From April to September America is likely to outgrow even China, which is tightening monetary policy and has suffered a 9% fall in its stockmarket since mid-February.

breakneck 极快而危险的

这个好消息并不局限于美国。即使是在接种疫苗和对抗新变种的落后、刺激措施较少的欧元区，制造业调查也很健康。拜登的支出将进一步提振全球对商品的需求。由于美国经济大量吸收进口，美国的贸易赤字已经比疫情爆发前增加了50%以上。但世界上其他国家的步伐将赶不上美国的惊人步伐。3月9日，由富裕国家组成的经济合作与发展组织(OECD)预测，到2022年底，美国经济规模将超过大流行前的预测，这在大型经济体中是独一无二的。从4月到9月，美国的经济增长甚至可能超过中国，中国正在收紧货币政策，自2月中旬以来股市下跌了9%。

Surging out of a crisis that had at its worst moment cut the number of people in work by 15% will be a triumph for America, and will stand in contrast to the puny recovery after the financial crisis. Mr Biden’s spending will provide welcome relief to those whose lives have been upended—today America is still missing 9.5m jobs. Thanks to extra cash for most parents, the country’s persistent and widespread child poverty will fall dramatically.

puny 微不足道的

在危机最糟糕的时候，美国的就业人数减少了15%，对美国来说，从这场危机中走出将是一个胜利，并将与金融危机后的微弱复苏形成鲜明对比。拜登先生的支出将为那些生活被颠覆的人提供可喜的安慰——今天美国仍然失去950万个工作岗位。由于为大多数家长提供了额外的资金，该国持续和普遍存在的儿童贫困状况将大幅下降。

Yet, though today’s policymakers have a guaranteed place in economic history, they may not come to be seen as heroes. That is because America is running an unpredictable three-pronged economic experiment that features historic levels of fiscal stimulus, a more tolerant attitude at the Fed towards temporary overshoots in inflation, and huge pent-up savings which no one knows if consumers will hoard or spend. This experiment has no parallel since the second world war. The danger for America and the world is that the economy overheats.

overshoot 冲出；冲过

pent-up 压抑的；积压的

hoard 贮藏

然而，尽管今天的政策制定者在经济史上有一定的地位，他们可能不会被视为英雄。这是因为美国正在进行一项不可预测的三管齐下的经济实验，其特点是财政刺激达到历史水平，美联储对暂时的通胀过度采取更为宽容的态度，以及大量被压抑的储蓄——没有人知道消费者会囤积还是消费。自第二次世界大战以来，这一实验没有先例。美国和世界的危险在于经济过热。

It is a risk that investors have been weighing up. America’s ten-year bond yields, which move inversely against prices, have risen by about one percentage point since last summer, on expectations of higher inflation and higher interest rates. Because of America’s pivotal role in the global financial system, its outlook for monetary policy spills across borders. In recent weeks Australia’s central bank has had to increase its bond purchases to prevent yields from rising too much. The European Central Bank was deciding whether to make a similar intervention as we went to press. Emerging markets with big deficits, like Brazil, or with large dollar-denominated debts, like Argentina, have reason to fear the tightening in global financial conditions following a turn in American monetary policy.

这是投资者一直在权衡的一种风险。自去年夏天以来，由于预期更高的通货膨胀率和利率，美国十年期债券的收益率(与价格反向波动)已经上升了大约一个百分点。由于美国在全球金融体系中扮演的关键角色，它对货币政策的展望也会跨越国界。最近几周，澳大利亚央行不得不加大债券购买力度，以防止收益率过高。在我们付印之际，欧洲央行正在决定是否进行类似的干预。像巴西这样赤字巨大的新兴市场，或者像阿根廷这样拥有巨额美元债务的新兴市场，有理由担心美国货币政策转变后全球金融状况的收紧。

The Fed is adamant that it will keep interest rates low and continue to buy assets until the economy is much healthier. Inflation will inevitably rise as a collapse in commodities prices early in the pandemic falls out of comparisons with a year earlier, but the Fed will ignore this. Under its new “average inflation targeting” regime, adopted last year, it is seeking to bring about inflation over its 2% target in order to make up for past shortfalls. That is particularly desirable because, for much of the past decade, the world economy’s problem has been too little inflation, not too much. Even if the economy eventually overheats, Jerome Powell, the Fed’s chairman, has argued that this, too, will be temporary. Longer-term inflation dynamics, he argues, “don’t change on a dime”.

be adament about sth/that… 坚持某事/认为…

dime 少量的钱

美联储坚持将利率维持在低位，并继续购买资产，直到经济更加健康为止。随着大宗商品价格在流感大流行初期的暴跌与去年同期相比有所下降，通胀将不可避免地上升，但美联储将忽略这一点。在去年采用的新“平均通胀目标制”下，央行正寻求使通胀率超过2%的目标，以弥补过去的不足。这是非常可取的，因为在过去10年的大部分时间里，世界经济的问题一直是通胀过低，而不是过高。美联储主席杰罗姆•鲍威尔认为，即使经济最终过热，这也将是暂时的。他认为，较长期的通胀动力不会不断改变。

Might they, however, turn on trillions of dollars? We have no reason to doubt the Fed’s near-terms plans, but neither it nor the markets can predict the eventual outcome of America’s experiment. The Fed might have to pour cold water on the economy, raising interest rates to get inflation down. That would be awkward, given how much it has recently emphasised its obligation to seek “broad based and inclusive” strength in the jobs market. Higher rates would puncture asset markets and might also precipitate conflict with an increasingly indebted government.

然而，他们会不会动用数万亿美元呢? 我们没有理由怀疑美联储的近期计划，但无论是美联储还是市场都无法预测美国实验的最终结果。美联储可能不得不给经济泼冷水，通过提高利率来降低通货膨胀。这将是尴尬的，因为它最近非常强调自己有义务在就业市场上寻求“广泛和包容性”的优势。更高的利率会挫伤资产市场，还可能引发与负债日益加重的政府的冲突。

####### 但是长期的通胀变动是否会因为数万亿美元而改变呢？我们没有理由质疑美联储的短期计划，但无论是美联储还是市场都无法预测这一美国实验的最终结果。也许美联储最后还得通过加息来降低通货膨胀，给过热的经济泼一盆冷水。想到它最近如此强调在推动就业市场“全面、广泛”发展方面的责任，这么做恐怕十分尴尬。高利率会打击资产市场，还有可能给债台日益高筑的政府带来隐患。

All the chips on red

Mr Biden’s stimulus is a big gamble. If it pays off, America will avoid the miserable low-inflation, low-rate trap in which Japan and Europe look stuck. Other central banks may copy the Fed’s new target. Massive fiscal stimulus may become the normal response to recessions. The risk, however, is that America is left with rising debts, an inflation problem and a central bank facing a test of its credibility.

拜登的刺激计划是一场豪赌。如果成功，美国将避免日本和欧洲陷入的悲惨的低通胀、低利率陷阱。其他央行可能会效仿美联储的新目标。大规模财政刺激可能成为应对衰退的正常反应。然而，风险在于，美国将面临不断上升的债务、通货膨胀问题以及央行可信度的考验。

拜登先生的刺激计划是一场巨大的赌博。如果如愿以偿，美国可以摆脱日本和欧洲陷入的低通胀、低利率陷阱。其它国家的中央银行也可能效仿美联储制定的目标。巨额财政刺激可能会变成应对衰退的常用工具。但其风险是，美国将面临增加的债务和通货膨胀问题，其央行的信用也会受到考验。

疫情给企业带来了一系列新的道德问题。“利益相关者资本主义”的前提不仅是企业应该考虑员工、客户以及股东的利益。这是因为，通过这样做，每个人都会受益；如果工人和顾客得到体面的待遇，股东就会受益。但大流行可能会使不同群体产生分歧。例如，客户可能希望公司坚持要求所有员工都接种疫苗，而不希望同样的规则适用于自己。

It might seem as if shareholders would want as many employees to return to work as possible, few potential customers to be excluded, and interactions in, say, shops and restaurants to be as free from restrictions as possible. But if a company gets a reputation for being an unsafe place to work, or for customers to visit, the effect on long-term returns could be significant. The perception of fairness is also essential. Woe betide the executive who jumps the queue, as in the case of Mark Machin, a Canadian pension-fund boss who travelled to the United Arab Emirates to get the jab. (He swiftly resigned.)

Woe betide 灾祸降临于

betide 降临于

股东们似乎希望尽可能多的员工重返工作岗位，尽可能少的潜在客户被排除在外，在商店和餐馆等场所的互动尽可能不受限制。但如果一家公司被认为是一个不安全的工作场所，或不适合客户参观，对长期回报的影响可能是显著的。对公平的感知也很重要。谁插队谁就会倒霉，就像加拿大养老基金老板马克•梅钦一样，他专差到阿联酋去注射疫苗。(他很快辞职。)

Companies are not just trading off safety and personal liberty. They risk discriminating against those who have yet to gain access to the vaccine or who, for religious or medical reasons, are unwilling or unable to be jabbed. And they do not have complete freedom of action. The legal situation will vary from country to country but it seems likely that, in many jurisdictions, companies will be able to insist on a vaccine mandate only for new employees.

freedom of action 行动自由

公司不仅仅是以安全和个人自由为代价的。他们有可能歧视那些尚未获得疫苗的人，或那些由于宗教或医学原因不愿意或不能注射疫苗的人。他们没有完全的行动自由。各国的法律情况各不相同，但在许多司法管辖区，企业似乎可以坚持只对新员工强制接种疫苗。

The question of vaccination is particularly pressing in health care. One might hope that medical workers would appreciate the need for it. Yet some health-care personnel may feel that having had covid-19, as many have, negates the obligation. Laura Boudreau of Columbia Business School in New York observes that, in this type of industry, “there is a duty for the employer to try to identify safe roles that the staff can play, and if not, to provide information to customers about whether there are unvaccinated staff in the role.” In other words, unvaccinated staff may end up being kept away from vulnerable patients.

在卫生保健方面，疫苗接种问题特别紧迫。人们可能希望，医务工作者会理解这种需要。然而，一些卫生保健人员可能认为，像许多人一样，感染了covid-19，就否定了这种义务。劳拉Boudreau纽约哥伦比亚商学院(Columbia Business School)的观察,在这种类型的行业，雇主有责任试图确定员工可以扮演的安全角色，如果不行，就向客户提供信息，告诉他们这个角色中是否有未接种疫苗的员工。换句话说，未接种疫苗的工作人员可能会被要求远离易受感染的病人。

Rules in other industries often lack consistency. Early in the pandemic, cruise ships helped to spread the disease. The Saga cruise line, which targets elderly travellers, is now insisting that passengers produce proof of vaccination, but makes no such demand of the crew. That makes sense. The passengers will largely be drawn from the rich world, where the elderly have been first in the queue for jabs. The crew will be younger and often from poorer countries. In both cases vaccines will have been harder to obtain. However, the industry is not taking a consistent approach. Swan Hellenic is insisting on vaccines for crew, not passengers. Victory Cruise Lines has made a jab mandatory for both groups.

其他行业的规则往往缺乏一致性。在大流行早期，游轮帮助传播了疾病。以老年旅行者为目标的Saga邮轮公司现在坚持要求乘客出示疫苗接种证明，但对船员没有这样的要求。这是有意义的。乘客将主要来自富裕国家，那里的老年人排在第一位接种疫苗。船员将更年轻，通常来自较贫穷的国家。在这两种情况下，疫苗将更难获得。然而，该行业并没有采取一致的方法。Swan Hellenic坚持为船员而不是乘客提供疫苗。胜利邮轮公司要求两组人都必须注射疫苗。

No vaccine is 100% effective. It is not yet clear whether people who have been vaccinated can still transmit the disease to others. So where staff come into contact with customers, companies may insist on social distancing or mask-wearing until case levels drop substantially. Even then, employees may catch the virus from each other.

没有疫苗是100%有效的。目前尚不清楚接种过疫苗的人是否还能将这种疾病传染给他人。因此，当员工与客户接触时，公司可能会坚持保持社交距离或佩戴口罩，直到病例数量大幅下降。即便如此，员工之间也有可能感染病毒。

Later, companies will have to consider what they should do when vaccines become more widely available. Some staff may have been unable to get a vaccine, because they have a medical condition (such as pregnancy) that excludes them. Do companies have a duty of care to protect such employees from colleagues who have refused to take the jab on principle?

之后，公司将不得不考虑当疫苗变得更广泛时他们应该怎么做。一些员工可能无法接种疫苗，因为他们有身体状况(比如怀孕)，不能接种疫苗。公司有义务保护这些员工不受那些原则上拒绝接种疫苗的同事的伤害吗?

In the case of most companies, Ms Boudreau says, it is very much in their interest “to reduce barriers for eligible staff to get vaccines, to have a dialogue with their staff to understand if and why they may be hesitant to get vaccinated, and to provide information and resources that may help those who are reluctant”. But that is probably as far as they can go.

eligible 有资格的

在大多数公司中,Boudreau女士说, “减少合格员工获得疫苗的障碍，与员工进行对话，了解他们是否以及为什么不愿接种疫苗，并提供信息和资源，帮助那些不愿意接种疫苗的人”，这非常符合他们的利益。但这可能是他们能做到的。

There may be another phase of the pandemic with new variants resistant to current vaccines. The type of jab people have received then becomes more significant. In short, managers will face a series of trade-offs. Their best option may be to accept the uncertainties, remain flexible, ensure the best possible hygiene standards for their staff—and hope that acquired immunity, vaccines and therapies make covid-19 no more life-threatening than seasonal flu.

大流行可能会进入另一个阶段，出现对现有疫苗具有抗药性的新变种。那时人们接受的注射类型变得更加重要。简而言之，管理者将面临一系列的权衡。它们的最佳选择可能是接受不确定性，保持灵活性，确保其工作人员尽可能达到最佳的卫生标准，并希望获得的免疫、疫苗和疗法不会使covid-19比季节性流感威胁生命。
