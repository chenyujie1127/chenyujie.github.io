Scientists’ pandemic response could be even faster next time

下一次科学家们对疫情的反应可能会更快

Vaccine development

疫苗研发

![image-20210813101805973](/Users/chenyujie/Library/Application Support/typora-user-images/image-20210813101805973.png)

New research suggests mass vaccination can be scaled up more quickly

新的研究表明，大规模疫苗接种可以更快地扩大规模

Aug 5th 2021

AS THE DELTA variant of covid-19 continues to spread across large parts of the world, the shame is that vaccines are still in such short supply. Worse, they are being hogged by rich countries even though the need elsewhere is so great. For that, blame politics. The triumph, however, is that vaccines exist at all. And for that, praise science.

hog 独占；多占

随着新型冠状病毒Delta变种在世界大部分地区持续蔓延，令人遗憾的是，疫苗供应仍然如此短缺。更糟糕的是，在其他地方对疫苗的需求如此巨大的时候，这些疫苗却被发达国家所霸占。对此，要怪就怪政治咯。不过，疫苗的存在就已经是胜利了。为此，赞美科学。

The sequence of the virus’s genome was made public on January 11th 2020, just one month after a new respiratory illness was first reported in Wuhan, in China. Days later, on January 15th, Moderna, working with America’s National Institutes of Health, had completed the design of the prototype molecule that would comprise its vaccine. Sixty-two days after that, on March 16th, human trials began. Maggie Keenan, a 91-year-old Briton, received the first clinical Pfizer vaccine on December 8th. By comparison, the vaccine against polio in America took 20 years to make the journey from trials to licence.

genome 基因组；全部遗传物质

在武汉首次报告一种新的呼吸道疾病后的一个月，该病毒的基因组序列就于2020年1月11日公布。几天后，也就是1月15日，Moderna与美国国家卫生研究院合作，完成了疫苗分子原型的设计。62天后，即3月16日，人体试验开始了。91岁的英国人Maggie Keenan于12月8日接受了辉瑞公司的首个临床疫苗。相比之下，美国的脊髓灰质炎疫苗从试验到获得许可花了20年时间。

And yet scientists could do even better. By the beginning of 2021, according to our statistical model of excess deaths, the pandemic had claimed between 4m and 8m lives. If mass vaccination had got under way even a bit sooner and scaled up just a little more quickly, hundreds of thousands of people could have been saved. That is a goal worth striving for in the next pandemic—and, recent research suggests, it is entirely possible.

然而科学家们可以做得更好。根据我们的超额死亡统计模型，到2021年初，这场疫情夺走了400万至800万人的生命。如果能够更早地开始大规模接种疫苗，再快一点扩大规模，成千上万的人本可以获救。在下一次大流行病的时候，这也是一个值得努力的目标，最近的研究表明，这是完全可能的。

The foundation for this is decades of scientific and medical research. Katalin Karikó, one of the pioneers of the mRNA technology that underpins the two most successful vaccines, made by Pfizer and Moderna, spent 30 years studying how mRNA can help fight disease. Her research was often too radical to attract government grants or institutional support. But it is part of a body of work that vastly enlarges the scope to cope with infectious diseases, including those that are new.

underpin 支持; 巩固

其基础是数十年的科学和医学研究。Katalin Karikó是信使RNA技术的先驱之一，信使RNA技术是辉瑞公司和Moderna公司生产的两种最成功的疫苗的基础。Katalin花了30年时间研究信使RNA如何帮助对抗疾病。她的研究往往过于激进，无法吸引政府拨款或机构支持。但这是一系列工作的一部分，这些工作极大地扩大了应对传染病的范围，包括那些新出现的传染病。

As we explain this week, the pandemic has seen a new set of biochemical and computational tools come to the fore in predicting the evolution of viruses. One approach is called “deep mutational scanning”, which observes random changes in a pathogen’s crucial proteins under laboratory conditions. Using machine learning, scientists can form predictions about which combinations of these mutations will, for instance, make the disease spread faster and hence come to dominate a pandemic in much the way that Delta is dominating covid-19.

to be/come to the fore 处于重要地位/变得重要

正如我们本周所解释的，这场疫情使得在预测病毒进化方面一套新的生化和计算工具脱颖而出。一种称为“深度突变扫描”的方法，它可以在实验室条件下观察病原体关键蛋白质的随意变化。利用机器学习，科学家可以预测这些突变的哪些组合将使疾病传播得更快，从而在很大程度上主宰着这场疫情，就像Delta变种控制着covid-19那样。

Armed with these predictions, manufacturers could prepare stockpiles of vaccines and therapies before pathogens have mutated and spread. One day, people could even be vaccinated pre-emptively. Such a degree of preparedness, and the rapid deployment of doses that it will make possible, could save many lives.

有了这些预测，制造商就可以在病原体变异和传播之前准备好储备的疫苗和疗法。某一天，人们甚至可以提前接种疫苗。这种程度的准备工作以及可能迅速有效利用的疫苗，可以挽救许多生命。

The same faster-is-better logic also applies to other areas of pandemic response. Testing and contact-tracing ought to be available as soon as the first signs emerge that a pathogen is going global. The pandemic has shown that large centralised testing facilities, while they are quick to get up and running from scratch, have slower turnaround times than smaller, more local facilities which can process samples on site.

on site 就地

这种“越快越好”的逻辑也适用于应对疫情的其他领域。一旦病原体在全球蔓延的迹象一出现，就应该进行检测和接触者追踪。此次疫情表明，大型集中检测设备虽然能够快速启动并从头开始运行，但它们的周转时间比更小、更本地化、可以现场处理样品的设施要慢。

Such facilities ought to be ready for the next pandemic, relying on rapid, purpose-built genetic testing that has only just started to come online in airports around the world. By the time the next pathogen arrives, the same technology which allows the covid-19 testing centre in Berlin’s new Brandenburg airport to turn around a sample in less than an hour should be widely available.

turn around 使..好转

这些设备应该能够依靠快速、特质的基因检测（这种检测才刚刚开始在世界各地的机场启动），为下一次大流行病做好准备。到下一种病原体到达时，让柏林新勃兰登堡机场的covid-19检测中心在不到一小时的时间内完成样本检测的技术应该会广泛应用。

Regulators must also play their part. Health authorities are already grappling with the modular nature of the new vaccine-production systems, streamlining their approval processes so that shots may be updated to confer protection against the coronavirus variants. The new vaccine “platforms” can churn out one vaccine as easily as another, with only minor changes. The authorities should start thinking about how to ensure the safety of platform-produced vaccines against entirely new pathogens without having to start the approval process from scratch each time.

streamline 提高..的效率

监管者也必须发挥自己的作用。卫生局已经在努力使其新的疫苗生产系统模块化，简化其审批流程，以便更新疫苗，使其能够提供针对冠状病毒变体的保护。新的疫苗“平台”可以轻松生产一种疫苗和另一种疫苗，只需要做一些微小的改变即可。当局应该开始考虑如何确保平台生产的以对抗全新的病原体的疫苗的安全性，而不必每次都从头开始审批程序。

There is no knowing when the next pandemic will come. New pathogens are emerging from complex, unpredictable environments all the time, often far from scrutiny or regulatory control. A fresh disease could be about to take off right now, as a freak bacterium escapes from an antibiotic-abusing factory farm, say, or a mutated virus sweeps out of a laboratory or a forest, as a bat passes it on to a new host which can infect humans. You cannot stop all pandemics, but you can prepare for them better. ■

freak 反常的

下一次大流行病不知道会在什么时候到来。新的病原体一直在复杂、不可预测的环境中出现，往往远离审查或监管控制。一种新的疾病可能马上就要爆发了，比如，一种奇怪的细菌从滥用抗生素的工厂化农场泄漏，或者一种变异病毒从实验室或森林中传出, 因为蝙蝠可以将其传给一个可以感染人类的新宿主。你无法阻止所有的流行病，但你可以为它们更好地做好准备。■

Turkey’s deadly fires raise the heat for Erdogan

土耳其致命的火灾给埃尔多安（土耳其总理）增加了压力

Up in smoke

被烧毁 ![image-20210812100134922](/Users/chenyujie/Library/Application Support/typora-user-images/image-20210812100134922.png)

The president’s reputation as a capable manager is in ashes

总统那“作为一个有能力的管理者的“声誉已经灰飞烟灭

Aug 7th 2021

THE FIRES crept up the hills, scattered and grew, helped by the wind, and raced down towards the shore. In some places, desperate locals rushed to the sea, filling up plastic buckets with water to ward off the flames approaching their homes. Others ran or drove for their lives. The sky turned grey, then orange. By the time the smoke had cleared, stretches of Turkey’s coastal paradise, once covered in pine forests and olive trees, were in ashes.

火苗爬上了山坡，在风的帮助下散开了，越来越大，向着海岸蔓延。在一些地方，绝望的当地人冲到海边，用塑料桶装满水，以阻挡接近他们家园的火焰。还有一些人跑着或开车逃命。天空先是灰色，然后是橙色。当烟雾散去时，曾经被松树林和橄榄树覆盖的土耳其沿海乐园的大片区域已化为灰烬。

At least eight people have died in the fires along Turkey’s southern and western coasts at the end of July, following record temperatures and a severe drought. Thousands more have been evacuated, including tourists from seaside resorts, some boarding boats to escape. Nearly 160,000 hectares of forest have burned in Turkey this year, according to the European Forest Fire Information System, four times the average between 2008 and 2020. Wildfires have also been raging this summer elsewhere in the region (see map).

7月底，土耳其南部和西部海岸发生大火，造成至少8人死亡。此前，土耳其经历了创纪录的高温和严重的干旱。数千人被撤离，其中包括住在海滨度假胜地的游客，一些人登船逃生。据欧洲森林火灾信息系统的数据，今年土耳其有近16万公顷的森林被烧毁，这一数字是2008年至2020年期间平均水平的4倍。今年夏天，该地区其他区域也发生了森林大火(见地图)。

The government of President Recep Tayyip Erdogan announced in early August that most of the fires were under control. But on August 5th parts of the region remained ablaze. In Milas, near the Aegean coast, flames spread to a thermal power plant. Officials said they had removed explosive materials from the plant and emptied its hydrogen tanks.

总理雷杰普·塔伊普·埃尔多安8月初宣布，大部分火灾已经得到控制。但8月5日，该地区部分地区仍在燃烧。在爱琴海海岸附近的米拉斯，大火蔓延到一家热电厂。官方说他们已经从热电厂移走了爆炸性材料，并清空了氢气罐。

The government, caught badly off guard by the disaster, is also feeling the heat. As local mayors pleaded for help, ministers acknowledged they had no working water bombers, and belatedly resorted to helicopters. The head of the agency responsible for the planes admitted he had gone to a wedding at the height of the crisis. Turkey eventually accepted offers of help from Russia, Azerbaijan and the EU. Volunteers and firefighters poured in from across the country, as did police trucks mounted with water cannons, typically used against protesters. Locals seethed, sensing the government had abandoned them to their fate. To smother the outcry, Turkey’s media watchdog accused some television stations of spreading “fear and anxiety” about the fires and threatened them with “the heaviest of sanctions”.

to be off (one’s) guard 不提防

to catch sb off guard 使某人措手不及

belatedly 迟来地

water bombers 森林灭火飞机

seethe 发怒

smother 抑制

outcry 强烈的抗议

政府也被这场灾难搞的措手不及，承受着压力。当当地市长请求帮助时，部长们承认他们没有可用的森林灭火飞机，并最终求助于直升机。负责飞机事务的机构负责人承认，他曾在危机最严重的时候去参加一个婚礼。土耳其最终接受了俄罗斯、阿塞拜疆和欧盟的援助。志愿者和消防队员从全国各地蜂拥而来，警车上安装了高压水枪（通常用来对付抗议者）赶来。当地人怒不可遏，觉得政府已经抛弃了他们。为了平息抗议，土耳其媒体监督机构指责一些电视台传播有关火灾的“恐惧和焦虑”，并威胁要对他们实施“最严厉的制裁”。

![image-20210812100102118](/Users/chenyujie/Library/Application Support/typora-user-images/image-20210812100102118.png)

Mr Erdogan, an authoritarian leader admired by his supporters as a capable manager, has not shone. He has increasingly seemed overwhelmed by the crises roiling his country. On July 31st he arrived in Marmaris, an area besieged by the fires. His motorcade blocked traffic as he tossed packages of tea from his bus at local residents. Days later, he pledged that the government would rebuild destroyed property and cover the rents of those left homeless.

motorcade 车队

埃尔多安是一位专制的领导人，他的支持者称赞他是一位有能力的管理者，但他并没有表现出色。他似乎要逐渐被困扰他国家的危机压垮了。7月31日，他抵达被大火包围的马尔马里斯地区。他的车队阻塞了交通，因为他从车上向当地居民扔茶包。几天后，他承诺政府将重建被毁的财产，并支付那些无家可归者的租金。

Turkey is reaping the effects of decades of environmental destruction, compounded by climate change. Earlier this summer, thick sheets of marine mucilage, more crudely known as “sea snot”, spread over the Marmara Sea, to Istanbul’s south, the result of rising temperatures, a construction frenzy and industrial run-off. A severe drought and the diversion of water for farming were probably to blame for the deaths of thousands of baby flamingos at a saline lake in Anatolia. Each year the forests lining the country’s coasts recede further inland, replaced by rows of vacation homes and hotels. Mr Erdogan’s ministers have promised to keep developers away from the areas scorched by this summer’s fires. Even if they stick to their word, the fires have shown how much harder it has become to keep climate change at bay.

土耳其正受着数十年环境破坏的影响，再加上气候变化的影响。今年夏天早些时候，由于气温上升、建筑热潮和工业流失，厚厚的海洋黏液，也就是俗称的“海鼻涕”，在马摩拉海蔓延开来，一直延伸到伊斯坦布尔南部。在安纳托利亚的一个盐湖上，一场严重的干旱和农业用水的分流可能是造成数千只小火烈鸟死亡的原因。每年，该国沿海的森林都在向内陆深处退去，取而代之的是成排的度假屋和酒店。埃尔多安的部长们已经承诺让开发商远离今夏被大火烧焦的地区。就算他们信守承诺，这场大火也表明，遏制气候变化变得多么困难。

The chip shortage is a self-solving problem

芯片短缺是一个自我解决的问题

Chips leader

芯片领导

![image-20210811110245062](/Users/chenyujie/Library/Application Support/typora-user-images/image-20210811110245062.png)

Government subsidies will lead to overcapacity and waste

政府补贴将导致产能过剩和浪费

Aug 5th 2021

ONE FIRM’S crisis is another’s opportunity. A shortage of semiconductors has helped pump up the valuations of firms such as Nvidia, whose chips power everything from video-gaming to machine learning and data centres. But boom time for sellers means misery for buyers. Carmakers, whose products have become computers on wheels, are among the victims. Profits at Ford, America’s second-biggest carmaker by volume, fell by half in the most recent quarter amid a global shortage of chips. Analysts say the industry might build around 5m fewer cars this year, all for want of their tiniest components.

一家公司的危机是另一家公司的机会。半导体的短缺使得像英伟达这样的公司的估值大幅上升。英伟达的芯片为从“视频游戏”到“机器学习”以及“数据中心”的所有产品提供动力。但卖家的繁荣时期意味着买家的痛苦时期。汽车制造商（他们的产品已经成为车载电脑）也在受害者之列。由于全球芯片短缺，美国销量第二大的汽车制造商福特在最近一个季度的利润下降了一半。分析师表示，汽车业今年可能会减少500万辆左右的汽车产量，所有这些都是由于小部件的紧缺。

Carmakers are not the only firms feeling the pinch. Apple and Microsoft have also warned that they will be affected. Politicians are being drawn in, too. Chips will be on the agenda later this month when America’s vice-president, Kamala Harris, visits Vietnam, which has a flourishing electronics industry. Angela Merkel, the outgoing German chancellor, has lamented Europe’s small share of global chip production.

to feel the pinch 感到手头拮据

to be on the agenda 列入日程表

汽车制造商并不是唯一感到不好过的公司。苹果和微软也警告称，它们将受到影响。政客们也被卷入其中。本月晚些时候，当美国副总统Kamala Harris访问拥有蓬勃发展电子工业的越南时，芯片将被提上议程。即将离任的德国总理默克尔也曾抱怨欧洲在全球芯片生产中所占份额太小。

The shortage is the result of a sudden surge in demand. Chipmaking is a cyclical business which, between the peaks and troughs, has been enjoying strong growth for decades as computers creep into every corner of society. That trend was amplified by the pandemic. Locked-down consumers shopped online, logged into meetings remotely, and wiled away the hours with video-streaming and video-gaming. The result has been a spike in demand for the semiconductors that power the data centres and gadgets that make such things possible, clogging factories with orders.

to have peaks and troughs 有高峰和低谷

creep into 不知不觉在…中产生

短缺是因为需求突然激增。芯片制造是一个周期性的行业，在高峰和低谷之间，由于计算机已经渗透到社会的每个角落，芯片这几十年来一直享受着强劲的增长。这一趋势因疫情而进一步扩大。被封锁在家的消费者在网上购物，远程参加会议，用流媒体视频和视频游戏消磨时间。结果就导致数据中心所需的半导体以及使这些事情成为可能的一些相关设备的需求激增，从而使得工厂订单积压。

The crisis has had three consequences, two encouraging and one less so. The first is an investment boom. Big producers such as Intel, Samsung and TSMC are planning to spend hundreds of billions of dollars on extra capacity over the next few years. As in many markets, high prices are the best cure for high prices.

这场危机产生了三种后果，其中两种后果振奋人心，另一种则不那么令人振奋。首先是投资热潮。英特尔、三星和台积电等大型制造商计划在未来几年斥资数千亿美元扩大产能。与许多市场一样，高价格是解决高价格的方法。

![image-20210811110212796](/Users/chenyujie/Library/Application Support/typora-user-images/image-20210811110212796.png)

The second is that the chip industry’s customers are adapting, too. When demand collapsed early in the pandemic, carmakers cut their orders with chipmakers. The car industry’s size and clout mean that it is used to ordering suppliers around. But when demand recovered, it found itself at the back of the queue, because of long lead times and competition for capacity from the even bigger and more influential tech industry.

lead time 投产前研制周期 ; (in delivery) 订货交付

第二，芯片行业的客户也在适应。当需求在疫情初期萎缩时，汽车制造商削减了与芯片制造商的订单。汽车行业的规模和影响力意味着它已经习惯于向各地的供应商订货。但当需求恢复时，它发现自己排在了队伍的最后，因为交货时间长，而且还要与来自规模更大、影响力更大的科技行业的产能竞争。

The unpleasant experience of being the supplicant rather than the boss has prodded carmakers to take tighter control over supplies of vital components. Following in the tyre-treads of Tesla, Volkswagen has announced plans to develop driver-assistance chips in-house. Other firms are forging closer relationships with chipmakers. Toyota, a Japanese firm, has weathered the shortage relatively well, partly because it was slower to cut orders when the pandemic hit. In June Robert Bosch, a big supplier of automotive parts, cut the ribbon on a €1bn ($1.2bn) chip factory of its own in Dresden. Redesigned supply chains will be more resilient.

supplicant 哀求者

prod 催促；鼓励

作为哀求者而非老板的不愉快经历促使汽车制造商对关键部件的供应采取更严格的控制。继特斯拉的轮胎爆胎之后，大众汽车宣布了在内部开发驾驶员辅助芯片的计划。其他公司正在与芯片制造商建立更紧密的关系。日本丰田汽车公司相对较好地经受住了短缺的考验，部分原因是在疫情爆发时，该公司削减订单的速度较慢。今年6月，大型汽车零部件供应商Robert Bosch在德累斯顿为自己10亿欧元(合12亿美元)的芯片工厂剪彩。重新设计的供应链适应能力会更强。

The third, unwelcome effect has been a surge of techno-nationalism. America is planning to hand out billions of dollars to lure chipmakers back from East Asia. Europe wants to double its share of global production, to 20%, by 2030. Even Britain has declared the fate of a small chip factory in Wales to be a matter of national security.

The habits and flaws tycoons share with dynastic rulers

IN THE EARLY 15th century many of the Portuguese voyages of discovery around Africa and into Asia were financed by Prince Henry of Portugal, whom historians dubbed “Henry the Navigator”. When Christopher Columbus sought finance for his planned westward voyage to the “Indies”, he first turned to the king of Portugal before achieving success with Ferdinand and Isabella of Spain. Monarchs financed explorations because they believed such trips would boost their power and their treasuries.

a voyage of discovery or exploration 探索之旅

背景：哥伦布为了实现自己的计划，到处游说了十几年。1485年，哥伦布向葡萄牙国王约翰二世展示了他的计划。哥伦布也曾经游说过英国、法国和意大利，但都被拒绝。直到1492年，西班牙女王伊莎贝拉（Queen Isabella）慧眼识英雄，她说服了国王斐迪南二世（KIng Ferdinand），使哥伦布的计划才得以实施。这些国家拒绝的原因之一就是因为哥伦布提出一些过分的要求，例如他要求“航海司令”的头衔，10%的战利品回报，并且要求将他发现的每个国家的总督权过继给他的后代。

在15世纪早期，葡萄牙的亨利王子资助了许多环绕非洲和亚洲的航海探险，历史学家称他为“航海家亨利”。当哥伦布为他向西“印度群岛”航行的计划寻求资金时，他最先求助于葡萄牙国王（无果），然后才寻得西班牙的国王费迪南和西班牙女王伊莎贝拉的资助（计划得以实施）。君主们资助探险是因为他们相信这样的旅行会增加他们的权力和财富。

In the 21st century corporate executives have become deeply involved in adventure and exploration. Sir Richard Branson of Virgin and Jeff Bezos of Amazon have just travelled to the edge of space. Elon Musk of Tesla has developed the SpaceX programme and is talking of the eventual colonisation of Mars. Messrs Musk and Bezos competed for the contract to operate future Moon landings. Mr Bezos even offered to part-finance the project.

在21世纪，企业高管们已经深深投身于冒险和探索之中。维珍的理查德•布兰森爵士和亚马逊的杰夫•贝佐斯刚刚抵达了太空边缘。特斯拉的埃隆•马斯克开展了SpaceX计划，并正在谈论火星的最终殖民化。马斯克和贝佐斯竞争实行未来登月的合同。贝佐斯甚至提出为该项目提供部分资金。

In itself, this is a remarkable development. Sixty years ago, when the space race was between America and the Soviet Union, few could have imagined that individual businessmen would ever have the resources to enter the fray. The shift says something about the extremes of wealth in the 21st century.

fray 争斗

enter or join the fray 介入争斗

就其本身而言，这是一个显著的发展。60年前，当美国和苏联还在进行太空竞赛的时候，很少有人能想象到个体商户会有财力参与这场竞赛。这种转变反映了21世纪财富的极端性。

The resemblance to absolute monarchs does not stop with exploration. Like past rulers, modern tycoons build their own monuments in the form of corporate headquarters, not just skyscrapers in London and New York but the vast, low-rise campuses in Silicon Valley. Whereas the ancient dynasts travelled in horse-driven coaches, modern CEOS separate themselves from the public in chauffeur-driven limos and private jets.

Like monarchs of old, executives have to deal with rival sources of power. They face the equivalent of feudal barons, in the form of boards of directors who may try to unseat them. And they need to contend with ambitious princelings, who in the modern era are younger executives who would like their job. The good news is that whereas an unseated monarch was likely to be executed, a dethroned boss can enjoy a generous pay-off.

princeling 幼年王子; 小王公

dethrone 废黜

Then there is their ability to control time. At the court of Louis XIV, France’s “Sun King”, the rhythm of the day was entirely devoted to the monarch’s habits, with the luckiest courtiers watching him get dressed, have lunch and go to bed. Modern CEOs also have the ability to change the schedules of those around them. If he or she gets up at 5am to send messages, someone on the staff will feel obliged to rise early and answer them. Similarly, if the CEO likes to hold Zoom conferences on weekends, or have working dinners on a Friday night, the family life of subordinates will suffer.

courtier 侍臣

其次是他们控制时间的能力。在法国“太阳王”路易十四的宫廷里，一天的节奏完全按照君主的习惯来安排，最幸运的侍臣们会看着他穿衣服、吃午饭和睡觉。现代的CEO也有能力改变周围人的日程安排。如果他（或她）早上5点起床发信息，员工中就会有人觉得有必要早起回复。同样地，如果CEO喜欢在周末召开Zoom会议，或者在周五晚上吃工作餐，下属的家庭生活就会受到影响。

In his book “Fall”, John Preston recounts that when Robert Maxwell, the publishing tycoon, was dissatisfied with his food, he would sometimes sweep the plate on to the floor and leave others to clear it up. intercept 拦截

与君主的另一个相似之处是傲慢的倾向。 John Preston在他的著作《Fall》中写道，当出版大亨Robert Maxwell对自己的食物不满意时，他有时会把盘子扫到地上，让别人来收拾。Maxwell还在他手下员工的电话里装了窃听器，并听取了他们的对话，这也让人想起了路易十四，他曾拦截过侍臣的邮件。

Lavish entertainment is a further common denominator. Monarchs held elaborate balls and competed to show off their wealth. Modern tycoons pay rock stars to perform at their birthdays. Carlos Ghosn, the boss of Nissan, even held an extravagant party at the Sun King’s former digs in Versailles.

dig in 尽情地吃

奢侈的娱乐是另一个普遍特征。君主们举办精致的舞会，竞相炫耀他们的财富。现代巨头们花钱请摇滚明星在他们生日时表演。日产的老板Carlos Ghosn甚至在太阳王凡尔赛宫的故居举办了一场奢华的派对。

Royal dynasties added to their empires through both military conquest and strategic marriages. Modern executives achieve the same effect through mergers and acquisitions, using their financial clout to buy smaller rivals and reduce the threat of disruptive competition. In effect, ancient monarchs were monopoly providers of security services, who received payment in the form of taxation and conscription. Their abiding sin was too much ambition; Philip II of Spain’s military overreach in battling England and the Netherlands was followed by the country’s steady decline as a global power, for example.

to overreach oneself 不自量力

皇家王朝通过军事征服和战略联姻来扩大他们的帝国。现代高管通过并购达到了同样的效果，他们利用自己的财务影响力收购较小的竞争对手，减少破坏性竞争的威胁。实际上，古代君主是安全服务的垄断者，他们以税收和征兵的形式收取报酬。他们长久以来的罪过就是野心太大;例如，西班牙的菲利普二世在与英国和荷兰的战争中过度扩张，随后这个国家作为全球强国的地位逐渐衰落。

The same trap awaits modern tycoons. Often they make the mistake of taking on too much debt by acquiring businesses that do not mesh with the rest of the enterprise. Or, like many an ancient ruler**, they make the mistake of fighting on two fronts.** Space-obsessed Mr Bezos is still executive chairman of Amazon. Mr Musk is trying to make both rockets and Tesla cars. The greatest danger to monarchs may come when they seem at the height of their powers.

同样的“陷阱”将降临到现代巨头们身上。他们经常犯的错误是，通过收购与企业其他部门不匹配的企业而背负了太多的债务。或者，像许多古代统治者一样，他们犯了两条战线作战的错误。痴迷于太空的贝佐斯仍是亚马逊的执行董事长。马斯克先生也在试图同时制造火箭和特斯拉汽车。君主们面临的最大危险可能出现在他们似乎处于权力巅峰的时候。

The promise of open-source intelligence

The people’s panopticon

panopticon：an area where everything is visible

![image-20210809101504315](/Users/chenyujie/Library/Application Support/typora-user-images/image-20210809101504315.png)

对于不法分子和有隐瞒的政府来说，这是一个受欢迎的威胁

THE GREAT hope of the 1990s and 2000s was that the internet would be a force for openness and freedom. As Stewart Brand, a pioneer of online communities, put it: “Information wants to be free, because the cost of getting it out is getting lower and lower all the time.” It was not to be. Bad information often drove out good. Authoritarian states co-opted the technologies that were supposed to loosen their grip. Information was wielded as a weapon of war. 上世纪90年代和本世纪头十年的最大希望是，互联网将成为一股推动开放和自由的力量。正如在线社区的先驱Stewart Brand所言:“信息有望自由，因为发布信息的成本一直在降低。” 这是不可能的。坏消息常常驱逐好消息。专制国家采用了本应放松其控制的技术。信息被用作战争武器。在这种失望之中，有一项发展为新的希望提供了理由:正在兴起的开源网络情报时代(OSINT)。

New sensors, from humdrum dashboard cameras to satellites that can see across the electromagnetic spectrum, are examining the planet and its people as never before. The information they collect is becoming cheaper. Satellite images cost several thousand dollars 20 years ago, today they are often provided free and are of incomparably higher quality. A photograph of any spot on Earth, of a stricken tanker or the routes taken by joggers in a city is available with a few clicks. And online communities and collaborative tools, like Slack, enable hobbyists and experts to use this cornucopia of information to solve riddles and unearth misdeeds with astonishing speed.

incomparably 无与伦比地; 绝佳的

stricken 受折磨的

hobbyist 业余爱好者

cornucopia 丰盛

a cornucopia of vegetables/information 大量的蔬菜/信息

unearth 发掘; 发现

misdeed 恶行; 不法行为

从单调的仪表盘摄像头到可以看到整个电磁频谱的卫星，新的传感器正在以前所未有的方式调查地球以及地球上的人。他们收集的信息越来越便宜。20年前，卫星图像的价格是几千美元，而今天，它们通常是免费提供的，而且有着绝佳的高质量。只需点击几下鼠标，你就可以获得一张地球上任何地点的照片，一艘受损的油轮或一个城市中慢跑者走过的路线。在线社区以及像Slack这样的协作工具，让业余爱好者和专家能够以惊人的速度利用这些丰富的信息来破解谜题和发现犯罪行为。

Human Rights Watch has analysed satellite imagery to document ethnic cleansing in Myanmar. Nanosatellites tag the automatic identification system of vessels that are fishing illegally. Amateur sleuths have helped Europol, the European Union’s policing agency, investigate child sexual exploitation by identifying geographical clues in the background of photographs. Even hedge funds routinely track the movements of company executives in private jets, monitored by a web of amateurs around the world, to predict mergers and acquisitions.

imagery 图像

document 记录

sleuth 侦探

人权观察分析了卫星图像来记录缅甸的种族清洗。微型卫星可以为非法捕鱼船只的自动识别系统贴上了标签。业余侦探通过识别照片背景中的地理线索，帮助欧洲刑警组织(欧盟的警察机构)调查儿童性剥削。就连对冲基金也会定期跟踪公司高管乘坐私人飞机（这些私人飞机由世界各地的业余人士组成的网络监控）的动向，以预测公司的并购活动。

OSINT thus bolsters civil society, strengthens law enforcement and makes markets more efficient. It can also humble some of the world’s most powerful countries.

In the face of vehement denials from the Kremlin, Bellingcat, an investigative group, meticulously demonstrated Russia’s role in the downing of Malaysian Airlines Flight MH17 over Ukraine in 2014, using little more than a handful of photographs, satellite images and elementary geometry. It went on to identify the Russian agents who attempted to assassinate Sergei Skripal, a former Russian spy, in England in 2018. Amateur analysts and journalists used OSINT to piece together the full extent of XX internment camps in XX. In recent weeks researchers poring over satellite imagery have spotted XX constructing hundreds of nuclear-missile silos in the desert.

Such an emancipation of information promises to have profound effects. The decentralised and egalitarian nature of OSINT erodes the power of traditional arbiters of truth and falsehood, in particular governments and their spies and soldiers. Although OSINT might not prevent Russia from invading Ukraine or XX from building its gulag, it exposes the flimsiness of their lies. Eliot Higgins, Bellingcat’s founder, is right when he describes his organisation as “an intelligence agency for the people”. Liberal democracies will also be kept more honest. Citizens will no longer have to take their governments on trust. News outlets will have new ways of holding them to account. Today’s open sources and methods would have shone a brighter light on the Bush administration’s accusation in 2003 that Iraq was developing chemical, biological and nuclear weapons. That would have subjected America’s invasion of the country to greater scrutiny. It might even have prevented it.

自由民主国家也将更加诚实。公民将不再需要信任他们的政府。新闻媒体将有新的方式来追究他们的责任。今天的开源和方法将会使布什政府在2003年对伊拉克发展化学、生物和核武器的指控更加明朗。这将使美国对该国的入侵受到更严格的审查。它甚至可能阻止了它。

Some will warn that OSINT threatens national security—as when, for example, researchers use data from fitness trackers to reveal remote CIA outposts and radar satellites to locate American missile-defence systems. But, if OSINT can tell the world about such things, a country’s enemies are already able to know them. Pretending otherwise does not make states any safer.

Others will point out that OSINT can be wrong. After the Boston Marathon bombing in 2013 internet users scrutinised the crime scene and identified several suspects. All were innocent bystanders. Or OSINT could be used by bad actors to spread misinformation and conspiracy theories.

其他人会指出，OSINT也可能弄错。2013年波士顿马拉松爆炸案发生后，互联网用户仔细检查了犯罪现场，并确定了几名嫌疑人。他们都是无辜的旁观者。或者，OSINT可能被坏人利用来传播错误信息和阴谋论。

However, every source of information is fallible and the scrutiny of imagery and data is more empirical than most of them. Hence, when OSINT is mistaken or malign, competing OSINT is often the best way to put the record straight. And over time, researchers and investigators can build a reputation for honesty, sound analysis and good judgment, making it easier for people to distinguish trustworthy sources of intelligence from charlatans.

to put or set the record straight 澄清事实

charlata 假内行

然而，每一种信息来源都是不可靠的，对图像和数据的审查比大多数信息来源靠的更多的是经验。因此，当OSINT犯错或诽谤，与OSINT竞争通常是澄清事实的最好方法。随着时间的推移，研究人员和调查人员可以建立诚实、合理分析和良好判断的声誉，使人们更容易区分可靠的情报来源和骗子。

The greatest worry is that the explosion of data behind open-source investigations also threatens individual privacy. The data generated by phones and sold by brokers let Bellingcat identify the Russian spies who last year poisoned Alexei Navalny, an opposition leader. Similar data were exploited to pick out a senior Catholic priest in America, who resigned last month after his location was linked to his use of Grindr, a gay dating app.

最大的担忧是，开源调查背后的数据爆炸也会威胁到个人隐私。通过电话生成并由经纪人出售的数据，让Bellingcat得以确认去年毒死反对派领袖Alexei Navalny的俄罗斯间谍。类似的数据被用来认出美国的一位资深天主教牧师，他在使用同性恋约会应用Grindr后，他的位置被链接起来，他于上月辞职。

A see-through world

一个透明的世界

The privacy of individuals in a digital age is fraught with trade-offs. At the level of states and organisations, however, OSINT promises to be a force for good. It is also unstoppable. Predicting viral evolution may let vaccines be prepared in advance

预测病毒的进化可能可以让疫苗提前准备好

Combating future viruses

抗击未来的病毒

![image-20210807124428348](/Users/chenyujie/Library/Application Support/typora-user-images/image-20210807124428348.png)

New techniques could programme people’s immune systems against future pathogens

programme 安排

新技术可以让人类的免疫系统抵御未来的病原体

Aug 5th 2021

GENERALLY, IMMUNE systems mount responses only against pathogens that have already infected the bodies they are protecting. Science, though, can shorten the path to immunity by vaccination. This involves presenting the immune system with harmless or lookalike versions of dangerous pathogens so that it may create antibodies and killer cells hostile to the real thing in advance of any actual infection, thereby reducing its danger.

一般来说，免疫系统只对已经影响了它们所保护的身体的病原体产生反应。然而，科学可以通过接种疫苗缩短获得免疫力的途径。这涉及到向免疫系统提供无害或相似的危险病原体，以便它可以在任何真实感染之前产生抗体和致命细胞以对抗真正有危害的病原体，从而降低其危险。

Like immune responses themselves, however, vaccination generally has to wait for the appearance of the pathogen in question before it can do its stuff. There is therefore a delay between a pathogen’s arrival on the scene and the deployment of a vaccine against it. That delay costs lives. Even in the case of covid-19, which has prompted the fastest vaccine-development programme the world has ever witnessed, millions are reckoned to have died by the time vaccinations began to be given in the rich world at the end of 2020.

然而，就像免疫反应本身一样，疫苗接种通常要等到有问题的病原体出现后才能起作用。因此，在病原体到达现场和针对它的疫苗研发之间存在一个延迟。这种延迟需要付出生命的代价。以covid-19(即使它促成了世界上有史以来最快的疫苗开发项目)为例，但据估计在发达国家于2020年底开始接种疫苗时，已有数百万人死亡。

But, just as vaccination introduces immune systems to pathogens that are remote from them in space, new techniques which have come to the fore during the current pandemic offer the possibility of introducing them to pathogens that are remote from them in time—pathogens, indeed, that have not yet evolved, but which are likely to do so in the future. Thanks to a combination of high-throughput DNA-sequencing technologies and modern machine-learning it is now possible not merely to observe which variants of a virus are circulating, but also to suggest how they are likely to change. Understanding in this way what a virus might look like in the months and years to come gives those designing vaccines and therapies a leg up, enabling them to prime more immune systems sooner, so that fewer people die.

to be/come to the fore 处于重要地位/变得重要

to prime sb about sth 事先告知某人

但是, 正如接种疫苗引入免疫系统以让病原体远离它们所在的空间，在当下疫情期间出现的新技术让免疫系统可以让病原体在时间上远离它们提供了可能，确实，病原体现在尚未进化，但是未来他是有可能进化的。由于高通量DNA测序技术和现代机器学习的结合，现在不仅可以观察到病毒的哪些变种正在传播，而且还可以指出它们可能如何变化。通过这种方式了解病毒在未来几个月或几年里可能的样子，这给那些疫苗和疗法的设计人员带来了帮助，这使他们能够更快地启动更多的免疫系统，从而减少死亡人数。

The starting point for these predictions is the sort of work going on in the laboratory of Jesse Bloom, a virologist at the Fred Hutchinson Cancer Research Centre, in Seattle. Dr Bloom and his colleagues grow variants of coronavirus spike protein (the molecule which such viruses use to attach themselves to cells they are about to infect) in Petri dishes. They then scan through these to discern which mutations have what effects.

这些预测的起点是Jesse Bloom（西雅图 Fred Hutchinson癌症研究中心的病毒学家）实验室正在进行的工作。Bloom博士和他的同事们在培养皿中培育了冠状病毒刺突蛋白(病毒用来附着在它们将要感染的细胞上的分子)的变种。然后他们对这些蛋白进行扫描，以辨别哪些突变具有哪些影响。

They have named this technique deep mutational scanning. It uses an array of yeast cells that have been genetically modified to express a part of the spike protein called the receptor-binding domain (RBD). As the yeast cells churn out their RBDs, many emerge, thanks to errors inherent in their production, with slight deviations in their structures from that of the original wild-type virus. Dr Bloom’s team then test the RBDs from each yeast cell to see how tightly they bind to ACE2, a receptor protein found on the surfaces of some human cells, to which the coronavirus attaches itself before entering those cells. RBDs that bind tightly have their underlying genomes sequenced, to determine which mutations are present.

他们将这种技术命名为深度突变扫描。它使用一系列经过基因改造的酵母细胞来表达被称为受体结合域(RBD)的刺突蛋白的一部分。当酵母细胞大量生产RDB时，由于生产过程中固有的错误，许多RDB的结构与原始野生型病毒略有偏差。然后，Bloom博士的团队测试了每个酵母细胞的RDB，看看它们与ACE2的结合有多紧密。ACE2是一种存在于一些人类细胞表面的受体蛋白，冠状病毒在进入这些细胞之前会附着在它上面。对紧密结合的RDB其底层基因组进行测序，以确定存在哪些突变。

When Dr Bloom’s team ran this scan in the summer of 2020, on spike from a version of the virus then circulating, they spotted a mutation called N501Y which appeared to confer a binding advantage. A few months later, that mutation appeared in the Alpha variant, which for several months was dominant across much of the world. Dr Bloom says it would be “charitable” to say that he and his colleagues had predicted the emergence of N501Y. It was by no means the only mutation of interest to turn up. But even so, having a limited set of such mutations to focus on is useful for narrowing the field of research.

当Bloom博士的团队在2020年夏天进行这项扫描时，他们发现了一种名为N501Y的突变，这种突变似乎带来了结合优势。几个月后，这种变异出现在Alpha变种中，在几个月的时间里，Alpha变种在世界上很多地方都占主导地位。Bloom博士说，如果说他和他的同事预测到了N501Y的出现，那也是“可以理解的”。这决不是出现的唯一（他们）感兴趣的突变。但即便如此，专注于一组有限的突变有助于缩小研究领域。

Getting the message across

One firm taking advantage of that narrowing is Flagship Labs 77, a company based in Boston that has until recently been working in secret. FL77, as it is known for short, is a spin out from Flagship Pioneering, a biotechnology incubator run by Noubar Afeyan, a venture capitalist. Moderna, a trailblazer of the messenger- RNA-based technology that helped speed up the production of coronavirus vaccines, was also a Flagship Pioneering company, and Mr Afeyan is its chairman.

旗舰实验室77是一家利用这种“缩小领域”的公司，这家总部位于波士顿的公司直到最近还在秘密开展工作。FL77（简称）是Flagship Pioneering的分拆公司，Flagship Pioneering是一家由风投资本家Noubar Afeyan经营的生物技术孵化器。Moderna是信使RNA技术的先驱，其帮助加快了冠状病毒疫苗的生产，也是一家Flagship Pioneering公司，Afeyan先生是该公司的董事长。

FL77’s researchers are trying to combine experimental data of the sort Dr Bloom is collecting with computation, in order to predict how viruses may evolve. That information could be used to develop vaccines and therapeutic antibodies pre-emptively. Whereas Dr Bloom’s laboratory predicts only single mutational hops, FL77 can currently manage five or six. The firm calls its system “Global Pathogen Shield”. The details remain confidential, but in June it published a paper outlining the project’s goals. This described the scale of the challenge involved in keeping pace with viral evolution—namely that biology is so diverse that even looking at a small slice of possible mutations leads to a problem which rapidly grows beyond the plausible limits of observation, to one on the scale of counting and categorising all of the atoms of which Earth is composed.

FL77的研究人员正试图将Bloom博士收集的实验数据与计算结合起来，以预测病毒可能如何进化。这些信息可以用来先发制人地开发疫苗和治疗性抗体。尽管Bloom博士的实验室预测只有单个突变的跳跃，但FL77目前可以控制5到6个突变。该公司称其系统为“全球病原体盾牌”。细节仍然保密，但在6月，它发表了一篇论文，概述了该项目的目标。这描述了跟上病毒进化的步伐所面临的挑战的规模，也就是说，生物学是如此多样化，即使只是观察一小部分可能的突变，也会导致问题迅速发展，超出观察的合理范围，到计算和分类组成地球的所有原子的尺度上。

The conventional response to such overwhelming odds has been observation rather than experimentation. The World Health Organisation’s Global Influenza Surveillance and Response System does this for flu. It monitors which viruses are circulating in the southern hemisphere when it is winter there, in order to focus attention on which strains will be relevant during the next northern-hemisphere winter, and vice versa. During the coronavirus pandemic, organisations such as Nextstrain and GISAID have kept track of variants of SARS-CoV-2 in a similar way.

对于这种压倒性的优势，传统的反应是观察而不是实验。世界卫生组织的全球流感监测和反应系统就是这样应对流感的。它监测南半球冬季时哪些病毒在那里传播，以便将注意力集中在哪些相关毒株将在下一个北半球冬季出现，反之亦然。在冠状病毒大流行期间，Nextstrain和GISAID等组织以类似的方式跟踪了SARS-CoV-2的变异。

FL77 aims to take this much further—not only tracking which variants of a virus are where, but also predicting how they will evolve. It does this by feeding into a piece of software called Octavia data from a scaled-up version of Dr Bloom’s deep mutational scanning that runs assays on between 1m and 10m variants.

FL77的目标是更进一步，不仅追踪病毒的变种在哪里，而且预测它们将如何进化。它通过将Bloom博士深度突变扫描的放大版数据输入一个名为Octavia的软件来实现这一目标，该扫描可以对100万到1000万个变异进行分析。

Octavia’s job is to recognise patterns in the Petri-dish data—for example, which of the millions of mutations tend to lead to tighter binding, and also which lead to poorer neutralisation by antibodies—and then to extrapolate those across all possible variants of spike. This leads to predictions about which mutations will defeat antibodies, and which will spread more easily. That, says the paper, “makes it possible to define a protective antibody repertoire”, whether through vaccination or manufacturing of antibody proteins themselves. FL77 calls this an “antibody net”.

Octavia的工作是识别培养皿数据中的模式，例如，在数百万种突变中，哪一种倾向于导致更紧密的结合，又哪一种导致抗体的中和作用较差，然后推断出所有可能的突变。这就可以预测哪些突变会击败抗体，哪些突变会更容易传播。这篇论文说，无论是通过疫苗接种还是制造抗体蛋白，“这使得定义保护性抗体库成为可能”。FL77称之为“抗体网”。

Dr Bloom, who is advising FL77, and who holds patents on deep mutational scanning, says the value of these kinds of predictions has become clear with the development of messenger-RNA vaccines. These are not just quick to make, but quick to update. Their manufacturing process starts with the gene for the viral protein that the immune system is desired to attack, and ends with a strand of RNA which encodes that specific protein.

Bloom博士是FL77的顾问，并拥有深度突变扫描的专利。他说，随着信使RNA疫苗的发展，这类预测的价值已经变得清晰起来。这使得不仅制作快，更新也快。它们的制造过程从免疫系统想要攻击的病毒蛋白质的基因开始，到编码特定蛋白质的RNA链结束。

In covid-19 vaccines, the protein in question is spike. Updating vaccines to take account of predicted variants of spike is merely a matter of inserting the relevant genetic code at the start of the manufacturing process. At the least, such predictions would permit a library of candidate vaccines to be held ready, in anticipation of rapid manufacturing. At its most ambitious, FL77 imagines vaccinating people against variants of a pathogen that are not yet circulating, but are likely to.

在covid-19疫苗中，存在问题的蛋白质是刺突。更新考虑了预测到的刺突变种的疫苗，仅仅是在生产过程开始时插入相关遗传密码的问题。至少，这样的预测可以使候选疫苗库随时准备就绪，以应对快速生产。FL77最有野心的设想是给人们接种一种尚未传播但很可能传播的病原体变种。

Deep mutational scanning may have other uses, too. Gabriel Victora, an immunologist at Rockefeller University in New York, thinks predicting the evolution of a pathogen in this way will be useful not just for designing antibodies and vaccines, but also for detecting parts of the virus which change only rarely, and aiming antibodies at what would thus be reliable targets.

深度突变扫描可能还有其他用途。纽约洛克菲勒大学的免疫学家Gabriel Victora认为预测一种病原体的进化以这种方式不仅会有助于设计抗体和疫苗,而且对变化很少的病毒的检测部分也有帮助, 而且可以将抗体瞄准可靠的靶标。

![image-20210807124404262](/Users/chenyujie/Library/Application Support/typora-user-images/image-20210807124404262.png)

This, though, is difficult. The shape of any given segment of a protein depends on the rest of the molecule of which it is a part. Yet, for the immune system, the shape of its target is a crucial feature that it needs to learn in order to recognise its foe. So, though predictive approaches like FL77’s might spot segments of viral proteins which are unlikely to change, getting the immune system to look at them specifically is difficult, because expressing an isolated protein segment in a way that makes it the same shape as it is when it is part of a bigger structure is tricky.

然而，这是困难的。任何给定的蛋白质片段的形状都取决于它所在的分子的其余部分。然而，对于免疫系统来说，为了识别它的敌人，靶标的形状是它需要学习的一个关键特征。因此,虽然像FL77这样的预测方法可能能够发现不太改变的病毒蛋白质的片段, 但让免疫系统看他们具体形状是困难的, 因为将一个分离的蛋白质片段表达成与它是一个更大结构的一部分时相同的形状是很棘手的。

A more brute-force approach is simply to show the immune system all of the protein structures that are likely to emerge in future, so that it makes antibodies against the lot. Dr Victora says that immune systems have no known limit to their capacity to absorb information about pathogens. Instead, the problem with this approach may come if the system preferentially makes antibodies to some of the predicted variant proteins, but not others.

一种更强力的方法是简单地向免疫系统展示所有可能在未来出现的蛋白质结构，这样它就能制造针对这些结构的抗体。Victora博士说，免疫系统掌握病原体信息的能力的极限还未知。相反，如果免疫系统优先对一些预测的变异蛋白质产生抗体，而不是其他的蛋白质，这种方法的问题可能会出现。

Strain gauge

Seasonal flu vaccines already grapple with this problem when updating immune systems with information about the strain predicted to be circulating in the coming winter. Even after vaccination, immune systems may tend to make antibodies against the old virus instead. It is not clear whether the same thing will happen with updated messenger-RNA vaccines.

季节性流感疫苗已经在努力解决这个问题，用预测到的将在即将在冬天传播的病毒株的信息更新免疫系统。即使在接种疫苗后，免疫系统也倾向于产生对抗旧病毒的抗体。尚不清楚更新后的信使RNA疫苗是否会发生同样的情况。

No programme will ever be able to predict the evolution of the entire array of pathogens which can plausibly infect human beings. But for those already known to pose a threat, systems like Octavia may be able to see far enough into the future to offer benefits. “We don’t have to be able to predict arbitrarily,” says Dr Bloom. “We don’t need to predict mutation in a decade. Just a radius of five to six mutations from where we are now. That’s good enough.”

没有任何计划能够预测可能感染人类的所有病原体的演变。但对于那些已经知道会造成威胁的病原体来说，像Octavia这样的系统可能能够看到足够远的未来，从而提供好处。 Bloom博士说:“我们不需要武断预测，我们不需要预测10年内的突变，我们只需要在我们现有研究的范围内预测5-6个变种，这就足够了。”

FL77 is already doing this. The most radical version of the firm’s vision—vaccinating against variants and strains of pathogens that are yet to emerge—is some way off, if it ever happens. Protecting people by programming their immune systems against future pathogens, not just those already circulating, would be a fundamental shift in the meaning, purpose and ethics of vaccination. But even in the absence of that, pathogen prediction should soon serve to speed existing sorts of vaccination programmes. And every increase in the speed of vaccine development means thousands of saved lives. ■

FL77已经在这样做了。该公司的愿景最激进的一个版本是要对尚未出现的病原体变种和菌株进行疫苗接种，如果真的发生的话，这还有一段路要走。通过规划免疫系统来保护人们免受未来病原体的侵害，而不仅仅是那些已经传播的病原体，这将是疫苗接种的意义、目的以及道德的根本转变。但即使没有这一点，病原体预测也应该很快有助于加快现有各种疫苗接种计划。疫苗研发速度的每一次提高都意味着成千上万的生命得以挽救。■

PAMELA ROCCHI was born a boy, but felt from an early age she was a girl. At 16 she was kicked out of school for responding angrily when a teacher called her by her birth name. She moved to a city far from her home town of Alcorta in central Argentina and worked for two years as a prostitute. Now, aged 35, she is back in Alcorta, where she works in local government and is running to be the town’s president.

PAMELA ROCCHI 虽然生下来是个男孩，但她从小就觉得自己是个女孩。16岁时，因为老师叫她“男生名字”，她愤怒地对此进行回应，所以她被学校开除了。她搬到了远离家乡阿根廷中部城市阿尔科塔，做了两年的妓女。现在，35岁的她又回到了阿尔科塔，在当地政府工作，并正在竞选该镇的镇长。

Although trans people are often poor and occasionally the victims of hate crimes, life has lately become much easier for them. In 2012 Argentina became the first country in the world to allow individuals to change their gender on legal documents without permission from a doctor or judge—a process known as gender self-identification. Argentina’s law is “still considered the gold standard among activists around the world”, says Maria Sjödin of OutRight Action International, an advocacy group. hormone therapy 激素治疗

目前尚不清楚阿根廷是如何作出1%配额的决定的。没人知道这里有多少变性人。阿根廷中部城市Rosario自2016年以来试图雇佣25名跨性别者(每年5名)。到目前为止，它已经招募了15人。乌拉圭也有招聘配额，但未能填补这一空缺。

Data don’t lie, but they can lead scientists to opposite conclusions

数据不会撒谎，但它们会让科学家得出相反的结论

Methods and madness

![image-20210805095811016](/Users/chenyujie/Library/Application Support/typora-user-images/image-20210805095811016.png)

Analytical methods can also influence results

分析方法也会影响结果

Jul 28th 2021

ONE OF THE biggest concerns in science is bias—that scientists themselves, consciously or unconsciously, may put their thumbs on the scales and influence the outcomes of experiments. Boffins have come up with all sorts of tactics to try to eliminate it, from having their colleagues repeat their work to the “double blinding” common in clinical trials, when even the experimenters do not know which patients are receiving an experimental drug and which are getting a sugar-pill placebo.

boffin 科研工作者

placebo 安慰剂

双盲试验，是指在试验过程中，测验者与被测验者都不知道被测者所属的组别（实验组或对照组），分析者在分析资料时，通常也不知道正在分析的资料属于哪一组。旨在消除可能出现在实验者和参与者意识当中的主观偏差和个人偏好。

偏差是科学中存在的最大的担忧之一，科学家们自己可能会有意识或无意识地把他们的拇指放在天平上，影响实验的结果。研究人员想出了各种各样的方法来消除它，从让他们的同事重复他们的工作，到临床试验中常见的“双盲”试验，即使是实验人员也不知道哪些患者服用的是实验性药物，哪些患者服用的是糖丸安慰剂。

But gathering the data and running an experiment is not the only part of the process that can go awry. The methods chosen to analyse the data can also influence results. The point was dramatically demonstrated by two recent papers published in a journal called Surgery. Despite being based on the same dataset, they drew opposite conclusions about whether using a particular piece of kit during appendix-removal surgery reduced or increased the chances of infection.

go awry 出岔子

但是，收集数据和进行实验并不是这个过程中唯一可能出岔子的地方。分析数据时所选择的方法也会影响结果。最近发表在《Surgery》期刊上的两篇论文戏剧性地证明了这一点。尽管基于相同的数据集，他们得出了相反的结论，即在阑尾切除手术中使用特定的试剂盒是否减少或增加了感染的机会。

A new paper, from a large team of researchers headed by Martin Schweinsberg, a psychologist at the European School of Management and Technology, in Berlin, helps shed some light on why. Dr Schweinsberg gathered 49 different researchers by advertising his project on social media. Each was handed a copy of a dataset consisting of 3.9m words of text from nearly 8,000 comments made on Edge.org, an online forum for chatty intellectuals.

柏林欧洲管理技术学院的心理学家Martin Schweinsberg领导的一个大型研究小组最近发表了一篇论文，有助于解释其中的原因。Schweinsberg博士通过在社交媒体上宣传他的项目，召集了49名不同的研究人员。他们每人拿到了一份数据集的副本，该数据集包含来自Edge.org（一个为健谈的知识分子提供的在线论坛）上发表的近8000条评论组成的390万字的文本。

Dr Schweinsberg asked his guinea pigs to explore two seemingly straightforward hypotheses. The first was that a woman’s tendency to participate would rise as the number of other women in a conversation increased. The second was that high-status participants would talk more than their low-status counterparts. Crucially, the researchers were asked to describe their analysis in detail by posting their methods and workflows to a website called DataExplained. That allowed Dr Schweinsberg to see exactly what they were up to.

guinea pigs 试验对象

Schweinsberg博士让他的试验对象去探索两个看似简单的假设。第一个是“女性参与谈话的倾向会随着其他女性人数的增加而增加”。第二个是“地位高的参与者会比地位低的参与者说得更多”。关键是研究人员会被要求在一个名为DataExplained的网站上公布他们的方法和工作流程以详细描述他们的分析。这使得Schweinsberg博士能够清楚地看到它们在做什么。

In the end, 37 analyses were deemed sufficiently detailed to include. As it turned out, no two analysts employed exactly the same methods, and none got the same results. Some 29% of analysts reported that high-status participants were more likely to contribute. But 21% reported the opposite. (The remainder found no significant difference.) Things were less finely balanced with the first hypothesis, with 64% reporting that women do indeed participate more, if plenty of other women are present. But 21% concluded that the opposite was true.

最后有37项分析被认为足够详细可以包括在内。结果证明，每个分析师都采用不同的方法且得到不同的结果。约29%的分析师报告称，地位高的参与者更有可能做出贡献。但有21%的人持相反意见。(其余的认为没有发现显著差异)。对于第一个假设，得到的结果就不那么平衡了，有64%的人报告称，如果有很多其他女性在场，女性确实参与的更多。但21%的人认为事实恰恰相反。

The problem was not that any of the analyses were “wrong” in any objective sense. The differences arose because researchers chose different definitions of what they were studying, and applied different techniques. When it came to defining how much women spoke, for instance, some analysts plumped for the number of words in each woman’s comment. Others chose the number of characters. Still others defined it by the number of conversations that a woman participated in, irrespective of how much she actually said.

plump for 选中

问题不在于这些分析在任何客观意义上都是“错误的”。之所以出现这种差异，是因为研究人员对他们所研究的内容选择了不同的定义，并应用了不同的技术。例如，在定义女性发言的数量时，一些分析师选择了每位女性评论的单词数。其他人选择字符的数量。还有一些人将其定义为女性参与的对话数量，而不管她实际上说了多少。

Academic status, meanwhile, was defined variously by job title, the number of citations a researcher had accrued, or their “h-index”, a number beloved by university managers which attempts to combine citation counts with the importance of the journals those citations appear in. The statistical techniques chosen also had an impact, though less than the choice of definitions. Some researchers chose linear-regression analysis; others went for logistic regression or a Kendall correlation.

accrue 积累

h-index又称为h指数或h因子（h-factor），是一种评价学术成就的新方法。h代表“高引用次数”（high citations），一名科研人员的h指数是指他至多有h篇论文分别被引用了至少h次。h指数能够比较准确地反映一个人的学术成就

同时，对于“学术地位”定义也有所不同，包括工作头衔、被引用的次数，或者他们的“h指数”（大学管理者喜欢这个数字，他们试图将被引用次数与被引用的期刊的重要性结合起来）。选择的统计技术也有影响，尽管不及“学术地位”定义的选择大。一些研究者选择了线性回归分析;其他人则选择逻辑回归或肯德尔相关性系数。

Truth, in other words, can be a slippery customer, even for simple-sounding questions. What to do? One conclusion is that experimental design is critically important. Dr Schweinsberg hopes that platforms such as DataExplained can help solve the problem as well as revealing it, by allowing scientists to specify exactly how they chose to perform their analysis, allowing those decisions to be reviewed by others. It is probably not practical, he concedes, to check and re-check every result. But if many different analytical approaches point in the same direction, then scientists can be confident that their conclusion is the right one. ■

换句话说，即使是听起来很简单的问题，事实也可能是一个狡猾的顾客。要做什么吗? 一个结论是，实验设计是至关重要的。Schweinsberg博士希望，像DataExplained这样的平台可以帮助解决问题，并揭示问题，让科学家详细说明他们选择如何进行分析，让其他人审查这些决定。他承认，对每个结果进行反复检查可能并不实际。但是，如果许多不同的分析方法指向同一个方向，那么科学家就可以确信他们的结论是正确的。■

Who’s against the jab

Attitudes to covid-19 inoculation

对covid-19疫苗接种的态度

Our statistical model throws light on America’s vaccine hesitancy

我们的统计模型揭示了美国人在接种疫苗方面的犹豫不决

Jul 31st 2021

A FOURTH WAVE of covid-19 infections is sweeping across America. It is strongest in the heartland and southern states: cases per 100,000 people are highest in Louisiana, Florida and Arkansas; Missouri has the highest hospitalisations. But the rapidly spreading Delta variant threatens other places, too. Since vaccinations have stalled at around 155m adults, or 60% of the population aged 18 or over, few if any parts of the country have reached herd immunity. The new wave is likely to crash everywhere.

heartland 中心区域

第四波疫情感染正在席卷美国。在其中心区域以及南部各州的确诊率最高: 路易斯安那州、佛罗里达州和阿肯色州每10万人中的确诊率最高; 密苏里州的住院率最高。但迅速蔓延的Delta变种也威胁到了其他地方。由于疫苗接种停滞在1.55亿成年人(占18岁或18岁以上人口的60%)左右，该国几乎没有任何地区达到群体免疫。新一波（疫情）浪潮很可能会席击全球。

Identifying the causes of vaccine hesitancy can help policymakers decide where to target their efforts. The Economist has collaborated with YouGov, a pollster, to collect weekly surveys on Americans’ intent to get vaccinated for covid-19. Using the demographic profiles of some 24,000 Americans, we have built a statistical model to estimate how likely each respondent is to say they have received, or will get, their jab—and to reveal the biggest causes of hesitancy.

确定“接种疫苗犹豫”的原因可以帮助决策者决定他们要朝哪儿努力。《经济学人》与民意调查机构YouGov合作，每周收集一次关于美国人接种covid-19疫苗意愿的调查。我们利用大约24000名美国人的人口统计资料，建立了一个统计模型，用来估计每个受访者说他们已经或将要接受疫苗注射的可能性，并揭示疫苗接种犹豫的最大原因。

According to our modelling, the single greatest predictor of whether an American has been vaccinated is whether they voted for Joe Biden or Donald Trump last November. Relative to the profile for the average American—a white, 49-year-old female with some college education who earns a middle-class income, lives in a Midwestern suburb and did not vote in 2020—the impact of voting for Mr Trump is a 13 percentage-point reduction in vaccine probability. Holding everything else equal, Mr Biden’s supporters were 18 points likelier to get their jabs. Whether someone was a self-proclaimed conservative or liberal ranked second.

self-proclaimed 自称的; 自封的

根据我们的模型，美国人是否接种疫苗的唯一最大预测因素是他们去年11月是投票给了拜登或特朗普。相对于普通美国人(49岁的白人女性，受过大学教育，收入中等，住在中西部郊区，在2020年没有投票)，投票给特朗普的影响使接种疫苗的可能性降低了13个百分点。在其他条件不变的情况下，拜登的支持者接种疫苗的可能性高出18个百分点。自封为保守派或自由派的人排在第二位。

![image-20210804095324428](/Users/chenyujie/Library/Application Support/typora-user-images/image-20210804095324428.png)

But many other factors also matter. African-Americans were disproportionately less likely than other racial and ethnic groups to receive their shots; Hispanics and Hindus were more likely to do so. Geographic factors mostly cut along expected lines: people in blue-state cities were likelier to get a vaccine, those in rural, redder regions were disproportionately hesitant.

disproportionately 不成比例地

blue-state 蓝色州 [传统上支持民主党的州]

red-state 红色州 [传统上支持共和党的州]

但许多其他因素也很重要。与其他种族和民族相比，非裔美国人接受注射的可能性要低得多; 西班牙人和印度人更有可能接种。地理因素大多与预期一致:蓝色州城市的人更有可能接种疫苗，而农村和红色州地区的人则更多的是对接种疫苗犹豫不决。

Prominent conservative television hosts, such as Fox News’s Sean Hannity, made a spectacle of endorsing the covid-19 vaccine last week. Yet according to YouGov’s most recent poll, conducted on July 24th-27th, the share of Republican adults who say they will not get the jab has held steady at 30%. The phase of the pandemic where Americans would listen to such messaging might well be over. After all, they have been seeing adverts about jabs for months. Instead of focusing on partisans, public-health policy could be designed to increase access for poor and rural Americans, and focus on outreach and information-dissemination for the uneducated.

spectacle 引人注目的景象

make a spectacle of oneself 出洋相

outreach 超过; 伸出

dissemination 传播

著名的保守派电视主持人，如福克斯新闻的Sean Hannity，上周大秀了一场支持新冠疫苗的表演。然而，根据YouGov在7月24日至27日进行的最新民调表示，不会接受疫苗注射的成年共和党人的比例稳定在30%。美国人听取此类信息的疫情阶段很可能已经结束。毕竟，他们已经看到了几个月的疫苗广告。公共卫生政策不应着眼于党派，而应着眼于增加穷人和农村美国人的接种疫苗的机会，并将重点放在为未受教育的人提供外联服务和信息传播上。
