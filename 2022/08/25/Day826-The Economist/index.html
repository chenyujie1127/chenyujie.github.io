Fiddling with constitutions is usually a risky distraction

Most of the time they should be left alone

大多数时候，他们应该被“单独留下”

Aug 25th 2022

A man walks into a library and asks for a copy of the French constitution. “I’m sorry,” replies the librarian. “We don’t stock periodicals.” By one count, France has had 16 constitutions since its first in 1791. Its current version, which dates from 1958, has been amended dozens of times. But it is a beacon of stability compared with many other places. On September 4th Chileans will vote on a new charter. If it is approved, which it should not be, it would replace a document that has been amended 60 times since 1980. The rest of Latin America is equally keen on change. One calculation in 2009 put the average lifespan of a Latin American constitution at 16.5 years, compared with 77 in western Europe. A string of changes in the intervening years will have done little to close that discrepancy.

一名男子走进图书馆，想要一本法国宪法。图书管理员回复道，“对不起，我们不存期刊。” 据统计，法国自1791年制定宪法以来已有16部宪法。目前的版本始于1958年，已经修改了几十次。但与许多其他地方相比，它是一个稳定的灯塔。9月4日，智利人将对新宪法进行投票。如果它获得通过(这是不应该的)，它将取代自1980年以来修改了60次的文件。拉丁美洲的其他国家也同样渴望变革。2009年的一项计算显示，拉丁美洲国家宪法的平均寿命为16.5年，而西欧国家为77年。在此期间发生的一系列变化将无助于消除这种差异。

In Africa around a dozen countries have amended their constitutions since 2015, mostly to boost or prolong the powers of incumbent leaders. In July Tunisians voted to gut their country’s democratic charter, which dates only from 2014. Kais Saied, Tunisia’s president, can now rule by decree.

自2015年以来，非洲大约有12个国家修改了宪法，其主要目的是加强或延长现任领导人的权力。7月，突尼斯人全民公投废除了2014年才颁布的国家民主宪章。突尼斯总统凯斯•赛义德现在可以通过法令进行统治。

Constitutional change is not always bad. Many of the alterations to France’s constitution have been to conform with rules of the European Union. Ireland’s repeal in 2018 of its abortion ban was both democratic and wise. America’s introduction of presidential term limits in 1947 was a sensible tweak. Even some of Chile’s 60 amendments made sense.

修改宪法并不总是坏事。法国宪法的许多修改都是为了与欧盟的规则保持一致。爱尔兰在2018年废除堕胎禁令既民主又明智。美国在1947年引入总统任期限制是一个明智的调整。甚至智利的60次的修正中的有一些也是有意义的。

But most fiddling is unhelpful. This week we examine two baleful trends. **One is the tendency of autocrats**—like Vladimir Putin in Russia, Recep Tayyip Erdogan in Turkey and Mr Saied in Tunisia—to amend charters to grant themselves more power. The second is the habit of Utopians to pack constitutions with growing numbers of highly specific “social rights”. Both should be resisted.

但大多数的“摆弄”都是无益的。本周，我们将研究两种有害的趋势。一个是独裁者的倾向——比如俄罗斯的弗拉基米尔·普京，土耳其的雷杰普·塔伊普·埃尔多安以及突尼斯的赛义德——通过修改宪章以赋予自己更多的权力。第二，乌托邦主义者习惯于在宪法中加入越来越多具体的“社会权利”。这两者都应该被抵制。

The risks of making strongmen stronger are obvious. Constitutions embody the idea of rule by law, not rule by a fallible individual. It is dangerous to entrust any leader with untrammelled power, which is why leaders who seek it usually disguise their aim. Mr Putin, when asking voters in 2020 to rubber-stamp his increased powers and to extend his reign until 2036, bundled them with others to make pensions inflation-proof and bolster the minimum wage. Russians voted yes; it was a rotten bargain.

让强人变得更强大的风险显而易见。宪法体现的是法治的理念，而不是由犯错的个人统治。把不受约束的权力托付给任何领导人是危险的，这就是为什么寻求不受限制的权力的领导人通常会掩饰他们的目标。普京在2020年要求选民对他增加的权力进行批准，并将其统治延长到2036年，他将这些权利与其他权利捆绑在一起，使养老金能够抵御通胀，并提高最低工资。俄罗斯人投了赞成票；这是一笔糟糕的交易。

A constitution should establish basic rights, such as freedom of speech and fair trials. It should lay the ground rules for how a state works, such as: is it a presidential system or a parliamentary one? Then it should stop. The ever-shifting details of public policy—pensions, minimum wages and so forth—should be left to the government of the day. Policies must adjust to changing circumstances; budgets must add up. That is harder if lots of big items are set in stone.

宪法应该确立基本权利，如言论自由和公正审判。它应该为一个国家的运作制定基本规则，比如：是总统制还是议会制？然后它应该停下来。公共政策中不断变化的细节，如养老金，最低工资等等，应该留给今天的政府。政策必须适应不断变化的环境；预算必须增加。如果许多重大项目都是固定不变的，这就更难了。

Packing a constitution with inessential things weakens respect for it. If almost every law is the highest in the land, then none is. And the more “rights” are included, the greater the likelihood of a clash. Ecuador’s constitution of 2008, for example, protects both the rights of landowners to oppose resource extraction, and the right of the government to develop the economy. When those conflicted, indigenous landowners’ rights were trampled. Chile’s new draft charter may not be a naked power grab like Russia’s, but it is a confusing mess of micromanagement. It would require the state to enforce a right to sports, for children to be taught empathy for animals and for the joys of Chilean cooking to be promoted. It proclaims a right to housing, but also bans property speculation, making it hard for anyone except the government to invest in building homes.

在宪法中加入不必要的东西会削弱对它的尊重。如果几乎每条法律都是最高的，那么没有一条是最高的。“权利”包含的越多，冲突的可能性就越大。例如，厄瓜多尔2008年的宪法既保护土地所有者反对资源开采的权利，也保护政府发展经济的权利。当两者发生冲突时，当地的地主的权利遭到践踏。智利新起草的宪章可能不像俄罗斯那样赤裸裸地攫取权力，但却是微观管理的混乱局面。它将要求国家加强体育运动权利，教育孩子们对动物的同情，并推广智利烹饪的乐趣。它宣布拥有住房的权利，但也禁止房地产投机，这使得除了政府以外的任何人都很难投资建设住房。

Drafters of new charters and the voters who are consulted on them should bear two principles in mind. The first is simplicity. Constitutions are best when the rules are clear and short. The second is caution. The more a constitution is changed, the more prone it is to change in the future; once again, this tends to undermine its authority.

新宪章的起草者和征求意见的选民应该牢记两个原则。首先是简单。简明扼要的规则是最好的宪法。第二是谨慎。宪法修改得越多，将来就越容易改变；这会又一次削弱了它的权威。

No real-life constitution is perfect. America’s is admirably short but too hard to update, which is one reason the Supreme Court keeps reinterpreting it. Voters in many countries are unhappy with their governments, so the idea that a fresh constitution might reshape their societies for the better is appealing. But it is usually an illusion. A constitution is a starting-point; governments must still navigate the daily complexities of governing. A flawed or dated constitution can be incrementally improved, as Chile’s old one should be. But unless a society has undergone a revolution, such as the end of [] or apartheid, it is rash to rewrite its basic law from scratch. ■

现实生活中没有完美的宪法。美国的宪法非常简短，但是很难更新，这也是最高法院不断重新解释宪法的原因之一。许多国家的选民都不满意他们的政府，因此一部新的宪法可能会使他们的社会变得更好的想法很有吸引力。但这通常是一种错觉。宪法是一个起点；政府仍必须应对日常复杂的治理问题。一个有缺陷或过时的宪法可以逐步改进，就像智利的旧宪法那样。但是，除非一个社会经历过革命，比如[]或种族隔离的终结，否则从头重写其基本法是轻率的。■
