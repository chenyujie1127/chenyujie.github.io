Stay cool on Kherson

对赫尔松保持冷静

The War in Ukraine

乌克兰战争

![image-20220818094849844](/Users/chenyujie/Library/Application Support/typora-user-images/image-20220818094849844.png)

Why Ukraine’s army should not rush to recapture the whole province

为什么乌克兰军队不应该急于夺回整个市

Aug 17th 2022

In the years to come, nato armed forces will queue at the door of Ukraine’s general staff to learn from the commanders who halted the Russian army’s march on Kyiv and Odessa and inflicted more than 60,000 casualties in six months of war. Yet a huge test still lies ahead: Ukraine wants its territory back.

在未来的几年里，北约武装部队将在乌克兰总参谋部门前排队，向那些阻止俄罗斯军队向基辅和敖德萨进军、在六个月的战争中造成6万多人伤亡的指挥官们学习。不过，摆在面前的仍然是一个巨大的考验：乌克兰想夺回自己的领土。

There is growing chatter that the Ukrainian army is preparing a counter-offensive in and around the city of Kherson in the south. It has damaged or severed most of the bridges that connect the city to other Russian-held territory, isolating Russian forces on the western side of the Dnieper river. It is pounding Russian ammunition dumps and command posts with rockets. Ukrainian generals are buoyant; one says the city could be liberated by the end of the year.

有越来越多的传言称，乌克兰军队正准备在南部城市赫尔松及其周围发动反攻。它破坏或切断了该市与其他俄罗斯控制地区之间的大部分桥梁，这使在第聂伯河西岸的俄罗斯军队陷入孤立状态。它正在用火箭袭击俄罗斯的弹药库和指挥所。乌克兰的将军们很乐观；有人说，这座城市可能在今年年底前就能解放。

Such triumphal talk masks a more complex reality. Russia has been pouring troops into Kherson and digging in. Urban warfare is slow and costly for the troops waging it and the civilians in the way. Russia has captured Mariupol, Severodonetsk and other Ukrainian cities because it had little compunction about destroying them in the process. Ukraine would rather Kherson remained intact. An effective Ukrainian counter-offensive is more likely to resemble a protracted campaign of siege and attrition than blitzkrieg on the steppe.

这种胜利的言论掩盖了一个更复杂的现实。俄罗斯一直在向赫尔松市投入大量兵力，并不断挖掩体。对于发动城市战争的军队和阻碍城市战争的平民而言，城市战争是缓慢且昂贵的。俄罗斯已经占领了马里乌波尔、北顿涅茨克以及其他乌克兰城市，这是因为它在摧毁这些城市的过程中几乎没有感到内疚。乌克兰则想要赫尔松完好无损。有效的乌克兰反攻更像是一场旷日持久的包围战和消耗战，而不是草原上的闪电战。

In weighing an attack, Volodymyr Zelensky, Ukraine’s president, must strike a balance. His Western allies, especially in Europe, are having to cope with spiralling gas prices and looming shortages. He wants to show them clear progress. Some of his own compatriots are also getting restless. And he is keen to disrupt the sham referendum Russia is planning to justify the annexation of Kherson. “We will liberate everything,” he promised this month. But the truth is that Ukraine needs more time to train soldiers, acquire weapons and soften up Russian defences.

乌克兰总统弗拉基米尔•泽连斯基在发动进攻时必须保持平衡。他的西方盟友，尤其是欧洲盟友，正不得不应对不断上涨的油价和迫在眉睫的短缺问题。他想向他们展示明确的进展。他的一些同胞也开始焦躁不安。他还热衷于破坏俄罗斯计划举行的虚假公投，该公投是为了证明吞并科尔森的正当性。他本月承诺，“我们将解放一切”。但事实是，乌克兰需要更多的时间来训练士兵、获取武器并削弱俄罗斯的防御力量。

Right now, the war looks like a stalemate. Russian advances in the eastern Donbas region have slowed to a crawl. Ukraine’s probing north of Kherson has also been fruitless in recent weeks. Both sides are still rebuilding their armed forces, battered from six months of war. Russia will use the coming months to stiffen its defences, resupply its badly mauled battalions and replenish them with well-paid volunteers.

目前，这场战争看起来陷入了僵局。俄罗斯在东部顿巴斯地区的进展已经减慢。最近几周，乌克兰对赫尔松北部的探查也没有取得结果。六个月的战争让双方的武装力量都受到重创，目前双方仍在重建其武装力量。俄罗斯将利用未来几个月的时间来加强防御，为遭受重创的部队补充物资，并向他们提供高薪的志愿兵。

On current trends, however, it is Ukraine that will be in a stronger position to break the stalemate early next year. It has manpower shortages of its own, but Ukrainian recruits are undergoing basic infantry training in Britain. The 10,000 troops produced by that effort will help Ukraine establish the new brigades it needs for an offensive, although it will need the scheme to continue and grow after that. More arms are also arriving every week. On August 8th America announced its biggest shipment yet, including new ammunition for the himarsrocket launchers that have tormented Russian forces.

然而，按照目前的趋势，乌克兰将处于更有利的地位，在明年初打破僵局。乌克兰自身也存在人力短缺问题，但乌克兰新兵正在英国接受基本的步兵训练。这一努力所产生的1万名士兵将帮助乌克兰建立发动进攻所需的新部队，不过，乌克兰还需要在进攻之后继续该计划，并壮大其规模。每周都有更多的武器抵达。8月8日，美国宣布了其迄今为止最大的一次运输，其中包括用于折磨俄罗斯军队的高机动性火箭发射器的新弹药。

Ukraine’s partners must be patient; wars of attrition do not turn on a sixpence. Western governments may be nervous about maintaining public support for Ukraine as heating bills soar in the winter months. Vladimir Putin, Russia’s president, is no doubt assuming that fatigue will set in. But capturing Kherson is not the only way to stave it off.

乌克兰的伙伴们必须要有耐心；消耗战不会以“六便士”开始。随着冬季取暖费飙升，西方政府可能对维持公众对乌克兰的支持感到紧张。毫无疑问，俄罗斯总统弗拉基米尔•普认为这种疲劳将开始显现。不过收回赫尔松并不是唯一暂时消除“疲劳”的办法。

Spectacular strikes like the ones Ukraine has been conducting in Crimea this month, well beyond the range of its known weapons, show clearly that it is still outfoxing its adversary and maintaining the initiative. The latest attack, on August 16th, destroyed an ammunition depot and an electricity substation 200km behind the front lines. The methodical destruction of Russian military power will eventually pave the way for the territorial gains Ukraine craves. ■

乌克兰本月在克里米亚进行的大规模轰击，远远超出了其已知武器的射程，这清楚地表明，它仍在智胜对手，保持主动权。最近的一次袭击发生在8月16日，该次袭击摧毁了前线后方200公里处的一个弹药库和一个变电站。有条不紊地摧毁俄罗斯的军事力量，最终将为乌克兰渴求的夺回领土铺路。■
