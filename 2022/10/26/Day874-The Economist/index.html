Joe Biden’s protectionism is costly for America and the world

乔·拜登的保护主义让美国和世界付出了高昂的代价

Oct 27th 2022

America’s midterm elections are less than a fortnight away. When voters go to the polls on November 8th, surging prices will be uppermost in their minds. Annual inflation is running at just over 8%, nearly a 40-year high and the hottest in many voters’ lifetimes. For decades, inflation was quiescent enough that most Americans could ignore it. Now the cost of the weekly shop is dominating voters’ daily lives—and fuelling their ire.

距离美国中期选举还有不到两周的时间。当选民在11月8日去投票时，飞涨的物价将是他们最关心的问题。年通货膨胀率略高于8%，接近40年来的最高点，也是许多选民一生中最高的一次。几十年来，通货膨胀一直很平静，以至于大多数美国人都可以忽略它。现在，每周购物的成本已经主导了选民的日常生活，并加剧了他们的愤怒。

Joe Biden’s colossal economic stimulus in early 2021 first set inflation on its feverish trajectory, before Russia’s invasion of Ukraine sent prices spiralling further. Voters may soon punish him for it, among other perceived excesses. According to the Pew Research Centre, a polling firm, four-fifths of Americans say the economy will be “very important” to their voting decisions; three-quarters are “very concerned” about the price of food and consumer goods.

乔·拜登在2021年初推出的大规模经济刺激计划首次将通胀推上了狂热的轨道，随后俄罗斯入侵乌克兰，使物价进一步飙升。选民可能很快就会在他其他可以察觉到的过分行为中因为这一点而惩罚他。根据民意调查公司皮尤研究中心的调查，五分之四的美国人认为经济对他们的投票决定“非常重要”; 四分之三的人“非常关心”食品和消费品的价格。

Yet the consequences of nearly two years of Bidenomics go well beyond stoking inflation. Bidenomics takes on two of the biggest long-term threats facing America: the rise of an increasingly autocratic [], and the looming dangers of climate change. In the past year Mr Biden has signed three landmark bills, on infrastructure, semiconductors and the environment, which together contain plans to spend $1.7trn. With his executive actions, these amount to full-blown industrial policy. The result is unlike anything seen since Congress threw its weight behind America’s car- and chipmakers in the 1980s. The government will dole out $180bn in subsidies and tax credits to local firms over the next five years. At 0.7% of gdp, that is more than supposedly dirigiste France.

This phase of Bidenomics is praise worthy in its aims. But its protectionism makes America less likely to achieve them. It is as if, having correctly identified his destination, Mr Biden has tied his legs together before setting off. The costs of this hobbled approach will be borne both by America and its allies.

这一阶段的拜登经济的目标是值得称赞的。但它的保护主义使美国不太可能实现这些目标。这就好像在正确确定了目的地之后，拜登在出发前把双腿绑在了一起。这种“捆缚双腿”的做法的代价将由美国及其盟友共同承担。

America rightly wants to maintain its technological lead in advanced chipmaking over []. Semiconductors are growing in military significance, and could become crucial as artificial intelligence reshapes warfare. Another sensible worry relates to America’s dependence on [] for crucial green-energy kit such as batteries. At home Mr Biden wants to spruce up rusting infrastructure and reduce carbon emissions. He has pursued these goals with subsidies and “Buy America” rules, and Democrats might hope that boasting of their support for domestic manufacturing and middle-class jobs will pick up votes on the campaign trail.

美国有理由希望保持其在先进芯片制造方面技术领先地位，超越[]。半导体在军事上的重要性日益增长，随着人工智能重塑战争，它可能变得至关重要。另一个合理的担忧与美国在电池等关键绿色能源设备上对[]的依赖有关。[少一句] 在国内，拜登希望改善锈迹斑斑的基础设施，减少碳排放。他通过补贴和“购买美国货”规则来实现这些目标，民主党人可能希望吹嘘他们对国内制造业和中产阶级就业的支持会在竞选过程中获得选票。

The trouble is that protectionism is a poison pill that weakens the whole enterprise. It hurts friend and foe alike, sapping America’s alliances of good faith and encouraging others to respond in kind. The European Union and South Korea complain that buyers of their electric vehicles will not benefit from America’s new subsidies, which will favour cars assembled in North America, and which may breach the World Trade Organisation’s rules. The eu is readying its own chip subsidies, which will compete wastefully with America’s. America’s attempts to woo Asian countries away from []. 问题是保护主义是一颗毒丸，会削弱整个企业。它伤害了朋友和敌人，削弱了美国的善意联盟，并鼓励其他国家以牙还牙。欧盟和韩国抱怨说，他们的电动汽车买家不会从美国的新补贴中受益，这一补贴将有利于在北美组装的汽车，而且可能违反世界贸易组织的规则。欧盟也准备好了自己的芯片补贴，与美国竞争，这一举动损耗相当大。[少了两句半..]。

At home, too, protectionism makes the aims of Bidenomics harder to achieve. Subsidising electric cars assembled in North America will make them more expensive and lower-quality, dulling the incentive to go green. The new laws often require projects to pay government-mandated “prevailing wages”, or to use American iron and steel. The Department of Commerce’s plan for doling out chip subsidies pledges to take on an array of social problems, from giving jobs to the poor to helping businesses owned by women. The red tape will raise costs to consumers and taxpayers still further.

美国现行工资法令（Prevailing Wage Laws）规定，雇主必须根据工作类型和项目地点，向所有受雇于公共工程项目（包括所有联邦工程项目及32个州的州级工程项目）的雇员按劳工部确定的现行工资标准支付薪资。如果雇主不遵守相应规定，可能会面临数千美元的罚款，严重者甚至还会面临刑事处罚。

在国内，保护主义也使得拜登经济的目标更难实现。对在北美组装的电动汽车进行补贴将使它们变得更贵、质量更低，从而削弱人们追求环保的动力。新法律通常要求项目支付政府规定的“现行工资”，或者使用美国的钢铁。美国商务部发放芯片补贴的计划承诺将解决一系列社会问题，从为穷人提供就业机会到帮助女性所拥有的企业。繁文缛节将进一步增加消费者和纳税人的成本。

Rather than putting up barriers, America should reap the benefits of openness. More high-skilled immigration would boost both its green-tech and chipmaking industries. Easing permitting rules might do more than subsidies to encourage the building of green infrastructure. Tax deductions can favour investment. Free trade in crucial technologies with allies would make it cheaper for the democratic world both to decarbonise and to avoid excessive dependence on autocracies. And if the intent is to hinder [] chipmaking, America can do so without bungs to industry, as its recent ban on selling advanced chip technology to [] shows.

美国应该从开放中获益，而不是设置障碍。更多的高技能移民将促进绿色科技和芯片制造业的发展。放宽许可规定可能比补贴更能鼓励绿色基础设施的建设。税收减免有利于投资。与盟国在关键技术方面的自由贸易，将使民主世界以更低的成本去碳，并避免对独裁政权的过度依赖。如果意图是阻碍[]芯片制造，美国可以做到这一点，而不会对工业造成伤害，正如其最近禁止向[]出售先进芯片技术所显示的那样。

Mr Biden might reply that he has to work within the boundaries of the politically possible. At least his protectionist instincts are being channelled towards some productive ends: Democrats got nowhere trying to impose a federal cap and trade system to curb carbon emissions more efficiently. And both the infrastructure and semiconductor bills have attracted bipartisan support. Promising a manufacturing renaissance might help Democrats win seats that once looked hopeless.

拜登可能会回答说，他必须在政治上可行的范围内行事。至少他的保护主义本能正被引导到一些富有成效的目标上: 民主党人试图强加一个联邦总量管制和交易体系，以更有效地遏制碳排放，但毫无进展。基础设施和半导体法案都得到了两党支持。承诺制造业复兴可能会帮助民主党赢得曾经看起来无望的席位。
