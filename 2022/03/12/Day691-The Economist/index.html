Confronting Russia shows the tension between free trade and freedom

Liberal governments need to find a new path that combines openness and security

自由派政府需要找到一条结合开放和安全的新道路

Mar 19th 2022

THE INVASION of Ukraine is the third big blow to globalisation in a decade. First came President Donald Trump’s trade wars. Next was a pandemic in which cross-border flows of capital, goods and people almost stopped. Now armed conflict in Europe’s breadbasket, besieged Black Sea ports and sanctions on Russia have triggered a supply shock that is ripping through the world economy. Wheat prices have risen by 40%, Europeans may face gas shortages later this year, and there is a squeeze on nickel, used in batteries, including for electric cars. Around the world many firms and consumers are grappling with supply chains that have proved too fragile to depend on—yet again.

入侵乌克兰是十年来全球化遭受的第三次重大打击。第一次是特朗普总统发起的贸易战。第二次是一场新冠大流行，届时资本、货物和人员的跨境流动几乎停止。现在欧洲产粮区的武装冲突、被围住的黑海港口以及对俄罗斯的制裁引发了一场供应冲击, 其正席卷世界经济。小麦价格已经上涨了40%，今年晚些时候，欧洲可能会面临天然气短缺，镍(用于电池，包括电动汽车)也会出现短缺。在世界范围内，许多公司和消费者都在努力解决供应链问题，因为事实证明，他们的供应链过于脆弱而无法再次依赖。

If you look beyond the chaos, Vladimir Putin’s warmongering also raises a question about globalisation that is uncomfortable for free-traders such as The Economist. Is it prudent for open societies to conduct normal economic relations with autocratic ones, such as Russia and XX, that abuse human rights, endanger security and grow more threatening the richer they get? In principle, the answer is simple: democracies should seek to maximise trade without compromising national security. In practice, that is a hard line to draw. Russia’s war shows that a surgical redesign of supply chains is needed to prevent autocratic countries from bullying liberal ones. What the world does not need is a dangerous lurch towards self-sufficiency.

撇开混乱不谈，普京的好战言论还提出了一个关于全球化的问题，这让《经济学人》等自由贸易主义者感到不安。开放的社会与专制国家(如俄罗斯和XX)进行正常的经济关系，这是一种谨慎的做法吗? 这些专制国家践踏人权，危及安全，并随着富裕程度的提高而变得更具威胁性。原则上，答案很简单: 民主国家应在不损害国家安全的情况下寻求贸易最大化。实际上，这是一条很难划定的界限。俄罗斯战争表明，为了防止专制国家欺负自由国家，需要对供应链进行“外科手术式“的重新设计。世界不需要的是朝着自给自足的方向危险地蹒跚而行。

For most of the past few decades, it has been clear how to trade with the enemy. In the cold war the West and the totalitarian Soviet bloc conducted trade in energy and grain but had a low overall level of interlinking. After the Berlin Wall fell, it was widely assumed that free trade and freedom would conquer the world together, reinforcing each other. And for a while they did. In the 1990s the share of countries with democratic rule rose as tariffs fell and more container ships crossed the oceans. Russians got their first taste of Big Macs and the ballot box within an 18-month spell. Bill Clinton welcomed XX’s entry into the global trading system in 2000, predicting that it would have “a profound impact on human rights and political liberty” there.

在过去几十年的大部分时间里，如何与敌人进行贸易已经很清楚了。冷战时期，西方和苏联集团进行了能源和粮食贸易，但整体联系水平较低。柏林墙倒塌后，人们普遍认为自由贸易和自由将共同征服世界，相互促进。在一段时间内，他们确实做到了。上世纪90年代，随着关税的降低和更多集装箱船的越洋，实行民主统治的国家比例上升。在18个月的时间里，俄罗斯人第一次尝到了巨无霸的滋味，也第一次拥有了投票权。2000年，比尔•克林顿对XX加入全球贸易体系表示欢迎，并预测这将对那里的“人权和政治自由产生深远影响”。

But in the past decade and a half liberty has retreated, with the share of people living in democracies falling below 50%. In many autocratic places, including XX and the Middle East, political reform appears unlikely. The result is a globalised economy in which autocracies account for 31% of GDP, or 14% excluding XX. Unlike the USSR, these autocracies are economically intertwined with liberal societies. A third of democracies’ goods imports are from them, and a third of multinational investment in autocracies is from democracies. Open societies trade over $15bn a day with closed ones, buying XX-made PCs and Saudi oil, and selling Bulgari and Boeings.

但在过去15年里，自由已经“退却”，生活在民主国家的人口比例降至50%以下。在包括XX和中东在内的许多专制国家，政治改革似乎不太可能。其结果是经济全球化，专制统治占GDP的31%，不包括XX则占14%。与苏联不同，这些专制国家在经济上与自由社会交织在一起。民主国家三分之一的商品进口来自专制国家，而对专制国家的跨国投资有三分之一来自民主国家。开放社会与封闭社会每天的贸易额超过150亿美元，购买XX制造的个人电脑和沙特石油，出售宝格丽和波音。

Russia’s invasion has shown the West the perils of trading with adversaries. One concern is moral. All those deals for Urals crude and Black Sea wheat bankrolled Mr Putin’s repression and his rapidly increasing military spending. Another is security, with Europe addicted to Russian gas and many industries reliant on inputs including fertilisers and metals. Such dependency may make autocracies stronger, weaken democracies’ resolve and expose them to retaliation in a war. No country embodies this Faustian pact more than gas-dependent Germany.

俄罗斯的入侵向西方展示了与对手进行贸易的危险。一个担忧是道德问题。所有这些乌拉尔原油和黑海小麦交易，为普京的镇压和迅速增加的军事开支提供了资金。另一个担忧是安全问题，欧洲依赖俄罗斯的天然气，许多行业依赖化肥和金属等输入。这种依赖可能会让专制政权更加强大，削弱民主国家的决心，并让它们在战争中遭到报复。没有哪个国家比依赖天然气的德国更能体现这一浮士德式契约。

This tension between the logic of free trade and support for political liberalism will create deeper fissures. Already the world has faced years of what The Economist has called slowbalisation, with trade and capital flows falling relative to GDP. Some autocracies may now seek to decouple further from the West. XX views the collapse of Russia’s fortress economy in the face of Western sanctions as a botched experiment from which to learn before it considers going to war over XX. Saudi Arabia is cosying up to XX. The world’s autocracies have too little in common to form a cohesive economic bloc, but they are united in their desire to reduce the influence the West has over them, in areas from tech to currency reserves.

自由贸易的逻辑与对政治自由主义的支持之间的这种紧张关系，将造成更深的裂痕。多年来，世界已经面临着《经济学人》所称的“慢球化”，即贸易和资本流动相对于GDP下降。一些专制国家现在可能寻求与西方进一步脱钩。XX将俄罗斯堡垒经济在面对西方的制裁时的崩溃视为一次拙劣的尝试，在考虑对XX发动战争之前，其可以从中吸取教训。沙特阿拉伯正在讨好XX。世界上的专制国家没有太多共同之处，以至于无法形成一个有凝聚力的经济集团，但他们都希望在从科技到外汇储备等领域减少西方对他们的影响。

The temptation in the West, meanwhile, is to pivot towards a more limited kind of trade with military allies, or even to outright self-reliance. Consider President Joe Biden’s recent state-of-the-union address which included a promise that “everything from the deck of an aircraft-carrier to the steel on highway guardrails is made in America from beginning to end. All of it.”

与此同时，西方面临的诱惑是转向与军事盟友进行更有限的贸易，甚至转向完全自力更生。美国总统乔•拜登在最近的国情咨文演讲中承诺:“从航空母舰的甲板到高速公路护栏上的钢铁，从头到尾都是美国制造。所有都是。”

A retreat by the West to cold-war spheres of influence or self-reliance would be a mistake. The costs would be vast. Roughly $3trn of investment would be written off for less efficient production that fuels inflation and hurts living standards. It would be morally dubious: globalisation has helped over a billion people raise themselves from poverty, and trade and information links with the middle classes in autocracies sustain the cause of liberalism. It would not even boost democracies’ security. Supply chains get stronger through diversification, not concentration. And by walling themselves off, rich democracies would alienate countries that do not want to pick sides between the West, Russia and XX—countries that account for a fifth of world GDP and two-thirds of its people.

西方退回到冷战的势力范围或自力更生将是一个错误。这个代价将是巨大的。大约3万亿美元的投资将被冲销，用于生产效率较低的产品，这些产品加剧了通胀，损害了生活水平。这在道德上是不确定的:全球化已经帮助超过10亿人摆脱了贫困，与专制国家的中产阶级的贸易和信息联系支撑着自由主义的事业。它甚至不会促进民主国家的安全。供应链是通过多元化而不是集中而变得更强大。发达的民主国家通过降自己封闭起来，会疏远那些不想在西方、俄罗斯和XX之间选边站的国家，这些国家的GDP占世界的五分之一，人口占世界的三分之二。

How then should globalisation be reconfigured? In war, severing economic relations makes sense. In peace the goal should be to limit exports of only the most sensitive technologies to illiberal regimes. When autocracies have the power to intimidate, as Russia has with gas, the aim should not be national self-sufficiency, but rather to require firms to diversify their suppliers, in turn stimulating investment in new sources of supply from energy to electronics. These choke-points make up about a tenth of global trade, based on the export earnings of authoritarian powers from goods where they have a leading market share of over 10% and where it is hard to find substitutes.

那么，应该如何重新配置全球化? 在战争中，切断经济关系是有道理的。和平时期的目标应该是限制向反自由政权出口最敏感的技术。当专制国家有能力进行恐吓时，就像俄罗斯在天然气问题上所做的那样，目标不应该是国家自给自足，而是要求企业多元化供应商，进而刺激从能源到电子产品等新供应来源的投资。这些瓶颈约占全球贸易的十分之一，这是基于专制国家的出口收入，这些国家的商品拥有超过10%的领先市场份额，而且很难找到替代品。

Interdependence day

Mr Putin has given a harsh lesson that in these areas democracies must change their posture. The war is a tragedy, but it is also a moment of clarity. The vision of the 1990s, that free trade and freedom would go hand in hand, has fractured. Liberal governments need to find a new path that combines openness and security, and prevents the dream of globalisation turning sour. ■

普京给了我们一个严酷的教训: 在这些领域，民主国家必须改变姿态。这场战争是一场悲剧，但它也是一个清晰的时刻。上世纪90年代自由贸易和自由将携手并进的愿景已经破碎。自由派政府需要找到一条结合开放和安全的新道路，并防止全球化的梦想破灭。■
