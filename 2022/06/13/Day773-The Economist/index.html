The Fed and the ECB turn on a dime

美联储和欧洲央行变化无常

The week central banks changed course

本周各国央行改变了方向

![image-20220621103039101](/Users/chenyujie/Library/Application Support/typora-user-images/image-20220621103039101.png)

The contradictions in policymakers’ goals are being exposed

政策制定者目标中的矛盾正在暴露出来

Jun 15th 2022

The multi-trillion dollar world of central banking has just turned on a dime. A week ago the Fed was expected shortly to raise interest rates by half a percentage point. The European Central Bank (ecb) had just announced an end to its bond purchases. The Bank of Japan was unusual in its commitment to maintaining monetary stimulus despite growing disquiet about the plummeting yen. By the time our weekly edition was published, however, the Fed had raised rates by three-quarters of a point; the ecb had held an emergency meeting and said it was working on a new tool to intervene in bond markets; and the Bank of Japan, ahead of its meeting on June 16th-17th, had joined the government in lamenting the yen’s weakness.

在6月17日之前举行的货币政策会议上，日本央行决定维持目前的大规模货币宽松措施，即保持短期利率为负值，并购买政府债券以保持长期利率在0%左右。日本央行将坚持不懈地继续实施货币宽松政策，以支持仍在从新冠的影响中恢复的经济。同时，日本央行加强了被称为“限额操作”的措施，即以特定的收益率购买无限数量的日本国债，并重申了其抑制长期利率上升的立场。

规模达数万亿美元的央行业务发生了重大变化。一周前，市场预计美联储将很快加息50个基点。欧洲央行刚刚宣布结束购债计划。日本央行在对日元暴跌日益感到不安的情况下，仍承诺维持货币刺激，这是不同寻常的。然而，到我们的周刊出版时，美联储已经将利率上调了75个基点；欧洲央行已召开紧急会议，表示正在创造一种干预债券市场的新工具；日本央行在6月16日至17日的会议之前与政府一道哀叹日元的疲软。

Turmoil in policy has coincided with tumult in markets. Tighter monetary policy has been crushing American stocks, which are down by almost 20% from their January peak. Cryptocurrencies are crashing, eviscerating investors who were late to the party. The spread between ten-year yields on safe German debt and risky Italian debt reached over 2.4 percentage points the day before the ecb’s announcement. Bond yields have risen and traders have punished currencies, like the yen and sterling, issued by central banks that are unlikely to keep pace with the Fed.

政策的动荡与市场的混乱同时发生。紧缩的货币政策一直在打压美国股市，美国股市从一月份的最高点下跌了近20%。加密货币正在崩溃，这重创了那些迟来的投资者。在欧洲央行宣布(结束购债计划)这一决定的前一天，安全的德国国债和有风险的意大利国债之间的十年期收益率差超过了2.4个百分点。债券收益率已经上升，交易员们也在打压那些不太可能与美联储步调一致的央行发行的货币，比如日元和英镑。

The turbulence is exposing central banks’ contradictory goals. The Fed insists it will tame inflation, which reached an annual rate of 8.6% in May. Yet it is loth to admit that doing so will probably require a recession. Even its hawkish pivot this week leaves monetary policy looking muddled. Rules of thumb for how central bankers should respond to underlying inflation say rates should be much higher. The Fed’s dawdling over the past year has worsened the conflict between its competing goals of stable prices and low unemployment.

这种动荡正暴露出各国央行相互矛盾的目标。美联储坚持认为它将抑制通货膨胀，五月份通货膨胀率达到8.6%。然而，它不愿承认这样做可能会导致经济衰退。就连美联储本周的强硬立场也让货币政策看起来混乱不堪。关于央行行长应该如何应对潜在通胀的经验法则表明，利率应该变得更高。美联储在过去一年里的拖延，加剧了其稳定物价和低失业率这两个相互矛盾的目标之间的冲突。

![image-20220621082947118](/Users/chenyujie/Library/Application Support/typora-user-images/image-20220621082947118.png)

The ecb faces an even tougher dilemma. A side-effect of tighter monetary policy is a squeeze on indebted countries. Governments’ finances will be put under pressure both by the rise in underlying rates and the increase in spreads permitted by a lack of bond-buying. Without a backstop, Italy’s debts of over 150% of gdp could become unsustainable—a possibility which would leave the country vulnerable to a self-fulfilling market panic. The ecb says it will not tolerate the threat such a crisis would pose to the integrity of the currency union. But it is not supposed to sacrifice its inflation target to debt-sustainability concerns. Nor can it underwrite governments to the extent that they can run up vast tabs at the expense of the central bank.

美联储收紧货币政策可能会对一些经济复苏已经疲软的国家造成冲击，特别是那些美元债务较高的国家，因为美国利率水平上升可能会提高这些国家偿还美元债务的成本。

欧洲央行面临着一个更为艰难的困境。收紧货币政策的一个副作用是会给负债国家带来压力。由于基础利率上升和债券购买不足导致利差扩大，政府财政将面临压力。如果没有担保，意大利超过 GDP 150%的债务将变得不可持续，这种可能性将使这个国家容易受到自我实现的市场恐慌的影响。欧洲央行表示，它不会容忍这种危机对货币联盟完整性构成威胁。但它不应该为了债务可持续性的担忧而牺牲其通胀目标。它也不能以央行为代价，为政府支付巨额费用。

The Bank of Japan is trading off economics against politics. The yen’s weakness could help the country hit its 2% inflation target, having undershot it for almost the entire period since it was announced in 2013. The trouble is that expensive imports are squeezing living standards, alarming the government. Earlier this month Haruhiko Kuroda, the bank’s governor, had to apologise for saying that households were becoming more accepting of price rises.

日本央行正在经济与政治之间进行权衡。日元走软可能有助于日本实现2%的通胀目标，自2013年宣布这一目标以来，日本几乎在整个时期都未能达到这一目标。问题是昂贵的进口商品正在降低人们的生活水平，这给政府敲响了警钟。本月早些时候，日本央行行长黑田东彦不得不为自己所说的“日本家庭越来越能接受物价上涨”而道歉。

In this environment the central-banking playbook of the 2010s is failing. It was built around low global inflation and designed to dispel the notion that rates might rise. Pivots are rare because policymakers have been unwilling to surprise markets by breaking their “forward guidance”—even the Fed’s decision this week appears to have been leaked two days earlier. Restricting themselves to sequential policy changes, signalled in advance, makes central banks slow on their feet. The result is more volatility in markets and interest rates, not less. As competing goals make the course ahead harder to predict, central banks would do well to stay nimble. ■

在这种环境下，央行在2010年代的业务策略正在失效。它是建立在全球低通胀的基础上，旨在消除“利率可能上升”的想法。这种转向是罕见的，因为政策制定者不愿意破坏他们的“前瞻性指引”，让市场感到意外，甚至美联储本周的决定也是在两天前才泄露的。将自己限制在预先发出信号的连续政策变化上，会让央行行动迟缓。其结果是市场和利率波动加剧，而非减弱。由于相互竞争的目标使未来的道路更加难以预测，各国央行最好保持灵活性。■
