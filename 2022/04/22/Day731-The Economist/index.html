The anger that buoyed Marine Le Pen will not go away

Marine Le Pen心中的愤怒不会消失

Apr 30th 2022

IN THE END, it wasn’t close. Emmanuel Macron stormed to a second term as France’s president, trouncing his nationalist-populist rival Marine Le Pen by 58.5% to 41.5% on April 24th. Gloomy liberals noted that her vote share rose by eight points since their previous encounter in 2017. Nonetheless, despite pandemic, war and inflation, Mr Macron has achieved something no French president has managed for 57 years. He won re-election while also controlling a majority in the national legislature (so disgruntled voters had no one else to blame). France and Europe have dodged a calamity. Had Ms Le Pen won, she would have undermined NATO, appeased Vladimir Putin, challenged the legal foundations of the EU and stoked racial tension at home.

最后，比分并不接近。4月24日，埃马纽埃尔·马克(Emmanuel Macron)以58.5% 对41.5% 的优势彻底击败了其民族主义-民粹主义的对手Marine Le Pen，成功连任法国总统。悲观的自由派人士指出，与2017年的上次会面相比，她的支持率上升了8个百分点。不过，马克龙先生尽管面临有疫情、战争和通货膨胀，他还是取得了57年来任何一位法国总统都未能取得的成就。他赢得了连任，同时也控制了国家立法机构的多数席位(所以不满的选民没有其他人可以指责)。法国和欧洲躲过了一场灾难。如果Le Pen女士获胜，她将削弱北约，安抚弗拉基米尔•普京(Vladimir Putin)，挑战欧盟的法律基础，并在国内煽动种族紧张局势。

Mr Macron tried to sound humble in victory. Standing before the Eiffel Tower, he admitted that: “Our country is beset by doubts and divisions.” He added: “Today’s vote requires us to consider all of the hardships of people’s lives and to respond effectively to them and to the anger expressed.” Ms Le Pen articulated that anger, which is one reason why she did as well as she did, though she offered few coherent remedies. The quality of Mr Macron’s response will determine whether his second term is a success or a failure.

马克龙试图让自己在胜利时显得谦逊。他站在埃菲尔铁塔前，承认道:“我们的国家被怀疑和分歧所困扰。” 他补充说道:“今天的投票要求我们考虑人们生活中的所有困难，并对他们以及他们所表达出的愤怒作出有效的回应。” Le Pen女士明确表达了她的愤怒，这也是她表现得如此出色的原因之一，尽管她几乎没有提出条理清楚的补救措施。马克龙回应的质量将决定他的第二任期是成功还是失败。

First, he will need to keep his majority when the French choose a new parliament in June. His party, La République en Marche (LREM, note the last two initials), and its allies currently hold 60% of seats. French legislative elections are unpredictable, but there is a good chance he will retain his grip.

首先，他需要在6月法国选举新一届议会时保持多数席位。他的政党共和国前进党(LREM，注意最后两个缩写)及其盟友目前拥有60%的席位。法国议会选举是不可预测的，但他很有可能会继续掌权。

What, though, should Mr Macron do with his fresh mandate, and the freedom that comes from not now being eligible to seek re-election? One of his main bits of unfinished business is to tackle a pension system that France’s rickety finances can ill afford. He has promised to raise the retirement age from 62 to 65. The snag is, that will be deeply unpopular, and he has already hinted that he might settle on 64. Is he ready to face down furious protests by workers yearning for early retirement? Or will he cave, as he did to the gilets jaunes and their fuel-tax protests in 2018? His plan to pay teachers more if they take on more tasks will upset teachers’ unions. His campaign against red tape has far to go. He seems to have given up on making French labour markets any more flexible. He needs to be tough.

不过，马克龙会利用他的新权力做什么？他会如何应对因现在没有资格寻求连任而带来的自由? 他未完成的主要工作之一是解决养老金体系问题，法国摇摇欲坠的财政状况难以承受。他承诺将退休年龄从62岁提高到65岁。问题是，这将是非常不受欢迎的，而且他已经暗示他可能会选择64岁。他准备好挫败那些渴望提前退休的工人们的愤怒抗议了吗？或者他会选择像他在2018年对黄马甲和他们的燃油税抗议所做的那样屈服？他的计划是，如果教师承担更多的任务，就给他们更多的工资，这将使教师工会感到不安。他反对繁文缛节的运动还有很长的路要走。他似乎已经放弃让法国劳动力市场更加灵活。他需要强硬。

At the same time, he must find ways to tackle the inequality that fuels support for the radical left of Jean-Luc Mélenchon, who ran a strong third in the first round of the presidential race, as well as for Ms Le Pen. France’s big cities hum with tech startups, global-minded financiers, swanky shops and restaurants. In the crime-ridden banlieues around them, and in the smaller towns, the grimy post-industrial north and the beautiful but economically stunted south, it is a different story. Fractured France, forgotten France, call it what you will—it is there, and it does not vote for centrists like Mr Macron, whom Mr Mélenchon and Ms Le Pen call le président des riches.

同时，他必须找到解决不平等问题的方法，这种不平等加剧了对激进左派Jean-Luc Mélenchon的支持， Jean-Luc Mélenchon在第一轮总统选举中以强劲优势获得第三名，Le Pen女士也是如此。法国的大城市充满了科技初创企业、具有全球视野的金融家、奢华的商店以及餐馆。在他们周围的郊区，犯罪猖獗，在较小的城镇，肮脏的后工业化的北部和美丽但经济落后的南部，情况就完全不同了。分裂的法国，被遗忘的法国，随你怎么称呼它，它就在那里，而且它不会把选票投给像马克龙这样的中间派，Mélenchon和Le Pen称马克龙为“富人的总统”。

Mr Macron has a good plan, revolving around lots more education and training; but such things take time and money, and the centrists may not have enough of either. Mr Macron must surely care about his legacy. If the march of the radicals continues, perhaps under the leadership of Marion Maréchal, Ms Le Pen’s slightly less polarising but no less nationalist niece, the election in 2027 will be perilous. Mr Macron’s great success has been to dominate the centre ground of French politics. Alas, this means the only serious opposition to him now comes from the expanding extremes of right and left. That is not a comfortable prospect.

马克龙有一个很好的计划，其围绕着更多的教育和培训；但这些事情需要时间和金钱，而中间派可能两者都不够。马克龙肯定会关心自己的政治遗产。如果是激进派接手，也许会在Marion Maréchal的领导下，2027年的选举将是危险的。Marion Maréchal是Le Pen的侄女，其两极化程度较低，但其民族主义程度同样高。马克龙的巨大成功在于他主导了法国政治的中心地带。唉，这意味着现在唯一对他真正的反对是来自于不断扩大的极右和极左势力。这不是一个令人满意的前景。

Nor will Mr Macron’s challenges stop at France’s borders. One cannot doubt his ambition: he wants to rebuild Europe, not just his own country. Having fought off the populist dragon not once but twice, and with a solid record of economic stewardship that has given France the second-highest growth rate in the G7, he looks, for the time being, like Europe’s natural leader. Germany’s Olaf Scholz is floundering, his party and coalition bickering endlessly over Ukraine. Britain has marginalised itself, and Italy’s Mario Draghi is on the way out; an election due in the next year threatens to bring Italian nationalist-populists to power. And Mr Macron’s views have a lot of support in Europe. The EU has become more economically interventionist, less fiscally restrictive and more inclined to beef up its own defences: more French, in other words.

马克龙面临的挑战也不会局限于法国边境。人们不能怀疑他的野心：他想要重建欧洲，而不仅仅是他自己的国家。他不止一次而是两次击退了民粹主义的巨龙，并凭借稳定的经济管理记录使法国成为七国集团中增长率第二高的国家，他目前看起来就像欧洲天生的领袖。德国的奥拉夫•朔尔茨(Olaf Scholz)正在苦苦挣扎，他的政党和执政联盟在乌克兰问题上争吵不休。英国已将自己边缘化，意大利总理马里奥•德拉吉(Mario Draghi)即将出局；明年即将举行的选举可能会把意大利民族主义-民粹主义推上权力宝座。马克龙的观点在欧洲得到了很多人的支持。欧盟已经变得更倾向于经济干预，更少的财政限制，更倾向于加强自己的防御：换句话说，更像法国。

But progress will be hard. Big changes in Europe require consensus, and in each area where Mr Macron has big plans, he will face rigid opposition. The Poles and other central Europeans are wary of anything that might challenge the primacy of NATO; the “frugals”, a group of flinty northern countries, do not want to pay for another round of EU largesse. Beleaguered at home, Mr Scholz may not be the helpful partner that Mr Macron will need. Winning elections is hard: securing a legacy yet harder.

但进展将是艰难的。欧洲的重大变革需要共识，而在马克龙有重大计划的每一个领域，他都将面临严格反对。波兰和其他中欧国家对任何可能挑战北约主导地位的事情都持谨慎态度；“节俭者”，即一群强硬的北方国家，不想为欧盟的又一轮慷慨买单。由于在国内处于困境，朔尔茨可能不是马克龙所需要的得力伙伴。赢得选举是困难的：保住“遗产”就更难了。
