Europe should levy a high tariff on Russian energy

欧洲应该对俄罗斯能源征收高额关税

The case for energy sanctions

能源制裁的理由

Imposing sanctions on oil and gas is worth the economic costs

对石油和天然气实施制裁是要付出经济代价的

Apr 30th 2022

Even as Russia has invaded Ukraine, terrorised civilians and flattened Mariupol, the European Union has spent €44bn ($46bn) buying its oil and gas. That could be about to change. On April 27th Russia announced that it had stopped supplying gas to Bulgaria and Poland, which had refused its demand to pay in roubles. Because the eu can easily buy oil, but not gas, from elsewhere, Russia is attempting to exploit its point of vulnerability.

就在俄罗斯入侵乌克兰、恐吓平民并将马里乌波尔夷为平地之际，欧盟却花了440亿欧元(合460亿美元)购买其石油和天然气。这种情况可能即将改变。4月27日，由于保加利亚和波兰拒绝了俄罗斯以卢布付款的要求，俄罗斯宣布停止向这两个国家供应天然气。由于欧盟可以轻易地从其他地方购买石油，但无法购买天然气，俄罗斯正试图利用其弱点。

Europe is considering how to respond. Already it was entertaining the possibility of sanctions on Russian oil. But it fears that it has the weaker hand. Olaf Scholz, Germany’s chancellor, has said that an embargo would plunge Europe into recession without doing much to hurt Vladimir Putin and his war effort. Russia’s gas is used both to heat homes and to power factories, and among Europe’s big economies Germany and Italy are especially dependent on it. Predicting what would happen without Russian gas is hard, because it depends on how quickly supply chains adapt around the disruption. Optimistic academics say the initial cost to Germany’s economy of a full energy embargo could be just 0.5% of lost GDP; Germany’s central bank puts the bill at 5% of GDP.

欧洲正在考虑如何应对。它已经在考虑对俄罗斯石油实施制裁的可能性。但它担心自己手中的牌更弱。德国总理奥拉夫•朔尔茨曾表示，禁运将使欧洲陷入衰退，但不会对弗拉基米尔•普京及其战事造成太大伤害。俄罗斯的天然气既可以用于家庭取暖，也可用于工厂发电，在欧洲的大型经济体中，德国和意大利尤其依赖天然气。很难预测没有俄罗斯天然气的情况会发生什么，因为这取决于供应链适应中断的速度。乐观的学者表示，全面能源禁运对德国经济造成的初始成本可能仅会导致GDP下降0.5%；德国中央银行将该账单定为GDP的5%。

Although Mr Scholz is right that energy sanctions would be costly, he underestimates the harm they would do to the Kremlin. He says that Russia probably cannot spend the foreign exchange it earns from oil and gas sales, because its financial system has been cut off from much of the world. There is some evidence for this. South Korea has timely trade data and participates in sanctions: in March its exports to Russia fell by 63%. With its central bank’s foreign-exchange reserves frozen, Russia has had to enforce capital controls and raise interest rates in order to support the rouble.

尽管朔尔茨先生认为“能源制裁将会代价高昂“，这是正确的，但他低估了制裁会对俄罗斯政府造成的伤害。他说，俄罗斯可能无法花掉石油和天然气销售所赚的外汇，因为它的金融体系已经与全球大部分地区切断了联系。这是有证据的。韩国拥有实时的贸易数据，并参与了制裁：3月份，韩国对俄罗斯的出口下降了63%。由于俄罗斯央行的外汇储备被冻结，俄罗斯不得不实施资本管控并提高利率以支撑卢布。

Yet swathes of the global economy, including India and most of the Middle East and Africa, continue to trade freely with Russia. Secondary sanctions, which would punish anyone transacting with Russia, are not in place. Some Russian banks can still interact with the West. As a result, even with its central bank under sanctions, it is likely that Russia is making creative use of freshly acquired hard currency from selling energy, including to buy arms. It need put only enough financial intermediaries between its transactions and those taking place in New York or Frankfurt.

然而，全球经济的一部分，其中包括印度以及大部分中东和非洲，仍保持着与俄罗斯的自由贸易。惩罚任何与俄罗斯交易的人的二级制裁还未准备就绪。一些俄罗斯银行仍然可以与西方进行互动。因此，即使其央行受到制裁，俄罗斯很可能正在创造性地利用通过出售能源刚获得的硬通货，包括用来购买武器。它只需要在其交易和在纽约或法兰克福进行的交易之间设置足够的金融中介。

The case for reducing the flow of cash into Mr Putin’s coffers therefore stands. And it is sufficient to justify the economic pain involved. In 2020 eu member states implemented lockdowns which caused annual GDP to shrink by almost 6% in order to slow the spread of covid-19. Cutting off funding to an invader that would threaten to convert putative success in Ukraine into aggression against other European countries is both morally sound and strategically desirable.

因此，减少流入普京金库的现金流的理由是成立的。这足以证明所包含的“经济痛苦”是合理的。2020年，欧盟成员国为了减缓新冠疫情的传播实施封锁，导致年度GDP缩水近6%。切断对一个侵略者的资金支持这在道德上是合理的，在战略上也是可取的。该侵略者有可能会将在乌克兰取得的假定成功转变为侵略其他欧洲国家。

What is more, the design of sanctions could lower their economic cost. One idea is to levy a high tariff on Russian energy, rather than ban it altogether. To keep selling, Russia would probably need to cut prices so that its oil remained competitive with other countries’. (Redirecting all its oil elsewhere would be an enormous logistical challenge.) A high tariff could in effect seize some of Russia’s oil profits without disrupting supply. The consequences for gas prices are harder to predict. But because Russia cannot move gas pipelines any more easily than Europe can find alternative suppliers, it would probably absorb at least part of a tariff.

更重要的是，设计制裁可以降低他们的经济成本。有这样一个想法，即对俄罗斯能源征收高额关税，而不是完全禁止它。为了继续销售，俄罗斯可能需要降低价格，以保持其石油在与其他国家的竞争中具有竞争力。(将所有石油转移到其他地方将是一个巨大的运输挑战。) 高关税实际上可以在不中断供应的情况下攫取俄罗斯的部分石油利润。这对油价的影响很难预测。但由于俄罗斯运输天然气管道比欧洲寻找替代供应商更困难，因此它可能至少会承担一部分关税。

In either case, taking action may provoke Mr Putin to cut supplies further. If a tariff morphs into an embargo, so be it. Russia’s war would become harder to sustain—and in the decades to come Europe would be more secure. ■

无论哪种情况，采取行动都可能激怒普京先生进一步削减石油供应。如果关税变成了禁运，那就这样吧。俄罗斯的战争将变得更加难以维持——在未来的几十年里，欧洲将更加安全。■
