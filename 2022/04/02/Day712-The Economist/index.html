Is investing in Twitter a meme too far for Elon Musk?

对埃隆·马斯克来说，投资推特的模因是否太过了?

Another Musk-have

又一个马斯克所有

![image-20220409090410011](/Users/chenyujie/Library/Application Support/typora-user-images/image-20220409090410011.png)

The self-styled Technoking may be overextending himself

这位自称“Technoking”的人可能有点不自量力了

Apr 9th 2022

WHAT WILL he do with it? That was the big question after Elon Musk let it be known on April 4th that he had amassed a stake of 9.2% in Twitter, making him the social-media firm’s largest shareholder. Will the world’s richest man buy more shares or even take Twitter private? Will the boss of Tesla take a hands-on role in Twitter’s management? Will the libertarian troll push to bring back Donald Trump, kicked off the platform after inciting an assault on the Capitol in January 2021? Speculation mounted after Twitter said a day later that Mr Musk would join its board.

他会用它做什么? 4月4日，马斯克公开表示，他已持有Twitter 9.2%的股份，并成为这家社交媒体公司的最大股东。这位世界上最富有的人会购买更多的股票，甚至将Twitter私有化吗? 特斯拉的老板会亲自参与Twitter的管理吗? 这位自由意志主义喷子会努力让特朗普回归吗? 特朗普在2021年1月煽动了一场针对国会大厦的攻击后，被踢出了Twitter平台。Twitter在一天后表示，马斯克将加入其董事会，这引发了更多的猜测。

As is his wont, Mr Musk will reveal his plans in his own time and probably in his own tweets to the 80m people who follow him on the platform (not many fewer than followed Mr Trump before he got the boot). In posts published before he announced the investment, he complained that Twitter “serves as the de facto public town square” but fails “to adhere to free-speech principles”. He urged the company to open up the algorithm that decides which tweets users see. In light of his well-documented sympathies for cryptocurrencies and their underlying technology, the blockchain, he could try to turn Twitter into a decentralised service controlled by users.

按照他的习惯，马斯克将在自己的时间，很可能是在他自己的Twitter上，向在该平台上关注他的8000万人(不比在特朗普被抛弃前关注他的人少多少)披露自己的计划。在宣布这项投资之前，他发表了一些帖子，上面抱怨Twitter“实际上是一个城市公共广场”，但却未能“遵守言论自由原则”。他敦促该公司开放决定用户看到哪些推文的算法。鉴于他对加密货币及其底层术区块链的充分支持，他可以尝试将Twitter转变为一种由用户控制的去中心化服务。

It is hard to see how that would make the company more profitable. Investors rejoiced anyway. Some may be believers in the “Elon markets hypothesis”, which holds that stocks should be valued based not on fundamentals but on their proximity to Mr Musk. Others may hope that he can really shake things up. Twitter has been a much bigger cultural success than a commercial one. Before Mr Musk’s move sent its share price up by a third, the firm’s market value had been languishing around $30bn, not much higher than where it was when it went public in 2013. By comparison, its social-media rival Meta (née Facebook), briefly became a $1trn company and its market capitalisation is up more than five-fold in the same period despite a recent tumble (it is currently worth $631bn).

很难看出这将如何使公司更赚钱。不管怎样，投资者还是很高兴。一些人可能相信“埃隆市场假说”，该假说认为，股票估值不应基于基本面，而应基于它们与马斯克的距离。其他人可能希望他能真正改变局面。Twitter在文化上的成功远远大于商业上的成功。在马斯克的举措将其股价推高三分之一之前，该公司的市值一直徘徊在300亿美元左右，与2013年上市时的水平相差无几。相比之下，其社交媒体竞争对手Meta (原名Facebook)的市值一度达到1万亿美元，尽管近期暴跌(目前价值6310亿美元)，但其市值在同一时期增长了5倍以上。

Whatever Mr Musk’s designs for Twitter, one near-certainty is that they will require money, time and attention. That raises another question: is the self-styled Technoking overextending himself?

无论马斯克对Twitter有什么打算，几乎可以肯定的一点是，它们将需要资金、时间和关注。这又引出了另一个问题:这位自称“Technoking”的人是否有点不自量力？

Financially, he isn’t. The investment in Twitter, which cost less than $3bn, is chump change for Mr Musk—about 1% of his net worth. A bigger concern, especially to investors in his other firms, is over his workload. Twitter comes on top of several big corporate commitments. Besides running Tesla, a $1.1trn electric-car giant with nearly 100,000 employees, he heads up SpaceX, a privately held rocketry firm valued at $100bn. He also helped found two drilling startups, one making big holes to build tunnels (The Boring Company), the other making tiny ones to implant electrodes in the brain (Neuralink). Adding a Twitter board seat to his résumé may overtax even a functioning workaholic and astute delegator like Mr Musk. Now 50 years old and the father of eight, he has been putting in 100-hour weeks for decades, as he recently revealed in an interview.

在财务上,他不是。对Twitter的投资不到30亿美元，对马斯克来说只是一笔小钱，大约是他净资产的1%。更大的担忧，尤其是对他其他公司的投资者来说，是他的工作量。除了对几家大公司的承诺以外，又来了个Twitter。除了掌管着市值1.1万亿美元、员工近10万人的电动汽车巨头特斯拉，他还领导着估值1000亿美元的私营火箭公司SpaceX。他还帮助创立了两家钻探初创公司，一家是挖大洞来建造隧道(The Boring Company)，另一家是挖极小的洞来在大脑中植入电极(Neuralink)。在他的简历上增加一个Twitter董事会席位，即使是像马斯克这样的工作狂和精明的委派者，也可能会负担过重。他今年50岁，是8个孩子的父亲，他最近在一次采访中透露，几十年来，他每周工作100小时。

Where Mr Musk may be most over extended is in his trolling—not so much of his numerous critics (though he does plenty of that in his spare time) but of regulators. America’s Securities and Exchange Commission was already after him for allegedly violating a court agreement to have his tweets lawyered before publishing, reached after he tweeted in 2018 that he had “funding secured” to take Tesla private, which he ended up not doing.

马斯克先生最不自量力的地方可能在于他对监管机构的“抨击”，而不是他无数的批评者(尽管他在业余时间做了很多这样的事)。美国证券交易委员会已经在追查他，理由是他违反了一项法庭协议，即在发布Twitter之前先获得律师批准。该协议是在他2018年发推文称，他已“获得资金”将特斯拉私有化后达成的，但他最终没有这么做。

The Twitter investment may get him into further trouble. He made it public a few days after the deadline for such disclosures. And his filing suggested that he would be a passive investor, which seems at odds with his joining the board. Expect his Twitter habit to raise even more eyebrows now that he is no longer just a big user but a large shareholder, too. ■

对Twitter的投资可能会让他陷入更多的麻烦。他在这类披露的截止日期后几天公布了这一消息。他提交的文件表明，他将成为一个被动投资者，这似乎与他加入董事会不一致。预计他的Twitter习惯会让更多的人感到惊讶，因为他不再只是一个大用户，而是一个大股东。■
