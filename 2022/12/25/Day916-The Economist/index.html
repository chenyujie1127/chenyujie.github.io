What the Kevin McCarthy saga means for America’s Congress

Power struggles, public humiliation and a government shutdown may follow

权力斗争、公众羞辱和美国政府停摆可能随之而来

Jan 4th 2023

With the anguish of someone who has spent a decade pursuing a prize, only to see it slipping away from his grasp, Kevin McCarthy told his colleagues in the House Republican caucus, “I’ve earned this job.” And, in a way, he had. The Republican from Bakersfield has kissed an army of frogs, including lobbyists, donors and cranks like congresswoman Marjorie Taylor Greene, to shore up his position, only for 20 House Republicans to turn around and accuse him of infidelity. This tale has its comic side: at one point Matt Gaetz, one of the “Never Kevin” Republican members, wrote to the Architect of the Capitol complaining that some guy called Kevin had moved his desk into the Speaker’s office and that it should be removed forthwith. But the McCarthy saga—the kind of power struggle and public humiliation that Washington loves—also augurs ill for the progress of important legislation in the 118th Congress.

凯文·麦卡锡对众议院共和党核心小组的同事们说，“这份工作是我应得的。” 他花了十年时间都在追求一个目标，却眼看着它从手中溜走，这让他非常痛苦。在某种程度上，他确实赢得了（这份工作）。为了巩固自己的地位，这位来自贝克斯菲尔德的共和党人亲吻了一群青蛙，包括说客、捐赠者和女议员玛乔丽·泰勒·格林这样的怪人，结果20名众议院共和党人改变主意指责他不忠。这个故事有其滑稽的一面: “永远不选凯文”的共和党成员马特·盖兹曾写信给国会大厦建筑师，抱怨一个叫凯文的家伙把他的桌子搬到了议长办公室，应该立即把它搬走。但麦卡锡的故事——华盛顿所钟爱的那种权力斗争和公开羞辱——对第118届国会的重要立法进展也是不祥之兆。

Choosing the Speaker of America’s House of Representatives, an important office whose holder is second-in-line for the presidency, ordinarily requires only one round of voting. The last exception to this was a century ago, when progressive Republicans held up their party’s candidate. With no Speaker, no legislation can progress and no new members can be sworn in. This leaves the people temporarily without representation. The move is therefore a drastic one for the Never Kevins to make. So why did they do it?

美国众议院议长是总统的第二顺位继承人，这一重要职位的选举通常只需要一轮投票。上一次例外发生在一个世纪前，当时进步派共和党人阻碍了本党候选人。没有议长，立法就无法进行，新议员也无法宣誓就职。这使得人民暂时没有代表。因此，这一举动对于“永远不选Kevin的人”来说是一个极端的举动。那么他们为什么要这么做呢?

Not, surely, in opposition to Mr McCarthy’s proposed agenda. Released before the midterm elections in November 2022, his “Commitment to America” would struggle to fill a postcard with policies that are within the power of Congress to enact. Nor do the Never Kevins have much of an agenda themselves. Instead, the plan seems to have been to break the toys so that nobody else can play with them. That dynamic has been present in the Republican caucus for a while. John Boehner, who struggled with the “Hell No” caucus of House Republicans when he was Speaker from 2011 to 2015, observed: “What they’re really interested in is chaos…Every time they vote down a bill, they get another invitation to go on Fox News or talk radio. It’s a narcissistic—and dangerous—feedback loop.” And it is getting worse.

肯定不是反对麦卡锡先生提出的议程。他在2022年11月中期选举之前发布的《对美国的承诺》的议程，很难用国会有权制定的政策来填满明信片。“永远不选凯文的人”也没有什么议程。相反，他们的计划似乎是把玩具打碎，这样其他人就不能玩了。这种情况在共和党核心小组中已经存在了一段时间。约翰·博纳在2011年至2015年担任众议院议长期间，曾与众议院共和党人的“绝对不”小组斗争过，他说:“他们真正感兴趣的是混乱……每次他们投票否决一项法案，他们就会收到另一个参加福克斯新闻或电台谈话节目的邀请。”这是一个自恋的且危险的反馈循环。” 而且这种情况越来越糟。

While this was going on, Democrats lined up in an orderly fashion behind Hakeem Jeffries, a moderate Democrat from New York. After his elevation to Minority Leader-elect, Mr Jeffries said po-faced things about the chaos on the other side being a sad day for democracy, while his colleagues tittered at the McCarthy spectacle. If they were less concerned with extracting partisan advantage, Democrats might settle on some sensible Republican to back as Majority Leader and hope that a few Republicans could support the same candidate. There is no constitutional reason why the moderates in both parties could not club together, seize control of the legislative agenda and tell the extreme wings of their parties to lump it. Alas, party discipline, tribal loyalty and the threat of primary challengers combine to make this impossible. Instead, Republicans will struggle on until they have a Speaker.

与此同时，民主党人有序地站在来自纽约的温和民主党人哈基姆·杰弗里斯身后。当选少数党领袖后，杰弗里斯一本正经地说，另一方的混乱对民主来说是悲伤的一天，而他的同事则对麦卡锡的“壮观场面”窃笑不已。如果民主党人不太关心获取党派优势，他们可能会选择一些明智的共和党人作为多数党领袖，并希望少数共和党人能支持同一位候选人。没有宪法上的理由可以解释为什么两党的温和派不能联合起来，控制立法议程，并告诉他们党内的极端势力将就一下。唉，政党纪律、部落忠诚和初选挑战者的威胁加在一起，使这一切不可能实现。相反，共和党人将继续“奋斗”，直到他们拥有一位议长。

A weakened Speaker will make it even harder for Congress to pass contentious legislation this year. One beholden to the House Republicans’ berserker caucus could find it difficult to avoid a government shutdown, to raise the debt ceiling or to provide additional support to Ukraine’s armed forces. This comes after Congress had been on a winning streak, reforming the process for certifying elections and reaffirming gay marriage as the law of the land in 2022. Even divided Congresses can sometimes accomplish quite a lot. But this one is setting out with lead weights attached to its boots. ■

一个被削弱的议长将使国会今年更难通过有争议的立法。对众议院共和党狂暴核心小组负责的人可能会发现，要避免政府关门、提高债务上限或为乌克兰武装部队提供额外支持，都很难。此前，国会一直在取得连胜，改革了选举认证程序，并在2022年重申同性婚姻为美国法律。即使是分裂的国会有时也能完成很多事情。但这个国会出发的时候靴子上绑着铅块。■
