Russia needs a respite, so the West must help Ukraine fight on

Keep calm and carry on

保持冷静，继续前进

The mishap in Poland should concentrate minds on tackling the cause of the conflict

波兰的不幸事件应该使人们集中精力解决冲突的根源

Nov 16th 2022

The rocket that landed in Poland on November 15th, killing two people, was mercifully not a missile from Russia. But the Ukrainian interceptor which fell to the ground would never have been launched were it not for Russia’s brutal aerial assault on Ukraine that day—its biggest barrage of missiles against Ukrainian cities and infrastructure so far. Russia is escalating because it is losing. The West must keep its cool and intensify its support for Ukraine. This is a time for bold steps.

11月15日降落在波兰的导弹造成两人死亡，幸运的是，这不是来自俄罗斯的导弹。但是，如果不是俄罗斯当天对乌克兰发动了残酷的空袭，乌克兰那枚坠落在地上的拦截导弹就永远不会发射，那场空袭是迄今为止对乌克兰城市和基础设施发动的最大规模的导弹攻击。俄罗斯正在逐步升级，因为它正在失败。西方必须保持冷静，加强对乌克兰的支持。现在是采取大胆措施的时候了。

Poland and its allies deserve praise for their calm response. As Russia’s state media gloated over the death of Poles and accused its enemies of a “provocation”, nato governments collected evidence and avoided raising the temperature. Volodymyr Zelensky, Ukraine’s president, made a rare misstep in painting the accident as a Russian missile attack. But Russia’s behaviour courts disaster. In March it struck a base just 15 miles from the Polish border. The same month, a Soviet-era drone carrying explosives crashed in the Croatian capital, Zagreb. In September a Russian jet inadvertently fired an air-to-air missile close to a British spyplane in international airspace over the Black Sea. Russia does not want a war with nato. But it hopes that such danger will induce nato to back down.

波兰及其盟友的冷静应对值得称赞。当俄罗斯国家媒体对波兰人的死亡幸灾乐祸，并指责其敌人是“挑衅”时，北约各国政府收集证据，避免火上浇油。乌克兰总统弗拉基米尔•泽连斯基在将此次事故描述为俄罗斯导弹袭击时，犯了一个罕见的错误。但俄罗斯的行为会招致灾难。今年3月，它袭击了距离波兰边境仅15英里的一个基地。同月，一架携带炸药的苏制无人机在克罗地亚首都萨格勒布坠毁。9月，在黑海上空的国际空域，一架俄罗斯喷气机无意中向一架英国侦察机附近发射了一枚空对空导弹。俄罗斯不想和北约开战。但它希望这样的危险会促使北约做出让步。

That must not happen. Some American officials are showing signs of nerves. Mark Milley, America’s top general, has compared the conflict to the stalemate of the first world war. He has cast doubt on Ukraine’s ability to shift the front lines further and suggested that it should consider negotiations with the Kremlin. His diagnosis and prescription are both flawed.

这绝不能发生。一些美国官员表现出紧张的迹象。美国最高将领马克·米莱将这场冲突比作第一次世界大战的僵局。他对乌克兰进一步转移前线的能力表示怀疑，并建议乌克兰应该考虑与克里姆林宫进行谈判。他的“诊断”和“处方”都有缺陷。

Russia’s army is in dire shape and on the retreat. Its withdrawal from Kherson earlier this month reflects a serious shortage of artillery ammunition (see Europe section). Precision munitions are running out, which is why Russian attacks on Ukraine’s energy grid rely on Iranian drones. And even as the seasons bring mud and bitter cold, Ukraine well stocked with Western winter gear, will keep fighting.

俄军状况糟糕，正在撤退。本月早些时候，其从赫尔松的撤出反映了火炮弹药的严重短缺(见欧洲部分)。精确制导弹药正在耗尽，这就是为什么俄罗斯对乌克兰电网的攻击依赖于伊朗无人机。尽管季节带来了泥泞和严寒，乌克兰已储备了充足的西方冬季装备，其将继续战斗。

Russia needs a pause. It should not be given one. For Ukraine to press its advantage, it requires a steady supply of Western arms, ammunition and equipment.

俄罗斯需要暂停一下。不应该给它一个“喘息”机会。乌克兰要想发挥自己的优势，就需要稳定的西方武器、弹药和装备供应。

Top of the list is air defence. As its Soviet-era systems run low on ammunition it needs additional Western launchers that can be replenished more easily—including longer-range Patriot batteries. Air defences can also spark a virtuous cycle, allowing Ukraine to ramp up its own arms production without fear of having factories destroyed. Its army hopes to raise as many as a dozen new brigades for a future offensive. They will need a large fleet of armoured vehicles.

最重要的是防空。由于苏联时代的系统弹药不足，乌克兰需要更多的西方发射器，这些发射器可以更容易地补充弹药——包括射程更远的爱国者炮台。防空系统还可以引发良性循环，让乌克兰可以加大自己的武器生产，而不用担心工厂被摧毁。其军队希望为未来的进攻募集许多新旅。他们需要一支庞大的装甲车队。

On November 15th the Biden administration asked Congress for over $37bn in emergency aid to Ukraine—more than the defence budgets of Australia, Canada or Italy. Europe must show the same sense of urgency. Russia’s air attacks should serve as the trigger for the dispatch of tanks, such as German Leopards. European allies should create a fund to support the provision of Sweden’s Gripen fighter jets, which are easy to maintain and well suited to Ukraine’s flexible operations.

11月15日，拜登政府要求国会为乌克兰提供370亿美元的紧急援助，比澳大利亚、加拿大和意大利的国防预算都要多。欧洲必须表现出同样的紧迫感。俄罗斯的空袭应该成为派遣坦克（如德国豹）的导火索。欧洲盟友应该设立一项基金，支持瑞典的鹰狮战斗机的供应，这种战斗机易于维护，非常适合乌克兰灵活的作战行动。

Jaw, jaw and war, war

This week’s incident, and the danger that setbacks on the battlefield will lead Russia’s president, Vladimir Putin, to escalate, including with nuclear weapons, show the importance of maintaining channels of communication with Russia. That is why Bill Burns, the director of the cia, was right to meet his Russian counterpart in Turkey on November 14th. The door to a future diplomatic settlement should be left open, when both Ukraine and Russia are ready for one. But a ceasefire now would be deeply disadvantageous to Ukraine, halting its momentum and giving Russia breathing space to restock its arsenal and prepare a fresh army. This is not the moment to let up. ■

本周的事件，以及战场上的挫折将导致俄罗斯总统弗拉基米尔·普京升级的危险，包括核武器，表明了与俄罗斯保持沟通渠道的重要性。这就是为什么中情局局长比尔·伯恩斯11月14日在土耳其会见俄罗斯同行是正确的。如果乌克兰和俄罗斯都准备好了，那么未来外交解决的大门应该是敞开的。但现在停火将对乌克兰极为不利，它会遏制乌克兰的势头，给俄罗斯喘息的空间以补充武器库，并准备一支新军队。现在不是歇下来的时候。■
