History will judge Vladimir Putin harshly for his war

历史将严厉批判普京发动的战争

Where will he stop?

他将在哪里停下来?

![image-20220225093240064](/Users/chenyujie/Library/Application Support/typora-user-images/image-20220225093240064.png)

Russia’s president has launched an unprovoked assault on his neighbour

俄罗斯总统对其邻国发动了无端攻击

Feb 25th 2022

BY THE TIME it began, early on a gloomy grey morning on February 24th, the onslaught against Ukraine ordered by Russia’s president, Vladimir Putin, had acquired a sickening inevitability. Yet nothing about this war was inevitable. It is a conflict entirely of his own making. In the fighting and the misery that is to come, much Ukrainian and Russian blood will be spilled. Every drop of it will be splattered on Mr Putin’s hands.

2月24日一个阴沉的早晨，当战争开始时，俄罗斯总统普京下令对乌克兰的进攻已经达到了一种令人恐惧的不可避免的地步。然而这场战争并不是必然的。这完全是他自己造成的冲突。在接下来的战斗和苦难中，乌克兰和俄罗斯将付出大量的“鲜血”。每一滴都会溅到普京的手上。

For months, while Mr Putin had remained in seclusion, amassing roughly 190,000 Russian troops on the borders with Ukraine, the question had been: what does this man want? Now that it is clear he craves war, the question is: where will he stop?

几个月来，普京一直“闭关锁国”，在俄乌边境集结了大约19万俄军，但问题是:这个人想要什么? 现在很明显他渴望战争，问题是:他会在哪里止步?

To hear Mr Putin on the eve of the invasion, he would like the world to believe that he will stop at nothing. In his battle speech, recorded on February 21st and released as he unleashed the first volleys of cruise missiles against his fellow Slavs, Russia’s president railed against “the empire of lies” that is the West. Crowing over his nuclear arsenal, he pointedly threatened to “crush” any country that stood in his way.

在入侵前夕普京称，他希望全世界相信，他会不择手段。在他于2月21日录制的战斗演讲中，这位俄罗斯总统怒斥西方是“谎言帝国”，在他第一次向斯拉夫人发射巡航导弹时，该演讲才公布。他对自己的核武库洋洋得意，并刻意地威胁要“摧毁”任何阻碍他的国家。

Early reports, some unconfirmed, only underlined the scale of his ambition. There had been speculation that Russia’s president might be satisfied with control over Donetsk and Luhansk, regions of eastern Ukraine containing small, Russian-backed enclaves that were the object of last-minute diplomacy. But all that has crumpled in the face of a vast assault.

一些未经证实的早期报道只是强调了他的雄心壮志。此前有人猜测，俄罗斯总统可能会对控制顿涅茨克和卢甘斯克感到满意，这两个乌克兰东部地区都有俄罗斯支持的小型飞地，它们是最后一刻外交努力的目标。但在一场大规模的袭击面前，这一切都崩溃了。

The reports said that Russian land forces had crossed from the east, heading for Kharkiv, Ukraine’s second city; from the south, heading from Crimea towards Kherson; and from Belarus to the north, heading for Kyiv, the capital. It was unclear in what strength they were moving. But Mr Putin seemingly covets all of Ukraine, just as American and British intelligence reports had claimed all along. In acting, he has set aside the everyday calculus of political risks and benefits. Instead he is driven by the dangerous, delusional idea that he has an appointment with history.

报道称，俄罗斯陆军已从东部越境，前往乌克兰第二大城市哈尔科夫; 从南面，从克里米亚前往赫尔松; 从白俄罗斯向北，前往乌克兰首都基辅。目前还不清楚他们移动的速度。但普京似乎觊觎整个乌克兰，正如美国和英国情报报告一直宣称的那样。在行动上，他把政治风险和利益的日常风险放在一边。相反，他被一种危险的、妄想的想法所驱使，其认为自己与历史有约。

That is why, should Mr Putin seize a large swathe of Ukraine, the gatherer of the lands will not stop to make peace at its borders. He may not invade the NATO countries that were once in the Soviet empire, at least not at first. But, bloated by victory, he will subject them to the cyber attacks and information warfare that fall short of the threshold of conflict.

这就是为什么，如果普京占领了乌克兰的大片土地，这些土地的收集者将不会停下来在其边境上媾和。他可能不会入侵曾经属于苏联帝国的北约国家，至少一开始不会。但是，由于胜利的膨胀，他将使他们遭受网络攻击和信息战，而这些攻击和信息战还不足以引发冲突。

Mr Putin will threaten NATO in this way, because he has come to believe that NATO threatens Russia and its people. Speaking earlier this week, he raged at the alliance’s eastward expansion. Later, he decried a fictitious “genocide” that he says the West is sponsoring in Ukraine. Mr Putin can’t tell his people that his army is fighting against their Ukrainian brothers and sisters who gained freedom. So he is telling them that Russia is at war with America, NATO and its proxies.

普京将以这种方式威胁北约，因为他已经开始相信北约威胁着俄罗斯及其人民。本周早些时候，他对北约东扩表示愤怒。后来，他谴责了一个虚构的“种族灭绝”，他说西方在乌克兰支持这种种族灭绝。普京不能告诉他的人民，他的军队正在与获得自由的乌克兰兄弟姐妹作战。所以他告诉他们，俄罗斯正与美国、北约及其代理人交战。

The abominable truth is that Mr Putin has launched an unprovoked assault on the sovereign country next door. He is obsessed with the defensive alliance to its west. And he is trampling the principles that underpin peace in the 21st century. That is why the world must inflict a heavy price for his aggression.

令人憎恶的事实是，普京对邻国这个主权国家发动了无端攻击。他痴迷于西方的防御联盟。他践踏了支撑21世纪和平的原则。这就是为什么世界必须为他的侵略付出沉重代价的原因。

This starts with massive punitive sanctions against Russia’s financial system, its high-tech industries and its moneyed elite. Just before the invasion, when Russia recognised the two republics, the West had imposed only modest sanctions. It must not hesitate now. Even though Russia has set out to build a fortress economy, the country is still connected to the world and, as the initial 45% fall in Russia’s stockmarket suggests, it will suffer.

首先要对俄罗斯的金融体系、高科技产业和富有的精英阶层实施大规模的惩罚性制裁。就在入侵前，当俄罗斯承认这两个共和国时，西方只施加了适度的制裁。它现在不能再犹豫了。尽管俄罗斯已经开始建立堡垒经济，但这个国家仍然与世界联系在一起，正如俄罗斯股市最初45%的下跌所表明的那样，它将遭受损失。

True, sanctions will harm the West, too. Oil prices soared above $100 a barrel on the invasion. Russia is Europe’s main supplier of gas. It exports metals like nickel and palladium and along with Ukraine it exports wheat. All of that will present problems at a time when the world economy is struggling with inflation and supply-chain glitches. And yet, by the same measure, the fact that the West is prepared to suffer for sanctions sends Mr Putin the message that it cares about his transgressions.

诚然，制裁也会损害西方。俄罗斯入侵之后，石油价格飙升至每桶100美元以上。俄罗斯是欧洲主要的天然气供应国。它出口镍和钯等金属，并与乌克兰一道出口小麦。在全球经济正与通胀和供应链阻塞问题作斗争之际，所有这些都将带来问题。然而，以同样的标准衡量，西方准备承受制裁的事实向普京传递了这样一个信息:它们在意普京的越权行为。

A second task is to reinforce NATO’s eastern flank. Until now, the alliance has sought to live within the pact signed with Russia in 1997, which limits NATO operations in the former Soviet bloc. NATO should rip it up and use the freedoms that creates to garrison troops in the east. That will take time. Meanwhile NATO should prove its unity and intent by immediately deploying its 40,000-strong rapid-reaction force to the frontline states. These troops will add credibility to its doctrine that an attack on one member is an attack on all. They will also signal to Mr Putin that the further he pushes in Ukraine, the more likely he is to end up strengthening NATO’s presence on its border—the very opposite of what he intends.

第二项任务是加强北约的东翼。到目前为止，北约一直寻求遵守1997年与俄罗斯签署的协议，该协议限制了北约在前苏联地区的行动。北约应该将其撕毁，并利用由此带来的自由在东部驻军。这需要时间。与此同时，北约应该立即向前线国家部署其4万多名快速反应部队，以证明其团结和意图。这些部队将增加其信条的可信性，对一个成员的攻击就是对所有成员的攻击。他们也会向普京发出信号:他在乌克兰的行动越深入，他就越有可能加强北约在其边境的存在，这与他的意图恰恰相反。

And the world should help Ukraine defend itself and its people. They will bear the burden of the suffering. Only hours into the war came the first reports of military and civilian deaths. Volodymyr Zelensky, the president, called on his compatriots to resist. They must choose how and where to repel Mr Putin and his armies and proxies, should he install a puppet government in Kyiv. NATO is not about to deploy troops to Ukraine—rightly so, for fear of a confrontation between nuclear powers. But its members should give Ukraine assistance by providing arms, money and shelter to refugees and, if need be, a government in exile.

世界应该帮助乌克兰保卫自己国家和人民。他们将承受痛苦的负担。战争刚刚开始几个小时，就出现了第一批军人和平民死亡的报道。乌克兰总统Volodymyr Zelensky号召他的同胞们反抗。如果普京在基辅建立一个傀儡政府，他们必须选择如何以及在哪里击退普京及其军队和代理人。由于担心核大国之间的对抗，北约不会向乌克兰部署军队，这是正确的。但其成员国应向乌克兰提供援助，为难民提供武器、资金和庇护所，必要时还应组建流亡政府。

Some will say that it is too risky to challenge Mr Putin in these ways—because he has lost touch with reality, or because he will escalate, miscalculate or hug XX. That would itself be a miscalculation. After 22 years at the top, even a dictator with an overdeveloped sense of his own destiny has a nose for survival and the ebb and flow of power. Many Russians, unclear about a crisis that has come from nowhere, may be unenthusiastic about waging a deadly war against their brothers and sisters in Ukraine. That is something the West can exploit.

有些人会说，用这些方式挑战普京太冒险了，因为他已经脱离了现实，或者因为他会升级、误判或拥抱XX。这本身就是一种误判。在掌权22年之后，即使是一个对自己命运过于敏感的独裁者，也对生存和权力的涨落有着敏锐的嗅觉。许多俄罗斯人不清楚这场不知从何而来的危机，他们可能对自己在乌克兰的兄弟姐妹发动一场致命的战争不感兴趣。这是西方可以利用的。

Accommodating Mr Putin in the hope that he will start to behave nicely would be more dangerous still. Even XX should see that a man who rampages across frontiers is a threat to the stability it seeks. The freer Mr Putin is to advance today, the more determined he will be to impose his vision tomorrow. And the more blood will be spilled in finally getting him to stop. ■

迁就普京，希望他能开始表现得好一点，这将更加危险。就连XX也应该看到，一个越境横行的人对其寻求的稳定是一种威胁。普京今天越是自由地前进，他明天就越有决心将自己的愿景强加于人。更多的鲜血最终会让他停下来。■
