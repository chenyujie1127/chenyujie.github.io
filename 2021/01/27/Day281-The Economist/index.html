The marathon of covid-19 vaccination

新冠疫苗接种马拉松

Infections v injections

感染v注射

inoculate 给..接种

留意某人/某事物

即使政府在加紧推进疫苗接种计划，他们也需要密切关注接下来会发生什么

Jan 20th 2021

IT HAS BEEN described as a race between infections and injections. If so, infections are still winning. About 5m new cases of covid-19 a week are being recorded around the world. As we went to press, some 51 countries had begun to administer vaccines, according to Our World in Data, a website; over the previous week 17m people had been vaccinated, but the global total of doses was still below 50m. Only five countries had given the first dose to more than 5% of its population.

go to press 付印

administer 管理 ; 治理 ; 施行 ; 执行 ; 给予 ; 提供

它被描述为感染和注射之间的竞赛。如果是这样的话，感染仍是赢家。全球每周新增covid-19病例约500万例。在我们付印前，大约有51个国家已经开始接种疫苗，这是我们的世界数据网站提供的数据;在前一周，已有1700万人接种了疫苗，但全球总剂量仍低于5000万。只有五个国家向其5%以上的人口注射了第一剂疫苗。

The inoculation effort is generating frustration in countries like France, which got off to a slow start, and rejoicing in Britain, which has so far done well. Both the despair and the joy are premature. Plenty will happen in the months before most countries create enough immunity to suppress the spread of the virus. In the interim, much will depend on how successfully their governments manage lockdowns.

premature 过早的；仓促的

接种疫苗的努力在法国等国家产生了挫败感，法国开始时进展缓慢，而在英国则令人欣喜，到目前为止进展良好。绝望和快乐都是过早的。在大多数国家产生足够的免疫力来抑制病毒的传播之前，这几个月内会发生很多事情。在此期间，这在很大程度上将取决于它们的政府如何成功地管理封锁。

At the moment most of their energy is going into sorting out the logistics of vaccine distribution, which they can directly control. However, as Britain may be discovering, vaccine supply is what matters most. The good news is that more doses will become available, as manufacture scales up and new vaccines win regulatory approval. One from Johnson & Johnson, expected to report trial results late this month, could inoculate 1bn people during 2021.

目前，他们的大部分精力都用在了疫苗配送的物流上，这是他们可以直接控制的。然而，正如英国可能会发现的那样，疫苗供应才是最重要的。好消息是，随着生产规模的扩大和新疫苗获得监管部门的批准，将会有更多的疫苗可供使用。强生(Johnson & Johnson)的一款疫苗预计将于本月晚些时候报告试验结果，2021年可为10亿人接种疫苗。

While countries wait for supplies, the central role in keeping the virus at bay will be played by non-pharmaceutical interventions (NPI), including masks and lockdowns**.** Modelling from Britain suggests vaccination’s benefits will take time to show up in intensive-care wards. These are full of people in their 50s and 60s because those older than this are often too frail for ventilators and other interventions. When intensive-care wards are full, mortality is a quarter higher than expected. If highly infectious new variants of the virus take hold, NPI regimes may even need to be tightened, as Germany’s was this week.

ventilator 呼吸机

take hold 蔓延开来；变得根深蒂固

在各国等待供应物资的同时，非药物干预(NPI)将发挥遏制病毒的核心作用，包括口罩和封锁。英国的模型显示，疫苗接种的好处需要一段时间才能在重症监护病房体现出来。这些都是五六十岁的人，因为超过这个年龄的人往往太虚弱，无法使用呼吸机和其他干预措施。当重症监护病房人满为患时，死亡率比预期高出四分之一。如果具有高度传染性的新型病毒变种流行起来，NPI制度甚至可能需要加强，就像德国本周所做的那样。

![image-20210127102711843](/Users/chenyujie/Library/Application Support/typora-user-images/image-20210127102711843.png)

Lockdowns impose a burden on freedom and a heavy financial cost. Pressure will therefore mount for vaccinated people to be able to move around freely, even if at first they will be too few in number to make much difference to the economy. If governments ignore calls for “vaccine passports” to permit this, the vaccinated are likely to change their behaviour regardless. Some businesses may introduce informal systems.

call for sth 要求；需要

They are calling for talks to be extended.

他们要求延长会谈。

This calls for a celebration!

这该庆祝一下！

That was not called for!

多此一举

封锁给自由带来负担，并造成沉重的财政成本。由于接种疫苗的人能够自由活动，因此将会面临更大的压力，即使一开始接种疫苗的人数量太少，无法对经济产生很大影响。如果政府无视“疫苗护照”的要求，那么接种疫苗的人很可能会改变他们的行为。一些企业可能会引入非正式的制度。

For vaccine passports to make practical sense, epidemiologists need to understand how well vaccination stops people spreading the disease—because it would be harmful if those who were safe infected others yet to receive a jab. Early work from Israel, which has inoculated a greater share of its population than any other country, suggests that the Pfizer-BioNTech vaccine does indeed slow transmission a bit, but more research is needed to inform an effective passport regime.

jab 打针

为了使疫苗护照具有实际意义，流行病学家需要了解疫苗在多大程度上阻止了人们传播疾病，因为如果那些安全的人感染了尚未接种疫苗的人，这将是有害的。以色列的早期工作表明，辉瑞-生物技术公司的疫苗确实减缓了一些传播，但是需要更多的研究来为有效的护照制度提供信息。

Vaccine passports also raise ethical questions. When so much rides on having one, the question of who gets a jab when will matter even more than it does today. If groups are arbitrarily pushed back in the queue, programmes will become mired in controversy. Vaccination must be free to rich and poor alike, and allocated by efficacy and clinical need. That can be hard to reconcile with the requirement not to waste scarce supplies, which argues for convenience.

ride on 取决于

arbitrarily 武断地

push back 推迟；往后推；使..后移

efficacy 功效

疫苗护照也引发了伦理问题。在如此多的事情都取决于拥有“疫苗护照”的时候，谁何时接种疫苗的问题将比今天更重要。如果团体排在队伍后面，项目将陷入争议。疫苗接种必须对富人和穷人都是免费的，并根据疗效和临床需要进行分配。这很难与不浪费稀缺资源的要求相协调，后者认为这是为了方便。

There are complications, too. If vaccinated people can roam, those with acquired immunity will clamour for the same freedom: they are no more infectious, after all. Yet antibody tests are unreliable. It is not clear how long such immunity lasts or, in individuals, when they actually caught the disease and the clock started ticking. Some fear the implications for privacy if the state can check on citizens’ health. In principle, vaccine passports are a good idea. In practice, governments face a host of questions—some of which can be answered only through more epidemiological research. They need to start work on it today. ■

acquired immunity 获得免疫，后天免疫

clamour 大声疾呼

clamour for sb to do sth 大声疾呼要某人做某事

clamour for/against sth/sb 大声疾呼支持/反对某事物/某人

这其中也有复杂的因素。如果接种过疫苗的人可以漫游，那些获得性免疫的人也会大声要求同样的自由:毕竟他们不再具有传染性。然而抗体测试并不可靠。目前还不清楚这种免疫能持续多久，也不清楚个人何时真正感染上这种疾病，并开始计时。一些人担心，如果国家可以检查公民的健康，会对隐私造成影响。原则上，疫苗护照是个好主意。在实践中，政府面临着一大堆问题——其中一些只有通过更多的流行病学研究才能得到答案。他们需要今天就开始工作。■
