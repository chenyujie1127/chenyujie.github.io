From “techlash” to “techslog”

Ludwig Siegele: US technology editor, The Economist, SAN FRANCISCO

2021 IN BRIEF

![image-20210108100040189](/Users/chenyujie/Library/Application Support/typora-user-images/image-20210108100040189.png)

Reining in the tech giants, whether through lawsuits or legislation, will take time

参考译文：

今年3月【时间】，特斯拉的老板埃隆·马斯克(Elon Musk)【人物】在特拉华州【地点】出庭受审，他被控违反受托责任，并通过特斯拉2016年收购SolarCity(一家由他的堂兄弟创办的公司，但财务状况不佳)牟取暴利。

“A STRONG AND widespread negative reaction to the growing power and influence of large technology companies, particularly those based in Silicon Valley.” That is how the Oxford English Dictionary (OED) defines “techlash”. The word, popularised in these pages in 2013, has since been used in thousands of articles. Yet despite the term’s popularity, not a lot has actually happened to rein in the power of the tech titans. This will change in 2021. The techlash will begin in earnest—in the courts and in parliaments.

词汇积累&短语搭配：

techlash 指的是对大型科技巨头公司的批评与抵制。

参考译文：

《牛津英语词典》（OED）对“techlash”的定义是：“对大型科技公司日益增长的权力和影响的强烈且广泛的抵制，特别是对在硅谷的大公司。”。这个词自2013年起在这些网页上流行起来，此后在数千篇文章中被使用。然而，尽管这个词很受欢迎，但实际上并没有做什么事情来真正控制科技巨头的力量。在2021年，这种情况将会改变。对科技巨头公司的抵制将从法庭和议会正式开始。

Most headlines will be about the antitrust lawsuits in America. The most potentially consequential was filed by the Department of Justice, in conjunction with some states, against Alphabet in October 2020. It accuses Google’s parent of having illegally defended its dominance in online search. Epic Games, meanwhile, the maker of “Fortnite”, a popular online game, is pursuing a case against the way Apple controls access to its App Store and the 30% cut it takes on most transactions there. And the Federal Trade Commission may launch an antitrust suit against Facebook, accusing it of using its market power to stifle competition.

【这段主要讲对美国科技巨头的诉讼：包括Alphabet、Apple以及Facebook】

大多数头条新闻都是关于美国的反垄断诉讼。最具潜在影响的诉讼是美国司法部(Department of Justice)与一些州在2020年10月对Alphabet提起的诉讼。它指责谷歌的母公司非法维护其在在线搜索领域的主导地位。与此同时，流行的在线游戏《堡垒之夜》的开发商Epic Games正在就苹果公司控制其应用商店访问的方式，以及苹果公司从大多数交易中收取30%的提成提起诉讼。联邦贸易委员会可能会对Facebook发起反垄断诉讼，指控其利用其市场力量扼杀竞争。

Yet any of these lawsuits—and other, unexpected ones—could easily drag on for years. Policymakers across the Western world now mostly agree that **lengthy after-the-fact (ex post) court proceedings are not the best way to deal with tech giants. What is needed, they say, is a reform of antitrust law and forward-looking (ex ante) regulation. A committee of America’s House of Representatives published a report in October 2020 that lays out what such a reform might look like. It mainly calls for rules modelled on the Glass-Steagall Act, repealed in 1999, which separated investment and commercial banking to avoid conflicts of interest. An equivalent for the tech industry would separate digital platforms from the goods and services that are offered on top of them. In other words, Amazon would be banned from selling its own products within its online marketplace and Apple could no longer offer apps of its own.

然而，任何这些诉讼和其他意想不到的诉讼都很容易拖延数年。现在，西方世界的政策制定者大多认同，冗长的事后法庭程序不是对付科技巨头的最佳方式。他们说，现在需要的是反垄断法的改革和前瞻性的(事前的)监管。美国众议院的一个委员会在2020年10月发布了一份报告，阐述了这样一项改革可能的样子。它主要呼吁仿效1999年被废除的《格拉斯-斯蒂格尔法案》(Glass-Steagall Act)，该法案将投资银行和商业银行业务分开，以避免利益冲突。对科技行业而言，类似的举措将把数字平台与在其之上提供的商品和服务分开。换句话说，亚马逊将被禁止在其在线市场销售自己的产品，而且苹果将不能再提供自己的应用程序。

The European Union is planning to go even further. Its Digital Services Act, due to be unveiled by early 2021, will include rules on how big online firms should moderate content, deal with user complaints, treat rivals that also use their platforms, interconnect with competing services and share data. Google, for instance, could be forbidden from giving its own services a leg-up in its search results, and Facebook might be compelled to open up Messenger and WhatsApp, its messaging services, to allow interoperability with rival systems such as Telegram and Signal.

参考译文：

欧盟正计划更进一步。其《数字服务法》（Digital Services Act）将于2021年初公布，其中将包括大型网络公司应如何审核内容、处理用户投诉、对待同样使用其平台的竞争对手、与竞争对手的服务互联以及数据共享等规定。例如，谷歌（Google）可能被禁止在搜索结果中给自己的服务提供支持，Facebook可能被迫开放Messenger和WhatsApp这两种消息服务，以实现与Telegram和Signal等竞争对手系统的互操作性。

But critics of the tech titans should not get their hopes up. These legislative efforts to rein in the tech giants will also take time. The incoming Biden administration will probably want to deal with more pressing questions, such as the economy and health care, before turning to technology regulation. The eu is known to be slow: it took a decade to produce its landmark General Data Protection Regulation. And the longer the pandemic drags on, the more dependent people will find themselves on the services the tech giants provide, and the more entrenched their positions will become.

参考译文：

但批评科技巨头的人士不应抱太大希望。这些控制科技巨头的立法努力也需要时间。即将上任的拜登(Biden)政府可能会在转向技术监管之前，先处理更紧迫的问题，比如经济和医疗保健。众所周知，欧盟行动迟缓:它花了十年时间才制定出具有里程碑意义的《通用数据保护条例》(General Data Protection Regulation)。疫情拖得越久，人们就越依赖科技巨头提供的服务，他们的地位也就越稳固。

The word “techlash” evokes quick change. But if history is any guide, tech regulation will be a slow process, with many fits and starts: more hard slog than rapid whiplash. The word “techslog” would be more approp­riate—though it seems unlikely to make it into the OED.

词汇积累&短语搭配：

参考译文：

“techlash”这个词唤起了快速变化。但如果历史可以作为参考的话，科技监管将是一个缓慢的过程，有很多起起落落:与其说是快速的鞭策，不如说是艰难的跋涉。“techlog”一词可能更合适——尽管它似乎不太可能被收录进牛津英语词典。
