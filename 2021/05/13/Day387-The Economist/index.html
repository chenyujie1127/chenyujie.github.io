The transition from banking may make financial services cheaper, quicker and fairer. But it will threaten privacy and sovereignty and expand the role of state

银行业的转型可能会使金融服务变得更便宜、更快、更公平。但这将威胁到隐私和主权，并扩大国家的作用

May 6th 2021

NOBODY LIKES banks. Their technology is often primitive. Their users are hit with unpredictable fees. Their functions matter, yet their coverage is incomplete. This can relegate swathes of people in rich countries and entire populations of poor ones to the fringes of society. Many of the biggest make most profits from trading and fees, not providing services to ordinary customers. And worst of all, their failures can cause catastrophic damage for which they bear only a fraction of the costs.

relegate 降低..的地位

relegate sth to the scrap heap 把某物当作废物处理掉

没人喜欢银行。他们的技术通常很原始。他们的用户受到不可预测的费用的打击。它们的功能很重要，但覆盖范围并不完整。这可能使发达国家的大量人口和贫穷国家的全部人口处于社会边缘。许多大银行的大部分利润来自交易和收费，而不是向普通客户提供服务。最糟糕的是，它们的失败可能导致灾难性的损失，而它们只承担一小部分成本。

It would seem rational, therefore, to cheer the fierce new competition that is reducing banks’ traditional role. As capital markets expand, it is becoming easier to match assets with liabilities that naturally fund them, reducing the risk of bank runs or failures. A proliferation of payment technologies is upending how people and firms conduct their lives, giving millions of underserved customers previously unimaginable access to finance. Data can be used instead of assets to secure loans, making it possible for people and businesses to borrow money against their character, not their collateral. The great promise of finance can be offered to many more people. The great costs are diminished. Better-informed firms should also temper the instability inherent in today’s form of credit provision.

proliferation 激增

underserved 服务不完备的

collateral 抵押物

temper 缓和;使..下降

因此，为正在削弱银行传统角色的激烈新竞争欢呼似乎是合理的。随着资本市场的扩张，将资产与自然为其提供资金的负债相匹配变得越来越容易，从而降低了银行挤兑或倒闭的风险。支付技术的普及正在颠覆人们和公司的生活方式，为数百万未得到充分服务的客户提供此前无法想象的融资渠道。数据可以代替资产来获得贷款，这使得个人和企业可以根据自身的特性(而不是抵押品)来借钱。金融的伟大前景可以提供给更多的人。巨大的成本减少了。消息灵通的公司也应该缓和当前信贷规定形式固有的不稳定性。

But this revolution brings with it new problems. Because they benefit from network effects, payment platforms will tend to concentrate into just one or two giant intermediaries. As more activity migrates away from banks, existing monetary-policy mechanisms may become obsolete. The risk of losing control over the monetary system has lit a fire underneath usually staid central bankers. Projects to accelerate fast payment systems have cropped up worldwide. So have efforts to create central-bank digital currencies, a digital form of paper cash to compete with the digital payments of private giants. In China, where payments firms are most advanced, more than 100,000 people are already using a digital yuan in pilot projects.

staid 古板的

croop up 出现; 被提及

pilot 试用

但这场革命也带来了新的问题。由于受益于网络效应，支付平台往往会集中于一两家大型中介机构。随着更多的活动从银行转移出去，现有的货币政策机制可能会过时。失去对货币体系控制的风险，在通常古板的央行官员们下面点燃了一把火。加速快速支付系统的项目已经在世界各地涌现。创造央行数字货币的努力也是如此，央行数字货币是一种与私营巨头的数字支付相竞争的数字形式的纸币。在支付公司最先进的中国，已经有超过10万人在试点项目中使用数字人民币。

The roll-out of what amounts to a new monetary system thus comes with risks. Many of the issues it raises are as tricky as the financial stability and inclusion problems of old. As people move their money into CBDCs they will pull it out of banks, threatening the system developed to foster loans without overt state interference in credit allocation. The digital involvement of the central bank in everyday transactions would not only increase the risk of such interference but also be a gift to those wishing to snoop on citizens. And the concentration of the economy in digital money, whether it is run by private firms or by a central bank, could provide a new route for malicious actors to destabilise systems through cyberwarfare.

snoop 窥探

malicious 怀有恶意的

因此，相当于新货币体系的推出也伴随着风险。它引出的许多问题与过去的金融稳定和包容性问题一样棘手。当人们将资金转移到“央行数字货币”时，他们将从银行取出资金，这将威胁到为促进贷款而发展起来的、没有国家公开干预信贷分配的体系。央行对日常交易的数字化介入不仅会增加这种干预的风险，还会给那些希望窥探公民的人一份礼物。而且，无论是由私营企业还是央行运营，数字货币经济的集中，可能为恶意行为者通过网络战破坏系统稳定提供了一条新途径。

Yet there should be ways to minimise each of these problems. The best defence against the unhealthy spreading of state influence over lending decisions is to keep the current fractional-reserve system alive. This may mean constraining the scope of CBDCs and managing them at arm’s length from the central bank—perhaps through third parties. They should not pay interest and the value of their balances should perhaps be capped. CBDCs may risk empowering authoritarians, but in democratic countries there is usually adequate separation of powers within government to stop this.

keep sb at arm’s length figurative 与某人保持一定距离

然而，应该有办法将这些问题降到最低。防止国家对贷款决策的不良影响扩散的最佳方法是维持目前的部分准备金制度。这可能意味着限制央行数字货币的范围，让它们与央行保持一定距离，或许可以通过第三方进行管理。他们不应该支付利息，他们的余额价值或许应该被限制。央行数字货币可能会增加专制主义者的自主权，但在民主国家，政府内部通常有足够的权力分离来阻止这种情况的发生。

To some observers, it might seem better if central bankers in democratic, capitalist countries eschew this thicket of problems altogether. But the problem with abstaining from creating CBDCs is that network effects do not stop at national or currency boundaries. XXXX can reach 400m customers outside XX through its partners. Imagine a currency crisis in an emerging country a decade from now, when people and businesses can choose to make and collect payments in yuan instead of in their local currency, in the time that it takes to generate a QR code on a phone.

eschew 戒绝；避开

abstain from sth/doing sth 刻意回避/不做某事

在一些观察人士看来，如果民主资本主义国家的央行行长们能完全避开这一错综复杂的问题，情况可能会更好。但刻意不创建央行数字货币的问题在于，网络效应并不仅限于国家或货币边界。XXXX可以通过合作伙伴接触到XX以外的4亿客户。想象一下，10年后，一个新兴国家会发生货币危机，届时人们和企业可以选择使用人民币支付和收款，而非使用本国货币，只需花费在手机上生成二维码的时间。

For small countries, CBDCs may seem necessary merely to hang onto their monetary sovereignty. The bigger question is what the central bankers behind the world’s reserve currencies should do. If they do not issue their own digital money they leave open two possible paths. One is that they become reliant on private firms such as Facebook. The other is that their currencies are sidelined by those with easy-to-use digital money, notably XX.

hang on to 保留

sideline 把..排除在外；使靠边；使不再发挥重要作用；排挤

对于小国来说，央行数字货币似乎仅仅是为了保住其货币主权。更大的问题是，全球储备货币背后的央行行长们应该怎么做。如果他们不发行自己的数字货币，他们就留下了两条可能的路径。一是他们变得依赖像Facebook这样的私人公司。另一个原因是，他们的货币被那些拥有易于使用的数字货币的国家边缘化了，尤其是XX。

This risk may seem quite small for now. And XX capital controls still stand in the way. Moreover global citizens might value privacy more than cheap, instant, cross-border payments. But it is still unsettling, especially since digital money could become a method of social control. XX has already experimented with programming digital cash: it issued stimulus payments that expired if they went unused so as to kick-start the economy more effectively. In a world without physical cash, programmed money could restrict its use to stop it being spent on, say, foreign books or newspapers.

kick-start 推动

目前看来，这种风险似乎很小。XX的资本管制仍然是阻碍。此外，全球公民可能更重视隐私，而不是廉价、即时的跨境支付。但这仍令人不安，尤其是数字货币可能成为一种社会控制手段。XX已经尝试了编码的数字现金:为了更有效地推动经济，XX发放了刺激性资金，如果没有使用，这些资金就会到期。在一个没有实物现金的世界里，程序化的货币可以限制其的使用，以防止被用于购买外国书籍或报纸。

Central bankers that run reserve currencies can head off the potential nightmare of leaving Facebook or XX’s government in charge of global payment systems by issuing digital currencies of their own. If they do it sensibly, digital money could even become a force for good, not just a defence against disaster. “There is a parallel with biological evolution,” said Sir Jon Cunliffe of the Bank of England in March. Evolution is generally slow, but at times of geological or environmental change it can be rapid. A bewildering number of life forms may appear and many do not survive. This is “a helpful lens through which to look at what’s happening now,” Sir Jon suggested. When people look back at this period they will see banking in a period of very rapid change. The hope is that it may emerge in a better state.■

head off 拦截；阻止

通过发行他们自己的数字货币，管理储备货币的央行可以阻止让Facebook或XX政府负责全球支付系统的潜在噩梦。如果他们能明智地做到这一点，数字货币甚至可能成为一种向善的力量，而不仅仅是一种抵御灾难的手段。英国央行的Jon Cunliffe爵士在今年3月表示:“这与生物进化类似。”进化通常是缓慢的，但在地质或环境变化的时候，它可以是快速的。可能会出现数量惊人的生命形式，其中许多无法存活。Jon Cunliffe爵士表示：这是“一个观察现在正在发生的事情的有用的镜头”。当人们回顾这一时期时，他们会看到银行业正处于一个快速变化的时期。希望它能以更好的状态出现。■
