Opposition to the Tokyo Olympics is intensifying in Japan

在日本，反对东京奥运会的呼声越来越高

Faster, stronger, superspreader?

更快，更强，超级传播者?

Vaccinations are proceeding slowly. But organisers insist the games will be “safe and secure”

疫苗接种工作进展缓慢。但组织者坚称，奥运会将是“安全可靠的”

May 20th 2021

AS THE OLYMPIC torch passed through Hiroshima this week, organisers kept the relay away from the streets. A torch-lighting ceremony took place without spectators. Thomas Bach, the president of the International Olympic Committee (IOC), cancelled a planned visit. Hiroshima, along with Tokyo, the host city, and seven other prefectures, is under a state of emergency amid a fourth wave of covid-19.

relay 轮换者

prefecture （日本，法国，意大利等国的行政区划）县，州，区

本周，当奥运火炬在广岛传递时，组织者没有在街道上进行传递。一场没有观众的火炬点燃仪式举行了。国际奥委会主席Thomas Bach取消了原计划的访问。由于第四波covid-19疫情，广岛、主办城市东京和其他七个县进入紧急状态。

The event underlined the contortions required to host a global sporting extravaganza during a pandemic. Japan has so far avoided explosive outbreaks of the virus, with fewer than 12,000 deaths since the start of the pandemic, thanks in part to stringent border controls in place since last spring. But more than two-thirds of those fatalities have come this year. The latest wave, fuelled by more infectious variants of the virus, has strained regional health systems, while vaccinations have been slow to flow. The Olympics are scheduled to kick off in two months. About 15,000 athletes and perhaps 90,000 hangers-on from some 200 countries are set to enter Japan. Many Japanese worry that they will be the losers: 60% would rather not hold the games at all.

contortion 歪曲；扭曲

extravaganza 铺张奢华的表演

fatality 死者

kick off 开始

hanger-on 马屁精

[释义] a person who tries to be friendly and spend time with rich and important people, especially to get an advantage

这一事件突显了在大流行期间举办一场全球性体育盛会所需要的种种扭曲。到目前为止，日本避免了病毒的爆炸性爆发，自大流行开始以来，死亡人数不到1.2万人，这在一定程度上要归功于去年春天以来实施的严格边境控制。但是超过三分之二的死亡是在今年发生的。最近的一波疫情是由更具有传染性的病毒变体引发的，已经使地区卫生系统不堪重负，而疫苗接种却进展缓慢。奥运会定于两个月后开幕。来自约200个国家的约1.5万名运动员和约9万名随行人员*(不明白这个词的意思为什么是马屁精？**)*将进入日本。许多日本人担心他们会成为输家:60%的日本人宁愿根本不举办奥运会。

Organisers insist the Tokyo Olympics can be held safely. They point to recent virus-free sporting events, such as the Masters, an American golf tournament. They aim to create bubbles around the Olympic village and competition sites, to isolate participants from locals. Though vaccination will not be mandatory for athletes, Mr Bach claims that 80% of those in the Olympic village will be vaccinated by the start of the games. Foreign spectators will not be allowed to attend. A decision on local spectators is expected in June.

bubble 无菌室

组织者坚持认为东京奥运会能够安全举行。他们提到了最近没有病毒的体育赛事，比如美国的高尔夫锦标赛Masters。他们的目的是在奥运村和比赛场地周围制造无菌室，将参赛者与当地人隔离开来。尽管不会强制运动员接种疫苗，但Bach声称，奥运村80%的运动员将在奥运会开始前接种疫苗。外国观众将不被允许参加。有关当地观众的决定预计将于6月做出。

Many doctors and public-health experts remain unconvinced. Resources that could be used for testing, treating or vaccinating the local population may be diverted to the games. Increased movement of people within Japan, such as during public holidays in early May, has tended to beget rises in cases. With events planned in nine prefectures including Tokyo, training camps across the country, and volunteers coming from all over, more movement is inevitable—and with it, more cases, argues Taniguchi Kiyosu, the head of a big hospital in Mie prefecture and a co-author of an editorial in the British Medical Journal calling for the games to be cancelled. Dozens of towns have scrapped their plans to be bases for foreign teams to train.

editorial 社论

scrap 取消

许多医生和公共卫生专家仍然不相信。可用于检测、治疗或为当地居民接种疫苗的资源可能会被转移到奥运会上。日本国内人口流动的增加，比如5月初的公共假期，往往会导致病例增加。Taniguchi Kiyosu，三重县一家大医院的院长，也是《英国医学杂志》一篇呼吁取消奥运会社论的合著者, 声称：随着包括东京在内的9个县的活动计划，全国各地的训练营，以及来自各地的志愿者，更多的运动是不可避免的，也会有更多的病例。数十个城镇已经取消了作为外国球队训练基地的计划。

New viral variants complicate things even more. The more infectious B.1.1.7 strain already circulates widely. The convergence of people from across the world could allow yet more to enter and mutations to form. “Japan will become a mixing vessel,” says Mr Taniguchi. Participants could bring the virus back to their home countries, many of which have less robust health systems, says Oshitani Hitoshi, a virologist who sits on an expert panel advising the government. “Just one imported case can trigger a large outbreak,” he says.

sit on sth 是..的成员

新的病毒变种使事情更加复杂。传染性更强的B.1.1.7菌株已经广泛传播。来自世界各地的人们的汇聚可能会让更多的人进入并形成突变。Taniguchi表示:“日本将成为一艘混合船。”为政府提供咨询的专家小组成员、病毒学家Oshitani Hitoshi说，与会者可能会把病毒带回他们的国家，其中许多国家的卫生系统不够健全。仅仅一个输入病例就可能引发大规模疫情。

Japanese people might feel safer if they had as much protection from the virus as Olympic athletes. According to The Economist’s covid­19 tracker, just 4.5% of Japan’s adult population has received at least one dose of the vaccine, a shade behind Myanmar. Suga Yoshihide, Japan’s prime minister, wants to finish vaccinating the elderly by the end of July. But only two-thirds of Tokyo’s wards reckon they will be able to. Supply is not the problem. Nikkei, a business daily, recently estimated that at least 10m doses remain unused in freezers.

shade 些许

如果日本人能像奥运会运动员一样对病毒有同样多的防护，他们可能会感到更安全。根据《经济学人》对covid-19的追踪，只有4.5%的日本成年人至少接种过一剂疫苗，这一比例略低于缅甸。日本首相Suga Yoshihide希望在7月底前完成为老年人接种疫苗的工作。但是只有三分之二的东京的住院部认为他们能够做到。供应不是问题。日本经济新闻(Nikkei)，一家商业日报，最近估计至少有1000万剂疫苗在冰箱中未被使用。

Nor, at this stage, is the problem demand. Although Japanese tend to express a high degree of hesitancy about vaccines in general, that reluctance does not appear to extend to the covid-19 vaccine, says Shimoaraiso Makoto of Japan’s covid-19 task force. When reservations for a mass-vaccination centre in Osaka opened this week, the 25,000 slots on offer were booked up in around 25 minutes.

hesitancy 不愿

to have some hesitancy about doing sth 对是否做某事迟疑不决

在现阶段，需求也不是问题所在。日本covid-19特别工作组的Shimoaraiso Makoto说，尽管日本人一般倾向于对疫苗表现出高度的犹豫，但这种不情愿似乎没有扩展到covid-19疫苗。当大阪的一个大规模疫苗接种中心本周开始接受预订时，25000个名额在25分钟内就被预定一空。

Instead, logistical tangles and bureaucratic risk-aversion have slowed the roll-out. Booking websites and call centres have suffered technical glitches. The health ministry stuck to rules requiring local clinical trials for foreign vaccines. Only the Pfizer-BioNTech shot has been approved for use so far. Japan also lacks staff to administer vaccines, in part because Japanese law allows only registered doctors or nurses to do so. An exception has been made for dentists; officials are now wringing their hands over whether to let pharmacists give jabs too. A mistake by an inexperienced practitioner could have fa­tal knock­on effects, given Japan’s history of vaccine hesitancy, argues Mr Oshitani: “We are very, very sensitive to any adverse event in Japan. One single such event may halt the whole vaccination process.”

tangle 混乱

to wring one’s hands 扭绞双手 [表示焦虑、悲伤或绝望]

相反，后勤的混乱和官僚主义的风险规避延缓了该项目的推出。订票网站和呼叫中心都出现了技术故障。卫生部坚持要求外国疫苗在当地进行临床试验的规定。到目前为止，只有 Pfizer-BioNTech的疫苗被批准使用。日本也缺乏注射疫苗的工作人员，部分原因是日本法律只允许注册医生或护士这样做。牙医是例外;官员们现在正为是否让药剂师也进行注射而绞尽脑汁。鉴于日本在疫苗接种方面犹豫不决的历史，缺乏经验的从业人员的一个错误可能会产生致命的连锁反应，Oshitani认为:“我们对日本发生的任何不良事件都非常、非常敏感。一个这样的事件可能会终止整个疫苗接种过程。”

Nonetheless, the pace of vaccination is accelerating and likely to quicken more in the coming weeks. Japanese officials were expected to approve the Moderna and AstraZeneca vaccines as The Economist went to press. The defence ministry plans more mass-vaccination centres. That will please citizens unhappy with the government’s dawdling. More than 80% of Japanese believe the vaccine roll-out is moving too slowly. Approval of Mr Suga’s cabinet has in turn slid to record lows.

dawdle 拖延

尽管如此，疫苗接种的步伐正在加快，而且在未来几周内可能会进一步加快。在本期《经济学人》付印时，日本官员预计会批准Moderna和阿斯利康的疫苗。国防部计划建立更多的大规模疫苗接种中心。这将取悦那些对政府的拖沓感到不满的公民。超过80%的日本人认为疫苗的推广进展太慢。日本首相内阁的支持率也随之下滑至历史新低。

Cancelling the Olympics might help allay many voters’ suspicions that he is giving priority to the games over their health. But it is not as simple as it seems. Olympic host-city contracts give the IOC the exclusive right to call off the games. It remains eager to move forward. If the Japanese government were to cancel unilaterally, the IOC would have the right to seek damages in court, says Matsumoto Taisuke, a sports-law expert at Waseda University in Tokyo. Japanese officials also dread the impression they would create if Japan bailed out, while XX, its regional rival, pulls off the Winter Olympics in Beijing early next year. Instead, Mr Suga has taken to repeating a single phrase like a mantra: Japan, he says, will put on a “safe and secure games”. Much, from the fate of his premiership to the course of the pandemic around the world, **rides on that promise.**■

call off 取消；停止

dread 惧怕

bail out 放弃；退出

[释义] to stop doing or being involved with something

pull off 取得

ride on 取决于

取消奥运会可能有助于减轻许多选民的怀疑，即他将奥运会置于他们的健康之上。但这并不像看上去那么简单。奥运会主办城市合同赋予国际奥委会取消奥运会的独家权利。它仍然渴望向前迈进。东京早稻田大学的体育法律专家Matsumoto Taisuke表示，如果日本政府单方面取消比赛，国际奥委会将有权在法庭上寻求赔偿。日本官员还担心，如果日本退出，而其地区竞争对手XX明年初将在北京举办冬奥会，他们将会给人留下印象。相反，日本首相Suga开始像念咒语一样重复一句话:他说，日本将举办一场“安全可靠的比赛”。从他的首相生涯到全球疫情的过程，很多事情都依赖于这一承诺。■
