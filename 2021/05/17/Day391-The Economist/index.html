The Queen’s Speech reveals the meaning of Johnsonism

女王的演讲揭示了约翰逊主义的意义

British politics

英国政治

![image-20210517094801990](/Users/chenyujie/Library/Application Support/typora-user-images/image-20210517094801990.png)

The prime minister wants a big state that crimps civil liberties

crimp 束缚

总理想要一个限制公民自由的大国家

May 13th 2021

CONSERVATISM, AS PRACTISED by the British Conservative Party, is a capacious creed, open to a wide range of interpretations. For Margaret Thatcher, it meant the moral and economic discipline of the free market; for David Cameron, liberal centrism and the embrace of globalisation.

capacious 宽敞的; 容量大的

creed 信仰；信念

英国保守党奉行的保守主义是一种宽泛的信条，可以有各种各样的解释。对玛格丽特•撒切尔来说，这意味着自由市场的道德和经济纪律;对戴维•卡梅伦来说，自由中心主义和全球化的拥抱是最重要的。

Boris Johnson’s interpretation has been hard to decipher, partly because it has been obscured by the chaos of covid-19 and partly because he has never shown any commitment to a set of political ideas. So the Queen’s Speech, delivered on May 11th, in which the government presents its programme for the next session of parliament, was of particular interest. It was the clearest expression so far of what might one day be called Johnsonism.

decipher 辨认；理解

鲍里斯·约翰逊的解释很难解读，一方面是因为它被新冠疫情的混乱所掩盖，另一方面是他从未表现出对一套政治理念的承诺。因此，5月11日女王的演讲尤其引人关注，英国政府在演讲中提出了下一届议会的计划。这是迄今为止最清晰的表达，也许有一天会被称为约翰逊主义。

That Mr Johnson is now thought quite likely to be in power for long enough to have his own “ism” would have surprised many just a year ago. His management of the early stages of the pandemic was lethally slipshod, and there was much grumbling about his leadership. However, on May 6th the Conservatives thumped the Labour Party in a by-­election and in a series of local polls.

slipshod 马虎的；敷衍了事的

thump 重挫

约翰逊现在被认为很有可能继续执政到拥有自己的“主义”，这在一年前会让很多人感到惊讶。他对大流行早期阶段的管理是致命的粗心大意，人们对他的领导能力也有很多怨言。然而，5月6日在补缺选举和一系列地方民意调查中，保守党击败了工党。

Mr Johnson’s popularity springs from several sources. One is a successful vaccine roll­out, and consequent liberation of the population from lockdown. Another is Mr Johnson himself. His boisterous willingness to outrage liberal sensibilities goes down well outside the cities, and his ﬁnely tuned political instincts have led him to espouse a combination of cultural conservatism and statist economics that has more in common with Gaullism or Eisenhower’s “modern Republicanism” than it has with Thatcherism or Cameron­style conservatism.

boisterous 活泼好动的; 热闹的

sensibilities 感情

espouse 支持；拥护

statist 中央集权制经济的; 政府严控经济的

约翰逊先生的声望有几个来源。其中之一是疫苗的成功推出，随后将人们从封锁中解放出来。另一个是约翰逊本人。他对自由主义情感的强烈愤慨在城外得到了很好的认同，他敏锐的政治直觉使他推崇文化保守主义和中央集权经济学的结合，这种结合与戴高乐主义或艾森豪威尔的“现代共和主义”的共同点多于与撒切尔主义或卡梅伦式的保守主义的共同点。

Some of the intervention that the government promises is welcome. It plans to boost investment in R&D and to “level up” the country by splashing out on infrastructure and on vocational education, which both need money and attention. It promises to reform the planning system, which allows homeowners to veto development and thus condemns Britons to live in expensive rabbit-hutches.

splash out on sth 花大把的钱买某物

condemn sb to sth «circumstances, characteristic» 迫使…接受某状况 ‹person›

政府承诺的一些干预是受欢迎的。它计划增加研发投资，并通过在基础设施和职业教育上的大量投入来“提升”国家水平，这两方面都需要资金和关注。它承诺改革计划体系，该体系允许房主否决开发，从而使英国人生活在昂贵的兔笼中。

Mr Johnson’s solution to the problem of NIMBYism is to limit local authorities’ say on planning, giving central government more control over development. Whether or not he will really face down angry suburbanites in the Home Counties over new houses—he has already bottled out of a previous attempt—this approach derives from the fundamental problem with Johnsonism: his tendency to grab power. If local authorities do not want development, Mr Johnson’s answer is not to give them more say over taxation and thus an incentive to grow, but to force them to accept it. If parts of the country are poor, his answer is not to allow them to develop their own growth strategies, but to create a central fund to give them money.

NIMBYism 邻避主义 【Not In My Back Yard】

[背景] nimbyism就是这样一种社会现象：当人们发现住处附近要建一个不太受人欢迎的项目，如火葬场、监狱、化工厂、高速公路等时，人们就反对。这些居民就叫Nimby. 这种现象就叫Nimbyism. 国内发生的类似民众抗议活动都属于Nimbyism（字面意思是“不在我家后院”）.

facedown 决然把..压倒

suburbanite 郊区居民

the Home Counties 伦敦周围各郡

bottle out 因惧怕而放弃

约翰逊解决邻避主义问题的方法是限制地方当局在规划上的话语权，给予中央政府更多的发展控制权。不管他是否真的会在新房子的问题上面对愤怒的郊区居民（他已经在之前的尝试中因惧怕而放弃了），这种做法源于约翰逊主义的根本问题:他的夺权倾向。如果地方当局不想发展，约翰逊的答案不是在税收问题上给予他们更多的话语权，从而刺激经济增长，而是强迫他们接受。如果这个国家的某些地区很穷，他的答案是不允许他们制定自己的增长战略，而是创建一个中央基金来给他们钱。

The government is extending its control over the economy, too. Its plan for freeports is an attempt to direct investment to particular parts of the country. It is taking the opportunity that Brexit offers to give itself more discretion over handing out money to private companies and over using public procurement to further its levelling-up agenda. Both should send shivers down taxpayers’ spines.

discretion 自行决定的自由

send a shiver down sb’s spine 使某人不寒而栗

政府也在扩大对经济的控制。英国建立自由港的计划是试图将投资引向该国的特定地区。英国正利用英国脱欧带来的机会，在向私营企业发放资金和利用公共采购以推进其升级议程方面赋予自己更大的裁量权。两者都应该让纳税人不寒而栗。

The programme also includes a constitutional power-grab. Mr Johnson has had his sights set on the judiciary ever since the Supreme Court prevented him from proroguing Parliament over Brexit in 2019. That explains plans to limit the judges’ power to challenge the executive, as well as to restore the executive’s discretion over when to call an election, which Mr Cameron’s coalition government had renounced.

该计划还包括攫取宪法权力。自2019年最高法院阻止约翰逊因英国脱欧而延长议会休会以来，他就把目光投向了司法领域。这就解释了为何计划限制法官质疑行政机关的权力，以及恢复行政机关在何时举行选举方面的自由裁量权，卡梅伦的联合政府已经放弃了这一计划。

And as state power extends, so civil liberties will be crimped. The government has entertained new restraints on protest, limitations on asylum and voter-ID requirements. It looks set on meddling in culture and universities. These measures are all designed to press culturally conservative voters’ hot-buttons.

hot button 热点话题

随着国家权力的扩大，公民自由也会受到限制。政府对抗议活动、庇护和选民身份要求进行了新的限制。它似乎决意要干预文化和大学。这些措施都是为了给文化上保守的选民的热点话题施压。

Johnsonism unbound

For a prime minister who hopes to use his newly recovered mandate to call an early election and wants to be seen to be doing stuff, the extension of central-government power has a clear appeal. For this newspaper, which puts a high price on civil liberties and believes that smaller states and freer markets make for more prosperous countries, it does not. ■

对于一位希望利用新恢复的授权提前举行选举并希望被视为有所作为的总理来说，扩大中央政府的权力具有明显的吸引力。对于本刊来说，事实并非如此。本刊十分看重公民自由，并认为较小的国家和更自由的市场会使国家更繁荣，（扩大中央政府的权力）并不会（使国家更繁荣）。■
