Regardless of how covid-19 spread, we need to better prevent lab leaks

不管新冠如何传播，我们都需要更好地防止实验室泄漏

No more leaks

不再泄漏

Even a small reduction in the risk of another pandemic is worth the price

即使应对另一场大流行病风险只是小幅降低，这样的代价也值得付出

Aug 21st 2021

TO PREVENT THE next pandemic, it would help to know how this one started. However, the answer to that question might embarrass XX’s rulers. So they have obstructed investigations into how the virus emerged, and promoted far-fetched theories about it having originated somewhere else. The world thus has only a shaky understanding of what happened in XX province in the second half of 2019.

far-fetched 牵强的

shaky 靠不住的

为了预防下一次大流行病，了解这次疫情是如何开始的将会有所帮助。然而，这个问题的答案可能会让XX的领导人感到尴尬。所以，他们阻碍了对于“病毒是如何出现”的调查，并提出了一些牵强附会的理论，他们认为病毒起源于其他地方。因此，世界对2019年下半年XX省发生的事情不怎么了解。

American spooks are about to finish a 90-day review of covid-19’s possible origins, on President Joe Biden’s orders**. It is unlikely to reach a firm conclusion. Yet it is clear that** the answer XX least wants to hear—that the virus could have leaked from a lab in XX, or been picked up by a researcher collecting samples in the field—is plausible. And it would not be the first time this has happened. The flu pandemic of 1977, which killed 700,000 people, is now believed to have begun with a lab leak in north-east Asia—possibly in XX, possibly in the Soviet Union. With new laboratories being built all the time to handle dangerous pathogens, and with the era of synthetic biology just around the corner, the task of tightening lab biosafety has become urgent.

spook 间谍

(just) round or around the corner (around the bend) 即将来临

biosafety 生物安全性

根据拜登总统的命令，美国情报人员即将完成对covid-19可能来源的90天审查。但不太可能得到一个明确的结论。但很明显，XX最不想听到的答案貌似很合理，即病毒可能是从XX的一个实验室泄露出来的，或者病毒是由在该领域收集样本的研究人员发现的。这也不是第一次发生这样的事情了。1977年造成70万人死亡的流感病毒，现在被认为是由东北亚的实验室泄漏开始的，可能在XX，也可能在苏联。随着处理危险病原体的新实验室不断建立，随着合成生物学时代的到来，加强实验室生物安全的任务变得迫在眉睫。

People who work with deadly pathogens do so with scandalously little transparency or supervision. Rules in some places are not legally binding or subject to independent oversight. That has to change. The world needs to know who is working with which pathogens, where, and under what sort of safety protocols. (Intelligence agencies do not see this as part of their remit, but they should.) International standards are needed, and a global body to advise on the most dangerous kinds of work. This will be hard to accomplish, but there is a precedent. A committee of the World Health Organisation (WHO) monitors and inspects the two labs that hold smallpox, a deadly virus that was eradicated in the wild through vaccination, and reviews proposed research.

scandalously 骇人听闻地

remit 职权范围

那些与致命病原体打交道的人几乎都在极度缺乏透明度或监管的情况下工作。有些地方的规定不具有法律约束力，也不受独立监督。这种情况必须改变。全世界都需要知道谁在用哪种病原体，在哪里用以及在什么样的安全协议下工作。(情报机构不认为这是他们的职责范围，但他们应该这么做。)国际标准是必要的，而且还需要有一个全球性的机构对于最危险的工作提出建议。这将很难实现，但已有先例。世界卫生组织的一个委员会监测和检查了两个存放天花病毒（一种致命病毒，通过接种疫苗可以在野生环境里被消灭）的实验室，并审查了拟议的研究。

America, which could be leading the way, is not. Researchers who receive federal grants to study pathogens have to follow federal rules. Private labs are not so constrained. This will not do. Ideally there would be an agreed set of basic rules, enforced by a watchdog that is independent of the bodies that fund research. The backers of a project cannot be neutral arbiters of its safety.

美国本可以在这方面发挥带头作用，但现在却不是。接受联邦资助研究病原体的研究人员必须遵守联邦规定。私人实验室则没有这么多限制。这是不行的。理想情况下，应该要有一套商定的基本规则，由一个独立于资助研究的机构的监督机构来执行。一个项目的支持者不能对其安全性做中立的仲裁者。

Many other countries follow America’s lead on biosafety. So if it improves its rules and procedures, others will, too. That could be a step towards a proper set of global norms. And it would be in every country’s interest, even XX’s, to abide by such norms. Meetings this year to discuss a pandemic treaty offer a chance to harmonise rules on lab biosafety. Organisations such as the WHO, the G7 and the OECD could also do more to promote transparency and security.

许多其他国家也在生物安全方面效仿美国。因此，如果美国改善其规则和程序，其他国家也会这样做。这可能是朝着一套适当的全球规范迈出的一步。遵守这些规范符合每个国家的利益，甚至是XX的利益。今年讨论大流行病条约的会议给协调实验室生物安全规则提供了一个机会。世界卫生组织、七国集团以及经合组织等组织也可以在促进透明度和安全方面做得更多。

Governments need to ask whether certain kinds of research are too risky to be allowed. Gain-of-function research, which genetically alters organisms such as viruses in ways that can make them more dangerous, deserves special scrutiny. So does viral collection in the wild.

政府需要询问某些类型的研究是否因为风险太大而不能被允许。功能获得性研究是一种从基因上改变生物体(如病毒)，使其变得更危险的研究，它需要特别审查。野外的病毒收集也是如此。

New tools are needed as well as new rules. Real-time genomic surveillance at border crossings could catch novel pathogens as they emerge. Sewage and air could be sampled more widely. Ways to create vaccines even faster should be explored.

我们需要新的工具和规则。在边境口岸进行的实时基因组监测可以在新病原体出现时捕捉到它们。可以更广泛地对污水和空气取样。应该探索研制疫苗的更快地方法。

And if some scientists complain that all this will make their work costlier and harder, there is an obvious rejoinder. Perhaps 15m people have died directly of covid-19, and the resulting economic damage has impoverished many millions more. If better biosafety reduces the chance of another pandemic even slightly, it will be cheap at the price. ■

rejoinder 反驳

impoverish 使..贫穷

如果一些科学家抱怨这一切将使他们的工作更加昂贵和困难，那显然会有人反驳。可能有1500万人直接死于covid-19，由此造成的经济损失使数百万人陷入贫困。如果采取更好的生物安全性措施能稍微降低另一场大流行病的可能性，那么代价其实是很小的。■
