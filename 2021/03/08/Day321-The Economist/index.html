How to make a social safety net for the post-covid world

如何为后covid - 19世界构建社会保障体制

Welfare in the 21st century

21世纪的福利

![image-20210308122857584](/Users/chenyujie/Library/Application Support/typora-user-images/image-20210308122857584.png)

Governments must remake the social contract for the 21st century

政府必须重塑21世纪的社会契约

safety net（为面临重大困难且无助的人提供帮助的）安全网，保障体制

[释义] a system to help those who have serious problems and no other form of help

[例句]The welfare system was set up to provide a safety net for the poor.

建立福利制度以为贫困人口提供保障体制。

Mar 6th 2021

AFTER THE Depression and the second world war, voters and governments in rich countries recast the relationship between the state and its citizens. Now the pandemic has seen the old rules on social spending ripped up. More than three-quarters of Americans support President Joe Biden’s $1.9trn stimulus bill, which is due in the Senate and includes $1,400 cheques for most adults. And in the budget on March 3rd Britain extended a scheme to pay the wages of furloughed workers until September, even as public debt hit its highest level since 1945. Such boldness brings dangers: governments could stretch the public finances to breaking-point, distort incentives and create sclerotic societies. But they also have a chance to create new social-welfare policies that are affordable and which help workers thrive in an economy facing technological disruption. They must seize it.

rip up 撕毁

budget 预算案

breaking point 顶点

be at breaking point 达到极限

be close to breaking point 濒于崩溃

My patience had reached breaking point.

我的耐心已经到了极点。

sclerosis 硬化；僵化

institutional/economic sclerosis 机构/经济僵化

sclerotic：affected by sclerosis (= a medical condition in which body tissue or organs become harder)

大萧条和第二次世界大战之后，富裕国家的选民和政府重新塑造了国家和公民之间的关系。现在，大流行已经打破了关于社会支出的旧规则。超过四分之三的美国人支持乔·拜登总统1.9万亿美元的经济刺激法案，该法案将在参议院提交，其中包括向大多数成年人发放的1400美元支票。在3月3日的预算中，英国将向休假工人支付工资的计划延长到9月，尽管公共债务达到了1945年以来的最高水平。这种大胆做法带来了危险:政府可能会将公共财政拉伸到极限，扭曲激励机制，造成社会僵化。但他们也有机会创造出新的社会福利政策，这些政策负担得起，并帮助工人在面临技术颠覆的经济中茁壮成长。他们必须抓住机会。

The past year has seen a wild experiment in social spending. The world launched at least 1,600 new social-protection programmes in 2020. Rich countries have provided 5.8% of GDP on average to help record numbers of workers. Government debts are piling up, but so far low interest rates mean that they are cheap to service. The public’s mood had already been shifting. Britons used to grumble that layabouts sponged off the welfare state; now they are more likely to say help is too stingy. Last year over two-thirds of Europeans said they supported a universal basic income (UBI), an unconditional recurring payment to all adults. Affluent professionals have had their gaze drawn to the working conditions of those who deliver food and look after the sick. The struggles of women who have dropped out of the workforce to care for children and the elderly have become impossible to ignore.

layabout 懒汉

sponge 当食客

sponge on/off one’s friends 揩朋友的油

sponge off the state 依靠国家养活

stingy 吝啬的；微小的

drop out 退出

drop out (of school) 退学

He dropped out of politics. 他退出了政坛。

He dropped out of socirty. 他脱离了传统社会。

在过去的一年里，社会支出经历了一场疯狂的实验。到2020年，全世界至少启动了1600项新的社会保障计划。富裕国家平均提供了5.8%的GDP来帮助创纪录数量的工人。政府债务正在堆积，但到目前为止，低利率意味着它们的服务成本很低。公众的情绪已经发生了转变。英国人曾经抱怨说，懒汉们靠国家的福利过活;现在，他们更有可能说，帮助太吝啬了。去年，超过三分之二的欧洲人表示他们支持全民基本收入(UBI)，这是一种对所有成年人无条件的经常性支付。富裕的专业人士把目光投向了那些送餐和照顾病人的工作环境。那些为了照顾孩子和老人而离开工作岗位的女性的挣扎已经变得不可忽视。

The social safety net in many rich countries was creaking before covid-19 struck. Modelled on the ideas of Otto von Bismarck and William Beveridge, it had often failed to cushion workers from globalisation and technological and social change. In 1999-2019 the number of Americans aged 25-54 outside the labour force grew by 25%, or 4.7m, over six times more than the number who received help from the main assistance programme for displaced workers. As health-care and pension costs soared in recent years, governments cut back support for working-age people. Between 2014 and 2018 Britain’s state-pension bill grew in real terms by £4bn ($5.8bn), even as the rest of its welfare budget shrank by £16.5bn. A dwindling share of middle-income jobs and the growth of the gig economy fuelled fears that labour markets were changing faster than flat-footed governments could.

cushion 缓和..的冲击；使免遭损害

cushion sb/sth against/from sth 使某人/某物免遭某物的伤害

flat-footed 毫无准备的

在covid-19爆发之前，许多富裕国家的社会保障网络就已经不堪重负。它以奥托•冯•俾斯麦(Otto von Bismarck)和威廉•贝弗里奇(William Beveridge)的思想为范本，往往未能使工人免受全球化、技术和社会变革的影响。1999年至2019年，美国25-54岁的非劳动力人口增长了25%，即470万，比从主要援助计划中获得帮助的失业工人人数多6倍。随着近年来医疗和养老金成本的飙升，政府削减了对适龄劳动人口的支持。2014年至2018年，英国的国家养老金支出实际增长了40亿英镑(合58亿美元)，而其他福利预算却缩减了165亿英镑。中等收入就业岗位所占比例不断下降，零工经济(gig economy)的增长，加剧了人们的担忧，即劳动力市场的变化速度快于毫无准备的政府。

With the public and some economists cheering on, it is tempting for politicians to stoke the economy with more ad hoc spending, or put in place vast schemes such as UBI. Instead they need to take a measured, long-term view. The safety net must be affordable. Tight budgets, not milk and honey, will define the 2020s. The annual deficit of big advanced economies was 4% of their combined GDP even before the pandemic—and much ageing is still to come. Already bond yields are rising again . Social spending must flow quickly and automatically to those who need it—not, as in America, only during crises when a panicked government passes emergency legislation. And governments need to find mechanisms that cushion people more effectively against income shocks and joblessness without discouraging work or crushing economic dynamism.

cheer on 为..加油

ad hoc 临时的

land of milk and honey：

[释义]A country where living conditions are good and people have the opportunity to make a lot of money

[例句] Many Mexicans regard the United States as a land of milk and honey.

在公众和一些经济学家的支持下，政治家们倾向于通过更多的临时支出来刺激经济，或者实施大规模的计划，如UBI。相反，他们需要采取慎重的、长期的观点。保障体制必须是负担得起的。21世纪20年代将是预算紧张的年代，而不是简单的日子。甚至在大流行之前，大型发达经济体的年度赤字就已经占到其GDP总和的4%，而且很多老龄化问题还在继续。债券收益率已经再次上升。社会支出必须迅速而自动地流向那些需要它的人——不像在美国，只有在危机期间惊慌失措的政府通过紧急立法时才会如此。政府需要找到一种机制，在不阻碍工作或破坏经济活力的情况下，更有效地使人们免受收入冲击和失业的影响。

The first step towards satisfying these goals is to use technology to make ancient bureaucracies more efficient. Postal cheques, 1980s mainframe computers and shoddy data need to be relegated to the past. In the pandemic many governments temporarily short-circuited their existing systems because they were too slow. In Estonia and Singapore digital-identification systems and a disdain for form-filling became an asset in the crisis. More countries need to copy them and also to ensure universal access to the internet and bank accounts. The call for efficient administration may sound like tinkering but one in five poor Americans eligible for wage top-ups fails to claim them. Nimbler digital-payment systems will reduce the need for costly universalism as a fail-safe, and allow better targeting and quicker response times. Digital systems also permit the emergency option of making temporary cash payments to all households.

relegate 降级

short circuit 绕过

disdain 鄙视

nimble 灵活的；敏捷的；敏锐的

fail-safe 有自动保险装置的；万无一失的

实现这些目标的第一步是利用技术提高陈旧的/年老的官僚机构的效率。邮政支票、上世纪80年代的大型计算机和劣质数据需要被抛到脑后。在大流行期间，许多政府暂时绕过了他们现有的系统，因为它们太慢了。在爱沙尼亚和新加坡，数字识别系统和对填写表格的不屑成为危机中的一项资产。更多的国家需要复制它们，并确保普遍使用互联网和银行账户。要求有效管理的呼声听起来像是小修小补，但有五分之一有资格获得增加工资的美国穷人没有申请到。更灵活的数字支付系统将减少昂贵的通用性作为故障保险的需要，并允许更好的针对性和更快的响应时间。数字系统还允许向所有家庭提供临时现金支付的紧急选择。

That is the easy part. Balancing generosity and dynamism is harder. Part of the solution is to top up the wages of low-paid workers. Anglo-Saxon countries have done this well since reforms in the 1990s and 2000s. But wage top-ups are of little use to the jobless and are often scant compensation for people who lose good jobs to forces beyond their control. Paltry support for the unemployed in Britain and America preserves incentives to work but at high human cost. The sparsity of social insurance has undermined political support for creative destruction, the catalyst for rising living standards. Continental Europe tends to underwrite traditional workers’ incomes more generously. But the distortion of incentives leads to higher unemployment and divisions between coddled insiders and a precariat. Both sides of the Atlantic lack a permanent safety net that insures gig workers and the self-employed.

Anglo-Saxon 基于英国习俗的；受英国习俗影响的

Continental Europe 欧洲大陆

coddle 娇惯

insider 圈内人；知情者

the precariat 贫困、没有固定工作的）朝不保夕的人

这是容易的部分。要平衡慷慨和活力是很困难的。解决方案之一是提高低收入工人的工资。自20世纪90年代和21世纪初的改革以来，受英国习俗影响的国家在这方面做得很好。但是，增加工资对于失业者来说用处不大，对于那些因无法控制的力量而失去好工作的人来说，通常也没有多少补偿。在英国和美国，对失业者的微不足道的支持保留了工作的动力，但却付出了高昂的人力成本。社会保险的匮乏削弱了对创造性破坏的政治支持，而创造性破坏是提高生活水平的催化剂。欧洲大陆倾向于更慷慨地为传统工人的收入提供担保。但激励机制的扭曲导致失业率上升，以及娇惯的内部人士和朝不保夕的人之间的分歧。大西洋两岸都缺乏一个永久性的保障措施，以保障小型企业工人和个体经营者。

There is one country that combines labour-market flexibility with generosity: Denmark, which spends large sums—1.9% of GDP in 2018—on retraining and on advising the jobless. These interventions stop the unemployed from falling into dependency. The inadequacies of policies elsewhere are often glaring. Britain’s efforts have flopped. America’s comparable spending is less than a 20th as large as Denmark’s, even though the few lucky beneficiaries of its “trade-adjustment assistance” earn $50,000 more in wages, on average, over a decade.

flop 彻底失败地

beneficiary 受益人

有一个国家将劳动力市场的灵活性与慷慨相结合:丹麦，它在再培训和失业咨询上花费了大量资金——2018年占GDP的1.9%。这些干预措施防止失业者陷入依赖状态。其他地方政策的不足之处往往很明显。英国的努力失败了。尽管美国“贸易调整援助”的少数幸运受益者在十年间平均多挣5万美元，但美国的类似支出还不到丹麦的20分之一。

Bungee economics

For years social spending has favoured the elderly and an outdated safety net. It should be rebuilt around active labour-market policies that use technology to help everyone from shopworkers who are victims of disruption to mothers whose skills have atrophied and those whose jobs are replaced by machines. Governments cannot eliminate risk, but they can help ensure that if calamity strikes, people bounce back.■

atrophy 萎缩；衰退

bounce back 重整旗鼓

多年来，社会支出有利于老年人和过时的保障措施。它应该围绕积极的劳动力市场政策重建，这些政策利用技术帮助每一个人，从遭受破坏的商店工人到技能萎缩的母亲和工作被机器取代的人。政府无法消除风险，但它们可以帮助确保，在灾难发生时，人们会重新
