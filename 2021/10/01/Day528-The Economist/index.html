South Korea’s plan to fight fake news could hobble real journalism

韩国打击假新闻的计划可能会阻碍真正的新闻业

Off-target

偏离目标

![image-20211001110553764](/Users/chenyujie/Library/Application Support/typora-user-images/image-20211001110553764.png)

The ruling party wants to make it easier for people to sue the media for “emotional distress”

执政党希望让人们起诉那些造成他们“精神损失”的媒体更容易些

WHEN LAW MAKERS from South Korea’s ruling Minjoo party discuss their plan to impose tougher penalties on the media for reporting “fake news”, they stress that they merely want to safeguard vulnerable citizens. “It’s the least we can do to protect the people from fake news,” one legislator told reporters earlier this month. “We have to remember that our people’s basic rights and freedoms are more important than the freedom of the press,” said another. The proposed changes to the Press Arbitration Act of 2011, the party argues, would improve the quality of reporting and increase the public’s trust in it.

当韩国执政党共同民主党的立法者讨论他们对报道“假新闻”的媒体施加更严厉惩罚的计划时，他们强调，他们只是想保护脆弱的公民。本月早些时候，一名立法者告诉记者:“这是保护人们免受假新闻侵害的最起码的举措。另一个立法者说：“我们必须牢记，我们人民的基本权利和自由比新闻自由更重要”。共同民主党认为，对2011年《媒体仲裁法》的拟议修改将提高报道质量，并增加公众对其的信任。

Hogwash, says the conservative opposition, which quashed a first attempt to pass the bill last month by threatening a filibuster. The proposal, it argues, is an attempt by the government to shield itself from criticism. Other observers appear to agree. Last week, a coalition of activist groups including Human Rights Watch called on legislators from all parties to reject the bill when it is put to another vote in the National Assembly on September 27th.

hogwash 废话，胡说八道，瞎话

filibuster 阻挠行动

quash 撤销；否决

上个月，反对党保守党威胁要阻挠议案通过，从而使第一次通过议案的努力付诸东流。其辩称，该提议是政府试图保护自己免受批评的一种尝试。其他观察人士似乎也同意这一观点。上周，包括人权观察在内的一个激进组织联盟呼吁所有党派的立法者在9月27日国民议会再次投票时否决该法案。

The bill introduces new provisions allowing individuals and small businesses to go after reporters and media organisations for, among other things, causing “emotional distress” by spreading false information either intentionally or as a result of “gross negligence”, for instance without fact-checking. Critics worry the vague wording of some of the clauses would give the authorities too much discretion to decide which reports qualify for corrections or damages. A letter from the UN’s special rapporteur on freedom of expression, sent to the government in August, warned that the law could be used to limit ordinary news reporting and criticism of the government, and could see writers held liable for parody and satire or simply for expressing unpopular opinions.

go after 追赶；追求

rapporteur 特派调查员

parody 滑稽模仿作品

该法案引入了新的条款，允许个人和小企业追究记者和媒体机构故意传播虚假信息或因“重大疏忽”(例如未经核实)而造成“精神损失”等行为。批评人士担心，一些条款的模糊措辞将给当局太多的自由裁量权，以决定哪些报道符合惩罚或损害赔偿的条件。联合国言论自由特派调查员在8月寄给政府的一封信中警告说，这项法律可能被用来限制普通的新闻报道和对政府的批评，而且可能会让作家为模仿和讽刺或仅仅是表达不受欢迎的观点而被追究责任。

The ruling party’s concerns are not entirely unfounded. Like people everywhere, South Koreans are exposed to mountains of humbug. A noisy minority of mostly elderly right-wingers watch YouTube channels that spread misinformation about vaccines, American politics and President Moon Jae-in’s supposed chumminess with North Korea, the XX, the devil, or all three. Even beyond the radical fringe, most people get their news from online aggregators whose algorithms reward sensationalist clickbait and which take little responsibility for sourcing. The biggest newspapers have long-standing links to big conglomerates; the heads of public broadcasters change with the government. Unsurprisingly, less than a third of South Koreans say they trust the news.

unfounded 无根据的

humbug 欺骗行为

sensationalist 追求轰动效应者

执政党的担忧并非毫无根据。像世界各地的人一样，韩国人身处于堆积如山的谎言中。一小部分吵吵嚷嚷的右翼人士(主要是上了年纪的右翼人士)观看YouTube频道，这些频道传播有关疫苗、美国政治以及文在寅总统与朝鲜、XX、魔鬼或三者的所谓亲密关系的错误信息。即使在极端边缘之外，大多数人也从在线“信息聚合网站”获取新闻，这些“信息聚合网站”的算法会奖励那些哗众取众的“标题党”，它们对信息来源几乎不负责。最大的报纸与大企业集团有着长期的联系; 公共广播公司的负责人随政府更换。不出所料，不到三分之一的韩国人表示相信新闻。

But if legislators are indeed trying to solve these problems and bolster their democracy, they are going about it in a curious way, says Park Kyung-sin, a law professor and free-speech activist at Korea University in Seoul. Most fake news and conspiracy-mongering originates on social media, he notes. But the law only applies to newspapers and broadcasters, leaving the main source of falsehoods untouched. Mr Park suspects the party’s real motive is a feeling of being under siege by the press: they find it hard to accept that, in government, “they’re being criticised by all journalists, not just the right-wing media”.

但是，首尔高丽大学的法学教授、言论自由活动家Park Kyung-sin说，如果立法者确实试图解决这些问题，并加强他们的民主，他们是以一种奇怪的方式进行的。他指出，大多数假新闻和阴谋论都起源于社交媒体。但该法律只适用于报纸和广播公司，而不触及谎言的主要来源。Park先生怀疑这个政党的真正动机是一种被媒体包围的感觉:他们发现很难接受，在政府中，“他们受到所有记者的批评，而不仅仅是右翼媒体”。

It is all the more surprising given that press freedom in South Korea has improved significantly since Mr Moon took power in 2017. South Korea climbed 30 places in a global press freedom index compiled by Reporters Without Borders, a watchdog, after his election. And it is an unusual bill for a liberal democracy to propose, carrying as it does elements of similar laws passed in recent years by bossier places such as Singapore.

bossy 爱发号施令的

考虑到自文在寅2017年上台以来，韩国的新闻自由大幅改善，这就更令人惊讶了。在他当选后，韩国在监督机构无国界记者编制的全球新闻自由指数中上升了30位。对于一个自由民主国家来说，这是一项不同寻常的法案，因为它包含了新加坡等更强势的国家近年来通过的类似法律的内容。

The many complaints have not fallen on deaf ears. Minjoo legislators have responded by drafting a revised version of the bill. But it still fails to resolve the main worry, that the law would unduly restrict speech. Given the party’s absolute majority, it is likely to pass, unless legislators decide to withdraw it. If it does, South Korea will surely drop a few places in next year’s press-freedom ranking. ■

许多抱怨并没有被置若罔闻。共同民主党议员已经起草了修订案作为回应。但它仍未能解决主要的担忧，即该法律将过度限制言论。考虑到该党的绝对多数，它很可能会通过，除非立法者决定退出。如果真的通过，韩国在明年的新闻自由指数排名中肯定会下降几个名次。■
