The Biden presidency is likely to be heading towards gridlock

拜登总统很可能会陷入僵局

The World Ahead 2022

The president may resort to other ways to enable change

总统可能会采取其他方式来促成变革

Nov 8th 2021

by Idrees Kahloon: Washington correspondent, The Economist, Washington, DC

Idrees Kahloon:《经济学人》驻华盛顿记者，华盛顿特区

It is a harsh reality that 2022 is likely to be the last serviceable year of the Biden administration. Gridlock is endemic to the American system. It originates in a constitutional design premised on consensus, which seems increasingly unattainable in an era of hyperpolarisation, an effective supermajority requirement in the Senate and razor-thin majorities. Even under perfect conditions, when the White House and both chambers of Congress are under one party’s unified control, serious legislating is fiendishly difficult. It is not a coincidence that both Barack Obama and Donald Trump passed the most important laws of their administrations—a health-care reform and a large tax cut, respectively—in the first two years of their terms. Both suffered serious losses in the mid-term elections, losing control of one chamber, and with it the ability to legislate as they had hoped.

2022年很有可能成为拜登政府任期的最后一年，这是残酷的现实。陷入僵局是美国体系的通病。它起源于一种以共识为前提的宪法设计，在一个极度两极分化的时代，共识似乎越来越难以实现，这种共识是参议院的一种有效的绝对多数要求，也是极其微弱的多数。即使在完美的条件下，当白宫和国会参众两院都在一个政党的统一控制下，严肃的立法也是极其困难的。奥巴马和特朗普在他们任期的头两年分别通过了他们政府中最重要的法律，即医疗改革和大规模减税，这并不是巧合。两人在中期选举中都遭受了严重的损失，失去了对一个议院的控制权，也失去了他们所希望的立法能力。

President Joe Biden seems set to follow this unsatisfactory path. Although he managed to pass a successful stimulus bill at the start of his administration, his signature proposal—enormous expenditure on climate-change mitigation and European-style safety-net programmes, paid for by significantly higher taxes on the wealthy, and collectively known as “Build Back Better”—got stuck in legislative quicksand for much of 2021. As we went to press, warring factions of his Democratic Party were struggling to strike a deal. Something may yet pass, though the eventual compromise seemed likely to be a fraction of the $4trn in spending Mr Biden had hoped for. Yet even a partial accomplishment may look sizeable compared with what will come next.

美国总统乔•拜登似乎将走上这条不令人满意的道路。尽管在他执政一开始时，他成功地通过了一项刺激法案，他的签名提案在2021年的大部分时间里都陷入了立法困境，提案内容要求在减缓气候变化和欧洲式的安全网项目上投入大笔钱，这笔巨额支出通过对富人大幅增税来筹集，这一提案被统称为“重建更好未来”。当我们付印时，他所在的民主党的交战派系正在努力达成协议。尽管最终达成的妥协似乎只是拜登所希望的4万亿美元开支的一小部分，但仍有一些事情可能会获得通过。然而，与即将到来的相比，即使只是部分成就，也可能显得相当可观。

Election losses augur a lost final two years of Mr Biden’s term, at least legislatively speaking. Democrats have five seats to spare in the House of Representatives; they have zero seats to spare in the Senate. Only twice since 1938 has an incumbent president managed to see his party’s position in the House expand—and on both occasions the fortunate president also commanded an approval rating above 60%. Unfortunately for Mr Biden, his rating is a comparatively measly 44%. Based on historical associations, that is in line with a loss of 33 seats—spelling a loss of control in the House. Things look rosier for Democrats in the Senate, where only one-third of seats are contested every two years, and the is weaker. But the American system requires the consent of both chambers, and Republican support would not be forthcoming for any of the priorities that Democrats have campaigned on.

选举失败预示着拜登最后两年任期的失败，至少从立法上来说是这样。民主党在众议院还剩5个席位; 他们在参议院没有多余的席位。自1938年以来，在任的总统只有两次成功地让他的政党在众议院的地位扩大，而且这两次幸运的总统都获得了超过60%的支持率。不幸的是，拜登的支持率仅为44%。根据历史联系，这与失去33个席位是一致的，这意味着在众议院失去控制。民主党在参议院的情况看起来比较乐观，因为每两年只需要争夺三分之一的席位。但是，美国的体系需要得到参众两院的同意，而民主党在竞选中提出的任何优先事项都不会得到共和党的支持。

Optimists argue that Democrats could still accomplish much before November 2022—even in the face of poor electoral prospects. Perhaps. But if Mr Biden’s agenda-setting legislation is still being argued over, that will sap significant lawmaking energy. The prospect of looming elections will drain the rest. The problem with feeble majorities is that almost any intramural dissent is sufficient to scupper any proposal. Even if near-unanimous consent is achieved among sitting Democrats, the Senate’s byzantine rules, allowing the filibustering of legislation unless 60 senators are rounded up to break it, dooms many kinds of legislation. While the filibuster remains in place, there can be no serious revision of voting-rights rules, no increase of the minimum wage and no reform of the immigration system—to name just a few. Any proposals that can swerve the filibuster are unlikely to be very significant.

乐观主义者认为，民主党在2022年11月之前仍能取得很大成就，即使选举前景不佳。也许吧。但是，如果拜登的议程设定法案仍在争论中，那将消耗大量的精力在立法上。即将到来的选举前景将耗尽剩余的精力。微弱多数的问题是，几乎任何内部的异议都足以否决任何提案。即使在任的民主党人几乎一致同意，参议院错综复杂的规则，允许用冗长的演说去阻挠立法，除非召集60名参议员来破坏它，这注定了许多立法的失败。只要阻挠议案仍存在，就不可能对投票权规则进行重大修改，不可能提高最低工资，也不可能对移民制度进行改革，这只是其中的几个例子。任何能改变阻挠议事的提案都不太可能有重大意义。

One of the perks of the modern imperial presidency is that policymaking need not depend on the co-operation of a hopelessly fractious Congress—even though that method is preferable. Like his predecessors who were stymied after their first two years in office, Mr Biden may find his attention drawn to the issuance of expansive regulation through administrative agencies (especially relating to the environment), or the largely unilateral powers of pronouncing trade restrictions.

现代帝王式总统的好处之一是，政策制定不需要依赖于和极度暴躁的国会的合作，尽管这种方式更可取。就像他的前任总统们在执政的头两年就陷入困境一样，拜登可能会发现他的注意力被吸引到通过行政机构发布扩张性监管(特别是与环境有关的)，或者是单方面宣布贸易限制的权力上。

Having shown his isolationist tendencies in his unyielding commitment to the withdrawal from Afghanistan, Mr Biden is unlikely to resort to the other pastime of president-emperors, which is warmaking. More summitry may fill the presidential calendar instead. There is no doubt that the president would rather be remaking America by following in the footsteps of his idol, Franklin Roosevelt. But it is difficult to make Rooseveltian transformations without Rooseveltian majorities.

在坚定承诺从阿富汗撤军的过程中，拜登表现出了他的孤立主义倾向，他不太可能借助“帝王总统”的另一项“消遣”，即发动战争。取而代之的是，更多的峰会外交可能会填满总统的日程。毫无疑问，总统更愿意追随他的偶像富兰克林·罗斯福的脚步来重塑美国。但是，如果没有罗斯福式的多数，很难实现罗斯福式的转变。

Idrees Kahloon: Washington correspondent, The Economist, Washington, DC■
