Who will police Interpol?

谁来监管国际刑警组织?

Red notice alert

红色通缉令

The election of a worrying new president is just the latest thing to go wrong

令人担忧的(国际刑警组织)新主席的当选是最近一件出错的事情

文章音频

MATTHEW HEDGES, a British doctoral student, says he spent nearly seven months mostly in solitary confinement in a prison in the United Arab Emirates (UAE). He tells of being drugged, interrogated, blindfolded and forced to stand all day in manacles. He falsely confessed to being a spy just to end the agony, he says. He was eventually pardoned and freed. To his horror, the man he accuses of complicity in his torture, Ahmed Naser al-Raisi, the inspector-general of the UAEinterior ministry at the time, who was in charge of prisons, was neither sacked nor demoted. The UAE denies the claims and on November 25th Mr al-Raisi was elected Interpol’s new president.

MATTHEW HEDGES是一名英国博士生，他说他在阿联酋的一所监狱里度过了近七个月的时间，其中大部分时间都是单独监禁。他讲述了自己被麻醉，被审讯，被蒙住眼睛，被迫戴着手铐站一整天地经历。他说，为了结束痛苦，他谎称自己是间谍。他最终被赦免并释放。令他震惊的是，被他指控“参与对他实施酷刑”的Ahmed Naser al-Raisi既没有被解雇，也没有被降职。Ahmed Naser al-Raisi当时是阿联酋内政部的监察长，负责监狱管理。阿联酋否认了这些指控，并且在11月25日al-Raisi先生被选为国际刑警组织的新任主席。

Interpol was set up to help countries’ police forces work together to catch crooks. It has an unfortunate habit of employing them instead. Jackie Selebi, its president from 2004 to 2008, was later sentenced to 15 years in jail for corruption in his native South Africa. Meng Hongwei, the boss from 2016 to 2018, was summoned back to XX, disappeared, reappeared in the dock and got 13-and-a-half years for bribery. (His wife says he was framed.) A cynic might ask: whose side is Interpol on?

国际刑警组织的成立是为了帮助各国的警察力量合作抓捕骗子。令人遗憾的是它总是有雇佣“坏蛋”的习惯。2004年至2008年担任国际刑警组织主席的Jackie Selebi后来因在自己的祖国南非腐败而被判处15年监禁。2016年至2018年的国际刑警组织主席Meng Hongwei被召回XX，失踪后又出现在被告席上，且因受贿被判13年半监禁。(他的妻子说他是被陷害的。) 有愤世嫉俗的人可能会问: 国际刑警组织到底站哪一边?

The agency does not arrest people, but is essentially a communications hub. Its main tool is the “red notice”, which is akin to an international arrest warrant. Member states give Interpol the names of criminals on the lam and ask for a red notice. This used to be rare. In 2001 fewer than 1,500 were issued. That figure has grown more than sevenfold. In all, more than 66,000 red notices are active. Most are for real criminals, but autocratic regimes have found they can also be used to persecute exiled dissidents.

该机构不逮捕人，但其本质上是一个通讯枢纽。它的主要工具是“红色通缉令”，类似于国际逮捕令。成员国向国际刑警组织提供在逃罪犯的姓名，并要求发出红色通缉令。这在过去是很少见的。在2001年，红色通缉令发放数量不到1500份。这个数字已经增长了7倍多。总共有逾66000份工色通缉令处于“活跃”状态。大多数都是针对真正的罪犯，但独裁政权发现它们也可以被用来迫害流亡的异见人士。

Russia, responsible for 43% of the 7,500 red notices that have been made public, is a serial abuser. Petr Silaev, a green activist who found asylum in Finland, was accused of “hooliganism”, arrested in Spain and had to fight to avoid extradition to Russia. XX is another. In July a XX living in Turkey was detained in Morocco on the basis of an Interpol red notice. Democracies seldom hand over dissidents to autocratic regimes, but other autocrats have no such qualms. And red notices can do harm even without extradition. They can ruin careers and make it harder to board a plane or open a bank account. Many people wrongly assume that a red notice stems from a thorough investigation by an international body, rather than, say, a despot’s secret police.

在7500张红色通缉令中，俄罗斯占43%，俄罗斯是一个“连环施暴者”。Petr Silaev是一名在芬兰寻求庇护的绿党活动人士，他被指控犯有“流氓罪”，在西班牙被捕，不得不为避免被引渡到俄罗斯而抗争。XX是另一个“连环施暴者”。今年7月，一名生活在土耳其的XX族人因国际刑警组织的红色通缉令而在摩洛哥被拘留。民主国家很少将持不同政见者移交给独裁政权，但其他独裁者却没有这种顾虑。即使没有引渡，红色通缉令也会造成伤害。它们会毁掉你的职业生涯，让你更难登机或开户。许多人错误地认为红色通缉令来自国际机构的彻底调查，而不是独裁者的秘密警察部门。

Interpol staff are trying harder to weed out the most egregious requests. Reportedly, Turkish officials attempted to upload the names of 60,000 Gulenists onto Interpol’s database, but were rebuffed. (Members of this Islamist movement have been especially persecuted in Turkey since some of them backed a coup in 2016.) American senators introduced a bill in May that would require additional checks before American police act on Interpol notices.

国际刑警组织的工作人员正在努力排除最过分的请求。据报道，土耳其官员试图将6万名葛兰分子的姓名上传到国际刑警组织的数据库，但遭到了拒绝。(自2016年支持政变以来，这个伊斯兰教运动的成员在土耳其尤其受到迫害。)美国参议员在五月份提出了一项法案，要求在美国警察对国际刑警组织发出“红色通缉令”行动之前进行额外的检查。

But more is needed. For a start, Interpol’s president should be chosen in an open vote. Today’s secret ballots of all 195 member states make it hard to spot arm-twisting. That would still leave autocratic regimes with a lot of influence. So internal reforms are needed, too.

但还需要更多行动。首先，国际刑警组织的主席应该通过公开投票选出。如今所有195个成员国的无记名投票将很难发现“强迫”的迹象。这仍然会使独裁政权拥有很大的影响力。因此，也需要进行内部改革。

Requests for red notices from abusers should be subject to strict scrutiny. Currently there is not much, and it is opaque. Persistent abusers should be shut out. In 2012 Interpol blocked Syria’s access to its databases, but in October it inexplicably let the blood-drenched regime back in. Making Interpol more transparent and rigorous will cost money. Its annual budget is just €145m ($164m)—less than that of the New Orleans police department. Rich democracies should chip in more.

应严格审查“滥用者”发出红色通缉令的请求。目前没有太多“审查”，而且也不透明。应将长期“滥用者”拒之门外。2012年，国际刑警组织阻止叙利亚进入其数据库，但在10月，它又莫名其妙地让这个血腥的政权可以访问其数据库。要想使国际刑警组织更加透明且更加严格是要花钱的。它的年度预算只有1.45亿欧元(1.64亿美元)，这比新奥尔良警察局的预算还少。富裕的民主国家应该多投点钱。

Interpol belongs to the machinery of international governance, an area of competition between democracies and autocracies. It is supposed to bolster the rule of law, by helping catch real globetrotting criminals. It must do more to resist being captured by dictators and autocrats who would like to turn it into a branch of their own secret police. ■

国际刑警组织属于国际治理机构，这是民主国家和专制国家之间竞争的领域。它被认为是通过帮助抓捕真正的“环球旅行”的罪犯来加强法治。它必须做更多的工作来抵抗那些想要把“国际刑警组织”变成他们自己的秘密警察部门分支的独裁者。■
