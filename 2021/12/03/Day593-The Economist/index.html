The Democrats’ fiscal policy makes a mockery of their progressive pledges

民主党的财政政策歪曲其进步承诺

SALT in the wounds

伤口上撒“盐”

It is regressive and self-defeating

这是一种倒退以及自我毁灭


THE TAX plans of President Joe Biden were once full of lofty promises. He and Democrats in Congress would reverse Donald Trump’s tax cuts, make the wealthy pay more and fully fund all manner of desperately needed climate and social-policy programmes with the proceeds. The middle class would rise and the top 1% would manage. As the messy drafting of Mr Biden’s main spending bill—the Build Back Better Act—nears its conclusion after months of wheeling and dealing, it is clear that, when it comes to tax, the result is not lofty at all.

拜登总统的税收计划曾充满了崇高的承诺。他和国会中的民主党人将推翻特朗普的减税政策，让富人支付更多的税，并用所得的资金资助各种迫切需要资金的气候和社会政策项目。中产阶级将会增加，而最富有的1%将会被“利用”。经过几个月的周旋和交易，拜登主要支出法案（即《重建更好未来》法案）的混乱起草接近尾声，很明显，在税收方面，结果不及预期。

The president was unable to whip his slim congressional majorities into reversing Mr Trump’s tax law and increasing marginal rates on capital gains, corporate profits or top individual incomes. And so his plan to raise revenue is a hotch-potch of unorthodox measures, including a new minimum tax on corporate-book income, an excise tax on stock buy-backs and a new surtax on those with incomes above $10m. The wisdom of these measures can be debated. What cannot is a last-minute addition to the bill that would spend hundreds of billions of dollars subsidising the richest residents of New York and California.

总统无法利用其在国会微弱的多数优势扭转特朗普的税法，提高资本利得、企业利润或最高个人收入所得的边际税率。因此，拜登的增税计划是一系列另类措施的大杂烩，其中包括对公司账面收入征收新的最低税，对公司股票回购征收消费税，对收入超过1000万美元的人征收新的附加税。这些措施是否明智值得商榷。但不明智的是在最后一刻在法案上增加的一项内容，即支出数千亿美元补贴纽约和加州最富有居民。

The state-and-local-tax (SALT) deduction lets Americans cut their federal-tax liability if they pay lots of income and property tax at the state and local level. Before 2017 this provision was limitless, letting plutocrats in high-tax states deduct all the property tax on their mansions and the state income tax on their millions, at the expense of federal taxpayers everywhere. Mr Trump’s tax law capped the tax exemption at $10,000. Rather than welcome this as a step towards their goal of more redistribution, Democrats in high-tax states moaned that they had been punitively targeted. Just before Build Back Better passed the House of Representatives on November 19th, they raised the cap to $80,000 a year.

如果美国人在州和地方级缴纳了大量的所得税和财产税，州和地方税的扣除将使他们减少联邦税收义务。在2017年之前，这项规定是没有上限的，这可以让高税收州的富豪们以牺牲各地联邦纳税人的利益为代价，扣除他们豪宅的所有财产税和对他们数百万的州所得税。特朗普的税法将免税限额限制在1万美元。在高税收州的民主党人并不欢迎这一“朝着更大的再分配目标迈出的一步”的举措，相反他们抱怨称，他们受到了惩罚性的打击。就在11月19日众议院通过《重建更好未来》法案之前，他们将扣除上限提高到了每年8万美元。

The result is a fiscal fiasco. In the next five years the benefit will cost $275bn. Exactly none of it will go to the bottom 60% of earners. Instead 70% of the gains will go to the top 5%. For a party that came to power condemning Mr Trump’s tax reform for being regressive, the stain of hypocrisy will be hard to wash out.

其结果就是财政惨败。未来5年，这项“扣除上限”的福利措施将损失2750亿美元。确切地说，这其中的钱没有一项会落到最低收入的60%的人手里。相反，70%的收益将流向前5%的富人。对于一个上台时谴责特朗普的税改政策是倒退的政党来说，伪善的污点恐难洗掉。

If the politics are bad, the policy is worse. The central promise of Mr Biden’s agenda was to craft a more muscular state that will be able to grapple with long-term threats such as climate change, pandemics and social dysfunction. Yet what is supposedly built back better will count for nothing if it does not exist. In order to pay for that $275bn giveaway, the Democrats have jettisoned some of Mr Biden’s pledges, including free tuition at community college, and scheduled others to disappear in a few years’ time. They are betting that a future Congress will be shamed into finding the money to save these from the axe. That may turn out to be wishful thinking.

如果说政治不行，政策就更糟了。拜登议程的核心承诺是打造一个更强大的国家，它能够应对气候变化、流行病和社会失调等长期威胁。然而，如果这一承诺根本就不存在，那些“重建更好未来”将毫无意义。为了支付2750亿美元的“赠品”，民主党放弃了拜登的一些承诺，包括社区大学免学费，并计划在几年内取消其他承诺。他们打赌，未来的国会将会感到羞愧，不得不去寻找资金以弥补这些“减免”的钱。这可能是一厢情愿的想法。

It is also callous. The expanded child benefits have significantly reduced poverty among the most needy Americans, but the programme is due to expire after just one more year. If Democrats held back the hundreds of billions from the SALT deduction, they could almost completely fund a permanent child-benefit scheme. They prefer to give each member of the top 1% of earners an average tax cut of $15,000, five times the amount the benefit pays for each child.

这一法案(指最后那一条)也是冷酷无情的。扩大的儿童福利大大减少了最贫穷的美国人的贫困，但该计划将在仅仅一年之后到期。如果民主党人从“州和地方扣除”中收回数千亿美元，他们几乎可以完全资助一个永久的儿童福利计划。(不过) 他们更倾向于给收入最高的1%的富人每人平均减税1.5万美元，这一福利是支付给每个孩子的五倍。

The best hope of improving the bill comes in the Senate, where all Democrats must vote for it to pass. Mr Biden is so desperate to see the bill into law that he will not criticise it. Some, like Bernie Sanders, have proposed a compromise limiting the larger SALT deduction to families making less than $400,000 a year—which tells you just how regressive the current bill is.

改善该法案的最大希望来自参议院，在参议院所有民主党人必须投票支持该法案通过。拜登如此迫切地希望该法案成为法律，以至于他不会对其进行批评。一些人，比如Bernie Sanders，已经提出了一个妥协方案，限制年收入低于40万美元的家庭获得更大的“州和地方扣除”税额，这也说明当前法案是多么的倒退。

There is no way to tweak the SALT deduction to make it desirable. It should be scrapped entirely. But because that will not happen, the debate is whether it can be wrestled into something less bad. Given the steady degradation of Build Back Better, optimism would be unwise. ■

我们没有办法调整州和地方额，使其更为可取。它应该被完全废除。但是，由于这种情况不会发生，争论的焦点在于，是否可以将其转变成不那么糟糕的局面。鉴于《重建更好未来》法案的持续退化，乐观主义将是不明智的。■
