Japanese companies try to reduce their reliance on Chinese manufacturing

日本公司试图减少对中国制造业的依赖

Asian supply chains

亚洲供应链

![image-20210923133059744](/Users/chenyujie/Library/Application Support/typora-user-images/image-20210923133059744.png)

But only a bit

但只有一点点

AT THE END of the month the production line of a Toshiba factory in Dalian will come to a halt, 30 years after the Japanese electronics giant opened it in the north-eastern Chinese city. Once a totemic example of global supply chains expanding into China, the closure exemplifies how these are being reconfigured. The short answer is: delicately and at the margin.

totemic 图腾的

to have a totemic significance 具有象征意义

本月末，日本电子巨头东芝在中国东北城市大连开设的一条生产线将关停。这曾是全球供应链向中国扩张的标志性例子，它的关闭说明这些供应链正在如何重新配置。简言之，答案就是: 小心翼翼以及看利润率。

Toshiba’s plant in Dalian has spanned a sea change in Asian business patterns. When it opened, Japan was the undisputed linchpin of the region’s trade and manufacturing networks. By 2019 Japan’s $390bn in intermediate-goods trade with big Asian economies was vying for runner-up status with South Korea and Taiwan. China, with $935bn-worth, was way ahead.

undisputed 无可争辩的

linchpin 关键因素; 关键人物

runner-up 亚军

东芝在大连的工厂使亚洲商业模式发生了翻天覆地的变化。当它开放时，日本是该地区贸易和制造业网络无可争议的关键。到2019年，日本与亚洲大型经济体之间3900亿美元的中间产品贸易，正与韩国和中国台湾争夺亚军地位。中国以9350亿美元遥遥领先。

Hourly wages commanded by Chinese workers have risen tenfold in nominal terms this century, to $6.20. That is still a quarter of Japanese rates but twice the pay of Thai workers, who were at parity with Chinese ones as recently as 2008. If that were not enough, geopolitical tensions are souring relations between the increasingly heavy-handed XXX and the world’s rich democracies.

sour 使..恶化

本世纪以来，中国工人的名义时薪上涨了10倍，达到6.20美元。这仍然是日本工资的四分之一，但是泰国工人工资的两倍。就在2008年，泰国工人的工资还与中国工人持平。如果这还不够，日益强硬的XXX和世界上发达的民主国家之间的地缘政治关系的紧张局势正在恶化。

These trends help explain why China’s share of Japan’s new outbound foreign direct investment has steadily declined since 2012. The number of manufacturing affiliates that Japanese companies have in China stopped growing almost a decade ago, while new affiliates elsewhere in Asia—notably India, Indonesia, Thailand and Vietnam—have continued to mushroom. Toshiba will offset some of the forgone capacity with expansion in some of its 50 factories back home and also in Vietnam, one of its 30 overseas facilities. It is tapping the Japanese government’s year-old subsidy scheme to encourage reshoring and diversification of supply chains (and whose unspoken aim is to reduce reliance on China).

mushroom 迅速增长

这些趋势有助于解释为什么自2012年以来，中国在日本新的对外直接投资中所占的份额一直在稳步下降。近10年前，日本企业在中国的制造业分支机构数量停止增长，而在亚洲其他地区，尤其是印度、印尼、泰国和越南，新的分支机构却在继续增长。东芝将通过扩大在国内和越南的50家工厂来弥补部分产能损失。越南是东芝30家海外工厂之一。它正在利用日本政府长达一年的补贴计划来鼓励供应链的回流和供应链多元化(其未言明的目标是减少对中国的依赖)。

Many other Japanese firms find themselves in a similar situation. This month OKIElectric Industry, a smaller Japanese electronics-maker, announced that its factory in Shenzhen, set up 20 years ago, would stop making printers. That capacity would move to existing factories in Thailand and Japan. Still, most are not rushing to exit China altogether. A survey last year for the Japan External Trade Organisation, a government body, found that 8% of Japanese companies said they were planning to reduce or eliminate their Chinese presence, less than the average for Japanese firms in other countries. Many global companies, from Hasbro (an American toymaker) to Samsung (a South Korean technology giant) are making a similar calculation. Toshiba itself will maintain a second, part-owned factory in Dalian.

许多其他日本公司也发现自己处于类似的情况。本月，日本一家规模较小的电子产品制造商OKIElectric Industry宣布，其在深圳成立20年的工厂将停止生产打印机。这些产能将转移到泰国和日本的现有工厂。不过，大多数公司并不急于完全退出中国。去年，日本贸易振兴机构(一个政府机构)的一项调查发现，8%的日本公司表示他们计划减少或取消在中国的业务，低于日本公司在其他国家的平均水平。从美国玩具制造商Hasbro到韩国科技巨头三星，许多跨国公司都在进行类似的考虑。东芝本身将在大连保留第二家部分所有权的工厂。

Even the most tub-thumpingly patriotic executive would hesitate to sever tieswith the world’s second-biggest economy. This would disrupt profitable relationships with Chinese suppliers and manufacturing know-how. Such things take years to forge. But at the margin, where companies find themselves pressed by the imperatives to cut costs and guarantee stable future supplies, China no longer looks like the place to be. ■

即使是最爱国的高管也不愿与世界第二大经济体断绝关系。这将破坏与中国供应商和制造技术之间有利可图的关系。这样的东西需要数年的时间来锻造。但就利润率而言，企业发现自己面临削减成本和保证未来稳定供应的紧迫任务，中国似乎不再是最佳选择。■
