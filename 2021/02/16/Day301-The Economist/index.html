The search for ET may soon yield an answer

对外星人的探索可能很快就会有答案

Life, the universe and everything

生命，宇宙和一切

![image-20210216094748085](/Users/chenyujie/Library/Application Support/typora-user-images/image-20210216094748085.png)

Whether there is intelligence out there is a different matter

是否有智慧存在就是另外一回事了

Feb 13th 2021

MOST SCIENTIFIC research has practical ends. But some still pursues goals better described by the field’s original name: “natural philosophy”. One of its most philosophical questions is, “Is there life elsewhere in the universe?”

ends 目的

大多数科学研究都有实际的目的。但有些人仍然追求这个领域的最初的名字“自然哲学”所描述的更好的目标。其中一个最具哲学意义的问题是，“宇宙的其他地方是否存在生命?”

It is philosophical for two reasons. One is its grand sweep. If there is life elsewhere, particularly of the intelligent sort, that raises the question of whether humans might ever encounter it, or its products. If there is not—if all the uncountable stars in creation waste their light on sterile, lifeless worlds—then life on Earth must be the result of a stroke of the most astronomically improbable good luck. As Arthur C. Clarke, a science-fiction author, is reputed to have said: “Two possibilities exist. Either we are alone in the cosmos or we are not. Both are equally terrifying.”

sterile 贫瘠的

astronomically 极其地

improbable 奇异的

stroke 一次

a stroke of bad/good luck 背运/好运

它之所以具有哲理性，有两个原因。其一是它的大席卷。如果在其他地方存在生命，特别是有智慧的生命，那么人类是否可能会遇到它们，或者它们的产物。如果没有——如果宇宙中无数的恒星把它们的光浪费在贫瘠的、没有生命世界上——那么地球上的生命一定是极其奇异的好运的结果。正如科幻小说作家阿瑟·c·克拉克(Arthur C. Clarke)所说:“存在两种可能性。”我们在宇宙中要么是孤独的，要么不是。两者都同样可怕。”

The other reason the question is philosophical is that there has, historically, been too little evidence to settle it. Arguments about life in the cosmos must extrapolate from a single example that is itself poorly understood. Biologists still lack a bulletproof theory of how earthly life began. Other planets are far away and hard to study. That leaves room for all sorts of theories. Perhaps life is rare. Perhaps it is common, but intelligence is not. Or perhaps even intelligent life is common, but the technology that lifts it up ends by destroying it (a popular line of thinking after the development of nuclear weapons).

historically 历史上

extrapolate 推断

earthly 尘世的；可能的

这个问题属于哲学范畴的另一个原因是，从历史上看，解决这个问题的证据太少了。关于宇宙中生命的争论必须从一个本身就不太了解的例子中推断出来。生物学家仍然缺乏关于地球生命起源的“防弹”【可靠的】理论。其他行星离我们很远，很难研究。这就给各种理论留下了空间。也许生命是稀有的。也许这很常见，但智力却不常见。或者甚至智慧生命也很常见，但提升智慧生命的技术最终会摧毁它(这是核武器发展后的一种流行思维)。

This paucity of data will soon change. A variety of telescopes and spacecraft are, or soon will be, looking for signs of life in places ranging from the moons and planets of the solar system to other stars in Earth’s corner of the Milky Way. In particular, this search will employ powerful telescopes to try to find chemical signatures of life in the atmospheres of planets orbiting stars other than the sun. An alien astronomer looking at Earth, for instance, would be struck by the persistence of both oxygen and something that it reacts with in the atmosphere, and might conclude—correctly—that living organisms were responsible for keeping them there.

the Milky Way 银河

数据的缺乏很快就会改变。各种各样的望远镜和宇宙飞船正在或即将在从太阳系的卫星和行星到银河系地球一角的其他恒星的地方寻找生命的迹象。特别是，这次搜索将使用强大的望远镜，试图在围绕太阳以外恒星运行的行星的大气中发现生命的化学特征。例如，外星天文学家观察地球时，会被大气中持续存在的氧气及其与之发生反应的物质所震惊，并可能正确地得出这样的结论，认为生物是它们留在那里的原因。

An unambiguous detection of alien life would count as one of the momentous discoveries in the history of science. Exactly what would happen next would depend on what was found. News of a “biosignature” on a planet dozens of light-years away would shake the world. It would be strong evidence that life is indeed common in the cosmos. That conclusion could upend humanity’s understanding of its place in the universe.

momentous 重大的

light year 光年

对外星生命的明确发现将被视为科学史上的重大发现之一。接下来究竟会发生什么取决于发现了什么。几十光年之外的行星上有“生物特征”的消息将震惊世界。这将是有力的证据，证明生命确实在宇宙中普遍存在。这一结论可能会颠覆人类对自身在宇宙中位置的认识。

A few adventurous scientists might suggest using a radio telescope to beam a message, in the hope that, if anything intelligent lives there, it will, decades later, send a reply. Still, the sheer distances involved mean that there would be few immediate, practical consequences. By contrast, finding life closer to home—beneath the Martian regolith, say, or in the oceans under the frozen surfaces of the solar system’s icy moons—would lead to a flurry of action. A sample-return mission would give biologists the ability to compare earthly life with the unearthly sort, a process that could shed new light on the workings and origins of both.

beam 定向传送

still 还有；仍然

sheer 完全的

Martian 火星的

regolith 风化层; 表土;

workings 运转

一些有冒险精神的科学家可能会建议使用无线电望远镜发送信息，希望如果那里有任何智慧生命，它会在几十年后回复。尽管如此，距离之远意味着不会有什么直接的、实际的后果。相比之下，在离地球更近的地方寻找生命——比如在火星风化层下，或者在太阳系冰卫星冰冻表面下的海洋里——则会引发一连串的行动。样本返回的任务将使生物学家有能力将地球上的生命与非地球的生命进行比较，这一过程可能会为这两种生命的运转和起源提供新的线索。

And if nothing is found? That too would be a piece of data, albeit of a less dramatic sort. It would not prove that no life exists elsewhere in the cosmos, but it would be evidence that it is, at least, rather uncommon.

如果什么也没找到呢?这也将是一份数据，尽管不是那么引人注目。它不能证明宇宙的其他地方不存在生命，但它可以证明生命至少是不常见的。

Half a century ago, returning from the arid and sterile lunar surface, the Apollo astronauts found a new appreciation for Earth’s joyous blooms of life and colour. If there are no aliens nearby, such sentiments might grow stronger. A jewel is all the more valuable for being rare. ■

半个世纪前，阿波罗号宇航员从干旱贫瘠的月球表面返回时，对地球上欣欣向荣的生命和色彩有了新的认识。如果附近没有外星人，这种情绪可能会变得更强烈。宝石因稀有而更有价值。■
