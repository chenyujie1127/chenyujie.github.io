How America can rid itself of both carbon and blackouts

美国如何摆脱碳排放和停电

America and climate change

美国和气候变化

![image-20210222110210728](/Users/chenyujie/Library/Application Support/typora-user-images/image-20210222110210728.png)

This is the moment for an ambitious attempt to deal with climate change

现在正是应对气候变化的雄心勃勃的时刻

Feb 20th 2021

TEXAS PRIDES itself on being different. Yet it is in the grip of a winter storm that typifies the Snowmageddon-size problems facing energy in America. Although nobody can be sure if this particular freeze is a sign of climate change, the growing frequency of extreme weather across the country is. Texan infrastructure has buckled. The problem is not, as some argue, that Texas has too many renewables. Gas-fired plants and a nuclear reactor were hit, as well as wind turbines. Worse, Texas had too little capacity and its poorly connected grid was unable to import power from elsewhere. Texas shows that America needs both a cleaner grid and a more reliable one.

pride oneself on doing sth 为某事物/做某事而自豪

typify 成为..的特征

buckle 垮掉；撑不住

clean 边缘整齐的；平滑的；流线型的；简洁流畅的

德州以与众不同而自豪。然而，它正处于一场冬季风暴的控制之中，这是美国能源面临的雪灾规模问题的典型代表。虽然没有人能确定这次特别的冰冻期是否是气候变化的迹象，但全国各地日益频繁的极端天气确实是（气候变化的迹象）。德克萨斯的基础设施已经崩溃。问题并不像一些人所说的那样是德州有太多的可再生能源。燃气发电厂和一个核反应堆以及风力涡轮机都受到了冲击。更糟糕的是，德州的电力容量太少，其连接不良的电网无法从其他地方输送电力。德州的情况表明，美国既需要一个更清洁的电网，也需要一个更可靠的电网。

Plans to overhaul American energy will come before Congress in the next few months. President Joe Biden has said that he wants fossil-fuel emissions from power generation to end by 2035 and the economy to be carbon-neutral by 2050. America is not just the world’s second-largest emitter, but also a source of climate-related policy, technology and, potentially, leadership. What is about to unfold in Washington will set the course in America for the next decade—and quite possibly beyond.

emitter 排放者

美国能源改革计划将在未来几个月提交国会。美国总统乔•拜登(Joe Biden)曾表示，他希望到2035年结束发电产生的化石燃料排放，到2050年实现经济碳中和。美国不仅是世界第二大排放国，还是气候相关政策、技术和潜在领导力的来源。华盛顿即将发生的事情将为美国下一个十年甚至很可能更久的未来定下方向。

Time is pressing. Neither Mr Biden nor his successors may get a second chance to recast policy on such a scale. Global emissions from fossil fuels and cement production in 2019 were 16% higher than in 2009. It will be even harder to limit climate change to less than 2°C above the pre-industrial level, the global threshold from which America’s target for 2050 comes. To be carbon neutral, the world must curb emissions by 7.6% a year for a decade, a steeper decline than in 2020, when covid-19 cut demand for oil and coal. For America, delaying action to 2030 would nearly double the cost of reaching net zero or, more likely, mean it overshoots its targets.

时间是紧迫的。拜登和他的继任者都不会有第二次机会在如此规模上改写政策。2019年全球化石燃料和水泥生产排放比2009年高出16%。将气候变化限制在比工业化前水平(美国2050年目标的全球门槛)高出2摄氏度以内将更加困难。要实现碳中和，全球必须在10年内每年减排7.6%，降幅大于2020年，当时covid-19降低了对石油和煤炭的需求。对美国来说，推迟到2030年采取行动将使达到净零的成本增加近一倍，或者更有可能意味着它超出了目标。

Yet there are grounds for hope. Although the Republican Party is against almost all action, voters are increasingly alarmed by climate change. Two-thirds of them think the federal government is doing too little about it, and that share includes plenty of younger Republicans. Although the fossil-fuel lobby remains powerful, many Republican business donors want more action—partly because asset managers are urging firms to align their strategies with the net-zero world Mr Biden envisions.

然而，有理由抱有希望。尽管共和党反对几乎所有的行动，但选民对气候变化的担忧与日俱增。他们中有三分之二的人认为联邦政府在这方面做得太少，这一比例包括很多年轻的共和党人。尽管化石燃料游说团依然强大，但许多共和党商业捐助者希望采取更多行动——部分原因是资产管理公司正敦促企业将其战略与拜登先生所设想的净零世界相一致。

Most encouraging of all, the costs of power from wind and solar have plunged by 70% and 90% over the past decade. Along with cheap gas, this has already helped America decarbonise at an impressive rate, despite Donald Trump’s rolling back of fossil-fuel regulations. Price has not been the only factor; more than half of the states have some sort of clean-energy mandate, a device that Mr Biden wants to introduce on a national scale.

of all 在所有…中 ; 最为 ;

roll back 削弱..的影响

device 手段

最鼓舞人心的是，风能和太阳能发电的成本在过去十年里分别下降了70%和90%。再加上廉价的天然气，这已经帮助美国以惊人的速度脱碳，尽管唐纳德·特朗普(Donald Trump)撤销了化石燃料法规。价格并不是唯一的因素;超过一半的州都有某种清洁能源的授权，这是拜登先生想在全国范围内推行的一项措施。

This involves a regulatory framework that favours renewable-energy developments and grid connections to hook them up. It will take a lot of extra investment—about $2.5trn in the coming decade, say researchers at Princeton (see ). In a new book, Bill Gates, a billionaire philanthropist, argues that research is needed into a host of areas such as energy storage, advanced nuclear reactors to complement renewables and technologies for clean concrete-making and other activities that are hard to decarbonise. Without these, even if a clean grid is powering electric cars and light trucks, it will displace only around half of emissions.

这涉及到一个有利于可再生能源开发和电网连接的监管框架。普林斯顿大学的研究人员说，在未来十年，这将需要大量额外投资，大约2.5万亿美元。亿万富翁慈善家比尔•盖茨在他的新书中指出，需要对许多领域进行研究，如能源储存、先进的核反应堆，以补充可再生能源、清洁混凝土制造技术以及其他难以脱碳的活动。如果没有这些，即使一个清洁的电网为电动汽车和轻型卡车提供动力，它也只能替代大约一半的排放。

America is good at innovation, but new ideas need to be deployed at scale, not languish in the lab. One tool is a carbon price which, if it were high enough and if investors believed it would last, would signal what improvements were needed where. But for all its attractions, carbon pricing failed in Congress in 2009. Although many economists and opinion-makers on the right favour it, Republican politicians do not. And even if a carbon price were in place, public-private co-operation would still be needed for America to act as fast as Mr Biden proposes.

美国擅长创新，但新想法需要大规模应用，而不是在实验室里停滞不前/被忽视。一个工具就是碳价格，如果这个价格足够高，而且投资者相信它会持续下去，它就会表明哪些地方需要改进。但尽管碳定价有诸多吸引力，2009年却未能在国会通过。尽管许多经济学家和右翼舆论制造者支持它，但共和党的政客们却不这么认为。而且，即使碳排放价格到位，美国仍需要公私合作才能像拜登先生提议的那样迅速采取行动。

For all those reasons, an ambitious climate-oriented infrastructure bill looks like Mr Biden’s best chance of getting new policy on climate through the Senate. Unfortunately such a plan will be lucky to attract any Republican votes. Yet, if mustering the 60 needed to see off a Senate filibuster is improbable, a plan could be stripped of some measures, including a clean-energy standard, and passed with a simple majority through the parliamentary manoeuvre known as reconciliation. The bill must still be of a scale and ambition that matches America’s challenge.

filibuster 阻挠行动

strip off 除去

manoeuvre 策略

基于所有这些原因，一项雄心勃勃的以气候为导向的基础设施法案似乎是拜登先生在参议院通过气候新政策的最佳机会。不幸的是，这样的计划将很幸运地吸引共和党人的选票。然而，如果召集所需的60人来阻止参议院的阻挠是不可能的，这项计划可以取消一些措施，包括清洁能源标准，并通过被称为和解的议会策略以简单多数获得通过。该法案的规模和雄心必须与美国的挑战相匹配。

Failure to act would bring big risks. For a start, it would make America less competitive in the new clean-energy economy. China is the dominant producer of solar panels and batteries; it has also invested in foreign mines to secure minerals needed for them. Europe has its own “green deal” to boost its clean-energy industries. It plans to tax imports from countries that do not pledge to lower their emissions.

不采取行动将带来巨大风险。首先，这会降低美国在新的清洁能源经济中的竞争力。中国是太阳能电池板和电池的主要生产国；中国还投资了外国矿山，以获得它们所需的矿产。欧洲也有自己的“绿色协议”来促进其清洁能源产业。它计划对来自那些不承诺减少排放的国家的进口商品征税。

America would also be deprived of global influence over climate. It has direct control over only about 10% of the world’s greenhouse-gas effluvia. If it wants the benefit of a stabler climate—and with it a stabler world economy, stabler geopolitics and much avoided suffering—it needs to influence the other 90%, too. Mr Biden has appointed John Kerry, a former secretary of state, to spearhead that effort. America is to rejoin the Paris agreement on February 19th, making it a full participant in the UN conference to be held in Glasgow, in Scotland, in November, when countries will be able to lodge new and more ambitious pledges to cut emissions. If America tables goals and gives evidence that it will back them with domestic policy, it will gain influence. China’s two big development banks have doled out $51bn for foreign coal plants since 2008. America should be part of a push against such subsidies.

effluvia 废料；臭气

spearhead 先锋；先头部队

act as (a) spearhead 担当前导

lodge 正式提出

table 把..提交讨论；搁置

dole out 发放

美国也将失去对全球气候的影响。它只直接控制了世界上10%的温室气体排放。如果它想从一个更稳定的气候，伴随而来的更稳定的世界经济、更稳定的地缘政治以及更多的避免痛苦中获益，它也需要影响其他90%的国家。拜登已经任命前国务卿约翰•克里(John Kerry)作为这项工作的先锋。美国将于2月19日重新加入《巴黎协定》，成为将于11月在苏格兰格拉斯哥举行的联合国大会的正式参与者，届时各国将能够提出新的更雄心勃勃的减排承诺。如果美国提出目标，并提供证据表明它将以国内政策支持这些目标，它将获得影响力。自2008年以来，xx两家大型开发银行已向外国燃煤电厂拨款510亿美元。美国应该参与反对此类补贴的行动。

Enough drifting

Unfortunately, America brings little credibility to action on climate. Mr Trump took pleasure in subverting it, but his country’s poor record precedes him. George W. Bush declined to implement the Kyoto protocol. Congress has not considered serious climate legislation since 2009. Today must be different. There will never be a better chance for Mr Biden to show real ambition. If the blackouts in Texas are any guide, it would not just be the world that would thank him, but Americans, too. ■

blackout 停电；灯火管制

不幸的是，美国在气候问题上的行动缺乏可信度。特朗普乐于颠覆它，但美国的糟糕记录早在他之前就出现了。小布什拒绝执行《京都议定书》。自2009年以来，国会就没有认真考虑过气候立法。今天必须有所不同。对拜登来说，再也没有比这更好的机会来展示他真正的野心了。如果德克萨斯州的停电事件能起到什么指导作用的话，那么不仅全世界会感谢他，美国人也会感谢他。■
