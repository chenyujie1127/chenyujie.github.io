Buy American is an economic-policy mistake

买本地货的愚蠢行为

President Biden’s protectionism sullies his economic agenda

protectionism 贸易保护主义

sully 损害

拜登总统的贸易保护主义损害了他的经济议程

Jan 30th 2021

MODERN PRESIDENTS of the United States do not sit in the Oval Office for long before reaching for the pen. Since entering the White House on January 20th Joe Biden has signed nearly 40 executive orders and proclamations. Many are welcome; some are crucial. He is overturning some of the harshest immigration restrictions imposed by Donald Trump and restoring America’s support of the Paris agreement on climate change. One of Mr Biden’s edicts, however, is an early economic-policy mistake: the tightening of rules obliging America’s federal government to prefer domestic suppliers to foreign ones. It could be a sign of worse errors to come.

overturn 撤销

edict 命令；法令

oblige 强迫

最新的美国总统在椭圆形办公室坐不了多久就会伸手去拿笔。自1月20日入主白宫以来，乔·拜登已经签署了近40项行政命令和公告。很多是受欢迎的;有些是至关重要的。他推翻了唐纳德·特朗普施加的一些最严厉的移民限制，恢复了美国对气候变化巴黎协议的支持。然而，拜登的一项法令是一个早期的经济政策错误:强制美国联邦政府偏爱国内供应商而不是外国供应商的规则变得更加严格。这可能预示着更严重的错误即将到来。

The “Buy American” agenda is long-running. A law passed in 1933 requires the federal government to prefer domestically produced goods to foreign ones; it is reinforced by an unwieldy mass of similar state and local rules. The stimulus Mr Biden oversaw as vice-president after the global financial crisis targeted American suppliers with American supply chains. President Trump issued reams of executive orders aiming to favour domestic firms over their foreign competitors. One of his rule changes, which raises the bar for an item to be considered American-made and increases the mark-up over global prices which domestic firms can charge, has still to come into effect.

long-running 长期的

domestically 在国内

domestically produced goods 本国产品

unwieldy 复杂的；运作不灵的

reams 大量的

mark up 给..加价

“购买美国货”议程是长期的。1933年通过的一项法律要求联邦政府偏爱本国产品而不是外国产品;大量类似的州和地方法规又强化了这一点。全球金融危机后，拜登担任副总统期间监管的刺激计划针对的是拥有美国供应链的美国供应商。特朗普总统发布了大量旨在偏袒国内企业而非外国竞争对手的行政命令。他的一项规则修改仍然在生效，这项修改提高了被视为美国制造的商品的门槛，并提高了国内企业可以收取的全球价格的加价幅度。

Mr Biden campaigned on going further than Mr Trump, whom he castigated for not doing enough. It will take more than a stroke of the pen to fulfil that promise. As past administrations have discovered, America’s commitments to the World Trade Organisation (WTO) constrain its ability to discriminate in favour of its own firms. As a result, Mr Biden’s order only fiddles at the margins, confirming Mr Trump’s last changes, giving the White House more oversight of exemptions from the rules and introducing a vague new requirement that Washington’s purchases should promote employment.

castigate 严厉批评

discriminate 区别对待

discriminate in favour of 对…特别优待；另眼看待

discriminate in favour of sb/sth 偏爱某人/某物

fiddle 篡改

拜登在竞选时表示，他比特朗普走得更远，他指责特朗普做得不够。要实现这一承诺，光大笔一挥是不够的。正如历届政府所发现的那样，美国对世界贸易组织(WTO)的承诺限制了其对本国企业特别优待的能力。其结果是，拜登的命令只是在边缘上摆弄，证实了特朗普的最后修改，让白宫对豁免规则有了更多的监督，并引入了一个模糊的新要求，即华盛顿的购买应该促进就业。

Yet the president has the support in Congress to go further if he wants to. He has promised a diplomatic effort to modernise—code for weaken—the WTO rules. And they can in any case be circumvented by sending money to states and attaching conditions on how they spend it, which is how the Democrats might structure their stimulus and infrastructure spending.

in any case 不管怎样 ; 不管怎样，无论如何 ; 不管怎样，再者

circumvented 设法回避 ; 规避 ; 绕过 ; 绕行 ; 绕道旅行

structure 安排

然而，如果总统愿意的话，他在国会有足够的支持可以走得更远。他承诺将通过外交手段使世贸组织规则现代化(即弱化规则)。无论如何，他们都可以通过向各州提供资金并对其支出方式附加条件来规避，这就是民主党可能会采取的结构刺激计划和基础设施支出的方式。

Presidents and voters like Buy American because they think it creates jobs. In a direct sense, it does. But by locking firms out of global supply chains and shielding them from competition it promotes inefficiency, destroying more employment than it creates. By one estimate America would gain a net 300,000 jobs if it got rid of its local-content rules.

总统和选民喜欢“买美国货”，因为他们认为这能创造就业机会。从直接意义上说，确实如此。但是，通过将企业排除在全球供应链之外，保护它们免受竞争的影响，它会导致效率低下，摧毁的就业机会比它创造的就业机会还多。据估计，如果取消本地内容规则，美国将净增加30万个工作岗位。

There is no evidence that buying at home boosts innovation either, whatever its advocates might claim. In fact, it almost certainly hampers productivity increases in the long term. And coddling local firms is a raw deal for taxpayers. From February American firms will be able to charge their government up to 20% more than prevailing global prices.

coddle 悉心照顾

raw 不公正的

a raw deal 不公的待遇

get/have a raw deal 受到不公正待遇

give sb a raw deal 对某人苛刻

无论其倡导者会怎么说，也没有证据表明在国内购买能促进创新。事实上，从长期来看，它几乎肯定会阻碍生产率的提高。溺爱本地公司对纳税人来说是不公平的。从2月份开始，美国公司可以向政府收取比全球现行价格高出20%的费用。

Perhaps the sturdiest economic rationale for buying local is that it stops the short-term stimulatory effect of new spending—of which Mr Biden is planning a lot—from leaking overseas. But that should not be a big concern for America in 2021 because its stimulus is so enormous. Were Mr Biden’s $1.9trn spending proposal to pass, it would take America’s cumulative pandemic spending to more than a quarter of its pre-crisis GDP (before considering the additional effects of any infrastructure bill). America can afford a little leakage. In any case, the lesson from 2009 is that requirements to buy American hamper stimulus by forcing firms to look for new suppliers before they can start projects. It is not encouraging that Mr Biden’s order will make it still harder to obtain exemptions from the existing mountain of red tape.

rationale 根本原因

red tape 繁文缛节

购买本土产品的最坚实的经济理由或许是，它阻止了拜登计划大量增加的新支出的短期刺激效应外泄到海外。但对于2021年的美国来说，这应该不是什么大问题，因为它的经济刺激是如此巨大。如果拜登1.9万亿美元的支出提案获得通过，那么美国在大流行期间的累计支出将超过危机前GDP的四分之一(在考虑任何基础设施法案的额外影响之前)。美国可以承受一点点的泄漏。无论如何，2009年的教训是，购买美国货的要求会迫使企业在开工前寻找新的供应商，从而阻碍经济刺激。拜登的命令将使其更难从目前堆积如山的繁文缛节中获得豁免，这并不令人鼓舞。

It is a relief that, unlike Mr Trump, Mr Biden supports the rules-based system of global trade. He will not sabotage the WTO or go out of his way to antagonise America’s allies, whom he wants to rally against XX. But his soft protectionism—which, thanks to the Trumpification of the Republican Party, is more popular in Congress than it has been in decades—will rankle the outside world; the European Union, which recently reached an investment agreement with XX, is already bristling at his executive order. In his instincts about the economics of trade, America’s new president is not so different from his predecessor. That is bad news for America and for the world. ■

sabotage 蓄意破坏

antagonise 引起..的反感

rankle 使..怨恨

bristle at sth 对..大发光火

令人欣慰的是，与特朗普不同，拜登支持基于规则的全球贸易体系。他不会破坏世贸组织，也不会刻意与他想团结起来对抗XX的美国盟友作对。但是他的软贸易保护主义——多亏了共和党的特朗普化，这在国会比过去几十年都更受欢迎——将激怒外部世界;欧盟最近与XX达成了一项投资协议，已经对他的行政命令感到愤怒。在他对贸易经济的直觉上，美国新总统与他的前任并没有太大的不同。这对美国和世界来说都是坏消息。■
