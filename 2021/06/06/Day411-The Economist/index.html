The new geopolitics of global business

全球商业的新地缘政治

The world economy

世界经济

China and America dominate like never before

中美前所未有的主导地位

America and China now dominate global business. That is a wake-up call for other countries

中美正主导着全球商业，这给其他国家敲响了警钟

Jun 5th 2021

TWENTY YEARS ago this week the share price of a startup run by an obsessive called Jeff Bezos had slumped by 71% over 12 months. Amazon’s near-death experience was part of the dotcom crash that exposed Silicon Valley’s hubris and, along with the $14bn fraud at Enron, shattered confidence in American business. XX, meanwhile, was struggling to privatise its creaking state-owned firms, and there was little sign that it could create a culture of entrepreneurship. Instead the bright hope was in Europe, where a new single currency promised to catalyse a giant business-friendly integrated market.

hubris 傲慢自大

entrepreneurship：skill in starting new businesses, especially when this involves seeing new opportunities

20年前的这一周，一家初创公司的股价在过去的12个月里下跌了71%，这家公司的掌门人是一个叫杰夫·贝佐斯的痴迷者。亚马逊的濒死经历是互联网泡沫破裂的一部分，它暴露了硅谷的狂妄自大，再加上安然(Enron) 140亿美元的欺诈案，粉碎了人们对美国企业的信心。与此同时，XX正在努力将其摇摇欲坠的国有企业私有化，几乎没有迹象表明XX能够创造出一种创业文化。相反，光明的希望在欧洲，在那里，一种新的单一货币有望推动一个巨大的商业友好型一体化市场。

Creative destruction often makes predictions look silly, but even by these standards the post-pandemic business world is dramatically different from what you might have expected two decades ago. Tech firms comprise a quarter of the global stock­ market and the geographic mix has become strikingly lopsided. America and, increasingly, China are ascendant, accounting for 76 of the world’s 100 most valuable firms. Europe’s tally has fallen from 41 in 2000 to 15 today.

lopsided 向一侧倾斜的

ascendant 占优势的

创造性破坏常常让预测看起来很愚蠢，但即使按照这些标准，大流行后的商业世界也可能与你20年前的预期大相径庭。科技公司占据了全球股市的四分之一，而且地理分布已经变得惊人地不平衡。美国和中国日益崛起，在全球100家最有价值的公司中占了76家。欧洲的这一数字已经从2000年的41个下降到今天的15个。

This imbalance in large part reflects American and Chinese skill, and complacency in Europe and elsewhere. It raises two giant questions: why has it come about? And can it last?

complacency 自满

come about 发生

这种不平衡很大程度上反映了美国和中国的技术水平，以及欧洲和其他地方的自满情绪。这提出了两个巨大的问题:为什么会出现这种情况? 这种情况会持续下去吗?

In themselves, big companies are no better than small ones. Japan Inc’s status soared in the 1980s only to collapse. Big firms can be a sign of success but also of sloth. Saudi Aramco, the world’s second-most-valuable firm, is not so much a $2trn symbol of vigour as of a desert kingdom’s dangerous dependency on fossil fuels. Even so, the right sort of giant company is a sign of a healthy business ecology in which big, efficient firms are created and constantly swept away by competition. It is the secret to raising long-run living standards.

sweep away 卷走；彻底消除

就其本身而言，大公司并不比小公司好多少。上世纪80年代，日本公司的地位一路飙升，但最终却倒闭了。大公司可能是成功的标志，但也可能是懒惰的标志。沙特阿拉伯国家石油公司，世界上第二大最有价值的公司，与其说它是一个2万亿美元的活力象征，不如说是一个沙漠王国对化石燃料的危险依赖。即便如此，一个合适的巨型公司是一个健康商业生态的标志，在这种生态中，大型、高效的公司被创造出来，并不断地被竞争淘汰。这是提高长期生活水平的秘诀。

One way of capturing the dominance of America and XX is to compare their share of world output with their share of business activity (defined as the average of their share of global stockmarket capitalisation, public-offering proceeds, venture-capital funding, “unicorns”—or larger private startups, and the world’s biggest 100 firms). By this yardstick America accounts for 24% of global GDP, but 48% of business activity. XX accounts for 18% of GDP, and 20% of business. Other countries, with 77% of the world’s people, punch well below their weight.

yardstick 衡量标准

punch 力量

要想了解美国和XX的统治地位，一种方式就是比较它们占全球经济产出的比重与它们在商业活动中的份额(其定义为它们在全球股票市场资本、公开发行收益、风险资本融资、“独角兽”或更大的私有初创企业以及世界上最大的100家公司中所占份额的平均值)。按照这个标准，美国GDP占全球的24%，但商业活动占48%。XX占GDP的18%，占商业的20%。其他人口占世界人口77%的国家，其表现远低于其权重占比应有水平。

Part of the explanation is Europe’s squandered opportunity. Political meddling and the debt crisis in 2010-12 have stalled the continent’s economic integration. Firms there largely failed to anticipate the shift towards the intangible economy. Europe has no startups to rival Amazon or Google. But other countries have struggled, too. A decade ago Brazil, Mexico and India were poised to create a large cohort of global firms. Few have emerged.

squander 浪费

部分原因是欧洲错失了机会。政治干预和2010- 2012年的债务危机阻碍了欧洲大陆的经济一体化。那里的企业基本上没有预料到向无形经济的转变。欧洲没有能与亚马逊或谷歌匹敌的初创公司。但其他国家也在苦苦挣扎。十年前，巴西、墨西哥和印度准备创建一大批全球公司。几乎没有公司展露头脚。

Instead, only America and China have been able to marshal the process of creative destruction. Of the 19 firms created in the past 25 years that are now worth over $100bn, nine are in America and eight in China. Europe has none. Even as mature tech giants like Apple and Alibaba try to entrench their dominance, a new set of tech firms including Snap, PayPal, Meituan and Pinduoduo are reaching critical mass. The pandemic has seen a burst of energy in America and China and a boom in fundraising. Firms from the two countries dominate the frontier of new technologies such as fintech and and electric cars.

marshal 引领

critical mass：the size that something needs to reach before a particular change, event, or development can happen

相反，只有美国和中国能够引领创造性破坏的过程。在过去25年创建的、目前价值超过1000亿美元的19家公司中，9家在美国，8家在中国。欧洲没有一家。就在苹果和阿里巴巴等成熟科技巨头试图巩固其主导地位之际，Snap、PayPal、美团和拼多多等一批新的科技公司正在达到（一定数量级的）临界规模。这场疫情在美国和中国迸发了一股能量，并引发了资金筹集的热潮。这两个国家的公司主导着金融科技和电动汽车等新技术领域的前沿。

The magic formula has many ingredients. A vast home market helps firms achieve scale quickly. Deep capital markets, networks of venture capitalists and top universities keep the startup pipeline full. There is a culture that exalts entrepreneurs. XX’s tycoons boast of their “996” work ethic: 9am to 9pm, six days a week. Elon Musk sleeps on Tesla’s factory floor. Above all politics supports creative destruction. America has long tolerated more disruption than cosy Europe. After 2000, XXa’s rulers let entrepreneurs run riot and laid off 8m workers at state firms.

run riot 撒野；疯涨

这个神奇的（占主导地位的）“配方”有很多成分构成。广阔的国内市场有助于企业迅速扩大规模。深厚的资本市场、风险投资家的网络和顶尖大学让初创公司的渠道不断爆满。有一种推崇企业家的文化。XX的巨头们夸耀自己的“996”职业道德:每天早上9点到晚上9点，每周工作6天。埃隆·马斯克睡在特斯拉工厂的地板上。最重要的是，政治支持创造性破坏。长期以来，美国比舒适的欧洲能够容忍更多的混乱。2000年之后，XX的统治者放任企业家横行，并在国有企业解雇了800万工人。

The recent erosion of this political consensus in both countries is one reason this dominance could prove unsustainable. Americans are worried about national decline, as well as low wages and monopolies (roughly a quarter of the S&P 500 index merits antitrust scrutiny, we estimated in 2018). The Economist supports the Biden administration’s aim to promote competition and expand the social safety-net to protect workers hurt by disruption. But the danger is that America continues to drift towards protectionism, industrial policy and, on the left, punitive taxes on capital, that dampen its business vim.

merit 值得

punitive 苛刻的

vim 活力

两国最近这种政治共识的削弱，是这种主导地位可能不可持续的原因之一。美国人担心国家衰落，以及低薪和垄断(我们在2018年估计，大约四分之一的标准普尔500指数公司值得反垄断审查)。《经济学人》支持拜登政府促进竞争和扩大社会保障网络的目标，以保护因生产中断而受到伤害的工人。但危险在于，美国继续向保护主义、产业政策和(左派的)苛刻的资本税的方向漂移，这些都会抑制其商业活力。

In XX President XXX sees big private firms as a threat to the XX Party’s power and social stability. The cowing of tycoons began last year with XX, the co-founder of XXXX, and has since spread to the bosses of three other big tech firms. As party officials seek to “guide” incumbent private firms in order to achieve policy goals, such as national self-sufficiency in some technologies, they are also more likely to protect them from freewheeling competitors.

cow 恐吓

incumbent 在任的

freewheel 随心所欲地行动

在XX，XXX将大型私营企业视为对XXX权力和社会稳定的威胁。对巨头们的恐吓从去年XXXX的联合创始人XX开始，后来蔓延到其他三家大型科技公司的老板。由于官员试图“指导”现有的私营企业以实现政策目标，比如国家在某些技术上的自给自足，他们也更有可能保护它们免受随心所欲的竞争者的影响。

The more America and XX intervene, the more the rest of the world should worry about the lopsided geography of global business. In theory the nationality of profit-seeking firms does not matter: as long as they sell competitive products and create jobs, who cares? But if firms are swayed by governments at home, the calculus changes.

calculus 权衡

美国和XX干预得越多，世界其他国家就越应该担心全球商业地理的不平衡。从理论上讲，追求利润的公司的国籍并不重要:只要他们销售有竞争力的产品并能够创造就业机会，谁在乎呢?但如果公司受到国内政府的影响，这种权衡就会改变。

As globalisation unwinds, rows are already erupting over where multinational firms produce vaccines, set digital rules and pay taxes. European hopes of being a regulatory superpower may become a figleaf for protectionism. Others with less clout may erect barriers. To assert its sovereignty, India has banned XX social media and hobbled American e-commerce firms. That is the worst of both worlds, depriving local consumers of global innovations and creating barriers that make it even harder for local firms to achieve scale.

figleaf 遮羞布

随着全球化进程的展开，关于跨国公司在哪里生产疫苗、制定数字规则和纳税的争论已经爆发。欧洲想要成为监管超级大国的希望可能会成为贸易保护主义的遮羞布。其他影响力较小的国家可能会设立障碍。为了维护自己的主权，印度禁止了XX的社交媒体，并让美国的电子商务公司陷入困境。这是两个世界中最糟糕的，它剥夺了当地消费者对全球创新能力，并制造了障碍，使当地企业更难实现规模。

It’s the acorns, not the oaks

是橡子而非橡树

oaks from little acorns grow 参天大树，生于毫末

It would be a tragedy if only two countries in the world proved capable of sustaining a process of creative destruction at scale. But it would be even worse if they turned away from it, and other places admitted defeat and put up barricades. The best gauge of success will be if in 20 years’ time the list of the world’s biggest companies looks absolutely nothing like today’s. ■

如果世界上只有两个国家被证明有能力维持大规模的创造性破坏进程，那将是一场悲剧。但如果他们放弃，并且其他地方承认失败，竖起路障，那就更糟了。衡量成功的最佳标准是，20年后的全球最大公司名单是否与今天的完全不同。■
