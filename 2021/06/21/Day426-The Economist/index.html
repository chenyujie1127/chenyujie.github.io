Joe Biden’s summit with Vladimir Putin yielded only modest gains

拜登和普京的峰会只取得有限胜利

Worth the air miles

值得飞行里程

![image-20210621101245834](/Users/chenyujie/Library/Application Support/typora-user-images/image-20210621101245834.png)

But it beats the turbulence of the recent past

但它解决了最近的动荡问题

Jun 19th 2021

THE AIM, President Joe Biden said, before setting off on a European tour that ended in a summit with his Russian counterpart on June 16th, was to “make it clear to Putin and XX that Europe and the United States are tight”. He achieved that, more or less. After meetings of the G7 and NATO and an EU-American pow-wow, it is clear that the rich countries of Europe and North America (and Japan) share common concerns about the two great autocracies that confront them. Whereas Donald Trump showed a strange fondness for dictators, Mr Biden is doing a fair job of uniting democracies to call the autocrats out.

pow-wow 讨论会；商谈

美国总统拜登在启程前往欧洲访问之前表示，此行的目的是“向普京和XX明确表明，欧洲和美国关系密切”。这场旅行也以6月16日拜登与俄罗斯总统的峰会结束。他或多或少的实现了这一目标。在七国集团、北约和欧美会议之后，很明显，欧洲和北美(以及日本)的发达国家对他们所面临的两个大专制国家有着共同的担忧。特朗普对独裁者表现出了奇怪的喜爱，而拜登在团结民主国家、赶出独裁者方面做得不错。

Mr Putin and Mr Biden arrived in Geneva without much room for manoeuvre. Mr Biden wants to look tough on Russia, but finds himself constrained. He needs Russian help on a range of issues, including climate change, keeping nukes out of Iran, ending the war in Syria, forging new arms-control treaties and, perhaps most of all, avoiding an escalation of the conflict in Ukraine that might suck America in.

manoeuvr 用策略

nuke 核武器

arms control treaty/talks 军控条约/谈判

escalation 恶化；加剧；上涨；增加

普京和拜登抵达日内瓦时没有多少回旋余地。拜登想对俄罗斯采取强硬态度，但却发现自己受到了约束。在一系列问题上，他需要俄罗斯的帮助，比如气候变化、阻止伊朗使用核武器、结束叙利亚战争、缔结新的军控条约，或许最重要的是，避免乌克兰冲突升级，这可能会使美国卷入其中。

Mr Putin is constrained, too. He faces dissent at home, and would gain from a relaxation of economic sanctions. Russia has turned itself into a “fortress economy” to withstand these, but that makes life harder for ordinary Russians. Mr Biden cannot give him such relief, so long as the repression that is necessary to Mr Putin’s political survival goes on, and in the absence of a humiliating withdrawal from Ukraine.

普京也受到了约束。他在国内面临异议，并将从放松经济制裁中获益。俄罗斯已经把自己变成了一个“堡垒经济”以承受住这些，但这让普通俄罗斯人的生活更加艰难。只要对普京政治生存所必需的镇压继续下去，而且没有屈辱性地从乌克兰撤军，拜登就无法让普京松一口气。

Still, they talked for four hours, and both men agreed that it had been constructive. After the summit, Mr Biden put the issues into three buckets. The first is those areas where America and Russia share common interests and can work together. These include climate change, Iran, the Arctic and arms control, where there was an outline agreement to restart stalled talks. Both sides agreed to return their ambassadors, who were recalled earlier in the year, after Mr Biden called Mr Putin a “killer”. On these issues grown-up, plodding diplomacy will resume.

plod 沉重缓慢的行进

尽管如此，他们还是谈了四个小时，两人都认为会谈是有建设性的。峰会结束后，拜登将问题分为三个部分。第一个部分的领域是美国和俄罗斯拥有共同利益并可以合作的领域。这些问题包括气候变化、伊朗、北极和军备控制。在这些问题上，双方达成了重启陷入僵局的谈判的纲要协议。在拜登称普京是“杀手”后，双方同意送还今年早些时候被召回的大使。在这些问题上，成熟的、缓慢的外交将重新开始。

The second bucket is trickier. Mr Biden warned Mr Putin that America will protect its own interests and those of allies, over cyber-attacks or the territorial integrity of Ukraine. The trouble is that no one knows how far Mr Biden would be willing to go, and Mr Putin simply denies everything. He insists, for example, that Russia, not America, is the main victim of hacking.

第二个部分比较棘手。拜登警告普京，美国将在网络攻击和乌克兰领土完整问题上保护自己和盟友的利益。问题是，没人知道拜登愿意走多远，而普京则对一切予以否认。例如，他坚持认为，黑客攻击的主要受害者是俄罗斯，而不是美国。

The third area is human rights, where Mr Biden says America will always defend universal values and warned of “devastating consequences” if Alexei Navalny, an opposition leader, should die in prison. However, by putting them in their own bucket, he hinted that human rights are not the reddest of red lines. Critics will call this détente at best, a sell-out at worst. Others will see it as sensible. Relations with Russia are too important to be seen only through one lens.

第三个方面是人权。拜登表示，美国将始终捍卫普世价值，并警告说，如果反对派领导人Alexei Navalny死在监狱里，将面临“灾难性后果”。然而，他通过把这个问题放在他们自己的桶里，以暗示人权并不是最红色的红线。评论家们往好了说这是缓和，往坏了说这是出卖。其他人会认为这是合理的。与俄罗斯的关系太重要了，不能只通过一个镜头来看待。

Mr Putin may welcome a breathing space, especially if he is, more or less, left alone within his borders. If he continues troublemaking, it is unclear how much stomach Mr Biden has for a fight. Like Mr Trump and Barack Obama, he rightly sees XX, not Russia, as the urgent challenge. He would love to leave Russia to the Europeans, letting America devote its full attention to Asia. But the Europeans are not up to the task. They are divided among themselves and parsimonious.

parsimonious 吝啬的

普京可能会乐于接受一个喘息的空间，尤其是如果他或多或少被留在自己的疆域内。如果他继续制造麻烦，目前还不清楚拜登有多大的斗志。与特朗普和奥巴马一样，他正确地将XX(而非俄罗斯)视为紧迫的挑战。他很想把俄罗斯留给欧洲人，让美国把全部注意力放在亚洲。但是欧洲人不能胜任这项任务。他们各自为政还非常吝啬。

What is left if finger-wagging achieves little and it is too hard to restrain autocracies? Mr Biden talks of outcompeting them. Yet the G7 summit missed a chance to show leadership, pledging to donate fewer than 1bn doses of covid-19 vaccine to the less fortunate, a tenth of what is needed. It also failed to add flesh to its grand ideas about a Build Back Better World (an infrastructure plan to compete with XX’s Belt and Road Initiative). Outdoing Russia economically is not hard, but XX is a different matter. Mr Biden’s domestic agenda is hobbled by congressional gridlock and deep political polarisation. America will find that contest a more severe one. ■

如果指指点点收效甚微，又很难约束独裁，那还剩下什么呢? 拜登谈到要战胜他们。然而，七国集团(G7)峰会错失了展示领导力的机会，承诺向较不幸的人捐赠不到10亿剂covid-19疫苗，这仅为所需的十分之一。它也未能为其“重建更美好世界”(一个与XX“一带一路”倡议相竞争的基础设施计划)的宏伟构想增添实质内容。在经济上超越俄罗斯并不困难，但XX是另一回事。拜登的国内议程因国会僵局和严重的政治两极化而受阻。美国将会发现这场竞争更加激烈。■
